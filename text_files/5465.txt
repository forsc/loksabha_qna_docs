                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5000






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2019






National Action Plan Vision 2022




Sukanta Majumdar













                                                    Raja Amareshwara Naik
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether it is a fact that the Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is working on a National Action Plan Vision 2022 and if so, the details thereof;(b) the details of the various schemes being implemented by the Government for the development of animal husbandry, dairy and poultry in various States of the country during the last three years and the current year;(c) the total number of farmers who have been benefited by these schemes so far;(d) whether it is a fact that there is huge potential for growth of animal husbandry and dairy industry in India and if so, the details thereof and the details of study conducted by the Government in this regard; and(e) the efforts made by the Government in this regard and the development work executed by the Government in this direction out of these efforts? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(DR. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a)	and (b) The Government of India has formulated a National Action Plan 2022 and the following schemes being implemented during the last three years and the current year in various States/UTs of the country as per objectives of National Action Plan.i.	Rashtriya Gokul Mission(RGM)ii.	National Programme for Dairy Development (NPDD)iii.	National Dairy Plan-I(NDP-I) iv.	Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS)v.	National Livestock Mission(NLM)vi.	Livestock Health & Disease Control(LH&DC)vii.	Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries(c) Rashtriya Gokul Mission is not a beneficiary oriented scheme. However, benefits of the scheme are being accrued to all farmers engaged in rearing of cattle and buffaloes in the form enhancement in milk productivity. Under NPDD scheme, 2.59 lakh additional milk producers have been enrolled as on 18.07.2019 whereas under NDP-I, 15.7 lakh additional milk producers have been enrolled till March 2019. Further, 3.69 lakh dairy entrepreneurs have been benefitted under DEDS scheme till 31.03.2019. Under NLM, the total number of farmers who has been benefitted is annexed. Livestock Health & Disease Control is also not a beneficiary oriented scheme.(d) and (e) There is huge potential for growth of animal husbandry and dairy industry in India. As per 19th Livestock Census-2012, there are approximately 190.9 million cattle, 108.7 million buffaloes, 65.07 million sheep, 135.17 million Goats and 10.29 million Pigs in India. The milk production in the country in 2016-17 was 165.4 million tonnes which has increased to 176.3 million tonne in 2017-18 and has registered an annual growth rate of 6.6% in 2017-18. In order to complement and supplement the efforts of the States/UTs  for development of animal husbandry and dairying sectors, the Government of India is implementing various schemes, namely, Rashtriya Gokul Mission, National Programme for Dairy Development, Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme, National Dairy Plan-I, National Livestock Mission and Livestock Health & Disease Control.***
