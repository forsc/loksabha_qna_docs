                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

586






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2021






Insurance Cover for Drone




K. Shanmuga Sundaram














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of CIVIL AVIATIONbe pleased to state:-(a) whether the Government proposes to bring comprehensive insurance cover for drones under aviation liability instead of following Motor Vehicles Act;(b) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor;(c) whether the drone operators are having a minimum third-party liability at present which is insufficient to meet the claims in case of major accident to people and if so, the details thereof;(d) whether the Government also proposes to increase the third-party liability at par with aviation or aircraft liability; and(e) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) to (e) As per Unmanned Aircraft System (UAS) Rules, 2021, all unmanned aircraft except the nano class shall be operated in India with a valid third party insurance policy to cover the liability that may arise on account of a mishap involving such unmanned aircraft and causing death or bodily injury to any person or damage to property.The compensation payable in such cases shall be assessed in such a manner and procedure as specified in the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 and rules made there under.
