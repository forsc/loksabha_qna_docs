                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1063






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2021






Waterway in Bundelkand




Pushpendra Singh Chandel














                        Will the Minister of





PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government proposes to start waterways through rivers, especially in the Yamuna and its tributaries in Bundelkhand; and (b)	if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        (a) & (b)  	Yamuna river stretching from Jagatpur (6 km upstream of Wazirabad Barrage) in Delhi to the confluence of the Ganga and Yamuna rivers at Sangam, Prayagraj in the State of Uttar Pradesh for a length of 1089 km has been declared National Waterway-110 (NW-110) by the National Waterways Act, 2016.   As per Detailed Project Report (DPR), the River has not been found viable for cargo movement.   River Betwa (NW-19), a tributary of Yamuna in Bundelkhand has been declared a NW for a stretch of 67 Km.   As per DPR, the river has not been found viable.                                                *****
