                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2626






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.08.2021






Railway Projects in Odisha




Manjulata Mandal














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Railways is considering to sanction the missing link projects namely Bangiriposi-Gorumahisani in Mayurbhanj District and Buramara-Chakulia in Odisha and Jharkhand to provide the much needed connectivity to augment the existing Railway network;                                (b)  if so, the details thereof; and(c) whether the Government of Odisha has offered land free of cost for the Odisha portion of the above projects to make them viable considering greater interest of the people and the State?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMUNICATIONS AND               ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI ASHWINI VAISHNAW)(a) to (c):  Proposals of two new lines connectivity namely Buramara - Chakulia (55 km) and Bangriposi - Gorumahisani (62 km) have been examined.  Buramara - Chakulia new line has been found preferable to Bangriposi – Gorumahisani new line due to its better alignment, passing through populous region and flat terrain.  State Government of  Odisha has offered to provide land free of cost in both the projects alignments for the Odisha portion.Buramara - Chakulia Road New line project (55 Km) has been identified by Odisha Rail Infrastructure Development Limited (ORIDL) a Joint Venture Company of Government of Odisha (51%) and Ministry of Railways (49%) for development. The proposal of ORIDL has been taken up for grant of in-principle approval for taking up pre-investment activities.*****
