                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1116






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2021






Eligibility for NET




Arvind Kumar Sharma














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has prescribed minimum percentage of marks in post graduate degrees to appear in National Eligibility Test (NET) as a benchmark for competence of students for being a lecturer;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government has taken note that this limit of minimum percentage has deprived many students to appear in NET;(d) if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government thereto?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION (SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) to (d): Under the provisions of the University Grants Commission (UGC) Act, 1956, the UGC is mandated to take all steps for the promotion and co-ordination of University education and for determination and maintenance of standards of teaching, examination and research in Universities.The UGC has informed that General/Unreserved/General-EWS candidates who have secured at least 55% marks (without rounding off) in Master’s degree or equivalent examination from universities/institutions recognized by UGC in Humanities and Social Science (including languages), Computer Science and Applications, Electronic Science etc. are eligible to appear in UGC-NET. Relaxation of 5% is allowed for candidates belonging to Other Backward Classes (Non-Creamy Layer), Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Persons with Disability and Third gender.******
