                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

772






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2022






Bank Credit Schemes




Badruddin Ajmal














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the schemes being run by different banks in different States for the purpose of providing credit to unemployed youths at present;(b)	the details of the number of beneficiaries of such self-employed schemes during the last three years, State/Bank-wise; (c)	whether the Government provides any assistance to banks for such schemes; and  (d)	if so, the details thereof?   






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FINANCE(DR. BHAGWAT KARAD)(a) to (d): Banks provide loans under various credit-linked schemes of Government, under which unemployed youth may also avail credit facilities, for productive purposes. Unemployed youth, amongst others, may avail credit facilities for self-employment under various Government run schemes, which include, inter alia, the following —i.	Prime Minister Employment Generation Program (PMEGP) to bring together widely dispersed traditional artisans/ rural and urban unemployed youth and give them self-employment opportunities to the extent possible, at their place, wherein financial assistance is provided to the borrowers in the form of margin money subsidy of upto 35% of the project cost;ii.	Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM) scheme through its Self Employment Program (SEP) for providing financial assistance through easy access to credit to individuals/groups including street vendors /hawkers of urban poor for setting up gainful self-employment ventures /micro-enterprises, suited to their skills, training, aptitude and local conditions, wherein financial assistance is provided in the form of interest subvention of interest charged above 7%, with an additional interest subvention of 3% available on prompt repayment;iii.	Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM) scheme enables bank credit access to eligible beneficiaries for productive purposes on all credit from banks/financial institutions availed under the scheme, wherein financial assistance is provided in the form of interest subvention of interest charged above 7% subject to a maximum of 5.5%, with an additional interest subvention of 3% available on prompt repayment;iv.	Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojaya (PMMY) to create an inclusive, sustainable and value based entrepreneurial culture, in collaboration with our partner institutions in achieving economic success and financial security, wherein financial assistance is provided in the form of interest subvention of 2% for a period of 12 months for credit availed under Shishu loan category of the scheme and credit guarantee cover of National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company Limited (NCGTC) for all categories of loans availed under the scheme; andv.	Stand-up India to promote entrepreneurship at grass root level for economic empowerment and job creation, wherein financial assistance is provided in the form of margin money subsidy upto 15% of the project cost through Credit Linked Capital Subsidy Scheme for Technology Upgradation along with various other subsidies available under different schemes of Government and credit guarantee cover of NCGTC.Further, as per inputs from Public Sector Banks, the bank-wise and state-wise details of number of total beneficiaries under above mentioned five schemes for last three years is as per Annex. *** AnnexNumber of total beneficiaries in last three years viz.
