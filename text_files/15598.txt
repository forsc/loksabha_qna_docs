                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4285






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






Grid Connected Solar and Wind Power Plants




Narayana Swamy Abbaiah














                        Will the Minister of





NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the grid connected solar and wind power plants which have come up during the last five years, State-wise;(b) ) whether the plants are utilizing their full capacity;(c ) if so, the details of installed capacity of power generation and actual power generated by such plants; and(d) if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (I/C) FOR NEW & RENEWABLE ENERGY, POWER and MoS for  SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP(SHRI R.K. SINGH)(a) The State-wise details of the grid connected solar and wind power plants, which have come up during the last five years, are given in Annexure-I and Annexure-II respectively(b) to  (d) Since, most of the solar and wind power plants are set up with private sector investment, as such it is imperative for them to utilise their full capacity so as to have financial viability of the plants.  As on 29.02.2020, cumulative solar and wind power installed capacity was 35065 MW and  37669 MW respectively. As per, Central Electricity Authority the solar and wind power projects generated 34.80 BU and 54.40 BU respectively from April 2019 to December 2019.*****
