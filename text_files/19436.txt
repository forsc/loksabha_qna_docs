                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

889






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Ayurvedic Hospital/Medical College




Keshari Devi Patel













                                                    Kanakmal Katara
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) the number of Ayurvedic Hospitals and Medical Colleges which are providing medical facilities and education across the country as on date, State/UT-wise;(b) whether the Government has derecognized almost 50 percent Ayurvedic hospitals/dispensaries and medical colleges, if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise;(c) whether such derecognized Ayurvedic Medical Colleges were not complying to the norms/owing to complaints of fraud/lack of quality education being imparted and if so, the details thereof;(d) the names of the Ayurvedic Medical Colleges which were recognized this year along with the terms of recognition and the details thereof, college-wise; and(e) whether there is any plan to attract international students towards Ayurvedic Medical Colleges?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS AND ADDITIONAL CHARGE OF MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(SHRI KIREN RIJIJU)(a): 	  As on 01.04.2018, there were 3205 Ayurvedic Hospitals in the country. Out of 3205 Ayurvedic Hospitals, 19 Hospitals are under CGHS and Central Government Organizations. As on 27.01.2021 there are 414 Ayurvedic Medical Colleges functioning in the country. State/UT-wise detail on number of Ayurvedic hospitals and Ayurvedic Medical Colleges is furnished at Annexure I.(b):   No, At present there are only 12 Ayurvedic Medical Colleges derecognized under the section 21 of the Indian Medicine Central Council (IMCC) Act 1970. State-wise detail of derecognized Medical Colleges is furnished at Annexure II. However, Public health being a state subject, the details of derecognized Ayurvedic hospitals/dispensaries, State/UT wise, is not maintained by Ministry of AYUSH. (c):	All these derecognized Colleges are not fulfilling the standard prescribed      under the Indian Medicine Central Council (IMCC) Act 1970.(d):	Degree of 11 Ayurvedic Medical Colleges are recognized in this year (2020-21) by Ministry of AYUSH. The details thereof is furnished at Annexure III(A). Information regarding related Gazette notifications is furnished at Annexure III(B).(e):  Ministry of AYUSH under its Fellowship Scheme extends financial support to the eligible foreign nationals for undertaking degree courses of AYUSH systems including Ayurveda in premier Institutes in India. The Scheme provides ample opportunity for promotion and propagation of AYUSH systems across the globe by attracting international students towards attaining education in AYUSH systems of medicine. The Scheme is implemented through Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR). 104 scholarships are offered every year to eligible foreign nationals for pursuing UG, PG and Ph.D. courses in various AYUSH Institutes in India. The Fellowship Scheme aims at achieving recognition and acceptance of our traditional medicine systems abroad.
