                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3964






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Incidence of Poaching/Death of Wild Animals




Badruddin Ajmal














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether a large number of cases related to poaching/killing/death of rhinoceros and elephants in Assam have come to the notice of the Government;(b)	if so, the number of such cases registered from 2017 onwards, till date;(c)	whether a number  of  people have also been killed by wild elephants in Assam in recent times;(d)	if so, the details of such incidences occurred from 2017 onwards, till date, year-wise and incident-wise;(e)	the details of the financial help provided to the victims of families by the Government; and(f)	the steps taken by the Government to control the incidents of poaching and death of rhinoceros?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	and(b)	As informed by the Government of Assam, 103 cases related to poaching/killing/death of Rhinoceros and Elephants in Assam have been registered from 2017 onwards, till date.(c) and (d)	The details of people killed by wild elephants in Assam from the year 2017 onwards year-wise and incident-wise are at Annexure- 1. (e)	The details of the financial help provided to the victims of families by the Assam Government is at Annexure- 2.(f)	The important steps taken by the Government to control the incidents of poaching and death of rhinoceros include:i.	Ministry has initiated an important Rhino conservation programe called ‘Rhino DNA Indexing System’ or ‘RhoDIS’ to counter poaching threats and aid population management. ii.	Special Rhino Protection Force of 82 personnel has been created in Kaziranga National Park with the help of NTCA. This force is trained to combat the problem of poaching of rhinoceros, tigers, etc. in Kaziranga.iii.	Electronic Eye is set up with 8 nos. of tower location for visual monitoring of suspicious movement of Rhino Poachers.iv.	A Special Task force has been working since 2012 and still in place.v.	Besides the regular patrolling mechanism, 154 anti-poaching camps with armed guard are inside the Park for protection of Rhino and other wildlife.vi.	Training and capacity building courses are organized for the three tiger reserves of Assam. These training courses help in better protection of rhinoceros also.vii.	Local villagers are sensitized by organizing awareness programmes regularly.viii.	WhatsApp group of the coordination committees has been created in each of the elephant corridors of Assam to prevent the death of wild animals.ix.	Control room has been setup at the State Headquarter and DFOs level with toll free phone to receive messages/complaints on 24x7 basis on elephant/other animal depredation problems,.x.	Wildlife Crime Control Bureau coordinates with State/UTs and other enforcement agencies to gather intelligence to preventpoachingof wild animals including rhinoceros.	*****
