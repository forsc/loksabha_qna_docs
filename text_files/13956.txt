                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2747






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.03.2020






Traditional Weavers




Faizal P.P. Mohammed














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware that clusters of traditional weavers still exist in Southern India, especially in Tamil Nadu and Kerala, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether there are any Centrally funded schemes for modernization of their machines and marketing of their products, if so, the details thereof; (c) whether the Government is considering the design intervention of National Institute of Design in all such centres in a phased manner so as to preserve the traditional crafts for the future; and (d) if so, the details along with the steps taken in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF TEXTILES(SMT. SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a):	The Government of India has conducted 4th all India Handloom Census during 2019-20. As per this Census, number of Handloom weavers and allied workers  in Southern States of India are  as follows:-Sl.No.	Name of State	Number ofWeavers	Number ofAllied Workers	Total1	Andhra Pradesh	1,27,662	49,785	1,77,4472	Telangana	25,930	21,922	47,8523	Tamilnadu	1,97,818	45,757	2,43,5754	Karnataka	27,175	27,616	54,7915	Kerala 	15,480	6,604	22,084Total	3,94,065	1,51,684	5,45,749(b): Ministry of Textiles is implementing Block Level Clusters under National Handloom Development Programme (NHDP) and Comprehensive Handloom Cluster Development Scheme (CHCDS) to extend financial assistance to handloom weavers for purchase of Hathkargha Samvardhan Sahayata (HSS) items i.e. Looms, Jacquard, Dobby etc. to upgrade their technology, improve quality of the fabric and enhance productivity.   Under the Handloom Marketing Assistance (HMA), a component of National Handloom Development Programme (NHDP), marketing platform is provided to the handloom weavers/agencies to sell their products directly to the consumers, by organizing various expos/marketing events in domestic as well as overseas markets. (c) & (d): In order to provide design support to the Handloom weavers, financial assistance is provided in the sanctioned Block Level Clusters for engagement of designers qualified from reputed institutions including National Institute of Design (NID)/National Institute of Fashion Technology (NIFT). ***
