                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1850






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.02.2021






Textile Training Centres




Umesh G. Jadhav













                                                    Sunil Baburao Mendhe
                                                








                                                    Feroze Varun Gandhi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) the number of beneficiaries under the Integrated Skill Development Scheme during the year      2019-2020 and as on December 2020, State-wise including Maharashtra;(b) the total number of textile training centres which have been registered and functioning across the country, under Samarth Scheme State-wise including Maharashtra, Karnataka and Uttar Pradesh; (c) the budget allocated and utilized for this purpose, State-wise; (d) whether the Ministry of Textiles has partnered with government agencies from various States and sectoral organizations for entry level training and job creation in both traditional and organized segments of textile value chain; and (e) if so, the details thereof, State-wise especially in the State of Maharashtra? 






ANSWER




MINISTER OF TEXTILES(SMT. SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a):	Integrated Skill Development Scheme (ISDS) was implemented till 2018. Presently, Ministry of Textiles is implementing Samarth Scheme for Capacity Building including skill development in Textile Sector.(b):	Details of training centres under Samarth, State wise, including Maharashtra, Karnataka and Uttar Pradesh is given in Annexure-I.(c):	Under Samarth scheme, the cost of training programme is released directly to Implementing Partners (IP). Details of budget allocation and utilization during the financial year 2019-20 and the current year is as below:                                                                                                                    (Rs. in crore)Financial Year	Budget Estimate (BE)	Fund utilized2019-20	100.5	72.062020-21	150	57.78 (until January, 2021)(d) & (e):	Thirteen (13) State Government Agencies have partnered with Ministry of Textiles for conducting training under Samarth Scheme, details of which are given in Annexure-II. The State Government of Maharashtra has not partnered with the Ministry of Textiles for implementing Samarth Scheme in the State. However, there are 43 (forty three) training centres in the State of Maharashtra set up by various textiles manufacturers for training under the Scheme.
