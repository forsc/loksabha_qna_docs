                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

562






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2021






Rural Areas Covered under Jal Jeevan Mission




Adoor Prakash














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of rural population covered under Jal Jeevan Mission so far, State-wise; (b) whether the Government has set any target for achieving the goals under Jal Jeevan Mission; (c) if so, the details thereof and the progress achieved by each State; and (d) the details of the funds released to the States including Kerala and the amount utilized so far?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL) (a) to (c)	Since August, 2019,Government of India, in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) – Har Ghar Jal, to provide tap water connection to every rural household by 2024, with an estimated outlay of Rs. 3.60 lakh Crore.At the time of announcement of Jal Jeevan Mission on 15th August, 2019, out of 18.93 Crore rural households, 3.23 Crore households (17%) were reported to have tap water connections. As on date, out of 18.98 Crore rural households across the country, 7.76 Crore households (40.89%) are reported to have potable tap water supply in their homes. The State/ UT-wise details of rural households having tap water supply is at Annex–I.(d) 	The details of the funds released and the amount utilized (including unspent balance of previous years) under JJM during 2019-20, 2020-21 & 2021-22 is at Annex–II.*****
