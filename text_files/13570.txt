                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2414






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.03.2020






Road Accidents




Ravneet Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether it is a fact that India has recorded the highest number of road accident deaths amongst 200 countries as per a recent World Road Statistics report; (b) 	if so, the details thereof along with number of road accidents in the country during the last two years, State/UT-wise; (c) 	whether the Government has taken any initiative or allocated/managed any funds through private participation for strengthening of road safety mechanism in the country; and (d)	 if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	&  (b)  As per World Road Statistics, 2018 report of Indian Road Federation, India ranks 1st in number of person killed in road accidents across the 199 countries. 		As per available information, total number of road accidents and the persons killed  in the country during the calendar year 2017 and 2018 are as under:- Year	Total Accidents	Number of persons killed   2017	   4,64,910	   1,47,913  2018	   4,67,044	  1,51,417The State/UTs wise of road accidents and persons killed in road accidents for the last two years are at Annexure.(c) & (d)    The Ministry of Road Transport & Highways has implemented scheme of setting of Institute of Driving Training and Research (IDTR) and Regional Driving Training Centre (RDTC). Under these schemes State Government, and/or any other agencies such as State Transport Undertakings, Transport Companies/ Associations, NGOs, Private Party / Automobile Associations/Vehicle Manufacturers’ Association/Autonomous Body/Private Vehicle Manufacturers authorized and recommended by the State Governments, in collaboration with the Government can send proposals. Besides, the Ministry also launched a scheme for setting up of Driving Training Centre (DTC) to provide quality training to commercial vehicle drivers, improve road and environment safety and strengthen overall mobility on roads. Any entity such as a State Undertaking/ NGO/ Trust/ Cooperative Society/ Vehicle Manufacturer/ Firm etc. i.e. any legal entity registered under a State or Central Government Law are eligible to apply under the scheme. Further, the ministry also implements a scheme of financial assistance to NGOs for administering road safety advocacy programmes to create awareness about road safety.
