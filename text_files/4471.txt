                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

367






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.07.2019






Delhi-Rajkot-Delhi Flight




Mohanbhai Kalyanji Kundariya














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has received representations to start additional flight operations for Delhi-Rajkot-Delhi sector and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has given concurrence to operate everyday an additional Red Eye/ Fly by night flight on Delhi-Rajkot-Delhi sector due to nonavailability of slots between 06.00 to 22.00 hrs. at New Delhi and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the slots at New Delhi are lying vacant due to a recently defunct airline;(d) if so, whether the Government will utilize the vacant slots at New Delhi to operate additional flight on Delhi- Rajkot-Delhi sector between 06.00 to 22.00 hrs.; and(e) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) to (e): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.Statement in reply to parts (a) to (e) of the Lok Sabha Starred (*) Question No. 367 for  18.07.2019 regarding ''Delhi-Rajkot-Delhi Flight''.;  ; (a) & (b): Yes, Sir. A representation has been received from the Hon''ble Member of Parliament Shri Mohanbhai Kundariya for starting operation to/from Rajkot with A-320 aircraft on various routes including Delhi-Rajkot-Delhi. Also, Airports Authority of India (AAI) had received request from Air India in January, 2019 for operation of additional flight at Delhi-Rajkot-Delhi sector with arrival time 0400 hours IST (Indian Standard Time) and departure at 0440 hours IST. The same was denied by AAI as the slot times requested by Air India at Rajkot Airport were beyond watch hours, which are 0630-0800 hours & 1600-1800 hours on Monday, Thursday, Saturday and 1600-2000 hours on Tuesday, Wednesday, Friday and Sunday.; ; (c): The Government has constituted a Slot Allocation Committee having representatives from DGCA, AAI and JV/Private Airport operators for allocation of domestic slots vacated by Jet Airways to other scheduled airlines on adhoc basis, in a most transparent and equitable manner. As per the Standard Operating Procedure (SoP) devised for the purpose, all the domestic slots of recently defunct airline at New Delhi airport have been allocated to other airlines and no slot is lying vacant. ; ; (d) & (e): Does not arise. However, Delhi International Airport Ltd. (DIAL) received a request from Air India on 8th July, 2019 for Delhi-Rajkot flight and DIAL has approved a 1250 hours departure and a 1645 hours arrival.
