                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2600






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

14.12.2021






Garib Kalyan Rojgar Abhiyaan




P. C. Mohan














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the expenditure made under Garib Kalyan Rojgar Abhiyaan (GKRA) so far;(b) 	the details about the infrastructure developed under GKRA;(c) 	whether the Government is planning to carry the programme beyond the initial mandates; and(d) 	the fresh steps taken by the Government to promote skilling and entrepreneurship for rural development?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a) to (c) In order to boost employment opportunities in the wake of COVID pandemic, Government of India had launched a campaign known as Garib Kalyan Rojgar Abhiyaan (GKRA) of 125 days on 20th June, 2020 to boost employment and livelihood opportunities for returnee migrant workers and similarly affected citizens in rural areas. The objectives of the Abhiyaan were to provide immediate employment and livelihood opportunities to the distressed, to saturate the villages with public infrastructure and creation of livelihood assets to boost the income generation activities by giving focus on 25 works {including works under Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (Mahatma Gandhi NREGS)} in 116 selected districts across 6 States of Bihar, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Rajasthan and Uttar Pradesh. A total of 12 Ministries/Departments of Government of India  participated in the Abhiyaan. The GKRA ended on 22nd October, 2020 and a total of 50.78 crore person-days employment were generated with a total expenditure of Rs. 39,293 crore during the Abhiyaan.  The details of achievements/works completed under the GKRA are given at Annexure. (d) Start-up Village Entrepreneurship Programme (SVEP), is a sub-scheme under the Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM) programme to support the Self-Help Groups (SHGs) and their family members to set-up small enterprises in the non-farm sector. SVEP develops an eco-system for enterprise development in the rural areas which consists of Community Enterprise Fund (CEF) for enterprise financing, Cadre of Community Resource Persons-Enterprise Promotion (CRP-EP) for providing Business Support Services comprising of preparation of business plans, trainings, market linkages etc. and dedicated Block Resource Center (BRC) for providing all necessary information to entrepreneurs. As on 31st October, 2021,  a total number of 232 project proposals and 158 Detailed Project Reports (DPR) have been approved covering 29 States and Union Territories.  There are two skill development programmes for rural youth under DAY-NRLM,  namely, Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushal Yojana (DDU-GKY) and Rural Self Employment Training Institutes (RSETIs). Both these programmes are aimed at increasing employability of rural youth either for wage or self-employment.
