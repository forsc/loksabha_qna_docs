                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.07.2019






Illegal Mining




Mansukhbhai Dhanjibhai Vasava














                        Will the Minister of





MINES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether illegal mining has caused huge losses to the national treasury; (b)	if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government thereto; (c)	whether the State Governments have been given the right to enact laws to prevent illegal mining; and (d) 	if so, the details thereof along with the details of the State Governments which 	have 	enacted laws in this regard so far?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF MINES, COAL AND PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS (SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI)(a) to (d): As per section 23C of the Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 (“MMDR Act 1957”), State Governments are empowered to make rules for prevention of illegal mining, transportation and storage of minerals for both major and minor minerals and for purposes connected therewith. Therefore, information regarding the cases of illegal mining is not maintained centrally. However, based on the information received from the State Governments in the quarterly returns on illegal mining submitted to Indian Bureau of Mines (a subordinate office under the Ministry), year-wise details of cases of illegal mining detected and action taken by State Governments are given in Annexure.
