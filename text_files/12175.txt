                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1093






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2020






Fluorosis




Magunta Sreenivasulu Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether many States are heavily affected with extreme fluoride in water causing fluorosis, if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise including Andhra Pradesh;(b) whether the Government implements a National Programme for Prevention and Control of Fluorosis, if so, the details thereof;(c) the number of persons affected by fluorosis, including the children suffering from dental, skeletal and non-skeletal fluorosis in the country, State/UT-wise; and(d) the steps taken by the Government to save the people from contaminated drinking water and from such diseases?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	Yes.  As per the latest data provided by the States in Integrated Management Information System (IMIS) of Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation, there are 7,161 fluoride affected habitations including 261 habitations from Andhra Pradesh.  Details are at Annexure-I.(b):	In order to tackle the problem of Fluorosis, the National Programme for Prevention and Control of Fluorosis (NPPCF) under the umbrella of National Health Mission (NHM) was initiated as a new health initiative in the 11th Five year Plan (2008-09).  At present, 157 districts of 19 States including 9 districts of Andhra Pradesh are covered under NPPCF. Under the programme, financial and technical support is provided to States/UTs including Andhra Pradesh to strengthen their healthcare systems based on the requirements posed by the States/UTs in their Programme Implementation Plans (PIPs) within their overall resource envelope. (c):	State/UT-wise details of the number of cases, including children, suffering from suspected Dental and Skeletal Fluorosis in the country is given at Annexure-II. No data for Non Skeletal Fluorosis is available.(d):	Government of India assists States by providing financial and technical assistance to provide potable water to rural population through the centrally sponsored scheme Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) of Department of Drinking Water & Sanitation. In March  2016,  Department of Drinking Water & Sanitation with  the  recommendation  of  NITI Aayog, released a sum ?1,000 Crore to various Arsenic & Fluoride  affected  States  for installation  of Community water  purification  plants and commissioning of piped water supply schemes.  Further, in March 2017, National Water Quality Sub-Mission (NWQSM) was launched as a part of National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP), which has now been subsumed under JJM, to provide safe drinking water to 27,544 Arsenic/ Fluoride affected rural habitations in the country.   In addition, to save the people from contaminated drinking water, the following steps are taken by the Government of India under the NPPCF Programme of Ministry of Health and Family Welfare:(i)	Comprehensive management of Fluorosis cases in the affected areas by concerned district authorities;(ii)	Capacity building for prevention, diagnosis and management of Fluorosis cases;(iii)	Health Education and Publicity to create awareness about fluorosis and its awareness;
