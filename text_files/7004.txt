                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

819






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.11.2019






Emergency Landing of Flight




Adala Prabhakara Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether an Indigo flight from Goa to Delhi made an emergency landing at Goa’s Dabolim Airport in the first week of October, 2019 due to engine trouble and if so, the details thereof; and(b) the steps being taken by DGCA to check the aircraft of the private airlines air-worthy and to avoid untoward incident to the passengers?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) On 29.09.2019, M/s Indigo while operating flight No. 6E-336 (Goa - Delhi) experienced engine failure (Engine No. 1). The snag appeared in engine during the climb. The crew shut-off the affected engine and requested for priority landing. Aircraft landed back at Goa and was grounded for further inspection. The aircraft was released for further flight after the replacement of the affected engine.(b) An Aircraft is maintained as per the detailed procedures/ guidelines and timelines provided by the manufacturer of the aircraft, which forms part of the approved maintenance programme of the operator. Thus the aircraft is maintained in an airworthy state by continuously following the approved maintenance programme. Whenever, an aircraft experiences technical snags during operation, the technical snags are corrected based on manufacturer''s guidelines before the aircraft is permitted to be released for operations.The Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) carries out Surveillance/Spot Checks of the airlines at regular intervals for ensuring that the aircraft are maintained as per manufacturer instructions. In case of violations, enforcement action is taken against the organization/personnel responsible for lapses.
