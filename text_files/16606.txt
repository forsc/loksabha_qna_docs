                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

449






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.09.2020






Modernisation of State Police Forces




M. K. Raghavan














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has drawn any plan for modernization of State Police force; (b) if so, whether the plan also includes installation of CCTV cameras across the towns and cities; (c) if so, the total amount released for installation of CCTV cameras, State-wise, during the last three years; (d) the reasons for most of these CCTVs becoming non-functional and the money spent by each State for annual maintenance and service; and (e) the details of steps likely to be taken to rectify these shortcomings and fixing of responsibilities?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (c):	‘Police’ and ‘Public Order’ are State subjects as per the Seventh Schedule of the Constitution of India. As such, it is the responsibility of the State Governments to plan and take initiative to modernize  its  police  forces,  including  installation  of CCTV.  Central -2-L.S.US.Q.NO.449 FOR 15.09.2020Government, on its part, under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of ‘Assistance to States for Modernisation of Police’ (erstwhile scheme of ‘Modernisation of State Police Forces’), supplement efforts of the State Governments in acquisition of advanced weaponry such as INSAS Rifles & AK series rifles; all types of intelligence equipments including Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs), Night Vision Devices (NVDs), CCTV Surveillance system & body worn camera systems; modern communication equipment and state-of-the-art equipment for  security / training / forensics / cybercrime / traffic policing. Further, ‘construction’ and ‘purchase of operational vehicles’ is permitted in the insurgency affected north-eastern States and Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected districts. Every year, States formulate their State Action Plans (SAPs) as per their requirements and strategic priorities.No separate details are kept of amounts released for specific items of machinery or equipment.(d) & (e):	It is the responsibility of the State Governments to maintain CCTV systems.*********
