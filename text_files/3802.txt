                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3544






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Infrastructure of Kendriya Vidyalaya in Bihar




Mahabali Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of Kendriya Vidyalayas in Bihar that do not having their own buildingsas a result of which the required education infrastructure is not available in theseschools;(b) whether teaching of higher classes is not possible to be carried out in suchschools in the absence of proper buildings because required space is notavailable for setting up desired laboratories etc. and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government has fixed any time limit under the programme forconstructing buildings etc. for the said schools; and(d) if so, the details thereof, school-wise?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) As on date, there are 17 Kendriya Vidyalayas (KVs) running without their ownbuildings in the State of Bihar. The details of these 17 KVs are as under:<pre>i. Buildings underconstructionBela, Harnaut & Jhajhaii. Land yet to betransferred bySponsoring AuthorityLakhisarai, Buxar, Motihari, Siwan, Banka, Chhapra, AFSPurnia, CRPF Jhaphan, Gopalganj, Hajipur, Darbhanga,Barauni, Aurangabad & Maharajganj.(b) As per the norms of Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan, due to lack of permanentbuildings, teaching-learning process is affected.(c) & (d) Construction of permanent buildings for KVs is a continuous process, whichdepends upon identification of suitable land, completion of lease formalities in favour ofKVS by the sponsoring authorities, submission of drawings / estimates by constructionagency, availability of funds and requisite approvals etc. As such, no definite time framecan be given in this regard.</pre>*****
