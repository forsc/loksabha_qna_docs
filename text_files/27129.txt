                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

254






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.08.2021






Single Point Registration Scheme of NSIC




Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar













                                                    Dhanush M Kumar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the National Small Industries Corporation (NSIC) facilitates the growth of small enterprises by increasing the share of Government purchases from this sector and if so, the details thereof; (b)	whether the number of micro and small industries, registered under Single Point Registration Scheme of NSIC has increased over the years and if so, the details thereof during the last three years, year and State-wise;(c)	whether the NSIC has launched a programme exclusively for women entrepreneurs; (d)	if so, the number of women who were able to take entrepreneurship during the above period with the help of NSIC in the country including the States of Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu; and (e)	whether the Government provides any financial support to such entrepreneurs and if so, the details thereof during the said period?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NARAYAN RANE)(a) to (e): A statement is laid down on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (e) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *254 FOR ANSWER ON 05.08.2021(a) & (b): 	The National Small Industries Corporation Limited (NSIC) provides support for development of micro, small & medium enterprises (MSMEs) through various schemes & services such as raw material distribution, credit support for raw materials procurement, tender & consortia marketing and technical assistance to MSMEs. NSIC also registers micro and small enterprises (MSEs) under its Single Point Registration Scheme (SPRS). The units registered under SPRS are eligible to get the benefits under Public Procurement Policy for Micro & Small Enterprises Order 2012 as notified on 23.03.2012 and subsequently amended from time to time. State-wise details of MSEs registered under SPRS of NSIC during the last three years is given at Annexure. The details of procurement made from MSEs during the last three years are as under:									(Rs. crore)<pre>Year	Procurement from MSEs2018-19	40399.702019-20	39665.102020-21	40443.21			Source: MSME Sambandh Portal</pre>(c) to (e): 	NSIC has not launched any programme exclusively for women entrepreneurs. However, Ministry of MSME is implementing various schemes and programmes for growth and development of MSMEs including women owned MSMEs in the country. More than 4 lakh women have been supported through various schemes of this Ministry during the last three years. These programmes are aimed at providing credit support for new enterprise creation as well as expansion of existing enterprises, skill development, technology upgradation, enhancing competitiveness and marketing support to MSMEs. Under Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP), a credit-linked subsidy programme, higher subsidy to the tune of 35% is provided to women entrepreneurs for setting up their unit. In Coir Sector, this Ministry implements ‘Skill Upgradation & Mahila Coir Yojana’ under Coir Vikas Yojana, aimed at skill development of women artisans engaged in the Coir industry in the coir producing States of the country. In addition, under the Public Procurement for MSEs Amendment Order, 2018, a special provision has been created to support women-owned MSEs which mandates 3% procurement from them by Central Ministries/Departments and Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs).
