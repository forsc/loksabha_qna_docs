                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2446






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2019






Integrated Check Posts




Mahua Moitra














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the status on increasing the number of Integrated Check Posts between the State of West Bengal and Bangladesh along with the progress on each of those projects; and(b) the status of signing the agreement for free movement of trucks between India and Bangladesh to further effective and efficient trade between the countries?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a):	Government of India has operationalized one Integrated Check Post (ICP) at Petrapole in West Bengal along Indo-Bangladesh Border on 12th February,2016. Subsequently, on 17th December, 2018, ‘in principle’ approval was accorded, inter-alia for acquisition of land and preparation of Detailed Engineering Report (DER) for development of ICPs at 05 (five) locations viz; (i) Ghojadanga, (ii) Changrabandha, (iii) Fulbari, (iv) Mahadipur and (v) Hili, along the Indo-Bangladesh Border in West Bengal. Government of West Bengal has constituted a team headed by Special Secretary, Department of Home & Hill Affairs, to assess the availability of land for these 5 ICPs.(b):	The Motor Vehicle Agreement between Bhutan, Bangladesh, India and Nepal was signed in June 2015, for seamless movement of vehicles across borders.*****
