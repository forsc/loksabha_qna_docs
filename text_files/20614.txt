                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1713






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2021






Feeder Level Solarisation under PM-KUSUM




Parbatbhai Savabhai Patel













                                                    Naranbhai Bhikhabhai Kachhadiya
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy has issued any guidelines for implementation of Feeder Level Solarisation under Component-C of Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan (PM-KUSUM); (b) if so, the details thereof; and (c) whether any guidelines have also been issued for the State of Gujarat under PM-KUSUM?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (I/C) FOR NEW & RENEWABLE ENERGY, POWER and MoS for  SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP(SHRI R.K. SINGH)(a) to (c) The Ministry of New and Renewable Energy on 4.12.2020 has issued guidelines for implementation of Feeder Level Solarisation in the country, including in the State of Gujarat, under Component-C of Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan (PM-KUSUM). In Feeder level solarisation, solar plants can be installed through RESCO/CAPEX mode for feeding power to single or multiple agriculture feeders for which Central Financial Assistance (CFA) @ 30% of the cost of solar power plant is provided considering the cost of solar power plant as Rs. 3.5 Cr/MW.  There is provision of higher CFA of 50% in case of NE States, hilly states/UTs and Island UTs for implementation through CAPEX mode. The detailed Feeder Level Solarisation guidelines are available at www.mnre.gov.in.*****
