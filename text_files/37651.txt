                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CHEMICALS AND FERTILIZERS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4911






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.04.2022






Supply of Cheap Medicines in Maharashtra




Rajan Baburao  Vichare














                        Will the Minister of





CHEMICALS AND FERTILIZERS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of Janaushadhi Kendras currently running smoothly in Maharashtra; (b)the extent to which cheap medicines are being provided to the poor through these Janaushadhi Kendras; and (c) the details of estimate made on amount of money being saved by the patients by purchasing generic medicines from Janaushadhi Kendras instead of other chemist shops?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF CHEMICALS AND FERTILIZERS(SHRI BHAGWANTH KHUBA)                               (a): As on 28.02.2022, about 630 Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Kendras (PMBJKs) have been opened in the State of Maharashtra. (b):  The medicines at PMBJKs are priced on the principle of a maximum of 50% of the average price of the top three branded medicines. Thus, the price of Jan Aushadhi Medicines is cheaper at least by 50% and in some cases, by 80% to 90% of the market price of branded medicines.(c): During financial year 2020-21, sale of about Rs. 665.83 cr. has been made under the scheme, which has led to estimated savings of about Rs. 4,000 cr. to the citizens. During the current financial year, i.e., 2021-22, till 28.02.2022, sales of about Rs. 814.21 cr. have been made, leading to estimated savings of about Rs. 4800 cr. to the citizens.*****
