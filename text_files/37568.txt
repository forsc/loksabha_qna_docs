                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5003






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.04.2022






Health Infrastructure in Rural Areas




Khan Saumitra













                                                    Raju Bista
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the funds allocated for the development of health infrastructure in rural areas of the country during the last three years, State/UT-wise;(b) the details of various programmes, projects, Schemes run by the Government along with financial assistance provided to the West Bengal during the last three years;(c) the funds allocated to Darjeeling and Kalimpong for the purpose during the last three years;(d) the present status of Critical Care Units being constructed with central grants at NBHCH, Siliguri in Darjeeling; and(e) the status of oxygen generators set up in West Bengal through PM Cares fund?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a)	to (e): The details of funds allocated for the development of healthcare infrastructure in rural areas of the country under National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) during the last three years, State/UT-wise is at Annexure.  Financial Support provided to the State of West Bengal under the following Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Department of Health & Family Welfare is as under: (i)	National Health Mission (NHM): It is one of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme under which the Government of India provides financial and technical support to States/UTs to strengthen their health care systems. It comprises of two sub-missions viz. National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) and National Urban Health Mission (NUHM). During the last three years i.e. from F.Y. 2018-19 to F.Y. 2020-21 funds to the tune of Rs.1116.09 crore, Rs.1749.32 crore and Rs.1895.01 crore have been released respectively to the State of West Bengal.  (ii)	ECRP-I: The ‘India COVID-19 Emergency Response and Health System Preparedness Package-I’ (ECRP-I) of Rs.15,000 crore has been approved by the Cabinet on 22ndApril 2020 with an objective to prevent, detect and respond to the threat posed by COVID-19. Funds to the tune of Rs. 310.98 crore have been released to the State of West Bengal under this Package through Agency-NHM. It is 100% centrally funded. (iii)	ECRP-II:  The Cabinet has also approved the scheme, “India Covid-19 Emergency Response and Health Systems Preparedness Package-II” (ECRP-II) on 08th July 2021 for an amount of Rs.23,123 crore for the F.Y. 2021-22. The scheme aims to accelerate health system preparedness for immediate responsiveness for early prevention, detection and management of COVID-19, with the focus on health infrastructure development including for Pediatric Care and with measurable outcomes.Funds to the tune of Rs.604.76 crore has been released to the State of West Bengal through Agency-NHM and is as per approved Centre-State funding under NHM. Public health and hospitals is State subject, hence the primary responsibility for proper implementation of the scheme lies with the State/UT Governments. Further, allocation for establishment of 22 bedded Critical Care Blocks in West Bengal has been provided under Pradhan Mantri Ayushman Bharat Health Infrastructure Mission (PM-ABHIM).  49 PSA Plants have been commissioned in the State of West Bengal under PM Cares Fund, all of which have become functional.
