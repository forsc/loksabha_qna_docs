                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4396






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






Fare and Facilities of Suburban/Non-Suburban Areas




Sisir Kumar Adhikari














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether Delhi/NCR under Northern Railway is still under non-suburban division; (b) if so, the total suburban and non-suburban divisions under Indian Railways as on date;(c) the details of differences in fare and facilities of suburban and non-suburban areas of Railways;(d) the rules in place to upgrade non-suburban stations to suburban and providing commuting benefits therein;(e) whether the Government is planning to introduce uniform suburban train services of Delhi/New Delhi/Anandvihar and Sarai Rohilla at par with Kolkata, Mumbai and Chennai; and(f) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMUNICATIONS AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI ASHWINI VAISHNAW)(a) Yes, Sir. Delhi/NCR under Northern Railway is a non-suburban category.(b) As on date, the current list of areas already notified as sub-urban includes Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata and Secunderabad.(c) The details of important  difference of fare over Suburban and Non-suburban areas for second class and first class ordinary services are  as under: (i) As per Indian Railway Conference Association (IRCA), for passenger fare there are separate base fare table for suburban and non-suburban ordinary services. (ii) Minimum chargeable basic fare for second class of ordinary suburban services is ? 5 and for second class non-suburban ordinary services is ? 10.(d) Ministry of Railway’s consideration for notification as sub-urban routes  is based on the pattern of traffic, availability of rail infrastructure and the notification issued by State Governments from time to time identifying areas as suburban in their master plan etc.(e) & (f) No, Sir. There is no such plan.*****
