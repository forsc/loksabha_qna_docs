                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4644






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2019






Vocationalisation of Education




Amar Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has revised Centrally Sponsored Scheme forVocationalisation of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education to integrateemployability education into school education under Samagra Shiksha in Punjab;(b) if so, the details thereof including the list of schools allocated for itsimplementation;(c) the funds allocated under this scheme during the last three years; and(d) the steps the Government will take for its early implementation?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (d): Yes, Sir. The erstwhile Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Rashtriya MadhyamikShiksha Abhiyan of which Vocationalisation of Secondary and Higher SecondaryEducation was a component, has been subsumed under the Centrally SponsoredScheme of ‘Samagra Shiksha – an integrated scheme for school education’ from2018-19. The component of Vocationalisation of School Education under ‘SamagraShiksha’ seeks to integrate vocational education with general academic education withan aim to prepare educated, employable and competitive human resource for varioussectors of the economy and the global market. Under the scheme, National SkillsQualification Framework (NSQF) compliant vocational courses are taught to thestudents opting for the vocational courses from class 9th to 12th in the schools which arecovered under the scheme. At the secondary level i.e. class IX and X, vocationalcourses are offered to the students as an additional subject. At Senior Secondary level,i.e. class XI and XII, vocational courses are offered as a compulsory (elective) subject.The curriculum for the courses comprises of the modules on vocational skills andemployability skills which include communication skills, self-management skills, ICTskills, entrepreneurial skills, and green skills for enhancing the employability of thestudents.The Vocationalisation of School Education component of ‘Samagra Shiksha’ is underimplementation across the country including Punjab. Till 2018-19, the scheme hasbeen implemented in 8654 schools. 55 job roles in 19 sectors are covered under thescheme. The scheme has been implemented in 780 schools in Punjab till 2018-19. Anamount of Rs. 3003.42 Crore has been approved for the implementation of the schemein the country during the last three years including Rs. 274.30 Crore for Punjab.* * * * *
