                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3569






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2022






Cities under Smart Cities Mission




Gopal Chinayya Shetty














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has included cities under the Smart Cities Mission;(b)	if so, the details thereof and the norms under which these cities have been selected;(c)	the status of the progress of the cities identified, State-wise; and(d)	the total investment required for the identified cities from which the Government proposes to mobilize the resources?






ANSWER




                                                                              THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE                    MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                        (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a) to (d): Government of India launched the Smart Cities Mission (SCM) on 25 June 2015.  100 Smart Cities have been selected through a two-stage challenge process. In Stage-I, intra-State competition was conducted to select potential Smart Cities to participate in Stage-II at the National level.  In Stage-II, each potential Smart City submitted its Smart City Proposal (SCP) which was evaluated by an eminent panel.  Finally, 100 Cities were selected for development as Smart Cities through 4 rounds of competition from January 2016 to June 2018.As per SCM Guidelines, Central Government will provide financial support to the extent of ? 48,000 crore over five years i.e., on an average ? 100 crore per city per year.  An equal amount on a matching basis will be contributed by the State Government/Urban Local Body (ULB).  Apart from these funds, around ? 42,028 crore (21%) has been proposed from convergence with other Missions, ? 41,022 crore (21%) from Public-Private Partnership (PPP), around ? 9,843 crore (4.8%) from loans, ? 2,644 crore (1.3%) from own resources and remaining from other sources.As on 4 March 2022, 100 Smart Cities have tendered out 6,928 projects worth ? 1,91,238 crore; work orders have been issued in 6,282 projects worth ? 1,65,503 crore; 3,576 projects worth ? 59,958 crore have been completed.  State/City-wise progress of Smart City Projects is given in Annexure-I. So far, Government of India has released           ? 29,213.60 crore for 100 Smart Cities, out of which ? 25,177.65 crore (86%) has been utilized.                                          *****
