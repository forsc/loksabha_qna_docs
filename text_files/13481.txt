                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

211






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.03.2020






Private Airlines




Arun Sao














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of airlines including private airlines operating in the country at present;(b) the details of the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) policy with regard to granting permission to private airlines for operating in the country;(c) whether there is any monitoring policy including the guidelines available with the DGCA for monitoring the operations of private airlines and if so, the details thereof;(d) whether certain private operators have applied for licence; and(e) if so, the details thereof during each of the last three years and the current year and the action taken on their applications?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) to (e): A Statement is laid on the table of the House.STATEMENT IN RESPECT OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 211 REGARDING "PRIVATE AIRLINES" TO BE ANSWERED ON 05.03.2020.; ; (a)   Details of the airlines operating in the country at present is attached as Annexure-I. (b)   The Air Operator Certificate (AOC) for Scheduled Air Transport Service is granted under the provision of Rule 134 and Schedule XI of the Aircraft Rules, 1937. It requires an initial NOC from Central Government, which is granted by Ministry of Civil Aviation after ensuring the laid down eligibility requirements. Subsequent to grant of initial NOC from Ministry of Civil Aviation, Directorate General of Civil Aviation grants AOC after ensuring the compliance of relevant regulatory requirements as per Air Operator Certification Manual (CAP3100/3300/3400), which is a structured 5 phase process for grant of AOC by DGCA. (c)   After grant of AOC, the airlines are subject to continued oversight through Annual Surveillance Program and other inspections/audits as per guidelines contained in DGCA''s Surveillance Procedure Manual. (d) & (e)   Details are attached at Annexure-II.
