                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    EXTERNAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2590






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.08.2021






Presidency of UNSC




S. Ramalingam













                                                    T. R. Paarivendhar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





EXTERNAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether India is going to assume Presidency of the 15 nation body United Nations Security Council (UNSC) in August, 2021;(b)	if so, the details thereof; (c)	whether India will take necessary steps with international communities to get the support by its contribution and claim right to become a permanent member of UNSC; and (d)	if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EXTERNAL AFFAIRS    [SHRI V. MURALEEDHARAN](a) & (b)	Yes, India will assume Presidency of the United Nations Security Council (UNSC) in August, 2021. During its presidency, India will take several initiatives in the UNSC.  These include:(i)	High-level Virtual meeting on “Enhancing Maritime Security: A case for International Cooperation”.(ii)	High-level Open Debate on “Protecting the Protectors: Technology and Peacekeeping”.(iii)	High-level briefing on “Threats to International Peace and Security caused by Terrorist Acts”.(c) & (d)	The Government accorded the highest priority to assume permanent membership for India in an expanded United Nations Security Council. India is actively engaged in the ongoing Inter-Governmental Negotiations (IGN) on UNSC reform at the UN and has been working with other reform-oriented countries through its membership of the G-4 (India, Brazil, Germany and Japan) and the L.69 Group (cross-regional grouping of developing countries of Asia, Africa and Latin America). UNSC reform and expansion of its membership, with India as a permanent member, continues to be one of India’s main priorities at high-level bilateral and multilateral engagements.***
