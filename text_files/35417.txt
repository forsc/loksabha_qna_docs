                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

254






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.03.2022






Sacred Groves in Meghalaya




Vincent H Pala














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)   	whether any steps have been undertaken to promote Sacred Groves in Meghalaya for a sustainable community –driven future and if so, the details thereof;(b)  	Whether any funds have been allocated and released for the preservation of these Sacred Groves and if so, the details thereof; and(c)  	Whether any study has been undertaken by the Government to assess the potential of Sacred Groves  in creating a sustainable future and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BHUPENDER YADAV)(a) to (c): Statement is laid on the table of the House.*** Statement referred to in reply to parts (a) to (c) of Lok Sabha Starred Question No. 254 to be answered on Monday, the 21st March, 2022 on Sacred groves in Meghalaya by SHRI VINCENT H PALA(a)	to(c)	Sacred groves are community conserved land parcels, which usually have a significant religious connotation for protecting the community, and can be notified as Community Conserved Areas under Wildlife(Protection) Act 1972, and Biodiversity Heritage Sites under the Biological Diversity Act, 2002.The Ministry supports protection and conservation of Sacred groves with a strong emphasis on community involvement and eco-development activities.The details of funds released for the activities in respect of Sacred Groves in Meghalaya by the Department of Forestsand Environment, Government of Meghalaya are at Annexure.The details of studies undertaken and completed for Sacred groves in Meghalaya are as follows:1.	Inventorisation (Growing Stock Assessment) of 40 Nos Sacred Groves 2.	Phytodiversity and Phytosociological characterisation of 10 Nos Sacred Groves having an area of 216.76 Ha3.	Preparation of Management Plans of 12 Nos Sacred Groves4.	Boundary Survey for 133 Sacred Groves***
