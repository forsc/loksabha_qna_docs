                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

400






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






Gramin Dak Sevaks




Sudhakar Tukaram Shrangre














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of people recruited as Gramin Dak Sevaks (GDS) since 2014, postal circle and State-wise; (b) 	the details of the eligibility requirements for GDS along with its method of selection; (c) 	the promotional avenues available for GDS for absorption into regular civil posts; and (d) 	the details of the GDS friendly initiatives taken by the Government during the last one year?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMUNICATIONS & ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI ASHWINI VAISHNAW)(a) to (d)	 A statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT TO BE LAID ON THE TABLE OF THE LOK SABHA IN RESPECT OF PARTS (a) TO (d) OF THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 400 FOR 30TH MARCH, 2022 REGARDING ‘GRAMIN DAK SEVAKS’.(a) 	Sir, the number of people engaged as Gramin Dak Sevaks since the year 2014 is 93219. The number Postal Circle-wise/State-wise is at Annexures-I &II respectively. (b)	Sir, the eligibility requirements and method of selection for Gramin Dak Sevaks  are as under:- (i)	The minimum and maximum age for the purpose of engagement to GDS posts is 18 and 40 years respectively as on the date of notification of the vacancies. The upper age limit is relaxable for those belonging to Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe (SC/ST) upto 5 years and those belonging to other Backward Caste up to 3 years.(ii)	Secondary School Examination pass certificate of 10th standard with passing marks in Mathematics and English (having studied as compulsory or elective subjects) conducted by any recognized Board of School Education of the Government of India/State Governments/ Union Territories shall be mandatory educational qualification for all approved categories of Gramin Dak Sevaks.(iii)	The candidate should have studied the local language at least up to 10th standard as compulsory or elective subject.(iv)	Gramin Dak Sevaks are engaged through GDS online engagement process on the basis of merit in 10th class and by following other eligibility criteria.(c)	Sir, the promotional avenues available for Gramin Dak Sevaks for absorption in regular Civil posts are as under :-(i)	Gramin Dak Sevaks having 5 years and 3 years of continuous engagement are eligible for absorption against 50% of vacancies of Multi Tasking Staff (MTS) on seniority-cum-fitness basis and 25 % of vacancies of MTS through Competitive Examination respectively. (ii)	Gramin Dak Sevaks upto the age of 50 years (Relaxable for those belonging to Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe upto 5 years and those belonging to other Backward Caste upto 3 years) and having 5 years of continuous engagement are eligible for Limited Departmental Competitive Examination for 50% of vacancies of Postman cadre and 25% of vacancies of Mail Guard cadre.(iii)	Unfilled vacancies of Postal Assistants/Sorting Assistants out  of the promotion quota are offered to Gramin Dak Sevaks subject to fulfilment of relevant eligibility conditions. (d)	Sir, a number of GDS friendly initiatives have been undertaken by the Department during the last one year which include:-(i)	Keeping in view unprecedented times due to Covid -19 pandemic, financial assistance upto Rs.50,000/- from welfare fund has been granted to the GDSs affected by Covid-19.  (ii)  	Furnishing of security of Rs. 1,00,000/- (Rs. one lakh) on engagement as GDS in the form of Fidelity Guarantee Bond or National Saving Certificates has been done away with. (iii)	Instructions were issued on regularization of the period of absence from duty in respect of Gramin Dak Sevaks during the pandemic.(iv)	2120 dependents of the deceased GDSs have been engaged on compassionate grounds. (v)	For the first time, relaxation of maximum upto 3 years for acquiring prescribed educational qualification for compassionate engagement has been introduced for Dak Sevak posts. (vi)  	Combined Duty Allowance to Branch Postmasters who are working at single handed Branch Post Offices has been provided.
