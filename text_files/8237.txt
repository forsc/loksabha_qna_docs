                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

172






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2019






Urban Waste Management Projects




Behanan Benny














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the major Centrally funded urban waste management projects in the country sanctioned/proposed to be sanctioned during the current financial year, State and project-wise indicating the cost of each project; (b) 	whether any proposal for such projects from the State Government of Kerala is pending with the Union Government; and (c) 	if so, the details thereof and the action taken by the Government thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI PRAKASH JAVADEKAR)(a)to (c): A statement is laid on the Table of the House.***Statement referred to in reply to parts (a) to (c) of Lok Sabha Starred Question No. 172 to be answered on Friday, the 29th November, 2019 on “Urban Waste Management Projects” by Shri Benny Behanan(a) The Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA) has overall mandate to take periodic review of the measures taken by the States and local bodies for improving their solid waste management practices and execution of solid waste management projects.MoHUA is implementing the Swachh Bharat Mission-Urban (SBM-U) under which various solid waste management projects have been taken up. Under this programme, during the mission period from2014-15 to 2019-20, an allocation of Rs. 7424.24 Cr has been madefor  such projects. An amount of Rs 5024.45 Cr has been released till November, 2019.Further, SBM-U is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme and not a Central Sector Scheme hence, all projects in the country under SBM-U are not fully sanctioned/ proposed/ financed by the MoHUA. The projects are proposed by the State Government/ UT Administrationfor part fundingand sanctioned by the State High Power Committee (SHPC). Only 35% of the project cost is released by central government as Viability Gap Fund (VGF) to the States/ UTs.The details of the urban waste management projects financed by MoHUA during the current financial year 2019-20 is enclosed as Annexure-A(b) and (c) No proposal from the State Government of Kerala is pending with the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs.
