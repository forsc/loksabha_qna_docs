                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1096






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.02.2022






Missing Children




 Bhagirath Chaudhary














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of missing children and those living on footpath in the country each year alongwith the details thereof during the last three years;(b) whether it is a fact that parameters has been laid down in respect of deciding the future and citizenship of the said missing/lost children every year and that thousands of children residing in several shelter homes/ashrams and that of those lying in jails and juvenile homes and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government proposes to enact a law for enlisting the names of such children in citizen list alongwith their religion across the country; and(d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI AJAY KUMAR MISHRA)(a)	National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) compiles and publishes statistical data on crimes in its publication “Crime in India”. The published reports are available till the year 2020. As per published information, the details of State/UT-wise number of Missing Children (below 18 years) during the years 2018-2020 are at Annexure-I. Further, the details of children living on footpath in the country is not maintained by NCRB. (b)	Ministry of Women & Child Development is implementing a Centrally Sponsored  Scheme i.e.  Child Protection Services  Scheme  through  the  State Governments/UT Administrations on pre-defined cost sharing financial pattern. The objective of the Scheme is to assist States/UTs in providing the safety net as envisaged under the Juvenile Justice Act, 2015 and Rules there-under, to support the children in difficult circumstances. It also aims at reduction of vulnerabilities to situation and actions that lead to abuse, neglect, exploitation, abandonment and separation of children from parents. Ministry of Women and Child Development has issued the ‘Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for cases of missing children’ to assist Police, Child Welfare Committees and Juvenile Justice Board in dealing with the cases of missing and found or recovered children. The objective of the SOP is to put in place guidelines while dealing with cases of missing children and to work in coordination with the concerned stakeholders and respond with urgency to issues of missing child.As per information furnished by Ministry of Women  & Child Development, The State/ UT-wise details of children residing in various Child Care Institutions during the years 2018-19 to 2020-21 are at Annexure-II. Further, as per published information by NCRB, the State/ UT-wise details of Children in Jails belonging to age-group 16-18 years, during the years 2018-2020 are at Annexure-III.(c)	No, Sir.(d)	Does not arise in view of reply to part (c) above.
