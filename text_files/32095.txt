                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3601






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






Non-agricultural Informal Sector




Parvesh Sahib Singh














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)the estimated size of the non-agricultural informal sector in the country as a percentage of the total informal sector, State-wise;(b)the policy in place/in the pipelines for helping small and informal firms grow over time into medium or larger formal sector firms; and(c)whether the Government is of the view that an urban social welfare programme along the lines of the National Rural Employment Guarantee Act is necessary for upliftment of the urban informal class?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a):		As per the Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS), during 2019-20, the share of workers in informal sector among total workers in usual stasus (ps+ss)  engaged in non- agricultural sector was 69.5%.  The State-wise percentage share of workers in  informal sector  among total workers  engaged in non-agricultural sector is annexed. (b) to (c):	In order to help small scale firms, the Government has launched several schemes viz.  Khadi Vikas Yojana (KVY), Modified Market Development Assistance (MMDA), Gramodyog Vikas Yojana, Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP),  Coir Vikas Yojana,   Scheme of Funds Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI), A Scheme for Promoting Innovation, Rural Industry and Entrepreneurship (ASPIRE), Credit Linked Capital Subsidy and Technology up-gradation Scheme (CLCSTUS) etc. by the  Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises. Furthermore, the Government is Implementing schemes for the urban informal class through Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM) and PMSVANidhi  to provide affordable Working Capital loan to street vendors.*                                               *******
