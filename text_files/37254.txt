                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEAVY INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4285






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.03.2022






Development of Heavy Industries




Hemant Patil














                        Will the Minister of





HEAVY INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of various schemes launched for the development of heavy industries in the State of Maharashtra during the last three years, district-wise;(b)	whether the Government proposes to start new schemes in Maharashtra in near future and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and(c)	the amount of funds sanctioned for Maharashtra for the development of heavy industries in the budgetary allocation for the year 2022-23?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR HEAVY INDUSTRIES(SHRI KRISHAN PAL GURJAR)(a):	In order to encourage investment in technology upgradation, skill development and augmentation of modern manufacturing capacities in the Capital Goods sector, Ministry of Heavy Industries notified an all-India Scheme for “Enhancement of Competitiveness in the Indian Capital Goods Sector” (CG Scheme) in the year 2014.  The details of projects sanctioned under the scheme in Maharashtra are as follows:(i)	C4i4 - Pune: A common engineering facility center named SAMARTH Udyog centre for Industry 4.0 has been established by Samarth Udyog Technology Forum (SUTF) in Pune district of Maharashtra, in order to demonstrate Industry 4.0 solutions to enable the industries to adopt the same and improve their productivity. (ii)	Technovuus by ARAI, Pune: (https://technovuus.araiindia.com) -  TechNovuus is a Technology innovation platform established by Automotive Research Association of India (ARAI) in the Pune district of Maharashtra.  It encourages industries to seek solutions for their technological problems from subject experts, scientists, students and researchers at an online platform.  Presently it has 11,600 registered users.(b) & (c):	The Phase 2 of the Scheme for “Enhancement of Competitiveness in the Indian Capital Goods Sector” has been notified on 25th January, 2022 under which proposals can be submitted by Industries, from anywhere in the country. Budget allocation is not done state wise in the scheme.***********
