                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1901






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.09.2020






Job Creation under MSMEs




Girish Bhalchandra Bapat














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the data on number of jobs created under Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) for the last five years, age, gender, caste, State/UT-wise; and(b)	the steps being taken by Government to provide more non-farm jobs in MSMEs in rural India, with special reference to Maharashtra?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OFMICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI  PRATAP CHANDRA SARANGI)(a) & (b):	As per 73rd round of National Sample Survey (NSS) (July 2015- June 2016) conducted by Ministry of Statistics & Programme Implementation (MoSPI), the estimated employed persons in MSME Sector are about 11.10 crore. Under the Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP), in rural area, enhanced subsidy, 35% of project cost is provided for encouraging, setting up of micro enterprises in rural area. Under the scheme, the estimated employment generated (number of persons) in micro enterprises during the years 2017-18, 2018-19, 2019-20 and 2020-21 (as on 31.08.2020) has been 3.87 lakh, 5.87 lakh, 5.33 lakh and 1.10 lakh, respectively. The State/UT wise details are attached as annexure-I. The Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) implement various other schemes for the growth and development of MSME sector. These include Micro and Small Enterprises-Cluster Development Programme (MSE-CDP), Scheme for Promotion of MSMEs in North Eastern Region and Sikkim, Tool Rooms and Technology Centers, Mission Solar Charkha (MSC), Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI), Procurement and Marketing Support Scheme, Entrepreneurship Skill Development Programme (ESDP), Credit Guarantee Scheme for Micro and Small Enterprises (MSEs) and Credit Linked Capital Subsidy and Technology Upgradation Scheme (CLCS-TUS). Recently, Post Covid-19, Government has taken a number of initiatives under Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyan to support the MSME Sector in the country especially in Covid-19 pandemic. Some of them are:-	i)   Rs 20,000 crore Subordinate Debt for MSMEs.	ii)  Rs 3 lakh crores Collateral free Automatic Loans for business, including MSMEs.	iii) Rs. 50,000 crore equity infusion through MSME Fund of Funds.	iv) New revised criteria for classification of MSMEs.v) New Registration of MSMEs through ‘Udyam Registration’ for Ease of Dong  Business.	vi) No global tenders for procurement up to Rs. 200 crores, this will help MSME. An online Portal “Champions” has been launched on 01.06.2020 by Hon’ble Prime Minister. This covers many aspects of e-governance including grievance redressal and handholding of MSMEs. Through the portal, total 19,593 grievances have been redressed upto 17.09.2020.RBI has also announced several measures to Reduce Financial Stress of MSMEs. The above initiatives help direct/ indirect employment generation in the Country including non-farm jobs in rural India including in Maharashtra.As per Udyog Aadhaar Portal, number of MSMEs registered (since September 2015 to 30.06.2020) in Maharashtra was 19,78,536. As per Udyam Registration Portal, number of MSMEs registered (from 01.07.2020 to 16.09.2020) in Maharashtra was 87,348 .
