                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5486






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2019






Pollution from Mining Activities




Nalin Kumar Kateel














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is aware that pollution from mining activities is causing serious health problems among the people living in adjacent mining areas; (b) 	if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise; (c) 	whether the Government has any proposal to end pollution in order to protect the citizens living in such areas and if so, the details thereof; (d) 	whether the Government is contemplating to bring stringent provisions to prevent the pollution caused by mining activities; and (e) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) & (b) Mining projects can contribute to pollution in nearby areas, and may cause health problems if the approved Environment Management Plans (EMP) are not implemented in letter and spirit. (c) to (e) The Central Government has issued Environment Impact Assessment (EIA) Notification, 2006, under the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, which mandates the requirement of prior environmental clearance (EC) for all new projects or activities, expansion or modernization of existing projects or activities listed in the Schedule to the Notification. These include mining of minerals/coal, requiring EC from the concerned regulatory authority. These projects are required to undertake EIA studies to evaluate the impact of mining activities on different environmental components namely air, water, land, socio-economic, etc.  and prepare EMPs accordingly. Based on likely impacts, EMPs suggested by the project proponent, are examined for their efficacy and adequacy and finalized to ensure protection of environment and citizens living in adjoining areas. *****
