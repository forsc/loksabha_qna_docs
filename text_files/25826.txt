                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

160






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Concessions for MRO Activities




Gaddam Ranjith Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of CIVIL AVIATIONbe pleased to state:-(a) whether Begumpet in Hyderabad has been identified for Maintenance, Repair and Overhaul (MRO) of aircraft in the country;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) the details of tax and other concessions given for MRO activities;(d) whether any royalty is being paid to the State;and(e) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Jyotiraditya Scindia)(a) to (e) A statement is laid on the table of the House.******STATEMENT TO BE LAID ON THE TABLE OF THE LOK SABHA IN REPLY TO STARRED QUESTION NO 160 REGARDING CONCESSIONS FOR MRO ACTIVITIES;  (a)  and (b) Yes Sir. The bid documents are currently under preparation.; (c)  The key tax concessions and other benefits provided for MRO activities include the following: i.  GST on MRO has been reduced from 18% to 5% with full Input Tax Credit from 1 April 2020. ii.  Custom duty has been waived on tools, toolkits and aircraft components imported by MROs. iii.  The period for utilization of duty-free imported components has been extended from one year to three years. iv.  Rules have been simplified for export of leased aircraft and recycled components. v.  Foreign manufacturers are being encouraged to set up their MRO facilities in India. vi.  Foreign aircraft lessors are being encouraged to carry out the re-delivery checks and MRO of their India-based aircraft in India itself. vii.  Civil-military convergence in MRO activities is being facilitated. ; The following concessions have been proposed by AAI for MRO entities:; i.  Concession fee, in any manifestation, including airport royalty, would not be leviable in new contracts. It would also not be leviable from the respective date of renewal of existing contracts. ii.  Airport land rentals are proposed to be rationalized to 40% of the prevailing rates as floor price for the bid process, with an escalation of 5.5% per annum.;  (d) and (e) No royalty is being paid to the State since there is no MRO entity currently operating at Begumpet.
