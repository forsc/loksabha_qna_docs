                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1088






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.09.2020






Bio-Medical Waste Disposal




Su Thirunavukkarasar














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)       whether it has come to the notice of the Government that bio-medical wastes generated from various hospitals in the country are being disposed off irresponsibly thereby posing a serious threat to the health of the citizens; (b)       if so, the details thereof; (c)       whether any notices have been issued to hospitals across the country for lack of adequate facilities towards proper disposal of bio-medical wastes; (d)       if so, the details thereof; and (e)       the action taken against such erring hospital authorities?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO) (a)&(b)  Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) has reported that, complaints have been received regarding improper management of bio-medical waste by Healthcare Facilities (HCFs) and Common Biomedical Waste Treatment Facilities (CBWTFs) from time to time, which are against the provisions of Bio-medical Waste Management Rules, 2016 (BMWM Rules, 2016) . A total of 99 public complaints and 27 Public Grievances were received in the last two years. The common violations observed related to failure to practise pre-treatment of laboratory waste, lack of Effluent Treatment Plants (ETPs) for treatment of liquid waste etc. CPCB had referred the said complaints and grievances to concerned State Pollution Control Boards/ Pollution Control Committees (SPCBs/PCCs) for appropriate action against erring HFCs/ CBWTFs, under the rule provisions. (c)to(e)    CPCB has issued Notices to 10 HCFs located in Rajasthan for violating the provisions of BMWM Rules, 2016. For gross violation of Rules, CPCB issued directions under section 5 of Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 along with imposition of Environmental Compensation charges against HCFs located in Assam and Madhya Pradesh. SPCBs/PCCs have also reportedly issued show-cause notices/ directions to 16,956 HCFs in 2018-2019.  *****
