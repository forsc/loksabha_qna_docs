                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

387






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2021






Losses incurred by BSNL and MTNL




T. R. Paarivendhar














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the public sector telecom companies viz., MTNL and BSNL are reported to be in loss due to lack of technological upgradation and inability to compete with private telecom operators; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Union Government has any proposal to revive these loss making public sector companies and if so, the details thereof; and (d) 	whether the Government proposes to close down these units in near future and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS, EDUCATION ANDELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a)&(b) 	Yes Sir. Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) and Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Limited (MTNL) have incurred losses consecutively since 2009-10. The Telecom sector is facing financial stress due to stiff competition and reduction in tariffs. For BSNL and MTNL, stiff competition in mobile segment and absence of 4G services (except in few places for BSNL) in the data-centric telecom market is eroding their competitive strength.Details of losses incurred by BSNL and MTNL for the last three years is as under:(in Rupees crore)<pre>Profit/(Loss) incurred by	2017-18	2018-19	2019-20BSNL 	(7,993)	(14,904)	(15,500)MTNL	(2971)	(3398)	(3811)</pre> (c)	The Government approved a revival plan for BSNL on 23.10.2019.  The revival plan, inter-alia includes reduction in employee costs through a Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS) for the employees of age 50 years and above, administrative allotment of spectrum for 4G services with funding through budgetary allocation, debt restructuring by raising of sovereign guarantee bonds, monetisation of assets to generate resources for retiring Debt, CAPEX and other requirements and in-principle approval for merger of BSNL  and MTNL.(d)	Government has no plan to close down BSNL and MTNL.************
