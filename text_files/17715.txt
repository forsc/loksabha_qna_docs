                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1518






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.09.2020






Vocal for Local




Margani Bharat














                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	Whether the Government is translating PM’s slogan ‘vocal for local’ in the food processing sector;(b) 	The details of micro food enterprises in the country, State-wise;(c)  	whether it is not true that there are thousands of micro food enterprises which needs technical, marketing, financial and other support to realize the above slogan of the PM;(d) 	Whether the Government is going to help them in every area of their operations; and(e) 	Whether the Government has any proposal to ensure that all the micro food enterprises get Food Safety and Standard Authority of India (FSSAI) Standards for global outreach?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES (SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a).	Yes Sir.(b).	As per  NSSO Report on Economic Characteristics  of Unincorporated Non-agricultural Enterprises (Excluding Construction) in India; NSS 73rd Round, there are an estimated 25 lakh unregistered food processing enterprises in the country. The State-wise details are at Annexure. (c) & (d). Yes Sir.	As part of Aatmanirbhar Bharat Abhiyan, Ministry of Food Processing Industries has launched an all India centrally sponsored "PM Formalisation of Micro food processing Enterprises (PMFME) Scheme" for providing financial, technical and business support for upgradation of existing micro food processing enterprises. It is to be implemented for a period of five years from 2020-21 to 2024-25 with an outlay of Rs.10,000 crore.  Two lakh micro food processing units will be directly assisted with credit linked subsidy.  	The Scheme aims to enhance the competitiveness of existing individual micro-enterprises in the unorganized segment of the food processing industry and promote their formalization and support FPOs/ SHGs/ Producers Cooperatives along their entire value chain. Support will be provided to micro food enterprises in preparation of DPRs for credit, skill training, common infrastructure with sorting, grading, processing, food testing, incubation, packaging& storage facilities, branding and marketing of their produce etc.(e) 	 The scheme envisages support to micro food processing enterprises for obtaining FSSAI license and maintaining hygiene standards.*****
