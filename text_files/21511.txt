                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MINES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2680






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.03.2021






People Affected by Mining




Jayant Sinha














                        Will the Minister of





MINES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the steps taken by the Ministry to promote and protect the interests of those affected by mining and working in mines;(b)	the gains for Jharkhand under such schemes and initiatives; and(c)	the details of the use of funds under such schemes across various developmental heads as of date?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF MINES, COAL AND PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS(SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI)(a): Section 9B introduced through the MMDR Amendment Act, 2015, prescribes the establishment of District Mineral Foundation (DMF) in any district affected by mining related operations. The objective of the DMF is to work for the interest and benefit of persons and areas affected by mining related operations. Ministry of Mines has circulated guidelines for implementation of Pradhan Mantri Khanij Kshetra Kalyan Yojna (PMKKKY) on 16.09.2015 through funds under DMF with directions to the State Governments to incorporate the same in to the rules framed by them for the DMF.   The objective of PMKKKY scheme are (a) to implement various developmental and welfare projects/programs in mining affected areas that complement the existing ongoing schemes/projects of State and Central Government; (b) to minimize/mitigate the adverse impacts, during and after mining, on the environment, health and socio-economics of people in mining districts; and (c) to ensure long-term sustainable livelihoods for the affected people in mining areas. Care has been taken to include all aspects of living, to ensure substantial improvement in the quality of life. High priority areas like drinking water supply, health care, sanitation, education, skill development, women and child care, welfare of aged and disabled people, skill development and environment conservation will get at least 60 % share of the funds. For creating a supportive and conducive living environment, balance funds will be spent on making roads, bridges, railways, waterways projects, irrigation and alternative energy sources.(b): In Jharkhand, total DMF collection is Rs. 6,533 crore till January 2021. Amount sanctioned against 19,288 projects is Rs. 5,169 crore. A total sum of Rs. 2,983.91crore has been spent on various projects.
