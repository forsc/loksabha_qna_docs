                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5610






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2019






Healthcare Facilities in Bengal




Raju Bista














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government is aware of the acute shortage of proper healthcare facilities in the entire North Bengal region; (b) if so, the details of rural primary healthcare facilities in North Bengal region and its functioning; (c) the criteria put in place for a region to qualify for a centrally funded multispeciality hospital like AIIMS;(d) whether the Government proposes to develop health infrastructure through centrally funded hospitals in the North Bengal region; and (e) if so, the details thereof and the necessary steps taken in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b):	“Public Health and Hospital” being a State Subject, the primary responsibility of constructing proper healthcare facilities including rural Primary Healthcare facilities lies with respective State Governments.However, to address the healthcare challenges, National Health Mission (NHM) supplements the efforts of the State/UT governments to provide accessible, affordable and quality healthcare to all those who access public health facilities. NHM support is provided to State/UTs for bridging the gap of infrastructure by setting up of new facilities and upgradation of existing facilities based on the requirement posed by them in their Programme Implementation Plan (PIPs) within their resource envelop. The details of healthcare facilities in various districts / region of the State with their functional status are not maintained centrally. However, as per Rural Health Statistics 2017-18, shortage/shortfall in health facilities (Sub Health Centres, Primary Health Centres and Community Health Centres) and number of sub divisional hospital and district hospital available in the State of West Bengal is given at Annexure-I. Further, as per the information received from the State, West Bengal State has taken all actions to create required health facilities in North Bengal region which has 160 Primary Health Centres and 2276 Sub Health Centres and district wise status is given in Annexure-II. (c) to (e):   Site for new AIIMS that are now being established in the country are finalized through Challenge Method. Details regarding Challenge Method are given at Annexure-III. Under Pradhan Mantri Swasthya Suraksha Yojana (PMSSY), an AIIMS in Kalyani, West Bengal has been approved at a cost of Rs. 1754 crore and is under construction; Upgradation of following four (4) Medical Colleges have been undertaken under PMSSY:     (a)  Kolkata Medical College, Kolkata (Phase-I)     (b)  BS Medical College, Bankura,     (c) Govt. Medical College, Malda; and     (d)  North Bengal Medical College, Darjeeling             (Phase-III).Status of the above projects are given at Annexure-IV. As per the information received from the State of West Bengal, a number of healthcare facilities including Super Speciality Hospitals and Medical Colleges and Hospitals have been created in North Bengal region.
