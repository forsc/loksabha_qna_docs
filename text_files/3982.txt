                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3681






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.07.2019






Strength of Security Personnel in DMRC




Gautam Gambhir














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the existing strength and requirement of security personnel in Delhi Metro; (b) whether violation of security rules has increased every year in Delhi Metro;(c) if so, the details thereof and the total number of such cases reported and the action taken against the guilty personnel during each of the last three years and the current year;(d) whether the Government has also received complaints that people are entering metro stations without proper frisking and getting their bags checked;(e) if so, the details thereof and the action taken in this regard; and(f) the corrective steps taken by the Government to improve security in Delhi Metro along with the other measures taken to check recurrence of such cases in future?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a)  to (f):	The existing strength of Central Industrial Security Force (CISF) personnel in Delhi Metro is 12964. No increase in security rules violation has  been  noticed.  Instances  of  passengers  entering  into Metro system --2--L.S.US.Q.N. 3681 FOR 16.07.2019without security checking, found to be crossing the Metro tracks, pick-pocketing, carrying illegal arms etc whenever detected are dealt with as per law. Various measures have been put in place by CISF as well as Delhi Police to prevent crime in Delhi Metro. These, inter-alia, include regular patrolling in Metro Stations by Delhi Police, formation of Flying Squads by CISF, installation of CCTV and surveillance system, deployment of crime intelligence wing personnel, police assistance booths at major Metro stations, etc.*****
