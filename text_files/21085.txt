                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

202






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.03.2021






Schemes for Enhancing Fisheries Production




Sunita Duggal














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of schemes implemented by the Government for farmers engaged in fisheries;(b) 	whether the Government has framed any scheme to enhance the fish production;(c) 	If so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor;(d) 	the total funds allocated under various schemes by the Government for welfare of fisheries sector during the last three years, State-wise;(e) 	whether the Government has any plan for fisheries production in waterlogged land; and(f) 	if so, the details thereof and if not , the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRYAND DAIRYING(SHRI GIRIRAJ SINGH)(a) to (f):	A Statement is placed on the Table of the House.***** Statement referred to in reply to the Lok Sabha Starred Question No *202 put in by Shrimati Sunita Duggal, Honourable Member of Parliament  for answer on 9thMarch, 2021 regarding Schemes for Enhancing Fisheries Production(a) to (c):	The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is presently implementing a flagship scheme namely “Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY)”- to bring about Blue Revolution through sustainable and responsible development of fisheries sector in India” with highest ever investment of            Rs. 20050 crore in fisheries sector for a period of 5 years with effect from the current financial year 2020-21.  PMMSY intends to addresses the critical gaps in fish production and productivity, quality, technology, post-harvest infrastructure and management, modernisation and strengthening of value chain, traceability, establishing a robust fisheries management framework and fishers’ welfare. In addition, a dedicated fund namely, ‘Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund’ (FIDF) with a total fund size of Rs.7522.48 crore has been set up for providing concessional finance for creation and strengthening of fisheries infrastructure facilities. (d) : Under the erstwhile scheme on Blue Revolution: Integrated Development of Management of Fisheries, projects with total outlay of Rs. 3386.57 crore were sanctioned to various States for development of fisheries including welfare activities during the last three years from 2017-18 to 2019-20. The State-wise details of projects sanctioned during the last three years are annexed.(e) and (f) : PMMSY among others also has a focus on productive utilization of waterlogged area for fisheries development and the activities taken up primarily includes stocking of fingerlings, pen culture in open water bodies and facilities for post -harvest handling. Projects with outlay of Rs. 95.55 crore have been approved under the Blue Revolution and PMMSY for development of fisheries in waterlogged land. *****
