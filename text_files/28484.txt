                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

221






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2021






Status of Global Initiative for Academic Network




Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar













                                                     Bhavana Gawali (Patil)
                                                








                                                    Dhanush M Kumar
                                                








                                                    Ganesan Selvam
                                                








                                                    C.N. Annadurai
                                                








                                                    Gautham Sigamani Pon
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the present status of Global Initiative for Academic Network (GIAN) programmealong-with its aims and objectives;(b) the number of Universities and faculty that participated in the programme;(c) the details of international faculties hired under GIAN programme to imparteducation and enhance knowledge of different courses in various highereducation institutions during each of the last three years and the current year;(d) whether the programme has helped to improve the global rankings of IndianUniversities and if so, the details thereof; and(e) the other steps taken or proposed to be taken by the Government to provideopportunity to the students to seek knowledge and experience from reputedinternational faculty?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) Ministry of Education initiated the Global Initiative for Academic Network (GIAN)in Higher Education which aimed at tapping the talent pool of scientists andentrepreneurs, internationally, to encourage their engagement with the institutes ofHigher Education in India so as to augment the country''s existing academicresources, accelerate the pace of quality reform and elevate India''s scientific andtechnological capacity to global excellence. The Standing Finance Committee (SFC)has approved the continuation of Global Initiative for Academic Network (GIAN)scheme for a period of five (5) year i.e. 2021-2022 to 2025-2026.-2-(b) A number of 3554 faculties from 203 Institutions/ Universities participated in thescheme, as coordinators of GIAN Courses.(c) In the year 2018, 377 foreign faculties and in the year 2019, 200 foreign facultiesvisited under this programme. However, due to Covid pandemic, no foreign facultyvisited in the year 2020 and 2021 (till November, 2021).(d) One of the major components in global ranking procedure is the internationalcollaboration and visit of foreign faculty and students. This scheme is improving thevisibility of the Indian higher education Institutions worldwide through the academiccollaboration of foreign faculty in terms of delivering lectures, co-authoring of coursematerials and initiating potential research collaboration.(e) Higher Education Institutions also enter into MoUs with foreign Universities forvarious collaboration programme, which also provides the opportunity to the studentsto seek knowledge and experience from reputed international faculty.*****
