                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TRIBAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

163






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.11.2019






Annual Income for Tribal Communities




 Chandra Sekhar Bellana













                                                    Adala Prabhakara Reddy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether Indian Council of Social Science Research, New Delhi has conducted a study on “Economic Value of Bilgiri Rangaswamy Temple Wildlife Sanctuary” (BRTWLS) located between Western Ghats and the Eastern Ghats on generating annual income for the tribal communities living in the vicinity of the sanctuary in Karnataka;(b) if so, details thereof; and(c) whether the Government would replicate the scheme in other States, including Vizianagaram and Visakhapatnam in Andhra Pradesh for the benefit of Tribal communities and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	to (b)  : As per the information provided by the  Indian Council of Social Science Research (ICSSR), New Delhi, they have not conducted study on “ Economic Value of Bilgiri Rangaswamy Temple Wildlife Sanctuary” (BRTWLS)  located between Western Ghats and the Eastern Ghats on generating annual income for the tribal communities living in the vicinity of the sanctuary in Karnataka.  However, ICSSR has awarded a Research Project to Dr. M. Balasubramanian, Assistant Professor, Institute for Social and Economic Change (ISEC), Bengaluru on “Economic Value of Forest Resources: A Case study of Nine Districts of Karnataka”. The study commenced with effect from 1-02-2018 and is an ongoing project.Economic Value of Bilgiri Rangaswamy Temple Wildlife Sanctuary (BRTWLS), the Western Ghats in Karnataka is the part of the study taken on Economic value of Forest Resources. This study has estimated the value of carbon sequestration, provisioning services, soil erosion prevention and recreation services. It has used market price methods for estimating the value of provisioning services and individual travel cost method for estimating the value of recreation services, while the value of carbon sequestration and soil erosion prevention has been estimated based on the secondary data. As per the study, average annual income from non-timber forest products has been between Rs. 10000 and 12000 for the sample households in the BRT wildlife sanctuary.(c)	There is no such proposal under consideration.*****
