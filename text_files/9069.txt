                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2710






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.12.2019






Coastal Rail Routes




Chunni Lal Sahu














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has any proposal for reducing distance of freight coastal rail route and saving time and cost thereby;(b) if so, the details of the action plan likely to be prepared with regard to coastal rail route from Mumbai to Kolkata; (c) whether international ports like Paradip, Golapur, etc. are connected with the rail route and if so, the details thereof; (d) whether the Government has taken any decision in this regard along with the estimated cost of the said route; and(e) whether any action has been taken so far in this regard and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) & (b) No, Sir. The coastal rail route from Mumbai to Kolkata is already connected on rail network via Mangalore, Kanniyakumari, Chennai, Vizag and Paradeep. (c) & (d) Paradeep port is connected with Cuttack on Indian Railway Network. Further, a work of new line between Haridaspur-Paradeep having a length of 82 Kms at a cost of `1844.47 Cr is in various stages of execution. There is no port by name Golapur. (e) Does not arise.*****
