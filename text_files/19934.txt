                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

114






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.02.2021






Promotion of Dairy and Animal Husbandry among Farmers




Sunil Kumar Singh













                                                    Kani K. Navas
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether keeping in view the target of doubling the income of each of the farmers by the year 2022, the Government has prepared any comprehensive scheme for promoting dairy and animal husbandry among the farmers;(b) if so, the steps being taken in this regard alongwith the progress made so far to achieve the said target; and(c) the amount of funds allocated by the Government under the said scheme during the current financial year 2020-21 and proposed to be allocated during the next financial year 2021-22, particularly with regard to Maharashtra?






ANSWER




                                                        (ANSWER) THE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING                                                            (SHRI GIRIRAJ SINGH)  (a) to (c) A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.  STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.114 PART (a) TO (c) ON “PROMOTION OF DAIRY AND ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AMONG FARMERS” TO BE ANSWERED ON 09.02.2021.(a)& (b)  Keeping in view of the targets of doubling income of each of the farmers by the year 2022 the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying has been implementing comprehensive scheme white revolution for promoting Dairy and Animal Husbandry among the farmers. Following are the major components of the scheme:i.	Rashtriya Gokul Mission ii.	National Livestock Missioniii.	Livestock Health and Disease Controliv.	National Animal Disease Control Programmev.	National Programme for Dairy Developmentvi.	Animal Husbandry Infrastructure Development Fundvii.	Dairy processing and Infrastructure Development Fundviii.	Supporting Dairy Cooperative and Farmer Producer Organisations engaged in dairy activitiesDue to the efforts made by Government of India milk production has increased by 44.10% over the past six years from 137.7 million tones during 2013-14 to 198.4 million metric tones during 2019-20. The total Egg production in the country during 2019-20 has been estimated as 114.38 Billion registering a growth 53% over the past 6 years as compared to estimates of 74.75 Billion in 2013-14.  The total Meat production in the country during 2019-20 is estimated as 8.60 Million Tonnes showing a growth rate of 38% over the year 2013-14 which was 6.23 Million Tonnes.(c) Financial allocation made during 2020-21 and 2021-22 under above schemes including funds released to Maharashtra during 2020-21 is given in the following table. Schemes implemented by the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying are demand driven and funds are released on the basis of the proposal received from the States.
