                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2022






Welfare Schemes for Women and Children




Rajveer (Raju Bhaiya) Singh













                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	whether the Government has launched Mission Shakti, Mission Vatsalya, Saksham Anganwadi and Poshan 2.0. to provide integrated benefits to women and children;  (b) 	if so, the details along with the physical and financial progress of these schemes, State-wise;  (c) 	whether the Government has announced to upgrade two lakh anganwadis under the Saksham Anganwadi Scheme;  (d) 	if so, the details thereof; and  (e) 	the other steps being taken by the Government for welfare of women and children affected by COVID-19 pandemic? 






ANSWER




ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a) to (e): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House. ******    STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.328 FOR ANSWER ON 25.03.2022 ASKED BY SHRI RAJVEER SINGH (RAJU BHAIYA) AND DR. SUKANTA MAJUMDAR REGARDING ‘WELFARE SCHEMES FOR WOMEN AND CHILDREN’ (a) & (b) Yes Sir. To ensure efficient implementation, presently in the XVth Finance Commission Cycle, all the schemes for women and children of the Ministry have been clubbed together into three verticals, viz. (1) Saksham Anganwadi & Poshan 2.0 for improving nutrition indicators in the country (2) Mission Shakti, for safety, protection and empowerment of women; and (3) Mission Vatsalya, for protection and welfare of children. The details of these schemes are as under:  (i) Saksham Anganwadi & Poshan 2.0: This programme has been reorganized into 3 primary verticals: (i) Nutrition Support for POSHAN and for Adolescent Girls (ii) Early Childhood Care and Education [3-6 years] and (iii) Anganwadi Infrastructure including modern, upgraded Saksham Anganwadis. (ii) Mission Shakti: Mission Shakti comprises of two sub-schemes ‘Sambal’ and ‘Samarthya’ for safety and security of women and empowerment of women respectively. The existing schemes of One Stop Centres (OSC), Women Helplines (181-WHL) and Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP) have been made part of Sambal sub- scheme; while the existing schemes of Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY), Ujjwala, Swadhar Greh and Working Women Hostel and National Creche Scheme have been subsumed in ‘Samarthya’. (iii) Mission Vatsalya: Under Mission Vatsalya, the Child Protection Scheme(CPS) has been strengthened for better outreach and protection for children in need and care in a mission mode with the objective to: (i)Support and sustain Children in difficult circumstances. (ii) Develop context-based solutions for holistic development of children from varied backgrounds. (iii) Provide scope for green field projects for encouraging innovative solutions. (iv) Cement convergent action by gap funding, if required.  The physical progress of Saksham Anganwadi & Poshan 2.0, Mission Shakti & Mission Vatsalya are annexed at Annexure-IA, IB & IC, respectively.   The financial progress of Saksham Anganwadi & Poshan 2.0, Mission Shakti & Mission Vatsalya are annexed at Annexure-IIA, IIB & IIC, respectively. (c) & (d) Yes Sir. Under Saksham Anganwadi and Poshan 2.0 scheme, Government of India has decided to strengthen, upgrade and rejuvenate two lakh AWCs @40,000 AWCs per year for improved nutrition delivery including Poshan Vatikas for stimulating the creative, social, emotional, cognitive and intellectual development of children under 6 years of age in convergence with education development programmes, for providing/adding more services with better infrastructure including internet/wifi connectivity, LED screens, water purifier/installation of RO Machine and Early Childhood Care and Education with smart learning aids, ausio-visual aids and child friendly learning equipments, Art Work (Educational painting, Practice board for children, information board), Wall Painting (Cleaning, Sanding, Repairing). (e) Steps being taken by the Government for welfare of women and children affected by COVID-19 pandemic are as under: (i) To ensure continuous nutritional support to Anganwadi beneficiaries during the phase of Covid-19 pandemic, Anganwadi Workers and Helpers distributed Supplementary Nutrition at the doorsteps of beneficiaries once in 15 days. "Poshan Tracker”, robust ICT enabled digital platform has been implemented, to ensure real time monitoring of supplementary nutrition by use of data analytics for prompt supervision and management of services, tracking of all AWCs, AWWs and beneficiaries on defined indicators.  (ii) Announced PM CARES for Children scheme to support children who have lost both parents or surviving parent or legal guardian or adoptive parents due to COVID-19 pandemic. (iii) Ensuring that all its institutions and statutory authorities remained operational and available for providing assistance to women and children during the COVID-19 period.  (iv) During lockdown, advisories and guidelines were also issued from time to time for encouraging COVID appropriate behavior for women and children in  institutions; prevention and combating of human trafficking during the pandemic, monitoring of Child Care Institutions and coping strategies during COVID times for mental health support to women, children and care givers.  ******   Annexure-IA PHYSICAL PROGRESS UNDER SAKSHAM ANGANWADI & POSHAN 2.0(ANGANWADI SERVICES SCHEME)        Anganwadi Centres Sanctioned and Operational, SNP & PSE Beneficiaries under Anganwadi Services  Scheme Quarter Ending 30 June, 2021                                                S.
