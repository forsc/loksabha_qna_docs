                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4274






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2019






Ragging in Medical Colleges




Ashok Kumar Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government is aware of the inhumane behaviour or brutal death of students in medical colleges and other medical institutes due to alleged ragging;(b) if so, the details thereof along with the complaints received in this regard medical college/institution/year-wise as on date;(c) the steps taken/proposed to be taken by the Government to check alleged ragging in the medical colleges and institutes; and(d) if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a)	& (b):   The Government is aware about problems of ragging in the medical colleges. Status of ragging cases received by the Medical Council of India (MCI) in the last five years is enclosed as Annexure- I.(c)	and (d):	 To control and check ragging in medical colleges, the following measures have been taken by the MCI : 1. Before starting of fresh batch of admissions, circular are issued to all the medical colleges to provide information on the following points:-(A)	(a) Anti-Ragging  Committee composition in the college ( name of members with their telephone numbers and e-mail IDs)(b) Number of Incidences of ragging reported and action taken in hard/soft copy, if any. (c). Number of FIRs lodged, if any.(d). Punishment awarded, if any (B)   Inclusion of specific information on Anti-Ragging in admission brochures/prospectus/booklets.(C)  Installation of CCTV Cameras in all the vulnerable places of college, Hospital and Hostels. (D) Ensuring submission of online undertaking by each students & every parent at www.antiragging.in and www.amanmovement.org.(E)  Medical colleges to get NAAC accreditation.(F) Placing of Anti-Ragging posters and hoardings in different parts of medical college/hostel.2.	 MCI has provided a link ( ugc.ac.in/page/videos-Regarding-Ragging.aspx) in their   website regarding the Four short films and a documentary film for information and counseling of students on the ill effects of ragging. Also, all the Universities/Institutions had been requested by the MCI to show these films regularly to the students during orientation and other programmes. 3.  The MCI has also amended  the “Medical Council of India (Prevention and prohibition of Ragging in Medical Colleges/Institutions) Regulations 2009” to root out ragging in all its forms from medical colleges/institutions in the country. 4.  For the convenience of students/parents, a separate page on Anti – Ragging Information with details of Nodal Officer has been uploaded on the website of the MCI ( Annexure-II)
