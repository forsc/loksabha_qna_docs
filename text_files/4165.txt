                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3726






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.07.2019






Decline in Indigenous Breed of Cows




Devji Mansingram Patel














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                     (a) whether a decline of 32 per cent in indigenous breed of cows has been reported during the last five years and if so, the details thereof; (b) the types of indigenous breeds of cow, buffalo, goat, sheep and camel available across the country at present, State-wise; and (c) the amount of fund incurred by the Government on PashudhanSanjivani and Animal Health Cards (Nakul Swasthya Patra) Schemes for conservation and improvement of indigenous breeds of cow, buffalo, goat, etc. across the country including Rajasthan during each of the last three years?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY & DAIRYING (DR. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a)As per Livestock Census, the population of indigenous cows has marginally decreased from 89.24 million in 2007 to 89.22 million in 2012.(b) The list of registered indigenous breeds of cow, buffalo, goat, sheep and camel available across the country is given at Annexure I. (c)  An allocation of Rs 148 crores has been approved under the scheme for implementation Pashu Sanjivani component and GoI share of Rs 85.74 crore has been released to various States across the country. The total approved amount for Rajasthan state under Pashu Sanjivani component is Rs. 1230.59 lakhs and released amount as GoI share is Rs. 738.36 lakhs.
