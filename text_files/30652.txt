                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2126






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2021






Audit of Manufactured and Allocated Ventilators




Srinivas Kesineni














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has undertaken an audit to ascertain if the ventilators manufactured and allotted to hospitals during the COVID-19 pandemic are functional and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor ;(b)  whether the Government has mapped the allocation of such ventilators State/UT/ district-wise and at the hospital level, if so, the details thereof; and(c) the details of the ventilators allotted to State/UT/district-wise specifically to Vijaywada in Andhra Pradesh?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE HEALTH & FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) An Independent assessment of Installation, Commissioning and Operation of ventilators was carried out.  35 Teams were constituted which physically visited the   States/UTs. Each team comprised a representative each from Director General of Heath Service (Dte.GHS), Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO) and HLL Lifecare Limited. The teams co-opted one member each from each user hospital and manufacturer whose ventilator is supplied in the hospital.These 35 teams covered 606 Districts and 1699 hospitals in 35 States/Union Territories.  38729 “Make in India” ventilators were evaluated. As assessed during this exercise around 90% of ventilators were found to be functional and rest were made fully functional after some interventions by manufacturers of these ventilators. (b) & (c) State wise allocation of Ventilators is made based on the demand projected by the States/UTs.  Number of ventilators provided State/UT wise is enclosed at annexure. Allocation to Districts and Hospitals is made by the State Government. A total of 313 Ventilators were provided to District Vijayawada in Andhra Pradesh. ***
