                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1643






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Sagarmala Seaplane Service




Kripanath Mallah














                        Will the Minister of





PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is planning to kick off ambitious project of Sagarmala Seaplane Services with potential airline operators within the country;(b) 	 if so,  the  details  thereof  including  the  funds estimated for this project; and(c) 	the details of the target likely to be set for this along  with  the  time  by  which  it  is  likely  to  be implemented?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)  	Sagarmala Development Company Limited (SDCL), a CPSE under Ministry of Ports, Shipping and Waterways (MoPSW) had invited Expression of Interest (EoI) for Seaplane Services whereby airplane operators were invited to undertake development and operation in India. Further, MoPSW and Ministry of Civil Aviation (MoCA) have signed an Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) on 15th June, 2021 to jointly facilitate the development of Sea Plane Services in India under RCS-UDAN Scheme. (b) & (c) Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs had approved MoCA’s proposal for revival of existing unserved / underserved airports/ airstrips/ heliports/ water aerodromes of the State Governments, Airports Authority of India and Civil Enclaves, on 6th March, 2017, at an estimated cost of Rs 4500 crore. The revival of an airport/ water aerodrome/ heliport awarded under RCS-UDAN where it requires for operationalization of RCS-UDAN flights is undertaken under this scheme.                                              *******
