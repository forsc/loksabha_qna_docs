                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4443






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.03.2020






Scheme for Adolescent Girls




Chandra Sekhar Sahu













                                                    Vincent H Pala
                                                








                                                    Pritam Gopinath Munde
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)      whether the Government has launched Scheme for Adolescent Girls (SAG) across the country and if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise; (b)      whether the introduction of the said scheme has reduced the incidence of anaemia among the adolescent girls and if so, the details thereof; and(c)	if not, the reasons therefor along with the steps being taken by the Government to improve the incidence of anaemia among adolescent girls across all States/UTs? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a) to (c)    The Government is implementing Scheme for Adolescent Girls (SAG), a Centrally-sponsored scheme, introduced in the year 2010-11 in 205 districts across the country on a pilot basis. Subsequently, Government approved expansion and universalisation of the Scheme for out of school Adolescent Girls in a phased manner in the age group of 11-14 years on 16.11.2017. 303 additional districts were added in 2017-18 and the scheme was extended to all the districts across the country w.e.f. 01.04.2018.The Scheme aims at all-round development of out-of-school Adolescent Girls (AGs) of age 11-14 years and has two components Nutrition and Non Nutrition. Under the Nutrition component of the scheme, each out of school Adolescent Girl of age 11-14 years is provided 600 calories, 18-20 grams of protein and micronutrients @ Rs.9.5 per beneficiary per day for 300 days in a year.  The non- nutrition component has built in factor to motivate out of school girls to go back to formal schooling or skill training. Government is also providing IFA Supplementation, Health check-up & referral services, food fortification, dietary diversification etc. in convergence with Ministry of Health & Family Welfare under Non-Nutrition component of the Scheme. Nutrition beneficiaries under Scheme for Adolescent Girls during last three years is at Annexure-I.  State/UT-wise funds released during the last three years and the current year is at Annexure-II.In addition to Scheme for Adolescent Girls, Government has launched Poshan Abhiyan since 18th December, 2017 to address the problem of malnutrition.  The goals of Poshan Abhiyan are to achieve improvement in nutritional status of Adolescent Girls, Pregnant Women and Lactating Mothers and children 0-6 years. As per NFHS-IV, anaemia among women in the age group of 15-49 years was 53.10% whereas as per CNNS (2016-18) anaemia among adolescent girls in the age group of 12-19 years was 40%.
