                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

295






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.07.2021






Doubling Farmers Income in Maharashtra




Vinayak Bhaurao Raut














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the steps taken to double farmers’ income by 2022 and the funds allocated for the same during the last five years, scheme-wise for the State of Maharashtra;(b)	the current growth rate of the agriculture sector;(c)	the percentage of growth rate of agriculture sector which has to be achieved in order to double the farmers’ income; and (d)	the steps taken by the Government to achieve the said growth rate for the agriculture sector? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE ???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????         (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (d): The Government has adopted several developmental programmes, schemes, reforms and policies that focus on higher incomes for the farmers. All these policies & programmes are being supported by higher budgetary allocations, non-budgetary financial resources by way of creating Corpus Funds like Micro Irrigation Fund and Agri-marketing scheme to strengthen eNAM and GrAMs, as also in the Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying to promote dairy and fishery sectors. There have been several reforms to unleash the potential and these include Market Reforms like Model APLMC (Promotion & Facilitation) Act, 2017; Establishment of Gramin Agriculture Markets (GrAMs); Agri-Export Policy, 2018; The Farmers Produce Trade and Commerce (Promotion & Facilitation) Act, 2020; The Farmers (Empowerment & Protection) Agreement on Price Assurance and Farm Services Act.  2020; Amendments to Essential Commodities Act, 1955; Promotion of 10,000 FPOs with necessary financial support under Atma Nirbhar Package (Agriculture)and Supplementary Income transfers under PM-KISAN; Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojna  (PMFBY);  Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY); Increase in Minimum Support Price (MSPs) for all Kharif & Rabi Crops ensuring a minimum of 50 percent of profit margin on the cost of production; Har Med Par Ped; Bee-Keeping; Rashtriya Gokul Mission; Blue Revolution; Interest Subvention Scheme; Kisan Credit Card (KCC) that now offers production loan to even dairy & fishery farmers besides agricultural crops etc.  A list of interventions and scheme launched for the benefit of farmer is at Annexure-I.	The funds allocated scheme-wise for the State of Maharashtra during FY 2016-17 to FY 2020-21 is at Annexure-II.	The Growth rate of Gross Value Added (GVA) of Agriculture & Allied Sector (at Current Prices) is 6.6%, as per Provisional Estimates of Annual National Income, 2020-21, released on 31st May, 2021 by National Statistical Office (NSO).The last Situation Assessment Survey was conducted by NSSO in 2012-13.
