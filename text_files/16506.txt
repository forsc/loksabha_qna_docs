                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

268






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.09.2020






Impact of Lockdown on Harvesting




Balubhau (Alias Suresh Narayan) Dhanorkar














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the harvesting process, which usually starts in mid-April, was thrown completely off-balance due to the nation-wide lockdown; (b) 	if so, the reasons for the same; and (c) 	the details of loss in crops, crop-wise and State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (c):	 No, Sir.  In order to ensure timely and safe harvesting of rabi crops during the lockdown period due to pandemic COVID-19, the Ministry of Home affairs, Government of India had issued comprehensive guidelines vide their letter No. 40-3/2020-DM-1(A) dated 15.04.2020 on the measures to be taken for containment of Covid-19, which also ensured smooth functioning of Agricultural operations.  Besides, the Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare had issued the Standard Operations Procedures (SOPs) dated 18.04.2020 for farmers for their safety and keeping social distancing while undertaking farm related activities. All the harvesting of rabi crops was completed timely.As a result, the production of rabi foodgrains  increased from 143.70 million tonnes in rabi 2018-19 to 153.27 Million tonnes  in rabi 2019-20 (4th Advance estimates).******
