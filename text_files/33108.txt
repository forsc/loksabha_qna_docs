                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CHEMICALS AND FERTILIZERS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

483






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2022






PMBJP Centres in Andhra Pradesh




Raghu Ramakrishna Raju Kanumuru














                        Will the Minister of





CHEMICALS AND FERTILIZERS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of CHEMICALS AND FERTILIZERS be pleased to state: (a) whether Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Pariyojana (PMBJP) Centres are functioning in the State of Andhra Pradesh; (b) if so, the details thereof, district-wise; (c) whether the Government has identified any places for opening new PMBJP centres in the State; and (d) if so, the efforts being made to set up the said centres at the earliest?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) & (b): As on 31.12.2021, about 183 Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Kendras (PMBJKs) have been opened in the State of Andhra Pradesh covering all the districts of the State. District-wise list of PMBJKs functional in Andhra Pradesh is enclosed as Annexure.(c) & (d): The Government has set a target to open 10,500 Kendras across the country by March 2025. In order to achieve the same, letters at various levels are sent regularly by the Department of Pharmaceuticals and Pharmaceuticals & Medical Devices Bureau of India (PMBI), the implementing agency of the Scheme, to the State Governments, including the State of Andhra Pradesh for expediting opening of PMBJKs in government premises. PMBI also regularly organizes workshops and seminars to encourage private entrepreneurs/NGOs/Civil Societies and unemployed pharmacists for opening of PMBJKs.
