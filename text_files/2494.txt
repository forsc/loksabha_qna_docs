                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2303






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.07.2019






Constraints to Complete Central Institutions




Jayadev Galla














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is facing any constraints to complete the construction of Centralinstitutions in Andhra Pradesh as mandated by Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014;(b) the status of progress of each of the institution under Schedule 13 of the AP Reorganisation Act;(c) whether the Government proposes to release a White Paper about implementation of variousprovisions relating to the Ministry in Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) No Sir.(b) The details are as below:<pre>S.No Name of Institute Status of Institutions1 Indian Institute ofTechnology,TirupatiIn pursuance of the Budget Announcement 2014-15, IIT Trupati has beenestablished as a part of Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014. Theacademic session of IIT-Tirupati started in 2015-16 from its temporarycampus at Chandalwada Venkata Subaiah College of Engineering, ReniguntaRoad, Tirupati.Land measuring 589.55 acres at Merlapaka Village, Yerpedu (Mandal),Chittoor District (Near Tirupathi) has been approved for permanent campus.The construction of Transit Campus is completed. The institute has a studentstrength of 631 and faculty strength of 55. As on date, an amount ofRs. 203.36 crore has been released to IIT-Tirupati.2 National Instituteof Technology,TadepalligudemNIT Andhra Pradesh has started functioning from its temporary campuslocated at Sri Vasavi Engineering College, Tadepalligudem since 2015. StateGovernment of Andhra Pradesh has provided land at Tadepalligudem, WestGodavari District for the construction of permanent campus. The entireconstruction work has been bifurcated into two segments ‘1A’ and ‘1B’ forsmooth operations. .Rs 460.50 Cr has been approved for construction of permanent campus.3Indian Institute ofManagement,VishakhapatnamThe Institute started its operation from its temporary campus i.e. AndhraPradesh University Vishakhapatnam. PGP course has started. Cabinet on 5thSeptember, 2018 approved an amount of Rs. 594.32 crore for establishmentpermanent campus. An amount of 105.32 Crore has been released.The State Government has allocated 230.25 acres of land in VillageGambheeram, District Vishakhapatnam for permanent campus of IIMVishakhapatnam.4 Indian Institutes ofScience Educationand Research,TirupatiThe Cabinet has approved the setting up of Indian Institute of ScienceEducation and Research (IISER), Tirupati with a capital cost of Rs. 1137.16crore. IISER Tirupati has started functioning on temporary campus since2016. Rs 525 Cr has been sanctioned under Higher Education FundingAgency.5 Indian Institute ofInformationTechnology DesignandManufacturing,KurnoolIIITDM Kurnool has been established as per the AP Reorganisation Act, 2014.A site selection committee on 01.04.2015 ascertained the suitability of landidentified by Government of Andhra Pradesh at Jagannathgattu DinnedevaraPadu (v), Kurnool(M), Kurnool District, for construction of permanentcampus. Since 2015 lllTDM Kurnool is functioning from temporary campus ofIIITDM Kancheepuram. A sum of Rs. 297.0 Cr have been allocated asfinancial assistance out of which Rs. 256.0 Cr is for construction of theinstitute and Rs. 41 Cr for recurring expenditure. A total of Rs 43.46 croreshas been released. In July 2018 the Institute has shifted to its permanentcampus. The total student strength is of 328 students.6 Central University,AnanthapurThe Union Cabinet in its meeting held on 16th May, 2018 has accorded inprincipalapproval for establishment a Central University by the name ofCentral University of Andhra Pradesh in village Janthaluru Anantapur Districtin the State of Andhra Pradesh with provision of funds of Rs.450 crore formeeting the first phase expenditure. University of Hyderabad is mentor ofthe Central University of Andhra Pradesh. An amount of Rs.8.00 Crores hasbeen released to University of Hyderabad (Mentor University) for CentralUniversity of Andhra Pradesh during the financial year 2018-19.The Central University (Amendment) Bill 2018 was introduced in Lok Sabhaon 14.12.2018. However, the Bill could not be passed in the 16th Lok Sabhaand it lapsed as the 16th Lok Sabha got dissolved.7 Tribal University,VizianagaramOperationalisation of the Central Tribal University in the State of AndhraPradesh from transit campus by a mentor University i.e. Andhra Universityhas been announced on 24th January 2019.An amount of Rs.0.50 Crore has been released to Andhra University (MentorUniversity) for Central Tribal University of Andhra Pradesh, Vijayanagramduring the financial year 2018-19.The Central University (Amendment) Bill 2018 was introduced in Lok Sabhaon 14.12.2018. However, the Bill could not be passed in the 16th Lok Sabhaand it lapsed as the 16th Lok Sabha got dissolved.</pre>(c) to (e) No Sir. Keeping in view the present status/ progress of work relating to establishment ofvarious educational institutes, the Ministry does not feel necessity to release a WhitePaper.***
