                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1734






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay National Welfare Fund




Raja Amareshwara Naik













                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                








                                                    Rajveer (Raju Bhaiya) Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has set up Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay National Welfare Fund for sportspersons to assist outstanding sportspersons of yesteryear living in indigent circumstances who had brought glory to the country in sports;(b) if so, the details thereof including sportspersons who received financial help during the last three years, State-wise;(c) whether the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports (MoYAS) and YuWaah (Generation Unlimited India) signed on a partnership to empower young people in India and if so, the details thereof;(d) whether the MoYAS launched campaign Cheer India with Indian Railways to encourage and cheer the Olympics-bound athletes and if so, the details thereof;(e) the steps being taken by the Government in this regard; and(f) whether the Ministry of Youth Affairs has signed collaboration with UN to develop the potential of young people through meaningful engagement and participation in social, civic and community initiatives and if so, the details thereof and other steps being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER FOR YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS[SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR](a) Yes, Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay National Welfare Fund for Sportspersons (PDUNWFS) was set up in 1982 by the Government to provide assistance to outstanding sportspersons living in indigent circumstances, families of deceased sportspersons, for medical treatment to sportspersons or family members, to sportspersons injured during training or participation in sports competitions, to sportspersons for training and for procurement of equipment or for participation in national and international sports events, to coaches and support personnel or their family members and for medical treatment to coaches and support personnel or their family members. (b) The sportspersons who were given financial assistance during each of the last three years (as on 05.07.2021) is enclosed at Annexure ‘I’.(c)  Yes. Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports and United Nations Children’s Emergency Fund (UNICEF) have signed a “Statement of Intent” on 20.07.2020 to establish YuWaah, Generation Unlimited (GenU), a global multi-stakeholder platform in India. As per the Statement of Intent, the objectives of this project are: ·   Support young people by providing entrepreneurship classes (online and offline) with successful entrepreneurs and experts, towards establishing entrepreneurial mindset among young people.·   Upskilling of young people on 21st century skills, life skills, digital skills through online and offline channels and support them through self-learning, for their productive lives and the future of work.·   Create linkages with aspirational economic opportunities to connect young people with employment opportunities, including building pathways to connect them with jobs or self-employment. For this, innovative solutions and technology platforms will be engaged to maximize the scale and reach.·   Providing career guidance support to young people through career portal as well as through job-readiness and self-exploration sessions to make young people career-ready. The role of Department of Youth Affairs in this project is to provide relevant experts to participate on the YuWaah Technical Working Groups/ Task Forces. (d) & (e) Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports (MYAS) and Indian Olympic Association (IOA) have jointly launched the nationwide campaign “Cheer4india” with Indian Railways to boost the morale of Tokyo 2020 Olympic-bound Indian contingent and with the objective to sensitize people of the Nation about Olympics.The campaign includes:        (1) Olympics Quiz- “Road to Tokyo 2020”        (2) Fan Engagement Drive activities like             (a) Selfie Points             (b) Best Slogan/Digital poster contest             (c) Theme song for Indian contingent             (d) Social media filters etc.        (3) Olympics Symposiums and DialoguesA call was given by Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports to all the Ministries/Departments/State Governments as well as private partners to become a part of the Cheer4India campaign and support our Indian athletes as one Nation.(f) Yes. Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports (MoYAS) and United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) / United Nations Volunteers (UNV) agreed on strengthening youth volunteering in India through a joint project titled “Strengthening of Nehru Yuva Kendra Sangathan (NYKS) and National Service Scheme(NSS)”. The project is aimed at providing catalytic support to the Youth Volunteer Schemes of the MoYAS and intended to facilitate youth participation and voice in peace and sustainable development programmes in order to realize of full social, economic and human potential of youth. The primary objectives of the Project are:(i)         Strengthen National Youth Volunteer Infrastructure of Department of Youth Affairs (DoYA);(ii)       Strengthen capacity of rural youth clubs for increased and diversified opportunities for youth volunteers;(iii)      Increase participation, recognition and contribution of youth volunteers in development process.The project “Strengthening NYKS and NSS” adopted a collaborative approach and included key partners in designing and implementing it.Further, Department of Youth Affairs had been releasing India’s annual voluntary contribution to UNDP/UNV since 2007-08 as India’s annual voluntary contribution. The United Nations Volunteer (UNV) Programme was created by the United Nations General Assembly in 1970 with the objective to provide developing countries with an additional source of qualified and trained manpower at low cost and opportunity to young professionals from all nations to take part in development activities worldwide. ****
