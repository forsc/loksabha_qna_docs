                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

271






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.09.2020






Loss to Floriculture during Lockdown




Andimuthu Raja














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has conducted any survey on the loss occurred in floriculture across the country, especially in Tamil Nadu during the lockdown; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Government has provided any special financial assistance to the farmers due to their unsold produce; (d)	 if so, the details thereof; and (e) 	the details of the steps taken by the Government to uplift the floriculture farmers after the lockdown?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) & (b):	No survey has been reported to be conducted by the State Government, in Tamil Nadu during the lockdown period.  However, Govt. of Goa, Govt. of Karnataka and Govt. of Punjab have reported that a survey has been conducted in their States.  Govt. of Goa has reported a loss of Rs. 45.65 lakhs in an area of 5.6 ha.  while  Govt. of Punjab has reported a loss of Rs. 420 lakh.   Govt. of Karnataka has reported a incurring loss of 11,685 hectare of area under flower crops.   (c) & (d):	No special financial assistance has been extended to the farmers for their unsold produce by the Government.   Govt. of Karnataka has provided an amount of Rs. 31.8375 crores under the special programme ‘Compensation to the flower growing farmers’.  [(e):	The assistance is provided to floriculture farmers under Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH), a Centrally Sponsored Scheme, for area expansion of flowers and support for Integrated Nutrient/Pest Management, planting material of flowers under protected structures and post-harvest management of flowers.  The details of the cost norms and assistance provided are given in attached Annexure.  The assistance may also be availed by the State Governments under the action plan of MIDH.
