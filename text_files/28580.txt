                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

829






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2021






Sale of Air India




Su Thirunavukkarasar














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Union Government has recently sold Air India;(b) if so, the details thereof along with the status of debt burden of the ailing airline;(c) whether the Government has taken any steps to protect the rights and privileges of employees serving in the airline;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) and (b): The financial bids by two Qualified interested bidders were received by the Transaction Advisor on 15.09.2021. M/s Talace Pvt Ltd, a wholly owned subsidiary of M/s Tata Sons Pvt. Ltd won the bid being the highest bidder.; Share Purchase Agreement has been signed with the qualified bidder on 25.10.2021. ;The total debt of Air India as on 30.09.2021 was Rs. 51,554.47 crore (provisional);;(c) to (e): Interests of the employees including job security for one year, Provident Fund and medical facility etc., have been duly taken care by the Government and incorporated in the Share Purchase Agreement signed with the qualified bidder on 25.10.2021.
