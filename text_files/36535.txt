                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3894






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2022






Infant Mortality Rate




Ganesh Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the infant mortality rate in the country is 34 per thousand as per NITI Aayog;(b) if so, whether this figure is 39 per thousand for children below five years and most of the children die due to diseases like diarrhoea and pneumonia;(c) whether more than 10 lakh children die in the country each year due to malnutrition and 4 out of 10 children are malnourished and underweight particularly in Bihar, Meghalaya and Madhya Pradesh; and(d) if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) to (d): As per the Sample Registration System (SRS) report of Registrar General of India (RGI), the Infant Mortality Rate (IMR) is 30 per 1,000 live births in 2019 at National Level. As per the Sample Registration System (SRS) Bulletin of Registrar General of India (RGI), the Under 5 Mortality Rate (U5MR) is 35 per 1,000 live births in 2019 at National Level. As per SRS 2010-13 report of Registrar General of India, major causes of child mortality in India are - prematurity & low birth weight (29.8%), pneumonia (17.1%), diarrheal diseases (8.6%), other non-communicable diseases (8.3%), birth asphyxia & birth trauma (8.2%), injuries (4.6%), congenital anomalies (4.4%), ill-defined or cause unknown (4.4%), acute bacterial sepsis and severe infections (3.6%), fever of Unknown origin (2.5%), and all other remaining causes (8.4%).According to NFHS-5 (2019-21), the percentages of malnourished children under five years of age (classified as stunted, wasted and underweight) in the State of Bihar, Meghalaya and Madhya Pradesh is placed as below:S. No.	States	Children under 5 years who are stunted (height-for-age)* (%)	Children under 5 years who are wasted (weight-for-height)* (%)	Children under 5 years who are underweight (weight-for-age)* (%)1	Bihar	42.9	22.9	41.02	Meghalaya	46.5	12.1	26.63	Madhya Pradesh	35.7	19.0	33.0* Below -2 standard deviations, based on WHO standard*****
