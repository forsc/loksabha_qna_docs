                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2042






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.09.2020






PM SVANidhi Scheme




Pocha Brahmananda Reddy













                                                    Beesetti  Venkata Satyavathi
                                                








                                                    Talari Rangaiah
                                                








                                                    Kotagiri Sridhar
                                                








                                                     Chandra Sekhar Bellana
                                                








                                                    Su Thirunavukkarasar
                                                








                                                    Parvatagouda Chandanagouda Gaddigoudar
                                                








                                                    S. Jothimani
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	whether the Government has launched Pradhan Mantri Street Vendor’s Atmanirbhar Nidhi (PM SVANidhi) during the Covid-19 crisis and if so, the details thereof;b)	whether the Government has any data regarding the total number of Street Vendors in India and if so, the details thereof, State/District-wise;c)	whether the Government is considering income support i.e. providing working capital to the street vendors under this scheme;d)	the details of applications received, funds allocated and disbursed under the scheme till date, State-wise;e)	the steps being taken/to be taken to improve the coverage of the scheme in coming years; andf)	whether the Government has estimated the level of internet accessibility of street vendors which will help in digital repayment of loans, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor? 






ANSWER




                                                        (a):	Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs has, on June 01, 2020, launched Prime Minister Street Vendor’s Amta Nirbhar Nidhi Scheme (PM SVANidhi). It aims at facilitating collateral free working capital loans upto Rs 10,000 of 1 year tenure, to about 50 lakh street vendors across the country. It also provides for incentives in the form of interest subsidy @ 7% per annum on regular repayment of loan and cash-back upto Rs. 100 per month on undertaking prescribed digital transactions. Further, on timely or early repayment, the vendors will be eligible for the next cycle of working capital loan with an enhanced limit. For the purpose of scheme administration, an end-to-end solution through an IT platform has been developed in collaboration with Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI). The loan processing under the Scheme has commenced on July 02, 2020.(b):	The Street Vendors (Protection of Livelihood and Regulation of Street Vending) Act, 2014 is implemented by the respective States/ Union Territories by framing of Rules and Schemes. As per provision of the Act, the Town Vending Committee shall, within such period and in such manner as may be specified in the scheme, conduct a survey of all existing street vendors, within the area under its jurisdiction. As informed by the States/UTs, the number of street vendors identified, so far, is 18,25,776. State/Union Territory wise detail is at Annexure-I.(c):	PM SVANidhi aims at facilitating collateral free working capital loans upto Rs 10,000 of 1 year tenure, to an estimated about 50 lakh street vendors vending in the urban areas belonging to urban / rural areas across the country. (d): 	The approved outlay for PM SVANidhi scheme is Rs.600 crore. A statement giving State/Union Territories wise details of loan applications received, loan amount sanctioned and disbursed up to September 17, 2020 isat Annexure-II.(e):   In order to extend coverage, the street vendors who are left out of the Urban Local Body-led identification survey, or rural vendors vending in urban areas, have been made eligible to take benefit under the Scheme on production of a Letter of Recommendation issued by the ULB. Other initiatives include launching PM SVANidhi Web Portal and a Mobile App for lending institutions and Urban Local Body officials, a digital module for Letter of Recommendation, and integration of Common Service Centres with the application process. Further, the Radio Jingles were broadcast and newspaper advertisement published in Hindi and other regional languages in July-August 2020.Also, States / UTs have been provided Information, Education and Communication (IEC) material in local languages for outreach and dissemination of the benefits to the vendors to avail the facilities. (f):    No Sir.                                           *****
