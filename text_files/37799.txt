                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT be pleased to state:(a)    	whether the Government has conducted a detailed State-wise study of the performance/impact of the Poshan Abhiyaan and if so, the details thereof;(b)    	the details regarding achieving the five objectives under Poshan Abhiyaan in Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, district-wise;(c)    	the rates of stunting, under-nutrition, anaemia and low birth weight among infants, children, adolescent girls and women in the above mentioned States, district-wise over the period of the Poshan Abhiyaan;(d)    	the details of funds released and utilized by both Union Government and State Government thereon; and(e)    	the number of beneficiaries of the said scheme, state/UT-wise including Odisha? 






ANSWER




(a) 	POSHAN Abhiyaan was launched on 8thMarch, 2018, with an objective to reduce malnutrition from the country in a phased manner and achieve improvement in nutritional status of Children from 0-6 years, Adolescent Girls, Pregnant Women and Lactating Mothers in a time bound manner through components like ICT Application, Convergence, Community Mobilization, Behavioural Change & Jan Andolan, Capacity Building, Incentives and Awards and Innovations. The status of malnutrition (stunting, wasting, and underweight) in the country amongst children under 5 years and women (15-49 years) is provided by the National Family Health Survey (NFHS) conducted periodically by the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare (MoH&FW). As per the report of NFHS-5 (2019-21), the nutrition indicators for children under 5 years have improved as compared with NFHS-4 (2015-16). Stunting, Wasting and Underweight has reduced from 38.4% to 35.5%, 21.0% to 19.3% and 35.8% to 32.1% respectively. The State/UT wise prevalence of Stunting Wasting and Underweight as per NFHS 5 is at annexure -I(b) & (c) The objectives of POSHAN Abhiyaan were to achieve improvement in nutritional status in terms of wasting, stunting and anaemia of Children from 0-6 years, Adolescent Girls, Pregnant Women and Lactating Mothers, in a time bound manner.
