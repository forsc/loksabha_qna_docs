                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TRIBAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1281






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.09.2020






Culture Conservation of Tribal Population




Gowdar Mallikarjunappa Siddeshwara














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)   the details of expenditure incurred by the Government for the development of tribal areas in the country during the last three years;(b) whether special attention has been given to the cultural conservation of tribal population during that period; and(c)   if so, the details thereof along with the expenditure incurred during the said period and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        (a):   Tribal Sub-Plan (TSP) [also called as Scheduled Tribe Component (STC)] is the dedicated source of fund for tribal development across the country.  41 Central Ministries has been mandated by NITI Aayog to earmark Tribal Sub-Plan funds in the range of 4.3% to 17.5% of their total Scheme allocation every year for tribal development. Besides, State Governments are mandated to earmark TSP funds in proportion of ST population in the State. The details of expenditure incurred by the Central Government for the welfare and development of Scheduled Tribes in the country during the last three years is as under:(Rs. in crores)Government	2017-18	2018-19	2019-20STC Component of Central Ministries / Departments	30020.25	33381.93	41943.43(b) & (c): The Government has established Tribal Research Institutes in 24 States and 1 Union Territory. Core responsibility of TRIs is to function as a body of knowledge & research and as a think tank for tribal development,  preservation of art & culture of tribal people. Funds are provided to the TRIs under the scheme ‘Support to Tribal Research Institute (TRI)’ to carry out various activities viz. research studies, evaluation studies, organization of training/seminar/workshop, organization of tribal festivals, baseline survey, publications, documentaries / documentation, organization of exchange visits, construction of museums, including tribal freedom fighters’ museum etc. Conservation of culture of tribal population is also done through organization of national and regional level tribal festivals and various research studies under the scheme ‘Tribal Festival, Research, Information and Mass Education’.In order to acknowledge the heroic and patriotic deeds of tribal people, theGovernment has decided to establish a state-of-the-art Tribal Museum in the States where Tribals lived, struggled against the Britishers and refused to be bowed down so that the coming generations may know how tribals were far ahead in making sacrifices. The repository also envisages to have folk songs, photos/videos regarding their evolution, place of origin, lifestyle, eating habits, architecture, education level, traditional art, folk dances and other anthropological details of the tribes in India so that the rich cultural heritage of tribals can be demonstrated world over.    In order to ensure synergy in the functioning of all Tribal Research Institutes (TRIs) across the Country, the Government is contemplating to set up the National Tribal Research Institute to provide hand holding support for realizing the resolve of the Government to develop TRIs as a think tank and knowledge hub for tribal related issues.
