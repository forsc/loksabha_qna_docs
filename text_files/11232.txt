                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

188






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2020






Women Literacy Rate




Jayadev Galla














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether it is true that the rate of women literacy among all groups in rural areas ofundivided Andhra Pradesh is just 50 per cent which is not even the national average;(b) whether it is also true that the rate of men literacy among all groups in rural areas ofundivided Andhra Pradesh is just 69 per cent which is again less than the national average;(c) if so, the reasons for this situation inspite of various Government schemes andprogrammes for literacy;(d) whether Saakshar Bharat is not being implemented among the females in AP since itsadult female literacy rate is above 50 per cent; and(e) if so, the steps taken by the Government to address this issue?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a) to (c) As per Census 2011, the rate of women literacy among all groups in rural areas ofundivided Andhra Pradesh in age group of 7 and above is 51.54 per cent compared to thenational average of 57.93 per cent. The rate of men literacy among all groups in rural areas ofundivided Andhra Pradesh in age group of 7 and above is 69.38 per cent compared to thenational average of 77.15 per cent. Various factors viz. poverty, gender and social inequities,etc. are impeding literacy in the country.(d) & (e) The scheme of Saakshar Bharat was implemented in the rural areas of 410 districtsin 26 States and one UT, including 19 districts in undivided Andhra Pradesh, that had adultfemale literacy rate of 50 percent and below as per Census 2001, with special focus onwomen and other disadvantaged groups, from October 2009 to May 2014. After bifurcationof the state in June 2014, the scheme was implemented in 10 districts in Andhra Pradesh and09 districts in Telangana respectively. As per the results of Learners Assessment testconducted by NIOS, a total of 19,25,102 learners (Male- 3,46,886 and Female – 15,78,216)and 20,31,913 learners (Male- 4,49,497 and Female – 15,82,416) were certified in the statesof Andhra Pradesh and Telangana respectively from 2014-15 to 2017-18.****
