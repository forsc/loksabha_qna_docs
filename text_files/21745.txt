                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2910






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2021






Forest Cover




Tirath Singh Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total forest cover along with the percentage increase/decrease in forest cover across the country during the last ten years;(b)	whether the forest area has decreased due to forest fires and other reasons in the Himalayan region, particularly in Uttarakhand;(c)	whether the Union Government has providedor proposes to provide green bonus to the Himalayan States including Uttarakhand; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	Forest Survey of India, Dehradun, an organization under the Ministry carries out the assessment of forest cover of the country biennially and the findings are published in India State of Forest Report (ISFR). The forest cover assessment is a wall-to-wall mapping exercise based on remote sensing supported by intensive ground verification and field data from National Forest Inventory. ISFR-2019 is the latest report in the series. As per ISFR 2019, total forest cover of the country is 7,12,249 square kilometers which is 21.67% of the geographical area of the country. The current assessment shows an increase of 3976 square kilometer of forest cover at the national level as compared to the previous assessment i.e. ISFR 2017.
