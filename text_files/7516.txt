                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1163






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.11.2019






Quality of Education




Sumedhanand Saraswati














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has formulated any guidelines or made rules to assess thequality of education being imparted in the deemed universities of the country;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) if not, the reasons therefor; and(d) the details of the measures taken/proposed to be taken by the Government toimprove the quality of education of these universities particularly in respect of theinfrastructure, faculty and research facilities?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (d): Yes, Sir. The University Grants Commission (UGC) has notified the UGC(Institutions Deemed to be Universities) Regulations, 2019 to regulate the Deemed to beUniversities in the country. In order to assess and improve the quality of highereducation in Deemed to be Universities, UGC conducts annual monitoring of theperformance and academic outcomes of these Institutions on the following performanceparameters:<pre>i. Graduate outcomesii. Linkage of the students with the society/industryiii. Training of the students in essential professional skills such as team-work,communication skills, leadership skills, time management skills, soft skills, etc.iv. The teacher student ratio shall not be less than 1:20v. Induction programmes for studentsvi. To adopt learning outcome based curriculum frameworkvii. Use ICT based learning tools for effective teaching learning processesviii. To adopt CBCS and academic flexibility for interdisciplinary learningix. Examination reforms to ensure that the student assessment is based onunderstanding of conceptsx. To ensure that at least half of the students passing out appear for examinationssuch as GATE/JAM/UGC-NETxi. Tracking of the student progress after completion of coursexii. Ensure induction and refresher programmes for teachersxiii. Compulsory participation in NIRF rankingsxiv. Effective implementation of measures for quality improvement as per the qualitymandate of UGC.</pre>*****
