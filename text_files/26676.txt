                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2435






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.08.2021






Wasteland to Arable Land




Ranjeeta Koli













                                                    Rama Devi
                                                








                                                    Mansukhbhai Dhanjibhai Vasava
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                                     Wasteland to Arable Land2435.SHRIMATI RANJEETA KOLI:     SHRI MANSUKHBHAI DHANJIBHAI VASAVA:     SHRIMATI RAMA DEVI:Will the Minister of RURAL DEVELOPMENT be pleased to state:(a)	whether arable land in the country is decreasing year by year, if so, the details thereof during each of the last three years and the current year along with the steps taken by the Government to prevent the decline of arable land; (b)	the total area of arid land in Bihar at present and its percentage share out of the total land;(c)	whether the Government proposes to convert barren land into arable land, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?(d)	the details of the schemes implemented by the Union Government to convert arid land/barren land into agricultural/arable land during the last three years particularly in Bihar;(e)	the amount of fund allocated and utilized in this regard, year-wise;(f)	the area of total arid land converted into agricultural land so far with the help of above mentioned works and funds allocated; and(g)	whether the Union Government has been getting cooperation of the State Governments for converting wasteland into arable land, if so, the details thereof along with the details of the works carried out in this regard during the last three years, State-wise and if not, the action taken by the Union Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                                                                    ANSWER                       MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT                                    (SHRI FAGGAN SINGH KULASTE)(a)	The details of Arable land, as per the publication on Land Use Statistics for 2017-18 (latest available), provided by the Directorate of Economics and Statistics, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare are given as Annexure I. As per this information, slight decrease in the arable land, has been observed from 2014-15 to 2017-18. The Government of India has been implementing, Per Drop More Crop, Har Khet Ko Pani, Accelerated Irrigation Benefit Programme and Watershed Development Component of PMKSY to prevent the decline of arable land.(b) According to Directorate of Economics and Statistics, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, out of 93.60 lakh hectares of geographical area of Bihar, 4.31 lakh hectares (4.60 %) is barren and unculturable land.(c)to(e) As per the Seventh Schedule of Constitution of India, ‘land’ falls under the purview of State Government and, therefore, it is for the State Governments to take suitable steps for transforming barren land into arable land. However, Government of India supplements the efforts of State Governments through various programmes.The Department of Land Resources has been implementing an area development programme i.e. Integrated Watershed Management Programme (IWMP) w.e.f. 26.02.2009 for development of rainfed / degraded areas in 28 States including Bihar (now 27 States and 2 UTs of the Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh). The major activities taken up under the programme inter alia includes ridge area treatment, drainage line treatment, soil and moisture conservation, rain water harvesting, nursery raising, afforestation, horticulture, pasture development, livelihoods for assetless persons. The IWMP has been amalgamated as the Watershed Development Component of the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (WDC-PMKSY) with effect from 2015-16.Under WDC-PMKSY (erstwhile IWMP), since 2009-10, Rs. 19926.29 Cr. has been released as Central share to States / UTs including, Bihar. The State / UT & Year-wise details of Central funds released and funds utilized during the last three years, including Bihar, are given as Annexure II.(f)to(g) As per the records, between 2014-15 to 2020-21, a total of 15.88 lakh hectares additional area has been brought under protective irrigation in 27 States including Bihar and 2 Union Territories of Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh. The State/Year-wise details of Central share of funds released during the last three years are given as Annexure II.The Union Government has been getting cooperation from States / UTs in implementation of the WDC-PMKSY for the development of rainfed / degraded areas. The State/Year-wise details of achievements made under WDC-PMKSY, during the last three years are given as Annexure III.                                            *****
