                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1245






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.09.2020






Promotion of Tourism in Bihar




Janardan Singh Sigriwal














                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the special steps taken by the Government to promote tourism in Bihar and the outcome thereof; (b) 	whether the Government has constituted National Tourist Advisory Council (NTAC); (c) 	if so, the details thereof along with the structure of the said Council; (d) 	the terms and reference of the said Council; and (e) 	the steps taken by the Government for developing India as a global tourist attraction?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR TOURISM (INDEPENDENT CHARGE)(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a):	Ministry of Tourism promotes India as a holistic destination including its various tourism products. Activities are undertaken for promoting varied tourism products and destinations of India including Bihar in the Domestic and International markets under the Domestic Promotion and Publicity including Hospitality (DPPH) and Overseas Promotion and Publicity including Marketing Development Assistance (OPMD) schemes.Bodh Gaya in Bihar is a significant Buddhist Heritage site. The Ministry of Tourism, Government of India organizes an International Buddhist Conclave biennially. Participants in the Conclave include eminent Buddhist scholars, opinion makers, tour operators and media personalities from Overseas.The Ministry of Tourism has organized Road Shows in Yangon (Myanmar), Ho Chi Minh City (Vietnam), Phnom Penh (Cambodia) and Bangkok (Thailand) in May 2018 for promotion of India’s Buddhist Heritage and Buddhist sites including Bodh Gaya.In 2019, Buddhist heritage sites were promoted through various road shows organised in East Asia, South East Asia, Americas, Australasia and Europe. The Ministry of Tourism has produced a short film on Buddhist sites and has a dedicated website on important Buddhist sites in India i.e indiathelandofbuddha.in. The website showcases Bodh Gaya also. Under the Regional Connectivity Scheme Udan 3, 46 tourism routes have been incorporated by the Ministry of Civil Aviation. These include Gaya (Bodhgaya) for improved connectivity. Gaya is already also connected with important Buddhist source markets such as Thailand, Srilanka, Bhutan, etc.(b) to (d):	National Tourist Advisory Council (NTAC) was constituted vide order dated 27th October 2016 with the validity of three years. Secretary (Tourism) is the Vice Chairman of the Council. Details of the members of the Council and terms are attached at Annexure.(e):	Ministry of Tourism took the campaigns to the next level, the ‘Incredible India 2.0’ campaign was launched in September 2017, which marks a shift from generic promotions undertaken across the world to market specific promotional plans and content creation. The campaign focuses on digital and social media and the promotion of destinations including Bihar and tourism products.********
