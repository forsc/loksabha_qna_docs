                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4026






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Pollution from Chemical Waste




Anumula Revanth Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Ministry has taken steps in coordination with the Ministry of Jal Shakti and with the State Government of Telangana to ensure no waste especially chemical waste from pharmaceutical industries are dumped into the river Musi; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; (c) 	the steps being taken/proposed to protect Musi River water from contamination and pollution; and (d) 	the details of funds allocated by the Government for the same during the last three years, year-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) and (b)	There is no dumping of any chemical waste into river Musi.  Total wastewater generation from the industries is 9.65 MLD in the catchment area and out of which 5.65 MLD is being treated with captive ETPs and remaining through three Common Effluent Treatment Plants with a capacity of 4.56 MLD. (c) 	As per the information available, a total of 1960 MLD of sewage is being generated in the catchment of River Musi, 772.5 MLD of STPs are existing and proposals for 1259 MLD are under different stages. The solid waste management is done through 100% door to door collection and is being managed at integrated MSW processing facility including Scientific Land fill.(d)	The State Government has accorded Administrative Sanction for 17 STPs with a total treatment capacity of 376.5 MLD vide G.O.Rt.No.374, MA&UD Dept., Dt:11.09.2020 for Rs.1280.87 Crores with 40:60 ratio of Government and Private participation.*****
