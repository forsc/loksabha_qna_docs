                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2005






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2021






LNG Terminal




Kalyan Banerjee














                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has given its nod to set up Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG) terminal at Kukrahati along the river Hooghly for storage and re-gasification of LNG; (b) if so, the details of project and its commercial commission therein; and(c) the present status of the 348-kilometer pipeline from Dobhi in Bihar to Durgapur of West Bengal under Pradhan Mantri Urja Ganga project to supply natural gas with completion details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a) & (b) No Sir. Under provisions of Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (PNGRB) Act, establishment of Liquefied Natural Gas  (LNG) terminal does not require Government of India’s approval.(c) The 348-kilometer pipeline from Dobhi in Bihar to Durgapur in West Bengal is part of Jagdishpur-Haldia & Bokaro-Dhamra Pipeline (JHBDPL) project, popularly known has “Pradhan Mandri Urja Ganga Project” which has been commissioned and dedicated to the nation by Hon’ble Prime Minister on 07.02.2021. *****
