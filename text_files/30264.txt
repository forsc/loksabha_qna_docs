                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2007






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2021






Overhauling of Urban Planning Practice




Sajda Ahmed














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has reviewed the present urban 	planning practice in the future context of country and if so, the 	details thereof; (b) 	whether the Government has assessed the impact of Prime 	Minister’s Awas Yojana (PMAY)-Urban on basic infrastructures 	of the cities and if so, the details thereof;(c) 	the number of towns counted as urban in terms of population 	and are still administratively rural across the country, State-	wise; (d) 	whether the Government proposes for statutory masterplaning 	of those cities and if so, the details thereof; and (e) 	the steps taken by the Government to overhaul the urban 	planning practice in the country?






ANSWER




                                                                     THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                           (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a): Urban Planning is a state subject and Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA) has only an advisory role. It has issued Urban and Regional Development Plan Formulation and Implementation Guidelines, 2014 which have been circulated to all the States and UTs to act as a guide to prepare Master Plans duly taking into account all the emerging issues of urban planning and development.(b):  No such study has been undertaken by this Ministry. However, in pursuance of Government’s vision of ‘Housing for All’, Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs is implementing Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana-Urban for giving Central Assistance to States/Union Territories for providing all-weather pucca houses to all eligible urban households by year 2022.(c):  The number of towns counted as urban but administratively rural are 3,892. These areas are named ‘Census Towns’. The details are given in Annexure.(d):  Preparation of Master Plan comes within the purview of State Government.  In order to promote the efficient urban planning by the State  Governments  and  UT  Administrations,  a  sub-scheme    was launched by MoHUA for formulation of Geographical Information System (GIS) based Master Plans with an objective to develop common digital geo-referenced base maps and land use maps, and Master Plan Formulation for 500 cities that were selected under Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT). The Sub-Scheme was approved in October 2015 with 100% central funding having an outlay of ? 515 crore. Under the Sub Scheme, draft GIS Maps have been generated for 446 towns, out of which final GIS maps have been prepared for 277 towns. As on date, using the said GIS maps, 207 towns have formulated draft Master GIS Plans and out of that 97 towns have formulated final GIS based Master Plans.The state-wise details of Master Plans are given in Annexure.(e):  Realizing the significance of planned development, a sub –scheme was launched by Government of India on Formulation of Local Area Plan (LAP) & Town Planning Scheme (TPS) under Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT). 25 cities across various States/UTs have been identified for implementation under the sub-scheme. The sub-scheme provides financial assistance of ? 2.0 crore for each city for formulation of Local Area Plan and Town Planning Scheme.  NITI Aayog has also brought out a Report on Urban Planning Capacity Reforms which has recommended various steps to overhaul the urban planning practice (This report is available on the website of NITI Aayog https://www.niti.gov.in)                                *****
