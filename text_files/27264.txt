                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2861






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.08.2021






Projects under Bharatmala Pariyojana in Rajasthan




Jaskaur Meena














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the salient features of Bharatmala Pariyojana; (b)	the percentage increase in road construction under this pariyojana in the State of Rajasthan including Dausa district during the last three years and the current year;(c)	the funds sanctioned, allocated and utilised for the purpose during the said period; (d)	whether the Government has sanctioned some more projects in the State under the said pariyojana recently; (e)	if so, the details thereof, district-wise including Dausa district along with the estimated cost of the same; and (f)	the time frame set for completion of the said projects?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a)	Government of India had approved Bharatmala Pariyojana Phase-I in October, 2017 with an aggregate length of about 34,800 km (including 10,000 km residual National Highways Development Programme stretches) at an estimated outlay of Rs. 5,35,000/- crores. (b)	Projects of 2,537 km length have been planned in the State of Rajasthan under Bharatmala Pariyojana Phase-I. 45 projects having 2,236 km length with cost of Rs. 39,669 crore have been awarded till June, 2021. (c)	For the 2,537 km length of project, a total capital cost of about Rs. 45,804 crore has been sanctioned. (d) to (f) Delhi Mumbai Expressway, India’s longest expressway, having length of 1,352 km with total capital cost of about Rs. 90,000 crore, traverses through the State of Rajasthan and, particularly through the district of Dausa. The Delhi – Jaipur (Dausa) – Lalsot section of this Expressway has been targeted for completion in the Year 2021-22.  *****
