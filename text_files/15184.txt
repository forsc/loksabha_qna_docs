                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3809






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2020






Ban on Distribution of Kerosene under PDS




Nayab Singh














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is planning to ban the distribution of kerosene oil at fair price shops operating in the country; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Government is making changes in the existing system of ration distribution at fair price shops; and (d) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a) & (b):  		There is no proposal to ban the distribution of PDS Kerosene to States/UTs.(c) & (d): 		Under National Food Security Act, 2013 (NFSA), the eligible households are entitled to receive foodgrains, namely- Wheat, Rice & Coarse-grains through Public Distribution System (PDS) at highly subsidized price. Under the Act, the Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) households are eligible to receive 35 kg of foodgrains per family per month, whereas the Priority Households (PHH) beneficiaries are eligible to receive 5 kg of foodgrains per person per month.    		Further, clause 9(9) of Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS) (Control) Order, 2015, empowers the State/UT Governments to allow sale of commodities other than distribution of foodgrains through Fair Price Shops to improve the viability of the fair price shop operations. In addition to this, there is no proposal under consideration to change in the existing system of ration distribution at fair price shops in the Department.                                     ****
