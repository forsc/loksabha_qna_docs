                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    POWER






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

914






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.11.2019






Hydro Projects in North-Eastern Region




Mimi Chakraborty














                        Will the Minister of





POWER


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether North-Eastern region of the country has a potential of 60 thousand megawatt of hydro-electric power production and if so, the details thereof;(b) 	whether the said region has achieved only 2.4% of the generation target and if so, the details thereof;(c) 	the action plan of the Government to achieve full potentiality thereof; and(d) 	the status and new proposal of setting up of hydro and other power projects for North-Eastern region?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR POWER, NEW & RENEWABLE ENERGY AND THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR SKILL DEVELOPMENT & ENTREPRENEURSHIP( SHRI  R.K. SINGH) (a) & (b) : 	The assessed Hydro-electric power potential from major/medium schemes (i.e. schemes having capacity above 25 MW) in North-Eastern region is 62604 MW as per reassessment studies by CEA. Out of this, schemes totaling to 3596 MW (5.74%) are in operation. The state-wise details of potential and its status of development in North-Eastern region is enclosed at Annexure-I. The list of projects in operation in North-Eastern region is enclosed at Annexure-II.(c) : 			Government had taken several policy initiatives in the past to give a boost to hydropower development in the country including its North-Eastern region viz., National Electricity Policy 2005, Hydro Power Policy 2008, Tariff Policy, 2016, Right to Fair Compensation & Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013. Further, Govt. of India in March, 2019 has  approved  the  following  measures  for  promoting hydropower in  the country and improving viability of the sector:i.	Declaring Large Hydro Power (LHPs) (> 25 MW projects) as Renewable Energy source.ii.	Hydro Purchase Obligation (HPO) as a separate entity within Non-Solar Renewable Purchase Obligation (RPO).iii.	Tariff rationalization measures for bringing down hydro power tariff in the initial years.iv.	Budgetary Support for Flood Moderation/Storage Hydro Electric Projects (HEPs).v.	Budgetary Support towards Cost of Enabling Infrastructure, i.e. roads/bridges.		Rs. 1.5 crore per MW for projects upto 200 MW.		Rs. 1.0 crore per MW for projects above 200 MW.(d) : 			A total of 8 Number of Hydro Electric Projects in North Eastern Region aggregating to 3733 MW are under construction. The details of these projects are enclosed at Annexure-III.                                          **************
