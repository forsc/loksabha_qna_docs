                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4292






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






Widening of NH-3




Vivek Narayan Shejwalkar














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether traffic flow rate on Piprai-Nirawali section on National Highway (NH-3) is very high at 36000 P.C.U. necessitating its conversion into six-lane at the earliest; (b)	if so, whether the Government proposes to widen the said road into six-lane under the Bharatmala Pariyojana and if so, the details thereof;(c)	the time by which the said work is likely to be started and completed; (d)	whether the Government proposes to construct western bypass on NH-3 from Rairu to Tigra via Gole Pahadiya Girwai Naka in view of the long stretch of eastern bypass constructed earlier; and (e)	if so, the details thereof along with the time-frame set for the purpose






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) to (e)	The average traffic on Piprai-Nirawali section of National Highway (NH-3) is about 43,927 PCU.  The work for Detailed Project Report (DPR) for upgradation of Piprai-Nirawali stretch has been initiated.  Moreover, the procurement for preparation of DPR of bypass on Western side of Gwalior city has been started. Development of road stretches under various schemes is taken up post finalisation of alignment, cost estimates, land acquisition requirement etc. based on outcome of Detailed Project Report (DPR)/ Feasibility Study with due consideration to traffic density, up-gradation requirements, lane configuration (i.e. 2-lane/4-lane/6-lane etc.), Right of Way (ROW) determination, project viability, inter-se-priority and availability of funds.*****
