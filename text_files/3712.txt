                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3384






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.07.2019






YouTube News Channels




Naba (Hira) Kumar Sarania














                        Will the Minister of





INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of registered YouTube news channels in the country as on date;(b)	whether the Government has prepared any guidelines in this regard, if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether the journalists of the said YouTube news channel have been given recognition, if so, the details thereof including their names;(d)	the number of journalists who have been given recognition by the Press Information Bureau in the country so far, and (e)	the facilities given to the said journalists?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF  ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE; AND MINISTER OF INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING (SHRI PRAKASH JAVADEKAR)(a) & (b) :  According to Ministry of Electronics & Information Technology, at present, YouTube news channels are not required to register with  the Government.  However, YouTube is an intermediary as defined in the Information Technology Act, 2000 and as per Section 79 of this Act, certain due diligence is to be followed by intermediaries.  Further, intermediaries are required to inform the users of computer resource not to host, display, upload, modify, publish, transmit, update or share any information that is grossly harmful, defamatory, obscene, pornographic, paedophilic, harms minor in any way, violates any law for the time being in force, etc. (c) : Press Information Bureau (PIB) of this Ministry grants accreditation to News Media representatives who reside at the Headquarters of the Government of India.  PIB does not give accreditation to the journalists of the YouTube News Channels.(d) : PIB has given accreditation to 2452 media persons in the year 2019 so far.  (e) : Ministry of Information and Broadcasting has constituted Journalist Welfare Scheme (JWS), which provides one time ex-gratia relief on urgent basis to Journalists for treatment of major ailments.  The relief is also provided to dependent family member in event of death of the Journalist.  Additionally, the benefit of Railway concession and CGHS facilities are given to PIB accredited Journalists administered by Ministry of Railways and Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, respectively.**********
