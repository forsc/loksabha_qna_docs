                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

866






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Disbursement of Loans to MSMEs




Vinod Kumar Sonkar













                                                    Raja Amareshwara Naik
                                                








                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether loans of about Rs. 1,20,000 crore have been disbursed to MSMEs out of             Rs. 3 lakh crore announced in the relief package and if so, the details thereof;(b)	whether the Ministry proposes to create Land Bank and Social Micro finance Institution for Entrepreneurs and persons who want to run small shops and businesses and if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether the Ministry proposes to set up 15 new Technology Centres (TCs) and upgrade 18 existing TCs to create skilled manforces and if so, the details thereof;(d)	the number of MSMEs provided loan under the Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyan especially in 115 aspirational districts; and (e)	the other steps being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NITIN GADKARI)(a): 	The Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) is being implemented through the National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company Ltd. (NCGTC) which is under the administrative control of Department of Financial Services (DFS), Ministry of Finance. DFS has reported that as on 09.09.2020, an amount of Rs. 163,103 crore has been sanctioned to 42,01,060  borrowers and an amount of   Rs. 117,885 crore has been disbursed to  25,01,216 borrowers under the scheme.  (b):  	No Proposal for creation of Land Bank and Social Micro finance Institution for Entrepreneurs is being considered in this Ministry.(c): 	Yes, Sir. The  Programme was approved by the Government at a cost  of Rs. 2200 Crore with World Bank assistance to set up 15 new technology centres and  upgradation of the existing Technology Centers. (d): 	The Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) was announced under the Aatma Nirbhar Bharat package. The details of disbursement are given in response to part (a) above. The administrative Ministry of  ECLGS - Ministry of Finance, Department of Financial Services (DFS) has intimated that district –wise data in respect of ECLGS in not maintained. (e): 	Post Covid-19, Government has taken a number of initiatives under Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Package to support the MSME Sector in the country. Some of the prominent initiatives are  (i) Rs.20,000 crore Subordinate Debt for MSMEs; (ii) Rs.3 lakh crore Collateral free Automatic Loans for business, including MSMEs; (iii) Rs.50,000 crore equity infusion through MSME Fund of Funds; (iv) New revised criteria for classification of MSMEs; (v) New Registration of MSMEs through ‘Udyam Registration’ for Ease of Doing Business; (vi)  No global tenders for procurement up to Rs.200 crores; (vii) Scheme for easy access to credit for street vendors.
