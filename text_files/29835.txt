                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1491






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.12.2021






Mismatch Between Growth of FPIs




Gowdar Mallikarjunappa Siddeshwara














                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has done any realistic assessment of a mismatch between the growth of food processing industries and stagnant farm income, despite tremendous potential in food processing industries; (b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	the major steps that the Government has taken to create a cold chain infrastructure in different parts of the country; and (d)	the number of mega food parks that have so far been established in the country under Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojna (PMKSY) along with details on their distribution?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) & (b):  Food processing industries source most of the raw materials from farmers. Therefore, growth of the sector will have positive impact on farmer’s income. However, farmer’s income is not dependent only on this source. Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation through national level surveys bring out data on income of agricultural households including net receipt from farming of crops and animals. The average monthly income of agricultural households has grown positively along with growth in Gross Value Added (GVA) of food processing sector in recent years as indicated in the table below:	2012-13	2018-19	Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) (%)Average monthly income per agricultural household (in Rs.) #	6,426	10,218	8.04Gross Value Added of food processing sector * (Rs. in lakh crore)	1.30	2.32	10.13# NSS 70th round, Key Indicators of Situation of Agricultural Household in India, for agricultural year 2012-13 data and NSS 77th round, Situation Assessment of Agricultural Households and Land and Livestock Holdings of Household in Rural India, for agricultural year 2018-19 data.* National Accounts Statistics, Central Statistics Office(c):	The Government is implementing various schemes to create cold chain infrastructure in different parts of the country as under:i. 	Ministry of Food Processing Industries is implementing a Scheme for Integrated Cold Chain, Value Addition and Preservation Infrastructure as one of the components of Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana (PMKSY) with the objective of reducing post-harvest losses of horticulture and non-horticulture produce and providing remunerative price to farmers for their produce.ii. 	Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare is implementing the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH) for various horticulture activities including setting up of cold storages through State Governments.iii. 	National Horticulture Board (NHB) is implementing a scheme namely “Capital Investment Subsidy for Construction/ Expansion / Modernization of Cold Storages and Storages for Horticulture Products”.(d): 	41 Mega Food Park projects have been approved and 22 Mega Food Parks have been     made operational in the country. A list of these 41 Mega Food Parks including their location is attached at Annexure.******
