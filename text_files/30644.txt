                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2099






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2021






Eye Hospitals




Sunil Dattatray Tatkare













                                                    Amol Ramsing Kolhe
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                








                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a): the number of eye hospitals functioning in the country, State/UT-wise;b) Whether these hospitals have shortage of senior doctors/specialists resulting in hardship to patients and if so, the details thereof and the correctives steps taken in this regardc): whether theCentral Governmenthospitals and its associated hospitals including All India Institute of Medical Sciences lack adequate basic facility including advanced eye treatment and if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor;d) whether the Government has received any proposals from the State Governments of Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu for setting up of eye hospitals and if so, the details thereof; ande)  the steps taken /being taken by the Government to improve the facility in eye hospitals including timely retrieval and transplantation of eyes and also to promote eye donation in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR.BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a)	to (c): Health is a State subject.  However, Central Government supplements the efforts of State Governments by providing technical and financial support.  The Government of India has Centrally Sponsored scheme namely “National Programme for Control of Blindness and Visual Impairment”(NPCBVI)under which the tertiary eye care services are provided at Eye Care Hospitals, Regional Institute of Ophthalmology(RIO) and other hospitals of central government. Apart fromthem, the eye care hospitals are set up by State Governments, Local Bodies, NGOs and in the private sector also.  The complete data in this regard is not available.  The information on the number of hospitals as made available by the State Governments is given atAnnexure-I.The details of the data available on ‘Vision Atlas’ prepared in 2020 by the Dr. Rajendra Prasad Centre for Ophthalmic Sciences, AIIMS, New Delhi is given at Annexure II.Some of the States have reported shortage of Doctors/Specialists at eye hospitals.As far as the central government hospitals and its associated hospitals including All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) are concerned, they do have adequate basic facility and also the facilities for advanced eye treatment.  Under the NPCBVI, Ministry of Health and Family Welfare provides funds for identified equipments and modular eye OTs at RIOs and Central Government Eye Hospitals.  The programme also provides funds for strengthening of eye wards at district hospitals in States/UTs.(d)	No, Sir.(e)	The Government has taken following steps to improve the facility in the eye hospitals, timely retrieval and transplantation of eyes and also to promote eye donation in the country: 1)	Central Government provides financial assistance to Regional Institutes of Ophthalmology for strengthening and up-gradation of (RIOs) to become Centres of Excellence (COE) in various sub-specialties of ophthalmology and also other partners like Government Medical College, District Hospitals, Sub-district Hospitals, Vision Centres;2)	Financial Assistance for construction of dedicated Eye Wards and Eye OTs in District Hospitals of the States.3)	Assistance to Eye Banks and Eye Donation Centres for improvement in cornea collection and eye banking services;4)	For an efficient eye banking system, a three tier organization structure is followed. At the top of the pyramid is the Eye Bank Training Center followed by Eye Banks and at the base of the pyramid is the eye retrieval/Donation center.5)	Pattern of assistance provided by Central Government under NPCBVI is attached at Annexure-III.*****
