                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

855






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Piped Potable Water to Rural Households




Shrirang Appa Barne













                                                    Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik
                                                








                                                    Bidyut Baran Mahato
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to provide piped potable water to every rural household by 2024 and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether any problems are being faced in the implementation of the Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) and if so, the details thereof;(c) the details of the budgetary allocation for the Mission, State-wise;(d) the number of households in Maharashtra that have received piped potable water under the Mission; and (e) the steps taken/being taken by the Government to complete the work in time bound manner?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a)To enable every rural household in the country to have potable water at service level of 55 litre per capita per day (lpcd) through Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC) by 2024, Government of India, in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) with an estimated outlay of Rs. 3.60 lakh Crore.(b) COVID-19 pandemic has affected the pace of implementation of the Jal Jeevan Mission in States/ UTs.(c) The details of State/ UT-wise central allocation under JJM for 2020-21 is annexed.(d) As reported by State of Maharashtra, out of 142.36 lakh rural households across the State, as on 14.09.2020, 64.21 lakh rural households are provided with tap water connection.(e) To complete the work in a time-bound manner followiong action has been taken:i.)	Every State/ UT has been advised to prepare ‘State Action Plan’, projecting year-wise plan for providing tap connections. ii.)	Govt. of India holds regular review meetings with all States/ UTs for expediting the implementation of the Mission.iii.)	Central financial assistance, to all eligible States/ UTs, is being provided as per the operational guidelines for the implementation of Jal Jeevan Mission.iv.)	In consonance with 73rd Amendment of Constitution of India and to instill the sense of ownership among villagers, Gram Panchayat or its sub-committee/ user group i.e. Village Water and Sanitation Committee (VWSC), Paani Samiti, etc. are to plan, implement, manage, operate and maintain in-village water supply system. Thus, Jal Jeevan Mission is a demand-driven, decentralized, community-managed programme.****
