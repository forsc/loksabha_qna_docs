Capital Investment




Chandeshwar Prasad














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF FINANCEDEPARTMENT OF ECONOMIC AFFAIRSLOK SABHA UNSTARRED QUESTION NO.1186TO BE ANSWERED ON 25th NOVEMBER (MONDAY), 2019AGRAHAYANA 4, 1941(SAKA) CAPITAL INVESTMENT†1186. SHRI CHANDESHWAR PRASAD: Will the MINISTER OF FINANCE be pleased to state:(a)	whether the Government is taking several encouraging steps to promote capital investment abroad; (b)	if so, the details of the steps taken in this regard during the last three years; (c)	 the details of announcements made in this regard so far; and (d) 	the details of capital investment abroad during the said period, nation-wise?






ANSWER




ANSWERMinister of State (Finance) (ShriAnurag Singh Thakur)(a)	To (d):  Yes Sir.   The steps taken by the Government to promote capital investment abroad  during last three years, inter-alia, include the following:    (i)	 Certification requirement by Statutory Auditors of Indian Party  under   Regulation  15 sub-regulation (v) of  Foreign Exchange Management (Transfer or Issue of any Foreign Security) Regulations 2004  was amended by the Reserve Bank of India vide Notification No.FEMA.369/2017-RB dated 14th November, 2017 to facilitate the Statutory Auditors of the Indian Party certify that law of the host country does not mandatorily require auditing of the books of accounts of JV/WOS and the figures in the Annual Performance Report (APR) are as per the unaudited accounts of the overseas JV/WOS.(ii)	In order to align the FEMA guidelines with the objectives of FATF,  a clause was inserted  under Regulation 6 sub-regulation (2) of Foreign Exchange Management (Transfer or Issue of any Foreign Security) Regulations 2004 by the Reserve Bank of India vide Notification No.FEMA.382/2016-RB dated  2nd January, 2017, prohibiting an Indian party from making direct investment in an overseas entity  located in the countries identified by the FATF as ''non co-operative countries and territories''.
