                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3437






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






Maithili Language




 Gopal Jee Thakur














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether Maithili language is included under the Eighth Schedule;(b) whether this is the colloquial language for crores of people;(c) whether this language has not been included at primary level in CBSE/CTETcourse;(d) whether the Government proposes to include this language at primary levelof education; and(e) if so, the time by which it is likely to be included along with the detailsthereof?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) & (b): Yes Sir.(c): Schools are free to offer any regional language of India at primary level asper the needs of students. Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) doesnot give any directions in this respect.The Government has appointed CBSE as the Examination ConductingBody for Central Teacher Eligibility Test. Further, the Government has askedCBSE that CTET should be conducted in 20 languages.-2-(d) & (e): As per the recommendations of National Curriculum Framework-2005and other educational policy documents, mother tongue should be taught as thefirst language and subsequently two more languages should be taught at schoollevel. Education being a subject matter of both State and Centre, States are freeto make the policy of education independently.***
