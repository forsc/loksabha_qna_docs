                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2669






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.03.2020






Universalisation of ICDS




P.R. Natarajan














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	whether the Government has any proposal in place to universalise the Integrated Child 	Development Services (ICDS) scheme; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; and (c) 	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a) to (c) The Scheme of ICDS was launched in 1975 with 33 Projects and 4,891 Anganwadi Centres (AWCs) and was gradually expanded to 5,652 Projects and 6 lakh sanctioned AWCs in the country, by the end of IX Plan. The coverage of ICDS, till IX Plan, was thus not universal as it covered only about 42% of the 14 lakh habitations. To comply with the directions of the Hon’ble Supreme Court and to fulfill the commitment of the Government to universalize the Anganwadi Services Scheme, it has been expanded to 7075 Projects and 14 lakh AWCs which have been already sanctioned to States/UTs thereby covering the entire country.	The Anganwadi Services today operates through a network of 7075 fully operational Projects and 13.79 lakh AWCs as on 30.09.2019. The services are currently being provided to 7.96 crore beneficiaries of which 6.40 crore are children under six years of age and 1.56 crore are pregnant women and lactating mothers. Pre-school education is provided to 2.85 crore children of 3-6 years of age as on 30.09.2019.
