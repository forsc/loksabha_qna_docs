                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1177






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2021






Employment Growth




Rahul Kaswan













                                                    Manne Srinivas Reddy
                                                








                                                    Ramya  Haridas
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)whether the Government has the employment Growth data available in the country during the last three years and if so, the State-wise details thereof; (b)whether it is a fact that the country’s unemployment rate has risen to more than 8.5 per cent due to economic slowdown and contraction of industrial output; (c)the number of employable men and women in urban and rural areas presently categorised as unemployed; and (d)the labour participation rate of male and female workers in the economy and the steps taken by the Government to revive job creation and employment for youth and the further action plan to solve the unemployment issue in a perspective manner?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a) to (c):	As per the Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) conducted by National Statistical Office (NSO), the estimates of Worker Population Ratio (WPR) for persons aged 15 years and above defined as the number of employed persons to total population on usual status basis during the last three years has shown an increasing trend i.e it stood at 46.8% (2017-18), 47.3% (2018-19) and 50.9% (2019-20) respectively. The State-wise WPR is given at Annexure. Contd..2/-:: 2 ::		As per the Annual Periodic Labour Force Survey(PLFS) conducted by National Statistical Office (NSO), the estimates of Unemployment rate (UR) for persons aged 15 years  and above on usual status basis during the last three years are on declining trend i.e 6.0% (2017-18), 5.8%( 2018-19) and 4.8% (2019-20) respectively. As regards revival of factory output data, reflected through Index of Industrial Production (IIP) , there has been a significant growth of 23.5% during April-September 2021-22 as compared to the corresponding period growth of -20.8% during 2020-21.(d): 		As per the Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) conducted by National Statistical Office (NSO), the estimates of    Labour Force Participation  Rate (LFPR) for male and female persons for aged 15 years and above on usual status basis during 2019-20 is 76.8% & 30.0 % respectively . Government is providing fiscal stimulus of more than Rupees Twenty seven lakh crore as part of the Aatmanirbhar Bharat financial Package which comprises various long term schemes/ programmes/ policies for making the country self-reliant and to create employment opportunities in the country.*                                                 *******
