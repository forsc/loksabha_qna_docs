                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1729






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.03.2020






National Child Labour Project




Rajyavardhan Singh Rathore














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)the number of districts where the National Child Labour Project (NCLP) is notified and operational, State-wise; (b)the reasons for the low number of districts where the Project is being implemented; (c)the estimated number of child labourers in the country and the number of rescued child labourers, State-wise; (d)the number of NCLP Training Centres in the country and the number of child labourers rescued and trained in these Centres, State-wise; and (e)the obstacles faced over rescue of child labourers?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (IC) FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI SANTOSH KUMAR GANGWAR)(a):	 	The number of districts where the National Child Labour Project (NCLP) is notified and operational, State-wise  is  at Annexure-I. (b) to (e):	National Child Labour Project (NCLP) Scheme is implemented by the District Project Societies (DPS) under the chairmanship of the administrative head of the  district namely, District Magistrate/Deputy Commissioner/District Collector of the district under administrative control of the State Government. Under the NCLP Scheme, the DPS at District level are required to conduct the baseline survey to identify child labour. Based on the number of child labour identified and rescued from the survey, Special Training Centres   are opened for rehabilitation of the child labour in the age group of 9-14 years.              As per census 2011, there were 43.53 lakh main workers in India in the age group of 5-14 years.             At present the number of Special Training Centers under NCLP Scheme is 2500 (approximately).  The number of child labourers rescued and trained in STCs State-wise during the year 2018-19 is at Annexure-II.**                                                           ******
