                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

389






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






LWE Affected Districts




Meenakashi Lekhi














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected districts in the country during the last three years, year-wise;(b) the measures being taken by the Government to reduce the incidence of LWE in the country;(c) the development works being carried out in the districts that have recently been relieved of the status of LWE affected districts;(d) whether the Government has formulated special assistance programme, schemes or integrated action plan for the districts which were affected by Left Wing Extremism; and(e) if so, the details thereof along with the financial and other assistance provided to the said districts, State-wise including Srikakulam district of Andhra Pradesh?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY) (a)  to (e):	    A Statement is laid on the Table of the House. --2--STATEMENT  REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION No. *389 FOR 23.03.2021(i)	As per Seventh Schedule of the Constitution of India, subjects of Police and Public Order are with the State Governments. However, the Government of India (GoI) has been supplementing the efforts of States affected by left wing extremism. A National Policy and Action Plan was promulgated in 2015 to address Left Wing Extremism (LWE). It envisages a multi-pronged strategy involving security related measures, development interventions, ensuring rights and entitlements of local communities etc. While on security front, the Central Government assists the LWE affected State Government by providing Central Armed Police Forces battalions, training, funds for modernization of State police forces, equipment & arms, sharing of intelligence, construction of Fortified Police Stations etc; on development side, the Central Government has taken various measures including construction of roads, installation of mobile towers, improving network of banks, post offices, skill development and education facilities in the LWE areas.(ii)	The GoI is providing funds for capacity building of the LWE affected States, under various scheme like Security Related Expenditure (SRE) Scheme,  Special  Infrastructure  Scheme  (SIS),  to  fight  the   LWE   menace --3--L.S. S.Q. NO *389 FOR 23.03.2021effectively. Under these schemes funds are provided state-wise on reimbursement claims submitted by the States on expenditure incurred for operational purposes and capacity building in LWE affected districts which also include Srikakulam in Andhra Pradesh, a district covered under the SRE Scheme. The funds provided to States in last 03 years and current financial year are given in Annexure-I. (iii)	To give further impetus to development in the most LWE affected districts, funds are provided to the States under ‘Special Central Assistance (SCA)’ to fill the critical gaps in public infrastructure and services. Presently 30 districts across 7 States are covered under the scheme. The scheme was approved in 2017 and Rs.2541.24 crore has been released to the States till date. The list of the 30 districts presently covered under the scheme is at Annexure-II.(iv)	On development front, apart from flagship schemes of Government of India (GoI) has taken several specific initiatives in LWE affected States, to improve road connectivity, Telecommunication network and financial inclusion as below:i.	Construction of 5422 km roads is envisaged under Road Requirement Plan-I. Of these 4970 km roads are constructed. Of these 2046 km road has been constructed after April-2014.--4--L.S. S.Q. NO. *389 FOR 23.03.2021ii.	The Road Connectivity Project for LWE Affected Areas (RCPLWEA) was approved in December-2016. Under the scheme 9268 km roads have been sanctioned. Of these 3060 km are completed.iii.	To improve telecommunication connectivity, 2335 mobile towers are installed in phase-I of the Mobile Tower Project. Further GoI has approved installation of 4072 mobile towers in phase-II of the Mobile Tower Project in May-2018.iv.	For financial inclusion of the local populace in these areas, 1170 Bank Branches, 959 ATMs and 12628 Banking Correspondents in 30 Most LWE Affected Districts in last 05 years.(v)	The steadfast implementation of the policy has resulted in consistent decline in violence. The incidents of LWE violence have reduced by 70% from all time high of 2258 in 2009 to 665 in 2020. Similarly resultant deaths (Civilians + Security Forces) have reduced by 82% from all time high of 1005 in 2010 to 183 in 2020. The geographical spread of LWE violence has also constricted. The districts reporting violence are reduced from 76 (2013) to 53 (2020). The year wise number of districts reporting LWE related violence for the last 03 years are as under:- 2018	2019	202060	61	53*****
