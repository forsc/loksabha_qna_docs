                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2329






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2021






Density of Colleges




Rajiv Pratap Rudy














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the density of colleges per lakh of the young population, State/Union Territory (UT)-wise;(b) the details of the parameters considered to establish a college in a State/UT;(c) whelther Bihar has sufficient colleges in comparision with its young population;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) if not, the steps taken or proposed to be taken by the Government to increase the college density inBihar?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EDUCATION(DR. SUBHAS SARKAR)(a) As per the All India Survey on Higher Education (AISHE) 2019-20, the density of colleges per lakh ofthe young population (age group 18 to 23 years) in different States/UTs is given in Annexure-1.(b) Education being in the Concurrent List, establishment of new institutions is the responsibility of bothCentral and State Government. However, recognising the need for central assistance, the CentralGovernment has implemented various schemes supporting State Governments. The Department ofHigher Education is implementing a centrally sponsored scheme of Rashtriya Uchchatar ShikshaAbhiyan (RUSA) with an aim of promoting access and equity, inter alia, the scheme provides centralassistance to States for creation of Model Degree colleges, infrastructure grants to colleges anduniversities, creation of universities either through upgradation of existing autonomous colleges orclustering of colleges.(c) to (e): As per the All India Survey on Higher Education (AISHE) 2019-20, the total number of colleges,the number of colleges per lakh of the young population (age group 18 to 23 years), average enrolmentper college and the Gross Enrolment Ration (GER) in higher education for all States and UTs includingBihar is given in Annexure-1. The details of support provided to the State of Bihar under the CentrallySponsored Scheme of Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) of the Ministry of Education aregiven in Annexure-2.*****
