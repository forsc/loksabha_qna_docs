                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1638






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Employment Schemes




Gopal Chinayya Shetty














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether several villages are lagging behind in terms of development after independence;(b)	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor;(c)	the names of employment schemes and the names of villages where these schemes are being implemented;(d)	the shortcomings found in the existing employment schemes; and(e)	the names of schemes being formulated for the ensuing years for the rural development after removing such shortcomings?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) & (b):	Based on the pre-defined development parameters village score card has been prepared for different States/Union Territories (UTs), which is at Annexure I, on which appropriate action is taken by the concerned State/UT Governments.(c):	Schemes of Ministry of Rural Development, including employment generation schemes like Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS), Deen Dayal Antyodaya Yojana- National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM), are implemented throughout the country.(d) & (e):	Third-party evaluation of Ministry of Rural Development’s Schemes are carried out regularly and actions, as appropriate, are taken on the findings, including restructuring  of Schemes.*****
