                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4314






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






Satna Smart City




Ganesh Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  	the salient features of Smart Cities Mission (SCM); (b) 	whether Satna City of Madhya Pradesh has been declared as a Smart City and if so, the details of each of the projects proposed under Smart Cities Mission (SCM) for Satna city; (c) 	whether work orders for Satna City under Smart Cities Mission (SCM) have been issued and if so, the details thereof including the status of each of the works along with the time by which it is likely to be completed and if not, the reasons there for; and (d) 	whether it is a fact that not even a single work has been completed in Satna City and if so, the reasons there for along with the efforts being made to complete proposed projects in District Satna?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                            (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)	Government of India launched the Smart Cities Mission (SCM) on 25th June, 2015 for development of  100 cities as Smart Cities. The selection of 100 Smart Cities has been completed through 4 rounds of selection from January, 2016 to June, 2018. A total of 5,151 projects worth of Rs. 2,05,018 crore have been proposed by the 100 Smart Cities as part of their Smart City Proposals (SCPs). As per Smart Cities Mission Statement and Guidelines, the Central Government proposes to give financial support to the extent of Rs. 48,000 crore over five years i.e. an average of Rs. 500 crore per city over the Mission period. An equal amount on a matching basis, is to be provided by the State/Urban Local Bodies. Apart from these, around Rs. 42,028 crore (21%) is expected from convergence with other Missions, Rs. 41,022 crore (21%) from PPP, around Rs. 9,843 crore (4.8%) from loans, Rs. 2,644 crore (1.3%) from own resources and remaining from other sources.  The Smart Cities are expected to complete their projects within 5 years from the date of their selection.(b)	 to (d) The Satna city of Madhya Pradesh was selected to be developed as Smart City in June 2017 in the Round-3 of the Smart Cities Challenge.
