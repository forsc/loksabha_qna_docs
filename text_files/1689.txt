                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1419






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.07.2019






Mudra Yojana




Rajiv Pratap Rudy













                                                    Naba (Hira) Kumar Sarania
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of applications received, sanctioned and the quantum of funds released under MUDRA yojana since its inception under Shishu, Tarun and Kishore categories, category-wise, year-wise, State-wise and district-wise including Bihar;(b) the details of the number of bank branches that have provided loans under MUDRA yojana, bank-wise, State-wise and district-wise;(c) whether it has come to the notice of the Government that some banks are not coming forward to offer loans to the aspiring entrepreneurs, if so, the details thereof; and(d)  the corrective measures taken by the Government to make MUDRA yojana more effective and to further simplify the loan process under the yojana?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF FINANCE(a) & (b)   The Category-wise, Year-wise, State-wise and District-wise number of Pradhan Mantri Mudra (PMMY) loans sanctioned including in the state of Bihar since 2016-17are at Annexure-I. District wise data for 2015-16 was not captured in the MUDRA portal. Public Sector Bank wise and State wise details of PMMY loans extended since FY 2016-17are at Annexure-II and Annexure III respectively. In so far as number of branch wise Mudra applications is concerned data is not centrally maintained.(c) The complaints received from time to time in respect of implementation of PMMY including turning down of loan applications are redressed in coordination with the respective banks. To bring in customer centricity regular instructions by Reserve Bank of India and the Government have been issued to redress customer grievances which, inter-alia, includes denial of loans. Such public grievances are to be acknowledged within three days of receipt and are to be disposed off within a maximum time limit of 60 days under intimation to the petitioner.(d)  The Government has taken various steps towards effective implementation of the PMMY scheme. These, inter alia, include simplification of application forms, facility for on-line application for Mudra loans, extension of Mudra to activities allied to agriculture, refinance from MUDRA Ltd, Credit Guarantee Scheme, nomination of Mudra Nodal Officer, weekly video conferences to monitor the progress and publicity campaigns etc.*****
