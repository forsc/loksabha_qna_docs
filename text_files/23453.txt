                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4417






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






Pucca Houses in Uttar Pradesh & Maharashtra




Rekha Arun Verma













                                                    Vinayak Bhaurao Raut
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of new rural pucca house constructed in Uttar Pradesh and Maharashtra during the last three years;(b) 	whether the Government has any State funding pattern for this purpose; and(c) 	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a)	To achieve the objective of “Housing for All”, in rural areas of the country, the erstwhile rural housing scheme Indira Awaas Yojana (IAY) has been restructured into Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana- Gramin (PMAY-G) w.e.f. 1st April, 2016. The scheme aims to provide pucca house with basic amenities to all houseless households living in kutcha and dilapidated house in rural areas. The details of rural pucca houses constructed in Uttar Pradesh and Maharashtra during the last three years (financial years 2017-18 to 2019-20) are as under:State	                 Houses constructed (units in nos).*Uttar Pradesh	             1422665Maharashtra	              513250* Includes both IAY and PMAY-G houses Source: AwaasSoft as on 17.03.2021.(b) & (c) Yes Sir. The fund sharing pattern under PMAY-G between Central and State Government is in the ratio of 60:40 except in case of the UT of Jammu & Kashmir, North-Eastern States and Hilly States, wherein the sharing pattern is 90:10. In respect of UTs (including Ladakh), the PMAY-G is fully funded by the Central Government. *****
