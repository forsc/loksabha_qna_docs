                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

331






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2022






Benefits of UDAN Scheme




Malook Nagar














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of people benefitted under the Ude Desh Ka Aam Naagrik (UDAN) scheme; and(b) the details of the steps being taken by the Government to ensure that the benefits of the said scheme reach to each citizen?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) & (b):Under Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS)- UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik),  approximately 86.05 lakhs passengers have travelled on UDAN flights since its commencement on 21.10.2016 till 09.01.2022. RCS - UDAN aims to stimulate regional air connectivity for making air travel affordable to the masses. UDAN is a market driven scheme. Interested airlines based on their assessment of demand on particular routes submit their proposals at the time of bidding under UDAN. The salient features of the UDAN scheme are as under:i) The scheme offers a unique opportunity to a common person to fly at an affordable price, for which the Government has capped the Airfare for the seats where incentives are extended to airlines under the RCS scheme launched in the country.;ii) UDAN scheme has sparked significant increase in helicopter services in hilly areas and islands through use of heliports.;iii) Till date, out of 948 valid routes, 403 RCS routes (connecting 65 airports including 8 heliports & 2 water aerodromes) have commenced under the scheme.******
