                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3436






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






Literacy




Ajay Nishad














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has fixed the target of achieving hundred percent literacy byeradicating illiteracy from the country by the year 2022;(b) if so, the details of the steps taken/ proposed to be taken for this purpose during the lastfour years;(c) whether the Government has provided financial assistance for this purpose during the lastthree years;(d) if so, the details thereof along with the details of the present illiteracy rate, State-wise;(e) the role of State Governments and Non-Governmental Organisations (NGOs) to achievethe target of hundred per cent literacy;(f) whether the Government proposes to seek assistance of experts to eradicate illiteracy; and(g) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a) & (b) The scheme of Saakshar Bharat was implemented in phased manner since October2009 in the rural areas of 410 districts in 26 states and 1 UT that had adult female literacyrate of 50 percent and below as per Census 2001, and including left wing extremism affecteddistricts, irrespective of their literacy rate. The target was to raise the overall literacy rate ofthe country to 80 per cent and reduce gender gap to 10 percentage point by end of XII fiveyear plan. A statement indicating State and UT wise and gender-wise learners appeared anddeclared successful in the Basic Literacy Assessment Test conducted by National Institute ofOpen Schooling between August, 2010 to March, 2018, under Saakshar Bharat Programme isat Annexure-I. Overall 7.64 crores learners have been certified as literates.(c) & (d) A statement indicating the State/UT-wise central financial assistance providedunder the scheme of Saakshar Bharat during the last three years
