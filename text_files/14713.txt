                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3334






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.03.2020






Aadhaar Based Payments under PMMVY




K. Muraleedharan














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	the salient features of the Rajiv Gandhi National Creche Scheme (RGNCS) and National Creche Scheme;(b)	the total number of creches/day care facilities set up and functional under the said schemes, State/UT-wise;(c)	the total funds allocated under the scheme during the current and previous years, State/UT-wise;(d)	whether a large number of creches set up under the National Creche Scheme are non-operational/defunct due to lack of funding and if so, the details thereof along with the current status of the scheme, State/UT-wise including Kerala;(e)	whether the Government of Kerala has taken over creches run under the erstwhile RGNCS, if so, the details thereof; and(f)	whether there is a mechanism in place to monitor and evaluate the working of NGOs and other organization engaged in providing facilities under the said schemes and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a)	National Creche Scheme (earlier named as Rajiv Gandhi National Creche Scheme) is being implemented as a Centrally Sponsored Scheme through States/UTs with effect from 01.01.2017 to provide day care facilities to children (age group of 6 months to 6 years) of working mothers. The salient features of the National Creche Scheme are as follows: i.	Daycare Facilities including Sleeping Facilities.ii.	Early Stimulation for children below 3 years and pre-school Education for 3 to 6 years old children.iii.	Supplementary Nutrition ( to be locally sourced)iv.	Growth Monitoringv.	Health Check-up and ImmunizationFurther, the guidelines provide that :i.	Crèches shall be open for 26 days in a month and for seven and half (7-1/2) hours per day.ii.	The number of children in the crèche should not be more than 25 per crèche with 01 Worker and 01 helper respectively. iii.	User charges to bring in an element of community ownership and collected as under:  (a)	BPL families - Rs 20/- per child per month.(b)	Families with Income (Both Parents) of upto Rs. 12,000/- per month – Rs. 100/- per child per month  (c)	Families with Income (Both Parents) of above Rs. 12,000/- per month – Rs. 200/- per child per month.(b)	As on 11.03.2020, 6453 creches are functional across the country under the                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             National Creche Scheme. The total number of creches/day care facilities set up and functional under the said scheme, State/UT-wise is at Annexure I.(c)	The total funds allocated and released under the scheme during the current and previous years, State/UT-wise are at Annexure II. (d)	No such information is available with the Ministry. Grants have been released to the State Governments/UT Administration for crèches found functional and taken over by them. (e)	As on date, the Government of Kerala has taken over 479 creches under the erstwhile RGNCS and running under National Creche Scheme.(f)	Regular and strict Monitoring is conducted at different levels to ensure effective running of the Scheme and also to ensure that the beneficiaries are delivered services as envisaged in the Scheme. Monitoring of implementation of the scheme is being undertaken through meetings and video conferences with the officials of State Governments/ UT. Ministry officials undertake field visits to ascertain the status of implementation of the scheme.
