                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1900






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.09.2020






Development of MSME Clusters




Annasaheb Shankar Jolle














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether there is a scheme for developing industrial corridor or cluster only for the Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) and if so, the details thereof; and(b)	the policies and schemes available for the entrepreneurs so that they can develop clusters in PPP model? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OFMICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI  PRATAP CHANDRA SARANGI)(a):	Yes, Sir. Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME)  implements the Micro and Small Enterprises Cluster Development Programme (MSE-CDP) for enhancing the productivity and competitiveness of Micro and Small Enterprises by extending financial assistance for establishment of Common Facility Centres (CFCs); establishment / upgradation of Industrial Estates (Infrastructure Development (ID) projects)/ development of Flatted Factory Complexes; Marketing Hubs / Exhibition Centres by Associations; Thematic Interventions and Support to State Innovative Cluster Development Programme. (b):	Under MSE-CDP scheme, the component of setting up of Common Facility Centre (CFC) involves non-recurring grant-in-aid from Government of India, contribution from concerned State Government and minimum 10% of admissible project cost as private contribution from Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV), formed by Micro & Small Enterprises of the cluster.
