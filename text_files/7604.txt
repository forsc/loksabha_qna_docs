                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1272






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.11.2019






Grant of Mid Day Meal Scheme




Meenakashi Lekhi














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the amount that has been utilised out of the demand for grants forMid Day Meal Scheme and the details of amount that has been utilized for thesame;(b) whether it is a fact that in spite of Mid Day Meal Scheme, the nutrition level ofchildren is not at desired level and if so, the gaps identified in the implementationprocess; and(c) the steps taken/to be taken to improve the implementation of the MDMS atgrassroot level in the country?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): Under Mid-Day Meal Scheme (MDMS) during the year 2018-19, an amount ofRs. 9518.08 crore has been utilized as against budget provision of Rs. 9947.74crore. The State and UT-wise details of fund utilized is given at Annexure.(b) & (c): As per the provisions of National Food Security Act, 2013, children uptoclass VIII or within the age group of 6-14 years are entitled to one mid day mealfree of charge, every day except on school holidays, in all schools run by localbodies, Government and Government aided schools so as to meet nutritionalstandards specified in the Act. The Mid-Day Meal Guidelines prescribe thefollowing nutritional and food norms under the Mid-Day Meal Scheme forimproving the quality of mid-day meal.<pre>ItemsFor children of PrimaryclassesFor children of UpperPrimary classesA) Nutritional Norms (Per child per day)Calorie 450 700Protein 12 gms 20 gmsB) Quantum of Food Norms (Per child per day)Food-grains 100 gms 150 gmsPulses 20 gms 30 gmsVegetables 50 gms 75 gmsOil & fat 5 gms 7.5 gmsSalt &condimentsAs per need As per need</pre>The Government of India has issued guidelines on quality, safety and hygienein school level kitchens to all the States and UTs. These guidelines inter-aliaprovide for instructions to schools to procure Agmark quality and branded itemsfor preparation of mid day meals, tasting of meals by 2-3 adult members ofSchool Management Committee including at least one teacher before serving tochildren and to put in place a system of testing of food samples by accreditedlaboratories. Further, the MDM Rules, 2015 provide for mandatory testing offood samples by Government recognized laboratories to ensure that the mealsmeet nutritional standards and quality. The Government has also adopted anelaborate monitoring mechanism at Central, State and District levels to ensurequality food is served to children under the Scheme. In addition to directingStates and UTs to carry out Social Audit, the Centre constitutes Joint ReviewMissions (JRMs) consisting of educational and nutritional experts, whichreview the scheme through field visits from time to time. The reports of JRMsare shared with concerned States and UTs for taking suitable action on thefindings.*****
