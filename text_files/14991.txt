                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3592






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2020






Status of Bhatinda-Srinagar Gas Pipeline




Hasnain Masoodi














                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the status of Bhatinda-Srinagar gas pipeline in Jammu and Kashmir;(b) the number/details of LPG Bottling plants in Jammu and Kashmir;(c) the steps taken by the Ministry to setup CNG filling station for vehicles in Jammu and Kashmir; and(d) the steps taken by the Ministry to setup H-CNG filling stations for vehicles in Jammu and Kashmir?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) Petroleum & Natural Gas Regulatory Board (PNGRB) has authorized 725 km long Bhatinda-Jammu-Srinagar Natural Gas Pipeline (BJSPL) to GSPL India Gasnet Limited (GIGL) which is developing the authorized pipeline project. PNGRB has informed that about 102 km long pipeline section from Jalandhar-Amritsar in Punjab as a part of BJSPL has been commissioned.(b) At present, there are four LPG bottling plants including one in Leh with a total bottling capacity of 155 TMTPA in Jammu and Kashmir.(c) & (d) Establishment of Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) stations is the part of development of City Gas Distribution (CGD) Network and the same is carried out by the authorized entities based on techno-commercial viability and pipeline connectivity in the area. At present, PNGRB has not authorized any Geographical Areas (GAs) in the Union Territory of Jammu and Kashmir. At present, there are no CNG or H-CNG stations in Jammu and Kashmir.*****
