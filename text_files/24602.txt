                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

663






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2021






Expansion of Airports




Balubhau (Alias Suresh Narayan) Dhanorkar














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of CIVIL AVIATIONbe pleased to state:-(a) the number of airports expanded and modernised  by the Government during the last five years;(b) the number of above-mentioned airports privatised during the last five years;(c) whether the Government proposes to privatise more airports in coming years;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) if not, the steps being taken by the Government to retain the airports?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a): During the last five years, Airports Authority of India (AAI) has completed Major Engineering Infrastructure works at 47 airports. These are Madurai, Bareilly, Kolkata, Agartala, Barapani, Raipur, Ranchi, Thiruvananthapuram, Lilabari, Khajuraho, Jaipur, Darbhanga, Ahmedabad, Dehradun, Puducherry, Chandigarh, Bokaro, Fursatgunj, Chennai, Imphal, Guwahati, Dibrugarh, Rupsi, Gorakhpur, Vijayawada, Allahabad, Amritsar, Hindon, Kadapa, Mangaluru, Goa, Udaipur, Kolhapur, Lucknow, Jammu, Delhi, Tezu, Kishangarh, Belgaum, Hubli, Tirupati, Rajahmundry, Tuticorin, Salem, Coimbatore, Silchar and Calicut. Additionally, under Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS)- Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik (UDAN), 59 airports including 5 helipads and 2 water aerodromes have been upgraded/revived for commencement of RCS operations since the launch of the scheme in October, 2016.;(b): During the last five years, AAI, after bidding process, has awarded 6 airports viz. Ahmedabad, Lucknow, Mangaluru, Jaipur, Guwahati and Thiruvananthapuram under Public Private Partnership (PPP) to M/s Adani Enterprises Ltd. (AEL) for operation, management and development for a period of 50 years.(c) & (d): AAI Board in its 190th Meeting held on 05th September, 2019 has approved leasing of another six airports namely Bhubaneshwar, Varanasi, Amritsar, Raipur, Indore and Trichy for undertaking operations, management and development through PPP.;(e): Does not arise in view of (c) & (d) above.
