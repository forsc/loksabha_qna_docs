                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

8






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2021






Skill Development Schemes in Uttar Pradesh and NER




Anurag Sharma













                                                    Rebati Tripura
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of Skill Development schemes currently being implemented in Uttar Pradesh specially in Jhansi and Lalitpur and North Eastern Region especially in Tripura during last two years; (b)	whether the Government proposes to formulate schemes specially for the skill development in remote/tribal area if so, the details thereof; and(c)	the amount of funds allocated by the Union Government to Uttar Pradesh and North Eastern Region (NER) for the said schemes ?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	The Government has adopted a multi-pronged strategy for the overall skill development of people living across the country including in deprived areas, States and skill sectors. The Ministry has been implementing Pradhan Mantri Kaushal VikasYojana (PMKVY), Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) Scheme and National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS), for providing short term Skill Development training and Craftsman Training Scheme (CTS), for long term training, to the youth belonging to all section of the society across all over India, including UP and the North Eastern Region, including Tripura.(b)	There is no exclusive specific scheme for the STs; however, the following additional benefits are given to tribal candidates under the different skill development of schemes: S.No.	Scheme	Benefits for tribal candidates1	JSS	Course fees are waived for the SC, ST, Divyangjan& BPL category beneficiaries2	CTS	Benefits are extended as per State/UT policy(c) 	The outcomes and fund allocations under important schemes being implemented in the States of UP, Tripura, Districts of Jhansi and Lalitpur and the NER by Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE) for skill development of youth are given below:-i)	Pradhan Mantri Kaushal VikasYojana: The details as required are placed at Annexure I.ii) 	Jan Sikshan Sansthan (JSS) Scheme: This scheme aims at providing vocational education/ skills to non-lierates, neo-literates as well as school dropouts by identifying skills that have a market in the region of their establishment. There are 50 Jan Shikshan Sansthans in the State of Uttar Pradesh. However, there is no JSS Centre in the districts of Jhansi and Lalitpur as on date. In Uttar Pradesh, Rs. 22.04 crores and Rs. 11.23 crores for 2020-21 and 2021-22 respectively have been disbursed under the Scheme. Further, there are 19 JSS centres in the North Eastern region including 2 centres in Tripura (Dhalai and Agartala).In North Eastern Region, Rs. 04.94 crores and Rs. 4.14 crores for 2020-21 and 2021-22 respectively have been disbursed under the Scheme.ii) 	National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS): This scheme is for promoting apprenticeship training and increasing the engagement of apprentices by providing financial support to industrial establishments undertaking apprenticeship programme under  ‘The Apprentices Act, 1961’. Training Consist of Basic Training and On-the-Job Training/Practical training at workplace in the country. The total Apprentices trained since inception of the scheme in the State of UP is 95,233 and in the state of Tripura is 1,564. No funds have been allocated in last two years. iii) 	Craftsmen Training Schemes (CTS): Long term training programme in 137 trades are provided through 14,788 ITIs in the country. There are 19 ITIs in the Tripura Distict and 8 ITIs in Jhansi and Lalitpur Districts. No funds have been allocated in last two years to the State of UP and NER.
