                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5299






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.07.2019






Water Logging at IGI Airport




Gautam Gambhir














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there have been instances of major water-logging problem due to rains at Indira Gandhi International (IGI) Airport in Delhi during the last three years and the current year;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether any steps have been taken to prevent recurrence of such incidents; and(d) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) & (b)  No, Sir. There have been no instances of major water logging during the last three years and current year at Indira Gandhi International (IGI) Airport.;(c) &(d) Delhi International Airport Limited (DIAL) has taken some proactive measures which include the following :1. DIAL maintains a Comprehensive Annual Monsoon Preparedness Plan and strictly adheres to it.;2. High power pumps are installed at strategic locations to evacuate the water in case of heavy rains.;3. DIAL continuously follows up with DDA/PWD and other concerned stakeholders to widen their drain network to cater the outflow of water from IGI Airport area.
