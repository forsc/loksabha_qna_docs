                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1169






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2021






HS Codes and Tariff Rates




Srinivas Kesineni














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the HS Codes and the tariff rates of the products for which the tariffs have been raised during the last five years; (b) 	the segregation of the HS Codes of these products according to the BEC (Rev 5) classification and for which category of products tariffs have been raised the most;(c) 	whether these tariff increases have catered to correct the inverted-tariff structure problem; and(d) 	if so, the details thereof along with the other non-tariff measures that the Government is taking in this direction?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(SHRI PANKAJ CHAUDHARY)(a), (b), (c) and (d): Tariff rates have been increased in the ranges of 2.5% to 70% for 1170 Harmonized System (HS) Codes at 8-digit level in the last 5 years. Tariff rates are based on the Harmonized System Nomenclature (HSN) codes prescribed by World Customs Organization (WCO). These are not based on BEC (Rev 5) classification. These tariff increases and non-tariff measures have multiple objectives such as correcting inverted duty structure, encouraging domestic manufacturing etc.	.*****
