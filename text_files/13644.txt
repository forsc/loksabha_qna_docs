                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2466






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.03.2020






Works under Jal Shakti




Dhal Singh Bisen














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the works proposed to be done under the Ministry of Jal Shakti; (b) the details of the works to be done by the Ministry in Madhya Pradesh for which it has received proposals along with the time of its receipt; (c) whether any proposal in this regard is pending with the Ministry and if so, the details thereof; and (d) the number of projects in Madhya Pradesh which are currently in progress along with the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) to (d) The Ministry of Jal Shakti has been created by the Government in May, 2019 to deal with water issues in an integrated manner under a single umbrella. The Ministry includes two Departments namely, the Department of Water Resources, River Development & Ganga Rejuvenation (DoWR, RD & GR) and the Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation (DoDW & S).             Water Resources Projects are planned, funded, executed and maintained by the State Governments themselves as per their own resources and priority. To supplement their efforts and to encourage sustainable development and efficient management of water resources, the Central Government provides technical and financial assistance to State Governments through various schemes and programmes.             Out of 99 prioritized projects under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) - Accelerated Irrigation Benefits Programme (AIBP), 14 projects (and 7 phases) are in the State of Madhya Pradesh. During 2016-20 (so far) Central Assistance (CA) and State Share of Rs.588.86 crore and 824.43 crore respectively have been released for these projects. An additional Ultimate Irrigation Potential (UIP) of 162.19 Th.ha  has been created during 2016-19. Out of above 14 projects (and 7 phases), 8 projects (and 03 phases) have been reported to be completed in Madhya Pradesh.              In Madhya Pradesh there are 14 projects in the list of 99 prioritized projects and all have been included under CADWM programme. These projects target CADWM works in balance Culturable Command  Area  (CCA)  of  5.95  lakh ha.  The total cost of these projects is estimated at Rs.2536.98 crore Contd…P/2//2//with central share of Rs.1259.00 crore. During 2016-20 (till date), Central share of Rs.251.44 crore has been released to Madhya Pradesh for CADWM works of these projects, State has reported completion of CAD works in CCA of 2.23 lakh ha. has been reported by State during this period.               Government of India, in partnership with the States, has launched Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM), which aims at providing potable water at service level of 55 litre per capita per day (lpcd) to every rural household, through Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC) by 2024, with an estimated cost of Rs. 3.60 lakh Crore. In the current financial year 2019-20, an amount of Rs.571.60 Crore has been allocated to Madhya Pradesh and the entire amount has been released. The Ministry received request of Madhya Pradesh Jal Nigam, the project implementing agency of Government of Madhya Pradesh for the following Externally Aided Projects:- (1)	Madhya Pradesh Jal Nigam had made a request vide letter dated 14.02.2020 for allowing the State to take up two new schemes under the ongoing New Development Bank assisted MP Rural Water Supply Project (Loan No. 17IN02). It was informed that the estimated cost of EPC and consultancy work of ongoing New Development Bank assisted MP Rural Water Supply Project was Rs.4247.20 Crore, whereas after Bidding process completion cost will be Rs.3535.87 Crore. The proposed two new schemes to be taken up cost Rs.555.97 Crore benefiting additional 362 villages and additional population of 4.2 lakhs. (2)	Government of Madhya Pradesh had earlier submitted a proposal for implementation of 19 Rural Multi Village Surface Based Water Supply Schemes in various districts of the State in 2015-16. These 19 Rural Multi Village Surface Based Water Supply Schemes were to be implemented in 9 districts of the State. Government of Madhya Pradesh had submitted proposal for project in 2016, which were recommended to Department of Economic Affairs vide O.M. dated 21.10.2016. Madhya Pradesh Government submitted the revised proposals of Rs.4,308 Crore vide letter dated 12.12.2019. DEA vide O.M. dated 15.01.2020 requested Department of Drinking Water & Sanitation (DDWS) to appraise the DPRs provided by Govt. of Madhya Pradesh with a recommendation for posing the project for JICA ODA loan to Govt. of Japan/JICA. DDWS vide O.M. dated 22.01.2020 (copy enclosed) provided its recommendation for the project subject to its compliance as per Jal Jeevan Mission and also informed DEA that DDWS’s role is limited to technical examination/recommendation of the DPRs of the project which had been already recommended. The Loan Agreement is expected to be signed in March, 2020 between DEA and JICA, and the project is expected to commence from April, 2020 onwards.There is no proposal pending from Government of Madhya Pradesh in JJM. *****
