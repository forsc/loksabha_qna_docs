                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COAL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3762






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.12.2019






CSR Fund




S.R. Parthiban













                                                    Sukhbir Singh Jaunapuria
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COAL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)          whether the Government has formulated any scheme to utilise Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) fund of coal company to develop coal producing areas and if so, the details thereof; (b)          the details of major development works/activities undertaken from (CSR) funds by coal PSUs;(c)           the quantum of funds accrued and utilized by coal PSUs for the purpose during the last three years and the current year;(d)          the status of physical progress of such development works/activities; (e)          whether any of the coal PSUs is in default of spending CSR funds and if so, the details thereof along with the corrective action taken by the Government thereon; and    (f)          whether any compensation is being given to the families of mine workers who are victims of mine accidents and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




 MINISTER OF PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS, COAL AND MINES(SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI)  (a): Development work/activities are undertaken by Coal India Limited (CIL) and its subsidiaries and NLC India Limited (NLCIL) under Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) as per Scheduled VII of Companies Act 2013. (b) to (d) : Major development works/activities undertaken from CSR funds by coal PSUs are infrastructure development, skill development, social empowerment, water supply, health and sanitation, sports and culture and education facilities etc. In addition to above, Coal India Limited & its subsidiaries and NLCIL have undertaken construction of toilets in Government schools of various states under Swachh Bharat Programme. The details of funds allocated for CSR activities during last three years and current year along with progress of these activities are given below.                                                                                                                      (Rs. Crores)CIL and its SubsidiariesYear	Budget	Expenditure	Status of the Physical Progress2016-17	442.75	489.67	Attached as per Annexure-A.2017-18	383.05	483.78	2018-19	353.98	416.47	2019-20@	404.27	110.90	NLC India Limited and its subsidiaries2016-17	43.46	37.92*	Completed2017-18	43.59^	45.03	Completed2018-19	45.73	53.15	Completed2019-20	47.65	7.21@	Activities undertaken are in progress. * Rs. 6.27 Cr has been carried forward to 2017-18.^ Rs. 6.27 Cr (includes carried forward amount for 2016-17)@ up to 09/2019 (e):  The coal PSUs are not in default of spending CSR funds. Coal India and its subsidiaries collectively as well as NLCIL have achieved CSR fund utilization during each of the last three years (FY 16-17 to FY 18-19).   (f) Compensation is being paid to the families of mine employees, who are victims of mine accidents, as per provision of the Employees Compensation Act (ECA)-1923 (Principal Act) (amended upto 2017). During the last three years and current year CIL and NLCIL has given a compensation of Rs.11.86 Cr(Approx) and Rs. 59.06 Lakh, respectively. Apart from compensation paid as per provision of the ECA-1923, Ex-gratia amount of INR 5,00,000 is also being paid by CIL to the eligible dependent of fatally injured employee (for contractual employees; it started from 21.12.2016). The said Ex-gratia amount has been revised to INR 15,00,000 with effect from 14.11.2019. *****
