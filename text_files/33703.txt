                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1125






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.02.2022






Gram Panchayats Nominated under SAGY in Maharashtra




Sanjay Haribhau Jadhav













                                                     Bhavana Gawali (Patil)
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of Gram Panchayats nominated under Sansad Adarsh Gram Yojana (SAGY) from the year 2019 in Maharashtra, Member of Parliament (MP)-wise alongwith its current status;(b) 	whether the number has increased at present, if so, the details thereof;(c) 	whether the Targeted Development Goals have been achieved in the State of Maharashtra, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and(d) 	the contribution of the Union government in implementation of rural development schemes in Maharashtra since its inception?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SHRI FAGGAN SINGH KULASTE)(a)&(b): Under Sansad Adarsh Gram Yojana(SAGY), 79 Gram Panchayats have been nominated from the year 2019 in Maharashtra. The details of SAGY GPs, Members of Parliament wise is given in Annexure-I (c ): SAGY programme has been  conceived as a convergence programme which works on the basis of planning and dovetailing of various government schemes in the adopted Gram Panchayats. As such there are no targets fixed for this programme.This programme leverages the leadership capacities of Hon’ble MPs in planning and execution of the programme. This programme is based on the vision of the MP and the aspirations of the adopted Gram Panchayats. In this programme Village Development Plans(VDP) are prepared by the Gram Panchayats and approved by the district authorities in consultation with the concerned MPs. The details of the VDPs of this programme are available on the SAGY web portal on https://saanjhi.gov.in/ (d): The Union Government allocates budgets, sanctions funds, formulates policies, and guidelines for effective implementation of Rural Development schemes in the whole country including Maharashtra. The Ministry also undertakes periodic assessments of the physical and financial progress achieved by the States/UTs. Progress of all the rural development schemes can be perused on their respective website. 1.	Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act 2005- https://nrega.nic.in/2.	Pradhan Mantri Awas Gram Yojana-G(PMAY-G)- http://pmayg.nic.in/netiay/Home.aspx3.	Deen Dayal Upadhyaya-Grameen Kaushalya Yojana(DDU-GKY) - http://ddugky.gov.in/4.	Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY) - http://omms.nic.in/5.	Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana - National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM)-https://aajeevika.gov.in/6.	National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP) - https://nsap.nic.in/7.	Shyama Prasad Mukherji Rurban Mission (SPMRM) - https://rurban.gov.in/A synoptic breakup of the major programmes of RD in the State of Maharashtra is given in Annexure-II******
