                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

282






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2020






DDU-GKY




Unmesh Bhaiyyasaheb Patil














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of funds allocated for the Deendayal Upadhyaya-Grameen Kaushalya Yojana (DDU-GKY) and the expenditure incurred therein during the last three years, State-wise;(b) 	the number of training centres operating in the country under the Scheme, State-wise;(c) 	whether the youths are taught to use smartphones and internet-based applications under the training programme, and if so, the details thereof;(d) 	whether under the Scheme the youths are trained and placed in the hospitality industry, and if so, the details thereof; and(e) 	whether the skills imparted under the Scheme for placement in one job can be used by the youth in other jobs as well, and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) Deendayal Upadhyaya-Grameen Kaushalya Yojana (DDU-GKY) is a demand driven scheme and funds are allocated to States as per demands against approved Action Plans. The State/UT-wise funds released under DDU-GKY during the last three years and current year (upto 15th January, 2020) is given at Annexure-1.(b) As per available information, State/UT wise details of operational training centers under Deendayal Upadhyaya-Grameen Kaushalya Yojana (as on 29th January 2020) is given at Annexure-2.(c) There is a provision for providing tablets to the candidates during training program. Further, there is a provision for minimum 80 hrs of mandatory training on computer skills during training program in which usage of internet and smart phones are taught. (d) Yes, Sir. The youths are trained and placed in the hospitality industry and Sector & Trade wise details are given at Annexure-3.(e) Yes, Sir. Alongwith domain skills of a particular job, there is a mandatory provision for providing non-domain skills which induce English, Computer skills and soft skills for a minimum of 160 hours. These skills can be used in other jobs also.*********
