                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3804






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.07.2019






Foreigner Tribunals




Abdul Khaleque














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) the total number of Foreigner Tribunal (FT) in Assam and the judges working therein, FT-wise and district-wise;(b) the total number of people who have been tagged as ‘declared foreigners’ by the said tribunals, FT-wise;(c) the number of cases disposed of by FTs per month;(d) the number of persons not declared as foreigners by FTs;(e) whether in most of the cases, alleged persons are not given proper chance to prove their citizenship and FTs pass judgement without hearing the alleged persons;(f) if so, the corrective measures taken by the Government; and(g) if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a):	Presently, 100 Foreigners Tribunals (FTs) are functional in Assam. Number of FTs and members working in different districts of Assam are at Annexure-A.-2-L.S.US.Q.NO.3804 FOR 16.07.2019(b):	Upto 31st March, 2019, 1,17,164 persons have been declared foreigners by Tribunals. Marking of ‘D’ voters is not done by Foreigner Tribunals.  The details of Declared Foreigners is at Annexure-B.(c):	The number of cases disposed of by FTs each month varies from month to month and Tribunal to Tribunal.(d) to (f):	Foreigners Tribunals function as per the procedure laid down in Foreigners Tribunals (Order) 1964.  As per order dated. 17.12.2014 of Hon’ble Supreme Court of India in WP(C) No.562/2012, a Special Bench of Hon’ble Gauhati High Court is regularly monitoring the functioning of the Foreigners Tribunals. A person not satisfied with opinion of FT is entitled to approach higher courts. Adjudication of Citizenship is a legal process and every person is given full opportunity to prove his claim of Citizenship.********
