                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1265






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2020






Funds to Non-Governmental Organisation




Shantanu Thakur














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)   	whether the Government is providing funds to Non-Governmental Organisations (NGOs) for setting up Skill Development Centers for youth in West Bengal; (b)  if so, the details thereof along with the funds sanctioned for Non-Governmental Organisations in West Bengal during the current financial year, district-wise;(c)  	the number of youth who got training in the said centers during the last three years along with the names of the trades in which they have been trained and the number of students who got employment; and (d) 	the number of Non-Governmental Organisations selected for funding to open/start Skill Development Centers in West Bengal?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)  Yes, Sir. 	(b) The details of funds sanctioned to Jan Shikshan Sansthans (JSS) during the current financial year, i.e. 2019-20 (upto 31.1.2020) in the State of West Bengal, district wise, is given at Annexure “A”(c)  The number of beneficiaries trained by JSSs of West Bengal during the last three years is given below.2016-17     :  99662017-18     :  49122018-19     :  5829  The list of trade/job roles in which beneficiaries trained by JSS is attached at Annexure-“B”.	Keeping in view the objective of supplementing the income level of the target group, the JSSs imparts training to the beneficiaries for their livelihood linkages, primarily in the skill-sets required in the informal sector of employment/wage-employment.	(d)     Presently, there are 8 Jan Shikshan Sansthans in the State of West Bengal.
