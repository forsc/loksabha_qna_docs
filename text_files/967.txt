                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

89






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.06.2019






Modernisation of Airports




Andimuthu Raja














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of international/ domestic airports across the country, State/ UT-wise including Tamil Nadu;(b) whether the Union Government is planning to makeover/modernise busiest airports in the country and if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise including Tamil Nadu;(c) the funds estimated for this makeover across the country, State/UTwise along with the details of the targets set in this regard; and(d) whether the Union Government has signed an MoU with the State Government of Tamil Nadu in this regard and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) to (d): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.Statement in reply to parts (a), (b), (c) & (d) of the Starred (*) Question No. 89 for answer on 27.06.2019 regarding ''Modernisation of Airports'';;  (a) There are 103 domestic airports, 10 Customs airports and 28 International airports in the country. The State/UT-wise details are at Annexure-I.;;  (b) Modernisation of airports is a continuous process and is undertaken by Airports Authority of India(AAI) and concerned airport operators from time to time depending on the availability of land, commercial viability, socio-economic considerations, traffic demand / willingness of airlines to operate to/from such airports etc. To meet the demand, AAI has embarked upon a Capital Expenditure (CAPEX) plan of Rs. 25,000 crores over a period of 5 years for development / modernisation / upgradation of  AAI airports in the country including Coimbatore, Chennai, Madurai, Trichy and Tuticorin in Tamil Nadu, Guwahati in Assam, Imphal in Manipur, Jaipur and Udaipur in Rajasthan, Leh, Jammu and Srinagar in Jammu and Kashmir, Agra, Kanpur and Varanasi in Uttar Pradesh, Vijayawada in Andhra Pradesh, Hyderabad in Telangana, Patna and Bihta in Bihar, Bhubneswar in Odisha, Rajkot in Gujarat and Kolhapur and Pune in Maharashtra.  ;  (c) The State/UT-wise details of the funds estimated and targets set for the modernisation of airports across the country are at Annexure - II.;;  (d) No, Sir. No MoU has been signed with the State Government of Tamil Nadu as the development works are taking place at AAI owned airports.
