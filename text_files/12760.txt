                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1627






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.03.2020






International Standard Facilities in Bundelkhand




Pushpendra Singh Chandel














                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of plans and efforts made to attract domestic and foreign tourists to Bundelkhand; (b) 	whether the Government has taken initiative to provide facilities as per international standards for tourists in Bundelkhand especially in Mahoba Charkhari, Kalinjar etc. and if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Ministry of Skill Development, NITI Aayog and including other institutions have informed the Government through their reports and recommendations that employment can be generated in abundance and quickly through development of tourism in economically backward Bundelkhand; and (d) 	if so the details of the action taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR TOURISM (INDEPENDENT CHARGE)(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) & (b):	Ministry of Tourism promotes India as a holistic destination including Bundelkhand under the Incredible India brand line.  Ministry as part of its on-going activities, releases print, electronic, online and outdoor media campaigns in the international and domestic markets.  Promotions are also undertaken through the Social Media accounts and Website of the Ministry.   The Ministry also provides financial assistance to State Governments/UT Administrations for organizing fairs, festivals and tourism related events which have the potential for promoting tourism and attracting tourists.The Ministry of Tourism under its schemes of Swadesh Darshan and PRASHAD provides financial assistance to State Governments/Union Territory (UT) Administrations/Central Agencies for development of tourism infrastructure in the Country.  One of the major objectives of the above scheme is to position tourism as a major engine of economic growth and generating employment opportunities.     The projects under the scheme are identified by the respective State Governments/Union Territory Administrations & then taken up for development in consultation with the State Governments/Union Territory Administrations and are sanctioned subject to availability of funds, submission of suitable Detailed Project Reports, adherence to scheme guidelines and utilization of funds released earlier.   Based on the proposals received from the State Governments, projects sanctioned under the Swadesh Darshan Scheme are given at Annexure-I.With the objective for Human Resource Development in the field of Hospitality and Tourism, under the Scheme “Assistance to Institute of Hotel Managements (IHM)/ Food Craft Institutes (FCI)/Indian Institute of Tourism and Travel Management (IITTM)/ National Council for Hotel Management & Catering Technology(NCHMCT), Ministry of Tourism has sanctioned an amount of Rs.240.00 Lakh to Bundelkhand University for implementing 5 years Integrated Course in Masters of Hotel Management and Catering Technology in the year 2018-19. Further, the Ministry has also sanctioned Rs. 475.00 Lakh for setting up of a Food Craft Institute (FCI) at Khajuraho under the same scheme during the year 2016-17.(c) & (d):	Under Skill India Mission, Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship is implementing a flagship Scheme known as Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) 2016-20 with an objective to provide skilling to one crore people under Short Term Training (STT) and Recognition of Prior Learning(RPL) across the Country for four years i.e 2016-20. Under PMKVY (2016-20), training is being imparted in 371 National Skills Qualification Framework (NSQF) aligned job roles of level 3 and 4 across 37 sectors including Tourism and Hospitality sector.  The details of skilling training in Tourism and Hospitality Sector undertaken by the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship is given at Annexure-II.********
