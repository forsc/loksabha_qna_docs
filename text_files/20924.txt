                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2175






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.03.2021






Business under Skill Development Training




Meenakashi Lekhi














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details on number of youth trained under the skill development of the Government; (b) 	the details on number of youth who got employment in various sectors after skill development training; and (c) 	the details of the number of youth who opened their own start-ups/businesses after skill development training?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) & (b):	The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE) is implementing the following schemes viz. Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY), Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) and National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS) for short-term skill training and Craftsmen Training Scheme (CTS) through the Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) for long-term training. The number of candidates trained so far under the above schemes since 2016-17 are as follows: Sl. No.	Schemes	Candidates trained in lakhs1.	PMKVY 	106.962.	JSS	10.963.	NAPS	9.604.	CTS	52.98Under the Short-term training component of PMKVY, 18.95 lakh candidates have been reported placed in jobs under various sectors including manufacturing sector. The Recognition of Prior Learning Component of PMKVY, JAA, NAPS and CTS do not have placement as a criteria. (c):	In order to inculcate entrepreneurship skills in the candidates for establishing business, the PMKVY scheme has a mandatory provision for training under 155 hours of an English, Employability & Entrepreneurship (EEE) module, besides the core curriculum of the course. Further, Training Providers provide support to the candidates for entrepreneurship development by facilitating bank linkages along with providing guidance and mentoring support. As on 19.01.2021, around 2.77 lakh candidates have been self-employed across the country.
