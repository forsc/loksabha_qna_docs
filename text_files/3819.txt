                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3588






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Voucher for Admission in Kendriya Vidyalayas




Rajiv Pratap Rudy














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there is a provision for admission of students in Central Schools(KVs) on vouchers issued to Members of Parliament;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) the total number of central schools where such coupons/vouchers wereavailed for admissions in each Parliamentary constituency across the country;(d) the number of Kendriya Vidyalaya schools across the country, State-wise anddistrict-wise; and(e) the total number of Parliamentary Constituencies in the country where there isno Kendriya Vidyalaya schools, State-wise?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) Yes, Sir.(b) Each Hon’ble Member of Parliament can refer 10 (ten) cases for admissionunder the scheme in an academic year but such recommendations shall be confinedto children whose parents belong to his constituency either by domicile or on accountof having been soon-before posted there or else on account of exigencies of service,migrate to his constituency. Such recommendations would be for admissions inKendriya Vidyalaya(s) (KVs) located in his constituency only. In case there is no KVin the constituency of the Hon’ble Member of Parliament (Lok Sabha), he/she mayrecommend these admissions in the KV(s) located in any neighbouring contiguousconstituency. For Member of the Rajya Sabha, the State from which the member hasbeen elected would be deemed to be his constituency for this purpose. Nominatedmembers of the Rajya Sabha and Lok Sabha can recommend 10 (ten) cases foradmission in any one or more KVs of the Country.<pre>i) These admissions shall be over and above the class strength.ii) These recommendations would be made for classes I to IX only.iii) These admissions would be made at the beginning of the academicyear and no admission would be allowed after the prescribed cut-offdate of the year.iv) The recommendations to be made shall be valid only if these are madein the prescribed format provided to each Member of Parliament byKVS (HQ). Recommendations sent in any other format / manner shallnot be considered.v) The children recommended by Hon’ble Members of Parliament mustbe otherwise eligible for admission as per the extant KVS AdmissionGuidelines.(c) There are 1041 KVs where coupons were availed for admission inParliamentary Constituencies across the country for the academic year 2019-20.(d) As on date, 1206 KVs are functioning in the across the country. State-wiseand districts-wise details are at Annexure-I.(e) As on date, 101 Parliamentary Constituencies are not having KVs. TheState/UT-wise details are at Annexure-II.</pre>
