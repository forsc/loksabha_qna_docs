                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

109






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.02.2021






CRCs for Differently Abled




M. K. Raghavan














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details and the objective of Composite Regional Centres (CRCs) for differently abled; (b) the current position of CRC at Kozhikode in respect of academics, rehabilitation, administration and infrastructure; and  (c) the future plans for growth of CRC at Kozhikode?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI KRISHANPAL GURJAR)(a) The Government has approved 21 Composite Regional Centres (CRCs) as outreach Centres/extended arms of the National Institutes under the Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities (DEPwD) as per list annexed.  The main objectives of CRCs are to provide rehabilitation services to all categories of Persons with Disabilities (PwDs), train rehabilitation professionals, workers and functionaries, undertake programmes of education and skill development and create awareness amongst parents and the community regarding needs and rights of PwDs. (b) & (c) CRC, Kozhikode has been operational since 2012.  It is presently conducting three long term courses in Special Education and two short term courses. It is well equipped with necessary infrastructure for providing various  rehabilitation services to PwDs like Physiotherapy, Occupational Therapy, Sensory Integration, Early Intervention, Vocational Training and Rehabilitation, Community based Rehabilitation, Speech Therapy, Audiology & Speech-Language Pathology, Special Education and Clinical and Rehabilitation Psychology.   It has a full time regular Director to look after the overall administration and functioning of CRC. As per need and availability of resources, it will conduct other courses and further enrich its services.*****
