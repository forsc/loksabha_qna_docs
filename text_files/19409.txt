                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)

                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

770






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






AYUSH Facilities in Rural Areas




Umesh G. Jadhav

                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) the details of AYUSH facilities available in the country particularly in rural areas, State/UT-wise, streamwise including Madhya Pradesh and Telangana;(b) the number of proposals received for setting up of AYUSH hospitals/centres from various States/UTs specially in rural areas including Maharashtra, Northern part of Karnataka, Odisha, Jharkhand, Andhra Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh and Bhagalpur district of Bihar;(c) the details of selection criteria for sanction of new AYUSH hospitals and facilities in these hospitals; and(d) the other measures taken by the Government to supplement the efforts of various State Governments in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS AND ADDITIONAL CHARGE OF MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(SHRI KIREN RIJIJU)(a):	The details of AYUSH facilities available in the country in rural areas, State/UT-wise, stream-wise including Madhya Pradesh and Telangana is furnished at Annexure-I.(b):	Public Health being a State subject, the primary responsibility for setting up of AYUSH hospitals/centres lies with the respective State/UT Governments. However, under Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM), there is provision of financial assistance for setting up of upto 50 bedded integrated AYUSH hospitals and co-location of AYUSH facilities at Primary Health Centres (PHCs), Community Health Centres (CHCs) and District Hospitals (DHs). As per the proposals received from various States/UTs including Maharashtra, Karnataka, Odisha, Jharkhand, Andhra Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh and Bihar through State annual Action Plans (SAAPs), total 101 numbers of up to 50 bedded Integrated AYUSH hospitals and 786 units of PHCs/CHCs/DHs for establishment of AYUSH units has been supported for undertaking addition/ alteration of existing premises; furniture, fixtures and equipments under NAM. The State/UT-wise details are furnished at Annexure-II. Further, as far as Bhagalpur district of Bihar is concerned no such proposal has been received. (c) & (d):	As per the NAM guideline funding of different components including setting up of upto 50 bedded integrated AYUSH hospitals is on a gap filling basis based on proposals received through State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs).The selection of AYUSH hospitals are being done by the State/UT Governments as per their felt need. Accordingly, as per the provision of the NAM guideline States/UTs are furnishing the proposals for setting up of upto 50 bedded integrated AYUSH hospitals through their SAAPs.Further, Ministry of AYUSH is supplementing the efforts of State Governments through NAM by making provision of financial assistance of Rs. 900.00 Lakhs as one time assistance for infrastructure development and Rs. 150.00 Lakhs as recurring assistance for salary of manpower & medicines/diet/consumables.
