                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3323






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






Stalled Projects




L.S. Tejasvi Surya














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of State-wise data of the number of stalled projects that will be provided funding from the Alternative Investment Fund proposed to be created;(b) the number of stalled projects eligible for funding in major cities viz. NCR-New Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Bengaluru, and Chennai; and (c) whether Rs. 10000 crore proposed expenditure from the Government will be over the Budgeted Expenditure for the present financial year and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        The MINISTER OF STATE (FINANCE)(SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR)(a) to (b):  As per the study commissioned by the Investment Manager of the first AIF/Fund under the Special Window for affordable and middle-income housing viz SBICAP Venture Limited, 2,202 housing projects in the affordable and mid-income segment comprising approximately 4.58 lakh launched units are stalled (including NPA/NCLT projects).  A Statement indicating details of stalled projects in major cities is at Annexure.  Funding will be provided by the AIFs under the Special Window if the project meets certain criteria such as: - (i)	Net-worth positive (ii)	Affordable and middle-income housing projects(iii)	On-going projects registered with RERA(iv)	Reference by existing lender (c): The total commitment of funds to be infused by the Government in the affordable and middle-income group housing sector through the AIFs under the Special Window would be up to INR 10,000 crores, which will be released through appropriate budget allocations on the basis of drawdown notices received from the investment manager.*****    AnnexureStatement referred to in reply to parts (a) to (b) of Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No.3323 for answer on 09.12.2019	Units	MSft	Value (in Cr.)Cities	Supply	Absorption	Unsold	Supply	Abso-rption	Unsold	Supply	Absorpt--ion	UnsoldNCR	199,961	150,934	49,027	285.4	215.4	70.0	135,363	101,374	33,990MMR	97,280	45,559	51,721	92.1	41.1	50.9	106,918	45,467	61,451Bengaluru	38,947	18,474	20,473	54.5	25.6	28.9	26,930	12,587	14,342Chennai	18,065	9,340	8,725	19.6	10.4	9.2	7,832	4,231	3,601Kolkata	17,004	6,962	10,042	18.9	8.6	10.3	7,064	3,508	3,556Pune	18,545	6,732	11,813	19.8	7.8	12.1	11,498	4,596	6,902Hyderabad	6,269	2,424	3,845	9.3	3.7	5.6	3,019	1,140	1,878Ahmedabad	2,319	763	1,556	3.4	1.0	2.3	1,264	368	896Tier-2	59,369	32,989	26,380	79.1	43.8	35.3	27,878	15,119	12,759Total	457,759	274,177	183,582	582	357	225	327,766	188,390	139,377	***
