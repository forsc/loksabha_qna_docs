                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2813






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.03.2022






PMKVY/SANKALP in Tamil Nadu




S. Ramalingam














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)       whether the Government is implementing any project under Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) and SANKALP (Skill Acquisition and Knowledge Awareness for Livelihood Promotion) in Tamilnadu,(b)       if so, the details thereof;(c)        the total number of such projects being implemented in Tamilnadu and number of beneficiaries benefitted under these projects for the last 3 years;(d)       the total amount spent by the Government on these various projects for the last 3 years; and(e)       whether the Government has any proposal to review PMKVY and SANKALP in near future and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




(a) to (c)          Under Skill India Mission, Ministry is implementing PMKVY and SANKALP scheme in the State of Tamil Nadu. Under PMKVY, training is being imparted to candidates in short term training and recognition of prior learning.Under PMKVY, since inception, i.e., 2015 as on 31.12.2021, 3.96 lakh candidates have been trained in short term training and 3.48 lakh candidates have been oriented in recognition of prior learning in the State of Tamil Nadu.Under SANKALP scheme, Rs. 12.18 crore has been released as State Incentive Grant (as Central share) to Government of Tamil Nadu in line with the State’s plan. In addition, Rs. 10 lakh each to aspirational districts namely Ramanathapuram and Virudhnagar were released to strengthen skilling related activities. Further, Ministry has deployed 37 fellows across 37 districts of Tamil Nadu under Mahatma Gandhi National Fellowship program to support the district skill ecosystem.(d)       The PMKVY (except PMKVY 1.0 (2015-16) has two components, namely,  Central component being implemented through National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC), and State component being implemented through State Skill Development Mission (SSDM) of the State/UT. Under State component of PMKVY 2.0, targets were approved based on the project submitted by the state-specific SSDM.Under Central component of PMKVY, there is no provision of State-wise funds allocation. In the State of Tamil Nadu, Rs. 138.26 crore has been spent during last three years, i.e., FY 2018-21 by NSDC towards the implementation of central component of PMKVY..[Under the State component of PMKVY, funds and corresponding physical targets have been allocated to the States for the implementation of scheme through SSDMs. Under this State component of PMKVY, Rs. 34.43 crore has been disbursed to the State of Tamil Nadu during last three years, i.e., 2018-21 for the implementation of State component of PMKVY across the State.Ministry under SANKALP scheme has released a total of Rs. 12.38 crore to Government of Tamil Nadu (Rs. 12.18 crore as State Incentive Grant (Central share) and Rs. 20 lakh to the two aspirational districts of Tamil Nadu).(e)       Presently, Ministry is implementing third phase of PMKVY, i.e., PMKVY 3.0 which was modified on the basis of the evaluation studies and learning from the implementation of PMKVY 1.0 and 2.0.Also, Ministry undertakes periodic reviews of the implementation of SANKALP and PMKVY in the States/UTs with the SSDMs. Further, for SANKALP scheme, joint implementation reviews are organized by the World Bank with the SSDMs.
