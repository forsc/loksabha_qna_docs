                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4031






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Implementation of JSSK Scheme




Preneet Kaur














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the details of the measures taken/being taken by the Government to address the unavailability of free entitlements for pregnant women and sick new-born/ infants under the Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakaram (JSSK) Scheme, in light of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether any alternatives/guidelines put in place or proposed/issued under the JSSK Scheme to provide essential care to the mother and her neonate, in the absence of easy access to institutional deliveries, in light of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic and if so, the details thereof; and(c) the details of the remedial measures taken/being taken by the Government to ensure the eligible beneficiaries receive the benefits of the JSSK Scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a): The entitlements under Janani Sishu Suraksha Karyakram continue to be provided  free of cost to all the pregnant women and sick new borns/ Infants who were accessing the public health facilities during the ongoing  COVID -19 pandemic as well.(b) & (c): Keeping in view the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic and the need for continuous availability of health care services, the Ministry has issued the following Guidance Notes to facilitate the states as follows:- i) Enabling delivery of essential health services during COVID -19 outbreak” dated 14th April 2020  ii) Guidance Note on “Provision of Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn, Child, Adolescent Health Plus Nutrition (RMNCAH+N) services during & post COVID-19 Pandemic” dated 26th May 2020.It has been stated therein that under no circumstances should there be a denial of essential services to women, newborn, children and critical services for women, children and adolescent.
