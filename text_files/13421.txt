                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2266






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.03.2020






Consumers of BSNL and MTNL




Vijay Kumar














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the consumers of mobile phones in rural and urban areas of Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Limited (MTNL) and Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) separately as on date, State/UT-wise; (b) 	the steps taken by the Government to enable these companies to compete with their private counterparts; (c) 	whether some areas in the country do not have telephone and mobile services; (d) 	if so, the details of such areas and the action taken by the Government to provide telephone and mobile connectivity in the said areas; and (e) 	the funds earmarked and spent for providing telecommunication facilities in the said areas and the role played/likely to be played by the private companies in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS,  HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a) 	Circle/Licensed Service Area wise detail of consumers of mobile phones in rural and urban areas of Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Limited (MTNL) and Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) separately as on date is at Annexure-I.(b) 	The Cabinet in the meeting held on 23.10.2019 approved the revival plan for BSNL and MTNL. The revival plan inter-alia, includes the measures to reduce the Staff cost through a Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS) for the employees of age 50 years and above, administrative allotment of spectrum to BSNL and MTNL for providing 4G services, monetisation of assets of BSNL/MTNL, debt restructuring by raising of sovereign guarantee bonds and in-principle approval for merger of BSNL & MTNL.  With implementation of these revival measure, it is expected that BSNL and MTNL will become competitive and start earning profits in coming years.(c) to (e) 	As on 31.12.2019, it is estimated that 27,721 villages (as per census 2011) in the country are not covered by mobile services.  State/Union Territories-wise list of uncovered villages is at Annexure-II.Mobile coverage in the uncovered villages in the country is being provided by the Government and TSPs in a phased manner.Further, following schemes funded by Universal Service Obligation Fund are in progress to provide mobile coverage in the uncovered villages in the country:•	Provision of mobile services in Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected areas: Under phase-I of the project, as on date out of 2355 identified locations, 2343 mobile towers are radiating.  Under phase-II of the project, 2217 tower locations have been identified with an estimated cost of Rs.3215 crore (excluding taxes) to provide mobile connectivity in the LWE affected areas in 10 states. •	354 Uncovered Villages Scheme: To provide mobile connectivity in 354 villages of uncovered border areas, Ladakh and Kargil region and other priority areas. •	Aspirational District scheme: A scheme for provisioning of 4G based mobile service in 502 uncovered villages of aspirational districts of Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh and Rajasthan with an estimated cost of Rs.687 crore has been approved by Digital Communication Commission (DCC) on 20.12.2019.
