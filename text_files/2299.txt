                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2096






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.07.2019






Illegal Groundwater Extraction in Tamil Nadu




S. Venkatesan














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the effective steps taken by the Government under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP); (b) 	whether the Government proposes to provide any special assistance to rural areas of the country with a view to resolve drinking water related problems; (c) 	if not, the reasons therefor; and (d)	the details of assistance provided to various States including Rajasthan in the country under the said programme during the last one year along with the effective steps taken to resolve the drinking water related problems?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FORJAL SHAKTI(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) to (d) While rural drinking Water Supply is a State subject, Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation under Ministry ofJal Shakti provides financial and technical assistance to States forimproving the coverage of piped water supply in rural areas under centrally sponsored National RuralDrinking Water Programme (NRDWP). The financial assistance provided (fundsreleased) to all States including Rajasthan under NRDWP during the last one year, is at Annexure.This Ministry has recently launched ‘Swajal’ programme which is a communitydemand driven, single village, preferably Solar Powered piped water supply schemesin Aspirational districts identified by National Institution for Transforming India(NITI) Aayog which will help in increasing the outreach of drinking water inunderserved rural areas. Further, this Ministry had launched National Water QualitySub-Mission (NWQSM) programme on 22.03.2017 to provide safe drinking water to27544 Arsenic / Fluoride affected rural habitations.Additionally, States can utilize enhanced funds provided under 14th FinanceCommission as well as funds provided directly to rural local bodiesfor improving drinking water supply which is one of the basic amenities.
