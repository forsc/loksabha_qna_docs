                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1544






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2020






Manpower in Food Processing Sector




Lorho S. Pfoze














                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether there is shortage of skilled manpower in the Food Processing Sector in the Country;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether the Government has launched any training programme to meet the shortage of skilled manpower;(d)	if so, the details thereof; and(e)	the funds earmarked for the purpose?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a) to (e):  As informed by Food Industry and Capacity Skill Initiative (FICSI), which works under Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE) and is supported by National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC), there is no reported shortage of skilled manpower in the Food Processing Sector.Further, as informed by Ministry of Skill Development  and  Entrepreneurship(MSDE),  under Skill India Mission, MSDE is implementing a flagship scheme known as Pradhan  Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) with an objective to provide skilling to one crore people under Short Term Training (STT) and Recognition of Prior Learning  (RPL) across  the country for  four years i.e. 2016-2020. The PMKVY scheme is implemented through various training Centres Pan India spanning across 37 sectors, including Food Processing sector.  The scheme can be availed by anyone fulfilling the criteria as per the scheme guideline including Socially backward classes i.e. SC/ST and OBC. As on 17th January 2020, over 0.30 lakh candidates have been trained/oriented, of which over 0.24 lakh candidates   have been certified in Food Processing.As informed by MSDE, the Budget Outlay of PMKVY is Rs.12000 Crores for four years, i.e. 2016-2020.*****
