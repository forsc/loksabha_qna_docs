                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4419






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.07.2019






Water Borne Diseases




Mohanbhai Kalyanji Kundariya














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government has taken note of high prevalence of various waterborne diseases due to intake of contaminated water in the country; (b) if so, the details thereof along with the funds earmarked, allocated and utilised by the States/UTs to tackle the situation during the last three years; (c) whether the Government proposes any awareness and immunization drive against certain waterborne diseases; and (d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b):	As per data from Central Bureau of Health Intelligence (CBHI), the details of cases and deaths due to waterborne diseases in the country; State/UT-wise during the last three years are given in Annexure I.Water borne diseases like Acute Diarrheal Disease, Cholera, Enteric fever, Hepatitis       A & E etc. are caused due to intake of contaminated water and unhygienic food. Government of India has launched Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme (IDSP) with the objective to detect and respond to disease outbreaks due to epidemic prone communicable diseases including outbreaks due to water borne diseases. To prevent the spread such outbreaks, under IDSP States/UTs are provided with additional manpower, training of identified Rapid Response Team (RRT) members for outbreak investigations, strengthening of laboratories for detection of epidemic prone diseases, IT equipment for data entry, analysis and data transfer, and provision of funds for operationalization of the programme. The details funds released to the States/UTs from the year 2015-16 to 2018-19 are given in Annexure II.As reported by the Ministry of Jal Shakti, Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation National Water Quality Sub-Mission (NWQSM) was launched under National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP) to provide safe drinking water to 27,544 arsenic/fluoride affected rural habitations in the country.The details of fund released under NWQSM during the last 3 years are as under:  S. No.	Year	Amount (in Cr.)1	2016-17	814.132	2017-18	2011.553	2018-19	864.66States can utilize up to 5% of funds released to them under NRDWP for water quality monitoring and surveillance.Further, 2% of the NRDWP funds are earmarked for Japanese Encephalitis/Acute Encephalitis Syndrome affected districts of the states (high priority districts).In addition, during March, 2016, with the recommendation of NITI Aayog, an amount of Rs. 1000 Crores was also released to various Arsenic & Fluoride affected States/UTs for installation of community water purification plants.(c) & (d):	Under Universal Immunization Programme (UIP), immunization is being provided free of cost against 2 water borne vaccine preventable diseases i.e. Polio and Rotavirus diarrhoea.
