                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1474






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.07.2019






Examination of Right to Education




Shivkumar Chanabasappa Udasi














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the new draft education policy proposes extending the Right of Children to Free andCompulsory Education (RTE) Act, which currently applies to classes I to VIII, to the entire schoolsystem from pre-school to class XII and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has conducted any survey/review to find out the number of childrengetting free education under the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE), 2009and if so, the details thereof; and(c) whether the Government has received requests from various States for additional funds for theeffective implementation of the said Act and if so, the details thereof and action taken thereon?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): The Committee for the Draft National Education Policy 2019 (Draft NEP 2019) under theChairmanship of Dr. K. Kasturirangan submitted its report to the Ministry on 31st May 2019.The draft NEP report inter-alia proposes the extension of Right of Children to Free andCompulsory Education (RTE) Act, downwards to include up to three years of early childhoodeducation prior to Grade 1, and upwards to include Grades 11 and 12. The Draft NEP 2019has been uploaded on MHRD’s website and also at innovate.mygov.in platform. At this stage,suggestions/comments have been invited from various stakeholders including the public,Government of India Ministries and State Governments. The Government will finalize theNational Education Policy only after considering the inputs/suggestions and comments of allstakeholders.(b) to (c): Samagra Shiksha supports the implementation of all the interventions, including forreimbursement to States/UTs towards section 12(1)(c) of the RTE Act, 2009 as per the normsof the scheme, both through direct programme funding and also in convergence with otherrelevant scheme of the Central and State Government. Accordingly, the annual plans underSamagra Shiksha are prepared by the States and UTs based on their requirements and priority,including for reimbursement towards section 12(1)(c), and this is reflected in their respectiveAnnual Work Plan and Budget (AWP&B). The plans are then appraised andapproved/estimated by the Project Approval Board (PAB) meeting held under theChairpersonship of Secretary (SE&L) in consultation with the States and UTs as per theprogrammatic and financial norms of the scheme and availability of budgetary resources.Accordingly, the PAB approval/estimate against reimbursement to Private Schools and thestatement on number of children admitted/studying under section 12(1)(c) of the RTE Act,2009 are at ANNEXURES. Further, nearly 11.98 Crore children in classes 1 to 8 are availingthe benefit of the education in Government and Government aided schools as per U-DISE2017-18 (Provisional).***********
