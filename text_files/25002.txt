                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

830






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2021






Kuttanad Wetland Ecosystem




Suresh Kodikunnil














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has agreed to grant financial assistance for the implementation of Kuttanad package for development of Kuttanad wetland ecosystem in Kerala;(b) 	if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Government has issued clearance for the said project; and (d) 	if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a)	to (d) Under the Kuttanad and Idukki package approved by the Government in 2008, the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEF&CC) had sanctioned two proposals from the State Government of Kerala i.e. (i)	“Measures for augmenting biodiversity in the backwaters along the bank of Vembanad” during the financial year 2008-09 at a cost of Rs. 37.85 lakhs and (ii) “Eradication & Utilization of Water Hyacinth” during the financial year 2010-11 at a cost of Rs. 79 lakhs.  	Further, Kuttanad wetland is a part of the Vembanad-Kol Ramsar Site. The State Government of Kerala had submitted an integrated Management Action Plan for five years for Vembanad-Kol during the financial year 2017-18. Under the scheme of National Plan for Conservation of Aquatic Eco-systems (NPCA), MoEF&CC had sanctioned an amount of Rs. 140.75 lakhs to the Government of Kerala on 60:40 cost sharing basis during the financial year 2017-18 which includes Rs. 84.45 lakhs as Central share and Rs. 56.30 lakhs as State share. The amount pertaining to Central share has been released to the State Government. *****
