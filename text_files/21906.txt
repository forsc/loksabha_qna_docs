                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3002






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Bidi Workers




S. Jothimani














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)the steps taken by the Ministry to address the plight of Bidi workers;(b)whether the scheme/s of the Ministry are addressing their health, education and well-being concerns;(c)if so, the details thereof; and(d)the States which have implemented such scheme/s?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (IC) FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI SANTOSH KUMAR GANGWAR)(a) to (c):	Ministry of Labour & Employment provides welfare to the Bidi workers through Labour Welfare Scheme (Health, Housing and Education). Under this scheme the following facilities are provided:-i. Healthcare facilities are provided to the beneficiaries through 10 Hospitals and 286 Dispensaries. ii. Housing subsidies of Rs.1,50,000/- (per beneficiary) is provided in three (03) installments at 25:60:15 ratio i.e. Rs.37,500/-, Rs.90,000/- and Rs.22,500/- respectively to the beneficiaries for construction of pucca houses through Revised Integrated Housing Scheme (RIHS). However, RIHS scheme has been converged with the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana from the year     2018-19 as per the suggestion of Expenditure Finance Committee. iii. Financial Assistance for Education is provided to the wards of the workers ranging from Rs.250/- to Rs.15,000/- depending upon their class/courses. (d):		The scheme is implemented across all over States/UTs of India.******
