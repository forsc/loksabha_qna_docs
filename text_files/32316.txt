                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3748






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.12.2021






Olympic Sports Academy in Uttar Pradesh




Brijbhushan Sharan Singh














                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the steps taken by the Government for the development of 46 sports being played at Olympic games;(b) the details of the Sports Academies established/set up in respect of the sports mentioned above, State/UT-wise and sports-wise;(c) whether the Government is contemplating to establish Olympic Sports Academy in Uttar Pradesh;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) if not, the reasons therefor? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS[SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR](a): 	Promotion and development of specific sports disciplines, including those sports being played at Olympic Games, is mainly the responsibility of the concerned National Sports Federations (NSFs). Government supplements the efforts of the NSFs by providing financial assistance to them under the Scheme of Assistance to NSFs for holding national championships, conduct of international events in India, preparation and participation of Indian sportspersons / teams in international sporting events, equipment support, etc. The Khelo India scheme of Government of India aims, inter alia, at strengthening the sports ecosystem to promote excellence in sports across the country.  The Khelo India Youth Games and Khelo India University Games are organized annually, where the technical conduct is benchmarked to international standards and protocols.  Further, under the Khelo India scheme, the Sports Authority of India has set up National Centres of Excellence (NCOE) in 24 disciplines, training centres at State level in 27 sports disciplines and extension centres of Training Centres in 17 sports disciplines across the country.  SAI also regularly conducts National Sports Talent Contests in 19 sports disciplines to identify and nurture sporting talent.  (b) to (e):	‘Sports’ being a ‘State’ subject, the primary responsibility to establish sports academies rests with the State/Union Territory Governments. The Central Government supplements their efforts. However, under the “Support to National/Regional/State Sports Academies” vertical of the Khelo India Scheme, sports academies are accredited and provided financial assistance by this Ministry for training of the Khelo India Athletes. A total of 237 sports academies have so far been accredited across the country, including 10 in Uttar Pradesh. State/UT-wise and sports-wise details of the same are at Annexure.*****
