                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1362






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.09.2020






Allocation of Funds For Educational Institutions




Pradyut Bordoloi














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the role of State Government and local authorities in the implementation of the National Education Policy, 2020;(b) the details of allocation of funds and tentative release for restructure and development of primary institutions, including Early Childhood Care Education(ECCE), State-wise;(c) the details of allocation of funds for establishment and development of higher education institutions (HIE), specifically for under served regions; and(d) the detailed plan of steps required to be taken to achieve the targeted Gross Enrollment Ratio (GER) in higher education ?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a):	The implementation of National Education Policy will require multiple initiatives and actions, which will have to be taken by multiple bodies in a synchronized and systematic manner. Therefore, the implementation of this Policy will be led by various bodies including Union and State Governments, CABE, education-related Ministries, State Departments of Education, Boards, NTA, the regulatory bodies of school and higher education, NCERT, SCERTs, schools, and HEIs along with timelines and a plan for review, in order to ensure that the policy is implemented in its spirit and intent, through coherence in planning and synergy across all these bodies involved in education.(b):	Ministry of Education has allocated funds for the implementation of Pre School Education, under Samagra Shiksha component in the year 2020-21. The details of funds State-wise and UT-wise are annexed.(c):	The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) is aimed at inter-alia correcting regional imbalances in access to higher education, especially in un-served and underserved areas by setting up quality higher educational institutions and upgrading the existing ones. As on 31.08.2020, under RUSA, central funds amounting to Rs 6369.72 crores have been released to States/UTs. Out of these funds, States/UTs have reported utilization of Rs. 3998.27 crores leaving an unspent balance of Rs.2371.46 crores with the States/UTs.(d): 	In order to increase the enrolment in higher educational institutions, various measures have been taken by Government which includes issuing of new UGC regulation for Open and Distance Learning that allows entry of reputed institutions to offer education on the distance mode. Use of ICT technology- SWAYAM portal to reach out of people and allow them to secure good quality education. Encouraging opening of institutions by State Governments through the Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) which aims to achieve equity, access and excellence in higher education. The scheme supports components such as upgrading autonomous colleges to universities, clustering colleges to establish a university, setting up of new professional colleges in un-served and underserved areas as well as providing infrastructure grants to universities and colleges to scale up capacity. Moreover, Higher Education Financing Agency (HEFA) has been established under the Companies Act, 2013 to leverage funds from market to finance improvement in infrastructure in top institutions of education.***
