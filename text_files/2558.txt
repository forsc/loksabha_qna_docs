                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2415






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.07.2019






Medical Check up of Students




Ravindra Shyamnarayan alias Ravi Kishan Shukla













                                                    Ajay (Teni) Misra
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to make the annual medical checkup of the students inaccredited and Government schools keeping in mind the health of school childrencompulsory;(b) if so, whether the Government have issued/proposes to issue any guidelines for all theaccredited and Government schools in this regard and if so, the details thereof; and(c) if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a) & (b): The Ministry of Health & Family Welfare (MoH&FW), Government of India,under the National Health Mission launched the Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK),which envisages Child Health Screening and Early Intervention Services, a systematicapproach of early identification and link to care, support and treatment.RBSK is a programme developed to improve the overall quality of life of children andprovide comprehensive care to all the children attending the Government and Governmentaidedschools as well as pre-school age group across the country. This programme involvesannual screening of children from birth to 18 years of age for four Ds- Defects at birth,Diseases, Deficiencies and Development delays, spanning 30 common health conditions forearly detection and free treatment and management, including surgeries at tertiary level.Children diagnosed with identified health conditions are provided early intervention servicesand follow-up care at the district level.-2-Rule 9.2.24 and 14.6 of revamped Affiliation Bye-Laws-2018 of Central Board ofSecondary Education (CBSE), provides for medical check up in schools. Also, under rule2.4.12 of revamped Affiliation Bye-Laws-2018, every Secondary and Senior Secondaryschool has to appoint a person on full time basis for performing the duties of Counselor &Wellness Teacher.Kendriya Vidyalaya, Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya (JNV) and Central Tibetan SchoolsAdministration provide facilities for regular medical check up of students. In the KasturbaGandhi Balika Vidyalayas (KGBV) run under Samagra Shiksha, there is a provision forempanelment of a doctor to regularly visit the KGBVs for medical check-up of girls.(c): Does not arise.*****
