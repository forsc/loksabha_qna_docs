                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

375






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2022






Decreasing Number of Air Passenger




Ranjanben Dhananjay Bhatt














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the number of air passengers from Delhi and Mumbai is continuously decreasing and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government proposes to take any step to increase the number of air passengers; and(c) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a): The month-wise number of passengers handled by Delhi and Mumbai airports from January 2020 to December 2021 is given in Annexure-I. Delhi and Mumbai Airports witnessed decline in passenger traffic during successive waves of COVID-19. ;(b) and (c):  Various steps taken by the Government to improve air connectivity, are as under:i) Ministry of Civil Aviation has launched Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) - UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik) on 21-10-2016. as a market driven scheme.;ii) Under this scheme, 403 RCS routes connecting 65 airports including 8 heliports & 2 water aerodromes have  been operationalized as on 31.01.2022.;iii) Covid guidelines/protocols for the Airline are framed in consultation with Ministry of Health & Family Welfare (MoHFW) and Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) to boost confidence of the air travellers during COVID-19 pandemic.;iv) The other steps include e-boarding, web check-in, contactless dropping of baggage, etc.
