                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4329






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






Accident Cases on National Highways




Kunwar Danish Ali














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of accident cases on National Highways that came to the notice in the country during the last three years, year-wise; (b) 	whether the road accidents are increasing day by day on express highways in the country; (c) 	if so, the details thereof along with the reasons therefor, State-wise; (d) 	whether all the National Highways fulfil their safety standards as prescribed by the National Highways Authority of India; and (e) 	if not, the details thereof and the action taken in this regard? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS      (SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a)	to  (c)  As per available information, total number of road accidents on National Highways and Express Highways in the country during the calendar years 2016 to 2018 are as under:- Years	Total Road Accidents on National Highways 	Total Road Accidents on Express Highways2016	1,42,359	19762017	1,41,466	21662018	1,40,843	5890 Road Accidents are multi-causal Over speeding, Drunken driving/consumption of alcohol, Driving on wrong side/lane indiscipline, Fault of the driver of motor vehicle, use of mobile phones, Mechanical defect/fault of the vehicle and    lack of knowledge on road signage are the various factors, which can broadly be categorized into human error, road condition/environment and vehicular condition, that lead to accidents on roads. (d)  & (e) National Highways are designed and constructed as per Indian Road Congress (IRC) specifications and Ministry of Road Transport & Highways (MoRTH) guidelines depending on the availability of ROW and site conditions. These codes and guidelines include provisions for Traffic signals, signs for pedestrians and motorists, Road Markings etc.*****
