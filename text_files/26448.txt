                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2233






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.08.2021






Teacher Training




Amar Singh













                                                    Behanan Benny
                                                








                                                    Kumbakudi Sudhakaran
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has taken initiatives for teacher training and orientations for online and remote learning;(b) if so, the details of the same including funds disbursed till date, and if not, the reasons therefor;(c) whether the Government has assessed the need to reform the National Education Policy, 2020 to reflect the realities of online and remote education;(d) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor ;(e) whether the Government has formulated any directives to restructure assessment for students through the virtual medium; and(f) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) to (d): The Department of School Education and Literacy has launched a National Initiative for School Heads'' and Teachers'' Holistic Advancement (NISHTHA)- a capacity building programme for "Improving Quality of School Education through Integrated Teacher Training". It aims to build competencies of all the teachers and school principals at the elementary stage. In view of the COVID-19 pandemic, NISHTHA at the elementary level was started online on the DIKSHA platform from October 2020 by the NCERT. NISTHA will be conducted for secondary/senior secondary teachers and on Foundational Literacy and Numeracy for Primary Teachers in 2021-22. During the year 2020-21, an amount of Rs. 5636.38 lakh has been sanctioned for NISHTHA online.The New Education Policy (NEP)-2020 envisages online dissemination of educational needs in remote areas as well. It can be accessed at https://www.education.gov.in/sites/upload_files/mhrd/files/NEP_Final_English_0.pdf.(e) & (f): Government has issued PRAGYATA guidelines for Digital Education for students, which can be accessed on the following link:https://www.education.gov.in/sites/upload_files/mhrd/files/pragyata-guidelines_0.pdf.*****
