                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3085






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Damage to Antiquities in Red Fort




Su Thirunavukkarasar













                                                    Ajay (Teni) Misra
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it is a fact that heavy damage has been caused by protestors and many priceless antiquities and precious items including several heritage items of archaeological importance have been damaged and missing during attack on Red Fort on 26th January, 2021; (b)	if so, the details thereof along with the estimated loss incurred thereon;(c)	whether the Government has sought a detailed report from the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) on damages and if so, the details thereof along with the action taken by the Government to recover the losses;(d)	whether the Government has recovered all the missing items and if so, the details thereof; and (e)	whether the Government has taken any action against the concerned miscreants and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE & TOURISM(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)	&(b)	During the incident, two brass finials of the dome at Lahori Gate were dislodged from their original   positions. Apart from that, the entrance plaza with turnstile   gates, baggage scanners, Door Frame Metal Detectors (DFMDs), other security   equipment and the booking counter at Lahori Gate were also damaged. (c)&(d)	The monument was inspected by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI). Two brass finials of the dome at Lahori gate, which   were dislodged from their original positions, have been recovered and now  kept in safe custody.  A police complaint has been lodged in this   regard.(e)	A Police Complaint has been lodged by ASI.*****
