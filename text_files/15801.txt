                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4171






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






NH Projects in Odisha




Chandrani Murmu














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of National Highway (NH) projects completed in Odisha during the last three years and the current year;(b)	the details of NH projects along with estimated cost sanctioned for the said State during the said period along with present status thereof;(c)	the details of NH projects which are proposed and pending for approval for the said State;(d)	the time frame set for completion of the pending NH projects;(e)	the hindrances being faced to complete the NH projects in the State within the time limit along with the action taken in this regard; and(f)	the action taken against the agencies which have failed to complete the projects within the stipulated time-frame?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a)	Twenty projects for development of various National Highways having an aggregate length of 1131.70 Kms have been completed in the State of Odisha during last three years and the current year.(b)	The details of NH projects in Odisha along with sanctioned cost during the said period along with present status thereof is attached as Annexure.(c) & (d) Proposals for development and maintenance of National Highways are considered based on their merit, inter-se- priority and availability of funds.(e)    The hindrances being faced is  mainly due to delays in acquisition of land, obtaining    approval for forest diversion,  tree felling, environment clearances, eviction of encroachments, shifting of utilities, etc.  Regular coordination meetings are being held with the concerned authorities of Central/State Govt. to sort out the issues. (f)       The defaulting concessionaires/contractors are being penalized by imposing penalties as     per provisions of contract agreements wherever the delay is attributable to the agency.
