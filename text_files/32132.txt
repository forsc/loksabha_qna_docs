                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3567






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






Poaching Incidents




Chandra Prakash Choudhary














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of tigers killed by poachers in Bandhavgarh Forest area of Madhya Pradesh during the last three years;(b)	whether an Inter-State racket of poaching of wild animals in Bandhavgarh forest was busted by the State Government of Madhya Pradesh recently and if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether the said poaching racket was operating from other States and involved residents of the Wildlife Sanctuary, for poaching of tigers in Madhya Pradesh and if so, the details thereof;(d)	whether the Government is aware that such rackets are also involved in poaching of wild animals in other States, if so, the reaction of the Government thereto; and (e)	whether the Government is considering to conduct any high level inquiry in this regard and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a)	As reported by the State of Madhya Pradesh, two tigers have been poached in last three years in Bandhavgarh Tiger Reserve.(b)	No Sir.(c)	Question does not arise.(d)	The State and Central enforcement agencies in coordination with Wildlife Crime Control Bureau, Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change conduct joint operations based on intelligence inputs to curb poaching incidents.(e)	Question does not arise.*****
