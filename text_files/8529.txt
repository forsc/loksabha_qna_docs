                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2090






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2019






Heritage Sites given to Monument Mitras




Jagdambika Pal 














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has given heritage sites/monuments to Monument Mitras for maintenance and conservation of these sites under ''Adopt a Heritage'' project;(b)	if so, the total number of sites given to Monument Mitras under Adopt a Heritage project and the details thereof, State-wise;(c)	the measures taken to improve the existing facilities; and(d)	the proposed measures taken by the Government to improve the condition of monuments/heritage sites in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE AND TOURISM(PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)&(b)	The Ministry of Tourism, Government of India has launched the “Adopt a Heritage: Apni Dharohar, Apni Pehchaan” project which is a collaborative effort by the Ministry of Tourism, Ministry of Culture, Archaeological Survey of India and State Governments/ UT Administrations for developing tourism amenities at heritage/ natural/ tourist sites spread across India for making them tourist friendly, in a planned and phased manner. The project aims to encourage companies from public sector, private sector, corporate citizens, NGOs, individuals and other stakeholders to take up the responsibility of developing and upgrading the basic and advanced tourist amenities at these sites. They would also look after the Operation & Maintenance of the same. Under the Adopt a Heritage project, 26 Memorandum of Understanding (MoU’s) have been signed and awarded to 12 Monument Mitras for twenty-four (24) sites and two (2) Technological interventions for the purpose of development and operation & maintenance (O&M) of approved tourism amenities at sites spread across India. List of the MoUs signed and awarded are placed at Annexure (c)	Providing basic facilities/amenities (e.g. drinking water, toilet blocks, facilities for physically challenged, pathways, cultural notice boards/signage, vehicle parking, cloak rooms, etc.) to the tourists visiting centrally protected monuments/sites are regular activities which the Archaeological Survey of India undertakes, as per needs. Improvement and upgradation of these public facilities/amenities is a continuous process. Basic public facilities are available at all World Heritage Sites and ASI’s ticketed monuments.(d)	Archaeological Survey of India is responsible for protection, conservation and maintenance of  centrally protected monuments, archaeological sites and remains declared as of national importance in the country which is a continuous process, where essential work is executed as per the requirements of different sites and availability of resources and over all they are in good state of preservation.ANNEXUREANNEXURE REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (a)&(b) OF LOK SABHA UNSTARRED QUESTION NO. 2090 FOR 2.12.2019List of Memorandum of Understanding (MoU’s) signedSN	   Name   of Site	State/ UT	Name   of Agency1	Gangotri Temple   Area & Trail to Gaumukh	Uttrakhand	Adventure Tour   Operators Association of India (ATOAI)2	Trail from   Village Stok to base camp of Mt.
