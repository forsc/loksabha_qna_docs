                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3179






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Restoration of Monuments in Bihar




Sanjay Jaiswal














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of monuments which are declared to be of national importance in Bihar; (b)	whether the Government has drawn any shot-term, mid-term and long-term plans for the conservation, restoration and overall development of the protected monuments in Bihar for posterity and to make them as popular tourist destinations and if so, the details thereof; and  (c)	the details of funds allocated and expenditure incurred on conservation, restoration, scientific preservation and environmental development on each protected monument in Bihar during the last three years including the current financial year?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE & TOURISM(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)	70 monuments /sites have been declared as of National Importance in the State of Bihar.(b)	Conservation, preservation and environmental development work of the protected monuments is taken up through Annual Conservation Programme for all centrally protected monuments / sites, including in the State of Bihar. The said programme is drawn at the beginning of every financial year incorporating the required conservation works considering need of the monuments and as per availability of fund and resources. Conservation Programme also includes provision for visitor facilities like toilets, drinking water, ramps for differently abled, illumination, sound and light shows, pathways, signages, cafeterias, etc. Providing and upgrading such facilities wherever required is a continuous process.
