                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3977






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






Dropout Rate in Universities/IITs/IIMs




A.K.P. Chinraj














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of Other Backward Class, Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe students drop out in all Central Universities, Indian Institute of Technology (IITs) and Indian Institute of Management (IIMs) from 2014 to 2020;(b) whether the Government has conducted any study in regard to the reasons of high dropout rate ofOBC, SC, ST students in these higher educational institutions; and(c) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EDUCATION(DR. SUBHAS SARKAR)(a) to (c) The data of dropped out students of Other Backward Class, Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe category of Central Universities is not maintained centrally. However as per available records, the number of drop out students from 2014 to 2020 in Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) are as under:<pre>S.No.	Institute	No. of Students Drop outOBC	SC	ST1.Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs)*	180	190	1102.Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs)	132	109	80*(for the year 2016-2020)</pre>The drop out/withdrawn is mainly on account of securing seat by the students in other departments / institutions of their choice or on any personal ground. The Government has taken various steps for the welfare of SC/ST students to pursue their higher education such as schemes of ‘waiver of tuition fees in IITs’, grant of national scholarships under Central Sector Scheme, scholarships in Institutes etc.***
