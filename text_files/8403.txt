                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1970






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2019






Mangrove Forests




Geetha Viswanath Vanga














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  the details of the increase, if any, in the Mangrove forests in Andhra Pradesh as compared to other States like Telangana especially in view of the increasing health problems faced by people especially in regions like Amravathi which is emerging as capital of Andhra Pradesh; and (b)   the details of the allocation of funds/sanction of funds in Andhra Pradesh during the last three years and the current year for increasing mangrove forests and the results achieved therein, district-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)   As per the records available with the Ministry and according to the India State of Forest Report (ISFR) 2017, published by the Forest Survey of India, the mangrove cover in Andhra Pradesh has increased by 37 sq. km. in year 2017 as compared to the mangrove cover assessed in year 2015. Details of the mangrove cover in other States are as follows:  (Area in sq. km.)State/UT	Assessment Year	2015	2017	Change w.r.t. ISFR 2015Andhra Pradesh	367	404	37Goa	26	26	0Gujarat	1107	1140	33Karnataka	3	10	7Kerala	9	9	0Maharashtra	222	304	82Odisha	231	243	12Tamil Nadu	47	49	2West Bengal	2106	2114	8A&N Islands	617	617	0Daman & Diu	3	3	0Puducherry	2	2	0Total	4740	4921	181 Further, there are no mangrove forest exists in the Telangana State. In addition, Amravathi is not an identified mangrove site for development of mangroves and there are also no mangrove forests exists. (b) During 2016-17, an amount of ?83.10 Lakh has been released to the State Government of Andhra Pradesh for conservation and development of mangroves. This amount has been carry forwarded during the financial year 2017-18 due to non-utilization during 2016-17. No funds have been released during the financial year 2018-19 and 2019-20 due to non-convened of the State Level Steering Committee (SLSC) meeting by the State Government of Andhra Pradesh.
