                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2149






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.09.2020






Women Jan Dhan Account Holders




 Dr. T. Sumathy (a) Thamizhachi Thangapandian














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it has come to the notice of the Government that the pension of many widows have been discontinued during the lockdown, if so, the reasons therefor;(b)	if not, the details thereof including the number of women currently receiving pension;(c)	whether the Government is aware that 500 rupees paid to women account holders and an advance pension of three months/grant of 1000 rupees for widows under Jan Dhan does not even cover basic sanitation costs of the said beneficiaries; and(d)	if so, whether the Government is formulating steps to ensure their safety and wellbeing and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a)	No decision has been taken to discontinue the widow pension during the lockdown.(b)	Widow pension is provided under Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme (IGNWPS), a component of National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP) of the Ministry of Rural Development to the widows belonging to Below Poverty Line (BPL) households only.  Under IGNWPS, monthly central assistance of Rs. 300 is provided to the widows of age 40-79 years and Rs. 500 is provided to the widows of age of 80 years or higher. During the year 2019-20, 59 lakh widows were covered under IGNWPS.(c)	& (d) During COVID-19  induced lockdown, financial assistance has been provided as ex-gratia @ Rs. 1000/- in two monthly installments (Rs 500 each) to the existing old age, widow and Divyangjan beneficiaries of NSAP under Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Yojana (PMGKY). An amount of Rs.2814.50 Cr has been released to the States/UTs for payment of ex-gratia. ****
