                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

779






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.02.2020






Construction of RamJanki Route




Baidyanath Prasad Mahto














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has any plan for construction of RamJanki route from Ayodhya to Janakpur;(b)	if so, the details and the present status thereof;(c)	the locations likely to be covered under the said route in Champaran region; and(d)	the time by which the work on the said project is likely to be started?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) to (d) This Ministry of Road Transport and Highways had notified the National Highway (NH)-227A which starts from Ayodha/Chhawni, connecting Kalwari, Barhalganj and Barhaj in the State of Uttar Pradesh; Siwan and terminating near Chakia in the State of Bihar. NH-104 further connects NH-227A to Sitamarhi and Nepal border. Further, in Champaran region of Bihar important places namely Kesaria, Chakia, Madhuban are covered by the route. The development and maintenance of National Highways is a continuous process. In this route the work of widening to 2-lane with paved shoulders/2-lane for 55 km length from Ayodha/Chhawni to Chhapia on NH-227A in State of Uttar Pradesh costing Rs. 282 crore and works costing  Rs 1302 crore for a length of 209 km of NH-104 in the State of Bihar have commenced. *****
