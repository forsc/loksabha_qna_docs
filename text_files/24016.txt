                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4986






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2021






Widening of NH in Uttar Pradesh




Shyam Yadav Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  whether the Government has identified the National Highways in Uttar Pradesh where the movement of trucks often leads to heavy traffic jam and if so, the details thereof; and (b) the details of highways selected for conversion from two-lane into four-lane to provide relief from such traffic jams during the last three years and the current year along with the present status thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) The details of National Highways(NH) in UP where movement of trucks often leads to heavy traffic jam is at Annexure – I.(b)The reasons for traffic jams are many like local encroachments, wrong parking, and other miscellaneous reasons other than the reasons of the capacity constraints of existing lane structure of NH. Continuous efforts are being made with the support of local authorities to remove such bottlenecks for smooth and uninterrupted movement of traffic. The details of National Highways selected for conversion from two lane into four lane during the last three years and current year along with the present status are at annexure-II.
