                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2930






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






Supply of Safe Drinking Water




Thalikkottai Rajuthevar Baalu














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the targets of 12th Five year Plan for providing all rural habitations, schools and anganwadis with safe drinking water by December, 2017 have been achieved; (b) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; (c) whether the Government has any plan to provide piped water supply to at least 50 per cent of the rural population at 55 litres per capita per day (lpcd) within the household premises or at a distance of not more than 100 metres from their households, if so, the details and the current status thereof; (d) whether it is true that a large number of States did not prepare States Specific Sectors Policy Frameworks and if so, the details thereof; and(e) if not, the measures taken by the Government for making the States to prepare the policy frameworks in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) & (b) Under erstwhile National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP), as reported by States/ UTs, as on 01.04.2018, out of total 17.25 lakh rural habitations, inter alia covering Government schools and anganwadis, 13.61 lakh habitations were fully covered with provision of 40 litre or more per capita per day (lpcd) and around 3 lakh habitations were partially covered, i.e. with provision of less than 40 lpcd whereas 64,743 habitations were having water quality issues.(c) Government of India has launched Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM), which aims at providing potable water to every rural household through Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC) at service level of 55 litre per capita per day (lpcd) by 2024.As reported, as on 02.12.2019, 18.41% [3.29 Crore] rural households have piped water supply with tap connection.(d) & (e) Based on the ‘National Policy Framework’ each State should prepare State specific Sector Policy Framework (SSSPF). As reported by States, a majority of States have prepared SSSPF, however, rural water supply being State subject, details are not maintained by this Department. ***
