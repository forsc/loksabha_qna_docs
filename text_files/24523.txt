                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

285






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.07.2021






Low Penetration of Crop Insurance




Kotagiri Sridhar













                                                     Chinta Anuradha
                                                








                                                    Pocha Brahmananda Reddy
                                                








                                                     Chandra Sekhar Bellana
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has recently identified 75 aspirational/tribal districts in the country where crop insurance penetration is very low; (b) if so, the details thereof, State-wise; and (c) the measures taken by the Government to ensure an increase in crop insurance penetration among farmers?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE ???? ??? ????? ?????? ?????? (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (c) :  Yes Sir.  A structured awareness campaign ‘Crop Insurance Week/Fasal Bima Saptah’ was initiated by the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare between 1st July 2021 to 7th July 2021 under Azadi Ka Amrit Mahotsav,  an initiative to celebrate 75 years of India’s independence, in all the districts/areas notified under Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)/Restructured Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme (RWBCIS) with special emphasis on 75 aspirational/tribal districts.  The State-wise details of selected 75 aspirational/tribal districts is annexed.   The core focus of the campaign was to increase the awareness and benefits of the scheme, sensitize the stakeholders and to increase overall enrollment of farmers, thereby helping them to reap the benefits of crop insurance with special focus on identified aspirational/tribal districts.  To increase the crop insurance penetration the campaign strategy aimed at: i.	Mobilization of multi-level stakeholders for successful implementation of Crop Insurance Week viz States/UTs, Banks, Common Service Centers (CSCs), Insurance Companies(ICs) including grassroot level association with Agricultural Technology Management Agency (ATMA) functionaries, Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRI), Famer Producer  Organizations (FPOs), Primary Agricultural Credit Society (PACS) etc.ii.	A 360-degree execution plan of all Information, Education and Communication (IEC) activities at the grassroots level through channels such as awareness vehicles, TV shows, print media, digital media,  Out of Home (OOH) advertising, behavioral change communication (Nukkad Nataks), etc.iii.	A uniform communication, key messages, and branding across all kharif 2021 notified locations.  The campaign was launched by the Union Minister for Agriculture and Farmers welfare along with release of PMFBY e-Brochure, FAQ Booklet and a PMFBY/RWBICS Guidebook to assist farmers and ground level coordinators in understanding the basics of the scheme. During the entire duration of the campaign, farmers were engaged through multiple IEC activities with the support of State Government and local level organizations viz Agricultural Technology Management Agency (ATMA) functionaries, Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRI), Farmer Producer  Organizations (FPOs), Banks, Primary Agricultural Credit Societies (PACS), Common Service Centers (CSCs), Insurance Companies (ICs).Following are the grassroot activities undertaken in the above-mentioned States to create widespread awareness and to increase crop insurance penetration during this week:i.	A total of 24 IEC Vans travelled in all 75 blocks of 75 Districts during kharif 2021 informing farmers on benefit of crop insurance, enrolment process and other scheme related information. Close to 500 vans were additionally deployed by Insurance Companies at individual level in other districts.ii.	More than 16 thousand ground level IEC activities viz farmer meeting, village level sensitization activities were reported by various stakeholders. iii.	A total of 5 crore audio calls were sent to farmers of all districts including aspirational and tribal districts. iv.	Distribution of PMFBY print material such as pamphlets, flyers across entire districts and blocks.v.	Special engagement workshop/seminars with Women Farmersvi.	Banners and Wall Paintings vii.	Nukkad Natak done in various villages/ blocksviii.	Display of Hoardings/Posters in Hindi and other regional languages ix.	Awareness and engagement activities on MyGov portal introducing PMFBY Quiz and Crop Insurance Pledge.The Crop Insurance week saw media coverage in close to 230 editions of national and regional newspapers and their e-versions, with state published Print Ads and coverage stories in respective regional newspapers.   The campaign also received a massive 326.61 million impression on its dedicated hashtags #CropInsuranceWeek, #PMFBYWeek on social media.
