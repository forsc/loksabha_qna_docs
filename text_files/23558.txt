                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4466






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






Growth of FPI Sector




Kapil Moreshwar Patil














                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government envisages and is expecting the processed food market to grow to $ 543 billion by 2020, a very high increase from mere $32.2 billion in 2016 and if so, the details thereof;(b)	whether the Government has recently sanctioned 32 projects in food processing sector with an investment of about Rs 406 Cr under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sampada Yojana (PMKSY) which is having a total outlay of Rs 6000 Cr for the implementing period 2016-20; and(c)	whether the Government proposes and has identified more projects to fast track in this sector and if so, the details of the projects under consideration?  






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES (SHRI RAMESWAR TELI) (a):	The Gross Value Added (GVA) of the food processing sector increased continuously from Rs.1.30 lakh crore in 2013-14 to Rs 1.61 lakh crore in 2015-16 and further to Rs. 2.08 lakh crore in 2018-19. The size of processed food market expands along with the increase in the size of GVA of the food processing sector. (b):	The Pradhan Mantri Kisan SAMPADA Yojana (PMKSY) was launched in July 2017 with an allocation of Rs. 6,000 crore for the period 2016-20 (extended up to 2020-21) with 7 component schemes namely; (i) Mega Food Park (MFP) (ii) Integrated Cold Chain and Value Addition Infrastructure (ICC) (iii)Creation / Expansion of Food Processing & Preservation Capacities (CEFPPC) (iv) Infrastructure for Agro-Processing Clusters (APC) (v) Creation of Backward & Forward linkages (CBFL) (vi) Food Safety and Quality Assurance Infrastructure (FTL), and (vii) Human Resource and Institutions. Operation Green was added as the 8th sub-scheme during 2018-19. During the year 2020-21, the Ministry of Food Processing Industries has sanctioned 155 projects with total project cost of Rs. 3305.19 crore and grant of Rs. 1051.34 crore. The scheme-wise projects and grant approved during 2020-21 are indicated as under: Status of projects sanctioned under PMKSY during 2020-21(up to 28.2.2021)                          (Rs. in Crore)Scheme	No of Projects sanctioned	Total Project cost	Total Grants ApprovedBFL	8	84.02	17.99CEFPPC	55	581.73	192.12APC	22	715.68	165.99Operation Greens	1	35.97	13.11Mega Food Park	0	0	0Cold Chain	45	1228.04	371.90Food Testing Laboratories	15	99.26	45.13Skill Development(under Human Resource and Institutions)	9	560.49	245.10Total	155	3305.19	1051.34 (c):	Besides PMKSY, the Ministry is implementing a Centrally Sponsored Scheme- PM Formalisation of Micro Food Processing Enterprises Scheme (PMFME) from the year 2020-21 for providing financial, technical and business support for upgradation / setting up of 2 lakh micro food processing enterprises based on One District One Product (ODOP) approach. The scheme is being implemented for the period 2020-21 to 2024-25 with an outlay of Rs. 10,000 crore.  *****
