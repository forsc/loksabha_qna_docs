                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3235






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.07.2019






Schemes to Prevent Malnutrition




Jugal Kishore Sharma














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the names of schemes being implemented by the Government in Jammu and Kashmir to prevent rising incidence of malnutrition among women;(b)	whether the Government has fixed accountability of anyone to ensure proper implementation of these schemes; and(c)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a):	As per the information received, Government of Jammu & Kashmir is implementing the following schemes under Umbrella ICDS (Integrated Child Development Services) Scheme to prevent rising incidence of malnutrition among women:•	Anganwadi Services: A package of Six (06) Services is provided which inter alia includes Supplementary Nutrition, Immunization and Nutrition & Health Education.•	Scheme for Adolescent Girls (SAG): Under this Scheme, supplementary nutrition is provided to adolescent girls to improve their nutritional status.•	Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana: Under this scheme, to compensate the wage loss and for better nutritional status of women, monetary benefit of Rs. 5000/- is provided to the pregnant women and lactating mothers.•	POSHAN Abhiyaan: The   issue   of malnourishment and aneamia among women is addressed through convergent approach of various departments like Department of Health & Family Welfare, Public Health Engineering (PHE), Rural Development, Consumer Affairs & Public Distribution, Urban Development, Tribal Affairs, Department of Information, etc.(b) 	& (c): The implementation of schemes is monitored through a three-tier system as under: •	State Level, headed by State Mission Director, ICDS, who conducts regular monthly meetings with Officers/Officials of subordinate Offices. Besides, field visits are conducted by the State Mission Director to assess and monitor the progress under various ongoing schemes implemented by the department.•	District Level, headed by District Programme Officer (DPO), who reviews the progress and monitors various ongoing schemes at the district level.•	Block/Project Level, headed by Child Development Project Officer (CDPO), who is assisted by a team of Supervisors. The supervisors are accountable for progress of different schemes. The target/achievements of different schemes are reflected in Monthly Progress Reports (MPRs) formulated by Supervisors. Besides, the Supervisors work in collaboration with different line departments like Health, Panchayat Raj, Education, etc., for smooth implementation of schemes.*******
