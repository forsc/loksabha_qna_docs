                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1431






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2021






Mobile Network Coverage




Rajyavardhan Singh Rathore














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the national and State-wise percentage share of BSNL and other private Telecom Service Providers (TSPs) with respect to mobile network coverage;(b)	 the number of panchayats which do not have mobile connectivity or network coverage in the State of Rajasthan, district-wise and the reasons therefor along with the measure/initiative taken by the Government in this regard; and(c)	 the time by which all rural areas of the country is likely to have hundred percent network coverage and telecom access and the measures taken by the Government in this regard; State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS, EDUCATION AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a)	The percentage share of wireless (mobile) subscribers of BSNL and private Telecom Service Providers (TSPs) in their respective License Service Areas (LSAs) consisting of State/UTs in the country is detailed at Annexure-I.(b)  131 number of Gram Panchayats in Rajasthan do not have mobile connectivity, details of which are given in Annexure-II. Mobile coverage in uncovered villages in the country is being provided by the Government and TSPs in a phased manner. TSPs install new mobile towers and BTSs to improve coverage and/or capacity of their networks based on their techno-commercial assessments.(c)  Department of Telecommunications (DoT) has estimated that as per survey carried out in 2020, around 5,72,551 villages out of 5,97,618 inhabited villages in the country have mobile coverage. State/UT-wise details are given in Annexure-III. 25,067 inhabited villages do not have mobile coverage. Mobile coverage in uncovered villages in the country is being provided by the Government and TSPs in a phased manner. In this regard, the measures taken by Government are inter-alia given below:•	Government has prioritized to reach remote areas of the country such as North-Eastern States, Islands, Himalayan States, Western Border States and more importantly the Left Wing Extremism affected areas in the first phase. •	BharatNet project is being implemented in a phased manner to provide broadband connectivity to the all Gram Panchayats (approx. 2.5 Lakh) in the country. As on 27.01.2021, a total number of 1,51,431 Gram Panchayats (including Block Headquarters) have been made Service Ready under the project.•	Under Left Wing Extremism (LWE) Phase -I scheme, 2343 mobile towers have been installed for provisioning of mobile services. Project has been completed.•	 LWE Phase-II scheme has provision for 2,542 number of towers locations for providing 4G mobile services.•	 A Comprehensive Telecom Development Plan (CTDP) is under implementation for North Eastern Region to provide mobile coverage in uncovered villages, along with National Highways and to strengthen transmission network.•	Scheme for providing mobile connectivity in 354 uncovered villages of Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Bihar, Gujarat, Uttarakhand, J&K, Ladakh, border areas and other priority areas is under implementation.•	A scheme for provisioning of 4G based mobile services in 502 uncovered villages of Aspirational districts of four states Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh & Rajasthan has been finalized.Moreover, the proposal of the Department of Telecommunications (DoT) for proliferation of Broadband through Public Wi-Fi Networks under the framework of Prime Minister’s Wi-Fi Access Network Interface (PM-WANI) has recently been approved by the Government. The proposal will enable proliferation of Broadband internet through public Wi-Fi hotspots which will facilitate broadband access in the country including rural areas.
