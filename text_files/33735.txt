                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

104






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.02.2022






Construction of Warehouses




Chandra Prakash Joshi














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number and capacity of warehouses constructed in the country, State-wise including Rajasthan during the last three years; (b) 	the basis on which decision is taken to construct warehouses in any State; (c) 	whether there is any plan to construct new warehouses in Rajasthan in the near future; (d) 	if so, the details thereof; and (e) 	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E RMINISTER OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY, CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION AND TEXTILES(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) to (e): 	A statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (e) OF THE STARRED QUESTION NO.104 FOR ANSWER ON 09.02.2022 IN THE LOK SABHA.(a):	State-wise details of number and capacity of warehouses constructed  during the last three years by Food Corporation of India (FCI) i.e. F.Y 2018-19, 2019-20, 2020-21 and upto Dec''21 are enclosed at Annexure-I. Additionally, the total number of warehouses in which construction has been made and state-wise storage capacities constructed by Central Warehousing Corporation (CWC) during last three year and current year is enclosed at Annexure-II. (b):	Storage gap assessment is the basis on which decision is taken to construct or hire warehouses by FCI. Storage gap is assessed for conventional godowns in procuring States based on the highest stock levels in last three years and in consuming States on the basis of 4 months (6 months in case of NE regions) requirement of foodgrain under National Food Security Act (NFSA) and Other Welfare Scheme (OWS).Storage gap assessment for construction of Wheat silo proposed under hub & spoke model is based on the principle of distribution of buffer stock in consuming regions as per allocation of these states under NFSA & OWS. The allocation of wheat for 17.75 months and rice for 4 months for states, is considered for assessment of storage gap.FCI continuously assesses and monitors the Storage capacity and based on the storage gap assessment, warehouses are constructed or hired. Further, decision for construction of warehouses by CWC is taken as per the availability of Government land and potential business demand arising from various depositors including FCI.(c) & (d):         Yes, Sir. Creation of additional capacities as per storage gap assessment is proposed for the State of Rajasthan.
