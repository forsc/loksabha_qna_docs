                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3566






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2021






Quality of Communication Services of MTNL/BSNL




Pushpendra Singh Chandel













                                                    Mala Rajya Laxmi Shah
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has formulated any scheme to provide state-of-art communication services of BSNL/MTNL including 5G services, on the lines of private telecom service providers to customers and if so, the details thereof;(b) 	the details of the efforts and expenditure made by the Government for technical and institutional upgradation of BSNL/MTNL during the last five years; (c) 	the number of new customers of BSNL/MTNL during each of the last three years, State/UT-wise along with the number of customers who have surrendered services of BSNL/MTNL during the same period, State/UT-wise including the district of Bundelkhand; and(d) 	the measures taken/being taken by the Government to increase the number of consumers ofBSNL/MTNL?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS, EDUCATION AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a)& (b)	The Government approved the revival plan for Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) and Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Limited (MTNL) on 23.10.2019, which inter-alia includes measures to reduce the Staff cost through a Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS) for employees of age 50 years and above, administrative allotment of Spectrum for providing 4G services with funding through budgetary allocation, monetisation of Land/Building and Tower/Fibre Assets to generate resources to retire debt, meet CAPEX and other requirements, debt restructuring by raising of Sovereign Guarantee Bonds and in-principle approval for merger of BSNL and MTNL.The Government has made fund allocation of Rs. 24,084 Crores for spectrum for 4G services in FY 2021-22. BSNL has invited Expression of Interest ( EoI) on 01.01.2021 for prior registration/Proof of Concept (PoC) from Indian Companies interested in participating in BSNL’s upcoming 4G tender.5G mobile services are yet to be launched in India.(c)	State/UT-wise details of new additions and surrender of Mobile and Landline customers of BSNL/MTNL is at Annexure-I & Annexure-II respectively. Number of connections added /surrendered in last 3 years in Bundelkhand region are as under:<pre>Year	Mobile	Landline	Addition	Surrender #	Addition	Surrender2018-19	244257	N/A	5355	63832019-20	212715		3118	89842020-21 (upto Jan ‘21)	188971		3053	22822</pre># BSNL has informed that it is not possible to find out exact number of mobile customers who have surrendered mobile services as customers use to discontinue mobile services on their own without surrendering the SIM.(d)       BSNL has informed that it is continuously adding mobile subscribers and its subscribers are increasing regularly over last five years. BSNL and MTNL continue to be dominant player in the wireline segment.With the commencement of 4G services on a large scale, BSNL expects to increase number of customers further.
