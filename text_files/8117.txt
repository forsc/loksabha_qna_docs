                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1689






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.11.2019






Houses for Homeless




Malook Nagar














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)    the number of homeless people in the country at present; and(b)    the details of the schemes being implemented at present to  provide houses to the homeless people?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	& (b):	Land and colonization are State subjects. Identification of eligible beneficiaries including homeless is done by States/Union Territories (UTs).  Central Government provides different levels of financial assistance under four verticals of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Urban) [PMAY(U)].  States/UTs have undertaken demand survey under the Scheme for assessing actual demand of housing.  The validated demand reported by States/UTs so far is around 112 lakhs.  Total houseless population in the country as per Census–2011 is 17,72,889 including 9,38,348 in urban areas.In pursuance of Government’s vision of “Housing for All” by 2022, Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA) is implementing the PMAY(U) since 25.06.2015 for addressing the housing requirement in urban areas.  The scheme comprises the following four verticals:S. No.	Vertical	Central Assistance per house1.	In-situ Slum Redevelopment (ISSR)	Rs. 1.00 lakh2.	Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS)	Interest subsidy of 6.5%, 4% and 3% on                                                 housing loan amounts upto Rs. 6 lakh, Rs. 9                                                 lakh and Rs. 12 lakh for beneficiaries                                                 belonging to Economical Weaker Section                                                (EWS)/Lower Income Group (LIG), Middle Income                                                 Group-I (MIG-I) and MIG-II categories                                                 respectively3.	Affordable Housing in                   Rs. 1.50 lakh        Partnership (AHP)	4.	Beneficiary-led individual house        Rs. 1.50 lakh        construction/ enhancements (BLC)	A total 93,00,949 houses have so far been sanctioned under the scheme; out of this 55,40,801 are at various stages of construction and 28,06,465 are completed/delivered.                                     *****
