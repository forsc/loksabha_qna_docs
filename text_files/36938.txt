                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4028






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






Provision of Amenities under Adarsh Smarak Scheme




Jaswantsinh Sumanbhai Bhabhor














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of amenities to be provided under the Adarsh Smarak Scheme along with the progress made thereunder;(b)	the list of protected Government monuments under this particularly in Gujarat;(c)	whether the Government proposes further inclusion and expansion of new tourist places under this scheme;(d)	if so, the details thereof; and(e)	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF CULTURE, TOURISM AND DEVELOPMENT OF NORTH EASTERN REGION(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a)	 Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) has identified 100 Centrally Protected Monuments (CPM) as “Adarsh Smarak” for upgradation of existing tourist facilities/amenities like drinking water, toilets, pathways, ramps, Wi-Fi, cafeteria, interpretation centre, brail signage, illumination, sound and light show, souvenir shop, etc. based on requirement and feasibility on case to case basis.Progress made in this regard is at  Annexure. (b)	 Five (5) CPMs identified in Gujarat, as Adarash Smarak are (i) Rani-Ki-Vav, Patan,  (ii) Champaner monument, Champaner, (iii) Archaeological Remains of a Harappan Port Town, Lothal, (iv) Dholavira and (v) Sun Temple, Modhera. (c)   to   (e)	The improvement and upgradation of public facilities/amenities is a continuous phenomenon and is taken up depending on the availability of resources.
