                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1470






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.07.2019






New Central Universities




Raghu Ramakrishna Raju Kanumuru














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is planning to establish 13 new Central Universities in the country andis allocating Rs. 3600 crores for their infrastructure facilities;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether any such universities are being established in the State of Andhra Pradesh, as per APreorganisation Act, 2014;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) the quantum of funds allocated for the same?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(Shri Ramesh Pokhriyal ''Nishank'')(a)to (e) No, Sir. At present, there is no proposal to set up a new Central University in the country.However, the Andhra Pradesh Re-organisation Act 2014 envisages for establishment of one CentralTribal University each in the State of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana and one Central University inthe successor state of Andhra Pradesh. Further, the Prime Minister announced a package for Bihar on18.8.2015 which includes establishment of a Central University near Bhagalpur at the historic site ofVikramshila University. There is provision of Rs 450 crore and Rs. 420 crore for meeting the firstphase expenditure towards establishment of the Central University and Central Tribal University inAndhra Pradesh respectively.Earlier, the Cabinet had approved the Revised Cost Estimates (RCE) of the 13 new CentralUniversities established under CU Act, 2009 which inter-alia included incurring an expenditure ofRs. 3639.32 crore for recurring expenditure and creation of necessary infrastructure for the periodfrom 2018-19 to 2020-21.*****
