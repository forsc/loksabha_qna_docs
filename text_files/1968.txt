                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

178






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.07.2019






Influx of Chinese Products




Sunil Kumar Mondal














                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether huge influx of Chinese products in the Indian market has affected the domestic manufacturers; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; and(c) 	the corrective steps taken/ being taken by the Government in this regard? 






ANSWER




                                                        ???????? ??? ?????? ?????? (???? ????? ????) THE MINISTER OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI  PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) to (c):	 A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.******** STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (c) OF THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 178 FOR ANSWER ON WEDNESDAY, THE 3RD  JULY, 2019. (a) & (b):		Total imports from China was USD 76.3 billion in 2017-18, which decreased to USD70.3 billion in 2018-19.(c): 		The following remedial steps have been taken in this regard:i.	In products where there has been injury to domestic industry, relief has been provided through anti-dumping duty on 97 products, countervailing measures on 3 products and safeguard duty for 1 product imposed against China. Details of trade remedial measures in force against import from China are placed in Annexure I. ii.	Basic Custom Duty has been increased on several products to protect domestic industry. iii.	Quality control orders are being enforced to check sub-standard imports. iv.	The Government through its initiatives such as Make in India, Ease of Doing Business, Startup India and sectoral schemes/programmes promotes investment in domestic manufacturing and endeavours to improve the competitiveness of domestic industry. *****
