                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4483






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






Education for Physically Challenged Persons




Ashok Kumar Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  whether any special projects have been implemented to provide education to physically challenged persons in the States during the last three years and till date; (b)  if so, the details thereof; (c) the details of the institutes set up for the deaf and the blind particularly, in the backward areas; (d)  the names of the agencies operating institutes for the deaf including the Government agencies;(e)  the details of the project proposals for setting up Non-Governmental Organizations for the welfare of the deaf, State-wise; and(f)  the present status thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT (SHRI KRISHANPAL GURJAR)(a) & (b) No, Sir. Relief to persons with disabilities is a State subject by virtue of Entry 9 of the State list of the Constitution of India. However, the Central government supplements the efforts of the State governments for the welfare of persons with disabilities. The Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities (DEPwD) is implementing various schemes for imparting education and rehabilitation of persons with disabilities, including for physically challenged persons and to create an enabling environment, that provides them with equal opportunities, ensures protection of their rights and enables them to participate as independent and productive members of society. A brief description of the main schemes of the Department are given at Annexure-I.(c) & (d) The Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities is implementing a Central Sector Scheme, namely, Deendayal Disabled Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS) under which grant-in-aid is provided to Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs)   for their projects relating to rehabilitation of persons with disabilities aimed at enabling them to reach and maintain their optimal physical, sensory, intellectual, psychiatric or socio-functional levels.A statement indicating State/UTs-wise details of Specials Schools for Deaf and Blind under DDRS are at Annexure-II and III, respectively. In addition, the Department runs 9 National Institutes for persons with disabilities, of which those specifically dealing with the deaf and the blind are as follows: (i) National Institute for the Empowerment of Persons with Visual Disabilities (NIEPVD), Dehradun, Uttarakhand is working in the field of visual disability. (ii) Ali Yavar Jung National Institute of Speech and Hearing Disabilities (AYJNISHD), Mumbai is working in the field of hearing disability. (iii) Indian Sign Language Research & Training Centre (ISLRTC), New Delhi has been set up specializing in hearing and speech disabilities.Main objectives of these Institutes are as under:i.	To conduct various courses and undertake training for capacity building.ii.	To provide rehabilitation care/services in the relevant field.iii.	To conduct research into various dimensions of the education and rehabilitation in the respective field.(e) & (f) For the Financial Year 2020-21, the Department received three project proposals of special school for deaf of Non-Government Organizations under DDRS, one each from the States of Gujarat, Telangana and Uttar Pradesh. Two project proposals from States of Telangana and Uttar Pradesh were recommended by the Screening Committee.*****
