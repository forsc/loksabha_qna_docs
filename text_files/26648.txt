                                    CORPORATE AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2106






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.08.2021






Active cases in National Company Law Tribunal, Mumbai




Chunni Lal Sahu














                        Will the Minister of





CORPORATE AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the number of active cases pertaining to the State of Chhattisgarh in National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT), Mumbai prior to their transfer to NCLT, Cuttack, case/company-wise;(b)	whether all the cases mentioned in point ‘a’ have been transferred to NCLT, Cuttack, if so, the date-wise separate details of companies;(c)	whether all the cases mentioned in point ‘a’ and ‘b’ have been received by NCLT, Cuttack;(d)	if so, the details thereof along with the date of receipt and name of the company, company-wise; and(e)	whether according to the Section 397/398 of the Companies Act (Companies Act 1956 and 241/242 (Companies Act 2013), the order of NCLT regarding the status of the comapny becomes Null and Void after six months, if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF STATISTICS AND PROGRAMME IMPLEMENTATION; MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF PLANNING AND MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF CORPORATE AFFAIRS(RAO INDERJIT SINGH)(a) to (d):	As per information given by National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT), a total of 91 cases of the State of Chhattisgarh were lying in Mumbai Bench of NCLT for transfer to Cuttack Bench, out of which a total of 62 cases have been transferred and received by Cuttack Bench, and records of 22 disposed of cases and 7 other cases have been ordered for transfer. Company-wise details of the transferred cases and cases under transfer are annexed.
