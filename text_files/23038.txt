                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4108






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






National Health Mission




Bharti Pravin Pawar














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the details of funds allotted and utilised under the National Health Mission (NHM) since its inception, State-wise including North Maharashtra;(b) whether the Government has taken/proposes to take any steps to allocate more funds to strengthen the National Health Mission and make it more effective;(c) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor;(d) the details of Community Health Centres (CHCs) and Public Health Centres (PHCs) established under NHM since its inception, State-wise including North Maharashtra; and(e) the steps taken by the Government to assess the implementation and progress of NHM along with its present status?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a): A statement showing funds released and utilized since the inception of the National Health Mission (NHM), State/UT-wise including Maharashtra is at Annexure -I.(b) & (c): The Government has continued to allocate more funds to strengthen the National Health Mission (NHM). Rs.31,100.00 crore has been allocated to NHM during 2021-22 BE as against Rs. 27,989.00 crore in 2020-21 BE, i.e.  an increase of 11.1%. Further, the National Health Policy, 2017, also envisages the States to increase their health spending to atleast 8% of their budget and two thirds of such expenditure should be on primary care. In addition, as per the Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) under NHM, States are required to increase their State’s Health Budget by atleast 10% annually. (d): It is the State Governments that establish the Public Health Centres (PHCs) and Community Health Centres (CHCs). Support is provided to CHCs and PHCs in the States/UTs under the National Health Mission (NHM). The number of CHCs and PHCs as on 31st March, 2019, State/UT-wise including Maharashtra is at Annexure-II.(e): The working of National Health Mission (NHM) has been regularly assessed, inter-alia, through external surveys such as, National Family Health Survey (NFHS) and Sample Registration System (SRS). Further, Common Review Mission (CRM) also undertake a review of NHM every year. Performance   of   National   Health   Mission   is   also   reviewed   by   the   Ministry periodically including through the Health Management Information System (HMIS), Reproductive and Child Health portal, NIKSHAY portal (for TB patients) and Comprehensive Primary Health Care Portal. To monitor the progress of Ayushman Bharat-Health & Wellness Centres (AB-HWCs), the AB-HWC portal has been created.To improve health infrastructure and services in public health facilities, Government has laid down Indian Public Health Standards (IPHS). These standards are used as the reference points for public health care infrastructure planning and up-gradation in the States/UTs.	In addition, performance audits by the Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) of India and Statutory Audits, Concurrent Audits etc., financial monitoring and management of funds is carried out through Public Financial Management System (PFMS) that track the flow of funds at different levels. The Ministry is also focusing on outcome based monitoring.
