                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4558






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.03.2020






Data on Mental Health




Maneka Sanjay Gandhi













                                                    Kotha Prabhakar  Reddy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the number of people treated for mental illness in Government hospitals during the period from 2015 to 2019, State/UT-wise;(b) whether the Government maintains data on the major mental health issues among people, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor;(c) the amount allocated, disbursed and utilised by States/UTs for providing assistance to mental health patients; and(d) whether the Government is measuring mental illness through IQ levels, if so, the details thereof and progress made therein?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	Public Health and Hospitals is a State subject. The State/UT-wise data regarding number of people treated for mental illness in Government hospitals is not maintained centrally. However, number of patients seeking treatment in Out-patient and In-patients Departments of the three Central Mental Health Institutions, viz. National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Sciences (NIMHANS), Bengaluru, Central Institute of Psychiatry (CIP), Ranchi and Lokopriya Gopinath Bordoloi Regional Institute of Mental Health (LGBRIMH), Tezpur during the period from 2015 to 2019 is at Annexure. (b) to (d):	As per the National Mental Health Survey, 2016, conducted in 12 States of the country, the prevalence of mental disorders in adults over the age of 18 years is about 10.6%. Common mental disorders include depression, anxiety disorders and substance use disorders. Mental illness is not measured through IQ levels. Under the District Mental Health Programme (DMHP), financial assistance upto 83.20 lakhs is provided to each district supported under the programme out of flexible pool for Non-communicable diseases under the National Health Mission, for early detection, management and treatment of mental disorders/illnesses. Under DMHP, funds released and utilized during 2018-19 were Rs. 105.27 cr and 47.56 cr respectively.
