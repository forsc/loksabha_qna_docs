                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1770






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2021






Development of Traditional Industries




Ajay Nishad














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the steps taken under Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of TraditionalIndustries (SFURTI) for development of traditional industries;(b) the list of industrial clusters set up under the saidscheme alongwith the amount of funds allocated for each cluster, State-wise;(c) 	the number of artisans and weavers provided assistance under the saidscheme particularly in Bihar and Uttar Pradesh; and(d) 	the number of clusters targetted to be set up under the said schemealongwith the percentage to which the target has been achieved in thisregard so far?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NITIN GADKARI)(a):  	Under the Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of TraditionalIndustries (SFURTI) Scheme, traditional industries and artisans are organized into clusters to add value to their production and enhance their income by supporting them in:(i)    Up-gradation of infrastructure by developing Common Facility Centers (CFCs) with latest machineries and creation of Raw Material bank,(ii)   Training and skill development of artisans, exposure visits, product development,(iii)  Developing new market linkages, including e-commerce tie ups,(iv)  Partnerships with institutes of eminence for creation of new designs, etc.(b) & (c):	A list showing the number of clusters approved since 2014-15 till 31.1.2021, amount of funds allocated for each cluster and number of artisans & weavers assisted is given at Annexure.  In Bihar and Uttar Pradesh, 3928 and 10624 artisans and weavers respectively are likely to be benefitted under the scheme.(d):   	Between 2014-17 it was proposed to set up 71 SFURTI clusters and during 2018-20, it was proposed to set up 100 SFURTI clusters.    As against this, 335 clusters have been approved, of which 82 clusters are functional.  Rs.785 cr. is committed for these 335 clusters which will benefit about 1.99 lakh artisans.
