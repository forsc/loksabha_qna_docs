                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1731






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Restructured Weather Based Fasal Bima Yojana




Bhagwanth Khuba














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                     (a) 	whether the Government mandated provisions are made available to farmers under the Restructured Weather Based Fasal Bima Yojana; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether any State Governments have sent proposals to the Centre for the crops-wise Restructured Weather Based Fasal Bima Yojana; and (d) 	if so, the details thereof, State-wise including Karnataka?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????            ( SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)  (a)  to (d):  	Yes Sir. With a view to provide protection against likely yield loss due to adverse weather parameters, especially to horticultural crops or crops for which past yield data is not available, weather index based Restructured Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme (RWBCIS) has also been introduced by the Government from Kharif 2016 season in the country alongwith Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY).	Similar to PMFBY, RWBCIS is available to all States/Union Territories and optional/voluntary for the States and they have to select insurance company, notify the crops & areas and provide weather data of current season to settle the claims under the scheme. During 2018-19, 12 States/Union Territories namely, Andhra Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Himachal Pradesh, Karnataka, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand and West Bengal opted for implementation of the RWBCIS.*****
