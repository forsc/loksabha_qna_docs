                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

950






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2021






Subsidy on Petroleum Products




Abhishek Banerjee













                                                    Nusrat Jahan Ruhi
                                                








                                                    Balubhau (Alias Suresh Narayan) Dhanorkar
                                                








                                                    Kani K. Navas
                                                








                                                    M. Selvaraj
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to take steps to reduce excise duty, VAT and dealercommission on petrol/diesel in the wake of Covid-19 pandemic across the country;(b) the percentage of Excise Duty being levied by the Government on petrol/diesel as ondate;(c) whether there is any plan of extending any subsidy/tax relief on petrol and diesel prices, ifso, the details thereof; and(d) the steps taken or being taken by the Government to restrict the petrol and diesel in thecountry in view of fall in prices in crude oil in the international market?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a) to (d): The Union Government levies Central Excise/Cess on petrol/ diesel, while theState Governments levy VAT. The excise duty rates on petrol and diesel have been calibratedto generate resources for infrastructure and other developmental items of expenditure keepingin view the prevailing fiscal position. Decisions to change the rates are taken considering theprevailing fiscal position and need for resources.The current Central Excise/Cess on Petrol is Rs.32.90/litre which includes Basic Excise Dutyof Rs. 1.40, Road & Infrastructure Development Cess of Rs.18, Agriculture andInfrastructure Development Cess of Rs. 2.50 and Special Additional Excise Duty of Rs.11.The current Central Excise/Cess on Diesel is Rs.31.80/ litre which includes Basic ExciseDuty of Rs.1.80, Road & Infrastructure Development Cess of Rs.18, Agriculture andInfrastructure Development Cess of Rs. 4 and Special Additional Excise Duty of Rs. 8. Thetotal excise duty incidence as percentage of retail selling on petrol and diesel is 32.4% and35.4% respectively, as on 16th July 2021.States levy VAT on the total amount of Base price and Central taxes. The VAT rates varyfrom state to state depending on the requirements of the respective State Government. As on22.07.2021, the VAT rates levied by State Governments on Petrol vary from Rs. 4.83 inAndaman & Nicobar Islands to Rs. 29.55 in Maharashtra, Rs. 29.88 in Rajasthan, andRs.31.55 in Madhya Pradesh. As on 22.07.2021, The VAT rates levied by State Governmentson Diesel vary from Rs.4.74 in Andaman & Nicobar Islands to Rs.21.82 in Rajasthan.Prices of petrol and diesel have been market-determined with effect from 26.06.2010 and19.10.2014 respectively. Since then, Public Sector Oil Marketing Companies (OMCs) havebeen taking appropriate decisions on pricing of petrol and diesel on the basis of internationalproduct prices and other market conditions. The OMCs have increased and decreased pricesof petrol and diesel according to changes in international prices and rupee dollar exchangerate. The Average price of Indian Basket of Crude oil has gone up from $19.90/bbl in April,2020 to $63.40/bbl in April, 2021 to $71.98/bbl in June, 2021.x-x-x-x-x
