                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4833






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2019






KVKs in Jharkhand




Jayant Sinha














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total number of Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) in Jharkhand;(b)	the details of the services offered by KVK, district-wise;(c)	the details of the total funds made available to each KVK and percentage of total funds provided to the farmers; and(d)	the details of the notable successes achieved in the KVKs in Ramgarh and Hazaribagh districts of Jharkhand?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) & (b):	There are 24 Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) in the State of Jharkhand. The district-wise details of services offered by KVKs of Jharkhand are given at Annexure-I.(c)	The details of funds made available to each KVK of Jharkhand and percentage of expenditure incurred on the KVK activities for the benefit of farmers are given at  Annexure-II.(d)	The recent notable successes achieved by KVK Ramgarh and KVK Hazaribagh are given as under:KVK- Ramgarh:•	Successfully tested the performance of technology options by laying out on-farm trials in farmers field on various crops under different thematic areas viz crop varieties; eco-friendly/ organic plant protection for insect- pest in brinjal; bio-intensive method for pest control in tomato; cultivation of sweet potato with good agricultural practices; cropping pattern of maize-cowpea for higher yield and profit during kharif season in upland areas. •	Demonstrated high yielding varieties of pulses crops like pigeon pea, chickpea, lentil and horse gram with increase in yield upto 20% over farmers practices and in oilseed crop the increase in yield has been recorded up to 33% over farmers practice whereas in vegetable crops demonstrations the increase in yield up to 27% over farmers practices.•	Decreased the disease infection from 17% to 5 % in goatry after organizing health and vaccination camps. •	 Increased the summer vegetable cultivation in rice fallow areas from 50 ha to 250 ha and rural youth are earning Rs 56000.00 /ha by watermelon, cucumber cultivation. •	Increased the judicious use of fertilizers for essentials plant nutrients.•	Increased the adoption of drought tolerance paddy varieties; wilt resistance tomato and brinjal variety and use of pro-tray and plastic mulching for off- season vegetable cultivation. •	Increased the drip irrigation area from 10 ha to 300 ha with plastic mulching for cucurbits, tomato and capsicum cultivation.•	Provided the quality planting material and cereal seed, vegetable seed through seed sale counter in KVK.KVK-Hazaribagh:•	Successfully tested the performance of technology interventions by laying out on-farm trials in farmers field on reducing the cost of production of paddy crop IR 64 (DRT-1) through Resource Conservation Technology (RCT); weed management practice on chickpea; assessed different intercrops like beetroot and French bean with cauliflower for high profitability; application of insecticide against management of mango shoot gall psylla; integrated management of yellow mosaic disease in moong bean; application of potassium and boron on growth and yield of carrot;  efficacy of Deltamethrin, Amitraz and Ivermectin in treatment of ecto-parasitic infestation in crossbreed cattle;  improved dual purpose backyard poultry birds among rural women; balance supplement for increasing of hemoglobin formation and body weight in women; value addition of tomato when available in plenty to increase the income of farm women.•	Demonstrated production potential of new varieties of crops. In pulses like pigeon pea increase in yield was up to 41% and in chickpea up to 37%; In oilseed demonstrations on groundnut the increase in yield was up to 45%; in paddy crop, the increase in yield was recorded up to 33%; demonstrations conducted on vegetable crops like potato, broccoli, onion; garden pea and tomato shown an increase in yield up to 64%. •	Produced 915 Quintals improved seed of paddy, wheat, maize, pulses, oilseeds and horticultural crops. •	Provided Kadaknath birds to 39 farmers @ 50 birds per farmer which increased their average income up to Rs. 40000.00/ farmer.•	 Provided mushroom spawn to 429 farmers for mushroom production helping income generation of Rs. 5000 - 6000/month/farmer.•	Provided training to as many as 242 women farmers who started scientific crop production, nutritional garden, mushroom production, piggeries, poultry and horticulture crops production. •	Provided training to farmers and farm women on value addition of mushroom pickle, mango pickle, aonla pickle, mix vegetable pickle etc.*******
