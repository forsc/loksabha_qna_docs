                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

584






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2021






Schemes in Rajasthan




Ranjeeta Koli













                                                     Imtiaz Jaleel Syed
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of schemes being run by the Government in Bharatpur and Alwar districts of Rajasthan; and (b) the quantum of funds to be spent on these schemes?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) & (b) Water resources development & management are planned, funded, executed and maintained by the State Governments themselves as per their own resources and priorities.  In order to supplement the efforts of the State Governments, Government of India provides technical and financial assistance to State Governments to encourage sustainable development and efficient management of water resources through various schemes and programmes.           Central Ground Water Board is implementing the Aquifer Mapping and Management (NAQUIM) Programme as a part of the Ground Water Management and Regulation Scheme throughout the country. In the State of Rajasthan, Bharatpur and Alwar districts have already been covered under the NAQUIM programme.Atal Bhujal Yojana (ATAL JAL) - Central Sector Scheme with World Bank assistance is being implemented from 1st April, 2020 with focus on community participation and demand side interventions for sustainable ground water management in identified water stressed areas of seven States including Rajasthan. In Rajasthan, the Scheme is being implemented in parts of 38 blocks of 17 districts including Rajgarh block of Alwar district. A tentative allocation of Rs.1189.65 crore has been made for implementation of the Scheme in Rajasthan of which Rs.4.95 crore has been released.Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission – Har Ghar Jal and Swachh Bharat Mission (Grameen) in rural areas of the country including Bharatpur and Alwar districts of Rajasthan. Further, water being a State subject, the planning, implementation and operation & maintenance of water supply schemes lies with State Government, as such, district-wise allocation/ release is not done by this Ministry.  *****
