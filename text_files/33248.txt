                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

587






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2022






Status of Health of Children in NER




Vincent H Pala














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of children under the age of five years in North-Eastern States of the country, State-wise; (b) the number of children with full immunization under the age of five years in NER, State-wise; (c) the number of malnourished children under the age of five years in NER, State-wise; (d) the number of stunted children under the age of five years in NER, State-wise; and (e) funds allocated for child health care to North-Eastern States under Central Government Schemes during the last five years, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) Based on the “Report of the technical group on projected population, India and States 2011-2036, National Commission on Population and Office of Registrar General of India”, the estimated numbers of children under the age of five years as on 1st March 2021 in North-Eastern States of the country are as follows:S. No.	Name of the State	No. of Under 5 Children1	Arunachal Pradesh	1,10,3762	Assam	30,13,6983	Manipur	2,27,8804	Meghalaya	2,36,7365	Mizoram	87,5526	Nagaland	1,57,8247	Sikkim	48,7448	Tripura	2,93,112(b) A child is considered fully immunized if the child has received 1 dose of Bacillus Calmette Guerin (BCG), 3 doses of Diphtheria, Pertussis and Tetanus toxoid (DPT), 3 doses of Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) and 1 dose of Measles within 1 year of age. Number of children fully immunized in North East Region as per HMIS portal is as follows:S. No.	Name of the State	No. of Children with Full Immunization1	Arunachal Pradesh	14,7842	Assam	4,43,9623	Manipur	21,4314	Meghalaya	46,5355	Mizoram	12,0676	Nagaland	11,9457	Sikkim	5,4448	Tripura	34,526Data Source - HMIS (from April 2021 to December 2021 as on 20 January 2022)(c) and (d):According to NFHS-5 (2019-21), the percentages of malnourished children under five years of age (classified as stunted, wasted and underweight) in the North-Eastern Region is placed as below:S. No.	States	Children under 5 years who are stunted (height-for-age)* (%)	Children under 5 years who are wasted (weight-for-height)* (%)	Children under 5 years who are underweight (weight-for-age)* (%)1	Arunachal Pradesh	28.0	13.1	15.42	Assam	35.3	21.7	32.83	Manipur	23.4	9.9	13.34	Meghalaya	46.5	12.1	26.65	Mizoram	28.9	9.8	12.76	Nagaland	32.7	19.1	26.97	Sikkim	22.3	13.7	13.18	Tripura	32.3	18.2	25.6* Below -2 standard deviations, based on WHO standard(e) State wise details of Central release (in Crore) under Reproductive and Child Health (RCH) Flexible Pool including Child Health [including Routine Immunization, Pulse Polio Immunization and National Iodine Deficiency Disorder Control Program (NIDDCP)] under National Health Mission (NHM) for the North Eastern States from 2016-17 to 2020-21 is placed below:(Rupees in Crore)S.
