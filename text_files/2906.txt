                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2668






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.07.2019






Availability of Foodgrains




Gitaben Vajesingbhai Rathva














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether it is a fact that during the Eleventh Five Year Plan, there had been 3.3 per cent annual increase in the availability of foodgrains, if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether benefits of this increased availability of foodgrains have reached the common consumer; and (c) 	if not, the reasons therefor and the Government’s reaction thereto?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a): Stock position of foodgrains and the percentage increase in availability of foodgrains (taking 1st April of each year as base date) during 2007-2012 is as under:                                                                                (Figs. in Lakh MTs)Stock as on	Rice (Including  Paddy in terms of Rice)	Wheat	Total	% of increase over previous year01.04.2007	131.71	45.63	177.34	-01.04.2008	138.35	58.03	196.38	10.7301.04.2009	216.04	134.29	350.33	78.3901.04.2010	267.13	161.25	428.38	22.2701.04.2011	288.20	153.64	441.84	3.1401.04.2012	333.50	199.52	533.02	20.63It can be seen in the above table that there has been an annual increase of more than 3.3 percent in availability of foodgrains (taking 1st April of each year as base date) except for the year 2011, when the stock was increased by 3.14 percent against the preceding year. ..........2/--2-(b):  Details of allotment and offtake of foodgrains (wheat & rice) during 2007- 08 to 2011-12 is as under:                                                                              (figs. In Lakh MTs)Year	ALLOTMENT	OFFTAKE	Wheat	Rice	Total	Wheat	Rice	Total2007-08	137.79	306.87	444.66	122.47	252.13	374.602008-09	183.95	274.70	458.65	148.86	246.74	395.602009-10	313.84	306.23	620.07	223.87	276.41	500.282010-11	321.83	363.03	684.86	230.69	299.65	530.342011-12	324.77	384.20	708.97	242.68	320.54	563.22It may be seen that the allotment and offtake had been steadily increasing from 2007-08 to 2011-12, thus indicating that the consumers have been benefitted under PDS.(c): 	Does not arise in view of (b) above.***
