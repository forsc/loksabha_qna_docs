                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

516






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.12.2021






Internet Service in Bihar




Dulal Chandra Goswami














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is framing any concrete plan to enhance internet facility in rural areas of the country including Bihar; (b)	 if so, the details thereof; (c) 	the steps taken by the Government to provide better internet facility in rural areas in the State of Bihar including Katihar district; and (d) 	the number of villages connected with internet in Katihar district till date?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS (SHRI DEVUSINH CHAUHAN)(a) to (d) 	Internet facility is provided in rural areas by the Government and the Telecom Service Providers (TSPs) through wireless mobile and fixed wireline connections in phased manner. As per data provided by Telecom Service Providers and field units of Department of Telecommunications, it is estimated that out of 5,97,618 inhabited villages (as per Census 2011) in the country, mobile wireless coverage has been provided in 5,72,551 villages. In Bihar 38,828 inhabited villages have mobile internet coverage including 1306 in Katihar district. 	In addition to this, in its endeavour to provide high speed internet/broadband connectivity, BharatNet project is envisaged in all the Gram Panchayats (GPs) and villages of the country including Bihar. In Katihar district, out of 235 GPs, a total of 233 GPs have been made service ready.
