                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1420






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.09.2020






Storage for Agricultural Products




T.N. Prathapan














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is aware that farmers in the country are facing difficulties caused by lack of proper storage facilities for agricultural products; (b) 	if so, the measures taken by the Government to solve this issue; (c) 	the details of Government established storage facilities for agricultural products across the country, State-wise; and (d) 	whether the Government has any plan to build new storage facilities in this financial year and if so, the details thereof, State-wise including the fund allocation for this?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (d):	 Government is promoting scientific storage facilities for storage of agricultural products by farmers in rural areas of the country. Government is promoting Agricultural Marketing Infrastructure (AMI) scheme, which is a sub-scheme of Integrated Scheme for Agricultural Marketing (ISAM), under which assistance is provided for construction/ renovation of godowns/ warehouses in the rural areas of various States to enhance the storage capacity for agriculture produce. AMI is a demand driven scheme and no state-wise allocation is made. However, the State-wise number of godowns/warehouses assisted under AMI sub-scheme is at Annexure-I.   In addition to above, Warehouse Infrastructure Fund (WIF) of National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) envisages extension of low cost loans to Public and Private sectors for construction/modernization of warehouses, silos, cold storages, cold chain infrastructure. State-wise details of projects sanctioned under WIF is at Annexure-II. The Government has approved a new central sector scheme for Financial Facility under Agriculture Infrastructure Fund of Rs. 1,00,000 Crore to provide a medium-long term debt financing facility for investment in viable projects for post-harvest market infrastructure   including    warehousing    facility and   community farming assets through Contd…2/--2-interest subvention  and financial support. So far, under the scheme NABARD has received a total of 3055 proposals including that of proposal for warehouses from Primary Agricultural Credit Societies (PACs) through state cooperative banks in 22 states for which in-principle sanction of Rs. 1568 crore has been accorded. The details are at Annexure-III.The Government is implementing Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH) under which financial assistance is provided for various horticulture activities including setting up of cold storages. MIDH is demand/entrepreneur driven scheme. Details of State-wise cold storages sanctioned under MIDH since 2017 to till now are at Annexure-IV. Central Warehousing Corporation (CWC) plans to create storage capacity of 142471 MT with its own funds. The State/Union Territory wise details of construction plan of CWC for financial year 2020-21 is at Annexure-V.
