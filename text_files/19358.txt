                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

902






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Shortage of Doctors in Rural Areas




Arvind Kumar Sharma













                                                    Alok Kumar Suman
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government has taken note of the fact that there is shortage of doctors and other health professionals in rural India, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether it is a fact that the Government has taken measures to address the shortage of doctors and to correct this ratio in Government healthcare centres operated in the country including Bihar particularly in rural areas, if so, the details thereof during the last two years and the current year, district-wise;(c) whether the Government has identified such areas in Bihar including district Gopalganj where there is acute shortage of doctors and medical facilities, if so, the details thereof;(d) the details of steps being taken by the Government to ensure better availability of specialist doctors in the country including Bihar; and(e) the other steps taken by the Government to provide CGHS wellness centers in Bihar including district Gopalganj?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a)	: Public Health and Hospitals being a State subject, all the administrative and personnel matters, including that of recruitment of doctors and other health professionals in rural areas, lies with the respective State Governments. However, as per Rural Health Statistics (RHS) 2018-19, State/UT wise details of availability of doctors and other health professionals and shortage thereof at Primary Health Centres (PHCs) as well as in Community Health Centres (CHCs), are annexed.(b)	: The shortage of doctors in public health facilities varies from State to State depending upon their policies and context. In order to meet the shortage of doctors in rural areas and to improve doctor-patient ratio, the States including Bihar, are encouraged to adopt flexible norms for engaging doctors for public health facilities.  Financial support is also provided to States for providing performance-based incentives, hard area allowance, providing accommodation and transport facilities in rural and remote areas, etc for engaging doctors in the public health facilities. Also, States are advised to put in place transparent policies of posting and transfer, and ensure rational deployment of healthcare professionals. State/UT Governments are impressed upon from time to time to fill up the vacant posts including those  of doctors.(c)	: As stated above, Public Health and Hospitals being a State subject, the responsibility to ensure availability of doctors and medical facilities in all districts/parts of the States, lies with the respective State Governments.(d)	: Government of India has taken various steps to optimize the number of specialists in the country – which inter-alia includes: increasing the number of seats in PG level at various medical educational institutes/medical colleges across the country; States to adopt flexible norms for engaging specialists for public health facilities by various mechanisms like ‘contracting in’ and ‘contracting out’ of specialist services under National Health Mission. State/UTs have also been allowed to offer negotiable salaries to attract Specialists including flexibility in strategies such as ''You quote, we pay". (e)	: CGHS is in operation in Bihar at Patna, Muzafarpur, Chhapra, Gaya and Darbhanga. Presently there is no proposal under the consideration of the Government to open new CGHS Wellness Centres in any other place in Bihar including Gopalganj.
