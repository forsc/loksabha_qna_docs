                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1934






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.09.2020






Evacuation of Indian Nationals Stuck Abroad




P.P. Chaudhary














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has received requests from the families of the Indian nationals stuck abroad;(b) if so, the details of action taken thereof;(c) whether special flights were arranged for the evacuation of Indian nationals stuck in countries hit by COVID-19 and if so, the details thereof;(d) whether fare was charged from the passengers and if so, the details thereof; and(e) the details of the costs incurred in such special missions during lockdown, if any?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a), (b) & (c):  Yes, Sir. The Government has received requests from families of Indian nationals stranded abroad. Requests were also registered in various Indian Embassies/Missions by the Indian nationals stranded in various countries. The Government has, therefore, undertaken the Vande Bharat Mission which is aimed at repatriation of stranded and distressed Indian nationals in various parts of the world due to the COVID-19 pandemic. As per information provided by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), till 31.08.2020, more than 11 lakh Indians (excluding land border crossings) have returned to India under the Vande Bharat Mission.The Vande Bharat Mission consists of special repatriation flights operated by Indian carriers and chartered flights operated by both Indian and foreign carriers. As per information provided by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), till 31.08.2020, a total of 5817 inbound flights have been operated under the Vande Bharat Mission. Out of these, 2292 flights have been operated by Air India Group while the remaining have been operated by private Indian carriers and foreign carriers. (d) Fares were charged from the passengers based on country of origin and class of travel.(e) The Ministry of Civil Aviation has not incurred any cost for the Vande Bharat Mission.
