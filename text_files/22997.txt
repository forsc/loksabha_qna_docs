                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3983






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Vaccination Doses




Anto  Antony













                                                    Manish Tewari
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the details of the people having received the first dose of the vaccine as on 1st of March 2021, State/UTwise;(b) the details of people who have received the second dose of the vaccine as on 1st March 2021, State/ UT-wise;(c) the details of number of doses that have been bought for India and the number exported, countrywise;(d) the number of cases of severe adverse event following immunization or death attributable to vaccination as on 1st March 2021; and(e) whether the Government has decided on the pricing of the vaccine for the private sector and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b): The National Expert Group on Vaccine Administration for COVID-19 (NEGVAC) has prioritized Health Care Workers, Front Line Workers, persons aged 60 years and above, and those aged between 45 to 59 years with 20 identified comorbidities for COVID-19 vaccination. The State/UT-wise details of 1st and 2nd dose as on 1st March 21 is at Annexure 1.(c): As on 15th of March 2021, a total of 1860 lakh doses of COVID-19 vaccines have been bought for India. As on 15th March 2021, a total of 5.86 crore doses of COVID-19 vaccines have been exported to 71 countries as grant, commercial sales by manufacturers and as part of international agreements of manufacturers with the multi-country COVAX facility. Country-wise details of the same is at Annexure 2.(d): As on 1st March 2021, no case of severe adverse event or death following immunization has been causally linked to COVID-19 vaccination based on the current evidence.(e): The COVID-19 Vaccines are currently not authorized by the Central Drugs Control Organization (CDSCO) for sale in open market in the country, therefore, no such price has been decided for private sector.ORThe COVID-19 Vaccination in private sector is available at a ceiling cost of INR 250 per dose per person.
