                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5049






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2021






New Ship Manufacturing Units




Sougata Ray














                        Will the Minister of





PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has any proposal to set up new ship manufacturing units in the country;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether the Government has any proposal to train the youngsters for the production of big vessels under Atmarnirbhar Bharat; and(d) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) & (b) Cochin Shipyard Limited, a public sector shipyard under the administrative control of Ministry of Ports, Shipping and Waterways, through its wholly owned subsidiary ‘Hooghly Cochin Shipyard Limited (HCSL)’ is currently setting up a modern shipbuilding facility at Nazirgunge, West Bengal at an estimated project cost of Rs. 169.76 crore. The facility is expected to be operationalized in the first quarter of the financial year 2021-22. HCSL targets to construct various types of vessels like Ro-Ro Vessels, River-Sea Cargo Vessels for bulk, liquids, containers, Passenger Vessels, other watercrafts for the inland waterways.(c) & (d) Cochin Shipyard Limited has commissioned a new Marine Engineering Training Institute viz. “Vigyana Sagar” which was dedicated to the nation by the Hon’ble Prime Minister of India Shri Narendra Modi on February 14, 2021. “Vigyana Sagar” is a new campus outside CSL factory premises with ultra-modern class rooms and accommodation facilities. The campus will enhance the scope of Marine Engineering Training to a higher level in line with the standards stipulated by the Directorate General of Shipping, Government of India and will also host associated skill development courses related to ship building and ship repair to equip the students to respond to the ever increasing needs of the country and contribute to the maritime sector.                                              ****
