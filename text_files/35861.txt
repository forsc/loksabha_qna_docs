                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    POWER






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2751






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Implementation of UDAY




Rajashree Mallick














                        Will the Minister of





POWER


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of villages that have been electrifiedunder Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Gram Jyoti Yojana (DDUGJY) , State- wise including districts of Odisha;(b) 	the number of households electrified under thesaid yojana, State -wise including Jagatsinghpur district of Odisha;(c) 	the number of States which have adopted Ujwal DISCOM Assurance Yojana (UDAY) to help converting DISCOMs into profit-making bodies; and(d) 	the current status of the implementation ofUDAY in different States?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF POWER AND NEW & RENEWABLE ENERGY (SHRI R.K. SINGH)(a) :		The Government of India launched the Deendayal Upadhyaya Gram Jyoti Yojana (DDUGJY) in December, 2014 for rural electrification works across the country. As reported by the States, all the inhabited un-electrified villages as per census 2011 stand electrified on 28th April, 2018 across the country under DDUGJY. A total of 18,374 villages were electrified under the scheme. State-wise number of inhabited un-electrified census villages electrified across the country including Odisha State, are at Annexure-I.(b) :		State-wise details of BPL Households covered under DDUGJY from 2015 to 2019 are at Annexure-II. A total of 84,242 households (81088 BPL and 3154 APL households have been electrified under DDUGJY (including RE) in Jagatsingpur district of Odisha...........2.- 2 -(c) :		Ujwal DISCOM Assurance Yojana (UDAY), a scheme for financial turnaround of Power Distribution Companies (DISCOMs) was launched by the Government on 20-11-2015 with the objective to improve operational and financial efficiency of the State DISCOMs.  A total of 32 States / UTs joined UDAY Scheme out of which 16 States i.e. Assam, Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Meghalaya, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Telangana & Uttar Pradesh signed UDAY MoUs for Operational as well as Financial turnaround while the rest of the States/ UTs joined for only operational turnaround. UTs of Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh have been counted as one State only as the signed UDAY MOU is for single State. States / UTs of Odisha, West Bengal, Delhi & Chandigarh did not join the scheme.(d) :    		UDAY was launched with an overall aim of operational and financial turnaround of State owned Distribution Utilities (DISCOMs) through efficiency improvements and financial restructuring in Generation, Transmission and Distribution Sectors. As a result, State Power Distribution Utilities have reported an overall improvement which include (i) Reduction in Aggregate Technical & Commercial (AT&C) losses from 23.70% in FY 16 to 20.93% in FY 20 and (ii) Reduction of Average Cost of Supply (ACS) – Average Revenue Realised (ARR) gap from Re.0.48 per kWh in FY 16 to Re.0.30 per kWh in FY 20. The details of the performance of the States on AT&C loss and ACS-ARR gaps are enclosed as Annexure-III and Annexure-IV respectively.*************
