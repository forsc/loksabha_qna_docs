                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM

UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

127






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2021






Creation of Employment at Tourist Destinations




Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao













                                                    Gowdar Mallikarjunappa Siddeshwara
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of TOURISM be pleased to state:(a)	whether it is a fact that the Union Government has taken note of large-scale scope for the creation of employment at tourist destinations and has undertaken a serious homework to exploit the resources;(b)	if so, the details thereof, including the names of tourist destinations where the local people have actually benefitted out of the initiative taken by the Union Government;(c)	whether it is also a fact that the Union Government has some plans to identify new tourist destinations in each State of the country for promotion and development as potential tourist destinations with basic tourism facilities based upon specially drawn-up master plans in the next three years; and(d)	if so, the details thereof, State-wise, particularly in respect of Maharashtra?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF TOURISM                                (SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a)&(b): Yes, Sir. The Ministry of Tourism started the Destination Based Skill Development training programme at 7 iconic sites i.e. TajMahal at Agra, Humayun Tomb, Red Fort, QutubMinar at Delhi, Mahabodhi Temple at Bihar, Colva Beach at Goa and Kaziranga at Assam in FY 2019-20 to train, upgrade and sensitize the local people residing near these tourism sites and destinations.This programme continued in Financial Year (FY) 2020-21 and upscaled to more than 150 new destinations. However, due to Covid-19 pandemic this programme took place at 44 destinations. The list of destinations is given at Annexure-I. This programme is continued during the current FY 2021-22. So far this programme has taken place at 24 destinations. The list of destinations is given at Annexure-II.(c)&(d) :Development and identification of new of tourism spots, is primarily the responsibility of the concerned State Government/ Union Territory Administration including the State Government of Maharashtra. However, Ministry of Tourism, Government of India under its schemes of SwadeshDarshan, PRASHAD and Assistance to Central Agencies, provides central financial assistance to State Governments/ Union Territories/ Central Agencies for development of tourism related infrastructure and facilities to provide an enriching tourism experience to visitors. The projects under the schemes are identified for development in consultation with State Governments/ UT Administrations and are sanctioned subject to availability of funds, submission of suitable
