                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1216






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2021






E-Shram Cards to Tea Garden Labourers




Naba (Hira) Kumar Sarania














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)whether the Government proposes to enact any new law for tea garden labourers in the country and the workers to whom e-SHRAM cards are being issued;(b)whether e-SHRAM cards are being issued to tea garden labourers also;(c)if so, the details State/district-wise number of workers to whom the said cards have been issued so far; (d)the details of the facilities which the tea garden labourers could get through these e-SHRAM cards;(e)whether the Government has adopted any new methods for providing the said cards to tea garden labourers who are semi-literate; and(f)the number of tea labourers working in different States of the country as per the Ministry and the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a) to (f):	On 26th August, 2021, Ministry of Labour & Employment has launched e-SHRAM portal which is a National Database of the Unorganised Workers.  It has been made available to the States/ UTs for registration of unorganised workers such as Building and Other Contd..2/-::  2  ::Construction workers, domestic workers, agricultural workers, self- employed workers, street vendors, small shopkeepers and other similar groups of Unorganised workers on e-SHRAM portal.   The tea garden workers, who are not members of ESIC/EPFO and in the age group of 16 to 59 years, are also being registered on the e-SHRAM Portal. The registration on e-SHRAM portal is based on self-declaration. There is no educational qualification prescribed for registering of unorganised worker including tea garden workers on eSHRAM portal. The State-wise details of the tea garden workers is annexed. All eligible registered unorganised workers including tea garden workers are entitled to get benefit of an accidental insurance cover of Rs 2.0 Lakh for a year free of cost through Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY).*                                                       *******
