                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE

                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1079






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2021






Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)




Prataprao  Jadhav













                                                    Devendrappa Y
                                                








                                                    Annasaheb Shankar Jolle
                                                








                                                    Umesh G. Jadhav
                                                








                                                    Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik
                                                








                                                    Shrirang Appa Barne
                                                








                                                    B. Y. Raghavendra
                                                








                                                    Sudheer Gupta
                                                








                                                    Bidyut Baran Mahato
                                                








                                                    Prathap Simha
                                                








                                                    Sanganna Amarappa Karadi
                                                








                                                    Rebati Tripura
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	the present status of Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) being implemented by the Government and challenges faced thereof while implementing b)	the quantum of funds allocated and utilised for the States/UTs for implementation of RBSK since its inception, State/UT-wise, year wise;c)	whether this programme has been able to achieve its objective if so the details thereof and if not, corrective steps taken in this regardd)	the number of Early Intervention Centres established in district hospitals and health centres in the country during the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise;e)	the mechanism put in place by the Government for regular monitoring of the programme to ascertain its efficacy; andf)	the steps taken by the Government to ensure that district hospitals across the country are fully equipped with technology and medicines to treat birth related defects deficiencies and diseases?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)a)  to c)  The objective of Rashtriya Bal Swasthaya Karyakram, (RBSK) is early identification and early management for selected health conditions - 4 D’s i.e. Defects at Birth, Developmental delays, Diseases and Deficiencies. Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW) is implementing RBSK in all States/UTs under National Health Mission.As reported by States/UTs, 109.9 crore children have been screened, 8.04 crore children have been identified with selected health conditions and 3.11 crore children have been provided secondary/tertiary care in F/Y 2013-14 till F/Y 2020-21 under RBSK since its inception.Some States and UTs faced implementation challenges on account of closure of Anganwadis and Schools in view of the prevailing pandemic situation.The details of State/UT-wise Approvals and utilisation for RBSK from F.Y.20l4-15 to 2020-21 as reported by States/UTs is given at Annexure 1.d)	As reported by States/UTs, 315 District Early Intervention Centres (DEICs) have been made functional. State/UT wise cumulative detail is given at Annexure 2.e)	The programme is regularly monitored under National Health Mission through regular review of quarterly reports, annual reviews during the appraisal process of Programme Implementation Plans, field visits and periodic meeting with State nodal officers and Common Review Missions.f)	The responsibility for strengthening of district hospitals primarily lies with the States/UTs. Support is provided under the NHM to States/UTs for infrastructure, essential equipment and required human resources at District Hospitals through DEICs including capacity building for comprehensive management of selected health conditions with special focus on children with developmental delay.
