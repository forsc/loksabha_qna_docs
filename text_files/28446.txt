                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TRIBAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

88






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2021






Development of Adivasi Community




Mahesh Sahoo














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware that 37 per cent of population in Pallahara in Angul district and Kalahandi districts of Odisha belongs to Adivasi community;  (b) if so, the details thereof whether the Government has any plan for social and economic development of these people;  (c) whether there is any proposal of special package for the economic development of these people; and  (d) if not, the reason thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        (a): As per census, 2011 the percentage of population of Scheduled Tribes (STs) in Palalahada (Palhada) sub-district in Anugul district of Odisha is 47.97 % and percentage of population of Scheduled Tribes (STs) in Kalahandi district of Odisha is 28.50%. (b): Government is implementing Tribal Sub-Plan (TSP)/Schedule Tribe Component (STC)/Development Action Plant for STs (DAPSTs) for overall development of tribal people across the country. Besides, Ministry of Tribal Affairs, 40 Central Ministries / Departments have been obligated by NITI Aayog for earmarking certain percentage of their total Scheme allocation every year as STC funds for tribal development. STC funds are spent by different Central Ministries / Departments under their schemes for various development projects relating to education, health, agriculture, irrigation, roads, housing, drinking water, electrification, employment generation, skill development, etc. for accelerated socio-economic development of Scheduled Tribes in the country including Odisha.  Ministry of Tribal Affairs provides additive to these initiatives by way of plugging critical gaps. A list of schemes/ programmes being implemented by Ministry of Tribal Affairs for the welfare of the tribal people in the country is placed at Annexure.State Governments are also supposed to earmark funds Tribal Sub Plan (TSP) in proportion to ST population (Census 2011) in the State with respect to total State Plan. (c) to (d): The State Government of Odisha has constituted 2 agencies i.e., Kutia Kandha Development Agency at Lanjigarh and Paudi Bhuyan Development Agency at Jamardihi under the Conservation-cum- Development (CCD) plan of the Scheme of Development of Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs) for focused development of the vulnerable tribal groups belonging to Kutia Kandha PVTG (Kalahandi district) and Paudi Bhuyan PVTG (Palhada sub-division of Angul district). During 2020-21 an amount of Rs.1202.00 lakh has been released to the State Government of Odisha for the implementation of various activities which are intended to benefit PVTGs including the population in Palhada and Kalahandi districts of Odisha. An amount of Rs. 3558.91 crore in 2020-21 and Rs. 3910.02 crore in 2021-22 (till 18.11.2021) has been released for the socio economic developments of tribal communities in the State of Odisha (including Palhada Sub-district and Kalahandi district) under various schemes of central Ministries/Departments including Ministry of Tribal Affairs (MoTA) under Tribal Sub Plan (TSP).
