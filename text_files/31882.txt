                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

310






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






Tiger Deaths




Vellalath Kochukrishnan Nair. Sreekandan














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it is a fact that a number of persons have lost their lives in the country during the year 2020 in tiger attacks;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether it is also true that the number of tiger deaths also increased during the said year;(d)	whether it is a fact that there was a decline in the number of poaching incidents during 2020 but the number of tiger deaths was quite high during the year; and (e)	if so, the reasons therefor and the steps taken by the Government to arrest the increasing deaths of tigers in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI. BHUPENDER YADAV)(a), (b), (c), (d) & (e):-A statement is laid on the Table of the House.***STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a), (b), (c), (d) & (e) OF THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *310 ON TIGER DEATHS DUE FOR REPLY ON 20.12.2021(a) & (b) Yes Sir, as reported by States, 44 persons have died due to tiger attacks in the year 2020.(c)	As per information received from States, there are 106 tiger deaths in the year 2020.  There is no drastic increase or decrease in tiger mortality as compared to previous years.(d)	Yes Sir, there are 14 poaching incidents in 2020 as compared to 27 in 2019.  The details of cause-wise tiger mortality in the year 2019 and 2020, as reported by States are at Annexure-I.(e)	The National Tiger Conservation Authority has issued generic guidelines for formulating a Security Plan for each tiger reserve which is operationalized in the overarching Tiger Conservation Plan, legally mandated under section 38 V of the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972.***
