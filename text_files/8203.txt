                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1840






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.11.2019






Piped Water Supply




Veena Devi













                                                    Jyotirmay Singh Mahato
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of number of households to which piped drinking water is supplied by the Government and the percentage of people and households covered under it, State-wise including West Bengal;(b) the total funds allocated/released and spent under the said scheme during the last three years and the current year and the funds likely to be spent for achieving this target, State-wise including West Bengal; and(c) the time by which the piped drinking water is likely to be supplied to all rural households?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a)As reported by States/ UTs, as on 21.11.2019, about 18.41% [3.29 Crore] rural households have tap connections with piped water supply (PWS).State-wise details are at Annex–I. However, the details of people provided with household connections in rural areas are not maintained by this Department.(b) Details of the total funds allocated, released and utilizedunder erstwhile National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP) during the last three years and under the Jal Jeevan Mission(JJM) in the current year, areat Annex–II. (c) JJM aims at providing potable drinking water at the service level of 55 litre per capita per day (lpcd) to every rural household of the country through Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC) by 2024.***
