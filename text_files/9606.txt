                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2991






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2019






Campaign to Protect Whale Sharks




Faizal P.P. Mohammed














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has launched any campaign  in Lakshadweep in order to protect  endangered  whale sharks;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether  Wildlife Trust of India (WTI) has been  able to rescue these endangered  animals by spreading  awareness  through these campaigns; and (d)	if so, the  details  thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)and (b) The Government has not launched any campaign in Lakshadweep specifically for whale sharks. However, fund has been provided to Lakshadweep for the conservation  of  a project “Marine  Dolphins (all marine mammals) of Lakshadweep  waters” under the component ‘Recovery programme   for saving critically endangered species’ under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of ‘Development of Wildlife Habitat’ .(c) and (d) As per information received from the Wildlife Trust of India (WTI), WTI in partnership with Gujarat Forest Department started the mass awareness campaign in Gujarat to save whale sharks during 2004 with an aim of stopping the killing of whale sharks.  Since 2004 till date, 745 whale sharks have been rescued and released by fishermen of Gujarat. Similar campaign has also been initiated by WTI in Kerala and Lakshadweep.***
