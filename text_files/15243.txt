                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3880






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2020






Detention Centres in Assam




Nusrat Jahan Ruhi














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total number of detention centres in Assam including new detention centres which are yet to be constructed and made operational;(b) the total capacity for each of these detention centres; (c) the number of people died in the National Register of Citizens (NRC) detention camps/centres in North-Eastern States specially in Assam during the last one year;(d) whether National Human Rights Commission has visited these camps in the last one year to monitor the living condition of the detainees;(e) if so, the details thereof; and(f) if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (f):	There are 06 (six) Detention Centres in Assam having capacity of 3,331 persons with the following details:SI.No.	Name of Detention Centre	Capacity1	Tezpur	7972	Silchar	4793	Dibrugarh	6804	Jorhat	6705	Kokrajhar	3356	Goalpara	370	Total	3,331-2-L.S.US.Q.NO.3880 FOR 17.03.2020	A Detention Centre at Matia, Goalpara with a capacity of 3000 is under construction.	There is no National Register of Citizens (NRC) detention camp/center is State of Assam.  10 inmates of the 06 Detention Centers in Assam, where declared foreigners/convicted foreigners are kept, died in different hospital from 1st March, 2019 to 29th February, 2020.	During last one year, from 1st March, 2019 to 29th February, 2020, three teams of National Human Right Commission (NHRC) have visited Detention Centers in Assam and have interacted with declared foreign nationals. *****
