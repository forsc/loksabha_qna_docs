                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2041






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.07.2019






Availability of Safe Drinking Water




Chandra Prakash Joshi














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the Government plan to provide safe drinking water throughout the year in the country at present; (b) 	the number of villages where safe drinking water is not available for 365 days even today; (c) 	whether the Government has made any scheme to provide safe drinking water in every village of the country for 365 days; and (d) 	if so, the number of villages to which drinking water has been provided under this scheme during the last three years?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FORJAL SHAKTI(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a to (d)Water supply is a state subject. Under National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP), Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation (DDWS) under Ministry of Jal Shakti provides financial & technical assistance to State Governments for rural drinking water supply. State Governments are vested with the power to plan, design, approve and implement rural drinking water supply schemes for improving the coverage of drinking water throughout the year in rural areas. DDWS maintains information in terms of habitations and not in terms of villages. Under NRDWP, coverage of drinking water is maintained in terms of Fully Covered habitations [i.e. getting more than 40 litre per capita per day (lpcd) safe drinking water], Partially Covered habitations (i.e. getting less than 40 lpcd safe drinking water) and Quality Affected habitations (i.e. getting drinking water from unsafe sources). As per the information furnished by the States on Integrated Management Information System (IMIS), 58037 rural habitations do not have access to safe drinking water.Under NRDWP, the Ministry supplements the effort of State Government for improving the coverage of safe drinking water in every rural habitations of the country. 	The number of habitations which have been provided safe drinking water under NRDWP during last three years is at Annexure.
