                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1704






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2021






Vacant Multi-Storey Houses




Mala Rajya Laxmi Shah













                                                    Pushpendra Singh Chandel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether a large number of newly constructed/under-construction multi-storey houses are vacant in metropolitan cities of the country including Delhi;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether there is any plan to make housing available to low and middle income groups at concessional rates; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                              (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)&(b):	Land’ and ‘Colonization’ are State subjects. Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs does not maintain data of real estate projects and details thereof. (c) &(d)	Presently, there is no scheme to make housing available to low and middle income groups at concessional rates.  However, in order to provide every urban poor household with a house by 2022, this Ministry is implementing Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Urban)-PMAY-(U). Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS) is an important component under this scheme which provides interest subsidy on home loans to Economically Weaker Section (EWS)/ Lower Income Group (LIG) and Middle Income Group (MIG) households. Cumulative assistance provided under CLSS as on 01.02.2021 isas under : Cash Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS)Criteria	ProgressUpto 30 sqm for EWS (annual household income up to Rs. 3,00,000), 60 sqm for LIG (annual household income from Rs.300001 to Rs. 6,00,000), 160 sqm for MIG-I (annual household income from Rs. 600001to Rs.12,00,000), and 200 sqm for MIG-II (annual household income from Rs. 1200001 to Rs. 18, 00,000)	EWS/LIG	MIG	Central Subsidy Amount 	No. of beneficiary households	Central Subsidy Amount	No. of beneficiary households	Rs. 20,776.65 Crore	8,62,914	Rs.11,991.46Crore	4,92,590	Further, scheme of Affordable Rental Housing Complexes (ARHCs) has recently been launched to utilize Government funded vacant houses of EWS/ LIG category to provide homes to urban migrants/ poor at affordable rent by public agencies through a concessionaire for a period of 25 years. Under ARHC Scheme, Private/Public entities are also incentivized to develop such complexes with single/ double bedrooms and dormitory type accommodation to be rented out at affordable rates to EWS/ LIG category of urban poor/ migrants.                                                         *****
