                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1937






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2021






Road Accidents




Jaskaur Meena














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the numbers of deaths due to road accidents on National Highways during each of the last three years and the current year in the country;(b) 	whether the said number is increasing every year and if so, the reasons therefor;(c) 	whether the Government proposes to undertake road safety audit of National Highways in the country to minimize the number of road accidents; and(d) 	if so, the details thereof and the time by which such proposal is likely to be implemented? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS (SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) & (b)     As per the information available with the Ministry, the total number of deaths due to road accidents on National Highways (including on Expressways) in the country during the last five calendar years i.e. 2016 to 2020 are given in the table below :- Year	Total Persons killed on National Highways	% age increase / decrease over previous year2016	52,075	-2017	53,181	2.122018	54,046	1.632019	53,872	-0.322020	47,984	-10.93The major causes of accidents on the National Highways (NHs) are vehicle design and condition, road engineering, over-speeding, drunken driving/ consumption of alcohol & drug, driving on wrong side, jumping red light, Use of mobile phone, etc.(c)     Ministry has issued guidelines dated 09th November, 2020 for improving the road safety through road safety audit at all stage (design stage, construction stage and O&M stage) by engaging independent Road safety expert.(d)     National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) conducts Road Safety Audits regularly as per Concession/ Contract Agreement for all stages (Design/Construction/O&M) of National Highways (NHs) through Independent/ Third party Safety Consultants. The recommendations of the Auditors are being complied by Project Implementation Units at regular interval. Till now more than 18,096 Km of Roads has been audited by the Ministry.*****
