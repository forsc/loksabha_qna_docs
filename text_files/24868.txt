                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

808






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2021






Immunization of Children




Aparajita Sarangi














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the details of the compulsory vaccines being administered to children in the country as part of their immunization;(b) whether the mandatory vaccination and immunisation programmes for children were disrupted due to the COVID-19 pandemic and if so, the details thereof;(c) the details of the data indicating the decline in the number of children vaccinated for each mandatory vaccine specific to each quarter since January 2020 to June 2021, State/UT-wise;(d) whether the Government has devised an alternate route of vaccine delivery to ensure proper implementation of immunisation programme; and(e) if so, the details thereof and the action taken to increase access to vaccines in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a): Under Universal Immunization Programme (UIP) Vaccines provided Nationally are Bacillus Calmette Guerin (BCG), Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV), Hepatitis B vaccine, Pentavalent vaccine, Inactivated Poliovirus Vaccine (IPV), Measles Rubella (MR) vaccine, Diphtheria, Pertussis & Tetanus (DPT) vaccine, Tetanus and adult diphtheria (Td) vaccine and at Sub-National level are Rotavirus Vaccine (RVV), Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine (PCV) and Japanese Encephalitis (JE) vaccine. Vaccination under UIP is not compulsory.(b) & (c): The utilization of Immunization services got affected due to the COVID-19 pandemic and there was slight decline in Immunization Coverage as reported by State/UTs in HMIS portal. The details of the data indicating the decline in the number of children vaccinated for each vaccine as reported by States/UTs in HMIS,  State/UT-wise, Quarter wise since January 2020 to June 2021, is placed at ANNEXURE-I-IV(d) & (e): Many steps have been taken by the Government to ensure that amidst the crisis, immunization programme is properly implemented and access to immunization services remains uninterrupted, these include:•	Development and dissemination of National guidelines to safely continue immunisation services during the COVID-19 pandemic, wherein Birth dose vaccination to be continued at all health facility with delivery points & Immunization services were being provided to beneficiaries who reach the immunization sessions sites in containment zones of lockdown areas due to COVID-19 with measures to prevent COVID-19 spread like physical distancing, use of mask, hand sanitizer etc.•	Appropriate communication material has been developed and shared with the States and UTs for addressing vaccine hesitancy and strengthening routine immunization with due precautions, during COVID-19 pandemic. •	Supply chain of vaccines and other logistics has been ensured during COVID-19 pandemic.•	Further Unvaccinated/Missed children were identified and were covered in Intensified Mission Indradhanush (Intensified Immunisation Drive) from February 2021 to March 2021.
