                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3030






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






Khelo India Centre




Gautam Gambhir













                                                    Rajan Baburao  Vichare
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the salient features of ‘Khelo India’ centres;(b) whether it is a fact that ‘Khelo India’ centres have been opened in different districts of the country with an aim of establishing India among top ten countries in the olympic games to be held in the year 2028;(c) if so, the details and number of ‘Khelo India’ centres established in different districts of the country including NCT of Delhi and the district of Thane of Maharashtra;(d) the details of the availability of trainers and equipment facility in the district level ‘Khelo India’ centres and the talented players selected so far, district-wise in Maharashtra; and(e) the steps taken by the Government to establish more such centres in the country including NCT of Delhi?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF YOUTH AFFAIRS & SPORTS { SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR }(a) As a part of the Government’s vision to strengthen the sports ecosystem in the country at the grassroots level, a cost-effective sports training mechanism has been developed wherein the former Champion Athletes would become coaches and mentors, so that their expertise and experience would be properly utilised for nurturing of athletes at the grassroots level and to ensure a sustained source of income for them. The grant to each Khelo India Centre (KIC) is provided for remuneration of the former champion athlete as a coach, support staff, purchase of equipment, sports kit, consumables, and participation in competition and events.(b) and (c) Yes, Sir. As on date, 478 Khelo India Centres have been approved in 375 districts of 29 States/UTs, details of which, including National Capital Territory of Delhi and Thane district of Maharashtra, along with the sports disciplines for which training is imparted, are at Annexure.(d) Selection of talent pools as well as hiring of coaches and procurement of sports equipment at the Khelo India Centres is done by the respective State/UT Government. Under the Khelo India Scheme, this Ministry provides each KIC with a non-recurring grant of ? 5.00 lakhs initially for upgradation of existing facility, field of play, sports equipment and consumables, etc. In addition, ? 5.00 lakhs per year is given as recurring grant each year for the next 04 years to meet the recurring expenditure such as procurement of sports equipment, consumable sports kit, etc. and remuneration of past champion athletes engaged as coach/mentor.(e) The Khelo India Scheme is a demand-driven scheme under which proposals when received from the State/UT Governments and other eligible entities are analyzed in this Ministry and then sanctioned according to the parameters of the Scheme subject to their completeness, technical feasibility and availability of funds under the Scheme. Accordingly, based on the proposals formally received in this Ministry and their technical evaluation, 7 KICs have been approved in the National Capital Territory of Delhi.****
