                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4805






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2021






Budget for CCTV Cameras




Krishna Pal Singh Yadav














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total number of railway stations where CCTV cameras have been installed as per the budgetary announcement;(b) the State-wise details thereof;(c) whether the Government proposes to use these cameras to prevent the entry of unauthorised persons into the passenger reservation centres of railways ticket area; and(d) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMERCE & INDUSTRY AND CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) & (b) Closed Circuit Television (CCTV) Cameras have been provided at 686 stations.  State wise details are appended.(c) & (d) Online monitoring through Close Circuit Television (CCTV) cameras is done at Passenger Reservation centre as well as other areas of major railway stations. If any unlawful/suspicious activity is observed, immediate action is taken.  Presently prevention of entry of persons is not done by using CCTV monitoring.  However, surveillance on persons indulging in suspicious /objectionable activities is maintained and intervention is made as and when the need arises. ******
