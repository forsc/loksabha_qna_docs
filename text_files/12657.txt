                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1435






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2020






Promotion of Animal Husbandry and Dairy Industry




Ranjeeta Koli














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether there is ample scope for promotion of animal husbandry and dairy industry in Rajasthan;(b)	if so, the details thereof along with the details of the study conducted in this regard; and(c)	the efforts made by the Government keeping in view the above potential and the progress achieved due to the efforts made in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER            THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING (DR. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a) to (c)  Yes, Sir. There is ample scope for promotion of animal husbandry and dairy industry in Rajasthan. As per 20th Livestock Census-2019, there are approximately 13.94 million cattle, 13.69 million buffaloes, 7.90 million sheep, 20.84 million Goats and 0.15 million Pigs in Rajasthan. In order to complement and supplement the efforts of the States/UTs including Rajasthan for promotion and development of animal husbandry and dairying sectors, the Government of India is implementing various schemes, namely, Rashtriya Gokul Mission, National Programme for Dairy Development, Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme, Dairy Processing and Infrastructure Development Fund, Supporting Dairy Cooperatives and Farmer Producer Organizations engaged in dairy activities, National Livestock Mission and Livestock Health & Disease Control.	The milk production in Rajasthan is increased from 16.93 million tonnes in 2014-15 to 23.67 million tonnes in 2018-19 and artificial insemination is increased from 32.90 lakh in 2015-16 to 44.07 lakh in 2018-19. The egg production in Rajasthan is increased from 13,202 lakh in 2014-15 to 16,616 lakh in 2018-19.*****
