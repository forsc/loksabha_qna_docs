                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

940






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2021






Virological and Epidemiological Research




Shashi Tharoor














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of universities that are currently offering specialty virology and epidemiology courses along with the number of students enrolled in these courses; (b)	the number of institutes that are dedicated to virological and epidemiological research in India; (c)	whether the Government has set up any new institutes in these fields during the last five years, if so, the details thereof; (d)	whether the Government considers that the number of such courses and institutes are adequate to cater to the study warranted in the emerging fields of virology and epidemiology, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; (e)	whether the Government intends to increase the number of courses and the number of institutes in these fields and if so, the details of the proposals under consideration; and (f)	if not, the reasons thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH ANDFAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARTI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a):	09 Universities/Institutes currently have been offering virology and epidemiology courses as per details given below: -S.N.	Name of Universities/ Institutes	Students enrolled1.	Savitribai Phule Pune University	313 ( from 15 batches 2005-2020)2.	Utkal University Bhubaneshwar	50 (in last 3 years)3	Sree Chitra Tirunal Institute of Medical Sciences and Technology Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala	291 (in last 20 years)4.	Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR)	58 (in last 3 years)5.	CMC, Vellore, Tamil Nadu	2 (2019-20)6.	Amity University, Uttar Pradesh	20 (2019-20)7.	Central University of Tamil Nadu, Tiruvarur	15 (2019-20)8.	Manipal Academy of Higher Education, Manipal	12 (2019-20)9.	Sri Venkateswara University, Tirupathy	40 (2019-20)Apart from the above, the Institutes of Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) an Autonomous Body under the Department of Health Research are also conducting PhD programmes in virology and epidemiology affiliated to the local (state) universities.(b):	The Government has set up 124 Virus Research and Diagnostic Laboratories (VRDLs) under the Department of Health Research’s Scheme ‘Setting up of nation-wide network of laboratories for managing epidemics and natural calamities’ in the Government Medical Colleges and Central Research Institutes. In addition, 14 ICMR institutes and 03 Universities namely Amity University, Uttar Pradesh, Manipal Academy of Higher Education, Manipal and S.V.U. College of Sciences, Tirupati are also conducting virological and epidemiological research.(c):	78 Virus Research and Diagnostic Laboratories (VRDLs) have been set up in the Government Medical Colleges and Central Research Institutes during the last five years. Details are given in the Annexure.d), (e) & (f):	42 new Virus Research and Diagnostic Laboratories (VRDLs) are proposed to be set up in the Government Medical Colleges and Central Research Institutes across the country during the 15th Finance Commission period from 2021-22 to 2025-26 under the Department of Health Research’s Scheme ‘Setting up of nation-wide network of laboratories for managing epidemics and natural calamities”Besides, National Centre for Disease Control (NCDC) had started the India Epidemic Intelligence Service (EIS) training Programme in 2012 to strengthen applied epidemiology training of frontline health workforce of central, state and local municipal health departments. This is a two-year competency-based training with emphasis on field epidemiology skill development while providing service in public health programmes. 	Till date EIS Officers have responded in more than 150 infectious disease outbreaks in 28 states and emergency situations including natural and manmade disasters. They have evaluated 50 disease surveillance systems at subnational level, leading to improvement of the systems through actionable recommendations.*****
