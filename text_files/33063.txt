                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LAW AND JUSTICE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

518






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2022






Vacancies in Indian Legal Services




Talari Rangaiah













                                                    Geetha Viswanath Vanga
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





LAW AND JUSTICE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	whether the Government has taken note that nearly 50 percent posts in Indian Legal services are lying vacant, if so, the reasons therefor;b)	whether the Government has undertaken consultation with concerned agencies, like DoPT, UPSC and SSC in this regard, if so, details thereof; andc)	the steps being taken by the Government to expedite the recruitment process and filling up these vacancies in a time-bound manner?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF LAW AND JUSTICE (SHRI KIREN RIJIJU)(a) to (c) – Presently around 64 out of 157 posts in the Indian Legal Services cadre are vacant, which works out to 41% of the total cadre strength in the Indian Legal Services cadre.  DPC proposals for filling-up vacancies falling under promotion quota out of these vacancies are regularly taken up with UPSC, the nodal authority to recommend appointments.  Necessary steps to expedite filling-up of these vacancies expeditiously are carried out on an ongoing basis.
