                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2664






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

14.12.2021






Welfare for PwDs




Pradeep Kumar Singh














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the funds released to various States for the welfare of Persons with Disabilities (PwDs) during the last three years in the country, year-wise specially in the State of Bihar;(b)	the details of the new schemes formulated for the welfare of persons with disabilities; and(c)	the steps taken by the Government to increase job avenues for the PwDs across the country?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SUSHRI PRATIMA BHOUMIK)(a)	Relief to the persons with disabilities is a State subject by virtue of entry ‘9’ of the State list of the Constitution of India. However, the Central Government supplements the efforts of the State Governments for the welfare of persons with disabilities. The Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities implements various Central Sector Schemes for the welfare of Persons with Disabilities through various implementing agencies includes its institutes/organisations, Voluntary Organisations and State Governments throughout the country including in the State of Bihar. The details of funds released under the Schemes of the Department in which funds are released to State Governments are at Annexure I and II.(b)	The Department is implementing various Central Sector Schemes namely Deendayal Disabled Rehabilitation Scheme (since 1999), Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids/Appliances (since 2005), Scholarship for Students with Disabilities (since 2012-13) and Scheme for Implementation of Persons with Disabilities Act (since 1999). There are following six sub-schemes under the Scholarship Schemes:i.	Pre-matric Scholarship for SwDs   (since 2014-15);ii.	Post-matric Scholarships for SwDs (since 2014-15);iii.	Scholarship for  Top Class Education for SwDs (since 2015-16);iv.	National Overseas Scholarship (since 2014-15);v.	National Fellowship for PwDs (since 2012-13), andvi.	Free Coaching for SwD (since 2017-18).Major Components of Scheme for Implementation of Persons with Disabilities Act (SIPDA) are:i.	Creation of Barrier Free Environment for PwDs (since 1998-99),ii.	Component of Skill Development (since 2013-14),iii.	Accessible India Campaign (since 2016-17),iv.	Unique Disability Identity Card (since 2014-15),In addition, National Trust, a statutory body under the Department and set up in 2000 under the National Trust for the welfare of persons with autism, cerebral palsy, mental retardation and multiple disabilities Act, 1999 implements the following major schemes for the welfare of Persons with Disabilities:(i)     Disha (Early Intervention and School Readiness Scheme for 0-10 years) (since 2015-16)(ii)    VIKAAS (Day Care Scheme for 10+ years) (since 2015-16)(iii)   Disha-cum Vikaas Scheme (Day Care) (since 2018-19)(iv)   Samarth (Respite Care Residential Scheme) (since 2015-16)(v)    Gharaunda (Group Home for Adults) (since 2015-16)(vi)	Samarth-cum-Gharaunda Scheme (Residential) (since 2018-19)(vii) ‘Niramaya’ Health Insurance Scheme (since 2015-16)(c)	The Government has enacted the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (RPwD) Act, 2016 which came into force on 19.04.2017. Section 34 of the said Act provides for not less than 4% reservation in Government vacancies for following categories of persons with benchmark disabilities (disability of 40% or more):(i)	blindness and low vision	1%(ii)	deaf and hard of hearing 	1%(iii)	locomotor disability including cerebral palsy, leprosy cured, dwarfism, acid attack victims and muscular dystrophy;	1%(iv)	autism, intellectual disability, specific learning disability and mental illness;	1%(v)	multiple disabilities from amongst persons under clauses (i) to (iv) including deaf- blindness.	In order to implement the provision of reservation in Government employment envisaged under the RPwD Act, 2016, the Ministry notified the list of 3566 posts ( 1046 in Group A, 515 in Group B, 1724 in Group C and 281 in Group D) suitable for various categories of persons with benchmark disabilities on 04.01.2021.Further, the Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities (DEPwD) implements the National Action Plan (NAP) for Skill Development of Persons with Disabilities (between the age group of 15 to 59 years) with a view to enhancing their scope of employability in both public and private sectors besides promoting self-employment. To promote entrepreneurship among PwDs, financial assistance in terms of loan on subsidized rates is provided by National Handicapped Finance and Development Corporation (NHFDC), a Central Public Sector Enterprise of the Department, under Divyangjan Swavalamban Yojna. NHFDC has also set up a Job Portal (www.disabilityjobs.gov.in) through which persons with disabilities can apply for jobs, skill trainings, education loans and self employment loans.*****
