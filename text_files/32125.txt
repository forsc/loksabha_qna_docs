                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3557






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






Funds under CAMPA




Manjulata Mandal













                                                    Sisir Kumar Adhikari
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it is a fact that the proposal of State of Odisha for release of Rs. 903.03 Cr. Under CAMPA APO 2021-22 has been received by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change;(b)	if so, whether there is any valid reason for delay in approval of the balance funds of Rs.37.25 Cr. and whether it will affect the afforestation and forest & wildlife management programmes in the State of Odisha during FY 2021-22; and(c)	the year-wise and State-wise details of budgetary allocation and expenditure on National afforestation programmes to improve the forest cover in the country from 2014-15 to 2021-22?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b) The Odisha State Compensatory Afforestation Fund Management and Planning Authority (State CAMPA) submitted their Annual Plan of Operation (APO) for the year 2021-22 with a proposed outlay of Rs.903.03 crore to the National Compensatory Afforestation Fund Management and Planning Authority (National Authority) for approval vide their letter no. 6360/10F(CAMPA))-21/2021 dated 20.03.2021. The APO was discussed in the 11th and 12th meeting of the Executive Committee of the National Authority held on 26.03.2021 and 27.04.2021 respectively and all  site-specific activities related to compensatory afforestation,  additional compensatory afforestation, penal compensatory afforestation, sensitive zone, wildlife conservation plan and regional wildlife management plan etc. and non-site specific activities related to artificial regeneration, assisted natural regeneration, forest protection, forest fire prevention and control operations, improvement of wildlife habitat, third party monitoring and evaluation, expenses of State Authority etc. amounting to Rs 675.67 crore were approved. These will help timely implementation of the afforestation and forest & wildlife management programmes in the State of Odisha during Financial Year 2021-22.In the 14th  meeting of the Executive Committee held on 22.07.2021, 23.07.2021 and 30.07.2021 remaining non-site activities related to forest protection, wild life management, forest and wildlife related infrastructure development amounting to Rs 190.11 crore were  approved. Additional approval for Rs 35.25 crore  for activities related to forest and wildlife related infrastructure development, publicity and awareness programmes, up-gradation of Forest IT, digitization of forest boundaries was issued on 09.11.2021 after the State CAMPA submitted details/justification with regard to these activities vide their letter no. 18063/10F(CAMPA) 21/2021 dated 27.10.2021. Thus, total activities amounting to Rs.901.03 crore included in the APO of the State CAMPA have been approved.(c)	The year-wise and State-wise amount releasedunder National Afforestation Programme of Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change to improve the forest cover in the country from 2014-15 to 2021-22 is attached Annexure-I. ****
