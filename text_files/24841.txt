                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

741






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2021






Vaccine Hesitancy




Nalin Kumar Kateel














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government has taken note of vaccine hesitancy among the people, if so, the details thereof and the steps taken by the Government to address the issue of vaccine hesitancy;(b) the present status of cumulative Covid Vaccination coverage as on 30th June, 2021 in variouscategories, State/UT-wise;(c) the details of Vaccine doses administered so far under Nationwide Vaccination Drive as on 30th June, 2021;(d) the number of persons in the age group 18-44 who have received their first dose and the number of people who have received Second dose since the start of vaccination drive, State/UT-wise;(e) the quantum of vaccines demanded and supplied to various States/UTs since the start of vaccination drive on 16th January, 2021 as on 30th June, 2021, State wise; and(f) the steps taken by the Government to accelerate the pace and expanding the scope of COVID vaccination throughout the country?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a):	The National COVID-19 Vaccination Drive is progressing at a steady pace and Covid vaccination has improved significantly from 2.35 lakh doses per day in the month of January 2021 to an average of 39.89 lakh doses per day in the month of June 2021. However, in order to raise awareness about COVID-19 vaccines, address vaccine hesitancy and  sustain vaccine confidence, a communication strategy is in place which is being implemented across all States/UTs.(b) & (c):	The details of cumulative doses administered  State/UT-wise under COVID-19 vaccination drive across various categories, as on 30th June 2021, is placed at Annexure-1.(d):	The number of persons in the age group 18-44 years who have received their first dose and second dose since the start of vaccination drive, State/UT-wise is placed at Annexure-2.(e):	COVID-19 vaccine doses procured centrally by Government of India are supplied free of cost to States/UTs based on pro-rata target population, the progress of vaccination and vaccine wastage. The State-wise details of doses supplied by Government of India till 30th June 2021 is placed at Annexure- 3.(f):	Government of India has ensured improved accessibility of safe COVID-19 vaccination services to all beneficiaries aged 18 years and above through increase in vaccination centres, citizen friendly upgradation of Co-WIN, establishment of 24x7 national call center and State call centers to address queries from general public on COVID-19 and the vaccination process. Government of India regularly reviews the progress of vaccination drive in all States/UTs to expedite its progress. Accessibility of vaccination is also being improved by involving Private COVID-19 Vaccination Centres (CVCs), Workplace CVCs & ‘Near to Home’ CVCs. Continued efforts are also being made to ramp up domestic production of vaccines.
