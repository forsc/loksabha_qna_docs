                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2238






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.09.2020






Promotion of Ayurvedic System of Medicine




Rajyavardhan Singh Rathore














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA,YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) the details of the ‘Integration of AYUSH with National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular diseases and Stroke (NPCDCS) Program’, including funds allocated for the same;(b) the details of ‘Integration of AYUSH with NPCDCS Program’ Centres, State/District-wise including Rajasthan;(c) the details of effectiveness of treatment offered for Cancer at these centers alongwith total number of people who have undergone treatment at each of these centres;(d) the total number of people employed at these centres, and the criteria for their recruitment in each State; and(e) whether any proposals have been initiated to expand the programme to more States and districts and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE (DR. HARSH VARDHAN)(a): Ministry of AYUSH in collaboration with Directorate General of Health Services, Ministry of Health & Family Welfare has implemented National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular Diseases and Stroke (NPCDCS) for health promotion, prevention and management of Non-communicable diseases or Lifestyle related disorders through its three research organizations, namely, Central Council for Research in Ayurvedic Sciences (CCRAS), Central Council for Research in Unani Medicine (CCRUM) and Central Council for Research in Homoeopathy (CCRH) in districts namely Bhilwara (Rajasthan), Surendranagar (Gujarat), Gaya (Bihar), Darjeeling (West Bengal), Krishna (Andhra Pradesh),  Sambalpur (Odisha) Nasik (Maharashtra) and Lakhimpur Kheri (UP). The Objectives of the program are as follows:1.	Health promotion of masses through behavioral change.2.	Disease prevention through early diagnosis of Non-Communicable Diseases (NCD).3.	Reduction in NCD burden and their risk factors.4.	Early management of NCDs through AYUSH treatment alone or as add on to standard care.The details of funds allocated by the research councils for the NPCDCS program during the financial year 2019-20 are as below:- S.No.	Name of the Research Council 	Funds Allocated1.	CCRAS 	12.83 crore2.	CCRH 	3.91 crore3.	CCRUM	2.99 crore(b):   The details of the Centres, State/District-wise is at Annexure:- (c):    Opportunistic screening for common cancers (breast, cervical and oral) among the population at different level of health facilities is carried and referral to higher centres for further diagnosis and treatment is done after providing the advice of diet and lifestyle modification. Under Homoeopathy, 63 patients who were provisionally diagnosed during screening were referred to CHCs. Out of these, 12 patients were treated with integrative care at the Centres.(d):	Total 550 number of staff are working in these centres. The desired qualifications as reported by the Research Councils for the staffs working under the NPCDCS programme are as under:- 1.	Research Associate- BAMS / MD (Homoeopathy)/ MD (Unani)/ BUMS 2.	Senior consultant- M.D. (Ayurveda) and age upto 65 years as on date of engagement.3.	Yoga Instructor/ Therapist- A Degree in Yoga/M.A. (Yoga)/M.Sc. (Yoga) and age not exceeding 30 years4.	Data Entry Operator/Office Assistant- 12th Pass or equivalent with certificate in Computer Course and age upto 27 years as on date of engagement. 5.	Multi-tasking staff- Matric pass or equivalent and age upto 30 years as on date of engagement.(e):     No.
