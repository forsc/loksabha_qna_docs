                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1501






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.09.2020






Effect of COVID-19 on Agriculture




S.R. Parthiban














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the loss to farmers and the agricultural sector due to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic and the subsequent lockdown has led to shortages in production of all major crops which in turn may result in increased demand;(b)	if so, the action taken by the Government to tackle this issue; and(c)	the details of the funds allocated to the State of Tamil Nadu for tackling the same? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????            ( SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)  (a):	The Ministry of Home affairs, Government of India had issued comprehensive guidelines on the measures to be taken for containment of Covid-19, which also ensured smooth functioning of Agricultural operations / sector and timely harvesting of crops. As a result, as per Fourth Advance Estimates 2019-20, the foodgrains production in the country is estimated at record 296.65 million tonnes which is higher by 11.44 million tonnes than the production of foodgrain during 2018-19 and higher by 26.87 million tonnes than the average production of foodgrains during previous five years’ (2014-15 to 2018-19).(b) & (c):	Does not arise. *******
