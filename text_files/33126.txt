                                    ANSWERED ON:

Assistance Provided by One Stop Centres




L.S. Tejasvi Surya














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)  	the number of One Stop Centres (OSCs) set up in the country district-wise especially in Karnataka under the Scheme; (b)  	the district-wise breakup of the number of women accessing OSC from Karnataka since 2015, year-wise; (c)  	the type of assistance sought by them since 2015 category-wise and year-wise; (d)  	whether the Government proposes to increase the number of OSCs keeping in view gender-based violence in the last year; and (e)  	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a):  The Ministry of Women and Child Development implements the One Stop Centre (OSC) Scheme across the country with effect from 1st April 2015 under Nirbhaya Fund with the objective to provide integrated support and assistance under one roof with a range of services including police, medical, legal aid and counseling, psychological support to women affected by violence and in distress, both in private and public spaces. Under the scheme, Central Government provides 100% financial assistance directly to districts for setting up and management of OSCs. As of now, 733 OSCs have been approved for setting up in 730 district of all 36 States /UTs, out of which 704 are operational in 35 States /UTs, which have assisted more than 4.50 lakh women. In the State of Karnataka, OSCs are operational in all 30 districts. The number of One Stop Centres (OSCs) set up in the country, including Karnataka is at Annexure-I. (b) & (c): The district-wise; year-wise details of the number of women assisted and type of assistance provided by OSCs in Karnataka since 2015  is at Annexure-II.
