                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

286






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2021






Setting up of Turmeric Board at Nizamabad, Telangana




Uttam Kumar Nalamada Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government proposes to set up a Turmeric Board in Nizamabad, Telangana for the procurement and marketing of turmeric; (b) 	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor; and; (c) 	the time frame within which it will be set up and the details of funds sanctioned for the same?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????	  (SHRI NARENDRA  SINGH TOMAR) (a) to (c):	A statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (c) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.   286  DUE FOR REPLY ON 16TH MARCH, 2021.(a) to (c):    Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India has upgraded its Divisional office at Nizamabad (Telangana) to Regional Office cum Extension Centre  in February, 2020 under Spices Board for promotion of turmeric as well as other spices with focussed attention on research, development,  post harvest operations, marketing and export in the region.    Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Government of India implements several development programmes for spices including turmeric through State Departments under Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH) with the assistance for development of infrastructure for post-harvest management and marketing of horticulture produce at farm/market level to enable farmers to get remunerative prices to turmeric crop. National Agriculture Market (e-NAM) scheme is also implemented to lower the transaction costs to buyers and stable prices. DAC&FW, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers’ Welfare is implementing Market Intervention Scheme (MIS) to protect the growers of perishable horticulture commodities from making distress sale in the event of a bumper crop during the peak arrival period.******
