                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3016






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2019






Prohibition of Child Marriage Act,2006




Bhagwanth Khuba













                                                    Ritesh Pandey
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government proposes to amend “The Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006”, to declare child marriage void ab initio instead of being voidable at the option of the contracting parties who were child at the time of marriage;(b)	if so, whether the Government is considering the same amendment in near future;(c)	whether the State rules under the Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006 have been drafted for the State of Uttar Pradesh; and(d)	if so, the details of the said rules and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a) & (b)  The Government of India has initiated Inter-Ministerial consultation for considering amendments in ‘The Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006’.(c) & (d)   As per section 19 of ‘The Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006’, the State Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, make rules for carrying out the provisions of this Act.  Government of Uttar Pradesh has informed that the State rules under ‘The Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006’ have not been made.
