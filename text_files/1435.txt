                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1264






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.06.2019






World Bank Assistance to Infant Health Programme




Ashok Mahadeorao Nete














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether there is a proposal by the World Bank to provide financial assistance for infant health programme in the country;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the World Bank had provided financial assistance for the said programme earlier, if so, the details thereof; and(d) whether any success has been achieved in establishing population growth in the country and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to (c):	 As per available information, world bank assistance is being provided for various health programmes having direct or indirect impact on the health of infants in the country. The details of assistance provided since 2016-17 till date are enclosed at                 annexure 1 (a & b).(d):	Yes, success has been achieved in reducing population growth in the country.(i)	The Decadal growth Rate has    reduced from 21.54% in Census 2001 to 17.64% in Census 2011.(ii)	The Average Annual Exponential growth rate has declined from 1.97 in Census 2001 to 1.64 in Census 2011.
