                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5628






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2019






Biodiversity of Naro Hills




Ganesh Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether Naro Hills in Satna district of Madhya Pradesh is home to various small and big animals and plants species including deciduous evergreen forests;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether Satna district has rare species of medicinal plants and several species are on the verge of extinction due to lack of proper care and if so, the details thereof;(d)	the reaction of the Government thereto;(e)	whether the forest department of Satna district has prepared a proposal to develop 500 acres of land dedicated to the nature; and (f)	if so, the details thereof ?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) & (b) Naro Hills in Satna district of Madhya Pradesh is home to various floral and faunal species.  These inter alia include animals such as tiger, leopard, wild cat, fox, bear, sambhar, neelgai, jackal, langur, monkey, rabbit, peacock, owl; and plants such as flame lily, parijat, kapuri, ratanjot, henna. Naro Hills is part of Vindhyan region and a dry deciduous forest.(c) & (d) Vindhyan region harbours several species of medicinal plants,  some of which are threatened. These inter alia include: Dillenia pentagyna, Plumbago capensis, Psilotum nudum, Drosera burmanii, Alectra chitrakutensis, Cordia macleodii, Costus speciosus, Strychnos nux-vomica, Plumbago zeylancia etc.(e) & (f) As per the information provided by Madhya Pradesh Biodiversity Board, a proposal has been prepared by Satna District Forest Department to notify Naro Hills under Satna Forest Division as Biodiversity Heritage Site under Section 37 of Biological Diversity Act, 2002, and Rule 22 of Madhya Pradwesh Biodiversity Rules, 2004, after following due process. The preliminary notification of Biodiversity Heritage Site, Naro Hills Satna has been issued vide Madhya Pradesh Gazette Notification No. 2392-1335-2017-Ten-2 dated 22.12.2018.  No objections have been received on the preliminary notification, which has been sent for concurrence of District Panchayat, Satna.*****
