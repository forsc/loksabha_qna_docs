                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1296






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.09.2020






Implementation of PMKVY




Kirit Premjibhai Solanki














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)       the salient features of the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) scheme and the current status of its implementation in the country; (b)       the total number of students that have received skill training under the program since its inception, State/UT wise; (c)        the total number of Trainer and Assessor academies that have been set up under the program, State/UT wise; and (d)       the details of the sectors and areas in which training is imparted to students and whether there are opportunities for placement after the training process? 






ANSWER




                                                        (a)       Under Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) (2016-20), two types of skill training are undertaken:  short duration skill development training, called Short-Term Training (STT) and Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)). As on 17.03.2020, 91.15 lakh candidates have been trained under PMKVY 2016-20 so far. The salient features of PMKVY are as follows:•	Scheme has two components: Centrally Sponsored Centrally Managed (CSCM) component implemented through National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) and Centrally Sponsored State Managed (CSSM) component implemented through States/State Skill Development Missions (SSDM);•	Training is imparted under NSQF (National Skill Qualification Framework) aligned job-roles;•	Recognition of Prior Leaning (RPL) component has been significantly enhanced for certifying the informally trained workers, particularly in the informal sector;•	Various digital interventions have been taken for effective and transparent implementation of scheme such as Direct Benefit Transfer, Aadhaar linkage of trainers and trainees, Biometric attendance of candidate;•	Training Centers have been Accredited & Affiliated through online SMART portal;•	Scheme has been converged with Skill India Portal for capturing of the real-time data with respect to life-cycle training process of trainees and training providers;      (b)       The State-wise number of candidates trained under STT or oriented under RPL in PMKVY 2016-20 is given at Annexure. (c)        As on 14.09.2020, four dedicated ‘Trainer-Assessor Academies’ have been established and operationalized with. two in Maharashtra and one each in Telangana and Uttarakhand. They impart Training of Trainers and Assessors programmes, as per the guidelines of NSDC. (d)       Under PMKVY (2016-20),  skill training is being imparted in more than 450 NSQF aligned job roles of levels 3 and 4 across 37 diverse sectors. Under the scheme, training centres facilitate placements of certified candidates through multiple initiatives like Rojgaar Melas, collaboration with industries and Sector Skill Councils. Further, Government has also launched the ASEEM (Atmanirbhar Skilled Employees Employer Mapping) portal which is a directory of all such skilled persons who have been trained. This directory is available to the industries for meeting their requirement of skilled personnel anywhere in the country.
