                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2398






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.03.2020






Door to Door Waste Collection




Kirron  Kher














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number and percentage of wards which have achieved cent percent door to door waste collection, State-wise including Chandigarh;(b) 	the number and percentage of wards with source segregation of waste, State-wise including Chandigarh; (c) 	the list of States and UTs with less than one third of source segregation of waste; and(d) 	the steps taken by the Government to ensure cent percent door to door waste collection and source segregation? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                        (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a) and (b) : As per information received from the concerned States/Union Territories (UT), 81,135 wards (96.05 percent) have adopted cent percent Door-to-door and 63,204 wards (74.8 percent) have adopted cent percent source segregation. The State/UT-wise details are at Annexure – I. (c) : The list of States and UTs with wards having less than one third source segregation of the waste generated is at Annexure –II. (d) : The Government has taken multi-pronged strategy to encourage citizens and Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) to start cent percent Door-to-Door Collection and Source segregation of waste. The key initiatives are as below:(i). 	Funds are released under Solid Waste Management (SWM) to the States and Union Territories (UTs) to enable them to plan, strategize and implement their action Plan; (ii). 	Government adopted an outcome based graded approach called Garbage Free City (GFC) Star Rating protocol to certify cities on the extent of management of solid waste being done by them. Door-to-Door Collection and Source segregation form important aspects of the protocol. The protocol also includes citizens’ feedback and validation of the extent of source segregation. (iii) 	Inclusion of dedicated indicator on source segregation under Swachh Survekshan.(iv). Various advisories and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) containing detailed guidelines and best practices for practicing source segregation, have been released by Government for use by all Stakeholders.                            *****
