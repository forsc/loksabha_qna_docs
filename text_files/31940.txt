                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3290






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.12.2021






Administration of Second dose of COVID-19 Vaccination




Lavu Sri Krishna Devarayalu














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the worst performing and best performing districts with respect to the second dose being given to the adult population;(b)	whether the Government has undertaken any systematic study to understand the reasons for the reluctance of adults to get their second dose;(c)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor;(d)	whether the Government has taken any steps to ensure door-to-door campaigns to encourage people to get their second dose, if so, the details thereof; and(e)	whether the Government has considered to providing any additional incentives to ASHA workers in order to ensure greater efficacy in disseminating information and urging adults to get their second dose, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therfor?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) to (e) The details of five best and five low performing district with respect to administration of second dose of COVID-19 vaccine to adult population is at Annexure-1.Regular review of COVID-19 vaccination is undertaken at National, State and District level to identify various challenges for COVID-19 vaccination including reluctance and to devise corrective measures. In order to raise awareness about safety and efficacy of COVID-19 vaccines, address vaccine hesitancy and sustain vaccine confidence, a communication strategy is in place. MoHFW has advised States/UTs to disseminate clear, consistent, transparent and factual information to general public to clear misconceptions and sustain vaccine confidence.Government of India has launched ‘Har Ghar Dastak Abhiyaan’ from 3rd November 2021 wherein missed beneficiaries for 1st dose and due beneficiaries for 2nd dose are identified and vaccinated through house-to-house activity. ASHA are provided incentive for mobilizing beneficiaries to immunization session site and for ensuring complete immunization at 1, 2 and 5 year age of child.
