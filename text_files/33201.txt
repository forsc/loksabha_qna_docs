                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

560






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2022






Ayushman Bharat Yojana




Jaswantsinh Sumanbhai Bhabhor














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the year-wise number of applications received for getting benefits under Ayushman Bharat Yojana in Dahod district of Gujarat since the launch of Ayushman Bharat Yojana(b) the number of applications approved out of total received applications;(c) the number of persons benefitted under Ayushman Bharat Yojana in district Dahod, Gujarat since the inception of this Yojana;(d) the total amount spent for providing benefits to the persons in district Dahod, Gujarat; and(e) the total amount spent on general public for their OPD treatment and also post major surgeries and admission to hospital under the said scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH ANDFAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) to (e): As of 28th January 2022, the number of beneficiaries verified in Dahod district and provided with Ayushman cards is 4.14 lakh.A total of 15,000 hospital admissions worth Rs. 29.61 crore are authorized for beneficiaries from Dahod district. The year-wise details of authorized hospital admissions (count and amount) are at Annexure.Ayushman Bharat-Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB-PMJAY) was launched on 23rd September 2018 to provide health coverage up to Rs. 5 lakh per annum per beneficiary family to around 10.74 crore poor and vulnerable families identified from Socio Economic Caste Census (SECC) data of 2011 on the basis of select deprivation and occupational criteria across rural and urban areas respectively. Up to 3 days of pre-hospitalization and 15 days’ post-hospitalization expenses such as diagnostics and medicines are also covered.
