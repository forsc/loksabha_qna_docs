                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

962






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.06.2019






Activities under AMRUT Scheme




Manoj Kishorbhai Kotak














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is implementing the Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) scheme in 48 cities in Maharashtra including Mumbai;(b) if so, the details of activities carried out under the scheme so far in all the cities specially Mumbai;(c) the quantum of funds sanctioned for these projects; and(d) whether any time frame has been fixed to complete these projects and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                         (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a).	Government of India is implementing Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) in 44 selected Mission cities of Maharashtra, including Navi Mumbai and Greater Mumbai.(b) & (c).	For the State of Maharashtra, Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs has approved State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) amounting to ?7,759.32 crore, including committed Central Assistance of ?3,534.08 crore under AMRUT. The State of Maharashtra has taken up projects amounting to ?7,798.49 crore, which is in excess of the approved SAAPs. The additional cost of the projects will be borne by the State.Under the Mission, State Government of Maharashtra has taken up 40 projects costing ?4,353 crore in the water supply sector, 31 projects costing ?3,173 crore in Sewerage and Septage Management sector, 1 project worth ?94 crore for Storm Water Drainage and 128 projects costing ?179 crore in the Parks & Green Spaces sector. City-wise details of projects taken up in Maharashtra is placed as Annexure.(d).	The State of Maharashtra has envisaged to complete the projects within the Mission period i.e. by 31stMarch, 2020.                            ****
