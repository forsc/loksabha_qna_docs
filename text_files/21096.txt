                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2334






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.03.2021






Improving Production of Pulses, Food Grains and Essential Edible Oils




Heena Vijaykumar Gavit













                                                    Unmesh Bhaiyyasaheb Patil
                                                








                                                    Sambhajirao Mane Dhairyasheel
                                                








                                                    Sujay Radhakrishna Vikhepatil
                                                








                                                    Sanjay(Kaka) Ramchandra Patil
                                                








                                                    Shrikant Eknath Shinde
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the projects carried out to support and improve production of pulses, foodgrains and essential edible oils in Maharashtra;(b)	the details of funds allocated, released and utilised for the above purpose during the last five years;(c)	the steps taken by Government to provide adequate financial, logistic and technical support to Maharashtra to improve production of pulses, foodgrains and essential edible oils; (d)	the details of funds allocated, released and utilised for the above purpose during the last five years; and (e) the steps taken by Government to support the revenue loss to farmers due to natural calamities like heavy winds/ rainfall and subsequent floods?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????C? ??????                          (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):	In order to improve production and productivity of foodgrains, Oilseeds and Oilpalm, Department of Agriculture Cooperation and Farmers Welfare (DAC&FW) is implementing Crop Development Programmes i.e. National Food Security Mission (NFSM) in the States including Maharashtra.          In addition, DAC&FW is also implementing crop production programmes like “Targeting Rice Fallow Area (TRFA)” for utilization of land left fallow after Kharif paddy in various districts of Maharashtra.Further, State can undertake crop development programmes under Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) with the approval of State Level Sanctioning Committee (SLSC) constituted under the Chairmanship of the Chief Secretary of the State.(b) & (d):	Allocation, release and expenditure under NFSM-Foodgrain components, NFSM-Oilseeds & Oilpalm and RKVY for the state of Maharashtra during the last five years are Annexed.(c):	DAC&FW has allocated an amount of Rs. 153.36 crore for NFSM-Foodgrain components and Rs. 39.38 crore for NFSM-Oilseeds & Oilplam as Central share for the State of Maharashtra during 2020-21. The Government of India allocates funds to the State considering crops, area coverage and resource available. Under NFSM, 33 Project Management Teams (PMTs) have been approved for Maharashtra as a logistic support to the State. The expenditure in the mission is shared between centre and state on 60:40 basis.	Besides, Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR), State Agricultural Universities (SAUs), Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) have been involved in crop development programmes for providing technical backstopping, demonstrating crop technologies and training to the farmers.(e)	In case of damage of crops due to natural calamities namely cyclone, drought, earthquake, fire, flood, tsunami, hailstorm, landslide, avalanche, cloudburst, pest attack, fresh and cold waves, the State Governments concerned provide relief to the affected farmers in the form of input subsidy from State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) placed at their disposal as per the Government of India’s items and norms.          Further, in case of calamity of severe in nature and beyond the coping capacity of SDRF, additional financial assistance is granted to the State Government concerned from National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF) to provide relief to the affected people including farmers.           Besides DAC&FW ensures comprehensive risk cover from crops of farmers against all non-preventable natural risks from pre-sowing to post-harvest stage under Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY).
