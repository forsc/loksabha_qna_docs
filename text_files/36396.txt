                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3869






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2022






Solid Waste Management Plant at Paradip Port




Rajashree Mallick














                        Will the Minister of





PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Paradip Port has a comprehensive solid waste management plant and if so, the percentage of total waste being managed including the total energy produced against the total requirement;(b)	whether the entire power requirement at Paradip port is met through green energy, if so, the percent of total power requirement generated from renewable energy sources, source-wise; (c)	the expected timeline by which Paradip port is likely to be a green port; and(d)	the steps taken under the Green Port Initiative at Paradip Port so far?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	No, Sir. All solid waste is being managed either by Paradip Municipality or through internal resources. There is no energy being produced from waste.(b)	No, Sir. 2.2 % of total power requirements is generated through Solar Power.(c) & (d)	Roof-Top Solar Plants for about 450 KWP capacity are installed in the Port area. No land is available for ground-mounted Solar Power Plant.                                               *****
