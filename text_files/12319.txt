Apprentices under Skill Development




Parbatbhai Savabhai Patel














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of apprentices who got training under various programmes during the last three years along with the names of trades, State/district/ trade-wise; (b)	whether some of the industries are not getting workers despite heavy demand of skilled youths due to the unavailability of skilled workers; (c)	if so, the steps taken/being taken by the Government to solve this problem; and(d)	whether training of people has generated self employment and if so, the details thereof, State/district/ trade-wise?

                                                        (a)	The number of apprentices trained under Apprenticeship training program implemented by MSDE under the “Apprentices Act 1961” (amended up to 2014) during last three calendar years is given below. State-district and trade wise details of apprentices is given in Annexure –I and Annexure-II respectively.Sr. No	Year	No. of Apprentices1	2017	1,23,3762	2018	1,73,7923	2019	1,93,452Contd...2.(b) and (c) 	Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship has taken following steps to meet the requirement of skilled manpower for  industries which are not getting skilled workers despite heavy demandi.	Introduction of two year Advanced Technical Diploma Program in IT, Networking & Cloud computing in National Skill Training Institutes (NSTI), Bangalore and National Skill Training Institutes (NSTI), Hyderabad jointly with IBM-India.ii.	Introduction of new-age courses like IoT -Smart Health Care, IoT –Smart City, 3D Printing, Drone Pilot, Solar Technician, Geo Informatics Assistant etc. in 12 National Skill Training Institutes(NSTI)  from 1st August, 2019 at the behest of National Association of Software Services Companies (NASSCOM) to meet the  skilled manpower requirement of future of jobs in IT-ITES sector. iii.	Implementation of Dual System of Training in ITIs jointly with industries, for their explicit skill need where on-job training’ (OJT) is conducted by industries themselves. iv.	Flexi MoU between Directorate General of Training and Industries for conducting training programmes as per their specific needs.(d)    Yes Sir, training of youth has generated self-employment.
