                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

194






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.02.2022






FDI in Textile Sector




Hasmukhbhai Somabhai Patel














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of Total amount of Foreign Direct Investment in the textile sector;. (b) the details of textile production during each of the last three financial years 2018-19, 2019-20, and 2020- 21. (c) whether Government has received any proposal of Foreign Direct Investment made in the textile sector during the 2020-21; and (d) if so, details thereof State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR TEXTILES  (SMT. DARSHANA JARDOSH)(a) to (d):	100% FDI in textile sector is through automatic route. A total of Rs.23,254.40 crores (USD 3840.89 Million) of Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) has been received from April, 2000 to September, 2021 in  textile sector. The information on textile production in various segments is as below:-Sl. No.	Segment	Unit	2018-19	2019-20	2020-21#1	Raw Cotton	Lakh Bales	333	365	3602.	Raw Silk	lakh ton	0.354	0.358	0.3373.	Jute (combined of Hessian, Sacking, Yarn)	Million Tons	1161	1165	910*4.	Man Made Fibre	Million Kg	1442	1898	1610*5.	Man Made Filament Yarn 	Million Kg	1160	1688	1326*6.	Cotton Yarn	Million Kg	4208	3962	3625*7.	Blended & 100% Non-cotton Yarn	Million Kg	1682	1702	1521*       * Provisional        #Year 2020-21 has been affected by global pandemic       (Source: Office of Textile commissioner)***
