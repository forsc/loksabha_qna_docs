                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4015






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.12.2019






Women at the Centre of Urban Planning




Sumalatha Ambareesh














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the extent of role of women at the centre of urban 	planning 	in the country;(b)	whether the Government has taken note of the Toshima’s 	experience in Japan 	where if women are neglected, 	demographic challenges can creep up on even 	superficially thriving districts in the country including in 	Karnataka, if so, the 	details thereof and if not, the 	reasons therefor;  (c)	the steps being taken by the Government to revamp 	public spaces as well remodeling of public toilets for 	women in the urban areas in the country including 	Karnataka along with funds sanctioned/spent during 	the last five years and the current year; and (d)	the steps being taken for cumulative impact of these 	female-centric urban 	planning and their role in 	development of 	the cities to meet the future needs of the 	cities and 	towns like Mandya in Karnataka?	






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	& (b): Urban Planning is a State subject. Government of India supports States in their efforts for planning and development. Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs has released Urban and Regional Development Plans Formulation and Implementation (URDPFI) Guidelines 2014 which contains a chapter on Special Requirements for Gender Sensitive Planning. It covers women’s travel, employment opportunities closer to home etc. The guidelines also advise that while preparing development plan, special attention must be paid on safety, security and participation of women, elderly, and other segments of society requiring special needs. (c) & (d) Under Swachh Bharat Mission-Urban, Community and Public Toilets (CTs/PTs) are being constructed in urban areas of the country with special features keeping in mind the requirements of women. This includes separate blocks within the CTs/PTs complex, with separate entrances and provisioning of sanitary pads vending machines and incinerators for safe and hygienic disposal of used sanitary pads. Besides this, other features include provision of mirrors, towels etc. within the CTs/PTs. Ministry has also released Model Building Bye Laws (MBBL), 2016 which has provision for daycare center facilities and segregated public toilets for women.                            *****
