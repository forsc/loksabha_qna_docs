                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3570






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






RUSA




Subhash Ramrao Bhamre













                                                    Amol Ramsing Kolhe
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Heena Vijaykumar Gavit
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the salient features along with aims and objectives of Rashtriya UchchatarShiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) and the status of the implementation of RUSA;(b) whether the Government has achieved the objectives since its inception, if so,the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor;(c) the details of financial allocations made under this scheme by the Governmentduring each of the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise;(d) whether the Union and State Government have prepared any higher educationplan on the scheme for upgradation of the universities and if so, the details thereofand if not, the reasons therefor; and(e) the other steps taken by the Government to create new universities throughupgradation of existing autonomous colleges and conversion of colleges in a cluster?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (c): Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) is a Centrally SponsoredScheme (CSS) being implemented by the Ministry of Human ResourceDevelopment. It is an overarching scheme, operated in mission mode for fundingthe state universities and colleges in order to achieve the aims of equity, access andexcellence. It seeks to improve the overall quality of existing State highereducational institutions by ensuring their conformity to prescribed norms andstandards and adoption of accreditation as a mandatory quality assuranceframework; correcting regional imbalances by facilitating access to high qualityinstitutions in rural & semi-urban areas; and providing adequate opportunities ofhigher education to socially deprived communities; promoting inclusion of women,minorities, SC/ST/OBCs and differently abled persons to ensure equity.Transformative reforms such as governance, academic, affiliation and accreditationreforms are pre-requisites in the implementation of the scheme in the States. All thefunding under RUSA is norm based and future grants are performance based andoutcome dependent.As on date, all the States/UTs except Lakshdweep are participating in RUSA.The details of the scheme are at rusa.nic.in.The RUSA Scheme has been instrumental in improving the access andequity in higher education. As per All India Survey on Higher Education (AISHE)report, the overall Gross Enrolment Ratio (GER) in Higher Education has improvedfrom 21.5 in 2012-13 i.e. at time of inception of RUSA to 25.8 in 2017-18. Enrolmentof female students and also of socially backward classes have considerablyimproved. RUSA mandates the rightsizing of State public universities in terms ofnumber of affiliated colleges to each university through affiliation reforms.Accordingly, States have bifurcated/trifurcated their existing universities.The details of the funds released to the States/UTs under RUSA during thelast three years and the current year are at Annexure.(d): Under RUSA, universities are supported under the component of InfrastructureGrants to Universities and Research, Innovation & Quality Improvement to improvethe overall infrastructure and to promote research and innovation in universities. Inaddition to this, under RUSA 2.0, 10 select State universities have been approvedRs. 100 core each under the component of Enhancing Quality and Excellence inSelect Universities.(e): Under RUSA, universities can be created by way of either upgradation ofautonomous colleges or by conversion of colleges in a cluster. Central support hasbeen approved for creation of 21 universities, out of which 11 are by way ofupgrading autonomous colleges and 10 are by way of clustering of colleges. Each ofthe University has been approved an amount of Rs. 55 Crores.***
