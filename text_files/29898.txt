                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENTLOK SABHAUNSTARRED QUESTION NO.1554TO BE ANSWERED ON 07.12.2021NSFDC †1554. SHRI RAJENDRA DHEDYA GAVIT: Will the Minister of SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT be pleased to state: (a) the details of various schemes being run by the National Scheduled Castes Finance and Development Corporation (NSFDC) for the people belonging to the Scheduled Castes; (b) whether the people are not able to avail benefits under above schemes run by NSFDC due to lack of information about the scheme; (c) if so, the measures being taken in this regard; and(d) the details of schemes being run and applications approved thereunder in the tribal dominated States of the country including Maharashtra?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(DR. VIRENDRA KUMAR) (a):  National Scheduled Castes Finance and Development Corporation (NSFDC), Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment provides financial assistance in the form of loan, at concessional rate of interest, to persons belonging to Scheduled Castes, having annual family income up to Rs. 3.00 lakhs, for income generating activities, to promote self-employment opportunities.The Schemes are implemented through State Channelizing Agencies (SCAs), nominated by respective State Governments/UT Administrations, and other Channelizing Agencies (CAs) like Public Sector Banks (PSBs), Regional Rural Banks (RRBs), Cooperative Banks/Societies and NBFC-MFIs with whom the Corporation has signed Memorandum of Associations (MoAs).Besides, NSFDC facilitates Skill Development Training for persons belonging to Scheduled Castes, by providing 100% course fee and Rs.1,500/- stipend per month (in non-residential programmes), for developing skills of the target group and make them employable either in Self/ Wage employment.NSFDC implements the following schemes at Pan India level, for eligible members of the Scheduled Castes: -A.	CREDIT BASED/LOAN SCHEMESNSFDC implements 12 Credit Based/Loan Schemes in various States/UTs of the country for the persons belonging to Scheduled Caste community. Twelve (12) schemes are as under:i.	Term Loanii.	Micro Credit Financeiii.	Mahila Samriddhi Yojanaiv.	Mahila Adhikarita Yojanav.	LaghuVyavasayYojanavi.	Green Business Schemevii.	Stand Up India Schemeviii.	Swachhta Udyami Yojanaix.	Educational Loan Schemex.	Vocational Education & Training Loan Scheme xi.	Aajeevika Microfinance Yojanaxii.	Udyam Nidhi Yojana,B. NON CREDIT BASED SCHEMES: - Skill development training programme under pradhan mantri dakshta aur kushalta sampann hitgrahi (pm-daksh) yojana: NSFDC, under its Skill Development Training Programme also facilitates upgradation of technical and entrepreneurial skills to its target group through Government Training Institutes (TIs) and Sector Skill Councils (SSCs), constituted by Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship and State Skill Development Missions (SSDMs) on Pan India basis, so that eligible members of the Scheduled Castes may engage in developmental activities by way of self/wage employment. From the year 2020-21, NSFDC is implementing the PM-DAKSH Yojana for Skill Training, launched by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment. The skill training under PM DAKSH is provided under four categories as under :a.	Up-skilling/Recognition of  Prior Learningb.	Short Term Trainingc.	Entrepreneurial Development Programmed.	Long Term Training  Interest Subvention Scheme: From the year 2020-21, NSFDC is implementing the Interest Subvention Scheme – Vanchit Ikai Samooh aur Vargon ki Arthik SahayataYojana (VISVAS) which was launched by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment to provide 5% interest subvention to the regular accounts of SC borrowers financed by various Banks & NBFC-MFIs.  The subvention would be available on individual loans up to Rs.2 lakh and SHG loans up to 4 lakh.(b & c):  No Sir, the benefits under the schemes run by NSFDC are being availed by its target group on the basis of information about NSFDC schemes from the SCAs and CAs, through whom the financial assistance under Credit/Loan schemes are provided. Accordingly, loan assistance proposals of interested persons are received from the SCAs/CAs.   For Non-credit/Skill Development Training, under PM DAKSH, from the current year, i.e. 2021-22, the interested persons register themselves directly through the PM DAKSH Portal which was launched by the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment, Govt. of India on 07.08.2021.(d):  Both credit and non-credit schemes of NSFDC, are being run in all the States/UTs, including the tribal dominated States and Maharashtra.
