                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

416






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.06.2019






New Banking Licences




Sougata Ray














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has any proposal to issue new banking licences in the country; (b)	if so, the details thereof; (c)	whether a number of State Governments applied for setting up of State level banks;(d)	if so, the details thereof and the action taken by the Government in this regard; and (e)	the details of the role of Reserve Bank of India and the Government in such types of banks?  






ANSWER




                                                        The Finance Minister(a) and (b): Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has issued Guidelines for ‘on tap’ Licensing of Universal Banks in the Private Sector, dated August 1, 2016 for granting licenses for universal banks on a continuous basis. RBI’s Second Bi-Monthly Monetary Policy, 2019-20 dated June 6, 2019, has announced RBI’s intention to issue Draft Guidelines for ‘on tap’ Licensing of Small Finance Banks by the end of August 2019. Post 2014, RBI has licensed two New Private Sector Banks (in 2015), ten Small Finance Banks and seven Payments Banks (during 2016 and 2017), details of which are at Annex.(c) and (d): RBI has informed that it has not received any application from any State Government for setting up of a new State level bank. (e): RBI issues licences to carry out banking business to co-operative banks under Section 22 of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949 (as applicable to Co-operative Societies). RBI regulates the banking functions of co-operative banks under the provisions of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949 and the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934. These banks are also required to comply with instructions/ guidelines etc. issued by RBI from time to time.***
