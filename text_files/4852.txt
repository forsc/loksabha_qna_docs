                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4402






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.07.2019






Amendment of Dentist Act




Shobha Karandlaje














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government proposes to amend the Dentist Act, if so, the details thereof, including the objectives thereof; (b) whether the Government has proposed to do away with the practice of nominating practising dentists who do not have requisite qualifications to the Dental Council of India (DCI), if so, the details thereof; (c) the number of dentists registered in Part A and Part B of the Dentist Act, 1948; (d) whether States/UTs like West Bengal, Kerala, Jammu and Kashmir, Puducherry, Punjab and Delhi have dentists registered in Part B, if so, the details thereof; (e) whether the DCI has stopped granting permission to establish new Dental colleges in India, due to dearth of job opportunities for students opting for BDS; and (f) the number of dental colleges and the number of dental graduates passing out every year in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b):      The Dentists (Amendment) Bill, 2019 has been passed by the Lok Sabha on 03.07.2019 and the Rajya Sabha on 08.07.2019. The Bill sought to amend the Dentists Act, 1948 so as to take away the mandatory requirement of the representation of Part B dentists in the Dental Council of India (DCI), State Dental Councils and Joint State Dental councils. (c):      The number of dentists registered in Part A and Part B of the Dentists Register are 2,71,798 and 979 respectively.(d):       A statement showing number of dentists registered in part B of these states is at Annexure.(e):      No.(f):      There are 313 Dental Colleges in the country with permitted intake capacity of 26,693 BDS admissions for the academic session 2019-20.…………………..
