                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3997






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






Green Belt Areas




Nayab Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the initiative being taken by the Government for insuring larger green belt areas in urban areas under the Public-Private Partnership (PPP) mode;(b)	whether the Government has identified newly developed green belt areas in rural and urban areas of the country; and (c)	if so, the details thereof, State-wise including Haryana?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to(c) The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change supports the States/Union Territories for carrying out afforestation activities in both forest and non-forest land including Urban and Rural areas under National Mission for a Green India (GIM) and CAMPA. Further, afforestation is being carried out by the States/Union Territories under various schemes of Central Government and State Government. As per report compiled in the Ministry under twenty-point programme, afforestation is being taken up annually over 2.0 million ha since 2019-20.Details of fund released/disbursedunder GIM and CAMPA including Haryanais enclosed at Annexure-I.In addition, the Ministryhas also launchedNagar Van Yojana (NVY) for providing wholesome healthy living environment, and contributingto growth of Smart, Clean,Green, Sustainable and Healthy cities.NVY envisages developing 400 Nagar Vans and 200 Nagar Vatikas in the country during the period of 2020-21 to 2024-25 with an objective to significantly enhance the tree outside forests and green cover, enhancement of biodiversity and ecological benefits to the urban and peri-urban areas apart from improving quality of life of city dwellers. So far, a total of 65 projects have been sanctioned in 22 States including Haryana. The State-wise details of Nagar Van projects sanctioned under Nagar Van scheme are given in Annexure-II. *****
