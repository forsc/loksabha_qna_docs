                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1588






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Effect of Small-Scale Fisheries on Marine Ecosystems




Shivkumar Chanabasappa Udasi














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is aware that even small-scale fisheries can destroy marine ecosystems and if so, the details thereof; (b)	the details of Centrally Sponsored Schemes for the development of Marine Fisheries in the country; and (c)	whether the Government has provided adequate funds under the schemes for welfare and safety of marine fishermen?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRYAND DAIRYING (SHRI PRATAP CHANDRA SARANGI)(a): 	Small-scale fisheries generally consist of traditional and eco-friendly fishing gears operated in the coastal waters.  The marine fisheries resources in the Indian coastal waters are highly diverse; predominantly comprising of small-scale and artisanal fleets. Marine fisheries are not in-exhaustive and can be subject to over-exploitation leading to loss of biodiversity and reduced availability of resources in case responsible fishing measures and management system are not adopted.(b): 	The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries,Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing various developmental schemes under the umbrella of “Blue Revolution Scheme” for overall development of Marine and Inland Fisheries, Post-harvest operations and Infrastructure developments towards realizing the “Blue Revolution” in the country.  Apart from this, a dedicated Fund i.e. Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund (FIDF) worth Rs.7,522.48 crore is also  being implemented by the Department to fill the infrastructure gaps in fisheries sector in the country. (c) 	Yes, sir. The funds released by the Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying under the various components of the Blue Revolution Scheme including schemes for welfare and safety of fishermen to promote fisheries in the country during the last five years are as below;Financial Year	Funds Released (In Crores)2014-15	353.352015-16	416.802016-17	424.112017-18	420.772018-19 	675.52Total	2290.55
