                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4487






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






Storage Capacity in Maharashtra




Hemant Patil














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether a large quantity of foodgrains is left to rot or is wasted because of lack of sufficient storage space; (b) 	if so, the details of quantity of foodgrains produced, procured, stored and damaged along with the required, available and leased storage capacity by Food Corporation of India (FCI) during each of the last three years and current year in Maharashtra; (c) 	the details of the steps taken for the construction of additional storage space with private partnership along with the assistance provided by the Government including the storage capacity created during the said period; and (d) 	the steps taken for sufficient storage and management of foodgrains to ensure adequate use of these foodgrains under various welfare schemes?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E RMINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT AND CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)¬(a): 	No Sir. No quantity of Central Pool Foodgrains stocks (wheat and rice) are left to rot or wasted due to lack of scientific storage space. (b): 	Does not arise in view of (a) above.(c): 	Government of India has chalked out an action plan for construction of Steel silos on PPP mode in the country. Silos with capacity of 29.25 LMT have been awarded till date and a capacity of 11.125 LMT is complete.(d): 	Government continuously assesses and monitors the storage capacity and based on the storage gap assessment, storage capacities are created/hired.*****
