                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4111






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Family Planning Programme




 Hema Malini














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether Family Planning Programme is the top most priority of the Government for population control, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Family Planning Programme has been successful in achieving its targets and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether sufficient financial resources are provided for the Family Planning Programme and if so, the details thereof; and(d) the status of the progress made regarding success/failure of the Family Planning Programme being run for population control in the country, State/UTwise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a): The Government accords top priority to the National Family Planning Program by providing family planning services both at facility and community levels namely expanded choices viz. Injectable contraceptive, Intra uterine contraceptive device (IUCD), Sterilization services, Condoms, and Oral Contraceptive Pills, including Home Delivery of Contraceptives. (b): As per National Family Health Survey (NFHS) IV data for the period 2015-16, the Total Fertility Rate has declined to 2.2 from the earlier 2.7 in NFHS III for the period 2005-06.28 out of 36 States/UTs have already achieved the replacement level fertility of 2.1 or less.(c): Sufficient financial resources are made available to States/ UTs for carrying out Family Planning activities under the National Health Mission (NHM). (d):  States/ UT-wise Total Fertility Rate (TFR) status as per earlier NFHS rounds is placed at Annexure.
