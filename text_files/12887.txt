                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

148






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.03.2020






Literacy Rate in Jharkhand




Vishnu Dayal Ram














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the reasons for low literacy rate in Jharkhand;(b) whether the Saakshar Bharat Programme has been implemented in Jharkhand and if so,the details thereof along with the funds allocation to Jharkhand to improve the literacyrate during the last three years;(c) the achievements made under the Saakshar Bharat Programme in Jharkhand;(d) whether any special provisions have been made to impart education in the left wingextremism affected districts in Jharkhand and if so, the details thereof; and(e) whether any special provisions have been made to impart education to the women andother disadvantaged groups in Jharkhand and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHARIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (e) A statement is laid on the Table of House.***STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART ( a ) to ( e) OF LOK SABHASTARRED QUESTION NO. 148 FOR 02.03.2020 ASKED BY SHRI VISHNU DAYALRAM REGARDING “LITERACY RATE IN JHARKHAND”.(a) Various factors viz poverty, gender and social inequities etc are impeding literacy inJharkhand State.(b) Yes, Saakshar Bharat Programme has been implemented in Jharkhand from 2009 tillMarch 2018. Details of fund allocation to Jharkhand to improve the literacy rate during the lastthree years is as under:-<pre>Year Central Share Released State Share Released Total funds released2016-17 6,30,24,000 4,20,16,000 10,50,40,0002017-18* -- -- --2018-19 6,00,00,000 4,00,00,000 10,00,00,000Grand Total 12,30,24,000 8,20,16,000 20,50,40,000* The State had a high unspent balance and important information/documents were notfurnished by the State, due to which funds could not be released.(c) In Jharkhand, as per Census 2011, adult literacy rate is 66.41 percent, as compared to thenational average adult literacy rate of 72.98 percent. Saakshar Bharat programme covered 410districts in the country, including 24 districts of Jharkhand.Basic Literacy Assessment Test was conducted by NIOS to assess the proficiency levelof functional literacy skills attained by learners. In Jharkhand, total number of 50,75,964 adultlearner appeared in the test and 41,44,515 succeeded (including 25.94 lakh women)(d)& (e) Under the Saakshar Bharat Programme, districts with female literacy rate of 50 percent or below (as per 2001 Census) and all districts worst affected by left wing extremistviolence, were eligible for coverage under the programme. Accordingly, a total 410 districts,including 30 worst affected left wing extremism (13 in Jharkhand) districts were identified aseligible for coverage under the programme.</pre>The programme achieved 7.64 crore adult literates, (including 5.38 crore women), againstthe target of 7 crore, as per NIOS Basic Literacy Assessment Test results, till the last testconducted in March 2018. The program covered 1.88 crore SCs, 1.08 crore STs, 75 lakhMinority adult learners who were certified literate. In the state of Jharkhand, 25.94 lakh femaleand 15.50 lakh male adult learners (total 41.44 lakh adults) were made literate, including 6.95lakh SC, 12.45 lakh ST and 4.65 lakh minority learners.
