                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1016






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.11.2019






Segregation of Waste




Shobha Karandlaje














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government mandates segregation of waste at source and if so, the details thereof;(b)	the twenty largest cities in terms of solid waste generation in India;(c) 	the percentage of waste that is segregated in these cities;(d) 	the name of cities which have notified their solid waste management policy under the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016; and(e) 	whether the Government has added any elements of extended producer’s responsibility in solid waste management and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)The Ministry in supersession of Municipal Solid Waste (Management and Handling) Rules, 2000, has notified Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016. In the new Rules, emphasis has been given for segregation of waste at source and handing over the segregated waste to waste collector or agency to promote the recyclers to develop sustainable market through recycling of waste.(b) and (c)As per the information available with the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA) 54.4 Million Ton per Annum (MTPA) of solid waste is generated in the country. The Statewise generation of municipal solid waste is tabulated in the Annexure. Out of 84,427 wards, 79,196 wards have door-to-door collection system and 57,810 wards have source segregation system. Some cities like Indore, Vishakhapatnam, Pune, Bhopal etc. are segregating their waste in all of its wards.(d) The Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016 mandates the State Government to prepare the state policy and prescribed the duties and responsibilities of the local authority and village panchayats to prepare the solid waste management plan as per the state policy and strategy on solid waste management. Approximately all State governments have prepared the State Policy for solid waste management, accordingly the local authorities are mandated to implement their own solid waste management plan.(e) The Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016 mandate all manufacturers/brand owners of disposable products such as tin, glass, plastics packaging etc. toprovide necessary financial assistance to local authorities for establishment of waste management system. Further, the brand owners who place their products in non-biodegradable packaging are mandated to put in place a system to collect back the packaging waste so generated.   *****
