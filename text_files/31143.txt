                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2807






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.12.2021






Time Limit for Ration Card




Ramesh Chander Kaushik














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the time limit fixed to issue ration cards to all the eligible persons of the country under ‘One Nation, One Ration Card‘; (b) 	the State-wise details of the number of eligible ration card holders and the cards issued till date along with the number of beneficiaries to whom ration cards are yet to be issued; and (c) 	the State-wise details of the agencies issuing the said cards?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E R  MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT AND CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a): 	Under One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC) plan, there is no requirement to issue new ration cards for the purpose of ration portability. Under this technology driven portability system, the NFSA beneficiaries can use their same/existing ration cards to access their quota of subsidised foodgrains from any Fair Price Shop (FPS) of choice in the country, with Aadhaar biometric authentication on an electronic Point of Sale (ePoS) device. (b): 	The National Food Security Act, 2013 (NFSA) provides for a maximum coverage of 81.35 Crore beneficiaries in the country to receive monthly benefit of highly subsidised foodgrains through the Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS). The Act also provides State/UT wise limit of beneficiary coverage. Further, TPDS under NFSA is operated in joint responsibilities of the Central and State/UT Governments, where inter-alia the operational responsibilities of identification of eligible beneficiaries/households within their jurisdiction, issuance of ration cards to them, etc. rests with the respective State/UT Governments. State/UT wise details of the ration cards under NFSA are at Annexure.
