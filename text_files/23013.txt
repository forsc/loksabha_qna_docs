                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4032






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Adulteration in Jaggery




A.K.P. Chinraj














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government is aware of high level adulteration in Jaggery in the State of Tamil Nadu, especially in district Namakkal, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has received any complaint, representation, letter in this regard, if so, the details thereof and action taken in this regard;(c) whether the punishment and fine imposed on persons involved in food adulteration is very low, if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor; and(d) the number of persons convicted in food adulteration cases during last five years, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b):  Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) has informed that reports regarding sale of sub-standard and adulterated food, including Jaggery, have come to its notice. As implementation and enforcement of Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006 and Rules and Regulations made thereunder primarily lies with State/UT Governments, Officers of the Food Safety Departments of States/UTs regularly conduct surveillance and enforcement drives to curb the adulteration in food products.The quality parameters for Jaggery have been prescribed under Regulation 2.8.4 of Food Safety and Standards (Food Product Standards and Food Additives) Regulations, 2011.  All Food Business Operators involved in processing/manufacture of jaggery have to comply with all the quality parameters prescribed under the said Regulations.  (c): The provisions relating to offences and penalties have been prescribed under chapter IX of the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006, wherein provision of graded penalty and punishment have been prescribed with reference to gravity of the offence relating to unsafe food.   Where unsafe food  results in death, a penalty of imprisonment for a term not less than seven years but which may  extend to imprisonment for life and also with fine of not less than ten lakh rupees is prescribed.(d): As per information made available by State/UT Governments, the details of food samples analysed, found non-conforming and action taken thereon, State/UT-wise, during last five years is at Annexed at Annexure-I, Annexure-II, Annexure-III, Annexure-IV and Annexure-V.  These include cases of conviction in food adulteration cases.
