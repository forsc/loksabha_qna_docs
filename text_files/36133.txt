                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3568






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2022






Upgradation of Airstrips in the Country




Amol Ramsing Kolhe













                                                    Sunil Dattatray Tatkare
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of airstrips indicating separately the inoperative or rarely operative airstrips in the country, location-wise;(b) the details of revenue generated and expenditure incurred on maintaining these inoperative or rarely operative airstrips in the country during the last three years and the current year, airstrip-wise;(c) the reasons for large number of airstrips in the country lying unused;(d) whether the Government proposes to make these airstrips operational and if so, the details thereof along with the steps taken by the Government in this regard till date; and(e) the other steps taken by the Government for the upgradation and modernisation of major airstrips in various States of the country?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a): The list of non-operational airports of Airports Authority of India(AAI) is enclosed as Annexure-l. ;(b): The airport-wise details of Revenue and Expenditure for the last three Financial Years in respect of non-operational airports of AAI is enclosed as Annexure-II. (c) to (e): Ministry of Civil Aviation has launched Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS)- UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik) on 21-10-2016 to enhance regional air connectivity from unserved and underserved airports in the country and  making air travel affordable to the masses. UDAN is a market driven scheme. Interested airlines based on their assessment of demand on particular routes, submit their proposals at the time of bidding under UDAN. An airport /airstrip /helipad /heliport /water aerodrome which is included in the awarded routes of RCS - UDAN and requires upgradation/development for commencement of RCS operations, is developed under the "Revival of unserved and underserved airports" scheme. The Government of India has approved an amount of Rs. 4500 crore for revival/upgradation of airports awarded under the  "Revival of unserved and underserved airports" scheme. Up till now, on the basis of four round of biddings under UDAN, 948 valid routes have been allotted to various airlines connecting 154 RCS airports including 14 water aerodromes & 36 Helipads in 31 States/UTs which have been identified for operation of RCS flights in the country. As on 17-03-2022, 409 RCS routes have been commenced connecting 66 airports which have been operationalized and include 2 Water Aerodrome & 8 Heliports.Expansion and development of airports including those in metro cities is a continuous process and is undertaken by AAI or the concerned airport operator from time to time depending upon commercial viability, traffic demand, availability of land etc. Issues of land availability for expansion and development of airports are regularly taken up with the Concerned stakeholders including respective State Governments. Based on the demand, the concerned airport operators at Delhi, Chennai, Kolkata, Bengaluru and Hyderabad airports have undertaken expansion work.******
