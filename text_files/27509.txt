                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3187






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.08.2021






Patient load at AIIMS, Delhi




Rahul Kaswan














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a)	whether the number of patients is continuously increasing in AIIMS Delhi;(b) 	if so, the reasons therefor;(c) 	the details of the number of patients visiting AIIMS, State/UT-wise; and(d) 	the necessary steps taken by the Government for the efficient and timely treatment of the patients visiting the said institute?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a)	& (b): Yes. The number of patients coming to AIIMS, New Delhi for treatment in OPD, emergency services as well as hospitalised in patient has seen an increasing trend except for the year 2020-21, which is due to operationalisation of exclusive COVID-19 related services in the Institute.  Excellence in patient care services and patients’ satisfaction are important reasons for it.¬¬¬¬¬¬¬(c):  The number of OPD registrations of patients who visited AIIMS, New Delhi in the last two years State/UT-wise is enclosed at Annexure-I.¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬¬(d):  In order to ensure efficient and timely treatment of the patients visiting AIIMS, New Delhi, new facilities/blocks such as Surgical Block, Mother and Child Block, New Private Ward, Burns and Plastic Surgery, Geriatric Block and New OPD Block have been added.  Further, in order to reduce the load of patients on AIIMS, New Delhi and to correct regional imbalances in availability of tertiary healthcare services, setting up of 22 new AIIMS in different parts of the country has been approved under Pradhan Mantri Swasthya Suraksha Yojana (PMSSY). Six of these AIIMS at Bhopal, Bhubaneswar, Jodhpur, Patna, Raipur and Rishikesh are already functional. *****
