                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1595






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2021






Prices of Steel and Cement




Rajiv Ranjan (Lalan) Singh














                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has taken notice of the big players in the steel and cement industry who are indulging in cartelisation to jack up prices, as per reports; (b) 	if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government thereto along with the  steps being taken by the Government in this regard; (c) 	whether the Government has any plans to set up a regulator to monitor and control the  artificial jacking up of prices of steel and cement by the big players in order to protect and boost the infrastructure sector; and (d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI SOM PARKASH)(a) & (b):	Yes Sir. Complaints regarding cartelisation by cement companies have been received. Competition Commission of India (CCI) is the appropriate authority to deal with such types of complaints. In respect of Cement Sector, CCI has found 7 numbers of contraventions, which are under investigation. In Case No. 29/2010 filed on 26.07.2010 by Builders Association of India Vs. Cement Manufactures & Ors., the Commission imposed total penalty of Rs. 6316.59 Crore on 10 Cement companies and Rs. 73.00 Lakh on Cement Manufacturers’ Association (CMA) u/s 27 of the Competition Act, 2002. The cement companies including CMA have challenged the aforementioned Order of the Commission before National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT) which is under adjudication.¬¬In respect of Steel Sector, CCI has received 7 numbers of complaints, which are under examination.(c) & (d):	There is no proposal under consideration at present for setting up of a regulating authority for steel or cement sectors.*******
