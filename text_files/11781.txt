                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

693






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.02.2020






Reduction of Duty-free Articles




L.S. Tejasvi Surya














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is considering a reduction in the limit of duty-free articles, a person can buy at duty-free stalls at airports, if so, the details of articles, the concurrent limit and the proposed limit thereof and the reasons therefor;(b) whether the Government has conducted a study to estimate the potential gain Indian airports can make by the reduction of the limit of duty-free purchases and if so, the details thereof;(c) the details of the duty-free standards followed articles-wise in different countries like Thailand, Singapore, Australia, New Zealand, USA, United Kingdom, Europe, Russia, Indonesia, China, Japan, Korea and Brazil;(d) whether the limit change in India will conform with global standards and if so, the details thereof; and(e) the yearly turnover recorded at duty-free stores of Indian airports for the last 10 years?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a): Reduction in the limit of duty-free articles a person can buy at duty free stalls at airports was one of the many suggestions made by the Department of Commerce for consideration in the Union Budget 2020-21. However, presently there is no proposal from either by Ministry of Civil Aviation or Department of Revenue on the same.;(b): No such study has been carried out by Ministry of Civil Aviation.;;(c) and (d): As per information received from Department of Commerce, details of duty-free standards followed in different countries is enclosed at Annex. Different countries have different norms for fixing these limits.(e): Upto the year 2014-15, Airports Authority of India (AAI) had awarded the license of Duty Free Stores on fixed license fee basis, for which details of turnover are not maintained. Since the year 2015-16, AAI has awarded Duty Free Stores contracts on revenue share basis at 15 airports, in which the concessionaires have recorded an approximate turnover of Rs. 400 crore per year.
