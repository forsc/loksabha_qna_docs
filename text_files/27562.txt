                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3176






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.08.2021






Damage to Crops by Wild Animals




Ranjeeta Koli














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether guidelines have been issued by the Union Government to State Governments to save crops of farmers from wild animals; (b)	if so, the details thereof; (c)	 whether the said guidelines are being complied with by State Governments; and (d)	 if so, the details thereof along with the reaction of the Union Government thereto?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) and (b) The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change has issued an advisory to States/UTs on 6th February, 2021 to deal with human-wildlife conflict. The measures suggested in the advisory include promotion of crops in forest fringe areas which are unpalatable to wild animals, agro- forestry models which include cash crops like chillies, lemon grass, khus grass etc. suitably mixed with tree/shrub species, and preparation and implementation of a comprehensive long-term plan for alternate cropping by State Agriculture /Horticulture Departments in vulnerable areas.(c) and (d) The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change frames legislations, policies and guidelines on wildlife conservation, management and development and provides financial and technical support to the States/UTs under Centrally Sponsored Schemes. The responsibility of wildlife conservation, management and development and implementation of the policies/guidelines lies with the State Government.	***
