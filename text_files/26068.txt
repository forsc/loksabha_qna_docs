                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1798






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Khelo India Scheme




Arun Sao














                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the salient features and objectives of ‘Khelo India’ Scheme;(b) whether the Government has provided adequate funds for youth and sports activities in the country;(c) if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise including Chhattisgarh;(d) whether the Government has received any proposal under ‘Khelo India’ Scheme for the construction of “swimming pool”, “multipurpose indoor hall”, “synthetic athletic track” in Mungeli district headquarter of Chhattisgarh; and(e) if so, the details thereof and the time by which approval is likely to be accorded by the Government to the said proposals?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF YOUTH AFFAIRS & SPORTS { SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR }(a) The ‘Khelo India – National Programme for Development of Sports’ with the twin objectives of mass participation and promotion of excellence in sports across the country was launched in 2016 and revamped in 2017. The Scheme has following 12 verticals:	i.	Play Field Development	ii.	Utilization and Creation/ Upgradation of Sports Infrastructure	iii.	Sports for Peace and Development	iv.	State Level Khelo India Centers	v.	Annual Sports Competitions 	vi.	Talent Search and Development  	vii.	Sports for Women	viii.	Support to National/Regional/State Sports Academies 	ix.	Promotion of Sports among persons with disabilities	x.	Promotion of rural and indigenous/tribal games	xi.	Physical Fitness of School going Children	xii.	Community Coaching Development(b) and (c) “Sports” being a State subject, the responsibility of development of Sports, including sanctioning adequate funds, planning strategies for improvement/development of youth and sports activities and construction of youth hostels and stadiums, rests with the State/Union Territory Governments. This Ministry supplements their efforts in this regard by providing financial assistance for promotion of sports activities through National Sports Federations, training in Sports Authority of India (SAI) Centres, support under Khelo India Scheme and for youth activities in the country through its organizations namely Nehru Yuva Kendra Sangathan (NYKS) and National Service Scheme (NSS).	State-wise allocation of funds is not done in respect of the Schemes of this Ministry. Details of allocation of funds to this Ministry during the current year for youth and sports activities are given hereunder: - <pre>Department of Youth  AffairsS. No.	Name of scheme	Allocation(Rs. in crore)1.	Secretariat Social Service 	35.002.	Development of Youth Institutions	358.503.	Schemes of Department of Youth Affairs	296.50	Total	690.00</pre><pre>Department of SportsS. No.	Name of scheme	Allocation(Rs. in crore)1.	Development of Sports Institutions 	803.632.	Encouragement and Awards to Sportspersons	363.803.	Khelo India – National Programme for Development of Sports	738.71	Total	1906.14</pre>(d) and (e) Yes, Sir. The Government has received the proposals under ‘Khelo India’ Scheme for the construction of “swimming pool”, “multipurpose indoor hall”, “synthetic athletic track” in Mungeli district headquarter of Chhattisgarh. The proposals have been returned to the State Government for prioritisation and resubmission. Project proposals are sanctioned according to the parameters of the Scheme subject to their completeness, technical feasibility and availability of funds under the Scheme. As such the time period by which approval is likely to be accorded by the Government to the said proposals cannot be ascertained.****
