                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2001






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.07.2019






Quality of Food




Ramcharan Bohra














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the food being served to passengers in trains is not qualitatively good and if so, the details thereof;(b)	whether the Railways has constituted any committee to conduct an enquiry in this regard;(c)	if so, the details and the outcomes thereof;(d)	whether this Government has received any complaints regarding quality of food over the years;(e)	if so, the action taken by the Government in this regard; and(f)	the steps taken/being taken by the Government to make the quality of food better in future?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a): No, Sir. However, it is continuous endeavour of Indian Railways to provide good quality catering services. Some complaints regarding quality of food are received from time to time which are appropriately addressed, data for which can be seen in the appendix. (b & c): Railways has not constituted any Committee to conduct an enquiry regarding food quality. However, Third Party Audits are conducted by IRCTC to check the quality of food on trains. (d & e): The number of complaints received regarding quality of food during the last three years along with the action taken in this regard are appended.(f):  Steps taken to improve quality of catering services: To provide safe, healthy and quality food to passengers, a new Catering Policy had been issued on 27th February 2017. As per Catering Policy 2017, Indian Railway Catering and Tourism Corporation Limited (IRCTC) has been mandated to carry out unbundling by creating a distinction primarily between food preparation and food distribution on trains. To ensure hygienic and safe food to passengers, following measures have been taken by Railways:I.	In order to upgrade quality of food preparation, IRCTC has upgraded 46 Kitchen Units in the last two years.  CCTV cameras have been installed in 38 Base Kitchens for monitoring purposes and web-based live streaming facility is being extended to these kitchens.II.	To upgrade the quality of meals to be served to the passengers, menus  are being rationalized by IRCTC.III.	Branded food providers have been roped in to provide e-catering services in trains.IV.	Food Safety Supervisors have been deployed at Kitchen Units to monitor food safety and hygiene practices.V.	Regular and surprise inspections are conducted by Railway officials including Food Safety Officers.VI.	System of printing barcode and other details like name of kitchen, date of packaging etc. on food packets has been initiated at few places and it is planned to upscale this all over.*****
