                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2724






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.12.2019






Decline in Number of Customers of BSNL and MTNL




M. K. Raghavan














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the reasons for the reported decline in the number of customers of Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) and Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Limited (MTNL) during the last three years; (b) 	whether the Government has conducted any study to understand and rectify drawbacks, if any, prevailing in the aforesaid Telecom companies and if so, the details thereof; and (c) 	whether the Government proposes to close the operation of these companies and promote their private counterparts and if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF COMMUNICATIONS, LAW & JUSTICE AND ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI RAVI SHANKAR PRASAD)(a) 	Number of customers of Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) and Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Limited (MTNL) for the last three years is as under:<pre>S. No.	PSU	 Number of customers (in crore) as on		31.03.2017	31.03.2018	31.03.20191	BSNL	11.51	12.41	12.692	MTNL	0.71	0.69	0.67</pre>Despite stiff competition, BSNL has managed to increase its customers.  However, there is marginal decline in the number of customers of MTNL.(b) 	BSNL and MTNL are incurring losses since 2009-10.  On the basis of their financial performance, BSNL and MTNL were classified by Department of Telecommunications (DoT) as “Incipient sick CPSE” in 2017 in accordance with the guidelines issued by the Department of Public Enterprises (DPE) and initiated the process for preparation of restructuring/revival plan for BSNL/MTNL. A comprehensive revival plan for BSNL/MTNL has since been approved by the Cabinet on 23.10.2019 which inter-alia, includes reduction in staff cost through voluntary retirement scheme (VRS), administrative allotment of spectrum to BSNL/MTNL for providing 4G services, monetization of assets of BSNL/MTNL, debt restructuring through sovereign guarantee bonds and in-principle approval for merger of BSNL and MTNL.(c) 	At present, there is no proposal to close the operations of BSNL and MTNL.************
