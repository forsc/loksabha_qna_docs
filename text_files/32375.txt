                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3807






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.12.2021






Development of Rural Areas under CSR




Diya Kumari














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether any new initiatives have been taken for equitable development of rural areas under Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) during the last three years, if so, the details thereof;(b) 	the details of expenditure incurred on rural development under Corporate Social Responsibility during each of the last three years; and(c) 	the specific areas under which these CSR activities have been carried out?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a) & (b)   As per the information shared by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MoCA), a broad framework for Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) has been provided through Section 135 of the Companies Act 2013 (‘Act’), Schedule VII of the Act and Companies (CSR Policy) Rules, 2014. Schedule VII of the Act indicates the activities that can be undertaken by the company as eligible CSR activities. Further, MoCA has clarified that items mentioned in Schedule VII are broad based and must be interpreted liberally to capture the essence of the subject enumerated. Item number (x) of Schedule VII mentions "Rural Development Projects" as eligible CSR activities. On the basis of filing made by companies in the MoCA registry, CSR funds spent by various companies for "Rural Development Projects" under the Act for the financial years 2017-18, 2018-19 and 2019-20 are given below:S.No.	Financial Year	      Expenditure incurred for the Rural Development projects (In Crores)1	2017-18	                      1,724.072	2018-19 	              2,428.453	2019-20	                      2,279.20*Source: National CSR Data Portal. All data related to CSR (State/UT-wise as well as development sector wise) as filed by companies in MoCA 21 registry is available in public domain at www.csr.gov.in. The expenditure details for FY 2020-21 are not yet available with MoCA.Further, the Ministry of Rural Development has leveraged the funds under CSR for the convergence-based schemes such as Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana (SAGY) and Shyama Prasad Mukherji Rurban Mission (SPMRM) for converging private / corporate investments with the government initiatives for the development of Gram Panchayats/clusters identified under these schemes. The details of expenditure incurred in the clusters of Shyama Prasad Mukherji Rurban Mission (SPMRM) are placed at Annexure-I. However, the expenditure incurred under CSR in SAGY is not maintained centrally by this Ministry.(c)    As per the information shared by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, the CSR activities are carried out in line with the activities indicated in the Schedule VII of the Act and Companies (CSR Policy) Rules, 2014. The same is placed at Annexure-II. *****
