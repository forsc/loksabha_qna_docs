                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1748






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Increasing Milk Production




Sukhbir Singh Jaunapuria













                                                    Ramcharan Bohra
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of total milk production in the country;(b)	whether the Government has fixed any target or prepared any scheme to increase milk production, if so, the details thereof and the steps being taken by the Government to achieve the target;(c)	whether the Government proposes to provide any incentive to the States in this regard;(d)	if so, the details thereof;(e)	whether the Government is concerned about the fact that most of the milk supply agencies in the country are facing the challenge of adulteration; and(f)	if so, the steps being taken by the Government to ensure the supply of pure and nutritious milk?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING (DR. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)Part (a) the total estimated milk production in the country is 176.35 million tonnes during 2017-18. Part (b) The National Action Plan for Dairy Development envisages milk production target of 254.55 Million Tonnes by 2022. This Department is supplementing efforts of the State Governments to develop dairy infrastructure across the country by providing financial support through Dairy Development Schemes namely, National Programme for Dairy Development (NPDD), Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme, National Dairy Plan-I, Supporting Dairy Cooperatives and Farmer Producer Organizations engaged in dairy activities and Dairy Processing and Infrastructure Development Fund (DIDF)  aiming at boosting up of milk production in the country..Cont.. (c) & (d) There is provision for providing central assistance/subsidy varying from 25% to 100% depending on dairy components has been made under various schemes. (e) & (f) As implementation and enforcement of Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006 primarily lies with State/UT Governments, Commissioners of Food Safety of States/UTs are regularly conducting surveillance and enforcement drives to curb the adulteration in milk and milk products. Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) has been strengthening food testing infrastructure by notifying more labs on a continuous basis.  FSSAI is also providing assistance to States/UTs to strengthen the State Food Testing Labs and is also providing Mobile Food Testing Labs besides spreading awareness among the consumers about food safety. The Department of Animal Husbandry & Dairying also supports cooperatives under National Programme for Dairy Development for purchasing equipment for milk adulteration testing.Under dairy development programmes mainly, National Programme for Dairy Development (NPDD) and Dairy Processing and Infrastructure Development Fund (DIDF), this Department has been assisting dairy cooperatives to strengthen and modernise milk procurement and distribution infrastructure to ensure supply of pure and nutritious milk across the country.
