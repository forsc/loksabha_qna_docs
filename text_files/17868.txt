                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1605






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.09.2020






Enhancing Agricultural Studies




Abdul Khaleque














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the steps taken to enhance agricultural studies in India; (b)	the number of Central Government institutes imparting Bachelors degrees and Masters degrees in agriculture studies in India; and (c)	whether the Government will consider establishing a Central Agriculture University in Assam, particularly in lower Assam, if so, the details of the same?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)	To popularize and enhance higher agricultural education, the Plan scheme “Strengthening and Development of Higher Agricultural Education in India” is in operation.The Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) provides limited need based funding for enhancing and strengthening student and faculty amenities, student hostels, modernization of laboratories, smart and virtual classrooms and state of the art equipment for Central instrumentation laboratory.  ICAR also supports curriculum development and Post Graduate research in basic and frontier science subjects.  Towards entrepreneurship development amongst students, ‘Student READY’ (Rural Entrepreneurship Awareness Development Yojana) programme has been implemented.To attract and sustain the interest of youth in agriculture education the Council provides Stipend and Fellowships/assistance under various programs.  Assistance in the form of fellowship for global exposure of the agriculture students is also provided.  The details of various fellowship/stipend/assistance programmes are given in Annexure-1. National Agricultural Education Project with World Bank support has also been implemented to revamp and reorient the higher agricultural education in the country and ensure reforms and quality assurance.ICAR conducts All India Entrance Examination for Admission (AIEEA) to 15% seats in Bachelor degree programmes and 25% seats each in Master’s and Doctoral degree programmes in accredited Agricultural Universities (AUs) under the ICAR-AU system. It ensures Pan-India competition and better quality of students.(b)	Three (03) Central Agricultural Universities (CAUs), four (04) ICAR Deemed Universities and four (04) Central Universities with Agricultural Faculty are imparting Bachelors degrees and/or Masters degrees in agriculture studies in the Country.(c)	No such proposal is under consideration.*******
