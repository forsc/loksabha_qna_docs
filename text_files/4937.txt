                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4457






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.07.2019






Local Content by Prasar Bharati




Dayanidhi Maran














                        Will the Minister of





INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the steps taken by Prasar Bharati to offer localized/regional content;(b)	the reasons that the short wave services are being considered to be taken off air despite their strategic use as a soft-diplomacy tool;(c)	whether the Ministry has taken any steps to increase the popularity of short wave, if so, the details thereof; and (d)	whether the Ministry is planning to strengthening broadcast infrastructure and network development and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE; AND MINISTER OF INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING (SHRI PRAKASH JAVADEKAR)(a) Prasar Bharati is offering localized/regional content through its 17 Doordarshan Regional Language Satellite Channels (RLSS) and through the 90 local radio stations of All India Radio network which broadcasts in all languages in the Eighth Schedule of the Indian Constitution, and in over 178 major dialects.   All the regional content is accessible through the Prasar Bharati Global Digital Platform NewsOnAir, on more than 70 YouTube channels and the websites of DD, AIR, AIR News and DD News. (b) & (c) Prasar Bharati Board has decided to close down obsolete Shortwave transmitters in Phased manner, which have outlived their useful life. However, in view of strategic significance of Shortwave, Prasar Bharati Board has decided to continue 3 Digital Radio (DRM) capable Shortwave Transmitters which are based on modern technology.(d)   		The Central Government has approved an amount of Rs. 1054.52 crore ùnder the Scheme of Prasar Bharati “Broadcasting Infrastructure and Network Development” for 3 years (2017-20) for strengthening infrastructure of Doordarshan and All India Radio. ***********************
