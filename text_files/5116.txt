                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4728






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2019






Borrowings from Foreign Countries




M. Selvaraj














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the Government borrowings, loans, credits  and grants from foreign countries especially from Non-Governmental foundations and voluntary organizations as on date, category/foundation/country-wise;(b) the details of rules under which it can be availed from foreign companies;(c) the details of restrictions, if any, for utilizing such funds in India; and(d) the details of pending loans and credits as on date?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR)(a)	to (d):  India accepts external assistance from bilateral partners in the form of loans, grants and technical assistance for development of infrastructure, social sector and for enhancement of knowledge/skills of Indian nationals at both Centre and States level.  The policy guidelines on official development assistance for development cooperation with bilateral partners issued on 07.12.2015, is available at ‘https://dea.gov.in/sites/default/files/GuidelineBDC_2.pdf’. 		The details of country-wise borrowings for various projects as on 12.07.2019 is at Annexure-I.
