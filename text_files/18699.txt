                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

47






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.02.2021






Reduction of Food Subsidy Bill




Sisir Kumar Adhikari














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether it is a fact that the Government has massively reduced the food subsidy bill from Rs 1.84 lakh crore to Rs 1.15 lakh crore in 2020-2021 budget which has indirectly impacted the livelihood of the farmers; (b) 	if so, whether FCI, whose share in the food subsidy bill last year was Rs 1.51 lakh crore had spent just Rs 75,000 crore in 2019-2020 therein; and (c) 	if so, the reasons behind the gap between allocation and actual spending during the last five years?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E R MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a) and (b): 	Food Subsidy is given by the Department of Food and Public Distribution to the Food Corporation of India (FCI) and to States directly in case of Decentralized Procurement (DCP) on the basis of quantity of foodgrains procured and distributed under National Food Security Act (NFSA) and Other Welfare Schemes (OWS). As per budgetary allocation provided by Ministry of Finance, Department of Food and Public Distribution releases food subsidy to FCI and DCP States. An amount of Rs. 1.84 lakh crore food subsidy (Rs. 1.51 lakh crore in Food subsidy to FCI + Rs. 0.33 lakh crore for Food subsidy to DCP States) was provided in BE 2019-20 and the same has been reduced to 1.15 lakh crore (Rs. 0.78 lakh crore Food subsidy to FCI + Rs. 0.37 lakh crore for subsidy to DCP States) in BE 2020-21.  Out of the total food subsidy of Rs. 1.51 lakh crore in BE 2019-20, an amount of Rs. 75000 crore only was released as per the directions of Ministry of Finance.
