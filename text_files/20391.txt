                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1767






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2021






PMKSY in Rajasthan




Hanuman Beniwal














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of physical progress, financial expenditure etc. for the rainfed agricultural areas under the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) in comparison to the target set by the Union Government in the context of Rajasthan State during the last three years, district-wise; (b) whether there has been any correspondence by the Government of Rajasthan with the Union Government regarding the approval of new scheme for water harvesting; and (c) if so, the action taken by the Union Government so far?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a)  As informed by the Department of Land Resources (DoLR), Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India, Integrated Watershed Management Programme (IWMP) is being implemented since 2009-10, for development of rainfed and degraded areas. In 2015-16, IWMP was amalgamated in the Watershed Development Component of the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (WDC-PMKSY). The activities undertaken inter-alia include ridge area treatment, drainage line treatment, soil and moisture conservation, rainwater harvesting, nursery raising, afforestation, horticulture, pasture development, livelihoods for asset less persons, etc. As further informed by DoLR, the overall target for the scheme of PMKSY-WDC is 11.5 lakh hectare, against which 13.3 lakh hectare has been brought under protective irrigation from 2015-16 to December, 2020.
