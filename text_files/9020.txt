                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2568






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.12.2019






Corporatisation of Coach Factories




M.K. Vishnu Prasad














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there is any plan to corporatize Coach Factories like Rae Bareli, Modern Rail Coach Factory, RCF units in Kapurthala and ICF in Chennai;(b) if so, whether all the stakeholders like contractors, employees, State Government etc. have been consulted;(c) the rationale behind corporatization of these coach factories;(d) whether the interests of workers, both direct and indirect, including contract workers would be taken care of and if so, the details thereof; and(e) the measures taken to ensure that the career prospects and terms and conditions of workers are not altered to their detriment, with details in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY( SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL )(a) to (e) A statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT  REFERRED  TO  IN  REPLY  TO  PARTS   (a)  TO  (e)  OF  UNSTARRED QUESTION NO. 2568 BY DR. VISHNU PRASAD M.K.  TO BE ANSWERED IN LOK SABHA ON 04.12.2019 REGARDING CORPORATISATION OF COACH FACTORIES(a) Ministry of Railways has envisaged preparation of an action plan for corporatization of its Production Units including Rail Coach Factories.(b) Consultations with stakeholders in this process has commenced and will continue as part of this initiative.(c) Corporatization is expected to be attended with the following benefits:(i)	State-of-the-art technology and modernization(ii)	Managerial autonomy(iii)	Superior operational efficiency due to better management practices(iv)	Export capability(v)	Increase Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME) business(vi)	Employment generation(vii)	Better benefits to employees(viii)	Making India an international hub for rolling stock manufacturing(ix)	Attract investments.(d) Such issues are to be decided in consultation with stakeholders at an appropriate later stage.(e) Does not arise.*****
