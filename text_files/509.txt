                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

586






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.06.2019






Rural Development Gap Analysis Survey




Balashowry Vallabbhaneni














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it is true that as per the Rural Development Gap Analysis Survey conducted by the Ministry, more than one third of the top ranking Panchayats are from Andhra Pradesh;(b)	if so, the details thereof; and(c)	the special incentives that the Ministry proposed to give to performing States and Panchayats to give a boost for performing in a better way?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) & (b):	Yes, one third GPs in Andhra Pradesh have scored 75 which is the benchmarking for good performing GPs. The details of such Panchayats is attached at Annex.(c):	Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan scheme of Ministry of Panchayati Raj, activities of which are broadly aligned for achieving Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) with thrust on Aspirational Districts as well, encompasses Incentivization of Panchayats scheme as one of its central components. As an encouragement for recognizing good work for improving delivery of services and public goods, best performing Panchayats or similar level bodies/States/UTs are incentivized through awards and financial incentives under Incentivization of Panchayats scheme. The awards are generally given on the National Panchayati Raj  Day celebrated on 24th April every year under various categories namely (i) Deendayal Upadhay Panchayat Shashaktikaran Puraskar (under General and Thematic categories for District, Intermediate and Gram panchayats) (ii) Nanaji Deshmukh Rashtriya Gaurav Gram Sabha Puraskar (for Gram Panchayats) (iii) Gram Panchayat Development Plan Award (iv) Child friendly Gram Panchayat award (introduced during March 2019) and (v) e-Panchayat Puraskar(for State/UTs only, this award has no financial incentive). This incentivization also create models for others to follow, focuses public attention on performance of the awarded rural local bodies /States/UTs and creates an eco-system  for overall good governance at local level.***** Annexure referred to in reply to part (a) & (b) of Lok Sabha Unstarred Q.
