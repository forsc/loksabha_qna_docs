                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1769






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2021






Jal Jeevan Mission




Nihal Chand Chauhan













                                                    Jaskaur Meena
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the salient features of Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM);(b) 	the funds sanctioned, allocated and utilized in Rajasthan under JJM during the last three years;(c) 	the details of the number of villages in Ganganagar and Dausa districts in Rajasthan covered thereunder;(d) 	the details of the targets set and achievements made under JJM so far particularly in regard to Ganganagar district; and(e) 	the steps taken by the Government to ensure speedy implementation of this Mission with a view to realizing its objectives in Rajasthan?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) 	The key features of the Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) are provisioning of potable water to every rural household through Functional Household Tap Connections (FHTCs) at a service level of 55 litre per capita per day (lpcd) of prescribed quality (BIS:10500) and supply on a regular and long-term basis.(b) 	The year-wise details of the Central fund allocated and drawn by the State of Rajasthan under erstwhile National Rural Drinking Water Programme (2017-18 & 2018-19) and JJM (2019-20) in last three years, is asunder:    (Amount in Rs. Crore)Financial Year	Central Fund	State share expenditure	Opening Balance	Fund allocated	Fund drawn by the State	Expenditure reported	2017-18	550.09	891.95	714.24	728.81	1,253.682018-19	536.75	692.13	655.41	878.48	887.052019-20	313.67	1,301.71	1,301.71	620.31	698.54 (c) 	The details of villages in Ganganagar and Dausa districts in Rajasthan as reported by the State is as under:											(HHs in lakhs)District Name	Total no. of villages	Villages with Piped Water Supply (PWS) system	Villages without PWS system		No. of villages	No. of households	No. of Households with tap connection	No. of Villages	No. of households Dausa	1,071	661	1.81	0.19	410	0.70Ganganagar	2,853	1,912	2.32	0.60	941	0.64                                                                                                  (Source: JJM – IMIS)(d) 	At the time of announcement of JJM, 17% (3.23 Crore) rural households (HHs) in the country were having tap water connections and by 31.3.2021, it was planned to have tap water supply in 34% rural households. Against this, so far 3.43 Crore households have been provided with tap water connections and as on date, 34.80% (6.67 Crore) rural households have tap water supply in the country. Since launch of JJM, 6.23 lakh (6.15%) households in Rajasthan have been provided with tap water connections. In the same period, about 46 thousand (15.52%) households have been provided with tap water connections in Ganganagar district.(e) 	A number of steps have been taken to plan and implement JJM with speed, inter alia which includes planning, regular review of implementation, workshops and conferences for capacity building and knowledge sharing, field visits by multi-disciplinary team to provide technical support, etc. A detailed Operational Guidelines for the implementation of JJM, Margdarshika for the use of Gram Panchayats/ Village Water & Sanitation Committee/ Pani Samiti and 100–days campaign guidelines have been provided to States/ UTs to facilitate planning and implementation. For online monitoring, JJM–Integrated Management Information System and JJM–Dashboard has been put in place along with provision for transparent online financial management through Public Financial Management System (PFMS). ***
