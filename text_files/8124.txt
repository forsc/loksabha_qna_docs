                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1715






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.11.2019






Development of Smart Cities




Sunil Kumar Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	whether the work of developing 15 cities as smart cities is likely to be started with the cost of Rs. 1770 crore this year and if so, the details thereof;(b) 	whether the work of AMRUT Scheme is also going to be started with that of the smart cities under which there is a plan to develop around 500 cities and if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the AMRUT Scheme is an initial part of the smart city project/ mission; and (d) 	if so, the details of the funds likely to be incurred on 500 cities under AMRUT Scheme? 






ANSWER




                                                        (a):	No, Sir. (b) Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme which was launched by the Hon’ble Prime Minister on 25.06.2015 for infrastructure projects in 500 cities across the country. The thrust areas of the AMRUT Mission are water supply, sewerage & septage management, storm water drainage, green spaces & parks, non-motorized urban transport and capacity building.  Smart Cities Mission was launched by the Hon’ble Prime Minister on 25.6.2015 and is being implemented in 100 cities across the country. (c): AMRUT Mission and Smart Cities Mission are separate Missions of Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs (MoHUA).  MoHUA encourages convergence in the implementation of both Missions. (d) : The total outlay under AMRUT is Rs. One Lakh Crore including a Central Assistance of Rs. 50,000 crore spread over 5 years i.e. from FY 2015-2016 to FY 2019-2020. The balance share of Rs. 50,000 crore has to come from States and Urban Local Bodies.                               *****
