                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

229






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2020






Vacancies in Kendriya Vidyalayas in Telangana




Manne Srinivas Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of Kendriya Vidyalayas (KVs) in Telangana, district-wise;(b) whether it is true that there are a large number of vacancies of teachers in KVs;(c) if so, the details of vacancies, KV-wise including posts of Principals and Vice-Principals; and(d) the steps taken to fill in the posts and by when all the vacancies are going to befilled?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) As on date, 35 Kendriya Vidyalayas (KVs) are functioning in the State of Telangana.District-wise details are at Annexure-I.(b)& (c) Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan has informed that as on 15.11.2019, there are 178teaching posts lying vacant against 1293 sanctioned posts in different KVs located in theState of Telangana. The KVs wise details of vacancies of teachers (including Principals &Vice-principal)are at Annexure-II.(d) During the year 2019-20 (upto January, 2020) 366 teachers including PGTs/TGTsand PRTs were appointed in the KVs in Telangana. Teachers are also engaged oncontractual basis for temporary duration by KVS to ensure that the teaching-learningprocess is not hampered.Filling up of vacancies is a continuous process and actionis taken from time to time as per the provisions of the relevant recruitment rules. Asthe process of filling up of vacancies involves various methods of recruitment suchas promotion, Limited Departmental Examination, Direct Recruitment etc. and takestime, no specific time frame for filling up of vacant posts can be given at this stage.Directions have also been issued to autonomous organizations includingKVSfrom time to time, for reviewing their vacancies and for taking prompt action forfilling up of vacant posts in the Vidyalayas.******
