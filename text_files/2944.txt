                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2515






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.07.2019






Support for Increasing Milk Production




Ashok Kumar Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 whether there is any plan to seek the Centre’s as well as the World Bank’s support to increase the milk production in the State of Uttar Pradesh in view of the difference between demand for and production of milk in the country; (b)	 if so, the details thereof; and(c)	 the steps taken or proposed to be taken by the Government to ensure the availability of milk according to the demand for milk in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY & DAIRYING (DR. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a)	& (b) Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing World Bank assisted National Dairy Plan – I in 18 States including Uttar Pradesh to support milk cooperatives and milk producer companies along with breeding improvement initiative. Besides this, this Department is implementing following Dairy Development Schemes for increase milk production across the country including Uttar Pradesh: (i)	Rashtriya Gokul Mission with the aim to develop and conserve indigenous breeds and enhancing their production and productivity-Central assistance. (ii)	Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme is being implemented with the objective of generation of self-employment opportunities in the dairy sector, covering activities such as enhancement of milk production, procurement, preservation, transportation, processing and marketing of milk-Central assistance.(iii)	National Programme for Dairy Development is being implemented with objective to create  infrastructure for milk procurement,  processing and marketing-Central assistance  (c)	The milk production during 2017-18 was 176.35 million tonnes. The milk production   at national level is by and large sufficient to meet the domestic demand of milk and milk products. The schemes for this are mentioned above.*****
