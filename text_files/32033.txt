                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3471






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






Plantation Drive




Chandeshwar Prasad














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the density of forest cover and green belt existing in the country in the last three years, and the number of trees added through plantation drives in the forest area, State-wise and year-wise; and (b) 	the measures undertaken to promote plantation, preservation and irrigation and the expenditure incurred on plantation corresponding to the target fixed, in this regard, during the rainy season and thereafter, in the year 2021, State-wise including Bihar?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) Assessment of Forest & Tree cover is undertaken by Forest Survey of India (FSI) biennially. As per the India State of Forest Report-2019 (ISFR 2019) the total forest and tree cover in the country is 8,07,276 sq km i.e. Very dense forest 99,278 sq km, Moderately dense forest 3,08,472 sq km, Open forest 3,04,499 sq km and Tree cover 95,027 sq km, which is 24.56% of the geographical area of the country. There is an increase of 5,188Sq. Km.in forest and tree cover i.e.Very Dense Forest (VDF) by 1,120 sq. km., Moderately Dense Forest (MDF) by 154 sq. km., Open Forest by 2,702 sq. km and Tree cover by 1212 sq. km. with respect to ISFR 2017. This increase in forest cover is due to plantation and conservation efforts taken up across the country under various schemes of Central and State/UT government including mass plantation drives. The State and Union Territories wise details of forest and tree cover as per ISFR 2019 are given at Annexure I.(b) The Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change supports the States/Union Territories for carrying out various afforestation activities through Centrally Sponsored Scheme namely National Mission for a Green India (GIM), National Afforestation Programme (NAP) and transfers fund under CAMPA to promote plantation, preservation and increase the soil & moisture conservation and green cover across the country. Further, the Ministry implements the Forest Fire Prevention and Management Scheme to extend support to the State/ UT Governments in forest fire prevention and management to prevent destruction of forests. Annual afforestation/tree plantation targets are fixed by the Ministry for all States/UTs including Bihar which are achieved through the concerted efforts. The State/UT wise details of afforestation/tree plantation targets fixed for the year 2021-22 are given in Annexure II.*****
