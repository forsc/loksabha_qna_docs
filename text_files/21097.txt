                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2335






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.03.2021






Agricultural Marketing Infrastructure




B. Y. Raghavendra













                                                    Anurag Sharma
                                                








                                                    Vijay Kumar Dubey
                                                








                                                    Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar
                                                








                                                    Dhanush M Kumar
                                                








                                                    Ganesan Selvam
                                                








                                                    C.N. Annadurai
                                                








                                                    Gautham Sigamani Pon
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is implementing capital investment subsidy sub-scheme to develop Agricultural Marketing Infrastructure including storage infrastructure and if so, the details thereof along with its components;(b) 	whether the Government has been able to create scientific storage capacity with allied facilities in backward rural areas of SC/ST in the country including in Karnataka, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu and Uttar Pradesh under Gramin Bhandaran Yojana;(c) 	the total number of rural godowns constructed till date and the total number of farmers benefited under this scheme particularly in the States of Karnataka, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu and Uttar Pradesh;(d) 	whether the Government has been able to increase storage capacity derived under the scheme, if so, the details thereof, State-wise;(e)	the amount of subsidy released during the last three years and the current year for the same, State-wise including the States of Karnataka, Maharashtra, TamilNadu and Uttar Pradesh; and(f) 	the other steps taken by the Government to build more storage facility for agriculture industry?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????		(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):	Yes Sir. For creation of Agricultural Marketing infrastructure including Scientific Storage infrastructure, the Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Govt. of India is implementing sub-scheme “Agricultural Marketing Infrastructure (AMI)” of Integrated Scheme for Agricultural Marketing (ISAM) across the country. During the XII plan, the two on-going schemes of Ministry of Agriculture & FW viz. (i) Grameen Bhandaran Yojana (GBY) implemented since 01.04.2001 and (ii) Scheme for Development / Strengthening of Agricultural Marketing Infrastructure, Grading & Standardization (AMIGS) implemented since 20.10.2004 has been subsumed into AMI w.e.f. 01.04.2014. There are two components under the scheme (i) Storage Infrastructure (ii) Marketing Infrastructure other than Storage. It is a demand driven, credit linked scheme wherein back ended capital subsidy @ 25% and 33.33% is available based on the eligible category of beneficiary.  Assistance is available to Individuals, Farmers, Group of farmers/growers, Agri-preneurs, Registered Farmer Produce Organizations (FPOs), Cooperatives, and state agencies etc.(b):	Yes Sir. Under AMI sub-scheme, Government is promoting creation of scientific storage capacity with allied facilities in the country including that in backward rural areas of SC/ST, which also includes the states of Karnataka, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu and Uttar Pradesh.  (c):	Since inception of the scheme, a total of 39,928 storage infrastructure projects (Godowns) have been assisted in the country, including 10,524 projects in Karnataka, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu and Uttar Pradesh. A total of 7 lakh farmers have been benefited through cooperatives under this scheme in the States of Karnataka, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu and Uttar Pradesh.(d):	Yes Sir. Since the launch of the Grameen Bhandaran Yojana (GBY) in 2001, a total of 68.20 million MT storage capacity has been assisted so far under the scheme. The state-wise details are at Annexure I.(e):	The State-wise subsidy released for storage component of Agricultural Marketing Infrastructure (AMI) sub-scheme during the last three years and the current year, including the States of Karnataka, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu and Uttar Pradesh is at Annexure II.(f):	In addition to AMI scheme, Government is providing financial support under Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF) in terms of interest subvention and credit guarantee for projects for post-harvest management and building community farming assets such as warehouse, cold-storage, silos, e-marketing etc. The Government is also promoting storage facilities under scheme of Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana - Remunerative Approaches for Agriculture & Allied Sector Rejuvenation (RKVY-RAFTAAR). Government had also been building more storage facilities for procurement purpose and for agriculture industry through agencies such as Food Corporation of India (FCI) and Central Warehouse Corporation (CWC) etc.
