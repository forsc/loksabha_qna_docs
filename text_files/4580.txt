                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4236






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.07.2019






Augmentation of Air Connectivity under UDAN Scheme in Jharkhand




Sanjay Seth














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the names of cities, new routes and closed airports which have been selected for operation of flight service under UDAN scheme in the country, State/UT-wise including Jharkhand;(b) whether the Union Government proposes to commence a third phase of the regional connectivity scheme, UDAN -3 to augment air connectivity to remote locations in the country including Jharkhand and if so, the details thereof;(c) the States with maximum number of airports and helipads which would see activation/operation under UDAN-2 Scheme;(d) whether the Union Government proposes to make it mandatory for the private air operators to sell their tickets at concessional rates to enhance air connectivity and if so, the details thereof;(e) the details of Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed by the Governments of Jharkhand and Karnataka with AAI to develop airports in the States; and(f) the funds allocated/spent and achievements/progress made under UDAN scheme so far?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) & (b): Under three rounds of bidding in Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS)-UDAN, 106 airports/waterdromes (76 Unserved, 20 Underserved and 10 waterdromes) and 31 heliports have been selected for air operations and routes awarded to Selected Airline Operators. List of such airports/waterdromes and heliports are at Annexure A and B respectively. Out of these, 40 RCS - UDAN Airports (17 underserved & 23 unserved) have been revived/developed for operationalization of RCS flights. The list of these airports is at Annexure-C. ;;(c):Maximum number of RCS airports & heliports have been awarded for operation of RCS flights in Uttarakhand. ;(d):  The primary objective of RCS is to facilitate/stimulate regional air connectivity by making it affordable. Under the Scheme, an airfare cap has been kept for an RCS seat only.;;(e) : Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) for development of existing airport at Dhalbhumgarh between Government of Jharkhand and Airports Authority of India was signed on 24.01.2019.;Another Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) for development of existing airport at Deoghar between Government of Jharkhand, Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Airports Authority of India was signed on 25.03.2017.AAI has not signed any MoU with Government of Karnataka to develop airports in the State.;;(f): Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs had approved the proposal on 06 March, 2017 for revival of existing unserved/ underserved airports / airstrips of the State Governments, Airports Authority of India and Civil Enclaves at an estimated cost of Rs. 4500 Crore. However, the revival of these unserved/underserved airports is ''demand driven'', depending upon firm commitment from airline operators as well as from the State Governments for providing various concessions.  To operationalize 186  routes, Rs.256 crore has been spent towards VGF. Details of the amount sanctioned and expenditure incurred for revival of development of airports of rounds 1 and 2 under RCS-UDAN are attached as Annexure -D.
