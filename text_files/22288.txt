                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3332






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2021






Publication of Government Notifications




A.K.P. Chinraj














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government notifications, Acts etc. are published only in Hindi and English and if so, the details thereof;(b) the reasons for not notifying all Government Notifications, Acts in Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Odia, Bengali; and(c) the estimated number of people from Non-Hindi speaking States who would understand the said Notifications, Acts?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a):	As per Section 3(3)(i) of the Official Languages Act, 1963 both Hindi and the English languages shall be used for resolutions, general orders, rules, notifications, administrative or other reports or press communiqués issued or made by the Central Government or by a Ministry, Department or office thereof or by a corporation or company owned or controlled by the Central Government or by any office of such corporation or company.However as per the provisions of the Authoritative Texts (Central Laws) Act, 1973  a translation in any language  (other than Hindi) specified -2-L.S.US.Q.NO.3332 FOR 16.03.2021in the Eighth Schedule to the Constitution, published under the authority of the President in the Official Gazette of any Central Act or of any Ordinance promulgated by the President, or of any order, rule, regulation or bye-law issued under the Constitution or under any Central Act, shall be deemed to be the authoritative text thereof in such language.(b):	On enactment of Constitution of India on 26 January 1950, it was decided that the Official Language of the Union of India will be Hindi and its script Devanagari, it was also decided that the English language will also be used for the next 15 years along with Hindi. Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Oriya and Bengali are included in the Eighth Schedule of the Union.(c):	As per Data on Language and Mother Tongue in Census 2011 of India, the details of Hindi and English Speaking population in Non-Hindi states (Region C) is at annexure.******
