                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3493






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






Green India Mission




Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao













                                                    Rajeshbhai Naranbhai Chudasama
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it is a fact that the Government had launched Green India Mission for protecting, restoring and enhancing India’s diminishing forest cover and for responding to climate change in 2014, with an outlay of Rs. 60,000 crore and if so, the details of the Mission; (b)	whether it is also a fact that the Government has allocated proportionate funds during each of the previous financial years and if so, the details thereof;(c)	the major works undertaken in each State out of the funds allocated and their visible effect on ground;(d)	whether it is also a fact that the Government proposes to revisit the Mission to fine tune it to achieve physical progress on ground; and(e)	if so, the time by which the final decision in this regard is likely to be taken? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to (c) The Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change supports the States/Union Territories for carrying out afforestation activities through Centrally Sponsored Scheme namely National Mission for a Green India (GIM). It aims at protecting, restoring and enhancing India’s forest cover and responding to climate change. GIM is being implemented in convergence with ongoing afforestation schemes of the Ministry namely National Afforestation Programme and funds under CAMPA, and afforestation schemes of line ministries namely Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme, Sub-Mission on Agroforestry, National Bamboo Mission, Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana etc. and State Plan Schemes.  Activities under GIM were started in the year 2015-16 with a financial outlay of Rs. 46,000 crores over the period of ten years. So far, a sum of Rs. 455.75 crore has been released to fourteen states and one union territory during the period 2015-16 to 2020-21 and afforestation activities were taken up over an area of 117757 ha and Alternative fuel energy devices has been distributed to 33,099 households. The funds released during the last six years are given in Annexure-1 and the State wise details of the afforestation undertaken and distribution of alternative fuel energy devices is placed at Annexure-2.(d) & (e) The Ministry has undertaken the revision of Mission document based on the learnings and experiences gained during the implementation and to contribute towards achieving national goals and international commitments.  ****
