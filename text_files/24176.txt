                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

52






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.07.2021






Preservation of Indian Languages in Modern Age




Malook Nagar














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the effective policies of the Government to maintain the existence of Indian Culture against the onslaught of social changes due to the influence of movies; and (b)	the details of the policies of the Government to maintain the cultural integrity?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR CULTURE, TOURISM AND DEVELOPMENT OF NORTH EASTERN REGION(SHRI G.KISHAN REDDY)(a) & (b) Ministry of Culture through its autonomous bodies is Promoting popularising & conserving Indian Culture.  The Centre for Cultural Resources and Training  under the aegis of Ministry of Culture organizes Orientation Courses and Thematic Workshop for In-service Government and Government aided school teachers and teacher educators throughout the year, for dissemination of Indian Culture at the national level by training them for incorporating the cultural component in class room teaching. These programmes help in conservation maintenance and development of diverse culture’s, awareness of  traditions of all parts of the country. The Training Programme introduces the participating teachers/students to the rich fabric of our artistic and cultural heritage. Centre for Cultural Resources and Training emphasizes to incorporate the components of Indian languages and  literatures of classical and folk traditions in all its training programmes for Preservation of Indian Languages in Modern Age.Likewise, for Sanskrit, Tamil and Telugu languages and the relevant ancient literature, Indian Heritage and History are being taught as part of the curriculum for the students of Diploma and Post Diploma Courses run by the Rukmini Devi College of Fine arts of the Kalakshetra Foundation. Centre for Cultural Resources and Training has designed Orientation Courses and Thematic Workshops focusing on promotion of   Indian Tangible and Intangible Cultural Heritages, Folk Art (dance/music/painting) and Craft Traditions.The activities initiated by Centre for Cultural Resources and Training are as follows:i.	Orientation Course ii. 	Thematic Workshops:(a)	 The Workshops are mainly on the following themes:(b)	 Role of Puppetry in Education(c)	 Role of Schools in Conservation of the  Natural  & Cultural Heritage                            (d)	 Integrating Craft Skills in School Education(e)	 Our Cultural Diversity(f)	 Theater Arts in Educationiii.   	Workshop for District Resource Personsiv.   	Distance Learning Programmev.   	Short Term Programmevi.   	E-Learning Workshopvii.  	Project ‘Sanskriti’ at Varanasiviii.  	Centre for North-Eastern CultureThe National School of Drama organizes various  theatre festivals which are not only a source of entertainment and learning but also a means of spreading a message of harmony and unity  and maintaining cultural integrity. The Bharat Rang Mahotsav  is  a national festival  organized by National School of Drama every year that presents the works of  most creative theatre workers in India, it has grown into an international event, hosting theatre companies from around the world.  The last Bharat Rang Mahotsav  included Natya Manthan – a two day round table, with participation by theatre scholars and practitioners from across India and abroad; and Natya Naad – special evenings of theatre music and song featuring the music of stalwarts and groups who have been integral to the theatre tradition of India.Bal SangamEvery alternate year the Theatre in Education Company organizes a national festival, Bal Sangam, which is essentially a cultural mela with an education objective with an ensemble of various performing traditional art forms that are presented by children belonging to traditional performing families, guru-paramparas and Institutions. The main objective of Bal Sangam is to encourage children to continue traditional performances so as to preserve our cultural heritage in this rapidly changing world.JashnebachpanJashne Bachpan showcases the works of different theatre groups working with and for children, representing various regions and languages from across the country.Poorvottar Natya SamarohEach year the National School of Drama, as a part of its Extension Programme, organizes a series of theatre workshops in the North Eastern states in  the form of the Poorvottar Natya Samaroh, the School provides  its students with a platform to showcase these productions. While most performances are in North-Eastern languages like Assamese, Manipuri, Mizo, Rabha, Garo and Nepali, there are also other plays in Bengali, Kannada, Marathi, Urdu and Hindi that are added to give the festival a variety in terms of production styles and texts drawn from a variety of sources. By underlining the unity in the diversity of human life and existence the Festival aims at achieving a space of meaningful interaction and engagement for all.*****
