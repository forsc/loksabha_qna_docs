                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2392






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.08.2021






Assistance to Families displaced by erosion in Assam




Badruddin Ajmal














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has provided any financial assistance to affected families displaced by erosion occuring every year at various locations of Medertari, Fakirganj, Salmara and Hazirhat area of south bank of river Brahmputra in Dhubri, South Salmara and Mankachar districts of Assam and whether they have been allotted alternative patta land; and(b) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) & (b):	As informed by the State Government of Assam, they have provided financial assistance, as per State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) norms, for immediate repairing of damaged houses to the erosion affected families of Dhubri and South Salmara-Mankachar under rehabilitation grant.  In the South Salmara–Mankachar District, 110 numbers of affected families were allotted land in the year 2018. In addition, State Government of Assam has empowered the Deputy Commissioners in the matter of allotment of Government land for ordinary cultivation in the rural areas to those indigenous land owner cultivators who have been rendered landless due to flood, erosion, earthquake and other natural calamities. ******
