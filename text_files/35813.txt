                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3111






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






Employability of PwDs




Abdul Khaleque














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there are schemes to enhance skills of persons with disabilities to increase the chance of their employability;  (b) if so, the detail of the schemes enforced at present;  (c) the number of persons with disabilities who have been trained and employed during the last five years and the current year, State-wise including Assam, districtwise; and  (d) whether there is any scheme whereby persons with disabilities can avail loan and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SUSHRI PRATIMA BHOUMIK)(a)   & (b)   Yes, Sir. With objective to provide skill training to persons with disabilities (PwDs) for enabling them to become self-reliant and productive members of the society in order to achieve gainful wage/self-employment and integrate them with the mainstream of the society, Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities (DEPwD) implements a "National Action Plan (NAP) for Skill Development of Persons with Disabilities (PwDs)" which was launched in March, 2015. NAP is implemented under the umbrella scheme – “Scheme for Implementation of Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 (SIPDA)” as per Common Norms guidelines of M/o Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE). Under NAP, for imparting training to persons with disabilities (Not less than 15 years and not more than 59 years of age), fund is released to training partners (ETPs) empanelled from Government (GOs) and Non-Government Organizations (NGOs) as well as organizations of the Department such as National Handicapped Finance and Development Corporation (NHFDC), National Institutes (NIs) and Composite Regional Centres (CRCs) directly.(c)   The number of persons with disabilities trained and employed through Training Partners in the States including the State of Assam is at Annexure.(d)   National Handicapped Finance and Development Corporation (NHFDC), a Central Public Sector Enterprise (CPSE) under the aegis of Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities implements  two flagship schemes for channelizing concessional loan through its agencies i.e. Divyangjan Swavalamban Yojana (DSY), which is individual centric and Vishesh Microfinance Yojana (VMY), which is for Self Help Groups/Joint Liability groups through various partner agencies for the welfare and rehabilitation of persons with disabilities. A concessional loan upto Rs. 50 Lakh is available under DSY scheme for income generating activities, pursuing higher education/vocational/skill training and purchase of assistive devices for PwDs. Further, a concessional credit upto Rs. 60,000/- is given to PwD for promoting sustainable and inclusive livelihoods under VMY scheme.
