                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    EXTERNAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3746






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2022






Emergency Fund for Citizens living Abroad




Gopal Chinayya Shetty














                        Will the Minister of





EXTERNAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Indian citizens, who have migrated abroad for livelihood, have to face at times many hardships due to lack of money in case of emergency;(b) 	whether the Government has taken any steps to help Indian citizens in abroad in case of emergency and to set up a fund for this purpose; and(c)  	if, so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EXTERNAL AFFAIRS[SHRI V. MURALEEDHARAN](a) to (c) 	In order to help Indian citizens abroad in case of emergency,  Indian Community Welfare Fund (ICWF) was established by the Government in all Indian Missions and Posts abroad. Indian Missions and Posts are utilizing ICWF on a means tested basis to assist Overseas Indian nationals in distress situations (Boarding and Lodging, Air passage, Legal Assistance, Emergency Medical Care, Transportation of Mortal Remains). Community Welfare activities and improvement in Consular Services including provisions for setting of legal panels in countries with sizable Indian diaspora, payment of fines/penalty for petty crimes for release of prisoners and assistance to distressed Indian women abroad are also covered in ICWF.***
