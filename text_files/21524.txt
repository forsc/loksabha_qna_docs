                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2594






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.03.2021






Digital Village Scheme




Y. S. Avinash Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of Electronics & Information Technology be pleased to state: -(a)	whether Digital Village Scheme introduced by the Government is working up to its desired objectives;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	the number of villages identified under this scheme , State/UT wise;(d)	the details of the funds sanctioned under the said scheme, State/UT-wise and the progress achieved by the government so far thereunder; and(e)	the manner in which this scheme is benefiting in the creation of job opportunities for the rural people?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) and (b): Yes, Sir. The Digital Village Scheme (ie Digital Village Pilot Project) is being implemented as per the approved objectives. Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) initiated “Pilot project- Implementation of Digital Village” on 31st October 2018 covering 700 Gram Panchyats/villages across the country. The Deliverables of the project are in the areas of Health, Education, Skill Development, Financial Inclusion including G2C & B2C: i.	Education Service: Courses offered under this project are as (i) Basic Computer Course (BCC), (ii) Course on Computer Concept (CCC), (iii) Tally – Kaushal Praman Patraii.	Health Services – Tele-Health &  Tele-Veterinary consultation iii.	Financial Inclusion- Financial inclusion Awareness programme  iv.	Solar street lights (8 Solar lights per village)v.	Skill Development - skill development in (i) Automotive Technician, (ii) Handset repairing, (iii) Field Technician- Home Appliances, and (iv) Electrical Technician.(c):   The number of villages identified under the project, State/UT wise:S.No.	State	Number of Village1	Andaman Nicobar	12	Andhra Pradesh	133	Arunachal Pradesh	214	Assam	335	Bihar	386	Chhatisgarh	277	Dadar Nagar Haveli	18	Daman & Diu	29	Goa	210	Gujarat	3311	Haryana	2212	Himachal Pradesh	1213	Jammu & Kashmir	2214	Jharkhand	2415	Karnataka	3016	Kerala	1417	Lakshadweep	118	Madhya Pradesh	5119	Maharashtra	3420	Manipur	1621	Meghalaya	1122	Mizoram	823	Nagaland	1124	Odisha	3025	Puducherry	426	Punjab	2227	Rajasthan	3328	Sikkim	429	Tamilnadu	3230	Telangana	3031	Tripura	832	Uttar Pradesh	7533	Uttarakhand	1334	West Bengal	22Total	700 (d): The Digital Village Pilot Project is being implemented across India through CSC e-Governance Service India Limited (as project implementing agency) under the Digital India Scheme which is a Central Sector Scheme of Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology. The total budget outlay of the project is Rs. 98.324crore (MeitY contribution- Rs. 69.965crore and Beneficiaries’ contribution- Rs. 28.358crore)) for the duration of 3 years.
