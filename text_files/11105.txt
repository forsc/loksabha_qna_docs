                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

123






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2020






Exemptions to attract Foreign Tourists




Sanjay(Kaka) Ramchandra Patil














                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the names of the countries which the Government has given any exemptions in the field of tourism so as to attract foreign tourists to India; (b) 	the names of the countries included in this list; (c) 	whether the Government has signed/proposes to sign any agreement with other countries to promote international tourism; (d) 	if so, the details thereof; and (e) 	whether there is possibility of organizing International Tourism conference by the Government for this purpose?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR TOURISM (INDEPENDENT CHARGE)(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) & (b):	No, Sir. Does not arise. (c) & (d): The signing of Agreement with various countries is an ongoing process and the Ministry of Tourism has signed Agreements/Memorandum of Understanding (MoUs)/Letters of Intent (LoIs)/Protocol in the field of tourism cooperation with other countries and multilateral fora, details of which is at Annexure. (e):	No, Sir. The Ministry of Tourism, however holds Joint Working Group (JWG) Meetings with countries. In addition, the Ministry of Tourism   regularly organises International Buddhist Conclave, India Tourism Mart, International Tourism Mart, etc. by inviting dignitaries of foreign Governments, leading tour operators, travel agents, media representatives, opinion makers, etc. from overseas to boost foreign tourist arrivals to the country.  ********
