                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1064






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.11.2019






Mahila Sashaktikaran Scheme




Midhun Reddy













                                                    Kotagiri Sridhar
                                                








                                                     Chandra Sekhar Bellana
                                                








                                                    Magunta Sreenivasulu Reddy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	Whether the Government has initiated a training and capacity building programme under the Mahila Sashaktikaran scheme at the block level;(b)	if so, the details thereof; and(c)	the total number of blocks that have been identified in the State of Andhra Pradesh under this scheme and the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVLOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a)	to (b): Mahila Shakti Kendra (MSK) Scheme, under Ministry of Women and Child Development, was approved in November 2017 as a centrally sponsored scheme, to empower rural women through community participation. Under the Scheme, Capacity Building of Women Collectives is envisaged in not more than 50% of the MSK blocks in 115 aspirational districts to address the livelihood needs of the women particularly those in remote/vulnerable areas where women are not in a position to move out from their immediate surroundings for formal skill training. This component is to be implemented in collaboration of NGO’s/ Cooperative Societies/ Krishi Vigyan Kendras.(c): Ministry of Women & Child Development has released funds (central share) for three aspirational districts in Andhra Pradesh namely Vizianagaram, Visakhapatnam and YSR Kadapa for undertaking block level activities in 8 blocks per aspirational district. A total number of 24 blocks @ 8 blocks per aspirational district were identified by the Government of Andhra Pradesh.
