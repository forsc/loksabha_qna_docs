                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5051






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.04.2022






Supply of COVID-19 Vaccines by G-7 countries




P.R. Natarajan














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has taken cognizance of the announcement by the G-7 countries for providing one billion doses of vaccines to developing countries;(b)	 if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government thereto;(c)	the number of such vaccine doses received from G-7 countries and North Atlantic Treaty organisation (NATO) allies so far, country/month-wise;(d)	whether the Government is aware of any shortage of vaccination in the country which is likely to hinder the achievement of vaccination for all; and(e)	if so, the details thereof along with the action taken thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) to (e): India is not a member of G-7 countries and India has not received any COVID-19 vaccine doses from G-7 countries and North Atlantic Treaty Organisation (NATO) member countries. India has enough vaccine available in the country to cover all eligible beneficiaries and adequate Covid-19 vaccine doses are made available to all States/UTs to vaccinate all eligible beneficiaries with 1st dose, 2nd dose and precaution dose.*******
