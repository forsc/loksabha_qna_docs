                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






Skill Development in NER




Agatha K. Sangma














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government plans on implementing any scheme for Skill Development of the unemployed youth of the North-Eastern Region (NER); if so, the details thereof State and year wise;(b)	whether the Confederation of Indian Industry has undertaken programmes in coordination with the Ministry and other stakeholders for up-skilling of youth in the region; and(c)	if so, the key features of the initiatives undertaken and the thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	I.	The Government has adopted a multi-pronged strategy for the overall skilldevelopment of people living across the country including in deprived areas, Statesand skill sectors. The Ministry has been implementing Pradhan Mantri KaushalVikas Yojana (PMKVY), Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) Scheme and NationalApprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS), for providing short term SkillDevelopment training and Craftsman Training Scheme (CTS), for long term training,to the youth belonging to all section of the society across all over India including the North Eastern Region.As per the Common Norms for Skill Development Schemes,  Additional Support for Special Areas/ Training in Special Areas, over and above the Base Cost, an additional amount equal to 10% of the Base Cost should be permitted for Skill Development programmes conducted in the North Eastern States, Jammu & Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Andaman & Nicobar Islands, Lakshadweep and districts affected by Left Wing Extremism (LWE) as identified by the M/O Home Affairs for the Integrated Action Plan. Under the JSS, course fees are waived for the SC, ST, Divyangjan & BPL category beneficiaries and under CTS benefits are extended as per State/UT policy in the NER States as well as in the rest of country.Details of implementation of the CSCM portion of the PMKVY are at Annexure-I.II.	Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship (MSDE) is also implementing following schemes in various States of the country including North Eastern Regionfor Skill Development: Upgradation of existing Government ITIs into Model ITIs: Existing Govt. ITIs in states have been taken up for Upgradation into Model ITI which is supposed to evolve as an institution showcasing best practices, efficient and high-quality training delivery and sustainable and effective industry relationship. Details of ITIs fund released under the scheme are given at Annexure – II.It is also running the following scheme particularly for the North Eastern Region:Enhancing Skill Development Infrastructure in NE States: The scheme envisages establishment of 34 new ITIs along with upgradation of 22 existing Government ITIs by introducing three new trades per ITI and supplementing infrastructure deficiencies in 28 ITIs by constructing new hostel, boundary wall and replacing old and obsolete tools and equipment. The details of coverage and fund released under the scheme are given at Annexure – III.III.	The Ministry focuses on apprenticeship as one of the key components for creating skilled manpower in India including NER and launched National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS) in order to reach maximum no of establishments including MSMEs and to promote apprenticeship training. The main objective of the scheme is to. promote apprenticeship training and to increase the enrolment of apprentices The details of candidates trained under NAPS and fund released are given at Annexure-IV.IV.	Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) aims to provide vocational training to non-literates, neo-literates as well as school drop-outs in rural regions by identifying skills that have a relevant market in that region. Over two-thirds of India’s population comprises rural citizens. The objective of JSS is to uplift this rural population economically by imparting essential skills training, thereby enabling local trades to grow and creating new opportunities for the natives of the region. Detail of total number of youth trained under JSS Scheme and grants-in-aid released to the North-Eastern Region (NER) are attached at Annexure-V.V.	MSDE through its Transformation of Aspirational Districts programme aims to identify challenges for skill development in districts and co-creating customized solutions for overcoming these challenges. The programme hinges on expeditiously transforming 117 districts that were identified across 28 states.  Under MSDE various schemes viz. Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY), National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS), Craftsman Training Scheme (CTS) and Jan Sikhshan Sansthan (JSS) are being implemented across Pan India including aspirational districts. A financial assistance of Rs. 10 lakh per Aspirational district as central share was provided to 117 Aspirational districts under SANKALP. Annexure-VI may be seen for districts in the NER.VI.	The Department of School Education and Literacy is implementing the scheme of Vocationalisation of School Education under the umbrella of ‘Samagra Shiksha - an integrated scheme for school education’, a Centrally Sponsored Scheme. The scheme aims at integrating Vocational Education with general academic education in all Secondary/Senior Secondary schools; enhancing the Employability and Entrepreneurial abilities of the students, providing exposure to work environment; and generating awareness amongst students about various career options so as to enable them to make a choice in accordance with their aptitude, competence and aspirations.  The scheme covers Government and Government aided schools.VII.	Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs is implementing a centrally sponsored scheme namely "Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana - National Urban Livelihoods 14ission (DAY-NULM)", in the statutory towns of the country, including North-Eastern States, to reduce poverty and vulnerability of urban poor households on a sustainable basis. Under the Employment through Skill Training and Placement (ESTP) component of the DAY-NULM, skill training is provided to the urban poor in market-oriented certified courses to place them in wage employment and/ or self-employment opportunities. A statement showing year-wise number of skill trained candidates in the North-Eastern region is at Annexure-VII.(b) 	Yes, Sir. (c)	Confederation of Indian Industry (CII) is the training provider for Customized Crash Course Programme for Covid Warriors. This programme has been designed and launched under PMKVY and it aims to skill, upskill and reskill targeted beneficiaries across healthcare and logistics sector, in order to meet an increasing demand in skilled professionals in these sectors during outbreak of COVID-19 pandemic. Training is being given across the country including backward and rural areas. It comprises of the following three components: (i)	Component 1: Fresh Skilling of candidates in 6 (six) healthcare sector job roles. The training duration of this special projects training will be of approximately 21-days of theory-based, classroom training followed by approximately 90 days On-the-Job Training (OJT) in healthcare facility such as Primary Health Centres, hospitals, diagnostic facility, sample collection centre, etc. (ii)	Component 2:Upskilling for candidates with prior experience / prior learning. The training under this component will be of approximately one-week duration as a bridge course on original 6 job roles.(iii)	Component 3: Training of drivers in handling and transportation of the Liquid Medical Oxygen (LMO). The training duration will be of 217 hours/ 27 days. The special training of HMV license-holder drivers in transportation of Hazardous Chemicals as well as LMO, along with focus on ‘defensive driving’ while transporting LMO will be conducted.Detail of candidates trained through this programme is as under: Training Program Name	Training Provider Name	Candidates Enrolled	Total Trained	Training Ongoing	Sum of Assessed in Assessment 1	OJT Enrolled	Batches Enrolled	No. of States Where Batches have been Enrolled	No. of Districts Where Batches have been EnrolledCustomized Crash Course Program for Covid Warriors	CONFEDERATION OF INDIAN INDUSTRY (CII)	1334	1316	18	705	592	69	6	10Grand Total	 	1334	1316	18	705	592	69	6	10Annexure IAnnexure referred to in reply to LSUQ 3476 to be answered on 20.12.2021 regarding ‘SKILL DEVELOPMENT IN NER’Details of trained in NER under PMKVY is given below:PMKVY 2.0 CSCM and PMKVY 3.0 CSCM (Including Covid Crash Course) update of North Eastern Region as per SIP reports till 21-Nov-21		PMKVY 2.0-CSCM	PMKVY 3.0-CSCM	PMKVY 2.0 (CSCM)+ PMKVY 3.0 (CSCM)Component	FY	 Trained / Oriented	 Certified	 Reported Placed	 Trained / Oriented	 Certified	 Reported Placed	Total Trained / Oriented	Total Certified	Total Reported PlacedShort Term Training (STT)	FY-16-17	                            2,402 	                             591 	                               88 	                                     -
