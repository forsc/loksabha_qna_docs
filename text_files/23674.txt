                                    MINES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

403






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2021






District Mining Fund




Sanjay Seth














                        Will the Minister of





MINES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether any measures have been taken by this Ministry to increase the spending from the District Mining Fund and if so, the details thereof;(b)	the guidelines for spending District Mining Fund in the State of Jharkhand and the total amount spent on all the districts including Ranchi during the last three years, work-wise; (c)	whether a Committee has been constituted for work planning sanction and to review the above mentioned amount and if so, the names of the members of the Committee; (d)	whether the Government proposes to have a provision for the role and suggestions of the Members of Parliament in this regard; and (e)	if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF MINES, COAL AND PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS(SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI) (a) to (e): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.  STATEMENT REFERRED IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (e) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 403 FOR ANSWER ON 24.03.2021 ASKED BY SHRI SANJAY SETH REGARDING DISTRICT MINING FUND.(a): Section 9B of MMDR Act empowers States to establish DMF and to take-up development programmes in mining affected areas. Ministry of Mines has circulated guidelines for Pradhan Mantri Khanij Kshetra Kalyan Yojna (PMKKKY) on 16.09.2015 with the direction to the State Governments to incorporate the PMKKKY into the DMF rules for taking up development and welfare projects/programs in mining affected areas. Further, Ministry of Mines on 28.03.2020 and subsequent amendment on 11.6.2020 has issued one time relaxation for using 30% of DMF for projects for medical interventions required for testing and treating patients affected by COVID-19 pandemic. A total of Rs. 519.85crore has been spent till 15th March, 2021 for combating Covid 19 pandemic.Hon’ble Prime Minister launched ‘Garib Kalyan Rozgar Abhiyan’ on 20th June 2020 focusing on generation of employment and livelihoods and creation of public assets in 116 districts of 6 States. The Abhiyan was a convergent effort of 12 different Ministries/Departments of Government of India to expedite implementation of 25 public infrastructure work and work relating to augmentation of livelihood opportunities, DMF being one of them. A total of Rs. 1042.82 crore was spent from DMF for 7583 projects under this scheme generating 50,974 man days of employment during the abhiyaan.As on 31.1.2021, total collection in DMF is Rs. 45,095.86 Crore and Rs. 20,337.35 Crore has been spent.
