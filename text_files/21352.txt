                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2531






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.03.2021






Tele-Density




Ravindra Shyamnarayan alias Ravi Kishan Shukla













                                                    Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik
                                                








                                                    Subrata Pathak
                                                








                                                    Shrirang Appa Barne
                                                








                                                    Rajendra Dhedya  Gavit
                                                








                                                    Chandra Sekhar Sahu
                                                








                                                    Sudheer Gupta
                                                








                                                    Ravindra Kushawaha
                                                








                                                    Bidyut Baran Mahato
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of tele density in the country, State/ UT-wise; (b) 	the details of the market share of Government telecom service providers, State/UT-wise; (c) 	the number of villages which do not have mobile and land line connectivity, State/UT-wise including Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh and Odisha; (d) 	whether the Government has proposed any plan in this regard for such States; (e) 	if so, the details and the salient features thereof, State/UT-wise; and (f) 	the details of funds allocated, released and utilized to strengthen the telecom network in various States during each of the last three years, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS, EDUCATION ANDELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a)	The service area wise tele-density in the country as on 31.01.2021is given in Annexure-I. (b)    The details of service area wise market share of Government telecom service providers (BSNL and MTNL) in the country as on 31.01.2021are given at Annexure-II.(c) 	State/UT wise list of total number of inhabited villages as per Census 2011, number of inhabited villages with mobile connectivity and inhabited villages where BSNL Landline exchanges are working is given at Annexure-III.(d) & (e)	For providing telecom connectivity in rural and remote areas of the country, the Department has taken the following initiatives:1.	The Department is executing the flagship Bharatnet Project to connect 2.5 Lakh Gram Panchayats (GPs) with an optical mix of media (i.e. OFC/Radio/Satellite). As on date 154096 GPs have been connected and equipment installed.2.	For provision of mobile services in Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected areas under Phase-I, the project has been completed by installing 2,343 towers. Under Phase-II- 4G mobile services in Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected areas for installation of 2,542 number of tower sites.3.	Under Comprehensive Telecom Development Plan (CTDP) for mobile connectivity in the North Eastern Region, a total number of 1,358 towers have been installed, out of which 1,355 sites are providing services.4.	Provision of 4G Mobile Coverage in 85 uncovered Villages and seamless 4G Mobile coverage of NH223 in Andaman & Nicobar Islands.5.	Mobile connectivity in 354 uncovered villages of J&K, Ladakh, Himachal Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Rajasthan, Gujarat, Uttarakhand, Border area & other priority areas. About 80 villages have been covered.6.	4G mobile connectivity in 502 uncovered villages of Aspirational Districts in four States namely Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh & Rajasthan. (f) 	The details of the funds disbursed/allocated under various schemes of Universal Social Obligation Fund (USOF) for strengthening the telecom network is given at Annexure-IV&V.
