                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2434






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2022






Train Accidents




Vellalath Kochukrishnan Nair. Sreekandan














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether there have been several train accidents in the country during the last six months and there was a sudden spurt in such accidents;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether the Railways took any action to find out the reasons for the increase in number of train accidents and if so, the outcome thereof;(d)	whether railwaymen  are yet to come into proper handling of trains as per previous level after dull operations of trains in the wake of Covid-19; (e)	if so, whether the Railways is considering to impart training, etc to railwaymen to prevent accidents; and (f)	if so, the steps taken in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMUNICATIONS ANDELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI ASHWINI VAISHNAW)(a) to (f): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House. *****STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (f) OF UNSTARRED QUESTION NO 2434 BY SHRI V. K. SREEKANDAN TO BE ANSWERED IN LOK SABHA ON 16.03.2022 REGARDING TRAIN ACCIDENTS(a): No, Sir. In fact the number of consequential train accidents has reduced as compared to previous years except for the year 2020-21, when number of passenger trains run was considerably less due to Covid-19 pandemic.(b): Does not arise.(c): Does not arise.(d): Indian Railway personnel have worked continuously throughout the Covid-19 pandemic period and thereafter to meet the transport needs of the country.(e) & (f):  Railwaymen are being imparted regular training as prescribed for safe functioning of railways.*****
