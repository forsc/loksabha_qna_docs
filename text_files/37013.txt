                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

372






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.03.2022






Mixed Farming




 Sunil Kumar Pintu














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is formulating any scheme to organise programmes in villages to attract people towards mixed farming in the country particularly Sitamarhi, Muzaffarpur,  Motihari and Darbhanga districts of Bihar;(b)	 if so, the details thereof; and (c)	 if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTSER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMES WELFARE ???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????	    (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)  to (c):      A Statement is laid on the Table of the House STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (c) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.  372 DUE FOR REPLY ON  29TH  MARCH, 2022.(a)	to (c):    Indian Institute of Farming Systems Research (IIFSR) of  Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) through  All India Coordinated Research Project  (AICRP) on Integrated Farming Systems and All India Network Programme on Organic Farming is undertaking the research on Integrated Farming Systems and organic farming in 26 States / Union Territories with the involvement of marginal and small farmers. Efforts are being made to popularize the mixed farming with the incorporation of traditional varieties of crops, native breeds of livestock and other indigenous practices among the farmers in integrated farming systems mode. In addition, ICAR- Recearch Complex for Eastern Region, Patna and its regional station located at Darbhanga is involved in conducting research on sustainable farming system models and their outreach on the fields through demonstrations and farmers melas. Darbhanga regional station is promoting the aquatic based farming systems including makhana.  In addition, The Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) coordinates work of 731 Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) having mandate of technology assessment and demonstration for its application and capacity development. KVKs impart training to sensitize farmers to adopt the scientific practices /methods and spread awareness about various scientific farm intensification practices like intercropping, relay cropping, integrated farming, mixed farming, etc. through various extension activities.	   Department of Agriculture and Farmers’ Welfare is also  implementing a Centrally Sponsored Scheme namely ‘Support to State Extension Programmes for Extension Reforms’, popularly known as ATMA Scheme, since 2005 in 684 districts of the country, wherein latest agricultural technologies and good agricultural practices such as mixed farming through different activities namely farmers’ training, demonstrations, exposure visits, Kisan Mela, mobilization of farmers groups and organizing farm schools on the fields of farmers are promoted.  	    Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Government of India is implementing Centrally Sponsored Scheme of  Rainfed Area Development (RAD) focusing on Integrated Farming System alongwith mixed farming across the country including Bihar.  Since inception and up-to 2020-21, an amount of Rs. 1434.73 Crore was provided as Central Assistance to States across the country including Rs.  10.68 Crore to the State of  Bihar to adopt various interventions of Integrated Farming System.   Besides, Schemes of     Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH); Sub Mission on Agro Forestry (SMAF); National Bamboo Mission and Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) helps in    promotion of   mixed farming   in the country including Bihar.     *****
