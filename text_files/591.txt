                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

458






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.06.2019






Promotion of Animal Husbandry




Gowdar Mallikarjunappa Siddeshwara














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has launched any special scheme to promote animal husbandry amongst farmers to earn additional income which is likely to utilise crop residue and also produce organic manure for use in agricultural activities to increase productivity; (b) if so, the details thereof; and (c) the details of technical and financial assistance extended to farmers for domestication of cows, buffaloes, sheep and goats in Karnataka, district-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING                                                                    (DR SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a) and (b) Yes, Sir. The Government is implementing various schemes to supplement the efforts of States/UTs to promote animal husbandry namely; I.	Rashtriya Gokul Mission II.	National Livestock MissionIII.	Livestock Health & Disease ControlThe Government is implementing National Livestock Mission since 2014-15 under which there is a provision of financial assistance under the component Establishment of high capacity Fodder Block Making units for converting crop residues into enriched fodder blocks thereby earning additional income.(c) Under Rashtriya Gokul Mission, an amount of Rs. 2,281.38 lakh has been released to the State of Karnataka for development and conservation of cattle and buffaloes population during the last five years. Similarly, under National Livestock Mission, an amount of Rs. 2,099.52 lakh has been released to the State of Karnataka for development of small Ruminants (sheep and goats), pigs and poultry during the last five years. However, no direct assistance to the farmers is provided by the Government of India.***
