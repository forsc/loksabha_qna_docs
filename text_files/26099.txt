                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

176






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.07.2021






Recognition of Medical Colleges




Gowdar Mallikarjunappa Siddeshwara














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) the names and number of medical colleges recognised by the Medical Council of India (MCI) in the country during the last three years; (b) the amount of funds allocated by the Government for providing facilities in these colleges after being recognised by MCI; (c) whether the Government has assessed the progress/improvements made in the construction of buildings, purchase of medical equipments, teaching facilities, etc. by utilising the allocated funds; and (d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE (SHRI MANSUKH MANDAVIYA)(a) to (d) A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO LOK SABHASTARRED QUESTION NO. 176* FOR 30TH JULY, 2021(a)	As informed by National Medical Commission (NMC),90 medical colleges have been established during the last three years. State/UT-wise details are at Annexure-I.(b) & (c) Ministry of Health and Family Welfare administers a Centrally Sponsored Scheme (CSS) for “Establishment of new medical colleges attached with existing district/referral hospitals”. Establishment of 157 new Medical Colleges has been approved under the scheme in three phases. The fund sharing ratio between the Central Government and States under the Scheme is 90:10 for NE/Special Category States and 60:40 for other States. Under the Scheme, a new medical college can be taken up by upgradation of a district hospital. As per the Scheme guidelines, such projects can be approved in districts where there are no pre existing Government or private medical college. Under this scheme, the State Government is the implementing agency and the planning, execution and commissioning of the projects is done solely by the State governments.Under Phase-I of the Scheme, 58 districts in 20 States/UT have been approved for establishment of medical colleges. Total cost of establishment of one Medical College under this Phase is Rs.189 crore. Entire Central Share of Rs.7541.10 crore has been released to the State/UT Governments for these medical colleges. Out of these 58 medical colleges, 46 have become functional.  Details are at Annexure-II.	Under Phase-II of the Scheme (launched in February, 2018), 24 new medical colleges in 8 States have been approved. The total cost of establishment of one Medical College under this Phase is Rs.250 crore Complete amount of Central Share of Rs.3675 crores have been released to the State /UT Governments. Details are at Annexure-III.Under Phase-III of the Scheme launched in August, 2019, 75 new medical colleges have been approved in 18 States/UTs. The total cost of establishment of one Medical College under this Phase is Rs.325 crore. Funds to the tune of Rs.5519.11 crore have been released to the State /UT Government. One (01) medical colleges has become functional. Details are at Annexure-IV.The Ministry also administers a Centrally Sponsored Scheme for Up-Gradation of Central/State Government Medical Colleges for increase of MBBS seats in existing medical colleges.  Under this scheme of increase of MBBS seats one time grant is provided for infrastructure and equipments. Till date, 48 Medical Colleges have been approved under the scheme to increase 3325 MBBS seat and Rs. 2451.1 Crore have been released to the State Governments. Details are at Annexure V. The Ministry also administers a Centrally Sponsored Scheme for strengthening and upgradation of State Government medical colleges for starting new PG disciplines and increasing PG seats. Under Phase II of the Scheme, launched in 2018, a total of 16 Government medical colleges have been approved for increasing 1741 PG seats. The Scheme entails funding of Rs. 1.20 crore per seat. Details are at Annexure VI . Under the Pradhan Mantri Swasthya Suraksha Yojana (PMSSY), there are two components viz. (a) Establishment of new All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) and (b) upgradation of Government Medical Colleges. So far setting up of 22 new AIIMS and 75 upgradation projects have been approved under the Scheme.  Each Government Medical College up gradation project interalia involves setting up of Super specialty blocks with creation of 8-10 Super Specialty Departments and creation of around 15 new post-graduation seats on an average.Progress of all these projects is assessed and monitored by the Government through periodic meetings with the states/ implementing agencies and through site inspections.
