                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

274






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.11.2021






ODOP in Kerala




Rahul Gandhi














                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the initiatives that are being undertaken by the Government to support Farmer Producer Organisations, Self Help Groups (SHGs) and Producer Cooperatives to promote products identified through the One District One Product (ODOP) approach in Kerala along with the details of the products identified for Wayanad parliamentary constituency;(b)	the details of applications received under PM Formalisation of Micro Food Processing Enterprises (PM-FME) Scheme in Kerala, district-wise;(c)	the details of credit linked grant and seed capital provided to FPOs/SHGs and Producer Cooperatives under PM-FME scheme, State-wise;(d)	whether the capacity building initiatives are being undertaken to strengthen FPOs, SHG sand Producer Cooperatives; and (e) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL) (a):	Ministry of Food Processing Industries is implementing a Centrally Sponsored "PM Formalisation of Micro food processing Enterprises (PMFME) Scheme" for providing financial, technical and business support for upgradation of micro food processing enterprises in the country including Kerala. Support to upgrade existing units or setting up of new micro units for individuals is provided through a credit linked grants-in-aid @35% up to a maximum grant of Rs. 10.00 lakh. The Scheme also provides support to food processing units of SHGs/ FPOs/ Cooperatives for credit linked grants-in-aid @35%, Seed Capital to SHGs, Incubation Centre, Common Infrastructure, Marketing & Branding and Capacity Building.  The Scheme has adopted One District One Product (ODOP) approach to reap the benefit of scale in terms of procurement of inputs, availing common services and marketing of products. ODOP for the scheme provides the framework for value chain development and alignment of support infrastructure. ODOP for a district is adopted on the basis of a recommendation of the State Government. In respect to the district of Wayanad, based on the proposal of Government of Kerala, Milk based products have been identified as ODOP. (b):	The details of applications received under the Scheme in Kerala, district-wise are at Annexure-I.(c):	 Under the scheme, seed capital support up to a maximum of Rs 40,000 per member is provided to members of SHGs involved in food processing activities. Till date, seed capital amount of Rs. 117.71 crores has been released for 34,578 SHG Members. The State-wise details of seed capital provided to SHG Members are at Annexure-II. Credit linked grant has not yet been released to FPOs / SHGs and Producer Cooperatives till date under the Scheme.(d) & (e): To enhance the capacity building through training and handholding support, Kerala has nominated Kerala Agricultural University, Thrissur as State Level Technical Institute, 22 Master Trainers on various food domains and Entrepreneurship Development Programmes, 66 District Level Trainers in 14 districts and 46 District Resource Persons have been provided training by NIFTEM / IIFPT and other national level research and training institutions through the State Governments. 760 training modules on various food products have been developed and are available on PMFME website (https://mofpi.nic.in/pmfme/) for use of prospective entrepreneurs / beneficiaries.   *****
