                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3533






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






Women Engaged in Agriculture




Subrata Pathak













                                                    Ravindra Shyamnarayan alias Ravi Kishan Shukla
                                                








                                                    Chandra Sekhar Sahu
                                                








                                                    Sudheer Gupta
                                                








                                                    Manoj Kumar Tiwari
                                                








                                                    Ravindra Kushawaha
                                                








                                                    Bidyut Baran Mahato
                                                








                                                    Prataprao  Jadhav
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether a large number of women in the country are actively participating in agriculture;(b)	if so, the details in this regard and the total number of women engaged in sowing, hoeing, weeding and harvesting in agriculture sector;(c)	whether the Government has set up any institute in which the problems and issues faced by the women in agriculture are to be examined and resolved;(d)	if so, the details of various capacity building programmes specially meant for women in farming; and (e)	whether Krishi Vigyan Kendras also impart training to women farmers in agriculture and allied sectors and if so, the details in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE  ???? ??? ????? ??????? ??????       (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)	& (b): As per the Census 2011 conducted by the Registrar General of India, the extent of women participation in agriculture as cultivators (main & marginal) is to the tune of 3.60 crore (30.33%) and as female agricultural labour (main & marginal) to the tune of 6.15 crore (42.67 %). As per Agriculture Census (2015-16), the total number of operational holdings of women is estimated as 20.44 million in the country. The specific data on number of women engaged in sowing, hoeing, weeding and harvesting in agriculture is not maintained in the Department of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare.(c ) & (d) : Government of India has established Central Institute for Women in Agriculture (CIWA) at Bhuvneshwar, Odisha under Indian Council of Agricultural Research to address the problems and issues of the women in agriculture. The institute undertakes research on Women in Agriculture and for identifying gender issues in agriculture and allied fields, gender-equitable agricultural policies/ programmes and gender-sensitive agricultural-sector responses and coordinating research on farm women through 13 All India Coordinated Research Projects (AICRP) centres in 12 States, across the country. A total 366 number of capacity building programmes for farm women were conducted by CIWA and AICRP on Home Science covering 16264 farm women during the period from 2017 to 2020.(e):  The Government has set up 725 Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) in the country with mandate of technology assessment and demonstration for its application and capacity development. The KVKs impart training to farmers including women farmers on various aspects of agriculture and allied sectors. The KVKs also organize women farmer specific training programmes on various topics like household food security by kitchen gardening and nutrition gardening; Design and development of low/minimum cost diet; Designing and development for high nutrient efficiency diet; Minimization of nutrient loss in processing; Processing and cooking; Gender mainstreaming through Self Help Groups; Storage loss minimization techniques; Value addition; Women empowerment; Location specific drudgery reduction technologies ; Rural Crafts; and Women and child care. During 2020-21, 5358 women specific trainings were organized by the KVKs with participation of 1.23 lakh women farmers. Besides, 3.17 lakh women farmers also participated in other 57757 training programmes along with male farmers.*****
