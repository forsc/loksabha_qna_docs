                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

874






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






National Mission for a Green India




Sanjay Jaiswal














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has launched the National Mission for a Green India to increase the forest cover in the country;(b)	if so, the details and the objectives achieved under the said mission;(c)	the adaptation and mitigation measures undertaken under the said mission;(d)	whether forest cover has increased after implementation of the provisions under the mission; and (e)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	National Mission for a Green India (GIM) is launched with the objective to increase forest/ tree cover on forest/ non-forest lands and improve quality of forest cover. It aims at protecting, restoring and enhancing India’s forest cover and responding to climate change. Activities under the Mission started in the year 2015-16 with a financial outlay of Rs. 46,000 crores over the period of ten years. (b)	The afforestation area sanctioned under GIM is 142684 ha and distribution of alternative fuel energy devices is 68707 households during the period 2015-16 to 2019-20. The State wise details of sanctioned afforestation area and distribution of alternative fuel energy devices to households is placed at Annexure-I.Besides GIM, afforestation taken up under the Compensatory Afforestation Fund Management and Planning Authority (CAMPA) is 212805.515 ha for the financial year 2020-21. The State wise details of afforestation area is placed at  Annexure-II.(c)	The adaptation and mitigation measures undertaken under the mission include promotion of natural regeneration and plantation of indigenous tree species, reduced forest fragmentation by conserving contiguous forest patches through landscape approach, effective fire prevention and fire management, weed management, sustainable harvesting of non-timber products, securing corridors for species migration and improving overall hydrological regime. The Mission also supports the adoption of improved fuelwood-use efficiency and alternative energy devices in the project-area.(d)	and (e) As per the India State of Forest Report (ISFR) 2019, Forest cover of the country is 7,12,249 sq. km which has shown an increase of 10576 sq. km as compared to                ISFR, 2015. This is mainly attributed to large scale afforestation taken up under various schemes of Central government including Green India Mission, State plan, and also by NGOs, civil societies, corporate houses etc. The State wise details of forest cover as per India State of Forest Report (ISFR) 2015 and 2019 is enclosed in Annexure-III. ****
