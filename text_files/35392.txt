                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2658






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Clean tap water to every Home




Chandra Sekhar Sahu













                                                    Girish Bhalchandra Bapat
                                                








                                                     Rahul Ramesh Shewale
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to provide clean tap water to every home in the country by 2024; (b) if so, the details and the number of villages, households, schools and anganwadi centers identified by the Government for the said purpose, State-wise particularly in Odisha and Maharashtra; (c) the number of villages, households, schools and anganwadi centers so far covered under Har Ghar Jal Scheme, State-wise; (d) the details of the funds allocated, released and utilized so far under Har Ghar Jal Scheme in various States particularly in Odisha and Maharashtra; (e) whether the Government Proposes to constitute Village Water & Sanitation Committee/ Pani Samiti to manage and monitor the issues under Jal Jeevan Mission; and (f) if so, the details thereof and the number of such Committees likely to be set up in various States?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) Government of India is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission, in partnership with States to make provision of tap water supply in adequate quantity of prescribed quality on regular and long-term basis to every rural household of the country by 2024. The mission will also cover schools, anganwadi centres, ashramshalas (tribal residential schools), health centres, Gram Panchayat building in the villages.(b) & (c) The State-wise details of villages, households, schools and anganwadi centers covered under Jal Jeevan Mission including in Odisha and Maharashtra are annexed (Annex – I). State wise information related to JJM is placed in the public domain, which is available on JJM dashboard at:https://ejalshakti.gov.in/jjmreport/JJMIndia.aspx(d) The State-wise details of funds allocated, released and utilized under JJM including in Odisha and Maharashtra are annexed (Annex - II).(e) & (f) In the operational guidelines for the implementation of JJM, village-level planning and community participation in all decisions pertaining to water supply systems have been envisaged to instil the sense of ownership among villagers. Gram Sabha will decide whether GP or its sub-committee will carry out the responsibilities of water supply management in the village. Wherever the sub-committee is chosen, i.e. VWSC/ Pani Samiti/ User Group, etc. it may be headed by Sarpanch/ Up-Sarpanch/ GP member/ traditional village head/ senior village leader as may be decided and Gram Sabha Panchayat Secretary/ Patwari/ Talati may act as Secretary. It may consist of 10-15 members comprising elected members of Panchayat up to 25% of the composition; 50% women members and remaining 25% may consist of representatives of weaker sections of the village (SC/ST) proportional to their population. The details of VWSCs constituted by States/ UTs under JJM  is annexed (Annex-III).****
