                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENTLOK SABHAUNSTARRED QUESTION NO. 134TO BE ANSWERED ON 02.02.2021PMS FOR OBCs 134. DR. SHRIKANT EKNATH SHINDE: SHRI UNMESH BHAIYYASAHEB PATIL: DR. SUJAY RADHAKRISHNA VIKHE PATIL: SHRI HEMANT SRIRAM PATIL: SHRI DHAIRYASHEEL SAMBHAJIRAO MANE: DR. HEENA GAVIT: Will the Minister of SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT be pleased to state:(a) whether Post Matric Scholarship (PMS) for the students of Other Backward Classes (OBCs) is being implemented in various States of the country; (b) if so, the details of the amount allocated, released and utilised under the scheme across the country including Maharashtra during the last three years and the current year, State-wise; (c) whether the funds allocated by the Government under the said scheme has not been fully utilized; (d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) the corrective steps taken by the Government in this regard ?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI KRISHAN PAL GURJAR)(a) & (b)  :- Yes, Sir. The PMS-OBC scheme is being implemented through States/UTs in the country. The details of amount allocated and released under the scheme across the country including Maharashtra during last three years is given in Annexure - I. (c) & (d): The Scheme is a fund limited scheme and funds are released to States/UTs on the basis of notional allocation made every year. Moreover, The subsequent grant is released to the States/UTs on receipt of Utilization Certificate for the previous release.During 2017-18, 2018-19 and 2019-20, 93.74%, 90.95% and 95.54% funds were released respectively. In 2020-21, against revised estimate (RE) of Rs.1100.00 Crores, 76.54% funds have been released upto 27.01.2021.(e):   The corrective steps taken by the Government:-(i)	The States/UTs implementing the OBC welfare schemes have been requested to on board on NSP portal of MSJ&E(ii)  The scheme Implementing Agencies have been impressed upon that the central release should not remain parked in State treasuries and that release of funds should be made on need basis. (iii) Furnishing of previous year UCs for further release of central assistance has been made compulsory.(iv) At the end of financial year, balance fund, if any, is distributed to scheme implementing agencies having more demand of central assistance than notional allocation.*****  								                      Annexure	POST MATRIC SCHOLARSHIP FOR OBC (as on 27.01.2021)	(Amount  in lakhs)Sl.
