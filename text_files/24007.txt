                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4954






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2021






Development of Hassan-Sakaleshpura NH-75




Prajwal Revanna














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the present status of work on Hassan-Sakaleshpura stretch of NH-	75 which is pending for many  years along with the reasons for 	such a long pendency;(b)	the funds allocated, released and utilised on the said stretch;(c)	whether the Government has blacklisted the concerned contractor for non-completion of work on the said stretch and if so, the details thereof; and(d)	the step taken by the Government to complete the said project in a stipulated time period? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) & (b)	The project from Hassan to Maranahalli section of NH-75 has been taken up on EPC mode in the State of Karnataka. The present physical progress is 24%. The work has been delayed due to (i) the Main contractor M/s Isolux Corsan India Engineering & Construction Pvt Ltd being declared insolvent by Hon’ble NCLT (ii) delay in quarry/mining permission (iii) due to Covid-19 pandemic and (iv) due to heavy rainfall in Ghat section. The funds allocated for road work is Rs.573.92 crores. Till now, Rs. 125.51 crore has been utilized/released. (c) & (d)   The firm has not been blacklisted but has been declared as insolvent by Hon’ble NCLT.  Since the original Contractor has been declared as an insolvent, therefore the Sub-Contractor is carrying out the balance work.*****
