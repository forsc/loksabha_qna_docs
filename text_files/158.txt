                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

42






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.06.2019






Forest Fires




Supriya Sadanand Sule













                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Heena Vijaykumar Gavit
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of incidents of forest fire that have been reported from different parts of the country in recent months, along with the details of human lives lost/public properties and forest wealth damaged, State/ UT-wise;(b) whether the Government has conducted any investigation by any Independent Agency on the alarming rise of forest fires in the country, if so, the details and outcome thereof;(c) whether the Government has provided financial assistance to the States affected by forest fire and if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise;(d) whether the Government has made any efforts to hold awareness campaign/ competition on local community level to prevent forest fires, if so, the details thereof;(e) whether the Government proposes to incentivise communities and forest departments and launch mass social movements to control forest fires, if so, the details thereof; and(f) whether the Government proposes to put in place any mechanism for quick response system for fighting forest fires and to incorporate new technology and involving the local community in firefighting and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI PRAKASH JAVADEKAR)(a)                    	Yes Sir, whenever forest fire occurs, the Forest Survey of Indiasends fire alert to different States to control forest fire. The details of forest fire incidents that occurred since January 2019 to 16th June,2019, as reported by Forest Survey of India, are as per Annexure–I.As per report received from states, Himachal Pradesh has reported loss of forest property of Rs. 81.91lakhandUttarakhand has reported a loss of Rs.55.08lakh. No loss of human lives has been reported by Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand. No other State has reported any such loss so far.(b)	The Ministry in collaboration with the World Bank has conducted a study on Situation Analysis of Forest Fire and released a study report titled “Strengthening Forest Fire Management in India” which contains various recommendations regarding forest fire prevention, detection, suppression, post-fire management, engaging with communities, coordination with other agencies etc. The inputs from the study were used by the Ministry in preparation of National Action Plan on Forest Fire which was issued in April 2018.  (c) 	Yes Sir. The Ministry has provided financial assistance to State/UT Governmentsaffected by forest fire from the Forest Fire Prevention and Management Scheme. The amount disbursed to various states during the last three years (as on 31.03.2019) is given in Annexure-II.(d)	The financial assistance under the Forest Fire Prevention and Management Scheme includes activities for awareness campaign/ competition of local community level to prevent forest fires. The State Forest Department undertakes activities of massawareness and training of local communities to prevent forest fire.(e) 	The State/UT Forest Departments involve local communities, Joint Forest Management Committees (JFMCs) and Eco-Development Committees (EDCs) etc. in various forest protection and conservation measures including prevention and control of forest fire. (f)	The National Action Plan on Forest Fire provides for effective response to fire disaster resulting in reduced impact and quicker recovery assatellite based forest fire alerts already sent by FSI   are in operation across the country. As per the National Action Plan on Forest Fire, provision for establishing a dedicated phone line and wireless network for early detection and quick response for fighting forest fire, establishment of watchtowers, ground crew station and location of fire station has been provided. National and State Disaster Response Forces, and army and paramilitary camps etc.areto be listed for quick response and access by the forest staff and local communities for forest fire control.Firefightingequipmentprovided at various places adjacent to the vulnerable forest fire prone areas will be utilized for quick response for fighting forest fire.***
