                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1026






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.02.2021






National Policy for Skill Development and Entrepreneurship




Manoj Kishorbhai Kotak














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether any evaluation of Skill Development Scheme has been conducted by 	third party agency as envisaged in the National Policy for Skill Development 	and Entrepreneurship; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether any study has been done to determine that the skilled person under 	PMKVY get good wages in India and abroad; and (d) 	if so, details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) & (b):	Yes, Sir. As envisaged in the National Policy for Skill Development and Entrepreneurship 2015, the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship has been undertaking regular monitoring and evaluation of its various schemes / initiatives for any necessary corrective measures. Details of agencies to whom third party evaluation of Central Sector Schemes have been assigned are given below:  Sl. No.	Name of the Scheme	Name of Agency undertaking the evaluation 1.	Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY)	Indian Institute of Public Administration, New Delhi2.	Jan Shikshan Sansthan	3.	Up-gradation of 1396 Government Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) through Public- Private Partnership 	4.	Skill Development in 47 Districts Affected by Left Wing Extremism	5.	National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme 	National Productivity Council, New Delhi6.	Up-gradation of existing Govt. ITIs into Model ITIs	7.	Enhancing Skill Development Infrastructure in North Eastern States 	National Institute of Labour Economics Research and Development, New Delhi(c) & (d):	NSDC had engaged Sambodhi Research and Communications to conduct a third-party impact evaluation of PMKVY 2.0. The objective of the Study was to evaluate the impact of PMKVY Short Term Trainings (STT) and Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL). The Sambodhi Study found that the mean monthly income of individuals trained and certified under PMKVY 2.0 – STT was 15 percent higher when compared to a comparison group of individuals who has not participated in PMKVY. Further, the average monthly income of RPL-certified individuals was found to be 19 percent higher than that of a comparison group of non-RPL certified individuals.
