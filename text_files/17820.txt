                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1488






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.09.2020






Underutilisation of Funds for Rural Development




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the funds allocated for Rural Development Programmes are being underutilised due to which poor people and farmers in the villages are not getting the benefits as targetted by the Government;(b) 	if so, the response of the Government thereto; and(c) 	the details of the steps taken by the Government to check its recurrence in future? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)	to (c) No Sir. Most of the funds allocated for rural development schemes are utilized by the States/UT Administrations. Budget Allocation and Expenditure Statement in respect of Rural Development Schemes for last 5 years is enclosed at Annexure-I.  The funds are released to States/UTs in accordance with the programme guidelines based on their absorption capacity and utilization of available funds. The Department has been constantly pursuing the issue with the State/UT governments through Performance Review Committee Meetings to tackle the problem of unspent balances and to ensure timely disbursement and sound financial management. Plan Finance Management System(PFMS) has also been implemented to ensure smooth fund flow management, reduce unspent balances and minimise leakages etc.In order to ensure that the programme benefits reach the rural poor including the farmers in full measure and to monitor the progress made under rural development programmes, the Ministry has evolved a comprehensive multi-level and multi-tool system of monitoring and evaluation of the implementation of rural development programmes, including Performance Review Committee Meetings, District Development Co-ordination and Monitoring Committee named as “DISHA”, National Level Monitors, Area Officers Schemes, Common Review Mission, Concurrent Evaluation and Impact Assessment Studies. Third-Party evaluation of the schemes of the Ministry of Rural Development is also carried out regularly and appropriate actions are undertaken based on its findings. The Ministry constantly modifies its schemes for more efficient utilization of resources, better coverage and higher impact on the mitigation of rural poverty.  Annexure-IAnnexure referred to in part (a) to (c) of the Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No. 1488 due for reply on 20.09.2020
