                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3417






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Four-year B.Ed. Course




S. Venkatesan














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the four-year B.Ed. course is prescribed for teacher education;(b) if so, the details thereof along with the qualification needed from a person to become ateacher; and(c) the scale of pay for teachers who will be appointed after doing four-year B.Ed. course?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) & (b) : There are three integrated programmes with four years B.Ed. course approved by theNational Council for Teacher Education (NCTE). One programme was notified in 2014 and theother two four year Integrated Teacher Education Programmes (ITEP) with in-builtspecialization for primary (including pre-primary) and secondary, both for Science and Artsstreams were notified vide notification dated 29th March, 2019.For a person to become eligible for appointment as a teacher in schools, the NCTE hasnotified the minimum qualifications vide notification dated 25th August, 2010 as amended fromtime to time. The details are at link: http://ncte.gov.in/Website/MinimumQualifications.aspx .(c) : As education is a subject in the Concurrent list of the Constitution, the recruitment andservice conditions of teachers are in the domain of respective State Government/UTAdministration.*****
