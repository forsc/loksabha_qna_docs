                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4399






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






National Bamboo Mission




Bidyut Baran Mahato













                                                    Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik
                                                








                                                    Shrirang Appa Barne
                                                








                                                    Rajendra Dhedya  Gavit
                                                








                                                    Chandra Sekhar Sahu
                                                








                                                    Sudheer Gupta
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether bamboo has been a key crop to double farmers‘ income, increase employment opportunities and improve the livelihood of the people in the country and if so, the details thereof;(b) the name of the States in which bamboo has been cultivated particularly commercially important bamboos;(c) whether the Government proposes to formulate any scheme to promote bamboo based cottage industries and if so, the details thereof;(d) the details of the nurseries set up under the Bamboo Mission in various States along with the details of the nurseries likely to be set up in the coming years, State-wise;(e) whether the Government has also set up bamboo markets in various parts of the country under the National Bamboo Mission and if so, the details thereof, State-wise; and(f) the steps taken/being taken by the Government to promote bamboo farming in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????		(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):	Yes Sir. The restructured National Bamboo Mission (NBM) was launched in 2018-19 for developing the complete bamboo value chain for connecting bamboo growers to the market (industry). The objectives include assisting farmers to set up nurseries as well as to increase the area under bamboo plantation to supplement farm income. The development of post harvest activities including setting up of common facility centers, treatment units, product development units, etc. is increasing employment and livelihoods for the local communities as well. For the purpose there is provision for skill development and awareness generation also.(b):	The 23 States in which NBM is being implemented, including cultivation of commercially important species are Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Karnataka, Uttarakhand, Bihar, Jharkhand, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Uttar Pradesh and Himachal Pradesh.(c):	NBM assists setting up of various product development units including handicrafts, furniture, jewellary, bamboo shoots processing, incense sticks, fibre extraction, charcoal etc. 278 nos. of such units have been set up so far. The Ministry of Micro Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME) is implementing a ‘Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI)’ across the country with the view to organize traditional industries and artisans into clusters to make them competitive and increase their income. The detail of Bamboo related clusters is placed at Annexure I.(d):	323 Nurseries has been set up under the NBM since inception. The State wise details are placed at Annexure II. Total 800 nos. of nurseries are likely to be set up during 2021-22 to 2025-26.(e):	Yes Sir. State wise details of the bamboo markets set up under restructured NBM placed at Annexure III.(f):	During 2018-19 to 2020-21 (28.02.2021), an area of 16604 ha has been covered by bamboo plantation under the restructured NBM benefitting 27111 farmers. Guidelines for the accreditation of bamboo nurseries and certification of planting material have been formulated to ensure availability of quality planting materials of the commercially important bamboo species to the farmers.For promoting bamboo farming, 204 nos. of workshop/ seminars, trade fairs, exhibition etc. have been organized. 10850 nos. of participants including farmers/ artisans & field functionaries have been trained. NBM is envisaged to work in a hub (industry) and spoke model so that backward and forward linkages are planned and implemented optimally.
