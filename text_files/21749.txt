                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2923






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2021






Attacks by Wild Animals




Akshaibar Lal














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether protected area has been established for wild animals like tigers, leopards and Cheetah in Katarniya Ghat Wildlife division in Bahraich district of Uttar Pradesh;(b)	 if so, the details thereof along with the number of wild animals including tigers and leopards in protected forest area currently;(c)	 the number of human deaths/fatalities caused by attacks by wild animals outside the protected area during the last five years;(d)	 the financial assistance provided by the Government to the people killed in such attacks; and(e)	whether the Government proposes to lay barbed fencing and construct walls so as to prevent animals from venturing beyond the protected areas and endanger human lives; and(f)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	and (b)	Katarniaghat Wildlife Sanctuary has been established for the conservation of wildlife and its habitat.As per information received from the State Government of Uttar Pradesh, number of tigers, leopards and elephants estimated in Katarniaghatis as follows:Species 	NumberTiger	29Leopard	39Elephant	70(c)	As informed by the State Government of Uttar Pradesh,the number of human deaths caused due to attack by wild animals outside Katarniaghat Wildlife Sanctuary is as follows:                        Year	No. of human deaths2015-2016	02016-2017	032017-2018	112018-2019	072019-2020	08(d)	As informed by the State Government of Uttar Pradesh,a sum of Rs 5.00 lakhs each has been paid as ex-gratia to 14 affected families from 1.12.2018 to 4.12.2020.(e) and (f)	As informed by State Government of Uttar Pradesh, there is no such proposal. However, protection measures like erecting chain link fencing and digging of animal proof trenches are being carried out in high conflict areas.*****
