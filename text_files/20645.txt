                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2024






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.02.2021






Review of Impact of COVID-19




Nama Nageswara Rao














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government has reviewed the impact of COVID-19 pandemic on the healthcare system and infrastructure in the country;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) if not, the reasons therefor;(d) whether the Government has any plan to make the private healthcare system more responsive to the emergency situations like the current pandemic; and(e) if so, the details thereof and action taken thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to (c):  ‘Public health and hospitals’ being a State subject, the primary responsibility of impact assessment of COVID on healthcare system and infrastructure lies with respective State Government.Government of India has not conducted any study/survey on impact of COVID-19 on healthcare system. However Ministry of Health & Family Welfare has continuously reviewed with States/UTs the impact of COVID-19 pandemic and availability of healthcare services and infrastructure. States/UTs have been provided with requisite technical, and financial assistance for strengthening of their healthcare system. Besides various advisories and guidance on continuation of essential health services, all the States/UTs have been provided with financial support under India COVID-19 Emergency Response and Health System Preparedness package.(d) & (e):  Health is a State subject. The responsibility of engaging private hospitals in treating COVID cases is that of the respective State Government.Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India issued advisory to all States/UTs to engage private hospitals through mutually agreeable arrangements. Rates under PM-JAY and CGHS packages have been advised. States have accordingly issued instructions to that affect.As per information shared by the State Governments/UT Administrations a total of 1503 private hospitals are designated as Dedicated COVID Hospitals and 2,478 Private hospitals are providing services as Dedicated COVID Health Center. Also 1,159 private laboratories are conducting COVID-19 testing in the country.
