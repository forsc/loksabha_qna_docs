                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3387






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






Skill Development in Agriculture Sector




Jaskaur Meena














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    	(a)	 whether the agricultural sector has immense employment potential 	and that the Government is providing any agriculture related training to create interest among the youth in agriculture and if so, the details thereof; 	(b)	 whether the training is being provided to the youth for getting employment in horticulture, bee-keeping and animal husbandary and if so, 	the details thereof; and 	(c)	 whether the Government proposes to formulate a plan to give any 	training in this regard in future or include these subjects under the same and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) to (c) Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship is implementing  its flagship scheme Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) through National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC), a Public Private Partnership organization. NSDC has facilitated creation of Sector Skill Councils (SSCs) which are industry led bodies to ensure addressing skill requirements of various sectors. The scheme promotes skilling of manpower in various sectors including agriculture through the Agriculture Sector Skill Council. There are three kind of training routes under the scheme: Short term Training (STT) for fresh skilling of school / college drop outs and unemployed youth, Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) to recognize the existing skills and Special Projects to address skilling requirements of the vulnerable groups and permit flexibility in conduct of Short Term Training. RPL under PMKVY has been able to undertake up-skilling of farmers via bridge course training through some of the projects. The scheme is being implemented across the country spanning 37 sectors including agriculture sector. Under PMKVY2016-2020, as on 11th Nov 2019, more than 5 lakh candidates have been trained under Agriculture sector. More than 300 candidates have been trained in Beekeeper Job role. In last three years, 67 training courses in Beekeeping & 60 training courses in Animal husbandry has been done by NSDC Training Provider.The beneficiaries have been oriented in skills such as ''Group Farming Practitioner'' which enables farmers to increase productivity and income by establishing market linkages and buy – back arrangements. Beneficiaries are being enrolled pan India including for job roles such as Dairy Farmer, Organic Grower, Floriculturist, Gardener, and Pulses Cultivator, etc.*****
