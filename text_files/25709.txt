                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1384






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.07.2021






Sub-Post Office




Balak Nath














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of single handed Sub-Post-Offices functioning at present in Rajasthan, district-wise; (b) 	whether the Government proposes to convert/ upgrade all these single handed Sub-Post Offices functioning in Alwar Lok Sabha constituency into double handed Post Offices and if so, the details thereof and the time by when it is likely to be done; and (c) 	whether the Union Government proposes to fill up the aforesaid vacant posts in various cadres at the earliest in Jaipur and Ajmer circle for smooth functioning of the postal department and if so, the details thereof and the time by when it is likely to be done and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS (SHRI DEVUSINH CHAUHAN)(a) 	Sir, there are 722 single handed Sub-Post Offices functioning at present in Rajasthan. The district-wise details are at Annexure-I.(b) 	The Department carries out periodic establishment review of the post offices as per the laid down instructions/ guidelines inter-alia taking into account the workload of the post offices concerned. The status of the post office is thereafter reviewed as per the result of the aforementioned review. At present, there is no proposal under consideration for upgradation of single handed Sub Post Offices functioning in Alwar Lok Sabha constituency. (c) Yes Sir. The filling up of vacant posts in various cadre is carried out through Departmental Examination and Direct Recruitment as an ongoing process. The cadre-wise details of filling up of vacant posts for Jaipur and Ajmer are as below: i.	The Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS) vacancies upto 2020 have been filled up. Of the 246 vacant posts of Postman and 55 vacant posts of Postal Assistant/Sorting Assistant, examinations were conducted for filling up of 201 posts of Postman and 34 posts of Postal Assistants/Sorting Assistant for which the results could not be declared as the matter is pending adjudication before Rajasthan High Court. The details are at Annexure II. The balance 33 vacant posts of Multi-Tasking Staff, 45 vacant posts of Postman and 22 vacant post of Postal Assistant/Sorting Assistant are for the recruitment year 2021.ii.	In addition, 53 posts of Postal Assistants/Sorting Assistant for vacancies of 2018, 2019 and 2020 and 2021 are to be filled through Direct Recruitment from Staff Selection Commission (SSC). For 13 vacancies of the year 2018, examination has been conducted by the Staff Selection Commission and candidates have been called for document verification and for 30vacancies for the years 2019 and 2020 Tier-II examination is yet to be conducted as per information available on Staff Selection Commission website. The balance 10 vacancies are for the recruitment year 2021.iii.	There are 36 Lower Selection Grade, 30 Higher Selection Grade I and 55 Higher Selection Grade II vacancies. The Departmental Promotion Committee for the cadre of Lower Selection Grade and Higher Selection Grade-II have been conducted up to the year 2020 and for Higher Selection Grade-I has been conducted up to the year 2016. In the above Departmental Promotion Committee of Higher Selection Grade-II and Higher Selection Grade-I, posts could not be filled up due to non-availability of eligible officials in the feeder cadre while the 36 Lower selection grade vacancies are for the recruitment year 2021.
