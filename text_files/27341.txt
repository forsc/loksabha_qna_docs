                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

273






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.08.2021






Proposal for Capacity Building




Raghu Ramakrishna Raju Kanumuru













                                                    Vishnu Dayal Ram
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the salient features of the capacity building in textile sector under Samarth scheme; (b) whether the Government has received any proposals under the said scheme; (c) if so, the details thereof along with the status of those proposals as on date; (d) the quantum of funds allocated, sanctioned and utilised during the last two years, State-wise; and (e) the number of persons trained under the said scheme during the last three years along with the number of those who secured jobs, post training, State-wise including Jharkhand and Assam?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF TEXTILES(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) to (e):-  A statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) to (e) OF THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *273 TO BE ANSWERED ON 06.08.2021.(a):	In order to meet the skill gap in the industry, Ministry of Textiles is implementing Samarth- Scheme for Capacity Building in Textile Sector- with the objective to provide demand driven, placement oriented skilling programmes to supplement the efforts of the industry in creating employment in textile and related sectors, covering the entire value chain of textiles, excluding Spinning and Weaving in organized sector. The salient features of the scheme are: -(i)	Implemented through Textile Industry/ Industry Associations, State Government  agencies and Sectoral Organizations of Ministry(ii)	Formulated as per broad skilling framework adopted by Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship (MSDE)(iii)	Comprise entry level skilling (Fresh workers) and Upskilling (existing worker)(iv)	Mandatory placement of trainees- 70% for entry level & 90% for upskilling under organized sector(v)	Aadhaar Enabled Biometric Attendance System (AEBAS) and Web based centralized Management Information System (MIS) for monitoring(vi)	Call centre for collecting feedback and grievance redressal(vii)	Physical verification of training centres with geo-tagging /time-stamped photographs(b) to (d): The proposals were invited from State Government Agencies/ Industry/ Industry Associations for undertaking training programmes under the Samarth Scheme. After evaluation of these proposals, the Ministry of Textiles has partnered with 13 State Government Agencies, 90 Textile Manufacturers,  11 Industry Associations and 4 Sectoral Organizations of Ministry of Textiles for training of 3.3 lakh beneficiaries in textile sector, after physical verification of training centres. There is no state-wise allocation of funds under Samarth and the cost of training programme is released directly to Implementing Partners (IPs).
