                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1979






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.03.2020






Development of Fishing Harbours in Andhra Pradesh




Balashowry Vallabbhaneni














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  whether it is true that the State Government of Andhra Pradesh submitted a proposal for development of fishing harbours in Machilipatnam in Krishna district of Andhra Pradesh; (b)  whether it is also true that the Ministry has sanctioned of Rs. 250 crore for this purpose; and (c)  if so,  the date on which the amount was sanctioned and the status of the proposal along with the time by which it is going to be completed?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING    	(SHRI PRATAP CHANDRA SARANGI)(a) to (c) : The Government of Andhra Pradesh has submitted a proposal for phase-II expansion of existing fishing harbour at Machilipatnam  in Krishna  District at an estimated cost of Rs. 252 crore and sought the concessional finance under the Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund (FIDF). The fishing harbour proposal of Government of Andhra Pradesh for phase-II expansion of existing fishing harbour at Machilipatnam in Krishna District at an estimated cost of Rs.252 crore has been recommended restricting the project cost of Rs. 150 crore for interest subvention upto 3% per annum by the Government of India under the Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund (FIDF). The Government of Andhra Pradesh has reported that they are obtaining necessary approvals from the State Government for availing loan from the National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) under FIDF and to take up the project of phase -II expansion of existing fishing harbour at Machilipatnam in Krishna District.
