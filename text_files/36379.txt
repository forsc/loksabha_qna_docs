                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3421






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2022






Discontinuation of Train No. 14795/96




 Dharambir Singh














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware of the discontinuation of train number 14795/96 for the last two years which used to run from Bhiwani to Chandigarh and connect with Himalayan Queen in Panipat and if so, the time by which it is proposed to be restarted;(b) whether there is a proposal to start this train from Rewari to Chandigarh as an independent train;(c) whether there is any proposal to run train number 04089/90 to Sadar Bazar which presently runs from Hisar to New Delhi; and(d) if so, the time by which it is likely to be done and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMUNICATIONS ANDELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI ASHWINI VAISHNAW) (a) and (b)  14095/96 Delhi Sarai Rohilla-Kalka Himalayan Queen Express and 14795/96 Bhiwani-Kalka Express were running amalgamated/de-amalgamated, with shunting at Panipat. Keeping in view the safety of passengers and to avoid shunting with passengers onboard, it has been decided to discontinue Slip Coaches/link trains and to introduce a new independent train between Bhiwani and Panipat.(c)  and (d) 04089/90 Special is being operated  between Hisar and New Delhi via Sadar Bazar (1.5 km from New Delhi).*****
