                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CORPORATE AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

140






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.07.2019






Mid Day Meal Scheme under CSR




Jayadev Galla














                        Will the Minister of





CORPORATE AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the present limit of percentage of spending by Corporate Sector on their CSR activities;(b) whether nearly 50,000 NonGovernmental Organisations (NGOs) are involved in the Mid Day Meal Scheme and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government has any proposal to permit part of 2 per cent of CSR funds be spent on Mid Day Meal Scheme;(d) if so, whether there is any plan to increase the percentage of spending on MDMS under CSR from 2 per cent to 3 per cent; and(e) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF FINANCEAND CORPORATE AFFAIRS                                  (Smt. NIRMALA SITHARAMAN)???????? ???????? ????? ?????? (??????????????????????)(a)	to (e): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN ANSWER TO PART (a) TO (e) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 140*(20THPOSITION) FOR 1ST JULY, 2019 REGARDING MID DAY MEAL SCHEME UNDER CSR.(a)	:Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013 (Act) mandates every company having net worth Rs. 500 crore or more, or turnover of Rs. 1000 crore or more, or net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more during the immediately preceding financial year, to spend at least two per cent of the average net profits of the company made during the three immediately preceding financial years, towards Corporate Social Responsibility in eligible activities as enumerated in Schedule VII of the Act.(b)	: The details about Non-Governmental Organisations (NGOs) involved in Mid Day Meals Scheme as perAnnual Work Plan & Budget, 2018-19, Ministry of Human Resources Development is at annexure.(c)	& (e) : Schedule VII of the Act enlists areas or activities that qualify towards CSR spend.As per Section 135 (3) & (4) of the Act, the Board of the company has to ensure that the activities as are included in its CSR Policy are undertaken by the company. Further, the Government does not give any directions to Corporates to spend in any particular in area or activity, including Government Schemes.***** ANNEXUREANNEXURE REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 140*Details of NGOs working in MDMSS.No. 	State/UT 	No. of NGOs working	No. of Kitchens	No. of Institutions covered	No. of Children covered 1 	Andhra Pradesh 	4 	18 	1794 	220107 2 	Assam 	1 	2 	596 	45234 3 	Bihar 	6 	29 	2748 	822485 4 	Chhattisgarh 	4 	5 	672 	101516 5 	Gujarat 	3 	9 	3327 	538000 6 	Haryana 	1 	4 	2328 	258792 7 	Jharkhand 	1 	2 	411 	51744 8 	Karnataka 	64 	68 	5417 	721295 9 	Madhya Pradesh 	23 	41 	3659 	361050 10 	Maharashtra 	3 	10 	1608 	248508 11 	Odisha 	6 	13 	2922 	296931 12 	Punjab 	2 	6 	511 	91943 13 	Rajasthan 	3 	7 	3527 	347001 14 	Telangana 	2 	6 	1468 	148589 15 	Uttar Pradesh 	112 	135 	7928 	948996 16 	West Bengal 	88 	194 	960 	169432 17 	Delhi 	44 	54 	2973 	1006333 	Total 	    367	603 	42849 	6377956 Source: Annual Work Plan &Budget, 2018-19, Ministry of Human Resources Development*****
