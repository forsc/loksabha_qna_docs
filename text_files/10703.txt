                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4123






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.12.2019






Construction of NHs in Manipur




Lorho S. Pfoze














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)the details and the present status of National Highways (NHs) under construction in Manipur along with the amount spent/sanctioned for the purpose, NH-wise; (b) the time-frame set for completion of work thereon, NH-wise; and (c) the measures taken by the Government to expedite the work on these NHs?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) & (b) 10 projects covering 159.85 km length at an estimated cost of Rs.1,606 crore are in progress in Manipur State.  Details of ongoing projects are at Annexure-I. The funds are allocated State wise not NH-wise.  Funds spent & projects sanctioned for development of NHs entrusted to State PWD in Manipur during last five years are as under:Year	Amount Spent                       (Rs. in Crore)	Projects sanctioned(Rs. in Crore)2014-15	47.48	107.292015-16	47.75	15.372016-17	34.51	918.202017-18	197.03	935.792018-19	650.51	28.78(c)  To expedite completion of NHs projects, steps/mechanism various steps are taken which include streamlining of land acquisition & environment clearances, premium re-scheduling, close coordination with other concerned Ministries,  revamping of dispute resolution mechanism, frequent review of progress at various levels with all stakeholders involved in road construction, etc.
