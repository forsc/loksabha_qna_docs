                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

482






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.06.2019






Mega & Mini Food Parks




Rajiv Pratap Rudy














                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details on the number of Mega and Mini Food Parks, Cold Chain Infrastructure and Food Testing Laboratories established under the Sampada scheme, State-wise including Bihar and in reference to Saran district; (b)	the details on the financial allocation and the investment made to improve food processing infrastructure under the said scheme, State-wise; (c)	whether the Government has made any assessment of the number of farmers benefited/getting benefit from this scheme, State-wise; and (d)	the details on the employment opportunities created especially in the rural areas through this scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI) (a):	A Statement indicating State/UT-wise number of approved projects Mega Food Parks (MFP), Agro Processing Clusters (APC) or Mini Food Parks, Cold Chain Infrastructure and Food Testing Laboratories (FTL) under the Pradan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana (PMKSY) sanctioned including in the State of Bihar is Annexure. (b):	 Under PMKSY, state-wise funds are not allocated. The schemes of PMKSY are demand driven. The Ministry provides credit-linked capital subsidy in the form of grants-in-aid to the individuals, farmers, Farmer Producer Organisations (FPOs), Entrepreneurs, Cooperatives, Societies, Self Help Groups (SHGs), Private Companies and Central/ State PSUs etc. under its various schemes for setting up of food processing industries/ units/ projects against the Expression of Interest issued by the Ministry from time to time. An amount of Rs.6000 crore has been allocated for PMKSY for its entire period of implementation i.e during 2016-2020 majority of which has been allocated for food processing infrastructure development. For the current year an amount of Rs.1101 crore has been allocated for the schemes of PMKSY. (c) & (d): The PMKSY is expected to leverage investment of  ? 31,400 crore, handle 334 lakh MT agro-produce valuing ? 1,04,125 crore, benefit 20 lakh farmers and generate 5,30,500 direct/ indirect employment in the country by the year 2019-20. No assessment has since been made on the impact of PMKSY.*****
