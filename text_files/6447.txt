                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

143






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.11.2019






Vocational Education




Nishikant  Dubey














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) the details of measures being taken by the Government to boost vocational education inthe country;(b) whether the Government proposes to set up Special Educational Zones to serve highereducation and skill development in the country; and(c) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): To boost vocational education in the country, University Grants Commission (UGC) isproviding skill based courses (through 188 Community Colleges, 289 Institutes offering B.Vocprogrammes and 68 DDU KaushalKendras) to 68,663 students and All India Council forTechnical Education (AICTE) is running vocational degree/diploma courses through 473institutions with approved intake strength of 35,450 students. Also, Ministry is working alongwith Sector Skill Councils (SSCs) under National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) toundertake Apprenticeship/Internship degree programmes with core focus on development ofknowledge, skills, aptitude and on-job training. Some of the sectors like Logistics & Retail havecommenced Degree Apprenticeship programmes in 15 institutions.(b) & (C): No, sir.* * * * *
