                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1762






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Setting up of AMSDC




Saptagiri Sankar Ulaka














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of CIVIL AVIATIONbe pleased to state:-(a) whether the Airport Authority of India (AAI) in collaboration with the National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) has set up an Aviation Multi Skill Development Centre (AMSDC) under the Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) and if so, the details thereof;(b) the details about other various projects undertaken by the AAI under its CSR initiative during the last two years;(c) whether the Government is also planning to set up such collaboration in Odisha;(d) if so, the time by which it will be set up and if not, the reasons therefor; and(e) whether the Government has received such proposal from Odisha and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) Yes, Sir. Airports Authority of India (AAI) has undertaken projects for skill development in aviation sector on Pan India basis as part of Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR). AAI has signed two tripartite MoUs with National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) and National Skill Development Fund (NSDF) for setting up of Aviation Multi Skill Development Centre (AMSDC) at Chandigarh and Mumbai.  These centres provide training in various skills in aviation sector such as customer service, airline reservation, cargo handling, security and ground handling.  So far, 1494 candidates have been trained out of which 842 have been placed with various employers.;(b) During FY 2019-20 & FY 2020-21, AAI has incurred expenditure of Rs 125.7 cr and Rs 40.7 cr respectively on various CSR Schemes covering sectors like Health & Nutrition, Skill Development, Education, Sports & Environment. etc. (c) & (d) No such proposal in under consideration at present.;(e) AAI has not received such proposal from Odisha.
