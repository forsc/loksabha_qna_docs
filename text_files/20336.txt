                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1599






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2021






Dry Ports in West Bengal




Locket Chatterjee














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  whether the Government has taken any steps to boost trade in the country by creating dry ports and if so, the details thereof; and(b)  whether any dry port has been created in West Bengal and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMERCE & INDUSTRY AND CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a)	 Yes, Sir. Existing policy of the Government of India encourages setting up of Inland Container Depots (ICDs) which are also known as dry ports. The policy prescribes norms to be met and approval is facilitated by an Inter-Ministerial Committee comprising, inter alia, Ministry of Commerce, Department of Revenue & Ministry of Railways. As per information available with Ministry of Railways, Container Corporation of India Limited (CONCOR), a Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSE) under Ministry of Railways Currently has 43 dry ports spread across the country besides a large number of dry ports owned by other private companies.(b)	As per information collected, there are 3 ICDs in West Bengal. These are at:(1)	 Majherhat, Kolkata owned by CONCOR,(2)	 Durgapur owned by M/s Allied ICD Services Limited (AISL); and (3)	New Jalpaiguri developed by The Siliguri Jalpaiguri Development Authority (SJDA) and run by M/s Pristine Hindustan Infra Projects Private Limited.	*******
