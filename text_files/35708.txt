                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3022






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






Essential Facilities to Police Stations




Su Thirunavukkarasar














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware that a number of police stations in the country do not have any vehicle, telephone, wireless set or mobile;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government has issued any direction to the States to equip police stations with adequate essential facilities;(d) if so, the details thereof along with the action by the States on Centre’s directive; and(e) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) and (b):	    Since ‘Police’ and ‘Public Order’ are State subjects as per the Seventh Schedule of the Constitution of India, it is primarily the responsibility of the State Governments/UT Administrations to equip police stations with adequate essential facilities. As per the figures compiled by the Bureau of Police Research and Development (BPR&D), as on 1.1.2020, the numbers of police stations in the country not having certain items are – (i) Vehicle : 257  (ii) Telephone : 638 (iii)  Wireless set or mobile : 143. The -2-L.S.US.Q.NO.3022 FOR 22.03.2022Central Government supplements the efforts of the State Governments towards equipping and modernizing their police forces, by providing financial assistance under the scheme of ‘Assistance to States for Modernisation of Police (ASMP)’. The financial assistance is provided for acquisition of various police related items including communication equipment as per the requirements and strategic priorities of the State Governments.(c) to (e):	A Review Committee studied reports of the previous Commissions and Committees on Police Reforms and submitted its report in March, 2005. The Committee shortlisted 49 recommendations. The recommendations of the Review Committee have been sent to State Governments and Union Territory Administrations for taking appropriate action.  One of the 49 recommendations of this Committee is that basic facilities should be provided in all police stations as per norms evolved by BPR&D. The State Governments and Union Territory Administrations have to take appropriate action on these recommendations.******
