                                    TRIBAL AFFAIRS

                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3312






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.08.2021






Covid-19 in Tribal Areas of Maharashtra




Hemant Tukaram Godse














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has introduced any new schemes for the development of Tribals across the country during Covid-19 pandemic including Maharashtra; (b)	if so, the details thereof; (c)	the details of the funds allocated to these new schemes, State and UTs wise; (d)	whether the Government has any mechanism to deliver these schemes to the tribals living in extremely remote areas; and (e)	if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR TRIBAL AFFAIRS(SMT. RENUKA SINGH SARUTA)   (a) to (c): The Ministry of Tribal Affairs (MoTA) has asked all States including Maharashtra to prioritize Livelihood and Health related proposals under Grants under 275(1) and SCA to TSS  for the development of tribals during Covid-19 pandemic.  Further,  MoTA envisages to revamp the existing scheme of Special Central Assistance to Tribal Sub-Scheme (SCA to TSS) for next five years i.e. 2021-22 to 2025-26 for integrated development of villages with significant tribal population. The Ministry has also envisaged a comprehensive livelihood development programme catering to STs for a period 2021-22 to 2025-26.Presently, Tribal Sub-Plan (TSP) (now called Scheduled Tribe Component (STC) at central level), is a strategy for earmarking funds for welfare and development of tribal people which includes support for education, health, sanitation, water supply, livelihood etc. Major part of infrastructure development in tribal dominated areas and provision of basic amenities to tribal people in the country is carried out through various schemes / programmes of concerned Central Ministries and the State Governments concerned, while the Ministry of Tribal Affairs (MoTA) provides additive to these initiatives by way of plugging gaps. Details of funds allocated by different Central Ministries / Departments under their schemes are reflected in the Union Budget as Statement 10B which is laid on the table of the House. Details of funds released by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs to the States under its schemes during the last three financial years is at Annexure 1.  Details of funds granted by different Central Ministries / Departments to the States under STC during last three financial years is at Annexure 2. Details of funds allocated and expenditure made by States / UTs under their Schemes out State TSP for welfare and development of STs during the last three financial years is at Annexure 3.(d) to (e)  To ensure that all schemes are delivered to tribals in extremely remote areas Ministry has end to end digitised all its schemes, where in every process from invitation of proposal for release of grant to state, NGO and TRI or for release of scholarship to ST students is monitored online through scheme specific e-gov application like DBT Portal (dbttribal.gov.in) for scholarship schemes, TRI portal (tri.tribal.gov.in) for research projects and ADIGRAMS  (Grant management System) for funds released to States.   Ministry has also developed a Performance dashboard (dashboard.tribal.gov.in) where the data of funds release and beneficiary is available in public domain and everyone can see how the funds of the Ministry are being utilized under various schemes. This has been made possible from the cooperation of all the states who are sharing the relevant data in a timely manner. Since major schemes for the tribal welfare are implemented by states, through performance dashboard the better performing states will motivate others to do better and will create a sense of accountability.
