                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1711






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Use of Drip Irrigation




Sanjay(Kaka) Ramchandra Patil














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 whether the Government is likely to give instructions to farmers for maximum use of drip irrigation keeping in mind the problem of drought across the country;(b)	  whether  the  Government  has proposed or is likely to propose any funds or subsidy scheme to promote the use of drip irrigation; and(c)	 if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER  OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):	 Department of Agriculture, Cooperation & Farmers Welfare (DAC&FW) is assisting farmers to use micro irrigation both Drip & Sprinkler through the scheme - ‘Per Drop More Crop’ component of Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY-PDMC) launched in 2015-16.           The Department encourages the farmers to take advantage of the scheme by wide publicity through press & print media, publication of leaflets/booklets, organization of workshops, exhibitions, farmer fairs, information on State/Government of India web portals etc.  In addition, Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) imparts training and organizes  field demonstrations through Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVK) to educate farmers for promotion of Micro Irrigation.  (b) & (c):	  The Government provides financial assistance @ 55% for small and marginal farmers and @ 45% for other farmers for installation of Drip and Sprinkler systems under the PMKSY- PDMC. In addition, some States provide additional incentives for encouraging farmers to adopt Micro Irrigation.   Further, 25% higher amounts have been taken into calculation of subsidy for the North Eastern and Himalayan states and 15% higher for States with low penetration of Micro Irrigation for larger adoption of systems by the farmers under the scheme.	Under the scheme, Central Assistance of Rs. 9285.84 crore  was released to States during the period of 2015-16 to 2018-19 and an amount of Rs. 3500.00 crore (BE) is allocated for the current year.   ********
