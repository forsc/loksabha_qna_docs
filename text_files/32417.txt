                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3849






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.12.2021






Impact of Irrigation on Farmers Income




Pushpendra Singh Chandel














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has conducted any assessment of increase in the income and prosperity of the farmers through various water projects in the country including Bundelkhand;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether investment in agriculture in the areas covered under water projects due to availability of water has also increased; and(d) if so, the details thereof, State-wise including Bundelkhand?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE ???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????	(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)& (b):  No specific study has been conducted for assessment of increase in the income and prosperity of the farmers through various water projects. However, Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare is implementing the Per Drop More Crop component of Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY-PDMC)since 2015-16. Recent evaluation study of the scheme conducted by NITI Aayog indicates that the Micro Irrigation is relevant in achieving the National Priorities such as substantially improving on-farm water use efficiency, enhancing crop productivity, generating employment opportunities etc.  Further, the scheme has been quite effective in terms of ensuring benefits to farmers with high productivity, reducing fertilizer usage, reduction in labour expenses, other input costs and overall income enhancement of farmers.Further, an independent evaluation study conducted byNABARD Consultancy Services (NABCONS) through National Rainfed Area Authority (NRAA) during 2014 on Special Package for Drought Mitigation in Bundelkhand Region of Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh has reported increase in irrigation potentialupto 23% in U.P. and 37% in M.P., besides, increase in productivity of cropsup to 51% in Kharif and up to 42% in Rabi in the project areas of Bundelkhand region.(c) & (d): 	Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) was launched during the year 2015-16 with the objective to enhance physical access of water and expand cultivable area under assured irrigation, improve on-farm water use efficiency, introduce sustainable water conservation practices etc. The PMKSY envisages achieving convergence of investments in irrigation at the field level through preparation of District and State irrigation plans. Thedetails of components of PMKSY are as under: i.	  Accelerated Irrigation Benefit Programme (AIBP): To focus on faster completion of ongoing Major / Medium Irrigation projects.  From  2016-17 onwards, 99 prioritized Projects have been identified for expeditious completion adopting innovative funding through creation of ‘Long Term Irrigation Fund’ under NABARD for Mission Mode completion of projects. Out of the prioritised 99 projects, 3 projects in Madhya Pradesh i.e. Singhpore Project, Sindh Project Phase II, Barriyarpur LBC and one project Arjun Sahayak Project in Uttar Pradesh falls in Bundelkhand region. This component is being implemented by Deptt. of Water Resources, River Development & Ganga Rejuvenation (DoWR,RD&GR). State wise details of Central Assistance released under PMKY-AIBP for prioritised 99 projects including projects in Bundelkhand region is given at Annexure I.ii.	  PMKSY– Har Khet Ko Pani (HKKP): Command Area Development & Water Management (CAD&WM) activities of AIBP projects, Source augmentation, distribution, ground water development, lift irrigation, repair, restoration, renovation (RRR) of traditional water bodies.  This component is being implemented by DoWR, RD&GR. State wise details of Central Assistance released under CAD&WM, Surface Minor Irrigation (SMI) scheme, RRR of Water Bodies scheme including projects in Bundelkhand region of Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh is given at Annexure II, III & IV respectively.iii.	PMKSY-Watershed Development Component (WDC): Ridge area treatment, drainage line treatment, soil and moisture conservation, water harvesting structure, livelihood support activities and other watershed works. This component is being implemented by Department of Land Resources. State wise details of Central Assistance released under PMKSY-WDC including projects in Bundelkhand region of Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh is given at Annexure V.iv.	PMKSY- Per Drop More Crop (PDMC): To address the water use efficiency at farm level through Micro Irrigation viz. Drip and Sprinkler Irrigation Systems. This component is being implemented by Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare (DA&FW). State wise details of Central Assistance released under PMKSY-PDMC including projects in Bundelkhand region of Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradeshis given at Annexure VI.
