                                    ANSWERED ON:

Skill Development of Skilled Manpower




Badruddin Ajmal














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether his Ministry was set up for co-ordination of all Skill Development efforts across the country removal of disconnect between demand and supply of skilled manpower etc; (b)	if so, the details of the schemes launched/being implemented at present by the Government to achieve its goal and target; (c)	the number of beneficiaries of the schemes/programs run by the ministry during last three years including the current year; and(d)	the details of fund allocated/utilized for these schemes, scheme-wise, year-wise and State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) :	Yes, Sir.(b)	The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship is delivering skills through following skill development schemes across the country:S.No.	Schemes	Objective1	Pradhan Mantri Kaushal VikasYojana (PMKVY)	With an aim to empower country’s youth and continue the journey of Skill India Mission in building India as the skill capital of the world, the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE) launched the third phase of its flagship scheme—  Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY 3.0) in January 2021. 2	Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) Scheme	Scheme of Jan ShikshanSansthan (NGOs) for Skill Development to impart vocational skills in non-formal mode to non-literate, neo-literates, persons with rudimentary level of education upto 8th and school drop-outs upto 12th standard in the age group of 15-45 years. The priority groups are women, SC, ST, minorities and other backward sections of the society.3	National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS)	This Scheme is for promoting apprenticeship training and increasing the engagement of apprentices by providing financial support to industrial establishments undertaking apprenticeship program under The Apprentices Act, 1961.  4	Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs)/ Craftsmen Training Scheme (CTS)	This scheme is for providing long-term training through 14,716 Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) operational across the country.  Further, under the Skill India Mission, the Government is implementing various Skill Development Schemes/Programmes across Central Ministries/Departments, for providing a range of Skill Development training programmes on pan India basis. Amongst other major schemes for skilling run by other Ministries, Ministry of Rural Development is implementing Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushalya Yojana (DDU-GKY) and Rural Self Employment Training Institutes (RSETIs) schemes, while the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs is implementing the Deen Dayal Antyodaya Yojana- National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM).
