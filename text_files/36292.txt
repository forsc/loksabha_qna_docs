                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3250






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2022






Impact of Covid-19 on Silk Industry




Manoj Kumar Tiwari













                                                    Sangamlal Kadedin Gupta
                                                








                                                    P.P. Chaudhary
                                                








                                                    Mahendra Singh Solanky
                                                








                                                    Rajbahadur Singh
                                                








                                                    Rajendra Agrawal
                                                








                                                    Brijbhushan Sharan Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government had conducted any study to understand the impact of COVID-19 pandemic on Indian silk industry; (b) if so, the details thereof; (c) the key findings of the said study, including those specific to Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh; (d) whether the Government has taken any actions thereon to alleviate the conditions of workers employed in the silk industry; and (e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR TEXTILES  (SMT. DARSHANA JARDOSH)(a) & (b):	Yes. Central Silk Board has conducted a study using online questionnaire during April-May 2020 to analyze the impact of Corona virus outbreak across the value chain in the silk industry. The responses were received from the farmers, reelers, weavers, traders and exporters from different states such as Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Haryana, Jammu & Kashmir, Karnataka, Kerala, Maharashtra, Mizoram, Tamil Nadu, Telangana and West Bengal. The total sample size used for the study was 1530 comprising 1043 farmers, 92 reelers, 188 weavers, 188 traders and 19 exporters.(c) to (e):	During the pandemic  period, core activities of the silk value chain were continued by the farmers, reelers, weavers, traders and exporters, thus helping in  subsequent revival of the sector. Central Silk Board is involved in innovative research in silk sector to address the issues and to develop the new technologies on various activities of silk value chain starting from sericulture farming to silk fabric production. ***
