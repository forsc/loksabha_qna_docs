                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

823






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Mission Indradhanush




Rita Bahuguna Joshi














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the details of main achievements of ‘Mission Indradhanush’ and the phases completed by it till now;(b) the manner in which the Government proposes to expand this Mission in future; and(c) whether any assessment of the impact of this campaign has been made and if so, the impact of the same on national health scenario?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	A total of nine phases of Mission Indradhanush (including Gram Swaraj Abhiyan which was carried out in 16,850 villages across 541 districts and Extended Gram Swaraj Abhiyan which was carried out in 48,929 villages across 117 aspirational districts) have been carried out covering 690 districts across all 36 States/UTs. During these phases of Mission Indradhanush a total of 3.76 crore children and 94.6 lakh pregnant women have been vaccinated.(b):	Mission Indradhanush (MI) aims to strengthen routine immunization with a focused approach on pockets of low immunization coverage. Expansion of this mission is envisaged by integrating Mission Indradhanush sessions in Routine Immunization micro-plans.(c):	Yes, the Government has assessed the impact of Mission Indradhanush. The first two phases of Mission Indradhanush resulted in 6.7% increase in full immunization coverage.  Coverage evaluation survey was carried out in 190 districts which were covered during phase five of Mission Indradhanush. The survey showed an average increase of 18.5% in full immunization coverage in these districts  as compared to the full immunization coverage reported in these 190 districts in the National Family Health Survey (NFHS-4).
