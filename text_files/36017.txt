                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COAL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3424






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2022






Production from Discontinued Mines




Gaurav Gogoi














                        Will the Minister of





COAL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has announced that 100 shut or production discontinued mines of Coal India will be operated in partnership with private companies;(b)	if so, whether a consultation meeting with private sector has been held in this regard and if so, the details of participants;(c)	the terms of revenue-sharing envisioned for such partnership;(d)	the reasons for shutting or discontinuing production in these 100 mines;(e)	whether such a step is counter-productive to the 7 February, 2022 statement of the Government with respect to eco-friendly use of abandoned coal mines;(f)	the impact of the decision to use de-coaled areas for extensive plantation, development of eco/mine tourism parks, agricultural ?elds, orchards, creating water bodies to augment ground water recharge and drinking water supply, etc.; and(g)	the e?ects of reopening of 100 mines on India’s coal phase down pledges made in COP26?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS, COAL AND MINES (SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI)(a)	: Government has tentatively identi?ed as of now 13 discontinued mines of Coal India Limited to be operated in partnership with private companies.(b)	:   Consultations with stakeholders of private sector for preparation of model document for tendering of these discontinued mines was held on 24.02.2022 at New Delhi and on 11.03.2022 at CMPDI (HQ), Ranchi. The following participants have attended the meeting:- 1.	 M/s Adani Enterprises2.	 M/s Shyam Steel, Kolkata3.	 M/s Birla Corporation4.	 M/s Odisha Coal and Power Limited5.	 M/s JSPL6.	 M/s GSECL, Vadodra7.	 M/s NALCO8.	 M/s JMS Mining Pvt. Ltd.9.	 M/s SMS Limited10.	M/s Maheshwari Mining Pvt. Ltd.11.	M/s WBPDCL, Kolkata12.	M/s Eimeco Elecon Limted13.	M/s Tata Steel14.	M/s EMIL, Kolkata15.	M/s BLA Power16.	M/s BGR Mining Infra Limited17.	M/s OMC Limited, Bhubhaneshwar18.	M/s AEL19.	M/s  UPRVUNL20.	M/s NTPC Ltd.21.	M/s RCCPL Pvt. Ltd.22.	M/s THDC India Limited23.	M/s Technoblast Mining Corporation24.	M/s BS Ispat Ltd.25.	M/s SCCL26.	M/s Hindalco27.	M/s South West Pinnacle Explosive Ltd.28.	M/s Jaypee Group29.	M/s Sainik Mining & Allied Services Ltd.30.	M/s Sandvik India31.	M/s Vedanta32.	M/s Dalmia Cement Bharat Ltd.(c)	: Model tender document is under preparation.(d)	: The reasons for shutting or discontinuing production was as follows:-1.	Viability issues / unprofitable operation of mines2.	Difficult geo-mining conditions3.	Lack of suitable technology to extract deeper resources, etc. (e): No, Sir.(f)	: After extraction of coal from these mines, de-coaled areas of these mines will also be used for extensive plantation, development of eco/mine tourism parks, agricultural ?elds, orchards, creating water bodies to augment ground water recharge and drinking water supply, etc. (g)	: The above decision to operate some discontinued mines in partnership with private companies, has been taken to meet the high demand of coal. It is not likely to a?ect India’s coal phase down pledges made in COP26.***
