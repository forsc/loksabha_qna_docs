                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    POWER






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

816






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2021






Implementation of UJALA Scheme




Jayadev Galla














                        Will the Minister of





POWER


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has compiled a data of the number of metered connections for the implementation of Unnat Jyoti by Affordable LEDs for All (UJALA) Scheme;(b)	if so, the details of the number of such connections in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana;(c)	the number of LED bulbs distributed under the UJALA Scheme in Andhra Pradesh, district-wise;(d)	the details of the number of defected bulbs that have been reportedly distributed under UJALA Scheme; and(e) 	the amount of energy targeted for saving along with the current amount of energy that has been saved by implementation of the said scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF POWER AND NEW & RENEWABLE ENERGY (SHRI  R.K. SINGH)(a) & (b): Unnat Jyoti by Affordable LEDs for All (UJALA) programme is being implemented by Energy Efficiency Services Limited (EESL). EESL has not compiled data of the number of metered connections for implementation of UJALA Scheme. However, as per information furnished by Central Electricity Authority (CEA), the total number of metered consumers in Andhra Pradesh is 1,77,60,935 and in Telangana is 1,38,20,574.(c): 	LED bulbs distributed under UJALA Scheme in the State of Andhra Pradesh District-wise are given at Annexure.(d): 	No defective bulbs have been distributed under UJALA scheme. There is a provision for replacement of the defective LED bulbs within the warranty period of one year without any additional cost to the consumer. The reported bulb failure rate under UJALA scheme is less than 1%.(e):	UJALA being a voluntary Scheme, no target was set for energy saving. As on date, under the UJALA Scheme 37.78 Crore LED bulbs have been distributed resulting into energy saving of 47.775 Billion kwh per annum.
