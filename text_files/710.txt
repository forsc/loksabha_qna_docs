                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

587






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.06.2019






Online Fraud




Gopal Chinayya Shetty














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) the details of internet users who have been victimised through online fraud during each of the last three years till date, State-wise;(b) the steps taken by the Government to protect people from online fraud;(c) the number of online fraud cases registered during the said period, State-wise;(d) the amount of money involved in it;(e) the number of cases solved/ unsolved by the police during the above period, State-wise; and(f) the steps taken by the Government to solve the unsolved cases?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (f):	‘Police’ and ‘Public order’ are State subjects as per the Constitution of India and States/UTs are primarily responsible for prevention, detection, investigation and prosecution of crimes through their law enforcement machinery. The Law Enforcement Agencies take legal action as per provisions of law against the cyber fraud offenders. -2-L.S.US.Q.NO.587 FOR 25.06.2019Central Government has taken steps to spread awareness about such frauds, issue of alerts/advisories, capacity building/training of law enforcement officers/ judges/ prosecutors, improving of cyber forensics facilities etc. to prevent such frauds and to speed up investigation. As per National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB), the State/UT wise details of cases registered, victims registered, cases charge sheeted, cases convicted, cases disposed of by police, cases pending investigation at the end of the year, persons arrested, persons charge sheeted and persons convicted under Cheating (section 420 IPC), forgery (section 465, 468 & 471 IPC) and Criminal Breach of Trust/Fraud (section 406, 408 & 409 IPC) for Cyber Crimes during 2014-2016 is at Annexure-I. Data on amount of money involved is not maintained centrally.******
