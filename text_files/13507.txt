Establishment of DMFs




Gowdar Mallikarjunappa Siddeshwara














                        Will the Minister of





MINES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the status of establishment of District Mineral Foundations (DMFs) in each of the districts affected by mining operations and details of action taken against States not establishing DMFs; (b) the amount of funds held by DMFs district-wise, the per cent utilization of these funds, and the details of projects funded by DMFs; (c) whether an oversight mechanism exists to prevent diversion of funds to purposes other than welfare of people in mining districts and if so, the details thereof; and (d) whether Ministry has come across incidents of Companies not contributing to DMFs and if so, the details thereof and the action taken thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF MINES, COAL AND PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS(SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI)(a): As per the Information available with Ministry of Mines, District Mineral Foundations (DMFs) have been constituted in 574 districts (out of 585 districts) in 21 States.(b): Information is enclosed as Annexure.(c): Ministry of Mines, issued PMKKKY guidelines on 17th September 2015 and State Governments have been instructed to incorporate these guidelines into the rules framed by them for District Mineral Foundation. As per the guidelines, mining affected areas have been classified into directly and indirectly affected area. It is also stipulated for spending 60% of the funds on works in High Priority Areas which deal with improving the basic human development index in the areas directly affected by mining. Additionally, in respect of villages affected by mining situated within the scheduled areas, approval of the Gram Sabha for all plans, programs and projects taken up under PMKKKY has also been necessitated. Moreover, it is also ensured that funds are transferred to all agencies and beneficiaries into their bank accounts only. Furthermore, the annual audit of the DMF funds ensures that the funds are being utilized as per the DMF rules defined by the State Government.(d): No such instances have been reported from States to the Ministry of Mines till now.*****Annexure referred to in reply to part (b) of Unstarred Question No – 2212(Number of pages-20)State wise project DetailsSr. No.	State	No. of Projects Sanctioned1	Andhra Pradesh	110112	Chhattisgarh	362943	Goa	44	Gujarat	127565	Jharkhand	174256	Karnataka	34147	Maharashtra	52638	Madhya Pradesh	89359	Odisha	1322810	Rajasthan	1692511	Tamilnadu	152412	Telangana	1867513	Assam	11414	Bihar	1915	Himachal Pradesh	5616	Jammu & Kashmir	3117	Kerala	018	Meghalaya	019	Uttarakhand	40420	Uttar Pradesh	69921	West Bengal	59Total	146836District wise Fund Collection & Utilization Report for Andhra Pradesh			As on January 2020Sr. No.	Name of District	Fund Collection (In Cr.)
