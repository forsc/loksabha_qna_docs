                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1023






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2020






TB and HIV Cases




Margani Bharat













                                                    Sisir Kumar Adhikari
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the details of Tuberculosis (TB) cases reported in the country during the last five years, year, district and State/UT-wise;(b) whether the Government has noted that 60 per cent of TB cases are from the productive age group 15 to 45 years and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether after taking several positive actions the tuberculosis patients are increasing in the country and in the last two years there has been an increase of more than 16 per cent of TB patients, if so, the details thereof and the extent to which undernutrition, smoking and pollution have contributed to the spread of TB;(d) whether with TB, HIV infections are also in a positive trend; and(e) if so, the details thereof and the proposed interventions formulated by the Government to eliminate/ eradicate TB and HIV in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	The details of District and State / UT-wise Tuberculosis (TB) cases reported in the country during the last five years is placed at Annexure 1(b):	Yes. The Government has noted that majority of TB cases are from productive age group of 15 to 45 years. As per latest India TB report, in 2018 about 59% of TB notified cases were from the productive age group of 15 to 44 years.Age group	Number and percentage <15 yrs	137570 (6%)15-44	1268705 (59%)>45 yrs	749620 (35%)(c):	Yes. The tuberculosis patients reported under the programme are increasing in the country. Due to increase in diagnostic capacity of the programme and enhanced case finding efforts, the total TB cases notified have increased 16% in 2018 and 9% in 2019.Nevertheless, the incidence of TB has reduced from 204 per lakh population in 2017 to 199 per lakh population in 2018. Undernutrition, smoking and indoor air pollution are among the main causes for Tuberculosis in India. Undernutrition and smoking contributed to 25% and 6% cases of Tuberculosis annually as per WHO estimates. Studies have shown exposure to solid fuels for cooking increased risk of Tuberculosis, with 4% increase in Tuberculosis cases with 10 units increase in Air Quality Index.(d):	No. The percentage of HIV infection among TB patients has decreased from 2.6% in 2015 to 2.3% in 2018.(e):	The Ministry is implementing following key initiatives/ activities.•	Collaborative TB-HIV activities for treatment of TB-HIV co-infected patients including Single window delivery of TB and HIV services for all HIV positive individuals through ART Centers.•	Intensified TB Case Finding (ICF) by deployment of rapid molecular diagnostics •	Infection control through Airborne Infection measures •	Reduction in TB infection through provision of Isoniazid Preventive Therapy (IPT).
