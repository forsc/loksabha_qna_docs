                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1516






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.07.2019






Purchase of Houses by Central Excise Department




Kanakmal Katara














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	Whether the Central Excise Department has purchased houses from Rajasthan Housing Board in Jaipur for its officers and employees;  (b)	if so, the number of houses purchased along with the amount of funds spent on their purchase;(c)	the number of houses out of the said houses lying vacant at present along with the reasons therefor;(d)	whether any written complaints have been received by the Government regarding the dilapidated conditions of some houses and the anti social activities in these vacant houses; and(e)	if so, the details thereof along with the action taken by the Government on these complaints?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	Yes(b)	12 Flats for residential purposes and 2 rooms for office purpose was purchased in the year 1997 by making payment of Rs. 1.02 Crores to Rajasthan Housing Board, Jaipur.(c)	9 Flats and 2 rooms for office purposes, remained vacant due to its poor condition and being unfit for residing. The proposal for repair and renovation work (Civil & Electric) has been approved by the HOD on 31.8.2018 and 31.10.2018. Presently, this work is being done through CPWD.(d)	No complaints have been received at Jaipur.(e)	In view of “d” above, question does not arise.
