                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3773






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.12.2021






Revenue through FM Radio




Ashok Mahadeorao Nete














                        Will the Minister of





INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is earning revenue through Frequency Modulation (FM) radio;(b)	if so, the revenue being earned as on date there from, State-wise;(c)	 the details of FM Radio stations working as on date, State-wise;(d)	the average estimated cost of setting up FM Radio station;(e)	whether the Government proposes to set up some more FM Radio stations; and(f)	if so, the details thereof and the names of those places, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING;  AND MINISTER OF YOUTH AFFAIRS & SPORTS(SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR)**********(a) & (b)     Yes Sir, the details of revenue being earned by Government through Private FM Radio stations in the form of annual fee during the current financial year, as on date, state-wise, are at  Annexure-I.  (c)  	Presently, 384 Private FM Radio channels are operational in 112 cities, spread across 26 States and 5 Union Territories.  In addition, Prasar Bharati is having 483 All India Radio (AIR) broadcasting centres with FM Transmitters across the country.  The state-wise details of these private and AIR FM transmitting stations are given at Annexure-II.(d)   	Prasar Bharati is having AIR FM transmitters in the range of 100 Watts to 20 kW power.  The average estimated cost of a typical 10 kW FM Station with 100 meter high tower is ?15.00 crore.(e)  & (f) 	Yes Sir, Under the extant policy guidelines on Expansion of FM radio broadcasting services through private agencies (Phase-III), 236 cities have been approved for auction of FM radio channels.  State-wise name of cities are at Annexure-III.  Prasar Bharati has approved setting up of FM transmitters at 116 more places.  State-wise name of places are at Annexure-IV
