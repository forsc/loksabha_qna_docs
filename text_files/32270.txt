                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3702






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.12.2021






Human Trafficking




Dayanidhi Maran













                                                    Gaurav Gogoi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has jurisdiction over all crimes related to human trafficking, if so, the details thereof;(b) the criteria and procedure for allocation to the National Investigation Agency (NIA); and(c) the rate of solving human trafficking cases by Anti Human Trafficking Units (AHTU) during the last five years along with the number of cases solved during the said period, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI AJAY KUMAR MISHRA)(a):	‘Police’ and ‘Public Order’ are State subjects under the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India. The responsibility to maintain law and order as well as investigation and prosecution of crimes, including the crime of human trafficking, vests with respective State Governments who are competent to deal with such offences under extant provisions of law.(b):	The National Investigation Agency (NIA) Act, 2008 was amended in 2019, inter-alia, authorizing the National Investigation Agency to investigate cases of human trafficking under Sections 370 and 370A of IPC, having inter-State and international  ramifications. Section 6 of the NIA Act provides for detailed criteria for investigation of Scheduled Offences, which inter-alia provides that where the Central Government is of the opinion that the offence is a Scheduled Offence and it is a fit case to be investigated by the Agency, it shall direct the Agency to investigate the said offence.(c):	The National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) compiles crime statistics reported to it by the States and Union Territories (UTs) and publishes the same in its annual publication ‘Crime in India’. The latest published report is of the year 2020. State/UT-wise details of cases of human trafficking reported, cases charge-sheeted, convicted, acquitted/discharged, cases in which trials completed and conviction rate during the last five years are at Annexure.*****
