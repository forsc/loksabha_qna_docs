                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4984






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2019






Special Package for Farmers affected by Natural Calamities




Abdul Majeed Ariff













                                                    Vellalath Kochukrishnan Nair. Sreekandan
                                                








                                                    H. Vasanthakumar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of welfare schemes launched by the Union Government during the last three years for the welfare of farmers hit by natural calamities, State-wise including Kerala; (b) 	the number of disaster-affected farmers getting benefited by these schemes, district-wise in Kerala; (c) 	whether Government would consider to undertake a survey in this regard to provide benefits to affected farmers; (d) 	whether it is true that the Government is considering to provide/has made any provision for giving a special package/financial assistance in districts where agriculture has been affected due to natural calamities, if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise including Kerala, if not, the reasons therefor; (e) 	whether the Government has received any demand in this regard, if so, the details thereof; and (f) 	the details of the funds spent under the PM- AASHA scheme for welfare of farmers during the last year and the current year, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????            ( SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)  (a) to (c):      The State Government is primarily responsible for providing necessary relief measures in the wake of natural calamities. For undertaking relief measures, funds are available with the State Government in the form of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF). Additional financial assistance, over and above SDRF, is considered from National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF) for natural calamities of severe nature and is approved on the basis of Memorandum received from the State Government, in accordance with established procedures.  Contd….2/--2-The Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare is concerned with providing relief under NDRF to farmers who have lost their crops due to drought, hailstorm, pest attack and cold wave/frost. An amount of Rs.15823.36 crore has been approved from NDRF during 2016-17 to 2018-19 to the States affected by the above said calamities (Annexure –I).The Government of India has evolved several schemes/programmes to address the need for drought mitigation and other requirements of the farmers under Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY), Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY), besides implementation of Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) and National Agriculture Market Scheme (e-NAM), etc. for protecting farmers’ interests. The States have been given flexibility under RKVY to plan region specific interventions for the farmers.Agriculture is a state subject. The State Governments are responsible for disbursement of funds to the affected farmers.  All individual beneficiary-oriented assistance is mandatorily/necessarily disbursed through the bank account of the beneficiary.  In order to improve the disbursement and transparency in providing relief to the beneficiaries under various items, the State Government has to prepare a consolidated list of individual beneficiaries in whose bank account funds have been transferred.  The list so prepared should be displayed on their website as well as the State/District and block/taluk levels for the purpose of verification and social audit.(d) & (e): 	Based on the memorandum submitted by the State Government of Kerala after flood and landslide in July-August 2018, the Government of India had approved an assistance of Rs. 3048.39 crore from NDRF for flood and Landslide and air bills for utilizing the services of Air force helicopter for rescue and relief as per actuals.The Government of India, in order to mitigate damages to the horticulture crops in the wake of flood in Kerala in 2018, had approved a special package of Rs.93.39 crore (Central share of Rs.56.03 & State share of Rs.37.36 crore) under Mission For Integrated Development Horticulture (MIDH) on 23rd August, 2018 and the entire amount of central share was released on 28.08.2018. During 2017-18 & 2018-19, Government of Kerala has not submitted any Memorandum seeking financial assistance from NDRF in the wake of drought. However, during 2016-17, Government of Kerala has submitted Memorandum seeking financial assistance from NDRF in the wake of drought. In response Government of India has approved Rs.112.05 crore from NDRF to the State Government (Annexure-I). (f):  	Under PM-AASHA, the procurement of Pulses and Copra has to be made under Price Support Scheme and for oilseeds, State/Union Territory (UT) Governments are offered a bouquet of Price Deficiency Payment Scheme (PDPS) and Price Support Scheme, with the flexibility to choose either of them in a given marketing season for particular oilseeds for the entire State/UT. Details of procurement made under price support system and numbers of farmers benefited are at Annexure –II.
