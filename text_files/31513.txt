                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

14.12.2021






Rural Unemployment




S.R. Parthiban














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether rural unemployment rate is increasing since the pandemic, if so, details of monthly data since 2020;(b) 	the steps taken by the Government to increase employment and skill development in rural areas; and(c) 	the steps that have been taken by Government to support the unemployed sectors of the rural background to meet their livelihood?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a)  As per the Annual Reports on Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) the estimate of Unemployment Rate (UR) by usual status for rural areas during July 2017 – June 2020 is given below: Estimates of Unemployment Rate (UR) by usual status for rural areas in percentagePeriod	                       Unemployment Rate (UR)July 2017 – June 2018	             5.3July 2018 – June 2019 	             5.0July 2019 – June 2020	             4.0Source: Annual Reports, PLFS : 2019-20 (b) & (c) Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (Mahatma Gandhi NREGS) is a demand driven wage employment programme which provide for the enhancement of livelihood security of the households in rural areas of the country by providing at least one hundred days of guaranteed wage employment in every financial year to every household whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manual work. It provides fall back options for livelihood for the rural households when no better employment opportunities are available.Project ‘UNNATI’, launched in December 2019 by Government of India, is a skilling project that intends to upgrade the skill base of the Mahatma Gandhi NREGS workers, and thereby improve their livelihoods. A total of 11,604 candidates have been trained so far. Start-up Village Entrepreneurship Programme (SVEP), is a sub-scheme under the Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM) programme to support the Self-Help Groups (SHGs) and their family members to set-up small enterprises in the non-farm sector. SVEP develops an eco-system for enterprise development in the rural areas which consists of Community Enterprise Fund (CEF) for enterprise financing, Cadre of Community Resource Persons-Enterprise Promotion (CRP-EP) for providing Business Support Services comprising of preparation of business plans, trainings, market linkages etc. and dedicated Block Resource Center (BRC) for providing all necessary information to entrepreneurs.  As on 31st October, 2021,  a total number of 232 project proposals and 158 Detailed Project Reports (DPR) have been approved covering 29 States and Union Territories. A total of 1.78 lakh enterprises have been formed and supported till October, 2021. There are two skill development programmes for rural youth under DAY-NRLM,   namely, Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushal Yojana (DDU-GKY) and Rural Self  Employment Training Institutes (RSETIs). Both these programmes are aimed at increasing employability of rural youth either for wage or self-employment.
