                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

730






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Vande Bharat Mission during COVID-19




Sukanta Majumdar













                                                    Raja Amareshwara Naik
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has launched its repatriation drive, Vande Bharat Mission (VBM) during the COVID-19 pandemic;(b) if so, the details thereof including expenditure incurred on VBM till date along with the number of persons brought back, phase and country-wise;(c) whether the Civil Aviation industry has been worst affected by lockdown due to COVID-19 Pandemic and if so, the details thereof including the Government''s reaction thereto;(d) whether the Government has planned to provide any stimulus package to provide relief to Civil Aviation sector and if so, the details thereof; and(e) the steps taken/being taken by the Government to revive Civil Aviation Industry in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) Yes Sir;(b) ;    i. The Ministry of Civil Aviation has not incurred any expenditure on the Vande Bharat Mission (VBM) till date. The VBM passengers paid for their travel.    ii. As per the information received from the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), more than 11 lakh Indians (excluding land border crossings) have returned to India till 31.08.2020, under VBM.     iii. The phase wise and country wise details of the persons brought back by Air India and Air India Express under VBM are attached herewith as Annexure.(c), (d) and (e) 1. Based on the information received from the stakeholders, the impact of COVID-19 on the civil  aviation sector are as under:;    i.	There have been regulatory restrictions on flight operations in domestic and international sectors leading to severe curtailment in operations and sub-optimal aircraft utilisation.     ii.	The domestic traffic has reduced from 58,530,038 during March-July 2019 to 12,084,952 during March-July 2020.   iii.	The international traffic has reduced from 9,345,469 during March-July in 2019 to 1,155,590 during March-July 2020.   iv.	The revenue of Indian carriers has reduced from INR 25,517 crore during April-June 2019 to INR 3,651 crore during April-June 2020.   v.	The revenue of airport operators has reduced from INR 5,745 crore during April-June 2019 to INR 894 crore during April- June 2020.   vi. In respect of Air India, the total revenue has reduced from INR 7,066 crore during April-June 2019 to INR 1,531 crore during April-June 2020.  vii.	The employment at airlines has reduced from 74,887 as on 31 March 2020 to 69,589 as on 31 July 2020.  viii. The employment at airports has reduced from 67,760 as on 31 March 2020 to 64,514 as on 31 July 2020.   ix. The employment at ground handling agencies has reduced from 37,720 as on 31 March 2020 to 29,254 as on 31 July 2020.   x.	The employment at cargo operators has reduced from 9,555 as on 31 March 2020 to 8,538 as on 31 July 2020.;    2. Efforts have been made to reduce the impact of the pandemic on the aviation sector. Some of the measures taken to support the aviation sector include, inter-alia:    i. Due to Covid-19 pandemic, domestic air services have been restarted in a calibrated manner. Initially one third (33%) of the summer schedule 2020 was allowed to be operated which was subsequently increased to 45% on 26 June 2020  and then to 60% on 02 September 2020.   ii. Operation of Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) - UDAN (Ude Desh Ka Aam Nagrik) flights were allowed without the above-mentioned restrictions.   iii. Exclusive air-links or Air Bubbles have been established with countries which include Afghanistan, Bahrain, Canada, France, Germany, Qatar, Maldives, UAE, UK and USA. These are temporary arrangements aimed at restarting international passenger services while regular international flights remain suspended due to COVID-19.   iv. Promoted private investments in existing and new airports through the Public Private Partnership (PPP) route.   v.	Adequate care taken to ensure that cargo terminals at all major airports were operational, whenever required.  vi.  GST rate reduced to 5% for domestic Maintenance, Repair and Overhaul (MRO) services. vii.  Encouraging Indian carriers to increase their share in International air cargo traffic. viii.  Route rationalisation in the Indian airspace in coordination with Indian Air Force for efficient airspace management, shorter routes and reduced fuel burn.
