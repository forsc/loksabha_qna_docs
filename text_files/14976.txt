                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3484






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2020






PNG Pipelines for Small Industries




S.T. Hasan













                                                    K. Muraleedharan
                                                








                                                    Achyutananda Samanta
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether any arrangements for the laying of PNG Gas Pipeline for the livelihood in small industries in Moradabad is likely to be made in order to ensure that their business is conducted without any impediments, if so, the details thereof including the time by which the said provision will be made available and if not, the reasons therefor;(b) whether the Government can provide details of total investment that has been made for the city gas distribution project in Bhubaneswar till date and the details of utilization of such investment;(c) whether the Government can provide details as to the number of households that have been provided with Piped Natural Gas (PNG) connections in Bhubaneswar till date including the time-frame within which all households in Bhubaneswar will receive PNG connection, if so, the details thereof;(d) whether it is a fact that Indian Oil Corporation Limited is planning to Operate 200 Compressed Natural Gas stations (CNG) in Kerala by 2022, if so, the details thereof; and(e) whether it is also a fact that presently CNG dispensation is limited to one district in Kerala and whether the dispensation of CNG in other districts would be made soon, if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (PNGRB) is the authority to grant authorization to the entities for the development of City Gas Distribution (CGD) network in Geographical Areas (GAs) as per PNGRB Act, 2006. PNGRB identifies GAs for authorizing the development of CGD network in synchronization with the development of natural gaspipeline connectivity and natural gas availability. PNGRB has authorized Torrent GasMoradabad Limited (TGML) and Torrent Gas Private Limited (TGPL) to develop City GasDistribution Network in Moradabad (part) District Geographical Area (GA) and Moradabad(Excluding already authorized area) District GA respectively. As on 31.12.2019, there areabout 5,640 PNG Domestic connections and 60 PNG Industrial connection provided byTGPL in Moradabad District.(b) & (c) PNGRB has authorized GAIL (India) Limited for development of CGD Network inKhordha District GA on 07.03.2018. Bhubaneswar is a part of Khordha District GA. As on31.12.2019, GAIL has provided about 5,000 PNG connections to household in KhordhaDistrict GA. GAIL has informed that it has made a budget provision of Rs. 243.53 crore(2018-2022) for development of CGD infrastructure and made capital expenditure as on29.2.2020 of Rs. 58.67 crore.(d) & (e) PNGRB has authorized 5 GAs i.e. Ernakulam District GA, Palakkad & TrissurDistricts GA, Kozhikode & Wayanad Districts GA, Malappuram District GA and Kannur,Kasargod & Mahe Districts GA to Indian Oil Adani Gas Private Limited (IOAGPL), a jointventure of Indian Oil Corporation Limited (IOCL) and Adani Gas Limited (AGL), which willexecute as per, minimum work program, which has provision for 259 CNG’s stations. Theentity is currently operating seven CNG stations in Ernakulam district GA.
