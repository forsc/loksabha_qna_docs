                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

333






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.07.2019






Farmers Living Below Poverty Line




Sukhbir Singh Jaunapuria














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has conducted any survey to evaluate the number of farmers living Below the Poverty Line (BPL); (b) 	if so, the details thereof, State-wise; (c) 	the details of the welfare schemes introduced by the Government for farmers during the last three years, scheme and State-wise; and (d)	 the number of farmers who got benefited from such schemes?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????		 	  (SHRI NARENDRA  SINGH TOMAR) (a) to (d):	A statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (d) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 333  DUE FOR REPLY ON 16TH JULY, 2019.(a)  & (b):	 No Sir, separate estimate on the number of farmers living Below Poverty Line is not available. Government conducts large sample surveys through NSSO for periodical estimation of population living Below Poverty Line. According to the erstwhile Planning Commission’s estimates based on Consumption Expenditure Survey (2011-12) conducted by National Sample Survey Office (NSSO), the rural population living Below Poverty Line (BPL) was 216.65 million in 2011-12. A statement on state wise population Below Poverty Line is in Annexure-I.(c) & (d): 	The Government has introduced Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) Scheme to provide an assured income support to farming community in the interim budget for 2019-20. Earlier, under  PM-KISAN, landholding farmer families, having cultivable land upto 2 hectares, were provided direct income support at the rate of Rs. 6,000 per year in three instalments of Rs. 2000 each. The 1st instalment under the Scheme has already been credited into the bank accounts of 35,603,344 beneficiaries and 2nd instalment to 31,077,444 beneficiaries as on 5th July 2019. State-wise details of beneficiaries under the scheme is given at Annexure-II. Lately, the ambit of the Scheme has been expanded to cover all farmers in the country irrespective of the size of their land holdings, subject to all other existing exclusions. With expansion of the ambit of the scheme, the expected expenditure of the Scheme for the financial year 2019-20 has been revised to Rs.87,217.50 crore, including the administrative charges of Rs.217.50 crore. Further, the Government has approved a pension scheme for all Small and Marginal Farmers (SMF) in the country, subject to certain exclusion clauses, with a view to provide them social security net as they have minimal or no savings to provide for old age and to support them in the event of consequent loss of livelihood. The scheme provides for payment of a minimum fixed pension of Rs.3,000/- per month to the eligible farmers on attaining the age of 60 years.Contd…2/--2-Besides, Government is implementing many other schemes for the upliftment of farmers, such as, National Food Security Mission, Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana, Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana, Pradhan Mantri Annadata Aay Sanrakshan Abhiyan (PM-AASHA), e-NAM (National Agriculture Market), Soil Health Card Scheme, National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture.
