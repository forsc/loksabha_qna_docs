                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4868






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.04.2022






Disruption in Elimination of TB by 2025 due to COVID-19




Mala Roy














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is likely to meet the target of eliminating Tuberculosis (TB) by 2025 amidst COVID-19 disruptions;	(b)	 if so, the details thereof and if not, the revised deadline set by the Government thereon; and(c)	the number of estimated TB deaths gone undetected and its likely fall out in the ensuring years?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a)	& (b) The Government has made steady progress towards achieving the target for elimination of Tuberculosis (TB) across the country by 2025. As per the Global TB Report, 2021 by the World Health Organization, the incidence of TB in India has reduced from 217/lakh population in 2015 to 188/lakh population in 2020. (c)	The number of deaths due to TB reported under the programme in the year 2021 for the patients initiated on TB treatment in 2020 is 76,002. ****
