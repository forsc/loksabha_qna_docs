                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4051






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Upgradation/Modernisation of Hospitals




Sambhajirao Mane Dhairyasheel













                                                    Unmesh Bhaiyyasaheb Patil
                                                








                                                    Sujay Radhakrishna Vikhepatil
                                                








                                                    Nishikant  Dubey
                                                








                                                    Heena Vijaykumar Gavit
                                                








                                                    Shrikant Eknath Shinde
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government is contemplating to upgrade/modernise the Government hospitals, particularly in rural areas of the country including Maharashtra and Jharkhand;(b) if so, the details of the Government hospitals upgraded/modernised during the last three years in Maharashtra and Jharkhand, district-wise, hospital-wise;(c) the funds allocated/proposed to be allocated for the purpose and the actual utilisation during last three years and the current year;(d) the number of private hospitals empanelled for cashless secondary and tertiary care hospitalisation in the country, State/UT wise; and(e) whether the Government has taken any steps to monitor the number of beneficiaries and allocation of funds for Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PMJAY) in the State of Maharashtra and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b): Public Health and hospitals, being a State subject, the primary responsibility of modernizing/upgrading and strengthening of existing health facilities lies with the State Governments. Under the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) however, financial and technical support is provided to States/UTs, for improving upon their healthcare systems including for the expansion of public health institutions, such as district hospitals, Community Health Centres (CHCs), Primary Health Centres (PHCs) and Sub-Health Centres (SHCs) in rural areas, including in Maharashtra and Jharkhand.	The Government is committed to setting up 1.5 lakh Health and Wellness Centres (HWCs) by upgrading Sub Health Centres (SHCs) and Primary Health Centres (PHCs) with the objective of providing comprehensive primary healthcare services to the community. More than 68,000 HWCs have already become functional in rural areas including in Maharashtra and Jharkhand. The PM AtmaNirbhar Swasth Bharat Yojana has been announced for developing capacities of primary, secondary and tertiary care health systems, strengthen existing national institutions, and create new institutions, to cater to detection and cure of new and emerging diseases. This will bein addition to the National Health Mission. The main interventions under the scheme are:a.	Support for 17,788 rural and 11,024 urban Health and Wellness Centersb.	Setting up integrated public health labs in all districts and 3382 block public health units in11 states; c.	Establishing critical care hospital blocks in 602 districts and 12 central institutions;d.	Strengthening of the National Centre for Disease Control (NCDC), its 5 regional branches and 20 metropolitan health surveillance units; e.	Expansion of the Integrated Health Information Portal to all States/UTs to connect all public health labs;f.	Operationalisation of 17 new Public Health Units and strengthening of 33 existing Public Health Units at Points of Entry, that is at 32 Airports, 11 Seaports and 7 landcrossings;g.	Setting up of 15 Health Emergency Operation Centers and 2 mobile hospitals; andh.	Setting up of a national institution for One Health, a Regional Research Platform for WHO South East Asia Region, 9 Bio-Safety Level III laboratories and 4 regionalNational Institutes for Virology.The details of district wise availability of health centres in Jharkhand and Maharashtra is given at Annexure-I(c): State/UT wise approvals and utilization under Healthcare Systems strengthening and new construction/renovation under NHM during last three years and the current year is given at annexure-II(d): As on 16th March 2021, a total of 8,119 private hospitals have been empanelled for secondary and tertiary care related hospitalizations, under the Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB-PMJAY). The State/UT wise breakup is at Annexure-III.(e): National Health Authority (NHA) - the implementing agency of AB-PMJAY is regularly assessing and monitoring the progress of the scheme on a real time basis. A dashboard with various key performance indicators (KPIs) has been designed for this purpose. The performance of the scheme is objectively evaluated in terms of cards issued, number of hospital admissions, quality of treatment provided, timely settlement of claims etc. Constant feedback is provided to the States/UTs with regards to the implementation of AB-PMJAY.As on 16th March 2021, a total of Rs. 809.53 crore have been released as Central share of funds (as per the funding pattern of 60:40 ratio of Centre and State share respectively) for the implementation of AB-PMJAY in the State of Maharashtra.
