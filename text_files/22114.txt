                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3389






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2021






Agri Infra Cess




V. Kalanidhi














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	Whether the Government has imposed Agri Infra Cess within the country;(b)	If so, the reasons therefor;(c)	The details of the number of items on which this cess will be imposed; and (d)	Whether the Government is aware that this cess will put an extra burden to the common people at a time when they are facing massive job loss, unemployment and poverty within the country?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????           (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) & (b):  Agriculture Infrastructure and Development Cess (AIDC) has been imposed vide Finance bill 2021-22 and came into effect immediately from 02.02.2021 under the provisions of the Provisional Collection of Taxes Act, 1931. This has been done for the purposes of financing the agriculture infrastructure and other development expenditure.(c):   Agriculture Infrastructure and Development Cess (AIDC) is an additional duty of excise has been imposed on Petrol and Diesel and as duty of customs on specified imported goods as enclosed in annexure. (d):   Ministry of Finance has informed that the imposition of AIDC on specified items, the Basic Custom Duty (BCD), Basic Excise Duty (BED) and Special Additional Excise Duty (SAED) rates on such items, as the case may be, have been simultaneously lowered so as to not put additional burden on consumers on most items.
