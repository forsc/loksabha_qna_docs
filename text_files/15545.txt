                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4191






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






Water Aerodrome




Kirit Premjibhai Solanki













                                                    Shantanu Thakur
                                                








                                                    Gitaben Vajesingbhai Rathva
                                                








                                                    Pradeep Kumar Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has taken a decision to set up water aerodromes in five States of the country namely  Odisha,  Gujarat,  Assam,  Maharashtra  and Andhra Pradesh, if so, the main features of these water aerodromes including their status;(b) whether their importance increases if they are constructed  near  the  tourist  places  and  the  places  of religious importance;(c)  whether  the  local  people  are  likely  to  get economic  opportunities  under  it and  if  so,  the  details thereof;(d)  whether  funds  have  been  allocated  for construction of water aerodrome in the Sabarmati Riverfront in Ahmedabad; and(e)  whether  any  operators  have  shown  interest  to run seaplanes if the necessary infrastructure is created and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) to (e): Airport Authority of India (AAI), the Implementing Agency, has awarded  Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) Routes for operation of RCS flights from 10 Water Aerodromes. Details of these Water Aerodromes is attached at Annexure. AAI has already appointed a Consultant for preparation of Detailed Project Report (DPR) for development of awarded Water Aerodromes.As per the National Civil Aviation Policy (NCAP) - 2016, RCS - UDAN is inter-alia implemented by way of revival of unserved or underserved airports. Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs has approved the proposal on 06 March, 2017 for revival of existing un-served / underserved airports / airstrips of the State Governments, Airports Authority of India and Civil Enclaves at an estimated cost of Rs. 4500 Crore. An airport /airstrip /helipad /heliport /water aerodrome which is included in the awarded routes of RCS - UDAN and requires upgradation/development for commencement of RCS operations, is developed under "Revival of unserved and underserved airports" scheme, subject to firm commitment from airline operators as well as from the State Governments for providing various concessions. ;Development of Water Aerodromes near tourist places would enhance the air connectivity to promote tourism.As per the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO) study- "Economic benefit of Civil Aviation: ripples of prosperity" the output and employment multiplier of Aviation are 3.25 and 6.10 respectively.
