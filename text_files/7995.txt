                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

160






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.11.2019






Widening of National Highways




Pushpendra Singh Chandel














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has formulated any specific scheme for widening of National Highways (NHs) in the country including Bundelkhand; (b)  if so, the details thereof along with the norms fixed in this regard; (c) whether people are being displaced after demolition of their houses due to widening/expansion of NH-34 in Sumerpur in district Hamirpur of Uttar Pradesh and if so, the details thereof; and (d) whether the Government has received proposal to construct bypass on NH-34 in Sumerpur to make it free from traffic congestion, preventing displacement of people at the same time and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) to (d)	A statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (d) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 160 ANSWERED ON 28.11.2019 ASKED BY KUNWAR PUSHPENDRA SINGH CHANDEL REGARDING WIDENING OF NATIONAL HIGHWAYS(a) & (b)	The widening of National Highways (NHs) is taken up as per inter-se-priority, traffic density and available resources. The Ministry had taken up detailed review of NHs network with a view to develop the road connectivity to Border areas, development of Coastal roads including road connectivity for Non-Major ports, improvement in the efficiency of National Corridors, development of Economic Corridors, Inter Corridors, Feeder Routes and Expressways under “Bharatmala Pariyojana”. The Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA) approved the proposal for investment approval for Phase-I of “Bharatmala Pariyojana” during its meeting held on 24.10.2017.
