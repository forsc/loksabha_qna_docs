                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1366






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.09.2020






Number of Universities to Provide Higher Education




Anurag Sharma














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of Universities to provide higher education in the Country State/UT-wise;(b) whether these Universities have facilities equivalent to foreign universities; and(c) the number of students studying in Arts, Science, Commerce, Medical Faculties of Indian Universities for higher education?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a): The University Grants Commission (UGC) has informed that as on 11.09.2020, there are 955 Universities in the country under its purview. State/UT-wise list is available at Annexure. As per All India Survey on Higher Education (AISHE), 2019-20, which is yet to be released, 1043 Universities to provide higher education in the country have registered on the portal.(b): Specific survey or study for comparing facilities of foreign educational institutions and Indian educational institutions (HEIs) has not been conducted. However, some world-renowned university rankings’ publications such as Times Higher Education World Ranking 2021 have more than 30 plus Indian institutions / universities amongst top 1000. Further, for internationalization of education in India and making India a preferred destination for foreign students, the Study in India programme has been launched on 18.04.2018. For enabling Higher Educational Institutions to become world-class teaching and research institutions, the UGC has notified ‘UGC (Institutions of Eminence Deemed to be Universities) Regulations, 2017’ and UGC (Declaration of Government Educational Institutions as Institutions of Eminence) Guidelines, 2017. (c): The UGC has informed that the number of students enrolled in Arts, Science, Commerce, Medical Faculties during 2018-19 are as follows: <pre>Faculty	Students EnrolmentArts*	12,004,666Science**	6,410,931Commerce	4,417,823Medicine***	1,409,273(Source AISHE portal: 2018-19)</pre>*Arts includes Humanities, Social Science & Languages etc.**Science includes Home Science, Computer Science/ Computer Applicatiion.***Medicine includes Ayurveda, Dentristry, Homeopathy, Nursing, Pharmacy, Public Health/ Social Preventive Medicine, Unani, Tibbia, Physiotherapy, Naturotherapy etc.******
