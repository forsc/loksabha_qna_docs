                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    EXTERNAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2840






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.07.2019






Infrastructure Development in Bhutan and Bangladesh




Feroze Varun Gandhi














                        Will the Minister of





EXTERNAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	Whether India is funding/supporting any infrastructure development projects in Bhutan and Bangladesh;(b)	If so, the details thereof;(c) 	Whether the Indian strategic investments in the region are comparable to the investments made by China and if so, the details thereof; and (d) 	The details of the steps being taken/planned by the Government to sustain and enhance bilateral infrastructure development co-operation with the above mentioned countries? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EXTERNAL AFFAIRS[SHRI V. MURALEEDHARAN](a) 	Yes. (b) 	GOI has committed assistance of Rs. 4500 crores for the 12th Five Year Plan of Bhutan (2018-23). Out of this, Rs. 2800 crores is earmarked for Project Tied Assistance for funding infrastructure development projects such as development of roads & bridges, construction of regional hospitals, schools, and covering sectors like ICT, capacity building,  industries, agriculture, e-governance. GOI is also assisting in hydro power development in Bhutan with four ongoing projects- 1200 MW Punatsangchhu-I, 1020 MW Punatsangchhu-II, 720 MW Mangdechhu and 600 MW Kholongchhu Hydroelectric Projects.   		In 2011, India extended first Line of Credit (LoC) of US$ 1 billion to Bangladesh, which was used for projects mainly in infrastructure developments projects in field of railways and public transport. The second LoC of US $ 2 billion to Bangladesh was announced in June 2015 during PM’s visit to Bangladesh. This is being used for infrastructure developments projects mainly in field of Power, Railways, Road Transport, ICT, Shipping, Health and Technical Education. Third LOC of US $ 4.5 billion to Bangladesh was announced in April 2017 during Bangladesh PM Sheikh Hasina’s visit to India. This US$ 4.5 billion LOC was the biggest LOC that India has so far extended to any country. Third LOC covers a wide range of areas such as power supply, port development, connectivity (rail and road), civil nuclear energy, etc. (c) & (d)	India’s investments and development projects are based on the development priorities of the country concerned and these stand firmly on their own merits. India will continue to undertake infrastructure development projects in consultation with the countries concerned and deepen our partnership further for mutual benefit. ***
