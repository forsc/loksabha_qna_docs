                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1678






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Sale of Air India’s Assets




Gurjeet Singh Aujla














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of CIVIL AVIATIONbe pleased to state:-(a) whether the Air India proposes to sell its assets such as residential, commercial properties across India as recommended by Kelkar Committee;(b) if so, the details of properties and estimated amount to raise from the sale;(c) whether Air India plans to clear pending payments of private operators hired by Air India on Haj pilgrimage and others before disinvestment; and(d) If not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) and (b): Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs while approving the Turn Around Plan (TAP) and Financial Restructuring Plan (FRP) for Air India Limited (AIL) in the year 2012, had also approved monetization of assets of AIL. Further, as per decision of Air India Specific Alternative Mechanism (AISAM) in 2018, the immovable assets are being monetized by AIL to offset its debt. AIL has identified 111 parcels of properties for monetization; out of which 106 parcels of properties are in India and rest 5 are overseas properties. The 111 parcels of  properties consist of 211 units which are under monetization.;Air India has so far received Rs. 738 crore from sale of 115 units from 2015 to 12th July, 2021. Air India also realizes about Rs. 100 crore per annum from lease rental income.;(c) and (d): There are no pending payments to any private operators hired by Air India for Haj pilgrimages.
