                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

698






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Outbreak of COVID-19




Su Thirunavukkarasar













                                                    Raghu Ramakrishna Raju Kanumuru
                                                








                                                    Y. S. Avinash Reddy
                                                








                                                    Parvatagouda Chandanagouda Gaddigoudar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether a large number of people in the country have been traced, tested, treated, recovered and died with the outbreak of COVID-19 pandemic including mutant strain from United Kingdom;(b) if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise;(c) the details of financial, medical and other assistance provided by the Government to the States/ UTs in order to overcome this viral disease;(d) whether India has also rolled out new vaccine for COVID-19 pandemic;(e) if so, the details thereof and the plan of action initiated for its distribution to the States/UTs to control the disease;(f) whether the Government has taken note that new Strain of Corona Virus is spreading in the country, if so, the details thereof; and(g) the precautionary measures being taken by the Government to avoid its spread in the country?






ANSWER




ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b):	Total number of people tested and found positive in India as on 3rd February 2021 is 1,07,77,284. The State/UT-wise details of persons total cases tested positive, recovered and died is at Annexure I. Till 28th January 2021, 136 travelers and their contacts have been tested positive for UK strain in India. State-wise details are:Andhra Pradesh	1Delhi	27Goa	4Gujarat	11Haryana	6Karnataka	14Kerala	10Ladakh (UT)	1Madhya Pradesh	2Maharashtra	11Odisha	3Punjab	4Rajasthan	4Tamil Nadu	9Telangana	7Uttar Pradesh	17Uttarakhand	1West Bengal	1Case details awaited	3(c):	Under the ‘India COVID19 Emergency Response and Health System Preparedness package’ of Rs 15,000 crores has been approved by the Cabinet on 22nd April 2020 with an objective to prevent, detect and respond to the threat posed by COVID-19. As up to 15th January 2021, Rs 11, 361.67 Crores have been utilized for the same. Of this, Rs.6309.90 crore has been released to the States/UTs, through Agency - National Health Mission (NHM). The details are given at Annexure-II.In addition, States are being supported in terms of supply of logistics. So far 1.69 crore of PPE Kits, 4.08 crores N-95 masks, 11.16 crore tablets of Hydroxychloroquine, 36,948 ventilators and 1,02,400 oxygen cylinders have been supplied to States/UTs/ Central Government hospitals so far (as reported on 2nd February 2021).  In addition, oxygen concentrators are also being supplied to States.(d):	Yes, COVID-19 vaccination has been launched in India on 16th January 2021.(e):	The National Expert Group on Vaccine Administration for COVID-19 (NEGVAC) has been established, which provides guidance on all aspects of COVID-19 vaccination including prioritization of population groups, procurement and inventory management, vaccine selection, vaccine delivery and tracking mechanism etc.The supply of COVID -19 vaccine is based on the number of beneficiaries registered under priority group for vaccination in Co-WIN portal.(f) & (g):	Yes, Government of India has taken note of mutant strain of SARS-CoV-2 reported from United Kingdom. Government of India has taken a number of steps to prevent spread of mutant SARS-CoV-2 variant spread in the country:i.	All flights to UK were banned till from December 23, 2020 to 7th January 2021.ii.	A surveillance system has been put in place to identify new UK variant of COVID 19 among those with travel history to UK from 23rd December 2020. States / UTs have been advised to maintain strict surveillance of Whole Genome Sequencing of cases.iii.	Resumption of limited international flight services to/from UK has been allowed with effect from 8th January 2021 in a calibrated manner by initially allowing flight movement to/from UK to five international airports i.e. Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad and Chennai only. iv.	All the passengers arriving from U.K. should be carrying Negative RT-PCR Test Report for which test should have been conducted within 72 hours prior to undertaking the journey. v.	All international passengers who arrived in India from UK or transit through UK are again mandatorily tested for COVID-19 by RT-PCR at the airport. vi.	All passengers and their contacts who tested positive for RT-PCR are isolated, their samples are sent for whole genome sequencing to detect UK variant.vii.	All international passengers who test negative at Airports are quarantined for 14 days.viii.	Detailed guidelines are available at: https://www.mohfw.gov.in/pdf/ExtensionofvalidityofStandardOperatingProcedureforEpidemiologicalSurveillance&ResponseforthenewvariantofSARSCoV2.pdf
