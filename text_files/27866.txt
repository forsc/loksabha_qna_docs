                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3565






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






Coverage under PMKSY




Rajyavardhan Singh Rathore














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of farmers and area of land covered under the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY), State-wise including Rajasthan, district-wise; (b)	the amount/funds distributed to various State Governments under the scheme; and(c)	whether the Government is taking any steps to cushion the impact of delayed monsoons on decreased sowing of Kharif crops on farmers across the country?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????      (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)&(b): Government is implementing the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) from 2015-16 with the motto of ‘Har Khet Ko Paani’ for providing end-to-end solutions in irrigation supply chain, viz. water sources, distribution network and farm level applications. The PMKSY has four components and the details are as under: i.	PMKSY- Accelerated Irrigation Benefit Programme (AIBP): This component is being implemented by the Department of Water Resources, River Development & Ganga Rejuvenation (DoWR, RD &GR). During 2016-17, ninety-nine (99) on-going Major/Medium irrigation projects (and 7 phases), in the country having balance estimated cost of Rs.77,595 cr. (Central Assistance-Rs.31342.50 crore and State Share-Rs.46253 crore) have been prioritised in consultation with States for completion in phases. Funding mechanism has been approved by the Government for providing Central and State Share under Long Term Irrigation Fund (LTIF) through National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD).  Out of these 99 prioritized projects, two projects namely Narmada Canal and Modernization of Gang Canal benefitting Jalore, Barmer and Sriganganagar districts of Rajasthan. AIBP works of both the projects have been reported to be completed. During 2016-17 to 2020-21, Central Assistance of Rs.458.56 crore has been released for these projects of Rajasthan. Total irrigation potential of 315.57 th ha. has been reported to be created upto March, 2021 in Rajasthan. Out of which, irrigation potential of 7.24 th ha has been reported to be created by these projects, during 2016-17 to 2020-21. State-wise detail of Central Assistance released & Irrigation Potential created is at given at Annexure-I.	Under Command Area Development & Water Management (CADWM) Programme, as per information provided by the States, about 20.67 lakh Ha of Culturable Command Area (CCA) has been developed during last 6 years (2015-16 to 2020-21) including Rajasthan. State-wise details of Central Assistance released and Culturable Command Area (CCA) developed under CADWM is given at Annexure-II. ii.	  PMKSY-Har Khet Ko Pani (HKKP): This component is also being   implemented by the DoWR, RD &GR.           The scheme of Surface Minor Irrigation (SMI) and Repair, Renovation and Restoration (RRR) of Water Bodies are a part of HKKP. Under SMI scheme, since 12th plan onwards, 6213 schemes are ongoing with an estimated cost of Rs. 13473 crore. Central Assistance (CA) of Rs. 7957 crore has been released to states upto March, 2021. Further, 3884 schemes have been reported to be completed upto March, 2021. Target irrigation potential creation of these schemes is 10.53 Lakh Ha and out of this, 6.85 Lakh Ha is reported to be created till March, 2021. During the current financial year, Rs. 17.72 crore has been released to SMI schemes till date.Under the RRR of Water Bodies scheme, since 12th plan onwards, 2228 schemes are ongoing with an estimated cost of Rs. 1914.86 crore. Central Assistance of Rs. 469.69 crore has been released to states upto March, 2021. Further, 1549 water bodies have been reported to be completed upto March, 2021. Target irrigation potential restoration of these schemes is 1.89 Lakh Ha and out of this, 1.31 Lakh Ha is reported to be restored till March, 2021. 2 clusters of 68 water bodies have been included during 2014-15 to 2017-18 from the State of Rajasthan under this scheme with total estimated cost of Rs. 187.82 crore and targeted Irrigation potential of 0.1319 Lha. So far, total Central Assistance of Rs. 62.18 crore has been released to these schemes and 66 schemes have been reported complete with achievement of 0.1019 Lakh ha Irrigation potential till date. Districts covered by these schemes are Ajmer, Banswara, Bhilwara, Bundi, Chittorgarh, Dholpur, Jalore, Jodhpur, Karoli, Kota, Madhopur, Pali, Pratapgarh, Sawai, Sikar, Sirohi, Tonk and Udaipur. State-wise details of Central Assistance released and potential achieved/ restored under SMI and RRR of Water Bodies schemes is given  as Annexure-III & IV.iii.	PMKSY- Watershed Development (WDC): The Department of Land Resources (DoLR) implements WDC for development of rainfed/degraded lands. DoLR has sanctioned, 8214 watershed development projects covering an area of about 39.07 million hectare  in 28 States (except Goa)  [now 27 States and UTs of J & K and Ladakh) including Rajasthan during the period 2009-10 to 2014-15 under Integrated Watershed Management Programme (IWMP). As per the decision at highest level, 1832 projects (345 uninitiated and 1487 in preparatory stage) were transferred to various States in 2018 to implement with their own budget.  Therefore, DoLR has been implementing only 6382 projects. The project period of the scheme got over on 31st March 2021.  IWMP was amalgamated as WDC in 2015-16. The State wise details of projects sanctioned, area covered, funds released as central share from 2009-10 to 2021-22 and farmers benefited (2014-15 to 2021-22,Q1)  are given at Annexure-V.iv. PMKSY-Per Drop More Crop (PDMC): This component is being implemented by the Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare (DA&FW). The PDMC focuses on enhancing water use efficiency at farm level through Micro Irrigation technologies viz. Drip and Sprinkler Irrigation. The State wise details of Central Assistance released, area covered under micro irrigation and number of farmers benefitted from micro irrigation under PDMC is given at Annexure-VI. District wise details of area covered under micro irrigation and number of farmers benefitted in the State of Rajasthan under PDMC is given at Annexure-VII.(c):  The DA&FW is implementing various programmes/schemes such as Rainfed Area Development Programme to promote Integrated Farming System for enhancing productivity and minimizing risks associated with climatic variability; National Food Security Mission for Cultivation of pulses and coarse cereals and oilseeds as these crops require less water; Crop Diversification Programme (CDP) to diversify cropping pattern away from water guzzling paddy; Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH) for holistic growth of horticulture sector covering fruits, vegetables, root and tuber crops, mushrooms, spices, etc.  Under these schemes, the financial assistance and advisories are provided to farmers for adopting the best practices for efficient water management and agro ecologically suitable crops.     Indian Council for Agricultural Research (ICAR)-Central Research Institute for Dryland Agriculture (CRIDA) is providing weather based agro-advisories to the farmers to cushion the impact of delayed monsoons during kharif season across the country. It has also prepared District Agricultural Contingent Plans to facilitate appropriate agro-advisories to farmers in the event of adverse climatic situations including delayed Monsoon. ICAR  suggests drought tolerant short duration varieties, diversified farming systems, in-situ and ex-situ water conservation measures to tackle such a situation.
