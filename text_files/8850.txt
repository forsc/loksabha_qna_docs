                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2312






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2019






Safe City Project




P. C. Mohan














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the works taken up in Bengaluru under the ‘Safe City’ project;(b) whether experts are of the opinion that installing CCTV surveillance and other such infrastructure may help in solving crimes, but are unsuccessful in deterring them; and(c) if so, the steps being taken by the Government to ensure higher safety for women in metropolitan city like Bengaluru?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (c) : As per available information, Bengaluru Safe City project involves use of technology to aid smart policing and safety management of women and includes:i.  	Enabling secure environment for women in identifying public places through    enhanced surveillance, GIS based crime mapping, deploying police vans in hotspots, setting up women police outposts and safety zones for women in vulnerable areas. --2--LS.US.Q.NO. 2312 FOR 03.12.2019ii.	Integrated support and counselling of women victims through deployment              of trained volunteers, helpdesks in police stations and hospitals. iii.   Gender sensitization of police and community awareness on crimes against women. These measures are likely to act as deterrent and increase women safety at public places apart from facilitating enhanced prediction capability leading to better preventive action by police.*****
