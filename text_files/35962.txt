                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3203






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






Assistance to individuals with Autism




Dharmendra Kumar Kashyap














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the schemes being implemented for assistance ofindividuals with autism;(b) the details of the beneficiaries, scheme-wise, State/UT-wise includingUttar Pradesh; and(c) the steps being taken by Government to improve awareness onintellectual disabilities like autism?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SUSHRI PRATIMA BHOUMIK)(a):  The Schemes of the Ministry are equally applicable to all categories of Persons with Disabilities covered under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 including persons with autism.  However, the National Trust for the welfare Persons with Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Mental Retardation and Multiple Disability implements the following schemes exclusively for persons with these four categories of disabilities:(i)	Disha: an early intervention and school readiness scheme for children in the age group of    0-10 years;(ii)	Vikaas: day care scheme for enhancing inter-personal and vocational skills for 10+ years;(iii)	Disha-cum-Vikaas: day care scheme;(iv)	Samarth: respite care for orphans or abandoned, family in crisis etc.; (v)	Gharaunda: group home for adults; (vi)	Samarth-cum-Gharaunda Scheme (Residential); and(vii)	Nirmaya: Health Insurance Scheme.(b):Details of the beneficiaries under the above schemes of the National Trust, State/UT-wise including Uttar Pradesh is at Annexure.(c): National Trust for the welfare of Persons with Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Mental Retardation & Multiple Disabilities has taken the following steps to raise awareness on Intellectual Disabilities like autism:(i)	Four training programmes were organised for training of Doctor’s (Pediatricians, Psychiatrists and Psychologists) as Master Trainers on Evaluation and Assessment of Autism and procedure for Certification held during the period from 2016 to 2018. (ii)	National Conference on Autism was organized in New Delhi on 03.04.2017 involving NGOs, Professionals and Parents of Divyangjan. (iii)	National Seminar on Building Legal Capacity of Persons with Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities (PwIDDs) was organised in collaboration with Indian Law Institute, New Delhi on 23rd October, 2019 to discuss issues being faced by Persons with Intellectual Development Disabilities. in various spheres of life to address the concerns of parents of persons.  (iv)	National Convention on Digital Best Practices and North East Summit for Persons with Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities (PwIDDs) was held on 07.08.2021 with focus on generating awareness in the seven States in North East Region of the Country. (v)	A virtual Meeting with officials of the Government of Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh was organized on 31st August 2021 for raising awareness and developing a roadmap for implementation of schemes and activities of the National Trust in the region.(vi)	A virtual seminar for ‘Assisted Living programme of Adult Persons with Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities (PwIDDs)’ was held on 07.09.2021.*****
