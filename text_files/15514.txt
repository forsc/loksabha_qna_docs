                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SHIPPING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

368






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






Expansion and Modernization of Ports




 Rahul Ramesh Shewale














                        Will the Minister of





SHIPPING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the extent to which the Government has achieved the aims and objectives of    expansion and modernization of major ports in the country;(b) the details of the funds provided by the Government or through PPP mode for expansion and modernization of the said ports across the country during each of the last three years and the current year, State-wise and port-wise;(c)  the cases of irregularities in expansion and modernization of the said ports which came to the notice of the Government during the said period, State-wise and port-wise; and(d)  the steps taken by the Government to ensure timely expansion and modernization of ports and to address the irregularities, if any?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) to (d).	A statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (d) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *368 TO BE ANSWERED ON 19th  MARCH, 2020 RAISED BY SHRI RAHUL RAMESH SHEWALE REGARDING “EXPANSION AND MODERNIZATION OF PORTS”.  (a)      The Government of India has taken concerted efforts in the direction of expansion and modernisation of major ports of the country.  The total traffic handled by the major ports during 2018-19 is 699.10 Million Tonnes (MT) against a capacity of 1514.09 Million Tonnes Per Annum (MTPA).   The detailed port-wise capacity and traffic growth during the last five years is at Annexure-I.   (b)	The cargo handling capacity of the major ports has been steadily going up during the last three years.  Infrastructure projects at major ports are mostly funded either through private investments (PPP projects) or through the Port’s Own Resources (IEBR).  A number of infrastructure development projects with a total cost of Rs. 13764.36 Cr. have been awarded by various major ports during the last three years and current year upto February, 2020. The details of these projects state-wise and port-wise are at Annexure – II.(c) 	As such no irregularities have been established in expansion and modernisation of major ports during the above period. (d) 	Government of India has put in place a proper mechanism in conceiving the projects of expansion and modernisation of ports right from preparation of feasibility report/DPR till commissioning and commencement of commercial operations.  These projects are monitored regularly at various levels in the concerned ports as well as in the Ministry to ensure timely completion. Major Ports submit monthly status report on projects to be awarded and projects to be completed to the Ministry, which is reviewed regularly.  Annexure -IA.
