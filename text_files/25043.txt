                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

700






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2021






AYUSH Medical Institutions and Hospitals




Upendra Singh Rawat













                                                    Sangamlal Kadedin Gupta
                                                








                                                    Hemant Patil
                                                








                                                    Shankar Lalwani
                                                








                                                    Rekha Arun Verma
                                                








                                                    Chandra Prakash Joshi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYUSH be pleased to state:(a) the details of AYUSH medical colleges/ universities/institutes/hospitals/AYUSH Health and wellness centers including integrated hospitals along with the total number of graduate/post-graduate level seats which are functional and which are upcoming under National AYUSH Mission (NAM) across the country, State/UT-wise, district-wise and stream-wiseparticularly Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan and Maharashtra;(b) the total funds allocated/utilised for AYUSH medical colleges/universities/institutes/hospitals/wellness centers in the last three years, State/UT-wise/district wise/ stream-wise;(c) the details of the steps being taken by the Government for better access and promotion of AYUSH services in the country including research works in post-graduate AYUSH colleges, adequate publicity through media, allocation of special funds for promotion etc; State/UT-wise/stream-wise;(d) whether any criteria have been fixed for establishing new AYUSH institutions in the private sector and if so, the details thereof; and(e) the details of AYUSH medical colleges/ universities/institutes/hospitals/ AYUSH Health andwellness centers etc. which have been derecognized, State/UT-wise, district-wise, stream-wise and the reasons for derecognition therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(DR. MUNJPARA MAHENDRABHAI)(a):	738 Ayurveda, Siddha, Unani, Sowa Rigpa and Homoeopathy Medical Colleges, 3781 Ayush hospitals, 4,448 Ayush Health and Wellness Centres (HWCs) are available across the country including in the States of Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan and Maharashtra. The details are at Annexure -I. (b):	State/UT wise status of funds allocated and utilised under National AYUSH Mission for various activities including for AYUSH medical colleges/universities/institutes/ hospitals / wellness centers during the last three years is furnished at Annexure- II. (c):	The details of steps taken by the Government for better access and promotion of AYUSH services in the country including research works in post-graduate AYUSH colleges, adequate publicity through media etc. is furnished at Annexure-III. (d):	The regulation 13A of Indian Medicine Central Council Act, 1970, provides for applying to the Central Government for establishing new Ayurveda, Siddha, Unani & Sow-Rigpa colleges including in the private sector. Further, the criteria for establishing new AYUSH institutions is prescribed under the Establishment of New Homoeopathy Medical College, (Opening of New or Higher Course of Study or Training and Increase of Admission capacity by a Medical college) Regulations, 2011.(e):	There are no de-recognized colleges in Ayurveda, Siddha, Unani and Sowa Rigpa. However, Central Council of Indian Medicine (CCIM) had recommended 14 colleges (13 Ayurveda and 01 Unani) for de-recognition and the Ministry of Ayush has taken up the process of de-recognition with respective State Governments/Universities. The list of such 14 colleges is furnished at Annexure IV. Further, a list of derecognized Homoeopathy colleges is also furnished at Annexure-IV. These Homoeopathy colleges could not maintain the standards prescribed under the HCC Act, 1973.
