                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1780






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Funds for NBCFDC




Giridhari Yadav













                                                    Rama Devi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the amount/funds provided by the Government to the National Backward Classes Finance and Development Corporation (NBCFDC) during the last three years; (b) whether the said amount is adequate for the development and welfare of backward classes; (c) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reaction of the Government thereto; and (d) the corrective steps taken by the Government in this regard along with the outcome thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI KRISHAN PAL GURJAR)(a): The statement showing the amount/funds provided by the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment, Government of India to the National Backward Classes Finance and Development Corporation (NBCFDC) and amount disbursed to Channel Partners for financing the target group during the last three years is at Annexure–I.(b) to (d): The Corporation has been provided approx. Rs.100 Crores from the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment towards the budgetary support every year & has been able to meet the entire demand of funds received from the Channel Partners (State Channelizing Agencies/Regional Rural Banks/Public Sector Banks) with the equity received from the Ministry and the recovery of dues from its Channel Partners.As on date, the authorized share capital of NBCFDC is Rs.1500 Crores and paid-up share capital is Rs.1340 Crores. In consideration of the same, the process for enhancement of authorized share capital from Rs.1500 Crores to Rs. 2500 Crores has been initiated so as to enable Corporation to continue availing equity in the coming years.	****
