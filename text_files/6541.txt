                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

337






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.11.2019






Agriculture in Arid Land




Riti Pathak













                                                    Jugal Kishore Sharma
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 the funds allocated by the Union Government to various States/UTs to promote arid land agriculture during the last three years and the current year, State/UTwise including Madhya Pradesh and Jammu and Kashmir; (b)	 the total area of land brought under arid land agriculture in the country during the last three years, State-wise;(c)	 the incentives provided to the farmers for arid land agriculture; and (d) the extent to which the success has been achieved in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER  OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (c)	About 19.3 m.ha area of the country is covered under arid region. The Department of Agriculture Cooperation & Farmers’ Welfare (DAC&FW)  is implementing various programmes /schemes for development of agriculture in the country including arid zones. The major programmes include:(i)	National Food Security Mission (NFSM).(ii)	Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH)(iii)	Pramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)(iv)	Rainfed Area Development (RAD) component of National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA).(v)	Per Drop More Crop-Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY-PDMC).            Financial assistance / incentives to farmers for the activities varies from @ 25% to 65% as per the operational guidelines of respective schemes.            The State wise Central Assistance released and physical achievements made under the schemes during the last three years and current year including Madhya Pradesh and Jammu & Kashmir are given at Annexure-I to IX.       (d):	The improved technologies and financial assistance under the programme have resulted in increased resilience and risk management by the farmers. The efforts have resulted in increased production, productivity and cropping intensity. The food production in the country has been increased from 252.02 million tonnes in the year 2014-15 to 284.95 million tonnes in the year 2018-19.
