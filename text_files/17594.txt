                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1365






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.09.2020






Fraudulent University




Upendra Singh Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether a large number of Universities running in various parts of the country have been found to be fake;(b) if so, the details of all such cases during the last three years; and(c) the steps that being taken by the Government to prevent the growth of such fraudulent Universities; and(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (e): Based on the complaints received from the students, parents, general public and also through electronic print media, University Grants Commission (UGC) has released the list of 24 so-called Universities which are offering Degree Programmes without recognition under Section 2(f) and Section 3 of the UGC Act, 1956. Out of these 24 so-called Universities, 3 cases have been identified in the last three years. The details of these so-called Universities are available on the UGC’s website www.ugc.ac.in. UGC has no connection with these so-called Universities. In this regard, letters have been sent by UGC to the concerned State Governments from time to time for taking action against these so-called Universities. *****
