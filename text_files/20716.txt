                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1881






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.02.2021






Persons affected by Wild Animals




Manickam Tagore B














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has implemented anyscheme for providing ex-gratia payment for the personsaffected by wild animals;(b)	 if so, the details thereof and total number ofex-gratia amount paid for the affected persons in allstates for the last three years, State/UT-wise; (c)	 whether the Government has created any criteriaunder the scheme towards restriction of population intowild area premises; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) and (b)The Ministry provides financial assistance to States/UTs under the Centrally Sponsored Schemes of ''Development of Wildlife Habitats'', ‘Project Elephant’and ‘Project Tiger’ for management of wildlife and its habitats in the country. This includesex-gratiaamount to be paid to persons affected due to depredation by wild animals. The details of ex-gratia released to the States/UTsunder these schemes during the last three years are given in Annexure-I, II and III. Apart from this, States/UTs also pay ex-gratia   to persons affected by wild animals.(c) and (d) The Ministry provides financial assistance to States /UTs for management of wildlife and its habitats, which includes   activities like restoration of natural water bodies, creation of artificial ponds, waterholes,and augmenting food/fodder sources at various places within Protected Areas.It also covers construction/erection of physical barriers such as solar powered electric fence, bio-fencing using cactus, boundary wall, animal proof trenches etc. to prevent the entry of wild animals outside forests. ***
