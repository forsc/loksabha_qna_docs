                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1221






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.11.2019






Women Literacy in Jharkhand




Annpurna Devi













                                                    Pashupati Nath Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the current level of women literacy rate in Jharkhand and level thereof at national level;(b) the reasons for low women literacy rate in Jharkhand;(c) the measures taken by the Government to enhance the women literacy rate in Jharkhand; and(d) the details of the success achieved through the measures taken by the Government?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHARIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) The literacy data in the country is collected through decennial census operationconducted by Registrar General of India. The last Census was held in 2011. A statementindicating State/UT-wise female literacy rate in the country including Jharkhand, as per Census2011 is annexed. The female literacy rate in Jharkhand is 55.42 per cent compared to the nationalaverage female literacy rate of 64.63 per cent.(b) Various factors viz poverty, gender and social inequities etc are impeding female literacyin Jharkhand State.(c) & (d) In order to improve the level of literacy, the scheme of Saakshar Bharat wasimplemented in a phased manner since October 2009 in the rural areas of 20 districts inJharkhand state that have adult female literacy rate of 50 per cent and below as per Census 2001,including left wing extremism affected districts, irrespective of their literacy rates, with specialfocus on women and other disadvantaged groups, since October 2009.Under the Saakshar Bharat Programme, against the overall target of 7 Crore adultliterates, around 7.64 Crore learners (including 5.37 crore women) successfully passed thebiannual Basic Literacy Assessment Tests conducted by National Institute of Open Schooling(NIOS) between August, 2010 to March, 2018.In the State of Jharkhand, under the Saakshar Bharat Programme, 41.45 lakh adults(including 25.94 lakh women) were certified as literates, as per NIOS assessment test.**
