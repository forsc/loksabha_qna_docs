                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

851






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Cases of Road Accidents




 Rahul Ramesh Shewale














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the number of road accidents have increased in the country despite several measures taken by the Government in this regard during each of the last three years and the current year; (b) 	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor along with the reaction of the Government thereto, State/UT-wise; (c) 	whether the Committee on Road Safety has submitted its report to the Supreme Court of India; (d) 	if so, the main recommendations of the Committee along with action taken/being taken by the Government thereon so far; (e) 	whether the Government proposes to set up a “Crash Fund” to provide immediate compensation to the next of kin of the accident victims; and (f) 	if so, the details thereof along with the other steps taken/being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                          THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS      (SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a)	     As per available information, total number of road accidents,  in the country during the calendar year 2017 to 2020 (Upto June, 2020) are as under:- Year	Total Accidents2017	4,64,9102018	4,67,0442019	4,49,0022020(Upto June- Provisional)	1,60,797    The State/UTs wise of road accidents for the last three years and the current Year (Upto June, 2020 provisional) are at Annexure-I(b)	Road accidents occur due to multiple causes such as over speeding, use of Mobile phone, drunken driving/consumption of alcohol/ drug, overloaded Vehicle, vehicular condition, poor light condition, jumping red light, overtaking, neglect of civic bodies, weather condition, fault of driver, driving on wrong side, defect in road condition, defect in condition of motor vehicle, fault of cyclist, fault of pedestrian etc.(c) & (d)   The Committee on Road Safety was constituted by the Hon''ble Supreme Court in April, 2014 in Writ Petition (Civil) No 295 of 2012 – S. Rajaseekaran Vrs Union of India and Others with the mandate to measure and monitor implementation of various road safety laws by the States/UTs and the concerned Ministries of the Central Government. As per Order of the Hon''ble Supreme Court, the Committee has been submitting its Reports to the Supreme Court from time to time. (e) & (f)    The Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019 was published in the Official Gazette on 09th August, 2019. Section 51 of the Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019 provides for insertion of Section 164B which provides for the Central Government to constitute a fund to be called Motor Vehicle Accident Fund for the following, namely:- (i) 	Treatment of the persons injured in road accidents in accordance with the scheme framed by the Central Government under section 162;(ii) 	Compensation to representatives of a person who died in hit and run motor accident in accordance with schemes framed under section 161;(iii) compensation to a person grievously hurt in a hit and run motor  accident in accordance with schemes framed under section 161; and(iv)  Compensation to such persons as may be prescribed by the Central Government
