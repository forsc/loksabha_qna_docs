                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2690






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.07.2019






Rehabilitation of Surrendered Ultras




Naba (Hira) Kumar Sarania














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the condition of several surrendered ultras who have shun the violence in North-Eastern States including Assam in the country is not good or they have no employment; (b) whether the Government proposes to introduce any employment scheme for them and if so, the details thereof;(c) the number of meetings held by the Government with their outfits during the last three years and the outcome thereof;(d) the necessary steps taken by the Government for the upliftment and employment of the said people in Assam;(e) the facilities being provided by the Government to the above mentioned people at present;(f) whether these facilities are adequate to take care of their families and if so, the details thereof; and(g) whether the Government proposes to raise any battalion consisting of the ultras who have surrendered and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (f):	The Central Government is implementing a Surrender-cum-Rehabilitation   Scheme   for   the   North   Eastern   States   of Arunachal -2-L.S.US.Q.NO.2690 FOR 09.07.2019Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Nagaland and Tripura to wean away the misguided youth and hardcore militants who stray into the fold of militancy and find themselves trapped into that net. The scheme has been revised w.e.f. 01.04.2018 to make it more attractive. Following benefits are extended under this scheme:i.	An immediate grant of 4 lakh to each surrenderee, which is to be kept in the name of the surrenderee as fixed deposit in a bank for a period of 3 years. This money can be utilized as collateral security/ margin money against loan to be availed by the surrenderee from the bank for  self employment; ii.	Payment of stipend of Rs. 6,000 per month to each surrenderee for a period of three years. iii.	Incentives for weapons/ammunitions surrendered by the militants. iv.	Vocational training in a trade/vocation of liking of the surrenderees for self-employment. v.	 Funds for construction of rehabilitation camps. -3-L.S.US.Q.NO.2690 FOR 09.07.2019Govt. of Assam is implementing a rehabilitation programme under the Scheme ‘Swabalamban’ to impart training to Suspension of Operations  and surrendered cadres of different militant groups in various skills and tradecrafts with provisions of financial aid and bank loan to make them capable of self-employment. 1800 number of surrendered/SoO group cadres have been imparted training, under the Scheme.   	The Government of India holds talks with Suspension of Operations/Ceasefire Groups through interlocutors appointed for the purpose.  The State Governments hold talks with the Groups with which they are in Suspension of Operations agreements.(g):	No Sir.  *****
