                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4665






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2020






Educationally Backward Districts in Jharkhand




Jayant Sinha














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of educationally backward districts identified in Jharkhand and the proposed measures to develop the same;(b) the details of the report on Performance Grading Index for the State of Jharkhand in 2017-18 and 2018-19; and(c) the details of digital upgrading scheme of schools and the number of schools upgraded so far in Jharkhand as of January 2020, district-wise?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a): There are 203 Educationally Backward Blocks in Bokaro, Chatra, Deoghar, Dhanbad, Dumka, Giridih, Godda, Gumla, Hazaribagh, Hazaribagh/ Garhwa, Koderma, Lohardaga, Pakaur, Palamu,  Paschimi Singhbhum, Purbi Singhbhum, Ranchi and Sahibganj districts of Jharkhand. The Department of School Education & Literacy has launched a Centrally Sponsored Integrated Scheme for School Education- Samagra Shiksha with effect from 2018-19 which subsumes the three erstwhile Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA), Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA) and Teacher Education (TE). The scheme envisages school education as a continuum from pre-school to senior secondary level and aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education for all. Under the scheme, preference is given to Special Focus Districts (SFDs), Educationally Backward Blocks (EBBs) and Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected districts while planning different interventions. The Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalayas are also set up in Educationally Backward Blocks(EBBs) of a State/ Union Territory where the female rural literacy rate is below the national average.Financial assistance is also provided to States and UTs for undertaking various activities to ensure universal access to education including opening/ strengthening of schools upto senior secondary level, construction of school buildings & additional classrooms, setting up of residential schools/ hostels, transport allowance and undertaking enrolment & retention drives and facilitating children with special needs for inclusive education including stipend to girl students with disability etc.(b): Ministry of Human Resource Development has designed a 70 indicator based matrix Performance Grading Index(PGI) to grade the States/ UT. The grades obtained by the State of Jharkhand in PGI 2017-18 and 2018-19 are given below:<pre>State	Grade in PGI	2017-18	2018-19Jharkhand	Grade V	Grade IIGrade II- Score 751-800, Grade V- 601-650</pre>(c): In order to provide digital access, there is a provision for setting up of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) laboratories in Government schools for classes VI to XII under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Samagra Shiksha. The details of schools as provided by the State of Jharkhand is at Annexure.****
