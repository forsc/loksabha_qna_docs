                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1220






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2020






Colleges Functioning in the Country




Arun Sao














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the State-wise number of the Government and private colleges functioning in thecountry including Chhattisgarh as on date;(b) the number of colleges in which posts of principals and professors are lying vacantalong with the number of such colleges which lack library and other basic amenities;(c) the number of colleges receiving grants from University Grants Commission (UGC);(d) the norms for providing grants to private colleges; and(e) the steps taken by the Government to promote qualitative education in private andGovernment colleges?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): The University Grants Commission (UGC) has informed that total 38,179 colleges arefunctioning in the Country. As per AISHE Report 2018-19 the State-wise detail of numberof Government & Private Colleges functioning in the country is at Annexure-I.(b): The posts of Principals and Professors are filled up by the State Governmentsconcerned. However, out of 37645 Colleges which have uploaded their data on AISHEPortal 2018-19, there are 25315 sanctioned posts of Principals and 24962 are in position.A detail of availability of Infrastructure in colleges is given at Annexure-II.(c): Total 6013 Colleges are receiving grants from University Grants Commission underdifferent schemes.(d): Colleges which are recognized under section 2(f) and 12(B) and Govt. Aided in natureare provided assistance. Providing of financial assistance under schemes are also subjectto fulfilment of conditions laid down in the respective UGC guidelines.(e): The schemes viz. General Development Assistance to Colleges; Construction ofWomen’s Hostel for Colleges; Autonomous Colleges (Release of Grants only); MinorResearch Project for Colleges; Seminar/Symposia/Conference for Colleges; FacultyDevelopment Programme for Colleges; Internal Quality Assurance Cell for Colleges;Development of Sports Infrastructure & Equipments in Colleges; Granting Special Heritagestatus to Colleges; Visually Challenged Teachers; and Committed Liabilities (old UGCSchemes) are being implemented to promote qualitative education in GovernmentColleges. Further, the affiliating universities are also mandated to monitor the quality ofeducation in its affiliated colleges including the private colleges under the provisions ofUGC (Affiliation of Colleges by Universities) Regulations, 2009 as amended from time totime.*****
