                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2363






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.08.2021






Decrease in Employment due to Rise in Population




Rakesh Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the employment opportunities to youth decrease due to rise in population in the country and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the living standard of people has not improved due to availability of limited resarvces compared to the population of the country, if so, the details thereof;(c) whether there is a need to control rapid increase in population of the country;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) whether the Government proposes to take any steps in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) & (b):	Total population and population in age group 15-29 years of the country and corresponding figures of total workers as per Census 2001 & Census 2011 are given below: 		Census 2001	Census 2011	Age-Group	Population	Workers	Population	Workers	All	102,86,10,328	40,22,34,724	121,08,54,977	48,18,88,868	15-29	  27,34,02,415	13,47,71,641	  33,33,64,636	15,01,40,651  Living Standard of the people is not compiled from Census Data.(c) to (e):	A National Population Policy was formulated in the  year 2000 with the long term objective of attaining population stabilization by 2045. Several initiatives have been taken under the National Family Planning Programme providing broad range of services. ********
