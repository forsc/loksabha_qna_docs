                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4510






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






ODOP




Kuruva Gorantla Madhav














                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the State-wise number of districts covered under One District One Product (ODOP);(b) 	whether the districts in the State of Andhra Pradesh have been covered under the said initiative;(c) 	if so, the details thereof;(d) 	if not, the details and the number of districts that are still not covered under ODOP in Andhra Pradesh; and(e) 	whether the initiative achieved its goal of boosting exports in the country and if so, the details thereof? 
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        ???????  ??? ?????? ???????? ??? ????? ?????? (???? ??? ??????)  THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI SOM PARKASH)(a) to (e):  	The Central Government has initiated the One District One Product (ODOP) in different States/UTs of the country. ODOP is seen as a transformational step towards realizing the true potential of a district, fueling economic growth, generating employment and rural entrepreneurship, taking us towards the goal of Aatmanirbhar Bharat.  ODOP initiative is operationally merged with ‘Districts as Export Hub’ (DEH) initiative of the DGFT, Department of Commerce, with the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT) as a major stakeholder.  Institutional mechanism in the form of State Export Promotion Committee (SEPC) and District Export Promotion Committee (DEPC) have been constituted in all the 36 States/UTs to provide support for export promotion and address the bottlenecks for export growth in the districts. Districts Export Action Plans include specific actions required to support local exporters/manufacturers in producing/manufacturing identified products in adequate quantity and with the requisite quality, for reaching potential buyers outside India. The plans also focus on the interventions required to provide ecosystem for innovation/ use of technology at the district level to make them export competitive.  Government of India works with the States/UTs at the local level to implement the initiative to create conducive environment for the exporters and manufacturers to export their products to international markets. Yes, all the districts in the State of Andhra Pradesh have been covered under this initiative. The State-wise/ district-wise details of products identified under ‘District as Export Hub’ initiative are available at the following link: http://exporthubs.gov.in/images/pdf/Final%20Product%20List.pdf ******
