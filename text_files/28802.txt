                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

452






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.11.2021






Growth in Rural Wages




Saptagiri Sankar Ulaka














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the growth in rural wages, after adjusting for inflation since January, 2014, month-wise;(b) 	whether the Government is of the opinion that current rural wage rates are adequate enough to maintain a rural household;(c) 	if so, the reasons thereto; and(d) 	if not, the measures being taken by the Government to increase rural wages?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a):       As per the information shared by Labour Bureau, Ministry of Labour & Employment, Labour Bureau compiles and maintains average daily wage rate data in respect of selected agricultural and non-agricultural occupations on the basis of the data collected by the Field Operation Division (FOD) of National Statistical Office (NSO), Ministry of Statistics & Programme Implementation from a fixed set of 600 sample villages spread over 20 States.      Month-wise average daily wage rate  in respect of Non Agricultural Occupation along with Consumer Price Index for Rural Labourer (CPI-RL) from January 2014 to September, 2021 is given at Annexure-I. (b) to (d):         As per section 6(1) of Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, 2005 (Mahatma Gandhi NREGA), the Central Government may, by notification, specify the wage rate for unskilled work for its beneficiaries. Accordingly, the Ministry of Rural Development notifies wage rate  under Mahatma Gandhi NREGA every financial year for States/UTs. To compensate the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (Mahatma Gandhi NREGS) workers against inflation, the Ministry of Rural Development revises the wage rates every financial year based on Consumer Price Index for Agricultural Labourer (CPI-AL). The wage rate is made applicable from 1st April of each financial year. State/UT-wise notified wage rates for unskilled worker under Mahatma Gandhi NREGA for the financial years from 2014-15 to 2021-22 is given at Annexure-II.
