                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3061






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Compressed Biogas




Manoj Rajoria














                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the present status of the SatatYojana launched in the year 2018 for production of compressedbiogas using it as fuel; and(b) the state-wise details of the target fixed and achieved under this scheme and the amount spentthereon?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) “Sustainable Alternative Towards Affordable Transportation (SATAT)” scheme waslaunched on 1st October 2018 wherein Oil and Gas Marketing Companies (OGMCs) are invitingExpression of Interest (EoI) from potential entrepreneur to procure Compressed Bio Gas (CBG).Under this scheme few of the enablers like assured price for offtake of CBG with long termagreements by OGMCs; inclusion of bio manures produced from CBG plants as FermentedOrganic Manure (FOM) under Fertilizer Control Order 1985; inclusion of CBG projects underPriority Sector Lending by RBI have been provided.Till February 2021, OGMCs have issued 871 Letters of Intent (LoIs) to entrepreneurs forprocurement of CBG. So far 9 CBG plants have been commissioned and started supply of CBGunder SATAT scheme.(b) These plants are located in Andhra Pradesh (1No.), Gujarat (3 No.), Haryana (1 No.),Maharashtra (3 No.) and Tamil Nadu (1No.)The plants are being set up by entrepreneurs basedon feed stock availability and on techno-commercial feasibility across the country.*****
