NABARD Schemes




Ajay Nishad














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the National Bank Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) has formulated or proposes to formulate any scheme to provide financial assistance to the rural artisans of the country; (b) if so, the details thereof State/UT-wise including Bihar; (c) the details of the financial assistance provided by NABARD in this regard during the last three years and the current year, scheme-wise and State-wise; and (d) the number of artisans benefitted through various schemes funded by NABARD during the said period, State/UT-wise? (e) the steps taken by the Government to provide more employment in PSUs to local people?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(a) to (c):As apprised byNABARD, they support developmental programmes in the off-farm sector in areas such as skill development, entrepreneurship development, promotion of Off-Farm Producers’ Organizations in the handloom and handicrafts sector, registration of Geographical Indication, etc., to enhance the skill sets and income of the rural people including unemployed youth, artisans, weavers, agri-labourers, etcThe details are provided in Annexure.(d):As apprised by NABARD, cumulatively, NABARD has supported training of 9.58 lakh rural youth, through 35557 programmes, with grant assistance of ?174.37 crore by gainfully engaging rural youth both in wage as well as self-employment. Similarly, 40 Off Farm Producer Organisations covering around 14043 artisans and weavers have been supported with grant assistance of ?17.35 crore across 20 States. During 2021-22, 05 OFPOs with grant support of ? 3.67 Crore have been sanctioned.NABARD has so far supported Geographical Indication (GI) registration of 88 products, including Black Pottery (Nizamabad), Wall hanging (Ghazipur), Soft Stone Jali work (Varanasi), Gulabi Minakari (Banaras), Handmade Dari (Mirzapur), etc., with grant assistance of ?. 174.24 lakh. During 2021 – 22, ? 32.32 lakh grant assistance was sanctioned for registration of 18 GI products. During 2021 – 22, an amount of ?.0.91 crore has been sanctioned for this purpose for registration of 15 products. A project was also sanctioned for registration of 1000 artisans of 17 different GI crafts, skilling and training of 300 Artisans of 2 GI crafts i.e. Varanasi Glass Beads & Mirzapur Handmade Darito in Uttar Pradesh during 2021-22. (e): Employment in Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs) depends upon their requirement as per their expansion/investment plan. The action in respect of provision of jobs by public sector to local people, if any, is to be taken by the Board of the concerned CPSE and the responsibility for the same vests with respective Board of CPSE/its administrative Ministry/Department.****** AnnexureDetails of financial assistance (State-wise/UT wise including Bihar) provided by NABARD to the rural artisans of the country during the last three years and the current yearFINANCIAL YEAR 2018-2019(Rs.
