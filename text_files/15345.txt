                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4036






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2020






Adarsh Railway Stations




Y. S. Avinash Reddy













                                                    C.N. Annadurai
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has planned to develop selected railway stations in every parliamentary constituency as Adarsh Railway Stations; (b) if so, the details thereof; (c) the number of railway stations selected under this programme in the State of Andhra Pradesh, particularly in Kadapa District; (d) whether Kadapa Railway Station has been selected for development as Adarsh Railway Station and if so, the details thereof; (e) the developmental works proposed to be taken up in this railway station and the works completed so far; (f) whether the Railways has noticed that the execution of developmental works is lagging behind the target and if so, the details thereof; and(g) the status and progress of the railway stations selected as Adarsh Railway Station as on date?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) and (b): 1253 stations have been selected for development under Adarsh Station Scheme nation-wide based on the identified,    need for upgradation of amenities.  However, there is no criterion for selection of railway stations on the basis of parliamentary constituency.(c):  46 stations have been selected under Adarsh Station Scheme in the state of Andhra Pradesh.  No station has been identified yet as Adarsh station in Kadapa district. (d) to (f): Cuddapah Railway station (serving the town of Kadapa) has not been identified for development under Adarsh Station Scheme. However, works for replacement of Coach Indication Board at platform nos. 1, 2 and 3 have been completed.(g): 1253 stations have been identified for development under Adarsh Station Scheme, out of which 1181 stations have already been developed so far.*****
