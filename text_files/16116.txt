                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4641






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2020






Development of Ancient Buddhist Centre of Amravati




Kinjarapu Ram Mohan Naidu














                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has taken any steps for developing the ancient Buddhist centre of Amravati, Andhra Pradesh as a tourist destination; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; (c) 	the details of progress made in establishing Buddhist tourism circuit in Andhra Pradesh; (d) 	whether the Government has received funds from any international organization or foreign country for the development of Buddhist tourist sites in the country; and (e) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR TOURISM (INDEPENDENT CHARGE)(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) to (c):	Based on the proposal received from State Government of Andhra Pradesh, Ministry of Tourism has approved the following projects for development of Buddhist centres in the State.i.	“Development of Amaravati Town, Guntur District as a Tourist Destination” approved in 2015-16 under PRASHAD Scheme at the cost of Rs.28.36 Crore. An amount of Rs.22.69 Crore has been released so far.ii.	“Development of Buddhist Circuit: Shalihundam-Thotlakonda- Bavikonda- Bojjanakonda- Amravati- Anupu in Andhra Pradesh” approved in 2017-18 as Buddhist Circuit under Swadesh Darshan Scheme at the cost of Rs.52.34 Crore. An amount of Rs.26.17 Crore has been released so far.iii.	An amount of Rs.1847.50 lakh was sanctioned for project “Development of Amravati” by Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs under their “HRIDAY” Scheme. (d): No, Sir. (e) : Does not arise.********
