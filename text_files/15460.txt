                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3938






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2020






Rural Postal Life Insurance




Amol Ramsing Kolhe













                                                    Shriniwas Dadasaheb Patil
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Ratansinh Magansinh Rathod
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                








                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has achieved the objective of Rural Postal Life Insurance (RPLI) Scheme and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	the details of various types of insurance products offered under the RPLI and the salient features of the same along with the number of such insurance products sold during each of the last three years, State/ UT-wise; (c) 	whether the number of policies sold under the RPLI has declined during the recent period and if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor; (d) 	whether the Government proposes to introduce various training programmes on the lines of similar programmes run by the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority to increase the base of RPLI in the rural areas and if so, the details thereof and the steps taken by the Government in this regard; and(e) 	whether the Government has launched Digital Advancement of Rural Post Office for a New India (DARPAN) project in the country and if so, the details thereof and the success achieved so far by the Government thereunder?    






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS,  HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a)	Yes, Sir. The Government has substantially achieved the objective of Rural Postal Life Insurance (RPLI) Scheme. RPLI scheme is envisaged to provide insurance cover to the people living in rural areas in general and to benefit weaker sections and women workers of rural areas in particular. As on date, there are 250.36 lakhs RPLI policies, out of which 80 lakhs policies have been issued to women living in rural areas of the country. (b)	Details and salient features of six (6) types of insurance products offered under the Rural Postal Life Insurance (RPLI) Scheme are as under:<pre>Product Name	Gram Suraksha (Whole Life Assurance)	Gram Santosh (Endowment Assurance)	Gram Suvidha (Convertible Whole Life Assurance)	Gram Sumangal (Anticipated Endowment Assurance)	Gram Priya 	Gram Bal Jeevan Bima (Children Policy)Maturity age/ Policy term	At the age of 80 years or death, whichever is earlier.	At the pre-determined age of 35, 40, 45, 50, 55, 58, or 60 years	Can be converted to an endowment assurance after 5 years	Policy term of 15 years or 20 years	Policy term of 10 years	Maturity age: 18 to 25 yearsEntry age limits(in years)	19 to 55	19 to 55	19 to 45	19 to 40	20 to 45	Child: 5 to 20Parent : Max 45Sum assured limit (in Rs)	10,000 to 10,00,000	10,000 to 10,00,000	10,000 to 10,00,000	10,000 to 10,00,000	10,000 to 10,00,000	20,000 to 1,00,000Survival benefit	-	-	-	Term 15 years: 20% in year 6, 9, 12 and balance on maturityTerm 20 years: 20% in year 8, 12, and 16 and balance on maturity	20% in year 4 and 7 and balance on maturity	-Loan facility	After 4 years	After 3 years	After 4 years			</pre>Postal Circle (State/UT) - wise RPLI policies issued during each of the last three years (FY 2016-17, FY 2017-18 and FY 2018-19) is enclosed as Annexure-I. (c)	No, Sir. The number of policies sold under RPLI has increased from 4,05,793 in FY 2016-17 to 5,23,899 in FY 2017-18 to 7,72,650 in FY 2018-19. (d)	To increase the base of RPLI in rural areas, sales force of RPLI comprising of Gramin Dak Sevaks, Departmental Employees and Direct Agents are given in-house training through various institutions and licentiate training in coordination with the Insurance Institute of India, Mumbai, an institute recognized by the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India.  (e)	To make optimum utilization of post offices across the country, Department of Posts has launched Digital Advancement of Rural Post Office for a New India (DARPAN) Project in all 1,29,076 Branch Post Offices in rural areas. Under the project, the Branch Postmasters have been provided with SIM based handheld devices for carrying out online postal and financial transactions. Till date,more than 29 crores transactions involving an amount of Rs. 34,728 crores have been carried out through the DARPAN device.
