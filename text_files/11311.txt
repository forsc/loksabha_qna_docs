                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

403






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2020






Fund for Disaster Management




N.K. Premachandran














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has sanctioned fund for disaster management of flood, drought and other natural calamities to various States;(b) if so, the details of funds sanctioned to each State during the last two years;(c) whether the Government has received request from the Government of Kerala for sanctioning of funds on account of flood;(d) if so, the details thereof;(e) whether the Government has initiated action for sanctioning of funds for the management of losses sustained due to flood in Kerala;(f) if so, the details thereof and the action taken thereon;(g) if not, the reasons for not sanctioning funds for disaster management of flood in Kerala;(h) whether the Government proposes to stop the disbursement of fund for the disaster management of flood in various States; and(i) if so, the details and the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)-2-L.S.US.Q.NO.403 FOR 04.02.2020(a) and (b):	   The primary responsibility for disaster management rests with the State Government.  The concerned State Governments undertake relief measures in the wake of natural disasters including floods from the State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) already placed at their disposal in accordance with Government of India’s approved items and norms. Additional assistance is extended from the National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF) as per established procedure. State-wise details of releases from SDRF and NDRF during the year 2018-19 to 2019-20 (as on 29.01.2020) are attached at Annexure. (c) to (g):	Based on the request of Government of Kerala and assessment made by the Inter Ministerial Central Team (IMCT), the Central Government had released Rs. 2904.85 crore from National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF) to Government of Kerala as additional relief assistance towards floods of 2018. As informed by the State Government of Kerala, an amount of Rs. 2244 crore only (Rs.1297.75 crore in the financial year 2018-19 and Rs. 946.61 crores in the current financial year till 03.12.2019) has been utilised. 	Further, as informed by the Government of Kerala, an amount of          Rs.2113.98 crore is available in their SDRF as on 01.04.2019. After release of Centre’s share of 1st installment for year 2019-20,  Rs. 2194.38  crore  is -3-L.S.US.Q.NO.403 FOR 04.02.2020available in their SDRF for providing relief to the people affected due to notified natural calamities including floods during 2019-20.  	For floods of 2019, before the receipt of Memorandum from the Government of Kerala, an IMCT had visited the affected areas of the State. After the visit of IMCT, the Government of Kerala submitted their memorandum and sought additional financial assistance of Rs. 1624.09 crore from NDRF. Based on the memorandum and in terms of items and norms for SDRF/NDRF, IMCT has submitted its report. The report of IMCT is considered by High Level Committee as per established procedure. (h):	No Sir.  (i) :	Does not arise. *********
