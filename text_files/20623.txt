                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1840






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2021






Development of Bio-Energy System




Ravindra Kushawaha













                                                    Ravindra Shyamnarayan alias Ravi Kishan Shukla
                                                








                                                    Subrata Pathak
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has prepared any guidelines for development of bio-energy system in the country;(b)	if so, the details thereof; and(c)	the amount of financial assistance provided during the last three years and the current year for the said purpose, State-wise including Bihar?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (I/C) FOR NEW & RENEWABLE ENERGY, POWER and MoS for  SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP(SHRI R.K. SINGH)(a) & (b) The Ministry is implementing the following schemes for development of bio-energy  in the country :-1. Scheme to support promotion of biomass based cogeneration in sugar mills and other industries. Under the scheme, Central Financial Assistance @ Rs 25 Lakhs / MW of surplus exportable capacity for bagasse cogeneration projects and Rs 50 Lakhs / MW of installed capacity for non-bagasse cogeneration projects is provided to cogeneration projects utilizing biomass like bagasse, agro-based industrial residue, crop residues, wood produced through energy plantations, weeds, wood waste produced in industrial operations, etc.2. Programme on Energy from Urban, Industrial, Agricultural Wastes/ Residues and Municipal Solid Waste for recovery of energy in the form of Biogas or BioCNG or Power from Urban, Industrial and Agricultural Waste / Residues such as MSW, vegetable and fruit market wastes, slaughterhouse waste, agricultural residues and industrial wastes & effluents. The financial assistance available under this Programme is as follows:a. Biogas generation:  Rs 1.0 crore per 12000cum/day (Maximum Rs 10Cr/project); b. BioCNG generation (including setting of Biogas plant): Rs 4.0 Crore per 4800Kg/day (Maximum Rs 10Cr/project);c. Power generation based on Biogas (including setting of Biogas plant): Rs 1.5 Cr to Rs 3.0 Crore per MW(Maximum Rs 10Cr/project);d. Power generation from MSW: Rs 5.0 Crore per MW (Maximum Rs 50Cr/project);e. Power generation from solid waste other than MSW: Rs 0.50 Crore per MW (Maximum Rs 10Cr/project);f. Biomass Gasifier:•         Rs. 2,500 per kWe with duel fuel engines  for electrical application•         Rs. 15,000 per kWe with 100% gas engines for electrical application•         Rs. 2 lakh per 300 kWth for thermal applications.3. New National Biogas and Organic Manure Programme (NNBOMP) aims at setting up small biogas plants (1-25 m3) for meeting cooking energy and lighting needs of mainly rural and semi-urban households of the country.  Under the NNBOMP Scheme, CFA is provided for setting up biogas plants of size from 1 m3 to 25 m3. The subsidy in the range of Rs. 7,500/- per plant of 1 m3 to Rs. 35,000/- per plant of 20 to 25 m3. Besides this, financial support is also provided for construction, supervision etc. There is also provision for support for skill development.4.  Biogas based Power Generation (Off-Grid) & Thermal energy application Programme (BPGTP)  provides clean energy solutions through biogas production to reduce consumption of diesel, kerosene and electricity  by installation of medium size biogas plants of size 30 m3 to 2500 m3  per day with power generation installed capacity of 3 KW to 250 KW .(c) Financial assistance provided during the last three years and current year State–wise including Bihar is placed at Annexure.*****
