                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1303






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.07.2021






KCC for Animal Husbandry




Jagdambika Pal 














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has extended the benefit of the Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme to farmers engaged in Animal Husbandry and Fisheries, if so, the details thereof;(b)	the number of farmers engaged in Animal Husbandry and Fisheries who have been provided a Kisan Credit Card till date, the details thereof, State-wise; (c)	the amount of credit that has been extended to farmers engaged in Animal Husbandry and Fisheries till date, the details thereof, State-wise;(d)	the details of steps taken by the Government to promote animal husbandry and fisheries being taken up by traditional farmers; and(e)	whether the Government has any plans to support and promote co-operatives amongst such farmers, if so, the details thereof including number of such existing cooperatives, State-wise? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF FISHERIES ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a)	Yes Sir, The Reserve Bank of India vide circular FIDD.CO.FSD. BC.12/05.05.010/2018-19 dated February 04, 2019 has extended KCC facility to Animal Husbandry farmers and Fisheries for their working capital requirements wherein farmers either individual or joint borrower, Joint Liability Groups or Self Help Groups including tenant farmers having owned /rented/leased sheds are eligible for getting benefits under the scheme. The loan limit is arrived at based on the Scale of Finance (SoF) methodology. The Scale of Finance is fixed by the State Level Technical Committee (SLTC) in consultation with District Level Technical Committee (DLTC) based on local cost worked out on the basis of per acre/per unit/per animal/per bird etc. Further, Reserve Bank of India vide circular FIDD.CO.FSD.BC.No.10/05.02.001/2019-20 dated August 26,2019 has extended the benefits of Interest Subvention at 2% and Prompt Repayment Incentive (PRI) at 3% to fisheries and animal husbandry farmers (upto a maximum limit of Rs. 2 lakhs) to meet their working capital needs under the KCC scheme. The scheme is continuing for the year 2020-21.(b)	& (c) Total 6,63,055 farmers engaged in Animal Husbandry and Fisheries were provided Kisan Credit Card as on March,2021. As reported by Reserve Bank of India, State/UT-wise details of operative KCC accounts and amount outstanding in Animal Husbandry and Fisheries as on March, 2021 is furnished in the Annexure- I.(d)	Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing following schemes to promote animal husbandry and fisheries being taken up by traditional farmers;(i)	Entrepreneurship Development and Employment Generation (EDEG) under National Livestock Mission (NLM)(ii)	Animal Husbandry Infrastructure Development Fund (AHIDF)(iii)	Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY)(e)	 Yes sir, in order to support and promote cooperatives, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing following schemes;(i)	National Programme for Dairy Development(NPDD)(ii)	Dairy Processing Infrastructure Development Fund (DIDF)(iii)	Supporting Dairy Cooperatives and Farmer Producer Organizations engaged in dairy activities (SDCFPO)(iv)	Pradhan Mantri Matsya SampadaYojana (PMMSY)The State wise details of number of existing dairy cooperatives and fisheries cooperatives are given in Annexure- II and Annexure-III respectively.*****
