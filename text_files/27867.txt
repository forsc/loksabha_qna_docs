                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

308






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana




Unmesh Bhaiyyasaheb Patil













                                                    Heena Vijaykumar Gavit
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the status of villages adopted under the first phase of Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana (SAGY) in Maharashtra;(b) 	the status of drinking water, toilets, power, roads, telephone connectivity and construction of immovable assets in these villages along with inspection reports thereof, State-wise;(c) 	whether the progress of works being undertaken in villages under the second phase of the Yojana is satisfactory, if so, the details thereof and the time by which it is likely to be completed;(d) 	the details of the expenditure incurred and assets created under Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) during the first and second phases of the Yojana, Statewise and village-wise; and(e) 	whether the Government has given any direction to the State Governments for taking necessary steps to extend works under the said model villages and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SHRI GIRIRAJ SINGH)(a) to (e): A statement is laid on the Table of the House. Statement referred in reply to Lok Sabha Starred Question  No. 308 on ‘Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana(SAGY)’ to be answered on 10 August 2021(a) & (b): Hon’ble Members of Parliament from Maharashtra have identified 70 Gram Panchayats during Phase-I(2014-16) of Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana(SAGY) . The status of the projects undertaken in these GPs is given in Annexure-I. At the national level, a separate web-based MIS is available at SAGY website (http://saanjhi.gov.in). The status of drinking water, toilets, power, roads, telephone connectivity and construction of immovable assets in the Gram Panchayat identified under SAGY, State Wise as per the information uploaded by States/UTs on SAGY portal (http://saanjhi.gov.in) is given in Annexure-II. This MIS monitors the progress of identification of Gram Panchayats under SAGY, preparation of VDP and its uploading on the website, the number of projects planned in the VDP and the progress of implementation of VDP is monitored in terms of completion of the projects taken up in the VDP. The details of Gram Panchayats identified under SAGY and its progress are available on saanjhi.gov.in. (c) &(d) :  The progress of works being undertaken in the Gram Panchayats identified under the second phase of the Yojana is satisfactory. Out of 502 Gram Panchayats identified under Phase-II(2016-19), 430 GPs have prepared their VDP containing 21,554 works. Out of 21,554 works, 15,093(70%) have already been completed. The details of assets created under CSR during the first and second phases of the yojana, State Wise and village-wise as per  the information uploaded by States/UTs on SAGY portal, is given in Annexure-III. SAGY is a scheme founded on 100% convergence and has no dedicated fund of its own. Expenditure incurred to complete the activities proposed under VDP under different State and Central schemes are not available in the MIS.(e) : Yes Sir. The detailed directions  to the State Government and the steps taken by  Government of India to extend works in SAGY GPs are given in Annexure-IV.******** Annexure-I  referred to in reply to parts (a) & (b)  of Lok Sabha Starred Question  No. 308 on ‘Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana(SAGY)’   for answer on 10 August 2021The status of villages adopted under the first phase(2014-16) of Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana (SAGY) in Maharashtra;Annexure-II  referred to in reply to parts (a)&(b) of  Lok Sabha Starred  Question  No. 308 on ‘Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana(SAGY)’  for answer on 10 August 2021The status of drinking water, toilets, power, roads, telephone connectivity and construction of immovable assets in the Gram Panchayat identified under SAGY, State Wise as per the information uploaded by States/UTs on SAGY portal (http://saanjhi.gov.in)Annexure-III  referred in reply to parts  (c)&(d) of   Lok Sabha Starred Question  No. 308 on ‘Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana(SAGY)’   for answer on 10 August 2021The details of assets created under CSR during the first and second phases of the Yojana, State Wise and village-wise as per  the information uploaded by States/UTs on SAGY portal (http://saanjhi.gov.in).Annexure-IV  referred to in reply to part (e) of   Lok Sabha Starred Question  No. 308 on ‘Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana(SAGY)’   for answer on 10 August 2021The Government has given the following directions  to the State Government and taken following steps to extend works in SAGY GPs.i.	The States/UTs have been requested to conduct State Level Empowered Committee meetings once in a quarter to ensure the seamless convergence of schemes among different Government Departments.ii.	The States/UTs have been requested to expedite the completion of pending works that have been proposed under the Village Development Plan of SAGY.iii.	The States/UTs have been directed to appoint resource persons under SLEC for supporting the implementation of SAGY at the State and District level in accordance with Para 12 (c) of the Guidelines of  SAGY Guidelinesiv.	The National Level Committee meeting headed by the Secretary, Rural Development has been conducted wherein all the 20 members Ministries/Departments have been requested to issue necessary advisories and to advise the State Government to arrange to complete the pending VDP activities that are mapped with their respective schemes/programmes and also to saturate all the major programmes/schemes in the SAGY GPs on priority. v.	24 Schemes of 17 Ministries of Government of India have been amended / appropriate advisories have been issued to accord priority to SAGY in respective schemes for ensuring convergence .vi.	The Performance Review Committee (PRC) meeting is held twice a year in which all Rural Development programmes are reviewed by the Secretary, Rural Development. All the implementing departments (Rural Development/Panchayati Raj etc.)  in the States/UTs participate in the meeting under the Chairmanship of their respective Principal Secretaries/Additional Chief Secretaries. States/UTs have been requested to focus on implementing pending projects in SAGY GPs during the PRC meeting.vii.	In order to establish an organic connection in the system and with the Hon''ble MP concerned to get his guidance and directions in transforming the identified GPs into Adarsh Gram, the State Governments have been requested to appoint a competent Charge Officer of sufficient seniority, for every Gram Panchayat who coordinates the implementation at the local level and is fully responsible and accountable for the implementationviii.	Capacity building programmes for the State Nodal Officers, State Team of Trainers and Charge Officers of SAGY Gram Panchayats were organized through National Institute of Rural Development and Panchayati Raj, Hyderabad..Gram Panchayat functionaries and other State stakeholders of  SAGY have  also been  trained on preparation of Village Development Plan  by NIRD&PR.ix.	The Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana (SAGY) is reviewed periodically by the  National Level Monitors and by the Common Review Mission (CRM) each year  to assess the performance of the yojana.********
