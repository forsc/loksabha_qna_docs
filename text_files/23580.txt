                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MINES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4811






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2021






Minerals Conservation and Development Rules




Vinod Kumar Sonkar













                                                    Raja Amareshwara Naik
                                                








                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                








                                                    Nisith Pramanik
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                








                                                    Rajveer (Raju Bhaiya) Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





MINES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has recently amended the Mineral Conservation and Development Rules to make timely supply of mineral by the mines, which is an annual mandatory exercise;(b)	if so, details thereof;(c)	whether the Government has collected data for 700 mines of major minerals in the country for rating under sustainable development framework;(d)	if so, the details thereof;(e)	whether the Indian Bureau of Mines recently felicitated star ratings to various mines in the country and if so, the details thereof, State-wise; and(f)	the other steps and measures being taken by the Government for the strengthening of mines sector in India?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF MINES, COAL AND PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS(SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI)(a):	No, Sir.(b):	Does not arise.(c) & (d): Yes, Sir. 931 lessees have filed online self-assessment report for 2019-20 and validation of these reports is being carried out by IBM  through field inspection  for star rating of mines  under Mineral Conservation and Development Rules, 2017. Result of the inspection is as under:- Rating	No of leases (Provisional Rating) 5 Star rating	1974 Star rating	3103 Star rating	2612 Star rating	561 Star rating	90no Star rating	17 	931 (e) & (f):  57 mines were felicitated for the year 2016-17 for star rating during the third National Conclave on Mines and Minerals held on 20th March 2018 at New Delhi. The facilitation for 2017-18 & 2018-19 are yet to be done. Star rating of mines for the performance of  year 2019-20 is under progress. State-wise details of facilitation (2016-17) is as under: Sl.  No.	State	2016-171	Andhra Pradesh	12	Bihar	-3	Chhattisgarh	74	Goa	-5	Gujarat	126	Himachal Pradesh	-7	Jharkhand	28	Karnataka	89	Madhya Pradesh	810	Maharashtra	811	Meghalaya	-12	Odisha	113	Rajasthan	914	Tamilnadu	115	Telangana	- 	Total	57   *****
