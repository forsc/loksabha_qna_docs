                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3167






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Protection of Archaeological Places from Theft




Rodmal Nagar














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether incidents of theft have been reported from places of archaeological importance and protected monuments;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether any criteria has been fixed for protection or places of archaeological importance; and(d)	the action taken by the Government to check such incidents?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE & TOURISM (SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)	Yes, Sir.(b)	The details are at Annexure I.(c)	As per the criteria specified under the Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act, 1958, a monument or site should be of historical, archaeological or artistic interest and not less than 100 years old.(d)	Archaeological Survey of India is committed towards protection of the monuments, sites and antiquities under its jurisdiction. Besides regular watch and ward staff of Archaeological Survey of India, Private Security Guards, State Police Armed Guards, and Central Industrial Security Force have been deployed as per requirement. Whenever any theft of antiquity is reported, FIR is lodged in concerned police station and ‘Look Out Notice’ is issued to law enforcing agencies (like CBI, police, etc.) including Custom Exit Channels to keep vigil to trace stolen antiquity and prevent their illegal export. Andhra Pradesh	Theft of a Nandi idol from Sri Anadavalli Ammavaru Temple, Parasurameshwara Swamy Complex, Gudimallam, Chitoor district	Stone	5-10-2017	F.I.R Lodged	Not recovered•	No theft is reported from Centrally Protected Monuments/Sites/Museums maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India in the year 2018 and 2019•	Statement giving details of theft of antiquities from Centrally Protected Monuments/Sites/Museums maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India in theyear 20201.
