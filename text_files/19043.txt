                                    QUESTION NO:

                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2021






Digital Literacy




Pinaki Misra














                        Will the Minister of





ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of Electronics & Information Technology be pleased to state:-(a)	the digital literacy rate in the country, urban and rural, and state/UT-wise;(b)	the definition of ''digital literacy'' in the National Digital Literacy Mission, Digital Saksharta Abhiyan and the Pradhan Mantri Gramin Digital Saksharta Abhiyan schemes;(c)	the total number of people covered under the aforementioned schemes, State/UT-wise;(d)	whether any steps are being taken by the Government to address the disparity in the progress of existing schemes across different states and UTs, and if so, the details thereof, and if not, the reasons therefor;(e)	whether the Government has any plans to expand the coverage of digital literacy schemes, and if so, the details thereof, and if not, the reasons therefor; and (f)	whether any steps are being taken by the Government to improve the country''s digital infrastructure of the Country, and if so, the details thereof ?






ANSWER




                                                        (a):  National Sample Survey Office (NSSO) had published the 71st Round Survey Report titled ‘Social Consumption - Education Survey 2014’, which inter-alia covers IT Literacy. As per this report, the proportion of households in the Country having computer was found to be around 14% (only 6% of rural households and 29% of urban households possessed computer). The State/UT-wise proportion of households having access to computer is at Annexure-I. Further, for the age 14 years and above, the ability to operate computer in rural and urban areas was reported as under:Proportion (per 1000) of population (age 14 years and above and can operate computer)  by types of ability to use a computerRural	UrbanAble to use computer word processing typing	Able to use internet for searching desired information	Able to use internet for sending e-mails	Able to use computer word processing typing	Able to use internet for searching desired information	Able to use internet for sending e-mails234	218	193	840	809	758(b): As per the National Digital Literacy Mission, Digital Saksharta Abhiyan and the Pradhan Mantri Gramin Digital Saksharta Abhiyan schemes, the Digital Literacy is defined as “Digital Literacy is the ability of individuals and communities to understand and use digital technologies for meaningful actions within life situations”.(c): In the NDLM/DISHA schemes, 1,09,86,469 candidates were registered, 88,08,856 candidates were trained out of which 53,67,092 were duly certified. The State/UT-wise detail is at Annexure-II.Under the PMGDISHA scheme, as on 28.01.2021, 4,16,61,579 candidates have been registered, 3,40,20,338 candidates have been trained and out of which 2,47,31,689 have been duly certified. The details of State/UT-wise training details is given at Annexure-III.(d) and (e): To address the disparity in the progress of existing schemes across different states and UTs, initiatives are taken up to scale up awareness on digital literacy through registration/ certification drives, online workshops, seminars and other activities by using e-learning, print and electronic media, etc. to cover various segments of the population in an all-inclusive manner. In order to ensure equitable geographical reach to all the Gram Panchayats across the country, so far, 2,46,868 gram panchayats have been covered, under the PMGDISHA Scheme which is under implementation presently with a target to make six crore persons digitally literate in rural areas, covering around 40% of the rural population (one person per household) across States/UTs. (f): Following steps are taken by the Ministry of Communications to improve digital infrastructure of the Country:•	National Digital Communications Policy-2018 was notified on 22nd October, 2018 with strategic objectives which inter-alia include broadband i.e. high speed internet for all•	National Broadband Mission (NBM) was launched on 17th December 2019 with objectives which inter-alia include Broadband access to all villages and facilitate universal & equitable access to broadband services across the country especially in rural and remote areas •	BharatNet project is being implemented in a phased manner to connect all the Gram Panchayats (approx. 2.5 lakh) by broadband/high speed internet infrastructure in the country. As on 15.01.2021, a total of 1,51,504 GPs (including Block Headquarters) have been made service ready.
