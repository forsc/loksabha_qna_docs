                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3078






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.08.2021






Augmented and Virtual Reality Technology




T.R.V.S. Ramesh














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government proposes to implement Virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) technologies in various medical institutions in the country;(b) if so, the details thereof; (c) the manner in which the said technology is going to be beneficial in better understanding the practical concepts of learning for the medical students; and(d) the number of government and affiliated medical intuitions that are in existence in the country, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR HEALTH & FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) to (c): Minimum Requirements for Annual M.B.B.S. Admissions Regulations, 2020 of National Medical Commission (NMC) provides for that every medical institution shall have a Skills Laboratory where students can practice and improve skills pre-specified in the  competency  based  undergraduate  curriculum.  The  skills  laboratory  attempts  to recreate  the  clinical  environment  and  tasks  which  future  health  care  workers  have  to  perform  with  various  levels  of complexity and fidelity. As per information provided by the NMC, Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR) technologies are used in few medical institutions in India. These technologies are for teaching medical students and help them learn to treat the patients or perform surgeries in a virtual environment.  (d): There are 289 Government and 269 private medical colleges / institutions in the country. The State/ UT wise list of Government and private medical colleges / institutions is attached at Annexure.
