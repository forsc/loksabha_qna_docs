                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3633






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2020






Objective of NSDM and PMKVY




Manne Srinivas Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the main objectives of the National Skill Development Mission and the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana have been achieved so far;(b)	if so, the key indicators that denote such achievement;(c)	the details of the sector-wise list of skill development centres established in Telangana in each district and the number of persons who have been skilled and placed under PMKVY and other schemes in the years 2018-19 and 2019-2020; and(d)	the details of the percentage of all placed and employed candidates who have lost employment due to various reasons?






ANSWER




                                                        (a), (b) and (d) 	 The objective of the National Skill Development Mission (NSDM) is to scale up skill development efforts in India through outcome based implementation framework. Under NSDM, the key indicators include convergence of schemes/programmes under standardised skill ecosystem, skill development training, sustainable livelihood, employment, leveraging public infrastructure, trainers etc. Under framework of NSDM, Ministry is implementing Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) to train 10 million people in the country during period of 2016-20. As on 17.01.2020, 73.47 lakh candidates have been trained through certified trainers, empanelled training centres including leveraging public infrastructure. Out of the 32.34 lakh certified candidates, 16.61 lakh candidates have been reported to be placed in diverse sectors which accounted for 51% percentage of the total certified candidates. (c)	Under PMKVY (2016-20), as on 17.01.2020, 420 training centres have been empanelled to impart skill development training at district level in the State of Telangana. Under this Scheme, in the State of Telangana, 1.49 lakh and 1.23 lakh candidates have been trained and certified, respectively, under short term training courses. Out of these certified candidates, 0.77 lakh candidates have been placed in diverse sectors. Also, 0.79 lakh candidates have been oriented under Recognition of prior learning (RPL) component of the scheme.
