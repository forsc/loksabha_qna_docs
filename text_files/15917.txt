                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4432






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.03.2020






Development of Forest Area




 Dharambir Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the policy of the Government towards developing forest areas in the 	country, State-wise including Haryana;(b) 	the steps taken/schemes formulated by the Government regarding forest area in Haryana during the last three years along with the progress made in this regard; and (c)	the scheme-wise details of budget allocated to various States including Haryana during 	the last three years and the current financial year?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) to (c) The National Forest Policy (NFP), 1988, envisages a minimum of one-third of the total land area of the country under forest or tree cover. For increasing and improving forest cover in the country, including in Haryana, the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEF&CC) has taken several initiatives. These include Centrally Sponsored Schemes (CSS), such as National Afforestation Programme, National Mission for Green India and Development of Wildlife Habitats. Compensatory Afforestation Fund Rules, 2018 also contain provisions for taking up assisted natural regeneration, artificial regeneration and silvicultural operations in forest, which contribute to improvement and increase in forest cover. Afforestation and tree planting activities, including in non-forest areas, are also taken up under various programme/funding sources of other Ministries of Government of India such as, Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme, Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana and under the schemes/plans of States and Union Territories and also by corporate bodies, public institutions, civil society/NGOs public sector undertakings, etc. As per the information received from Government of Haryana, plantation of 1.67 crore, 1.34 crore and 98.53 lakh seedlings were undertaken in the years 2017-18, 2018-19 and 2019-20 respectively. State-wise details of fund released by the Ministry for conservation, development and expansion of forests under National Afforestation Programme(NAP), Green India Mission (GIM) and Development of Wildlife Habitats (DWH) Schemes during the last three years and the current year to States/UTs including Haryana are given in Annexure-I, II, III respectively. Under Compensatory Afforestation Funds (CAF), funds were released during 2016-17, 2017-18 and 2018-19 by the Ad-hoc Compensatory Afforestation Fund Management and Planning Authority (CAMPA).The Statement of State-wise release is at Annexure IV. After formation of National Authority under Compensatory Afforestation (CAF) Act, 2016, a sum of Rs. 47436.18 Crore have been deposited in State funds as per the provision of the CAF Act, 2016. State-wise details of fund deposited in State fund are at Annexure V.*****
