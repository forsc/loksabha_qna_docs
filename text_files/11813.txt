                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

879






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.02.2020






Safety of  Women Employees




Anubhav Mohanty














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether women employees of the airline companies, particularly those performing on flight duties, are sexually harassed by their superiors while on flight to seek undue favours and if so, the details thereof;(b) the details of the airline companies which do not have Internal Complaints Committee and existing mechanism to handle complaints of their women employees;(c) whether various complaints have been received by the airline companies, including women employees of the National Career during the last three years and if so, the details thereof;(d) the action taken by the airlines on these complaints during the said period; and(e) whether a number of complaints are still pending and if so, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) and (b): The complaints related to sexual harassment of women employees of the airlines are dealt in accordance with the provisions of Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013. Airlines are also required to comply with this Act.;;(c) to (e): The airline companies by virtue of being private entities deal internally with such complaints. All airlines are required to adhere to the provision of the Act. The Civil Aviation Requirements (CAR) do not require compliance/details under the Act to be submitted by airline companies to Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA), the Safety Regulator. However, all the private domestic airlines have constituted Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) to investigate the complaints of alleged Sexual Harassment made by the women employees of the airline companies, in compliance with the Act.;;The national carrier, which is a Government entity has also constituted ICC for Air India and its subsidiary airlines. These Complaints Committees investigate the complaints of alleged Sexual Harassment made by the women employees of the company. Air India and its subsidiary airlines have taken action on the complaints based on the recommendation of ICC.The details of complaints of alleged sexual harassment  received by Air India/Air India Express/Alliance Air during the last three years and the status of these complaints are attached at Annexure.
