                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2447






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.07.2019






Inclusion of Sites in the Tentative List of World Heritage List




Jayadev Galla














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether Amaravati Development Authority of Andhra Pradesh has requested the Government to declare five Tourist Places in Andhra Pradesh as Heritage places;(b)	if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government thereto; and(c)	the efforts made by the Government to include these sites recommended by Amaravati Development Authority in the Tentative List of World Heritage Sites?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE AND TOURISM(PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)	Yes, Sir.  (b)	The five monuments are (i)Gandikota Fort, District Kadapa, (ii)Fort at Chandragiri, District Chittoor, (iii)Buddhist Caves at Guntupalli, District Krishna and (iv) Stupa at Amaravati District Guntur and (v) Fort at Kondaveedu, District Guntur in Andhra Pradesh. However, except Fort at Kondaveedu, four monuments are already declared as centrally protected monument under the Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act, 1958.(c)	The historical importance of these monuments in the context of World Heritage and its universal value are under examination.
