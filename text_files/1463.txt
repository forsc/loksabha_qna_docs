                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1223






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.06.2019






Medical Colleges




Meenakashi Lekhi













                                                    Hemant Tukaram Godse
                                                








                                                    Ravneet Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the details of medical colleges established/set up during the last three years, State/UT, district-wise including Maharashtra, Gujarat and Punjab;(b) the details of States/UTs which have sought financial assistance and funds allocated and utilized for projects/schemes for the medical colleges;(c) whether Government in consultation with States/UTs are planning to increase/open a number of new medical colleges, State/UT-wise particularly in Punjab;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) the steps taken/being taken by the Government to address the issue of illegal capitation fees in private medical colleges?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	Details are given at Annexure - I.(b) to (d):	Ministry of Health & Family Welfare is implementing a Centrally Sponsored Scheme for “Establishment of new medical colleges attached with existing district/referral hospitals”. The fund sharing ratio between the Central Government and States under the Scheme is 90:10 for NE/Special Category states and 60:40 for other States. Under the Phase-I of this Scheme, the total cost of establishment of one Medical College is Rs.189 crore. 58 districts in 20 States/UT have been identified and approved under Phase-I of this Scheme including one in Punjab. Funds to the tune of Rs.7507.70 crore have been released to the State/UT Governments for the approved medical colleges under the Scheme till date. Further, Phase-II of this Scheme has been approved on 7th February, 2018. The objective of the Phase-II of the scheme is to ensure the availability of one medical college in every 3 Parliamentary Constituencies and one Government medical college in each State. The total cost of establishment of one Medical College under this Phase is Rs.250 crore. 24 new medical colleges in 8 States have been identified under this Scheme to establish new Medical Colleges attached with existing district/referral hospitals. The State Governments have identified these locations on Challenge mode. Out of 24 medical colleges, 17 have been approved till date and funds to the tune of Rs.1375.13 crore have been released to the State Governments for the approved districts under the Scheme. Details of funds released under Phase-I and Phase-II of the Scheme are given in Annexure-II & Annexure-III respectively.(e):	In the case of Government Medical Colleges, the respective State Government are responsible for fixation of fee and in the case of Private unaided medical Colleges, the fee structure is decided by the Committee set up by the respective State Government under the Chairmanship of a retired High Court Judge in pursuance of the directions of the Hon’ble Supreme Court of India. It is for the Committee to decide whether the fee proposed by an Institute is justified and the fee fixed by the Committee is binding on the Institute.     Further, the Hon’ble Supreme Court in its order dated 09.05.2017 had directed that the students who secure admission in MBBS course pursuant to the common counseling shall deposit with the counseling committee the demand draft towards the fee payable to the Institution/ Colleges/Universities
