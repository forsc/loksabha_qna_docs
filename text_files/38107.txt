Status of ST Beneficiaries




Manoj Rajoria














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the amount spent by the Government for the welfare of Scheduled Tribes for upgrading education and employment since the year 2014 and the number of beneficiaries Year/state-wise;(b) whether any agency/institution has been appointed by the Government to assess the development of these beneficiaries or to achieve the goals of the said schemes; and(c) the current status of the targets set by the Government for the development of Scheduled Tribes beneficiaries?






ANSWER




                                                        (a): Ministry of Tribal Affairs is implementing various schemes/programmes for promotion of education and providing employment to the Scheduled Tribes in the country. Details of the funds provided by the Ministry under schemes/programmes for promotion of education and providing employment and number of beneficiaries/achievements under some schemes of the Ministry are given at Annexure.(b): Schemes being implemented by Ministry of Tribal Affairs are evaluated, from time to time, by independent agency to find out the impact of the schemes on the welfare and development of Scheduled Tribes (STs) and to ensure that, wherever necessary, further refinements in the schemes are carried out so as to make the schemes more effective and to provide benefits to the STs. Ministry has done evaluation of its schemes through Indian Institute of Public Administration and Bharat Rural Livelihood Foundation (BRLF) in recent past. Further, NITI Aayog has also evaluated the schemes through the third party (M/s.KPMG).  (c):  Most of the schemes of Ministry of Tribal Affairs are demand driven and funds are provided to the State Governments based on the proposals received from them after appraisal and approval by the Ministry. As regards Eklavya Model Residential Schools (EMRS), Government has decided to establish an EMRS in every block with more than 50% ST population and at least 20,000 tribal persons. Accordingly, the target is to establish 740 EMRSs in the country. As on 22.03.2022, total 679 EMRSs have been sanctioned and of them, 367 are functional. Under Pre-Matric and Post-Matric Scholarship, the Ministry provides scholarships to around 33-35 lakhs ST students every year. Progress under some other schemes has also been provided in the Annexure.
