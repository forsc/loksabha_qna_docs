                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

488






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.04.2022






Special Train from Kishkindha to Ayodhya




Umesh G. Jadhav













                                                    Sanganna Amarappa Karadi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Railways has received any  proposal for starting special train from Kishkindha in Karnataka to Ayodhya in Uttar Pradesh;(b)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and(c)	the time by which the decision is likely to be taken and implemented in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMUNICATIONS AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI ASHWINI VAISHNAW) (a)	to (c)  A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.***** STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (c) OF STARRED QUESTION NO. 488 BY DR. UMESH G. JADHAV AND           SHRI SANGANNA AMARAPPA TO BE ANSWERED IN LOK SABHA ON 06.04.2022 REGARDING SPECIAL TRAIN FROM KISHKINDHA TO AYODHYA(a)	to (c) Yes, Sir.  Kishkindha is a village in Gangavati Taluka, served by Gangavati station on Karatagi – Hubballi section.  Introduction of train service between Gangavati and Ayodhya is operationally not feasible due to non availability of terminal facilities at Gangavati and terminal constraints at Ayodhya and path constraints enroute.  However, introduction of train including Special trains is an ongoing process on Indian Railways subject to operational feasibility, traffic justification and availability of resources.  *****
