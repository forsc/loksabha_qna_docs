                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2756






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Performance of MSME Sector under PMEGP




Jyotsna Charandas Mahant














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has started any new component for the units performing better under the Prime Minister‘s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP);(b) 	if so, the details thereof and the amount of funds provided under the said component;(c) 	the number of micro enterprises which have received benefits of new component during the last three years, State-wise including Chhattisgarh; and(d) 	the number of persons in the country for which employment has been generated under the said scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES (SHRI BHANU PRATAP SINGH VERMA)(a):	Yes, Sir.(b): 	 Ministry of MSME is implementing Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP) since 2008-09 for generating employment opportunities in the country by setting up micro-enterprises in non-farm sector. 	From the year 2018-19, Ministry has introduced a new component under PMEGP providing further financial assistance (2nd dose of assistance) for expansion/ upgradation of the existing PMEGP/ REGP/ MUDRA units. The maximum cost of the project under the manufacturing sector for up-gradation is Rs. 1 crore and for the Service/ Trading sector it is Rs. 25 lakh. Maximum subsidy would be 15% of the project cost (20% for NER and Hill States). The balance amount of the total project cost is provided by Banks as term loan.(c) & (d):	State/UT-wise number of micro-enterprises benefitted under the new component, Margin Money subsidy disbursed and estimated employment generated during the last three years including in the State of Chhattisgarh is given at Annexure.
