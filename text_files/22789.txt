                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4016






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Repository for Medicinal Plants




Devendrappa Y














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA,YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government has been preparing to create a repository to conserve medicinal plants;(b) if so, the details thereof; and(c) the financial assistance provided for conservation of medicinal plants and infrastructure development in the country during the last three years?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS AND ADDITIONAL CHARGE OF MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(SHRI KIREN RIJIJU)(a) & (b):	Yes, The National Medicinal Plants Board (NMPB), Ministry of AYUSH, Government of India has established one National Raw Drug Repository (NRDR) at Central Ayurveda Research Institutes (CARI, CCRAS), Jhansi, Uttar Pradesh and three Regional Raw Drug Repositories (RRDRs) as follows:I.	All India Institute of Ayurveda (AIIA), New Delhi for Trans-Ganga Plain Region (State Covered- Haryana, Chandigarh, Delhi, Punjab),II.	National Institute of Ayurveda (NIA), Jaipur for Western Dry Region (State Covered- Gujarat and Rajasthan) andIII.	National Institute of Siddha, Chennai for Southern Plateau Region (State Covered- Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Andaman, Puddcherry, Tamil Nadu, Telangana). The above RDRs would play a significant role in collection, documentation, and authentication of raw drugs of Medicinal Plants collected largely from the respective agro-climatic region. The key objectives of establishing these repositories are :-a.	Act as accredited reference repository for authenticated raw drugs (plants, animals and mineral/metal origin) of AYUSH system.b.	Act as model centre for availability of certified ‘Reference Standards’ of drugs for quality assessment.  c.	Act as centre for quality certification of raw drugs.d.	Act as centre for market surveillance and monitoring with respect to availability of raw material in trade.e.	Act as centre for collection, compilation and documentation including digitization of information on raw drugs used in AYUSH system.  f.	Act as model centre to spread information on training and create general awareness related to quality raw drugs.(c):	Yes, National Medicinal Plants Board (NMPB), Ministry of AYUSH under its central sector scheme supported training programmes, workshops, seminars for cultivation / production, processing and research activities of medicinal plants in project mode in different states for Conservation of Medicinal Plants and infrastructure Development in the country. During last three years, a total of Rs. 1109.996 Lakh released to support NRDR and RRDRs projects. Details are at Annexure-I.	Financial assistance provided to different States under Central Sector Scheme for Conservation of Medicinal Plants and infrastructure development in the country during the last 3 Years is at Annexure-II.
