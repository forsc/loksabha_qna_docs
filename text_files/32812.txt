                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

220






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.02.2022






Allocation of Baardana




Vijay Baghel













                                                    Arun Sao
                                                








                                                    Sunil Kumar Soni
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the procedure of allocation of ‘Baardana’ (Jute tags) to the States adopted by Jute Commissioner, Kolkata; (b) the quantity of ‘Baardana’ demanded by the Government of Chhattisgarh for the year 2021-2022 and the quantity for which the money has been paid; and (c) the details of the quantity/number of Baardana allocated to various States including Chhattisgarh by the Union Government during the last five years; year and State-wise?






ANSWER




THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR TEXTILES  (SMT. DARSHANA JARDOSH)(a):	Generally six months prior to every crop season (Rabi & Kharif), different State Government agencies including FCI plan their requirements of jute bags. After receipt of requirement of jute bags from all the states & FCI, a consolidated month-wise plan for requirement of jute bags is devised by Department of Food & Public Distribution (DF&PD), Govt. of India. Moreover, while preparing the month-wise schedule, the manufacturing capacity of the jute industry is also taken into consideration by the DF&PD. Thereafter, the plan document is circulated to all concerned including the O/o Jute Commissioner. Based on such plan and after receipt of indents and requisite funds from the State indenting agencies, Production Control cum Supply Orders are issued by O/o Jute Commissioner to jute mills covering the indented quantity.(b):	For the year 2021-22(April/March), a total quantity of 2,14,194 bales has been demanded by Government of Chhattisgarh.
