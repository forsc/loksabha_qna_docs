                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2430






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.08.2021






Chain Snatching




Parvesh Sahib Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is taking steps to stop incidents of chain snatching faced by women in the territory of Delhi, if so details thereof;(b) the number of chain snatching incidents reported during the last two years in Delhi and conviction ratio in those cases; and(c) the steps taken/being taken by Delhi Police to ensure safety and security of women in Delhi?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a): Delhi Police has taken various measures to curb chain snatching cases which inter-alia include, dynamic identification of crime-prone areas and deployment of police resources including pickets, foot patrolling, PCR Vans and Emergency Response Vehicles (ERVs) to enhance visibility and prevent crime; identification, surveillance and arrest of active criminals;  integrated patrolling by local police, PCR staff and Traffic police; anti- snatching teams in all districts to develop  intelligence, identify and arrest -2-L.S.US.Q. NO.2430 FOR 03.08.2021street crime offenders; gathering of macro-intelligence against criminal gangs; and public participation through schemes like ‘Eyes and Ears’ Scheme.(b): Details of chain snatching cases reported during the last two years are as under:Year	No. of cases reported	No. of persons arrested2019	1604	10412020	1184	859(Source: Delhi Police)(c)  Delhi Police has instituted a number of measures to prevent and expeditiously detect crimes against women, of which the major ones include integration of 112 India Mobile App with panic button in mobile phones; making registration in the ‘Himmat Plus’ app simple and bilingual; stationing of all women PCR vans in vulnerable areas; exclusive women help desks in Police Stations; dedicated women helpline number; regular liaison with civic agencies for lighting of dark stretches; constitution of Police Station-level women safety committees; and imparting self-defence training to women/girls.  ******
