                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2135






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2021






Aspirational Districts Programme




Chandra Prakash Choudhary














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether his Ministry has been implementing Aspirational Districts Programme for improving healthcare facilities in the targeted districts;(b)	if so, the details thereof including achievements under the programme so far, district-wise especially in Jharkhand;(c)	the details of the funds allocated for healthcare facilities under the Programme so far, district-wise in Jharkhand;(d)	whether health and nutrition constitute major weightage in selection of Aspirational Districts in the country and if so, the details thereof;(e)	whether these districts in Jharkhand are lagging behind from other parts of the country in terms of major health indicators and if so, the reaction of the Government thereto; and(f)	the remedial steps being taken/ proposed to be taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a)    NITI Aayog has been implementing the Aspirational District (AD) Programme since it was launched on January 5, 2018 for improving socio-economic indicators in the selected backward districts. Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW) is supporting all States/UTs in implementation of various schemes/initiatives for improving healthcare facilities under National Health Mission (NHM) based on the Annual Program Implementation Plan (APIP) submitted by States/ UTs.  (b)   ADs in general have shown improvement with respect to the Health and Nutrition indicators. While most of the districts have improved, some of the districts have shown very rapid progress. Districts wise details of achievements in Health and Nutrition sector for the State of Jharkhand is attached at Annexure-1  (c)    As the main strategy of the Aspirational District program is based on convergence of existing schemes which have their own funding arrangement. However, in order to foster competitive spirit and for addressing the critical gaps, additional allocation via challenge route is provided by NITI Aayog. Every month, districts are assessed on basis of monthly progress and the best performing Districts in overall terms and in each of the five sectors are identified. The first and second rankers in overall terms are awarded Rs. 10 crores and Rs. 5 crores respectively. The first ranker from each of the five sectors taken up in ADs including Health & Nutrition sector is awarded Rs. 3 crores each. The details of fund sanctioned to the Aspirational Districts of Jharkhand for the Health & Nutrition Sector is given at Annexure-II (d)  Yes, Health and Nutrition has been assigned major weightage of 30% in selection of ADs. The details are given in Annexure-III(e) & (f) Latest Health & Nutrition Sector Scores (Oct 2021) of 112 Aspirational Districts as per the Champions of Change Dashboard is given at Annexure-IV. The progress of the Aspirational Districts on the Key Performance Indicators are regularly monitored and the districts are ranked on monthly basis in Champions of Change Dashboard by NITI Aayog. In order to provide focused attention for overall development of these ADs, NITI Aayog has nominated senior officers of the rank of Joint Secretary/Additional Secretary as Central Prabhari Officers for each of the 112 districts for guidance and monitoring of the Aspirational District Program.  MoHFW has developed Operational guidelines and Supportive Supervision checklists to monitor and provide technical assistance for improving the Health interventions across the ADs. Special provisions for AD program include extra financial resources for ADs, relaxation of norms for hiring of manpower, flexibility to hire specialists with top up allowances and priority sanctions of infrastructure development in ADs under National Health Mission and other health system strengthening efforts.
