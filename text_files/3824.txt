                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3590






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Dropout Rate in School




Kunwar Danish Ali














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether it is a fact that the dropout rate in Government Schools in Uttar Pradesh is veryhigh especially in Amroha;(b) if so, the reasons therefor;(c) the amount of budget allocated/ released in the last five years for the betterment ofGovernment schools in Uttar Pradesh and the amount spent so far;(d) whether the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan and School Chalo Abhiyan could not decrease therate of dropout problem in Uttar Pradesh;(e) if so, the details thereof; and(f) the steps being taken by the Government to eradicate the dropout problem in UttarPradesh?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) & (b) Unified District Information System for Education (U-DISE) collects informationfrom all the schools (government, aided and private) annually on various educational indicators,and is used to calculate including the annual average dropout rate. As per U-DISE, the annualaverage dropout rate at elementary level in Uttar Pradesh has reduced from 7.7% in 2013-14 to7.24% in 2017-18. Similarly, the annual average dropout rate at elementary level in Amrohadistrict of Uttar Pradesh has reduced from 9.9% in 2013-14 to 8.6% in 2017-18. A studyentitled “National Sample Survey to Assess dropout rate at the elementary level in 21 States”was commissioned by the Government of India. According to the study, the reasons fordropouts are economic issues, children staying for household work, lack of interests in studiesand migration of families etc.(c) The details of Central Share released and expenditure incurred under Sarva ShikshaAbhiyan (SSA) during 2014-15 to 2017-18 and under Samagra Shiksha during the year 2018-19for Elementary Education to Uttar Pradesh is given as under:<pre>(Rs. in lakh)S.No. FinancialYear Scheme Central share released Expenditure*1 2014-15SSA449867.53 780113.362 2015-16 505434.30 1205725.473 2016-17 505433.99 1458836.034 2017-18 424980.68 645175.275 2018-19Samagra Shiksha(ElementaryComponent)447268.82 652974.17</pre>*Expenditure against receipts from Central release, Stateshare release, and miscellaneous receipts, if any.(d) to (f): The Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA) operational from 2000-2001 till 2017-18, hassignificantly contributed towards the government’s efforts to provide access to education in thecountry, including Uttar Pradesh. The programme was a centrally sponsored scheme of theGovernment of India and was implemented in partnership with the respective State Governmentand Union Territory Administration for universalizing elementary education across the country.Its overall goals included universal access and retention, bridging of gender and social categorygaps in education and enhancement of learning levels of children. The programme, since itsinception, was conceived as additional support to the States and UTs. The SSA provided for avariety of interventions, inter-alia to enhance retention and reduce dropout rates.Under Samagra Shiksha, financial assistance is provided to States and UTs forundertaking various activities to reduce dropout and increase the enrolment at elementary levelby opening/upgradation of new schools, construction of school buildings & additionalclassrooms, setting up of Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalayas (KGBV), setting up of residentialschools/hostels, free uniforms, free text books and undertaking enrolment & retention drives.Further, special training for age appropriate admission of out of school children and residentialas well as non-residential training for older children, Seasonal hostels/residential camps, SpecialTraining Centres at worksites, Transport/ Escort Facility are also supported to bring out ofschool children to the formal schooling system. Also, mid-day meal is provided to students atthe elementary level of education. Further, under the student oriented component for thechildren with special needs, financial assistance is provided for identification and assessment ofchildren with special needs, aids and appliances, Braille kits and books, appropriate teachinglearning material, transport and escort facility and stipend to girls students with disability etc.*****
