                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2061






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.03.2020






Insurance Policy for Farmers’ Cattle




Sanganna Amarappa Karadi














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is paying any compensation on account of the death of farmers’ sheep and goats, if so, the details thereof;(b) if so, the details of compensation ratio of the Union and/or State Government for the same; and(c) whether the Government is planning to start the insurance policy for the claim on death of farmers’ cattle, if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(DR. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a) to (c):	National Livestock Mission (NLM) has been implementing Risk Management and Insurance component under Sub-mission of Livestock Development in conjunction with State Governments with an objective of Management of risk and uncertainties by providing protection mechanism to the farmers & cattle rearers against any eventual loss of their animals due to death. Under the scheme, it covers the indigenous / crossbred milch animals, pack animals (Horses, Donkey, Mules, Camels, Ponies and Cattle/Buffalo Male) and Other livestock (Goat, Sheep, Pig, Rabbit, Yak and Mithun). National Livestock Mission (NLM) is a Demand-Driven scheme. Benefit of subsidy is restricted to 5 animals per beneficiary per household for all animals except sheep, goat, pig and rabbit. In case of sheep, goat, pig and rabbit, the benefit of subsidy is restricted based on ‘Cattle Unit’ and one cattle unit is equal to 10 animals i.e for sheep, goat, pig and rabbit. Pattern of Financial Assistance is annexed herewith.
