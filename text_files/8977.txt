                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2492






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2019






Poor Households in Tamil Nadu




S. Ramalingam














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the number of households found to be deprived and poor households in Tamil Nadu as per the Socio Economic Caste Census (SECC);(b)	the details of the number of houses allotted under the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana-Gramin (PMAY-G) within Tamil Nadu during the last three years and the current year;(c)	whether cases of eligible beneficiaries left out in SECC Data within Tamil Nadu has come to the knowledge of the Government;(d)	if so, the details thereof, district-wise and the reasons therefor; and(e)	the time by which these beneficiaries will be covered under this yojana?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a): According to the Socio Economic and Caste Census 2011 (SECC 2011), in Tamil Nadu, there are 38,549 households that are categorized as automatically included and 47,04,939 households as deprived. (b):	Selection of beneficiaries under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana-Gramin (PMAY-G) is based on housing deprivation parameters of SECC, 2011 subject to 13 point exclusion criteria followed by Gram Sabha verification. Households that are houseless or having zero, one or two room kutcha house (kutcha wall and kutcha roof) are provided financial assistance for construction of pucca house under PMAY-G. Under PMAY-G, a total of 5,27,552 houses has been allotted to the State of Tamil Nadu during the last three years and the current year. The district-wise details are given in Annexure-I.(c) to (e): Yes. In order to include eligible households, who could not be included in the Permanent Wait List (PWL) of PMAY-G, Ministry of Rural Development has developed Awaas+ App for States to upload details of such identified potential households. A separate list of households affected by Gaza Cyclone has also been uploaded by the State on Awaas+ since they did not find any mention in PWL or Awaas+ earlier. The district-wise details on the data uploaded by Tamil Nadu are given in Annexure-II. An Expert Committee in the Ministry of Rural Development is analyzing the data uploaded by the States in Awaas+.****
