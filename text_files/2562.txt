                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2420






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.07.2019






Establish Higher Institutes in J&K




Farooq Abdullah














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the safety of Kashmiri students has been a major concern in recent pastand if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government proposes to establish State level higher institutes ofprofessional studies in collaboration with private sector universities to ensure thatstudents find good learning opportunities within the State; and(c) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): As law & order is a State subject, the primary responsibility for the safety andsecurity of students, whichever part of the country they come from, is that of the StateGovernment. However, the Government of India has issued advisories from time to timeto ensure safety of students of Jammu & Kashmir who are studying in various parts ofthe country outside Jammu & Kashmir. As informed by Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA),MHA has issued Advisories dated 16.02.2019 and 22.02.2019 to the Chief Secretariesand Director Generals of Police of all States/Union Territories to take all necessarysteps to ensure safety and security of Kashmiri students. A list of State Nodal Officerswho may be contacted by J&K students/Kashmiris facing any difficulty was uploaded onthe website of MHA at https://mha.gov.in and States/UTs were advised to give widepublicity to the names of Nodal Officers.As informed by the University Grants Commission (UGC), UGC has issued aletter to Higher Educational Institutions (HEIs) dated 22.02.2019 regarding the safety ofstudents from Kashmir studying in various higher educational institutions requestingthem to ensure the safety and protection of the students on the campus. A copy of theletter is available on the UGC website at www.ugc.ac.in. All India Council for TechnicalEducation (AICTE) has also issued a similar letter to all Vice Chancellors/Principals ofHEIs.(b) & (c): The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan(RUSA), inter alia, provides central assistance to the State Governments for setting upof New Professional Colleges. Under RUSA, the State of Jammu & Kashmir has beenapproved Rs. 491.01 crores under various components including Rs. 26 crores each forestablishing 3 new professional colleges in the districts of Kathua, Ganderbal andPoonch.****
