                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2777






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.08.2021






Loss of Income in Toll Collection




Shashi Tharoor














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  the  estimated  loss  of  income  in  toll  collection since the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic including the period  when  toll  collection  was  discontinued,  month-wise;(b) whether the Government has taken any measures to  reduce/offset  the  said  losses;(c)  the  details  of  toll  collection  during  the  current financial  year  2020-21  till  date  vis-a-vis the  previous financial year 2019-20; and(d) the number of days on which the toll plazas on various  NHs  were  unable  to  function  due  to  protests during  the said  period along with  the estimated  loss  of income  as  a  result  thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) An estimated amount of Rs 3512.62 Crore in Financial Year (FY) 2020-21, is the approximate loss of revenue in fee collection at fee plazas of National Highways Authority of India due restrictions of COVID-19.(b) To improve transparency and efficiency in fee collection, Government has declared all lanes of fee plazas on National Highways as “FASTag lane of the fee plaza” w.e.f. midnight of 15th/16th February 2021. (c) The approximate user fee collections in FY 2019-20 and 2020-21 are Rs. 27682.89 Crore and Rs. 28548.05 Crore respectively.(d) Due to sustained protest by farmers in the States of Punjab, Haryana and Rajasthan, 58 fee plazas were not operational for 12 days to a maximum of 182 days in FY 2020-21 resulting in an estimated loss of Rs 814.13 Crore. 	Sporadic incidents of protest were observed at few plazas such as Thrivallam fee plaza in the state of Kerala and Padmanavpur & Sukhupada fee plazas in the state of Odisha.*****
