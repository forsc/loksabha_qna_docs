                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3535






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Plan for Tribal Dominated Areas




Vijay Baghel














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to make special provision in the next plan for the tribal dominated areas in the State of Chhattisgarh; and (b) if so, the details thereof including the proposed increase in the annual plan?






ANSWER




                                                         (a)& (b):	Ministry of Tribal Affairs (MoTA) has identified 19 priority Districts where tribal population is more than 25% including LWE affected Districts to focus on overall development (Annexure 1). NITI Aayog has also included the following 10 tribal dominated Districts of Chhattisgarh in the list of Aspirational Districts for spearheading the development process in these areas:No. of Districts	Name of Districts10	Bastar, Bijapur, Dantewada, Kanker, Kondagaon, Korba, Mahasamund, Narayanpur, Rajnandgaon, SukmaBesides, Ministry of Home Affairs has put in place a National Policy and Action Plan to address Left Wing Extremism (LWE) problems that envisages a multi-pronged strategy involving security related measures, developmental interventions, ensuring rights & entitlements of local communities etc. Details of LWE affected Districts of Chhattisgarh including tribal dominated Districts are as under:No. of Districts	Name of Districts14	Balod, Balrampur, Bastar, Bijapur, Dantewada, Dhamtari, Gariyaband, Kanker, Kondagaon, Mahasamund, Narayanpur, Rajnandgaon, Sukma, KabirdhamFunds earmarkedby the Central Government for development across the country including that of Chhattisgarh under various schemes Central Ministries including MoTA for the year 2019-20 have increased to Rs. 51283.53 crore from Rs. 37533.14 crore during 2018-19, which is about 37% more than the last financial year. Government has adopted multi-pronged strategy for overall development of tribal people and tribal dominated areas across the country including Chhattisgarh which includes the following:(i)  	As per the extant provisions, States including Chhattisgarh has to earmarked funds for tribal development from the total plan outlays of the State not less than the population proportion of STs in State as per 2011 Census and in tune with problem share of the ST population.(ii) 	Central Ministries earmark funds for tribal development as per the prescribed percentage out of their total scheme allocation. Statement showing provision of funds for overall tribal development catering to Education, Health, Agriculture, Livelihood, Housing, Drinking water & Sanitation, Employment Generation, Skill Development, Women and Child Development etc. during 2017-18 and 2018-19 are as below:	(Rs. in Crore)TSP component	2017-18	2018-19Central Ministries / Departments 	1805.31	1688.33State Component	20237.22	18950.15Total:	22042.5320638.48(iii)	Funds released under the Special Central Assistance (SCA) for the most LWE affected districts in Chhattisgarh by Ministry of Home Affairs during last two years is as under:(figures in ? crore)State	2017-18	2018-19	Total	Districts	Funds released	Districts	Funds Released (as on30.01.19)	Chhattisgarh	08.00	40.00	08.00	160.00	200.00(iv)	Ministry of tribal Affairs (MoTA) under its scheme under ‘Special Central Assistance to Tribal Sub-Scheme (SCA to TSS)’ and Grants under Article 275(1) of the Constitution provides funds to the State Governments including Chhattisgarh. Share of allocation to the States under these scheme / Grants  is determined in the proportion of (a) 2/3rd based on tribal population and (b) 1/3rd based on tribal dominated areas. (iv)	MoTA is running a flagship scheme of ‘Eklavya Model Residential School (EMRSs)’ wherein it is intended to set up 33 EMRSs by 2022 in tribal dominated Blocks where actual tribal population of 20000 constituting at least 50% of the total population in the Block. List as at Annexure 2. Besides 42 EMRSs has already been sanctioned to the State of Chhattisgarh. Status of existing EMRSs in the State of Chhattisgarh is as under:	Sanctioned 	FunctionalEklavya Model Residential Schools (EMRS)	42	25(v) 	Tribal Cooperative Marketing Development Federation of India (TRIFED) functioning under the administrative control of MoTA envisages to set up Vandhan Kendras in tribal dominated areas including those of Chhattisgarh to primarily cater to the training and development of value chain of Minor Forest Produce.  (vi) Under Pre-matric and Post Matric Scholarship schemes of MoTA, funds are given to the beneficiaries based on demand through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) mode.(vii) Under the provisions of Forest Rights Act, 2006, MoTA recognizes and vests the forest rights and occupation in forest land in forest dwelling Scheduled Tribes and other traditional forest dwellers who have been residing in such forests for generations but whose right could not be recorded. As per the information received from State Government of Chhattisgarh upto 31.03.2019, under FRA 8,90,240 claims have been filed and 4,23,218 titles have been distributed to the extent of 28,81,246.84 acres of forest land.(viii)	Details of Funds provided by MoTA under its various schemes during 2017-18 and 2019-20 are as under:(Rs. in Lakh)S.No.	Name of the Scheme	Fund Released		2017-18	2018-191	Development of Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs)	1089.50 	1051.50 2	Grants under proviso to Article 275(1) of the Constitution	10964.49 	11352.92 3	Minimum Support Price for Minor Forest Produce(MSP for MFP)	89.41 	197.31 4	Post-Matric Scholarship	3811.26 	4609.57 5	Pre-Matric Scholarship	1805.30 	4755.63 6	Special Central Assistance to Tribal Sub- Schemes	14327.57 	10342.65 7	Support to Tribal Research Institutes	168.73 	504.49 	Total:	32256.2632814.07(ix)	Funds allocated so far under various schemes of MoTA for tribal developmentin Chhattisgarh for the year 2019-20 is as below:(Rs. in lakh)S.No.	Name of the Scheme	Allocation of funds for Chhattisgarh1	Development of Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs)	10.102	Grants under proviso to Article 275(1) of the Constitution	13501.626	Special Central Assistance to Tribal Sub- Schemes	10257.61	Total:	23769.33(x)	In the Union Budget 2019-20, various measures have been envisaged by the Government to cater to different sectors of development, which includes benefits for tribal people and tribal dominated areas as at Annexure 3. ***** 	Annexure 1Annexure 1 referred to in reply to part(a) & (b) of the Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No. 3535 for answer on 15.7.2019List of Priority Districts in Chhattisgarh identified by Ministry of Tribal AffairsS.No.	Name of District	Population (in Lakh)	% ST population in the District 1.
