                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4152






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






ASPIRE Scheme




Kumbakudi Sudhakaran














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government under A Scheme for Promoting Innovation, Rural Industry and Entrepreneurship (ASPIRE) scheme proposes for setting up of 80 livelihood business incubators and 20 technology business incubators with the aim of producing 75000 skilled entrepreneurs in agro-rural industries and if so, the details thereof; and(b) 	the number of such initiatives commenced by the Government and the progress made so far under the scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NITIN GADKARI)(a) & (b):	Yes, Sir. A Scheme for Promoting Innovation, Rural Industry and Entrepreneurship (ASPIRE) has been launched by the Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME) since 18.03.2015 with an aim to set up  Livelihood Business Incubators (LBI) and  Technology Business Incubators (TBI) to enhance entrepreneurship and to promote innovation in rural industry.A statement showing the details of the 75 LBIs and 16 TBIs that have been approved since 2015-16 is placed at Annexure. 33697 persons have taken admission in different LBIs till 31.12.2019, of which 29767 persons have passed out. Further, 5351 persons have set up their own enterprises and 4169 persons have gained employment.
