                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3370






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.07.2019






National Bamboo Mission




Badruddin Ajmal














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware about failure of National Bamboo Mission with regard to conservation of forest reserves in Assam;(b) if so, the details of diminishing rate of forest reserves per year for last three years; (c) whether the Government has proposed any Central Team to enquire into such decline of forest reserve and if so, the details thereof;(d) whether the Government has received any proposal from the State Government of Assam for conservation of forest reserves; and (e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO) (a)	The restructured National Bamboo Mission, launched in April 2018, is implemented only on non-forest Government land and private farmers’ fields. Assam is one of the States where this scheme is being implemented. However, prior to this, a total area of 17591 hectares in forest areas in the State of Assam was covered under the Mission.(b)  & (c) As  per  the  latest  India State of Forest Report (ISFR) -2017 published by Forest Survey of India, Dehradun, the total forest cover of Assam State is 21,105 square kilometre which is 35.83% of total geographical area of the State. The forest cover of Assam has increased by 567 square kilometre as compared to that of ISFR-2015. (d) & (e)   Yes,  the  Government  of  Assam  from  time to  time has  been  submitting  proposals under various schemes for protection and conservation of forests and protected areas in Assam such as Project Tiger, Project Elephant, Forest Fire Prevention and Management Scheme, National Afforestation Programme, Integrated Development of Wildlife Habitat Programme, Assam Project on Forest and Biodiversity Conservation (Externally Aided), CAMPA etc.***********
