                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2590






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

14.12.2021






Promotion of Camel Milk Production




Sangamlal Kadedin Gupta













                                                    P.P. Chaudhary
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware of the overall decline of population of camel over the past few years; (b) if so, the details of decline in number of camels during the last two years, district-wise;(c) the details of steps taken or proposed to protect the interests of camel breeders;(d) whether the Government is planning to increase production and promotion of Camel milk to benefit the breeders; and(e) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a): There is an overall decline of population of camel as per the last two Livestock Censuses conducted in 2012 and 2019. The total camel population in the country has declined from 400 thousand number to 252 thousand number in 20th Livestock Census (2019)compared with 19th Livestock Census (2012). 	The camel population at all-India level during last two Livestock Censuses along with difference over the previous Livestock Census is as under:Description	19th Livestock Census (2012)	20th Livestock Census (2019)Camel population (in thousand number)	400	252Difference (in thousand number)	-117	-148(b): The data on camel population is being collected by the Ministry at every five-year interval under Livestock Census. The district-wise number of camels along with difference in number during last two Livestock Censuses for the districts having camel population either in 20th Livestock Census or in 19th Livestock Census is placed at Annexure-I.(c) to (e): There is Livestock Insurance Scheme under NLM division which include camels with other animals.The Central Government has established National Research Centre on Camel in 1984 under Indian Council of Agricultural Research in Bikaner, Rajasthan to promote research and development on camels and its research will help further to increase production and promotion of Camel milk to benefit the breeders.
