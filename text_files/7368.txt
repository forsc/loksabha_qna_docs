                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

944






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.11.2019






Land Reserved for Forests




Devusinh Jesingbhai Chauhan














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total area of land reserved for forests in Gujarat, district-wise; (b)	the number of people illegally residing on the forest land for several years, district-wise; (c)	whether the Government is formulating/has formulated any policy to allot pattas to these people for construction of houses and if so, the details thereof; (d)	 if not, whether the Government proposes to evict these people from the forest land; and(e)	 if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	Forest Survey of India, Dehradun, an organization under the Ministry carries out the assessment of forest cover of the country biennially and the findings are published in India State of Forest Report (ISFR). As per the latest report i.e. ISFR- 2017, the total recorded forest area in Gujarat State is 21859.22 square kilometre. The district-wise break up of forest area is given in Annexure-I.  (b)	As per the information received from the State of Gujarat, the district-wise statement of individuals who were claimants of forest land is given in Annexure II.  (c)	As per information received from the State of Gujarat, 88226 claimants have been given titles, that include the claims for house construction. Separate data for claims for construction of houses is not maintained by the State Government.(d) & (e) The Scheduled Tribes and other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act 2006, provides a framework for recording the forest rights so vested and the nature of evidence required for such recognition and vesting in respect of forest land in respect of member or members of a forest dwelling Scheduled Tribe or other traditional forest dwellers who have been residing in such forests for generations but whose rights could not be recorded. As per the information from the State of Gujarat, such cases of allowing rights/allotting pattas are considered after scrutiny under the said Act.****
