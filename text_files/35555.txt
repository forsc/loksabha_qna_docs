                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2822






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.03.2022






Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalayas




Ratansinh Magansinh Rathod













                                                    Amol Ramsing Kolhe
                                                








                                                    Sunil Dattatray Tatkare
                                                








                                                    Chandan Singh
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Ajay Nishad
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                








                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is providing residential schools under Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalayas (KGBVs) for the girl students from Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribes/Other Backward Castes, Minority Communities and Below Poverty Line (BPL) families in educationally backward blocks;(b) if so, the number of students provided such educational facilities during the last five years and the current year State/UT-wise;(c) the norms/criteria adopted for opening of KGBVs in the country along with the objectives for setting up such Vidyalayas;(d) the number of KGBVs functioning along with number of teachers in these Vidyalayas at present, State/UT-wise;(e) whether the female literacy rate in rural areas was below national average and if so, whether the KGBVs has been able to improve the rural literacy rate in educationally backward blocks; and(f) whether the Government is considering to open more KGBVs in the country, particularly in all the Educationally Backward Blocks of the country so as to improve female literacy rate and if so, details thereof State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                         MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EDUCATION(SMT. ANNPURNA DEVI)(a) & (b) : Yes sir. Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalaya (KGBV) is a provision under Samagra Shiksha, a flagship scheme of Department of School Education and Literacy, Ministry of Education. KGBVs are residential schools from class VI to XII for girls belonging to disadvantaged groups such as SC, ST, OBC, Minority and Below Poverty Line (BPL). As on 10.03.2022, a total of 5627 KGBVs have been sanctioned in the country. Out of it, 5018 KGBVs are operational with the enrolment of 665130 girls. Category-wise enrolment of girls in KGBVs is as under:<pre>Total KGBVs Functional	Enrollment	SC Girls	ST Girls	OBC Girls	Muslim Girls	BPL Girls	Total Girls5018	183091	172837	236352	26438	46412	665130                                         (Source : Prabandh)</pre>State/UT-wise list of KGBVs with the number of girls enrolled during the last five years and in the current year is at Annexure-I. (c) :KGBVs are setup in Educationally Backward Blocks (EBBs) of the country where    female rural literacy rate is below  the  national  average i.e. 46.13% as per Census 2001 with the objective to ensure access and quality education to girls belongs to disadvantaged groups and to reduce gender gaps at all levels of school education.Initially, the  EBBs  were  defined as  blocks  where  rural  female  literacy rate  is  below  the  national  average i.e. 46.13%  as  per  Census  2001  and Gender  Gap  in  Literacy  being  above the  national  average  of  21.59%.  Both these criteria had been earmarked by the Registrar General of India (RGI). Further, the definition of EBBs has been revised in 2010 to include blocks where female rural literacy rate is below the national average i.e. 46.13% as per Census 2001.(d) :State/UT-wise list of functioning KGBVs along with number of teachers as on 10.03.2022 is at Annexure-II.(e) :The female rural literacy rate has increased from  46.13% as per Census 2001 to 57.93% as per Census 2011. (f) :Presently, all the EBBs are covered with KGBVs. *****
