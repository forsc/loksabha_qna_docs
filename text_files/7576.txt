                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1214






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.11.2019






Samagra Shiksha Scheme (SSA)




Alok Kumar Suman














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether it is fact that the Government has upgraded Upper Primary School to CompositeSchools under the Samagra Shiksha Scheme (SSA) in district Gopalganj of Bihar;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the SSA is likely to be helpful in strengthening the infrastructure of schools indistrict Gopalganj of Bihar; and(d) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a) to (d): Under the erstwhile scheme of Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan nowsubsumed under Samagra Shiksha, 25 schools have been approved for upgradation fromupper primary school to composite school. Details are at Annexure.The Department of School Education and Literacy has launched an Integrated Scheme forSchool Education – Samagra Shiksha w.e.f. 2018-19, which subsumes the three erstwhileCentrally Sponsored Schemes of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA), Rashtriya MadhyamikShiksha Abhiyan (RMSA) and Teacher Education (TE). The new integrated schemeenvisages school education as a continuum from pre-school to senior secondary level andaims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education. Under Samagra Shiksha, States andUTs are supported for strengthening/upgradation of existing government schools and forcreation and augmentation of infrastructure facilities based on proposals received fromrespective State/UT. The scheme also provides for annual maintenance and repair of existingschool building, toilets and other facilities to upkeep the infrastructure in good condition.****
