                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1952






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.07.2019






Common Service Centres




Janardan Singh Sigriwal














                        Will the Minister of





ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY be pleased to state:-(a)	the number of Common Service Centre (CSC) functioning at present in the country, State/UT-wise particularly in each district of Bihar;(b)	the details of work done by CSC in Bihar during each of the last three years;(c)	whether the Government has conducted any study regarding fulfilment of purpose for which the said scheme has been established;(d)	if so, the details and the finding thereof; and(e)	if not, the reason therefor ?






ANSWER




                                                        ):   As on 31 May, 2019, there are in total 3,65,918 functional CSCs across the Country while in the state of Bihar total 31,758 CSCs are functional. The details of State/ UT-wise functional CSCs are at Annexure–I and the District-wise details of functional CSCs of Bihar are at Annexure –II.(b): 	The details of work done by CSC in Bihar during each year of the last 3 years is at Annexure-III.(c), (d) and (e): Yes, Sir.  A mid-term impact assessment on CSC scheme has been conducted by Indian School of Business, Hyderabad in the year 2018-19. CSCs are network of various service delivery centre to deliver Government-to-Citizen (G2C) and Business-to-Citizen (B2C). Some of the finding are as: •	Central and State Government to encourage and promotion the growth of entrepreneurship, which would result in benefits and prospects of taking up entrepreneurship as a career option. •	Broadband internet access and connectivity to the last mile would enable greater success rates of both existing and aspiring VLEs who want to set up and lead CSC centres across the far-flung areas of the country. •	To provide training to the VLEs about business strategies, notably, marketing, finance, operations, and investments in human capital that facilitate identification of market opportunities and development of sustainable businesses around such opportunities. •	Greater investments in the digital literacy of the citizens would enable them to a wealth of information and
