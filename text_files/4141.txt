                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3691






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.07.2019






Credit Guarantee Enhancement Scheme for SCs




Jayadev Galla














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the aims and objectives of Credit Guarantee Enhancement Scheme for Scheduled Castes; (b) the performance of the scheme since it was launched in 2014; (c) the number of beneficiaries under the scheme in the country during the last four years, year, location and State-wise; (d) whether it is not true that there have been demands to increase credit guarantee cover under this scheme from a minimum of Rs. 15 lakh to Rs. 50 lakh; and (e) if so, the action taken by the Government thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a): The Department of Social Justice & Empowerment, Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment, Government of India, has sponsored the “Credit Enhancement Guarantee Scheme for Scheduled Castes” under its social sector initiatives. The objective of the Credit Enhancement Guarantee Scheme for Scheduled Castes (CEGSSC) is (i) to promote entrepreneurship amongst the Scheduled Castes, by providing Credit Enhancement Guarantee to Member Lending Institutions (MLIs), who in turn provide financial assistance to these entrepreneurs. (ii) to promote financial inclusion of SC entrepreneurs and to enhance direct and indirect employment for SC population.(b): The scheme was launched on 6th May, 2015 i.e. during financial year 2015-16.  Since Financial Year 2015-16, 25 Member Lending Institutions are registered under it. Upto the Financial Year 2018-19, loans aggregating to Rs. 39.18 crore have been sanctioned by some of the Member Lending Institutions against which the total guarantee cover of Rs.28.01 crore has been provided by IFCI.(c): There are 16 companies benefitted under the scheme during the last 4 years.  The details of companies benefited during the last four years, year-wise, location wise and State-wise are at Annexure-I.(d) & (e): Initially loans between Rs. 25 lakhs and Rs. 5 crore were covered under the original scheme. The scheme was revised to reduce the minimum cover to Rs. 15 lakhs as against the Rs. 25 lakhs as per the original scheme.****
