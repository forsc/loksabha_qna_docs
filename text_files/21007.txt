                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TRIBAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2087






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.03.2021






Number of Tribals in the Country




Mohanbhai Kalyanji Kundariya














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                     (a) the details of the tribal population (in crores) in the entire country at present, State-wise and UT-wise;(b) the number of schemes introduced by the Government for tribals during the last 7 years in addition to the schemes implemented earlier;(c) the details of beneficiaries of the schemes, State/ UT-wise;(d) whether the Government has digitized any tribal language; and(e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a): State/Union Territory wise population of Scheduled Tribes in India as per census, 2011 is given at Annexure-I.(b) & (c): Details of the schemes introduced by Ministry of Tribal Affairs during the last seven years are as given below: Government of India launched “Vanbandhu Kalyan Yojana (VKY)” during 2014-15 for holistic development of tribal people across the country. Since 2016-17, separate allocation of funds under VKY has been discontinued as the fund requirement under the VKY strategy was envisaged to be met out of the funds available under the various schemes of Central Government and State Government. The beneficiary details of VKY is not centrally maintained. VKY was launched in addition to earlier scheme of Special Central Assistance to Tribal Sub-Scheme (SCA to TSS).  During the year 2013-14, Scheme titled “Mechanism for Marketing of Minor Forest Produce (MFP) through Minimum Support Price (MSP) and Development of Value Chain for MFP” was introduced by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs to provide much needed safety net and support to people belonging to Scheduled Tribes and other traditional forest dwellers whose livelihood depends on collection and selling of MFP. Van Dhan Vikas Karyakram (VDVK) is primarily the training and development of value chain component under the scheme. Van Dhan Vikas Karyakram is an initiative targeting livelihood generation for tribal population by harnessing the wealth of forest i.e. Van Dhan. Introduced in the year 1997-98, Eklavya Model Residential School (EMRS) was a component under ‘Grants under Article 275(1) of the Constitution’. As per Budget announcement 2018-19, EMRS programme was revamped during the year 2018 and it was made a separate Central Sector Scheme. Further, as informed by Government of Andhra Pradesh the details of schemes introduced during the last seven years are given at Annexure – II. State/UT wise details of beneficiaries of the schemes/programmes of Ministry of Tribal Affairs are given at Annexure-III. (d) & (e) :  Ministry of Tribal Affairs provide the funds to Tribal Research Institutes (TRIs)  under the Scheme “Support to TRIs” for various activities including construction of museum, library, research works, seminar/workshop, publication of books, development of documentaries, organisation of tribal festivals including language primers.                Further, under the component “Financial Assistance for support to Centres of Excellence” of the scheme "Tribal Festival, Research, information and Mass Education", financial assistance is provided to reputed Institutions for carrying out research study programmes including documentation of tribal languages. In this regard, Ministry has sanctioned projects to Bhasha Research and Publication Centre during 2018-19 and 2019-20 which inter-alia covered activity of identification and documentation of threatened/dying languages. The organization has carried out documentation of Korku, Nihali, Kolami, Vadi, Halpati, Dungra Bhili, Dhavadi, Dhatti, Thali, Nahal and Seheriya languages. Government of Andhra Pradesh has informed that “Tribal Cultural Research and Training Mission (AP TRI)” with the financial support of Ministry of Tribal Affairs (MoTA), Government of India, New Delhi has developed Primers in 6 Tribal Languages such as Savara, Koda, Kuvi, Adivasi Oriya, Koya and Sugali for classes first and second initially at primary level in the academic year 2020-21 and for Classes third and fourth will be done in 2021-2022 as per the Government new curriculum and syllabus.These Primers are digitalized and will be placed in public domain by end of March, 2021. Government of Himachal Pradesh has informed that ''Kinnauri (pahari)'' of Scheduled district of Kinnaur has been digitized  and project on "Documentation of ''Kinnauri (pahari)'' dialect and development of Artificial Intelligence based translation system to translate text from English to ''Kinnauri (pahari)'' " is under process.Annexure-Ireferred to in reply to part (a) of Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No. 2087 for 08.03.2021 regarding ‘Number of Tribals in the Country’State / UT wise Scheduled Tribe populationS.
