                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1690






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






New Greenfield Airports




Raghu Ramakrishna Raju Kanumuru














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of CIVIL AVIATIONbe pleased to state:-(a) whether the Government has granted in-principle approval for setting up of new Greenfield Airports across the country;(b) if so, the details thereof along with the present status of these airports;(c) whether any such airport is being developed in the State of Andhra Pradesh and if so, the details thereof; and(d) the expenditure involved along with the manner in which it will be met in respect of each of the airports being set up in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) & (b): Government of India (GoI) has accorded ''in-principle'' approval for setting up of 21 Greenfield Airports across the country namely Mopa in Goa, Navi Mumbai, Sindhudurg and Shirdi in Maharashtra, Bijapur, Hassan, Kalaburagi and Shimoga in Karnataka, Dabra (Gwalior) in Madhya Pradesh, Kushinagar and Jewar (Noida) in Uttar Pradesh, Dholera and Hirasar in Gujarat, Karaikal in Puducherry, Dagadarthi, Bhogapuram and Orvakal (Kunoor) in Andhra Pradesh, Durgapur in West Bengal, Pakyong in Sikkim, Kannur in Kerala and Hollongi (Itanagar) in Arunachal Pradesh. So far, six Greenfield airports namely,  Shirdi in Maharashtra, Durgapur in West Bengal, Pakyong in Sikkim, Kannur in Kerala, Orvakal (Kunoor) in Andhra Pradesh and Kalaburagi in Karnataka have been operationalized.;(c): In-Principle approval has been granted in respect of three Greenfield airports viz. Bhogapuram in Vizianagaram District, Oravakal in Kurnool District and Dagadarthi Mendal in Nellore District of Andhra Pradesh. Out of this, Oravakal in Kurnool District have been operationalized in March, 2021.;(d): The responsibility of implementation of airport project including funding of the project rests with the concerned airport developer including Airports Authority of India and the respective State Government (in case the State Govt. is project proponent). Airports Authority of India has undertaken development of Hollongi (Arunachal Pradesh), Hirasar (Gujarat), Dholera (Gujarat) and Khushinagar (Uttar Pradesh) airports at estimated project costs of Rs. 646 Crore, Rs.1405 Crore, 1375 Crore (Phase I) and 448 Crore respectively. Estimated Project cost involved in respect of remaining eleven Greenfield airports is:- Mopa (Rs 3000 Cr.), Navi Mumbai (Rs 16,250 Cr.), Sindhudurg (Rs. 450 Cr.), Bijapur (Rs 150 Cr.), Hassan (Rs 592 Cr.), Shimoga (Rs 220 Cr.), Dabra (Rs 200 Cr.), Jewar (Rs 8,914 Cr.-Phase I), Karaikal (Rs 50 Cr.), Dagadarthi (Rs 293 Cr.) and Bhogapuram (Rs 2,500 Cr.).
