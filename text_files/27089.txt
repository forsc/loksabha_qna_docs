                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2828






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.08.2021






Growth of Indian Aviation Sector




Sudheer Gupta













                                                    Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik
                                                








                                                    Ravindra Shyamnarayan alias Ravi Kishan Shukla
                                                








                                                    Subrata Pathak
                                                








                                                    Shrirang Appa Barne
                                                








                                                    Chandra Sekhar Sahu
                                                








                                                    Ravindra Kushawaha
                                                








                                                    Bidyut Baran Mahato
                                                








                                                    Prataprao  Jadhav
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the growth rate of the Indian Aviation sector during each of the last five years;(b) the estimated number of direct employment in the aviation and aeronautical manufacturing sector in the country;(c) whether the aviation industry has been worst affected globally due to the COVID-19 pandemic and if so, the details in this regard;(d) the corrective steps taken by the Government in this regard; and(e) whether Airport Authority of India and Air India have been maintaining all Covid-19 protocols so far and if so, the details in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a)  The growth of Indian aviation sector in terms of aircraft movements, passenger handled and freight, for both domestic and international sectors, during the last five years is attached herewith as Annexure-1.(b)  The estimated number of directly employed personnel in the aviation and aeronautical sector is in excess of 2 lakh as on 31 Mar 2021.;(c)  Yes Sir. The aviation industry, both globally and in India has been adversely affected by COVID-19 pandemic due to the significant drop in air travel demand, stoppage of many domestic and international flights and Covid-related movement restrictions at several domestic and international locations.;The details of the decline in air traffic in India are as follows:i.  The number of domestic passengers has reduced from approximately 128 lakhs in January 2020 to approximately 77 lakhs in January 2021, registering a decline of approximately 40%.ii.  The number of domestic passengers reduced from approximately 124 lakhs in February 2020 to approximately 78 lacs in February 2021, i.e., registering a decline of approximately 37%. ;(d)  The measures taken by the Government to improve the civil aviation sector of the country include: -i.  Benefits under Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) 3.0 extended to civil aviation sector.ii.  Route rationalization done in Indian airspace in coordination with Indian Air Force for efficient airspace management, shorter routes and lower fuel consumption.iii.  GST on Maintenance, Repairs and Overhaul (MRO) in aviation has been reduced from 18% to 5% with full Input Tax Credit from 1 April 2020.iv.  Operation of Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) - UDAN (Ude Desh Ka Aam Nagrik) flights allowed without any capacity restrictions.v.  Exclusive air-links or ''Air Bubbles'' have been established with 28 countries.vi.  Private investments is being facilitated in existing and new airports through the PPP route.vii.  Indian carriers are being encouraged to increase their share in international air cargo traffic.viii.  Significant tax incentives and other benefits offered to aviation leasing companies located in GIFT Cityix.  Flying Training Organisations have been offered land at five AAI airports, namely Belagavi, Jalgaon, Kalaburgi, Khajuraho and Lilabari with zero airport royalty charges and rationalised land rentals.x.  AAI has come up with a business-friendly MRO policy with zero airport royalty charges and rationalised land rentals at eight AAI airports.;(e)  Yes, Sir.Air India has been maintaining Covid-19 protocols as advised by various authorities like Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW), Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA), Ministry of Civil Aviation (MoCA), Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) etc. and the State Governments. ;The key steps taken by Air India include the following:i. Guidelines have been issued for front line staff to maintain Covid-19 protocol in passenger handling at Airports.ii. Information regarding protocol for passengers for use of protective gear have been circulated.iii. General instructions and information for passenger process flow at airport under Covid-19 scenario have been issued for strict adherence.iv. Emphasis was given on contact-less passenger handling processes like 100% online check-in, submission of self declaration forms and self baggage drop etc.;Airports Authority of India (AAI) has been maintaining Covid -19 protocols at its airports. The key steps taken by AAI include the following:i. Thermal screening and RT-PCR testing of passengers.ii. Contact-less movement of passengers;iii. Hygiene and sanitization;iv. Following social distancing norms;v.  Display of ''Do''s and Dont''s'' and disposal of bio-hazard waste as per guidelines of MoHFW;
