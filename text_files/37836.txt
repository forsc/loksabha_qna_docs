                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4633






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

31.03.2022






Implementation of AMRUT




Hanuman Beniwal














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the detail of present status of the implementation of Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT), year and State-wise;(b)	the total State Annual Action Plan (SAAP) approved, completed and in-progress under AMRUT, State-wise; and(c)	the status of water, sewerage/sewer connections and Septage Management projects grounded under AMRUT, States and year-wise?






ANSWER




                                                                THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                           (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a) & (b):	Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) was launched on 25thJune 2015 in 500 selected cities across all the States and Union Territories (UTs) in the country. The Mission focuses on the development of basic urban infrastructure in the sectors of water supply; sewerage and septage management; storm water drainage; non-motorized urban transport and development of green spaces and parks. Mission also executed a set of 11 reforms to improve governance of Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) and Capacity Building activities for the ULBs.Under AMRUT, total approved plan size of all the States and UTs is ?77,640 crore, which includes committed Central Assistance of ?35,990 crore.  So far, States/UTs have taken up 5,970 projects worth ?82,411.14 crore, out of which 4,158 projects worth ?26,193.07 crore have been completed, 1,706 projects worth ?55,191.44 crore are under implementation and 106 projects worth ?1,026.63 crore are being grounded.Against the target to provide 139 lakh water tap connections to achieve universal coverage of water supply and to achieve 145 lakh sewer connections to enhance substantial sewerage network coverage in AMRUT cities, so far 126 lakh new water tap connections and 95 lakh new sewer connections have been provided under AMRUT and in convergence with other schemes.Under AMRUT, States /UTs have taken of construction of Sewage Treatment Plants (STPs) of capacity around 6,291 MLD (million litre per day), out of which STPs of around 2,021 MLD capacity have already been created and around 4,270 MLD of STPs are under progress. In addition, 55 Faecal Sludge Treatment Plants (FSTPs) have been taken up with total treatment capacity of around 2,630 MLD.Through projects under storm water drainage sector, around 3,770 water-logging points have been proposed to be eliminated through drainage projects of which around 2,300 points have been eliminated so far. Through projects under green spaces and parks sector, around 4,000-acre area has already been developed and around 1,400-acre area is under development.Reforms Implementation under AMRUT:(i)	Credit rating: Credit rating work for 485 cities have been awarded and completed in 470 cities. Out of 470 cities, 164 cities have received Investible Grade Rating (IGR), including 36 cities spread across 12 States/UTs with rating of A- and above.(ii)	Municipal Bonds: So far, 10 ULBs namely Ahmedabad, Amravati, Bhopal, Ghaziabad, Hyderabad, Indore, Pune, Surat, Lucknow and Visakhapatnam have raised a total of ?3,840 crore through issuance of Municipal Bonds for upgrading urban infrastructure.(iii)	Energy audit of water pumps: Energy audit has been completed in 397 cities (27 States/UTs). 11,939 pumps (26 States/UTs) identified for replacement and 895 pumps have been replaced.(iv)	Replacement of streetlights with LED lights: Field survey for replacement of streetlights with energy efficient LED lights has been completed in 440 cities (34 States/UTs). The estimated target for replacement of streetlights is 101 lakh, out of which 93 lakh streetlights have been replaced so far. This has resulted in estimated energy saving of 203.80 crore units (kWh) and reduction in CO2 emission by 16.30 lakh tonnes per year.(v)	Capacity building: Against the target to train 45,000 functionaries, 56,448 functionaries and elected representatives have already been trained so far.(vi)	Online Building Permission System (OBPS): OBPS has been implemented in 2,471 cities including 456 AMRUT cities. State/UT-wise total State Annual Action Plan (SAAPs) approved for entire Mission period and status of implementation of AMRUT projects against the approved SAAPs are given at ANNEXURE – I. (c):	State-wise status of implementation of water supply and sewerage and septage management projects as on date are given at ANNEXURE – II and ANNEXURE – III respectively.                                      ******
