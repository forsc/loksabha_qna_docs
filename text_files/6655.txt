                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

331






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.11.2019






Days of Employment under MGNREGA




Pritam Gopinath Munde














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the average days of wage employment provided per household has only been 50, instead of 100 days under Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) during the financial year 2018-19;(b)	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor;(c)	the names of the States which provided less number of days of employment under MGNREGA, State-wise;(d)	the steps taken by the Union Government to provide at least 100 days in a year; and(e)	whether the Government plans to launch a new project under which a stipend of up to Rs. 20,000 will be given to MGNREGA labourers who undergo training in skills such as masonry or carpentry and if so, the present status thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)&(b):	The Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (Mahatma Gandhi NREGA) is a demand driven wage employment programme. It provides livelihood security i.e., fall back option for livelihood for the rural households when no better employment opportunity is available. The employment provided to a household depends on the number of days of wage employment demanded by the household. As per Management Information System (MIS), the average days of wage employment provided per household under Mahatma Gandhi NREGA was 51 days during the financial year 2018-19.(c)&(d):	State/UT-wise details of average days of employment provided per household under Mahatma Gandhi NREGA during FY 2018-19 is given at Annexure. The Government is actively engaged with State Governments in establishing systems that ensure provision of work as per demand, transparency and accountability. To generate awareness about the provisions of the Scheme and to provide adequate employment opportunities to rural households under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS, all States/UTs have been requested for the following:(i) Initiate appropriate Information Education and Communication (IEC) campaigns       including wall paintings for wide dissemination of the   provisions of the Act.(ii) Expand scope and coverage of demand registration system to ensure that demand        for work under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS does not go unregistered.(iii) Prepare plans in a participatory mode and approve them in the Gram Sabha.(iv) Prepare realistic labour budget by the State. (v) Implementation of Software for Estimate Calculation Using Rural Rates for        Employment (SECURE)(vi) Organize ‘Rozgar Diwas’ periodically to capture latent demand under the        programme and to disseminate awareness about the provisions of the Act.(e):	No, Sir.
