                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3657






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






Dropout Rate in Government and Private Schools




Faizal P.P. Mohammed














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of students dropped out of Government and Private schools at the primary, secondary and higher levels between March 2020 to November 2021;(b) the number of children currently out of the education system; and(c) the number of Government and Private schools shut down in the country between March 2020 to November 2021?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EDUCATION(SMT. ANNPURNA DEVI)(a): The Department of School Education and Literacy (DoSEL), Ministry of Education has developed the Unified District Information System for Education Plus (UDISE+) system to record data on indicators of school education provided by all the States and Union Territories. As per Unified District Information System for Education Plus (UDISE+), dropout rates of students are computed by combining all types of school managements in a particular level of school education. The dropout rate for the year 2019-20, State-wise are at annexure.(b): DoSEL has also developed an online module for compiling the data of Out of School Children (OoSC) identified by each State/UT and their mapping with Special Training Centres (STC) on the PRABANDH Portal (http://samagrashiksha.in). The concerned State/UT validates the child wise information of the identified OoSC and STC uploaded by the concerned Block Resource Centre of the State for monitoring the progress of mainstreaming of OoSC.(c): The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009 provides for children’s access to elementary schools within the defined area or limits of neighbourhood. In pursuance to Section 6 of the RTE Act, all States have notified area or limits of their neighbourhood norms. Further, Section 8 of the RTE Act, 2009 also mandates that the appropriate government shall provide free and compulsory elementary education to every child and will ensure availability of a neighbourhood school.Education is in the Concurrent list of the Constitution and the opening and closing of schools are within the purview of respective State Government and UT Administration.******
