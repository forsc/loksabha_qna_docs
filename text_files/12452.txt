                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1269






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2020






Distance Education




V. Kalanidhi














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the number of courses being offered through distance education within thecountry including Universities;(b) whether the Government is planning to stop some of its course being offered through distanceeducation across the country;(c) if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor;(d) the time by which these are likely to be stopped;(e) whether this move has been opposed by some of the Universities running within the country;and(f) if so, the details thereof along with the action taken by the Government in this regard?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): University Grants Commission (UGC) is the regulatory authority to deal with the Open andDistance Learning in Higher education system of the country and accordingly, notified UGC(Open and Distance Learning) Regulations, 2017. During the current academic year 2019-20,UGC has approved 1592 programmes mainly in the areas of Art, Science, Commerce,Management and Computer Applications, etc., in 128 Higher Educational Institutions (HEIs) toimpart Open and Distance Education based on its various regulations on Open and DistanceLearning Education. All these details are already available on UGC website atwww.ugc.ac.in/deb.(b), (c) & (d): The UGC (Open and Distance Learning) Regulations, 2017 do not permit to run theprofessional programmes through ODL mode by Open Universities unless the same are permittedby the respective Statutory Councils in their respective fields. Accordingly, based on therecommendations of some of such Statutory Councils, UGC has stopped some programmes atundergraduate and post graduate level in the field of Agriculture, Horticulture, Hospitality &Hotel Administration and Catering & Hotel Management, etc. The recognition for such courseswithdrawn will be applicable from the next academic session with the instructions to the HEIs toprovide support such as faculty, requisite learning materials and on time examinations, etc, to thestudents already admitted in these programmes till the completion of their degrees.(e) & (f): UGC has received representations and appeals from some of the HEIs on the subjectmatter. However, the Commission heard their representations and disposed of the same as perUGC (Open and Distance Learning) Regulations, 2017.*****
