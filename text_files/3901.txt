                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3608






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






HPCL Bio-Fuels Limited




Sukanta Majumdar













                                                    Raja Amareshwara Naik
                                                








                                                    Khagen Murmu
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited (HPCL) through its subsidiary HPCL Biofuels Ltd. (HBL) operates Integrated Plants;(b) if so, whether the plants owe a huge amount as arrears to their customers during the current crushing season, if so, the details thereof along with the steps taken/being taken by the Government to pay outstanding dues within a stipulated time;(c) the details of production (sugar, ethanol and power) and profits made by HBL during the last three years, year-wise;(d) whether malpractices such as nepotism and black-marketing are reported in issuance of challans by the plant, if so, the details of review of functioning of the plants and remedial action taken thereon; and(e) whether the plant is emitting smoke causing pollution and posing public health hazard, if so, the details thereof and the action taken thereon along with the steps taken by the Government to review functioning of the plant?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF PETROLEUM & NATURAL GAS(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)******(a): Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited (HPCL), through its wholly owned subsidiary HPCL Biofuels Limited (HBL) is operating two integrated sugar, ethanol and cogeneration plants at Suguali and Lauriya, in East and West Champaran districts of Bihar.(b) As per information furnished by HBL, there is no outstanding payment towards cane procured upto 2017-18 crushing season.(c) The details of production (sugar, ethanol and power) and profit/loss made by HBL during the last three years, as furnished by HBL, are as under :ProductionFY 2016-17FY 2017-18FY 2018-19Sugar55,333 MT63,870 MT68,124 MTEthanol10,082 KL7,025 KL8,558 KLPower67,383 KW79,085 KW73,595 KWProfit/Loss ? 3038 lakh (loss) ? 7789 lakh (loss) ? 6766 lakh (loss)(d) HBL has reported that there are no instances of nepotism and black marketing. However, malpractices in issuance of challan as well as cane procurement from outside cane command area were reported in the crushing season 2017-18. Appropriate action is taken as per the procedure in respect of allegations/complaints received as well as established cases of malpractices/lapses. HBL has taken measures to make cane procurement more transparent and robust which include :(i) Capturing and verifying the exhaustive details of cane plantation by visiting the farmland through GPS enabled Hand Held Terminal Device.(ii) Adoption of new technologies like implementing GPS tagging of vehicles used for cane procurement.(e) In HBL, bagasse based co-generation plant of 20 MW capacity is the only source of generation of smoke. However, it is fitted with Electro Static Precipitator, a state of art pollution control device, to trap the urburnt fuel in the smoke. Moreover, HBL has 72 meters high chimney for diluting and disposing of the smoke.****
