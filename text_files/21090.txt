                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

206






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.03.2021






Development Works under MGNREGS




 Bhagirath Chaudhary













                                                    Sumedhanand Saraswati
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of kutcha and pucca development works carried out under the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS) along with the costs thereof during the last three years, State-wise and year-wise and the district-wise details in respect of Rajasthan, including the separate cost of the said works and the places where such works have been carried out;(b) 	whether with a view to providing relief to the farmers, the Government has been getting works relating to medbandi/hedging and construction of pits done in the fields by amending the said scheme;(c) 	if so, the details thereof along with the number of beneficiaries in Rajasthan, district-wise;(d) 	whether the Government proposes to use machinery also for the said works by amending the said scheme; and(e) 	if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)	 to (e)  A statement is laid on the Table of the House. Statement in reply to Parts (a) to (e) of Lok Sabha Starred Question No. 206 for answer on 09.03.2021(a)  There are 262 permissible works under Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (Mahatma Gandhi NREGS) of this, 182 works are related to Natural Resource Management out of which 85 are water related works and out of total works, 164 works are related to agriculture and allied activities. These works are derived from Schedule I of the Act. In the Schedule I, the works have been classified under major categories as under;As per Schedule I Section 4(3), Para 4(1),I.	Category: A:  Public works relating to Natural Resource ManagementII.	Category: B:  Community assets or Individual assets for vulnerable sections (only for households in Paragraph 5) III.	Category: C:  Common Infrastructure (Including for NRLM) Compliant Self Help GroupsIV.	Category: D:  Rural InfrastructureIn the Management information System (MIS) of Mahatma Gandhi NREGS, provision has been made to capture the details of works in 17 categories. In this regard, the detail is at Annexure-I.States/UTs-wise details of ongoing works, completed works and expenditure on works under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS during last three years are at Annexure-II.  The district-wise ongoing works, completed works and expenditure on its work under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS in the State of Rajasthan during last three years are at Annexure-III. (b) & (c) As per Schedule I of the Mahatma Gandhi NREGA, contour trenches and contour bunds are permissible activity under Mahatma Gandhi NREGA. The work is permissible for community as well as individual beneficiaries. The beneficiaries covered under Contour bund/trench work in the State of Rajasthan are 6888 numbers. The details of individual works related to contour bunding and contour trenches taken up in the States of Rajasthan under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS are at Annexure-IV(d) & (e) Para 22 of Schedule-1, Mahatma Gandhi NREGA, lays down that “As far as practicable, works executed by the programme implementing agencies shall be performed by using manual labour and no labour displacing machine shall be used.” However there may be activities in executing works which cannot be carried out by manual labour, where use of machine may become essential for maintaining the quality and durability of work to be completed under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS. A suggested list of machines which can be used under Mahatma Gandhi NREGA, are at Annexure-V. *****Annexure-I referred to in reply to part (a) of Lok Sabha Starred Question No.206 dated 09.03.2021
