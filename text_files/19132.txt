                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

595






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2021






Scarcity of Potable Water




Rajeshbhai Naranbhai Chudasama














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware of scarcity of potable water in most of the villages in the country covered under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP); (b) if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government thereto; (c) whether the Government proposes to review the NRDWP with respect to their outcome in terms of physical and financial targets during the last three years, State-wise; and (d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) & (b) Under erstwhile National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP), the provision of water supply was monitored in terms of habitations having provision of minimum 40 litre per capita per day (lpcd) of potable drinking water with sources at a reasonable distance. As reported by States/ UTs, as on 01.02.2021, 79.20% rural habitations having 78.12% population have provision of minimum 40 litre per capita per day (lpcd) of potable drinking water and 17.93% rural habitations having 19.33% population have service level of less than 40 lpcd potable water, whereas 2.88% rural habitations having 2.54% population with water sources having quality issues.(c) & (d) Under the erstwhile NRDWP, the physical progress was captured in terms of coverage of habitations with provision potable water. Since August, 2019 Jal Jeevan Mission – Har Ghar Jal is under implementation in partnership with States, to provide every rural household with tap water supply by 2024. Now, physical outcome since 2019-20 is being captured in terms of households provided with tap water connections. Year-wise physical achievement in the last three years is at Annex–I and financial progress is at Annex – II.****
