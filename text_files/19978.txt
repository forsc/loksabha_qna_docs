                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1451






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2021






Internet Services in Villages




Kinjarapu Ram Mohan Naidu














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 the number of villages which are not connected with internet services in the country, especially in Andhra Pradesh;(b) 	the time by which each and every village in the country is expected to get internet connectivity;(c) 	the funds allocated/released/utilized and financial assistance given by the Government during the last three years for the said purpose, State/UT-wise;(d) 	the steps taken by the Government to increase digital literacy in rural areas; and(e) 	the funds allocated and the financial assistance given by the Government during the last three years to increase digital literacy?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS, EDUCATION AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a)	Internet is currently accessed through mobile wireless 3G/4G technologies and fixed wireline broadband in the country.As per data collected from the Telecom Service Providers (TSPs) in 2020, it is estimated that 37,439 inhabited villages (as per Census 2011) in the country including 2,095 inhabited villages in Andhra Pradesh do not have 3G/ 4G mobile internet coverage. (b)	These uncovered villages are being covered with high speed internet facility under schemes of the Government and Telecom Service Providers in a phased manner.(c)	State/UT-wise fund allocated by the Government during the last three years for the BharatNet and other schemes for increasing the internet connectivity in rural areas is given in Annexure 1A & 1B. (d)	The Government has taken following steps to increase digital literacy in rural areas of the country: •	In the years 2014 to 2016, two Schemes entitled “National Digital Literacy Mission” (NDLM) and “Digital Saksharta Abhiyan” (DISHA) were implemented by the Government with a target to train 52.50 lakh candidates in digital literacy across the country including rural India. Under these two schemes, a total of 53.67 lakh beneficiaries were trained, out of which around 42% of candidates were from rural India.•	In February 2017, the Government of India has approved a scheme titled “Pradhan Mantri Gramin Digital Saksharta Abhiyan (PMGDISHA)” to usher in digital literacy in rural India by covering 6 crore rural households (one person per household) at a total outlay of Rs. 2,351.38 crore (approx.). Under this Scheme, in order to ensure equitable geographical reach, it is envisaged to cover each of the 2,50,000 Gram Panchayats across the country. So far, more than 4.21 crore candidates have been enrolled and around 3.44 crore have been trained, out of which more than 2.51 crore candidates have been certified under the PMGDISHA Scheme.(e)	 So far, a total of Rs.1088 Crore has been released for implementation of the PMGDISHA scheme. The funds allocated by the Government for the said Scheme including current financial year are as under:</pre>Financial Year	2017-18	2018-19	2019-20	2020-21Amount( in Cr)	100	438	400	150 </pre>
