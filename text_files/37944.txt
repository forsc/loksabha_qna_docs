                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5096






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.04.2022






Impact of Pollution on Monuments




Sumalatha Ambareesh














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it is true that the Government has put in place specific mechanism at selected monuments to assess the damage caused by pollution and if so, the details thereof;(b)	the total number of monuments selected after assessing the damage caused by pollution;(c)	if so, the details thereof, State-wise and district-wise; and(d)	the funds allocated and spent for protection of national monuments?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF CULTURE, TOURISM AND DEVELOPMENT OF NORTH EASTERN REGION(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a)   to  (c)	Yes, Sir.  The Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) has put in place specific mechanism at select monuments to assess the damage caused by pollution. The Science Branch of ASI regularly monitors the air quality and level of pollutants along with other physical parameters in the vicinity of select national monuments. Six monuments have been selected to assess the damage caused due to pollution. The state wise and district wise details are given as under;1.	Bibi-Ka- Maqbara, District Aurangabad, Maharashtra 2.	Charminar, District Hyderabad, Telangana3.	Shore Temple, Mahabalipuram, District Kanchipuram, Tamilnadu.4.	Sri Brihadeswara Temple, District Thanjavur, Tamilnadu.5.	Sun Temple, Konark, District Puri, Odisha.6.	Taj Mahal, District Agra, Uttar Pradesh.(d)	The funds allocated and spent by ASI for conservation and preservation of national monuments for the last three years are as under:                                                                 (Amount Rs. in Lakhs)S. No.	Financial Year	Allocated 	Expenditure 1.	2018-19	40637.86	40502.012.	2019-20	43560.68	43538.763.	2020-21	26090.00	26083.41
