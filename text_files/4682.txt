                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MINORITY AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4237






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.07.2019






Implementation of Sachar Committee Recommendations




Mohammad Azam Khan














                        Will the Minister of





MINORITY AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has formulated any policy or plan to implement the recommendations of Sachar Committee during the period of 2014 to 2019;(b)	if so, the details thereof, State/district-wise; and(c)	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MINORITY AFFAIRS(SHRI MUKHTAR ABBAS NAQVI)(a)	to (c): The Sachar Committee Report on the condition of Muslim community of India was submitted on 17.11.2006. The details of the status of implementation of the recommendations of the Sachar Committee report are available on the website of this Ministry www.minorityaffairs.gov.in. During the period 2014 to 2019, the Government took various initiatives for the socio-economic and educational empowerment and upliftment of every section of the society including the six centrally notified minorities namely Jain, Buddhist, Sikh, Parsi, Muslim and Christian. The budget of the Ministry has increased from Rs 3511 crores in 2013-14 to Rs 4700 crores in 2019-20.The government during the last five years has provided 3.18 crores scholarships to students from amongst the six centrally notified Minorities namely, Jain, Buddhist, Sikh, Parsi, Muslim and Christian. Out of these, girls constitute more than 50% of the beneficiaries. The three scholarship schemes are now being implemented through the National Scholarship Portal and since 2015, the scholarships schemes are being implemented under direct benefit transfer (DBT) mode to improve the efficiency and bring transparency by removing duplicity and stopping pilferages.Under the Pradhan Mantri Jan Vikas Karyakram (PMJVK), during the last five years, the  Ministry  has  sanctioned  a  number  of  projects under  the  scheme  which  includes  25  Degree  Colleges,  1152  School  Buildings, 20228 Additional Class Rooms, 506 Hostels, 71 ITIs, 5 Polytechnics, 411 Sadbhav Mandaps, 92  Residential  Schools,  530  Market  Sheds,  5090  Aanganwadi  Centres, 821 Health Projects, 2285 Water Supply Projects, 11676 Pucca Houses, etc. The State- wise   details   of   the   projects   are   available   on   the   Ministry’s   website   at http://www.minorityaffairs.gov.in.During the last five years 3,93,711 youths have been provided employment oriented skill development training under various schemes of this ministry.During the last five years, 59,988 students have been provided free coaching under various coaching schemes of the ministry.The National Minorities Development and Finance Corporation (NMDFC) has provided  concessional loans of Rs 2582.30 crores in the last five years to 5,67,794 beneficiaries belonging to the six centrally notified minorities for self-employment and income generating ventures. The details of the schemes implemented for the socio-economic and educational empowerment of six centrally notified minorities by Ministry of Minority Affairs  including the period 2014 to 2019 are as under:-Scholarships: -Pre-Matric Scholarship Scheme – For Class I to X.-Post-Matric Scholarship Scheme – For Class XI to Ph.D.-Merit-cum-Means based Scholarship Scheme – For Professional and Technical Courses.-Maulana Azad National Fellowship – For M.Phil. and Ph.DFree Coaching and Allied Scheme (NayaSavera) – For Competitive Examination of Professional Courses and Government Jobs. “PadhoPardesh”– Scheme of Interest Subsidy on Educational Loans for Overseas Studies.NaiUdaan - Support for students clearing Prelims conducted by UPSC, State Public Service Commission, etc.HamariDharohar - A scheme to preserve rich heritage of Indian minority communities under the overall concept of Indian Culture.JiyoParsi – Scheme for containing population decline of small minority community.NaiRoshni–Leadership Development of Minority Women and to instil confidence.Employment-orientedSkill Development Initiatives –SeekhoAurKamaoNaiManzil – A scheme for formal school education & skilling of school dropouts.USTTAD (Upgrading the Skills and Training in Traditional Arts/Crafts for Development) and organizing of HunarHaats for providing opportunityand market support to the artisans and craftsmen.PradhanMantri Jan VikasKaryakram(PMJVK):- It is an area development scheme. The Ministry has identified 1300 backward Minority Concentration Areas (MCAs) for the implementation of this scheme.Maulana Azad Education Foundation (MAEF) implements educational and employment related schemes:--Begum Hazrat Mahal National Scholarship for Meritorious Girls belonging to the six centrally notified Minorities.-Gharib Nawaz Employment Training for providing short-term job-oriented skill development courses to youths belonging to minority communities.-Bridge Course for madarsa students & school dropouts by Aligarh Muslim University, Aligarh and Jamia Millia Islamia, New Delhi under Nai Manzil scheme. -Swachh Vidyalaya Intiative.NMDFCprovides concessional loansfor self-employment and income generating venturesto persons from the six centrally notified minorities. Further, the Prime Minister’s New 15 Point Programme for the Welfare of Minorities, is an overarching programme, covering various schemes/initiatives of the participating Ministries/Departments and is implemented throughout the country. All the schemes/initiatives included in the programme are implemented by the participating Ministries /Departments, either exclusively for or by earmarking of overall physical /financial targets for minorities. The programme aims to promote education as well as social welfare of minorities by ensuring equitable share in economic activities and employment through existing and new schemes, enhanced credit support for self-employment and recruitment to State and Central Government jobs.The details of the ongoing schemes/programmes are available on the website of this Ministry www.minorityaffairs.gov.in; www.maef.nic.in and www.nmdfc.org.*****
