                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1790






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.11.2019






Road Safety Awareness




S.R. Parthiban














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has recorded/maintained the data of accidents in the areas where violations of traffic rules are comparatively higher in number and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether the Government proposes to introduce/implement road safety awareness in the educational curriculum and if so, the details thereof;(c) 	whether the Government has a proposal to make it statutory to instruct regarding road safety awareness and if so, the details thereof; and (d) 	whether the Government proposes to initiate an award for good Samaritans and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a)	     The Ministry analyses road accidents based on the data collected from the Department of Police of the States/UTs. During the calendar Year 2018, under the category of Traffic Rule Violations, over speeding is a major killer, accounting for 64.4% of the persons killed followed by driving on the wrong side of the road which accounted for 5.8% of the accident related deaths. Use of mobile phones accounted for 2.4% of the deaths with drunken driving accounting for 2.8% of the persons killed.(b)	     Hon’ble Supreme Court has directed States for inclusion of Road safety in school curriculum. (c)	to (d)    No Sir, at present, there is no such proposal in the Ministry.*****
