                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2271






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.03.2020






Pending Cases under the Consumer Actagainst Railways




Upendra Singh Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)   the number of cases that are pending under the  Consumer (Protection) Act against the Railways;(b)  whether the Railways has identified those responsible for providing deficient/negligent service and recovered the damages paid by the Railways on the verdict of the consumer court; and(c)     if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a): 	  A total of 3225 cases are pending under the Consumer (Protection) Act against the Railways, as on 21.02.2020.(b) & (c):  Yes, Sir. Railways have identified, wherever possible, those responsible for providing deficient/negligent service, and wherever possible recovery was made.	Consumer Court cases were filed mostly  on account of theft of unbooked luggage, refund of tickets, unauthorised passengers, deficiency in services, coach maintenance & cleanliness, wrong ticket issued, and luggage & parcel  etc.   Railways have recovered damages to the tune of ? 1,53,742/- in the last three years (i.e. from 2017-2020, upto 21.02.2020).******
