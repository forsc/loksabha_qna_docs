                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1710






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Facilities under AMRUT




Margani Bharat














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	Whether 1.39 crore water connections, improved sewerage, LED street lights, etc. by 2020 with an outlay of Rs.77,640 crores were promised under Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) and if so, the details thereof;(b)	whether 500 cities has been identified for this purpose, but so far only 0.38% of outlay has been utilized during three years and if so, the details thereof. (c)	the reasons for this unacceptable performance in implementation of Government’s flagship programme;(d)	the status of implementation of each of the thrust area identified under AMRUT since 2015, State/year-wise;(e)	the details of cities identified in Andhra Pradesh under this scheme and status of each of the city; and(f)	the details of budget allocation for current financial year and proposed allocation, if any, for the next financial year?






ANSWER




                                                                 THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                            (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a):	The Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) was launched on 25 June 2015 in selected 500 cities and towns across the country.The Mission focuses on development of basic infrastructure in the AMRUT cities and towns in the thrust area of water supply; sewerage and septage management; storm water drainage; green spaces and parks; and non-motorized urban transport.	Further, Capacity Building programme to empower municipal functionaries for timely completion of projects anda set of 11 Reforms for improvement in service delivery, mobilization of resources and making municipal functioning more transparent and functionaries more accountable, have been made part of the AMRUT Scheme. 	In order to assess the gap between number of households having water tap/sewerage connections and those not having these facilities, Service Level Improvement Plans (SLIPs) were prepared on the basis of Census-2011 data by each Urban Local Body (ULB) of AMRUT cities/towns. Once the service level gaps were computed, plans to bridge the gap through infrastructure development were prepared by each State /UT in the form of State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs). 	Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs has already approved State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) of all the States/UTs amounting to `77,640 crore for the entire Mission period, which includes committed Central Assistance (CA) of `35,990 crore.	Progress made in implementation of AMRUT projects are as under:(i)	5,740 projects worth `79,993 crore have been grounded by States/ UTs against the total approved SAAPs of `77,640 crore. Of this, works worth around `52,000 crore have been physically completed so far.(ii)	Around 107 lakh new water tap connections and 79 lakh sewer/ septage connections have been provided so far under AMRUT and convergence with other schemes.(iii)	Around 1,600 MLD (million litre per day) capacity is already created through AMRUT projects and works to develop additional 4,400 MLD of Sewage Treatment Plants (STPs) are under progress.(iv)	Around 3,800 acres of permeable green space is developed through Green Spaces and Parks projects taken up under AMRUT.	Following achievements have been made under Reforms component of AMRUT:(i)	Credit Ratings: Credit rating work for 485 cities have been awarded and completed in 470 cities, of which 164 cities have received Investible Grade Rating (IGR), including 36 cities spread across 12 States/UTs with rating of A- and above.(ii)	Municipal Bonds: 10 ULBs namely Ahmedabad, Amravati, Bhopal, Ghaziabad, Hyderabad, Indore, Pune, Surat, Lucknow and Visakhapatnam have raised `3,840 crore through issuance of Municipal Bonds for upgrading urban infrastructure.(iii)	Online Building Permission System (OBPS): With a view to facilitate Ease of Doing Business (EoDB) in Construction Permits, Online Building Permission System (OBPS) has been made operational in 2,471 cities, including 452 AMRUT cities to facilitate paperless approvals and issuance of building permissions. This has led to quantum jump in India’s rank in the World Bank Doing Business Report (DBR) in construction permits component of EoDBto 27 in 2020 from 186 in 2017.(iv)	Replacement of streetlights with LED lights and Energy audit of water pumps: With a goal to reduce the amount of energy required to provide civic services besides reducing CO2 (Carbon Dioxide) emissions. 89 lakh streetlights have been replaced resulting in expected energy savings of 192.66 crore units (kWh) worth ?900 crore per year and 15.41 lakh tonnes reduction in CO2 emission per year. Additionally,  667 pumps have been replaced with energy efficient pumps against 11,385 water / sewer pumps identified for replacement through energy audit.	Further, under Capacity Buildinginitiative of AMRUT, 55,682 functionaries and elected representatives have been trained.(b) & (c):	AMRUT Scheme is being implemented in 500 cities and townscovering all States and UTs. The total central outlay forprojects for the entire Mission period is `40,000 crore (`35,990 crore for AMRUT Projects & `4,010 crore for JnNURM projects), out of which `29,442 crore have already been released to States and UTs, which constitutes 73.6% of the total Central outlay for AMRUT projects. Utilization certificates (UCs) of `22,681 crore (i.e. 77% of central project release) have been received so far against this. In addition, 3,092 crore have been released for other components of AMRUT viz Administrative and Office Expenses (A&OE), Reform Incentives, GIS based Master Plan and Local Area Plan – Town Planning Scheme (LAP-TPS) etc. against which Utilization certificates (UCs) of `2,428 crore have been received so far.(d):	The status of implementation of projects in the thrust area of water supply; sewerage and septage management; storm water drainage; green spaces and parks; and non-motorized urban transport, State/year-wise, are given at ANNEXURE – I to ANNEXURE - V.(e):	AMRUT scheme is being implemented in 33 cities / towns of the State of Andhra Pradesh. The status of projects in each city of Andhra Pradesh is given at ANNEXURE - VI.(f):	The Budget allocation of AMRUT scheme for the current Financial Year 2021-2022 is Rs.7,300 crore.  No Budget allocation has been proposed for the next financial year 2022-2023 so far.                                     ******
