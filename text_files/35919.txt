                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2531






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Bridge over Kanupur Spill Channel




Jual Oram














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the construction of a bridge over the spill channel of Kanupur irrigation project on the Rimuli -Roxy- Rajamunda section of National Highway-52 has been delayed for a long time;(b)	if so, the details thereof along with the reasons therefor;(c)	whether it is a fact that the Government of Odisha has agreed to bear the partial cost of the construction of the bridge; and(d)	if so, the details thereof and the steps taken by the Government to expedite the construction work of the bridge?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI) (a)	Yes Sir. (b) to (d)	Construction of Bridge over Kanupur Spill Channel was originally planned for execution by Kanupur Irrigation Project Authority, Government of Odisha on cost sharing basis [National Highways Authority of India (NHAI):80% :: State Government:20%]. The work was awarded in 2011 but same was terminated due to technical difficulties. Subsequently, work was awarded by NHAI on 05.05.2021 and same has been started on 18.09.2021 with scheduled completion by March, 2023. The present physical progress of work is 4.5%.        *****
