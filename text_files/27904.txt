                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3458






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS)




Manoj Rajoria














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total amount of subsidy provided under Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS) by Animal Husbandry and Dairying Department and the Entrepreneurship Development and Employment generation Programme for promotion of animal husbandry and dairy industry along with the number of beneficiaries during the last five years and the details thereof, Statewise; (b) the increase registered in milk production in the country during the last five years and the details thereof, State-wise; and(c) whether the Government proposes to formulate any scheme for the protection of stray cows even if the conservation of cattle is a matter related to the State Legislature, and if so, the details thereof






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)a) The amount of subsidy provided by Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying (DAHD) to National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) under Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS) and Entrepreneurship Development and Employment Generation (EDEG)-National Livestock Mission during last five years is as under:-Year	Funds released by DAHD to NABARD (Rs. in crore)	DEDS	EDEG2016-17	240.00	84.962017-18	203.02	125.502018-19	323.00	175.002019-20	250.00	139.992020-21*	0.00	112.60			*DEDS discontinued from 2020-21	State-wise number of beneficiary and total amount of subsidy disbursed by NABARD under DEDS and EDEG Programme during the last five years is at Annexure-I.(b) Milk production in the country has increased from 155.49 million tonnes in 2015-16 to 198.40 million tonnes in 2019-20. The State-wise detail of milk production during last five years is at Annexure-II.(c) Protection of Stray Animals comes under the purview of the State Governments. Many States are taking measures and have formulated scheme to support Gaushalas to keep the unproductive cattle. However, schemes implemented by the Animal Welfare Board for the welfare of animals including stray cattle are as under:  1)	Regular grant: : Animal Welfare Board of India provides financial assistance to the recognized Animal Welfare Organisations as regular grants towards maintenance of animal shelters, animals medicines, purchase of medical equipments and conducting of veterinary camps etc and rescued cattle maintenance grant for maintenance of the animals rescued from illegal transportation / slaughter houses. 2)	Scheme for Shelter House for looking after the animals: Under this scheme assistance are provided to establish and maintain shelter houses for distressed animals in the country. Primarily, Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) and Society for Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (SPCAs) are given grants for construction of boundary walls, shelter, water tank, drains, in-house Dispensary, medical equipment, contingencies etc. 3)	Scheme for Provision of Ambulance Services to Animals in Distress: Under this scheme the animal welfare organizations are given grants for purchase of suitable vehicles for transportation, rescue and also for providing emergency services to animals in distress.4)	Scheme for Relief to Animals during Natural Calamities: Every year, there are natural calamities in the form of flood, drought, earthquake etc. In such circumstances, there is an immediate requirement for provision of fodder, the adequate shelter, medical attention etc. for the affected animals. Funds for relief to such animals are provided under this scheme through the AWOs. ****
