                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2349






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.07.2019






Disability Quota in Universities




 Imtiaz Jaleel Syed













                                                    Asaduddin Owaisi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the minimum quota for persons with disability in Universities and institutions ofhigher education including Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) and IndianInstitutes of Technology (IITs) etc.;(b) whether 32 of the country’s top Universities and Institutions have failed toaccommodate disabled students under this quota;(c) if so, the details and the reasons therefor;(d) whether Government has issued any directions to all the Universities and highereducational institutions to fill up the quota of disabled students;(e) if so, the details thereof; and(f) whether the Government proposes to amend the 1995 Act to relax norms toenable such students to get admissions in these institutions along with the stepsbeing taken by the Government in this regard?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a): The Government has enacted the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016(RPwD Act, 2016) which has come into force with effect from 19.04.2017. As perSection 32 of the RPwD Act, 2016, it is mandatory for all Government institutions ofhigher education and other higher education institutions receiving aid from theGovernment to earmark five percent of seats for persons with benchmarkdisabilities. These provisions are also followed over the educational institutions likeCUs, IITs, IIITs, NITs.(b) & (c): The University Grants Commission (UGC) has informed that enrollment ofstudents with PwDs is being done. As on 01.04.2019, 3064 students were enrolledin 40 Central Universities under Persons with Disabilities category. Further, theUGC has not received any complaint in respect of Central Universities for denial ofadmission to persons with benchmark disabilities.630 out of 12461 seats have also been earmarked for PwDs in JEE(Advanced), 2019. 259 students with disabilities were studying in the IIMs inFebruary, 2019.(d) & (e): The UGC has issued instructions to all the Universities, from time to time,to fill up the quota of disabled students. The UGC has also circulated a copy of theRPwD Act, 2016 for compliance in Universities and affiliated colleges.(f): The persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunity Protection of Rights and FullParticipation) Act, 1995 has been repealed through enactment of the RPwD Act,2016.******
