                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

101






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2021






Funds for Schools




S. Jagathrakshakan














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government agrees with the view that along with physical orinfrastructural investments, schools must allocate dedicated resources and adopt totrain the trainer approach to make the learning lasting;(b) if so, the measures taken by the Government in this regard; and(c) if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) to (c): Samagra Shiksha is an overarching programme for the schooleducation sector extending from pre-school to class XII and aims to ensure inclusiveand equitable quality education at all levels of school education. The IntegratedScheme launched in 2018-19, envisages the ‘school’ as a continuum from preschool,primary, upper primary, secondary to senior secondary levels. The schemenot only provides support for the implementation of the RTE Act but has also beenaligned with the recommendations of NEP 2020 to ensure that all children haveaccess to quality education with an equitable and inclusive classroom environmentwhich should take care of their diverse background, multilingual needs, differentacademic abilities and make them active participants in the learning process.The following major steps have been taken by the Government to improvequality of education:-1. Central RTE Rules have been amended on 20th February, 2017 to includereference on class-wise, subject-wise Learning Outcomes at Elementary level.2. A 70 indicator based matrix Performance Grading Index (PGI) has beendeveloped to grade the States and UTs.3. National Initiative for Proficiency in Reading with Understanding andNumeracy (NIPUN Bharat) has been launched for ensuring that every child inthe country necessarily attains foundational literacy and numeracy (FLN) bythe end of Grade 3.4. Codification of Learning Outcomes has been developed for the foundationalstage.5. Learning Outcomes for Secondary level have been notified.6. An initiative has been launched to improve learning outcomes at theElementary level through an Integrated Teacher Training Programme calledNISHTHA under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Samagra Shiksha inAugust 2019. The aim of this training is to motivate and equip teachers toencourage and foster critical thinking in students, handle diverse situationsand act as first level counsellors.Due to the COVID pandemic and in order to provide continuouslearning opportunities to the teachers at the elementary level, NISHTHA onlinewas launched using DIKSHA platform in October 2020 to reach out to theremaining elementary teachers. NISHTHA online for elementary teacherscovers 18 courses in 11 languages. Teachers were oriented on variousaspects related to Learning Outcomes, Competency Based Learning andTesting, Learner-centered Pedagogy, School Safety and Security, Personalsocialqualities, Inclusive Education, ICT in teaching-learning includingArtificial Intelligence, Health and well-being including yoga, new Initiatives inSchool Education including library, eco club, youth club, kitchen garden,School Leadership qualities, Environmental Concerns, Pre-school, PrevocationalEducation and School Based Assessment in a joyful learningmanner.7. NISHTHA 2.0 for Secondary/Senior Secondary level teachers with 66 moduleswas launched on 29th July 2021.8. NISHTHA 3.0 on Foundational Literacy and Numeracy with 12 modules forPrimary level teachers was launched on 7th September 2021.9. Support is provided under Samagra Shiksha for improvement of quality ofeducation for various interventions such as (i) Universal Access includingInfrastructure Development and Retention; (ii) Foundational Literacy andNumeracy, (iii) Gender and Equity; (iv) Inclusive Education; (v) Quality andInnovation; (vi) Financial support for Teacher Salary; (vii) Digital initiatives;(viii) RTE Entitlements including uniforms, textbooks etc.; (ix) Support forECCE; (x) Vocational Education; (xi) Sports and Physical Education; (xii)Strengthening of Teacher Education and Training; (xiii) Monitoring; (xiv)Programme Management; and (xv) National Component.*****
