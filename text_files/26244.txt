                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1994






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.07.2021






Ayurvedic College




Dhal Singh Bisen














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYUSH be pleased to state:(a) the provisions to set up new Government/ Non-Government Ayurvedic Colleges;(b) the details of the districts in which the proposals for setting up of new Ayurvedic Colleges have been received from the public representatives or from State Governments, State/UT-wise;(c) the time by which new Ayurvedic Colleges are likely to be opened and if not, the reasons therefor;(d) whether the Government will start integrated courses with a view to overcome the shortage of Ayurvedic doctors as the arrangement was there until 1975; and(e) if so, the time by which it is likely to be implemented and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF AYURVEDA, YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(AYUSH) (SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a)	:  Indian Medicine Central Council Act, 1970 (IMCC Act) has been repealed and replaced with National Commission for Indian System of Medicine Act, 2020 and all the provisions of NCISM Act, 2020 has come into force with effect from 11th June, 2021. Link for Gazette notification:  (https://egazette.nic.in/WriteReadData/2021/227525.pdf). Further,  as per Regulation  59 (2) of NCISM Act, 2020, Medical standards, requirements and other provisions of the Indian Medicine Central Council Act, 1970 and the rules and regulations made thereunder shall continue to be in force and operate till new standards or requirements are specified under this Act or the rules and  regulations made thereunder. Therefore, Establishment of New Medical College, Opening of New or Higher Course of Study or Training and Increase of Admission Capacity by a Medical College Regulations, 2019 shall applicable to setup new Government, Non-Government Ayurvedic Colleges.       Link for Gazette notification:        https://egazette.nic.in/WriteReadData/2019/207346.pdf.(b)	 :  There is no proposal received from public representatives for setting up of new Ayurvedic Colleges from the academic year 2021-22. However, the Ministry of AYUSH has received one hundred twenty four (124) new proposals from the Private Trusts/ Societies including 01 from State Government of Gujarat for setting up of new Ayurvedic Colleges for the academic year 2021-22, out of them fifty six (56), have been forwarded to Central Council of Indian Medicine (CCIM) and sixty eight (68) have been returned back to applicants because of incomplete/ deficient applications. The details of proposals State/UT-wise are placed in Annexure. (c)	 :  NCISM in its 2nd meeting held on 29.06.2021 decided that a person or Institute may submit a scheme to establish new Ayurveda/Unani/Siddha/Sowa Rigpa Colleges, increase intake Capacity in UG & PG courses and to open new PG courses; to the Medical Assessment and Rating Board for Indian System of Medicine from 1st October 2021 to 31st October 2021 for the academic session 2022-23. The information is also available in website i.e.  https://cdn.ayush.gov.in/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/Notice-NCH.pdf.(d)	 & (e):   There is no such proposal under consideration of this Ministry.
