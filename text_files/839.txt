                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

787






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.06.2019






Post Offices




Harish Dwivedi














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 whether post offices have been set up in all the villages in the country;(b) 	if so, the details thereof, State/ UT-wise and the number of post offices likely to be set up during the current financial year;(c) 	whether e-mail, internet and computer facilities have been provided in all the post offices of the country and if so, the details thereof; and(d) 	if not, the time by which the said facilities are likely to be provided in all the post offices?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF COMMUNICATIONS, LAW & JUSTICE AND ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI RAVI SHANKAR PRASAD)(a) & (b) 	No, Sir. There are a total of 5,97,608 inhabited villages in country as per census, 2011, of which 1,42,650 villages are having post offices, which serve other villages as well. The State/ UT-wise details of number of villages having post offices is Annexed. A total of 2000 Branch post offices in Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected areas of 90 districts, 145 Branch post offices in other areas and 114 Sub post offices are likely to be opened during the current financial year 2019-20.(c) & (d) 	Out of total 1,56,600 post offices functioning in the country, 1,29,161 Branch post offices in the rural areas have been provided with SIM-based Hand Held Devices for carrying out online transactions. All the 25,585 Departmental post offices have been provided with computer facilities, out of these 25,174 have internet facilities for carrying out online transactions. The remaining post offices will be provided with internet facility as and when Telecom Services Providers have the network facility in these location.
