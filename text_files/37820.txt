                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4948






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.04.2022






Availability of AYUSH Medicines in Primary Health Centres




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) whether the Government is ensuring availability of high quality AYUSH medicines in Government Primary Health Centres; (b) if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise; and(c) the funds allocated to Gujarat for the AYUSH medicinal conservation and development during the last three financial years?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AYUSH (SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a) & (b)Public Health being a state subject, to ensure the availability of high quality AYUSH medicines in Primary Health Centres comes under the purview of respective State Government/UT Governments. However, under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of the National AYUSH Mission (NAM), there is a provision of financial assistance for the supply of essential AYUSH medicines through AYUSH facilities including Primary Health Centres in the States/UTs. Ministry of Ayush has approved grants for the supply of essential AYUSH medicines including contingency for Primary Health Centres under NAM from 2014-15 to 2021-22 is furnished in Annexure-I.(c) The funds allocated to Gujarat for the AYUSH medicinal plants conservation and development under the Central Sector Scheme for Conservation, Development and Sustainable Management of Medicinal Plants during the last three financial yearsis furnished in Annexure-II.
