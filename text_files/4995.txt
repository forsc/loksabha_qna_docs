                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

417






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2019






Vocational Education




Queen Oja














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the present status of the vocational education centres located in North-Eastern States including Assam;(b)	the funds allocated to above-mentioned States during the last five years;(c)	whether the Government is contemplating to bring a new policy for more effective implementation of vocational education; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (d): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (a) to (d) OF THE LOK SABHASTARRED QUESTION NO. 417 FOR REPLY ON 22.07.2019 ASKED BY SHRIMATIQUEEN OJA, HON’BLE MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT REGARDINGVOCATIONAL EDUCATION(a) to (b): Under Ministry of Human Resource Development’s purview, various skill orientedcourses are implemented by University Grants Commission (UGC) and All India Council forTechnical Education (AICTE) through Community Colleges, Bachelor of Vocation courses(BVoC) & Deen Dayal Upadhyaya (DDU) Kausal Kendras to boost the employability ofyouth. All the universities and colleges in the country have the option of adopting theseapproved skill education courses. Currently, total 81 institutions (42 institutions offeringB.VoC. degree programmes, 34 Community Colleges and 5 DDU Kaushal Kendras) arerunning vocational courses in the North-Eastern States including Assam. Rupees 158.43 crorehas been allocated to these institutions situated in the North-Eastern States for vocationaleducation during the last 5 years. The list of the B.Voc. colleges, Community Colleges andDDU Kaushal Kendras in the North-Eastern States with the details of allocation of funds tothem during last 5 years is at Annexure.(c) to (d): In addition to above, the following initiatives for vocational education have beentaken by the Government:<pre>(i) Scheme for Higher Education Youth for Apprenticeship and Skills (SHREYAS).(ii) Development of new and updated curricula for B.Voc. courses.(iii) Launch of a portal for facilitating Internships by AICTE.(iv) Internship made mandatory by AICTE.</pre>
