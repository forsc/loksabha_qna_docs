                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2526






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2021






National Skills Qualifications Framework (NSQF)




Rajendra Agrawal














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)       whether the number of beneficiaries receiving training under the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana fell sharply due to Covid-19 pandemic; (b)       if so, the details thereof; (c)        whether the Government has assessed the impact of the pandemic on levels of skills acquired as outlined in the National Skills Qualifications Framework (NSQF); (d)       if so, the details thereof; (e)       the details of the steps taken by the Government during the last two years to ensure effective implementation of the scheme; (f)        the number of youths who received skill training in Uttar Pradesh under the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana in the last five years district wise; and (g)       the placement records of candidates who benefitted from the scheme in Uttar Pradesh?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)       Yes, Sir. Due to the COVID-19 induced lockdown and restrictions, the training activities could not be conducted as envisaged. Further, in order to mitigate the effect of COVID-19 pandemic on skill development training, on-line skill training has been introduced in both short term and long term skilling ecosystem. On-line short term training is being imparted through eSkill India portal and  long term training is through Bharat Skill portal.(b)       The above reply (a) may be referred to. (c)        Ministry initiated the special programme for training of reverse migrants under PMKVY, provided COVID allowance to training centres, introduce on-line skilling, implementing Customized Crash Course programme for COVID warriors to mitigate the effect of COVID-19 on skill development training programmes. (d)       The above reply (c) may be referred to. (e)       For the effective implementation of skill development training in last two years and to mitigate the impact of COVID-19 pandemic on skill development training, various initiatives have been taken by the Ministry. Some of them are given below: (i)         Special programme for training of reverse migrants under PMKVY: Ministry has implemented special programme for training of reverse Shramik (migrant) impacted from COVID-19 under its flagship scheme PMKVY. This component has covered 116 districts of 6 States, namely, Assam, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Rajasthan and Uttar Pradesh selected under Garib Kalyan Rojgar Abhiyan (GKRA) of Ministry of Rural Development. As on 10.07.2021, 70,823 migrants have been trained / oriented under this programme.  (ii)       COVID Allowance to training centres:  Common Norms Committee (CNC) for Skill Development Schemes has decided that 1.5% of the average training cost per trainee for 3 months, or 1.5% of the hourly base cost rate (as applicable as per category of trade undergoing the training) may be allowed as COVID Allowance. (iii)       ''Customized Crash Course Programme for COVID Warriors'': Ministry is implementing special programme, ''Customized Crash Course Programme for COVID Warriors'' with the aim to train about one lakh COVID warriors in six healthcare-related job roles. Under this special programme, 58,623 candidates have been trained/oriented as on 3.12.2021. (iv)       Introduction of On-line skill training: On-line skill training has been introduced in both short term and long term skilling ecosystem. On-line short term training is being imparted through eSkill India portal and  long term training through Bharat Skill portal. (f)        The district-wise number of candidates trained / oriented in the State of Uttar Pradesh under the PMKVY in the last five years (i.e. 2016-21), is given at Annexure. (g)       PMKVY has two training components, namely, Short Term Training (STT) and Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL). Under PMKVY, placement opportunities are being provided to STT certified candidates, while RPL does not mandate placements as it recognizes the existing skills of candidate. In the State of Uttar Pradesh, under PMKVY-STT, as on 21.11.2021 since inception i.e. 2015, 7,89,481 candidates have been certified; out of which, 3,29,991 candidates have been placed in various sectors.
