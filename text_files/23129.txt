                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

368






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2021






Education Through TV/ Radio




Kotha Prabhakar  Reddy













                                                    Geetha Viswanath Vanga
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether following the study lessons being telecast for students during COVID-19 lockdown, the School Education Departments of States have now begun broadcasting short audio lessons for students on the radio and some departments have asked heads of schools and teachers to be made aware of schedule of lessons being aired and inform the students about the same through the All India Radio primary channel 10 to benefit students who were finding it hard to access video lessons on some TV Channels, online mode, etc.;(b) if so, the details thereof; and(c) the action taken thereon as on date, State-wise?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION   (SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)	Part (a) to (c)   A Statement is laid on the table of the House.*********** STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (A) TO (C) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 368 ASKED BY SHRI KOTHA PRABHAKAR REDDY AND SHRIMATI VANGA GEETHA VISHWANATH, HON’BLE MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT REGARDING EDUCATION THROUGH TV/ RADIO.(a) Education is a subject in the Concurrent List of the Constitution. During the period oflock down, online classes were conducted by employing various digital tools in the form of DIKSHA portal (https://diksha.gov.in/), SWAYAM Portal (swayam.gov.in), e-Pathshala (https://epathshala.nic.in/), NROER (National Repository of Open Educational Resources) portal (https://nroer.gov.in/welcome), and CBSE podcasts etc. Educational material is available on DIKSHA in all the languages of the eighth schedule of the constitution. Course-based programs have been broadcast on radio and TV where online mode was not available. During the period of lockdown, the States and Union territories have used the medium of radio and TV for education.Under PM eVIDYA, the Ministry of Education has allocated 12 DTH TV Channels (One Class-One Channel from classes 1 to 12). It is broadcast on Doordarshan DTH and Jio TV.471 pieces of curriculum-based radio programmes (Classes 1 - 8) have been produced by NCERT and the same are being broadcasted through 88 Radio Stations (12 GyanVani FM Radio Stations, 76 Community Radio Stations) across the country. Also, for class 9th to 12th, educational programs are broadcast on 156 radio/community radio stations of NIOS.(b) & (c) State/UT wise list is annexed.**********
