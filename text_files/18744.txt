                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has implemented any policy/strategy to educate/make consumers aware about their rights across the country particularly in rural areas and if so, the details thereof; (b)	whether the Government has sought participation of Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRIs) for effective implementation of the said policy/strategy in rural areas of the country; (c)	if so, the details thereof along with the response received from PRIs in this regard; (d)	whether the complaints of violation of consumer rights increased in the country during each of the last three years and the current year; and (e)	if so, the details thereof along with the reasons for the same along with the action taken/being taken on such complaints, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI DANVE RAOSAHEB DADARAO)(a)	     : 	Yes, Sir. The Government carries out a multi-media “ Jago Grahak  Jago” campaign on pan-India basis covering both rural and urban areas through print, electronic, outdoor and social media. Consumer Awareness information has been disseminated through post offices and Krishi Vigyan Kendras.  Grants-in-aid are released to States/UTs for creating consumer awareness in the respective States/UTs in their regional languages so as to further ensure that the campaign reaches the rural and backward areas of the country.  Consumer awareness and education is also promoted through fairs and melas in rural and urban areas. (b)  & (c) :       The Government has involved of PRIs in rural areas through Ministry of Panchayati Raj for effective implementation to educate and make consumers aware about their rights across the country.
