                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3120






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2019






Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan




Shashi Tharoor














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA), that aims to provide quality antenatal care free of cost and universally to all pregnant women on the 9th of every month, is meeting its objectives;(b) if so, the details thereof, State/UT and district-wise;(c) the number of women benefited so far from the said scheme in Kerala, particularly in Thiruvananthapuram district;(d) the measures taken to encourage private medical practitioners to extend such essential antenatal care;(e) whether the Government proposes to take steps to improve awareness about one of the important but neglected components of antenatal care, supplementary nutrition, under thePMSMA; and(f) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA) program aims to provide fixed-day assured, comprehensive and quality antenatal care universally to all pregnant women free of cost on the 9th of every month. As part of the program, a minimum package of antenatal care services are provided to pregnant women in their 2nd/ 3rd trimesters at Government health facilities (PHCs /CHCs /SDHs /DHs/Urban health facilities etc.) Following are the objectives of the program;•	Ensure care provision by a physician/ specialist in at least one ante-natal visit in the second or third trimester.•	Improve the quality of care provision during ante-natal visits. This includes ensuring provision of services such as diagnostic, screening for clinical conditions, appropriate management of any existing clinical condition like anemia,, pregnancy induced hypertension, gestational diabetes etc. and  proper counselling services.•	Offer additional opportunity to pregnant women who have missed their ante-natal visits due to any reason.•	Identification and line listing of high risk pregnancies based on obstetric/medical history and existing clinical conditions. Appropriate birth planning and complication readiness for each pregnant women especially those identify with any risk factor or comorbid condition.•	Special emphasis on early diagnosis, adequate and appropriate management of women with malnutrition conditions like anemia, is provided during these clinics. Special focus is also given to adolescent and early pregnancies, as these pregnancies need extra and specialized care.This program has been meeting its objective successfully and following achievements have been made so far.•	More than 2.22 crore antenatal check-ups have been conducted at PMSMA sites.•	Over 6000 volunteers are registered across all State/UTs.•	PMSMA is conducted at over 17000 health facilities across all State/UTs.•	More than 11.75 lakh high risk pregnancies have been identified. (b) & (c) State/UT and District wise details is in "Annexure-A".So far, 28377 Pregnant women received Antenatal care in Kerala. Similarly, 920 Pregnant women received Antenatal care in Thiruvananthapuram district. (d):	Following steps have been  taken to encourage private medical practitioners to extend such essential antenatal care;•	Appeal to Private Medical Practitioners; Appeal to private sector doctors to volunteer & join hands for PMSMA by Hon''ble Prime Minister on the occasion of "MANN KI BAAT" episode on 31st July''2019.•	Centralise PMSMA Portal: Centralise PMSMA Portal has been created for registering private practitioners.To Register as a volunteer Doctors can:o	Register by calling  "18001801104" - Call centre, agent will respond and register the volunteer.o	Register by SMS "PMSMA space their name to 5616115"  Volunteer will receive a call back from the call centre.o	Register by logging on to www.pmsma.nhp.gov.ino	Register through the PMSMA mobile application.•	PMSMA ‘IPledgeFor9’ Achievers Awards:   PMSMA ‘IPledgeFor9’ Achievers Awards have been devised to celebrate individual and team achievements and acknowledge voluntary contributions for PMSMA?	Commitment from FOGSI & IMA: MoHFW & IMA/ FOGSI join hands to pro- mote PMSMA through platforms such Lok Sabha TV, video conferences with member doctors, articles in annual reports etc.FOGSI felicitates PMSMA volunteers at its National level Meet.(e) & (f):	Supplementary Nutrition, one of the six services under the Anganwadi Services, is now a legal entitlement under the National Food Security Act,2013. Section-4,5 & 6 of the said Act pertain to entitlement regarding nutrition support to pregnant and lactating mothers and children under the Anganwadi Services. Nutritional standard for pregnant women and lactating mothers as per the Schedule II of the said Act are as follows:Category	Type of Meal	Calories (Kcal)	Protein (Gm)Pregnant women & Lactating Mothers	Take Home Ration	600	18-20 In addition, during PMSMA every pregnant women is counselled individually or in groups, on importance of nutrition including iron folic acid consumption and calcium supplementation, adequate rest, care during pregnancy, importance of breastfeeding  & complementary feeding etc.Apart from above, safe motherhood booklet are distributed to all pregnant women during ANC registration to create awareness. Ideal ANC Videos for health care service providers have been uploaded in PMSMA portal which guides the health worker to provide quality ANC services.
