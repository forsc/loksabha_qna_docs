                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

437






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2022






Zero Emission Cities under SCM




Sanjay Jaiswal














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	whether cities are evaluated for climate change resilient infrastructure 	and facilities under the Smart Cities Mission(SCM) and if so, the details 	thereof;(b) 	whether the Government proposes to launch a special climate Smart 	Cities Mission, focussed especially on building climate change resilient 	cities in the country and if so, the details thereof;(c) 	whether the current Climate Smart Cities Assessment Framework 2.0 is 	binding on Urban Local Bodies and if so, the details thereof;(d) 	whether the Government is taking steps for promoting ‘Race to Zero’ and 	other campaigns to create zero emission cities; and(e) 	if so, the details thereof along with the details of the funds allocated for 	the same?






ANSWER




                       THE MINISTER OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                          (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)	to (c): Government of India launched the Smart Cities Mission (SCM) on 25 June 2015. 100 Smart cities have been selected through 4 rounds of competition from January 2016 to June 2018.  Climate Smart Cities Assessment Framework (CSCAF) was launched in 2019 as a mechanism to strengthen climate-sensitive development practices in cities. Report of the    second edition of the assessment (CSCAF 2.0) which was open to all Smart Cities, Capital cities and cities with population above 5 lakhs in 2011 Census was released on 25 June 2021. To support climate action across India’s cities, the Climate Center for Cities (C-cube) has been established under National Institute of Urban Affairs (NIUA).  There is no proposal under consideration of Government for launching a Climate Smart Cities Mission. (d) & (e):  The decision regarding participation of Cities/Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) in ‘Race to Zero’ or similar campaigns is taken by respective State Governments/ULBs. SCM promotes and fosters climate sensitive development through various initiatives.                                      ****
