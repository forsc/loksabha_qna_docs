                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

938






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.11.2019






Pollution at Jabalpur




Rakesh Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the data received through Pollution Mapping Tools installed by the Central Pollution Control Board in Jabalpur, Madhya Pradesh is collected online; (b) 	if so, the details of such data; (c) 	whether the level of pollution there is above the stipulated limits and if so, the details thereof; (d) 	whether the Government has taken any remedial measures to deal with it; and (e) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	& (b) Madhya Pradesh Pollution Control Board has installed an online Continuous Ambient Air Quality Monitoring Stations (CAAQM) in Jabalpur and connected to CPCB in the month of September 2019. The data from September 17, 2019 to November 17, 2019 is given at Annexure -I. (c)	Earlier, the air quality data in Jabalpur was getting monitored manually and as per the analysis of data annual average concentration of SO2 and NO2was within the National Ambient Air Quality Standard (NAAQS) of 50µg/m3 and 40 µg/m3 respectively. However, annual average concentration PM10and PM2.5during 2018 exceeded NAAQSof 60 µg/m3 and 40 µg/m3 respectively.(d) & (e) The Central Government has launched National Clean Air Programme (NCAP) under the Central Sector “Control of Pollution” Scheme as a time bound national level strategy to tackle air pollution problem across the country in comprehensive manner. Based on ambient air quality data for the period 2011 – 2015 and WHO report 2014/2018, 102 non-attainment cities including 6 cities namely Bhopal, Dewas, Indore, Sagar, Ujjain, Gwalior fromMadhya Pradesh have been identified.States of non-attainment cities including Madhya Pradesh have been directed to prepare and implement city specific Clean Air Action Plan. Further, the Central Government has already notified 6 waste management rules covering solid waste, plastic waste, e-waste, bio-medical waste, C&D waste and hazardous wastes in 2016.Notifications regarding dust mitigation measures for construction and demolition activities issued. Emission standards for industrial sectors have been revised from time to time.BS-IV standards adopted from 1st April, 2017. Leapfrogging from BS-IV to BS-VIfuel standards since 1st April, 2018 in NCT of Delhi, in NCR since October 2019 andfrom by 1st April, 2020 in the rest of the country for both fuel as well as vehicles.*****
