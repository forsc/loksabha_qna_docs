                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4213






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






Seaplanes under UDAN Scheme




Dushyant Singh














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is planning to launch seaplanes under UDAN Scheme and if so, the details of the routes proposed for seaplanes under UDAN scheme;(b) the number of aerodromes to be developed for seaplanes in next three years and the cost associated with the development of aerodromes;(c) whether the major dams can be used for seaplanes; and(d) if so, the safety concerns regarding the use of dams for seaplanes and the steps taken to mitigate the risks attached?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a): Airport Authority of India (AAI), the Implementing Agency, has awarded  Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) Routes for operation of RCS flights from 10 Water Aerodromes. Details of these Water Aerodromes are at Annexure.;;(b): Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs approved a proposal on 06 March, 2017 for revival of existing un-served / underserved airports / airstrips of the State Governments, Airports Authority of India and Civil Enclaves at an estimated cost of Rs. 4500 Crore. An airport /airstrip /helipad /heliport /water aerodrome which is included in the awarded routes of RCS - UDAN and requires upgradation/development for commencement of RCS operations, is developed under "Revival of unserved and underserved airports" scheme, subject to firm commitment from airline operators as well as from the State Governments for providing various concessions.Approximate cost of development of these 10 Water Aerodromes is Rs. 360 crores.;(c) and (d): Yes Sir. However, RCS - UDAN is market driven scheme. The interested airlines based on their assessment of demand on particular routes submit their proposals at the time of bidding under RCS - UDAN from time to time. Further, Directorate General of Civil Aviation has issued Civil Aviation Requirements Section 4 - Aerodrome Standards & Licensing , Series ''F'', Part IV on procedures and requirements for licensing of Water Aerodrome. ;
