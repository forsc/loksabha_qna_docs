                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2935






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.03.2022






SANKALP




Mahabali Singh














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)       the details of the areas where training is being imparted under the Skill Acquisition and Knowledge Awareness for Livelihood Promotion (SANKALP) scheme;(b)       whether employment is being provided after the said training;(c)        whether the said training is improving the skills of the people; and(d)       the district-wise details thereof in respect of Bihar? 






ANSWER




                                                        (a)      Skill Acquisition and Knowledge Awareness for Livelihood Promotion (SANKALP) is an outcome based Centrally Sponsored Scheme aimed to implement the mandate of the National Skill Development Mission (NSDM). SANKALP aims to address the ongoing challenges like bringing about convergence, infusing quality in skill development programs and making them market relevant and accessible in the context of short-term training. SANKALP has three key result areas namely (i) Institutional Strengthening at Central, State and District level; (ii) Quality Assurance of skill development programmes; and (iii) Inclusion of marginalized population in skill development programmes. Unlike other skill development schemes, SANKALP is not a training scheme, it focuses upon pilots/ projects inline with the Result Areas as stated above which may include training of trainers, capacity building of State and district officials etc.(b)	SANKALP envisages improving quality, strengthening skill development institutions and including disadvantaged sections in skill training for livelihood promotion. (c)	With a view to increase access to quality and market relevant training, several initiatives/pilot projects are being implemented under SANKALP, including  Mahatma Gandhi National Fellowship (MGNF) Program, Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) in Gram Panchayats, pilot project for skill training of  women artisans, skill training of sewage workers, Adarsh Gram Skill camp (RPL), Capacity building of State and District officials and Training of Trainers etc.(d)	For the implementation of the SANKALP Scheme, funds amounting to a total of Rs.26.07 crore have been released to the State of Bihar as per details below: i.	Rs 24.77 crore as State Incentive Grant (Central share).ii.	Rs 1.30 crore for 13 Aspirational districts namely Aurangabad, Khagaria, Nawada, Muzaffarpur, Banka, Jamui, Sitamarhi, Begusarai, Katihar, Araria, Shiekhpura, Purnia and Gaya (Rs. 10 lakh per aspirational district).In addition to the above, as a part of the Azadi ka Amrit Mahotsav under SANKALP, Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) camps were conducted during January, 2022 in two districts of Bihar namely Patna and Muzaffarpur under which 400 workers were imparted training/ oriented.
