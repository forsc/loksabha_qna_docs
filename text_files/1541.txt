                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1464






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.07.2019






Data Bank of Students




Gowdar Mallikarjunappa Siddeshwara














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details and the number of students studying in foreign countries as on date,country-wise including Australia;(b) whether the Government proposes to prepare a data bank of all students going abroadfor studies;(c) if so, the details thereof and the time by which such a data bank would be prepared;and(d) whether the Government proposes to issue any guidelines for students desirous ofgetting admission in other countries for studies and if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): Ministry of External Affairs has informed that estimated country-wise number ofIndian students abroad, as per available inputs from Indian Missions abroad till 24.06.2019, is10,90,219. Country-wise details of students studying in other countries, including Australia,is attached as Annexure.(b) and (c): To create a database of Indian students going abroad, on 15 July, 2016, Ministryof External Affairs has developed a “Students Registration Module” within the MADADportal which enables Indian students abroad to register voluntarily and provide data abouttheir courses, location, institution, duration of the course, etc. The registration of students inthe portal is an ongoing process, as the movement of Indian students to other countries,change of visa status, and their return to India etc, are dynamic in nature. Ministry of ExternalAffairs, through its Missions/Posts abroad, has been regularly interacting with Indian studentsstudying abroad to register their details on the students module voluntarily and alsoencouraging the Indian student organizations and Indian Community Associations abroad forthe same through social media platforms.(d): Ministry of External Affairs and Indian Missions abroad issue advisories from time totime asking Indian students to carry all documentation and undertake due diligence to ensurethat the foreign institutions to which they are seeking admission have proper authorizationand capacities.
