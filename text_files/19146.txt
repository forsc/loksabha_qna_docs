                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

677






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2021






Jal Jeevan Mission




Gajendra Singh Patel













                                                    Sangamlal Kadedin Gupta
                                                








                                                    Dulal Chandra Goswami
                                                








                                                    Manoj Kumar Tiwari
                                                








                                                    Ramdas Chandrabhanji Tadas
                                                








                                                    Chandra Prakash Joshi
                                                








                                                    John Barla
                                                








                                                    (Dr.) Ram Shankar Katheria
                                                








                                                    Nayab Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the works being undertaken under Jal Jeevan Mission by the Government; (b) 	the works completed by the Government so far to achieve the targets fixed under Jal Jeevan Mission; (c) 	the targets fixed by the Government so far and current status thereof, State-wise; (d) 	the details of the actions taken under the said scheme so far, State-wise particularly in Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Haryana and Bihar including Pratapgarh and Wardha; and (e) 	the total funds released to Maharashtra so far by the Government under the said Mission?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) to (c)	To enable every rural household in the country to have potable tap water supply in household by 2024, Government of India, in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) – Har Ghar Jal.At the time of announcement of the Mission in August, 2019, only 3.23 Crore (17%) out of then 18.93 Crore rural households, were having tap water connections. Since then, as on date, 3.33 Crore households have been provided tap water connections and at present about 6.56 Crore (34.25%) rural households are having tap water supply. Further, all rural households in Goa and Telangana are reported to have tap water supply.State/ UT-wise achievements made under JJM is in public domain and available on the JJM dashboard @ https://ejalshakti.gov.in/jjmreport/JJMIndia.aspx.(d)	Every State/ UT has been requested to prepare action plan to provide tap water connection to every rural household by 2024. State/ UT-wise reported timeline to achieve the goal of JJM are as under:Year of completion	States/ UTs2020	Goa;2021	Bihar, Puducherry, Telangana and Andaman &Nicobar Islands;2022	Gujarat, Himachal Pradesh, Haryana, Jammu & Kashmir, Ladakh, Manipur, Meghalaya, Punjab, Sikkim and Uttarakhand;2023	Arunachal Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Karnataka, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Mizoram, Nagaland, Tamil Nadu and Tripura;2024	Assam, Andhra Pradesh, Jharkhand, Maharashtra, Odisha, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh and West Bengal.(e) 	In 2019-20, out of Central allocation of Rs. 847.97 Crore grant to Maharashtra, State could draw only Rs. 345.28 Crore (40%). In 2020-21, out of Rs. 1,828.92 Crore allocated to Maharashtra, as on date, the State has been able to draw Rs. 457.23 Crore (25%).****
