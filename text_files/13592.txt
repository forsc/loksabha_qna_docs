                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2437






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.03.2020






Ground Water Contamination




Chandra Prakash Joshi














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there has been any decrease in the level of ground water contamination across the country during the last three years; (b) if so, the details thereof, State-wise; and (c) if not, the factors responsible for the same?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) to (c) Central Ground Water Board (CGWB) generates ground water quality data of the country on a regional scale as part of its ground water quality monitoring program and various scientific studies. These studies indicate the occurrence of contaminants such as Flouride, Arsenic, Nitrate, Iron and Heavy Metals beyond permissible limits (as per BIS) for human consumption in isolated pockets in various States / UTs. State-wise details of contamination of ground water are given at Annexure.Commonly observed contaminants such as Arsenic, Fluoride and Iron are geogenic, whereas contaminants such as nitrates, phosphates, heavy metals etc. owe their origin to various human activities including domestic sewage, agricultural practices and industrial effluents.No appreciable change in the status of ground water contamination as reported by CGWB (on a regional scale) has been observed during last three years. ***** ANNEXUREAnnexure referred to in reply to parts (a) to (c) of Unstarred Question No.†2437
