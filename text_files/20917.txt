                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2280






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.03.2021






PNG Connections




Amol Ramsing Kolhe













                                                    Sunil Dattatray Tatkare
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                








                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has achieved the target of providing Piped Natural Gas (PNG)connection from 25 lakhs to one crore connection as reported recently;(b) if so, the details thereof and if not, the corrective steps taken in this regard;(c) the number of private operators who have been allowed to provide PNG to the housesduring the last two years and the success achieved therein;(d) whether the Government’s attention is drawn to denial of providing PNG by IGL toconsumers living in private colony in South West Delhi who have deposited requisiteamount for new connection even though PNG is provided in the said locality;(e) if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor and the corrective steps to providePNG to these consumers in a time-bound manner including the other steps taken by theGovernment to provide PNG in all cities of the country in a time-bound manner?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) Government is taking steps to enable growth of CGD sector in the country includingallocating domestic natural gas to CGD sector as top priority under no cut category. As per dataprovided by PNGRB, as on 31.12.2020, the total number of domestic PNG connection in Indiawas approximately 72.47 lakh.(b) Regular monitoring including meetings with other Central Government Departments andStates Governments are held to increase the coverage of CGD network/PNG connection in thecountry. In this regard, various Ministries have issued guidelines for increased penetration ofCNG/PNG in their jurisdiction and few of them are:i. Public Utility Status granted to CGD Projects by Ministry of Labour and Employment.ii. Ministry of Defence has issued guidelines for use of PNG in its residential area/unitlines.iii. Department of Public Enterprises has issued guidelines to Public Sector Enterprises tohave the provisions of PNG in their respective residential complexes.iv. Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs has directed to CPWD and NBCC to have theprovisions of PNG in all Government Residential Complexes.v. Department of Promotion of Industry & Internal Trade notified amended rules 2018 toease out process to set up CNG dispensing facilities at existing OMC Retail Outlets.(c) PNGRB has authorized 22 private operators to provide PNG to the houses during the lasttwo years. They are in the process of establishing infrastructure as per the Minimum WorkProgramme. About 55,000 domestic PNG connections and about 260 CNG stations have beencompleted by them upto 31.12.2020.(d) IGL has informed that they are working towards providing PNG to all the households inits authorized GAs, including NCT of Delhi including for those who have deposited requisiteamount for new connection, where PNG is available in the same locality, except for the caseswherein it is technically not feasible due to safety concerns.(e) Petroleum & Natural Gas Regulatory Board (PNGRB) is the authority to grantauthorization to the entities for the development of City Gas Distribution (CGD) network inGeographical Areas (GAs) as per PNGRB Act, 2006. PNGRB identifies GAs for authorizing thedevelopment of CGD network in synchronization with the development of natural gas pipelineconnectivity, natural gas availability and techno commercial viability. So far as 232 GAs havebeen authorised for development of CGD network across the country upto 10th CGD biddinground covering more than 400 districts in the country in 27 States/Union Territories.
