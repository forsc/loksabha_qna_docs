                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

712






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2021






Ayurvedic Colleges




Chandeshwar Prasad














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYUSH be pleased to state:(a) the details of Ayurvedic Colleges along with the number of graduate/post graduate level seats available in Bihar at present;(b) the number of research works done in the post graduate Ayurvedic colleges during the last three years and the details of the medicines developed from the said research works;(c) whether the effective medicines developed through these researches are being produced regularly and used for treatment of patients; and(d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(DR. MUNJPARA MAHENDRABHAI)(a):	At present there are eight (8) Ayurvedic Colleges functioning in Bihar including 02 Government Ayurvedic Colleges. Further, at present there are four hundred twenty three (423) Under Graduate seats and Sixty eight (68) Post Graduate seats available in Ayurvedic Colleges of Bihar. The details are placed at Annexure-I (b) to (d):	The research work done in Post- Graduate Ayurvedic Colleges is not utilized to develop medicines.  The research work data generated in teaching Hospital associated with Ayurvedic Colleges is used for training of students and also in public Health related matters.However, Government of India has established Central Council for Research in Ayurvedic Sciences, an autonomous body under the Ministry of AYUSH as an apex organization for undertaking, coordinating, formulating, developing and promoting research in Ayurvedic sciences on scientific lines. The activities are carried out through 30 Institutes/Centers/Units located all over the India and also through collaborative studies with various Universities, Hospitals and institutes.  CCRAS is also continuously engaged in development of new Ayurvedic drugs through clinical trials. So far, CCRAS has developed 09 new drugs and has been transferred to various industries through National Research Development Corporation (NRDC) for wider utility of public. The details are placed at Annexure –II
