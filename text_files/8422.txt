                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2021






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2019






Forest/Green Cover




Girish Bhalchandra Bapat














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has any mechanism for monitoring and assessment of the forest/green cover in the country and if so, the details thereof, along with the forest cover, State/UT-wise; (b)	whether the Government proposes to utilise remote sensing technology for the said purpose and if so, the details thereof including the benefits of the said technology in terms of time and cost;(c)	whether the Government has any plan for promoting afforestation by planting trees like Sal in various States; and (d)	the details of the funds allocated, sanctioned and utlised during the last two years and the current year, year-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) & (b) Yes, Sir. Since 1987, assessment and monitoring of forest cover/green cover of the country is carried out by Forest Survey of India, Dehradun (FSI), an organization under the Ministry. The forest cover assessment is made using remote sensing technology supported by intensive ground truthing. Remote sensing technology covers very large areas in a repetitive manner at different scales & resolutions and allows easy collection of data of the Earth features namely water, agriculture, forest, habitation etc. in a faster and cost-effective manner.   The result of forest cover assessment is published in areport known as India State of Forest Report (ISFR) on biennial basis.So far 15 such reports have been published. As per the latest report i.e. ISFR-2017, the total forest and tree cover in the country is 8,02,088 square kilometre (forest cover 7,08,273 square kilometre, tree cover 93,815 square kilometre) which is 24.39% of the geographical area of the country. There is an increase of 8,021 square kilometre (forest cover 6,778 square kilometre, tree cover 1,243 square kilometre) of total forest and tree cover in the country including urban areas compared to that of ISFR-2015.  The detail of forest cover/green cover in the States/UTs is given in Annexure-I.(c) &(d) For promoting afforestation in the country, afforestation and conservation activities are being taken up under various Centrally Sponsored Schemes such as National Afforestation Programme, Green India Mission and Development of Wildlife Habitats which are being implemented by the Ministry. Afforestation activities are also taken up under various programmes/funding sources such as Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme, Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, Compensatory Afforestation Funds and under concerned State/UT schemes/plans.Details of fund released by the Ministry for conservation, development and expansion offorests under National Afforestation Programme, Green India Mission and Development ofWildlife Habitats during the last two years and the current year to States/UTs are given in Annexure-II, III, IV respectively.Under Compensatory Afforestation Funds (CAF), the details of State-wise funds released duringlast two years and part of 2019-20 by the Ad-hoc Compensatory Afforestation FundManagement and Planning Authority (CAMPA) are given in Annexure V. After formation of National Authority under CompensatoryAfforestation (CAF) Act, 2016, a sum of Rs. 47436.18 Crore have been deposited in Statefunds as per the provision of the CAF Act, 2016. State-wise details of fund deposited inState fund are at Annexure VI.**********
