                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4654






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

31.03.2022






Sustainable Cities




Kripanath Mallah














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	Whether    the   Government     has   signed   a   Memorandum   of Understanding (MoU) on Sustainable Cities India Programme within the country and if so, the details thereof;(b) 	the details of the target set and the funds proposed to be allocated under the said programm ; and(c)	 the time by which it is likely to be implemented?






ANSWER




                                                                  THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                       (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a) to (c): A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) titled `Sustainable Cities – India Program’ has been signed between the World Economic Forum (WEF), Geneva, Switzerland, and National Institute of Urban Affairs (NIUA), Delhi, an autonomous body under the aegis of Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs (MoHUA) with the objective to align and scale efforts by national, State and city governments, businesses, and research institutes to mainstream integrated solutions for sustainable urban ecosystem transition. No funds have been allocated by the Government of India for the purpose and WEF & NIUA shall bear costs and expenses related to their involvement in the Project. The scope of the collaboration is given below :-•	Promote sharing of best practice policy ideas, technologies, and business models to support the cities in implementing climate relevant measures through the Forum’s toolbox of solutions. •	Foster cross-sector collaboration critical to urban sustainability transitions through a series of design-thinking workshops for supporting climate action in Indian cities. •	Identify and contribute policy, business models and other solutions for urban de-carbonization and resilience to be added to the open-source Toolbox of Solutions which can be replicated in India and in other emerging markets.The term of the Agreement is 12 months starting from February, 2022, renewable by mutual agreement between both parties.                                  *******
