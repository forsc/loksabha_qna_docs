                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3102






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.07.2019






Schemes for Development of MSMEs




Kapil Moreshwar Patil














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is satisfied with the implementation of various schemes for  development of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs);(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether loan based capital subsidy scheme is being implemented for technological  upgradation of MSMEs of the country;(d)	if so, the details thereof; and(e)	the number of MSMEs covered under this scheme in the Country during the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise including Maharashtra?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI  NITIN GADKARI)(a)&(b):	Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME) implements different schemes and programmes aimed at promotion and development of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs). These include the schemes/programmes such as Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP), Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI), A Scheme for Promoting Innovation, Rural Industry and Entrepreneurship (ASPIRE), Credit Guarantee Scheme, Credit Linked Capital Subsidy Scheme (CLCSS), Micro and Small Enterprises - Cluster Development Programme (MSE-CDP), etc. (c)to(e):	Ministry of MSME implements a Credit Linked Capital Subsidy Scheme (CLCSS) across the country. The Scheme facilitates technology up-gradation for MSEs by providing capital subsidy up to a maximum limit of Rs 15.00 lakhs. The number of MSEs benefitted under the scheme in the Country during the last three years and current year, State/UT-wise including Maharashtra, is given in Annexure.
