                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1295






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.07.2021






Pulses under PDS




Rajmohan Unnithan














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has any plan to include pulses under the Public Distribution System, keeping in view the fact that production of pulses has significantly increased in the country during the last five years; and (b) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b) : Under the Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS), the Government makes allocation of foodgrains (wheat and rice) as per the National Food Security Act, 2013 (NFSA, 2013), that is, 5 kg of foodgrains per person per month to priority households, and 35 kg of foodgrains per household per month in case of households covered under Antyodaya Anna Yojana. Further, in 2020-21, as a part of economic response to Covid-19 to meet the challenging situation arising out of the Pandemic, pulses were allocated under the Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan (PMGKAY) for distribution through the Public Distribution System (PDS) to NFSA beneficiary households at the rate of 1 kg per household per month, free of charge, for a period of eight months, from April to November, 2020. About 19 crore beneficiary households were covered under the Programme.*****
