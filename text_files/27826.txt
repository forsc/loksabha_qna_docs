                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3459






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






Grazing Land




Mala Rajya Laxmi Shah













                                                    Pushpendra Singh Chandel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of grazing land in the country including Uttarakhand and Bundelkhand; (b) 	whether the grazing land in Uttarakhand and Bundelkhand is in poor condition and appropriate use is not being done;(c)  	if so, the details thereof; and (d)  	the measures taken by the Government for proper management of grazing land? 






ANSWER




 MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????	    (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (d):	 As per the Land Use Statistics, the details of permanent pastures and other grazing lands in the country including Uttarakhand state and Bundelkhand region for the year 2017-18 (latest available) are given below.                                                                                                              (Thousand hectares)Year	All India	Uttarakhand	Bundelkhand region2017-18	10338	208	273  Source: DES, Ministry of Agriculture & FW Note: Bundelkhand region includes seven districts of Uttar Pradesh (Chitrakut, Banda, Jhansi, Jalaun, Hamirpur, Mahoba and Lalitpur) and six districts of Madhya Pradesh (Chhatarpur, Tikamgarh, Damoh, Sagar, Datia and Panna) 		As per Seventh Schedule of Constitution of India, land comes under the purview of State Government. However, Government of India supplements the efforts of State Government through various programmes.Further, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing a centrally sponsored scheme namely, National Livestock Mission in all States and UTs wherein financial assistance is provided for fodder production under the component Fodder Production from Non-Forest wasteland/rangeland/grassland/non arable lands. *******
