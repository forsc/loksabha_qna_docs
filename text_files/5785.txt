                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5439






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.07.2019






Training for Safe Driving




Dhanush M Kumar













                                                    Amol Ramsing Kolhe
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Heena Vijaykumar Gavit
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government proposes to impart training to people for safe driving in view of large number of road accidents and if so, the details thereof and if not, the corrective action taken in this regard; (b) 	whether there are very few number of training and research institutes for driving in the country and if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor; (c) 	whether the Government proposes to set up more such institutes in various States/UTs of the country; (d) 	if so, the details thereof along with the locations identified for this purpose and the time by which these institutes are likely to be set up, State/UT-wise; (e) 	whether the Government is extending financial assistance to the States to implement this scheme and if so, the details thereof; and (f) 	whether the Government has any mechanism to keep a check on the utilization of funds sanctioned to the States/UTs for setting up of driving centres/such institutes and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a)	to (c)  The C. M. V. Rules 1989  provides for driving training as it directly as well as indirectly helps in ensuring good driving skills and knowledge of rules of road regulation among drivers. The Government of India implements scheme for setting of model Institutes of Driving Training and Research (IDTR), Regional Driving Training Centre (RDTC) and Driving Training Centre (DTC) at district level. (d)    State-wise details of DTI/IDTR/RDTC sanctioned by the Ministry is given at Annexure.  No DTC has been sanctioned so far. (e)     The financial assistance under these schemes are as under:-Sl. No.	Scheme	Financial assistance1	Institutes of Driving Training and Research (IDTR)	Maximum Rs.18.50 crore per IDTR2	Regional Driving Training Centre (RDTC)	Maximum Rs.5.00 crore per RDTC3	Driving Training Centre (DTC)	50% of the project cost subject to a maximum of Rs 1.00 crore per DTC(f)     Funds under IDTR/RDTC is released in installments as per the provisions under the scheme on the basis of the progress report, Utilisation Certificate of previous installment and recommendation of CIRT engaged for appraising the progress and recommendation for next installment based on the milestones fixed under the schemes. Adequate provisions have also been made under the scheme of Driving Training Centre (DTC) to keep a check on the utilization of funds.
