                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1109






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.09.2020






Violence Against Women




Amar Singh














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the Government has taken note of the increasing incidence of men indulging in violence against women by way of social & economic threats/ prejudices; (b)	if so, the remedial steps taken/ being taken by the government in this regard; (c)	whether age-old practice of dowry has driven many women to suicide each year if demands for dowry are not met; and (d)	if so, the measures taken/ being taken by the government in containing the menace?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a) & (b) : The year-wise data of crime against women as per National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) during the years 2016 to 2018 is at Annexure-I.The Government attaches high priority to ensuring safety and security of women and has undertaken various legislative and schematic interventions in this regard. These include legislations such as ‘The Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2018’, ‘The Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2013’, ‘The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013’, ‘The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2006’, ‘The Indecent Representation of Women (Prohibition) Act, 1986’, ‘The Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961’, ‘The Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956’ etc. and schemes such as those of One Stop Centres, Universalisation of Women Helpline, Mahila Police Volunteers, Swadhar, Ujjawala etc. (c) : The data of suicides committed by women on account of dowry related issues during 2017-2019 as per NCRB report is at Annexure-II. (d) : ‘Police’ and ‘Public Order’ are State subjects under the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India.
