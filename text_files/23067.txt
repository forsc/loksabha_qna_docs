                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4116






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Gender Budgeting System




Jayanta Kumar Roy













                                                    Raja Amareshwara Naik
                                                








                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                








                                                    Nisith Pramanik
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                








                                                    Rajveer (Raju Bhaiya) Singh
                                                








                                                    Vishnu Dayal Ram
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	whether the Government has adopted Gender Budgeting (GB) in order to strategize gender mainstreaming across sectors and at all levels of governance in the country, if so, the details thereof indicating the current status and progress made in this regard;(b) 	the funds allocated/released to Central/State Government/Autonomous Institution for mainstreaming gender concern during the last three years;(c) 	whether the gender responsive budgeting statement has any impact on actual allocation of funds during the project design phase and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefore;(d) 	whether all the State Governments/UTs have adopted Gender Budgeting and if so the details thereof, if not, the details of State Governments/UTs that have not yet adopted Gender Budgeting;(e) 	whether the Government has conducted any study to assess the impact of adoption of Gender Budgeting, if so, the details thereof; and (f) 	the other steps taken by the Government to strengthen the Gender Responsive Budgeting in the country. 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a) The Government of India has adopted Gender Budgeting in 2005-06 as a budgetary strategy to advance gender mainstreaming at the national and State levels with focus to translate gender commitments into budgetary commitments across all sectors. So far Gender Budgeting is being done by 37 Central Ministries by way of reconstitution of Gender Budgeting Cells and designation of nodal officers. Gender Budgeting has been adopted by 27 States/UTs. (b)  The funds released to national and State Government institutions during the last three years is as under: S. No.	Financial Year	Amount Released1	2017-18	Rs. 1.50 Cr.2	2018-19	Rs. 3.14 Cr.3	2019-20	Rs. 1.70 Cr.  (c) The Gender Budget Statement (GBS) is a reporting mechanism to assess budgets which are being allocated for women and girls by different Ministries/Departments. For the Financial Year, 2021-22, an amount of Rs. 1,53,326.28 Cr. has been allocated for the women and girls by 43 Ministries/Departments/UTs. In this regard, an amount of Rs. 25,260.95 Cr. has been allocated under Part ‘A’ (100% women oriented schemes) and an amount of Rs. 1,28,065.33 has been allocated under Part ‘B’ (30-99% women oriented schemes) for Financial Year 2021-22.  (d) The following 27 States have implemented GB:  Early Adopters	Subsequent Adopters	Recent AdoptersOdisha (2004-05)Tripura (2005-06)Uttar Pradesh (2005)Karnataka (2006-07) Gujarat (2006)Lakshadweep (2006-07)West Bengal(2005-06)	Madhya Pradesh (2007-08) Jammu &Kashmir (2007-08) Arunachal Pradesh (2007-08) Chhattisgarh (2007-08) Uttarakhand (2007-08)Himachal Pradesh (2008)Assam (2008-09)Bihar (2008-09)Kerala (2010-11)Nagaland (2009)	Rajasthan (2011)Dadra and Nagar Haveli (2011-12)Andaman & Nicobar Islands (2012) Punjab (2012)Maharashtra (2013)NCT of Delhi (2013-14)Jharkhand (2015-16)Andhra Pradesh (2017)Tamil Nadu (2018-19)Manipur (2020) Following 9 States/UTs have yet not adopted Gender Budgeting: Goa	TelanganaHaryana	ChandigarhMeghalaya	LadakhMizoram	PuducherrySikkim	  (e) No study has been conducted to assess the impact of adoption of Gender Budgeting, (f) Ministry of Women and Child Development engages with all Ministries/Departments with respect to the analysis of the gender component of the various schemes/programmes being run by the Government of India. Stakeholder collaborations have been done with some multilateral agencies. *****
