                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

80






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Pollution by Cement Plants




Ganesh Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of cement plants/units established in Satna district, Madhya Pradesh, plant/unit-wise including date of establishment; (b) 	whether Government has taken note of the adverse effect of dust pollution generated by cement plants on the health of people in and around Satna district; (c) 	the details of complaints received against such cement plants/units along with the action taken thereon; (d) 	whether closure directions can be issued without any prior show-cause notice in cases where grave injury to environment is caused by the industries and if so, the details thereof; and (e) 	whether the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) and the State Pollution Control Board (SPCB) have inspected the industrial units and scrutinized the nature of violations of various provisions of pollution legislations notified under the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 and other prescribed environmental norms and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI PRAKASH JAVADEKAR)(a) to (e): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.***** STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARAS (a) TO (e) OF THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION No. 80 DUE FOR REPLY ON 05.02.2021REGARDING ‘POLLUTION BY CEMENT PLANTS’ RAISED BY SHRI GANESH SINGH, HON''BLE MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT(a)	The details of the Cement Plants established in Satna district, Madhya Pradesh is at Annexure I.(b) & (c) The Central Government has prescribed emission norms vide Gazette Notification dated 25 August 2014, 9 May 2016 and 10 May 2016 as cement industry is a polluting industry.Complaints have been received about dust pollution arising due to storage of fuel materials in cement unitof Satna district. A joint team of Central Pollution Control Board and Madhya Pradesh Pollution Control Board conducted inspection of the unit and no violation of norms was observed.(d) Sub-section (5) of Section 4 of the Environment (Protection) Rules, 1986 empowers the Central Government to issue closure direction without issuing any prior notice in case where grave injury to the environment is caused by the industry.State Pollution Control Boards/Pollution Control Committees are empowered under Section 33A of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and under Section 31A of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 to issue closure directions.(e) CPCB and MPPCB has carried out inspections of different cement industries in Satna District and compliance of the stipulated norms under Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 has been reported. Details enclosed as Annexure II. ***
