                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LAW AND JUSTICE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3851






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2022






Electronic Voting Machine




D. Ravikumar














                        Will the Minister of





LAW AND JUSTICE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the expenditure on purchase of Electronic Voting Machines (EVM) has been allocated to the Legislative Department under the Ministry of Law and Justice; (b)	 if so, the reasons therefor along with the procedure followed for the purchase of EVMs and the role of Legislative Department in the process; (c)	 whether the expenditure on purchase of EVMs has not been allocated to the    budget for Election Commission of India or Department of Legal Affairs, if so, the reasons therefor;(d) the budget estimate, revised estimate and actual expenditure on the Legislative Department separately for different heads, including purchase of EVMs, for the last five years, year-wise; and(e) the number of EVMs that had been manufactured and the total number of EVMs received by the Election Commission of India between 2014-2021?






ANSWER




                                                        The MINISTER OF LAW AND JUSTICE(SHRI KIREN RIJIJU)(a):  Yes Sir.(b):  The procedure followed for the purchase of EVMs and VVPATs is as under:	(i)	Based on the estimated number of polling stations for oncoming 	elections, the Election Commission of India (ECI) determines the number of 	additional machines, (EVSs/VVPATs) if needed over and above its existing 	inventory.	(ii) The ECI then requests the Government for in-principle approval and necessary budget for the required number of machines along with tentative costs. (iii) On receipt of the proposal for in principal approval from the ECI, the Legislative Department places the matter before the Government as per the Delegation of the Financial Powers Rules, 1978, if required, to the Cabinet and on Cabinet’s approval, convey the same to the ECI.(iv) Upon receipt of the in principle approval from the Government, the necessary work order for said 	procurement is placed by the ECI on the PSU-manufactures, viz. M/s. Bharat Electronics 	Limited (BEL) and M/s Electronics Corporation of India Limiter (ECIL) for 	supply of machines.(v) Based on the allocation of funds the payments are made by the ECI to the PSUs, 	subject to the finalisation of the cost by the Price Negotiation Committee 	(PNC). PNC consists of AS/JS& FA (Law) one member each from the Legislative Department, Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance and ECI.(vi) Subsequently, based on the manufacturing plan and production, the machines are allocated by the ECI to various states/districts for timely deployment and use.(c):	No Sir.(d):	The information is annexed as Annexure A.(e):	The ECI has informed that the number of EVMs (including VVPATs) manufactured and supplied from 2014-2021 are as under:YEAR	Machine	Quantity(in lacs)2013-2014	Ballot Unit	3.82	Control Unit	2.50	VVPAT	NIL2017-2018	Ballot Unit	13.95	Control Unit	10.55	VVPAT	17.45*******  ANNEXURE-A (Figures in crore)						Budget Head	2017-18	2018-19	Budget Estimate	Revised Estimate	Actual Expenditure	Budget Estimate	Revised Estimate	Actual Expenditure2015 -
