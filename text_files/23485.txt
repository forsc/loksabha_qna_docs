                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4450






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






Migration from Border Villages




Pushpendra Singh Chandel













                                                    Mala Rajya Laxmi Shah
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has taken note of migration of people from the villages located along the borders in bordering States of the country;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	the details of the migration that took place during the last twenty years from these States; (d)	whether the Government has taken any initiatives to rehabilitate these completely abandoned villages or to promote new settlements with the cooperation of the States in these areas;(e)	if so, the details thereof including Uttarakhand, State-wise; and(f)	the details of the funds spent by the Government, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI) (a) to (c) :  As per available information migration data is not compiled at village level.                                      (d) to (f) : Border Area Development Programme(BADP) is implemented as a Core Centrally Sponsored Scheme to provide essential infrastructure facilities, improved connectivity and sustainable livelihood opportunities to the people living in remote and inaccessible areas situated near the International Boundary. BADP aims to encourage people to stay on in the border areas.-2-L.S.US.Q NO. 4450 FOR 23.03.2021Presently, this programme covers 16 States and 2 UTs having international land borders. Under BADP, fund is provided to the State Governments/UTs, ordinarily to be used for meeting the critical gaps in basic infrastructure, which are not getting covered under any regular scheme of any development Ministry.Details of funds allocated and released to States/UTs during the Financial Years 2017-18 to 2019-20 under BADP is Annexed.        *****
