                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4377






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.03.2020






Effects of Drought on Animals




Pushpendra Singh Chandel














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has taken cognizance of the adverse effect of drought on the habitats of animals, migratory and indigenous birds in various national parks and bird sanctuaries; (b)	if so, the details thereof; (c)	whether the Government has formulated any special scheme to overcome the shortage of water in Panna national park and tiger reserve and if so, the details thereof; and (d)	the steps being taken by the Government to protect the natural habitats of animals and birds under its proposed river linking projects and water management schemes? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	and (b)	Yes, Sir. The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change provides financial assistance to State Governments/Union Territories Administration for conservation of wildlife and habitats under the Centrally Sponsored Schemes–‘Development of Wildlife Habitats’, ‘Project Elephant’ and ‘Project Tiger’ which also include activities for ensuring water supply such as construction of water holes inside the forests, check dams, percolation ponds etc. Funds under the Schemes are also provided for desilting of existing earthen dams, check dams etc. in various National Parks and Sanctuaries. (c) Yes, Sir. Under the Project Tiger, the Ministry has released Rs. 461.38 Lakhs to Madhya Pradesh for Panna Tiger Reserve during the year 2019-20 for various activities to overcome shortage of water such as construction/deepening of earthen ponds, construction of saucers, maintenance of water holes, conservation and maintenance of rivers, rivulets and streams etc.(d) The proposals for developmental projects required to be taken in National Parks/Sanctuaries by State Governments/Union Territory Administrations are considered by the Standing Committee of National Board for Wildlife along with the recommendations of State Boards for Wildlife, and the Standing Committee of National Board for Wildlife makes recommendations along with suggestions for adequate precautionary measures  to protect the natural habitats of animals and birds.	***
