                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1258






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.11.2019






Committee for Promotion of e-Payments




Manoj Kishorbhai Kotak














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Reserve Bank of India has appointed a committee to suggest the promotion of e-payments in India;(b) if so, the details thereof along with the committee’s suggestions;(c) whether the panel has also suggested the removal of import duty on Point of Sale devices for three years and reduce the GST on digital transactions; and(d) if so, the reaction of the Government thereto? 






ANSWER




The MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(a) & (b) As apprised by Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), with a view to encourage digitization of payments and enhance financial inclusion through digitization, RBI had constituted a High-Level Committee on Deepening of Digital Payments (CDDP) under the chairmanship of Shri Nandan Nilekani.The CDDP report is available on the RBI website at the following address:https://rbidocs.rbi.org.in/rdocs/PublicationReport/Pdfs/CDDP03062019634B0EEF3F7144C3B65360B280E420AC.PDF(c) As informed by RBI and MeitY, the Committee recommended (at Recommendation 67 of the report of the Committee) that current import duty of 18% on POS machines be reduced to Nil for a period of three years to facilitate adequate expansion of acquiring infrastructure in the country. It also suggested to lower GST on acceptance infrastructure such as PoS terminals & associated device / accessories.(d)  As apprised by Department of Revenue, as of now no decision has been taken to change the GST/IGST rates on PoS machine and waiver of GST on transaction charges of Immediate Payment Service (IMPS) and Aadhaar enabled Payment System (AePS) for transactions upto Rs.5000.
