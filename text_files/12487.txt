                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1343






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2020






Enrollment of Differently Abled Students in Public Schools




Kani K. Navas














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the data of the number of differently abled students in the country enrolled in publicschools, State-wise;(b) whether the Government has proposed measures for improvement of facilities inGovernment schools for students who are differently-abled; and(c) if so, the details thereof and if not the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) The data of number of differently abled in the country, enrolled in government,government aided and private schools is at Annexure-I.(b) & (c) In the year 2018-19, the Department of School Education & Literacy, MHRD haslaunched Samagra Shiksha – an integrated scheme for School Education covering childrenwith disabilities from classes I to XII. The Scheme is governed and regulated by theprovisions of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009 andsupports all States & UTs in implementation of the Act. The RTE Act, 2009 enshrines theentitlement to free and compulsory elementary education, access to school and barrier freeaccess for all children including differently abled students.Under Samagra Shiksha, there is a dedicated component for Inclusive Education forChildren with Special Needs (CwSN) through which various provisions are made availablefor their educational needs such as, identification & assessment camps, provision of aids,appliances, assistive devices, teaching learning materials (TLMs), ICT resources,transportation, escort & scribe allowances and stipend for all girls with special needs (fromClass I to XII) etc. As per UDISE+ (2018-19 prov.), over 20 lakh differently abled studentshave been identified and are enrolled in schools.In addition, individualized support is provided through therapeutic interventions at theblock level. Further, children with severe impairment requiring individualised attention areprovided home based education through special educators. As per RTE Amendment of 2012,children with severe and/or multiple disabilities are entitled to opt for home based education.For the year 2019-20, Rs. 8.02 crore has been estimated for 44,289 children with disabilitiesbeing provided home based education.A separate provision for financial support of Special Educators has been made underSamagra Shiksha in order to appropriately address the unique educational requirements ofdifferently abled students from elementary to higher secondary levels. Financial supportunder the scheme is provided for differently abled students (As per the Schedule ofdisabilities of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act), 2016.For the year 2019-20, Rs. 1363.75 crore has been estimated for various interventionsof the inclusive education for children with special needs component under Samagra Shiksha.The Scheme also has provisions for ramps and disabled friendly toilets for barrier free accessto schools for differently abled students, the State & UT wise details are placed atAnnexure-II.Apart from the above there are other facilities also which are provided CWSN such asaids and appliances (Annexure-III), assistive devices, aids and equipments (Annexure-IV),etc. Apart from the above in order ensure the benefits to the girls’ with disabilities specialprovision for stipend to all the girls’ enrolled for a period of 10 months @ Rs.200 per month(Annrxure-V).The focus of Samagra Shiksha is on providing inclusive education to differently abledstudents wherein, children regardless of their abilities/disabilities participate and learntogether in the same class, thus creating an enabling educational environment for all students.*****
