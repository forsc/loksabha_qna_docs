                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

827






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Female Infanticide




Virendra  Kumar














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the details of cases of female infanticide during the last three years, State/UT-wise;(b) whether the Government is formulating any policy to check the rising cases of female infanticide in the country, if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government proposes to establish centres in every district of the country in order to check infanticide; and(d) if so, the time by which these centres are proposed to be opened and operationalised in the country, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a): As per the information received from National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) the number of female infanticide cases registered are 37, 31 and 36 for 2017, 2018and 2019respectively.State/UT-wise and year wise details are at annexed.(b) to (d): Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP) Scheme aims to address declining Child Sex Ratio (CSR) and related issues of empowerment of girls and women over a life cycle continuum. The objectives of the scheme are, to prevent gender biased sex selective elimination, to ensure survival and protection of the girl child and to ensure education and participation of the girl child. The key elements of the scheme include nation-wide media and advocacy campaign and also multi-sectoral intervention in selected districts.
