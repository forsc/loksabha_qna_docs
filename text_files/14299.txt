                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SHIPPING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3165






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2020






MoU during Maritime India Summit




Bhartruhari Mahtab














                        Will the Minister of





SHIPPING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the extent to which the Government has achieved the objectives of signing the  Memorandum of Understandings (MoU)/Agreements during Maritime India Summit, 2016, Sector-wise;(b)	the number of foreign nationals employed in Indian Shipping Sector and vice-versa as a result of the said MoUs/Agreements across the country, as on date, country-wise;(c)	whether the Government has received representations opposing the said MoUs/Agreements  from various quarters across the country;(d)	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor along with the present status of such representations; and(e)	the other steps taken by the Government to attract foreign investment in Shipping sector?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)  143 Agreements involving total investment of Rs. 80,063.54 crore were signed during Maritime India Summit 2016.  Out of the 143 agreements, 72 have been completed/ operational, 13 are on-schedule, 15 held in abeyance, 9 delayed, 9 awaiting administrative clearance, 8 awaiting environment clearance, 4 under litigation, 7 restructured/re-launched and 6 have been dropped. The organisation-wise details of Agreements signed (as on December, 2018) are at Annex. (b)   This data is not being maintained. (c)   As per information available no such representation has been received so far.(d)   Does not arise.(e)  The major steps taken to attract Foreign Investment in shipping sector are given below:(i)	Foreign Direct investment up to 100% under automatic route has been permitted in the Port Sector.  This has resulted in several Public Private Partnership (PPP) projects in the major ports. At present, a total 34 PPP projects with an investment of Rs 22,376.92 crore are operational and 13 additional PPP projects with an investment of Rs. 71,722.23 crore are under implementation.(ii)	A dedicated Investment Facilitation Cell has been established in Indian Port Association, New Delhi to facilitate investors keen to invest in India’s shipping and maritime sectors.
