                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

342






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.12.2019






Dams and Reservoirs near National Parks




Mansukhbhai Dhanjibhai Vasava














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of dams and reservoirs constructed near the national parks and bird sanctuaries across the country, State-wise; and (b) the remedial steps proposed to be taken by the Government to address the adverse impact if any, due to these constructions?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF JAL SHAKTI  (SHRI GAJENDRA SINGH SHEKHAWAT)(a)	& (b)	A statement is laid on the Table of the House. *** STATEMENT REFERRED IN REPLY TO PARAS (a) & (b) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.  †*342 TO BE ANSWERED ON 12.12.2019 ON “Dams and Reservoirs near National Parks” RAISED BY SHRI MANSUKHBHAI DHANJIBHAI VASAVA, M.P. Central Water Commission maintains national register of large dams as per information received from State Governments/ Dam owners from time to time. This register is constantly updated and available at  URL  http://cwc.gov.in/national-register-large-dams.As per National  Water Informatics Centre (NWIC) - GIS data base, for  few National Parks, Birds & Wildlife Sanctuaries for which point data (without physical extent & boundary) is available on GIS maps, the major & medium reservoirs in the vicinity of 1 km, 3 km, 5km & 10 km is attached at Annexure.All river valley projects require prior environmental clearance from the Ministry of Environment and Forests & Climate Change  (MoEF & CC). The environmental clearance process also determines comprehensive Terms of Reference (TOR) for addressing all relevant environmental concerns for the preparation of an Environment Impact Assessment (EIA) Report in respect of the project or activity for which prior environmental clearance is sought. Preparation of Environmental Management Plan is also required for formulation, implementation and monitoring of environmental protection measures during and after commissioning of projects. Further, Forest and Wild Life clearance is  also required to be obtained for clearance of projects and is subject to compliance of conditions imposed while granting such clearances.***** ANNEXUREAnnexure referred to in reply to parts (a) & (b) of Lok Sabha Starred Question No. †*342 to be answered on the 12th December, 2019 on “Dams and Reservoirs near National Parks”.
