                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3591






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2022






Registered MSMEs in West Bengal




Raju Bista














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of MSMEs registered in the districts of Darjeeling and Kalimpong during the last three years; (b) 	the details of funds/financial support provided by the Union Government to the MSME sector in the State of West Bengal and in the aforesaid districts during the last three years; and (c) 	the details of programmes and projects under which funds/financial support can be   applied for new MSME Start-ups?






ANSWER




                                                                   MINISTER OF STATE FOR MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES              (SHRI BHANU PRATAP SINGH VERMA)(a):	The details of MSMEs registered in the districts of Darjeeling and Kalimpong during the last three years are as at Anneure-I.  (b):	The details of assistance provided to West Bengal including the districts of Darjeeling and Kalimpong during the last three years under Credit Guarantee Scheme for Subordinate Debt (CGSSD) and Credit Guarantee Scheme for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE), are at Annexure-II.(c):		As informed by Department for Promotion of Industry & Internal Trade (DPIIT), Startup India initiative was launched by Hon’ble Prime Minister of India on 16th January, 2016. The flagship initiative aims to build a strong ecosystem for nurturing innovation and startups in the country that will drive sustainable economic growth and generate large-scale employment opportunities. The Action Plan comprises 19 action items spanning across areas such as “Simplification and handholding”, “Funding support and incentives” and “Industry-academia partnership and incubation”. A total of 36,216 startups have been recognized by DPIIT from April 2020 to 14th March 2022. In addition to facilitation for recognition, the Government has been working concertedly to support startups through the various schemes of Startups India viz. Seed Fund, Tax exemption u/s 80 IAC and u/s 56, Patent application and IPR protection, easier public procurement norms, etc.
