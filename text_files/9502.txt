                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3006






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2019






Food and Nutrition Security Analysis




Manne Srinivas Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    whether the Food and Nutrition Security Analysis, India, 2019 has made recommendations to resolve the issue of malnutrition; and(b) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a) & (b):   Report on Food and Nutrition Security Analysis, India, 2019 has been jointly published by Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI) and the United Nations World Food Programme (UNWFP). The report has attempted to analyse data from three dimensions of food availability, access to nutritious food and utilisation of food supplies in India.The report has inter-alia recommended agriculture diversification, sustainability of food productivity, policy support for promotion of farming, marketing and demand generation of traditional coarse cereals like maize, improving storage capacity,strengthening of safety nets programmes, improving Child Feeding Practices, Food Supplementation Programmes, Mother and Child Care Practices, Prioritise Maternal Anaemia, Focus on addressing  the Increasein Wasting Prevalence, Improvement in Water, Sanitation and Hygiene Practices, Gender issues, Food consumption pattern and behaviours, Greater use of technology for the various pillars of food and nutrition Security etc.
