                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1936






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.03.2020






Beneficiaries under PMAY-G




Arun Sao














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a) 	the number of beneficiaries for whom houses have been constructed in rural areas including in Chhattisgarh under Pradhan Mantri AwasYojana-Gramin(PMAY-G) during the last three years and the funds sanctioned by the Union Government for this purpose,State-wise;(b) 	the number of complaints regarding irregularities in the construction of houses received during the last one year and the action taken against the responsible officers; and(c) 	the total number of houses yet to be constructed in rural areas of Bilaspur and Mungeli districts of Chhattisgarh?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a): To achieve the objective of Housing for All by year 2022, the erstwhile rural housing scheme, Indira Awaas Yojana (IAY) has been completely restructured into Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana- Gramin (PMAY-G) w.e.f. 1st April, 2016. State/UT-wise number of houses constructed under PMAY-G for last 3 years is given in Annexure I. State/UT-wise Central Releases under PMAY-G during last 3 years are given in Annexure II.(b): As per Framework for Implementation (FFI) of PMAY-G,  a grievance redressal mechanism has been  set up at different levels of administration viz., Gram Panchayat, Block, District and the State level. An official of the State Government is to be designated at each level to ensure disposal of grievances to the satisfaction of the complainant. The official who is designated at each level is responsible for disposing off the grievance / complaint within a period of 15 days from the date of receipt of the grievance / complaint. The detail of the designated grievance redressal official (including name, telephone number and address) at each level for addressing the grievance and the procedure to file the grievance is to be prominently displayed in each Panchayat. There is also a procedure of lodging of complaints on the Centralized Public Grievance Redress And Monitoring System (CPGRAMS) portal (pgportal.gov.in).During  the year 2018-19, 252 complaints relating to irregularities in implementation of PMAY-G, including irregularities in allotment of houses (i.e. allotment of house to ineligible persons, non-allotment of house to eligible persons , etc.) were received in Ministry of Rural Development. These complaints have been forwarded to the concerned States /UTs for taking necessary action as per the provisions of the guidelines of the PMAY-G. (c): Number of eligible beneficiaries in the Permanent Wait List (PWL), Target Allocated, Houses Sanctioned, Houses Completed and Balance in PWL in Bilaspur and Mungeli Districts of Chhattisgarh, as on 29.02.2020, are as under: Districts	Total PWL	Target         Houses           Houses          Balance                                 Allocated*     Sanctioned	Completed	in PWLBilaspur	1,57,598	70,196	         70,196	          57,273	87,402Mungeli	          63,592	42,655	         42,655	          33,248	20,937*Allocation of target at district level is done by the State Government.*********
