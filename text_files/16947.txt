                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1147






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.09.2020






New Nirbhaya Fund




P.R. Natarajan














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the Government has constituted/ established a new Nirbhaya Fund;(b)	if so, the details of aims and objectives of the new fund; and(c)	the funds allocated/ utilized towards procurement of forensic kits for cases of sexual assault along with the number of beneficiaries so far, State-wise?






ANSWER




ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a)	& (b): The Nirbhaya Fund has been established by the Government for supporting projects specifically designed to improve the safety and security of women. It is a non-lapsable corpus fund, administered by the Department of Economic Affairs of the Ministry of Finance. The schemes/ projects under Nirbhaya Fund are either directly implemented by Ministries/ Departments of Central Government or through their Implementing Agencies (IAs)/ State Governments/ Union Territory Administrations.  (c):  Police and Public order are State subjects under the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India. The responsibilities to maintain law and order, protection of life and property of the citizens are primarily the concern of the States/UTs. However, in order to facilitate States/UTs to undertake efficient and timely investigation in cases of sexual assault, a total of 14950 Sexual Assault Evidence Collection (SAEC)Kits have been provided to them for use in such cases at a total cost Rs. 2.97 crore under Nirbhaya Fund.
