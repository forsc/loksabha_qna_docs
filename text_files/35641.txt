                                    QUESTION NO:

                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.03.2022






Projects under NIP




Locket Chatterjee













                                                    Nama Nageswara Rao
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of infrastructure projects in the country which have been included in the National Infrastructure Pipeline (NIP) announced by the Government, State-wise including Telangana; (b) the details of estimated expenditure of these projects, sector-wise and year-wise; (c) the component of investment to be made by the State Government and Private Sector, if any, in these projects; and (d) the manner in which the Government is planning to give benefits to the rural regions of the country under NIP as the majority of the population of India resides in villages?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(a) to (b): NIP is a first-of-its-kind, whole-of-government exercise to provide world-class infrastructure across the country & improve the quality of life for all citizens. To draw up the NIP, a bottom-up approach was adopted wherein all infrastructure projects of central, state and private sector projects were sought to be captured. NIP was launched with 6,835 projects which have been expanded to 9,335 projects (as on 14.03.2022). The list is dynamic and gets updated periodically. The NIP Total Capital Outlay sector and year-wise is available on NIP/IIG Dashboard Link(c) While launching NIP, it was envisaged that around 18-20% of NIP will be financed through the Centre’s budget & about 24-26% is will be financed through state’s budget. Remaining funding was expected to come from various sources such as bond markets, banks and NBFCs; private developers, multilateral agencies, internal accruals, PSUs, DFIs and asset monetisation. (d) Government intends to develop rural infrastructure under NIP to provide basic amenities such as civic services, housing etc. to improve their quality of life. Further, NIP also covers agro-based industries which help in poverty alleviation, better access to markets & job opportunities in rural regions. Government has initiated schemes such as Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Gramin) to provide housing in the rural areas, Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak to develop and improve the quality of rural roads and Jal Jeevan Mission (Rural) to provide tap water to all rural households.
