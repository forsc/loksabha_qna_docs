                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3216






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.12.2021






Swachh Survekshan, 2021




Thalikkottai Rajuthevar Baalu














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether Tamil Nadu and Rajasthan are placed at the bottom of Swachh Survekshan, 2021;(b)	 	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor;(c) 	the details of financial assistance given by the Union Government to these States during the last five years to improve cleanliness in cities in Tamil Nadu and Rajasthan; and(d) 	the measures proposed by the Union Government and the State Governments to improve the situation?






ANSWER




                                                                    THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                                   (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a) :  Out of the total 13 States ranked in Bigger States (States with more than 100 Urban Local Bodies) category, Tamil Nadu is placed at 12th Rank and Rajasthan at 11th Rank in the Swachh Survekshan, 2021.(b) : The details of ranking of State with more than 100 Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) is provided in the Annex. State rankings are finalized on the basis of performance of the State in three broad categories –i.	Support provided by the States to Urban Local Bodies (ULBs).ii.	Performance of the ULBs under Certification category focussing on ‘Star Rating of Garbage Free Cities’ focussing on solid waste management ‘ODF+/ODF++/Water+’ status of the cities’, focussing on access to functional community/public toilets and liquid waste management.iii.	Performance of cities under ‘Prerak DAUUR Samman’ categories which is directly related with performance of the ULBs in key Service Level Progress indicators focussing on both solid and liquid waste management.(c) :  Under Swachh Bharat Mission-Urban (SBM-U) as against an allocation of B 705.46 crores for Rajasthan, the State has availed B 611.35 crores. Against an allocation of B 1200.50 crores for Tamil Nadu, the State has availed B 1144.68 crores till  date. (d) : Government has launched Swachh Bharat Mission-Urban   (SBM-U) 2.0 on October 1, 2021 with the financial outlay of                B 1,41,600 crores of which B 36,465 crores will be shared by Government during the Mission period of 2021-2026 with the following objectives :(i). To achieve Sustainable Solid Waste Management.(ii). To achieve Sustainable Sanitation and treatment of used water.(iii). To create awareness along with large scale citizen outreach for ‘jan andolan’ and to institutionalize ‘swachh’ behaviour.(iv). To create institutional capacity to effectively implement programmatic interventions to achieve mission objectives.                                       *****
