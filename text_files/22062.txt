                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3050






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Schemes for Boys and Girls




Mahesh Sahoo














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has any mechanism to monitor the progress of various schemes launched by the ministry;(b) 	if so, the details of the outcome of such monitoring and progress made in the last five years; and; (c)	 the number of boys and girls benefitted under various schemes in Odisha, district-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) & (b):	The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE) has been implementing the following schemes/ programmes namely: (i) Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY); (ii) Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) scheme; (iii) National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS); and (iv) Craftsmen Training Scheme (CTS) through Industrial Training Institutes. The progress of the schemes is monitored through the respective online web portals as per details given below:Sl. No.	Schemes	Online portal	Progress made (candidates benefitted)1	PMKVY	www.skillindia.nsdcindia.org	126.82 lakh (PMKVY 1.0 and PMKVY 2.0 till 19.01.2021)2	JSS	www.jss.gov.in	15.09 lakh (from 2015-2021 as on 31.01.2021)3	NAPS	www.apprenticeshipindia.org11.01 lakh (from 2015-2021 as on 28.02.2021)4	CTS	https://www.ncvtmis.gov.in	64.01 lakh candidates enrolled from 2015 to 2019	Further, the trainings under the above schemes are also monitored through self-audit reporting, call validations and surprise visits. Besides, guidelines for “Qualitative Evaluation and Diagnostic” (QED) have been issued to the State Directorates for equipping ITIs to undertake quality improvement process through continuous and periodic assessment.(c)	The district-wise details of boys and girls who have benefitted under the Schemes viz. PMKVY, JSS, NAPS and CTS in Odisha are given at Annexure I, Annexure II, Annexure III and Annexure IV respectively.
