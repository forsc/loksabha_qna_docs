                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    DEFENCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2735






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.12.2019






Re-employment of Ex-servicemen




Rajmohan Unnithan














                        Will the Minister of





DEFENCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                     (a) the total strength of Ex-servicemen in India, State-wise;(b) whether the Government has any definitive plan/scheme to tap their skills and experience required during this years of service as they retire from the forces at comparatively early age and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government has conceived any program/scheme for their re-employmentand if so, the details thereof;(d) whether there is any uniform reservation system for the Ex-servicemen in the Central and State services and if so, the details thereof; and(e) the percentage of the Ex-servicemen in Central and State Government services?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE					(SHRI SHRIPAD NAIK)IN THE MINISTRY OF DEFENCE   ????? ????? ??????					(???? ??????? ????)   (a) Directorate General Resettlement (DGR)  maintains data on registration of              Ex-servicemen (ESM) for employment and their placement on Half Yearly basis based on data received from Rajya Sainik Boards (RSBs)/Zila Sainik Boards(ZSBs) across the country. State-wise data on ESM as on 30th June, 2019 is at Annexure ’A’.(b)  DGR conducts various Resettlement Training/ Skill Courses and prepares retiring/ retired officers and JCOs/ OR and their equivalent ranks in Air Force and Navy for a second career.Officers’ Training•	24 Weeks Management Courses at IIMs and other reputed B-Schools.•	Certificate courses in Management (usually upto 12 Weeks duration) like Management of Academic Institutions, Supply Chain Management, Retail Management etc.  •	Newly introduced courses certified by National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) in various skill sectors.•	Courses approved by Govt bodies like DG Shipping, DG Civil Aviation etc.•	60% Course fee is paid by DGR while 40% is paid by each individual officer.•	 Widows of officers are also eligible to undergo above courses.JCOs/OR and Equivalents’ Training •	Courses include IT, Security, Ma¬nagement and other Skill Sectors approved by NSDC as also courses conducted by Govt institutions/approved by Government Bodies.•	100% Course fee is paid by DGR.Widows/one dependent of JCOs/ORs are eligible to undergo any course sponsored by DGR.(c)  Yes, Sir. The following self-employment schemes run by DGR are in vogue to assist ESM for transition to civil jobs:-(i)	Allotment of BPCL/IOCL Company Owned Company Operated (COCO) Outlets/Retail Pumps(ii)	Management of CNG Station(iii)	Allotment of Regular LPG Distributorship(iv)	Security Agency Scheme(v)	ESM Coal Loading and Transportation Scheme(vi)	Allotment of Mother Dairy Milk Booths and Fruit & Vegetable (Safal) Shops(vii)	Retail Outlet Dealership (Petrol/Diesel)(viii) LPG Agency under Rajiv Gandhi Gramin LPG Vitrak (RGGLV) Scheme(d)  The details of reservation of Ex-servicemen in the Central Government jobs are as under.(i)	  Reservation in Central Govt. Ministries/ Departments is as under:?	10% direct recruitment posts upto the level of Assistant Commandant in Central Para Military Force.?	10% Direct recruitment posts in Group ‘C’.?	20% Direct recruitment posts in Group ‘D’.(ii)	Reservation in Central Public Sector Enterprise?	14.5% in Group ‘C’ Posts. ?	24.5% in Group ‘D’ Posts ( including 4.5% for disabled ESM/ dependents of servicemen killed in action)    (iii)	Reservation in Nationalized Banks?	 14.5% in Group ‘C’ Posts.?	24.5% in Group ‘D’ Posts    (including 4.5% for disabled ESM/dependents of servicemen killed in action)  (iv)	100% in Defence Security Corps.State Governments have also reserved State Government jobs for Ex-servicemen.  The percentage and groups in which these jobs have been reserved for Ex-servicemen by the State Governments varies from State to State.  (e)  DGR compiles data regarding implementation of reservation  policy for Ex-servicemen in Central Government Ministries/Departments, Nationalized Banks/Financial Institutions, Central Public Sector Enterprises(CPSEs) and Central Armed Police Forces(CAPF) on half yearly basis. The data as on 30th June, 2019 is at Annexure ’B’.DGR also maintains state-wise data on registration of Ex-servicemen for employment and their placement on half yearly basis based on data received from Rajya Sainik Boards/Zila Sainik Boards. The data as on 30th June 2019 is at Annexure ‘C’.*****
