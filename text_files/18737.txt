Procurement of Paddy and Other Crops




Keshari Devi Patel














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the quantum of paddy and other crops procured by the various procurement agencies including the number of procurement centres set up by these agencies in Uttar Pradesh during the last two years and the current year, agency and district-wise; (b) 	the steps being taken by the Government to procure farmers‘ crops at most and the details thereof; (c) 	the details of the arrangement made for the safe storage of the crops procured from the farmers, districtwise; and (d) 	the quantum of paddy and the other crops perished for not having the safe storage for the crops procured in Uttar Pradesh during the last five years, district-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E R MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a): 	Department of Food & Public Distribution (DFPD) extends  price support for procurement of paddy and wheat for central pool which are procured by the Food Corporation of India (FCI) and State Government agencies.   Further, Coarsegrains are procured at MSP by State Governments  as per the procurement plan prepared in consultation with FCI and approved by the Central Government, subject to  its distribution by the State Government  under National Food Security Act (NFSA)/ Other Welfare Schemes (OWS). Agency-wise number of procurement centres set up by FCI and other procuring agencies for the procurement of wheat, paddy & maize and quantity procured during the last two years and the current year in Uttar Pradesh is enclosed as Annexure- I, II, and III.District-wise procurement of wheat and paddy by FCI and other procuring agencies during the last two years and the current year in Uttar Pradesh is enclosed as Annexure-IV &V respectively.(b): 	The following steps are taken to procure farmers’ crops at most and to ensure that farmers  get remunerative price:i) 	Before the onset of each marketing season, Department of Food and Public Distribution, Government of India (GOI) holds a consultative meeting with the officials of Food Corporation of India, State Governments and others to make an assessment of availability of wheat and paddy/rice for procurement at MSP based on the prospects of production and market situation. ii) Minimum Support Price (MSP) operations are given wide publicity through pamphlets, banners, sign boards, radio, TV and advertisements through print & electronic media. iii) Farmers are made aware of the quality specifications and purchase system, etc. to facilitate bringing their produce conforming to the specifications. iv) Procurement centres are opened by respective State Government Agencies/  Food Corporation of India, taking into account the production, marketable surplus, convenience of farmers and availability of other logistics / infrastructure such as storage and transportation, etc. Large number of temporary purchase centres, in addition to the existing Mandis and depots/godowns are also established at key points for the convenience of farmers.(c): 	Grain available with FCI is stored and preserved scientifically.
