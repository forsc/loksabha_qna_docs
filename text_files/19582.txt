                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

710






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Impact of COVID-19 on Integrated Child Development Services




K. Muraleedharan













                                                    Anto  Antony
                                                








                                                    Karti P. Chidambaram
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    710.    	SHRI K. MURALEEDHARAN: SHRI ANTO ANTONY: SHRI KARTI P. CHIDAMBARAM:  Will the Minister of WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT be pleased to state:  (a) 	whether there has been a decline in the registration of beneficiaries under Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) scheme between 2014 and 2020, if so, the details thereof and reasons therefor;  (b) 	the total number of child beneficiaries under the ICDS scheme since 2014, year-wise;  (c) 	the total number of pregnant and lactating mothers registered as beneficiaries under ICDS scheme since 2014, year-wise;  (d) 	whether COVID-19 pandemic disrupted the implementation of ICDS scheme and if so, the details thereof;  (e) 	whether any additional duties or responsibilities were entrusted upon Anganwadi centres during the pandemic and if so, the details thereof; and  (f) 	whether the Ministry is considering any proposal to increase the honorarium paid to Anganwadi workers and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a) to (c)    Year-wise details of beneficiaries including children (6 months - 6 years) and Pregnant Women & Lactating Mothers under Anganwadi Services Scheme between the years 2014 - 2020 is Annexed.   Data available is as per digitized records.(d) & (e) During COVID-19, functioning of Anganwadi Centres were permitted as per guidelines issued by Government from time to time.  Accordingly, the distribution of food items and nutrition support were provided by Anganwadi Workers once in 15 days at the doorstep of beneficiaries, e.g., children, women and lactating mothers Anganwadi Workers and Helpers were engaged in COVID-19 awareness drive  and specifically assigned responsibilities to generate awareness about proper sanitation and health education to children and their parents,  conduct of community surveillance, etc.  (f)   Government of India has enhanced the honorarium of AWWs from ?3,000/- to ?4,500/- per month; AWWs at Mini- AWCs from ?2,250/- to ?3,500/- per month and AWHs from ?1,500/- to ?2,250/- per month .                                  *****AnnexureANNEXURE REFERRED IN REPLY TO PART (a), (b) & (c) OF LOK SABHA UNSTARRED QUESTION NO. 710 FOR  05.02.2021 REGARDING “IMPACT OF COVID-19 ON INTEGRATED CHILD DEVELOPMENT SERVICES” ASKED BY SHRI K. MURALEEDHARAN, SHRI ANTO ANTONY AND SHRI KARTI P.
