                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4514






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






Underground Optical Fibre Cable




Sadashiv Kisan Lokhande














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of Gram Panchayats (GPs) connected by underground Optical Fibre Cable (OFC) as on date, district-wise; (b) 	the details of complaints received regarding laying of cable lines and the action taken thereon; (c) 	whether GPs have not been provided with telephone connections despite completion of cable laying works; and (d) 	if so, the details of such GPs along with the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS (SHRI DEVUSINH CHAUHAN)(a)	BharatNet project is implemented in a phased manner to provide broadband connectivity to all Gram Panchayats (GPs) in the country. The District-wise number of Service Ready GPs on Optical Fibre Cable (underground/aerial) is given in Annexure-I.(b)	The details of complaints received regarding laying of cable lines and the action taken thereon is given at Annexure-II.(c) & (d)	The infrastructure created under BharatNet project can be utilized for provisioning of broadband/internet services through Wi-Fi Hotspots, Fibre to the Home (FTTH) connections, leased lines, dark fibre, backhaul to mobile towers, etc. The work of providing broadband facility (Wi-Fi hotspots and Fibre to the Home connections) in Phase-I GPs is implemented with funding from Universal Service Obligation Fund (USOF). As on 28.02.2022, total 2,13,834 Fibre to the Home (FTTH) connections have been provided and Wi-Fi hotspots have been installed in 1,04,288 GPs.Further, Bharat Broadband Network Limited (BBNL), the Executing Agency for BharatNet project, has signed 70 Agreements with Service Providers for providing broadband services across various States by utilizing BharatNet Phase-I network in the country. Further, four Agreements have been signed for providing services at pan-India level.For BharatNet Phase-II, the respective Implementing Agencies are responsible for the utilisation of the created network.
