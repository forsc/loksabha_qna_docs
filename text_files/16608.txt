                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

428






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.09.2020






Cultivation of Kharif Crop




Ramdas Chandrabhanji Tadas














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether Kharif crop has been cultivated more in the year 2020-21 in comparison to the previous years; (b) 	whether the Government proposes to increase Government prices of Kharif crops like paddy, soyabean, maize, jowar, bajra etc, if so, the details thereof;		(c) 	whether soyabean crops in Wardha, Amravati and Aavtmal in Vidarbh is ruined; (d) 	whether the Government has formulated any scheme for providing compensation for the loss to the farmers; and (e) 	if so, by when it is likely to be done?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????            ( SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)  (a):   Yes Sir. As per the reports from the States kharif crops have been sown in an area of 1104.54 lakh ha during 2020-21 as compared to 1045.18 lakh ha as on 11.09.2020, which is higher by 59.36 lakh ha.(b):	The Govt. of India has already increased and notified Minimum Support Price of crops for 2020 as given under:Contd…2/--2-                                                                               (Rs per quintals)Sl.No.	Kharif Crops	2019-20	2020-211	Paddy (Common)	1815	1868	         (Grade A)	1835	18882	Jowar  (Hybrid)	2550	2620	             (Maldandi)	2570	26403	Bajra	2000	21504	Ragi	3150	32955	Maize	1760	18506	Tur (Arhar)	5800	60007	Moong	7050	71968	Urad	5700	60009	Groundnut	5090	527510	Sunflower Seed	5650	588511	Soyabeen (yellow)	3710	388012	Sesamum	6485	685513	Nigerseed	5940	669514	Cotton (Medium Staple)	            5255	               5515	     (Long Staple)	5550	582515	Jute	3950	422516	Sugarcane*	275	285* Fair & remunerative price (FRP)(c):    The State Government of Maharashtra has informed that as per primary report soyabean has been affected in 162169 hectares due to continuous rain and pest attack. The district-wise affected area is as below: Sl.No.	District	Area affected as per primary report (ha.)1	Amravati	442772	Akola	60003	Buldhana	24004	Washim	130955	Wardha	431456	Nagpur	450747	Chandrapur	73608	Dhule	818	    Total	162169Contd…3/--3-(d) & (e):	The State Government is primarily responsible for taking necessary relief measures in the wake of natural calamities. For undertaking relief measures, funds are available with the State Government in the form of State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF), Additional financial assistance, over and above SDRF, is considered from National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF) for natural calamities of severe nature and is approved on the basis of Memorandum received from State Government, in accordance with established procedure. In order to insulate farmers against the loss of crop due to natural calamities. In order to insulate farmers against the loss of crop due to natural calamities, Government of India had launched Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) from April 2016.********
