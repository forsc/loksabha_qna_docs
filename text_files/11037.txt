                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

56






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2020






GST Compensation




P.R. Natarajan













                                                    Balubhau (Alias Suresh Narayan) Dhanorkar
                                                








                                                    Arvind Ganpat Sawant
                                                








                                                    A. Ganeshamurthi
                                                








                                                    Balashowry Vallabbhaneni
                                                








                                                    Kani K. Navas
                                                








                                                    A.K.P. Chinraj
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	whether many States are suffering high revenue shortfall since implementation of the Goods and Services Tax (GST) and if so, the details thereof since its implementation, State-wise and monthwise; b)	the details of GST reimbursement cess/compensation paid to the State Governments including that becoming due to the States as on date from its inception, State-wise including Maharashtra and year-wise along with the details of GST compensation pending to be paid to each States, month-wise along with the reasons therefor and the time-frame fixed in this regard; c)	whether it is true that the Union Government has released Rs. 35,298 crore to compensate various States for revenue losses due to implementation of GST for two months for August and September, 2019 and still has to pay compensation for the months of October and November, 2019; d)	if so, the details thereof along with the names of such States including Tamil Nadu to which amount has been released including the share/percentage of each State;e)	whether any timeline has been fixed for the clearance of pending payments to the States and continuance of such refunds to the States in future and if so, the details thereof;f)	whether many States have also been demanding for extending the period of GST compensation paid to the States beyond 2020 and if so, the details thereof and the decision taken in this regard along with the proposal, if any, to increase the GST compensation cess to the States in future;g)	whether the collection of revenue under GST has been achieved as estimated at the time of its inception and if so, the details of estimated demand, collection and balance as on date along with the details of arrears of GST collection from any companies/corporates as on date, company/ corporate-wise; andh)	whether the Government is considering restructuring of GST slabs and rates and new mechanism to handle revenue shortfall and the GST compensation to States and if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN MINISTRY OF FINANCE(Shri Anurag Singh Thakur)(a) to (e): As per provisions in Section 7 of the GST (Compensation to States) Act, 2017 loss of revenue to the States shall be calculated and compensation for loss of revenue to the State shall be provisionally calculated and released at the end of every two months’ period and shall be finally calculated for every financial year after receipt of final revenue figures as audited by the Comptroller and Auditor General of India, for 5 years. For providing such compensation to the States, compensation cess is being levied on certain luxury and demerit goods as per provisions in Section 8 of the GST (Compensation to States) Act, 2017, and compensation cess is being credited into a non-lapsable Fund known as Goods and Services Tax Compensation Fund which forms part of the Public Account of India. Accordingly, total GST compensation of Rs. 48785.35 cr, Rs. 81141.41 cr and Rs. 81043 cr has been  released to States/ UTs for FY 2017-18, 2018-19 and 2019-20 respectively as per details given in Annexure-I, Annexure-II and Annexure-III. (f): As per GST (Compensation to States) Act, 2017, the provisions for compensating loss arising on account of implementation of GST have been made for 5 years till 30th June, 2022.(g) The details of central Goods and Services Tax (GST) revenue collection and Targets (RE) during FY 2017-18, FY 2018-19 and FY 2019-20 till December, 2019 are tabulated below as:-(Amount in Crore) Tax Head	FY 2017-18	FY 2018-19	FY 2019-20	RE	Actual	RE	Actual	BE	Actual [April-December] (provisional)CGST	221400	203261	503900	457534	526000	368052IGST	161900	176688	50000	28948	28000	5423GST 	61331	62612	90000	95081	109343	70534Total	444631	442561	643900	581563	663343	444009(h): The GST rate with regard to goods & services is determined by the GST council as and when required. Presently no such proposal is under consideration with the Government.
