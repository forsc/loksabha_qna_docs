                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4388






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






Employment for Specially Abled




 Dr. T. Sumathy (a) Thamizhachi Thangapandian














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware that nearly 65% of the specially-abled have been out of employment, of which only one percent are from the organised sector during the lockdown and if so, the details thereof,  State-wise; (b) if not, the number of specially-abled who have been out of employment since the lockdown and the unemployment rate, State-wise;(c) the steps taken by the Government to mitigate this problem;(d) whether it is true that most of those ‘employed’ are engaged in the unorganised sector or doing odd jobs like vending, begging, if so, the measures  being taken by the Government to protect them; and(e) if not, the details of the jobs in which they are employed, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI KRISHANPAL GURJAR)(a) and (b) As per Census 2011, 36% of the total persons with disabilities (PwDs) are workers. State-wise number of PwD workers as per Census 2011 is at Annexure. The Ministry has not separately conducted any survey regarding loss of employment of PwDs during lockdown due to COVID-19 pandemic.(c) to (e) The Government has enacted the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (RPwD) Act, 2016 which came into force on 19.04.2017. Section 34(1) of said Act mandates reservation of not less than four per cent. of the total number of vacancies in Government establishments for persons with benchmark disabilities (disability of 40% or more). In order to broaden the scope for employment for persons with benchmark disabilities in Government establishments, the Ministry on 04.01.2021 notified the list of 3566 posts (1046 in Group A, 515 in Group B, 1724 in Group C and 281 in Group D) suitable for various categories of persons with benchmark disabilities. Further, the Government implements the National Action Plan for Skill Development of Persons with Disabilities so as to enhance their scope of employability in both Government and private sectors.      The Ministry also implements incentive scheme for providing employment to persons with disabilities (PwDs) in the private sector.The Ministry has issued comprehensive disability inclusive guidelines to all States/UTs on 26.03.2020 for protection and safety of persons with disabilities (Divyangjan) during COVID – 19. The Ministry of Rural Development has informed that due to spread of pandemic coronavirus disease (COVID-19), Rs. 80 crore has been released to the States/UTs under Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Yojna (PMGKYP) for payment of ex-gratia of Rs. 1000/- in two instalments (Rs. 500 each) to 8 lakh existing beneficiaries of Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme (IGNDPS).State-wise details of PwD employees are not maintained in the Ministry.*****
