                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5104






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.04.2022






Funds for NCAP




 Dr. T. Sumathy (a) Thamizhachi Thangapandian













                                                    Kinjarapu Ram Mohan Naidu
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	the details of funds utilized till date by each city out of total Rs. 375.44 crores released for initiating actions at city level for 114 non-attainment cities from 2018-2019 to 2020-2021 along with the activities undertaken, city-wise;(b) 	the details of any under-utilization of funds along with reasons for the same;(c) 	whether the Government monitors the activities undertaken using the said funds and if so, the details of monitoring mechanism for the same and if not, the reasons therefor;(d) 	the details of share of fund transferred to Urban Local Bodies for activities enlisted under National Clean Air Programme (NCAP); and(e) 	the details of other sources of funding for nonattainment cities to combat air pollution?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b):City-wise details of funds utilized till date is given at Annexure –I. The funds are released to support the implementation of City Specific action plans (CAPs) for the improvement of Air Quality. These CAPs include activities such as expansion of monitoring network, construction and demolition waste management facilities, non-motorised transport infrastructure, green buffers zone, use of mechanical street sweepers, composting units etc. (c) Under National Clean Air Program (NCAP), the Centrallevel Steering Committee, Monitoring Committee and Implementation Committee have been constituted and periodic review of the implementation progress is conducted. Also, State Level Steering Committee Chaired by Chief Secretary ; Monitoring Committee chaired  by Principal Secretary Environment Department;  City/ District level Implementation Committee chaired by Municipal Commissioner/ District Magistrate have been constituted which periodically reviews the status of progress of implementation of actions under NCAP. (d)15th Finance Commission (XV-FC) has identified 42 Urban Agglomerations (UAs) with million plus population for performance based grants based on improvement in air quality for period FY 2020-21 to 2025-26 under Million-Plus Cities Challenge Fund (MPCCF) and Rs. 12,139 crore has been allocated for the said purpose.  Funds to the tune of Rs. 4400 crore in 2020-21 and  Rs. 1833 crore in 2021-22have been disbursed to these cities for implementing the activites enlisted under NCAP. The details of the funds transferred to the urban local bodies is given at Annexure- II. Further, under NCAPfunds to the tune of Rs. 472.06 crore have been released to the Non-attainment cites.(e) City Action Plans of 132 Non-Attainment cities are to be implemented by the coordinated action of the state government and its agencies at the state and city levels. The funding for implementation of these plans is also to be mobilized through the convergence of resources from various schemes of Central Government such as Swachh Bharat Mission (Urban), Atal Mission For Rejuvenation And Urban Transformation (AMRUT), Smart City Mission, Sustainable Alternative Towards Affordable Transportation (SATAT, Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Hybrid and Electric vehicle - II (FAME) etc. and resources from State/UT Governments and its agencies such as Municipal Corporations, Urban Development authorities and industrial development authorities etc. Funding from NCAP/XV-FC is a performance-based supplemental grant for funding of unfunded critical gap of action plans.***
