                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2882






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.12.2021






Internet Connectivity in Remote Areas




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is taking steps to ensure internet connectivity in remote and rural areas;(b) 	if so, the details thereof;(c) 	the number of villages in the rural areas of the country where telecommunication towers have been set up during the last three years, State/UT-wise and telecom company-wise; and(d) 	the details of the plan for the next financial year?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS (SHRI DEVUSINH CHAUHAN)(a)	& (b): Internet facility is provided in rural areas by the Government and the Telecom Service Providers (TSPs) through wireless mobile and fixed wireline connections in phased manner. As per data provided by Telecom Service Providers and field units of Department of Telecommunications, it is estimated that out of 5,97,618 inhabited villages (as per Census 2011) in the country, mobile wireless coverage has been provided in 5,72,551 villages. In addition to this, BharatNet project is envisaged in all the Gram Panchayats (GPs) and villages of the country to provide high speed internet/broadband. As on 01.11.2021, a total of 1,66,088 Gram Panchayats have been made service ready in the country with broadband infrastructure under BharatNet. (c ) Telecom towers are shared by many Telecom Service Providers (TSPs) for installation of their Base Transceiver Stations(BTSs) which radiate the mobile signals. The number of villages in the rural areas of the country where BTSs have been set up by different TSPs during the last three financial years are at Annexure- A.(d)  Telecom Towers/BTSs are installed by various Infrastructure Providers/ TSPs based on various factors such as techno-commercial viability, Right of Way (RoW) permissions etc.
