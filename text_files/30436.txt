                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1876






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2021






Economic Loss due to Road Blockages




Aparajita Sarangi














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has accounted for economic loss arising due to blockages on roads and highways due to various reasons; (b) if so, the details thereof for the last three years; (c) whether the Government has taken measures to reduce instances of blockages of roads and highways arising out of natural disasters; (d) if so, the details thereof; (e) whether the Government has assessed economic losses arising due to blockades on roads and highways during protests; and (f) if so, the details thereof for the last three years?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS (SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) to (f) No assessment of economic losses due to blockage arising due to natural disaster has been made. However, a revenue loss of Rs. 2731 Cr. has been incurred during farmer agitation since October, 2020.       As regards avoidance of blockage due to natural disaster, it is taken care of during the planning stages of road construction. Roads are built following IRC specifications, which duly take into account temperature, flood and other natural disasters.  ******
