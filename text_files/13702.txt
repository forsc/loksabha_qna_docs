                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2474






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.03.2020






Urban Poverty Data




Kunwar Danish Ali














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	whether the Government has latest data on urban poverty in the country and if so, the details thereof, State-wise;b)	the criteria adopted by the Government to measure the urban poverty;c)	whether the Government has proposed to change the criteria and if so, the reasons therefor; andd)	if not, the extent to which the present criteria is efficient in presenting a true picture of urban poverty?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS				(SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)to(d):The methodology for estimation of poverty followed by the erstwhile Planning Commission is based on the recommendations made by experts in the field from time to time. The last data on poverty estimates was issued by erstwhile Planning Commission in 2011-12 following the extant Tendulkar Committee methodology and released through a Press Note issued on 22nd July 2013. According 	to this Press Note, the poverty ratio in urban areas of the country is 13.7 percent (531.25 lakhs) during 2011-12. The State-wise information is at Annexure-I.                                        *****
