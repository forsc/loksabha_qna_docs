                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

37






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.11.2019






Economics of Education Review




H. Vasanthakumar














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware of Economics of Education review study report thatsays about Food insecurity at home affecting children’s learning and if so, the detailsthereof;(b) whether the report asks for early intervention of Government in this regard and if so,the details thereof;(c) whether it may affect country’s economy through lower human capital accumulation;and(d) if so, the details thereof and steps being taken by the Government so far?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (d): No, Sir. However, it is stated that Mid-Day Meal Scheme, is an on-goingCentrally-Sponsored Scheme which covers all school children studying in Classes IVIIIof Government, Government-Aided, Special Training Centres includingMadarsas and Maqtabs supported under Samagra Shiksha. During 2018-19, 11.98crore children were enrolled under the Scheme in 11.34 lakh school across thecountry. A total of 24.95 lakh cook-cum-helpers have been engaged by theStates/UTs.The objectives of the Mid-Day Meal Scheme are to address two of the pressingproblems for majority of children in India, viz. hunger and education by:i. Improving the nutritional status of children studying in classes I – VIII.ii. Encouraging poor children, belonging to disadvantaged sections, to attend schoolmore regularly and help them concentrate on classroom activities.iii. Providing nutritional support to children of elementary stage in drought-affectedareas during summer vacation.******
