                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1902






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2021






NOC for Height Clearance for Buildings




Mohanbhai Kalyanji Kundariya













                                                    Dipsinh Shankarsinh Rathod
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether it is a fact that the applicants are not being given height clearances in spite of the fact that their residential/commercial buildings are not falling within the  pathway/funnel  of  Rajkot  Airport  and  if  so,  the details thereof;(b) whether it is true that the applicants are compelled to pay the independent hefty aeronautical study charges on  pretext  of  height  clearance  and  if  so,  the  details thereof;(c)  whether  the  Government  proposes  to  release uniform color coded maps airport-wise across the country giving clear indication on maximum height that a building can go up within the area;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a): The No Objection Certificate for height of the buildings/ structures around airports are issued by Airports Authority of India (AAI) in accordance with the provisions of Ministry of Civil Aviation (Height Restrictions for Safeguarding of Aircraft Operations) Rules, 2015 published vide GSR 751(E) dated 30.09.2015, and subsequently amended vide Ministry of Civil Aviation (Height Restrictions for Safeguarding of Aircraft Operations) Amendment Rule, 2020 published vide GSR 770(E) dated 17.12.2020.;(b): The expenditure towards aeronautical study, amounting to Rs. 20,00,000 plus GST, is to be borne by the applicant, only in case if he/she is not satisfied with the height of the structure/ building granted by AAI as per above rules.;(c) & (d): AAI has already issued colour coded Zoning maps for 91 airports across the country.;(e): Does not arise in view of reply to points (c) & (d) above.
