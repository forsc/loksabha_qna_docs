                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3314






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2022






Textile Waste




Dushyant Singh














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government attention is drawn to increased textile waste in the country in the form of discharge of dyes into water streams and dumping of cloths into garbage; (b) if so, the details of textile waste produced in India from the year 2016- 2021, state wise; (c) whether the Government has noticed rapid increase in greenhouse gas emissions over the time in the country and if so, the details of greenhouse gas emissions from textile industry in the country from 2016- 2021; (d) whether the Government has data on recycling and waste management of waste produced by the textile industry and if so, the details thereof; and (e) whether the Government has received any proposals to set up waste management plants at the textile industries, if so, the details thereof and if not, the other Government interventions to address this issue?






ANSWER




THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR TEXTILES  (SMT. DARSHANA JARDOSH)(a):	The waste generated from textile industry such as chemical residues, ETP sludge, etc. are categorized as Hazardous Waste, while waste cloths are categorized as general waste.  Government of India has notified Hazardous and Other Wastes (Management and Transboundary Movement) Rules, 2016 (HOWM Rule, 2016) under the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 for safe handling and management of hazardous and other waste.  Waste generated from production of textile and industrial use of synthetic dyes, dye-intermediates and pigments are covered under Schedule-I of the HOWM Rule, 2016.    Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) has prepared Standard Operating Procedures (SoPs) for utilization of waste generated from textile industry and circulated to SPCBs/PCCs for granting authorization after ensuring technical capabilities and equipment complying with the said SOPs.(b) & (d):	The state-wise details of number of hazardous waste generating units, quantity of hazardous waste generated and managed (in terms of recycling, utilization, disposal and storage) from 2016 to 2021 including textile hazardous waste,  are provided at Annexure-I, II, III, IV and V. (c) & (e): As per Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, which is dealing with the subject, specific information regarding greenhouse gas emissions from textile industry and proposals to set up waste management plants at the textile industries is not available with Central Pollution Control Board.
