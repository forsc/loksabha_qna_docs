                                    FINANCE

UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1240






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.09.2020






Centrally Funded Projects in Assam




Abdul Khaleque














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	the details of utilisation of Central fund for various schemes in the State of Assam;b)	the total amount of unutilised fund which was allocated to Assam in the last five years; andc)	the allocation for central funded projects in Assam in the current fiscal?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE (SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR) (a) & (b): 	The Central Government transfers funds to States under Tax devolution from divisible pool of Central taxes and duties, Finance Commission Grants, Centre’s share in Centrally Sponsored Schemes (CSS)/ Central Sector Schemes (CS) and through other transfers like Assistance to States from National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF), Special Assistance, Additional Central Assistance for Externally Aided Projects (EAP) etc. Other Central transfers like Centre’s share in CSS/CS to the State Governments including State Government of Assam are released by the concerned line Ministries/Departments as per extant scheme guidelines and utilization of funds during the previous years. The concerned State Government(s) and Central Ministries/ NITI Aayog reviews and monitor implementation of the schemes under the provisions of General Financial Rules (GFR) to ensure effective utilization of funds. Further, External Assistance/Loans received from Multilateral and bilateral agencies are passed on to the States in the form of Additional Central Assistance for Externally Aided Projects (EAP).
