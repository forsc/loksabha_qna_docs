                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

600






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2021






Shortage of Housing




A. Chellakumar














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has undertaken any study in the recent past to find out the shortage of housing especially for lower and medium income categories keeping in view the present demand and availability of houses in the country;(b) 	if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise;(c) 	whether the Government has formulated any plan in this regard; and(d) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                                    (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)&(b) ‘Land’ and ‘colonization’ being State subjects, the primary responsibility of undertaking surveys and assessment about the need for urban dwellings for different segments of the society lies with the State Governments. However, Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (erstwhile Ministry of Housing and Urban Poverty Alleviation) constituted a Technical Group (TG-12) on estimation of Urban Housing shortage of the country for the Twelfth Five Year Plan (2012-17). As per the Report of this Committee, the total housing shortage at the beginning of the 12th Plan period i.e. in 2012, was 18.78 million. After launch of Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana-Urban (PMAY-U)on 25.06.2015, States/Union Territories (UTs) have been requested to undertake demand survey for assessing demand of housing in urban areas. The assessed demand reported by States/UTs so far is around 112 lakh.State-wise Urban Housing shortage estimated by Technical Group on estimation of Urban Housing and Distribution of Housing Shortage and demand assessed by States/UTs under PMAY-U are at Annexure-I. (c)&(d) Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA) has been implementing PMAY-U for addressing the housing requirement of eligible beneficiaries of EWS/ Low Income Group (LIG), as per scheme guidelines, in urban areas. Scope of Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS) under PMAY-Uwas extended to include Middle Income Group (MIG)with effect from 01.01.2017.Under the scheme, about 110 lakh houses have been sanctioned so far as per details at Annexure - II.                                                 *****
