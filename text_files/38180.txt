                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5277






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.04.2022






Imparting Traing to Forest Workers




Vijay Baghel













                                                    Ranjeetsinha Hindurao Naik-Nimbalkar
                                                








                                                    Sudhakar Tukaram Shrangre
                                                








                                                    Arun Sao
                                                








                                                    Devji Mansingram Patel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has formulated any comprehensive policy to impart traing to forest  workers regarding the latest scientific and technological developments in forestry sector in the country;(b) 	if so, the details thereof;(c) 	whether the Union Government has received proposals from various State Governments regarding setting up of Forest Academy for the said purpose;(d) 	if so, the details thereof, State-wise;(e) 	the total number of forest workers imparted training in the country, State-wise details thereof particularly Maharashtra, Rajasthan and Chhattisgarh during the last three years; and(f) 	the details of total funds allocated and spent in this regard?	






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b) Yes Sir. Forestry training is provided by both Central Government and State Governments/Union Territory Administrations. The Indira Gandhi National Forest Academy (IGNFA), Dehradun and Central Academy for State Forest Service (CASFOS) under the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MOEF&CC) impart training to the Indian Forest Service (IFS) officers and State Forest Service (SFS) officers respectively. The Directorate of Forest Education (DFE) also undertakes training for Range Forest Officers (RFOs) at its selected centres as per the need.The DFE also coordinates the curriculum for the training of Range Forest Officers (RFOs) at different Range Forest Officer Training Colleges established and run by the respective State Governments. These institutes provide training on latest relevant contents in scientific and technological development including on silvi culture, restoration of degraded forests, improved afforestation techniques, wildlife conservation, survey of forests, and utilization of forest products. Such training also exposes the forest officers and staff to latest techniques including Remote Sensing, Geographical Information System (GIS), Information Technology (IT) and digital applications. Besides, the State Governments run various Forestry Training Academies/Training Schools to impart training to frontline forest officers and staff subjects in the field of forestry.(c) & (d) No Sir. The Ministry has not received any proposals from State Governments regarding setting up of Forest Academy for the said purpose;(e)  & (f) This Ministry doesn’t collate the number of forest workers imparted training in the country. However, the details of total fund spent by the Ministry for the training of IFS/SFS/RFOs during the last three years are as follow:-Year2019	2020	2021Total funds spent (?) 	43.939 Cr	28.63 Cr	29.10 Cr***
