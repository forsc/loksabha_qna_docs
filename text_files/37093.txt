                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4212






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.03.2022






Increasing Procurement Limit under MIS




Rahul Kaswan














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has received any proposal to increase the maximum limit of procurement cost from 25 per cent to 50 per cent proportionally borne by the Government of India and State Government to compensate loss on the sale of grains bought at cost price by State Government under Market Intervention Scheme (MIS);(b)	whether any proposal has been received with regard to providing 3 per cent interest subvention to the farmers who have repaid their crop loans in a time bound manner; and(c)	if so, the details thereof, Sate-wise including Rajasthan? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ??????? ?????? 	(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) : No such proposal has been received.(b) & (c) : 	Government of India is implementing the Interest Subvention Scheme(ISS) with a view to provide short term agri-loans to the farmers at concessional rate of interest.  Under the scheme, short term crop loan upto Rs. 3.00 lakh is available to farmers engaged in Agriculture and other allied activities at benchmark rate 9%,  Government of India provides 2% Interest Subvention (IS) on the benchmark rate.  Additional 3% subvention is also given to the farmers for prompt and timely repayment of loans; thus the effective rate of Interest comes down to 4% per annum. The benefits of ISS have also been extended to farmers in allied activities like Animal Husbandry and Fisheries to existing KCC holders within the overall limit of Rs.3.00 lakh and issue of new KCC for Animal Husbandry and Fisheries farmers with provision of benefit of IS & prompt repayment incentive (PRI) for loan amount upto Rs.2.00 lakh per annum. The interest subsidy provided to the banks (IS 2%) and farmers (3% PRI) in the last two years and current year are given as under:   	(in Rs. Crore)S. No.	Year	Final Grant1	2019-20	16236.862	2020-21	19831.753	2021-22	18142.3Under ISS, no direct funds/subsidy is released to the State Government or farmers.  Funds are released to Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) for Settlement of claims received under the scheme from various Banks.*****
