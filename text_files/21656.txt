                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2796






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2021






Treatment Packages under AYUSH




Upendra Singh Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                            Will the Minister of AYURVEDA,YOGA AND NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) the number of treatment packages being covered under AYUSH scheme, scheme-wise, stream-wise, State/ UT-wise;(b) whether the Government is planning to include more treatment packages under this Scheme and if so, the details thereof along with the reasons therefor; and(c) whether the Government has received any proposals from the States including Uttar Pradesh in this regard and if so, the details thereof and the time by which these are likely to be included?






ANSWER




                                                        (a):	There are Ayurveda-20, Unani-50, Siddha-27, Yoga-8, Naturopathy-8 treatment packages which have been covered under Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS) for treatment as per Ayurveda, Unani, Siddha, Yoga & Naturopathy systems. The number of procedures in Ayurveda-97, Unani-86, Siddha-49, Yoga-27 and Nauropathy-31 are also covered under this Scheme in all the CGHS covered cities wherever AYUSH wellness centres are functioning. (b): 	No.(c):	The government has not received any proposal.
