                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

703






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2021






Plan for Promotion of Indian Textile Industry




Badruddin Ajmal














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has outlined any plan and brought any policies during last five years for promotion of Indian textile industry which is facing challenges and tough competition in foreign market; (b) if so, the detail thereof; (c) whether the Government has identified some reasons including shortage of raw materials, increase in the textile input prices, obsolete machinery, power shortage, heavy excise duties and sick mills etc. for the stagnation of the Indian textile industry; (d) if so, the action plan formulated by Government to resolve these problems; and (e) the detail of the ways to promote domestic manufacturing and exports in the sector?






ANSWER




THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF TEXTILES  (SMT. DARSHANA JARDOSH)(a) & (b):	Several measures have been taken by Government to promote export of Indian textile. Some of the important measures are outlined below:(i)	Government launched special package of Rs. 6000 crores for Textile and Apparel sector in June, 2016 to boost employment and export potential in apparel and made-ups segments. Package also consisted of the Remission of State Levies (RoSL) scheme till 06.03.20219.(ii)	The RoSL scheme was replaced by Rebate of State and Central Taxes and Levies (RoSCTL) with effect from 07.03.2019 to 31.03.2020.(iii)	On 14thJanuary,2020, Rs. 600 crore was allocated for issuing scrips for special one-time ad-hoc incentive of up to 1% of FoB value provided for exports of apparel and made-ups to offset the difference between RoSCTL & RoSL + Merchandise Exports from India Scheme (MEIS) @ 4%  from 17.03.2019 to 31.12.2019. (iv)	On 14th July, 2021, Government has decided to continue RoSCTL Scheme till 31.03.2024.(c) to (e):	Government is implementing various schemes such as Amended Technology Upgradation Scheme(ATUF), National Handloom Development Programme(NHDP), National Handicraft Development Programme, Comprehensive Powerloom Cluster Development Scheme, Silk Samagra, SAMARTH- Scheme for Capacity Building in Textiles Sector, Technical Textiles etc. for overall development and promotion of domestic manufacturing and exports in textile sector in the country. Further, in order to ensure adequate availability of raw material at competitive price in the country, Government has removed anti-dumping duty on Purified Terephthalic Acid (PTA) (a key raw material for the manufacture of MMF fibre and yarn) originating in or exported from the People''s Republic of China, Iran, Indonesia, Malaysia, Taiwan, Korea R P and Thailand to India and also removed anti-dumping duty on acrylic fibre (raw material for acrylic yarn and knitwear industry) originated in or exported from Thailand to India. ***
