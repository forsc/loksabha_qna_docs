                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2319






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.07.2019






Recruitment Process for Universities




Sunil Kumar Mondal














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government plans to take steps to make recruitment process forUniversity teaching posts more efficient and if so, the details thereof; and(b) the steps taken/being taken by the Government to make the teaching profession moreattractive to draw in good talent and if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) & (b): Yes, Sir. The University Grants Commission (UGC), vide its D.O. letter No.F.1-14/2019 (CPP-II) dated 4th June, 2019, has prepared the Guidelines for Recruitment ofFaculty in Universities, Colleges and Institutions Deemed to be Universities outlining theselection procedure and the time frame for recruitment. These guidelines have beencirculated to all Universities to adhere to them. The Universities has also been requestedto take steps to ensure that the vacancies in the University as well as in the Collegesaffiliated to University are filled up at the earliest. A copy of the Guidelines circulated to allUniversities is available at https://www.ugc.ac.in/ugc_notices.aspx?id=2418.The Government has taken several steps to make the teaching profession more attractiveto draw in good talent. The Ministry had revised pay of teachers and equivalent cadres inuniversities and colleges based on the recommendations of 7th Central PayCommission.UGC had issued UGC(Minimum Qualifications for Appointment of Teachersand other Academic Staff in Universities and Colleges and other Measures for theMaintenance of Standards in Higher Education) Regulations, 2018 for minimumqualifications for appointment and other service conditions of University and Collegeteachers and cadres of Librarians, Directors of Physical Education and Sports formaintenance of standards in higher education. The said Regulation is intended to attractand retain the best quality teachers and to encourage research in Universities andColleges. The aforesaid regulation is available on UGC website athttps://www.ugc.ac.in/pdfnews/4033931_UGC-Regulation_min_Qualification_Jul2018.pdf.Through Operation Faculty Recharge Programme, UGC has created a pool of high qualityteachers at all levels for deployment in the Universities.Faculty Recharge is intended tostrengthen high quality research in science related disciplines at internationally competitivelevel and promote innovative teaching in the universities through induction of fresh talentat all levels of academic hierarchy, in selected departments/ centres. Individuals withexceptional creativity, zeal and commitment to research and teaching are to be selected.The guidelines of aforesaid UGC Operation Faculty Recharge Programmeis available athttps://www.ugc.ac.in/oldpdf/xiplanpdf/fucaltyrechargeguideline.pdf.*****
