                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

861






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.11.2019






Fund Raising by Air India




Pratima Mondal














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether Air India aims to meet its fund requirement by raising loans against the Government guarantee; and(b) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) & (b): The Government is committed to the disinvestment of Air India. The Government approved a Plan for Air India which focused on, inter-alia, operational efficiency, increase in revenue, cost saving etc. as well as a comprehensive financial package. The financial support, inter-alia, included providing Government guarantee of Rs. 7600 crore to Air India Limited for raising new debt for payment of stretched liability for the FY 2018-19. Out of the Rs. 7600 crore guarantee provided to Air India,  Air India raised Rs. 2500 crore from banks against this guarantee. Three loans have been drawn from NSSF in FY 2018-19 amounting to Rs. 2636 crore. Air India has sought Government guarantee for Rs. 1000 crore immediately, for raising funds from bank (this amount is within the limit of Rs. 7600 crore).
