                                    QUESTION NO:

                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2021






Customs, Culture etc. of Tribal Society




Guman Singh Damor














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total number of tribal groups across the country along with the State-wise details thereof; (b) the details of the steps taken for the conservation of various customs, culture and traditions of the tribal society so far; (c) whether there is any scheme to protect the religious places of tribal society; (d) if so, the State-wise details thereof; and (e) if not, whether the Government is likely to consider such a scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR TRIBAL AFFAIRS(SMT. RENUKA SINGH SARUTA)   (a): There are over 700 Scheduled Tribes notified under Article 342 of the Constitution of India, spread over different States and Union Territories (UTs) of the Country.  State/UT wise details are given at Annexure .(b):Ministry of Tribal Affairs is implementing two schemes namely “Support to Tribal Research Institutes (TRIs)” and “Tribal Festival, Research, Information and Mass Education”. Through these schemes, funds are provided to State Governments to strengthen and support the TRIs in their infrastructure needs, research & documentation activities, evidence based action and applied research to find solution and create replicate models of development, construction and maintenance of tribal museums, organization of tribal festivals, programs for preservation of tribal custom, traditions and cultural heritage in dance, music, painting etc. and training and capacity building programs. (c) to (e): Ministry of Tribal Affairs under its scheme “Support to Tribal Research Institutes (TRIs)” provides funds to State Governments for promotion and preservation of tribal custom, traditions and cultural heritage.
