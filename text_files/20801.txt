                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1931






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.02.2021






Research on Medicinal Plants in  Universities/Institutes




Shankar Lalwani













                                                    Bharatiben Dhirubhai Shyal
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY ,UNANI, SIDDHA ANDHOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) the number of universities/institutions which are carrying out research on medicinal plants in the country, State/UT-wise including Gujarat;(b) whether research in such institutions are beneficial in conceptualizing new formulations of Ayurvedic medicines;(c) if so, the details thereof; and(d) the steps being taken/likely to be taken by the Government to make the country a Global leader in Ayurveda?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS AND ADDITIONAL CHARGE OF MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(SHRI KIREN RIJIJU)(a): Total 57 universities/institutions are carrying out research on medicinal plants in the country, including Gujarat through research & development projects supported by  National Medicinal Plants Bard (NMPB), Ministry of AYUSH, under its Central Sector Scheme on ‘Conservation, Development and Sustainable Management of Medicinal Plants’. In addition, 42 research institutions / centre / units functioning under Research Councils, Ministry of AYUSH is also engaged to carry out research on medicinal plants. Besides, Council for Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR), through its 05 constituent laboratories, 58 universities/institutions supported through research & development projects by Department of Bio-technology (DBT) and Directorate of Medicinal and Aromatic Plants Research (DMAPR), Anand, Gujarat under Indian Council for Agricultural Research (ICAR) along with 26 ICAR- All India Coordinated Research Projects (AICRP) are also working to carryout research activities on medicinal plants. Details are at Annexure-I. (b)&(c):	Yes, researches undertaken at Central Council for Research in Ayurvedic Sciences under Ministry of AYUSH has developed 09 New drugs and technology has been transferred to various industries for commercialization. In addition, Council of Scientific & Industrial Research (CSIR) has developed herbal formulations by their supported researches at CSIR-CIMAP, CSIR-NBRI and CSIR-CDRI and technology has been transferred to industry for its commercialization. Details are attached at Annexure-II.(d):	To make country global leader in Ayurveda, Ministry of AYUSH undertakes various activities under Central Sector Scheme for promotion of International Cooperation, (IC Scheme) with the mandate of promotion of AYUSH among people in foreign countries. AYUSH experts are deputed to foreign countries to participate in international meetings, conferences, training programmes, seminars and on special assignment of the Government of India for promotion and propagation of AYUSH Systems. Financial assistance is also provided for presentation of AYUSH related scientific research papers. Through this Scheme incentives are provide to AYUSH drug manufacturers/ entrepreneurs/ AYUSH institutions, AYUSH drug and Hospitals etc. for participating in International exhibitions/ conferences/ workshops/ seminars/ road shows/ trade fairs, etc. for generating awareness amongst the participating public about the AYUSH systems of medicine. To boost export of AYUSH products, incentive are provided to AYUSH manufacturers for Market Authorization/ registration of AYUSH products with USFDA/EMEA/UK-MHRA/ NHPD (Canada)/ TGA and other international regulatory agencies, etc.. So far more than 50 products (Unani and Ayurveda) have been registered in 08 countries namely Kenya, USA, Russia, Latvia, Canada, Oman, Tajikistan and Sri Lanka. There is a provision of financial support up to Rs. 1.00 Crores for strengthening of Health Centre(s)/ Institution(s) in the foreign host country. As of now, Ministry of AYUSH has signed 25 Country to Country MoUs for Cooperation in field of Traditional Medicine and Homoeopathy. 23 MoUs for undertaking Collaborative Research / Academic collaboration and 13 MoUs for setting up AYUSH Academic Chairs have been signed with foreign Universities.  33 AYUSH Information Cell have been set up in 30 countries to disseminate authentic information about AYUSH systems of Medicine. Ministry of AYUSH under its Fellowship Scheme offers 104 scholarships every year to the eligible foreign nationals from 99 countries for undertaking undergraduate (UG), postgraduate (PG) and Ph. D courses in AYUSH system including Ayurveda in various premier AYUSH institutes in India. The Fellowship Scheme aims at promoting AYUSH education and achieving recognition and acceptance of our traditional medicine system abroad.
