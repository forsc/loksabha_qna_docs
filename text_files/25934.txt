                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1726






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Standard Operating Procedure for JJM




T.R.V.S. Ramesh














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has made any Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) to implement source sustainability practices under Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM);(b) if so, the details thereof; and(c) if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) & (b) Since August 2019, Government of India in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) – Har Ghar Jal to make provision of tap water supply to every rural household by 2024. In consultation with State Governments and other stakeholders working in water sector, Operational Guidelines for the implementation of Jal Jeevan Mission, inter alia which includes provision for sustainability of drinking water sources through source strengthening and augmentation measures, was firmed up and released on 25.12.2019. It is in public domain and available at: https://jalshakti-ddws.gov.in/sites/default/files/JJM_Operational_Guidelines.pdfUnder JJM, for long-term sustainability of drinking water sources and water supply systems, provisions of preparing Village Action Plan has been made, inter alia which includes augmentation and strengthening of local drinking water sources in convergence with other schemes at village level viz. MGNREGS, 15th Finance Commission tied grants to Rural Local Bodies (RLBs), Integrated Watershed Management Programme (IWMP), State schemes, District Mineral Development Fund, CSR funds, community contribution, etc. Further, Government of India has also launched Jal Shakti Abhiyan to promote rainwater harvesting and water conservation. (c) Doesn’t arise.*****
