                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

399






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.11.2019






Overflowing Food Stocks with FCI




Behanan Benny














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Ministry/Food Corporation of India has written to the Ministry of External Affairs to help reduce the burden of carrying overflowing food stocks with the Food Corporation of India (FCI) by looking into the possibility of providing surplus food as ‘humanitarian aid’ to deserving countries; (b) 	if so, the response from the Ministry of External Affairs thereto; and (c) 	the details of the quantum of the excess food stocks available for such ‘humanitarian aid’?






ANSWER




                                                         MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a):	Yes, Sir.(b):	No concrete proposal regarding liquidating the surplus stock of wheat and rice has been received from Ministry of External Affairs.(c):	As on 1.10.2019 a surplus stock of 187.96 LMT wheat and 146.66 LMT of rice is available with Food Corporation of India for humanitarian aid.****
