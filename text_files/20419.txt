                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

144






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2021






Availability of Potable Water




Heena Vijaykumar Gavit













                                                    Unmesh Bhaiyyasaheb Patil
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) aims to provide piped potable water to every rural household by 2024 and if so, the details thereof; (b) the details of the budgetary allocation made for the Mission, State-wise; (c) the details of blocks covered in Jalga on, Nandurbar, Kolhapur, Ratnagiri, Ahmednagar and Thane districts of Maharashtra; (d) the details of the number of households in Maharashtra that have received piped potable water under the Mission, district-wise; (e) whether any problem is being faced in implementation of the JJM and if so, the details thereof; and (f) whether the Government has recently reviewed the performance of JJM in the country including Bihar and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR JAL SHAKTI(GAJENDRA SINGH SHEKHAWAT)(a) to (f) A Statement of reply is laid on the Table of the House. Statement referred to in the reply to Lok Sabha Starred Question No. 144 due for reply on 11.02.2021(a)	Yes Sir. Government of India in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) – Har Ghar Jal to provide functional tap water connection to every rural household in the country by 2024. The estimated outlay of the mission is Rs. 3.60 lakh Crore. Under the mission, every household to get potable drinking water supply in adequate quantity of prescribed quality on long-term and regular basis. (b)	The details of budgetary allocations made under JJM for the year 2019-20 and 2020-21 is annexed.(c) &(d) As on 08.02.2021, as reported by the State of Maharashtra, out of 1.42 Crore rural households in the State, 86.16 lakh (60.52%) have tap water connections. District and village -wise details of number of households with tap water connections, including in Jalgaon, Nandurbar, Kolhapur, Ratnagiri, Ahmednagar and Thane districts of Maharashtra, is in public domain and available on the JJM dashboard:https://ejalshakti.gov.in/jjmreport/JJMIndia.aspx.(e)	Some major challenges, viz. presence of contaminants in ground water, paucity of dependable water sources in water–stressed areas and capacity of Gram Panchayats and local communities to plan, manage, operate & maintain the water supply schemes, and delay in release of the matching State share in some States especially during the Covid-19 pandemic, etc. are being faced in the implementation of JJM.(f)	Govt. of India has been regularly reviewing the implementation of JJM with States/ UTs including Bihar. Several meetings including conferences, workshops, review meetings through video conferences, field visits, etc. are being held from time to time. During these reviews, States/ UTs are advised to expedite the implementation to achieve the goal in a time-bound manner. ***
