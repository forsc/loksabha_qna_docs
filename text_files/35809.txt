                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3109






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






National Sports Federation




Ravneet Singh














                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has recently approvedthe continuation of scheme of assistance to NationalSports Federation in the country and if so, the detailsthereof along with the benefits to be accrued to theseNational Sports Federation by the continuation of thisscheme of assistance;(b) whether the Government is taking any othermeasures or developing a comprehensive policy for theimprovement in the functioning of the National SportsFederation in the country, if so, the details thereof State/UT-wise;(c) whether the Government is taking any measuresto improve infrastructure and training facilities at varioussports complexes in the country; and(d) if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise especiallyin Punjab?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS[SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR](a) 	Yes, Sir. Strengthening its support for sports in India, the Union Government has approved continuation of Scheme of Assistance to National Sports Federations (NSFs) with an outlay of Rs. 1575 crore for XV Finance Commission Cycle (2021-22 to 2025-26).Under this Scheme, financial support is extended NSFs for conduct of national coaching camps, procurement of sports equipment and consumables, training of athletes, hiring of foreign coaches and high performance directors, preparation and participation of Indian sportspersons / teams held in India and abroad, holding of international sporting events in India, etc.(b)	The National Sports Development Code of India, 2011 (Sports Code) is already in existence and operational. The Sports Code requires NSFs tofollow healthy management practices, accountability, transparency,impartial and transparent selection procedures, age and tenurerestrictions in respect of office bearers, basic universal principles of goodgovernance in sports, proper accounting procedures, prevention ofage fraud & sexual harassment of women, and holding of electionsas per model guidelines.(c) & (d) Yes Sir.Infrastructure facilities in centres of Sports Authority of India(SAI) are upgraded from time to time as per international standards to provide state-of-the-art training facilities to sportspersons. The details of ongoing projects at various SAI centres, across India (including Punjab),are placed at Annexure.*****
