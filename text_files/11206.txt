                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

76






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2020






Review of Mid Day Meal Scheme




Shobha Karandlaje














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is reviewing the Mid Day Meal Scheme in terms ofrevision of various components of the Scheme and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether a Joint Review Mission has been started to review the present status ofimplementation of the scheme and if so, the details thereof;(c) the number of children enrolled under MDMS database as on 31st December, 2019along with the number out of these, who had linked their Aadhaar numbers byOctober 31, 2019;(d) the number of duplicate names deleted after Aadhaar authentication, as reported byStates as on 31.10.2019; and(e) whether the Government has adopted any elaborate monitoring mechanism to ensurequality food is served to children under the Scheme and if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): As per the extant policy, all Ministries / Departments have been asked to undertakean outcome review of their ongoing Schemes at the end of 12th Five Year Plan forappraisal and further continuation. Accordingly, the Government reviewed theMid-Day Meal Scheme (MDMS) in 2018-19. The details of revisions/modificationsin the scheme are given at Annexure-I.(b): Joint Review Missions (JRMs) under the Mid-Day Meal Scheme were started in2009. So far, 11 JRMs have been conducted and 12th JRM is in process. JRMsconsisting of educational and nutritional experts, review the Scheme through fieldvisits from time to time.JRMs review various aspects of the implementation of the Scheme. The reports ofJRMs are shared with concerned States and UTs for taking suitable action on thefindings and recommendations.(c) & (d): As on 31st December, 2019 the number of children enrolled under MDMSdatabase is 11,60,05,995.As per information reported by States and UTs (except J&K, Ladakh, Assam &Meghalaya which are exempted), out of 11,02,24,554 enrolled students inElementary Level (Classes I -VIII) 9,67,52,617 (88%) students have Aadhaarnumber as on 31.10.2019. The States and UTs also reported that 6,40,435proxy/duplicate names have been deleted after Aadhaar authentication.(e): The Government has adopted an elaborate monitoring mechanism at Central, Stateand District levels to ensure quality food is served to children under the Scheme. Atnational level, an Empowered Committee, headed by Minister of Human ResourceDevelopment and also a National level Steering-cum-Monitoring Committee(NSMC) as well as Programme Approval Board (PAB) monitor the scheme andsuggest measures for its smooth and effective implementation.At the State level, a State level Steering–cum-Monitoring Committee headed by theState Chief Secretary and, at the District Level, a District Level Committee underthe Chairpersonship of the senior-most Member of Parliament of Lok Sabha fromthe district monitors the implementation of the scheme.At local level Gram Panchayats/Gram Sabhas, members of Village EducationCommittees (VECs), Parent-Teacher Associations (PTAs) and the SchoolManagement Committees (SMCs) monitor the regularity and wholesomeness of themid-day meal served to children, cleanliness in cooking and serving of the meal,timeliness in procurement of good quality ingredients, fuel, etc., implementation ofvariety in menu so as to make it attractive to children and ensuring social andgender equity on daily basis. In addition to directing States and UTs to carry outSocial Audit, the Centre constitutes Joint Review Missions (JRMs) to review thescheme through field visits from time to time.*******
