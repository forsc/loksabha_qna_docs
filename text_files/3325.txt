                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3168






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.07.2019






Exemption of Registration Fees for Battery Operated/Electric Vehicles




Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar













                                                    Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik
                                                








                                                    A. Ganeshamurthi
                                                








                                                    Su Thirunavukkarasar
                                                








                                                    Sudheer Gupta
                                                








                                                    Bidyut Baran Mahato
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the total number of electric/battery operated vehicles in operation in the country as on 	date, State/UT-wise;(b)	the amount being charged as registration fees for such electric/battery operated vehicles 	in the country at present;(c) 	whether the Government proposes exemption of registration fees for battery 	operated/electric vehicles to promote the use of eco-friendly vehicles in the country and if 	so, the details and objectives thereof;(d) 	whether the Government proposes to amend the Central Motor Vehicles Rules in this 	regard and if so, the details thereof and the time by which the amendment is likely to be 	made;(e) 	whether this step is likely to help in combating air pollution, reduce India’s dependence 	on oil imports and generate employment in the country and if so, the details thereof; and (f)	the other steps taken/being taken by the Government to boost the operation of battery 	operated/electric vehicles in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) The information regarding State/Union Territory-wise total number of electric/battery operated vehicles which are in operation in the country as on date is annexed.(b) This Ministry, vide GSR 1183 (E) dated 29.12.2016 has amended rule 81of the Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989 which specifies the amount being charged as registration fees for motor vehicles. The GSR 1183 (E) is available on the website of this Ministry. (morth.nic.in) (c) and (d) Yes, Sir. The Government has proposed exemption of registration fees for battery operated/electric vehicles to promote the use of eco-friendly vehicles in the country and has notified GSR 430(E) dated 18th June 2019 to amend the rule 81 of the Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989 for seeking comments and objections.(e) and (f) With the objective of combating air pollution, reduce India’s dependence on oil imports and generate employment in the country, the Government is promoting the use of eco-friendly vehicles in the country. To boost the operation of battery operated/ electric vehicles in the country, the Department of Heavy Industry has notified Phase-II of the FAME India Scheme [Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Electric (& Hybrid) Vehicles in India], vide S.O. 1300 dated 8th March 2019, with the approval of Cabinet with an outlay of Rs. 10,000 crore for a period of three years commencing from 1st April, 2019. The notification of the scheme and operational guidelines are available on the Department of Heavy Industry’s website [www.dhi.nic.in]. The Government has notified that the registration mark for Battery Operated Vehicles to be on a green background. The Government, vide S.O. 5333(E) dated 18th  October, 2018 has also granted exemption to the Battery Operated Transport Vehicles and Transport Vehicles running on Ethanol and Methanol fuels from the requirements of permit.
