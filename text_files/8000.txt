                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1654






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.11.2019






Hosting of Men’s Hockey World Cup




Jaisiddeshwar Shivacharya Mahaswamiji














                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether India is set to host Men’s Hockey World Cup for the fourth time;(b) if so, whether India is the only country to host these tournaments for the fourth time;(c) if so, the details thereof and the funds required for organizing this event; and(d) the number and names of countries likely to participate in this event?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS[SHRI KIREN RIJIJU](a) Yes Sir. Hockey India, the recognized National Sport Federation for discipline of Hockey in India, has confirmed that India has been allotted hosting of Men’s Hockey World Cup for the fourth time, which is scheduled in January 2023.(b) Earlier India hosted the Hockey World Cup tournaments in 1982, 2010 and 2018. The World Cup tournament in January 2023 would be the 4th such tournament in India.(c) & (d) Hockey India has informed that funds for hosting Hockey World Cup 2023 will be raised through sponsorships. As per present format 16 teams will participate in the event and details will be announced by the International Hockey Federation (FIH) close to the event.*****
