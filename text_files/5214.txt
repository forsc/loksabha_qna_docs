                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TRIBAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4713






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2019






Population of ST as per 2011 Census in Rajasthan




Kanakmal Katara














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the population of Scheduled Tribes (STs) in the country including Rajasthan as per 2011 census;(b) the details of employment oriented schemes being run for the welfare of STs at present; (c) the number of people belonging to STs benefited in Rajasthan through the said schemes during the last three years, Scheme-wise; and(d) whether there is any proposal of any special project for STs dominated areas including Rajasthan?






ANSWER




                                                        (a): As per Census 2011, total Scheduled Tribes (STs) population of India and Rajasthan is 10, 45,45,716 and 92,38,534  respectively. (b) to (c): (i)	Ministry of Tribal Affairs  administers two major schemes, namely, Grants under Article 275(1) of the Constitution and Special Central Assistance to Tribal Sub-Scheme (SCA to TSS), wherein funds are released to the State Governments  inter-alia for income generation among tribal people through skill development and various training programmes.For the employment generation in the State of Rajasthan under the scheme ‘Grants under Article 275(1) of the Constitution’ Rs. 1000 lakh covering 3.41 lakh beneficiaries in the year 2016-17 has been provided.  Further, under the scheme ‘SCA to TSS’ Rs.707 lakh covering 400 Families / 15000 tribal Self Help Group Members and Rs.204 lakh in the year 2018-19 have  been released for the same purpose.(ii)	  National Scheduled Tribes Finance and Development Corporation (NSTFDC) under the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, promotes entrepreneurial development amongst Scheduled Tribes. This Corporation provides concessional financial assistance to individuals or groups of STs for undertaking self-employment income generation activities through its channelizing agencies. Number of STs benefitted in Rajasthan under the various schemes of NSTFDC is given below:(Number of Beneficiaries)Sl. No.	Financial Year/Scheme	Term Loan	Micro Credit Scheme 	Adivasi Mahila Sashaktikaran Yojana 	Adivasi Shiksha Rrinn Yojana 1	2016-17	1267	0	207	12	2017-18	701	42	118	13	2018-19	1057	0	208	1(iii)	Under the schemes ‘Mechanism for Marketing of Minor Forest Produce (MFP) through Minimum Support Price (MSP) and Development of Value Chain for MFP’ (MSP for MFP)’ and ‘Institutional Support for Development and Marketing of Tribal Products / Produce’, Tribal Co-Operative Marketing Development Federation of India Limited (TRIFED) imparts skill development and capacity building training to tribal Minor Forest Produce (MFP) gatherers and tribal artisans. TRIFED has imparted training to 1,09,535 MFP Gatherers on different commodities. Out of these, 4148 tribal MFP Gatherers were trained in the State of Rajasthan during the period from 2008-09 to 2016-17.	(iv)	As informed by Ministry of Labour & Employment, National Career Service Centres (NCSC) for Scheduled Castes (SCs)/Scheduled Tribes (STs) have been set up to enhance the employability of SC/ST jobseekers through Counselling/Coaching/training etc. This scheme is exclusively meant for educated unemployed SC/ST candidates registered with Employment Exchange only.The number of people belonging to STs benefited in Rajasthan through the said scheme during the last three years is as under:									(Number of Beneficiaries)S.No.	Financial  Year	Vocational Guidance and Career Counselling service	Typing/Shorthand	Special Coaching Scheme	Computer Training1	2016-17	1872	34	60	1502	2017-18	3370	108	60	1503	2018-19	3574	94	60	75(v)	The Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, 2005 (MGNREGA) provides at least one hundred days of guaranteed wage employment in every financial year to every rural household including tribals whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manualwork. MGNREGS provides livelihood security i.e., fall back option for livelihood for the rural households when no better employment opportunity is available. Number of households having completed 100 days of employment under MGNREGS in Rajasthan during the last three years is given below.S.No.	Financial  Year	Households completed 100 days ofemployment (In lakh)1	2016-17	4.272	2017-18	2.283	2018-19	5.88(vi)	 Apart from the above, Government is implementing a number of schemes/programmes through which STs are also getting employment, income generating opportunities.(d): There is no proposal of any special project under consideration with the Ministry of Tribal Affairs.******
