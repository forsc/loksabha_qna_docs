                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

38






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.07.2021






Fish Mortality Rate




Sajda Ahmed














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is aware of the increase in fish mortality rate due to ecological degradation and if so, the details thereof ;(b)	the amount invested for the development of critical infrastructure in fisheries during the last three years, State-wise; and(c)	whether the Government has adopted any policy to check the pollution caused by the fishing gear in rivers, particularly by nylon and plastic materials and if so, the details thereof ? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a)	to (c):	A Statement is placed on the Table of the House.***** Statement referred to in reply to the Lok Sabha Starred Question No *38 put in by Shrimati Sajda Ahmed, Honourable Member of Parliament for answer on 20th July, 2021 regarding Fish Mortality Rate(a):	While it is a fact that the ecological degradation resulting in deterioration of water quality will have an impact on fish health, no information have been received recently in the Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying regarding increase in fish mortality rate due to ecological degradation as such.(b):	An investment to the tune of Rs.7338.83 crore has been approved during last three years for the States/Union Territories for developing production and post harvest related critical infrastructure in fisheries sector under the Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana, Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund and the erstwhile Centrally Sponsored Scheme on Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries. State-wise details of the investment approved during the last three years are furnished at Annexure.(c):	Fisheries being State subject, respective State Government/Union Territories under their respective legislations/policies, regulate and manage the fisheries in inland areas including issues such as regulation of use of fishing gear in rivers.                                     *****
