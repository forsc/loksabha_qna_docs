                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4072






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






Excavation at Palakkad Fort




Vellalath Kochukrishnan Nair. Sreekandan














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it is a fact that the Archeological Survey of India (ASI) has recovered cannon balls from Palakkad Fort which is widely known as Tipu’s Fort;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether ASI is considering to make more excavations at the said Fort to unearth many more historically important recoveries from the said Fort;(d)	whether it is also true that ASI presumes that there can be many more cannon balls buried on the premises of the said Fort;(e)	whether it is also true that ASI is taking steps to preserve the cannon balls recovered; and(f)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF CULTURE, TOURISM AND DEVELOPMENT OF NORTH EASTERN REGION(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) & (b) 	Yes, Sir. Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) recovered fifty (50) cannon balls of various sizes inside Palakkad Fort, which include forty-two (42) big and eight (08) small iron cannon balls. (c) to (f)	The fort is an ancient monument of national importance and finding archaeological remains during clearance is a common phenomenon. ASI takes necessary steps to preserve antiquities and their proper storage and display including the recent finds.
