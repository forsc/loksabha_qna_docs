                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3325






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.08.2021






State Annual Action Plan




Shivkumar Chanabasappa Udasi














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of State Annual Action Plans approved for the entire Mission period, including committed Central assistance, State-wise;(b)	the central assistance released to States/UTs under AMRUT so far along with the present status of implementation of AMRUT in the country State-wise;(c)	the city-wise details of projects taken up under AMRUT by the Government of Karnataka as on 31/3/2021; (d)	whether the NITI Aayog has conducted third party evaluation of Centrally Sponsored Schemes including AMRUT and if so, the details thereof; (e)	whether the Post Devolution Revenue Deficit Grant (PDRD) is provided to the States under Article 275 of the Constitution;(f)	if so, the names of the States recommended for PDRD Grant by the Fifteenth Finance Commission; and (g)	whether the Finance Commission Grants are primarily divided into four sub-heads and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FINANCE(SHRI PANKAJ CHAUDHARY)(a): Under Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT), Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs (MoHUA) has approved State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) of the States/Union Territories (UTs) amounting to ?.77,640 crore for the entire Mission, which includes committed Central assistance of ?35,990 crore. State-wise details are at Annexure-I.(b): Central assistance of ? 26,298 crore has been released so far to the States/UTs for implementation of projects under AMRUT. The State/UT-wise details are at Annexure-II. Under AMRUT so far works have been completed for 3,648 projects costing ?20,082 crore .The State/UT-wise status of implementation of AMRUT projects is given as Annexure-III.(c): For the State of Karnataka, SAAPs amounting to ?4,953 crore, including committed Central Assistance of ? 2,314 crore, have been approved for the entire Mission. As on 31.3.2021, State of Karnataka has taken up 401 projects worth ?5,119 crore, of which, 305 projects worth ?1,679 crore have been completed. The city-wise details of projects taken up under AMRUT by State of Karnataka are at Annexure-IV.(d): Yes, NITI Aayog, in its report titled “Evaluation of Centrally Sponsored Schemes in Urban Transformation Sector – Volume 2: Urban Transformation” published in February, 2021 has conducted third party evaluation of five CSS schemes of MoHUA namely, Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana (Urban), Smart Cities Mission,  Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT), Swachh Bharat Mission-Urban and Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana- National Urban Livelihood Mission (DAY-NULM). With regard to AMRUT scheme, the evaluation report has recommended for extension of Mission duration up to the year 2023 such that all projects get at least five years implementation duration.(e): Yes, Post Devolution Revenue Deficit Grant (PDRD) is provided to the States under Article 275 of the Constitution.(f):  Fifteenth Finance Commission (XV-FC) has recommended PDRD in 17 States namely Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Karnataka, Kerala, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Punjab, Rajasthan, Sikkim, Tamil Nadu, Tripura, Uttarakhand and West Bengal.(g): XV-FC has recommended grants for the following broad categories: (i) Post Devolution Revenue Deficit Grant, (ii) Local body Grants, (iii) Sector specific Grants, (iv)State specific Grants and (v) Disaster Management Grants. Allocation has been made in “Demand No. 40 - Transfers to States” in BE 2021-22 for the following:1.	Post Devolution Revenue Deficit Grant (? 1,18,452 crore)2.	Grants-in –Aid for State Disaster Response Fund (? 22,184 crore)3.	Grants for Local Bodies (? 67,015 crore) of which:        3.01- Rural Bodies (? 44,901 crore)        3.02 - Urban Bodies (? 22,114 crore)4.	Grants to Health Sector (? 13,192 crore)***
