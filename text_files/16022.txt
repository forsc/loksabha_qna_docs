                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4498






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.03.2020






HIV Infections




Hibi Eden














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the five major reasons identified for causing infection of HIV in India;(b) the number of HIV positive (HIV/AIDS) patients registered for treatment at Anti-Retroviral Treatment (ART) clinics under National AIDS Control Programme (NACP) in the years 2015-16, 2016-17, 2017-18 and 2018-19, respectively;(c) the age and gender profile of the patients;(d) whether the Government is aware of the data available at UN AIDS website which states that twenty lakh persons were living with HIV in 2017; and(e) if so, the statistical differences between UN AIDS website and his Ministry and the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	HIV infection in India is primarily concentrated among key population and the main high-risk behaviours identified for HIV infection in India include unprotected heterosexual behaviour, unprotected homosexual behaviour and unsafe injecting drug use behaviour. HIV infection has been also reported from HIV infected mother to child (during pregnancy, delivery and breastfeeding). Few cases of HIV infections are also reported through transfusion of HIV infected blood and blood products.(b):	The number of HIV positive (HIV/AIDS) patients registered for treatment at Anti-Retroviral Treatment clinics under National AIDS Control Programme in the reference period is as follows;Year	No. of Patients 2015-16	11,88,6192016-17	12,35,7112017-18	12,72,9392018-19	13,25,801(c):	The age and gender profile break-up of the HIV patients as on March 2019 is as below:Age Group	Gender	HIV PatientsAdults (> 15 Years)	Male	6,25,326	Female	6,16,002	Trans sexual/Tran gender	3,824Children(<15 years)	Male	45,524	Female	35,125Total	13,25,801(d) & (e):	There is no difference between data on HIV/AIDS reported by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoH&FW), Government of India and UNAIDS website.   As per latest HIV Estimations (2017) Report, India is estimated to have 21.40 lakh people living with HIV in 2017. MoH&FW periodically reports to UNAIDS through Global AIDS Monitoring mechanism.
