                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3682






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2022






Funding of Booster Dose and Vaccination of Children




Mala Roy














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the government has noted that the budgetary allocation for public health has gone down by Rs. 30000 crores this year;(b) if so, the manner in which the Government proposes to fund the precaution/booster doses for adults and vaccination of children below 15 years; and(c) the number of deaths that have occurred due to severe adverse effects following COVID-19 immunisation in the age group of 15-18 years? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) to (c) The Budget Estimate for public health in the financial year 2021-22 was INR 71,268.77 crore which is increased to INR 82,920.65 crore in Revised Estimate. In the current financial year i.e. 2021-22, INR 35,000 crore is provided over and above budgetary allocation of public health for procurement of COVID-19 vaccine under the National COVID-19 vaccination programme for free supply of COVID-19 vaccines to States/UTs.  Adequate COVID-19 vaccines are made available to States/UTs to administer precaution doses for adult and vaccination of children 12 years and above.As on 16th March 2022, a total of 9.11 crore vaccine doses are administered in the age group of 15-18 years and under Adverse Event Following Immunization (AEFI) on CoWIN-SAFEVAC, 16 deaths are reported in this age group.*******
