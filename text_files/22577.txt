                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

334






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2021






Implementation of SCM




Gopal Chinayya Shetty














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has implemented the Smart Cities Mission (SCM) and if so, the details thereof;(b)	the names of the cities selected in the country under the Mission as on date, State-wise;(c)	the facilities proposed to be provided in the cities included in the said Mission; and(d)	the details of the amount to be spent under this Mission, State/city-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                                     (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)	to (d) : A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.                                          STATEMENTSTATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY OF PARTS (a) to (d) OF THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. † *334 FOR ANSWER ON 18.3.2021  REGARDING “IMPLEMENTATION OF SCM”.(a) to (d) Government of India launched the Smart Cities Mission (SCM) on 25 June, 2015 for development of 100 cities as Smart Cities. The selection of 100 Smart Cities has been completed through 4 rounds of selection from January, 2016 to June, 2018. State/Union Territory (UT)-wise details of Smart Cities is given in Annexure-I.A total of 5,151 projects worth of ? 2,05,018 crore were proposed by the 100 Smart Cities as part of their Smart City Proposals (SCPs). The facilities proposed to be provided in the Smart Cities, inter alia,   relate to:i)	Promoting mixed land use in area-based developments;ii)	Housing and inclusiveness;iii)	Creating walkable localities;iv)	Preserving and developing open spaces;v)	Promoting a variety of transport options;vi)	Making governance citizen-friendly and cost effective;vii)	Giving an identity to the city; andviii)	Applying Smart Solutions to infrastructure and services. Central Government is to give financial support to the Mission to the extent of ? 48,000 crore over five years, or an average of ? 500 crore per city. An equal amount, on a matching basis, is to contributed by the State/Urban Local Body. Apart from these funds, around ? 42,028 crore (21%) has been proposed from convergence with other Missions, ? 41,022 crore (21%) from Public-Private Partnership (PPP), around ? 9,843 crore (4.8%) from loans, ? 2,644 crore (1.3%) from own resources and remaining from other sources. The State/UT/Smart City-wise details of Smart Cities Proposals (SCPs) is given in Annexure-II.                                  ******
