                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COAL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4056






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2020






Coal Production




Arun Sao














                        Will the Minister of





COAL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a.	the number of coal mines/blocks in Chhattisgarh where coal is not being produced;b.	whether the Government proposes to do away with the lease granted to coal mines which are closed due to unavailability of coal;c.	if so, the details thereof;d.	whether any technique has been developed to start the mining work in such closed mines; and e.	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF COAL, MINES & PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS(SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI)(a)        29 coal mines/blocks are located in Chhattisgarh where coal is not being produced so far.  Out of the said 29 coal mines, 6 coal mines are allocated under the provisions of the Coal Mines (Special Provisions) Act, 2015, 4 coal mines are allocated under the provisions of Mines & Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 and 19 coal mines belong to South Eastern Coalfields Ltd. (SECL),  a subsidiary of Coal India Ltd. situated in Chhattisgarh.  (b) to (c). SECL has decided to surrender mining lease granted to coal mines which are closed due to unavailability of coal as per details below : Sl. No.	Name of Mines	Type of Land to be surrendered	Approx. Area in Ha1.	West Chirimiri OC	Non-Forest Land	586.0002.	North Chirimiri OC	Forest Land	227.000 (d)          The mines having substantial reserve are studied in detail and then the technique (technology) of Extraction of the UG reserve by opencast method are adopted for extraction of the remaining reserve depending on suitability of the technology for those mines. (e)          Recently, SECL has adopted the following method for extraction of UG coal reserve: (i) Dharam UG mine: The UG reserve has been proposed for extraction by Chaal open cast mine.(ii) Mahamaya UG mine: The UG reserve has been proposed for extraction through open cast method for which a Project Report of 1.50 MTY capacity has been approved. ***
