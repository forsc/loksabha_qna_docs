                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3199






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2019






Upgradation of Medical Colleges/ Hospitals




Krupal Balaji Tumane














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government has requested the States/UTs to send proposals for strengthening of district hospitals and upgrading them to medical colleges;(b) if so, the details thereof and the number of such proposals received, State/ UT-wise;(c) the details of district hospitals upgraded to medical colleges and the financial assistance provided during the last three years and the current year under the scheme, State/UT-wise;(d) whether the Government is providing financial assistance to the States/ UTs for development of emergency medical units in Government hospitals, if so, the details thereof; and(e) the funds allocated by the Government for setting up of state-of-theart emergency medical units during the last three years?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	The Ministry of Health & Family Welfare administers a Centrally Sponsored Scheme for ‘Establishment of new Medical Colleges attached with existing district/referral hospitals’. The Phase-III of the Scheme has been approved by the Cabinet on 28.8.2019 to establish 75 more new medical colleges. The State/UT Governments have been requested to send their proposals under this Scheme along with Detailed Project Report as per Scheme guidelines for consideration by the Ministry. (b):	As on date, proposals of 64 medical colleges have been received. Details are at Annexure-I.(c):	Under Phase-I of the Scheme, 58 districts in 20 States/UT have been identified and approved. Total cost of establishment of one Medical College is Rs.189 crore to be shared between Centre and State in the ratio of 90:10 for NE/Special Category States and 60:40 for other States. Funds to the tune of Rs.7507.70 crore have been released to the State/UT Governments for the approved medical colleges under the Scheme. Out of 58 approved medical colleges, so far 42 medical colleges have become functional. Details are at Annexure-II. Further, under Phase-II of the Scheme, 24 new medical colleges in 8 States have been identified.  The total cost of establishment of one Medical College under this Phase is Rs.250 crore. The State Governments have identified the locations on Challenge mode. Out of 24 medical colleges, 22 have been approved till date and funds to the tune of Rs.2254.59 crore have been released to the State Governments. Details are at Annexure-III.(d) & (e):	Under National Health Mission, technical and financial support is provided to States/ Union Territories for strengthening their healthcare system including strengthening of district hospitals. More than 22,000 delivery points have been identified and additional funds for strengthening these facilities have been provided.     Further, Pradhan Mantry Swasthya Suraksha Yojna(PMSSY) is a Central Sector Scheme that has two components:i.	Setting up of new AIIMs; andii.	Up-gradation of existing Government Medical Colleges/ Institutions (GMCIs)        Upgradation of existing GMCIs under PMSSY involves construction of Super Speciality Block (SSB)/ Trauma Centre and procurement of Medical Equipments. Under PMSSY, construction of Trauma Centre/ Emergency Ward has been approved at the following existing GMCIs:a.	Government Medical College, Salem, Tamil Nadub.	IMS, BHU, Varanasi, Uttar Pradeshc.	Jawaharlal Nehru Medical College of Aligarh Muslim University, Aligarh, Uttar Pradeshd.	Nizam Institute of Medical Sciences, Hyderabad, Telangana e.	Government Medical College, Srinagar, Jammu and Kashmirf.	Vijayanagar Institute of Medical Sciences, Bellaryg.	Kozhikode Medical College, Keralah.	Govt. Medical College, Malda, West Bengal    PMSSY is being implemented by the Ministry directly through Executive Agencies and Procurement Support Agency. Funds are released directly to these agencies.
