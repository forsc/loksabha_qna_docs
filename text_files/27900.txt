                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3654






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






Welfare Schemes for Divyangs




Magunta Sreenivasulu Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total number of registered divyangs in the Country, State-wise and district-wise;(b)	the details of ongoing welfare schemes for divyangs, with special emphasis on those schemes related to the mobility of such persons;(c)	whether the Government proposes to provide battery-operated scooters or two-wheelers to them as it is an efficient as well as environment friendly alternative for transport; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SUSHRI PRATIMA BHOUMIK)(a)	As per Census 2011, the number of Divyangjans in the country is 2,68,14,994. State –wise details are at Annexure I.  The district-wise details are available on the website of the Registrar General of India (https://www.censusindia.gov.in/2011census/C-series/c-20.html).(b)	The Department is implementing the Scheme of ''Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids and Appliances (ADIP)'' under which funds are released to various implementing agencies  to assist the eligible   persons with disabilities (Divyangjan) in procuring durable, sophisticated and scientifically manufactured, modern, standard aids and appliances  including mobility devices, that can promote their physical, social and psychological rehabilitation by reducing the effects of disabilities and enhancing their economic potential.  The aids and assistive devices distributed under the ADIP Scheme for the mobility of Divyangjan include wheelchair, tricycle, motorized tricycle/wheelchair, smart cane, crutches, artificial limbs. The brief of the other major schemes of the Department is at Annexure-II.(c)	and (d) No, Sir. Under the Schemes of this Department, there is no provision to provide battery-operated scooters or two-wheelers to Divyangjan. However, there is a provision under the Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids and Appliances (ADIP) Scheme of this Department, for motorized tricycles and wheelchairs for severely disabled persons and for persons suffering from Quadriplegia (SCI), Muscular Dystrophy, Stroke, Cerebral Palsy, Hemipeligia and any other person with similar conditions, where either three/four limbs or one half of the body are severely impaired. The extent of subsidy provided under the scheme is Rs.25,000/-.  This is provided once in ten years to persons aged 16 years and above.*****
