                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4525






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.07.2019






Cervical Cancer




Gurjeet Singh Aujla













                                                    Hibi Eden
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government has taken cognizance that the number of women diagnosed with cervical cancer is on the rise, if so, the details thereof; (b) whether the Government has scrutinized the issue of under-use of Human Papilloma Virus (HPV) vaccination which helps to prevent cervical cancer and if so, the details thereof; (c) whether the Government proposes to make this vaccination mandatory and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; (d) whether the Government is taking any help from NGOs and international organization like WHO to prevent the said disease; and (e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	Based on the Indian Council of Medical Research’s Cancer Registry “Three-year Report of Population Based Cancer Registries: 2012-2014, Bengaluru, 2016:, decrease in incidence rate for cervical cancer is seen across all registries.(b) to (e):	National Technical Advisory Group on Immunization (NTAGI) is India’s apex technical advisory body to make recommendations for inclusion of any new vaccine in country’s Universal Immunisation Programme (UIP). NTAGI has recommended that the inclusion of specific HPV vaccines in the programme is subject to the outcome of pending Supreme Court judgement. Two writ petitions on this matter are subjudice.World Health Organisation (WHO) supports the initiatives of Government. Consultations on different health issues are also held with various stakeholders, including Non-governmental organizations (NGOs). The financing of NGO activities under the National Health Mission is through the State Programme Implementation Plans (PIPs).
