                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2972






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2021






Medical Colleges through PPP Mode




Uday Pratap Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government has launched any scheme to encourage the setting up of medical colleges through PPP mode in the country, if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise particularly in Madhya Pradesh;(b) the total number of new medical colleges sanctioned and being run at present, State/UT-wise;(c) whether the Government has received any proposals for setting up of medical college in Hoshangabad - Narsinghpur - Raisen of Madhya Pradesh and if so, the details thereof,      district-wise;(d) if not, whether the Government proposes to establish Medical colleges in these districts; and(e) the details of the funds allocated and utilized for setting up new medical colleges and hospitals in Madhya Pradesh during the last five years and the current year?






ANSWER




                                                        (a): The Department of Economic Affairs (DEA) vide its notification dated 20.07.2020 has allowed Viability Gap Funding to Medical Colleges for setting up in PPP mode by utilizing the existing District Hospitals subject to the condition that: -i.   States   fully   allow   the   facilities   of   the   hospital   to   the medical college;ii.   States   provide   land   to   the   medical   college   at   a concession. The Scheme provides the following:Sub Scheme – 1: VGF support for CAPEX upto 60% of the TPC will be provided to Health & Education sectors. The projects eligible under this category should have 100% Operational cost recovery. The Central Government will provide VGF support for CAPEX upto maximum 30% of the TPC of the project and State Government / Sponsoring Central Ministry/ Statutory Entity may provide further VGF support upto maximum 30% of the TPC.Sub-Scheme 2: It will be limited to support demonstration pilot projects in the Health and Education sectors only. The projects eligible under this category should have at least 50% Operational Cost recovery. The Central Government will provide VGF support for CAPEX upto maximum 40% of the TPC of the project and State Government Sponsoring Central Ministry/ Statutory Entity may provide further VGF support upto maximum 40% of the TPC. In addition, the Central Government and State Government / Sponsoring Central Ministry /Statutory Entity can provide upto 50% (25% + 25% each) of Operational & Maintenance costs for the first five years after Commercial Operation date.(b): Under Centrally Sponsored Scheme for establishment of New Medical colleges attached with existing district/referral hospitals, 157 new medical colleges have been sanctioned by the Government in three phases. The details of medical colleges approved under the scheme is at Annexure. Out of these 157 approved medical colleges, 47 medical colleges have become functional. These are at:-1.	Port Blair in A&N Islands 2.	Naharlagun in Arunachal Pradesh3.	Diphu in Assam,4.	Rajnandgaon & Ambikapur [Sarguja] in Chhattisgarh 5.	Nahan [Sirmaur], Chamba and Hamirpur in Himachal Pradesh6.	Dumka, Hazaribagh, Palamu (Daltonganj) in Jharkhand7.	Anantnag, Baramulla, Rajouri, Doda and Kathua in Jammu & Kashmir8.	Datia, Khandwa, Ratlam, Shahdol, Vidisha, Chhindwara and Shivpuri in Madhya Pradesh9.	Gondia and Nandurbar in Maharashtra10.	Falkawn in Mizoram11.	Baripada (Mayurbhanj), Koraput, Bolangir and Balasore in Odisha12.	Barmer, Bharatpur, Bhilwara, Churu, Sikar, Dungarpur and Pali in Rajasthan13.	Basti, Faizabad, Firozabad, Shahjahanpur and Bahraich in Uttar Pradesh14.	Birbhum (Rampur Hat), Purulia, Cooch behar, Diamond Harbour and Raiganj (North Dinajpur) in West Bengal.(c) & (d): The Government has not received any proposals for setting up of medical colleges in Hoshangabad – Narsinghpur - Raisen districts of Madhya Pradesh. Further, under Phase – III of the scheme, all 75 approved medical colleges have already been allotted. (e): Under CSS for establishment of new medical colleges funds are allocated for setting up of new medical colleges. The details of funds allocated in setting up of medical colleges in Madhya Pradesh during last five years is as under:-Rs.in crore.State	2016-17	2017-18	2018-19	2019-20	2020-21Madhya Pradesh	111	429.5	271.3	75.69	318.31As per the Utilization Certificates received from the State of Madhya Pradesh, till date Rs. 414.14 Crores has been utilized by the State Government.
