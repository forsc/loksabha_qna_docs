                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4554






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.03.2020






Popularisation of Ayurveda




Shankar Lalwani














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government has chalked out plans to popularise Ayurveda globally; (b) if so, the details thereof; (c) whether the Government has prepared plans to change the mindset of the people to adopt/embrace Ayurveda, which they don’t do, despite their familiarity with the system; and (d) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(SHRI SHRIPAD YESSO NAIK)(a)  to (d):  	Under Central Sector Scheme for promotion of International Cooperation, (IC Scheme), the Ministry of AYUSH undertakes various measures to promote and popularise AYUSH systems of medicine including Ayurveda across the globe.  		Ministry of AYUSH deputes AYUSH experts to foreign countries to participate in international meetings, conferences, training programmes, seminars and on special assignment of the Government of India for promotion and propagation of AYUSH Systems including Ayurveda  Financial assistance is also provided for presentation of AYUSH related scientific research papers in international conferences, workshops, seminar etc. 		Incentives are provided to AYUSH drug manufacturers/ entrepreneurs/ AYUSH institutions, AYUSH drug and Hospitals etc. for (a) participating in International exhibitions/ conferences/ workshops/ seminars/ road shows/ trade fairs, etc. for generating awareness amongst the participating public about the AYUSH systems of medicine and; (b) registration of AYUSH products with regulatory authorities of the foreign countries USFDA/EMEA/UK-MHRA/ NHPD (Canada)/ TGA etc.  		Ministry of AYUSH has set up 33 AYUSH Information Cell in 31 countries to disseminate authentic information about AYUSH systems of Medicine. Ayurveda day is being celebrated every year in more than 35 countries.	As of now, Ministry of AYUSH has signed 23 Country to Country MoUs for Cooperation in field of Traditional Medicine and Homoeopathy namely Nepal, Bangladesh, Hungary, Trinidad & Tobago, Malaysia, WHO Geneva, Mauritius, Mongolia, Turkmenistan, Myanmar, Germany (Joint declaration), Iran, Sao Tome & Príncipe, Equatorial Guinea, Cuba, Colombia, Japan  (MoC), Bolivia, Gambia, Republic of Guinea, China, St. Vincent & The Grenadines and Suriname. 22 MoUs for undertaking collaborative research in AYUSH systems of medicine and 13 MoUs for setting up AYUSH Academic Chairs have been signed with foreign institutes/ universities. The Chair undertakes academic and research activities related to AYUSH Systems of Medicine.  	Under ITEC Programme of MEA, the Ministry has deputed Ayurveda expert to Ministry of Health, Malaysia for providing their services in the Traditional and Complementary Medicine Unit at Port Dickson Hospital, Malaysia where the Government of Malaysia has started dedicated wings for Shirodhara and external Basti therapy. 	 Ministry of AYUSH though Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR)  offers 104 scholarships every year to the eligible foreign nationals from 98 countries for pursuing undergraduate (UG), postgraduate (PG) and Ph. D courses in AYUSH systems including Ayurveda in premier Institutes in India. Every year, young foreign nationals are admitted in the Indian institutes for obtaining qualification various AYUSH systems including Ayurveda.  The young AYUSH scholars after attaining qualification in AYUSH systems including Ayurveda from institutes in India act as our ambassadors for promoting and propagating the AYUSH systems including Ayurveda in the countries of their origin.	 ******
