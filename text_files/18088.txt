                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1826






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.09.2020






Privatisation of Freight Corridors




Asaduddin Owaisi













                                                     Imtiaz Jaleel Syed
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether after allowing private players to run passenger trains, Indian Railways is going to open two freight corridors to private operators;(b) 	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether Dedicated Freight Corridor Corporation of India (DFCCIL) has started the process in this regard;(d) 	if so, the details thereof;(e) 	the number of routes and trains allowed to be operated by private operators; and(f) 	the future plan for privatisation of freight corridors and passenger trains and the extent to which this process is likely to adversely affect the railway employees?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) to (f): As per the current mandate and scheme, Indian Railways will be the sole operator of Freight trains on Dedicated Freight Corridors (DFC) network.  The privately owned wagons/freight trains which are currently being operated on Indian Railways, will run on DFCs also.  There is no plan for privatisation of freight corridors.  *****
