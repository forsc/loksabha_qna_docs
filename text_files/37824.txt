                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4983






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.04.2022






National AYUSH Mission




Kripanath Mallah













                                                    Jaskaur Meena
                                                








                                                    Rajashree Mallick
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) the objectives and salient features of National AYUSH Mission (NAM); (b) the total amount of funds sanctioned, allocated and utilized under the NAM during the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise including Assam along with the details of the targets set and its achievements made so far; (c) whether the Government proposes to set up/ upgrade AYUSH hospitals and dispensaries across the country and if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise including Assam and Odisha; (d) the details of the estimated funds required for this purpose and the time by which these are likely to be upgraded; and (e) whether homeopathy has been included under the National Ayush Mission (NAM) and if so, details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AYUSH (SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a) The objectives and salient features of the National AYUSH Mission (NAM) are furnished inAnnexure-I. (b) Under NAM, the funds sanctioned/approved, allocated/central share released and expenditure/utilization reported by the State/UT Governments during the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise including Assam is furnished at Annexure-II.Under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission, the grant-in-aid has been provided to State/UT Governments as per the proposals reflected in their State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) based on their priority, need and as per the schematic provisions. Therefore, State-specific year-wise targets are not set up by the Central Government. However, the major activities supported to States/UTs and major activities supported to Assamunder the National AYUSH Mission during the last three years and the current year are furnishedinAnnexure-III.(c) & (d) Public Health being a State subject, to set up / upgrade AYUSH hospitals and dispensaries across the country including Assam and Odisha comes under the purview of respective State/UT Governments. However, under NAM, there are provisions for setting up of up to 50 /30 /10 beddedintegrated AYUSH Hospitals, upgradation of existing standalone Government AYUSH Hospitals, upgradation of existing Government/Panchayat/Government aided AYUSH Dispensaries and Construction of building to establish new AYUSH Dispensary. Under NAM, status of grants approved for integrated AYUSH hospitals, upgradation of existing standalone Government AYUSH Hospitals and upgradation of existing Government/Panchayat/Government aided AYUSH Dispensaries as per the proposals received from State/UT Governments through State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) is furnished at Annexure-IV. Since implementation comes under the purview of States/UT Government, no time line has been fixed by Central Government to completethe establishment/upgradation of each of these hospitals/dispensaries.(e) The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of the National AYUSH Mission envisages the strengthening and improving all AYUSH systems of healthcare including Homoeopathy. The grant-in-aid has been provided to State/UT Governments for strengtheningAYUSH System including Homoeopathy as per the proposals reflected in their State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) based on their priority need and as per the schematic provision.
