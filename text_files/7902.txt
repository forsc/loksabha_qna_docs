                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1524






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.11.2019






Setting up of New Companies




Janardan Mishra













                                                    Feroze Varun Gandhi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the number of new companies opened in India in the year    2018-19;(b) 	the details of the difference in the total number of new companies when compared to the previous financial year;(c) 	whether the Government has formulated any scheme to set up new industries in such places having abundant resources but no industries;(d) 	if so, the State-wise details thereof; and(e) 	if not, the steps being taken/to be taken by the Government in this regard? 
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        ???????? ??? ?????? ?????? (???? ????? ????) THE MINISTER OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI  PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) & (b): 	Information received from Ministry of Corporate Affairs in respect ofState/UT wise newly registered companies indicate that during 2017-18 & 2018-19. 1,23,938 companies were registered in 2018-19 as against 1,08,075 companies registered in 2017-18. State wise breakup is at Annexure I. (c) to (e):  	The primary responsibility of industrial development rests with the State Governments. Central Government encourages setting up of industries through various policy measures implemented by different Ministries and Departments.  Government has taken several steps to develop the industrial sector which, inter-alia, includes providing quality infrastructure, reducing logistics cost, skill development, Ease of Doing Business and adoption of technology, etc. Further, several new programmes/ initiatives have been undertaken to push the industrial sector of the economy such as Make in India, Skill India, Start-Up India and Stand-Up India, Modified Industrial Infrastructure Upgradation Scheme (MIIUS), Industrial Development Scheme for Himachal Pradesh (HP) & Uttarakhand, 2017; Industrial Development Scheme for Jammu and Kashmir, 2017;  North East Industrial Development Scheme (NEIDS), 2017; and the Scheme of Budgetary Support under Goods and Services Tax (GST) Regime to the units located in State of Jammu & Kashmir, Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh and North Eastern States including Sikkim. Centralised statistics of the industries set up is not maintained in the Department. *****
