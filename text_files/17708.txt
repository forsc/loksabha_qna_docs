                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1595






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.09.2020






Amendments to Essential Commodities Act




D. M. Kathir Anand














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the measures taken by the Government to tackle hoarding of commodities in light of the recent amendments in ‘The Essential Commodities Act’; and (b) 	if so, the details of measures taken/being taken to ensure that farmers get right price?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI DANVE RAOSAHEB DADARAO)(a)  : 	In respect of food items specified in the amendment of the Essential Commodities Act, the Central Government has already delegated its powers to the State Governments and Union Territories vide order dated 09.06.1978. So far, no State Government has submitted any proposal to impose stock limits on any of the items specified in the Essential Commodities (Amendment) Ordinance, 2020. The Central Government has already constituted a Group of central enforcement agencies to check hoarding, black marketing, speculative trading, abnormal price rise etc. The group meets frequently.Further, the States and Union Territories have already been advised to implement the Essential Commodities Act, 1955 and the Prevention of Black Marketing and Maintenance of Supplies of Essential Commodities Act, 1980 effectively to not allow hoarding in the essential food items. Action against hoarding of essential food items, specified in the Essential Commodities (Amendment) Ordinance, 2020 is regular enforcement activity by all the State Governments and Union Territories. (b)   : 	To ensure farmers right price of their produce the Central Government has procured food items like wheat, paddy, Onion, pulses, etc. from farmers at MSP. Government has decided to maintain a buffer of pulses up to 19.50 Lakh Metric Ton (LMT) and Onions up to 1 LMT under the Price Stabilisation Fund. The State are also advised to maintain such Fund. *****
