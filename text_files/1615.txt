                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1532






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.07.2019






Moral Education




Sanjay Seth













                                                    Naranbhai Bhikhabhai Kachhadiya
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is considering to make moral education a compulsory subject fromnursery to university level syllabus in the country including Jharkhand;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) if not, the reasons therefor; and(d) the details of the steps taken/being taken by the Government in order to inculcate moralvalues in the students of the country by taking advice from the educationists and spiritualscholars from different religions on this subject to prepare the above mentioned syllabus?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (d) : National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) has developedNational Curriculum Framework (NCF, 2005) which emphasises on inculcating moral values,attitudes and skills required for living in harmony with oneself and with others. Issues related toMoral Values, wherever possible have been incorporated in the textbooks developed for schoolstage as well as in the entire school life-the curriculum, classroom climate, school management,teaching-learning, teacher-pupil relationships etc.As per information received from Government of Jharkhand, they have adopted NCF,2005 and NCERT curriculum with local content. Moral education has been integrated in the textbooks in Elementary Education. In addition to this, a supplementary book for class III to classVIII on moral values has been developed by Jharkhand Council of Educational Research andTraining (JCERT) and moral values books for class VI to VIII have been printed and distributedfree of cost. For class IX to XII State has adopted NCERT text books.As per information received from University Grants Commission (UGC), moral educationis embedded in the syllabi of all courses in the stream of Humanities and Social Sciences atUniversity level. Further, some universities in the country are also running courses on spiritualand religious studies.
