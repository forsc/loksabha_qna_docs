                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1981






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.03.2020






Online Business by Fake Organisations




N.K. Premachandran














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the Government has taken note of the fact that fake organizations are doing online business without disclosing their identity and location; (b)	if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government thereto along with the action taken by the Government for compulsory registration of organizations and individuals doing online business for protecting the interest of consumers in this regard; and(c)	whether the Government proposes to make it compulsory for the organizations/individuals offering products and services to provide appropriate information on terms and conditions of the purchase, warranties etc., in the site itself and if so, the details of the action taken by the Government thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI DANVE RAOSAHEB DADARAO)(a) to (c) : A three tier quasi-judicial mechanism has been set up under the provisions of the Consumer Protection Act, 1986 to provide speedy and simple redressal to consumer disputes for both the online and offline modes. They have been empowered to give relief of a specific nature and to award, compensation whenever appropriate to consumers. Penalties for non-compliance of orders given by the quasi-judicial bodies have also been provided in the Consumer Protection Act, 1986.    The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 has been published in the official gazette on 09.08.2019. The new Act provides for provisions for framing rules, to protect the interests of consumers and for preventing unfair trade practices in e-commerce sector.*****
