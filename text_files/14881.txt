                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3546






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2020






Periodic Census of Central Government Employees




Gautam Gambhir














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                     Will the Minister of LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT be pleased to state:(a)	whether the Government has discontinued the periodic Census of Central Government Employees;(b)	if so, the reasons therefor; and (c)	if not, the year when the last census was undertaken and the time by which the next census is likely to be undertaken?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	 to (b):	 No, Sir(c)		The last report on Census of Central Government Employees (as on 31.03.2011) was brought in 2014. The decision for bringing out of next report will be taken after obtaining views from Department of Personnel and Training (DoPT) and National Statistics Office (NSO), Ministry of Statistics & Programme Implementation. 	 (b):  Employment generation coupled with improving employability is the priority of the Government. Government has taken various steps for generating employment in the country like encouraging private sector of economy, fast-tracking various projects involving substantial investment and increasing public expenditure on schemes such as Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP), Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS), Pt. Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushalya Yojana (DDU-GKY) and Deendayal Antodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM) run by Ministry of Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises, Ministry of Rural Development and Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs, respectively.  Employment generated through these schemes/programmes  in country to the extent available is given below:Schemes/Year	2014-15	2015-16	2016-17	2017-18	2018-19Estimated employment generated under  PMEGP  (in lakh)	3.57	3.23	4.08	3.87	5.87Persondays Generated under MGNREGS ( in crore)	166.17	235.12	235.64	233.74	268.00Candidates placed under DDU-GKY   (in lakh)	0.21	1.09	1.48	0.76	1.36Placement  under DAY-NULM  (in lakh) 	0.63	0.34	1.52	1.15	1.63Source: Concerned Ministries.(c): 	As per the results of annual Periodic Labour Force Survey conducted by NSO and  employment–unemployment survey conducted by Labour Bureau,  the State-wise estimated unemployment rate to the extent available is given at Annexure-I.(d): 	The State-wise detail of employment generated through major employment generation schemes such as Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP), Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS), Pt. Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushalya Yojana (DDU-GKY) and Deendayal Antodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM) in country to the extent available is given at Annexure- II, III, IV and V respectively.
