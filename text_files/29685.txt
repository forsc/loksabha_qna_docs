                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1301






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2021






Foreign Investment




Sadashiv Kisan Lokhande














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the quantum of foreign investment in the country so far during the last three years, State-wise;(b) the shortfall or abundance seen during the said period, state-wise;(c) whether there is a shortfall in foreign investment in the country due to administrative obstacles; and(d) if so, the steps taken or proposed by the Government to deal with such obstacles in order to increase the foreign investment in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        The MINISTER OF STATE (FINANCE)(SHRI PANKAJ CHAUDHARY)a & b). The state-wise details of FDI Equity inflow are maintained w.e.f. October, 2019 onwards. Accordingly, the financial year wise details of state-wise FDI Equity inflow reported in the country since October, 2019 are attached herewith at Annexure 1. c & d).  The FDI equity inflows have increased in 2018-19 to 2020-21 period as given in table below:S.No.	Year	    FDI Equity inflow (US$ million)1	2018-19	        44,3662	2019-20 (P)	49,9773	2020-21 (P)	59,636Further, the Government has put in place a liberal and transparent policy for FDI, wherein most of the sectors are open to FDI under the automatic route. The Government reviews the FDI policy on an ongoing basis to ensure that India remains investor friendly destination. In the last one year, review of the FDI policy have been undertaken in sectors such as Insurance, Defence, Petroleum & Natural Gas, Telecom, etc.
