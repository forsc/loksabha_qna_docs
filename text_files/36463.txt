                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3900






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2022






Development of Ayurveda/AYUSH System of Medicine




Uday Pratap Singh













                                                    Anil Firojiya
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of various works done/measures taken by the Government for development of Ayurveda/AYUSH System of Medicine during the last three years;(b) whether the Government proposes to develop all the 1773 dispensaries under the Ministry in rural areas as Health & Wellness Centres and if so, the details thereof; and(c)  if not, the reason therefor;(d)  the names of States where works are being carried out to promote AYUSH Health Services in the health Sector;(e)  whether any steps have been taken for the protection and safety of traditional system of medicine and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AYUSH (SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a) Ministry of Ayush is implementing Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM) though States/UTs for development and promotion of AYUSH systems. The National AYUSH Mission inter-alia makes provision for the following activities through AYUSH systems of medicine including Ayurveda system of medicine:-(i) AYUSH Health & Wellness Centres under Ayushman Bharat(ii) Co-location of AYUSH facilities at Primary Health Centres (PHCs), Community Health Centres (CHCs) and District Hospitals(DHs) (iii) Upgradation of existing standalone Government AYUSH Hospitals(iv) Upgradation of existing/setting up of new standalone Government/ Panchayat/Government aided AYUSH Dispensaries(v) Setting up of up to 50 /30 /10 beddedintegrated AYUSH Hospitals (vi) Supply of essential drugs to Government AYUSH Hospitals, Government Dispensaries and Government/Government aided Teaching Institutional AYUSH Hospitals (vii) AYUSH Public Health Programmes(viii) Infrastructural development of State Government Under-Graduate and Post-Graduate Educational Institutions.(ix) Establishment of new AYUSH Colleges in the States where availability of AYUSH teaching institutions is inadequate in Government Sector.Under NAM, grant-in-aid has been provided for different approved activities for development and promotion of AYUSH systems of medicines including Ayurveda during the last three years. The State/UT-wise details arefurnished at Annexure-I.Further Ministry of Ayush has taken various measures for development of AYUSH system of Medicine:1.	National Commissions for AYUSH Education including National Commission for Indian System of Medicine and National Commission for Homoeopathy were established in 2020 as new initiative of educational reforms in AYUSH sector.2.	Institute of Teaching & Research in Ayurveda (ITRA), Jamnagar, Gujarat is conferred the status of Institution of National Important by an Act of Parliament in 2020.3.	National Institute of Ayurveda (NIA), Jaipur, an autonomous organization under Ministry of Ayush recognized as Deemed to be University in De Novo category.4.	Establishment of AYUSH Grid has been emerged as an Information Technology backbone for AYUSH sector.5.	Ministry of Ayush developed National AYUSH Morbidity and Standardized Terminologies Portal for collection & maintenance of morbidity statistics.6.	Ministry of Ayush is implementing a Central Sector Scheme of Champion Services Sector Scheme on Medical Value Travel Services. 7.	Ministry of Ayushhas signed anMemorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Invest India for establishment of Strategic Policy and Facilitation Bureau (SPFB) in respect of Ministry of Ayushon 05.01.2021. 8.	In the context of Covid-19, Ministry of Ayush has taken various measures to combat the pandemic, including issuing various advisories for prevention and management, the National Clinical Management Protocol, “AYUSH for Immunity” campaign, 142 research studies on COVID-19, and AYUSH Sanjivani Mobile App based study.9.	World Health Organization (WHO) has announced setting up of Global Centre of Traditional medicine in India.10.	In the field of International Co-operation, Federal level Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) have been signed with 11 countries and Institute level MoUs signed with 24 countries.  28 AYUSH Information Cells have been set up in 25 countries so far. Further, signed Agreement with WHO for promotion of AYUSH systems.(b) & (c) Public Health being a State subject, the primary responsibility to developdispensaries as Health & Wellness Centresin the country including rural areas lies with the respective State/UT Governments. However, Ministry of Ayush is operationalizing 12,500 AYUSH Health and Wellness Centers (HWCs) as a component of Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM) through State/UT including rural areas in a phased manner. As per the proposal received from the State/UT Governments in their State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs), the Ministry of Ayush has approved for upgradation of 7513 Health facilities into Ayush HWCs in 34 States/UTs and Central Share of Rs. 551.73 Crore has been released to these State/UT Governments. 3138 AYUSH HWCs have been made functional under progressive category till date.(d)Public Health being a State subject, the primary responsibility to promoteAYUSH Health Servicesin the health sector lies with the respective State/UT Governments. However, Ministry of Ayush is implementing Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM) though States/UTs for development and promotion of AYUSH systems. Under NAM, grant-in-aid has been provided to States/UTs for different activities as per the proposals received from them in their State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs). The details of grant-in-aid released till date is furnished at Annexure-II.		(e)The Government of India, Ministry of Ayush has taken following steps for Safety of ASU&H drugs;i.  Implemented Centrally Sponsored Scheme under National Ayush Mission to strengthen State Government Quality Control Mechanism, office of State Licensing Authorities (Ayush), Ayush Pharmacies and Drug Testing Laboratories since 12th Plan. ii. Central sector Scheme for augmenting quality of Ayush drugs AyushOushadhiGunavattaEvamUttpadanSamvardhanYojana (AOGUSY) is being implemented from 2021-22 to 2025-26. The objective of the Scheme is as under;•	To enhance India''s manufacturing capabilities and exports of traditional medicines and health promotion products under the initiative of Atmanirbhar Bharat. •	To facilitate adequate infrastructural & technological upgradation and institutional activities in public and private sector for standardization, quality manufacturing and analytical testing of Ayush drugs & materials.•	To strengthen regulatory frameworks at Central and State level for effective quality control, safety monitoring and surveillance of misleading advertisements of Ayush drugs. •	To encourage building up synergies, collaborations and convergent approaches for promoting standards and quality of Ayush drugs & materials.iii. So far Government has published Ayurvedic Formulary of India (Part I-III) containing 986 Formulations, Siddha Formulary of India (Part I-II) containing 400 Formulations and National Formulary of Unani Medicine (Vol. I-VI) containing 1230 Formulations. Further, monographs of quality standards of 645 Single drugs and 203 Formulations of Ayurveda; 139 Single drugs and 1 Formulation of Siddha; 298 Single drugs and 201 Formulations of Unani; and 1117 drugs of Homoeopathy (Vol. I-X) have also been published.iv. The Government have strengthened 35 State Government Drug Testing Laboratories for testing of Ayurveda, Siddha, Unani and Homoeopathy medicines in the country. Under Rule 160A to 160J of Drug & Cosmetics Rules 1945, 65 private Drug Testing Laboratories are approved for testing of Ayurveda, Siddha, Unani and Homoeopathy Drugs. There is one Central Drug Laboratory i.e. Pharmacopoeia Commission for Indian Medicine & Homoeopathy Laboratory for the purpose of testing or Analysis of ASU&H Drugs.Further, Traditional Knowledge Digital Library (TKDL) is a pioneering initiative of the Council of Scientific & Industrial Research jointly with the Ministry of Ayush to prevent exploitation and to protect Indian traditional knowledge at Patent Offices worldwide.
