                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2069






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.02.2021






Study on Medicinal Plants




Shriniwas Dadasaheb Patil













                                                    Veena Devi
                                                








                                                    Vinayak Bhaurao Raut
                                                








                                                    Harish Dwivedi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government had conducted any study with regard to the availability of medicinal and aromatic herbs/plants in the country, if so, the details and the outcome thereof indicating the medicinal plants being grown in the country, State/UT-wise;(b) whether a number of medicinal and aromatic herbs/plants are on the verge of extinction and are likely to get extinct in the near future;(c) if so, the details of measures proposed/taken by the Government for their constant management, farming and commercial use in the country;(d) whether the National Medicinal Plant Board has undertaken any exercise for allotment of land in various States including Maharashtra, if so, the details thereof;(e) the funds allocated and utilized for promotion of medicinal plants during the current year; and(f) the benefits likely to accrue to farmers therefrom?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS AND ADDITIONAL CHARGE OF MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(SHRI KIREN RIJIJU)(a):	In year 2014, NMPB had supported a project titled “Medicinal Plants in India: An Assessment of their Demand and Supply” to Indian Council of Forestry Research and Education (ICFRE), Dehradun, to assess the demand and supply of medicinal plants in India in the national perspective, as well as in respect of the international Market. The detailed study report alongwith outcomes is available at https://www.nmpb.nic.in/sites/ default/files/Projects/Medicinal_Plants_in_India_An_Assessment_of_their_Demand_and_Supply.pdf The details of cultivation of medicinal plants across the country supported under medicinal plants component of NAM scheme are given at Annexure-I.(b):	According to the Botanical Survey of India (BSI), no hard core data on the extent of threat to the medicinal plants is readily available. However there are few medicinal and aromatic plants on the verge of extinction due to over exploitation and habitats destructions.(c) to (f):	Ministry of AYUSH is presently implementing following schemes:1.	“Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM)” Under ‘Medicinal Plants’ component of the NAM scheme supporting market driven cultivation of 140 prioritized medicinal plants in identified cluster/ zones with in the selected districts of States and implemented in a mission mode. As per the scheme guideline, the support is provided for:i).	Cultivation of prioritized medicinal plants on farmer’s land.ii).	Establishment of nurseries with backward linkage for raising and supply of quality planting material.iii).	Establishment of post-harvest management units with forward linkages.iv).	Establishment of primary processing, marketing infrastructure etc.Financial assistance in the form of subsidy for farmers is provided to encourage farming/cultivation of herbs/medicinal plants. Subsidy is provided for cultivation of medicinal plants, out of a prioritized list of 140 species @ 30%, 50% and 75% of cost of cultivation on farmer’s land.Ministry of AYUSH has supported 48,040 hectare area for cultivation of medicinal plants across the country including Maharastra during the last five years under medicinal plants component of NAM scheme. Details given at Annexure-I.Funds allocated and released under centrally sponsored scheme (NAM) for current year is given in table below:Funds allocated  2020-21(Rs. In Crore)	Amount Released(Rs. In Crore)126.35	7.782.	Central Sector Scheme on “Conservation, Development and Sustainable Management of Medicinal Plants” wherein following activities are supported:i).	In-situ conservation/ Ex-situ conservation.ii).	Livelihood linkages with Joint Forest Management Committees (JFMCs)/Panchayats/ Van Panchayats/ Biodiversity Management Committees (BMCs)/ Self Help Groups (SHGs).iii).	IEC activities like Training/ Workshops/ Seminars/ Conference etc.iv).	Research & Development.v).	Promotion, marketing and trade of medicinal plants produce.
