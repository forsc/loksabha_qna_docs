                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

471






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2022






Number of Ayurvedic Educational Institutes/Ayurvedic Hospitals




Nalin Kumar Kateel














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	the total number of Ayurvedic Educational Institutes/ Ayurvedic Hospitals functioning in the country, State-wise and district-wise; (b)	 whether the Government has formulated any action plan to preserve the ancient medical system of Ayurveda by encouraging research and development in this system; and (c)	if so, the details of the major research and development projects taken up by the Ministry during the last three years?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTR OF AYUSH(SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a)At present there are four hundred thirty four(434) Ayurvedic Colleges (Education Institutes)and their attached Hospitals functioning in the country. The State-wisedetails are placed at Annexure-I. (b)  Government of India has established Central Council for Research in Ayurvedic Sciences, an autonomous body under the Ministry of AYUSH as an apex organization for undertaking, coordinating, formulating, developing and promoting research in Ayurvedic sciences on scientific lines. The activities are carried out through 30 Institutes/Centers/Units located all over the India and also through collaborative studies with various Universities, Hospitals and institutes. The details of 30 institutes are placed asAnnexure –II.The core areas of research comprise Clinical Research; Medicinal Plant Research (Medico-ethno botanical Survey,Pharmacognosy& Cultivation); Drug Standardization & Quality Control; Pharmacological Research and Literary &FundamentalResearch.The Council is also conducting Research Oriented Public Health Care Programmes (Outreach Activities) which comprise of Tribal Health Care Research Programme, Ayurvedic Mobile Health Care Research Programme under Scheduled Castes Sub Plan (SCSP), and Ayurvedic Health Centres under North East Plan. Besides this,Health care services through Out–Patient Department (OPD), In-Patient Departments (IPDs) and Special clinics for Geriatric Health care are also being provided.(c)   Major Research projects taken up during the last three years:-?	Under Clinical research, research projects for validation of classical Ayurveda formulations in identified disease conditions to generate tangible evidence on their clinical efficacy and safety were undertaken.Also clinical trials on Ayurveda formulations in prioritized disease areas were undertaken under drug development.?	Undertaken Systematic Reviews and meta-analysis studies in 30 disease conditions.?	Undertaken research project on  Integration of AYUSH (Ayurveda) with NPCDCS (National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular diseases & Stroke) in three districts viz. Surendranagar (Gujarat), Bhilwara (Rajasthan), Gaya (Bihar)to demonstrate the feasibility of integration of Ayurveda involving 49 CHCs,  3 District Hospitals and 189 PHC .?	A collaborative project in association with Govt. of Maharashtra to  see the feasibility of introducing Ayurvedic interventions in Reproductive and Child Health (RCH) through integrative health care services in 30 selected PHCs of Gadchiroli district of Maharashtra was also undertaken ?	CCRAS COVID-19 studies-During the outbreak of COVID-19, research projects related to Prophylaxis, Management, awareness and pre-clinical studies were also undertaken in collaboration with reputed institutes.?	Research project related to Bio-instrumentation.?	Medicinal Plant Research projects related to pharmacognostical evaluation of crude drugs, development of In-Vitro and In –vivo propagation protocols, pictorial database of economically important plants, development of micro-forest, digitization of Herbarium sheets and development of Digital Herbarium, development of agro techniques, Establishment of National raw Drug repository were undertaken.?	Under Drug Standardization and Quality Control, research projects on chemo profiling of medicinal plant, Quality assessment of the single drugs and compound formulations , Qualitative and quantitative assessment of marker /Phyto-chemical reference standards of Ayurveda formulations  and development of quality standards of identified Ayurveda formulations were undertaken.?	Under Pharmacological Research, research projects on biological activity and safety of Ayurveda formulations/plants,genotoxicity studies on experimental models in different animal species were undertaken. ?	Literary research projects on Revival, Retrieval & Documentation of Ayurveda & Indian Medical Heritage, translation of Ayurveda classical literature, development of e-books, documentation and validation of LHTs and folk claim were undertaken.?	Research projects on development of IT based tools such as Prakriti assessment, Swaasthya assessment scale, NAMASTE portal  , Ayurveda diagnostics etc were also undertaken ?	Public Health oriented research projects on Tribal Health Care Research Programme under Tribal Sub Plan (TSP) -, Ayurveda Mobile Health Care Research Programmeunder Scheduled Castes Sub Plan (SCSP),Reproductive and Child Health Care (RCH) Programme under Scheduled Castes Sub Plan (SCSP) and Ayurvedic Health Centres under North East Plan- functioning at 19centres in 4 States i.e. Assam, Sikkim , Itanagar and Tripura were also undertaken . ?	CCRAS is also continuously engaged in development of new Ayurvedic drugs. So far, CCRAS has developed 11 technologies which have been transferred through National Research Development Corporation (NRDC). ***
