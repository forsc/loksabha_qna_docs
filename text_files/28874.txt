                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

326






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.11.2021






Suicides due to Drug Abuse




Srinivas Kesineni














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government can provide the data for suicides due to drug abuse for the last three years and if so, the details thereof, especially for the State of Andhra Pradesh;(b) whether the Government is aware of the increasing suicides due to drug abuse and alcoholism at the national level and if so, the actions/steps taken/being  taken by the Government to control the situation thereof;(c) whether the Government recognizes the problem of drug addiction and abuse among the youth of the country and if so, action taken thereof;(d) the awareness measures that have been undertaken by the Narcotics Control Bureau to curb substance abuse among teens and youth; and(e) whether the Government can provide data on the quantity of drug confiscated during the last three years and if so, the details thereof, drug category-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a)	As per the latest data published by the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) pertaining to the year 2020,  the number of suicides in the States/UTs (including  the state of Andhra Pradesh) due to Drug abuse/Alcoholic Addiction during 2018-2020, is at Annexure-I.  -2/...-2-LS.US.Q.NO.326 FOR 30.11.2021(b)	& (c) As per the said data, the total number of suicides due to drug abuse/alcoholic addiction during the years 2018, 2019 & 2020 were 7193, 7860 and 9169, respectively.   The Government is taking  sustained and coordinated action for arresting the problem of substance abuse among the youth. This includes:(i)	Launching of Nasha Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan (NMBA) in 272 most vulnerable districts. Under this, a massive community outreach is being done involving around 8000 youth volunteers. More than 42 lakh youth have been reached out,  under the Abhiyaan. (ii)	380 Integrated Rehabilitation Centre for Addicts (IRCAs) are being run for treating the drug users and to provide services of preventive education, awareness generation, motivational counseling, detoxification/de-addiction, after care and re-integration into the social mainstream. (iii)	80 Community based Peer led Intervention (CPLI) Centers are in operation with focus on vulnerable and at risk children and adolescents. Under this, peer educators engage children for awareness generation and life skill activities.(iv)	93 Outreach and Drop In Centers (ODICs) are set up to provide safe and secure space for substance users, with provision of screening, assessment and counseling. It also provides referral and linkage for treatment and rehabilitation services to substance dependents. -3/...-3-LS.US.Q.NO.326 FOR 30.11.2021(d)	Narcotics Control Bureau (NCB) has taken the following awareness measures  to curb  substance abuse among teens and youth :(i)	Facilitate awareness programme in Government & Private schools/colleges/other educational institutions all over India through its Regional, Zonal and Sub-Zonal Offices. NCB has conducted 10,195 awareness programmes during the year 2020 and 20,189 awareness programmes during the year 2021(till date). (ii)	NCB officials visit de-addiction centers to spread awareness about health issues related to drug abuse. (iii)	Organizes International Day against Drug Abuse and Illicit Trafficking on the 26th June every year to spread awareness among the general public about the ill effects of drug abuse and drug trafficking through various social media platforms by way of Audio Video messages of eminent personalities from the field of Politics, Bureaucracy, Sports, Films, Music, etc., and also through Telecom Service Providers, FM radios, Television Channels, etc.  Anti-drug messages were also released from the Twitter handles of the Hon’ble Prime Minster of India, the Hon’ble Union Home Minister and other eminent personalities to give a boost to this online campaign. (iv)	Coordinates with the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment (MoSJ&E) and the National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) to spread awareness against drug abuse and protect-4/...-4-LS.US.Q.NO.326 FOR 30.11.2021the youth, especially children, from falling prey to the drug menace. With the active support of NCB, NCPCR formulated a “Joint Acton Plan on Prevention of Drugs and Substance Use among Children and Illicit Trafficking” which was launched on 08.02.2021.  (e) Latest data published by the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) for the year 2020, on State/UT-wise seizures made under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 during 2018-2020 is at Annexure-II. *****
