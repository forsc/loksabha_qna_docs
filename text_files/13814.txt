                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2620






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.03.2020






Quality Tests for Medicines




K. Shanmuga Sundaram













                                                    Ravneet Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) the details of the number of medicines being supplied by pharmaceutical companies failing the standard quality test, State/UT-wise during the last three years, year-wise; (b) whether the Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation (CDSCO) has found five batches of medicine manufactured by three Government controlled drug manufacturers failing the quality tests, if so, the details thereof; (c) whether the Government has received any report in this regard, if so, the details thereof and the name of the companies along with the drugs found sub-standard or spurious; (d) the steps taken by the Government against the erring employees; and (e) whether these drug companies conducted dissolution test before marketing the paracetamol tablets, if so, the details thereof and the action taken to prevent any damages to the public who bought these medicines in good faith along with measures to ensure maintenance of quality standards by pharmaceutical companies?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. HARSH VARDHAN)(a): As per information received from various State/UT Drugs Controllers, number of drugs samples tested, number of drugs samples declared sub-standard and spurious/ adulterated and action taken thereon during the last three years and current year is enclosed as Annexure-I.(b) to (e):   Details of drugs declared as spurious/substandard/adulterated/misbranded by Central Drug Testing Laboratories are regularly uploaded on Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation (CDSCO) website (www.cdsco.nic.in) under the heading “Drug Alert”.    Details with regard to five batches of medicines manufactured by three Government controlled drug manufacturers failing the quality tests have also been uploaded on the website of CDSCO (www.cdsco.gov.in) in the month of January 2020 and a copy of the same is enclosed as Annexure-II.-2-        Licence for manufacturing, sale & distribution of drugs are granted by the State Licensing Authorities appointed by respective State Governments. State Governments are empowered to take action on violations of any of the provisions of Drugs & Cosmetics Act, 1940 and Rules made thereunder. In all the above cases, the State Drugs Controllers of the concerned State where the drugs were manufactured have been informed accordingly.        Out of above five cases, four had failed in the test for dissolution for Albendazole tablets and one failed in the test for disintegration for Diclofenac Sodium tablets I.P. ………………..
