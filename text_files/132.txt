                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT

                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

190






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.06.2019






Anganwadi Centres




H. Vasanthakumar














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the funds allocated, released and utilised by the  Government for  setting  up  of Anganwadi  Centres(AWCs) in the country during each of the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise;(b)	whether the Government has made any  assessment  with  regard  to  the requirements and availability of AWCs in the country and if so, the details and the outcome thereof,   State/UT-wise particularly in Tamil Nadu;(c)	whether  the  Government  has received  proposals  from  States/UTs  for setting up of more AWCs in their respective States/UTs; and(d)	if so,  the  details  thereof  along with the action taken by the Government on such proposals so far, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT (SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a)	The funds are released for setting up of Anganwadi Centres(AWCs) in the country under, General Component of Anganwadi Services. The details of the funds allocated, released and utilized by the States/UTs during each of the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise are at Annexure I to IV.(b)	to (d) Under the Anganwadi Services Scheme, a total of 14 lakh AWCs were sanctioned, most of which have already been allocated to States/UTs. The States/UTs have been advised to re-assess their requirement of AWCs. They have also been authorized vide guidelines issued on 1st December, 2014 to relocate AWCs. While relocating the AWCs, the aspect of availability of adequate land, building, water facilities, ease of access of pregnant women and children besides maximum travel distanced not being more than 5 km in case of urban areas and 3 km in rural areas and 1.5 km in tribal areas for the AWWs is to be kept in view.*********  STATEMENT REFERRED IN REPLY TO PART (A) OF LOK SABHA UN-STARRED QUESTION NO. 190 FOR 21.06.2019 ASKED BY SHRI H.VASANTHAKUMAR REGARDING ANGANWADI 	Annexure-I(Rupees in Lakhs)Sl.
