                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

893






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.02.2020






Residential Buildings at Mumbai Port Trust




Arvind Ganpat Sawant














                        Will the Minister of





SHIPPING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is aware of the poor condition of residential buildings on leased plots at Mumbai Port Trust (MPT) and if so, the details thereof; (b)	whether the Government has received any memorandum/complaints from the tenants/residents residing in these buildings regarding exorbitant rise in lease rent; and (c)	if so, the details thereof and the action taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	Yes, Sir. There are only Five (5) such residential buildings on leased plots of Mumbai Port Trust (MPT). In accordance with lease covenant entered between MPT and lessees, the onus of construction and maintenance of such buildings lie with the lessees. Further, Maharashtra Housing Area Development Authority (MHADA), under MHADA Act, have powers to undertake structural repairs of such old buildings requiring no involvement/NOC from MPT. These old buildings have been declared by MHADA as ‘Cessed Category Buildings’ and the lessees, accordingly, are paying ‘Repair Cess’ to Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai (MCGM) for maintenance of their respective buildings.(b)&(c)  Yes, Sir. 10 complaints received by MPT on CPGRAMS Portal in 2018 & 2019 on the rent issue have been replied.
