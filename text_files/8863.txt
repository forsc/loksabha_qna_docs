                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2340






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2019






Detention Camps in Assam




Badruddin Ajmal














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total number of detention camps in Assam;(b) the number of persons currently kept in these detention camps in Assam, camp-wise and gender-wise;(c) whether there is separate room for women and men detainees;(d) whether these detention camps are lacking essential and basic facilities and people with the tag of “D voters” and ‘declared foreigners’ by FTs are living miserable lives, deprived of human rights;(e) whether there has been news of rape of various women detainees;(f) if so, the action taken by the Government to prevent such incidents and take action against the culprits;(g) whether detainees are not allowed to connect with their family members to prove their citizenship; and(h) the procedure to visit a member locked in detention camp?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (h):   There are 06 (six) Detention Centres in Assam. The number of persons in different Detention Centres as on 28th November, 2019 is as follows: --2--LS.US.Q.NO. 2340 FOR 03.12.2019Sl No.	Name of Detention Centre	Male	Female	Grand Total1.	Goalpara	186	15	2012.	Kokrajhar	09	131	1403.	Silchar	57	14	714.	Dibrugarh	38	02	405.	Jorhat	132	64	1966.	Tezpur	224	98	322 Total	646	324	970Women and men detenues are kept in separate rooms, with sufficient essential and basic facilities. They are living with human dignity and they are not deprived of any basic facility. There is no report of any rape incident of women detainees in Detention Centres. The detainees are allowed to meet their family members and legal advisors and there is no restriction as regards this.*****
