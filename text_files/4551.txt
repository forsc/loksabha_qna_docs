                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4192






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.07.2019






Excessive Salt in Drinking Water




Prataprao  Jadhav














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether presence of excess salt in water is leading to serious diseases like kidney disease among the people of various States, particularly in Shegaon, Jalgaon, Jamod, Sangrampur, Khangaon, Nandura, Malkapur areas in Buldhana region of Maharashtra and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has taken or proposes to take any steps to reduce the excess quantity of salt in water in Buldhana region, if so, the details thereof; and(c) the funds allocated to the States, especially to Maharashtra in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) Rural Water supply is a State subject. For improving the coverage of safe drinking water to rural population, this Ministry supplements the efforts of the States by providing them with technical and financial assistance through the centrally sponsored scheme National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP). It is the State Governments who plan, design, approve, execute, operate & maintain the schemes for providing safe drinking water to rural population. The funds provided to the States under NRDWP can be utilized for taking up schemes in water quality affected areas on priority including salinity affected areas.As per the information entered by Government of Maharashtra at the IMIS of this Department, 23 rural habitations are affected from Salinity in drinking water as per details below:S.No.	Blocks	No. Of Salinity affected rural habitations1	Jalgaon (jamod)	92	Malkapur	13	Nandura	14	Sangrampur	125	Total	23As reported by State Government of Maharashtra, during the house to house survey conducted by Health Department of BuldhanaZilaParishad in 2018-19, patients have been observed with Kidney diseases inShegaon, JalgaonJamod, Sangrampur, Khangaon, Nandura, Malkapur areas in Buldhanadistrict of Maharashtra.(b)& (C) As reported by Government of Maharashtra, the State has sanctioned Regional Water Supply Scheme under NRDWP for 140 villages in Jalgaon-Jamod and Sangrampur tehsils. Out of 140 villages, 72 villages are under saline tract and regular water supply has been started. The State Government has also sanctioned scheme for HinganeGavhad and 13 villages in RRWSS in Nandura tehsil under NRDWP and regular water supply has been started from April, 2018 in 11 Villages. Govt. of Maharashtra has also installed 83 RO plants in various villages of the Buldhana district. The details of funds released to States, including state of Maharashtra under NRDWP during 2018-19 is placed at Annexure. ********
