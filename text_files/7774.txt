                                    QUESTION NO:

                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.11.2019






Scheme for Judicial Infrastructure




Manickam Tagore B














                        Will the Minister of





LAW AND JUSTICE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details about the fund allocation made by the Government under Centrally  Sponsored Scheme for Judicial Infrastructure for the last three years;(b) 	whether there is shortage of court rooms in district courts;(c) 	if so, whether the Government proposes to construct more court rooms under this  scheme along with the details thereof; and(d)	 the steps taken/being taken by the Government to construct adequate number of  court rooms in district courts across the country?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF LAW AND JUSTICE, COMMUNICATIONS AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI RAVI SHANKAR PRASAD)(a) :      The Budget Estimates, Revised Estimates and Actual Expenditure under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme (CSS) for Development of Infrastructure Facilities for Judiciary during last three years including the current financial year are given below:(Rs. in crores)Year	Budget Estimates	Revised Estimates	Actual Expenditure2017-18	621.20	621.21	621.212018-19	622.00	650.00	650.002019-20 (As on 21.11.2019)	710.00	-	702.86(b) & (c):  As per the information made available by the High Courts, presently, 19,422 Court Halls are available in the country which is sufficient for the working strength of 18,129 Judges/Judicial Officers of District and Subordinate Courts. In addition, 2,798 Court Halls are under construction. The target is to increase the number of Court Halls to match the Sanctioned Strength of 23,566 Judges/Judicial Officers.(d): 	The Centrally Sponsored Scheme (CSS) for development of infrastructure facilities for the judiciary is being implemented for providing financial assistance to State Governments/UTs for construction of court buildings and residential units of judges and judicial officers of district and subordinate courts. A sum of Rs. 7453.10 crore has been sanctioned so far under the Scheme from 1993-94, of which Rs. 4008.80 crore has been sanctioned since 2014-15 which is around 54% of the total release under the Scheme. During the current financial year a sum of Rs. 710.00 crore is budget allocation under the Scheme, of which a sum of Rs. 702.86 crore has already been sanctioned to States/UTs.***********  Annexure-IStatement referred to Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No. 1411 for Reply on 27.11.2019Statement showing State-wise release of Grants under Centrally Sponsored Scheme during the last 3 years.
