                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether two severe cyclonic storms namely Tauktae and Yaas had struck in Gujrat and Odisha respectively including adjoing States/UTs i.e. Tauktae on 18 May 2021 and Yaas in Odisha on 26 May 2021 causing therewith heavy damages to infrastructure, human being, standing crops, domestic animals etc.;(b) if so, the losses suffered, the names of those States that have been severely affected along with the report of damage two property, infrastructure, crops, cattle, fishing equipments and the number of peoplekilled/injured/displaced and missing, the details thereof, State-wise and district-wise;(c) whether the Government has assessed the impact of cyclone ‘Tauktae and Yaas‘ on fishermen and damage caused by it, if so, the details thereof;(d) whether the Government has sent Central teams to assess the damage or   Minister  &  Prime   Minister   visted   these   states   for   assising   the -2-L.S.US.Q.NO. 390 FOR 20.07.2021damages/implact of cyclones and if so, the outcome thereof and recommended allocation of funds to farmers for their crop losses and damages, the amount financial package / relief sought by the affected States and the assistance provided/likely to be provided by the Government, under Disaster Management Fund for Relief/Rehabilation works, State-wise including Tamil Nadu;(e) the steps taken/being taken by the Government for smooth co-ordination of all the concerned departments of Central/State Governments to minimize the losses due to such type of calamities in future togather with remedial steps taken/in pipeline for rehabilation/restoration of life of affected persons and infratructure in thses states; and(f) the number of NDRF and State personnel deployed and the number of persons rescued during these cyclones?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI) (a) and (b):    Cyclonic storm ‘Tauktae’ affected five States namely Gujarat, Maharashtra, Goa, Karnataka, Kerala, the UT of Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu (DNHⅅ), and Cyclone ‘Yaas’ affected three States namely Odisha, West Bengal and Jharkhand.   As   per   the  memoranda/ situation reports received  from  the  affected State Governments/UT Administration, details of reported losses/ damages is  given at Annexure. (c) and (d):    The primary responsibility of disaster management rests with the State Governments. To supplement the efforts, financial assistance is -3-L.S.US.Q.NO. 390 FOR 20.07.2021provided to the affected States from State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) and National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF) as per laid down procedure for management of relief necessitated by notified natural disasters. Memoranda/demands for financial assistance, have not been received from the State Governments of Goa, Kerala and Jharkhand. Financial assistance has been  sought by  States viz; Maharashtra - Rs. 92.37 crore, Karnataka - Rs. 10.89 crore, West Bengal - Rs. 4522 crore, Gujrat-Rs. 9836.01 crore and DNHⅅ - Rs. 56.53 crore.  Government of Odisha has not sought any financial assistance for relief measures. In the case of cyclones Taukate and Yaas, even before the receipt of memorandum from State Governments, the Central Government constituted Inter-Ministerial Central Teams (IMCT), which had visited the affected areas of Gujarat, Maharashtra, Goa, Karnataka, Odisha, West Bengal and Daman & Diu. In the aftermath of Cyclone Tauktae & Yaas, Hon’ble Prime Minister visited the State of Gujarat on 19th May 2021 and States of Odisha and West Bengal on 28th May 2021. In pursuance of the announcement made by the Hon’ble Prime Minister, Central Government released additional       financial  assistance of Rs. 1000 crore to Gujarat,  Rs. 500 crore to Odisha, -4-L.S.US.Q.NO. 390 FOR 20.07.2021Rs. 300 crore to West Bengal and Rs. 200 crore to Jharkhand, in advance, from NDRF for management of relief necessitated by cyclonic storms Taukate and Yaas. Besides, Central Government released the first installment of Central share of SDRF for 2021-22 amounting to Rs. 8873.60 crore on 29th April 2021 in advance to all States, including the cyclone affected States.As per the established procedure, the State Governments concerned, in the wake of a natural disaster, provide necessary relief to affected people, including farmers, from SDRF already placed at their disposal.  In addition, farmers are entitled for compensation under the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojna (PMFBY), which is being implemented by the State Governments concerned. (e):    The National Crisis Management Committee (NCMC), headed by Cabinet Secretary, monitors and coordinates the situation with the affected States and Central Ministries/ Agencies concerned during major disasters. Indian Metrological Department (IMD) issues regular and precise forecasts & warning bulletins to all the affected/ likely to be affected States/ UTs.There are well established institutional mechanisms at the National, State &   District  levels in the country for  appropriate  preparedness and taking-5-L.S.US.Q.NO. 390 FOR 20.07.2021prompt response measures for effective management of natural disasters. Central Government has established a robust early warning system and has significantly improved the accuracy of weather forecasts. Mock exercises and community awareness programmes are being regularly conducted to create awareness among people.The measures taken by the Central and State Governments have significantly improved the disaster management practices, preparedness, prevention and response mechanisms resulting in significant reduction in casualties during natural calamities, including cyclones, in the country. Further, strengthening of the disaster management is a continuing and evolving process of  governance.  (f):    For cyclone Tauktae, 71 teams (Gujarat-43, Maharashtra-4, Goa-1, Kerala-9, Karnataka-3, Tamil Nadu-5, Rajasthan-2, Daman & Diu-3, and Dadra and Nagar Haveli-1) of NDRF were deployed to assist the State/UT administrations.  Similarly in the case of cyclone Yaas, 113 teams (Odisha - 52, West Bengal - 46, Jharkhand - 8, Andhra Pradesh-3, Tamil Nadu-3 and A&N Islands -1) of NDRF were deployed to assist the State/UT administrations during the cyclones.  NDRF teams assisted the State administration in mass evacuation            of affected persons to safer places.
