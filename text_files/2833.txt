                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2639






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.07.2019






Agriculture Policy for Hilly Regions




Tirath Singh Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  	whether  the  Government  has formulated any separate agriculture policy/ action plan or proposes to formulate any such  scheme  for  the  hill  districts  of Uttarakhand  which  are  geographically different  from  plain  districts  and  do  not have irrigation and farming facilities unlike Tarai region and where agriculture is totally dependent on nature;(b)	 if so, the details thereof; and(c)	 the necessary steps taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER  OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (c):	 No, Sir.  However, though agriculture is a State Subject, Government of India facilitates the State Governments through various Centrally Sponsored and Central Sector Schemes for improving agriculture and thereby farmers’ welfare in the country including in Uttarakhand.   Central assistance in provided in the sharing pattern between Centre and States at 90:10 for 8 North Eastern and 3 Himalayan States (including Uttarakhand) and 60:40 for the rest of the States.  Central assistance for various schemes administered by the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers’ Welfare are demand driven and funds are released to the State Governments based on their Annual Action Plans and submission of utilization certificates and progress reports for previous releases.  A list of schemes is annexed at Annexure.
