                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1945






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.07.2021






National AYUSH Mission




Nalin Kumar Kateel














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYUSH be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government has launched a Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM) for implementation through States;(b) if so, the details and the implementation Status thereof, State/UT-wise;(c) whether the Government proposes to provide any special grant or aid for setting up of new Government educational institutions for AYUSH system of medicine in Karnataka and if so, the details thereof;(d) whether there is any proposal to make provision for budgetary allocation to develop Government Ayurvedic institutions as a Centre for Excellence in the country; and(e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF AYURVEDA, YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(AYUSH) (SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a) & (b): Yes, The Central Government has launched Centrally Sponsored Scheme of the National AYUSH Mission (NAM) in 2014 for im¬plementation through State/UT Governments. Under NAM, grant-in-aid is being provided to State/UT Governments as per the provision of NAM guidelines against the proposals received in State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs). The State/UT wise status of grant-in-aid provided and expenditure made is at Annexure-I. (c): Public Health being a State subject, the primary responsibility for setting up of new Government educational institutions for AYUSH system of medicine lies with the respective State/UT Governments including Karnataka. However, under NAM, there is provision of financial assistance for setting up of new AYUSH educational institutions in the States where it is not available in Government Sector. The status of grant-in-aid approved for new Government AYUSH educational institutions in the State of Karnataka under NAM is at Annexure-II.   (d) & (e): Ministry of AYUSH is running a scheme since 2007 for assistance to organisations engaged in AYUSH education, research and drug development etc. for upgradation to Centre for Excellence. Government aided Institutions/Undertaking/Universities including Government Ayurvedic Institutions are eligible to seek financial assistance up to Rs. 10 crores only.
