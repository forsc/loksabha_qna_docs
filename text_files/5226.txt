                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

429






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2019






Infrastructure of Fishing Ports




Ganesan Selvam














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING DEPARTMENT OF FISHERIESLOK SABHASTARRED  QUESTION No. *429TO BE ANSWERED ON 23RD JULY, 2019 Infrastructure of Fishing Ports *429.	SHRI SELVAM G:   Will the Minister of FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING ??????????, ??????? ?? ????? ?????? be pleased to state:(a) 	the details of the action taken so far by the Government to improve the infrastructure of various fishing ports in Tamil Nadu; (b) 	whether the Government has carried out any survey to identify fishing ports which lack infrastructure and other facilities, especially in Tamil Nadu and if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Government has received any proposal from various States for survey of fishing ports in certain coastal States; and (d) 	if so, the details of proposals submitted for establishment of new fishing ports during the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING (SHRI GIRIRAJ SINGH)(a)	to  (d)  :        A Statement is placed on the Table of the House. Statement referred to in reply to the Lok Sabha Starred Question No.429 put in by Shri Selvam G, Member of Parliament for answer on 23rd July, 2019 regarding Infrastructure of Fishing Ports (a) to (d) : 	 Department of Fisheries (DoF), Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme(CSS) on Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries provides financial assistance to the State Governments/State Agencies, Union Territories and Central Government Agencies/Organizations for development of new fishing harbours, fish landing centres and Up-gradation/expansion/ repair/renovation of the existing ones. The CSS on Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries provides funds for development of fishing ports to the State Governments/State Agencies, Union Territories and Central Government Agencies/Organizations, including Tamil Nadu and dovetails funds available under the Sagarmala Scheme of Ministry of Shipping. During the past three years and current financial year, central financial assistance of Rs. 203.67 crore has been provided to the Government of Tamil Nadu for construction of fishing harbours facilities at Thengapattinam, Poompuhar, Colachel, Mookaiyur, expansion of Chinnamuttam Fishing Harbour and one fish landing centre at Kunthukal. Besides, the Government of Tamil Nadu has reported construction of one new fishing harbour at Mallipattinam, modernization of two fishing harbours at Napattinam and Pazhyar with a total cost of Rs137.43 crore with the assistance of World Bank under Coastal Disaster Risk Reduction Project. The Government of Tamil Nadu further reported that the Chennai Fishing Harbour has been upgraded, modernized and renovated at total cost of Rs. 97.63 crore with financial assistance from various agencies such as NABARD, National Fisheries Development Board(NFDB) and Tsunami Rehabilitation Programme and NADP.  Formulation of proposals, construction, post-construction management of the  fishing harbours are done by the concerned State Governments /Union Territories(UTs) and in some cases by the Port Trusts.  To seek the central financial assistance under the CSS on Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries, the concerned State Governments/UTs/ Port Trusts are to complete pre-implementation activities, such as selection of site, completion of necessary engineering and socio-economic investigations, surveys, hydraulic model studies wherever required, land acquisition if required, obtain environmental clearance etc and to submit the Detailed Project Report (DPR). Further, at the request of the State Governments/UTs/Port Trusts, the Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying through one of its institutes namely Central Institute of Coastal Engineering for Fishery(CICEF), Bengaluru also assists the State Governments/UTs/Port Trusts in conducting necessary surveys to carry out reconnaissance surveys followed by detailed engineering & economic investigations and formulation of techno-economic feasibility reports and primarily construction plans for fishery harbour and fish landing centres, besides gives technical advice on engineering and economic aspects. During the past three years, CICEF, Bengaluru, based on the request of the concerned State/UT, conducted surveys at (i) two fishing harbours in Puducherry namely Thengaithittu and Karaikal to assess the infrastructure gaps and demands, (ii) phase-ii expansion of Astranga Fishing harbour in Odisha, (iii) Gopalpur Fishing Harbour in Odisha and (iv) Sassoon dock in Maharashtra. Details of the proposals submitted by the States/UTs for setting up new fishing harbours as well as expansion/modernisation of the existing ones during the past three years (2016-17 to 2018-19) and current year (2019-20) under the CSS are furnished at Annexure-I.   Details of the fishing harbour proposals submitted under Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund (FIDF) are furnished at Annexure-II.*****
