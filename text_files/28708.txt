                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

261






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.11.2021






Khelo India Centre




Manoj Kumar Tiwari













                                                    Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik
                                                








                                                    Ravindra Shyamnarayan alias Ravi Kishan Shukla
                                                








                                                    Subrata Pathak
                                                








                                                    Shrirang Appa Barne
                                                








                                                    Sudheer Gupta
                                                








                                                    Ravindra Kushawaha
                                                








                                                    Bidyut Baran Mahato
                                                








                                                    Prataprao  Jadhav
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the salient features of Khelo India centres;(b) whether the Government has opened/proposes to open Khelo India centres across the country;(c) if so, the details of such centres established and yet to be established across the country including NCT of Delhi, district-wise;(d) the  details  of  the  availability  of  trainers  and equipment facility in the district level Khelo India centres and the talented players  selected so far,  district-wise; and(e)  whether  the  Government  has  any  policy  to encourage sports amongst Tribals and Rural areas of the country and if so, the details in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF YOUTH AFFAIRS & SPORTS { SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR }(a) As a part of the Government’s vision to strengthen the sports ecosystem in the country at the grassroots level, a cost-effective sports training mechanism has been developed wherein the former Champion Athletes would become coaches and mentors, so that their expertise and experience would be properly utilised for nurturing of athletes at the grassroots level and to ensure a sustained source of income for them. The grant to each Khelo India Centre (KIC) is provided for remuneration of the former champion athlete as a coach, support staff, purchase of equipment, sports kit, consumables, and participation in competition and events.(b) Yes, Sir.(c) and (d) As on date, 453 KICs have been notified in 359 districts of 28 States/UTs, details of which, including National Capital Territory of Delhi, along with the sports disciplines for which training is imparted are at Annexure.(e) ‘Sports’ being a State subject, the primary responsibility of encouraging sports among citizens of the country, including in tribals and rural areas, rests with the respective State/Union Territory Governments. Central Government supplements their efforts. However, this Ministry runs a Central Sector Scheme, namely, the ‘Khelo India – National Programme for Development of Sports’ Scheme, wherein one of the verticals, ‘Promotion of rural and indigenous/tribal games’, is dedicated to the development of rural and indigenous/tribal games in the country. ****
