                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

220






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.03.2021






Increasing Crop Productivity under NFSM




Riti Pathak














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	   whether the Government has taken any initiatives for increasing the production and productivity of food crops under the National Food Security Mission (NFSM); and (b)	    if so, the details thereof, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????		 	  (SHRI NARENDRA  SINGH TOMAR) (a) & (b):	A statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) & (b) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.   220   DUE FOR REPLY ON 9TH MARCH, 2021.(a) &  (b):	In order to enhance production and productivity of food crops, the Government is implementing a Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National Food Security Mission (NFSM) since 2007-08 in the country. The mission aims at increasing foodgrain/food crop production through area expansion in niche regions and productivity improvement. Under NFSM, support is given for promotion of food crops viz., rice, wheat, pulses, nutri cereals (jowar, bajra, ragi & small millets), coarse cereals (maize & barley) and sugarcane. Assistance is given to the farmers, through States/Union Territories (UTs), for interventions like seed production, cluster demonstration on production and protection technology, certified seed distribution of newly released varieties/hybrids, integrated nutrient and pest management, distribution of plant protection chemicals, bio-pesticides, soil ameliorants, water saving devices like sprinkler sets, irrigation pipes, pumpsets and raingun, improved farm implements/tools etc., and trainings to farmers. The mission also provides support to Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) & State Agriculture Universities (SAUs)/ Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) for technology back stopping and transfer of technology to the farmer under supervision of Subject Matter Specialists/Scientists. The research organizations are supported for undertaking research projects that can help enhancing production and productivity of food crops.		The state-wise allocation and release of funds under NFSM programme is Annexed.
