                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

114






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2021






Financial Relief




Debasree Chaudhuri














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	the details of sectors which have been provided financial relief of Rs.20.97 lakh crores by the Government and whether it has desired positive impact;b)	whether any increase has been reported in employment opportunities due to this financial relief and if so, the details thereof;c)	whether economic activities have increased in the country due to this financial relief and if so, the details thereof; andd)	the extent to which the benefits have reached to the poor people due to this financial relief?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (FINANCE)(SHRI PANKAJ CHAUDHARY) (a) to (d): During the year 2020, Government had announced the following packages with the objective of fighting COVID-19 and making the country self-reliant, which including measures announced by the RBI, involved a total amount of Rs.29,87,641 crore: (i) A relief package titled Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Package on 26.03.2020 (ii) AatmaNirbhar Bharat Packages (ANB) 1.0, 2.0, and 3.0 announced on 13th -17th May, 2020, 12th October, 2020 & 12th November 2020 respectively.   Besides, a Rs. 6.28 lakh crore COVID stimulus package was also announced on 28.06.2021.   A Statement indicating details of these packages, including sectors covered is at Annexure-I.Atma Nirbhar Bharat Rozgar Yojana (ABRY) announced under ANB incentivizes jobs creation wherein Government of India contributes both the employees’ and employers’ share of contribution (12% of wages), in respect of eligible employees, in EPFO registered establishments, for two years.  The benefits are available for new employees employed on or after 1st October 2020 and up to 31st March 2022 or those who lost jobs during COVID 19 and did not join any EPF covered establishment up to 30.09.2020.   As on 18.11.2021, total benefits of Rs. 2561.10 Crore have been given to 39.35 lakh employees through 1,15,550 Establishments.   Further, to help MSMEs/Small businesses to meet their operational liabilities and resume businesses, Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) under ANB (revised from time to time) with an overall ceiling of Rs.4.5 lakh crore on guarantees as on date has helped businesses restart operations and retention of jobs. It has impacted around 5.45 crore employees as on 19.11.2021. Besides, Production Linked Incentive Scheme (PLI) for various sectors envisage creating national manufacturing champions and generate employment opportunities.  ANB comprises of various long-term schemes/programmes/policies whose objectives are to fight COVID-19 and to make the country self-reliant, outcomes of which would be visible over a period of time.  The increase in economic activities is evident by many high frequency indicators and parameters such as: (a) Industrial production in August, 2021 reaching 103 % of its pre-pandemic August 2019 level with manufacturing witnessing full recovery in most of the use-based categories (b) Eight-core industry output crossing its pre-COVID level of September 2019 led by coal, natural gas, steel, cement and electricity (c) PMI Services accelerating to a decadal high of 58.4 in October, 2021, on the back of sustained uptick in rail freight activity, growth in E-way bills and highway toll collections and increase in air freight and passenger traffic, among others (d) Increase in GST collections (e) Merchandise exports in October, 2021 crossing US$ 30 billion mark for the seventh consecutive month (f) merchandize imports in FY 2021-22 crossing their pre-pandemic 2019 levels in October 2021 (g) FDI achieving the US$ 20 billion mark in the first five months of FY 22 as in FY 21.  A Statement indicating benefits provided under the schemes to the poor, wherever applicable is at Annexure-II.
