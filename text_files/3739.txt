                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3414






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Studies of Yoga at Graduate/Post Graduate Level




Sumedhanand Saraswati














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether subject of Yoga is proposed to be introduced at graduate and postgraduatelevel in various universities of the country;(b) if so, the details thereof, State/ UT-wise;(c) whether any recognized courses of Yoga therapy are being run in any institutesof the country;(d) if so, the details thereof;(e) if not, the reasons therefor; and(f) the details of the minimum qualification fixed by the Government for Yogatherapist to run a Yoga therapy clinic?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a) & (b): The Ministry of Human Resource Development had constituted aCommittee under the chairpersonship of Prof. H.R. Nagendra on 15th January, 2016for making recommendations on Yoga Education in Universities. The Committee hadsubmitted its report in April, 2016 and prescribed curriculum for Yoga courses in itsrecommendations. As a follow up to the recommendations of the Committee, theMinistry asked the University Grants Commission (UGC) to promote five Yogacourses in Universities and Colleges. Course and Course Code-wise duration ofYoga courses are as follows:<pre>S.No. Course Course Code Duration1. Bachelors of Science (Yoga) B.Sc.(Yoga) 3 year to 6 year2. Masters of Science (Yoga) M.Sc. (Yoga) 2 year to 4 year3. Doctor of Philosophy (Yoga) PhD (Yoga)As per the PhDdurationspecified by UGC4. Post Graduate Diploma inYogaPGDY1 year to 2 year5. Post Graduate Diploma inYoga TherapyPGDYT 1 year to 2 year</pre>Further, to promote Yoga in Universities, Yoga Departments have beenestablished in the nine Central Universities, namely:<pre>1. Hemwati Nandan Bahuguna Garhwal University, Uttarakhand;2. Visva Bharati, Shantiniketan, West Bengal;3. Central University of Rajasthan, Rajasthan;4. Central University of Kerala, Kerala;5. Indira Gandhi National Tribal University, Madhya Pradesh;6. Manipur University, Manipur;7. Dr. Harisingh Gour Vishwavidyalaya, Madhya Pradesh;8. Central University of Himachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh;9. Central University of Haryana, Haryana.</pre>(c) to (e): Ministry of AYUSH has informed that Morarji Desai National Institute ofYoga (MDNIY), New Delhi, an autonomous institute of Ministry of AYUSH isconducting Post Graduate Diploma in Yoga Therapy (PGDYT) for medico &Paramedicos of one year duration in affiliation with Guru Gobind Singh IndraprasthaUniversity, New Delhi with effect from Academic Year-2019-20.(f): The minimum qualification for Yoga Therapist isi. a degree in Yoga or Yoga & Naturopathy with a minimum of 55% marks froma recognised University and two years experience in Yoga Therapy in areputed Institute/ Hospitalorany Graduate from a recognized University with Diploma of at least one yearduration from a recognized University in Yoga Education/ Yoga Studies/ YogaScience/ Yoga Therapy etc. with a minimum of 55% marks and practicalexperience in Yoga in a reputed Yoga Institute/ Hospital for a minimum periodof five years;ii. level II (Therapy level) of Yoga Professional Competence Exam of QCIsponsored by Ministry of AYUSH;iii. demonstration Skills in Yogic practices, for which skill test is conducted.******
