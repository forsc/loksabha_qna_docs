                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

496






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2021






Conversion of SHs into NHs




Heena Vijaykumar Gavit













                                                    Annasaheb Shankar Jolle
                                                








                                                    Umesh G. Jadhav
                                                








                                                    Unmesh Bhaiyyasaheb Patil
                                                








                                                    Sambhajirao Mane Dhairyasheel
                                                








                                                    Sujay Radhakrishna Vikhepatil
                                                








                                                    Shrikant Eknath Shinde
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has received proposals from the State Governments of Maharashtra and Karnataka for declaration of State Highways (SHs) as National Highways (NHs) during the last three years and the current year and if so, the details thereof, year wise;(b) the details of new NHs declared during the said period in both the States; (c) the length of SHs approved “In-Principle” for conversion into NHs subject to outcome of their DPRs in both the States during the said period, year-wise; (d) whether the Government/NHAI has any plans for asset monetization and if so, the details thereof; and (e) whether any new schemes are being introduced to create a network of highways and expressways and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) & (b)	The Ministry keeps on receiving proposals from various State Governments/UTs, etc., including from the State Governments of Maharashtra and Karnataka, for declaration of State roads as new NHs. The Ministry considers declaration of some State roads as new NHs from time to time based on requirement of connectivity, inter-se priority and availability of funds. The details of NHs declared in the states of Maharashtra and Karnataka during the last three years and current year are as follows:Year	Length of Roads  notified as NHs (in Km.) in the State of Maharashtra	Length of Roads  notified as NHs (in Km.) in the State of Karnataka2018-19 	0	1322019-20	174	02020-21	265	922021-2022 (as on date)	24	229(c)      None of SHs approved “In-Principle” for conversion into NHs in the State of Maharashtra and Karnataka during the last three years. (d)    32 NH stretches with length of 1574 Km have been identified by National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) for monetization during FY 2022.  (e)       The Government of India had approved Bharatmala Pariyojana Phase-I in October, 2017 with an aggregate length of about 34,800 km (including 10,000 km residual NHDP stretches) at an estimated outlay of Rs. 5,35,000.00 crores for development of about 9,000 km length of Economic corridors, about 6,000 km length of Inter-corridor and feeder roads, about 5,000 km length of National Corridors Efficiency improvements, about 2,000 km length of Border and International connectivity roads, about 2,000 km length of Coastal and port connectivity roads, about 800 km length of Expressways.*****
