                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

75






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.11.2019






Employment in Informal Sector




Mala Roy














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the people employed in the informal sector during the previous three financial years; and  (b)	the effect of demonetization on jobs in the informal sector?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (IC) FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT (SHRI SANTOSH KUMAR GANGWAR)(a) &  (b):  As per the results of Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) conducted by National Sample Survey Office, Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation during 2017-18, the percentage of workers engaged in proprietary and partnership enterprises in usual status basis (principal status+subsidiary) in non-agriculture and AGEGC sectors(The coverage of AGEGC Sector is [Ag]riculture sector [E]xcluding [G]rowing of [C]rops, market gardening, horticulture and growing of crops combined with farming of animals)was 68.4% in the country which is a major part of informal sector.Further, as per the results of Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) and annual employment-unemployment surveys conducted by Labour Bureau, Ministry of Labour and Employment, the estimated Worker Population Ratio  on Usual status (principal status+subsidiary) basis for persons aged 15years and above  to the extent available is  given below:State	PLFS 	Survey by Labour Bureau 	2017-18	2015-16	2013-14All India	46.8%	50.5%	53.7%(Note: Survey methodology and sample selection are different in PLFS and Labour Bureau survey).*****
