                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2290






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.08.2021






Facilities in Schools




Jamyang Tsering Namgyal














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of schools in the country in which basic amenities like drinking water and toilets are not available State/UT-wise including Union Territory of Ladakh;(b) whether the Government has set any target to provide the said amenities in each school in near future;(c) if so, the details thereof;(d) whether the Government has received any proposal from the Union Territory of Ladakh in this regard during the last two years; and(e) if so, the number of proposals approved and assistance extended by the Government?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) As per Unified District Information System for Education Plus (UDISE+), 2019-20, details of State/UT-wise number of Government schools in the country including Union Territory of Ladakh having drinking water and toilets facilities are at Annexure.(b) and (c) Education is in the concurrent list of Constitution and most of the schools are under jurisdiction of concerned States and UTs. State and UT Governments are the appropriate Governments under the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009, and have the responsibility and mandate to provide school infrastructure including drinking water and toilet facilities in schools in accordance with the norms laid down in the Schedule to the RTE Act, 2009 and as per respective State RTE Rules. States and UTs have been regularlyadvised to ensure that all the schools, including those under the non-Government sector (private, aided schools, etc.) in their jurisdiction should have provision for separate toilets for boys and girls; and safe and adequate drinking water facilities for all children.The Department of School Education and Literacy has launched an Integrated Centrally Sponsored Scheme for School Education – Samagra Shiksha w.e.f. 2018-19. Under Samagra Shiksha, States and UTs are supported for strengthening of existing Government schools, and for creation and augmentation of infrastructure facilities including drinking water and toilets as per Annual Work Plan and Budget of respective State/UT. The scheme also provides for an annual recurring composite school grant varying upto Rs. 1,00,000/- per annum depending upon the number of students, for all government schools, out of which at least 10% is to be spent on activities related to Swachhata Action Plan (SAP) for hygiene and sanitation. Central release to States and UTs for SAP Component under Samagra Shiksha for the years 2018-19, 2019-20 and 2020-21 are given below:YearAmount(Rs. in Lakh)2018-1920052.44682019-2022400.95002020-2122924.5575Under erstwhile SSA till 2017-18 and Samagra Shiksha effective from 2018-19, provision of 2.50 lakh drinking water facilities, construction of 4.12 lakh Boys’ toilets, 5.31 lakh separate girls’ toilets and 1.54 lakh toilets for Children with Special Needs (CwSN) have been sanctioned to States and UTs for elementary schools. Out of which, States and UTs have reported completion of 2.40 lakh drinking water facilities, construction of 3.89 lakh Boys’ toilets, 5.19 lakh separate girls’ toilets and 1.34 lakh toilets for CwSN, till 30.06.2021.Under erstwhile RMSA till 2017-18 and Samagra Shiksha from 2018-19, provision of 12,388 drinking water facilities, 34,063 Boys’ toilets, 36,158 Girls’ toilets’ and 1,878 toilets for Children with Special Needs (CwSN) have been sanctioned for secondary schools to States and UTs. Out of which, States and UTs have reported completion of 10,818 drinking water facility, construction of 28,508 Boys’ toilets, 28,605 separate girls’ toilets and 334 toilets for CwSN, till 30.06.2021.(d) and (e) The Ministry of Education has approved 144 drinking water facilities and 37 toilets with an estimate of Rs. 434.40 lakh in Government Schools in Union Territory of Ladakh during the years 2019-20 and 2020-21, based on the appraisal of the proposals received from them. A total amount of Rs.4290.80 lakh has been released to Union Territory of Ladakh during 2019-20 and 2020-21, for Non-Recurring components including School Infrastructure under Samagra Shiksha.* * * *
