                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

31






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2021






Digi Saksham Programme




Bidyut Baran Mahato













                                                    Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik
                                                








                                                    Subrata Pathak
                                                








                                                    Shrirang Appa Barne
                                                








                                                    Sudheer Gupta
                                                








                                                    Manoj Kumar Tiwari
                                                








                                                    Parbatbhai Savabhai Patel
                                                








                                                    Prataprao  Jadhav
                                                








                                                    Naranbhai Bhikhabhai Kachhadiya
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has launched any Digi Saksham Programme recently in joint venture with Microsoft India;(b)	if so, the details thereof and the key fact or salient features of the said programme;(c)	whether the Government proposes to provide free of cost training to rural youth in the field of digital skills including basic skills and advance computing through National Career Service Portal and if so, the details thereof and the steps taken in this regard;(d)	whether the Government is also considering free of cost training to disadvantaged group of the society who have been adversely affected by the ongoing Covid-19 pandemic and if so, the details thereof;(e)	the details of funds sanctioned and released under the said programme during the current year; and(f)	the number of youths, particularly from rural areas who get benefitted by the training under the said programme?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a) to (d) Yes, Ministry of Labour and Employment has partnered with Microsoft India and launched DigiSaksham - a joint digital skills programme to enhance the employability of youth by imparting digital skills that are required in an increasingly technology driven era. This initiative is an extension of the Government’s ongoing programs to support the youth from rural and semi-urban areas to acquire digital skills for better career opportunities. The salient features of the programme are: (i) to support government’s accelerated efforts to skill youth for employment; (ii) to develop standardized curriculum and content that would be able to meet the continuously evolving demands of the industry; and (iii) to transform ‘youth’ into a ‘workplace ready professional’ through knowledge acquisition and skill development.Under this programme, various free of cost courses such as Excel, Azure, Security Fundamentals and HTML are provided to the NCS jobseekers through Self-Paced Learning, Virtual Instructor Led Training Mode and Instructor Led Training Mode. The DigiSaksham Programme gives priority to the jobseekers of semi urban areas belonging to disadvantaged groups of the society, including those who have lost their jobs due to Covid-19 pandemic.(e) The Government of India has neither sanctioned nor released any funds for this programme. However, Microsoft India has contributed funds in this programme through its Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) mandate to train jobseekers on digital skills.(f) The total number of youths who got benefitted by the training of the said programme are as under:Jobseekers registered till date for training	13079Trained through Virtual Instructor Led Training Mode	2095Trained through Self-paced learning	5897	******
