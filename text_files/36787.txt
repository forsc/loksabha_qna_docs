                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4046






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






PMYY for Writers




Chandeshwar Prasad














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has launched Pradhan Mantri Yuva Yojana (PMYY) to train young and budding authors (of less than 30 years of age) in order to promote Indian heritage, culture and education system of the country;(b) if so, the details thereof and the salient features of the said scheme;(c) the number of such writers participating under the said Scheme;(d) whether the Government has any plan under the said scheme to translate the literature from one language to another Indian language in view of “Ek Bharat Shrestha Bharat” so as to ensure the exchange of literature and culture; and(e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN MINISTRY OF EDUCATION (Dr. SUBHAS SARKAR)(a) PradhanMantri – Mentorships’s Scheme for Young writers (PM-YUVA) has been launched by the Ministry of Educationon 29 May 2021 for young writers upto the age of 30 years.(b) The salient features of the scheme are as follows : i.A contest was organized to select young authors. The theme of the contest was ‘National Movement    of India’ with ‘Unsung Heroes’, “Role of Unknown Places in Freedom Movement’ etc as focus areas. ii. The contestants were asked to submit a manuscript of 5000 words to judge their suitability to      develop as a proper book under the mentorship scheme. iii. Proposals were invited in all twenty-twoofficial Indian languages and English iv. The selection was made by a Committee constituted by National Book Trust (NBT). v. The results were announced on 25.12.2021. vi. Mentors have been assigned to guide and develop the selected proposals into full-fledged books. vii. A consolidated scholarship of Rs. 50,000 per month for a period of six months per author is to       be paid under the mentorship scheme. viii. 10% royalty will also be paid by NBT on publication and sale of the books.  (c) A total of seventy-five authors have been selected through the All India contest. (d) & (e) The books selected under the PM-YUVA Scheme will be translated into different Indian      languages to ensure the exchange of Indian culture and literature in order to promote `Ek Bharat      Shrestha Bharat’.The translations will be undertaken after the publication in original language.                                               ***
