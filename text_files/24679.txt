                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

682






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2021






Employment Generation by KVIC




C.N. Annadurai













                                                    Anurag Sharma
                                                








                                                    Arvind Ganpat Sawant
                                                








                                                    Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar
                                                








                                                    Dhanush M Kumar
                                                








                                                    Ganesan Selvam
                                                








                                                    Gautham Sigamani Pon
                                                








                                                    Rebati Tripura
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of employment opportunities generated by the Khadi and Village Industries Commission (KVIC) in the rural and urban areas for SC/ST/OBC and economically weaker sections in Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Tripura and Uttar Pradesh during each of the last three years and the current year;(b) 	the quantum of funds spent for the purpose by KVIC during the said period, year-wise;(c) 	the contribution of Mahatma Gandhi Institute for Rural Industrialization (MGIRI) in regard to training and capacity building in rural industries;(d) 	whether the KVIC is implementing a new scheme for employment generation for various categories of persons including war widows, ex-servicemen and their dependent in rural and backward areas and if so, the details thereof and the achievement made so far; and(e) 	the other steps taken by the Government to provide more employment opportunities to Khadi workers from rural and backward area of the country?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NARAYAN RANE)(a) & (b): 	Social Category-wise units set up, Margin Money (MM) subsidy disbursed and estimated employment generated under Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP) in the States of Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Tripura and Uttar Pradesh during last three years and current year (up to 16.07.2021) is given at Annexure-I.(c):	Mahatma Gandhi Institute for Rural Industrialization (MGIRI) is working in following six verticals for strengthening rural industrialisation in micro and small industries sector through providing entrepreneurship development trainings, skill development trainings and quality testing and guidance services:i.	Khadi & Textile ii.	Bio processing and Herbal iii.	Rural Chemical Industries iv.	Rural Craft & Engineering v.	Rural Energy and Infrastructure vi.	Management and Systems MGIRI has conducted 168 trainings from 2018-19 to 2020-21 benefitting a total of 831 participants. MGIRI has also provided quality testing and guidance services to 125 entrepreneurs during the last three years.(d):	KVIC is implementing PMEGP scheme for employment generation for all categories of person including war widows, ex-servicemen, etc. in rural and backward areas. The scheme provides higher rate of subsidy for special categories including ex-servicemen and women, which is 25% in urban areas and 35% in rural areas. Since inception of the scheme in 2008-09 till 2019-20, 1755 ex-servicemen have set up PMEGP units with MM subsidy of Rs. 3951.32 lakhs. (e):	KVIC is implementing the Khadi Vikas Yojna Scheme for generating employment opportunities for Khadi workers across the country. Under the scheme, KVIC provides assistance to Khadi Institutions for development of Khadi and generation of employment opportunities under its various components, such asi.	Market Development Assistance (MMDA) to the Khadi institutions and Khadi artisans, ii.	Interest Subsidy to ensure availability of working capital on reduced rate of interest for Khadi institutions,iii.	Construction of work-sheds for providing Khadi artisans with sufficient space and congenial environment, iv.	Assistance to strengthen weak Khadi institutions,v.	Assistance for modernization of sales outlets of Khadi institutions, vi.	Training to the artisans.
