                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2761






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.07.2019






Optical Fibre in Saran District




Rajiv Pratap Rudy














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the total length of optical fibre laid under Bharat Net Project in the Saran district of Bihar along with the target set and achieved in this regard in the said district; and (b) 	the steps taken by the Government to completely cover the district under the said project and the time by which it is likely to be done along with the fund allocation made by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF COMMUNICATIONS, LAW & JUSTICE AND ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI RAVI SHANKAR PRASAD)(a)& (b)	BharatNet is being implemented in a phased manner to provide broadband connectivity at all Gram Panchayats (approx. 2,50,000) in the country, including Saran District of Bihar. Saran District has a total 339 Gram Panchayats (GPs). By laying 605 km optical fibre cable, 175 GPs have been made Service Ready and the remaining GPs are under implementation. All the GPs in the District are targeted to be connected by March 2020.The total estimated cost of BharatNet project, approved by the Cabinet on July 19, 2017, is Rs. 42,068 crore. The funds are allocated for the project as a whole and not District-wise.
