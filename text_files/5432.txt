                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4813






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2019






Recommendations of Ashok Dalwai Committee




Gaddam Ranjith Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether it is true that the Ashok Dalwai Committee recognized that one of the main sources of income for farmers is poultry;(b) whether it is true that the 146th recommendation of the Committee asked the Government to frame a scheme called ‘Rural Backyard Poultry Development’ to help small and marginal farmers; and (c) if so, the details of the steps taken/proposed to be taken to implement the above recommendation of the Committee? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE  MINISTER OF STATES FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(DR SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN) (a) Yes Sir. (b)  In the 146th recommendation of the Dalwai Committee, it was recommended that the Scheme Rural Backyard Poultry Development to be upscaled, from the one that currently supports BPL families for subsistence from backyard poultry, into enterprises comprising 200-400 birds. In case of low-in-put technology (LIT) birds, it would be possible to later upscale to 1,000-2,000 birds for larger scale poultry farming. (c) In the line of the recommendation, Department of Animal Husbandry & Dairying had introduced a scheme namely Innovative Poultry Productivity Project (IPPP- Broiler & LIT) under Innovative Project of ‘Productivity Enhancement’ as a component of Sub-Mission on Livestock Development under National Livestock Mission (NLM) for rearing of Broiler and Low-input technology (LIT) bird which supports the small entrepreneur in the rural sector.
