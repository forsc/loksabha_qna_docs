                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1217






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2021






JNV in Jharkhand




Chandra Prakash Choudhary














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas (JNV) are not being constructed atsome locations despite being sanctioned especially in Jharkhand and if so detailsthereof;(b) the details of the schools running on temporary buildings or not fully functionalin Jharkhand;(c) whether the Government is taking cognizance for restarting the constructionwork of the said Vidyalayas; and(d) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EDUCATION(SMT. ANNPURNA DEVI)(a) The Navodaya Vidyalaya Scheme envisages opening of one Jawahar NavodayaVidyalaya (JNV) in each district of the country. As on date, 661 JNVs have beensanctioned in the country, out of which 580 JNVs have permanent campus andconstruction work is in progress at different stages in 64 JNVs. 17 JNVs (including 01JNV at Palamu-I district of Jharkhand) could not be constructed due to non-receipt ofland from concerned State Government.(b) 05 JNVs at Pakur – II, Ramgarh, Palamu – I, Palamu – II and Khunti in the Stateof Jharkhand are functional from temporary buildings provided by the StateGovernment.(c) & (d): Construction of permanent buildings for JNVs is a continuous process,which depends upon the willingness of the concerned State Government/UTAdministration to make available requisite suitable land, free of cost, in favour ofNavodaya Vidyalaya Samiti (NVS) for construction of the permanent building. Foropening of JNVs, the concerned State Government/District Administration has toprovide 30 acres of land free of cost and free from all encumbrances along withsufficient temporary accommodation to run the Vidyalayas for 3-4 years or till ownpermanent buildings are constructed. Regular follow-up is being made with theconcerned State Governments for providing suitable land, free of cost for constructionof the 17 JNVs. Further, the progress of construction work is also monitored regularlywith construction agencies to expedite the on-going construction work.*****
