                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

285






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.07.2019






Medical Colleges




Pinaki Misra













                                                    Magunta Sreenivasulu Reddy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government proposes to build 180 Medical Colleges in the country, if so, the details thereof indicating the places/locations identified during the last three years and the current financial year, location and State/UT-wise; (b) the details of the criteria/norms adopted/being adopted by the Government to shortlist the cities/locations/areas for these new medical colleges; (c) whether the said criteria/norms are being diligently followed, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor along with the budgetary allocation made and utilised and the time period fixed for commencement, completion and operationalisation thereof; (d) whether the setting up of Medical Colleges is likely to be carried out under the Central scheme or in collaboration with the concerned States/UTs; and (e) whether the existing hospitals in various States are proposed to be upgraded to medical colleges, if so, the details thereof and if not, the manner in which infrastructure would be created for such new medical colleges?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE (DR. HARSH VARDHAN)(a) to (e):  A statement is laid on the Table of the HouseSTATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO LOK SABHASTARRED QUESTION NO. 285* FOR 12TH JULY, 2019(a) to (e):  No. However, the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare administers a Centrally Sponsored Scheme for “Establishment of new medical colleges attached with existing district/referral hospitals”. 82 new Medical Colleges are to be established under the scheme in two phases. The fund sharing ratio between the Central Government and States under the Scheme is 90:10 for NE/Special Category States and 60:40 for other States. The funds are released under the Scheme for civil work for medical college; hostel & residence for students/faculty; equipments; and for up-gradation of district hospital.Under Phase-I of the Scheme, 58 districts in 20 States/UT have been identified and approved. Total cost of establishment of one Medical College is Rs.189 crore. Funds to the tune of Rs.7507.70 crore have been released to the State/UT Governments for the approved medical colleges under the Scheme. 39 medical colleges have become functional. Phase-I of the Scheme was launched in January 2014 and extended till 2019-20.  Details are at Annexure-I.	Further, under Phase-II of the Scheme, 24 new medical colleges in 8 States have been identified.  The total cost of establishment of one Medical College under this Phase is Rs.250 crore. The State Governments have identified the locations on Challenge mode. Out of 24 medical colleges, 17 have been approved till date and funds to the tune of Rs.1375.13 crore have been released to the State Governments. The Phase-II of the Scheme was launched in February, 2018 and will be implemented till 2021-22. Details are at Annexure-II.The norms/criteria for identification of Districts under Phase-I of the Scheme were (i) District Hospitals/referral hospitals with bed strength of 200 or more, (ii) District/Referral hospitals of the districts where there is no medical college and (iii) Preference to underserved areas. Phase-II of the scheme was launched with the objective to ensure availability of one medical college in every 3 Parliamentary Constituencies and one Government medical college in each State on Challenge mode. This criterion has been followed to identify the locations.
