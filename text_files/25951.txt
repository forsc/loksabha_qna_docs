                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1824






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Aquifier Mapping in Chhattisgarh




Sunil Kumar Soni














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether ground water exploration and aquifer mapping programme has been implemented in Chhattisgarh; (b) if so, the details thereof including the districts in Chhattisgarh where the said programme has been implemented; and (c) whether the adequate rigs have been provided for the said programme as per the area of State and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)  Yes, sir. The Central Ground Water Board (CGWB) has taken up National Aquifer Mapping and Management program (NAQUIM) in the entire country, including Chhattisgarh.(b)  The aquifer mapping is aimed to delineate aquifer disposition and their characterization for preparation of aquifer/ area specific ground water management plans.  For Chhattisgarh, out of identified area of 96,000 sq km  for NAQUIM studies, so far, 63,762 sq. km. spread over 16 districts has been covered. Out of these 16 districts, studies in 11 districts (viz. Balrampur, Bemetara, Bilaspur, Durg, Jashpur, Kawardha, Korba, Mahasamund, Raigarh, Raipur and Surguja) have been fully covered whereas five districts (viz. Balod, Dhamtari, Gariyaband, Koriya, and Rajnanadgaon) have been partially covered. The remaining identified areas are targeted to be covered by March, 2023.(c)  Four inhouse drilling rigs have been deployed in the State for data generation. In addition, to meet the additional requirements of data generation, construction of wells through outsourcing have also been initiated in the State.*****
