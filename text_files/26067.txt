                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1765






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Centrally Sponsored Schemes




Jadon Chandra Sen














                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Union Government proposes to commence Centrally Sponsored Schemes at village level for participation of youths in sports in the country;(b) if so, the names of the districts where such tournaments have been organised for participation of youths in sports in Uttar Pradesh along with the way by which such a tournament has been helpful in promoting the youths as national level players over there; and(c) whether such tournaments are being organised in the distrct of Firozabad for the scrutiny of youths as national level sportspersons/players in Uttar Pradesh?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF YOUTH AFFAIRS & SPORTS { SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR }(a) to (c) ‘Sports’ being a State subject, the responsibility for development of sports and organising tournaments at local levels rests with the State/Union Territory Governments. Central Government supplements their efforts in this regard. However, this Ministry runs a Central Sector Scheme, namely, ‘Khelo India – National Programme for Development of Sports’ Scheme since 2016, wherein one of the verticals, namely, ‘Promotion of rural and indigenous/tribal games’, is dedicated to development of rural sports in the country.****
