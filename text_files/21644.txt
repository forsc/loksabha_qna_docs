                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2761






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2021






AYUSH Colleges




Ranjanben Dhananjay Bhatt













                                                    Saikia Dilip
                                                








                                                    Bheemrao Baswanthrao Patil
                                                








                                                    Ramesh Chander Kaushik
                                                








                                                    Tirath Singh Rawat
                                                








                                                    Basanta Kumar Panda
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) the total number of medical colleges including Unani/Homoepathy/Ayurvedic colleges proposed to be set up/expanded/upgraded and strengthened in various parts of the country, State/UT-wise, district-wise, especially in Gujarat, Telangana, Rajasthan, Haryana and North-eastern States including Himalayan region;(b) the total number of Graduates/Post-graduate seats available and likely to be made available in future in these AYUSH Colleges, State/UT-wise along with the time schedule set for setting up of these AYUSH colleges;(c) the research carried out in post graduate Ayurvedic medical colleges along with the details of the medicines developed during the last three years;(d) whether the medicines developed through these researches are being manufactured on regular basis, if so, the details thereof; and(e) whether the Government has set up/proposes to set up any herbal research institute in the Himalayan States, if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a):	Detailed information is as per Annexure-A (b):	Detailed information is as per Annexure-B (c) & (d):	The research work done in post- graduate ayurvedic college is not utilized to develop medicines. The research work data generated in teaching Hospital associated with Ayurvedic Colleges is used for training of students and also in public Health related matters.However, Government of India has established Central Council for Research in Ayurvedic Sciences, an autonomous body under the Ministry of AYUSH as an apex organization for undertaking, coordinating, formulating, developing and promoting research in Ayurvedic sciences on scientific lines. CCRAS is conducting clinical trial for validation of classical Ayurvedic formulations and also for new drug development. So far, the council has validated 125 classical Ayurveda formulations through clinical trial and generated their scientific evidence of clinical efficacy & safety in diseases of national importance.The clinical trial for 51 classical Ayurveda formulations are under process for generation of their scientific evidence of clinical efficacy & safety in diseases of national importance.CCRAS is also continuously engaged in development of new Ayurvedic drugs through clinical trials. So far, CCRAS has developed 09 New drugs and has been transferred to various industries through National Research Development Corporation (NRDC) for wider utility of public. The Council has also undertaken 13 new coded drugs which are at different phases of drug development. (e):	No, at present there is no such proposal under consideration of the Central Government.
