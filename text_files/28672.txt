                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

611






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.12.2021






Tea Branding




Pallab Lochan Das














                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the mechanism of Tea Branding in the country;(b)	whether it is a fact that Assam and West Bengal tea is being mixed with tea of other countries and are then sold in the market;(c)	if so, the details thereof; and(d)	the total tea exported and imported the Government during the last three years including cost at which the tea have been imported, country wise data?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY(SMT. ANUPRIYA PATEL)(a)	 : Tea Board promotes Indian Tea as a brand and its various varieties such as Darjeeling, Assam, Nilgiri, Sikkim, Kangra, Dooars-Terai and Tripura through various platforms i.e. Tea Board website, social media, promotional programs, Buyer Seller Meets, awareness campaign, Indian Missions abroad wherein a detailed description of uniqueness/characteristics & health benefits of Indian teas are displayed. Branding of Indian Tea and its varieties is also done through various stakeholders of Tea Industry. Tea Board has obtained GI (Geographical Indication) for three different unique regional teas namely, Darjeeling, Assam (Orthodox) and Nilgiri (Orthodox)for premium teas of India. Further, in order to strengthen the identity of Indian tea, the Tea Board has registered logos for Indian Tea as well as other teas viz., Assam, Nilgiri, Dooars -Terai, Tripura and Sikkim. (b)	& (c) : Teas are allowed to be imported into India. The percentage of tea import to total domestic production is about 2%, which  is mainly meant for re-export after value addition including blending. The Tea Board has issued instructions on 11.11.2021 directing not to blend imported tea with teas of Darjeeling (West Bengal)/Kangra/Assam (Orthodox)/Nilgiri (Orthothodox). (d): The Government of India does not export or import tea. ***
