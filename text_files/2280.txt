                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2216






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.07.2019






Operational/Non-operational Airports




Krupal Balaji Tumane













                                                    Sanjay Haribhau Jadhav
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of operational and non-operational airports in the country under the Airports Authority of India (AAI), State/UT-wise;(b) whether the Government/AAI has spent a large amount on the maintenance of non-operational airports during the last three years and the current year;(c) if so, the details thereof, State/UTwise;and(d) the steps taken by the Government/AAI for revival of non-operational airports?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a): The Airports Authority of India (AAI) owns and manages 136 airports in the country. The details are at Annexure - I.(b) & (c): Minimum expense has to be incurred by Airports Authority of India (AAI) to maintain these airports. The details are at Annexure-II.;(d): Ministry of Civil Aviation has launched Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS)-Ude Desh Ka Aam Nagrik (UDAN) in 2016 for promotion of Regional Connectivity by way of revival of un-served and under-served airports/airstrips, helipads of the State Governments, AAI, Civil Enclaves and CPSUs through a budgetary support of Rs.4500 crores.
