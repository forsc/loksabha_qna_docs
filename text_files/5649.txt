                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COAL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5163






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.07.2019






Coal Production




Rekha Arun Verma














                        Will the Minister of





COAL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the rank of India in coal reserves in the world;(b)	the estimated coal reserves and State-wise coal extracted out of it, State-wise;(c)	whether most of the coal reserves are in Eastern State and Chhattisgarh, Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh and Raniganj coal field in West Bengal have huge coal reserves of good quality of coal and if so, the details thereof; and (d)	whether the Government has signed Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to increase coal production in order to bridge the gap between demand and supply of coal in the country and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




 MINISTER OF PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS, COAL AND MINES (SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI) (a): India stands 5th in coal reserves in the world.(b & c):  Details of state-wise coal reserve as on 1st April, 2018 is given in Annexure-A. The detail of state-wise coal extraction/production for the last three from 2016-17 to 2018-19 (Provisional) is given in Annexure-B.(d): The Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) is signed between Coal India Limited (CIL) and Administrative Ministry (MoC) wherein coal production is kept as one of the parameters in such way that the target of the current year is kept with positive growth over the last year. During the current year, the coal production targets agreed upon is 660 million tonne (Mt) which is about 53 Mt above the actual production of about 607 Mt in the previous year.MoU is also signed between NLC India Limited (NLCIL) and Administrative Ministry (MoC) wherein there is no parameter with regard to coal production for the current financial year 2019-20.***** ANNEXURE-	 ASTATEWISE
