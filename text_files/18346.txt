                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2183






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.09.2020






DD/AIR in Border Areas




Ashok Mahadeorao Nete














                        Will the Minister of





INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the telecasting/broadcasting of Doordarshan and Akashwani is not clearly visible/audible in rural and border areas of the country; (b) 	if so, the reasons therefor; and (c) 	the steps taken to increase the number of transmitters, especially in the said areas, State/ UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE; MINISTER OF INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING; AND MINISTER OF HEAVY INDUSTRIES AND PUBLIC ENTERPRISES (SHRI PRAKASH JAVADEKAR)(a) & (b): All India Radio(AIR)’s broadcasting network provides extensive coverage throughout the country including rural and border areas. AIR transmitters cover 99.2% of the country by population and 92 % by area.On the other hand, Doordarshan (DD) through its DD FreeDish DTH Platform provides Doordarshan channels, AIR channels along with many private TV and FM channels throughout the country (except Andaman & Nicobar Islands). Prasar Bharati has also made DD and AIR channels available in Andaman & Nicobar Island in C-band through satellite.In additional to the above, Prasar Bharati is also providing its AIR and DD channels digitally through internet and also on NewsOnAir app available for Android and iOS platforms for mobile users.  (c)	In order to improve the coverage of AIR in the country, it has been decided to implement the following projects:i.	Installation of 32 FM transmitters (ranging 1 kW to 10 kW) in uncovered locations.ii.	Installation of 100 W FM transmitters at 111 locations for localized coverage.iii.	Installation of 5 mobile 5 kW FM transmitters along LOC in J&K.iv.	Installation of 50 kW SW digital transmitter at Kurseong (West Bengal). To supplement Terrestrial TV coverage in border areas, it has been decided to undertake projects to setup five new High Power TV Transmitters at Green Ridge, Patnitop and Rajouri (two) in J&K and Himbotingla in Ladakh.*****
