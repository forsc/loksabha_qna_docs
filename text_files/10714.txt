                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3994






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.12.2019






Inter-linking of NHs




H. Vasanthakumar














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government proposes to inter-link all National Highways (NHs) and widen the roads passing through Kanyakumari and nearby areas to ensure better connectivity and a hassle free traffic system;(b) 	if so, the details thereof;(c) 	the steps taken/proposed to be taken by the Government in this regard; and(d)	the time frame set for completion of work on all Expressways across the country including Kanyakumari?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) to (c)	 Kanyakumari District has three National Highways viz NH-44 (old NH-7), NH-66 (old NH-47)  and NH 944 (old NH-47B) passing through it.  National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) has taken up four laning of NH-66 and NH 944, two National Highway projects in Kanyakumari and nearby areas to ensure better connectivity and a hassle free traffic system, details of which are given at Annexure.  NH-44 (old NH-7) passing through Kanyakumari district is already four lane. (d) 	Bharatmala  Pariyojana including construction of identified Expressways is targeted for completion by the year 2021-22 as approved by CCEA.
