                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

186






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2019






Centres for Endangered Languages




Nalin Kumar Kateel













                                                    Doddaalahalli Kempegowda Suresh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) whether the University Grants Commission is providing financial assistance for creation of Centres for Endangered Languages in the Central and State Universities;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) the total number of such Centres set up for preservation and revitalisation of Endangered Languages in various Central and State Universities in the country during the last five years; and(d) the details of funds allocated and spent for the said purpose during the above period?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (d): A statement is laid on the Table of the House.*****STATEMENT AS REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) to (d) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.186 FOR 02.12.2019 ASKED BY SHRI NALIN KUMAR KATEEL AND SHRI D.K. SURESH, HON’BLE MEMBERS OF PARLIAMENT REGARDING CENTRES FOR ENDANGERED LANGUAGES.(a) & (b): Yes, Sir. Based on the recommendations of an Expert Committee constituted by the University Grants Commission (UGC) for studying the issues pertaining to preservation and promotion of Endangered Languages, a scheme for Promotion of Endangered Languages in State Universities and Establishment of Centres for Endangered Languages in Central Universities has been launched. To ensure the preservation of endangered languages, the identified universities have been grouped together into separate clusters viz.  North-Eastern Languages, Tribal Languages, Numeric and Coastal Languages and theoretical work of endangerment.(c) & (d): The details of amounts allocated and released for Centres for Endangered Languages to the 9 Central Universities and under Preservation and Promotion of Endangered Languages to the 7 State Universities are enclosed at Annexure.*****
