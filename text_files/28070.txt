                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3678






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






PMGSY Phase-III in Maharasatra




Hemant Patil













                                                    Ramdas Chandrabhanji Tadas
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of schemes sanctioned in Maharashtra under Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY) Phase-III;(b) 	whether the roads of Wardha and Amravati districts under Vidharbha region and other districts such as Nanded, Higoli and Yavatmal in the State of Maharashtra have been sanctioned under Phase-III;(c) 	if so, the details thereof; and(d) 	if not, the reasons for delay in according approval for the aforesaid work?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a) to (d):   Road works under Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY) are sanctioned as per the programme guidelines. After due scrutiny of Detailed Project Reports (DPRs) submitted by the States, observations are communicated to the States and works are sanctioned after full compliance of PMGSY guidelines.	Accordingly, 414 roads of 2,809.37 km length have been sanctioned to the State of Maharashtra under PMGSY-III. District-wise details of sanctioned works are given in the Annexure. ******
