                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3883






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2021






Impact of Termination of BllL’s Licence




K. Subbarayan














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware that more than 1000 workers of Bhadra International India Limited (BIIL) in the Chennai International Airport are left with uncertainty of employment after the BIIL''s license was terminated and if so, the details thereof; and(b) whether the Government will ensure continuity of their jobs with AAI or AI or whichever firm takes up the Cargo and ground handling work in the Chennai Airport?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) & (b): The license of M/s Bhadra International India Ltd. (BIIL) for providing Ground Handling Services at Chennai airport has not been terminated by the Airports Authority of India (AAI) but the contract expired due to completion of contract period on 22.09.2020.  AAI Cargo Logistics & Allied Services (AAICLAS), which is wholly owned subsidiary of AAI and Air India owned Air India Airport Services Limited (AIASL), engaged in warehousing and Ground Handling respectively, have decided to employ maximum number of employees of BIIL. A total of 1947 offers were made to former employees of BIIL. Only 608 of them have joined AIASL and AAICLAS.
