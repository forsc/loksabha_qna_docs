                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COAL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

127






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.07.2021






Utilization of CSR Funds




Sanjay Seth














                        Will the Minister of





COAL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  whether the Government has formulated any scheme to utilize Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) fund of coal companies for the development of coalfields especially in Jharkhand;(b)  if so, the details thereof along with the details of such development works being carried out; (c )       the quantum of funds accrued and utilized by coal PSUs for the purpose during the last three years and the current year;(d)       the status of physical progress of such development works/activities; (e)        whether any of the coal PSUs has defaulted in spending CSR funds and if so, the details thereof along with the corrective action taken by the Government thereon; and(f)        whether any relief is being provided to the families of the miners who suffered accidents and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER MINISTER OF  PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS, COAL AND MINES (SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI) (a) to (f): A statement is laid on the table of the House.   STATEMENT IN REPLY TO LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 127 FOR ANSWER ON 28/7/2021 ASKED BY SHRI SANJAY SETH, M.P. REGARDING UTILIZATION OF CSR FUND.    (a) & (b) : Development works/activities are undertaken by Coal India Limited (CIL) and its subsidiaries under Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR).  As per CSR policy of CIL, subsidiary companies undertake CSR works in their State(s) of operation and CIL undertakes CSR works in whole of India including the States covered by subsidiaries. Accordingly, Eastern Coalfields Ltd. (ECL), Bharat Coking Coal Ltd. (BCCL), Central Coalfields Ltd. (CCL) and Coal India Ltd. (CIL) have executed CSR works in the state of Jharkhand. The details of major CSR works in Jharkhand during the last three years and current year are enclosed as Annexure A.  (c) & (d):  The details of total CSR funds allocated by CIL subsidiaries operating in the State of Jharkhand and the actual expenditure in the State during the last three years and current year are given in the table below:Financial Year	Total CSR Statutory requirement as per Companies Act, 2013 of ECL, BCCL, CCL(Rs. crores)	Actual CSR Expenditure in the state of Jharkhand(Rs. crores)2018-19	52.62	50.972019-20	58.97	65.812020-21	55.30	71.332021-22 (Provisional)	57.58	 18.07(Apr. – June 2021)(Provisional)The physical progress of major CSR works in Jharkhand is attached as  inAnnexure A. (e):  No shortfall has been witnessed in utilization of  CSR funds in each of the last three years i.e. FY 18-19 to FY 20-21 in CIL and its subsidiaries collectively.  (f):   Although not a part of CSR, the following compensation is being provided uniformly in Coal India Limited and its subsidiaries:(A) Following compensation are being provided to the employee who suffered accidents or die due to mine accident:1.	Compensation under The Employee’s Compensation Act, 1923.2.	An amount of  ? 15 lakh is paid as ex-gratia, in case of fatal mine accident.3.	An amount upto ? 1,25,000/- is paid under the Life Cover Scheme in case of death.4.	In addition to compensation under the Employee Compensation Act, an additional amount of ? 90,000 is paid in case of death or permanent total disablement resulting on account of accident arising out of and in course of employment is paid to non-executive employees.5.	Upto ? 20 Lakhs as per Payment of Gratuity (Amendment) Act, 2018.6.	All employees are covered under the Coal Mines Provident Fund scheme with equal shares both by employees and the company. The accumulated amount is paid.7.	Payment of leave encashment and any other dues.8.	Pension as per Coal Mines Pension Scheme, 1998.9.	Compassionate Employment is provided to one dependent of the deceased employee. In lieu of compassionate employment, monthly monetary compensation is paid to the female dependent of the deceased employee. This amount is revised with every wage revision. Presently the Monthly Monetary compensation is paid at ? 26,292.97& ? 40,000 per month for non-executives and executives respectively  (B) Relief/compensation to the families of Contractors'' workers who suffered accidents or die due to mine accident: -1.	Compensation under the Employee''s compensation Act, 19232.	An amount of  ? 15 lakh is paid to the next of kin of the contractors’ worker in case of fatal mine accident by the contractor.3.	Amount of gratuity (if eligible) as per Payment of Gratuity (Amendment) Act, 2018 by the contractor.4.	Nominee of deceased contractors’ workers are eligible for payment of accumulated amount under EPF/CMPF.    Annexure-AMajor CSR works in Jharkhand during 2018-19 to 2020-21 and the current year S.
