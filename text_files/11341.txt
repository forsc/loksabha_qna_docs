                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

326






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2020






Criteria for Construction of Roads under PMGSY




Sumedhanand Saraswati














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of roads constructed since inception of Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY), year and State-wise along with the length of such roads constructed in Rajasthan, district-wise;(b) 	the criteria for construction of roads under the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana;(c) 	whether the criteria for construction of roads under the Yojana also includes recommendation letter from Member of Parliament;(d)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and(e) 	the details of the efforts being made by the Government to elicit views of Members of Parliament on construction of the said roads?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a): Under Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY), details of roads constructed since inception, Year and State-wise along with the length of roads constructed in Rajasthan is at Annexure-I. Details of districts wise roads constructed in Rajasthan under PMGSY is at Annexure-II.(b): The objective of PMGSY programme is to provide all-weather single connectivity to the eligible unconnected habitations in the rural areas with a population of more than 500 persons (Census 2001) and above in Plain areas. In respect of Special Category States (i.e Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir and Uttarakhand), the desert Areas (as identified in the Desert Development Programme), the Tribal (Schedule V) areas and selected Tribal and Backward Districts (as identified by the Ministry of Home Affairs and Planning Commission) the objective would be to connect eligible unconnected habitations with a population of 250 persons and above (Census 2001). For most intensive IAP blocks as identified by the Ministry of Home Affairs the unconnected habitations with population 100 and above would be eligible to be covered under PMGSY. PMGSY-II launched in May, 2013  envisages consolidation of the existing Rural Road Network to improve its overall efficiency as a provider of transportation services for people, goods and services. All new connectivity habitations and Up-gradation under PMGSY-I and Up-gradation of roads under PMGSY-II.  have already been sanctioned to Rajasthan. Government Of India (GOI) had approved the PMGSY III programme which mainly focuses on the consolidation of existing Through Routes and Major Rural Links that connect habitations to (i) Gramin Agricultural Markets (GrAMs )(ii)Higher Secondary Schools and (iii)Hospitals. The Government of India (GoI) has targeted to cover 1.25 lakh kms of road length with an expenditure of about Rs. 80,250 Crores for the country under PMGSY-III. The allocated road length under PMGSY – III to Rajasthan States is 8,662.50 km. The Rajasthan State has submitted a proposal of 298 roads of 2839.13 Km road length at cost of  Rs. 1393.26 Crores under PMGSY-III for which Pre Empowered Committee (Pre-EC) meeting was held on 10.01.2020. The compliance on the observations of Pre EC is awaited from the State Government. (c) to (e): As per PMGSY Guidelines, the priority of elected representative including Members of Parliament and Members of Legislative Assemblies are expected to be duly taken into account and give full consideration while finalizing District Rural Roads Plan (DRRP) and Core Network (CN).  The PMGSY Guidelines stipulate that a copyof Comprehensive New Connectivity Priority List (CNCPL)/Comprehensive Upgradation Priority List (CUPL)are to be sent to the concerned Member of Parliament(MP) by the Programme Implementing Unit(PIUs) seeking their suggestions for annual proposals and the MPs are provided at least 15days’ time to submit their suggestions. The Programme Guidelines also stipulates that the proposal of Hin’ble Members of Parliament are required to be given full consideration in the District Panchayat during the finalization of annual proposals under PMGSY.  In this regard, Members of Lok Sabha are to be consulted in respect of their Parliamentary Constituencies and Members of Parliament from Rajya Sabha are to be consulted in respect of the district of the State they represent , for which they have been nominated as Co-Chairman of the DISHA Committee of the Ministry of Rural Development.  Necessary instructions have been issued to states/UTs from time to time in this regard.*******
