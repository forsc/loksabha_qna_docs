                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1391






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.07.2019






Discontinuation of Central Schemes




Balubhau (Alias Suresh Narayan) Dhanorkar














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	Whether the Government has discontinued some of the Central schemes for the States;b)	If so, the details thereof;c)	The details of Central schemes which are now in operation and the amount allocated for the same during the last five years, scheme-wise; and d)	The details of total Central assistance to State’s Plans for each of the last three years?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	 to (d): Based on the recommendation of the Sub Group of Chief Ministers and in consultation with various Ministries/ Departments and other Stakeholders, GoI has rationalized the existing 66 Centrally Sponsored Schemes (CSSs) into 28 schemes  for CSSs which was communicated by NITI Aayog vide it’s O.M. dated 17.08.2016. It may also be stated that following the implementation of the recommendations of the 14th Finance Commission, the devolution to States has been increased from 32% to 42%, thereby increasing the resource availability with the States vis-a-vis the Centre. Further, the level of flexi fund available in each CSS has been raised from 10% to 25%. This was done to enable the States to satisfy the local needs and undertake innovations.  The trend in outlays in the Expenditure Budget of GoI for 28 CSSs for the last five  years shown below reflects an increase of around 60% in 2019-20 BE (I) compared to actual of 2015-16.
