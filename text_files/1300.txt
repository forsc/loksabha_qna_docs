                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1171






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.06.2019






Air Pollution in North India




Ravneet Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has constituted an Inter-State body to deal with adverse effects of air pollution in Northern India; (b) 	if so, the details thereof and if not the reasons therefor; (c) 	the details of locations of the monitoring stations sanctioned by the Government in the last five years particularly in Punjab; (d) 	the details of the targets, if any, set by the Government to reduce the levels of air pollution;(e) 	whether the Government is aware of a World Bank report that air pollution has impacted GDP by more than 5 per cent; and (f) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE  IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO) (a)	& (b) In order to address the air pollution across the country in a comprehensive manner, Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change has launched the National Clean Air Programme (NCAP). Overall objective of the NCAP is comprehensive mitigation actions for prevention, control and abatement of air pollution besides augmenting the air quality monitoring network across the country.The NCAP focuses on collaborative and participatory approach comprising all sources of pollution and coordination between relevant Central Ministries, State Governments, local bodies and other stakeholders.(c)	The details of locations of the monitoring stations sanctioned in the last five years in Punjab is given at Annexure.(d)	Taking into account the available international experiences and national studies, 20-30% reduction of PM2.5 and PM10 concentration is targeted by 2024 keeping 2017 as the base year for comparison.(e)	& (f) Reports estimating the economic impact of environmental degradation have been published by the World Bank from time to time. These reports need to be interpreted in proper perspective as they are based on different methodologies and involve various assumptions and uncertainties. ***
