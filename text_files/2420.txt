                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2233






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.07.2019






Solar Power Projects




Annasaheb Shankar Jolle













                                                    B. Y. Raghavendra
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of on-going Solar Power Projects in the State of Karnataka; (b) the present policy of the Government regarding participation of private sector in Solar Power Projects;(c) the quantum of funds allocated, sanctioned and released to the State of Karnataka for the said purpose during the last three years and the current year, District-wise;(d) whether the Government has identified a few localities or the solar zones in the State of Karnataka for further innovations and power generation; and(e) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (I/C) FOR NEW & RENEWABLE ENERGY AND POWER (SHRI R. K. SINGH)(a) The State Government of Karnataka has reported that in order to achieve the target of 6,000 MW by 2021set under its Solar Policy, Solar Power Projects of 5,864 MW capacity have been allotted in a phased manner out of which 4,445 MW capacity has been commissioned excluding 183.32 MW Solar Rooftop Projects. (b) The private sector participates in Solar Power Projects through competitive bidding, third party sale, captive projects, group captive projects and private solar parks.(c) The Government has sanctioned and released Rs. 270.28 crore to Karnataka so far for developing infrastructure works towards establishment of Solar Parks and the Viability Gap Funding (VGF) scheme. The year-wise details of funds released are as under:-Year	Amount (Rs. in Crore)2015-16	100.002016-17	79.802017-18	40.002018-19	50.48(d) and (e) Gadag and Bidar Districts in Karnataka have been identified as Zones with solar potential for connectivity under Inter-State Transmission Schemes (ISTS).*****
