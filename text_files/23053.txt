                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3990






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Policy for Rehabilitation of Sex Workers




Kinjarapu Ram Mohan Naidu














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the Government is planning to make any policy for rehabilitation of sex workers wishing to leave sex work and conditions conducive for sex workers to live with dignity and if so, the details thereof;(b)	the steps taken or proposed to be taken by the Government to ensure that children of the sex workers have the opportunity to choose a profession;(c)	the steps taken to ensure that the children of prostitutes do not face any discrimination in their day to day life due to stigma attached to the profession of their mother; and (d)	the steps taken to ensure that the mental health of a child and his or her overall development does not get affected due to social stigma attached to their mother’s profession? 






ANSWER




ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a) to (d)      Ministry implements the Ujjawala Scheme which is a comprehensive scheme for prevention of trafficking and rescue, rehabilitation and re-integration of victims of trafficking for commercial sexual exploitation.  The protective and rehabilitative homes provide basic amenities such as food, clothing, medical care, legal aid, education for rescued children and vocational training to provide them alternate livelihood options.   *****
