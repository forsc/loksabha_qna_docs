                        Will the Minister of





CHEMICALS AND FERTILIZERS


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	the details of production, consumption and requirement of fertilizers and pesticides during the last five years and the current year, fertilizer-wise and State/ UT-wise; (b) 	whether shortage of fertilizers has posed a serious problem for the farming community as it has led to spiraling of prices and hording of fertilizers during the previous year and if so, the details thereof; (c)	whether the Government has conducted/proposes to conduct any study to find out the reasons for increasing or decreasing of consumption of chemical fertilizers and if so, the details thereof and the steps taken to create awareness among farmers for the balanced use of fertilizers; (d)    whether the Government has envisaged to start production from units which were shutdown to meet the rising demand and if so, the details thereof; and (e)	the details of fertilizers imported/exported and expenditure incurred on fertilizer import during the last five years, year-wise along with the expenditure on subsidies accorded to fertilizers in the budget 2021-22 (RE) and 2022-23 and the reasons for difference between the two estimates?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR CHEMICALS &  FERTILIZERS                    (SHRI BHAGWANTH KHUBA)(a):  The details of production during the last five years and current year, fertilizer-wise and State-wise UT-wise are at Annexure-I.The details of consumption and requirement of fertilizers  during the last five years and current year, fertilizer-wise and State-wise UT-wise are at Annexure-II.The details of consumption of  selected pesticides ( Tech Grade)  State-wise  during the  years 2015-16 to 2019-20 are at Annexure-III, also details of production of pesticides ( Tech) during the year 2015-16 to 2019-20 are at Annexure-III-A.(b):   There is no shortage of fertilizer  in the country. However, in between the season, some States highlighted shortfall of DAP fertilizer, particularly in few districts.  Accordingly, based on the requests of State Government, DAP rakes were moved to meet the requirement.(c):    Possibility of increasing/decreasing use of fertilizers generally arises when it is applied without proper assessment of nutrient requirement of crop, contribution from soil and other sources, nutrient use efficiency of the fertilizer, availability of assured irrigation, mode, method and time of applications and policy. Indian Council of Agricultural Research under   All India Coordinated Research Project (AICRP) on Soil Test Crop Response (STCR)     has developed several soil test based fertilizer prescription equations which are being used under National Mission on Soil Health Card for fertilizer recommendations to farmers. The ICAR is suggesting soil test based balanced and integrated nutrient management through conjunctive use of both inorganic and organic sources (manure, bio-fertilizers etc.) of plant nutrients for judicious use of fertilizers and to improve soil health and fertility. The ICAR also imparts training, organises FLDs etc. to educate farmers on all these aspects as and when required.(d):   Government of India has mandated revival of Ramagundam, Gorakhpur, Sindri and Talcher units of Fertilizer Corporation of India (FCIL) and Barauni Unit of Hindustan Fertilizer Corporation Ltd (HFCL) through Joint Venture Company (JVC) of nominated CPSEs for setting up gas based ammonia-urea plants of 12.7 LMTPA capacity each. On operationalization of these plants would add 63.5 LMT urea production capacity per annum in the country(e) :   The details  of fertilizers imported /exported and expenditure incurred on fertilizer import during last five years along with  the expenditure  on subsidies accorded to fertilizers in the budget 2021-22 ( RE) and 2022-23 are given below:-Import of Urea from the year 2016-17 to 2020-21Year	              Qty in LMT	Value in Million US$2016-17         	 54.81	               1047.372017-18	                 59.75	               1295.872018-19	                 74.81	               2040.142019-20	                 91.23	               2302.952020-21	                 98.28    	       2580.27             Further, it is informed that all P&K fertilizers are covered under Open General License (OGL) under the Nutrient Based Subsidy (NBS) Scheme.
