                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2229






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.07.2019






Acute Water Shortage in Drought Hit Areas




Adoor Prakash














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is awareof acute shortage of drinking water in manyparts of the country especially the droughthit areas and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government hasidentified such regions and if so, the detailsthereof, State-wise; and(c) the action plan of the Government,if any for addressing these issues onpriority?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FORJAL SHAKTI(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) & (b) Water is a State subject. The identification of drought hit areas is done by States. After identification of such areas, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare is approached by States for financial assistance.  According to Ministry of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers’ Welfare, details of States/ UTs which faced drought like situation in 2018-19, are as under:Name of States	Number of DistrictAndhra Pradesh 	09Gujarat	11Jharkhand	18Karnataka	30Maharashtra	26Rajasthan	09Total	103		Source:Ministry of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers’ Welfare(c) This Ministry supplements the efforts of State Governments for improving coverage of rural drinking water supply by providing technical and financial assistance through centrally sponsoredNational Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP).To take relief measures for restoration of the drinking 	water supply in drought affected areas, States may utilize up to 25% of NRDWP funds as Flexi funds, in case of Natural Calamity. States can also take a long term measures using NRDWP funds for providing permanent solution to the drinking water problem in such areas.
