                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1858






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.02.2021






Female Juvenile Homes




Locket Chatterjee














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















1858. 	MS. LOCKET CHATTERJEE: Will the Minister of WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT be pleased to state: (a) 	the total number of shelter homes and female juvenile homes currently functional across the country along with the number of children and women residing in them, State-wise including West Bengal;(b) 	whether the Government has taken note of any incidents of torture and sexual exploitation of children at shelter homes, if so, the details thereof along with the number of such cases reported during the last three years, State/UT-wise including West Bengal; and(c) 	the steps taken by the Government for protection and welfare of children at such homes/institutions? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a): The total number of Child Care Institutions (CCIs) (including Observation Homes, Special Homes and Place of Safety for children in conflict with law), State/UT-wise including West Bengal, along with the number of children residing in them, supported under the Child Protection Services (CPS) is at Annexure-I, as per information furnished by the State/UT.(b) & (c): The Juvenile Justice (Child and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 (JJ Act) and the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Model Rule, 2016 framed thereunder mandate regular monitoring and inspection of CCIs. The primary responsibility of execution of the Act rests with the States/UTs.Under the Commission for Protection of Child Rights Act, 2005, the National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) and State Commissions for Protection of Child Rights (SCPCR) have been established, to oversee the implementation of the JJ Act. As informed by the National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR), number of complaints received in NCPCR relating to torture/ violence and sexual exploitation of children in CCIs during the last three years, i.e., 2017-18, 2018-19 & 2019-20, State/UT-wise including West Bengal details are at Annexure-II.Ministry has asked States/UTs to ensure regular monitoring and supervision of CCIs at the level of District Magistrates/Deputy Commissioners. An advisory has also been issued to the States/UTs recommending action to be taken in case of disruption to the life of children in case of any untoward incidence of abuse in CCIs.***Annexure-IAnnexure referred in reply to part (a) of the Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No.1858 for answer on 12.02.2021 raised by Ms. Locket Chatterjee regarding “Female Juvenile Homes”.
