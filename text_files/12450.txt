                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1335






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2020






Protection of Ancient Monuments in Backward and Rural Areas




Ashok Kumar Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has taken or proposes to take any measures for the protection of ancient monuments, State-wise particularly in the backward and rural areas of the country; (b)	if so, the details thereof along with the number of places of archaeological importance under the control of Central Government in the country especially in backward and rural areas;(c)	the funds allocated and expenditure incurred on these ancient monuments for maintenance and restoration during the last three years, monument-wise and State-wise including Uttar Pradesh; and  (d)	the other steps taken by the Government for development of these places?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE AND TOURISM (PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) &(b)	Yes Sir, The Central Government has declared 3691 monuments/ sites as of national importance throughout the country including monuments/ sites from backward and rural areas. A state-wise distribution of monuments is given at Annexure-I. (c)	Conservation, preservation and maintenance of monuments in the country, including those situated in backward and rural areas,  is met out from the regular budget of Archaeological Survey of India.Expenditure incurred during the last three years on conservation and preservation of all monuments of national importance including monuments situated in Uttar Pradesh is given at Annexure-II. (d)	Tourist facilities like drinking water, toilets, signages, ramps, wheel chair, benches, pathways, Wi-Fi, cafeteria, etc. are provided at monuments wherever tourist potential is high considering feasibility and resources.
