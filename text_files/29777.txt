                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1433






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.12.2021






Prevent Linguistic Discrimination in Films




Saptagiri Sankar Ulaka














                        Will the Minister of





INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it is a fact that Odia film “Khyanikaa - The Lost Idea” was pulled down from Over The Top (OTT) platform without any formal communication;(b)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor;(c)	whether the Government has taken any steps to prevent linguistic discrimination especially Odia films which is a classical language; and(d)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING; AND MINISTER OF YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS(SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR)(a) & (b): The Government has not issued any direction for pulling down of the Odia film “Khyanikaa - The Lost Idea” from any medium. (c) & (d):The Central Board of Film Certification (CBFC) certifies films for public exhibition in India in accordance with the Cinematograph Act 1952, the Cinematograph (Certification) Rules, 1983 and the Guidelines for film certification issued thereunder and ensures that there is no linguistic discrimination while evaluating a film. CBFC is also having a separate regional office in Cuttack, Odishathat caters to certification of Odia Films.The film“Khyanikaa - The Lost Idea” was certified by the Central Board of Film Certification (CBFC) on 29.12.2017 under UA (Universal Public Exhibition with Parental Supervision for children below 12 years) category for public exhibition in India.The Ministry of Information & Broadcasting organizesthe National Film Awards every year wherein films from across the country inHindi/Regional Languages are awarded under different categories.Also, films are selected from various Indian languagesfor exhibition in the Indian Panorama sectionof the International Film Festival of India (IFFI), Goa conducted every year. The Odiafeature film “Khyanikaa” was selected for screening in the Indian Panorama section of the 48th International Film Festival of India, 2017 organized in Goa.
