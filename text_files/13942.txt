                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2583






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.03.2020






Textile Parks




Vishnu Dayal Ram














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of textile parks which have been built out of the 59 textile parks which have been sanctioned under the Scheme for Integrated Textiles Park (SITP); (b) the number of fully functional parks out of the ones which have been built;(c) whether all the sanctioned parks have not been built or made fully functional and if so, the reasons therefor; (d) whether the scheme has had the impact that it was envisaged to have in terms of promotion of industrial clusters, geographical agglomerations of firms and hubs of production; and(e) if so, the corrective steps being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF TEXTILES(SMT. SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a) & (b):	The Government is implementing the Scheme for Integrated Textile Park (SITP) under which currently, 56 textile parks are under various stages of implementation which may be seen at ANNEXURE-I. Out of these, 22 parks have been completed as per extant scheme guidelines. The details are given at ANNEXURE-II.(c):	Out of the 56 textile parks sanctioned under SITP, 22 textile parks have been completed as per extant scheme guidelines. The remaining parks are under various stages of implementation.(d):	The Scheme for Integrated Textile Park (SITP) provides support for creation of world-class infrastructure facilities for setting up of textile units, with a Government of India grant upto 40% of the project cost  and Government of India grant upto 90% of the project cost for first two projects (each) in the  States of Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Tripura, Sikkim, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Union Territory of Jammu & Kashmir and Union Territory of Ladakh; with ceiling limit of Rs.40.00 crores for each textile park. The Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) formed by the representatives of local industry, Financial Institutions, State Industrial and Infrastructural corporations and other agencies of State and Central Governments  registered as a Corporate Body under Companies Act would submit their proposal directly to the Ministry for consideration. Ministry of Textiles holds periodic review of textile parks with all stakeholders including the State Governments in the Regional Textile Conferences. Ministry conducted a stakeholders’ meeting to review the Scheme of Integrated Textile Parks in July, 2019 under the chairpersonship of Hon’ble Minister for Textiles. Apart from this, meetings of Project Approval Committee (PAC) headed by Secretary (Textiles) are also held on regular intervals and SPVs also participate in the meeting to apprise of the progress of parks. Due to the impact of scheme, in the 22 completed textiles 1655 industrial units are operational which are generating an employment to 82,112 persons and bringing in an investment to the tune of   Rs. 9075.29 crores.(e):       Does not arise.
