                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

816






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Arsenic Contaminated Water




Gopal Chinayya Shetty














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the steps taken by the Government to address the problem of contamination in cities/towns/areas reporting Arsenic contaminated drinking water;(b) the policy decisions taken by the Government to solve the problem during the last three years and the steps taken by the Government to spread country-wide awareness in this regard; and(c) the details of districts/towns/cities/areas which are in grip of Arsenic contamination, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF JAL SHAKTI(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a)&(b) Government of Indiain partnership with States is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission – Har Ghar Jal to enable every rural household in the country to have potable water supply through Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC) by 2024. While allocating funds under JJM to States &UTs, 10% weightage has been given to the population affected by chemical contaminants including Arsenic. States/ UTs are advised to accord priority to quality-affected habitations. Further, States are also to provide interim relief  in quality-affected habitations through community water purification plants (CWPP) to meet with drinking and cooking needs until potable water supply through tap connection is provided.Under JJM, upto 2% of the allocation to States/ UTs can be utilized for Water Quality Monitoring & Surveillance activities (WQM&S)i.e. for setting up and strengthening of water quality testing laboratories, surveillance by community using field test kits(FTKs), awareness generation and educational programmes on water quality, etc.In March 2017, National Water Quality Sub-Mission (NWQSM) was launched (now subsumed under JJM), with the aim to provide safe drinking water to 27,544 Arsenic/ Fluoride affected habitations in the country.Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs has stated that the Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) launched by the Union Government on June 25, 2015, in selected 500 cities and towns across the country with focus on development of basic urban infrastructure in the Mission cities, interalia includes water supply.One of the key objectives of the Mission is to ensure that every household has access to a tap connection with assured supply of water and given first priority. Under AMRUT Mission, out of the total plan size of Rs.77,640 crore, Rs.39,010 crore (50%) has been allocated to water supply.(c) District-wise details of Arsenic-affected rural habitations is at Annexure.****
