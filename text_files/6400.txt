                                    QUESTION NO:

                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.11.2019






Funds for Vocational Training




Gitaben Vajesingbhai Rathva













                                                    Naranbhai Bhikhabhai Kachhadiya
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	The details of the fund approved by the Government for the Vocational Skill Training during the last three years, State/UT-wise;(b)	Whether the youths trained through the Vocational Skill Training are able to gain employment;(c)	If so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise; and(d)	The details of the sectors in which the employment is being made available?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) 	Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship, Directorate General of Training has formulated following schemes in various States for Vocational Skill Training:(1) Upgradation of existing Government ITIs into Model ITIs: The scheme was approved in Dec. 2014 for a total cost of Rs 300 crores.  Implementation period for the Scheme was 3 years i.e., till FY 2016-17. The scheme has now been extended by the Standing Finance Committee (SFC) for a total cost of Rs. 300 crore, in its meeting held on 09.08.2018 under the chairpersonship of Secretary (MSDE) till March 2020. (2) Skill Development in 47 Districts Affected by Left Wing Extremism:The scheme was formulated in March, 2011 @ a total cost of Rs. 241.65 crore with a duration upto 31st March, 2012. The scheme had been revised thrice. The cost of the scheme during the latest revision is Rs. 407.85 crore. The period of implementation of the scheme was upto 31st March, 2020. (3) Enhancing Skill Development Infrastructure in NE States: The scheme was formulated in 2011 at a total of Rs. 57.39 crore to enhance the existing infrastructure of skill development in North Eastern States, which had been revised with a new component for “establishment of 22 ITIs” in 8 North-eastern States. The cost of the scheme was Rs. 298.13 crore. The scheme has been further revised with enhanced coverage and cost of Rs. 417.93 crore in September 2018 and will be continued till 31st March, 2020. (4) Vocational Training Improvement Project (VTIP): The scheme, interalia covers upgradation of 400 Government ITIs have been covered in 34 States/UTs. The scheme was closed on September 2018.(5) Skills Strengthening for Industrial Value Enhancement (STRIVE): STRIVE is a new central sector World Bank funded project at a total cost of Rs. 2200 crore. The project duration is 5 years and the closing date is November 2022. 	(6) Implementation of ISO29990 in ITIs: Funds provided under "Implementation of ISO29990 in ITIs is as follows:2017-18        :		200 lacs (Expenditure 189.84 lacs)2018-19        : 		805 lacs (Expenditure NIL)2019-20         : 		05 lacs  (Expenditure as on 14.11.2019 NIL)(7) Scheme of Polytechnic: Community Development Through Polytechnic (CDTP) scheme envisages providing non-formal, short-term, employment-oriented skill development programmes, through AICTE approved Polytechnics, to various sections of the community, particularly the rural, unorganized & disadvantaged sections of the society, to enable them to obtain gainful self / wage employment. Duration of training usually ranges from three to six months. Till 2017 a total number of 12,05,807 trainees have been trained since 2009. However data on trained youths employed is not available in Polytechnic Division. DGT releases Grant in aid to the State / UT governments under the scheme.Reply of (b) to (d)i.          A tracer study was conducted by Mott MacDonald in year 2012 for 11028 passed outs from ITIs. The sample size of study was of 500 ITIs across 21 states. The employment of passed out trainees were 63.5%.ii.         Vocational Training is a concurrent subject of both Central and State Governments. The development of training schemes at National level, evolution of policy, laying of training standard, norms, conducting of examinations, certification, etc. are the responsibilities of the central government, whereas, day to day administration including admission in ITIs rests with the respective State Governments / UTs. Accordingly, employment data is maintained by the respective State/UTs.The details of funds approved / released by the Government in various states for the Vocational Skill Training during the last three years as given at Annexure -1 to Annexure -6.
