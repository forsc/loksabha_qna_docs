                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2306






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.03.2020






Implementation of AMRUT Scheme




Nalin Kumar Kateel














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the current status of implementation of Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) in the country with special reference to Karnataka; (b) 	the details/number of cities selected under this Centrally sponsored scheme in the State of Karnataka along with the projects taken up and implemented under the scheme till date; and (c) 	the funds allocated and spent under the scheme in Karnataka during the last three years, year and city-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                               (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a).	The focus of Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) is to provide basic infrastructure for water supply, sewerage & septage management, storm water drainage, non-motorised urban transport and development of green spaces & parks in 500 Mission cities.Under AMRUT, Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs has approved all the State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) amounting to ?77,640 crore for the entire Mission including Central share of ?35,990 crore. So far, works have been completed for 2,482 projects costing ?8,856 crore while 2,889 projects costing ?64,764 crore are under implementation. Further, projects costing about ?10,382 crore are at various stages of tendering. Some States/UTs have taken up projects in excess of their approved SAAP size. In such cases, the entire excess amount shall be borne by the State/UT.For Karnataka, all the SAAPs amounting to ?4,952.87 crore for the entire Mission have been approved including Central share of ?2,318.79 crore. So far, works for 189 projects costing ?935 crore has been completed while 207 projects costing ?4,160 crore are under implementation. Four projects costing ?24 crore are under tendering. (b).	27 cities have been selected under AMRUT in the State of Karnataka. The city-wise details of projects taken up under AMRUT by the State of Karnataka has been placed as Annexure-I.(c).	Under AMRUT, Central Assistance is not released project-wise or city-wise, but to the State/UT as per the Mission guidelines. The year-wise details of Central funds released to the State of Karnataka during the last three Financial Years and Utilisation Certificates (UCs) received has been placed as Annexure-II.                                          ****
