                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4690






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2021






5G Services




Margani Bharat














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 the manner in which reducing notice period for the rollout of 5G services will help telecom companies and consumers;(b) 	whether the consumers will get 5G services before the anticipated time and if so, the details thereof; and(c) 	the status of 5G spectrum auction undertaken by the Ministry?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS, EDUCATION AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a)	Reduced notice period for offering any new technology base station site for testing while rolling out networks for compliance of roll out obligations will provide more flexibility to telecom service providers (TSPs) for deploying new technologies.(b)	TSPs are free to deploy any technology using spectrum acquired through auctions/liberalized spectrum. The 5G services are to be introduced gradually and will advance to full range of services as ecosystem and demand for services grow.(c)	Government has conducted auction of spectrum in 700 MHz, 800 MHz, 900 MHz, 1800 MHz, 2100 MHz, 2300 MHz and 2500 MHz bands in March, 2021. Entire available 2308.80 MHz spectrum was put to auction; out of which the TSPs bid for a total of 855.60 MHz.
