                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4668






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2019






River Saraswati




Hanuman Beniwal














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details in regard to present status of search for Saraswati river, State wise;(b)	the details of achievements made till date and the budget provision for search of this river and the amount spent till date; and(c)	whether the Government has given any responsibility to the Government of Haryana and Rajasthan in this search and if so, the role of Rajasthan and Haryana Government in this search?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE & TOURISM(PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)	An Advisory Committee for Multidisciplinary Study (ACMS) for river Sarasvati for 2 years was constituted in September, 2015, the tenure of which was extended for 2 years in December, 2017. Two meetings of ACMS and two meetings of Sub-Committees were convened so far.(b)	The excavation at Binjore (in Rajasthan) was carried out during 2014-15 to 2016-17. Earlier excavations at Adibadri, Bhirrana, Hansi (all in Haryana); Baror, Karanpura, Chak 86, Tarkhanwala Dera (all in Rajasthan); Juni Kuran (in Gujarat) were carried out under Sarasvati Heritage Project since 2003. The details are at Annexure. The expenditure incurred site-wise is as follows:Adibadri	:	Rs.11,56,514/-Bhirrana	:	Rs.65,13,428/-Hansi	        :	Rs.7,25,000/-Baror   	:	Rs.26,60,000/-Karanpura	:	Rs.91,15,000/-Chak 86 & Tarkhanwala Dera   	:	Rs.9,41,199/-Juni Kuran	:	Rs.38,49,000/-Binjore	        :	Rs.1,40,04,173/-(c)	No, Sir. ANNEXURE           ANNEXURE REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (b) OF THE LOK SABHA UNSTARRED QUESTION NO. 4668 FOR   22.07. 20191.	Adi Badri, Distict Yamunagar, Haryana: Adi Badri is located on the bank of the river Som. The place is believed by some to be located on the confluence of the Saraswati-Somb confluence. The excavations carried out at three different sites here have revealed the remains datable from the historical period and early medieval times.2.	Baror, District Sri Ganganagar, Rajasthan: Excavation have unearthed the remains of the pre-Harappan and the mature Harappan cultures. The most significant objects include four pre-Harappan button seals, one Harappan seal besides numnberous other objects and plain as well as profesely decorated pottery. The excavation has yielded pottery decorated with painted motifs which have close relationship with the similar pottery from Kalibangan and Banawali on one hand, and the Baluchistan sites on the other.3.	Bhirrana, District Fatehabad, Haryana: The excavation has yielded a two-fold cultural sequence namely, pre-Harappan and Mature Harappan. Two arms of the Harappan enclosure wall and domestic structures, in addition to five Harappan seals, hundreds of beads of semi-precious stones such as lapis lazuli, carnelian, agate, steatite, and other material, copper tools and other pre-Harappan and Harappan objects of daily use were unearthed.  On chemical cleaning, two copper celts, revealed Harappan signs inscribed on them.  4.	 Binjore, District Sri Ganganagar, Rajasthan: The remains of residential complexes of mud bricks with Multi structural phases have been noticed along with variety of pottery on harappan affirnity. The major pottery found includes perforated jar, dish on stand, vases, basin, bowl, beaker, goblet and terracotta cake (mustika). The antiquities found include copper objects such as fish-hook, arrowheads, knife, celt, bangles, terracotta bangles, faience bangles, shell bangles, beads of carnelian, agate, faience, gold foil and ring, terracotta toy cart frame & bull figurines. Harappan seal made of steatite depicting figure of unicorn with five letters of Harappan script has also been found. 5.	Chak 86, District Sri Ganganagar, Rajasthan: It is located at a distance of nearly 200 to the north of Tarkhanwala Dera. The ancient mound measuring approximately 250 X 200 m is 3 m high from the surrounding plains. The excavation here has revealed two circular structures of wattle-and-daub and associated post holes. The pottery assemblage datable to late Harappan and Painted Grey Ware period was also unearthed. 6.	Hansi, District Hisar, Haryana: The excavation at this multicultural site has revealed evidence of habitation from Kushana (circa first century A.D.)  to British times. Some fragments of Painted Grey Ware have been noticed in later period fillings. Three coin hoards of the Bull and Horseman Type (circa 9th -11th centuries A.D.) are among the important antiquarian finds.7.	Juni Kuran, District Kachchh, Gujarat:  The excavation has revealed an impressive layout of the citadel, surrounded by massive defensive wall on all four sides. In the southern arm, there have been exposed a gate, corner bastion and a gate and large and broad platform. The pottery and antiquities recovered confirm to classical Indus types.   8.	Tarkhanwala Dera, District Sri Ganganagar, Rajasthan: Excavation has revealed structures of mud bricks and a hearth outside the house complex which may be attributed to Harappan period. The pottery assemblage consists of dish-on-stand, beakers goblets and basins, cylindrical and perforated jars. Most important finds of the season are the terracotta sealing and a potter’s kiln.
