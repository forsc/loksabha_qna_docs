                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2555






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.03.2020






AYUSH Hospitals




Abdul Majeed Ariff













                                                    Geeta Kora
                                                








                                                    A. Chellakumar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH)   be pleased to state: (a) the details of AYUSH hospitals functioning and proposed to be established in different parts of country, State/UT-wise and stream-wise including Kerala, Tamil Nadu and Jharkhand; (b) the funds allocated and released to the States for the purpose during each of the last three years and current year, State/UT-wise; (c) the details of staff strength including doctors and medical practitioners in each stream of AYUSH system of medicine, hospital-wise, location-wise; (d) the steps taken by the Union Government for supplementing the efforts of the State Government to promote AYUSH in Kerala; and (e) the steps taken by the Government to further improve the condition of existing AYUSH hospitals in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(SHRI SHRIPAD YESSO NAIK)(a):      The details of AYUSH hospitals functioning in the different parts of country, State/UT-wise including Kerala, Tamil Nadu and Jharkhand and stream-wise is furnished at Annexure-I. As health is a State subject, the establishment of AYUSH Hospitals in the different parts of country comes under the purview of concerned State/UT Governments. However, under Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM) there is a provision for financial assistance for setting up of upto 50-bedded integrated AYUSH Hospital. The State/UT-wise list of approved upto 50 bedded integrated AYUSH Hospitals including Kerala, Tamil Nadu and Jharkhand is furnished at Annexure-II. (b):      The fund allocated/released to States/UTs during last three years and current year, State/UT-wise is furnished at Annexure-III.Contd…….-2-(c):      Since, health is a State subject, the details of staff strength including doctors and medical practitioners in each stream of AYUSH system of medicine, hospital-wise, location-wise is not maintained by the Central Government.(d):     The Ministry is supplementing the efforts of the State Governments including Kerala to promote AYUSH system through Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM), which inter-alia envisages better access to AYUSH services, strengthening of AYUSH educational institutions, facilitate the enforcement of quality control of Ayurveda, Siddha and Unani & Homoeopathy (ASU & H) drugs and sustainable availability of ASU & H raw-materials by promoting medicinal plants in the States/UTs.(e):     Under Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM) provision has been made available for improving the conditions of the existing AYUSH hospitals in the country under the component of Upgradation of AYUSH Hospitals. As per the guidelines, existing exclusive/standalone Government AYUSH Hospitals are provided non-recurring Grant-in Aid for undertaking construction, renovation of the existing premises, procurement of furniture, fixtures & equipments and recurring Grant-in Aid for procurement of medicines, contingency and deployment of additional manpower. The State/UT Governments are required to submit their proposal through the State Annual Action Plan (SAAP) as per the guidelines of the scheme.…………………….
