                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4580






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






Production of Jute




Sunil Kumar Soni














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether public and private jute producing companies have achieved their production target set as per the requirement of States in the country during the years 2018-19, 2019-20, 2020-21, 2021-22 and in the current financial year and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	the total quantum of bardana (jute) procured for kharif crops in Chhattisgarh during the said period, place and year-wise; (c) 	the demand made by Chhattisgarh to Union Government for bardana (jute) for kharif crops as per the requirement of jute and set standards along with the contract signed for availability/distribution thereof during the said period; and (d) 	whether the Government has provided bardana (jute) as per the demand made by the State during the said period and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E RMINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT AND CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a): 	No Sir, Public and private jute producing companies/ Jute mills have not been able to meet the requirement of new jute bags by the States for procurement of foodgrains during the years 2018-19, 2019-20, 2020-21, 2021-22 (current financial year) due to their limited production capacity of jute bags. ……….2/--2-(b)to(d): 	Based on the requirement of packaging material by the State Procuring Agencies (SPAs) including Chhattisgarh & Food Corporation of India(FCI), commitment by jute industry & foodgrain procurement estimates, GoI prepares month-wise supply plan for all the SPAs & FCI. Accordingly, SPAs & FCI places indents with Office of Jute Commissioner which in turn issues Production Control & Supply Order under the Jute & Jute Textiles & Control Order, 2016 to different jute mills for supply of jute bags as per relevant BIS standard. In this regard, no contracts are signed.The details of demand and supply of bardana (jute) bags for Kharif crops to State of Chhattisgarh are as under:Year	Demand Qty (Bales)	Supply Qty (Bales)2018-19	259000	2231942019-20	270000	2655442020-21	350000	1104702021-22	283000	214064To avoid short supply of jute bags for packaging of foodgrains, following steps are taken: a)	Permission for use of good quality used jute bags, are granted to SPAs/FCI.  States/FCI are also permitted to arrange new jute bags from sources other than the Office of Jute Commissioner, Kolkata.b)	Dilution in packaging norms for use of High-density polyethylene or polypropylene (HDPE/PP) bags as per provisions of Jute Packaging Materials(JPM) Act, 1987 is obtained from Ministry of Textiles.  *****
