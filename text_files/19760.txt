                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1037






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.02.2021






Ports/Shipping Logistics Management




Midhun Reddy













                                                    Pocha Brahmananda Reddy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has constituted a working group to study the ports and shippinglogistics of the Oil and Gas Industry in India and if so, the details thereof; and(b) the measures taken by the Government during the last three years to streamline the logisticsmanagement of the Oil and Gas sector in the country?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) The Government on 01.05.2020 has constituted a Working Group for comprehensive studyon the current practices of Ports and Shipping logistics management with respect to oil and gasindustry with the following terms of reference:-(i) To study the current practices of Ports and Shipping logistics management with respectto oil and gas industry and identify the areas of intervention for debottlenecking ofoperational issues.(ii) To strengthen the ports and shipping logistics management.(iii) To envisage future road map and policy action on ports and shipping management.(b) In order to strengthen the coastal infrastructure to streamline logistics management of Oil &Gas sector, the following measures have been undertaken:-(i) Construction of a new Captive Jetty at Kamrajar Port, Ennore (Chennai) and agrassroot POL Terminal at Vallur (Chennai) along with associated PL connectivitywith new Captive Jetty and Chennai Petroleum Corporation Limited (CPCL).(ii) Augmentation of coastal receipt facility at Mangaluru Terminal for construction ofadditional tankage of approx 67000 Kilolitres.(iii) Augmentation of coastal receipt facilities at JNPT, Mumbai Terminal to enablereceipt of larger size coastal parcels, thereby reducing the overall logistics cost.(iv) Augmentation of LPG Import Terminal at Kandla from 0.6 to 2.5 Million MetricTonnes Per Annum (MMTPA).(v) Setting up of LPG Import Terminal at Paradip.(vi) MoU signed with M/s Mundra LPG Terminal Pvt. Ltd. for import of LPG in order tomeet the increasing demand in Country and land locked northern India.(vii) Kandla Gorakhpur LPG Pipeline for efficient and fast turnaround of vessels alongwith meeting the increasing LPG demand in the country.(viii) Paradip Haldia Durgapur LPG Pipeline commissioned up to Durgapur & Bankaleading to increased and efficient utilization of Haldia Import Terminal.*************
