                                                    Sunil Dattatray Tatkare

                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    whether National Bank for Agriculture and RuralDevelopment (NABARD) through its Rural InfrastructureDevelopment Fund provides low cost fund support toState Governments for quick completion of ongoingprojects in the rural sector;(b) if so, the details thereof including the criteriafixed for allotting funds for rural infrastructure throughNABARD;(c) the details of funds disbursed to various Statesfor rural projects by NABARD during each of the lastthree years and the current year, State/UT-wise includingUttar Pradesh;(d) the details of funds sanctioned/utilized and lengthof roads constructed during the above period, State/UT-wise; and(e)  the mechanism put in place to monitor theexecution and timely completion of projects undertakenwith the assistance of NABARD?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(a)	& (b):NABARD under Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF) provides low cost funds, allocated out of the priority sector shortfall of scheduled commercial banks to the State Governments for supplementing their efforts for completion of ongoing as well as creation of new critical infrastructure projects in rural areas.  The state-wise allocation of corpus under RIDF is based on six broad parameters, namely, geographical area, rural population &rural poverty rate, composite infrastructure development index, inverse Credit-Deposit (CD) ratio and per capita priority sector credit.(c) Details of State-wise disbursement made under RIDF by NABARD during the last three years and current year are provided in Annexure-I.(d) Details of funds sanctioned for road projects under RIDF during the above mentioned period are provided in Annexure-II.(e) Project monitoring is primarily the responsibility of the implementing Department of the concerned State Government. The monitoring of RIDF projects by NABARD is done on sample basis and is conducted through the following tools: -(i)	Desk Monitoring (off-site) and (ii)	Field Monitoring (on-site)These tools are supplementary and complementary to each other which facilitate timely physical completion of projects, avoid cost/time overrun and add quality to the project.The monitoring of the progress is also ensured by NABARD through prescribed returns, periodic reviews at departmental level including at the level of High Power Committee under the Chairmanship of Chief Secretary of the concerned State. Social monitoring of projects is ensured through suitable project display boards with physical and financial details at project locations as well as prominent places.*****
