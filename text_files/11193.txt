                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2020






Hygiene in Schools




S. Jagathrakshakan













                                                    Brijendra Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether any steps have been taken by the Government to educate children on issues ofgender sensitization and menstrual hygiene in schools in the country and if so, the detailsof the budget allocated for the purpose as on date;(b) if not, whether there is such proposal in the pipeline;(c) whether the Government has any proposal/plan for menstrual hygiene for the girlstudents from class 6 to 12 in the country in order to prevent school drop outs of girlchildren;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a) to (e): Gender sensitization has been incorporated as a core component of the NationalCurriculum Framework-2005. All textbooks and training material for teachers address thetopic.Under Adolescence Education Programme (AEP), NCERT has prepared Training andResource Materials to train teachers and organise activities with students to help them tounderstand and challenge, stereotypes and discrimination, reporting abuse and violationsagainst women from young age.Under the Integrated Scheme for School Education - Samagra Shiksha, State specificprojects for varied interventions under equity, including menstrual health and hygiene areemphasized for enhancing access, retention, quality and reducing drop-out of girls in schoolsby promoting enrolment drives, retention and motivation camps, gender sensitization modulesetc. State Specific Projects are sanctioned by the Project Approval Board as per the AnnualWork Plans proposed by the State/UT concerned. Such projects include Life Skills, Awarenessprogrammes, incinerator, Sanitary Pad Vending Machines. The amount of Rs. 15566.96 lakhhas been approved for State Specific Projects for the year 2019-20 under Samagra Shiksha.The Ministry of Health and Family Welfare supports the States/UTs in implementingthe Scheme for Promotion of Menstrual Hygiene among adolescent girls in the age group of10-19 years, living primarily in the rural areas.*****
