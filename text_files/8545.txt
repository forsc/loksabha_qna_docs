                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2095






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2019






Funds for School Education




Shankar Lalwani














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the programmes/schemes being run by the Government to ease the school education system;(b) the details of funds allocated by the Government for these programmes, State-wise;(c) whether the Madhya Pradesh Government has utilised entire funds earmarked for theseprogrammes; and(d) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) & (b): The Department has launched the Samagra Shiksha – an Integrated Scheme for SchoolEducation by subsuming the erstwhile Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan(SSA), Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA) and Teacher Education (TE) from 2018-19.It envisages the ‘school’ as a continuum from pre-school, primary, upper primary, secondary tosenior secondary levels. The statement showing State and UT-wise details on release of centralshare and expenditure incurred under Samagra Shiksha during 2018-19 and 2019-20 is at Annexure-I.Further, the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Mid-Day Meal (MDM) is implemented toimprove the nutritional status by providing hot cooked meal to children studying in Classes I-VIII ofGovernment, Government-aided Schools, Special Training Centres including Madrasas andMaqtabs. The statement showing State and UT-wise details on release of central share and utilisationunder Mid-Day-Meal during 2018-19 and 2019-20 is at Annexure-II.Ministry of Human Resource Development is also implementing an umbrella Scheme forProviding Education to Madrasas/Minorities (SPEMM) to encourage Madrasas/Maqtabs to introducemodern subjects in their curriculum and strengthen/augment school infrastructure in MinorityInstitutions. The scheme comprises of 2 schemes namely, Scheme for Providing Quality Educationin Madarsas (SPQEM) and Infrastructure Development in Minority Institutions (IDMI).The schemeis being implemented at National level. Both the schemes are voluntary in nature. 16 States haveavailed benefits under the scheme from 2014-15 onwards. The statement showing State and UT-wisedetails on release of central share under Scheme for Providing Education to Madrasas/Minorities(SPEMM) during 2018-19 and 2019-20 is at Annexure-III.The Central Sector Scheme ‘National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship Scheme’ is alsoimplemented to award scholarships to meritorious students of economically weaker sections to arresttheir drop out at class VIII and encourage them to continue the study at secondary stage. Under thescheme, which is boarded on National Scholarship Portal (NSP), the scholarships are disburseddirectly into the Students’ bank accounts by Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). No grant is released tothe State/UT Government.(c) & (d) : The utilisation of the funds is a continuous process and the funds which are remainingunutilised during a particular financial year are carried forward to the next year.*****
