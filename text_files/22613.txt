                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3818






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2021






Airports without Flight Operations




Ramcharan Bohra














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of airports functioning in the country without any flight operation at present;(b) the extent of revenue loss suffered by the Government as a result thereof;(c) the steps taken by the Government to start flight operations in all airports;(d) whether any scheme/process has been made by the Government to establish coordination with various States; and(e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a): Airports Authority of India (AAI) owns and maintains 137 airports/ civil enclaves across the country. There are no flight operations at 27 of these airports.(b): AAI has suffered loss of Rs. 51.39 Crore on account of maintenance of such non-operational airports in the FY 2019-20. (c) to (e): Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs has approved the proposal for revival of existing unserved/ underserved airports/ airstrips/ heliports/ water aerodromes of the Airports Authority of India, State Governments, Public Sector Undertakings and Civil Enclaves at an estimated cost of Rs. 4500 Crore. An airport which is included in the awarded routes of Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) - UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik) and requires upgradation/ development for commencement of RCS operations is developed under "Revival of unserved and underserved airports" scheme. The expansion of airports under the scheme is ''demand driven'', depending upon firm commitment from airline operators as well as from the State Government for providing various concessions.
