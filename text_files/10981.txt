                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4320






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2019






Dual Time Zone




Manish Tewari














                        Will the Minister of





SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has any plan of considering a dual time zone in the country in view of the time gap between Eastern States and Western States, if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether the Government has taken cognizance of a scientific paper brought out by scientists at the Delhi-based CSIR -National Physical Laboratory (NPL) regarding the feasibility for India to have two time zones as it would increase productivity in all the north-eastern States, if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Government has acknowledged that a separate time zone for the north-eastern region could help in saving 20 million kWh of electricity a year according to researchers at the NPL, if so, the details thereof; (d) 	whether the Government proposes to adopt the dual time zone taking into account the benefits of likely increase in productivity and electricity savings; and (e) 	if so, the details thereof and if not, the strategic or other reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE; MINISTER OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY; AND MINISTER OF EARTH SCIENCES(Dr. HARSH VARDHAN) (a) 	No, Sir.(b)to(e)	Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR) - National Physical Laboratory (NPL), published certain reports in science journals on this issue, inter alia, referring to saving of electricity, etc.  The matter was examined by a High Level Committee (HLC) comprising Secretary, Department of Science and Technology, Director, CSIR-NPL and Chief Secretary, Govt. of Tripura.  The HLC after considering the issue recommended not to have two time zones for India for strategic reasons.*****
