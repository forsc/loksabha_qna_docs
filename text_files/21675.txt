                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2918






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2021






Availability of Ayurvedic Manuscripts




E. T. Mohammed Basheer














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government has taken any measures for collection and preservation of old manuscripts of Ayurveda texts/manuals;(b) if so, the details thereof; and(c) if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) & (b):	Ministry of AYUSH has declared National Institute of Ayurveda(NIA), Jaipur as   Nodal Agency for Ayurveda Manuscriptology. A Manuscript Unit has been set up in the Institute for and digitization of Manuscripts and rare books. 		The Central Council for Research in Ayurvedic Sciences (CCRAS) has undertaken various projects as a part of Acquisition, Cataloguing, Digitization and Publication of Textbooks and Manuscripts under Central Sector Scheme of Ministry of AYUSH. The details of Manuscripts digitized are as under: -i.	1679 Manuscripts/rare books have been digitized under the project entitled “Survey, Cataloguing and Digitized inventory of Medical Manuscripts of Eastern India (Orissa, West Bengal and Bihar)”. ii.	770 Manuscripts have been digitized under the project entitled “Survey, Cataloguing and Digitized inventory of Medical Manuscripts of North-East India”.iii.	2703 Manuscripts have been digitized under the project entitled “Collection and Digitization of Medical Manuscripts from South India, Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh (2008-2012).  (c):		Does not arise.
