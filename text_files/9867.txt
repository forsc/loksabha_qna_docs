                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3257






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan




Kunar Hembram














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of works extended by the State Governments under Sarva ShikshaAbhiyan during the last three years;(b) whether any constraints related to funds are being faced in implementation ofSarva Shiksha Abhiyan;(c) if so, the details thereof, State-wise including West Bengal along with thereaction of the Government thereto;(d) whether the Government of West Bengal or any other State Governmentshave demanded for allocation of additional funds under this scheme; and(e) if so, the reaction of the Government thereto?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a) to (e) Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA) was operational till 2017-18. TheDepartment of School Education and Literacy has launched an Integrated Schemefor School Education – Samagra Shiksha which subsumes three erstwhile CentrallySponsored Schemes of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA), Rashtriya MadhyamikShiksha Abhiyan (RMSA) and Teacher Education (TE) from 1st April, 2018. SamagraShiksha focuses inter alia on improvement in school infrastructure in all States andUnion Territories by providing support for various interventions like upgradation ofschools, strengthening of infrastructure of existing schools and provision ofcomposite school grant to every school for providing a conducive learningenvironment, at all levels of school education.Under erstwhile Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan from 2016-17 to 2017-18 andSamagra Shiksha effective from 2018-19, States and UTs have reportedconstruction of 8343 school buildings, 76594 additional classrooms, provision of6255 drinking water facility, construction of 31903 Boys’ toilets, 23623 separate girls’toilets, 10286 CwSN toilets till 30.06.2019, at the elementary level.Every year, Annual Work Plan & Budget (AWP&B) under Samagra Shiksha isprepared by the respective States and UTs based on their requirements andpriorities for various interventions including strengthening of the school infrastructure.These plans/estimates are appraised and approved by the Project Approval Board inMinistry of Human Resource Development as per the programmatic and financialnorms of the scheme, availability of budgetary resources and physical & financialprogress. The Central share released to the States and UTs including West Bengalfor 2016-17 and 2017-18 under erstwhile scheme of SSA; and for 2018-19 underSamagra Shiksha is at Annexure.* * * *
