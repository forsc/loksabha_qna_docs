                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3076






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.12.2021






Strengthening the Aviation Infrastructure




Kripanath Mallah













                                                    C. Lalrosanga
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to strengthen the aviation infrastructure in the country including North East Region and if so, the details thereof, State-wise;(b) the details of the airports where infrastructure development works are going on in North East Region including Assam;(c) the steps taken by Government to connect all the State capitals of North East Region in the near future;(d) whether the Government has set any target in this regard and if so, the details thereof;(e) whether the Government has included Assam under this programme; and (f) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) to (f): Yes Sir, air connectivity in the North East Region (NER) is priority for the Government.The expansion and strengthening of aviation infrastructure facilities in the country including North East Region, is a continuous process and is undertaken on the basis of operational requirements for safety of aircraft operation. This leads to increased operational efficiency and capacity of Terminal Building which facilitates seamless movement of Aircraft and enhances passenger user experience.Development works including licensing of Tezu (Arunachal Pradesh) and Rupsi (Assam)  are completed and these airports are operational.Further, development works undertaken by Airports Authority of India(AAI) at Heliports, Advanced Landing Grounds (ALGs) and water aerodromes  in different states of the North East Region.Also, Ministry of Civil Aviation has launched Regional Connectivity Scheme - UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik) on 21-10-2016 to stimulate regional air connectivity from unserved and underserved airports in the country by making air travel affordable to the masses. RCS-UDAN is market driven mechanism. Development of regional air connectivity routes is  left to the market forces. Airlines undertake assessment of demand and nature of supply required on particular routes and participate in the UDAN bidding. Selection of airlines is done through a transparent bidding process.So far, under UDAN the Airports & Water Aerodromes identified/awarded in North-Eastern States including Assam is enclosed as Annexure and all the airports are operationalized except water aerodromes at Guwahati River Front and Umrangso reservoir. ;During RCS-UDAN-2.0 & 4.0 bidding round, helicopter routes / networks across North Eastern States namely Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya and Arunachal Pradesh have been awarded to the helicopter operators to provide connectivity to Hilly Terrains and far-flung areas and details are as follow:- 1. Arunachal Pradesh; Daparizo Itanagar, Tuting Walong Yinghiong & Ziro2. Assam: Nagaon(H) Geleki (H) Misa (H)3. Manipur: Jiribam(H) Moreh(H) Parbung(H) Tamenglong(H) Thanlon(H)4. Meghalaya: Sohra, Dawki & WilliamnagarThe identified airports in North-Eastern States have been connected with airports of major cities of the country and connectivity thereof are given below: a) Pakyong: connected with the major cities; Kolkata, Delhi & Guwahatib) Jorhat: connected with Kolkatac) Tezpur: Kolkatad) Rupsi: Guwahati & Kolkatae) Shillong: Kolkata, Imphalf) Lilabari: Kolkatag) Pasighat: Guwahati h) Tezu: Guwahati;i) Dimapur: Guwahati, Dibrugarh, Imphal & Shillong
