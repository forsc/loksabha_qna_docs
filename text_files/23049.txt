                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Working Women’s Hostels




A. Chellakumar













                                                    M. Selvaraj
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of hostels for working women being run by the Non-Government Organisations, category wise and State-wise;(b)	whether the Government has introduced the provision of day care centres in these hostels and if so, the details thereof(c)	the number of proposals received and sanctioned by the Government for construction of working women hostels in different parts of the country during each of the last three years and the current year, State Wise; and(d)	the funds sanctioned, released and utilized by the States during the said period?






ANSWER




ANSWERMINISTRY OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPEMNT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)  (a) Working Women Hostel scheme is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme and is run/implemented by the States/UTs. Funds are directly released to States/UTs.  State-wise list of hostels sanctioned by the Ministry is placed at Annexure I. (b) Provision is already there for Day Care Centre in the Working Women Hostel Scheme. As per Scheme Guidelines, for hostels constructed on public land, provision of a day care centre is mandatory. Normally, for a 100 bedded hostel, provision of 30 children is made in Day Care Centre. The Day Care Centre have  space of  approximately 15-20 sq. ft  per child including a small wash room.(c) Details of number of proposals received and sanctioned by the Government for construction of Working Women Hostels in different parts of the country during each of the last three years and the current year, State-wise are placed at Annexure II.
