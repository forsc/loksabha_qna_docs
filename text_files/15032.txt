                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3846






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2020






Deaths of Cows in Gaushallas




Deepak Baij














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of deaths of cows in Gaushallas due to various reasons during the last three years, State-wise;(b) whether it is a fact that the number of deaths of cows has increased due to carelessness in taking care, providing insufficient food, showing negligence in treatment of sick cows etc.;(c) if so, the details thereof; and(d) whether any survey has been carried out to assess the loss of crops suffered by the farmers due to stray animals every year ?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATES FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(DR SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a) to (c) As per the Constitution of India, preservation, protection and improvement of stock and prevention of animal diseases; veterinary training and practice is under state list. The panchayats and local municipalities are empowered to establish and run Cattle Pounds (Kanji Houses)/ Gaushala Shelters (community assets) at their respective jurisdiction. Therefore, the Central Government does not maintain information on death of cows in cow sheds. The prevention of animal diseases, veterinary care including arrangement of feed and fodder for an animal is also under state subject.      Animal Welfare Board of India is also providing some assistance for shelter, fodder, ambulance and treatment of animals in gaushalas. Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying, Government of India is also implementing schemes for disease control and fodder production etc. Financial assistance are provided to State Governments.    (d) No such survey has been carried out to assess the loss of crops suffered by the farmers due to stray animals every year. *****
