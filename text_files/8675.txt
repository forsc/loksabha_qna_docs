                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2212






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2019






IIIT




Ajay (Teni) Misra














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	whether Indian Institutes of Information Technology have proposed to start dual degree courses in view of the market demand;(b) 	if so, the names of the IIITs and the details of the courses proposed to be started in these institutions; and(c)	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(Shri Ramesh Pokhriyal ‘Nishank’)(a) to (c)	The four (04) Centrally funded Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs) namely at Allahabad, Gwalior, Jabalpur and Kancheepuram are already offering dual degree courses. In the twenty IIITs established under Public-Private-Partnership (PPP) mode and one Centrally funded IIIT at Kurnool (Andhra Pradesh), infrastructure is developing and hence, presently, they are not able to introduce dual degree courses.2.	The Board of Governors of these IIITs are empowered to start new courses, including dual degree course. ****
