                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

259






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






Regional Connectivity Scheme




Raj Hans Hans














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has drawn a road map for providing operations by airlines in remote areas of newly created Union Territories of J&K and Ladakh under the Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) and if so, the details thereof; and(b) the steps the Government proposes to take to strengthen airport infrastructure in these UTs?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) and (b): A Statement is laid on the table of the House.;STATEMENT IN RESPECT OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 259 REGARDING "REGIONAL CONNECTIVITY SCHEME " TO BE ANSWERED ON 05.12.2019;; ; (a): Ministry of Civil Aviation has launched Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) - UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik) on 21-10-2016 to stimulate regional air connectivity and making air travel affordable to the masses. RCS - UDAN is market driven mechanism. The interested airlines based on their assessment of demand on particular routes submit their proposals at the time of bidding under RCS - UDAN from time to time. State Government of Jammu & Kashmir (J&K) had signed tripartite agreement for implementation of RCS - UDAN in the State. There are thirteen unserved airports and one underserved airport in the Union Territories (UTs) of J&K and Ladakh available in the tentative list of underserved/unserved airports of RCS - UDAN scheme document for bidding by the interested airlines. ;;  Details of the RCS - UDAN routes connecting UTs of J&K and Ladakh awarded to the Selected Airline Operators in 2nd & 3rd round of bidding, are attached as Annexure.; ; (b): Following steps have been taken to strengthen Airport infrastructure in these UTs:;  i)   Extension of the runway at Jammu Airport by Airports Authority of India (AAI).;;  ii)   Appointment of management consultant for preparation of detailed plan by AAI for expansion of existing terminal building at Srinagar Airport.;; ; iii)   AAI has taken up the matter regarding construction of new terminal building at Leh Airport with the cost of Rs. 480 crore.; ; iv)   A pre-feasibility study has been carried out by multi-disciplinary team of AAI for development of Kargil Airport for civil operations suitable to operate Airbus 320 type of aircraft. AAI has also taken up the matter with International Civil Aviation Organization to further explore the feasibility of aircraft such as B-737 operations to Kargil Airport.; ; v)   A pre-feasibility study has been carried out by AAI for development of Thoise Airport under RCS operations.
