                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1825






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.03.2020






Darbhanga Engineering Colleges




 Gopal Jee Thakur














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether Darbhanga city is a major centre of education in North Bihar;(b) whether there are two engineering colleges i.e Darbhanga EngineeringCollege and another engineering college for women inaugurated by FormerPresident late Shri APJ Abdul Kalam;(c) whether these two colleges are not adequately developed;(d) whether the engineering studies will get a boost if any one of the two collegesis upgraded to IIIT;(e) whether the Government proposes to upgrade any one of the two institutesinto IIIT; and(f) if so, the time by which it is likely to be implemented?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (c): Darbhanga Engineering College and Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam Women’sInstitute of Technology (Formerly known as Women’s Institute of Technology)Darbhanga are state owned Government Engineering Colleges under Lalit NarayanMithila University(LNMU) in Bihar State and are approved by All India Council forTechnical Education (AICTE). Both the colleges have been granted extension ofapproval for Academic Year 2019-20 by AICTE as per norms prescribed in the ApprovalProcess Handbook (APH) 2019-20. There are no major deficiencies in infrastructure inboth the colleges, as per the Report of the Approval Process 2019-20.However, in Darbhanga Engineering College, there is a deficiency of faculty. Against arequirement of 72 faculty members, there is faculty strength of only 52 members postedin the institute. The details of the institutes are available on AICTE Dashboardhttps://facilities.aicte-india.org/dashboard/pages/dashboardaicte.php.(d) to (f): At present, there are 25 IIITs in the country and as of now, no proposal hasbeen received from State Government of Bihar to establish a new IIIT.*****
