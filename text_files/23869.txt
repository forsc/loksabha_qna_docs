                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5034






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2021






NRDWP in Haryana




Brijendra Singh














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the National Rural Drinking Water Programme is meeting its desired objectives in Haryana;(b) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and(c) the efforts made by the Government to provide drinking water in all the rural areas of Haryana by the year 2024, district-wise?






ANSWER




MINISTER  OF  STATE  FOR  JAL  SHAKTI(SHRI  RATTAN  LAL  KATARIA)(a) to (c) Under erstwhile National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP), now subsumed under the Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM), objective was to make provision of potable water in rural habitations with minimum 40 litre per capita per day (lpcd) and drinking water source at a reasonable distance. As reported by Haryana State, as on 22.03.2021, 98.17% rural habitations having 97.74% rural population have provision of minimum 40 litre per capita per day (lpcd) and 1.83% rural habitations having 2.26% rural population have service level of less than 40 lpcd potable water supply.Government of India,in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission to provide tap water supply to every rural household in the countryby 2024. As reported by States/ UTs, as on date, about 7.15 Crore (37.28%) rural households in the country have tap water supply. In Haryana, out of 31.03 lakh rural households, 26.88 lakh (86.63%) have tap water supply. Under JJM, the Haryana State has planned to provide tap water supply to every rural households by December, 2022.The district-wise details of rural households having tap water supply in Haryana, is available in public domain on JJM dashboard at:https://ejalshakti.gov.in/jjmreport/JJMIndia.aspx.***
