                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

712






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2021






Upgradation of Airport




Deepak Baij













                                                    S. Jothimani
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)  the  reasons  for  which  the  Lokpriya  Gopinath Bordoloi  International  Airport  was  leased  to  a  private player  after  utilizing  taxpayer  money  for  its  construction and  upgradation; (b)  whether  the  Government  proposes  to  recover the  money  spent  on  its  upgradation; (c)  if  so,  the  details  thereof  and  if  not,  the  reasons therefor; (d)  whether  the  Government  has  maintained  data  on the  revenue  generated  by  the  Lokpriya  Gopinath  Bordolo International  Airport;  and (e)  if  so,  the  details  of  revenue  generated  between 2014  and  2021,  separately  indicating  the  profits,  and  if not,  the  reasons  therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a): Airports Authority of India (AAI) has leased out Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International  (LGBI) Airport under Public Private Partnership (PPP) in the public interest and for better management of the airport in accordance with the provisions of AAI Act, 1994 (as amended in 2003). PPP at airports has also helped AAI in enhancing its revenues and focusing on developing airports at Tier-Il and Tier-lll cities.;(b) & (c): Yes Sir. As per the Concession Agreement, AAI gets an amount of Rs. 515.71 crore comprising of two components i.e. (a) deemed initial Regulatory Assets Base (RAB) for aeronautical & non- aeronautical assets and (b) Cost of Capital Work in Progress (CWIP) incurred by AAI up to the date of takeover of the airport by the Concessionaire.    (d) & (e): Yes, Sir. The details of revenue generated between 2014 and 2021 at LGBI Airport along with the profit/loss statement are given below in Rs. Crore:;Year           Revenue       Expenses    Profit (+)/Loss(-)2014-15       92.16            122.12         -29.962015-16      115.15          106.49           8.662016-17      167.64          130.55           37.092017-18      173.68          181.81           -8.122018-19      254.28          223.70           30.592019-20      242.24          209.05           33.192020-21      112.04          177.34          -65.30
