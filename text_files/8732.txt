                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

208






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2019






Buckwheat Crop




Subhash Ramrao Bhamre













                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the names of the States in which buckwheat is grown along with the total area in which it is grown; (b) 	whether the Government is aware that buckwheat is a good food, a relatively low input crop that has comparatively high yields even on marginal soil and also has medicinal value; (c)	if so, the steps taken by the Government to encourage farmers to grow buckwheat; (d) 	whether the attention of the Government has been drawn to a recent international symposium on buckwheat held in the country; and (e) 	if so, whether the Government has taken note of the discussions in the symposium and if so, the details thereof along with the other steps taken by the Government to intensify the use of buckwheat grains as a food crop so as to diversify the nutritional basket?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????		 	  (SHRI NARENDRA  SINGH TOMAR) (a) to (e):	A statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (e) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 208   DUE FOR REPLY ON 3RD DECEMBER, 2019.(a):	Buckwheat is grown in the hilly regions of the States of Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, Sikkim, Manipur, Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, West Bengal, Assam, Chhattisgarh, the Union Territory of Jammu & Kashmir and in the Nilgiris & Palani Hills.  The estimated area under buckwheat is around 25,800 hectares.(b) & (c):	From the nutritional perspective, buckwheat is an excellent source of high quality protein and dietary fibre with rich micronutrient density.  Buckwheat has nutraceutical properties of nutritional and medicinal importance.  Buckwheat is considered as a low input crop and can be grown in marginal, infertile, rocky and poorly tilled lands.  In order to encourage the farmers to grow buckwheat, Government of India is providing assistance to the farmers through implementation of the programmes of Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) and Mission on Organic Value Chain Development for North Eastern Region (MOVCDNER).  The States may also seek assistance for encouraging the production of buckwheat through Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY). (d) & (e):	The Government is aware of the 14th International Symposium on Buckwheat for “Diversifying Food Systems for Health and Nutritional Security” organized by International Buckwheat Research Association (IBRA) in collaboration with Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) at North Eastern Hill University, Shillong From September 3-6, 2019.  In order to intensify the use of buckwheat, ICAR-All India Co-ordinated Research Network on potential Crops has developed nutritionally rich five varieties and are promoted by ICAR and respective State Agricultural Universities (SAUs) through front line demonstrations, trainings and kisan melas for encouraging farmers to grow buckwheat.****
