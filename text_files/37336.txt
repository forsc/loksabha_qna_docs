                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4344






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.03.2022






Cyclone Shelters for Coastal Areas




S. Ramalingam














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government/National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) has any proposal to set up cyclone shelters permanently on the coastal areas of Tamil Nadu viz. Chennai/Nagai/Cuddalore which are very often affected due to heavy rain/floods/cyclone and natural calamities every year causing heavy loss to human loves/animals and properties;(b)  if so, the details thereof; and(c)  if not, whether such proposal will be considered for implementation in the future?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) to (c):	Multi Purpose Cyclone Shelters (MPCS) are built under the National Cyclone Risk Mitigation Project (NCRMP), which is a World Bank assisted centrally sponsored scheme.  It is being implemented by National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) in the 8 coastal States, in two phases.  Two States were covered in Phase-I, namely, Andhra Pradesh and Odisha; and six States are covered in Phase- II, namely, Goa, Gujarat, Karnataka, Kerala, Maharashtra and West Bengal.  At the time of the inception of NCRMP, Tamil Nadu State was already implementing a similar World Bank assisted project viz. “Coastal Disaster Risk Reduction Project” with a total outlay of Rs. 1481.80 crore, having similar components including Multi Purpose Evacuation Shelters (MPES).  Under the project, 121 nos. of MPES were built in 13 districts of Tamil Nadu.*****
