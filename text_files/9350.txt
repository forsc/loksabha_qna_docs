                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2942






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






Homeless People in Delhi




Magunta Sreenivasulu Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	the number of homeless people in Delhi;(b)	the measures taken to rehabilitate the homeless from public spaces like foot path, underneath flyovers and public places;(c)	whether the Government have any specific plans to impart skill and training to such people and relocate them and gainfully employ them under various schemes; and(d)	whether there is any scheme to educate such children who due to lack of permanent place of residence are unable to go to schools and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a): 	A survey of homeless in Delhi was conducted by the Delhi Urban Shelter Improvement Board (DUSIB) in 2014 with the help of concerned District Magistrates. According to the survey the number of homeless people was 16760.(b) to (d):	  It is the primary responsibility of Governments of the States/ Union Territories to provide  shelter  for  the  urban homeless. 	  However, in order to complement and supplement their efforts, Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs is administering Shelter for Urban Homeless (SUH)’ under Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM), through respective States/UTs. It focuses on providing permanent shelters equipped with basic facilities to the urban homeless. TheState level Project Sanctioning Committee (PSC) is competent to approve proposals for establishment of shelters. The shelters under DAY-NULM area space for convergence and provision of entitlements of social security, including admission of the children of the homeless to government schools, skill training under DAY-NULM and Prime Minister’s Kaushal Vikas Yojana. The Shelter Management Agency(SMA),Shelter Management Committeeand Executive Committee would be responsible to ensure the availability of these entitlements and benefits to the homeless. As reported by NCT of Delhi, there are 193 shelters functional. Motor driving skill training to 100 homeless persons is being imparted by DUSIB through Corporate Social Responsibility scheme and SMAs engaged by DUSIB do also assist the children for their admission in nearby schools.                                             *****
