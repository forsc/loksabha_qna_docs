                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4199






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.07.2019






Vision of ‘Housing for All’




Krupal Balaji Tumane














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of people provided houses so far under the Government’s vision of ‘Housing for All’ by 2022, State/UT-wise;(b) the present status of the schemes implemented thereunder in urban and rural areas of Nagpur and the norms for providing houses under the said schemes;(c) whether the schemes under the said vision have been running properly and if so, the details thereof;(d) whether the Government has reviewed the said vision and if so, the details thereof; and(e) whether the Government is considering to extend the time limit of the said vision and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                         (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)	& (b): The Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Urban) {PMAY(U)} Mission was launched on 25.06.2015 and the Mission comprises four components viz. In-Situ Slum Redevelopment (ISSR); Affordable Housing through Credit-Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS); Affordable Housing in Partnership (AHP); and assistance for Beneficiary-led individual house construction (BLC).The details regarding norms under these components are attached at Annexure I.State/UT-wise details of houses completed under PMAY(U) are enclosed at Annexure II. Under PMAY (U), in Nagpur district, a total of 44,385 houses have been sanctioned, out of which 16,013 houses have been grounded for construction and 10,591 houses have been completed.To achieve the Government’s objective of “Housing for All” by 2022, in rural areas, the erstwhile rural housing scheme, Indira Awas Yojana (IAY) has been restructured into Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana- Gramin (PMAY-G) with effect from 1st April, 2016.  Under PMAY-G, the identification of beneficiaries is based on the housing deprivation parameters prescribed in the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011 subject to exclusion criteria and after due verification by Gram Sabha. State/UT wise details of houses completed under PMAY-G are given at Annexure III. Under PMAY-G, in Nagpur district, a total of 18,347 houses have been sanctioned, out of which 10,593 houses have been completed.(c): Yes, Sir. Under PMAY (U), against the validated demand of 112 lakh houses from States/UTs, a total of 83.69 lakh houses have been sanctioned, out of which 48.37 lakh houses are at various stages of construction and 26.14 lakh houses have been completed.Under PMAY-G, the target under the scheme is construction of 2.95 crore houses by March, 2022, out of which 82.15 lakh houses have been completed.(d): No, Sir.(e): No, Sir.                     ******
