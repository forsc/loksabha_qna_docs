                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

246






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.09.2020






Job Cuts in the Agricultural Sector




Doddaalahalli Kempegowda Suresh














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether it is true that there are severe job cuts in the agricultural sector due to Covid -19 and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether the Government has any estimate of the number of farmers and landless agricultural labourers who lost their jobs and if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Government has taken any steps to provide relief to farmers in the wake of the Covid-19 crisis and if so, the details thereof; and (d) 	the other measures taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (d): 	As per  the estimates of Gross Domestic Product (GDP) for the First Quarter (April-June 2020-21) released by Central Statistics Office, the Agriculture & Allied sectors has recorded a growth of 3.4 percent over the corresponding period of the previous year. Moreover, the Government of India (GoI) is implementing Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act ((MGNREGA), 2005, through States/UTs which provides 100 days of wage employment in financial year to every rural household,  including landless agricultural labourers whose adult member volunteers to do unskilled manual work. In addition, GoI has launched Garib Kalyan Rojgar Abhiyaan (GKRA) on 20th June, 2020 to boost employment and livelihood opportunities for migrant workers returning to their native villages and similarly affected citizens in rural areas, in the wake of COVID-19 outbreak. The Abhiyaan covers 116 districts of six (6) states namely, Bihar, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Rajasthan and Uttar Pradesh.******
