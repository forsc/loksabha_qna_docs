                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3630






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2020






Academic Support to Destitute Children




Rama Devi













                                                    Ranjeeta Koli
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of destitute/orphan children who have passed the High SchoolExamination during the last three years, State-wise;(b) the role played by the Government in encouraging and assisting such children intheir education; and(c) the new proposals, if any, to take further the measures being taken by theGovernment in this regard?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (c): Education is a subject in the Concurrent List of the Constitution and majorityof schools are under the jurisdiction of the State Governments / Union TerritoriesAdministrations. As such, the data of destitute/ orphan children passing High Schoolexamination through various State/ Central Boards is not centrally maintained. Forfacilitating the education of children at the elementary level, the government through theIntegrated Scheme of Samagra Shiksha has been encouraging and assisting the statesin ensuring inclusive and equitable quality education as mandated under RTE Act,2009.***
