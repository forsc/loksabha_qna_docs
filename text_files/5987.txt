                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5496






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2019






Death of Animals in Delhi Zoo




Gautam Gambhir














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Delhi Zoo is losing its sheen as many of its enclosures have been lying empty following the death of several animals in the recent past;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether popular animals like Giraffe, Zebra, Ostrich, King Cobra etc. have died in the recent past due to various reasons and if so, the details thereof; and(d)	whether the Government has taken any corrective steps in this regard and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) & (b) Only one enclosure of Giraffe is lying empty since 09.06.2015 at National Zoological Park after the death of last Giraffe due to Myocarditis and Kidney failure.(c) As per the information received from National Zoological Park, none of these animals died in the recent past. (d) Efforts to acquire Giraffe, Zebra and Ostrich are being explored. King Cobra is not in the animal collection plan of National Zoological Park.****
