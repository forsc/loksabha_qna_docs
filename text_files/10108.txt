                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3512






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2019






Rural Wages and Inflation




Gurjeet Singh Aujla













                                                    Mimi Chakraborty
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the actual growth in rural wages, month-wise, after adjusting the current inflation rate since January, 2014 in comparison to the price index of 2010;(b)	whether the Government is of the opinion that the current rural wage rates are adequate/inadequate to maintain a rural household in the country;(c)	if so, the reasons therefor and if not, the action taken thereon; and(d)	the proposal of the Government to increase the rural wages thus boosting market economy therein?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):	As per the information shared by Ministry of Labour & Employment, Labour Bureau compiles and maintains Consumer Price Index Numbers for Agricultural and Rural Labourers (Base 1986-87=100) (CPI-AL & RL) and average daily wage rate data in respect of selected agricultural and non-agricultural occupations based on the data collected by the Field Operation Division (FOD) of National Sample Survey Office (NSSO) form a fixed set of 600 sample villages spread over 20 states. Month-wise rate in respect of Non Agricultural Occupation along with CPI-RL with effect from January 2014 onwards is at Annexure-I.(b)&(c):	As per section 6(1) of Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (Mahatma Gandhi NREGA), 2005, the Ministry of Rural Development notifies Mahatma Gandhi NREGA wage rates every year for States/UTs based on Consumer Price Index for Agricultural Labour (CPI-AL) published by Labour Bureau, Shimla. The wage rates are made applicable from 1st April of each Financial Year.  State/UT-wise wage rate under Mahatma Gandhi NREGA during the financial year 2014-15 to 2019-20 are given at Annexure-II.  (d):	To compensate the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (Mahatma Gandhi NREGS) workers against inflation, the Ministry of Rural Development revises the wage rates every year based on Consumer Price Index for Agricultural Labour (CPI-AL) published by Labour Bureau, Shimla for all States/UTs.  Annexure-I referred in reply to part (a) of Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No.3512 dated 10.12.2019Month-wise wage rate in respect of Non Agriculture Occupation along with CPL-RL with effect from January 2014 onwards.
