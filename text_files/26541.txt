Sunil Kumar Singh













                                                    Devji Mansingram Patel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the minor forest produce is a major source of livelihood and cash income for the tribals of the country and the local villagers living in the vicinity of the forest, if so, the State-wise details of the population dependent on the forest and forest produce including Rajasthan;(b) the State-wise details of quantity of minor forest produce procured at minimum support price in the country including Jharkhand and Rajasthan during the last three years;(c) the number of procurement centres set up for the same in Jharkhand and Rajasthan; and(d) the number of items of minor forest produce for which minimum support price has been fixed during the last three years and the details of the arrangements made for storage, processing, transportation and distribution of the minor forest?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR TRIBAL AFFAIRS(SMT. RENUKA SINGH SARUTA)   (a)       Tribes constitute over 8.6% of India’s population as per Census 2011. Minor Forest produces are a significant source of livelihood and income generation for majority of tribals living in the vicinity of the forest and contribution of minor forest produce to household income varies between 10 to 70 percent. State wise details of the population dependent on the forest and forest produce is not centrally maintained in Ministry of Tribal Affairs. Occupational distribution i.e. percentage of working and non-working population alongwith State wise Percentage distribution of marginal workers by Category including Rajasthan is at Annexure 1. (b) & (c)         As informed by Tribal Cooperative Marketing Development Federation of India (TRIFED), the State-wise details of quantity of MFPs procured at MSP across States including Jharkhand and Rajasthan are at Annexure 2. So far, twenty four (24) procurement centers have been set up in the State of Jharkhand and sixteen (16) procurement centers in the State of Rajasthan.
