                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

426






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2019






Investment in Food Processing Industry




Hemant Patil














                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the measures taken by the Government to promote investment in food processing industry since 2014, year-wise; (b)	the details of the funds and resources allocated for promoting food processing industry, State and year-wise since 2014; (c)	the details of the steps taken by the Government to improve and augment the cold storage facilities in the country, State and year-wise since 2014; (d)	the details of additions to the capacity of cold storage between 2014-2018, State-wise; and (e)	the details of the major challenges faced by the food processing industry and the steps taken to ease these constraints, State and year-wise since 2014?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES(SMT. HARSIMRAT KAUR BADAL) (a) to (e):  A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.          STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (a) TO (e) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *426 ANSWERED ON 23RD JULY, 2019 REGARDING INVESTMENT IN FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRY (a):	The year-wise major policy/fiscal measures and other steps taken by the Government to promote investment in the food processing industry is at Annexure-I. (b):	 No state-wise allocation of funds is made under the schemes implemented by the Ministry of Food Processing Industries (MoFPI) as the schemes are demand-driven and financial assistance to eligible applicants is provided against expression of interest issued by MoFPI from time to time. The year wise allocation of funds under the schemes of MoFPI for setting up of food processing industries is as below: Year	Allocation   of Funds (Rs. in crore)2014-15	600.002015-16	487.002016-17	688.562017-18	633.842018-19	870.332019-20	1101.00 (c) & (d): The Ministry of Food Processing Industries (MoFPI) does not set up stand-alone cold storage projects. However, under the scheme of ‘Integrated Cold Chain and Value Addition Infrastructure’ of Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana (PMKSY) implemented by MoFPI, a total capacity of 3.67 lakh MT cold storage has been created during the last 5 years, the state-wise details of which is at Annexure-II.  In addition, the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation & Farmers’ Welfare (DAC&FW) assists setting up of cold storage projects under its scheme, ‘Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH)’. During the last 5 years, a total 799 projects have been sanctioned with a total capacity of 32.03 lakh MT, the state-wise details of which is at Annexure-III. Also, the National Horticulture Board of Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers’ Welfare is implementing a scheme of ‘Capital Investment Subsidy for Construction/ Expansion/ Modernization of Cold Storages for Horticulture Products’ providing financial assistance for setting up of cold storage facilities. A total 236 number of projects have been sanctioned with a total capacity of 12.59 lakh MT during last 5 years, the state-wise detail of which is at Annexure-IV.  (e):	 The major identified challenges faced by food processing industry are lack of modern infrastructure, efficient supply chain, cold chain infrastructure, food testing laboratories, trained manpower and availability of cheaper credit. To address the infrastructural and logistic challenges, the Ministry of Food Processing Industries (MoFPI) has been implementing various Central Sector Schemes under Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana (PMKSY). To meet the credit requirement at concessional rates of interest for the food processing industries, a Special Food Processing Fund of Rs.2000 crore has been created with National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development.  Further, food and agro processing sector has been brought under the ambit of Priority Sector Lending (PSL). The MoFPI has two institutes viz; Indian Institute of Food Processing Technology (IIFPT), Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu and National Institute of Food Technology Entrepreneurship and Management (NIFTEM), Sonipat, Haryana offering academic programmes on food processing/technology and also undertaking research activities pertaining to food processing to bridge the human capital gap in the sector. *****
