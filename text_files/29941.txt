                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING

                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1597

Infrastructure Development and Post-Harvest Management under PMMSY




Kuldeep Rai Sharma

                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 the contribution of fisheries sector in the overall development of the country‘s economy; (b)	 the achievement made by Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY) in the overall production and export of fisheries sector; (c)	the major impact, including employment generation potential of the scheme since its inception;(d)	the State-wise allocation, percentage utilization of activities carried out under the Centrally Sponsored components of PMMSY with district-wise details for the State of Tamil Nadu and Maharashtra; (e)	 whether the Government has taken steps to enhance productivity in aquaculture from the present National average of 3 tonnes to about 5 tonnes per hectare and if so, the details thereof; (f)	 the steps taken for infrastructure development and post-harvest management in fisheries and aquaculture sector under PMMSY; and (g)	the other steps taken for enhancement of production and productivity in fisheries and aquaculture sector under PMMSY?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING		( SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a) :  Fisheries sector plays an important role in the socio-economic development of about 28 million fisher-folk, providing food, creation of employment opportunities, livelihood security. The contribution of the fisheries sector in the National Gross Value Added (GVA) during 2019-20 stood at   Rs.1,26,370 crore (at constant basic prices; 2011-12) constituting  0.95 %  to the national GVA and 6.42% of the agricultural GVA. Further the sector is growing at an average annual growth rate of 8.8% (constant price) in the last one decade. (b) to (d) :   The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing a flagship scheme “Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY) with highest ever investment of Rs. 20,050 crore  for a period of five years with effect from the financial year 2020-21 to 2024-25 in all States/Union Territories(UTs).  PMMSY aims to enhance fish production and productivity, create critical fisheries infrastructure, reduce post-harvest losses, modernize and strengthen value chain, double export, enhance domestic fish consumption with corresponding health benefits, attract private investments in fisheries sector and generate direct and indirect employment opportunities. Under PMMSY during 2020-21 to 2021-22, the Department of Fisheries, Government of India has so far accorded approval of project proposals worth of Rs. 5336.96 crore including Rs.148.23 crore and Rs. 398.87crore worth of projects of Government of Tamil Nadu and Maharashtra respectively.  The Central funds of Rs. 1223.96 crore have been released to various States/UTs and other implementing agencies under the PMMSY. The fish production has increased to 141.64 lakh tonnes during 2019-20 from 135.73 lakh tonnes during 2018-19 and fish and fisheries products of 11,49,510 tonne exported from India with an earning of Rs. 43720.98 crore during 2020-21. State-wise details of approved project cost and central funds released under the PMMSY are furnished at Annexure-I. As reported by Government of Maharashtra the district wise fund allocated under PMMSY during the year 2020-21 and 2021-22 is furnished at Annexure-II.(e) to (g): The PMMSY inter-alia provides financial assistance for development of inland fisheries and aquaculture, development of marine fisheries including mariculture and seaweed cultivation, development of fisheries in North-eastern and Himalayan States/UTs, development of ornamental and recreational fisheries, technology infusion, post harvest and cold chain infrastructure, markets and marketing infrastructure, development of deep sea fishing, aquatic health management, strengthening of safety and security of fishermen, insurance of fishing vessels and fishermen and livelihood support for fishers for conservation of fisheries resources in all the States/UTs which substantially contribute towards export. PMMSY inter alia focuses to enhance fish production and productivity through targeted interventions such as harnessing of the unrealized potential in Inland fisheries sector, expansion, intensification & diversification of aquaculture & mariculture, technological infusion such as RAS & Biofloc, improved availability of quality feed, establishment of brood banks & hatcheries for easy availability of quality seed, establishment of post-harvest infrastructure and management and modernization and strengthening of value chain, creation and strengthening of aquatic animal health management system and setting up of a robust fisheries management and regulatory framework.   *****
