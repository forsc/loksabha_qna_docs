                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

747






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Treatment of Contagious Diseases




Mala Rajya Laxmi Shah













                                                    Pushpendra Singh Chandel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government is taking steps to set up hospitals for the treatment of contagious diseases and to develop basic infrastructure and to increase their capacity for the prevention of the said diseases; and(b) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b):	“Health & Hospital” being a State subject, the primary responsibility of strengthening health care systems lies with the respective State Government. Under National Health Mission (NHM), States are being provided the required technical and financial assistance based on their proposals including for management of the COVID-19.The ‘India COVID-19 Emergency Response and Health System Preparedness package’ of ?15000 crores has been approved by the Cabinet on 22nd April 2020 with an objective to prevent, detect and respond to the threat posed by COVID-19.Under the package, ?6309.9 crores has been released to the States/UTs, through Agency- National Health Mission (NHM). The details are given at Annexure-I.The state wise details of essential medical equipment distributed to the States as on 24th January, 2021 is given at Annexure-II to VII.Also, the newly announced scheme, Prime Minister-Atma Nirbhar Swasth Bharat Yojana (PM-ASBY) envisages support to States for setting up of critical care blocks in 602 Districts and 12 Central Hospitals across the country, over the next 5 years. The PM Atma Nirbhar Swasth Bharat Yojana aims at developing capacities of primary, secondary and tertiary care health systems bystrengthening existing national institutions and creating new institutions, to cater to detection and cure of new and emerging diseases. This will be in addition to the National Health Mission. The main interventions under the scheme are: a.	Support for 17,788 rural and 11,024 urban Health and Wellness Centers;b.	Setting up integrated public health labs in all districts and 3382 block public health units in11 states; c.	Establishing critical care hospital blocks in 602 districts and 12 central institutions;d.	Strengthening of the National Centre for Disease Control (NCDC), its 5 regional branches and 20 metropolitan health surveillance units; e.	Expansion of the Integrated Health Information Portal to all States/UTs to connect all public health labs;f.	Operationalisation of 17 new Public Health Units and strengthening of 33 existing Public Health Units at Points of Entry, that is at 32 Airports, 11 Seaports and 7 landcrossings;g.	Setting up of 15 Health Emergency Operation Centers and 2 mobile hospitals; andh.	Setting up of a national institution for One Health, a Regional Research Platform for WHO South East Asia Region, 9 Bio-Safety Level III laboratories and 4 regionalNational Institutes for Virology.Also, under the Ayushman Bharat initiative, the Government is committed to setting up of 1.5 lakhs Health & Wellness Centers (HWCs), across the country by December, 2022. Sufficient resources have been provided to the States for the creation of the same and as many as 57,017 HWCs have become functional taking primary healthcare services to the community.More than 35 crores footfall have been recorded at HWCs so far for receiving primary care.
