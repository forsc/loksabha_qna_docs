                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5584






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2019






Health and Wellness Centres




P.R. Natarajan













                                                    Behanan Benny
                                                








                                                    Anurag Sharma
                                                








                                                    Manoj Rajoria
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	 Procurement of Anti Rabies Vaccine (ARV) is decentralized. As procurement of anti-rabies vaccine is done by the state governments, their availability status are accordingly maintained at the state level and no central database on availability of ARV is maintained. However, under National Rabies Control Programme (NRCP), 16 States (Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Jharkhand, Jammu & Kashmir, Manipur, Madhya Pradesh, Meghalaya, Odisha, Pondicherry, Sikkim, Tripura, Uttrakhand, Uttar Pradesh) have reported shortage of anti-rabies vaccines (ARV).(b):	 State/UT-wise deaths due to rabies, reported during the last two years in the country are given in Annexure. However, details of the deaths due to shortage of ARV are not available.(c):	 Ministry of Health and Family Welfare has taken note of the shortage of ARV reported by the States and several meetings were held in the Ministry to review the shortage of ARV reported by the States. The State Governments were requested to provide information on shortage of ARV and assistance needed from the Central Government. Drug Controller General of India was also directed to monitor the pharmaceutical firms producing ARV for ensuring regular supply of ARV to the States. States/UTs have also been advised to use Intradermal Route of ARV and ensure the availability of ARV in all health facilities for management of Animal Bite Victims. 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	Under Ayushman Bharat – Health & Wellness Centres (AB-HWCs), 1.5 lakh Health Sub Centres and Primary Health Centres are to be transformed into Health and Wellness Centres across the country by December, 2022. The state-wise detail of 19,821 operational AB-HWCs as on 18.07.2019 is at Annexure-I.(b):	The state-wise detail of Releases and Allocation under AB-HWCs for the F.Y. 2018-19 and 2019-20 is at Annexure-II. Besides this, various components of AB-HWCs like Human Resource, Free Drugs, Diagnostics etc. are also supported under regular National Health Mission (NHM) Budget.(c):	Services at AB-HWCs are free and universal to all individuals residing in the service area.(d):	Only those Sub Health Centres and Primary Health Centres that have required human resource are considered as operational AB-HWCs, besides satisfying other functionality criterion. To ensure and enable availability of medical professionals and para medics in AB-HWCs, necessary financial and technical support is being provided to the States under NHM. Besides this, NHM Conditionality framework and mechanisms of incentives encourage states to complete recruitment for vacant positions of the necessary human resources at PHC and SHCs being transformed as AB-HWCs.(e):	Following steps are being taken to ensure availability of trained professional to attend emergency cases in HWCs:-•	PHC level AB-HWCs have trained-medical officers to attend emergency cases in HWCs. •	Sub Health Centre level AB-HWCs is being manned by CHOs who are trained in Certificate Programme in Community Health that includes topics related to training in management of cases for basic medical emergencies and trauma.•	Ministry of Health and Family Welfare has also set up a Task Force to finalize the Operational Guidelines for service delivery protocols for management of Basic Medical Emergency and Trauma at HWCs.
