                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2013






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.03.2020






Fish Processing Industries




Kinjarapu Ram Mohan Naidu














                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the assistance provided to fish processing industries in the country especially in Srikakulam;(b)	whether the industries related to fish processing match international quality standards;(c)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the steps taken in this regard;(d)	the list of countries to which fishery products are exported along with the volume of exports; and(e)	the steps taken by the Government to promote export of fishery products?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES (SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a):	The Ministry of Food Processing Industries (MoFPI), under its Central Sector Umbrella Scheme- Pradhan Mantri Kisan SAMPADA Yojana (PMKSY), provides financial assistance in the form of grants-in-aid, inter alia, for creation of modern infrastructure and efficient supply chain for facilitating preservation and processing of both Agro and Marine produce including Fishery products. Under schemes of Integrated Cold Chain and Value Addition Infrastructure (Cold Chain), Creation of Backward and Forward Linkages (BFL) and Creation / Expansion of Food Processing & Preservation Capacities (Unit) of PMKSY financial assistance for processing and preservation and farm/market linkages are provided for all agro produce including fish. Under the schemes of Mega Food Parks and Infrastructure for Agro-processing Clusters of PMKSY financial assistance is provided for creation of common infrastructure for processing and preservation of agro and marine produce including fish. These schemes of PMKSY are demand-driven and implemented all across the country including in the State of Andhra Pradesh and Srikakulam under it. Under the schemes financial assistance is provided to farmers, entrepreneurs, organizations such as Central and State PSUs/Joint Ventures/Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs)/NGOs/Cooperatives/SHGs/ Public and Private Companies etc. to set up food processing industries/projects/units. Financial assistance is provided to the eligible applicant as per the Scheme guidelines and against the Expression of Interest issued by the Ministry from time to time. The Marine Products Export Development Authority (MPEDA), Ministry of Commerce & Industry also provides financial assistance to develop infrastructure facilities for production and export of value added marine products.Assistance for fish processing, marine & seafood sector has been provided to 32 Cold Chain projects, 2 BFL projects and 5 to Unit projects in the country by MoFPI under its Cold Chain, BFL and Unit Scheme with a total approved grants-in-aid of Rs.295.56 crore of which Rs.140.01 crore has been released. In Andhra Pradesh, 10 Cold Chain projects have been approved with a total grants-in-aid of Rs.94.05 crore of which Rs.36.68 crore has been released. MPEDA has provided assistance of Rs.21.91 crore to the fish processing units during 2014-15 to 2018-19. No eligible proposals from Srikakulam in fish sector has been received for assistance either in MoFPI or in MPEDA.(b) & (c): Fish processing units registered with MPEDA and approved by the Export Inspection Council of India are compliant to the regulations as per Hazard analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP) principles. Of the 607 processing plants registered with	MPEDA,344approved by European Union (EU) are compliant as per EU standards.(d):	The country wise export of fishery products during the last two years is given at Annexure-I.(e):	 The steps taken by MPEDA to promote export of marine products including fishery is given at Annexure-II.*****
