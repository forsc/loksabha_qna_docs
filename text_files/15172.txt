                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3801






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2020






Enemy Properties




Parvatagouda Chandanagouda Gaddigoudar














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total number of enemy properties located in the country and the approximate value thereof, Statewise;(b) whether the Government has set up a high level Committee for disposal of enemy properties vested with the Custodian of Enemy Property for India;(c) if so, the details thereof; and(d) the time by which the above proposal is likely to be implemented and the estimated amount of revenue likely to be generated by the Government as a result of this proposal?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a): 	As on date enemy shares of 302 companies and 12426 immovable enemy properties are vested in the Custodian of Enemy Property for India (CEPI). The details of vested immovable properties are given in Annexure-I. The value of the enemy properties has been estimated at about Rupees One Lakh Crore.(b) & (c): The Government of India has laid down detailed procedure for sale of enemy shares vide Order dated 18.02.2019. Further, the Government vide Orders dated 21.03.2018, 08.03.2019 and 22.01.2020 laid down the detailed procedures for  disposal of  immovable enemy  properties vested in the  Custodian of  Enemy-2/...-2-LS.US.Q.NO.3801 FOR 17.03.2020Property for India (CEPI). The details are given in Annexure-II.  Several Committees including the Alternative Mechanism consisting of Home Minister, Finance Minister and Minister of Road Transport and Highways have been set up by the aforesaid orders.(d): 	Vesting of an enemy property is an ongoing process as new properties are taken up for vesting after their identification and the  properties so vested are taken up for disposal  thereafter.   The disposal is subject to market processes and judicial interventions.  Therefore, no time limit has been fixed for disposal of enemy properties.
