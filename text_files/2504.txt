                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2324






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.07.2019






Start New IIT




S. Muniswamy














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has identified the places to start new Indian Institutes ofTechnology (IITs) in the country, State-wise including Karnataka;(b) the criteria for selecting these areas; and(c) the funds allocated and utilised for this purpose?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (c): Pursuant to Budget Announcements 2014-15 and 2015-16, 6 new IITs, one each atTiputati (Andhra Pradesh), Palakkad (Kerala), Jammu (J&K), Goa (Goa), Bhilai (Chhattisgarh)and Dharwad (Karnataka) have been established. Details of temporary campuses from wherethe IITs are operating currently and sites identified for permanent campuses are as under:-<pre>SNo.Name of IIT Temporary Campus Permanent Campus1. IIT Tirupati Chadalwada Venkata SubbaiahCollege of Engineering, ReniguntaRoad, TirupatiMerlapaka Village, Yerpedu(Mandal), Chittoor District2. IIT Palakkad Ahalia Integrated Campus,PalakkadPudussery West, Palakkad3. IIT Jammu KCS College of Education, Jammu, Khanpur Nagrota4. IIT Bhilai Government Engineering College,RaipurKutelabhata and Sirsavillage, Bhilai, Durg5. IIT Dharwad Water & Land ManagementInstitute (WALMI), DharwadKelagari village, Dharwad6. IIT Goa Goa College of Engineering Campus,Farmagudi, PondaSanguem and QuepemTaluka, South Goa</pre>As regards criteria for selection of sites, the concerned State Government is asked to identify atleast two sites measuring 500-600 acres having necessary social and physical infrastructuresuch as road, rail and air connectivity, free of cost and free from all legal encumbrances. Thesites are inspected by a Site Selection Committee comprising experts in the field of constructionas well as representatives of Central and State Governments. Based on the recommendations ofthe Committee, the most suitable site is approved. Government has approved Rs.7002.42 crorefor construction of Permanent Campuses of these IITs under Phase-A, out of which, an amountof Rs.1046.59 crore has been released as per demand of the IITs.*****
