                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

373






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2021






Closing Sanskrit Schools in Assam




Badruddin Ajmal














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	Whether the Government is aware that the Assam Government has decided to shut down State-run Sanskrit schools in the State;(b)	Whether the Government proposes to issue any instructions/guidelines to the Assam Government in this regard;(c)	if so, the detail thereof; and(d)	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF EDUCATION (SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (d) 	  A Statement is laid on the table of the House.********** STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (d) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *373 TO BE ANSWERED ON 22.03.2021 ASKED BY HON’BLE MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT SHRI M.BADRUDDIN AJMAL REGARDING “CLOSING SANSKRIT SCHOOLS IN ASSAM”.(a)	to (d)	: “Education” is in the Concurrent list of the Constitution. The scheme of Sanskrit Tolls is entirely within the domain of the State Government of Assam. The State has informed that it has taken a policy decision to convert the provincialised Sanskrit Tolls of the State into Study Centres under Kumar Bhaskar Varma Sanskrit and Ancient Studies University, Nalbari with effect from 01/04/2022 for study of Certificate/Diploma/Degree courses with a view to expand the teaching of Sanskrit and other ancient literature of India as well as of Assam in a comprehensive manner.The Government of Assam has further informed that the status, pay and allowances, service rules, service conditions, etc. of the teaching and non-teaching staff presently working in the provincialised Sanskrit Tolls including their seniority, etc. remain the same. * * *
