                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4886






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2021






Modernisation of Marine Navigation




Sukanta Majumdar













                                                    Raja Amareshwara Naik
                                                








                                                    Nisith Pramanik
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                








                                                    Rajveer (Raju Bhaiya) Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is planning to introduce a legislation “the Marine aids to Navigation Bill 2021” for development, maintenance and management of aids to navigation in the country;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	the details of the lighthouses installed across the country and funds allocated during the last three years, State-wise;(d)	whether the Government has identified heritage lighthouses for their development and if so, the details thereof;(e)	whether the Government has any plan to modernise the marine navigation with state-of-the-art technologies; and(f)	if so, the details thereof along with other measures being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) & (b) Yes,  Sir. “The Marine aids to Navigation Bill 2021” for development, maintenance        and management of aids to navigation in India; for training and certification of operators of aids to navigation; and to ensure compliance with the obligation under the maritime treaties and International Instruments to which India is a party; has been passed by the Lok Sabha on 22nd March, 2021.  (c)     195 Lighthouses have been established along the entire coastline of the country including group of Andaman & Nicobar Islands and Lakshadweep Islands. The lighthouses are established by the Central Government and funds are not allocated State-wise.  The details of expenditure during the last three years are as under: -<pre>Sl. No.	Year	Expenditure (Rs. in crore)1.	2017-18	53.782.	2018-2019	59.433.	2019-2020	88.14                                             </pre>(d)	18 Lighthouses have been identified and taken up for development as Heritage lighthouses as these lighthouses have completed 75 years since commissioning. The State-wise details is annexed. (e)&(f)  With the advancement of technology, the role of Marine Aids to Navigation has moved from a purely passive one based on “Visual Aids to Navigation” to “Radio and Digital based Aids to Navigation”.  The Central Government has established modern Marine Aids to Navigation such as Differential Global Positioning System (DGPS); National Automatic Identification System (NAIS) with 87 physical shore stations along the Indian Coastline to track all the vessels fitted with Automatic Identification System (AIS) transponder; and Vessel Traffic Service (VTS) in Gulf of Kachchh with 9 Radar networks with state-of-the-art technologies.
