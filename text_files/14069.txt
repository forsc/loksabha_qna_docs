                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PERSONNEL, PUBLIC GRIEVANCES AND PENSIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2940






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.03.2020






Increase in Technical Background Candidates in SSC Exam




Lorho S. Pfoze














                        Will the Minister of





PERSONNEL, PUBLIC GRIEVANCES AND PENSIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether it is a fact that there is an increase in the number of engineers and those with technical background in Staff Selection Commission (SSC) examinations, especially in Combined Graduate Level Examination during the last five years; (b) 	the stream-wise breakup of successful candidates in the Combined Graduate Level Examination conducted by SSC during the last five years; and (c) 	the steps taken by the Government/SSC to have a level playing field in SSC examinations so that no one stream is at a great advantage and another stream is at a disadvantage?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF PERSONNEL, PUBLIC GRIEVANCES AND PENSIONS AND MINISTER OF STATE IN THE PRIME MINISTER’S OFFICE(DR. JITENDRA SINGH) (a):  During last five years, results of Combined Graduate Level Examinations (CGLE) 2015, 2016 and 2017 have been declared. The final results of CGLE 2018 and CGLE 2019 have not been declared yet. The educational qualification of the candidates was not captured for CGLE 2015. Based on the data available for the years 2016 and 2017, the percentage of candidates selected from technical background is 56.81% and 66.22%, respectively.(b):  The stream wise break-up of the candidates finally recommended for CGLE 2016 and CGLE 2017 is enclosed as Annexure-1.(c):  The syllabus of the SSC examinations is designed in such a manner that it provides a level playing field for all the candidates, irrespective of their stream. The multiple tiers of the exam ensure that the candidates are examined on an array of topics comprising General Intelligence and Reasoning, General Awareness, English comprehension, Quantitative Aptitude etc.  *****
