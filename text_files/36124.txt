                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3561






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2022






Skill Training to Youth




Sanganna Amarappa Karadi













                                                    B. Y. Raghavendra
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Airport Authority of India (AAI) has entered into partnership with the Construction Industry Development Council(CIDC) to impart skill training to youth in the aviation sector;(b) if so, the details thereof along with the number of youth who have been received training;(c) the details of funds released to CIDC for skill training to youth:(d) whether job opportunities for those who complete the training have been given on priority and if so, the details thereof;(e) whether AAI also entered into a tripartite agreement with National Skill Development Fund and National Skills Development Corporation for imparting skills training to the youth in the country; and (f) if so, the details thereof along with the financial support given during the last three years and the current year?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) Airports Authority of India (AAI) has provided funding through its Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) to Construction Industry Development Council (CIDC) in order to provide job oriented skill training and employment opportunities through livelihood promotion in backward and remote areas as well as to help the marginalized sections gain meaningful employment, leading to their social and economic upliftment in the construction sector. The trades include mason, carpenters, plumbers, bar benders, welders, electricians, store keeper, site accountant, office assistant etc.;(b) & (c) Programme-wise details are at Annexure.;(d) The youth trained under the AAI-CIDC program are provided job opportunities by CIDC. Out of the total trained candidates under these programs, over 60 percent were placed in various job roles by CIDC.;(e) & (f) The Airports Authority of India (AAI) have signed two tripartite Memorandum of Understanding (MoUs) with National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) and National Skill Development Fund (NSDF) for setting up of Multi Skill Development Centre at Chandigarh and Mumbai. The details and outcomes of the MoU are as follows:i. The number of candidates trained in span of three years in Chandigarh are 1,681 and Mumbai are 720.ii. Job roles identified for the aviation centre in Chandigarh and Mumbai include airline customer service executive, airline reservation agent, airline cargo assistant, airline ramp executive, airline flight load controller, airline security executive, airport x-ray qualified staff, airport facility services and airline baggage handler. iii. The training programme aims at skilling as well as up-skilling in the Aviation sector. Financial support extended during the last three years and current year is as follows: i. FY 2018-19 - Rs 2 crore and Rs 60 lakhs respectively provided to Chandigarh and Mumbai Skill Development Centre for training related to Aviation Sector. ii. FY 2019-20 - NILiii. FY 2020-21 and FY 2021-22 - NIL, Due to the outbreak of Covid-19 pandemic and subsequent lockdown across nation, the training institutes were not functional, thereby impacting the training schedule.;******
