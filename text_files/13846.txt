                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2614






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.03.2020






Snow Leopards




Jasbir Singh Gill














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it has been reported that snow leopards has recently made Lower Shivalik hills region of Punjab, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh its habitat and if so, the details thereof; and      (b)	the total number of snow leopards in States of Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand and Union Territories  of Ladakh and Jammu and Kashmir?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) Natural habitat of snow leopard  is in the high range Himalayan mountains in the state of Himachal Pradesh,  Uttarakhand, Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, Union Territory of Jammu and Kashmir and Union Territory of Ladakh. Sighting or presence of snow leopard  has not been  reported in Lower Shivalik hill regions  of Punjab, Haryana and Himachal Pradesh.(b) The number of snow leopards in the States of Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand and Union Territories  of Ladakh and Jammu and Kashmir is estimated to be in the range of 400-700. Recently, the Ministry has launched a country wide initiative for estimation of   snow leopard numbers and population in its range States/UTs using a standard protocol namely “Snow Leopard Population Assessment  in India” for making regular assessment of snow leopard population.   ***
