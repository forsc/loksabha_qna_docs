                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

144






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2021






Trained Teachers for Children with Special Needs




Asaduddin Owaisi














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there is an acute shortage of trained teachers to take care of the children withspecial needs (CWSN) in the country;(b) if so, the number of such children in the country and number of teachers available for them;(c) whether Right to Free and Compulsory Education Act applies to all schools includingschools admitting such children; and(d) if so, the details thereof and steps taken or being taken by Government in this regard?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) & (b) It is to bring to notice that Education is in Concurrent list of subject. UnderSamagra Shiksha, there is a dedicated component for Inclusive Education for Children withSpecial Needs (CwSN) through which separate resource support has been made availablethrough the appointment of special educators in order to appropriately address the needs ofCwSN within the general school. In addition, various provisions are made available for theeducational needs such as, identification & assessment camps, provision of aids, appliances,assistive devices & teaching learning materials (TLMs) etc.As per Samagra Shiksha Scheme, the Special Educators may be posted at the block orcluster level or as per the requirement and can operate in an itinerant mode, covering a groupof schools where children with special needs are enrolled. The Special Educators on itinerantmode provide support to general teachers to address the diversified and unique needs of specialneeds students. As per UDISE+ (2019-20 prov.), there are 22,49,069 CwSN enrolled inschools. Under Samagra Shiksha for the year 2021-2022 financial support is provided to 28439Special Educators in Government and Government aided schools. 390323 schools areprovided resource support by these Special Educators on itinerant mode.(c) to (d) The Samagra Shiksha Scheme is governed and regulated by the provisions of theRight of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009 and supports all States& UTs in implementation of the Act. The RTE Act, 2009 enshrines the entitlement to free andcompulsory elementary education, access to school and barrier free access for all childrenincluding children with special needs. Further, the National Education Policy (NEP), 2020advocates full equity and inclusion as the cornerstone of education to ensure that all studentsare able to thrive in the education system. It also underscores within its framework, educationof children with special needs in order to ensure equitable quality schooling. The SamagraShiksha scheme has also been aligned with the recommendations of NEP 2020 to ensure thatall children have access to quality education with an equitable and inclusive classroomenvironment.*****
