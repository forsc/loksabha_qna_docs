                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3314






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2021






Registered Fishermen




Maneka Sanjay Gandhi














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of targets set under fisheries by the Government during the year 2020-21; and(b) the details of the fishermen registered in various States in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING:(SHRI PRATAP CHANDRA SARANGI)(a) and (b):  The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing a flagship scheme “Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY)- A scheme to bring about Blue Revolution through sustainable and responsible development of fisheries sector in India” with highest ever investment of Rs. 20,050 crore in the fisheries sector over a period of five years with effect from the current financial (2020-21) in all States/Union Territories.  This scheme envisages a target of 70 lakh tons of additional fish production, an average aquaculture productivity of 5 tons/hectare, Rs 1 lakh crores exports, creation of critical fisheries infrastructure, reduction of post-harvest losses to 10%, enhancing domestic fish consumption to 12 kg per capita and generating employment opportunities to about 55 lakh population. While the overall targets of the PMMSY are to achieve in five year period (2020-21 to 2024-25), the Department has set a target of 15% of the investments for the financial year 2020-21. The PMMSY has about 100 different activities which are demand driven and identification of beneficiaries are done by the concerned State/UTs through their established procedures. Furthermore, as per the report received from States/UTs, 2.8 crore people are engaged in fisheries and allied activities in the country. *****
