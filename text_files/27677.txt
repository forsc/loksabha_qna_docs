                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3283






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.08.2021






Incidents of Gas Leakage and Fire Break Out




Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao













                                                    Vishnu Dayal Ram
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether it is a fact that the number of incidents of gas leakage and fire break out inunderground natural gas pipelines, refineries and oil fields/wells has gone up;(b) if so, the details along with number of such incidents and the loss of revenuereported as a result thereof during each of the last three years and the current year;(c) whether it is also a fact that the oil companies/persons and other agencies havebeen found responsible/involved in such incidents and if so, the details thereof and theaction taken/proposed to be taken by the Government against them;(d) whether it is true that failure to comply with standard operating procedures was themain reason for the accidents during the said period and if so, the details thereof alongwith the other reasons for such accidents; and(e) whether the Government has conducted any enquiry in this regard and if so, thedetails and the outcome thereof and if not, the reasons therefor and the steps taken bythe Government in this regard?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a) & (b): The major onsite incidents in the last three years reported to Oil IndustrySafety Directorate (OISD) are given below:Gas Leakage and Fire IncidentsSector 2018-19 2019-20 2020-21 2021-22 (till July’21)Pipelines Nil Nil 01 NilRefineries 10 07 02 01Oil Fields/Wells 06 03 02 01Total 16 10 05 02(c) ,(d) &(e): The analysis of last three years data carried out by OISD indicates thatmajority of the accidents that take place in the oil & gas industry are mainly due todisregard to Standard Operating Procedures (SOP), equipment failure, poormaintenance & inspection and poor supervision. After Baghjan well fire incident, aThree-member High Level Committee headed by Shri S.C.L Das , DirectorGeneral, Directorate General of Hydrocarbons, and comprising of Shri B.C.Bora,former Chairman ONGC, Shri T.K.Sengupta, former Director of ONGC, wasconstituted by the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas vide it’s Order dated11June 2020 to inquire into the incidence of fire at Baghjan oilfield of Oil IndiaLimited (OIL) in Tinsukia, Assam. The committee has submitted its report. Inaddition, a committee was constituted by Oil Industry Safety Directorate (OISD),which has also submitted the report. The three-member High Level Committeeconstituted by the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas has taken into accountthe recommendations of OISD committee also. The major recommendations of thetwo Committees are as under:i. Preparation of work over plan by Multi-Disciplinary Teamii. Safety Audit of all rigs and installations on annual basis.iii. Safety training to officers of all level.iv. Development of strong Crisis Management Team.v. Well control training must for work over rig key personnel.vi. Review, revise and establish additional Standard Operating Procedurein all cross-sections of work, based on statutory requirements.vii. Introduction of IT enabled system for Drilling, Workover and Productionoperations.viii. Invoking of provision of Disaster Management Act, 2005 and Rules incase of blowout.ix. Competency mapping for all key personnel.x. Preparation of detailed guidelines to be followed after blowout.The report of the High Level Committee has been accepted by the Governmentand Oil India Ltd has been asked to take action on the recommendations ofreport and also against the officers responsible for lapses.
