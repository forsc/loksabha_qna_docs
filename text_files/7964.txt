                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1559






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.11.2019






Download Speed




Ramesh Chander Kaushik













                                                    Saikia Dilip
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has envisaged download speed of 50 megabyte per second (mbps) to every citizen of the country by 2020 under the National Telecom Policy and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether the Government proposes to take help of State Governments in creating basic infrastructure to achieve the envisaged target and if so, the details thereof; and (c) 	whether the Government has fixed any time limit for this and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF COMMUNICATIONS, LAW & JUSTICE AND ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI RAVI SHANKAR PRASAD)(a) to (c) 	The National Digital Communications Policy 2018 (NDCP-2018) which was notified on 22nd October, 2018, inter-alia aims to provide Universal Broadband Connectivity at 50 Mbps to every citizen of the Country by 2022 under Connect India Mission of the policy. NDCP envisages that all key stakeholders which includes State Governments to come together to forge a coalition to deliver the policy and its missions. The strategies to achieve this inter-alia include creating a collaborative institutional mechanism between Centre, States and Local Bodies for Common Rights of Way, standardization of costs and timelines; and removal of barriers to approvals.
