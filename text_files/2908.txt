                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2673






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.07.2019






Procurement and Storage of Coarse Grains




Rajeshbhai Naranbhai Chudasama














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the total quantum of stock of rice and wheat with the Food Corporation of India (FCI), as on date, the details thereof, State-wise; (b) 	whether there is any effort by the FCI to procure and store coarse grains also during the last three years; (c) 	the targets of FCI for storage of all foodgrains during the current year; and (d) 	the steps taken/being taken by the Government to reduce storage costs for the benefits of farmers?






ANSWER




                                                         MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a): 	The stock of foodgrains in the Central Pool as on 1st June, 2019 was 742.87 lakh tons consisting of 275.81 lakh tons of rice, 465.60 lakh tons of wheat and 1.46 lakh tons of coarse grains. The State-wise details of foodgrains stock held in the central pool by Food Corporation of India and State Agencies are at Annex- I (b): 	The Coarse grains are procured by the State governments under Minimum Support Price (MSP), based on their plan of procurement, with prior approval of Government of India & subject to distribution of procured coarse grain under National Food Security Act (NFSA)/ Other Welfare Scheme (OWS) in the procuring state itself. .............2/--2-(c): 	As on 31.05.2019, Storage capacity available with FCI and State agencies for storage is 862.45 Lakh MT (Covered – 739.76 LMT and CAP – 122.69 LMT). Out of this capacity, approx. 128 LMT is being used by State Government for storage of commodities other than Wheat & Rice and also for intermediary storage.           Besides, FCI continuously assesses and monitors the storage capacity in the country and based on the requirement and storage gap assessment, storage capacities are created under following schemes :-1.	Private Entrepreneurs Guarantee (PEG)2.	Centre Sector Scheme (CSS).3.	Construction of SILO''s           Government of India has formulated a scheme called Private Entrepreneurs Guarantee (PEG) Scheme for construction of covered storage capacity.  Under this Scheme, storage capacity is created by private parties, CWC, SWCs and other State Agencies for guaranteed hiring by FCI. Under PEG scheme, a capacity of 150.56 LMT has been sanctioned/ allotted to private investors, CWC and SWCs. A capacity of 142.62 LMT has already been completed as on 31.05.2019.          The target for creation of a storage capacity under PEG scheme during the current year i.e. 2019-20 is 7.60 LMT and Silos during the current year is fixed for a capacity of 4 LMT.(d): 	It is the endeavour of the FCI / State Governments to procure the produce of the farmers as quickly as possible to reduce the storage time / cost with farmers, for which procurement / purchase centres are opened for paddy as well as wheat in different parts of the country.***ANNEXURE-IANNEXURE REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (a) OF THE UNSTARRED QUESTION NO.2673 DUE FOR ANSWERED ON 09.07.2019 IN THE LOK SABHA.                   ?????? ????? ???? : ???????? : ?? ?????? : ????? ??? ???????? ??????
