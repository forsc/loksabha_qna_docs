                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1583






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Murder and Snatching Cases in NCR




Adoor Prakash














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has taken note that murder and snatching cases are increasing in the National Capital Region;(b) the number of cases reported during the last three years and the current year; and(c) the details of the preventive measures taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (c): In the National Capital Region (NCR) areas, other than National Capital Territory (NCT) of Delhi, the primary responsibility with respect to ‘Police’ & ‘Public Order’ lies with the State Governments concerned, as per the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India. 2.	As far as NCT of Delhi is concerned, there has been a steady decline in incidents of snatching during the last three years (2016 to 2018).  There is no trend in the cases of murders  during  this period. The --2--L.S.US.Q.NO 1583 FOR 02.07.2019number of murder and snatching cases registered by Delhi Police during the last three years and the current year (upto 31.05.2019)  is as under: Year 	Number of cases of Murder registered 	Number of cases of Snatching registered2016	528	95712017	487	82312018	513	69322019 (upto 31.05.2019)	211	28123.	Delhi Police has instituted a number of measures to both prevent and expeditiously detect crimes.  These include, action against organized crime, arrest/surveillance of notorious criminals, joint group patrolling in vulnerable areas to enhance Police visibility, enhanced focus on beat policing, including through re-organisation of beats, closer surveillance of criminals and citizen-centric policing through ‘Jan Sampark’ and other community approach programmes. ******
