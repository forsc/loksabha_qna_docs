                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

791






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.11.2019






Schemes for Entrepreneurs in MSME Sector




Gopal Chinayya Shetty














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the schemes available to the Micro, Small & Medium Entrepreneurs of poor and middle class;(b) 	the total number of such entrepreneurs functioning in the country and the details and the number of  entrepreneurs  among them belonging to poor and middle classes;(c) 	the quantum of total business being undertaken by Micro, Small & Medium Entrepreneurs and their contribution in the Gross Domestic Product of the country; and(d) 	the quantum of total business being undertaken by Micro, Small & Medium Entrepreneurs belonging to poor and middle classes?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NITIN GADKARI)(a):	 Ministry of MSME implements following schemes for development of MSMEs:I.	 Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP) to generate self employment opportunities through establishment of Micro Enterprises in the non farm sector.II.	 Micro & Small Enterprises -Cluster Development Programme (MSE-CDP).III.	 Interventions for Aspirational Communities and Areas: SC-ST Hub, Scheme for Promotion of MSMEs in N.E. Region and Sikkim etc.IV.	 Tool Rooms and Technology Centres (TCs) to support MSMEs by providing skilled manpower, access to advanced technology and business advisory services.V.	 Mission Solar Charkha (MSC) to increase employment avenues in the rural hinterland through eco-friendly and technology-led growth.VI.	Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries to provide support to the artisans.VII.	Procurement and Marketing Support Scheme to enhance the marketability of products and services in the MSME sector. Every Central Ministry/Department/PSU to compulsorily procure 25 percent from MSEs. Out of the 25% procurement mandated from MSEs, 3% has been reserved for women entrepreneurs.VIII.	Entrepreneurship Skill Development Programme (ESDP) to upgrade skills of prospective entrepreneurs, existing workforce and also develop skills of new workers and technicians of MSEs.IX.	 Credit Guarantee Scheme to support collateral free loan to MSEs.X.	Interest subvention of 2% for all GST registered MSMEs on fresh or incremental loans. XI.	National Manufacturing Competitiveness Programme to support the competitiveness of the Manufacturing Sector.(b) to (d):	As on 15.11.2019, the total number of micro, small & medium enterprises in the country registered on Udyog Aadhar Portal are 70.61 Lakhs, 8.70 Lakhs & 0.34 Lakhs respectively. As reported by Central Statistics Office (CSO), the share of MSMEs in total Gross Domestic Product (GDP) during the year 2017-18 was 29.7%.
