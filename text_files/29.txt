                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

83






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.06.2019






Primary Health Centres




Prataprao  Jadhav














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether one women dies every seven minutes in each State due to complications arising during child birth, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether there is any proposal to set up more Primary Health Centres (PHCs) in rural and remote areas of the country in this regard, if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the existing PHCs lack necessary medicines, doctors and para medical staff, if so, the details thereof; and(d) the number of complaints received in this regard during each of the last three years?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	No. As per latest SRS 2014-16 reports, MMR of India is 130 per 100,000 live births.State wise status of MMR is given at Annexure I(b):	Public health and hospitals being a State subject, the primary responsibility to set up any public health facilities including PHCs lies with the State Governments.Under NHM, technical and financial support is provided to State/UTs to strengthen their public health facilities, including setting up PHCs as per Indian Public Health Standards(IPHS), based on the requirment proposed by the States/UTs in their annual Programme Implementation Plans(PIPs) within their overall resource envelope.(c):	Under NHM, funds are also provided under Free Drugs Services Initiative (FDSI) to provide essential medicines at public health facilities, free of cost. An indicative list of 285 essential medicines at PHCs has been communicated to the States/UTs, which can be modified and adapted by the State / UT based on their disease burden.29 States/UT have IT enabled drug and vaccine management system.Under Free Drugs Service initiative, funds allocated to the States in past  three years is given at AnnexureII.Shortage of Doctors and Para Medical Staff in public health facilities including PHCs varies from State to State depending upon their policies and context. As per Rural Health Statistics (RHS) - 2018, State/UT wise availability of doctors at PHCs, and of Lab Technicians, Pharmacists and Nursing staff in PHCs and CHCs in rural areas and shortfall thereof is given at Annexure III.(d):	As stated above, public health &hospitals being a State subject,complaints received in respect of PHCs lacking necessary medicines, doctors and para medical staff are forwarded to respective State Governments for examination and appropriate action.
