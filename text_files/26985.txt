                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2683






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.08.2021






BharatNet Project and NOFN




Satyadev Pachauri














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the status of BharatNet project along with the date when it was launched; (b) 	whether the said project is an extension of National Optical Fibre Network (NOFN) which was launched in 2011; (c) 	the details of the States where all Gram Panchayats have already been linked with broadband connectivity under the BharatNet project; (d) 	the name of States where Gram Panchayats are still to be connected under the project; and (e) 	the funds allocated/released/utilized by the Government thereunder so far?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS (SHRI DEVUSINH CHAUHAN)(a) & (b)	The Union Cabinet on 25.10.2011 approved the setting up of National Optical Fibre Network (NOFN) which was subsequently renamed as BharatNet. 	As on 09.07.2021, by laying 5,30,928 km Optical Fibre Cable (OFC), 1,53,836 Gram Panchayats (GPs) have been made Service Ready on OFC media. Further, 4083 GPs have been made Service Ready on Satellite media. In total, 1,57,919 GPs have been made Service Ready under the BharatNet project in the country.(c) & (d)	As on 09.07.2021, all the GPs have been made Service Ready in the State/Union Territory (UT) of Kerala and Chandigarh. Further, almost all GPs have been made Service Ready in Haryana, Karnataka, Puducherry, Dadra & Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu. The State/UT-wise details of number of planned GPs, Service Ready GPs, and the GPs which are yet to be made Service Ready under BharatNet project, are given in Annexure-I.(e)	The State/UT-wise funds allocation is based on the requests received from the Implementing Agencies. As on 31.03.2021, an amount of about Rs. 24,201 crore has been allocated/utilized under BharatNet Project in the country.
