                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1475






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.07.2021






Closure of Companies




S. Jothimani














                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of registered companies that have closed since March 2020, month-wise;(b) 	the number of registered companies that closed since March 2020 in Tamil Nadu;(c) 	the number of registered companies that reported temporary cessation of operations since March, 2020, month-wise in the State of Tamil Nadu; and(d) 	the measures taken by the Government to bring the rising numbers of closures under control? 
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        ???????  ??? ?????? ???????? ??? ????? ?????? (???? ??? ??????)  THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI SOM PARKASH)(a) to (d): 	As per information received from Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA), the companies closed is not defined in the provision of the Companies Act, 2013.  17,583 companies were struck off as per the provisions of Section 248 of the Act.  The month wise details of such companies is given in Annex – I.   A total of 1888 companies, which registered in the State of Tamil Nadu have struck off since March, 2020 as per the provisions of Section 248 of the Act. There is no provision to maintain data for temporary cessation of operation of Companies. The Government has also announced various relief measures for several sectors under the Aatmanirbhar Bharat Packages announced on 13th May, 2020 to 17th May, 2020, 12th October, 2020 & 12th November, 2020 involving a total amount of Rs.29,87,641 crore (including amount provided for the Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Package, Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana from July to November, and measures announced by RBI). Besides, a Rs 6.28 lakh crore Covid stimulus package has also been announced on 28.06.2021. The details of the measures are at Annex-II. These measures will provide necessary impetus to the existing projects and help in boosting the economy.Some of the recent steps taken by DPIIT to boost the manufacturing sector are achieving integration of India Industrial National Land Bank (GIS Land Bank) in 17 states; covering over 400 industrial parks under Industrial Park Rating System 2.0. Steps like Startup India Seed Fund Scheme; Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups; Startup Ranking Framework; National Startup Awards etc has been taken to build a strong ecosystem for nurturing innovation and entrepreneurship; continuation of Footwear Leather & Accessories Development Programme (FLADP) 2021-26 for boosting leather sector. Details of measures taken by DPIIT to boost Industrial activities in country is provided at Annex-III.*******
