                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

458






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.07.2019






Mobile/Internet Connectivity




Pinaki Misra














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether approximately eleven thousand villages in the State of Odisha have no mobile connectivity, the highest in the country and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether the mobile towers in Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected areas of the State have low radiating antennas with low bandwidth thereby restricting the internet facility to insignificant levels, if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor along with the steps taken by the Government in this regard; and (c) 	the steps taken/proposed to be taken by the Government to improve the mobile/internet connectivity in the villages and the LWE areas of the State and the time by which the said task is likely to be accomplished?	






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF COMMUNICATIONS, LAW & JUSTICE AND ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI RAVI SHANKAR PRASAD)(a) to (c)	 A statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT TO BE LAID ON THE TABLE OF LOK SABHA IN RESPECT OF PARTS (a) to (c) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *458 FOR 24TH JULY, 2019 ON “MOBILE/INTERNET CONNECTIVITY”.(a) to (c) 	Mobile/internet connectivity in the uncovered villages and the LWE areas of Odisha are being provided by the Government and telecom service providers in a phased manner. As per data collected from the Telecom Service Providers (TSPs) in 2018, it was estimated that out of 47,677 inhabited villages (as per the Census 2011) in the State of Odisha, 37,737 villages were covered with mobile services while 9,940 villages were not covered by mobile services. District-wise details are at Annexure. As per latest information received from the Department of Telecommunication’s field unit, the number of uncovered villages have been reduced to 8,776 inhabited villages. Under the BharatNet project in Odisha, by laying 12103 km Optical Fibre Cable (OFC), a total of 3925 Gram Panchayats (GPs) (including Block Head Quarters (BHQs)) have been connected, out of which 3645 GPs (including BHQs)  have been made Service Ready for broadband connectivity. In addition, 15 GPs have been made Service Ready over Satellite media. Wi-Fi hotspots have been installed in 90 GPs in Odisha which are providing services.Under the Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected areas Phase-I project, Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) identified 2343 tower locations for mobile connectivity in 10 States including Odisha, which have been provided towers. Out of the 256 towers installed in Odisha, 132 towers were connected through Microwave media, 104 towers were connected through Very Small Aperture Terminal (VSAT) media and 20 towers were connected through Optical Fibre Cable (OFC) media. Tower sites with Microwave and OFC media have been connected with 2Mbps or above bandwidth. All 104 towers VSAT media which were initially connected with 512 Kbps bandwidth, have now been connected to 2Mbps. Following projects also are being implemented by the Government for providing/facilitating Mobile/internet connectivity in Odisha:(i)	Under LWE Phase-II project, the Government has approved installation of 252 towers for providing mobile connectivity.(ii)	Under BharatNet project, the Government has recently approved provisioning of 2 Wi-Fi Access Points (APs) in all 3811 GPs.
