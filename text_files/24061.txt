                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5014






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2021






New Airports




Nishikant  Dubey














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the new airports constructed by the Government during the last three years, State-wise;(b) the places to which air services have been started from these airports; and(c) the number of proposals for construction of new airports received from the State Governments during each of the last three years, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) & (b): During the last five years, four new Greenfield airports have been constructed by the Government namely, Shirdi in Maharashtra, Pakyong in Sikkim, Kannur in Kerala and Kalaburagi in Karnataka. Details of places connected by air services to these new Greenfiled airports is at Annexure A. Besides, 56 airports under the Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) - UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik), including three aforesaid greenfield airports namely Pakyong, Kannur and Kalaburagi, 5 heliports, 2 water aerodromes, have been operationalized for operating RCS flights across the country. Out of these 56 RCS airports, 37 number of RCS airports are unserved airports and commercial operations have started for the first time in these airports. Under RCS-UDAN, air services have commenced on 325 RCS routes across the length and breadth of the country.(c): During the last five years, the Government of India has received proposals from the State Governments for construction of new airports at  Noida (Jewar) and Safai in Uttar Pradesh, Hisar in Haryana, Shivdaspur (Jaipur) and Kota in Rajasthan, Kothagudem in Telangana, Singrauli in Madhya Pradesh, Karwar and Chickmangaluru in Karnataka, Rajkot in Gujarat, Mandi in Himachal Pradesh, Purandar (Pune) in Maharashtra, Kasargod and Kottayam in Kerala and Pantnagar in Uttarakhand. Out of these proposals, MoCA has granted  ''site clearance'' in respect of projects at Kothagudem ( withdrawn by the State Government for seeking site clearance for another location), Singrauli and Purandar (Pune). State Government of Maharashtra has submitted a proposal with a new site for airport at Purandar. State Government has been advised to submit a fresh proposal for grant of ''Site Clearance'' in the prescribed proforma as per Greenfield Airports Policy. In addition, ''in principle'' approval has been granted in respect of projects at Noida and Rajkot.
