                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3885






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2021






Drinking Water Schemes/Projects




Nishikant  Dubey














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of ongoing drinking water schemes/ projects in the country; (b) whether the Government has any scheme for providing clean drinking water especially in the remote areas of the country and if so, the details thereof; (c) whether the Government has any proposal/ scheme to meet the shortage of safe drinking water in the rural parts of the country especially in Santhal Pargana of Jharkhand; (d) if so, the details thereof along with the financial allocation made/proposed to be made in this regard; and (e) if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) to (d)	Government of India in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) – Har Ghar Jal to provide potable water in adequate quantity of prescribed quality on regular and long-term basis to every rural household, including those in rural parts of Santhal Pargana of Jharkhand, through tap water connection, by 2024. The estimated outlay of the mission is Rs. 3.60 lakh Crore.Water is a State subject. Government of India supplements the efforts of State Governments by providing financial and technical assistance. It is States, who plan, approve, implement, operate and maintain drinking water supply schemes. Thus, details of individual schemes/ projectsin States are approved, implemented and maintained by the respective State Government or its agency, responsible for the implementation in the field.(e) Doesn’t arise.****
