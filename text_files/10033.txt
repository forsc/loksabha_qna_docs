                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3533






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2019






Increase in Operational Expenses




Khagen Murmu














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether operational expenses of FCI have increased owing to increase in Minimum Support Price (MSP) of wheat and rice and expenditure on storage under Public Distribution System over the years; (b) 	if so, whether the Government has formulated/proposes to formulate any scheme to reduce the operational expenses without reducing MSP; and (c) 	the extent to which the operational expenses of Food Corporation of India are likely to be reduced after the implementation of the said scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E R MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a) to (c):  	The operational costs of Food Corporation of India (FCI) consist of Freight, Storage, Handling, Employee Cost, Interest and other expenses. These expenses are incurred after procurement is over and food grains are brought to godowns for storage and movement from surplus States to deficit States. These expenses are not related to Minimum Support Price (MSP) and depend on inflation and volume of operations.Reduction of operational costs is a continuous process. Various steps have been taken from time to time for the same like rationalisation of labour by pooling labour forces to lesser number of depots and deployment of contract labour, increase in number of bags handled by labourers per day, implementation of Depot Online System in FCI depots, etc.****
