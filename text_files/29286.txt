                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

649






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.12.2021






National Railway Station Development Corporation




Rajiv Ranjan (Lalan) Singh














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has closed the National Railway Station Development Corporation;(b) if so, the likely effect on the projects which has been undertaken by this corporation;(c) the details of the number of stations given to them for development and the current status thereof; and(d) the details of funds allocated and spent on such projects?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMUNICATIONS ANDELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI ASHWINI VAISHNAW)(a) and (b): Yes, Sir.  Government has given ‘in principle’ approval for closure of Indian Railway Stations Development Corporation (IRSDC).  The projects undertaken by IRSDC shall be handed over to respective Zonal Railways and Rail Land Development Authority (RLDA).  Zonal Railways and RLDA will further develop the station projects.(c) and (d): IRSDC was undertaking techno-economic feasibility studies for 63 stations across the country.  Rani Kamlapati Railway Station and Gandhinagar Capital (Gujarat) Railway Station have been commissioned and rest of the stations are at various stages of studies/ development. Rani Kamlapati Railway Station has been developed through Public Private Partnership with no cost to Railways and Gandhinagar Capital (Gujarat) Railway Station has been developed through equity participation of Government of Gujarat and Ministry of Railways.*****
