                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1144






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.09.2020






Technical Textile Sector




Anil Firojiya














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether any steps have been taken by the Government to promote the technical textile sector in the country;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government proposes to attach the handloom clusters to the textile industry for procuring the handloom products directly;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) if not, the reasons therefor? 






ANSWER




                                                        ?????? ?????? (??????? ?????? ?????? ?????)MINISTER OF TEXTILES(SMT. SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a) & (b):	With a view to promote technical textiles in the country, the Government has approved the proposal for creation of National Technical Textiles Mission (NTTM) for a period of 4 years (2020-21 to 2023-24) with an outlay of Rs.1480 crores. The NTTM will (i) focus on research and innovation and indigenous development of specialty fibres from Carbon, Nylon-66, Glass, Aramid and other high technology polymers; increase application of geo-textiles, agro-textiles, medical textiles, protective textiles and other segments of technical textiles in various application areas (ii) promote awareness amongst users, bring in large scale investments, and encourage high-end technical textiles products (iii) enhance India’s exports of technical textiles to 8 Billion USD from current level of 2 Billion USD by 2024 through focused attention on 10 highest  traded product (iv) create a robust human resources in the country, both through specialized higher education and skill development of technical manpower of the country.The focus of the Mission is for developing on usage of technical textiles in various flagship missions, programmes of the country including strategic sectors. The use of technical textiles in agriculture, aquaculture, dairy, poultry, etc. Jal Jivan Mission; Swachch Bharat Mission; Ayushman Bharat will bring an overall improvement in cost economy, water and soil conservation, better agricultural productivity and higher income to farmers per acre of land holding in addition to promotion of manufacturing and exports activities in India. The use of geo-textiles in highways, railways and ports will result in robust infrastructure, reduced maintenance cost and higher life cycle of the infrastructure assets.(c) to (e):	Yes, Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) has been signed between the Office of Development Commissioner (Handloom) and five Textile Companies on 28.1.2019 with an objective to promote the handloom sector and alleviate the socio-economic conditions of handloom weavers by way of improved earnings.  The MoU envisages the companies to facilitate and develop interface with handloom clusters and pockets and source their fabric requirement for growth and promotion of handloom segment in the country. ****
