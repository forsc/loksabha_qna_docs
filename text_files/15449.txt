                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    EXTERNAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4101






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2020






Shortage of Staff in C.P.O.




Achyutananda Samanta














                        Will the Minister of





EXTERNAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is facing a continued shortage of staff at various levels in the Central Passport Organisation (CPO) and if so, the details thereof; and  (b)	whether the Government is taking steps to fill up these vacancies, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor? 
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EXTERNAL AFFAIRS      [SHRI V. MURALEEDHARAN](a)     As on 01.03.2020, against the sanctioned strength of 2697 of Central Passport Organization and 21 posts sanctioned by the Union Cabinet for the Project Management Unit (PMU) of the Passport Seva Project, there are 37 vacancies at Group ‘A’ level, 303 at  Group ’B’ Gazetted level and 589 at Group ‘B’ non-Gazetted and Group ‘C’ level.   (b)   The Government has taken following steps to fill up the existing vacancies at the Passport Offices:(i) The regular placement of indent for recruitment of Office Assistant, Junior Passport Assistant, Assistant Superintendent, Junior Translators and Stenographers with Staff Selection Commission (SSC).  325 posts at various levels have been indented to SSC. Out of which, 87 dossiers have been received from SSC and offer of appointment letters have been issued to the candidates recently. (ii) The Ministry has also hired 361 Data Entry Operators and 60 Multi Tasking Staff from an Outsourced Agency as an interim measure against the vacant non-Gazetted posts for smooth functioning of the Passport Offices.   (iii)	 To fill up the existing vacancies at the Passport Offices, regular meetings of the Department Promotion Committee (DPC) have been held for the promotion of eligible officials to the next grade. (iv)	Appointing the officers on deputation basis as per Recruitment Rules of Deputy Passport Officer and Passport Officer.***
