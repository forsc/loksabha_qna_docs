                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3099






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






National Programme for Dairy Development




Rajesh Verma














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the farmers trained so far in the country under the National Programme for Dairy Development (NPDD), State-wise;(b)	whether the Government is implementing any scheme for the financially weaker farmers in dairy sector; and(c)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER FOR  FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a)	Under National Programme for Dairy Development (NPDD) scheme, so far, 12.26 lakh farmer-members have been brought under the ambit of dairy cooperative network. These farmer-members have availed the benefit of the scheme activities including training/orientation programs etc. The State wise details of farmers’ inducted/enrolled/added in the dairy cooperative network under NPDD scheme is at Annexure.(b) & (c) Supplementing the efforts of the State Government, Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying has been implementing following dairy development schemes, wherein provisions have been made for financial assistance to dairy cooperatives, milk producer companies etc, primarily for strengthening/creation of dairy infrastructure for quality milk procurement, its processing, marketing and working capital requirements:-i.	National Programme for Dairy Developmentii.	Dairy Processing and Infrastructure Development Fundiii.	Supporting Dairy Cooperatives and Farmer Producer Organisations engaged in dairy activitiesThe above schemes benefit farmer-members, including financially weaker farmers in the dairy sector.
