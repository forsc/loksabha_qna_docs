                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4919






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.04.2022






Universal Screening of Children for Leprosy and TB




C. R. Patil














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	whether the Government has taken appropriate steps for universal screening of children below 18 years for leprosy and tuberculosis (TB);  b)	if so the details thereof;c)	the objective of the RashtriyaBalSwasthayaKaryakram (RBSK), andd)	the number of children benefitted under the RBSK ?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a)   & (b):	Rashtriya Bal Swasthaya Karyakram  (RBSK) programme under Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn, Child, Adolescent health and Nutrition (RMNCAH+N) strategy, of National Health Mission, Government of India, provides screening services for selected health conditions to children through dedicated mobile health teams placed in every block. Children from birth to six years of age are screened at Anganwadi centres twice a year and children from 6 years till 18 years of age enrolled in Government and Government aided schools in classes 1st to 12th are screened once a year. Screening ofchildren for TB and Leprosy through RBSK Mobile Health teams has been introduced for early detection of TB and Leprosy. Screening tools used by RBSK Mobile Health teams have been suitably revised to include these diseases.RBSK Mobile Health Teams refer children suspected with these conditions to respective District/Block officials forthe Tuberculosis and Leprosy programmes forconfirmation ofpreliminary findings and appropriate management as per respective NationalDisease Control Programme Guidelines.(c) The objective of Rashtriya Bal Swasthaya Karyakram is early identification and early management for selected health conditions - 4 D”s i.e. Defects at Birth, Developmental delays, Diseases and Deficiencies. (d) As reported by States/UTs, under RBSK, 8.04 crores children were identified with selected health conditions and 3.11 crores children availed secondary/tertiary care, since inception in FY 2014-15. ************
