                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2872






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.08.2021






Allocation of Funds to Karnataka under JJM




Shivkumar Chanabasappa Udasi














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether  it  is  a  fact  that  the  Government  has increased the  amount  of funds allocated to Karnataka under the Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) in the financial year 2021-2022 and if so, the details thereof including the funds released during financial year 2019-20 and 2020-21, year-wise;(b)  the  details  of  the  present  status  of  tap  water supply in rural areas of Karnataka;(c) the total number of households  provided with tap water connection since the launch of the mission till date; and(d) the number of schools and anganwadi centres having access to piped water supply in Karnataka as on date?






ANSWER




      MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) 	Government of India has enhanced the allocation of Central grant under Jal Jeevan Mission to Karnataka from Rs.1,189.40 Crore in 2020-21 to Rs.5,008.80 Crore in 2021-22. During 2019-20 and 2020-21, the State of Karnataka has drawn ?546.06 Crore and ?446.36 Crore, respectively, out of the allocated Central fund under JJM. (b)	As on 30.07.2021,as reported by the State, out of total 91.19 lakh rural households in Karnataka, 31.14 lakh (34.15%) households have the provision of potable water supply through functional household tap connection.(c) 	Since the launch of JJM in August, 2019, provision of tap water supply has been made in more than 4.63 crore rural households in the country, out of which,6.63 lakh households are in Karnataka. (d)	As on 30.07.2021, out of total 42,015 schools and 53,901 anganwadi centres in Karnataka,41,665 schools (99.17%) and 51,587 anganwadi centres (95.71%)have access to potable tap water supply.*****
