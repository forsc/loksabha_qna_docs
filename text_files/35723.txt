                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3037






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






Increase Use of Sanskrit in Official Work




Pushpendra Singh Chandel













                                                    Mala Rajya Laxmi Shah
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the steps being taken by the Government for dissemination of Sanskrit language;(b) whether the Government proposes to increase the use of Sanskrit in official work;(c) if so, the details thereof;(d) whether the Government also proposes to create new posts for Sanskrit translators; and(e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS (SHRI AJAY KUMAR MISHRA)(a):  The details of the steps being taken by the Government for dissemination of Sanskrit language is annexed.(b) & (c):    As per Article 343(1) of the Constitution, the Official Language of the Union shall be Hindi in Devnagari script, the form of numerals to be used for the official purposes of the Union shall be the international form of Indian numerals.  As  per  Article  351 of the Constitution,  it shall be the -2-LS.US.Q.NO.3037 FOR 22.03.2022duty of the Union to promote the spread of the Hindi language, to develop it so that it may serve as a medium of expression for all the elements of the composite culture of India and to secure its enrichment by assimilating without interfering with its genius, the forms, style and expressions used in Hindustani and in the other languages of India specified in the Eighth Schedule, and by drawing, wherever necessary or desirable, for its vocabulary, primarily on Sanskrit and secondarily on other languages.(d) & (e):	No, Sir.*****
