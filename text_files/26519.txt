                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2147






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.08.2021






Role of ITDC in Promotion of Tourism




Kuldeep Rai Sharma













                                                    Amol Ramsing Kolhe
                                                








                                                    Sunil Dattatray Tatkare
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Ratansinh Magansinh Rathod
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                








                                                    Manickam Tagore B
                                                








                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the role of India Tourism Development Corporation (ITDC) in the hospitality industry and the promotion of tourism in the country along with the number of ITDC hotels in the country presently functioning; (b) 	whether the ITDC has not been able to achieve its objective for which it was set up and if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor; (c) 	whether the Government has taken note of the huge losses suffered by ITDC in recent years and if so, the steps taken by Government to streamline the economic condition of these hotels; (d) 	whether the Government has directed ITDC to transfer some of its hotels, beach resorts, travellers lodges/restaurants to the State Governments or jointly lease them with the State Governments and if so, details thereof and the reasons therefor; and (e) 	the steps taken by Government to improve the profitability of ITDC so as to avoid it from closure?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF TOURISM 			        (SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a):	At present, India Tourism Development Corporation Limited (ITDC) is running the following verticals:Ashok Group of Hotels: ITDC as part of one of its main objectives had been running various hotels, motels, restaurants, canteens, cafeterias etc. for the purpose of boarding, lodging and stay of tourists. A number of units have been handed over to respective State Governments while some were disinvested / divested as per directions of the Government of India. At present the following hotels etc. are being operated by ITDC / Joint Venture Company (JVC):<pre>S. no.	Name of Hotel	State / UT1	The Ashok Hotel	New Delhi2	Samrat Hotel	New Delhi3	Hotel Kalinga Ashok	Bhubaneswar, OdishaJoint Venture Hotels1	The Ashok Beach Resort	Puducherry2	Hotel Nilachal Ashok(Operations Closed and sub-judice)	Puri, OdishaRestaurant1	Taj Restaurant	Agra, Uttar Pradesh</pre>Ashok Travels and Tours (ATT) Division is providing various travel related services like air ticketing (domestic and international), car rental services and cargo handling.Ashok Consultancy and Engineering Services (ACES) Division has been preparing DPR’s / Feasibility Reports for Promotion / Development of tourism infrastructure in the Country. Further the division also executes various tourism infrastructure projects under Swadesh Darshan, PRASHAD and CFA Scheme of Ministry of Tourism and State Governments.Sound & Light Show (SEL) Division of ITDC has a major role of developing Tourism Infrastructure and SEL Projects. The company has been able to assist Ministry of Tourism and various State Govts. for preparation of Detailed Project Reports, Feasibility Reports, Tourism Master Plans etc. in almost every part of the country and has executed prestigious projects in North East and various other states. As far as implementation of SEL Shows is concerned, the organization is the oldest in the country and has contributed to give its best SEL shows at Red Fort, Purana Quilla, Cellular Jail, Konark Sun Temple, Ross island, Shilpgram Deoghar, Diu Fort, Tilyar lake Rohtak etc. The Division at present is implementing shows at Leh Palace, Kargil, Dal Lake, Udaigiri, Sarkis Roza Gujarat, Dhordo Kutch, Pinjore, Brahm Sarovar Kurukshtra etc.Ashok Events Division (AED):(i)  	Manages events, conferences and exhibitions etc. both at national and international level for various Ministries and Government Bodies.(ii) 	Provides designing and printing solutions (posters, brochures, folders, invite cards etc.).(iii)  	Manages Literature Distribution Centre, Palam on behalf of Ministry of Tourism.(iv)  	AED is the designated agency of the Ministry of Tourism for executing their events etc. in promoting Tourism.Ashok Institute of Hospitality & Tourism Management (AIHTM) vertical of ITDC, is a skill imparting centre in the Hospitality sector. AIH&TM is conducting various skill development programmes of Ministry of Tourism, Government of India (Hunar Se Rozgar, Skill Testing and Certification, Entrepreneurship Development Programme, Tourism Awareness Programme etc.) alongwith skill certification and Diploma Courses such as One Year Diploma in Hospitality Trades in association with NIOS, Ministry of HRD and One Year Diploma in Hospitality Management (DHM) with JMI Central University. AIH&TM is also conducting 3 years B.Sc. (Hotel and Hospitality Administration) Course in affiliation with NCHMCT-IGNOU and 3 years B.VoC. in Food Production in affiliation with JMI Central University. These courses help in generating skilled manpower in the hospitality trade and thus helps in bridging gap in requirement versus availability of trained manpower.Ashok International Trade Division (AITD) currently has Duty Free Shops at Seaports, thereby facilitating the International traveller to buy Duty free goods. The division currently has shop at 14 seaport locations.(b):  ITDC has been able to achieve its primary objectives for which it was set up and has been able to develop and promote tourism in the country over a period of time.(c): ITDC has not suffered losses during last 5 years except in the financial year 2020-21 due to COVID-19 impact and lock down restrictions. Profitability position of the Company during last five years is as follows:(In Rs. Lakh)<pre>Financial Year	Profit After Tax (PAT)	Total Comprehensive Income (TCI)2020-21	-2,720.29	-2,583.862019-20	2,195.47	1,429.902018-19	3,957.44	4,135.042017-18	1,770.72	1,322.322016-17	1,143.20	1,062.33</pre>(d):	Yes, as per its Disinvestment Policy, the Government of India is in the process of disinvesting the hotels and properties of ITDC through leasing out or transferring the property to the concerned State Governments / Ministry. The policy has been formulated keeping in view that running and managing hotels on professional lines is not the job of Government or its entities.Status of disinvestment is placed at Annexure.(e):	The steps taken to improve the profitability of ITDC, are as follows: (i)   	Renovation of some rooms in The Ashok Hotel, New Delhi in recent past.(ii)  	Renovation of 48 rooms, addition of a new swimming Pool for guests and a new licensed F&B outlet has been done in Samrat Hotel, New Delhi.(iii)  	Modernization of the products & upgradation of services in all Hotels & Catering Establishments of ITDC.(iv)  	Tie ups with most of the Online Travel Agents for online room bookings has been done.(v)  	ATT has closed down its non-viable units in various cities and also intends to improve its business of cargo segment by appointing more cargo agents to improve its performance.(vi) 	AED has been utilized by many Central Ministries, Government departments, Autonomous bodies etc. for executing their events in India and internationally for which Event Management Fee (EMF) is paid to ITDC- Ashok Events.(vii)  	AITD has been profitable and even as on date when due to the Novel Corona Virus COVID-19 Pandemic, most of the travel and tourism related business have taken big hits, ITDC''s Duty Free business continues to earn profits for ITDC and Government of India.*******
