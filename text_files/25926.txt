                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1700






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






DRIP Phase-II and III




P. Ravindhranath














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details and the present status of Dam Rehabilitation and Improvement Project-Phase II and Phase III (DRIP Phase II and Phase III) initiated by the Government through Central Water Commission across the country; (b) the time by which the above projects are likely to be completed; (c) whether some more projects are also proposed to improve the safety and operational performance of the dams across the country including Tamil Nadu; and (d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) The Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA) approved Phase II and Phase III of Dam Rehabilitation and Improvement Project (DRIP) on October 29, 2020. This scheme, when lauched formally after signing of loan agreements, will facilitate loans from World Bank/ Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank to participating State Govts. Participating States and other agencies will need to fulfill necessary Project Readiness Criteria (PRC) before signing the loan agreement with the funding agency. External assistance of US$ 500 M will be arranged in each phase, being co-financed by World Bank and Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank.(b)  DRIP Phase II and III together will last for 10 years, each phase continuing for six years with two years overlap.(c) & (d)   Nineteen (19) States (Andhra Pradesh, Chhattisgarh,  Goa, Gujarat, Jharkhand, Karnataka, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Manipur, Meghalaya, Odisha, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand, West Bengal) so far and three Central Agencies (BBMB, CWC and DVC) will participate in DRIP Phase-II & III.  Total budget outlay  for both phases is Rs 10,211 Cr (Phase II: Rs 5107 Cr; Phase III: Rs 5104 Cr) . 736 dams are proposed to be taken up for rehabilitation. The details of State/Agency-wise number of proposed dams along with estimated cost as per Union Cabinet''s approval is enclosed as Annexure-I.Government of Tamil Nadu was part of recently closed DRIP through its WRD (Water Resources Deptt.) & TANGEDCO (Tamil Nadu Generation and Distribution Corporation). These agencies are also part of proposed DRIP Phase II & III. As per the approved Cabinet Note, it is proposed to rehabilitate 59 dams of Tamil Nadu (of both WRD and TANGEDCO) with an estimated cost of 1064 Crores under both DRIP Phase II and III.***** ANNEXURE-I Annexure referred to in reply to part (c) & (d) of Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No. 1700 to be answered on 29.07.2021 regarding “DRIP PHASE-II AND III”.DRIP PHASE II AND PHASE IIIDetails of Proposed Dams and CostSr. No.	State / Agency	No. of dams	Estimated Cost(Rs. in Cr)1.
