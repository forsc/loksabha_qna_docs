                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4567






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






Schemes for Rural Development




Annasaheb Shankar Jolle














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the schemes and programmes implemented by the Ministry for the rural sector, in particular;(b) 	whether convergence between the Ministries have been envisaged as designed in District Development Coordination and Monitoring Committee (DISHA) so that resources are used optimally;(c) 	the outcome measured against the objectives set; and(d) 	the details of the outcome achieved so far?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a)	The Ministry of Rural Development (MoRD) is implementing Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (Mahatma Gandhi NREGS), Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM), Deen Dayal Upadhayay – Gramin Kaushalya Yojana (DDU-GKY), Rural Self Employment Training Institutes (RSETI), Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana – Gramin (PMAY-G), Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY), National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP), Shyama Prasad  Mukherji Rurban Mission (SPMRM) and Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana(SAGY) for development of rural sector. (b) to (d) District Development Coordination and Monitoring Committee (DISHA) is an effort to improve development co-ordination and monitoring within the Constitutional framework of responsibilities assigned to Central, State and Local Governments. These Committees are envisaged with the objective of ensuring quality of expenditure, particularly in the context of optimization of public funds spent under different programmes of Central Government. DISHA Committees meet regularly both at State level, with Chief Minister of the state concerned as Chairperson, and at district level, with Hon''ble Member of Parliament (Lok Sabha) elected from the district concerned as the Chairperson. During 2020-21, a total of 648 district-level DISHA Committee meetings have been conducted as on 18.03.2021. These committees monitor the implementation of the programmes in accordance with prescribed procedures and guidelines and promote synergy and convergence of different programmes.****
