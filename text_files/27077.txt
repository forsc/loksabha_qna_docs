                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2790






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.08.2021






Loss to Airports




Bhola Singh













                                                    Raja Amareshwara Naik
                                                








                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                








                                                    Rajveer (Raju Bhaiya) Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether about 75 percent of Government owned (Airport Authority of India operated airports suffer losses despite increase in air passenger traffic;(b) if so, the details of profits earned and loss suffered by such airports during the last three years;(c) whether the Government has set up the Airports Economic Regulatory Authority (AERA) for regulating tariffs and other charges  for the services rendered at major airports ;(d) if so, the details of increase in such tariffs and charges during the last three years;(e) whether the Government has amended the Airports Economic Regulatory Authority Act to allow private airport operators to charge predetermined tariffs and if so, the details thereof; and (f) the other corrective steps being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) & (b): Yes sir, about 75 percent of Airports Authority of India (AAI) operated airports are incurring losses. The details of profit and loss made by AAI airports for the last three years is enclosed as Annexure A.;;(c) & (d): Yes, Sir. Government has set up Airports Economic Regulatory Authority of India (AERA) under the AERA Act, 2008 to determine tariff of Major Airports for Aeronautical Services. There are 24 Major Airports regulated by AERA as on 31.03.2021. Out of these, there has been no increase in tariff during the last 3 years (i.e. between 01.04.2018 to 31.03.2021)  at 22 Major Airports. At 02 airports namely Bhubaneswar and Patna, tariff has been increased  during the last 3 years (i.e. between 01.04.2018 to 31.03.2021), details of which are at  Annexure B.;(e) & (f): AERA Act, 2008 was amended vide notification dated 19.09.2019 that AERA shall not determine the tariff or tariff structures or the amount of development fees in respect of an airport or part thereof, if such tariff or tariff structures or the amount of development fees has been incorporated in the bidding document, which is the basis for award of operatorship of that airport. Further, AERA shall be consulted in advance before incorporating the proposed tariffs in the bidding document.
