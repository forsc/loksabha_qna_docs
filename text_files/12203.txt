                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

952






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2020






Animals affected by Floods




K. Shanmuga Sundaram














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a). 	whether the Ministry has received any report regarding the 2015 floods in 	parts of Amreli Bhav Nagar area in Madhya Pradesh which resulted in wiping 	out of blue bulls, deers and Asiatic lions;(b) 	if so, the details thereof along with the animals which had been wiped out as a 	result of the flood; (c) 	whether the State Government has requested for transfer of Asiatic lions from 	Gujarat to its Palpur Kuno Sanctuary of Madhya Pradesh;(d) 	if so, the steps taken by the Ministry for expediting the transfer of Asiatic lions 	from Gujarat to Madhya Pradesh; and(e) 	whether the Palpur Kuno Sanctuary is located apart from the human settlement 	and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) and (b) As per the information received from the State Forest Department of Gujarat,  species wise details of wild animals died during the 2015 flash floods in  Amreli and Bhavnagar Districts of Gujarat are as follows:S. No.	Name of Species	No. of animals died during 2015 floods1.	Blue bulls	16702.	Deers	873.	Asiatic Lions	10(c) The State Government of Madhya Pradesh has requested for the translocation of Asiatic Lion from Gujarat to Kuno Wildlife Sanctuary in Madhya Pradesh. (d) The Hon’ble Supreme Court in I.A. No. 100 in Writ Petition (Civil) No.337/1995 in Centre for Environment Law, WWF-I vs Union of India & Others had vide their order dated 15th April 2013 directed the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change to take urgent steps for re-introduction of Asiatic lion from Gir forests to Kuno in accordance with the guidelines issued by IUCN. Hon’ble Court vide the said order had also directed the Ministry to constitute an Expert Committee for this purpose.In compliance to the directions of Hon’ble Supreme Court of India, the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change has constituted an Expert Committee under the Chairmanship of Additional Director General of Forests (Wildlife), MoEF&CC. The Expert Committee had convened six meetings so far.(e) The State Forest Department of Madhya Pradesh has informed that all 24 villages have been relocated from the Wildlife Sanctuary. The status of Wildlife Sanctuary has also been upgraded  to National Park adding an additional area of 404.0758 sq.km. That makes the area of Kuno National Park as 748.7618 sq.km.***
