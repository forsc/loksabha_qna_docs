                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

340






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2021






Allocation of Funds under Startups India Seed Fund




John Barla













                                                    (Dr.) Ram Shankar Katheria
                                                








                                                    Nayab Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)     	the funds allocated by the Government to promote startups under startups India  Seed Fund; (b)       the details of the upcoming programmes under the startup India Seed Fund; (c)    	whether the Government has plans to make startups more convenient to rural people;     and (d)      if so, the details thereof?  






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI SOM PARKASH)(a)&(b) :	Sir, the scheme has been approved for the period of next 4 years starting from 2021-22 with total budgetary provision of Rs. 945 Crore. It will be implemented with effect from 1st April 2021.(c)&(d) :	All initiatives under the Startup India are inclusive and are implemented across States, cities, towns and rural areas. The result is that now 30 States and Union Territories have specific policies to support startups. All States and UTs in India have at least one startup. The latest addition to this list is Lakshadweep where the first startup was recognized in February, 2020.  More than 42,000 startups are now recognized from more than 590 districts across the country. About 55% of startups are from Tier-1 cities and 45% are from other cities and towns.******
