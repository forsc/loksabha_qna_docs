                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2511






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2019






Gender Discrimination in MGNREGA




Adala Prabhakara Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Ministry has received any complaint with regard to implementation of MGNREGA in various States particularly regarding wages for both men and women;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether there is a lot of discrimination in providing employment to women folk; and(d)	if so, the corrective steps that the Government is taking to provide equal distribution of work and wages between men and women under the scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) & (b):	The Ministry, under Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (Mahatma Gandhi NREGS) receives complaints of irregularities/corruption and embezzlement including diversion of funds, non-payment of wages, lack of transparency etc. but not relating to wages for men and women.(c) & (d):	No, Sir. There is no gender based discrimination under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS. The Ministry along with the States/UTs has been making all efforts for improving the women participation under the Mahatma Gandhi NREGS. To facilitate greater participation of women wage seekers, a separate schedule of rate has been mandated for them with flexible working hours. The percentage of women participation was 54.57 under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS during the FY 2018-19.
