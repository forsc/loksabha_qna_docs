                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2344






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2019






Women Farmer Empowerment Programme




Kanimozhi Karunanidhi














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of targets achieved so far under the ‘Women Farmer Empowerment Programme’ under National Rural Livelihood Mission along with the Funds allocated and utilised under the said scheme;(b)	the details of additional support and assistance to women farmers under any other schemes;(c)	whether the Government has undertaken adequate steps to create awareness regarding this programme and its benefits among the farming community; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):	Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana (MKSP), a sub component of Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM) is being implemented since 2011. The primary objective to empower women in agriculture by making systematic investments to enhance their participation and productivity, as also to create and sustain their agriculture-based livelihoods. Three broad focus programmatic areas of MKSP are (i) Sustainable Agriculture (ii) Non-Timber Forest Produces (NTFP) and (iii) Value Chain Development. The subsidy is 44% in respect of women, SC/ST and all categories of candidates from North-Eastern and Hill States and 36% in respect of other categories. •	Under the Scheme ‘Integrated Schemes of Agricultural Marketing (ISAM)’, support for women is available to the extent of 33.33% for women against 25% assistance available for male farmers for storage infrastructure projects and other infrastructure projects. •	Under ‘Sub-Mission of Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM)’, 10% additional assistance is available for women for Procurement of Agriculture Machinery/Equipment including Post Harvest equipment. •	Under the Scheme ‘National Food Security Mission (NFSM)’, 10% additional assistance is available for women for Plant Protection equipment including eco- friendly light trap and seed treatment drum as well as supply of improved farm implements.(c) and (d): Yes, the Government has undertaken adequate steps to create awareness regarding this programme and its benefits among the farming community. Mass Media Support to Agriculture Extension being implemented by the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare for the welfare of farmers by using electronic, print and social media. The Ministry is continuously working to create awareness about the project through the State Rural Livelihoods Missions. The benefits of taking up sustainable practices in Agriculture, NTFP, and livestock are discussed in the SHG meetings. The learning from the practices adopted under MKSP is now being scaled up by the Rural Livelihoods Missions of States/UTs as a universal intervention under their DAY NRLM through its Annual Action Plans (AAPs). The IEC (Information, Education and Communication) strategies and campaigns of Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – National Rural Livelihood Mission cover all its sub components including MKSP and this Ministry is continuously supporting women farmer under DAY NRLM. Besides, compendium of Agro Ecological best practice document, films on MKSP and communication materials have been developed. As part of the public disclosure norms wall paintings are being done in the MKSP project areas.
