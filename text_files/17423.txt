                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

885






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Subsidy to MSME Workers




D. M. Kathir Anand














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has taken any steps to provide wage subsidy to MSME workers; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; and(c)	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NITIN GADKARI)(a)&(b):   No wage subsidy scheme, for MSME workers, has been introduced by the Govt. of India. (c):	The Government of India has, however, announced Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) as a part of Atmanirbhar Bharat Abhiyaan. Under the scheme, eligible MSME entities and business enterprises are provided fully guaranteed and collateral free additional credit of upto 20% of their outstanding as on 29.02.2020, with interest rate capped at 9.26% for Banks and Financial Institutions and at 14% for non-Banking Financial Institutions (NBFCs), and a moratorium of 12 months on principal repayment.  The Scheme aims at helping MSMEs to tide over the crisis caused by COVID -19 pandemic and meet their operational liabilities, including inter alia, payment of wages to workers.
