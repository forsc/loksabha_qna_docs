                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2075






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.07.2019






Toilet Facility in Houses




Ravindra Shyamnarayan alias Ravi Kishan Shukla














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the State/UT-wise total number and percentage of the houses in the country including Uttar Pradesh which have the toilet facility;(b) the budget provision made in the current five year plan for construction of toilets;(c) whether most of the toilets are not in use;(d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) the steps being taken by the Government to make the aforesaid toilets usable?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF JAL SHAKTI(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) 	State/UT-wise total number and percentage of rural households with toilets, as reported by the States/UTs on the online Integrated Management Information System (IMIS) of Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) are given at Annexure. (b) 	Since 2017-18, Five Year Plans have been discontinued.  However, the funds allocated under Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) during the last five years and the current year are as under:-Sl.No.	Financial Year	Funds allocated (Rs. in crore)1.	2014-15	2850.002.	2015-16	6525.003.	2016-17	10513.004.	2017-18	16948.275.	2018-19*	14478.10*6.	2019-20 (Interim Budget)	10000.00 *In addition, funds amounting to Rs.8698.20 crore have been raised through Extra Budgetary Resources during 2018-19.(c) and (d) 	As per the National Annual Rural Sanitation Survey (NARSS) 2018-19 results, 96.5% of the households who had access to toilets, used them.(e) 	Under Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin), the incentive for individual toilet has been increased from Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 12,000, to provide for water availability, including for storing water for hand-washing and cleaning. The Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) and National Rural Drinking Water Programme(NRDWP) guidelines also provide for convergence towards implementation of the  programmes  of sanitation and water, and to maximize the availability of water for sanitation purposes. Also under SBM(G), rural pans are used which uses only 1-2 liters of water for flushing.
