                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2201






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2021






Non-Active Ports




Magunta Sreenivasulu Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the non-active major and minor ports as on date, State-wise;(b) whether there is any proposal/plan/strategy to make such non-active/non-functional major and minor ports active and functional; and(c) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) All Major Ports in the country are active Ports. The details of all non active Minor ports are as under:<pre>State/UT	Total Minor Ports	Non-activeAndhra Pradesh	15	11Goa	5	4Gujarat	48	28Karnataka	12	3Kerala	17	12Maharashtra	48	-Odisha	14	12Tamil Nadu	17	8West Bengal	2	2Andaman and Nicobar Islands	24	6Daman & Diu	2	 -Puducherry	3	 -Lakshadweep	10	 -                                               </pre>(b) & (c) Ministry constituted Maritime States Development Council (MSDC) in the year 1997, an apex advisory body for the development of maritime sector and aims to ensure development of Major and Non-Major Ports, in consultation with State Governments, the future development of existing and new Minor Ports by the respective Maritime States either directly or through captive users and private participation. Till date, seventeen meetings of MSDC have been held.                                      *****
