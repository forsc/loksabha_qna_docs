                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

78






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2022






Kendriya Vidyalayas




D. M. Kathir Anand














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the steps taken by the Government to increase the number of Kendriya Vidyalayas (KVs) in the country; (b) whether the Government has any plans to establish at least one KV in every Lok Sabha Constituency where there is no KV at present; (c) if so, the details thereof; and (d) the total number of students provided admission in KVs through the discretionary quota of the Hon’ble Minister from 2015-16 to 2021-22, State/Region-wise and year-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) to (d) A statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (d) OF THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 78 RAISED BY SHRI D.M. KATHIR ANAND, HON’BLE MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT TO BE ANSWERED ON 07/02/2022 REGARDING KENDRIYA VIDYALAYAS. (a) to (c) Opening of new Kendriya Vidyalayas (KVs) is a continuous process. KVs are opened primarily to cater to the educational needs of the wards of transferable Central Government Employees including Defence & Para-Military personnel, Central Autonomous Bodies, Central Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs) and Central Institute of Higher Learning (IHL) by providing a common programme of education. Proposals for opening of new KVs are considered only if sponsored by Ministries or Departments of the Government of India / State Governments / Union Territories (UTs) Administrations and committing resources for setting up a new KV. The proposals received from various sponsoring authorities found fulfilling the mandatory pre-requisites for opening of new KVs have to compete with other such proposals under the “Challenge Method” and subject to approval of the competent authority.  KVs are not opened on the criteria of Parliamentary Constituency wise.  (d) The Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan region-wise and year-wise details of admissions made to KVs across the country, including various discretionary quota of Hon’ble Minister’s discretionary quota and special dispensation quota of Hon’ble Members of Parliament, etc., from the year 2015-16 to 2021-22 are annexed. ******
