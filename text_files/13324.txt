                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2227






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.03.2020






Chinese Products in Indian Market




M.K. Vishnu Prasad














                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether huge influx of Chinese products in the Indian market has affected the domestic manufacturers;(b) 	if so, the details thereof; and(c) 	the details of the corrective steps taken/ being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ???????? ??? ?????? ?????? (???? ????? ????) THE MINISTER OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI  PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) & (b):	Total merchandise imports from China were USD 76.3 billion in     2017-18, which decreased to USD 70.3 billion in 2018-19. Total imports from China during 2019-20 (April-January) were USD 57.9 billion. (c):			The following remedial steps have been taken in this regard:i.	In products where there has been injury to domestic industry, relief has been provided through anti-dumping measures on 89 products, countervailing measures on 6 products and safeguard measures for 1 product imposed against China. Details of these trade remedial measures in force against import from China are placed in Annex I.ii.	Basic Custom Duty has been increased on several products to protect domestic industry, including on toys, furniture and footwear items in the Union Budget 2020-21. iii.	Quality control orders (QCOs) are being enforced to check sub-standard imports. QCOs have been notified by DPIIT for items like Room Air Conditioners- Unitary & Split; Hermetic Compressors; Thermostats for Use in Refrigerators, Air Conditioners, Water Coolers and Beverage Coolers; Transparent Float Glass; Domestic Gas Stoves for use with LPG, etc. iv.	The Government through its initiatives such as Make in India, Ease of Doing Business, Startup India and sectoral schemes/programmes promotes investment in domestic manufacturing and endeavours to improve the competitiveness of domestic industry. v.	Government has also announced Phased Manufacturing Programme in Electronics, Mobiles, etc. in Union Budget 2020-21 to promote domestic manufacturing. *****
