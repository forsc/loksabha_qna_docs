                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

905






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2022






CTET Exam in Maithali Language




 Gopal Jee Thakur














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET) is held in twenty Indian languages and 	if so, the details thereof;(b) whether Maithili language, which is world’s sweetest and classical language and included both in the Eighth Schedule to the Constitution and in the syllabus of Central Board of Secondary Education, is likely to be included in CTET; and(c) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EDUCATION(DR. SUBHAS SARKAR)(a) to (c):The National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE) has circulated guidelines in accordance with the provisions of Para 8 for conducting Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) under Right of Education of Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009 subject to the condition that the Question Paper shall be bilingual (i) in languages as decided by the appropriate Government; and (ii) English Language.The Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET) is conducted by Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) in 20 languages viz., (i) English, (ii) Hindi, (iii) Assamese, (iv) Bengali , (v) Garo, (vi) Gujarati, (vii) Kannada, (viii) Khasi, (ix) Malayalam, (x) Manipuri, (xi)  Marathi, (xii) Mizo, (xiii) Nepali, (xiv) Oriya, (xv) Punjabi, (xvi) Sanskrit, (xvii) Tamil, (xviii)  Telugu, (xix) Tibetan, (xx) Urdu, since its first examination held on 01.04.2011. Advisory and implementation Committee of CTET in its 4th meeting held on 24.04.2014 decided that "Maithili language is not used as the medium of instruction for class I-VIII and is also a sub language of Hindi, the same has not therefore, been given as language option for the CTET examination." *****
