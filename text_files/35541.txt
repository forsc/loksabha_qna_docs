                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2942






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.03.2022






Conservation of Lakes and Ponds




Saikia Dilip













                                                    Ramesh Chander Kaushik
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of ongoing schemes of the Government for conservation and rejuvenation of natural ponds and lakes in the North Eastern States including Assam and other States of the country including Haryana; (b)	the details of funds allocated for the said schemes during the last three years; (c)	whether the said funds have been utilized within time limit; (d)	if so, the details thereof; and (e)	if not, whether the Government has fixed any accountability in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a)	Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change (MoEF&CC) is currently implementing a centrally sponsored scheme namely, National Plan for Conservation of Aquatic Eco-systems (NPCA) for conservation and management of wetlands (includes natural ponds) in the country including North Eastern States, Assam and Haryana, on cost sharing basis between Central Government and respective State Governments/UT Administrations. The scheme of Surface Minor Irrigation (SMI) and Repair, Renovation and Restoration (RRR) of water bodies is a part of Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yaojana (Har Khet Ko Pani) which is supported by Ministry of Jal Shakti by providing Central Assistance to States for creation and restoration of Irrigation Potential. (b) to (d) The details of funds released as central share for NPCA scheme during last three years is given at Annexure-1 and the details of Central Assistance (CA) released by Ministry of Jal Shakti during last three years for SMI and RRR of water bodies schemes are given at Annexure-2. Further, under the schemes the central share is released only after full utilisation of the previous grant given to the States/UTs. (e)	No Sir, does not arise.  ***
