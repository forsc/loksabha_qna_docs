                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

29






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.11.2019






Losses Due to Heavy Rains and Floods




Bidyut Baran Mahato













                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether many States in the country were affected by heavy rains and floods in the recent months and if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise;(b) the number of persons killed, injured and missing, the extent of loss of cattle and the details of damage to property including dams, bridges, roads, standing crops and high value horticulture crops reported, State/UT-wise;(c) the details of proposals received from the State Governments to compensate the said losses and the assistance sanctioned and released by the Union Government along with the criteria adopted for sanction of assistance, State/UT-wise;(d) whether the Government has sent Central Teams to the affected States toevaluate and ascertain the extent of crop and other losses, if so, the details and the outcome of assessment by the CentralTeams;(e) the details of NDRF teams deployed in the affected States along with the number of persons rescued, State/UT-wise; and(f) the measures taken to evolve a permanent mechanism to deal with such calamities along with the steps taken/being taken by the Government for providing training and scientific knowledge to the farmers to minimize such crop losses due to heavy rains and floods in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) to (f):  	   A statement is laid on the Table of the House. --2--STATEMENT IN REPLY TO THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *29 FOR 19.11.2019(a) and (b):  During the South-west Monsoon, 2019, a number of States/UTs are reported to have been affected by floods. As per information received from the State Governments/ UTs, 2,391 human lives and 15729 cattle were lost, 8,00,067 houses were damaged and 63.975 lakh hectares of cropped areas were affected due to floods/cloudburst/landslide during south-west monsoon, 2019. Details of the losses due to floods/ landslides/ cloudburst and other Hydro-meteorological hazards , as reported by the State Governments/ Union Territories during the south-west monsoon 2019 are at Annexure-I. (c) and (d): Primary responsibility for disaster management rests with the State Governments. Central Government extends all possible logistics and financial support to the States to supplement their efforts to meet the situation effectively. The State Governments undertake assessment of damages and provide financial relief in the wake of natural disasters including floods, from the State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) already placed at their disposal.  Additional financial assistance is extended from the National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF) as per the laid down procedure, which includes an assessment based on the visit of an Inter-Ministerial Central Team (IMCT).  Following the recent floods and rain oriented calamities, the Government of India   has   taken  a  significant  decision  on  19.08.2019   that  the  IMCT   will --3--LS.S.Q.No. 29 for 19.11.2019henceforth be constituted immediately in the aftermath of any natural calamity of severe nature, before receipt of memorandum, which will visit the affected areas of the States so as to have the first hand assessment of the damages caused and the relief work carried out by the State Administration.	Accordingly, Ministry of Home Affairs has constituted IMCTs for 14 States viz. Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, Bihar, Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Kerala, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh & Uttar Pradesh. IMCTs have visited the affected areas of 13 States for on-the-spot assessment of damages due to floods, 2019. The Government of Gujarat has informed that it does not intend to submit a memorandum for seeking assistance from National Disaster Response Fund. Further, only 11 State Governments have so far submitted their Memorandum seeking additional financial assistance from NDRF. Further, additional financial assistance under NDRF to the States will be considered as per the laid down procedure.    (e):	During the floods, peak deployment of National Disaster Response Force was 176 teams (on 08.08.2019). National Disaster Response Force has rescued/evacuated 98962 persons and rescued 617 livestock and provided medical assistance to 23,869 persons in the country.  State-wise details of deployment of National Disaster Response Force and their floods operations during Monsoon Season, 2019 is at Annexure-II.  --4--LS.S.Q.No. 29 for 19.11.2019(f):	Based on the forecast of weather and Monsoon the appropriate cropping pattern is recommended for the benefits of the farmers. Indian Council of Agriculture Research - Central Research Institute of Dryland Agriculture Hyderabad in collaboration with State Agriculture Universities prepares crop contingency plan for all problematic districts in the country to sustain agriculture production in the eventuality of any sudden change in weather. Based on the Crop Weather Watch Report advisories are issued to the States/ Union Territories for implementation of contingency plans. Besides there is also a system to send weather based advisory to registered farmers by SMS through m-kisan portal.******
