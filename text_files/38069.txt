                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4673






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

31.03.2022






Promotion of Electric Vehicles




Rodmal Nagar













                                                    Queen Oja
                                                








                                                    Bharatiben Dhirubhai Shyal
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to make use of Electric Vehicles (EVs) mandatory in all Government and Non-Government offices in the country so as to inspire other people; (b) if so, the time frame set for the purpose; and (c) the details of discount to be offered by the Government on the purchase of EVs?






ANSWER




                                                         THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS (SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)      (a) and (b) No sir, there is no such proposal. (c) Following steps have been taken by the Government for adoption of electric vehicles in the country: -1.	The Ministry of Heavy Industries formulated Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of (Hybrid &) Electric Vehicles in India (FAME India) Scheme in 2015 to promote adoption of Electric/Hybrid Vehicles. At present, Phase-II of FAME India Scheme is being implemented for a period of 5 years w.e.f 01.04.2019 with budgetary support of Rs. 10,000 crores.2.	GST on Electric Vehicles has been reduced from 12% to 5%. GST on chargers/charging stations has been reduced from 18% to 5%.3.	The Ministry of Road Transport and Highways vide S.O. 5333(E) dated 18.10.2018 exempted battery operated transport vehicles from the requirement of permits.4.	The Ministry of Road Transport and Highways,  vide GSR 525(E) dated 2nd August, 2021 has exempted Battery Operated Vehicles from the payment of fees for the purpose of  issue or renewal of registration certificate and assignment of new registration mark.5.	Ministry of Road Transport and Highways has issued advisory vide letter dated 12.08.2020 clarifying  that vehicles without batteries can be sold and registered based on type approval certificate issued by test agency. *****
