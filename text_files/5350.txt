                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4957






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2019






Setting up of SFAC




Hemant Tukaram Godse














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 whether the Government has set up  Small  Farmers’  Agri-Business Consortium  (SFAC)  in  various States of the country and if  so, the details thereof including  the  functions  performed  by SFAC;(b)	 the details of the projects/schemes being implemented, funds sanctioned and physical  achievements/progress  made  so far under the SFAC, State-wise;(c)	  whether  the SFAC has not been able to make much impact on the agriculture sector and if so, the reasons therefor;(d)	  whether  the  Government  has reviewed the functioning of SFAC to make it more effective; and(e)	 if so, the steps being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER  OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a): 	Yes Sir. The Government established Small Farmers’ Agri-Business Consortium (SFAC) as a Society in 1994 to facilitate agri-business ventures by catalyzing private investment through Venture Capital Assistance (VCA) Scheme in close association with financial institutions. SFAC has facilitated development of State level SFACs in 21 States by way of one time corpus support as matching contribution.  List of State level SFAC’s is at Annexure-I.   The main functions of SFAC are: (i) Promotion of development of small agribusiness through VCA scheme; (ii) Helping formation and growth of Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) / Farmer Producer Companies (FPCs); (iii) Improving availability of working capital and development of business activities of FPOs/FPCs through Equity Grant and Credit Guarantee Fund Scheme; (iv) Implementation of National Agriculture Market (e-NAM)  Scheme.(b):	 At present, SFAC is implementing the following schemes: i.	SFAC promotes development of small agribusiness through its Venture Capital Assistance (VCA) Scheme for value added processing and marketing linkages. The State-wise progress of VCA Scheme is at Annexure-II.Contd..2/--2--2-ii.	SFAC offers Schemes like Equity Grant and Credit Guarantee Fund Scheme (EGCGFS) for FPCs to improve availability of working capital and development of business activities. The State-wise progress of EGCGFS is at Annexure-III.iii.	SFAC is also the implementing agency for the National Agriculture Market (e-NAM) Scheme. The objective is to develop transparent, competitive online bidding system to facilitate farmers with remunerative prices. The Government has released an amount of Rs. 459.44 crore for development, operationalisation and maintenance of e-NAM scheme. The State-wise number of mandi integrated under e-NAM is at Annexure-IV. 			 So far, more than 1.64 crore farmers & more than 1.24 Lakhs traders have been registered on e-NAM platform.  Further trade transactions having total volume of 2,61 crore MT with total value of Rs.72,128 crore, have been recorded on e-NAM platform. iv.  SFAC was mandated by Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, Govt. of India, to support the State Governments in the formation of Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs). FPO projects are taken up by State Governments under general Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana – Remunerative Approaches for Agriculture and Allied Sectors Rejuvenation (RKVY-RAFTAAR) funds as well as under the National Demonstration Project under the National Food Security Mission (NFSM) and Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH).    So far, SFAC has helped 818 FPOs in registering as companies.  State wise list of FPOs promoted by SFAC is  at Annexure –V (c):      SFAC has emerged as a developmental Institution with its core aim and objective focused on increased production and productivity, value addition, provision of efficient linkages between producers and consumers. SFAC commissioned an independent Mid-term appraisal of the VCA Scheme for XII Plan through NABARD Consultancy Services (NABCONS). NABCONS has found the Scheme to be highly effective and significant. The Scheme has resulted in strengthening the forward linkages for farmers, and in achieving efficiency and higher price realization for farmers in the agriculture value chain.SFAC also commissioned an independent Evaluation Study of the EGCGFS for FPCs through NABCONS. NABCONS has found that the EGCGFS for FPCs has been useful to the FPCs in improving their business activities.(d) & (e):    The Government has reviewed the functioning of SFAC from time to time. Based on the review the annual action plans are formulated and associated budgets are released.
