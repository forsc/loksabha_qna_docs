                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3397






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.07.2019






Schemes for Empowerment of Fisherwomen




H. Vasanthakumar














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government  has launched any new scheme during the last three years with special focus on development of fisherwomen and their children;(b)	if so, the details thereof; and(c)	the financial allocations made under these new schemes, State/UT-wise particularly for Tamil Nadu and Kanyakumari district?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVLOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a)	to (c): As per the information given by Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry & Dairying, Government of India is implementing the umbrella Centrally Sponsored Scheme on “Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries” launched during 2015-16, with the following components:i.	Development of Inland Fisheries and Aquaculture,ii.	Development of Marine Fisheries, Infrastructure and Post-Harvest Operations,iii.	National Scheme on Welfare of Fishermen iv.	Monitoring, Control and Surveillance and other need-based Interventions (MCS),v.	Institutional Arrangement for Fisheries Sector,vi.	Strengthening of Database & Geographical Information System of the Fisheries Sector (SoDGIS),vii.	National Fisheries Development Board and its activities(NFDB)The eligible beneficiaries for aforesaid scheme are Fishers, Fish farmers, Entrepreneurs, Cooperatives, SHGs, any other private recipients etc. including women beneficiaries.  Under the Blue Revolution scheme the financial assistance is being provided for women beneficiaries to the tune of the 60% of the unit cost in respect of the all the beneficiary oriented components.
