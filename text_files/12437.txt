                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1247






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2020






CTET




Deepak Baij














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a): whether there has been a steep rise in the registration fees for candidatesappearing for 14th edition of Central Teaching Eligibility Test (CTET) conductedby the Central Board of Secondary Education;(b) if so, the details thereof along with the fees and the amount of funds receivedunder the registration head during the last five years;(c): the head-wise details of expenditure of the said funds received;(d): whether exorbitant fees for examinations hampers the opportunities forunemployed persons; and(e): whether the Government is likely to consider framing a policy wherein onlyapplicants who get employment/selected from these exams will have to pay thefees?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (c): The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) has informed that theCentral Teaching Eligibility Test (CTET) Examination has two papers to decide eligibilityto become a teacher i.e. One paper is to teach classes I to V, and the other is forteaching VI to VII. Fee for taking both exams by a candidate is Rs 1200, and there is nochange in this fee. 40% of the candidates appear for both the papers, one after theother on the same day. However, if a candidate wants to appear in only one of thepapers, the fees has been increased as follows:<pre>(i) from Rs.700 to Rs.1000/- (i.e., an increase of Rs.300/-) for UR/OBCcandidates; and(ii) from Rs.350/- to Rs.500/- (i.e., an increase of Rs.150/-) for the SC/STcandidates.</pre>The details of the fees collected and the expenditure made during the last fiveyears is Annexed. The income and expenditure shown in the statement also includesincome from other exams conducted by the unit that conducts CTET exams, upto FY2018-19. The Board prepares its annual income and Expenditure statement onfinancial year basis, i.e., from 1st April to 31st March. The fees are received in onefinancial year for a particular Examination, whereas the expenditure is incurred in nextfinancial year.(d): CBSE is a self-financing organization and meets all its expenditure and liabilitiesfrom funds collected through examination fees received from the candidates. TheBoard does not get any budgetary support from the Consolidated Fund of India and italso does not have any other source of income.(e): The Board is merely the examining body for conduct of Teacher Eligibility Test(TET) on behalf of the Central Government under Right to Education (RTE) Act. TheExam is conducted as per the guidelines framed by the National Council for TeacherEducation (NCTE) for conduct of Teacher Eligibility Test (TET).*****
