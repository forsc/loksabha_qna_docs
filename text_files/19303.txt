                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MINORITY AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

671






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2021






Educational Empowerment of Muslim Girls




Gaddam Ranjith Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





MINORITY AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of schemes being implemented for educational empowerment of   Muslim  girls;(b) the status of implementation of each of the above scheme in Telangana  during  each of the last three years and  the current year, year  and   district-wise;(c)  the details of physical targets set and achieved during the above period with          a  particular  reference to Telangana; (d) whether hostels for Muslim girls are also being proposed  by  the         Government; and (e)  if so, the details thereof with a particular reference to Telangana?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF MINORITY AFFAIRS (SHRI MUKHTAR ABBAS NAQVI) (a) : The Ministry of Minority Affairs is implementing  following schemes for the educational empowerment of students/beneficiaries including girls belonging to six notified minority communities i.e. Muslim, Christian, Sikh, Buddhist, Jain, Zoroastrian (Parsi) in all States across the country including Telangana:      (i)         Pre-matric Scholarship Scheme- The scholarship is provided to minority students studying in class I to X in Government/ recognized Private schools. To be eligible, students should not have annual parental/guardian’s income more than Rs.1.00 lakh and he/she should have secured minimum 50% marks in previous class. Minimum 30% scholarships are earmarked for girls.      (ii)         Post-matric Scholarship Scheme- The scholarship is provided to minority students studying in Class XI to Ph.D level in Government/ recognized Private schools/Colleges/Institutes. To be eligible, students should not have annual parental/guardian’s income more than Rs.2.00 lakh and he/she should have secured minimum 50% marks in previous class. Minimum 30% scholarships are earmarked for girls.                 (iii)       Merit-cum-Mean based Scholarship Scheme - The scholarship is awarded for pursuing professional and technical courses at under-graduate and post-graduate levels, in institutions recognized by the appropriate authority. 85 institutes for professional and technical courses have been listed in the scheme; for which full course fee is reimbursed. To be eligible, students should not have annual parental/guardian’s income more than Rs.2.50 lakh and he/she should have secured minimum 50% marks in previous class. Minimum 30% scholarships are earmarked for girls.                (iv)       Maulana Azad National Fellowship (MANF) Scheme- The Fellowships are awarded to research students pursuing regular and full time M. Phil and Ph.D courses. The Scheme covers all Universities/Institutions recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) under section 2(f) and section 3 of the UGC Act and is implemented through UGC. To be eligible, students should not have annual parental/guardian’s income more than Rs.6.00 lakh. Under the scheme, 30% fellowships are earmarked for girl students.               (v)     Padho Pardesh - Scheme of Interest Subsidy on educational loans for overseas studies- Under the scheme, the interest subsidy is awarded to students on the interest payable for the period of moratorium for the Education Loans taken for overseas studies to pursue approved courses of studies abroad at Masters, M.Phil or Ph.D levels. The scheme is implemented through the Canara Bank. To be eligible, students should not have annual parental/guardian’s income more than Rs.6.00 lakh. 35% scholarships are earmarked for girls.              (vi)    Begum Hazrat Mahal National Scholarship- Maulana Azad Education Foundation (MAEF) under M/o Minority Affairs also implements  Begum Hazrat Mahal National Scholarship scheme for meritorious minority girls studying in classes 9th to 12th.(b) & (c): The details of physical targets and achievement under the above mentioned scheme in the state of Telangana during the last three years are given in Annexure-I. District-wise achievement under Begum Hazrat Mahal National Scholarship scheme is given at Annexure-II. However, district-wise data is not collated for the other schemes. (d) & (e): The Ministry has implemented Pradhan Mantri Jan Vikas Karyakram (PMJVK) in 1300 identified Minority Concentration Areas (MCAs) of the country with the objective of developing socio-economic assets and basic amenities for improving quality of life of the people living in these areas .  Priority Sectors under the scheme are education, health, skill development and women centric projects.  The projects approved are community assets and the facilities created are available to the entire population living in the catchment area including Muslim Girls. Under PMJVK, projects are submitted by the State as per their felt need and infrastructure requirement in the identified MCA. The programme being demand driven, no physical target are set.    During the last three years and current year, projects of Rs. 6514.25 crore having central share of Rs. 4630.94 crore under PMJVK, have been approved in education sector. The projects include  133- Residential Schools, 22 - Degree Colleges, 298– Student Hostels, 6 - Navodayas Vidyalayas,  9251 - Additional Classrooms (ACR),  350- ACR blocks, 674 - Sanitation Projects in Schools, 8323- Smart Classes and Teaching Aids, etc. The above includes education projects approved in the State of Telangana also, which are 8 hostels (04 for girls and 04 for boys), 47 Residential Schools (26 for girls and 21 for boys) and 4 School Buildings (02 for girls and 02 for boys).  The total cost of these projects in Telangana are Rs.976.20 crores having central share of Rs.585.72 crores.Details of the projects approved under the programme are available at http://minorityaffairs.gov.in/en/pradhan-mantri-jan-vikas-karyakram-pmjvk-0..*****
