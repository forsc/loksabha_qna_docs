                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

780






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Migratory Birds




Rahul Kaswan














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of various migratory birds coming in the country particularly during winter season;(b)	whether it is a fact that the number of such migratory birds have come down over the decades;(c)	if so, the details thereof along with the reasons therefor; and(d)	the steps taken by the Government to protect such migratory birds along with the funds allocated/ utilized for the said purpose during each of the last three years and current year in Rajasthan, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)		India is a key habitat for a large number of migratory birds. According to literature published by experts and scientific institutions, about 460 species of migratory birds visit India, out of which 175 species undertake long distance migration using the Central Asian Flyway (CAF). The birds which use CAF include Egyptian Vultures, Plovers, Ducks, Storks, Ibises, Flamingoes, Pochards, Sociable Lapwing, European Roller, Eurasian Golden Oriole, etc. (b)	and (c)	There are no specific reports indicating that the number of migratory birds have come down over the decades.(d )	The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change provides financial assistance to the States/Union Territories including Rajasthan for protection of wildlife, including migratory birds and their habitats, under the Centrally Sponsored Schemes –Development of Wildlife Habitat, Project Elephant and Project Tiger. Details of financial assistance provided to States/Union Territories under the Scheme during the last three years and current year are at Annexure-1, Annexure-II and Annexure-III respectively.Other important initiatives taken by Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change for conservation of migratory birds include:i.	Several important habitats of migratory birds have been declared as Protected Areas. ii.	Rare and endangered migratory species of birds found in India, like Black Necked Crane, Great Indian Bustard, Bar Headed Geese, Himalayan Griffon, SarusCranes, HoubaraBustard etc. have been listed in Schedule-I of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, thereby providing them highest degree of protection.iii.	A Central Asian Flyway National Action Plan (2018-2023) was launched in 2018 with the goal of conserving migratory birds and their habitats.iv.	Notification of Eco-Sensitive Zones (ESZs) around many National Parks and Sanctuaries under the provisions of the Environment(Protection) Act, 1986 to further strengthen conservation of wildlife including migratory birds.v.	The Ministry has issued guidelines for mitigation of impacts of linear infrastructure on wildlife, including migratory birds.vi.	India is a signatory to the Convention on Migratory Species (CMS) and has also signed Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) on conservation of species including Siberian Cranes, Raptorsetc.vii.	The Ministry has also notified the Wetlands (Conservation and Management) Rules, 2017 for conservation of all wetlands in the country which are also the resting places /breeding grounds of a large number of migratory birds.***
