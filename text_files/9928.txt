                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3267






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana




Gaurav Gogoi














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)whether the Government has noted an increase in jobs and employment due to loans availed under the Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY); (b)if so, the details thereof;(c)whether the increase in jobs has been as high as expected and if not, the reasons therefor; (d)whether the expert committee to identify generation on non-farm economic activities under PMMY has finalised its report; (e)whether it is true that the Government has not been able to meet requisite expectations under the generating non-farm activities; and (f)if so, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (IC) FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI SANTOSH KUMAR GANGWAR)(a) to (f):  	Under the Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY), 20.84 crore loan accounts totalling an amount of Rs 10.24 lac crore have been sanctioned upto 01.11.2019. As per the findings of  the PMMY survey conducted by Labour Bureau, an attached office of the Ministry of Labour & Employment, around 5 crore persons (3.1 crore self-employed and 1.95 crore  hired workers) were working in establishments for which MUDRA  loans were availed between April, 2015 to March, 2018. The findings of the survey reveal that these loans enabled beneficiaries to sustain the economic activities they were engaged in. *****
