                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1934






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.07.2021






Pulse Polio Programme




Su Thirunavukkarasar














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a)	the details of the funds allocated, released and utilised for the polio vaccination programme along with the number of polio cases detected during the last three years, State/UT-wise;(b)	whether the Government has directed the States/UTs to defer polio immunization programme with the outbreak of pandemic in the country; (c)	if so, the details thereof along with its impact on vaccination drive / polio eradication in the country;(d)	whether the Government has recently re-launched the nationwide pulse polio programme; and (e)	if so, the details thereof along with the number of children benefited by this programme, State/UT-wise including Tamil Nadu?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR.BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)a)  The State/UT-wise details of the funds allocated, released and utilised for the polio vaccination programme are placed at Annexure ‘A’. No polio case was detected during the last three years in the country.(b) to (e) The National Immunization Day (NID) was rescheduled from 17th January 2021 to 31st January 2021in view of the massive countrywide COVID-19 vaccination drive roll out from 16th January 2021. The NID thereafter was conducted successfully on 31st January 2021 and more than 15.9 crore children were vaccinated. Hence,there was no adverse impact due to rescheduling of NID. The number of children benefited by this programme, State/UT-wise including Tamil Nadu is placed at Annexure ‘B’.
