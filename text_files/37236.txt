                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4400






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






Digital Village Project in Andhra Pradesh




Magunta Sreenivasulu Reddy













                                                    Reddeppa Nallakonda Gari
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY be pleased to state:(a)	whether the Government is aware that Digital Village Project envisaged to digitize the Gram Panchayats (GPs) from each district has expired in October, 2021;(b) 	if so, whether all the identified 700 GPs have been made completely digital;(c) 	if so, the details thereof with a particular reference to the State of Andhra Pradesh;(d) 	whether the Government is planning to replicate aforesaid pilot project in other GPs of the country and if so, the details thereof; and (e) 	whether any GPs have been digitized under CSC e-Governance Service and if so, the details thereof, with a particular reference to the State of Andhra Pradesh?






ANSWER




                                                        (a), (b) and (c): Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), Government of India (GoI) initiated the Digital Village Project on pilot scale. The pilot project covers 700 villages (one village from each district from each State/UT). The project was initiated on 31st October, 2018 for period of 3 years (i.eupto October, 2021). The Government has granted an extension to the project for a further period of one (1) year i.e. upto October, 2022. CSC e-Governance Service India Limited (CSC SPV) is implementing the project. The list of the 700 villages may be seen at https://docs.google.com/document/d/1ODney0gMJHrhaO57izoOIHymwXxY9_et/edit?usp=sharing&ouid=114104362247024536597&rtpof=true&sd=true. The following services are being offered under the project in all 700 villages:•	Education Service: Courses offered under this project are as - Basic Computer Course (BCC), Course on Computer Concept (CCC), Tally – KaushalPramanPatra•	Health Services – Tele-Health & Tele-Veterinary consultation •	Financial Inclusion- Financial inclusion awareness programme  •	Solar panel powered street lights (8 Solar lights per village)•	Skill Development - skill development in - Automotive Technician, Handset Repairing, Field Technician- Home Appliances, and Electrical Technician.•	Government to Citizen Services (G2C) - online registration for professional entrance examinations, Pan Card Services, new Aadhaar enrolment, its updation/modification, online registration for service selection board, online registration for public service commission, etc.•	B2C Services – Mobile / DTH recharge, ticket booking (Rail/Air), etc. One village from each of the 13 districts of the State of Andhra Pradesh are being covered under the project, the details of the villages are at Annexure-I.(d):	Currently, the pilot project is under implementation and no decision has been taken to cover additional villages as the pilot phase of the Digital Village project is still under implementation.(e):	CSC e-Governance Service India limited (CSC SPV) is offering citizen services through the digital medium in 4619 villages including 44 villages of Andhra Pradesh under its own initiative through funds sourced from Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR).
