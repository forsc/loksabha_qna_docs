                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1377






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.02.2021






Crop-residue Management Machines




Manickam Tagore B













                                                    Kani K. Navas
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the total number and types of machines identified for delivery to farmers in 2019 and 2020, State-wise; (b)	the details of the total number of crop-residue management machines sanctioned by the Ministry in 2019 and 2020, machine-wise and State-wise; (c)	the details of the total number and type of machines delivered to farmers in 2019 and 2020, Statewise; and (d)	the reasons for discrepancy, if any, between the planned amount and the delivered amounts for each year, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????            ( SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)  (a) to (d):	The Central Sector Scheme on ‘Promotion of Agricultural Mechanization for In-Situ Management of Crop Residue in the States of Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh and NCT of Delhi’ is being implemented through the State Governments. Based on the Annual Action Plans submitted  by the States at the beginning of each financial year, the funds are released to the State Governments for extending financial support @ 50% of the cost for purchase of machines by the individual farmers and @80% of the project cost to the Co-operative Societies of farmers, Farmers Producer Organizations (FPOs), Registered Farmers Societies and Panchayats for establishment of Custom Hiring Centres (CHCs) of crop residue management machinery identified for promotion under the scheme. Based on the information furnished by the State Governments, the details areenclosed in Annexure-I	The variation in number of machines targeted and  actual delivery is due to the reasons that machines have been delivered as per actual demand of the farmers & CHCs as per their choice of machine in the season.
