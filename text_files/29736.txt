                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1391






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.12.2021






Facilities for Differently-abled Persons




Sunita Duggal














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has any plan to provide barrier-free environment to People with Disabilities (PwD);(b) If so, the details thereof;(c) The common guidelines for the people of PwD; and(d) The total number of disabled person in India?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SUSHRI PRATIMA BHOUMIK)(a)   The Central Government has enacted the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (RPwD) Act, 2016 which came into force from 19.04.2017. Section 40-46 of the said Act deals with accessibility for Persons with Disabilities (PwDs) including accessibility in public building, access to transport and access to information and communication technology.Relief to the persons with disabilities is a State subject by virtue of entry ‘9’ of the State list of the Constitution of India. However, the Central Government supplements the efforts of the State Government for the welfare of PwDs. The Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities implements various Central Sector Schemes for the welfare of Persons with Disabilities through various implementing agencies including its institutes/ organizations, Voluntary Organizations, State Government throughout the country. The Department implements the following Central Sector Schemes to provide barrier-free environment to PwDs:The Ministry runs a scheme-‘Creation of Barrier Free Environment’, a sub-scheme of the Central Sector Scheme for Implementation of Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016  (SIPDA). Under the scheme, financial assistance is provided for making accessible existing Government buildings across the country through construction of lifts, ramps, railing, accessible toilets, tactile tiles and accessible websites, etc. for the convenience of PwDs. The details of the scheme are available on the website (www.disabilityaffairs.gov.in) of the Department. Further, the Accessible India Campaign (AIC) was launched on 3rd of December 2015 to create barrier free environment for safe, independent and dignified living of PwDs. The Campaign is implemented across three verticals, namely the Built-Up Environment, Transportation Sector and the Information and Communication Technology (ICT) ecosystem. The Ministry provides financial assistance for creation of barrier free environment in selected Government buildings in States and UTs and identified Government websites through the Campaign.(b)   The State/UT-wise funds released in the last three years with number of buildings funded under the scheme for Creation of Barrier Free Environment are available at Annexure-I.  The State/UT-wise details of the status of implementation of target to make selected State/UT Government buildings accessible for the Built-Up Environment component are at Annexure-II. The State/UT-wise details of the status of implementation of target for the Information and Communication Technology Ecosystem Component, to make selected websites accessible are at Annexure-III.(c)   The Department has notified the RPwD Rules 2017 on 15.06.2017. Under Rule 15 of these Rules, accessibility standards with respect to public buildings, passenger bus transport and Government website including documents to be placed on website have been prescribed.(d)    As per Census 2011, there are 2,68,14,994 persons with disabilities (PwDs) in India.****
