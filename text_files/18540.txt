                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2236






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.09.2020






COVID-19 Patients in Delhi




Parvesh Sahib Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the number of COVID-19 patients in Delhi who were/are being treated in hospitals under the Central Government; (b) the steps taken by the Government for ensuring availability of beds to patients and their real time monitoring during the COVID-19 pandemic in such hospitals;(c) the amount spent on the treatment of COVID-19 patients in such hospitals; and(d) whether the Government has taken any measures for the post COVID-19 care of patients and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a)   The number of patients that availed treatment in the three Central Government Hospitals in Delhi namely Safdarjung, Dr. Ram Manohar Lohia and Lady Hardinge Medical College & Associated Hospitals as on 21/09/2020 is as under:Name of Hospital	Number of patients Safdarjung Hospital	2184Lady Hardinge Medical College and Smt. Sucheta Kriplani Hospital	1362Dr. Ram Manohar Lohia Hospital	3458So far as All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), Delhi is concerned, 6622 Covid-19 positive patients have been treated in AIIMS COVID Centers at (i) Jai Prakash Narayan Apex Trauma Centre (JPNATC), Delhi and (ii) National Cancer Institute (NCI), Jhajjar, till 19/09/2020. (b)    As far as the aforementioned Central Government Hospitals are concerned the number of beds available for corona patients is as under:Name of Hospital	Number of bedsSafdarjung Hospital	276Lady Hardinge Medical College and Smt. Sucheta Kriplani Hospital	164Dr. Ram Manohar Lohia Hospital	227All India Institute of Medical Sciences, Delhi (JPNATC & NCI)	1515Daily census of patients and vacancy position of beds are routinely and regularly monitored for ensuring availability of beds to patients during the COVID-19 pandemic in these hospitals.(c)   Hospital funds are being utilised for purchasing medical equipment, drugs and general store items for COVID-19 wards/ICU patient care services. (d)    As far as the above Central Government Hospitals are concerned, post COVID follow up clinics have been started for providing care to discharged patients of COVID ward.
