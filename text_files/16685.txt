                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

359






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.09.2020






Sub Categorisation of SCs




Jagdambika Pal 














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENTLOK SABHAUNSTARRED QUESTION NO.359TO BE ANSWERED ON 15.09.2020SUB CATEGORISATION OF SCS †359. SHRI JAGDAMBIKA PAL: Will the Minister of SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT be pleased to state: (a) whether the Constitutional Bench of Supreme Court has directed to review its decision of 2004 (EV Chinnaiah vs State of Andhra Pradesh); (b) if so, the details thereof; (c) whether the States have been directed to sub-categorise it further; and (d) if so, the policy thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)   (a) & (b): A Constitution Bench of Supreme Court in its order dated 27.08.2020 in Civil Appeal No. 2317 of 2011 filed by the State of Punjab against Davinder Singh and Ors. has opined that its decision dated 05.11.2004  in E.V. Chinnaiah Vs. State of Andhra Pradesh (2005) 1 SCC 394) is required to be revisited by a Bench of Seven Judges or more as considered appropriate.(c) & (d): No, Sir. As of now decision of Supreme Court in E.V. Chinnaiah''s case holds the field.*****
