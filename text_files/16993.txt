                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    DEFENCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

580






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.09.2020






Illegal Occupation of land in Cantonment Region




Rajendra Agrawal














                        Will the Minister of





DEFENCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether any case of illegal occupation of land of residents of cantonment region have come to light;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether such encroachments have been causing serious operational problems, and if so, the details thereof; and(d) the steps taken by the Government to redress such problems and subsequent results of such steps, if any?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE					(SHRI SHRIPAD NAIK)IN THE MINISTRY OF DEFENCE   (a) & (b): Yes, Sir.  During the year 2018, 2019 and in 2020 (upto 31.08.2020), 7.586 acres of defence land inside cantonment under the management of Defence Estate Organization have been detected as encroached by the residents.  Out of above, defence land measuring 4.11 acres have got freed from encroachment during the same period.(c)  The illegal occupation of defence land may cause difficulties in Municipal administration viz. Providing water supply, sanitation and other municipal facilities.(d) The following steps have been taken to address the problem of encroachment:(i) Respective user Services who manage defence land are responsible for its protection, removal of encroachments and planning for its land use.(ii) Strengthening of Defence land management by way of computerisation and digitisation of land records, Survey, demarcation and verification of defence lands and land Audit.(iii)  Regular inspection of sites under respective jurisdiction is conducted by the CEO/DEO/Station Commander as the case may be.(iv)  Issue of detailed instructions by the Government emphasising the need for ensuring vigilance, detection and prevention of new encroachments.(v)  Court cases are being pursued to evict encroachers.(vi)  Close liaison is being maintained with revenue and civil police authorities to detect attempt to encroach upon the defence land by encroachers.(vii)  Regular patrolling is being carried out to safeguard the lands from encroachers and unauthorised construction.(viii)  Removal of encroachments on defence land under the provisions of Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971 as well as under the Cantonment Act, 2006.*****
