                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

622






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2022






Standardization of AYUSH Education




Goddeti Madhavi













                                                     Chinta Anuradha
                                                








                                                    Geetha Viswanath Vanga
                                                








                                                     Chandra Sekhar Bellana
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the Government has undertaken steps towards standardization of academic courses and improving the quality of AYUSH education in the country;(b)	if so, the details thereof; and (c)	the steps taken by the Government towards standardization of quality of AYUSH healthcare services, quality control of AYUSH drugs and strengthening of drug testing laboratories?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AYUSH(SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a)	Yes, the Government has undertaken steps towards standardization of academic courses and improving the quality of AYUSH education in the country.(b)	National commission for Indian system of medicine (NCISM) and National commission for Homeopathy (NCH) have been constituted to improve the quality of education, through regulatory provision & streamlining of registration, ethical practice throughout the country. NCISM & NCH are coming up with new modules of Education systems in AYUSH to standardize the academic courses and to develop competency based dynamic curriculum.Details of Measures taken by Ministry of Ayush to bring transparency & improve quality of AYUSH Education in the country is attached as Annexure-I(c)	Ministry of Ayush is working towards quality control and standardization of Ayush drugs and strengthening of drug testing laboratories. Ministry of Ayush along with Quality Control of India (QCI) provides voluntary certificate scheme for Ayush products in two levels namely Ayush Standard Mark and Ayush Premium Mark.Regarding Quality Control of Ayurveda, Siddha, Unani & Homoeopathy (ASU&H ) drugs Government has been enforced chapter IV-A of the Drugs and Cosmetics Act 1940 and Rules 1945. It is mandatory for the manufacturer of ASU&H drugs to obtain licence from the concerned State Licensing Authority (SLA) and comply with the prescribed Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) and quality standards of drugs given in the respective Pharmacopoeias. The SLA grants the licence after verification of the required infrastructural facilities, equipment / machinery, manpower of the manufacturing unit through inspection(s) conducted by the inspector. Ministry of Ayush has setup Pharmacopoeia commission for Indian Medicine & Homeopathy (PCIM&H), at Ghaziabad, U.P. to lay down Pharmacopoeial Standards and Formulary specifications for ASU&H drugs, which serve as official compendia for ascertaining the identity, purity and strength of the drugs included therein. Pharmacopoeia commission for Indian Medicine & Homeopathy also serves as Central drug testing cum appellate laboratory for testing of ASU&H drugs; for ensuring compliance to the prescribed quality standards. The standards of ASU&H medicines are published by the PCIM&H in the country which becomes part of Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 and Rules 1945 for ascertaining the quality standards of raw materials/drugs and implemented uniformly across India. Standards are not developed by the State/UT thus the State/UT wise details are not available. However, the details of Ayurvedic standards published by Government of India is as under:- a)	Ayurvedic Formulary of India (Part I-Ill) containing 986 Formulations, b)	Siddha Formulary of India (Part I-Il) containing 400 Formulations and c)	National Formulary of Unani Medicine 1230 Formulations. List of Pharmacopoeial and Formulary publications is attached as Annexure-II*****
