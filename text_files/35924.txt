                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2557






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Bharatmala Pariyojana




Jaisiddeshwar Shivacharya Mahaswamiji














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the extent to which the Government has achieved the objectives of Bharatmala Pariyojana in the country so far;(b)	whether the Government has conducted any study to ascertain the impact of the said Pariyojana on strengthening of border and coastal road networks in the country;(c)	if so, the details and outcome thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and(d)	the details of the budgetary allocation and utilization under the said Pariyojana since its inception in the country particularly in Maharashtra?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI) (a)	Out of the total length of 34,800 kms including 10,000 kms of residual NHDP projects approved under Bharatmala Pariyojana Phase-I, projects for a length of about 20,411 km have been awarded till now and works of length 8134 Kms have been completed.(b) & (c)	Detailed study of the freight movement between all districts has been conducted along with geo-informatics inputs. Various routes have been identified for development of infrastructure along border and coastal roads to boost export and import trade along with port connectivity routes to improve the efficiency of freight movement to the ports and coastal zones in the country. As such, a total length of about 2,000 km for border and international connectivity roads and approximately 2,000 km of coastal and port connectivity roads have been considered for development under Bharatmala Pariyojana Phase-I.(d)	Till now, projects with financial implication of approximately Rs. 6,18,686 crore for a length of about 20,411 km have been awarded (including projects for length of 1,962 km with cost of Rs. 62,846 crore in the State of Maharashtra).*****
