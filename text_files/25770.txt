                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    DEFENCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1589






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.07.2021






Management of COVID-19




Rita Bahuguna Joshi














                        Will the Minister of





DEFENCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the serving Army Medical Corps personnel mobilized for supporting civil hospitals in the management of COVID-19;(b) the number of retired and reservists mustered for the purpose of managing COVID-19;(c) the response from the retired and reservists personnel in this regard;(d) the general role performed by serving medical corps as well as retired personnel; and(e) the details of the response and support provided by the civil authorities?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE					(SHRI AJAY BHATT)IN THE MINISTRY OF DEFENCE   (a)  The details of manpower deployed for supporting civil hospitals in management of COVID-19 is as follows:	(i)	Medical Officer & Specialists	- 602	(ii)	Nurses				- 224	(iii)	Paramedics			- 1802(b)  236 Short Service Commission officers, who were due for release on completion of period of engagement were granted extension in view of the pandemic.	Also, Government approved a scheme called ‘Tour of Duty’ for recruitment of   Ex-Armed Forces Medical Services (AFMS) Medical Officers and Ex-Military Nursing Service (MNS) officers  released from service since 2017 on contractual basis.(c)  Response to the “Tour of Duty” offered to retired doctors of three Services to re-join and perform duties for COVID relief has been gradual and is entirely based on the dynamic need and severity of COVID Pandemic, which is now on the decline.(d) The Armed Forces Medical Services deployed technical manpower in terms of Physicians, Anaesthestists, Post Graduate Residents, Medical Officers, Hospital Administrators, Nurses and Para Medical Staff on COVID-19 patient care duties.	Further, veterans’ services were utilized for providing tele-consultation on            e-Sanjeevani platform.  Total 100 AFMS veterans were registered on the portal and have provided over 22000 tele-consultations.(e)  The state authorities provided support in terms of services such as security, fire fighting, bio-medical waste management, mortuary services, referral and ambulance services etc.  They also played an important role in ensuring oxygen supply, pre-fabricated hangers for patient housing and all medical equipment necessary for patient care.*****
