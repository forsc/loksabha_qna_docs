                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PANCHAYATI RAJ






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4952






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2019






Monitoring of Work done by the Panchayats




Manoj Rajoria














                        Will the Minister of





PANCHAYATI RAJ


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has been effectively monitoring and supervising the physical progress of works being undertaken by the Panchayats;   (b)  if so, the details thereof along with the number of reported instances of misuse of Government funds;   (c)  whether the Government is able to suitably cater to the needs of the Panchayati Raj institutions; and  (d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF PANCHAYATI RAJ(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)&(b) Yes Sir.High Level Monitoring Committees are constituted by State Governments to monitor and carry out concurrent evaluation of the Panchayats receiving the grants of the Fourteenth Finance Commission (FFC).  Coordination Committee has also been constituted by the Ministry of Panchayati Raj (MoPR) for monitoring the progress of expenditure of the FFC grants by the Panchayats. Monitoring of the physical progress of works is done through an Information Technology application of MoPR; ActionSoft portal/mActionSoft app is being used to upload the geo-tagged photographs of assets created out of FFC funds. Panchayats being a State subject, all reported instances of misuse of Government funds at the Panchayats are being duly forwarded to the respective State Governments for necessary follow up action.(c)& (d)Under FFC  grant,  amount of Rs. 2,00,292.20 crore have been devolved to the Gram Panchayats constituted under Part IX of the Constitution in 26 States for delivering basic service needs including water supply, sanitation including septic management, sewerage and solid waste management, storm water drainage, maintenance of community assets, maintenance of roads, footpaths and street-lighting, and burial and cremation grounds, and any other basic service within the functions assigned to them under relevant legislations.The State-wise status of allocation and release of FFC grants is given at Annexure.******
