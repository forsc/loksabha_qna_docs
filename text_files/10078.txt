                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3633






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2019






Easing Ration Card Process




Topon Kumar Gogoi














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether it is a fact that the Government is planning to ease out the process of getting Ration Card to the people left out of NFSA 13 in Assam; and (b) 	if so, the details thereof, and if not the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                         MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a)to(b):  	The NFSA, 2013 provides for all India coverage of upto 75% of the rural population and up to 50% of the urban population of the country including Assam for receiving highly subsidized foodgrains under Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS), thus covering about two-thirds of the total population.  Identification of beneficiaries under the Act is under two categories- households covered under Antyodaya Anna Yojna (AAY) to the extent and guidelines specified by the Central Government and the remaining households as priority households to be identified by the State/Union Territories (UT) Administration as per criteria evolved by them, within the coverage determined for the State/UT.	The State Government of Assam has informed that the target of selection of beneficiaries fixed for selection/benefited under NFSA,2013 is 2,52,18,001. The selection and inclusion of left-out eligible beneficiaries to achieve the target is a continuous process at District/Sub-Division level as per direction of the State Government by means of deletion of suspected ineligible beneficiaries in the database and inclusion of newly-selected eligible beneficiaries in their place.	Due to undertaking the continuous process of deletion of ineligible beneficiaries in the database and inclusion of newly selected eligible beneficiaries in their place, there are 2,48,96,805 nos. of beneficiaries under NFSA, 2013 in the State of Assam as on 6.12.2019.****
