                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3695






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2020






OCI Card Holders




Jagdambika Pal 














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total number of Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) card-holders globally, country-wise; and(b) the details of the measures being taken for their welfare?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a):      Till March 12, 2020,  35,79,011 Overseas Citizenship of India (OCI) cards have been issued to foreigners.Country- wise list is enclosed at “ANNEXURE”.(b):    Various benefits have been provided to the OCI cardholders by the Central Government through Gazette notifications dated 11th April, 2005, 6th January 2007 and 6th January 2009 issued under section 7B(1) of the Citizenship Act, 1955. These inter-alia include:-	(a)	grant of multiple entry lifelong visa for visiting India for any purpose;	(b)	exemption from registration requirement; --2--L.S.US.Q.NO. 3695 FOR 17.03.2020(c)	parity with Non-Resident Indians in respect of all facilities available to them in economic, financial and educational fields except in matters relating to the acquisition of agricultural or plantation properties;(d)	parity with Non-Resident Indians in the matter of inter-country adoption of Indian children;(e)	parity with resident Indian nationals in the matter of tariffs in airfares in domestic sectors in India, and also for same entry fee as for domestic Indian visitors to visit national parks and wildlife sanctuaries in India;(f)	Parity with non-resident Indians in respect of :-(i)	entry fees to be charged for visiting the national monuments, historical sites and museums in India;(ii)	pursuing the following professions in India, in pursuance of the provisions contained in the relevant Acts, namely: doctors, dentists, nurses and pharmacists; advocates; architects; chartered accountants; (g)	to appear for the All India Pre-Medical Test or such other tests to make them eligible for admission in pursuance of the provisions contained in the relevant Acts.*****
