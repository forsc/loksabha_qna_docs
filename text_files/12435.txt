                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1202






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2020






Opening of New Theatre Schools and National School of Drama




Kirron  Kher














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government plans to open new centres of prestigious theatre schools including National School of Drama in various parts of the country and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; (b) 	the details of the courses and number of seats in each course in the National School of Drama in Delhi and other cities; and (c)	the other steps taken by the Government to promote theatre in regional languages including Punjabi and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (IC) FOR CULTURE & TOURISM(PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)	It was proposed to open five centers of National School of Drama (NSD) in various parts of the country under 12th Plan. However, a decision was taken by the Sub Committee constituted by NSD Society in its meeting held on 03-04-2017 that NSD should not open more centers till the time NSD is made an Institute of National importance.Notwithstanding the above, NSD has opened following centers under its outreach programme:1. 	NSD Centre, Bengaluru – 1year course in Acting (2014)2. 	NSD Centre, Varanasi – 1 year course in Classical Indian Theatre (2018)3. 	NSD Centre, Sikkim – 1 year course in Acting (2012)4. 	NSD Centre, Agartala – 1 year course in Theatre in Education. (2014) (b)	The details are as under:Centers	Annual Intake	Courses/DurationNSD, New Delhi	26	3 years DiplomaBengaluru	20	1 year course in ActingVaranasi	20	1 year course in Classical Indian TheatreSikkim	20	1 year course in ActingAgartala	20	1 year course in Theatre in Education(c)	Government of India has established NSD with the objective of developing suitable pattern of teaching in all branches of Drama so as to establish high standards of Theatre Education in India and raising the technical standard of Indian Plays so as to make them aesthetically more satisfying and acceptable. NSD organizes a number of production oriented and technical oriented workshops under extension activities in all parts of the country for theatre enthusiastic for promotion of Theatre. Similarly various festivals are organized to showcase theatre productions including plays of regional languages for promotion of theatre.*****
