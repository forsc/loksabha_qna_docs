                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4074






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.12.2019






Employment for Urban Poor




Pratima Mondal














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether certain schemes are being implemented to provide employment to the urban poor in the country and if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise; (b)	the amount of funds sanctioned, released, actual expenditure made and the targets achieved including the number of persons given employment, State/UT-wise; and (c)	whether the Government proposes to expand the scope of the said schemes and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	The Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs is implementing Deendayal Antyodya Yojana - National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM), since 2014-15, to reduce poverty and vulnerability of urban poor households by enabling them to access gainful self-employment and skilled wage employment opportunities, for improvement in their livelihoods on a sustainable basis. The Employment through Skill Training & Placement (EST&P) component of the Mission aims to provide skills to the urban poor to increase their income through structured and market-oriented certified courses that can provide wage employment and/or self-employment opportunities which will eventually lead to better living standards and alleviation of urban poverty on a sustainable basis. Further, Self-Employment Programme (SEP) component focuses on financial assistance to individuals/groups/SHGs of urban poor for setting up gainful self-employment ventures or micro-enterprises. (b)	The funds are released to States/UTs for implementation of Mission as a whole and inter-se allocation of funds under various component are made by the State Governments depending upon their requirement and capacity. A statement showing State/UT-wise details of funds released by Government and utilised by States/UTs under Mission, during FY-2014-15 to 2019-20 (as on 31.10.2019) is at Annexure-I.				A statement showing details of physical progress of States/UTs under Mission during FY 2014-15 to 2019-20 (as on 31.10.2019) is at Annexure-II.(c)	The Mission has been extended to all statutory towns in the country since February, 2016.                                              *****
