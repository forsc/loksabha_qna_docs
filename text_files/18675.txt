                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

145






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.02.2021






Promotion of Organic Farming




Kalyan Banerjee














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether presently India has gross cropped area of 198.4 million hectares (mha) and net sown area of 140.1 mha; and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether it is a fact that almost 52 per cent of India’s agriculture is rain-fed and hilly regions use lower volumes of chemical fertilizers therein; (c) 	if so, the details thereof;(d) 	whether it is also a fact that the task force constituted by the union Government in 2016 recommended an annual budgetary allocation of at least Rs 12,500 crore for promoting organic farming in the country and if so, the details therefor; and (e) 	the action taken/being taken by the Government for serious budgetary push and comprehensive approaches to kick start the much-needed organic and natural farming movement in the country since 2016 thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????            ( SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)  (a):         As per the report on Land Use Statistics for 2016-17 (latest available), the gross cropped area and net sown area in the country is 200.2 million hectares and 139.4 million hectares respectively.(b) & (c):	As per the report on Land Use Statistics for 2016-17 (latest available), 51 per cent of India’s agriculture is un-irrigated / rain-fed. The All India Nitrogen, Phosphorous, Potassium (NPK) consumption during 2019-20 is 133.44 kg per hectare. States like Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, and North Eastern States have reported NPK consumption below the all India average.                  The State-wise / UT-wise details of NPK consumption is given at annexure.    Contd…2/--2-(d) & (e):	The Task Force on organic farming has been constituted for exploring:•	Possibility of production of different crops/plantation•	Interventerisation of Organic inputs (Manure, Biopesticides etc) in different states and also growing conditions.•	Policy reforms for promotion of organic farming in the country.Based on the policy directives, Government of India is also been promoting Organic Farming in the states through dedicated schemes namely Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY), Mission Organic Value Chain Development for North Eastern Region (MOVCDNER) has been initiated in 2015-16. Both the schemes aim at promotion of cluster/Farmers Producers Organisation (FPO) based on chemical free low input cost sustainable organic farming and support farmers from input procurement to market linkages and formation of organic farming clusters and with the budget allocation as under:•	Till date the budget allocation under PKVY scheme is: Rs. 2132 Crore•	Till date the budget allocation under MOVCDNER scheme is: Rs. 820 CroreParamparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY):               Government is committed to promote Organic Farming, with emphasis on sustainable productivity, food security and soil health. Government has prepared a road map to encourage the farmer for adopting organic farming with launching a new scheme i.e. Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) for promotion of Organic farming in our country. The PKVY Scheme is implemented in a cluster mode with min. 20 ha size and states have been asked to implement in cluster size of 1000 ha in plain area and 500 ha in hilly area to facilitate marketing of organic produce.Mission Organic Value Chain Development for North Eastern Regions: The scheme aims at development of certified organic production in a value chain mode to link growers with consumers and to support the development of entire value chain starting from inputs, seeds, certification, to the creation of facilities for collection, aggregation, processing marketing and brand building initiative. The main focus of the scheme is on export of produce. The scheme was approved with an outlay of Rs. 400 crore for three years.Contd…2/--2-             Beside, at present, the government is focusing more on marketing of production than production growth so that farmers can get better prices for their products including organic products. To promote organic products, a dedicated web portal- www.Jaivikkheti.in/ has also been created to encourage organic farming by directly connecting the producers to the consumers to get a better price for their products. It is a dedicated portal for organic farming. This portal has been developed as both an information platform and a marketing platform. Details of organic farming farmers, input suppliers, certification agencies, marketing agencies will be available on this portal. Under this, the RKVY / PGS group can build capacity building information, contract marketing / other groups and take advantage of direct marketing to sell their commodities to prospective buyers and consumers. Total 3.47 lakh farmers have been registered under Jaivikkheti portal.
