                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

199






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.07.2019






Drinking Water Coverage




 Bhavana Gawali (Patil)














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the fully covered, partially covered and habitations facing quality issues in terms of drinking water, State/UT-wise along with the details of States/UTs which are facing drought like situation; (b) the funds allocated, released and utilised under various drinking water schemes as well as the achievements made in this regard during each of the last three years and the current year including the details of the norms adopted for allocating/ releasing of funds thereunder, State/UT wise; (c) whether the Government has reviewed the achievements made in this regard and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor along with the measures taken to provide safe drinking water to all the habitations of the country; (d) whether some States have requested to release additional funds under the National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP); and(e) if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government on the request made by each State/UT? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI GAJENDRA SINGH SHEKHAWAT)(a) to (e) A Statement of reply is laid on the Table of the House. Statement referred to in the reply to Lok Sabha Starred Question No. 199 due for reply on 04.07.2019(a) Under National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP), as reported by States on Integrated Management Information System (IMIS), as on 28.06.2019, State/UT-wise details of the fully covered, partially covered and habitations facing quality issues in terms of drinking water are at Annexure-I.As per the Memorandum submitted by the States / UTs seeking financial assistance to Ministry of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare, details of States/ UTs which are facing drought like situation are at Annexure-II. (b) The State/UT wise details of funds allocated, released and utilised under various drinking water schemes of NRDWP during each of the last three years and the current year are at Annexure-III. The State/UT wise details of the achievements made under NRDWP during each of the last three years are at Annexure-IV. No achievement has been reported by the States in the current year 2019-20, so far. As per NRDWP, weightage for distribution of fund to all the states is as under:				Criteria	Weightage (%)Rural Population (as per last Census)	40Rural SC and ST population (as per last Census)	10States under Desert Development Programme (DDP), Drought Prone Area Programme (DPAP), Hill Area Development Programme (HADP) and special category Hill States in terms of rural areas	40Population (as per Integrated Management Information System [IMIS]) residing in habitations having affected by chemical contaminants including heavy metals (as on 31st March of preceding financial year)	10Total	100(c) Every year between February and March, Annual Action Plan (AAP) meetings are held with the Departments of State Governments dealing with rural drinking water supply, wherein the progress achieved under different components of National Rural Drinking Water programme (NRDWP) are reviewed and targets for the next financial year are fixed. The physical & financial progress of NRDWP is also reviewed in National / Regional / State level review workshops, meetings and video-conferences.Water is a State subject. Under NRDWP, this Ministry provides technical and financial assistance to the states to improve the coverage of safe drinking water in rural areas.   (d) & (e) States of Uttar Pradesh, Telangana, Bihar, Tamil Nadu, Nagaland and Maharashtra and Punjab have requested for additional assistance for providing drinking water to their rural population. Their request would be considered in view of finalization of new scheme for providing piped drinking water supply which is under consideration of the Government.
