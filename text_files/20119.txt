                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1272






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.02.2021






Houses for All




(Dr.) Ram Shankar Katheria













                                                    Ajay (Teni) Misra
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has fixed the target to provide pucca housing to poor beneficiaries having kutcha housing by 2022;(b) 	if so, whether any agency or institution has been established to conduct survey of kutcha housing, if so, the details thereof;(c) 	whether any priority list is prepared in village panchayats in this regard;(d) 	if so, the persons authorized to prepare the said list;(e) 	whether as per demand/need in rural areas, more than two crore housing units have been constructed in the country till now and the Government proposes to increase its pace so as to provide housing to all by 2022; and(f) 	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (d): In order to achieve the objective of “Housing for All”, the erstwhile rural housing scheme Indira Awaas Yojana (IAY) has been restructured into Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana- Gramin (PMAY-G) w.e.f. 1st April, 2016. Under PMAY-G, the beneficiaries are identified on the basis of housing deprivation parameters and exclusion criteria prescribed under Socio Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011 subject to due verification by Gram Sabha and completion of Appellate Process. The Permanent Wait List of the PMAY-G was prepared based on the eligible beneficiaries identified as per the SECC-2011. PMAY-G targets construction of 2.95 crore houses by March, 2022.(e)& (f): Yes, Sir. Since 01.04.2014 till 04.02.2021, a total of 2,00,33,085 rural homes have been constructed under rural housing schemes. This includes 1,27,81,323 PMAY-G houses constructed under PMAY-G Scheme and 72,51,762 houses constructed for erstwhile IAY Scheme. The introduction of policy and technical reforms under PMAY-G, which also impacted the IAY implementation, have resulted in  manifold increase in  completion of the houses during the said period. *****
