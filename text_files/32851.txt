                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

293






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2022






Aims/Objectives of PM SVANidhi Scheme




Diya Kumari













                                                    Ramesh Bidhuri
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the aims and objectives of the PM SVANidhi Scheme;(b)	the budgetary outlay and phases of implementation of the PM SVANidhi Scheme;(c)	the number of beneficiaries in the state of Rajasthan, district-wise and the details of the progress thereof;(d)	whether the Government has taken any steps for welfare and economical upliftment of Street Vendors across the country;(e)	if so, the details thereof; and(f)	the total number of Street Vendors benefitted under PM SVANidhi Scheme, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                                     THE MINISTER OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                  (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a):  Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA) has launched the Prime Minister Street Vendor’s AtmaNirbharNidhi (PM SVANidhi) Scheme on June 01, 2020  with  the  aim to  facilitate  collateral free working capital loan to street vendors to restart their businesses, which were adversely impacted during the Covid-19 pandemic.  The Scheme has the following objectives:i.	Facilitate collateral free working capital loan up to ?10,000, of 1 year tenure, with enhanced loan of ?20,000 and ?50,000 in the second and third tranches respectively, on repayment of earlier loans.ii.	Incentivize regular repayment, through interest subsidy @ 7% per annum; andiii.	Reward digital transactions, by way of cash back up to ?1,200 per year.(b): An amount of ?113.6 Cr was allocatedduring financial year 2020-21, while?200 Cr was allocated for the financial year 2021-22. In the first phase, Scheme targets to provide 30 lakh loans to street vendors with a total outlay of ?600 Cr.(c): The total number of PM SVANidhi beneficiaries in the State of Rajasthan is 66,737. The district-wise, number of beneficiaries in Rajasthan are at Annexure-I.(d) and (e): Yes, Sir. Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs has launched a program - ‘SVANidhi se Samriddhi’ under PM SVANidhi Scheme, in 125 select Urban Local Bodies (ULBs), on January 4, 2021. This programme facilitates linkages to 8 select Central Government welfare schemes designed to provide a safety net and social security for the eligible beneficiaries and their families. These 8 Schemes are (i) Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (ii) PM Suraksha Bima Yojana (iii) Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (iv) Registration under Building and other Constructions Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act (BOCW) (v) Pradhan Mantri Shram Yogi Maandhan Yojana (vi) National Food Security Act (NFSA) portability benefit – One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC)(vii) Pradhan Mantri Janani Suraksha Yojana and (viii) Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY).As on January 27, 2022, socio economic profiling is completed for 7.7 lakh PM SVANidhi beneficiaries along with 15 Lakh family members and a total of 16.40 lakh scheme benefits have been extended to them.In the State of Rajasthan, 9 cities, namely—Ajmer, Bharatpur, Bhilwara, Bikaner, Jodhpur-North, Jodhpur-South, Kota-North, Kota- South and Udaipur are selected under ‘SVANidhi se Samriddhi’ program. A total of 8,288 scheme benefits of the above-mentioned welfare schemes have been extended to eligible Street vendors and their families. (f): As on January 27, 2022, total number of Street Vendors benefitted under PM SVANidhi Scheme is 28,14,994.State and UT wise details are at Annexure-II.                                      *****
