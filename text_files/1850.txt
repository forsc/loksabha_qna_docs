                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1654






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Issuance of Challans by Delhi Police




Kaushal Kishore













                                                    Upendra Singh Rawat
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the directions given by the Delhi Police to issue challan of vehicles;(b) whether the Ministry of Home Affairs has issued instructions to the Delhi Traffic Police to challan the vehicles not on red lights but standing away from red lights behind the trees;(c) if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor;(d) whether the Government has received any complaints regarding issuance of challans by the Delhi traffic police personnel who hide behind trees; and(e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a): Delhi Police has reported that their personnel are regularly briefed and sensitized to enforce traffic rules and regulations in true letter and spirit in a professional manner and to focus also on ensuring smooth movement and regulation of traffic and less on prosecution.  Directions have been issued by Delhi Police to all the field functionaries of Traffic Unit to position themselves at some conspicuous place at the junctions.  The field supervisory officers have also been directed by Delhi Police to strictly ensure sufficient availability, visibility, punctuality and mobility of traffic staff at important roads/intersections. -2/...-2-LS.US.Q.NO.1654 FOR 02.07.2019(b):  No, Sir.(c):  In view of (b), does not arise.(d) and (e): As per Delhi Police, no complaint in this regard against any traffic police person has been received during the last three years and the current year (upto 15.06.2019).  ******
