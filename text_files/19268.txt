                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

562






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2021






Slow Pace of Rooftop Solar Power Project




Manne Srinivas Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there is a slow pace of rooftop solar power project and if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor along with the progress made thereunder;(b) the details of funds sanctioned/response received so far during the last three years and the current year, State-wise; and(c) the steps being taken for better Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) with Discoms along with the steps being taken to address the issues in metering?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (I/C) FOR NEW & RENEWABLE ENERGY, POWER and MoS for  SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP(SHRI R.K. SINGH)(a) During implementation of Phase-I of Rooftop Solar (RTS) programme in 2015-2020 the achievement reported on MNRE SPIN portal till 31.1.2021 is 2071 MW against the target of 4200 MW capacity. The factors affecting progress included lack of uniform regulations, involvement of multiple agencies, lack of awareness among the prospective beneficiaries, apprehension of possible revenue loss by DISCOMs, etc. These issues have been addressed in Ph-II of RTS Programme, which included making DISCOMs nodal agencies for implementation, separate funding for awareness and capacity building, incentivising DISCOMs for addition of RTS capacity above a certain percentage of base capacity, etc. Out of 4000 MW capacity targeted through central financial support in the residential sector under Ph-II of RTS Programme, over 2600 MW capacity has been sanctioned to various DISCOMs.Till 31.12.2020, states have reported cumulative installation of 3.73 GW of grid connected rooftop solar capacity. (b) Funds sanctioned for release under RTS Pogramme during the last three years and the current year are given follows:FY 2017-18:Rs. 167.93 CroreFY 2018-19: Rs. 446.78 CroreFY 2019-20: Rs.  290.66 CroreFY 2020-21 ( as on 31.12.2020): Rs. 297.21 CroreState-wise breakup is given in Annexure.(c) The Ministry of New and renewable Energy has specified different business models like rent a roof, utility model, RESCO, etc., for implementation of RTS programme. In addition, the Ministry has issued suggestive model operating procedures and timelines for different activities by DISCOMs. The model operating procedures includes timelines for installation of  meters.*****
