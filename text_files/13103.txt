                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2005






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.03.2020






Promotion of Fisheries in Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan




Keshari Devi Patel













                                                    Kanakmal Katara
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 the details of the steps taken to promote fisheries in Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan;(b)	 the details and number of fish farmers who have been provided subsidy in Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan, during the last three years;(c) 	the resultant benefit to the said fish farmers and whether any evaluation/review has been conducted by the Government in this regard, if so, the details thereof;(d)	 whether there is an urgent need to give special attention to utilise the abundant potential in the field of fisheries and aquaculture and also to invest in these sectors, if so, the steps taken to promote investment in these sectors;(e) 	whether quality seeds are being provided to fish farmers in Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan and training is being imparted to them to take care of them, if so, the details of the fish farmers who have received such training, district-wise;(f)	 the amount spent on training per year, during the last three years; and (g)	 the number and details of permanent training centres for providing training to the fish farmers, district-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRYAND DAIRYING  (SHRI PRATAP CHANDRA SARANGI)(a) to (c) : The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying under Centrally Sponsored Scheme (CSS) on Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries provides financial assistance to all the State Governments/Union Territories (UTs)  including Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan for development and promotion of fisheries. Besides, Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Fund (FIDF) has been created with a fund size of Rs. 7522.48 crore for providing access to concessional finance to all the States/UT’s including Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan for development of fisheries infrastructure in the country. Details of steps taken to promote Fisheries in Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan as reported by the State Governments are furnished in Annexure-I. It is reported by the Government of Uttar Pradesh that a total of 2,19,585 numbers of fish farmers have been provided subsidy in the State during last three years.  The Government of Rajasthan has reported that a total of 33 numbers of fish farmers in the State have been provided subsidy. The efforts of the Central and State schemes also contributed towards increase in fish production, fish farmer’s income, employment opportunities in the fisheries sector, entrepreneurship development, up-liftment of socio-economic status of fishers and fish farmers, besides creating multiple indirect benefits. In addition to the periodical review by the Department of Fisheries, the NITI Aayog, Government of India has taken up evaluation of the CSS on Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries and FIDF through an independent agency. (d) :	Yes, Sir. The Centrally Sponsored Schemes (CSS) on Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries and FIDF implemented by the Department of Fisheries are aimed towards harnessing of potential fisheries resources in a sustainable manner and also to attract the private investments in fisheries sector through providing financial assistance and interest subventions respectively. (e) to (g) :  	Yes, Sir. Financial assistance is provided to the farmers and fish farmers for the development of hatcheries, nursery/seed rearing units for the production and supply of quality fin fish and shell fish seeds to fish farmers, and the fishers and fish farmers are trained in fisheries and allied activities under both the Central Government and State Government schemes. The details of training provided to fish farmers and the amount spent on training during past three years along with the details of permanent training centres both in Rajasthan and Uttar Pradesh as reported  by the State Governments are furnished at Annexure-II. *****
