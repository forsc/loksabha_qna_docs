                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4505






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






Aadhaar Seeding under ONORC




Rajiv Pratap Rudy














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of eligible beneficiaries under National Food Security Act and the status of Aadhaar seeding of beneficiaries under the One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC) reform, in terms of percentage and in absolute numbers, State/UT and district-wise in Bihar; (b) 	the details of inter-State and intra-State portability transactions, State/UT and year-wise since the inception of ONORC scheme; (c) 	whether it is a fact that some States are yet to implement the ONORC scheme and if so, the details and reasons for delay along with the steps taken; and (d) 	the details of the additional borrowing done by the States for implementing the ONORC, State and year-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E RMINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT AND CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a): The National Food Security Act, 2013 (NFSA) provides for a maximum coverage of 81.35 Crore beneficiaries. Accordingly, at present around 80 Crore beneficiaries have been identified by the States/UTs under the Act, of which nearly 91% beneficiaries are Aadhaar seeded at the national level. Further, under the technology driven One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC) plan for portability of ration cards, all NFSA ration card holders/beneficiaries in the country are equally eligible. So far, the ONORC plan is enabled in 35 States/UTs, covering about 77 Crore beneficiaries, that is, 96.8% of the total NFSA population in the country.  A State/UT wise statement showing the current coverage of NFSA in percentage and in absolute number is at Annexure-I and a district wise status of the same in Bihar is at Annexure-II......2/--2-(b): 	A Statement showing State/UT-wise and month-wise inter-State and intra-State portability transactions since inception of ONORC plan in August 2019 is at Annexure-III.  (c): 	So far, the ONORC plan is enabled in 35 States/UTs, covering about 77 Crore beneficiaries, that is, 96.8% of the total NFSA population in the country. Department has done regular follow-ups with the remaining one State of Assam to rollout the ONORC plan at the earliest, by achieving the requisite technical readiness to implement the portability of ration cards.(d): The details of additional borrowings allowed by Ministry of Finance to the States during 2020-21 against the One Nation One Ration Card reform is as below:(Rs.
