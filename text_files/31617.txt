                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3079






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.12.2021






Targets under Smart Cities Mission




Nusrat Jahan Ruhi














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the current status of Smart Cities Mission in the country; (b) 	whether Smart Cities Mission is lagging way behind its targets and if so, the details thereof; (c) 	the number of cities in the country converted into smart cities under said mission; and (d)	the steps Government proposes to take for effective implementation of Smart Cities Mission in the country?






ANSWER




                                                                     THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                                  (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a)	to   (d):  Government of India launched the Smart Cities Mission (SCM) on 25 June 2015.  100 Smart Cities have been selected through 4  rounds  of    competition  from  January 2016 to June 2018.   As  on 12 November 2021,  these  Smart Cities have tendered 6,452 projects  worth Rs. 1,84,998 crore (90% of total projects); out of which 5,809 projects worth  Rs. 1,56,571 crore (85%) are under implementation/ completed.  There has been approximately 270% growth in tendering, 407% growth in projects under implementation/completed in the last   3 years.  Government of India has released Rs.27,359.60 crore under SCM, out of which Rs.  22,467.81 crore (82%) has been utilized. A Smart City Advisory Forum at the city level comprising of District Collector, MP(s), MLA(s), Mayor, CEO of SPV, local youths, technical experts etc. has been constituted in each Smart City to advise and enable collaboration among various local stakeholders.  At the State level, State level High Powered Steering Committee (HPSC) chaired by the Chief Secretary has been established to guide Mission implementation.  At the national level, implementation of SCM is being monitored by an Apex Committee headed by Secretary, Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA).  Besides, MoHUA nominee Directors on Boards of SPVs monitor progress in respective cities on a regular basis.  The Geo-spatial Management Information System (GMIS) of SCM collates project data in real-time; thus enabling effective monitoring of Mission progress. The period of implementation of SCM has also been extended upto June 2023 and all Smart Cities are expected to complete their projects within the stipulated time.                                                ***
