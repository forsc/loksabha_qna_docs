                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2778






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






Overbooking of Tickets




Devji Mansingram Patel













                                                    (Dr.) Ram Shankar Katheria
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether more tickets than the seats are booked by the airlines to earn more profits and if so, the details thereof including the steps taken by the Government to resolve the issue;(b) whether the Government has received any complaints regarding passengers not given a seat in the flight even after having a confirmed ticket due to overbooking;(c) if so, the details thereof including the action taken by the Government against such airlines;(d) whether there are different charges of all airlines for cancellation of tickets, seat of choice and travel certificate and if so, the details thereof; and(e) whether the Government proposes to fix uniform charges for the said purpose and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) to (c):  Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) receives details of passengers affected due to denied boarding from the scheduled domestic airlines as part of monthly submission of traffic data. In order to ensure appropriate protection for the air travelers in case of denied boarding, DGCA has issued Civil Aviation Requirements (CAR) Section 3- Air Transport, Series M, Part IV titled "Facilities to be provided to passengers by airlines due to denied boarding, cancellation of flights and delays in flights". As per the CAR, if the boarding is denied to passengers against their will, the airline shall not be liable for any compensation in case alternate flight is arranged that is scheduled to depart within one hour of the original schedule departure time of the initial reservation. Failing to do so, the airline shall compensate the passengers as per the following provisions:;;(i)   An amount equal to 200% of booked one-way basic fare plus airline fuel charge, subject to maximum of INR 10,000, in case airline arranges alternate flight that is scheduled to depart within the 24 hours of the booked scheduled departure.;(ii)  An amount equal to 400% of booked one-way basic fare plus airline fuel charge, subject to maximum of INR 20,000, in case airline arranges alternate flight that is scheduled to depart more than 24 hours of the booked scheduled departure. ;(iii)  In case passenger does not opt for alternate flight, refund of full value of ticket and compensation equal to 400% of booked one-way basic fare plus airline fuel charge, subject to maximum of INR 20,000. As submitted by the scheduled domestic airlines, airline wise details of passengers affected due to denied boarding with the status of facilities & compensation provided by them to the affected passengers for the period May, 2019 to October, 2019 are attached as Annexure-I. (d) and (e): The details are attached as Annexure-II. Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA), primarily being a safety regulatory body does not regulate the commercial activities of the airlines.
