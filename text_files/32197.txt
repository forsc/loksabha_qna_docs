                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3627






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






LIC Buildings




Arvind Ganpat Sawant














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of buildings owned by Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC) existing in Mumbai;(b)	whether it is true that LIC has acquired the buildings namely Angrewadi, New Badam Wadi, East & West villa, Devkaran Building, Bhatia Building and 45 other buildings in default and if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether these buildings are very old, nearly 100 years and are beyond repairs;(d)	if so, whether the structural audit of these buildings has been done by LIC and if so, the details thereof including the number of buildings found in dilapidated conditions and action taken to redevelop them;(e)	whether these buildings are known as “Cess Building” in Mumbai and hence MHADA has asked LIC to hand over these buildings to them, by keeping the ownership with LIC as it is for the redevelopment as per the laws existing in Maharashtra for these cess buildings to save the lives of the residents of these buildings; and(f)	if so, the action initiated by the LIC to protect the lives of the residents?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(a): LIC has informed that it owns 186 buildings in Mumbai.(b) to (f): LIC has informed that it owns properties named Angrewadi, AhilyaChawlBadamwadi, East and West Villa, DevkaranNanjee Building and Bhatia Bhavan in Mumbai, besides 45 other properties at various locations in Mumbai, which are known as “cess buildings” as the Maharashtra Housing and Area Development Authority (MHADA) levies cess on the same.  LIC has further informed that some of these buildings are nearly 100 years old and that repairs are being carried out either by MHADA or LIC. Further, structural audit for 24 buildings has also been done, the details of which are at Annex.With regard to redevelopment of cess buildings, LIC has apprised that MHADA specifies a set of processes and options for redevelopment which are examined and followed, as per the case.***AnnexureREPLY TO PART B  OF THE UNSTARRED QUESTION No. 3627 DUE FOR ANSWER ON 20.12.2021 IN LOK SABHADetails of structural auditedLIC buildingsSr. No.	Name of the Building01.
