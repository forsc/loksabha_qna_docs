                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3484






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Shortage of Teachers in Social Work Course




Harish Dwivedi














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there is shortage of teachers in Social Work course in private andGovernment Universities across the country, if so, the details thereof, University-wise;(b) the names of the Universities which have taken approval to offer Social Work course,State/UT-wise; and(c) the number of students declared passed in National Eligibility Test in Social Worksubject so far?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): No such data is maintained by the Central Government.(b): A statutory University can award degree specified under Section 22 of UniversityGrants Commission (UGC) Act.1956, with due approval of its competent authorities andStatutory Councils, wherever required and in accordance with the Regulations notifiedby the UGC, from time to time.(c): 13108 candidates have been declared passed in National Eligibility Test in ‘SocialWork’ from 1993 to December, 2018.*****
