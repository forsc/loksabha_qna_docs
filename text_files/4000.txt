                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3711






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.07.2019






Upgradation of Irrigation Facilities




Vishnu Dutt Sharma














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has formulated/likely to formulate any special scheme for upgradation of the irrigation facilities in the country including Madhya Pradesh and Bundelkhand; (b) 	if so, the details thereof and the time by which it is likely to be implemented; (c) 	whether the Government is formulating any scheme for providing irrigation facilities in rural and hill areas of Madhya Pradesh and Bundelkhand; and (d) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????    (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)  & (b):	The Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) was launched during the year 2015-16 with the motto of ‘Har Khet Ko Paani’ for providing end-to-end solutions in irrigation supply chain, viz. water sources, distribution network and farm level applications. The scheme is being implemented in various States of the country including Madhya Pradesh and Bundelkhand. The components of PMKSY are as under: i.	  Accelerated Irrigation Benefit Programme (AIBP): To focus on faster completion of ongoing Major and Medium Irrigation projects including National Projects. Implemented by Department of Water Resources, River Development & Ganga Rejuvenation.ii.	   PMKSY-Har Khet Ko Pani (HKKP): Source augmentation, distribution, ground water development, lift irrigation, diversion of water from water plenty to water scarce areas, repair, restoration, renovation of traditional water bodies. Implemented by Department of Water Resources, River Development & Ganga Rejuvenation.Contd….2/-- 2 -iii.	PMKSY-Watershed Development (WDC): Focuses on development of rainfed portions of the net cultivated area and culturable wastelands. Activities include ridge area treatment, drainage line treatment, soil and moisture conservation, rain water harvesting, nursery raising, afforestation, horticulture, pasture development, livelihood for asset less persons, etc. Implemented by Department of Land Resources.  iv.	PMKSY-Per Drop More Crop (PDMC): Focuses on enhancing water use efficiency at farm level through Micro Irrigation technologies viz. Drip and Sprinkler Irrigation. Implemented by Department of Agriculture, Cooperation & Farmers Welfare (DAC&FW).  During 2016-17, ninety-nine (99) on-going Major/Medium irrigation projects under PMKSY - AIBP, having ultimate irrigation potential of 76.03 lakh ha. have been prioritized in consultation with States, for completion in phases up to December, 2019. From 2016-17 onwards, Command Area Development & Water Management (CADWM) Programme is being implemented as a component of Har Khet Ko Pani (HKKP) for 99 AIBP prioritized projects in the country. Out of 99 prioritized AIBP Projects, depending on the requirements of State Governments, 87 projects spread over 17 States have been included for CADWM programme.(c) & (d):	Out of 99 prioritized projects under PMKSY-AIBP, 14 (including 7 phases) projects having ultimate irrigation potential of 8.72 L.ha , are in the state of Madhya Pradesh.  The details of the projects is given at Annexure-I.  		4 Projects out of 99 prioritized projects under PMKSY-AIBP benefit the Bundelkhand Region in Uttar Pradesh & Madhya Pradesh. Out of these 4 projects, 3 projects are from Madhya Pradesh and 1 project from Uttar Pradesh. Project-wise details is  given at Annexure-II. Besides, one scheme of 125 water bodies from the state of Madhya Pradesh were included in  2014-15 for the release of Central Assistance under the scheme of Repair, Renovation and Restoration (RRR) of Water Bodies. Planned potential and target storage revival of these water bodies are 33304 hectare and 438.347 MCM respectively. Out of these 125 water bodies, 31 water bodies are included from Panna (5), Damoh (14), Sagar (3) and Tikamgarh (9) district of Bundelkhand region of Madhya Pradesh.            Similarly 8 water bodies from Jhansi (3), Mahoba (2) and Banda (3) district and 12 water bodies of Lalitpur district from the Bundelkhand region of Uttar Pradesh were included in 2014-15 and 2015-16 respectively for the release of Central Assistance under the scheme of Repair, Renovation and Restoration of Water Bodies Scheme. Planned potential and target storage revival of these 20 bodies are 3447 hectare and 8.40 MCM respectively.
