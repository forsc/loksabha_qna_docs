                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA

                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3538






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2022






MRP of Domestic LPG Cylinders




Dhanush M Kumar













                                                    Amol Ramsing Kolhe
                                                








                                                    Sunil Dattatray Tatkare
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar
                                                








                                                    Ganesan Selvam
                                                








                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                








                                                    C.N. Annadurai
                                                








                                                    Gautham Sigamani Pon
                                                








                                                    Manjulata Mandal
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Maximum Retail Price (MRP) of domestic LPG cylinder includes home delivery charge also and if so, the details thereof; (b) whether the cases of overcharging for home delivery of domestic LPG cylinders above its MRP by LPG distributors have been reported and has come to the notice of the Government across the country particularly in small cities during each of the last three years and the current year; (c) if so, the details thereof, State/UT and company wise along with action taken/being taken by the Government in such cases so far; (d) the number and nature of complaints received by the Government against LPG distributors across the country during the said period, State/UT and Company wise along with the present status of such complaints; and (e) the other steps taken/being taken by the Government to bring transparency in the functioning of LPG distributors across the country along with achievements thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN MINISTRY OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a)   The retail Selling Price (RSP) of domestic LPG cylinders includes charges towards home delivery also.  The current applicable charges towards home delivery of various size of domestic LPG cylinders are as underProduct		Delivery charges for home delivery by distributors (Rs. Per refill)14.2 Kg (Domestic subsidized &Non-subsidized cylinder) (w.e.f. 01.10.2019)	27.605 Kg Domestic (Domestic subsidized & Non-subsidized cylinder)	13.805 kg Composite Non-Subsidized (w.e.f. 1.4.2021)	13.8010 kg composite Non-subsidized (w.e.f 1.04.2021)	19.44(b) to (d) Public Sector Oil Marketing Companies (OMCs) receive various complaints such as overcharging for domestic cylinders, delay in refill delivery, unaccounted sale of LPG cylinders, supply of underweight cylinders/pilferage, release of LPG connection to ineligible persons, non-home delivery of domestic refills by LPG distributors etc. In all such established cases, action is taken as per provisions of Marketing Discipline Guidelines (MDG) / Distributorship Agreement. The State/UT-wise details of overcharging on refills during the last three years and current year i.e.  2018-19, 2019-20, 2020-21 and April -Dec 2021 are at Annexure-I and other established cases of irregularities / malpractices are at Annexure-II. (e)	Major steps taken by the Government/OMCs to bring transparency in the functioning of LPG distribution across the country inter alia include:-?	Notification of “Liquefied Petroleum Gas (Regulation of Supply and Distribution) Order, 2000”, in order to regulate distribution of LPG.  ?	Notification of LPG Marketing Discipline Guidelines (MDG) by OMCs to be adhered to by the distributors. The MDG is revised from time to time to cover all aspects of LPG marketing and to keep a check on erring distributors.?	KYC initiative for all New Connections/Multiple Connections/Transferred/Inactive customers so that customers with proper identity and address proof are enrolled at distributors.?	Introduction of IVRS/SMS refill booking system across the country in all regular distributorships, wherein customers get SMS on refill booking / generation of cash memo.  ?	Introduction of e-Invoice by OMCs so that customer can view the exact price payable.?	Provision of digital payment facility to the consumers. ?	Provision of a unique toll free telephone number 18002333555 on industry basis, in order to have a convenient, easy and effective way for the citizen to provide their feedback. ?	Introduction of different colours for domestic and non-domestic LPG cylinders so that misuse of domestic LPG cylinders for unauthorised use is easily identifiable. ?	Introduction of PAHAL - Direct benefit transfer of LPG (DBTL) pan-India w.e.f. 01.01.2015. The customer pays non-subsidized price for all LPG refills. Subsidy is transferred to Bank Accounts. Further, w.e.f. 01.07.2016, the subsidy transfer is only to customers who have submitted their AADHAR number to the LPG distributor. ?	Facility of opting for another distributor operating in the same area under portability scheme, in case the customer is not satisfied with the services of the existing distributor.
