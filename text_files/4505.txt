                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4166






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.07.2019






Dehradun-Delhi National Highway Project




Ajay Bhatt














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the time when the construction work of Dehradun-Delhi National Highway project was 	sanctioned; (b) 	the present status of work on the said project; (c) 	the reasons for delay in completion of the said project; (d)	whether the Government has fixed any time limit for completion of work on the said 	project; and (e)	if so, the details thereof and the steps taken/being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI) (a) to (e) 	The connectivity from Delhi to Dehradun has been envisaged through Meerut and Muzaffarnagar.  The construction of Delhi – Meerut Expressway (Nizamuddin to U.P. Gate) has been completed in June, 2018.  The scheduled completion date for U.P. Border – Dasna section and Dasna – Meerut section (greenfield alignment) of Delhi – Meerut Expressway is May, 2020 and December, 2019 respectively. The road stretch from Meerut to Muzaffarnagar has already been constructed to four lane configuration. The work of four laning in Muzaffarnagar – Haridwar section has been terminated because the concessionaire could not achieve project completion due to financial crisis.  The bids for balance work on this road stretch have been invited with the due date as 13.08.2019.  The scheduled completion period is 18 months after appointed date. The work of four laning in Haridwar - Dehradun section has also been terminated because the concessionaire could not achieve project completion due to financial crisis.  The balance work of this stretch has also been awarded in November, 2018 with scheduled completion date as January, 2020.*****
