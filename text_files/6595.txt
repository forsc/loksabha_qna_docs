                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

408






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.11.2019






Increase in Agricultural and Horticultural Cooperatives




Rajiv Pratap Rudy














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the number of agricultural and horticultural cooperatives has increased in the country; (b)	 the number of agricultural and horticultural cooperatives operating in the country, State-wise and year-wise during the past 5 years; (c) 	whether the Government has taken steps to increase the number of cooperatives and empower them in the recent years; (d) 	if so, the details thereof; and (e) 	whether the Government has taken steps to increase international competitiveness of cooperatives and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????            ( SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)  (a)  & (b):	Government of India does not maintain this information.(c) & (d):	Yes Sir. National Cooperative Development Corporation (NCDC) is a statutory corporation under the administrative control of Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare is mandated to promote, develop and educate Cooperative Sector. NCDC has disbursed loan to the tune of Rs. 28140.38 Cr during 2018-19 to Cooperatives.Government of India under Central Sector Integrated Scheme on Agriculture Cooperation (CSISAC) provides subsidy for development of Cooperatives through National Cooperative Development Corporation (NCDC) and provide assistance to Cooperatives for Education and Training though National Council for Cooperative Training (NCCT) and National Cooperative Union of India (NCUI). The grants-in-aid of Rs. 228.23 crores and Rs. 137.14 crores have been released during 2017-18 and 2018-19 respectively. An outlay of Rs. 135.00 crores has been approved for 2019-20.(e):	NCDC along with others organized the first ever India International Cooperatives Trade Fair on 11-13 October, 2019 at Pragati Maidan, New Delhi. The fair provided a platform to Indian Cooperatives to interact with international cooperatives and business houses.*******
