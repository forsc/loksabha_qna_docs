                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

321






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.11.2021






Implementation of National Rural Livelihood Mission




Kripanath Mallah














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the salient features of the National Rural Livelihood Mission;(b) 	the details of the funds sanctioned, allocated and utilized under this scheme within Assam so far;(c) 	the details of the target set and achievements made so far within Assam so far along with its response;(d) 	whether the Government is planning to train Self-Help Groups (SHG) to contain spread of virus and uptake of COVID-19 vaccination among rural population within the country, if so, the details thereof;(e) 	whether the Government has set up SAKSHAM Centres under this mission within the country; and(f) 	if so, the number of centres set up in Assam, district wise along with its response?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)a)	Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM) is a programme for providing livelihoods for rural poor women by organizing them into Self Help Groups (SHGs) and facilitating them to access credit from Banks and also in taking up various livelihoods activities for enhancing their incomes. The programme is being implemented in all the States and Union Territories, except Delhi and Chandigarh. The salient features of the DAY-NRLM are as follows:i.	Promotion of Sustainable Institutions of the poorii.	Ensuring access to financial servicesiii.	 Promote diversified livelihoods opportunities for the poor, in farm and non- farm sectors.iv.	Promotion of Skill Development and Access to Jobsv.	Promotion of Social Inclusion and Human Development                    As of   31st October 2021, the Mission is being implemented in 6768 blocks in 706 districts across all 29 States and 5 Union Territories   cumulatively, 7.95 crore women have been mobilized into more than 72.62 lakh SHGs.  b)	The details of the funds allocated, released and utilized under DAY- NRLM within Assam so far are given at Annexure –I.c)	The details of the target set and achievements made so far within Assam under DAY- NRLM so far along with its response are given at Annexure- II.d)	The Ministry has conducted two rounds of training for the Self-Help Groups (SHG) members on prevention, precaution and related measures for dealing with COVID-19.  The first round of trainings was conducted in June - August 2020 focusing on COVID appropriate behaviors and about 5.10 Crore SHG members were trained in it. 	The focus of second round of the trainings conducted during April-June, 2021 was on management of mild and asymptomatic COVID cases at home, promotion of health seeking behavior and immunity building measures and to encourage COVID-19 vaccination among rural population.  About 5.57 Crore SHG members were trained in this round. State Missions under the DAY-NRLM are being continuously encouraged to mobilize SHG members for COVID-19 vaccination.e)	Yes Sir. f)	In Assam, 10 SAKSHAM Centers have been launched. District-wise details are as under: Sl. No.	District Name	No. of SAKSHAM Centres1	DHEMAJI	        22	GOLAGHAT	23	HAILAKANDI	24	NAGAON	        25	TINSUKIA	2 The main objective of the SAKSHAM center is to provide financial literacy & facilitate the delivery of financial services to SHG members and the rural poor.  These Centers are being managed by SHG Federations with the help of trained community resource persons.
