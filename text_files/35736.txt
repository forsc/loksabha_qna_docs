                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3050






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






Dissemination of Agricultural Research




Sarmistha Sethi













                                                    Vivek Narayan Shejwalkar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware that the outcome of research in agriculture sector is not reaching farmers and the missing gap has become a hindrance in the development of the agricultural sector in the country, if so, the details thereof;(b) the steps being taken by the Government to disseminate information to farmers related to scientific research in agriculture;(c) whether the Government has given assistance to farmers to use modern and State of the Art instruments for agricultural purposes;(d) if so, whether the Government in coordination with State Governments is providing support to farmers for purchasing these instruments at subsidised rates, if so, the details thereof;(e) whether there is any proposal to increase the number of Krishi Vigyan Kendras in India; and(f) if so, the details thereof, State-wise, including Odisha?






ANSWER




THE MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ?? ????? ?????? ??????		      (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) & (b): The Government of India, under the aegis of Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR), have established 731 Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) in various districts across the country to disseminate information to farmers related to outcome of scientific research in agriculture. The activities of KVKs include On-farm testing to identify the location specificity of technology under various farming systems; Frontline demonstration to establish the production potential of improved agricultural technologies on the farmers field; Capacity development of famers for knowledge and skill up gradation; providing farm advisories using ICT and other media means on varied subjects of interest to famers; and production of quality seeds and planting materials for availability to the farmers. In order to develop awareness of improved agricultural technology among the farmers, a large number of extension activities are also taken up by the KVKs. The KVKs disseminated information related to outcome of scientific research in agriculture to 4.45 crore farmers during 2020-21.Besides, Government is also implementing a Centrally Sponsored Scheme namely “Support to State Extension Programmes for Extension Reforms” popularly known as ‘ATMA (Agricultural Technology Management Agency) Scheme’ to support efforts of State Governments in making available latest agricultural technologies through various extension activities like, farmers training, demonstrations, exposure visits, kisan mela, mobilization of Farmers Groups and organizing Farm Schools etc.(c) & (d): The Government is implementing Sub Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM) through State Governments from April 2014 for promotion of farm mechanization by establishment of Custom Hiring Centres of hi-tech and high value farm equipment, distribution of various agricultural implements, demonstration and capacity building of stakeholders. Since 2018-19, the Government is also providing support to the States of Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh and NCT of Delhi and 60 KVKs of these States through a Central Sector Scheme on “Promotion of Agricultural Mechanization” to encourage farmers through training and demonstration on use of new farm machineries and implements for In-Situ Crop Residue Management. Besides, KVKs also organize training for farmers in the area of farm mechanization.(e) & (f):  So far, 731 KVKs have been established and there is provision of establishing 14 more KVKs in the country. State/UT wise list of districts for KVKs provisioned is given in Annexure-I.*****
