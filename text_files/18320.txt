                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MINORITY AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1939






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.09.2020






Minority Student Beneficiaries of Scholarship Programmes




Karti P. Chidambaram














                        Will the Minister of





MINORITY AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of minority student beneficiaries of Central educational scholarship programmes during the last three years, programme and State-wise; (b) the number of minority beneficiaries of Central employment-oriented skill development programmes during the last three years, programme and State-wise; (c) the number of beneficiaries of the Maulana Azad National Fellowship during the last three years, State-wise; (d) whether the Government is considering any new educational programmes for students of minority groups and if so, details thereof; (e) whether the Government recognises concerns that minority students may suffer to a great extent as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic and lockdown; and (f) if so, the measures taken to redress the situation and if not, reasons therefor? 
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF MINORITY AFFAIRS(SHRI MUKHTAR ABBAS NAQVI(a)	Ministry of Minority Affairs implements three scholarships schemes namely Pre-Matric Scholarship Scheme, Post-Matric Scholarship Scheme and Merit-cum-Means based Scholarship Scheme for educational empowerment of minority students. Besides, Maulana Azad Education Foundation an autonomous body under the administrative control of Ministry also implements Begum Hazrat Mahal National Scholarship Scheme for Meritorious Girls belonging to the economically weaker sections of Minorities. Details of minority students benefitted under the above mentioned Scholarship Schemes during last three years are given at Annexure A. (b)	This Ministry implements following schemes related to skill development of minorities: (i)	Seekho Aur Kamao - Skill development scheme for youth of 14 - 35 years age group and aiming at improving the employability of existing workers, school dropouts etc. (ii)	Nai Manzil - A scheme for formal school education & skilling of school dropouts   launched in  Aug. 2015.(iii)	USTTAD (Upgrading the Skills and Training in Traditional Arts/Crafts for    Development) - Launched in May 2015 aims to preserve rich heritage of traditional skills of indigenous artisans/craftsmen.  Under this scheme Hunnar Haats are also held all over the country to provide nation-wide marketing platform to Minority artisans & entrepreneurs and to create employment opportunities.  During the last three years 7560 persons have benefitted from the Scheme. (iv)	Gharib Nawaz Employment Scheme started  in 2017-18 by MAEF for providing short term job oriented skill development training to  minority youth.The above schemes are implemented through implementing agencies empanelled under the respective schemes/programmes and there are no state-wise allocations or beneficiary targets. Information of State/UT wise number of beneficiaries under the Seekho aur Kamao, Nai Manzil and Gharib Nawaz Employment Schemes for the last three years is at Annexure B. (c)	Maulana Azad National Fellowship Scheme provide fellowships in the form of financial assistance to minority students. State-wise details of beneficiaries of the Maulana Azad National Fellowship during the last three years is at Annexure C. The details of all the above schemes mentioned in replies to parts (a) to (c) are available at website of ministry i.e. www.minorityaffairs.gov.in./ www.maef.nic.in.  (d)	No Sir. There is no proposal for any new educational programmes for students of minority under consideration in the Ministry.  (e)	& (f):  Ministry of Education has analysed   the problems in education sector due to COVID-19 and has taken initiative to provide psychological support to students including minorities, teachers and families for mental health and wellbeing during the Covid-19 outbreak and beyond. Under the initiative, a web page named Manodarpan has been created on the Ministry of Education (MoE’s) website. The webpage contains advisory, practical tips, posters, videos, dos and don’ts for psychological support, FAQ and online query system. There is a provision of tele-counseling to address their mental health and psychological issues.*******
