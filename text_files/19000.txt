                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MINES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

438






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2021






Mining Exemptions




Pankaj Chowdhary














                        Will the Minister of





MINES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government proposes to cancel mining-exemptions provided to various States in violation of the Forest Rights Act, 2006;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	the State/UT-wise details of the mining-exemptions provided in violation of the said Act; and(d)	the steps taken or proposed to be taken to protect the rights of tribes?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF MINES, COAL AND PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS(SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI)   (a): The Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006  does not provide any exemption to any category of projects including mining. (b) & (c): Does not arise, in view of (a) above.(d): A detailed circular was issued by M/o Tribal Affairs to the Chief Secretaries of all States/UTs for compliance of the the provisions of the The Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 before diversion of forest land on 07.03.2014. Copy of the same is annexed. *****
