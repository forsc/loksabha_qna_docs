                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

776






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2021






Credit Guarantee Scheme




P.P. Chaudhary













                                                    Krishna Pal Singh Yadav
                                                








                                                    Rajbahadur Singh
                                                








                                                     Pratap Chandra Sarangi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	the aims, objectives, expected outcomes and the funds allocated for the Credit Guarantee Scheme for Sub-ordinate Debt;(b)	the details of beneficiaries, since implementation of the scheme; State/year-wise; and (c)	whether the Government is taking necessary steps to increase uptake of the scheme and if so, the details thereof;?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NARAYAN RANE)(a):	Under Atma Nirbhar Bharat Package, the Government launched the Credit Guarantee Scheme for Sub-ordinate Debt in 2020 with a view to  provide credit facility to the promoters of stressed MSMEs viz. SMA-2 and NPA accounts who are eligible for restructuring as per RBI guidelines on the books of the lending institutions. Under the scheme, the promoter would infuse the credit in the MSME as quasi equity or sub-debt. The approved outlay for the scheme is Rs.4,000 crore. (b):	The state-wise details of beneficiaries are given in the Annexure. (c):	 In order to increase the off-take of the scheme, the Government has taken several measures which inter-alia include measures such as (i) extending the validity of the scheme till March 31, 2022; (ii) holding a series of consultations with the stake holders; (iii) clarifications issued in some of the operational guidelines of the scheme; and (iv) organising workshops for training of Bank officials,  etc.
