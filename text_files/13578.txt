                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2413






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.03.2020






Prevention of Coronavirus




Lavu Sri Krishna Devarayalu














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has initiated precautionary measures to prevent the spread of Novel Corona virus;(b) whether random audits of the catering kitchens available airports at flights have been conducted to ensure quality, quantity and hygiene;(c) whether monetary compensation is being given to the affected passengers who are offered the unserviceable seats; and(d) whether food is available to the passengers on demand especially to those who are having some medical conditions?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) & (b): Yes, Sir. The Ministry of Civil Aviation is assisting Ministry of Health & Family Welfare by taking various measures to prevent the spread of novel Coronavirus disease. Instructions have been issued to conduct universal screening of all passengers arriving on international flights, making in-flight announcements, getting the ''Self-Declaration'' forms filled by all the passengers coming to India and to place the signages at strategic locations at airports. Besides, the Directorate General of Civil Aviation has also implemented various steps to ensure safety and hygiene at airports.;;(c): Airlines normally do not allocate unserviceable seats to its passengers. However, in case a seat becomes unserviceable, the airline tries to accommodate the passenger on available alternate seat. ;;(d): Yes, Sir. Normally, airlines provide special meals to passengers having medical conditions, provided the medical condition/specific meal requirement has been reported at least 48 hours prior to departure.
