                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1455






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2020






Computerization of Fair Price Shops




Sumalatha Ambareesh














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Union Government is working on computerization of all Fair Price Shops across the country, if so, the details of the number of Fair Price Shops computerized in the country till date, State-wise and district-wise; (b) 	whether the Government has set any time-frame to complete the process of computerization of all the Fair Price Shops in the country, if so, the details thereof; and (c) 	whether the Government proposes to take further measures to ensure direct benefit transfer to the beneficiaries under the Public Distribution System (PDS), if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E R MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a) and (b): 	Department in association with all States/UTs is implementing a scheme on ‘End-to-End Computerization of Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS) Operations’ including automation of Fair Price Shops (FPSs) to bring transparency and efficiency in the allocation and distribution of highly subsidised foodgrains to beneficiaries covered under National Food Security Act, 2013 (NFSA). So far, nearly 4.76 Lakh (88%) out of total 5.39 Lakh FPSs in the country have been automated by installing ePoS devices. In this regard, Department monitor the progress at the State/UT level only, thus, a statement showing State/UT wise total number of FPSs and automated FPSs is annexed. Further, continuous efforts are being made by the Department to complete the installation of ePoS devices in remaining FPSs of few concerned States at the earliest.(c): 	Direct Benefit (Cash) Transfer of Food Subsidy under NFSA is presently implemented fully in two UTs of Chandigarh and Puducherry w.e.f. September 2015 and in urban areas of Dadra & Nagar Haveli UT w.e.f. March 2016.
