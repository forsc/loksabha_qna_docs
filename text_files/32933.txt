                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

357






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2022






Prakasam Barrage under DRIP




Raghu Ramakrishna Raju Kanumuru













                                                    Y. S. Avinash Reddy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Ministry proposes to include Prakasam Barrage for release of financial assistance under Dam Rehabilitation and Improvement Project (DRIP); (b) if so, the details thereof; and (c) the details of the other projects which are being considered for sanction of DRIP aid in the State of Andhra Pradesh?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI BISHWESWAR TUDU)(a) & (b)  The number of dams identified for inclusion under DRIP programme are based on proposals received from the participating States. The individual States have done a review of the status of their dams and have identified those dams that are in need of rehabilitation and improvement. Govt. of Andhra Pradesh has not proposed the Prakasam Barrage for its rehabilitation under the externally funded Dam Rehabilitation & Improvement Project (DRIP) Phase-II & III. (c)  State of Andhra Pradesh has shown willingness to participate in the DRIP Phases-II & III. Thirty one (31) dams of Andhra Pradesh have been proposed for comprehensive rehabilitation under the said programme at a tentative cost of Rs. 777.00 Crore.
