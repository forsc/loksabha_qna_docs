                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1372






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.09.2020






Shortage of Teachers in Higher and Technical Education




Chandeshwar Prasad














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether it is a fact that there is huge shortage of teachers in various higher and technical education institutions of the country; (b) if so, the details thereof; (c) whether Government considers that due to shortage of teachers, educational standard of those institution is low; and (d) if so, the steps Government is going to take to resolve this problems?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (d) : 	Occurring of vacancies and filling up is a continuous process. The vacancies keep arising due to retirement, resignation and additional requirements on account of enhanced students’ strength. The institutions are adopting various measures to address faculty shortages in order to ensure that studies of students are not affected which inter-alia, includes engaging research scholars, contract, re-employed, adjunct and visiting faculty. The institutions publish a year round rolling advertisement to attract faculty. University Grants Commission (UGC) vide its letters dated 04.06.2019, 31.07.2019, 05.09.2019 and 22.10.2019 has already issued directions to higher education institutes to fill up the vacant posts.***
