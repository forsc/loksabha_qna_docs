                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3216






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.08.2021






Malnourished Children in Tribal Areas




Krishna Pal Singh Yadav














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    	the number of tribal children having access to Integrated Child Development Scheme (ICDS); (b) 	whether any special funds have been allocated for malnourished tribal children and if so, the details thereof; and (c) 	the details of the Government run programmes for malnourished children in Madhya Pradesh? 






ANSWER




ANSWER MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a) According to data on ICDS-RRS portal, the number of tribal children having access to Anganwadi Services (erstwhile Integrated Child Development Scheme), as on 31st December, 2020,  was 8391638. (b) Under Anganwadi Services, an amount of Rs.1323.16 crores has been released under the ST component to States/UTs during 2020-21. (c) Under Anganwadi Services, a Centrally Sponsored Scheme,  severely malnourished children in the age group of 6 months to 6 years are given food supplement of 800 calories of energy and 20 - 25 gms of protein.  Apart from this, the State Government of Madhya Pradesh is providing third meal to all identified severe underweight children based on monthly growth. Further, the State has started Integrated Management of Acute Malnourished Children (I-MAM) to facilitate treatment and management at health facility (NRC) for all identified SAM children with medical complications. *****
