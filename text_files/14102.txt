                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2819






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.03.2020






Discontinuation of Trains




Akshaibar Lal














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Lucknow Bench of Allahabad High Court in case No. 1011/2016 has passed an order in 2019 to stop the running of trains from Bahraich to Mailani route of North-Eastern Railway;(b) if so, the details thereof; and(c) whether the Government will appeal in the court to continue the said train facility for the benefit of lakhs of people belonging to the districts of Bahraich and Lakhimpur which they have been getting for the last of hundred of years?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY (SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL) (a) to (c) In terms of the directions of Hon’ble High Court, the Committee of Secretaries had decided to discontinue train services on Nanpara-Mailani section, upon the completion of BG work on the Gonda-Sitapur-Mailani section.  Accordingly, upon the commissioning of Sitapur-Mailani section on 14.02.2020, Meter Gauge trains on Nanpara-Mailani section has been discontinued w.e.f. 16.02.2020. During the hearing on Public Interest Litigations filed by the resident of Nanpara-Mailani sector, the Hon’ble court, on 24.02.2020, has directed Indian Railways to “take a decision to manage the affairs in the manner so that lacs of passengers are not put to inconvenience”. The matter is under examination.*****
