                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2754






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Driver Training Institute




Rattan Lal Kataria














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)   whether the Government proposes to set up driver training institutes in Public Private Partnership (PPP) mode in the country;(b)    if so, the details thereof;(c)    the number of such institutes set up in the States of Haryana, Punjab, Himachal Pardesh and Jammu-Kashmir, district-wise;(d)    whether these institutes are also working on any plan to impart training to drivers of new technology vehicles; and(e)    if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS (SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) & (b)  The Ministry of Road Transport & Highways has implemented a nation-wide scheme of setting up of Driver Training Institutes (DTIs) since FY 2002-03. The scheme was renamed as Institute of Driving Training & Research (IDTR) in 12th FY Plan period (2012-17) with central financial assistance of Rs.17.00 crore and land requirement of 10 acre. A new component as Regional Driver Training Centre (RDTCs) with financial assistance of Rs.5.00 crore and land requirement of 3 acre was added in the 12th Five Year plan period. During the 14th Finance Commission Cycle period, another new component as Driving Training Centre (DTC) at district level with financial assistance of 50% of the project cost (Maximum Rs.1.00 crore) and land requirement of 2 acre was added. The scheme continues during the current 15th Finance Commission Cycle period with financial assistance of Rs.17.25 crore for one IDTR, Rs.5.50 crore for one RDTC and 50% of the project cost (Maximum Rs.1.00 crore) for one DTC.Till date, 31 IDTRs including erstwhile DTIs and 6 RDTCs have been sanctioned by the Ministry under the scheme to different States/UTs. (c)  Details of Training Institutes set up in the States/U.T. of Haryana, Punjab,Himachal Pradesh and Jammu-Kashmir, under the said scheme of MoRT&H, are as under: S. No.	State/UT	Project	District/location1	Himachal Pradesh	DTI	Jessore2	Haryana	DTI	Bahadurgarh3	Haryana	IDTR	Bhiwani4	Himachal Pradesh	IDTR	Sarkaghat5	Jammu & Kashmir	IDTR	Jammu  (d) & (e) The basic objective of the scheme is to set up a full-fledged training institute with driving test track to provide quality training to commercial vehicle drivers, improve road and environment safety and strengthen overall mobility on roads. The details of main activities/programmes at these institutions are •      Impart Induction Training Course in Heavy Motor Vehicles Driving.•     Impart Induction Training Course in Light Motor Vehicles Driving.•     Conduct Training Course for Driving Instructors of Private Driving Schools.•   Conduct three-days Training Course on Safe Transportation of Hazardous Goods.•   Conduct one-day Refresher Training Course on Safe Transportation of Hazardous Goods.•   Conduct Refresher and Orientation Training Course for Drivers who are in service.•  Test and select drivers by various organizations.*****
