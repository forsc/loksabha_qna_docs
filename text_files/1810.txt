                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1606






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Fake Currency Notes




Vishnu Dayal Ram














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) whether the para military forces and other agencies have recovered a large number of fake currency notes being smuggled from across the border;(b) if so, the details and value of fake currency notes of two thousand and five hundred denomination recovered so far; and(c) the reaction of the Government thereto?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (c): As per the data available with the  National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) the details of the Fake Indian Currency Notes (FICN) seized by various security agencies during the current year (upto 18.6.2019)  is at Annexure A.	The Government has taken various steps to check the smuggling and circulation of fake Indian currency notes in the country, which inter alia, include:---2--L.S.US.Q.NO. 1606 FOR 02.07.201i)	 FICN Coordination Group (FCORD) has been formed by the MHA to share intelligence/information among the security agencies of the state/centre to counter the problem of circulation of fake currency notes in the country. ii)	 A Terror Funding and Fake Currency Cell (TFFC) has been constituted in NIA to investigate terror funding and fake currency cases.iii)	A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) has been signed between India and Bangladesh to prevent and counter smuggling and circulation of fake currency notes.iv)	 Security at the international borders has been strengthened by using new surveillance technology, deploying additional manpower for round the clock surveillance, establishing observation posts along the international border, erection of border fencing and intensive patrolling. *****
