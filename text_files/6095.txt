                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5587






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2019






Plantation of Trees




Rakesh Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether any programmes are being conducted to create awareness among school children towards clean environment and if so, the details thereof;(b)	whether the Government has formulated any programme to ensure participation of school children in plantation campaigns and awareness programmes and if so, the details thereof;(c)	if not, whether the Government is likely to set any target for large scale tree plantation during the monsoon season and if so, the details thereof;(d)	whether the Government proposes to make tree plantation and their management, an integral part of school curriculum; and(e)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	“The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change is implementing Environment Education, Awareness and Training scheme with the objective to promote environmental awareness among all sections of the society and to mobilize people’s participation for conservation of environment. Under the National Green Corps (NGC) programme of the Ministry, about one lakh schools have been identified as Eco-clubs, wherein, nearly thirty lakh students are actively participating in various environment protection and conservation activities, including plantation drives and other issues related to the environment management.The Ministry has launched the Green Good Deed (GGDs) movement in January 2018 which is social movement with the aim of bringing about mass environmental awareness in the society at all levels.Further, the Ministry has also been involved in promoting environment education to all sections of the society through various awareness programmes. Various outreach activities, theme-based exhibition galleries, etc. organized from time to time by the National Museum of Natural History, New Delhi and its four Regional Museums, located in Mysuru, Bhopal, Bhubaneswar and Sawai-Madhopur”.(b)	In order to ensure participation of school children in plantation campaigns and awareness programmes, a scheme named “School Nursery” is under implementation since 2016 from the Compensatory Afforestation Fund in which plants are raised by school children and planted. 1000 plants per nursery and 505 such school nurseries have been established in 13 States. The details of funds released to various States under this scheme is in Annexure-I.      (c) 	Mass plantation during the monsoon are carried out by various State Governments from the fund made available by the Central and State Governments and other agencies. The Ministry provides funds for afforestation under National Afforestation Programme, Green India Mission and Compensatory Afforestation Fund. Afforestation targets in the country under 20 Point Programme are fixed annually by the Ministry in consultation with the States. Plantation target of 1.87 m. ha and 12137 lakh seedlings have been fixed for the current year (2019-20) under Twenty Point Programme – Annexure-II.    (d)& (e)	There is no proposal under consideration of this Ministry to make tree plantation and their management an integral part of school curriculum.***
