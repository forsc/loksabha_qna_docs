                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3548






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






Trans-Border Conservation Zones




Amar Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of Centrally Sponsored Schemes for the conservation of wildlife in established trans-border areas, State-wise;(b)	whether the Government has signed any memorandum in the last three years to establish trans- border conservation zones, if so, the details thereof;(c)	the details of the areas covered under the aforementioned conservation zones, State-wise; and (d)	whether the Government has maintained any data on the advantages of having such zones in border states, if so, the details of the studies conducted on the same?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) Trans-border Protected Areas have not been established in the country. However, for the protection and management of wildlife and its habitats, including those in the border areas, the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change is implementing the Centrally Sponsored Schemes of ‘Development of Wildlife Habitat’, ‘Project Tiger’ and ‘Project Elephant’. (b) No, Sir.(c) and (d) Question does not arise*****
