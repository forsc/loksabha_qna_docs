National Rural Drinking Water Programme

                                                    Prataprao  Jadhav
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether National Rural Drinking Water programme is functioning in various States and if so, the details thereof; (b) whether the Government has fixed any target for supply of piped drinking water and if so, the details thereof; (c) the details of State-wise access to water supply; (d) the details of funds allocated, released and utilized under National Rural Drinking Water Programme during each of the last three years and the current year, State-wise; and (e) the details of physical targets fixed for improvement of access to water supply during these years and the extent to which targets have been achieved during these years?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) & (b) After subsuming the then National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP), since August, 2019, Government of India in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) – Har Ghar Jal to provide tap water connection to every rural household by 2024.(c) As reported by States/ UTs, as on 19.07.2021, with sources at a reasonable distance, 79.12%  rural habitations have provision of minimum 40 litre per capita per day (lpcd) of potable drinking water and 18.07% rural habitations have service level of less than 40 lpcd potable water. However, 2.81% rural habitations are reported to have water quality issues in drinking water sources.(d) The State-wise details of funds allocated, released and utilized under NRDWP in 2018-19 & under JJM in 2019-20, 2020-21 & 2021-22 is annexed.(e) At the time of announcement of Jal Jeevan Mission on 15th August, 2019, out of 18.93 Crore rural households, 3.23 Crore households (17%) were reported to have tap water supply. The Operational guidelines for the implementation of Jal Jeevan Mission was released on 25th December, 2019 and since then, year-wise details of rural households planned and given tap water connections are as under:Year (Timeline)	Households planned to be provided tap water connections	Cumulative Achievement	In %	Nos. in Crore	In %	Nos. in Crore31.03.2020	21	3.98	 21.43	4.0631.03.2021	34	6.44	 38.48	7.2931.03.2022	54	10.24	 40.89	*7.76* as on 19.07.2021*****
