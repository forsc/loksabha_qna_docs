                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

977






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2020






Textile Hub Centre




Geetha Viswanath Vanga














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether there is a proposal to develop Guntur and Prakasam as the textile hub centre in view of its great potential;(b)	if so, the details thereof and the present status of proposals along with the support of the Central Government in this regard;(c)	whether the Government proposes to set up an Integrated Textile City in Andhra Pradesh which may lead to increase in the exports also, if so, the details thereof and progress made therein; (d)	whether a delegation from India visited China to study these aspects which at present provides employment to about 32 million people in India and examined the scope to generate employment for about 10 million more jobs in this textile sector in the upcoming three years; and(e)         if so, the details thereof and the steps being taken in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ?????? ?????? (??????? ?????? ?????? ?????)MINISTER OF TEXTILES(SMT. SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a)	& (b):  The Government is implementing the Scheme for Integrated Textile Park (SITP) which provides support for creation of world-class infrastructure facilities for setting up of textile units, with a Government of India grant upto 40% of the project cost  and Government of India grant upto 90% of the project cost for first two projects (each) in the States of Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Tripura, Sikkim, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand and Jammu & Kashmir; with ceiling limit of Rs. 40.00 crores for each textile park. Till date, 56 textile parks have been sanctioned under SITP which are under various stages of implementation. Out of which twenty two parks have been completed as per scheme guidelines. Out of the 56 textile parks under SITP, 5 textile parks are located in the State of Andhra Pradesh, the details of which may be seen as per ANNEXURE.(c):    At present, Ministry of Textiles does not have any proposal for setting up any Integrated Textile City in Andhra Pradesh.(d) & (e):  No delegation from Ministry of Textiles have visited China for studying the scope of generation of employment in textile sector. ****
