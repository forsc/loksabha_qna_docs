                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

103






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.11.2019






City Gas Distribution Network




Anubhav Mohanty













                                                    Ramya  Haridas
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is in the process of developing additional 13,105 km. gas pipelines as part of National Gas Grid to increase the availability of natural gas across the country, if so, the details thereof;(b) the existing geographical area and population covered under the current City Gas Distribution (CGD) network and the details and status of projects currently underway under the National Gas Grid;(c) the time by when the Government expects to complete the laying of additional pipelines and cover 100 per cent of the population and the number and length of pipelines completed during the last three years and the current year;(d) the details of the percentage of work completed under the development of the City Gas Distribution Network for Jagatsinghpur and Kendrapara districts;(e) whether the projected milestones as set out in the plan have been achieved, if so, the present status of the project, if not, the reasons therefor; and(f) the steps taken by the Government to complete the same on time?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) to (c) To increase the availability of natural gas across the country, the Government has envisaged to develop the National Gas Grid. At present about 16,500 km natural gas pipeline isoperational. Apart from that, Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (PNGRB) has authorized pipeline which are at various stages of execution. Details of partly commissioned and under construction pipelines are at Annexure-I & Annexure-II respectively. Further, PNGRB is taking steps for development of natural gas pipeline in order to cover additional area of the country through National Gas Grid. PNGRB has informed that approximately 1820 km of natural gas pipelines have been commissioned during April, 2016 – March, 2019 and commissioned about 530 km pipeline up to September, 2019 in the current financial year.With regard to coverage of population under City Gas Distribution Networks, PNGRB has so far authorized 229 Geographical Areas (GAs) covering 406 districts for development of CGD Network across the country till the 10th CGD Bidding Round, which covers around 70% of India’s population and 53% of its area. These projects are at various stages of execution.(d) to (f) PNGRB has authorized Bharat Gas Resource Limited (BGRL) for development of CGD Network in Jagatsinghpur and Kendrapara Districts GA under the 9th CGD Bidding Round. As per Minimum Work Program, the commitment of CGD entities are required to be achieved over a period of 8 years and under normal conditions, gas supply to this GAs is to commence from the second year onwards. PNGRB monitors the progress of the CGD projects and CGD Authorization Regulations also provide for imposition of penalty for non-achievement of the assigned targets.*****
