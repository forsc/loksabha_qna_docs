                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1688






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






National Social Assistance Programme




Sanjay Haribhau Jadhav













                                                    Krupal Balaji Tumane
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the main features of the National Social Assistance Programme;(b)	the funds allocated and spent during the last five years and the current year, State and Union Territory-wise;(c)	whether the beneficiaries have been selected from BPL families under the said programme;(d)	if so, whether this criteria is justified and if so, the reasons therefor; (e)	whether the Government proposes to effectively implement the said programme and ensure that its benefits reach the needy; and(f)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)  & (b):	Under the National Social Assistance Programme(NSAP), five separate welfare schemes, namely: a) Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme(IGNOAPS), b)Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme(IGNWPS), c) Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme(IGNDPS), d) National Family Benefit Scheme and e) Annapurna Scheme are being implemented. In respect of first three schemes, targeted beneficiaries are provided with monthly financial assistance ranging between Rs.200/ to Rs500/, as the case may be. Under the National Family Benefit Scheme, below poverty line (BPL) households are entitled to receive a one-time lump-sum amount to the tune of Rs.20,000/- on the death of primary breadwinner aged between 18 and 59 years, whereas under the Annapurna Scheme, 10KG of food grains per month is provided free of cost to those identified beneficiaries who are otherwise eligible but are not receiving the old age pension.  State/UT-wise details of funds allocated/spent under NSAP during last five years and current year are at Annexure.(c) & (d):	Yes sir,  the mandate of the National Social Assistance Programme is to extend the assistance to the beneficiaries belonging to Below Poverty Line(BPL)  families only.(e) & (f):	For effective implementation of NSAP, information technology interface has been strengthened. With this initiative, list of the  beneficiaries has been digitized and against over 15 crore transactions through Direct Benefit Transfer(DBT) mode recorded  in Financial Year 2017-18, during 2018-19, 21.27 crore DBT transactions  have been reported by the States/ Union Territories.   ******
