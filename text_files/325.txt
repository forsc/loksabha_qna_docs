                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

343






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.06.2019






UGC Guidelines for Vacancies




T.N. Prathapan














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether UGC has put up any guidelines to fill up vacancies in various Central and StateUniversities across the country and if so, the details thereof;(b) the details of the vacancies which are yet to be filled in various Central Universitiesincluding in Kerala, CU-wise;(c) whether the Government has relaxed any existing criteria for the appointment of teachersand non-teaching staff in the higher educational institutions and if so, the reasons therefor;(d) the manner in which the University Grants Commission (UGC) ensures competence andquality in selection process as the selection process is purely based in individual universities;(e) whether there is any mechanism to ensure monitoring on the faculty recruitment driveacross the country and if so, the details thereof; and(f) the details of measures which the UGC has taken/proposed to take to avoid nepotism andunnecessary favour in the selection process?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): Yes Sir. The University Grants Commission (UGC) has prepared the GuidelinesforRecruitment of Faculty in Universities, Colleges and Institutions Deemed to be Universitiesoutlining the selection procedure and the time frame for recruitment which has beencirculated to all Universities to adhere to the guidelines. The Universities have also beenrequested to take steps to ensure that the vacancies in the University as well as in theColleges affiliated to University are filled up at the earliest. A copy of the Guidelines circulatedto all Universities is available at https://www.ugc.ac.in/ugc_notices.aspx?id=2418(b): A detailed statement of sanctioned posts of teaching staff and vacant teaching posts(category-wise) in various Central Universities including Central University of Kerala in thecountry under the purview of UGC as on 01.04.2019 is availableathttps://www.ugc.ac.in/pdfnews/4445697_LSPQ-No-343-Ann-II-TEACHING-POSITION-IN-CU-AS-ON-01-04-2019.pdf(c): There is no relaxation in existing criteria for appointment of teachers. For appointmentin respect of non-teaching employees, Central Universities to recruit the non-teaching positionas per their Cadre Recruitment Rules which have to be framed with the approval of theirstatutory bodies in consonance with the guidelines framed by Department of Personnel andTraining, Government of India/University Grants Commission.(d): UGC has laid down UGC Regulations on Minimum qualifications for appointment ofteacher and the academic staff in the University and its Colleges and other measures for themaintenance of standards in Higher Education, 2018 which defines that the overall selectionprocedure and incorporate transparent, objective and credible methodology of analysis of themerits and credentials of the applicants based on the weightage given to the performance ofthe candidate in different relevant parameters.(e): Occurring of vacancies and filling up is a continuous process. The University GrantsCommission (UGC) continuously monitors it with Universities. However, the onus of filling upthe teaching posts lies on Central Universities which are autonomous bodies created underActs of Parliament. Also, UGC vide its D.O. letter No.F.1-14/2019 (CPP-II) dated 4th June,2019 has prepared the Guidelines for Recruitment of Faculty in Universities, Colleges andInstitutions Deemed to be Universities outlining the selection procedure and the time framefor recruitment. These guidelines have been circulated to all Universities to adhere to them.The Universities has also been requested to take steps to ensure that the vacancies in theUniversity as well as in the Colleges affiliated to University are filled up at the earliest. A copyof the Guidelines circulated to all Universities is available athttps://www.ugc.ac.in/ugc_notices.aspx?id=2418.Further, this Ministry has requested all Higher Educational Institutions (HEIs) to fill up facultyrelated data on National Higher Education Resource Centre (NHERC) portal athttps://nherc.in in order to fill up all vacancies in various higher educational institutions on anurgent basis.(f): In order to make the system more credible, UGC Regulation, 2018 provides thatuniversities may assess the ability for teaching and/or research aptitude through atransparent and merit based process in order to rule out nepotism.
