                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.02.2021






Storage of Foodgrains by Private Agencies




Harsimrat Kaur Badal














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the rates of rent offered by Food Corporation of India (FCI) for hiring  the godown/storage space in Punjab and Haryana for storage of foodgrains; (b) 	whether large corporates are also being promoted/engaged for creation of Silo space for storage by FCI; (c) 	if so, the details of rate agreement and salient points of the deals between FCI and large corporates; (d) 	whether this has adversely affected the farmers and consumers‘ interests; and (e) 	if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government thereto?

FCI floats open E-tender enquiries for silos in which the eligible entities who fulfill the minimum eligibility criteria can participate. The project cost of silo projects may vary from Rs. 22 Crore to Rs. 85 Crore depending upon the capacity and type of silos. The eligibility requirement for Technical capacity is 100% of Project cost and financial capacity is 25% of the project cost. As the project cost is not high, even medium scale enterprises participate. FCI has awarded silo projects to 13 different bidders. All silo projects are in PPP mode which has been awarded after following a transparent and open competitive bidding process.(c):	Question does not arise............3/--3-(d):	No Sir.  Steel Silo storage with bulk handling facility is highly mechanized and modernized way of storing of food grains in bulk. It ensures better preservation of food grains and enhances its shelf life.
