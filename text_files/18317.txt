                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2047






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.09.2020






Rozgar Yukt Gaon Model for Khadi Sector




Raja Amareshwara Naik













                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has approved ‘Rozgar Yukt Gaon‘ to introduce enterprise-based business model in the Khadi sector and if so, the details thereof ;(b) 	the details of villages covered, business partners and funds spent, State-wise;(c) 	the details of beneficiaries and employment opportunity created for artisans so far, year and State-wise; (d) 	whether the Government is implementing Gramodyog Vikas Yojana to develop a sustainable model of Khadi based enterprises in the villages and if so, the details thereof, State-wise; and (e) 	the steps being taken by the Government for development of village based industries?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OFMICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI  PRATAP CHANDRA SARANGI)(a):	Ministry of MSME has introduced Rozgar Yukt Gaon (RYG) under Khadi Gramodyog Vikas Yojana, during 2019-20 with a view to introducing enterprise led model replacing subsidy-led model. The Scheme aims to create 12500 direct and 5765 indirect employment opportunities in 50 villages which are deprived of opportunities and sustainable livelihood support system by providing 10000 charkhas, 2000 looms and 100 warping units (@ 200 Charkhas, 40 looms and 2 warping units for each village) throughout the country. An amount of Rs. 41.00 Crores has been earmarked towards the cost of implements etc for the implementation of the above scheme.(b) & (c):	KVIC issued an Expression of Interest (EoI) on 23.02.2020 and the same has been re-issued on 29.08.2020 through leading News Papers and also uploaded the EoI in the Web Portal of KVIC, inviting online proposals from the entitled agencies for identification of Khadi Business Partners (KBP) for implementation of Rojgar Yukt Gaon (RYG) Scheme. The tentative state-wise targets allocated, with number of villages to be covered under RYG scheme are given in Annexure-I.  (d):	Ministry of MSME is implementing the Khadi Gramodyog Vikas Yojana (KGVY) with sub-schemes of Khadi Vikas Yojana (KVY) and Gramodyog Vikas Yojana (GVY) from 2019-20 onwards.  Khadi Programme is promoted under the Khadi Vikas Yojana. Khadi programme is implemented by Khadi Institutions though the entrepreneurs are also eligible to take up the Khadi programme. As of now, 2737 number of Khadi Institutions are working in the country. State-wise list of working Khadi Institutions is at Annexure-II.(e):	Ministry of MSME is implementing the Gramodyog Vikas Yojana, a sub-scheme of KGVY for development of village-based industries. The Village Industries under the purview of KVIC are broadly classified under six verticals for the purpose of implementation of its programmes which are as follows:1.	Agro Based & Food Processing Industry (ABFPI) includes industries viz. Pulses & Cereals Processing Industry, Gur &Khandsari Industry, Palmgur Industry, Fruit & Vegetable Processing Industry, Village Oil Industry, Spices and Condiments, Collection of Minor Forest Produce, Bamboo, Cane & Reed, Medicinal Plant etc.2.	Mineral Based Industry (MBI) includes industries viz. Pottery for food industry, Lime, Plaster of Paris products, Stone cutting, polishing, crushing &carving, Brass metal and other metal crafts, etc.3.	Handmade Paper, Leather & Plastic Industry (HMLPI) includes industries viz. Handmade Paper and paper Products, Paper Conversion, Leather Industry, Plastic Industry, Natural Fibre other than Coir. 4.	Rural Engineering and New Technology Industry (RENTI) includes industries viz. Bio-Gas, Non-Conventional Energy, Bio-Manure, Vermi-compost, Carpentry & Black-smithy, Agri Implements & Tools, Electric & Electronic Products Industry, Dry Dairy & Manufacturing of household metal utensils & articles.5.	Wellness & Cosmetics Industry includes Soap & Oil Industry, Aromatic Oils & fragrances, Cosmetic and beauty products, Hair Oils and Shampoos, Toiletries, etc.6.	Service & Textile Industry (STI) includes Small Business, Maintenance and Servicing Ministry of MSME have recently approved ‘Operational Guidelines’ in respect of ‘Agarbatti making industry’, ‘Bee Keeping Activity’ and ‘Pottery Activity’ to create sustainable employment for rural artisans and enhance their incomes.
