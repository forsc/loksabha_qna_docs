                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2172






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.09.2020






Yoga and Naturopathy Bill




Raju Bista














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) whether the opinions/suggestion of Yoga- Naturopathy experts had been sought by theGovernment while drafting the proposed Yoga- Naturopathy Bill as per the assurance given by Government during passing of National Commission for Indian Systems of Medicines (NCIM) Bill, 2019 to bring about an independent Bill for Yoga and Naturopathy;(b) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and(c) the details of the proposals of the Government for the registration of such persons who have been practicing Naturopathy/graduates in Naturopathy?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE (DR. HARSH VARDHAN)(a) & (b):	The Central Government had entrusted the review of Homoeopathy Central Council (HCC) Act, 1973 and Indian Medicine Central Council (IMCC) Act, 1970 to a Committee Chaired by Vice-Chairman, NITI Aayog in the year 2016. The Committee consulted with the State/UTs and sought views and suggestions of eminent medical professionals, experts in the fields of public health, former Secretaries (Department of AYUSH), Presidents/ Vice- President/Members of the CCIM, CCH, concerned system, lawyers etc. A large number of representations were also received from public suggesting their views, which have been duly considered by the Committee while drafting the three Bills, namely (1) The National Commission for Indian System of Medicine, (NCIM) Bill, 2018, (2) The National Commission for Homoeopathy, (NCH) Bill, 2018 and (3) The National Commission for Yoga and Naturopathy Bill, 2018. (c):	At present, there is no Central legislation to regulate the education and practice of Naturopathy. However, a Naturopathy Certification Board was constituted by the Ministry in February, 2020 to look after the registration of Naturopathy professionals.
