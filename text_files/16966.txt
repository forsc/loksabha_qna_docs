                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SHIPPING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

825






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Growth Rate of Shipping Industry




P. Ravindhranath














                        Will the Minister of





SHIPPING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the annual growth rate of shipping industry across the country as compared to the world shipping industry during the last three years, country-wise;(b)	whether the Government has any proposal to increase the growth of the indigenous shipping industry through National Maritime Development Programme (NMDP); (c)	if so, the details thereof; and(d)	the list of ports which are under the purview of approved implementation of “Sagarmala” Programmes towards privatization and the time frame fixed for such implementation?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) the details of the annual growth rate of shipping industry across the country as compared to the world shipping industry during the last three years, country-wise are as follows:(i) The total number of Indian seafarers who were employed on Indian or foreign flag vessels increased as under:<pre>Year	 Total No. of seafarers engaged	Growth %2017	 154349	7.232018	208799	35.272019	234886	12.49                                 </pre>The data of seafarers employed country-wise is not maintained.  (ii) The number of vessels under Indian flag have increased from 1313 as on 01.04.2017 to 1431 as on 01.04.2020.(iii) The tonnages of vessels under Indian flag have increased from 11.55 MT as on 01.04.2017 to 12.68MT as on 01.04.2020. The comparative position indicating the no. of vessels and tonnage for last three years is as below:<pre>As on 	Coastal	Overseas	Total	No. of vessels 	Gross tonnage 	No. of vessels 	Gross tonnage	No. of vessels	Gross Tonnage01.04.2017	908	1.52MT	405	10.02MT	1313	11.55MT01.04.2018	934	1.48MT	448	11.10MT	1382	12.58MT01.04.2019	947	1.50MT	458	11.28MT	1405	12.78MT01.04.2020	975	1.48MT	456	11.20MT	1431	12.68MT                                                </pre>(iv) The growth rate of World shipping along with growth rate in India, as ascertained from Indian National Shipowners’ Association, is as below;Growth Rate of World and India During 2018-2020 (In 1000 dwt)<pre>Country	1st Jan 2018	1st Jan 2019	1st Jan 2020World Fleet	1833549	1881589	1961597% of Growth  	3.45	2.6	4.3Indian Fleet	18792	19175	19372% of Growth	9.86	2.04	1.03                                              </pre>(b) & (c): In order to make the shipping industry more attractive and competitive, the Government has taken various measures in recent years. These are reduction in GST from 18% to 5% on bunker fuel used in Indian flag vessels; providing cargo support to  Indian shipping industry through Right of First Refusal (RoFR); allowing shipping enterprises based in India to acquire ships abroad and flag them in the country of their convenience; bringing parity in the tax regime of Indian seafarers employed on Indian flag ships vis-à-vis those on foreign flag ships;  removal of licensing requirement for chartering of foreign registered ships for  encouraging coastal movement of agriculture and other commodities, fertilizers,   EXIM laden Transshipment Containers and Empty containers, etc.(d) There is no Major port under purview of the approved implementation of ''Sagarmala'' programmes towards privatization.                                          *****
