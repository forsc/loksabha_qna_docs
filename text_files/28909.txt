                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

324






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.11.2021






Fishers Opinion on Indian Marine Fisheries (IMF) Bill




Abdul Majeed Ariff














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY & DAIRYING??????????, ??????? ?? ????? ?????? be pleased to state:(a)	whether the Government has held any stakeholder consultation with regard to the Indian Marine Fisheries (IMF) Bill, 2021, if so, the details thereof; (b)	whether the Government has translated the said Bill to any of the regional languages and circulated the same to solicit the opinion of traditional fishers on the Bill, if not, the reasons therefor; and (c)	whether it is true that the said Bill is fundamentally against the interests of the traditional marine capture fishers, fish workers, and fisherwomen in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a)	Yes, Sir. The Department of Fisheries in the Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying has held extensive consultations with stakeholders on the Draft Indian Marine Fisheries (IMF) Bill, 2021. Government of coastal States and Union Territories (UTs), Members of Parliament (LokSabha and Rajya Sabha) from coastal areas, Fishermen Associations/Federations/NGOs as well as Central Government Departments/Ministries have been consulted on the said draft Bill.(b)	The draft Indian Marine Fisheries (IMF) Bill, 2021 has been translated into Bengali,Gujarati,Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Odia, Tamil andTeluguand uploaded on the website of Department of Fisheries, Government of Indiaand also circulated to all coastal States and UTs.(c)	No, Sir, The Draft Indian Marine Fisheries (IMF) Bill, 2021is primarilyaimed to promote the livelihood and socio-economic well-being of traditional and small-scale fishers.*****
