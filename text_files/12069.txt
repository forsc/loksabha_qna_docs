                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

999






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2020






Mega Parks for Ayurvedic Medicines




 Dharambir Singh














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH)  be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government proposes to set up mega park for the production of ayurvedic medicines in Haryana as part of the measures to promote indigenous medicines; (b) if so, the details thereof; (c) whether the Government has invited private investors for setting up each park; (d) if so, the number and details of investors who have shown interest in it till date; and (e) the budget earmarked for setting up such parks?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to (e):   As per the information received from Haryana State Directorate of AYUSH, there is no proposal in pipeline to setup mega park for the production of Ayurvedic Medicines.  Ministry of AYUSH implemented a Central Sector Scheme for Development of Common Facilities for AYUSH Industry Clusters during the period of 11th and 12th Five Year Plans. The scheme intended to provide financial assistance to Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) registered as a company formed by a group of at least 15 AYUSH enterprises located within 100 kilometers radius.      The funding pattern was in the ratio of 60% of the project cost subject to maximum of Rs. 15.00 crore as grant in aid and 40% to be arranged by SPV through equity and borrowing from Banks/Financial Institutions and other sources for setting up common facilities of processing, manufacturing, testing, standardization, adoption of new technologies, scientific studies, marketing infrastructure, brand development, promotion etc. to encourage creation of social capital for collective initiatives in an industrial state, zone, park or area in a state. Details of the financial support provided under the scheme to set up ten AYUSH clusters are given in the annexure.  In the current financial year, budget provision of Rs. 1.00 Crore existed at BE and Rs. 33.00 lakhs at RE.
