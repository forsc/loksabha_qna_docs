                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1355






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.07.2021






Garib Kalyan Rojgar Abhiyan




Malook Nagar














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of migrant labourers provided with employment under the ‘Garib Kalyan Rojgar Abhiyan‘ alongwith the amount of wages fixed thereunder;(b) 	if so, the details thereof, State-wise and districtwise; and(c) 	the details of the work done under this ‘Abhiyan‘such as rural accomodation for poor, plantation, provision of potable water through Jal Jeevan Mission, Panchayat Bhawan, community toilet, rural mandis, rural roads, cattle shed and construction work of Aanganwadi Bhawan etc., State-wise and district-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a)&(b):	Government of India had launched a campaign know as Garib Kalyan Rojgar Abhiyaan (GKRA) of 125 days on 20th June, 2020 to boost employment and livelihood opportunities for returnee migrant workers and similarly affected citizens in rural areas, in the wake of COVID-19 pandemic. The objectives of the Abhiyaan were to provide immediate employment & livelihood opportunities to the distressed, to saturate the villages with public infrastructure and creation of livelihood assets to boost the income generation activities and enhance long term livelihood opportunities by giving focus on 25 works (including works under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS) in 116 selected districts across 6 States of Bihar, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Rajasthan and Uttar Pradesh. A total of 12 Ministries/Departments of Government of India has participated in the Abhiyaan. There is no single wage rate fixed for the worker. Every work was having its own wage rates applicable. A total of about 50.78 crore person-days employment has been generated during the Abhiyaan with a total expenditure of Rs. 39,293 crore. State-wise detail of employment provided is given at Annexure-I and district-wise details of employment provided Garib Kalyan Rojgar Abhiyaan is given at Annexure-II.(c):	The State-wise & district-wise details of 25 works done under the Abhiyaan are given at Annexure-III. The list of 25 focused works is given below:1.	Community Sanitary Complex2.	Gram Panchayat Bhawan3.	Works under 15th FC funds4.	National Highway works5.	Water conservation & Harvesting works6.	Construction of Wells7.	Plantation (including through CAMPA funds)8.	Horticulture9.	Anganwadi Centers10.	Rural housing11.	Rural connectivity works12.	Railway works13.	Works under Shyama Prasad Mukherjee Rurban Mission14.	PM KUSUM works15.	Laying of Optical Fiber Cable under Bharat Net16.	Works under Jal Jeevan Mission17.	Works under PM Urja Ganga Project18.	Training through KVKs for livelihoods19.	Works through District Mineral Funds20.	Solid and liquid waste management works21.	Farm ponds22.	Cattle sheds23.	Goat sheds24.	Poultry sheds25.
