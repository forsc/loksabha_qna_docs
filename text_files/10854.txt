                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4201






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2019






Funds to Prevent HIV Disease




Vishnu Dayal Ram














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) the funds allocated for prevention, care and treatment of HIV/AIDS during the last three years, State/UT-wise; (b) whether the funds being allocated to States for the purpose have been reduced by the Central Government; (c) if so, the reasons therefor;(d) the factors considered for earmarking funds for prevention, care and treatment of HIV/AIDS, State-wise; and (e) whether the HIV prevalence rate in the North Eastern States is higher than the average of India and if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to (c):   The overall funds allocated to the States/UTs under National AIDS Control Program (NACP) have increased from 913.10 crore in 2016-17 to 915.20 crore in 2017-18 and to 958.40 crore in 2018-19. State/UT wise allocation under NACP in the last three years is at Annexure.   Over and above the funds allocated to States/UTs, HIV Test Kits and Anti-Retroviral Drugs are procured centrally and supplied to States/UTs.  (d):   The funds to the States/UTs under the NACP are allocated in consultation with States/UTs AIDS Control Societies as per their Annual Action Plan. The Annual Action Plan takes into account the local situation across prevention, detection, treatment and retention services keeping community in the center and supported by critical enablers of laboratory services, strategic information and program management. (e):    Yes.   As per the Government HIV Estimations Report for 2017, the HIV prevalence rate among 15-49 years old population in the North Eastern States is 0.24% in comparison to the national average of 0.22%. The HIV prevalence rate in the States of Manipur, Mizoram and Nagaland is 1.43%, 2.04% and 1.15% respectively. This is on account of high-risk behavior denoted by the injecting drug uses and unsafe sexual practices.
