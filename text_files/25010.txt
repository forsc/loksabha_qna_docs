                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

852






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2021






Green Environment




Anil Firojiya














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has signed an agreement with any other country in the direction of green environment;(b)	if so, the name of the country and the nature of the said agreement;(c)	whether any advisory has been issued regarding import of live exotic species and to streamline the process of its maintenance; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) and (b):	 Government of India through the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, has signed Memorandum of Understanding (MoUs) / Memorandum of Cooperation (MoCs) / Agreements with 23 countries, for bilateral cooperation in the field of environment, forests and climate change.  The countries are Australia; Austria; Bangladesh; Bhutan; Brazil; Canada; China; Cyprus; Denmark; Egypt; Finland; France; Israel; Japan; Mauritius; Morocco; Netherlands; Russia; Sweden; Switzerland; Turkmenistan; USA & Vietnam.  MoU / MoC / Agreements are umbrella arrangements under which bilateral cooperation is carried out with the said country in the environmental focal areas.     (c) and (d):	Yes, Sir.  Considering the significance of import and export of exotic live species, the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change had issued an advisory to streamline the process for import and possession of exotic live species in India on 16th July 2020, including, inter alia, developing an inventory of exotic live species in India through Voluntary Disclosure Scheme; Procedure for Import of exotic live species; Registration / Declaration of progenies of the imported exotic live species through the online portal “PARIVESH”, etc. ***
