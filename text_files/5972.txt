                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5491






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2019






National Medical Commission Bill




Y. S. Avinash Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government has observed that there are some shortcomings in the proposed National Medical Commission (NMC) Bill, if so, the details thereof; (b) whether the Government has received any representation regarding the noted shortcomings and remedial measures for incorporation; (c) if so, the details thereof; and (d) the steps being taken by the Government to revise the NMC Bill?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to (d):     Based on the recommendations of the Group of Experts (GoE) headed by Late Prof. Ranjit Roy Chaudhary and the Department Related Parliamentary Standing Committee on Health & Family Welfare, a four member committee headed by Vice Chairman NITI Aayog framed the National Medical Commission Bill, after due consultation with stakeholders and inviting comments from public. The Bill provides for constitution of National Medical Commission in place of Medical Council of India. The draft Bill was re-examined by a Group of Ministers (GoM) constituted for the purpose. The GoM had approved the draft Bill with some modification. The National Medical Commission Bill, 2017 after having been approved by the Cabinet was introduced in the Lok Sabha on 29th December, 2017 and was subsequently referred to the Parliamentary Standing Committee on Health & Family Welfare. The Committee, after due consultation with all stakeholder, had presented its 109th report on the NMC Bill, 2017 to both the Houses of Parliament on 20th March, 2018. Most of the recommendation of the Committee were accepted and based on those recommendations the Ministry had moved official amendments to the Bill. However, the Bill could not be passed and got lapsed on the dissolution of 16th Lok Sabha. Now, the National Medical Commission Bill 2019 has been introduced in Lok Sabha on 22nd July, 2019.………………….
