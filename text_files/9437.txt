                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2915






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






Pollution in Ayad River




Arjunlal Meena














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether polluted water and industrial waste are being dumped in Ayad river and polluting the Udaisagar lake in Udaipur in Rajasthan; (b) if so, the steps taken by the Government to check the said pollution; and (c) the funds allocated for this purpose?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)As informed by Water Resources Department, Rajasthan, the point- wise reply is as under:-(a)  Yes, polluted water (sewage) is being dumped in Ayad river, thereby, in Udaisagar lake of Udaipur district in Rajasthan.(b) & (c)   Efforts being made by different agencies to check pollution are:-(i) 	Demarcation of river boundary by Urban Improvement Trust (UIT) and Municipal Corporation, Udaipur.(ii) 	Ayad river cleaning drive has been carried out with the support of Industrial houses by district administration, UIT and Municipal Corporation.(iii) 	Abatement of Ayad river with laying of trunk sewer and trapping of all 139 drains falling in to it under Rajasthan Urban Infrastructure Development Program (RUIDP) project costing       Rs.126 crore.(iv) 	Construction of 25, 10 & 5 Million Litres per Day (MLD) Sewage Treatment Plants (STPs) under Hybrid Annuity Model costing Rs.79.99 crore.  *****
