                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

629






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2021






Pace of Road Construction




Nama Nageswara Rao














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government will be able to achieve the ambitious road construction target of 40 km per day set for the current fiscal year in view of the prevailing Covid pandemic and lack of participation from the private sector; (b) if so, the details thereof along with the average road construction till June 2021, State-wise; (c) the present status of Yadgiri- Warangal four-laning project in Telangana which was approved in December 2015 with anticipated completion time of November 2018; (d) the details of the time and cost overrun in this project and reasons for delay in completing the project; and (e) the revised time by which the project is likely to be completed?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a)	The Ministry has set a target of construction of 12,000 km of National Highways in the Financial Year 2021-22.(b)	State-wise length of National Highways constructed in the year 2021-22 till June 2021 is at Annexure-I.(c)	, (d) & (e)	The original scheduled completion date of the Yadgiri-Warangal four-laning project in Telanganawas 28.11.2018. However, the project got delayed due to delay in land acquisition, Court intervention, poor mobilization of resources by Contractor and additionalworks of 4 VUPs on the Connecting roads.Sanctioned Cost of the Project is Rs.1906.97 crore and Expenditure as on 15.07.2021 is Rs.1626.17 crore.Provisional Completion Certificate of the project has beenissued on 30.09.2020.
