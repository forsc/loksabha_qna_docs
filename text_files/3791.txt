                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3508






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Impact of EWS Reservation




Su Thirunavukkarasar














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has passed constitutional amendments during January, 2019 forgranting 10% quota in jobs and admission for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS)candidates across all classes;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government has received any representation from the affected students orparents of the student community due to implementation of this 10% EWS quota foradmission in PG professional courses;(d) if so, the details thereof;(e) whether the Government has any proposal to increase the seats in colleges anduniversities including private ones to compensate the 10% EWS quota to adjust the othercommunity students for admission in various colleges and various courses; and(f) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) & (b): Yes, Sir. The Articles 15(6) and 16(6) have been inserted in the Constitution, videthe Constitution (One Hundred and Third Amendment) Act, 2019. This enables the State toprovide the benefits of reservation on preferential basis to the Economically Weaker Sections(EWSs) in civil posts and services in the Government of India and admission in EducationalInstitutions. In pursuance of the Constitution (One Hundred and Third Amendment) Act, 2019and according to the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment OM dated 17.01.2019,Department of Higher Education and Department of Personnel and Training have issuedOrders dated 17.01.2019 and 19.01.2019 providing 10 % reservation in admission to CentralEducational Institutions and Central Government posts and services respectively.(c) & (d): No, Sir. The Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD) has not receivedany representation from the affected students or parents of the student community due toimplementation of this 10% EWS quota for admission in PG professional courses.(e) & (f): Instructions have been issued by MHRD for all the Central Educational Institutions(CEIs) for increasing the total number of seats, over a period of two years, to provide 10%reservation for EWS, without adversely affecting the proportionate seats of ScheduledCastes/ Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes. In this regard, an amount of Rs.4315.15 Crores has been sanctioned for creation of additional 2,14,766 seats (1,18,983additional seats during 2019-20 and 95,783 additional seats during 2020-21) in 158 CEIs.
