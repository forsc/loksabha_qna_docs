                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

421






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.11.2021






National Sports Awards




Amol Ramsing Kolhe













                                                    Sunil Dattatray Tatkare
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                








                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the number of sportspersons who have been shortlisted and given Khel Ratna National Sports Awards for the year 2021 by the Selection Committee;  (b) the details of norms formulated/criteria fixed for selection of sportspersons for receiving National Sports Awards;  (c) whether the Government has also awarded special honours and cash award to sportspersons who have brought glory to the country; (d) if so, the amount of cash award announced and given to sportspersons during each of the last three years and the current year; (e) whether the Government is providing any assistance for livelihood to sportsperson after their retirement or at elderly age, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and  (f) the details of facilities provided to sports persons under Khelo India Programme during each of the last three years and the current year?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER FOR YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS[SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR] (a) Sir, the details of sportspersons who have been given the Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award for the year 2021 are: <pre>S. No.	Name of the sportsperson (S/Shri)	Discipline1.	 	Neeraj Chopra 	Athletics2.	 	Ravi Kumar 	Wrestling3.	 	Ms Lovlina Borgohain	Boxing4.	 	Sreejesh P. R. 	Hockey 5.	 	Ms Avani Lekhara	Para Shooting6.	 	Sumit Antil	Para Athletics 7.	 	Pramod Bhagat	Para Badminton 8.	 	Krishna Nagar	Para Badminton 9.	 	Manish Narwal	Para Shooting 10.	 	Ms Mithali Raj	Cricket 11.	 	Sunil Chhetri	Football 12.	 	Manpreet Singh	Hockey </pre> (b): The details of norms formulated/criteria fixed for selection of sportspersons for receiving various National Sports Awards are at Annexure–I. (c) and (d): Yes, the Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports gives cash awards to sportspersons for their winning medals in international sports events in the form of incentive under the ‘Cash Awards to medal winners in international sports events and their coaches’ scheme.  The amount of cash award given to sportspersons during each of the last three years and the current year (as on 26.11.2021) is :<pre>S. No.	Year	Number of sportspersons given cash award	Total amount of cash award (in crore Rs.)1	2018-19	307	Rs. 24.86 crore2	2019-20	599	Rs. 44.96 crore3	2020-21	393	Rs. 15.48 crore4	2021-22	74	Rs. 16.41 crore </pre>(e) Yes, sir. This Ministry is providing assistance for livelihood to sportspersons after their retirement in the form of monthly pension under the scheme ‘Sports Fund for Pension to Meritorious Sportspersons’.  Under the scheme, sportspersons who are Indian citizens and have won medals in Olympic Games, Commonwealth Games, Asian Games and World Cup / World Championship (in Olympic and Asian Games disciplines) and Paralympic Games, are given lifelong monthly pension ranging from Rs. 12,000/- to 20,000/-, after they attain the age of 30 years or retire from active sports, whichever is later. The pension payments are made through Life Insurance Corporation(LIC) of India, for which Ministry purchases annuities for individual pensioners by making onetime lump sum payment to LIC.At present, 821 sportspersons are getting lifelong pension under the scheme. (f)      Under the Khelo India Scheme implemented by the Sports Authority of India (SAI), there are various verticals which provide elaborate facilities to sportspersons:(i) The ‘Talent Search and Development’ verticals of the Scheme is for identification and nurturing sporting talent to attain excellence at international levels.  (ii) As of now, 2964 Khelo India Athletes (KIAs) selected in 21 disciplines including para sports are being trained at 237 academies accredited across the country, under the ‘Support to National / Regional / State Sports Academies’ vertical of the Scheme.  A sum of Rs.6.28 lakh per annum per athlete is being provided to these academies as assistance.(iii) 28 centres in 27 states / UTs are being upgraded to international standard with financial support for manpower, sports equipment, sports science etc. under the vertical ‘State Level Khelo India Centres’ of the Scheme as ‘Khelo India States Centre of Excellence’. (iv) All centres notified as Khelo India Centres are eligible to receive Rs.5 lakh per discipline as one time grant and Rs.5 lakh per discipline as recurring grant.  As of now, 453 Khelo India Centres across the country have been established. (v) Khelo India Scheme also promotes rural / indigenous and tribal games providing support to Mallakhamb, Kalaripayyattu, Gatka and Thang-Ta, and 283 medal winners of these games have received scholarship (Rs.10 lakh per month per athlete) until the first quarter of the current year.  (vi) 267 infrastructure projects for sports have also been sanctioned under the Khelo India Scheme all over the country.****
