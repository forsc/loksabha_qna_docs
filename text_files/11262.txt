                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEAVY INDUSTRIES AND PUBLIC ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

293






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2020






Crash Test of Cars




Devusinh Jesingbhai Chauhan














                        Will the Minister of





HEAVY INDUSTRIES AND PUBLIC ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware that recently some popular brand of cars fail the crash test as per Global New Car Assessment Programme;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether it is true that during the test all the popular cars were rated zero star for safety;(d) whether the Government will issue instructions to car manufacturers to stop production of cars rated zero star for safety and if so, the details thereof;(e) whether the Government will expedite its decision to mandate front and side impact crash tests for all cars to save the life of occupants; and(f) the Government''s view point in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF HEAVY INDUSTRIES & PUBLIC ENTERPRISES                   (SHRI PRAKASH JAVADEKAR)(a) to (d): The  car crash test as per Global New Car Assessment Program (Global NCAP) are voluntary assessments and there is no provision under Central Motor Vehicle Rule (CMVR) for compliance to these Global NCAP test protocols.  The information on these tests is available on Global NCAP website http://www.globalncap.org/results.(e) & (f):  The following frontal and lateral safety standards (crash related standards) have been notified since October 2017 for all new car models and from October 2019 for all the models manufactured in India. (SO 1139 (E) dated 28thApril 2015)i.	AIS 098 - Requirements for the Protection of the Occupants in the event of an Offset Frontal Collision (Technically aligned with UNECE R 94)ii.	AIS 099 - Approval of Vehicles with regards to the Protection of the Occupants in the event of a Lateral Collision (Technically aligned with UNECE R 95)In addition, Govt. of India has also mandated other safety requirements like driver air bag, Seat Belt reminders, over speed alert system, manual over ride for the passenger car doors and Rear parking assist.                                         --------------
