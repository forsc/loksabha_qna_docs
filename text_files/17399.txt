                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

692






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme




Dushyant Singh













                                                    Pinaki Misra
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the funds sanctioned and disbursed under Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) to various MSMEs  and the eligibility criteria for the same; (b)	total amount of credit disbursed to MSMEs for each category, category-wise;(c)	 the average credit disbursed to MSMEs, the number of MSMEs which were disbursed credit and total number of MSMEs registered in the country;(d)	the amount of credit disbursed to MSMEs industry across the country, sector-wise;(e)	the number of MSMEs that are currently operating at fifty percent capacity or greater;(f)	whether the Government would consider expanding ECLGS to cover first-time borrowers and cooperative banks and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefore; and (g)	whether the Government would consider providing direct payroll assistance to MSMEs given the impact of COVlD-19 and if not, the reasons therefore ?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NITIN GADKARI)(a) to (d):	The Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) is being implemented through the National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company Ltd. (NCGTC) which is under the administrative control of Department of Financial Services (DFS). Under this scheme, 100% guarantee is provided by National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company (NCGTC) to Member Lending Institutions (MLIs), for extending credit facility to eligible borrowers including MSMEs,  up to 20% of the borrower’s total outstanding credit up to Rs. 50 crore, excluding off-balance sheet and non-fund based exposures, as on 29th February, 2020.  DFS has reported that as on 09.09.2020, an amount of Rs. 163,103 crore has been sanctioned to 42,01,060  borrowers and an amount of   Rs. 117,885 crore has been disbursed to  25,01,216 borrowers under the scheme.  Details of the state- wise, bank-wise loans disbursed by the Public Sector Banks is at Annexure I.  (e):	Most of the MSMEs are in informal sector; therefore, data pertaining to capacity utilisation of MSMEs are not available. (f):	In the present form, the Scheme is valid only for existing customers on the books of the Member Lending Institutions (MLI). Borrower accounts should be classified as regular, SMA-0 or SMA-1 as on 29.2.2020.  Ministry of MSME has a parallel scheme viz. Credit Guarantee Scheme for Micro and Small Enterprises for collateral free credit up to Rs.200 lakh. This scheme is also open for the first time borrowers. (g):	Government of India under Atmanirbhar Bharat package has already announced measures such as ‘Extending the Employees Provident Fund Support for business and organised workers for salary months of June, July and August 2020 and ‘EPF Contribution has been reduced for Employers and Employees for 3 months to 10% from 12% for all establishments covered by EPFO for 3 months’.
