                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4357






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.07.2019






One-Stop-Centres




Thomas Chazhikadan













                                                    Sunil Kumar Mondal
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether One-Stop-Centres has been setup in Kottayam district of Kerala, if so, the details thereof; (b)	the total funds allocated, released, sanctioned and utilised under the said scheme along with the percentage of funds utilised by it; (c)	whether the Government has proposed to upgrade or introduce a new scheme to improve the condition of women affected by violence of any kind under One-Stop-Centre; and (d)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT (SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a) 	& (b): One Stop Centre (OSC) at Kottayam district of Kerala has been approved under One Stop Centre Scheme. An amount of Rs.6,91,075/- has been sanctioned and released for setting up of OSC and Rs.24,34,686/- has also been released to Kottayam district for construction of the OSC building during the year 2018-19.   (c) 	& (d):  Since the OSC Scheme is a comprehensive scheme which provide for a range of services for violence affected women including police facilitation, legal counselling, psycho-social counselling, medical aid and temporary shelter in an integrated manner under one roof to women affected by violence, Government is currently not considering to upgrade or introduce a new scheme.    ********
