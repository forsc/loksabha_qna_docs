                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEAVY INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3204






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






Critical Power Train Components of EVs




Nama Nageswara Rao














                        Will the Minister of





HEAVY INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is aware of the challenges in the supply chain for critical power train components of Electric Vehicles (EVs) and if so, the details thereof;(b)	the steps taken by the Government to promote hybrid/electric technology in transportation in order to give boost to EV supply chain; and(c)	whether the Government proposes to give more thrust on e-mobility under the smart city project, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR HEAVY INDUSTRIES(SHRI KRISHAN PAL GURJAR)(a):	Sir, in order to encourage manufacturing of Advanced Automotive Products (AAT) for Auto Sector including critical power train components of electric vehicles, the Government has approved Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme on 15th September, 2021 as per details as notified at ANNEXURE-I.(b):	Following steps have been taken by the Government for adoption of electric vehicles in the country:i.	the Ministry of Heavy Industries formulated a Scheme Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of (Hybrid &) Electric Vehicles in India (FAME India) Scheme in 2015 to promote adoption of electric/ hybrid vehicles (xEVs) in the country.  At present, Phase-II of FAME India Scheme is being implemented for a period of 5 years w.e.f. 01st  April, 2019 with a total budgetary support of Rs. 10,000 crores. ii.	The Government on 12th May, 2021 approved a Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme for manufacturing of Advanced Chemistry Cell (ACC) in the country in order to bring down prices of battery in the country.  Drop in battery price will result in cost reduction of electric vehicles.iii.	Electric Vehicles are covered under Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme for Automobile and Auto Components, which was approved on 15th September, 2021 with a budgetary outlay of Rs. 25,938 crore for a period of five years.iv.	GST on electric vehicles has been reduced from 12% to 5%; GST on chargers/ charging stations for electric vehicles has been reduced from 18% to 5%.-2-v.	Ministry of Road Transport & Highways (MoRTH) announced that battery-operated vehicles will be given green license plates and be exempted from permit requirements.vi.	MoRTH issued a notification advising states to waive road tax on EVs, which in turn will help reduce the initial cost of EVs.(c):	The Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs has informed that under Smart Cities Mission (SCM), efficient urban mobility and public transport is one of core infrastructure elements of a Smart City.  Some cities have implemented projects in e-mobility sector that include procurement of e-buses for public transport and e-rickshaws to enhance Intermediate Public Transport (IPT) share.  In addition, for better solid waste management, some Smart Cities are using e-vehicles for waste collection and transportation."******
