                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

771






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.11.2019






Implementation of AMRUT Scheme in Maharashtra




Manoj Kishorbhai Kotak














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is implementing the Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) scheme in 48 cities in Maharashtra including Mumbai;(b)	if so, the details of activities carried out under the scheme so far in all the cities specially in Mumbai;(c)	the funds sanctioned for these projects; and(d)	the time frame targeted to complete the projects?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) is being implemented in 44 cities, including Navi Mumbai and Greater Mumbai, of the State of Maharashtra.(b)	Under AMRUT, Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs has approved State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs)amounting to Rs.7,759.32 crore of the State of Maharashtra for basic infrastructure development in water supply, sewerage and septage management, storm water drainage, green area and parks. The approved SAAPs includes committed Central Assistance of Rs.3,534.08 crore. The State of Maharashtra has taken up 198 projects worth Rs.7,825.66 crore in 43 cities.  No project has been taken up in the city of Greater Mumbai.  Cost of projects taken up in excess of approved SAAPs will be borne by the State itself. City-wise and sector-wise details of projects taken up by the State of Maharashtra is placed as Annexure.(c)	Central Assistance of Rs. 1,532.52 crore has been released to the State of Maharashtra for AMRUT Projects.(d)	AMRUT was launched on 25June, 2015for a period of 5 years and will end on 31 March, 2020. Projects under AMRUT are envisaged to be completed within the Mission period.                                       *****
