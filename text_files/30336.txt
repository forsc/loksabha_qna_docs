Safety Audit of Dams




Sougata Ray













                                                    Dean Kuriakose
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has any concrete mechanism to conduct the safety audit of dams in the country which are constructed for various purposes like irrigation and hydel purposes; (b) if so, the details thereof; (c) the details of the agency which is conducting periodical safety audit; (d) the details of such dams which already completed 100 years and are threat to the life and property; (e) the action taken to de-commission such dams; and (f) whether the Government has received any proposal/petition to decommission the Mullapperiyar Dam from the Government of Kerala and if so, the details thereof along with the criteria for de-commissioning a dam?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI BISHWESWAR TUDU)(a) to (c).    Yes.At present the safety of the dams in India is the principal concern of the State Agencies/Organizations who own the dams. Dam owners generally carry out pre-monsoon and post- monsoon inspection of their dams. Some States have also constituted the Dam Safety Review Panel (DSRP) for comprehensive audit of their dams.Besides, Union Government has also constituted the National Committee on Dam Safety (NCDS) headed by Chairman, CWC. This body has representation from States also. The Committee oversees dam safety activities in various States/Organizations and suggests measures to bring dam safety practices in line with best practices in the world. This Committee is non statutory.The Dam Safety Bill, which has just been passed by the Rajya Sabha provides a statutory framework for surveillance, inspection, operation and maintenance of specified dams for prevention of dam failure related disasters.Contd…P/2//2//(d) As per the National Register of Large Dams (2019) compiled and maintained by the Central Water Commission, there are 227 large dams in India which are more than 100 years old (i.e. constructed on/before 1921). The list of these dams is enclosed at Annexure-I.(e)  Proposals for decommissioning of dams, if required, need to be formulated by the dam owners.(f) Yes. The proposal for decommission of existing dam and construction of a new dam was proposed by the State of Kerala during the deliberation held with the Empowered Committee  for Mullaperiyar dam, constituted on the direction of the Hon''ble Supreme Court in 2010. The Empowered Committee, in its report dated 23.04.2012, had considered, among other issues, between the States of Kerala  and  Tamil Nadu, the construction of a new dam proposed by the State of Kerala.  The issue of construction of new dam in the downstream region of Mulla Periyar Dam as per the offer made by Kerala to Tamil Nadu, had also been dealt by the Hon’ble Supreme Court in its judgment dated 07.05.2014 [in the case of Original Suit No. 3 of 2006 (State of Tamil Nadu versus State of Kerala & Anr.)].***** ANNEXURE-IANNEXURE REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (d) OF LOK SABHA UNSTARRED QUESTION DIARY NO. 1942 TO BE ANSWERED ON 09.12.2021 ON “SAFETY AUDIT OF DAMS”.Large Dams more than 100 yrs old- as per NRLD 2019S.N.
