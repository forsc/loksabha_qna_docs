                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1674






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Agrarian Distress




Dean Kuriakose














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 the details of the special packages approved  by  the  Union  Government  for selected  districts/areas  for  mitigation  of agrarian  distress  during  the  last  fifteen years;(b)  	the  present  status  of  such packages along with the details of total fund approved,  sanctioned, utilized and lapsed, the details thereof, package-wise;(c) 	 whether  the  Government  has received any proposal for revival of such packages for which implementation period  is already over, if so, the details thereof;(d) 	 whether  the  Government  is considering  to revive  Idukki package  of Kerala due to severe crisis in agriculture sector  after  flood  and  growing  farmer suicides in Idukki region of Kerala; and(e)	 if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER  OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (c):     As per available information, the special/rehabilitation packages approved by the Government of India (GoI) include inter-alia:(i)	A rehabilitation package of Rs. 16978.69 crore for 31 identified districts, in four States of Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra, Karnataka and Kerala was approved in 2006 to be implemented over a period of 3 years. The Government had extended the period for implementation of non credit components of the package by 2 more years i.e. up to 30th September, 2011. (ii)	During 2006-07, Government of India had sanctioned a rehabilitation package for a total amount of Rs.765.24 crore in order to mitigate the hardship of farmers in the suicide prone districts of Kerala State viz; Palakkad, Wayanad and Kasaragod.  This package has been implemented through District Collector Wayanad, Palakkad and Kasargode, Soil Conservation Department, Jalanidhi, Irrigation Department, Mission Director Soil Health Management (SHM), National Bank for Agricultural and Rural Development (NABARD), Local Self Government Department, Co-operation Department, Water Resource Department, Fisheries Department, Animal Husbandry and Rural Department and Agriculture Department.(iii)	      Kuttanad Package: -The scheme envisaged for the improvement of the livelihood of farmers in Kuttanad area.  (Alappuzha, Kottayam and Pathanamthitta districts).Contd…2/--2-(iv)	Idukki Package: -The scheme envisaged for the improvement of the livelihood of farmers in Idukki district.(v)	     Kasaragod package: -The scheme envisaged for the improvement of livelihood of farmers in Kasaragod district.(vi)	 A package of Rs.3094 crore for Bihar had been given by the Government of India in August, 2015.  The present status of the packages is at annexure-II.(vii)	A Special package for completion of Irrigation Projects to address agrarian distress in Vidharbha and Marathwada and other chronically drought prone areas of rest of Maharashtra has been approved during 2018-19 as per details given below: “8 Major and Medium Irrigation (MMI) projects and 83 Surface Minor Irrigation (SMI) projects with a balance cost of Rs. 13,651.607crore as on 01.04.2018 is planned to be completed in a phased manner up to 2023-24.  The targeted additional potential through these projects is 3.77 Lakh hectare.  During 2018-19, and 2019-20, Rs.500 crore and Rs.100 crore have been released so far.”(viii)	The Government of India in 2009 approved a special package for implementing drought mitigation strategies in Bundelkhand region at a cost of Rs.7266 crore comprising Rs.3506 crore for Uttar Pradesh and Rs.3760 crore for Madhya Pradesh, to be implemented over a period of 3 years starting from 2009-10. It was envisaged to provide an Additional Central Assistance (ACA) to the tune of Rs.3450 crore for implementation of the package. The share of Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh in ACA was envisaged to be Rs. 1596 crore and Rs.1854 crore respectively. The balance cost of the package was to be met by converging resources from ongoing central sector and centrally sponsored schemes. Further, keeping in view the demands from the Minister of State in the Ministry of Rural Development and other Members of Parliament from Bundelkhand, Government of India  approved an Additional Central Assistance of Rs.200 crore (Rs.100 crore each for the State Governments of Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh) to provide drinking water in the Bundelkhand region.	Department of Agriculture & Cooperation also agreed to provide an amount of Rs. 586.5 crore from on-going Centrally Sponsored Schemes/Programmes for the period  2009-2012 of XI Five Year Plan.	Government of India also approved continuation of Bundelkhand Special Package during the 12th  Plan period (2012-2017) under Backward Regions Grant Fund (BRGF) with an outlay of Rs. 4400 crore to address various issues of Bundlekhand region for sustaining foodgrain production and also for providing livelihood support in the region with overall objective of socio-economic development of area, besides, prevention of  rural to urban migration. This allocation was in addition to allocation of regular funds under Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH) scheme. Accordingly, a special package of Rs. 93.39 crore with Rs.56.03 crore as Government of India share was approved to Government of Kerala towards replanting of damaged horticulture crops in the flood affected areas of Kerala during 2018-19 under MIDH.             The entire GoI share of Rs.56.03 crore was released to Government of Kerala on 28.08.2018. State Horticulture Mission Kerala has informed that it has utilized Rs. 22.85 crore of GoI Share out of Rs. 24.93 crore received from Kerala State Finance and the remaining Rs. 27.01 crore out of total of Rs. 56.03 crore sanctioned by GoI is yet to be released by the Kerala State Finance. (xi)	Special Package for GAJA Cyclone: - GAJA Cyclone hit Tamil Nadu on 16.11.2018 and caused severe destruction in the districts of Nagapattinam, Tanjavur, Pudukottai, Tiruvarur, Sivagangai, Karur, Trichy, Dindigul, Madurai, Ariyalur, Cuddalore and Theni.              An amount of Rs. 123.24 crore including GoI share of Rs. 73.94 crore (Rs. 50.00 crore additional funds + Rs. 23.94 crore under flexi fund) was released to Government of Tamil Nadu as Special Package during 2018-19 under Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH).          Further, an amount of Rs. 92.00 crore has been approved to Coconut Development Board, Kochi towards Cutting & Removal of fallen /damaged coconut trees, distribution of Coconut seedlings and setting-up of nurseries. The entire amount was released to Government of Tamil Nadu during 2018-19. The State Government of Tamil Nadu has informed that funds sanctioned towards Gaja Cyclone Special Package were received from Tamil Nadu State Finance at the closure of the financial year and no expenditure could be incurred as the season for planting had been over in the affected Gaja fields and model code of conduct on account of General Elections to the Lok Sabha was also in force. It has further been informed that preliminary activities such as identification of beneficiaries, issue of work order, arrangements for raising pro tray vegetable, seedlings in the State Horticulture farms have been completed.Contd..4/--4-(xii)	In addition to above, Additional Financial Assistance(ACA), over and above State Disaster Response Funds (SDRF) is considered from National Disaster Response Funds (NDRF) for natural calamities on receipt of memorandum from state Government an in accordance with extent norms and procedures. The financial assistance under SDRF/NDRF is given by way of relief and not for composition of loss as suffered / claimed.          Assistance approved from NDRF for natural calamities coordinated i.e. drought, hailstorm pest attack and cold wave / frost since 2004-05 is at Annexure-I.         Details of funds released utilised and present status of various packages are at Annexure-II.(d) & (e):	  Government of Kerala requested the Government of India to extend the period of Idukki package for another two years. Government of India advised that the schemes mentioned under the Idukki package should be further proceeded under the schemes like Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) so that no hindrance in the implementation of the schemes focusing improvement of livelihood of the farmers in Idukki occurs.
