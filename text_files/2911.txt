                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEAVY INDUSTRIES AND PUBLIC ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2615






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.07.2019






Government/Public Investment in CPSUs




Manish Tewari














                        Will the Minister of





HEAVY INDUSTRIES AND PUBLIC ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the Government/ Public investment in Central Public Sector Undertakings (CPSUs) incorporated by Acts of Parliament, Navratna Companies and Mini Navratna Companies from 1.4.2014 to 31.3.2019, CPSU and year-wise;(b)	the details of sick CPSUs during the last five years, CPSU and year-wise;(c)	whether the Government is of the view that the Public Sector has a role to play in the economic development of the country and if so, the details thereof; and(d)	the Government’s perspective investment plan over the next five years in the Public Sector?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER FOR HEAVY INDUSTRIES AND PUBLIC ENTERPRISES(SHRI ARVIND GANPAT SAWANT)a)	      As per the information available in Public Enterprises Survey that is laid before the Parliament every year, the details of Central Government Investment in operating Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs) including Navranta & Miniratna CPSEs during the years 2014-15 to 2017-18 are given at Annex - I.b)	        DPE has issued Guidelines dated 29.10.2015 for “Streamlining the mechanism for revival and restructuring of sick/ incipient sick and weak Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs)". As per these guidelines, the CPSEs having negative net worth are considered as sick. As per information available with DPE, there were 52, 45, 48, 54 and 56 CPSEs having negative net worth as on 31.03.2014, 31.03.2015, 31.03.2016, 31.03.2017 and 31.03.2018 respectively. The details of CPSEs with negative net worth as on 31.03.2018 are given at Annex-II.(c & d) :    Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs) play a major role in fulfilling developmental priorities of the country. They contribute to macroeconomic objectives such as growth, advancement in manufacturing and technology, price stabilization etc., as well as to fulfill socio-economic objectives such as employment generation and skill development.                          CPSEs function under the control of administrative ministries. The decisions relating to investments in the CPSEs are taken by the concerned administrative ministries with the approval of competent authority considering techno-economic feasibility.*****
