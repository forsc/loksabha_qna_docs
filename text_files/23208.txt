                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4445






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






Biofloc Fish Farming




Balashowry Vallabbhaneni














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of steps taken by the Government to promote and push biofloc fish farming in the country; and (b)	the reasons why the Government is making it a demand-driven scheme and the same cannot be implemented in States where aquaculture farming is in abundance like in the State of Andhra Pradesh?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING:(SHRI PRATAP CHANDRA SARANGI)(a) and (b):  The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing a flagship scheme “Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY)- A scheme to bring about Blue Revolution through sustainable and responsible development of fisheries sector in India” with highest ever investment of Rs. 20,050 crore in the fisheries sector over a period of five years with effect from the current financial (2020-21) in all States/Union Territories including Andhra Pradesh.  The PMMSY inter-alia promotes adoption of technologies for enhancing production and productivity, post-harvest management including biofloc farming. The activities relating biofloc farming primarily implemented in association with the States/UTs on cost sharing basis under the PMMSY. The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying, based on the viable proposals received during the current financial year (2020-21) (as on date), has approved 994 units of biofloc units at a total investment of Rs. 10257 lakh  for 16 States and UTs.  *****
