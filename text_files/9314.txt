                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2856






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






Bypass Project in Mangalore City




Nalin Kumar Kateel














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the new Greenfield alignment for Mangalore City bypass project was undertaken under the Bharatmala Pariyojana Phase-I and if so, the details thereof;(b) 	whether the Government is aware that the proposed bypass between Mulki and Thokkottu is likely to facilitate commercial and heavy vehicles to move towards Bengaluru and Kasaragod and if so, the details thereof;(c) 	whether the Government has fixed any timeframe for completion of the said project and if so, the details thereof; and(d) 	the time by which work on the said project is likely to start?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI) (a)	Government of India had approved Bharatmala Pariyojana Phase-I in October, 2017 for development of highway stretches with an aggregate length of about 34,800 km (including 10,000 km residual NHDP stretches) at an estimated outlay of Rs. 5,35,000.00crores.  Under Bharatmala Pariyojana, 28 nos. Ring Roads as well as 191 nos. of choke points/congestion points have been identified for development including provision of intervention at Mangalore in the State of Karnataka. The preparation of Detailed Project Report (DPR) for intervention at Mangalore city (Ring Road/bypass) has been initiated.(b) to (d)	A bypass from chainage 348.000 of NH-66 (near Mulki) up to chainage 390.600 of NH-66 (near Talapady) has been envisaged. The preparation of Detailed Project Report (DPR) for this work has been initiated. Development of highway stretches is taken up post finalisation of alignment, cost estimates, land acquisition requirement etc. based on outcome of DPR/ Feasibility Study, project viability, inter-se-priority and availability of funds.*****
