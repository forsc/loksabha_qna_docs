                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    STATISTICS AND PROGRAMME IMPLEMENTATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2735






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.03.2021






Restarting of MPLAD Scheme




Agatha K. Sangma














                        Will the Minister of





STATISTICS AND PROGRAMME IMPLEMENTATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the stopping of MPLAD funds for two years has adversely affected the local development works in the backward States like North Eastern Region and Meghalaya;  (b) if so, whether the Government is considering to restart the MPLAD scheme from this financial year; and  (c) if not, the reasons therefor? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF STATISTICS AND PROGRAMME IMPLEMENTATION AND MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF PLANNING (RAO INDERJIT SINGH) (a) The Government has received references from Hon’ble Members of Parliament from across the country including North Eastern Region and Meghalaya that the development activities are also likely to get affected after the decisions of the Government not to operate MPLADS during FY 2020-21 and FY-2021-2022. However, the Government has not conducted any survey to assess the impact on local development works in the backward States like North Eastern Region and Meghalaya after the decisions of the Government not to operate MPLADS during FY 2020-21 and FY-2021-2022.  (b) No Sir. (c) The Government has decided not to operate the Members of Parliament Local Area Development Scheme (MPLADS) for two years, that is, during FY 2020-21 and FY 2021-22 and decided to place the MPLADS funds for FY 2020-21 and FY 2021-22 at the disposal of the Ministry of Finance for strengthening its efforts in managing the challenges of COVID-19 which has been declared as a global pandemic and a notified disaster in the country.                                                       *****
