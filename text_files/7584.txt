                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1181






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.11.2019






Heritage Sites Identified for Upgradation




Pradeep Kumar Singh














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of heritage sites  State/ UT-wise identified by theGovernment for overall development for Tourism point of view and wheresubstantial amount of work had since been undertaken and the detailsthereof, State/UT-wise;(b)	whether the Government plans to revive all the identified heritagesites and also to take up further upgradation of all such sites, and ifso, the details thereof including the action taken/proposed to be taken inthis regard;(c)	whether the Government had included any of the heritage site fromBihar; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE AND TOURISM(PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)	Archaeological Survey of India is responsible for protection, conservation and maintenance of  3691 monuments, archaeological sites and remains declared as of national importance in the country which is a continuous process, where essential work is executed as per the requirements of different sites and availability of resources and over all they are in good state of preservation. Further, besides conservation and maintenance of  monuments, archaeological sites and remains, providing basic facilities/amenities (e.g. drinking water, toilet blocks, facilities for physically challenged, pathways, cultural notice boards/signage, vehicle parking, cloak rooms, etc.) to the tourists visiting centrally protected monuments/sites are regular activities which the Archaeological Survey of India undertakes, as per needs and resources.  Improvement and upgradation of these public amenities is a continuous process.(b)to(d)	There are no such plan for revival of protected monuments as conservation work on them is executed as per archaeological norms and principle where main requirement is maintaining its originality, authenticity and integrity.  There are 70 number of protected monuments/sites of Archaeological Survey of India in Bihar. The essential conservation work and tourist amenities of these monuments are also attended
