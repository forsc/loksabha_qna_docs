                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

319






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Revenue Earnings under GST




Kuldeep Rai Sharma














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of revenue earnings under the Goods and Services Tax (GST) since its implementation, month-wise;(b) the details of revenue earnings one year prior to introduction of GST, month-wise;(c) the details of GST to be paid and already paid to various States/UTs including the Andaman and Nicobar Islands since implementation of GST, month and State/UT-wise;(d) whether it is a fact that GST compensation has gone up during thelast few years and if so, the details thereof;(e) whether the States/UTs are losing revenue due to GST; and(f) if so, the manner in which the Government is planning to go ahead in this regard to shore up GST revenue?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        (a) to (f): A Statement is laid on the table of the House.***** STATEMENT AS REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARA A TO F OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO 319 FOR ANSWER ON 15th JULY 2019(a) The month-wise gross collection of Goods and Services Tax (GST) for FY 2017-18, FY 2018-19 and FY 2019-20 are as under:(Figures in Rs. Crore)Month	2017-18	2018-19	2019-20April	--	103459	113865May	--	94016	100289June	--	95610	99,939July	21572	96483	August	95633	93960	September	94064	94442	October	93333	100710	November	83780	97637	December	84314	94726	January	89825	102503	February	85962	97247	March	92167	106577	Total	740650	1177369	314093Average	89885	98114	104698It is clear from the above table that the average gross GST collection in the FY 2018-19 has been Rs. 98,114 crores which is higher than the previous year’s average gross GST collection of Rs. 89,885 crores.(b) The Central indirect taxes [Customs +Central Excise +Service Tax] revenue earnings (month-wise) during one year prior to introduction of GST including revenue not subsumed in GST is given below: -(Rs. In crore)Month	Central indirect taxes [Customs +Central Excise +Service Tax] revenueJuly, 2016	66,878August, 2016	65,254Sept, 2016	71,623Oct, 2016	77,644Nov, 2016	69,534Dec, 2016	74,548Jan, 2017	73,678Feb, 2017	67,216March, 2017	91,152April, 2017	74,132May, 2017	73,100June, 2017	73,896(c) The details of total GST accrued to States/UTs including SGST and IGST Settlement (Regular & Provisional) month-wise and State-wise are as given in Annexure.(d)   The details of compensation released to the States including UTs of Delhi and Puducherry after implementation of GST w.e.f. 01.07.2017 are as per details given below: - Period for which compensation has been released	Amount (Rs. in  Crore) 2017-18 (Jul, 17 to Mar, 18)	481782018-19 (Apl, 18 to Mar, 19)	81177Total	129355(e) & (f) The total GST collection of the States/UTs for the year 2018-19 is Rs. 518447 crores against GST collection Rs. 291100 crores in the FY 2017-18(only Aug’17 to Mar’2018). The GST collection of the States/UTs has been showing steady improvement over the period of time. In addition, they are also assured a growth of 14% for a period of five years through payment of compensation by the Central Government.Concerted efforts have been made to improve tax compliance. Extensive automation of business processes, application of e-way bill mechanism, targeted action on compliance verification, enforcement based on risk assessment and proposed introduction of electronic invoice system are the steps taken for increasing the revenue collection.*****
