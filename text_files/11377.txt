                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

370






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2020






Sugamya Bharat Abhiyan




Pinaki Misra














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the targets for the Sugamya Bharat Abhiyan during the years 2016 to 2018 have been achieved and if so, the details thereof;(b)	whether there is any plan to expand the reach of the mission to all other Government departments, websites, documents and buildings;(c)	if so, the details of  implementation of the scheme, State-wise especially in Odisha; and;(d)	whether appropriate incentives are being designed to incentivise the private sector for the same and if so, the details thereof?  






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI KRISHANPAL GURJAR)(a) No Sir. Accessible India Campaign (Sugamya Bharat Abhiyan) was launched on 03.12.2015 as a nation-wide flagship campaign for achieving universal accessibility. The campaign focuses on enhancing the accessibility of built environment, transport system and information & communication eco-system to make the life of Persons with Disabilities more convenient.Observing the slow but steady  progress being made in implementation of Sugamya Bharat Abhiyan, revised deadlines have been extended to March, 2020 by the Central Advisory Board (CAB) Chaired by Hon’ble Minister of Social Justice Empowerment, the highest policy making body in the Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities.        Till 31.12.2019, under built up environment, Rs.354.45 Crores have been released to States/UTs to make 1058 identified buildings accessible. Out of these, 251 buildings have been made accessible. CPWD has made 998 central government buildings accessible.         Under the transportation system,709 A1, A&B category railway stations have been provided short term facilities and 603 railway stations have been provided long term accessible features. Furthermore, 35 International Airports and 55 Domestic Airports have been made partially accessible and out of 1,45,287 buses, 35,720 buses have been provided with basic accessibility features.          Under Information and Communication Technology ecosystem, 354 identified websites of States/UTs and  95 central government websites have been made accessible.(b) to (c) Yes Sir. All States/UTs governments including Odisha and Central Ministries/Departments have been requested to enforce the accessibility standards for making all public buildings barrier free and accessible.  They have been requested for strict implementation of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act (RPwD), 2016 with special efforts to make all services including transport and Information and Communication Technology, accessible as per accessibility standards namely Harmonized Guidelines and Space Standards for Barrier Free Built Environment for Persons with Disabilities and Elderly Persons, 2016 issued by Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA), Code of Practice and Bus Body Design and Approval, 2014 issued by Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH) and Guidelines for Indian Government Websites, 2016 issued by Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), which have been notified and circulated to all States/UTs.          The details of State wise implementation of Sugamya Bharat Abhiyan till 31.12.2019 under the targets of built-up environment and websites are at Annexure-I and Annexure-II.(d)  No, Sir. There is no scheme in the Central Government to incentivise the private sector in this regard.******
