                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3388






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.08.2021






Development of Ramayana Circuit




 Sunil Kumar Pintu














                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the progress of development of tourism in the country especially in Sitamarhi of Bihar; (b) 	the details of the amount allocated for the development of Ramayana circuit in Sitamarhi along with the amount spent, year-wise during the last three years; (c) 	whether there is any proposal to construct magnificent temple at Punauraham, Sitamarhi like Ayodhya temple and to develop the area as tourist centre; (d) 	if so, the details thereof along with the time by which Punauradham, Sitamarhi is likely to be developed in this way; and (e) 	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF TOURISM 			               (SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (e):	Development of tourism is primarily the responsibility of the concerned State Government/UT Administration; However Ministry of Tourism has sanctioned 76 projects for total amount of Rs.5588.45 Crore under Swadesh Darshan Scheme and 37 projects for total amount of Rs.1214.19 crore under ‘National Mission on Pilgrimage Rejuvenation and Spiritual, Heritage Augmentation Drive Scheme (PRASHAD)’ across the country including Bihar. In  addition,  projects  pertaining to  development of  tourism infrastructure  for a total amount of Rs.483.1 Crore were also sanctioned during 2012-13 till now under ‘Assistance to Central  Agencies  for Tourism Infrastructure Development Scheme’. Sanctioning of projects under the schemes is a continuous process.  The projects are identified for development in consultation with the State Governments/ UT Administrations and are sanctioned subject to availability of funds, submission of suitable detailed project reports, adherence to scheme guidelines and utilization of funds released earlier. No project in Punauradham, Sitamarhi is under consideration in the Ministry of Tourism.******
