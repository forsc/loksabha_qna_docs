                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4038






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Plantation of Trees




Ranjeeta Koli














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Union Government is implementing any scheme to give financial and technical assistance to the people in Rajasthan for plantation of trees; (b)	if so, the details thereof, scheme-wise; (c)	the total financial assistance allocated/sanctioned to the State Government of Rajasthan during the last three years, year-wise; and (d)	the number of trees planted with the said assistance during the last three years, year-wise?  






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) and (b) The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) implements two major afforestation schemes for increasing forest resources in the country, including Rajasthan, namely National Afforestation Programme (NAP) for tree plantation in degraded forests through people’s participation and National Mission for a Green India (GIM), for plantation on landscape basis. Besides, afforestation is a major activity under the Compensatory Afforestation Fund as per the provisions of the Compensatory Afforestation Fund Act, 2016 and the Rules made thereunder.  (c) and (d) During last three years, an amount of Rs. 3.35 crore has been released to Rajasthan under NAP. No fund has been released under GIM during this period to Rajasthan. Further, an amount of Rs. 1748.26 crore, as share of the State as per Compensatory Fund Act, 2016, has been transferred to State Compensatory Afforestation Management Planning Authority (CAMPA) of Rajasthan from National Authority of CAMPA during 2019 for taking up various activities including afforestation as per approved Annual Plans of Operation (APO).
