                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3566






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






Schemes for BTC Farmers




Naba (Hira) Kumar Sarania














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of various schemes run by the Ministry; (b) 	the schemes being operated for Assam and BTC farmers along with the details of funds allocated for schemes during the last three years; (c) 	the number of beneficiary farmers of BTC and Assam under farmer schemes, district-wise;(d) 	whether farmers face difficulties in availing bank loan along with the details of Government schemes formulated in this regard; and (e)  the details of arrangement made by the Government to provide training in new technology to the farmers of the country, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFAR E????????????????????????   (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)	List of some of the important beneficiary oriented schemes implemented by the Department is given in Annexure-I(b) to (c)	Beneficiaries of schemes are selected by the States and therefore, such details are available with them. However, funds allocated to Assam including for BTC farmers during last 3 years are given in Annexure-II.(d)	In order to ensure timely and affordable credit facilities for farmers, Department of Agriculture and Farmers’ Welfare is implementing the Interest Subvention Scheme (ISS) and Kisan Credit Card Saturation Drive. ISS is available to farmers availing short term crop loans up to Rs.3.00 lakh at an interest rate of 7% per annum for one year. Additional 3% subvention is also given to the farmers for prompt and timely repayment of loans; thus reducing the effective rate of interest to 4% per annum. The benefits of ISS have also been extended to farmers in allied activities like Animal Husbandry and Fisheries.	Department of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare in collaboration with Department of Financial Services and Banks is running KCC saturation drive since February, 2020 giving special focus on PM KISAN beneficiaries. The target of the drive is to cover 2.5 crore farmers under KCC. Government has taken the following steps to make the credit system easy for the farmers.(i)	 All the charges including processing fee, inspection, ledger folio charges, service charges for loans upto Rs.3.00 lakh were waived off. (ii)	 Collateral fee loan limit for short term agri-credit has been raised from Rs.1.00 lakh to Rs.1.60 lakh by RBl. (iii)	 State Governments have been advised to hold bank-wise and village wise camps to collect KCC application forms from eligible farmers and the applications collected therein to be submitted to the Bank''s branch. (iv)	 KCC is to be issued within 14 days from the receipt of completed application. The progress is to be monitored by State Level Bankers Committee (SLBC). (v)	The KCC has been extended to the farmers doing allied activities including Animal Husbandry, dairying and fisheries.  Till 30.07.2021, 2.31 crore farmers have  been issued KCC with sanction loan limit of Rs. 2,36,450 crore.(e)	Presently there are four numbers of Farm Machinery Training & Testing Institute(FMTTIs) at ,Budni (Madhya Pradesh), Hisar (Haryana), Anantapur (Andhra Pradesh) and Biswanath Chariali (Assam) imparting training to various categories of trainees ie farmers, women, youths. etc in the field of farm mechanization,	Moreover, a Centrally Sponsored Scheme “Support to State Extension Programmes for Extension Reforms”, popularly known as ATMA Scheme was launched in May, 2005. Presently ATMA is being implemented as a component of Sub-Mission on Agricultural Extension (SMAE) in 691 Districts of 28 states and 5 UTs.The aim of the scheme is to revitalize and augment  State’s extension system and to provide a farmer friendly extension services delivery to farmers for their capacity building in modern agricultural technologies and good agricultural practices in different thematic areas through various extension activities like Farmers Training, Demonstrations, Exposure Visits, Kisan Melas, mobilization of Farmers Interest Groups (FIGs) and setting up of Farm Schools. Under the scheme grants-in-aid is released to State/UT Governments. Details of trainees state wise for FMTTIs and ATMA are attached as Annexure-III.
