                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.09.2020






Most Favoured Nation Status




Manoj Kishorbhai Kotak














                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is planning for the withdrawal of Most Favoured Nation (MFN) status to China to reduce the export-import business with that country; (b)	if so, whether any assessment has been made by the Government regarding the loss after the withdrawal of MFN status to China;(c)	if so, the details thereof along with the details of the goods imported from and exported to China in the last three years; (d)	whether the Government is planning to support traders/manufacturer in AtmaNirbhar Bharat Yojana; and (e)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF  COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY  (SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL) (a):   No such proposal is under consideration at this time.   (b)  & (c) : Does not arise in view of (a) above. The details of import-export under the top 50 Principle Commodity groups with China during the last three years, April-July 2019 and April-July 2020 are attached as Annex-I and Annex-II.(d) & (e) Government has announced Aatma Nirbhar Package of Rs. 20.97 Lakh Crore to address the problems faced by the industry, along with  reforms in a number of sectors. The Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS), with a provision of Rs 3 Lakh Crore, is a 100% credit guarantee and envisages collateral free loans for MSMEs. Financial support has been envisaged for stressed MSMEs, with infusion of Rs. 20,000 Crore equity support through subordinate debt. A Fund of Funds has been created to infuse equity worth Rs. 50,000 Crore in the MSME Sector through setting up of a Rs. 10,000 Crore Corpus Fund. The Government has also issued the Public Procurement (Preference to Make in India) Order 2017, to promote participation of local suppliers / manufacturers in public procurement.
