                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3918






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Beautification of Ponds




Harish Dwivedi














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the name of the schemes being implemented for the maintenance and beautification of natural ponds in the country; and(b) 	the number of proposals for the maintenance and beautification of ponds from the State Government of Uttar Pradesh that have been approved under the said scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change (MoEF&CC) is currently implementing a centrally sponsored scheme namely, National Plan for Conservation of Aquatic Eco-systems (NPCA) for conservation and management of wetlands (includes natural ponds) in the country on cost sharing basis between Central Government and respective State Governments. The scheme covers various activities such as interception, diversion and treatment of wastewater, shoreline protection, lake front development, in-situ cleaning i.e. desilting & deweeding, storm water management, bioremediation, catchment area treatment, Lake Beautification, survey & demarcation, bio-fencing, fisheries development, weed control, biodiversity conservation, education and awareness creation, community participation, etc.Further, works related to water resources development & management are planned, funded, executed and maintained by the State Governments themselves as per their own resources and priorities. In order to supplement the efforts of the State Governments, Government of India provides technical and financial assistance to State Governments to encourage sustainable development and efficient management of water resources through various schemes and programmes such as Surface Minor Irrigation (SMI), Repair, Renovation and Restoration (RRR) of Water Bodies Schemes etc. under PradhanMantriKrishiSinchayeeYojana (PMKSY)-HarKhetKoPani (HKKP).(b) Under the scheme of NPCA, an amount of Rs. 151.53 crore has been released (central share) for conservation and management of 16wetlands in theState of Uttar Pradesh. Further, 2 clusters of 20 Water Bodies have been included during 2014-15 to 2015-16 from the State of Uttar Pradeshunder RRR of water bodies scheme of PMKSY (HKKP) with total estimated cost of ?49.5 crore and target irrigation potential of 3447 Ha. So far, Central Assistance (CA) of Rs. 16.40 crore has been released to state and 8 schemes have been reported complete with achievement of 2354 Ha. irrigation potential till date.****
