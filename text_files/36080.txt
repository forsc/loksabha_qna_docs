                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3330






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2022






IIP Institute




Prathap Simha













                                                    Umesh G. Jadhav
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is considering to make Indian Institute of Packaging (IIP) an institute of national importance; (b)	if so, whether the Government is considering to bring in innovations in packaging that can boost exports; (c) 	if so, whether there is any plan to start a new IIP Institute in Karnataka; and (d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a)     	     :    The Department of Commerce has prepared a draft Bill for according status of institute of National Importance to the Indian Institute of Packaging (IIP).(b)            : 	One of the key activities of IIP is to formulate packaging specifications and develop innovative and cost-effective packaging for domestic and export markets. The IIP works with the exporters to address their packaging needs and continuously provides solutions to them as per their requirements.(c) & (d) : 	The Department of Commerce has sanctioned an outlay of Rs 6.50 crore for setting up a centre of IIP at Sompura Industrial Area, Bengaluru, Karnataka".*****
