                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

173






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.02.2021






Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization




Raja Amareshwara Naik













                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                








                                                    Nisith Pramanik
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether as per Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM) guidelines, there is no mechanism to restrict participation of imported machinery; (b) 	if so, the reasons therefor; (c) 	whether India have been witnessing influx of agriculture machinery from foreign countries including China and Europe; (d) 	if so, the details thereof during the last three years; (e) 	whether many Government departments have already imposed conditions of local value addition as eligibility criteria for subsidy, whereas there are no such conditions in SMAM scheme; (f) 	if so, the reasons therefor and the remedial measures taken/being taken by the Government thereon; (g) 	whether the Government should consider in bringing certain local value edition criteria so that investment in this sector can be encouraged and domestic manufacturers can be provided level playing field in true spirit of Locals for Vocals; and (h) 	if so, the details thereof and the steps being taken by the Government to become Atmanirbhar?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????            ( SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)  (a)   & (b):	The Guidelines of Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM), provide an exhaustive list of machines and equipment along with applicable subsidy, irrespective of their country of origin, as complete freedom to beneficiary is available for selection of agricultural machinery and equipment as per his/her choice, provided that the machine should have been tested by the Farm Machinery Training & Testing Institutes (FMTTIs) or the institutions identified by the Department of Agriculture Cooperation & Farmers Welfare (DAC&FW).(c)   & (d):	Some of the equipments and machines on a limited scale are imported from other countries including China. During the last three years, some of the major machinery that have been imported include Power weedders, Brushcutters, Sprayers (manual, engine and battery operated), Combine harvesters (track type) and Rice transplanters.(e) to (h):	The Government as a policy is promoting make in India products and encouraging domestic manufactures for manufacturing agricultural machinery and equipment through Research & Development support, skill development, simplification of rules and procedures for testing of machines & equipment, preference to make in India products in public procurement and to provide level playing field to domestic manufacturers  import restrictions under Foreign Trade Policy etc. *******
