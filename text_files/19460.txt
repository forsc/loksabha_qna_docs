                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1003






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.02.2021






Plantations of Workers




Anto  Antony














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)whether it has come to the notice of the Government that labourers in the rubber, tea and coffee plantations are leading a miserable life due to the collapse of price of produce and mismanagement of plantations and lack of Government support;(b)if so, the details thereof;(c)whether the Government proposes to identify the rubber, tea and coffee plantations which have been closed down and which are functioning nominally so as to mitigate the hardships of plantation labourers; and (d)if so, the details and the steps taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE (IC) FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI SANTOSH KUMAR GANGWAR)(a) & (b):	The Government has not received any such representation from  the trade unions or individual workers of the plantation sector i.e. tea, coffee and rubber.In respect of tea, the plantation workers are paid wages as per the negotiated agreement between the producer associations and workers unions under the aegis of the respective State Government. (c) & (d):	In respect of Tea, there are 1569 tea gardens in   the country and at present only 16 tea gardens are closed in the country and   rest are functioning. The details of closed tea gardens along with current status is annexed.  In case of closed Tea Gardens, the Government of India through Tea Board has been implementing “Tea Development & Promotion Scheme” during the Medium Term Framework period (2017-20), which has been extended upto 31.03.2021.  Under this scheme, Rs.4000 are disbursed to the wards of workers of closed tea gardens as book & uniform grant.  There is no proposal to identify plantations which have been closed down in respect of Rubber & Coffee Plantations.*                                                    ********
