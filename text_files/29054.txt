                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

962






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2021






Capacity Building Programme for Women in Dairy Farming




Naranbhai Bhikhabhai Kachhadiya













                                                    Parbatbhai Savabhai Patel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	whether  National Commission for Women has launched nationwide training and capacity building programme for the women in dairy farming;(b) 	if so, the details and main objectives thereof;(c) 	the names of the training institutes empanelled for the purpose, State/UT-wise including Gujarat;(d) 	whether any preference will be given to tribal and rural women of Gujarat in the said training programme and if so, the details thereof; and(e) 	whether the Government proposes to provide any financial incentive to the women after the completion of training and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




(a) and (b)   The National Commission for Women (NCW) has initiated a training and capacity building program for women in dairy farming with the objective that training in new technology about value added dairy products, innovations in packaging, product development, etc. will improve the viability of women-led dairy organisations/farmers.    (c) NCW has awarded this programme to the following 12 institutions:No.	Name of the Institution1.	Lala Lajpat Rai University of Veterinary and Animal Sciences, Haryana2.	Gandhi Krishi Vigyana Kendra University of Agricultural Sciences, Bangalore,  Karnataka3.	Krishi Vigyan Kendra, KVK,  Jhabua, Madhya Pradesh4.	Maharashtra Animal & Fishery Sciences University, Nagpur, Maharashtra5.	Maharana Pratap University of Agriculture and Technology, Udaipur, Rajasthan6.	Sri Karan Narendra Agriculture University, Jobner, Rajasthan7.	KVK Gudamalani, Barmer, Agricultural University Jodhpur, Rajasthan8.	Tamil Nadu Agricultural University, Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu9.	Indian council of Agricultural Research , Krishi Vigyan  Kendra, Needamangalam, Tamil Nadu10.	Dr. J Jayalalitha Fisheries University, Tamil Nadu11.	Banda University of Agriculture and Technology,  Uttar Pradesh12.	Pt. Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Pashu Chikitsa Vigyan Vishwavidyalaya  Evam Go-Anusandhan Sansthan (DUVASU), Mathura, Uttar Pradesh (d) The programme is available to all women engaged in dairy farming for developing their skills in value addition of milk products so as to enhance their incomes. NCW has not given any preference to any specific categories of women in this programme. (e) While the said programme does not include any element of financial incentive to the trained women, they can access Government of India''s existing schemes like the Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana and the schemes and programmes of the Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying such as the Central Sector scheme ‘Supporting State Dairy Cooperatives and Farmer Producer Organizations engaged in dairy activities’ for their needs pertaining financing, market access etc. ******
