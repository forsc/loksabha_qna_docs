Skill Development in LWE Regions




Vishnu Dayal Ram














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a) the details of the skill development programs undertaken under the Skill Development Initiative in Left Wing Extremism (LWE) Regions in Jharkhand;b) the details of the funds allocated for Jharkhand State under this initiative; andc) the number of persons that benefitted from this initiative in Palmu and Garwha districts in Jharkhand?






ANSWER




                                                        a) 	Under Skill India mission, Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE) is implementing various programs related to skill development across the country including the 19 Left Wing Extremism (LWE) notified districts in Jharkhand. The major programs implemented by the MSDE are as below:i.	Skill Development in 47 districts affected by Left Wing Extremism (LWE)ii.	Upgradation of existing Government ITIs into Model ITIsiii.	Craftsmen Training Scheme(CTS)iv.	Skill Strengthening for Industrial Value Enhancement (STRIVE)v.	Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY)vi.	National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS)vii.	Jan ShikshanSansthan (JSS).The brief of the above program is given at Annexure-I and the district wise detail at Annexure-IIb) The details of the funds allocated and released to the State of Jharkhand are as follows:(Rs. in Lakhs)Sl. Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY)	Under Skill India Mission, Ministry is implementing its flagship scheme Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) to impart skill development training to prospective youth of the country including candidates from LWE Districts in Jharkhand. Presently, the third phase of PMKVY i.e. PMKVY 3.0 is being implemented. PMKVY has two training components, namely, Short Term Training (STT) and Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL). Under the PMKVY scheme as on 31.12.2021, a total of 2.02 lakh candidates have been trained from the LWE Districts in the State of Jharkhand. The district wise details are given at Annexure-II. 2.	Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) Scheme	The Scheme of Jan Shikshan Sansthan (NGOs) for Skill Development is a Central Sector Scheme.JSSs are imparting skill training to non-literate, neo-literates, persons with rudimentary level of education upto 8th standard and school drop-outs upto 12th standard in the age group 15-45 years. The priority groups are women, SC, ST, minorities and other backward sections of the society. Under the Scheme of JSS, 13.09 lakh beneficiaries have been trained since 2018-19, out of which, 18,634 beneficiaries have been trained in the state of Jharkhand.  At present, there are currently 13 JSSs in the State, namely,Bokaro, Dumka, Garhwa, Giridih, Gumla, Hazaribagh, Khunti, Latehar, Pakur, Palamu, Ramgarh, Ranchi and Simdega.  There is no provision for allocation of funds to the States/UTs under this Scheme.3.	National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS)	This Scheme is for promoting apprenticeship training and increasing the engagement of apprentices by providing financial support to industrial	establishments undertaking apprenticeship programme under The Apprentices Act,1961. Sofar,10.7 lakh apprentices have been trained under this Scheme since its inception in August 2016, outof which, 26,688 apprentices are from the Stateof Jharkhand. Presently 4378 apprenticeship are engaged in Jharkhand.4.	Craftsmen	Training Scheme(CTS)	This scheme is for providing long-term training in 143 trades through 14,730 Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) across the country. 325 ITI sarein the State of Jharkhand. So far, 12.15 lakhcandidates have been imparted training acrossthe country, out of which, 25,540 candidates are from the State of Jharkhand. The administration of ITI sunder the Scheme is with the respective State Government/UT Administration. There is no provision for allocation of funds to the States/UTs under this Scheme.5.	Skill Development in 47 Districts Affected by Left Wing Extremism Scheme	This is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme. The scheme inter-alia envisages funding to State Governments for establishment of 47 new ITI s@ one ITI per district in 47 Districts of 10 States, and the establishment of 68 Skill Development Centres (SDCs) in 34 districts of 9 States. Under the scheme, 16 new ITI sand 20 new SDCs are being established in the State of Jharkhand. So far, an amount of Rs. 74.34 crores (Central Share) out of allocated fund i.e. Rs. 91.11 crores (Central Share) for establishment of it is and SDCs has been released to the State of Jharkhand for the purpose.6.	Upgradation of existing Government it is into Model it is Scheme	This is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme. Under this scheme, existing Government it is in a State have  been  taken up for upgradation into Model ITIs, which are supposed to evolve as an institution showcasing best practices, efficient and high-quality training delivery and sustainable and effective industry relationship.So far, an amount of Rs. 6.30 crores (Central Share) has been released out of Rs.7.00 crores (Central Share) fund allocated to the State of Jharkhand for the purpose.7.	Skills Strengthening for Industrial Value Enhancement (STRIVE)	Skills Strengthening for Industrial Value Enhancement (STRIVE) a World Bank assisted project for a total cost of Rs. 2200 crore. The project duration is 5 years i.e., from 2017-2022. The objective of the project is to improve relevance and efficiency of skills development provided through ITIs and apprenticeship. Three ITIs one each in the district Bokaro,Dhanbad and East Singhbhum is selected under STRIVE.
