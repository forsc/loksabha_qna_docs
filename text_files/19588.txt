Violence/Abuse against Children




Janardan Singh Sigriwal














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    VIOLENCE/ABUSE AGAINST CHILDREN 779. 	SHRI JANARDAN SINGH SIGRIWAL: Will the Minister of WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT be pleased to state: (a) 	the number of childcare homes/centres functioning across the country, State/UT-wise including Bihar; (b) 	whether the Government has taken note of rising incidence of violence/abuse children in these centre in recent years; (c) 	if so, the details of incidents reported during each of the last three years and current year, State/UT-wise including Bihar; (d) 	the steps taken by the Government in each of the aforementioned cases; and (e) 	whether the Government has decided to close down more than 500 childcare homes across the country and if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor, State/UT-wise including Bihar?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a) to (e): The number of Child Care Institutions (CCIs) currently supported under the Child Protection Services (CPS) Scheme, State/UT-wise including Bihar is at Annexure-I.The Juvenile Justice (Child and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 (JJ Act) and the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Model Rule, 2016 framed thereunder mandate regular monitoring and inspection of CCIs. The primary responsibility of execution of the JJ Act lies with the States/UTs. Under the Commission for Protection of Child Rights Act, 2005, the  National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) and State Commissions for Protection of Child Rights (SCPCR) have been established, to oversee the implementation of the JJ Act. As informed by the National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR),  46 complaints relating to child exploitation, violence and sexual abuse in CCIs  have been brought to its notice during the last three years, including  the current year 2020-21. The State/UT-wise details are at   Annexure-II.Ministry has asked States/UTs to ensure regular monitoring and supervision of CCIs at the level of District Magistrates/Deputy Commissioners.  An advisory has also been issued to the States/UTs  recommending action to be taken in case of disruption to the life of children in case of any untoward incidence of abuse in CCIs.As per the report submitted by the States/UTs, 436 Institutions were closed on account of non-compliance of provisions of the JJ Act from 01.08.2019 to 01.08.2020, details are at Annexure-III. ***  Annexure-IAnnexure referred in reply to part (a) to (e) of the Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No.779 for answer on 05.02.2021 raised by Shri Janardan Singh Sigriwal regarding “Violence/Abuse Against Children”.
