                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

231






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.03.2020






One Stop Centres




Naranbhai Bhikhabhai Kachhadiya













                                                    Jaswantsinh Sumanbhai Bhabhor
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the Government approved the proposal for setting up of 180 additional One Stop Centres (OSCs) for women empowerment across the country during 2018;(b)	if so, the details thereof along with the number of said centres established till date, State-wise including Gujarat;(c)	whether any specific services are being provided to women in the said centres and if so, the details thereof; and(d)	the number of women benefitted under the said scheme so far, State-wise:






ANSWER




ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT (SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a)	to (d) : A statement is laid on the Table of the House.  STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (a) to (d) OF THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.231 FOR 06.03.2020 ASKED BY SHRI NARANBHAI KACHHADIYAAND SHRI JASWANT SINGH BHABHOR REGARDING ONE STOP CENTRES(a) to (d) : One Stop Centre Scheme, under Nirbhaya Fund is implemented since 1st April, 2015.  The Government of India approved setting up of 483 OSCs for violence affected women across the country during the year 2018 i.e. from the period between 01.01.2018 and 31.12.2018.  As on date, a total of 728 OSCs are approved in 724 districts across the country. Out of these, 680 OSCs are operational so far.The objective of these OSCs is to provide immediate, emergency and non-emergency access to a range of services under one roof including medical help, police facilitation, psychological counselling, legal counselling and temporary shelter to women affected by violence.
