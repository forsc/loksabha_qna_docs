                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2153






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2019






Primitive Tribal Group in Jharkhand




Veena Devi














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the population of primitive tribals is decreasing continuously in the country, especially in Jharkhand and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has started/is sponsoring ‘Primitive Tribal Group Development’ for the overall development of primitive tribes and if so, the details thereof;(c) the amount released for the development of tribals and the amount utilised during last three years; and(d) the outcome of development schemes for the upliftment of such tribals?






ANSWER




                                                        (a): There are certain tribal communities who have declining or stagnant population, low level of literacy, pre-agricultural level of technology and are economically backward in the country. These groups are among the most vulnerable section of our society as they are few in numbers, have not attained any significant level of social and economic development and generally inhabit remote localities having poor infrastructure and administrative support.  75 such groups have been identified in 18 States including Jharkhand & Union Territory of Andaman & Nicobar Islands and categorized as Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs). List of PVTGs State / UT wise is given at Annexure – I. Population of PVTGs as available from Census 2011, are given at Annexure – II.(b) to (d): Ministry of Tribal Affairs is implementing the scheme of “Development of PVTGs” for the activities like education, housing, land distribution, land development, agricultural development, animal husbandry, construction of link roads, installation of non-conventional sources of energy for lighting purpose, social security or any other innovative activity meant for the comprehensive socio-economic development of PVTGs.The interventions under the scheme are demand driven and funds are provided to State Governments based upon proposals received from the State Government duly approved by Executive Committee and after appraisal and approval thereof by a Project Appraisal Committee (PAC) constituted in this Ministry for the purpose.
