                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

385






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2020






Staff Shortage in Prisons




Gurjeet Singh Aujla














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether it is a fact that in most of the prisons, the staff strength is much below the national average as per the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB);(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether most of the jails are over-crowded and medical assistance provided inside the premises is not adequate; and(d) if so, the details in this regard and the steps being taken by the Government to provide adequate doctors and dispensaries in jails?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) & (b):	National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) compiles prison statistics reported to it by States and Union Territories (UTs) and publishes the same in its report “Prison Statistics India”. Latest published report is of the year 2018. State/UT-wise sanctioned and actual strength of total jail staff as on 31.12.2018 are given in Annexure-I. (c) & (d):	State/UT-wise details of capacity, inmate population and occupancy rate of jails as on 31.12.2018 are given in Annexure-II.  State/UT-wise details of number of prison inmates per Medical Staff are given in Annexure-III. -2-L.S.US.Q.NO.385 FOR 04.02.2020	‘Prisons’ and ‘persons detained therein’ are State subjects as per Entry 4 of List II of Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India. The States and UTs are competent to take appropriate action in their respective jurisdictions to provide adequate medical facilities to prison inmates. However, the Ministry of Home Affairs has issued advisories to States & UTs for initiating special recruitment drives to fill up the vacant posts of prison officials and appointing adequate number of psychiatrists, counsellors and doctors. A Model Prison Manual, 2016 has also been circulated to all States and UTs which has a dedicated chapter on ‘Medical Care’. **********
