                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

369






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2019






Entrepreneurship Among Women




Kaushal Kishore














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	whether any fresh initiatives have been taken by the Ministry to promote entrepreneurship among the women in the country, specially in Uttar Pradesh;(b)	if so, the details thereof; and(c) 	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a)	to (c)  A statement is laid on the Table of the House.******* Statement referred to in reply to Part (a) to (c) of Lok Sabha Starred Question No.369 for 13.12.2019 by Shri Kaushal Kishore regarding ‘Entrepreneurship Among Women’(a) to (c) The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship is the nodal Ministry for Entrepreneurship. The programs of the Ministry on Entrepreneurship development include:Pradhan Mantri Yuva Udyamita Vikas Abhiyaan (PM YUVA) was launched in 2017for creating an enabling ecosystem for Entrepreneurship Development through entrepreneurship education and training across the country. It is being implementedthrough the Institutes of Higher Learning (Universities, Colleges and Premier Institutes), Schools, Industrial Training Centers (lTls) and Entrepreneurship Development Centers (EDCs) for a period of five years. First phase of PM YUVA was conducted in 239 empanelled Institutes of Higher Learning under the scheme throughout the country including 21 Institutes in Uttar Pradesh. The pilot project on PM YUVA 2.0 is being implemented in six districts of Uttar Pradesh namely Ghaziabad, Gautam Buddh Nagar, Fatehpur, Siddharthnagar, Chandauli and Varanasi.With an aim to promote a culture of entrepreneurship among India’s youth and inspire them to set-up their own enterprise and create employment opportunities for others, National Entrepreneurship Awards Scheme (NEAS) has been instituted in 2016. Under the scheme, awards are conferred to the aspiring first generation entrepreneurs including women to recognize and honour their efforts and achievements and those individuals/organizations who are working as eco-system builder in the field of entrepreneurship development. The awards also seek to highlight model of excellence for others to emulate and improve upon.To encourage women entrepreneurship within the country, a pilot project ‘Economic Empowerment of Women Entrepreneurs and Start-ups by Women’ is being run in collaboration with Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) Germany. The project,pilots incubation and acceleration programmes for micro women entrepreneurs to start new businesses and scale up existing enterprises in Assam, Rajasthan and Telangana. The project has the target to pilot the incubation programme with 250 women and the acceleration programme with 100 women.Interventions for entrepreneurship development by other Ministries include:Start-Up India,under the Ministry of Commerce and Industry,aims to build a strong eco-system for nurturing innovation and Startups in the country under which 10% of the total corpus of Fund of Funds amounting to Rs 1000 crore, has been reserved for women led start-ups. Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP), by the Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises, is a major credit-linked subsidy scheme to promote and set up micro, small and medium enterprises and to generate employment in rural and urban areas of the country. Under the scheme women entrepreneurs are provided 25% and 35% subsidies for projects set up in urban and rural areas respectively. For women beneficiaries, own contribution is only 5% of the project cost while for general category it is 10%. All the entrepreneurs including women are eligible for 2 weeks Entrepreneurship Development Programme (EDP) after the sanction of their projects from banks for financial assistance to setup their unit.  The Ministry has launched a portal “UdyamSakhi” for encouraging women entrepreneurs to aid, counsel, assist and protect their interests. UdyamSakhi network is a platform for emerging Women Entrepreneurs of India to get support, to understand current scenario of industries and to get guidance in various aspects of entrepreneurship. It helps Indian women to start, build and grow businesses and creating business models revolving around low-cost products and services to resolve social inequities. *****
