Private Participation in Mining Sector




 Bhavana Gawali (Patil)













                                                    Krupal Balaji Tumane
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





MINES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has allowed private participation in mining sector in the country; (b)	if so, the details thereof along with the existing norms fixed in this regard;(c)	the number of mining lease accorded to public and private sector companies during each of the last three years and the current year, company/ mineral and State/UT-wise; and(d)	the details of revenue/royalty accrued from these companies during the said period, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF MINES, COAL AND PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS(SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI)(a) & (b): Yes, Sir. As per section 5(1) of the Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) (MMDR) Act, 1957, mineral concessions are granted by the State Governments to any person who is either an Indian national or a company as defined in clause (20) of section 2 of the Companies Act, 2013 and satisfies such conditions as may be prescribed by the Central Government. The mining lease is granted through auction by a method of competitive bidding in respect of all major minerals specified in the MMDR Act, 2015.(c) & (d):  64 nos. of mining leases have been granted through auction process to private sector by the States.
