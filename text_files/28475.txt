                                                    Rebati Tripura
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has observed Shikshak Parv recently and if so, the details thereof alongwith the theme of the Parv and programmes organized to mark the occasion;(b) the funds sanctioned and utilized for this purpose;(c) the various academic initiatives to modernize the education sector launched by the Government during the Parv;(d) whether the Government has taken steps to train the teachers to rapidly changing era in the country, particularly in remote, rural and backward areas to learn about new systems and techniques for efficient teaching after the implementation of the New Education Policy, 2020 and if so, the details thereof and the outcome thereof;(e) the steps taken by the Government to open more primary and secondary schools in the State of Maharashtra;(f) the other steps taken by the Government to make our education system globally competitive and also make the youth future secured; and(g) whether the Government proposes to train teachers in remote areas, rural and backward villages after implementation of New Education Policy 2020 and if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a,b&c): Yes, Sir. In order to widely disseminate the learnings of schools on quality, inclusive practices and sustainability, Shikshak Parv was organized from 5thSeptember, 2021 to 17th September, 2021 through virtual mode. Policy makers, administrators, academicians, experts, school principals, head-teachers and teachers participated in the Shikshak Parv to share their experiences, learnings and discuss the way forward to realise the aspirations of NEP-2020. The theme of this year’s Shiksak Parv was “Quality and sustainable schools-learnings from schools in India”.The theme was further subdivided into following nine sub-themes : -(i) Nurturing inclusive classrooms(ii) Promoting Quality and Sustainable schools(iii) Foundational Literacy and Numeracy(iv) Culture of Innovation in our Schools(v) Innovative Pedagogy to promote enjoyable and engaging learning(vi) Technology in Education: NDEAR(vii) Transforming the system of assessment-Holistic Progress Card(viii) Stimulating Indian Knowledge Systems, Arts and Culture(ix) Reimagining Vocational Education and Skill BuildingWebinars of ninety (90) minutes duration were held on each of the sub-themes.Hon’ble Prime Minister of India graced the inaugural conclave held on 7th September, 2021 and launched following initiatives to modernize the education sector:- (i) Indian Sign Language Dictionary (audio and text embedded sign language video for the hearing impaired, in conformity with Universal Design of Learning), (ii) Talking Books (audiobooks for the visually impaired), (iii) School Quality Assurance and Assessment Framework of CBSE, (iv) NISHTHA teachers'' training programme for NIPUN Bharat and (v) Vidyanjali portal (for facilitating education volunteers/ donors/ CSR contributors for school development).Shikshak Parv was celebrated within the overall budget of the Ministry of Education.(d) & (g): For training of teachers, particularly in remote, rural and backward areas, to learn about new systems and techniques for efficient teaching , this Department has launched NISHTHA online on the DIKSHA platform in October 2020. NISHTHA online covers 18 modules in 11 languages. Teachers are oriented on various aspects related to3Learning Outcomes, Competency Based Learning and Testing, Learner-centered Pedagogy, School Safety and Security, Personal-social qualities, Inclusive Education, ICT in teaching-learning including Artificial Intelligence, Health and well-being including yoga, new Initiatives in School Education including library, eco club, youth club, kitchen garden, School Leadership qualities, Environmental Concerns, Pre-school, Pre-vocational Education and School Based Assessment in a joyful learning manner through the platform. Each module consists of guidelines, primers; training package with QR coded e-content, videos and other e-resources. It includes multiple approaches for interaction i.e., Text Modules along with videos, Live Sessions by National level Resource persons on DTH SwayamPrabha TV Channel and Interactive Voice Response System. 30 States & UTs and 8 autonomous organisations of the Government (CBSE, KVS, NVS, AEES, Sainik Schools, CTSA, CICSE and EMRS) have conducted online NISHTHA courses in 11 languages (Assamese, Bengali, Bodo, English, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Odia, Punjabi, Telugu, and Urdu).Around 24 lakh teachers have completed NISHTHA online training at elementary level by June, 2021. NISHTHA online training has been further expanded for secondary teachers and for teachers teaching at foundational levels.(e): The Government is continuously taking steps to strengthen School Education in the state of Maharashtra including Primary and Upper Primary Schools. Details of various strengthening components since 2018-19 are given in Annexure I.(f): Samagra Shiksha scheme, implemented as a Centrally Sponsored Scheme, is an integrated scheme for school education covering the entire gamut from pre-school to class XII. The scheme treats school education as a continuum and is in accordance with Sustainable Development Goal for Education (SDG-4). The scheme not only provides support for the implementation of the RTE Act but has also been aligned with the recommendations of NEP 2020 to ensure that all children have access to quality education with an equitable and inclusive classroom environment which should take care of their diverse background, multilingual needs, different academic abilities and make them active participants in the learning process.The major interventions, across all levels of school education, proposed under the scheme are: (i) Universal Access including Infrastructure Development and Retention; (ii) Foundational Literacy and Numeracy, (iii) Gender and Equity; (iv) Inclusive Education; (v) Quality and Innovation; (vi) Financial support for Teacher Salary; (vii) Digital initiatives; (viii) RTE Entitlements including uniforms, textbooks etc.; (ix) Support for ECCE; (x) Vocational Education; (xi) Sports and Physical Education; (xii) Strengthening of Teacher Education and Training; (xiii) Monitoring; (xiv) Programme Management; and (xv) National Component.The details of other initiatives launched by the Department of School Education and Literacy to make our education system globally competitive and also make the youth future secured are given in the Annexure -II.*****
