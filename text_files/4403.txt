                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    DEFENCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3956






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.07.2019






Shortage of Pilots in Air Force




Ramcharan Bohra














                        Will the Minister of





DEFENCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  the category-wise number of aircrafts being used at present for imparting training to Indian Air Force pilots;(b)  whether there is a shortage of pilots in Indian Air Force;(c)  if so, the details thereof, and the reasons therefor; and (d)  the steps taken/proposed to be taken by the Government to meet the shortage?  






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE					(SHRI SHRIPAD NAIK)IN THE MINISTRY OF DEFENCE   ????? ????? ??????					(???? ??????? ????)   (a)  Adequate number of following aircrafts are being used for imparting training to Indian Air Force (IAF) pilots: 1. Basic Trainer Aircraft; 2. Intermediate Jet Trainer; and3.  Advance Jet Trainer(b) & (c):  The available strength of pilots in the IAF is sufficient to meet the current operational requirements.(d)  Every year, the IAF participates in a number of career fairs/exhibitions, releases advertisements in newspapers/television and conducts numerous lectures in schools and colleges to motivate the target group. *****
