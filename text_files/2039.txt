                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COAL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1993






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.07.2019






Coal Exploration Survey




Rahul Kaswan














                        Will the Minister of





COAL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a.	whether plenty of coal reserve has been found in the course of coal exploration survey conducted during the last few years;b.	if so the details thereof;c.	whether the Government proposes to carry out mining of coal after the survey conducted in Churu and Bikaner area of Rajasthan; andd.	if so, the time by which it is likely to be started?






ANSWER




 MINISTER OF PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS, COAL AND MINES (SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI) (a& b):   Coal Exploration Survey is a continuous process. After completion of exploration survey in a block, coal resources assessed and reported in Geological Report. As per coal Inventory of India published by Geological Survey of India (GSI), coal resources added by all agencies during last 5 years are given below: Resource Added during	Total Resources Added(Billion Tonnes)2013-14	2.6502014-15	5.0312015-16	2.2062016-17	6.3462017-18	3.871TOTAL	20.104 (c& d):   Availability of coal in Churu and Bikaner area of Rajasthan is not yet confirmed and accordingly, presently, there is no proposal to carry out mining of coal in that area.   *****
