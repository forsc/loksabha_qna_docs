                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

274






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






Farmers benefitted under PMKSY




Anubhav Mohanty














                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total number of farmers benefited under the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana (PMKSY) during each of the last four years so far;(b)	the total number of direct/indirect employment generated under the PMKSY during the said period; and(c)	whether the Government has any proposal to improve the employability of the yojana and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER FOR FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES (SHRI PASHUPATI KUMAR PARAS)  (a) to (c): A statement is laid on the Table of the House.  STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO THE PARTS (a) TO (c) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.274 FOR ANSWER ON 22ND MARCH, 2022 REGARDING “FARMERS BENEFITED UNDER PMKSY”.(a) & (b): Year wise estimated number of farmers benefited and direct and indirect employment generated from projects completed under component schemes of Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana (PMKSY) since 2017-18 is placed at Annexure.(c):	In order to further streamline implementation of the scheme to help achieve its objectives, including employment generation, Government has approved continuation of restructured PMKSY till 2025-26 with an outlay of Rs 4600 Crore. The scope of component scheme “Operation Greens” has been extended   from Tomato, Onion & Potato (TOP) to 22 perishable products. Various other measures undertaken in this regard include- (i) Creating awareness of benefits available under its schemes; (ii) Submission of proposals through online portal; (iii) Dashboard for monitoring the implementation of the projects; (iv) Regular review meetings with the promoters/state nodal departments/ banks to help remove bottlenecks.*****
