                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3169






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2019






Shortage of Doctors and Staff  in Hospitals




Ganesh Singh













                                                    Jyotirmay Singh Mahato
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state: (a) whether there is an acute shortage of doctors including specialists/super specialists staff in hospitals and dispensaries run by his Ministry in the country and particularly in Indian Institute of Ayurveda; (b) if so, the details thereof, State/UTwise including West Bengal; (c) the details of the sanctioned strength, actual strength and vacant posts in the said categories in these hospitals and the reasons for the posts lying vacant along with the steps taken by the Government to fillup these vacant posts, State/UTwise; (d) whether there is a shortage of infrastructure, beds and medical equipments in these hospitals, if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor along with the remedial measures taken by the Government in this regard; (e) the number of patients got treated in hospitals of Indian Institute of Ayurveda during the last three years, State/UT-wise and the capacity available in these hospitals with regard to the number of patients to whom treatment can be provided; (f) the steps taken/being taken by the Government to promote Ayurvedic treatment in the country during the last three years; and (g) the efforts being made by the Government to provide better and effective medical facilities to the beneficiaries/ seekers of AYUSH system of medicine with ease of time? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(SHRI SHRIPAD YESSO NAIK)(a): No.  However, there is no Indian Institute of Ayurveda under the aegis of Ministry of AYUSH.(b):   Does not arise.(c):   The details of the posts in the National Institutes functioning and having attached hospitals under the aegis of Ministry of AYUSH are given in Annex-I.(d):   No.(e):    There is no Institute of Ayurveda under the aegis of Ministry of AYUSH.  However, the number of patients treated in hospitals attached with the Ayurveda National Institutes under Ministry of AYUSH are  given in Annex.-II.(f):   The hospitals attached with National Institutes provides the best quality treatments and medicines  which is dispensed free of cost to patients.  Every effort is made to promote Ayurvedic Treatment through their  Bedded Hospitals and OPDs and also by organizing specialty Camps, Community Health Programs etc. regularly.(g):    Organizes regular Camps in the Campus as well as in various parts from time to time through which Consultations, Treatments, are provided along with handbills and other IEC materials projecting various activities, health care needs, patient care etc. for the benefit of general public and beneficiaries.  This is a continuous process and the Institutes put their efforts to provide better and effective medical facilities to the general public.
