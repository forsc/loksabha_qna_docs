                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2975






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






Sea Plane Service




Deepak (Dev) Adhikari














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to start sea plane service in the country;(b) if so, the details thereof; and(c) the time by which it is likely to start functioning?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) to (c): Details of the routes connecting ten (10) water aerodrome sites awarded under Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS)- UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik) are as under:;i)   Nagarjuna Sagar, Telangana;ii)   Umrangso Reservoir, Assam;iii)   Kharghuli Area, Guwahati, Assam;iv)   Statue of Unity (Sardar Sarovar Dam), Gujarat;v)   Sabarmati River Front, Gujarat;vi)   Shatrunjay Dam, Gujarat;vii)   Prakasham Barrage Reservoir, Andhra Pradesh;viii) Swaraj Dweep (Havelock Island), Andaman & Nicobar Islands;ix)   Shaheed Dweep (Neil Island), Andaman & Nicobar Islands;x)   Long Island, Andaman & Nicobar Islands;Procedures and requirements for licensing of water aerodromes have been laid down in Civil Aviation Requirements, Section 4, Series ''F'', Part IV, issued by Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA). The Selected Airline Operators (SAOs) are obligated to commence RCS flight operations on a RCS-route within a period of 180 days from the issuance of Letter of Award or within two months from the date of readiness of the airports/heliports/water aerodromes, whichever is later, unless any extension is granted by the Implementing Agency. A multi-disciplinary team constituted by Airports Authority of India (AAI) has conducted pre-feasibility studies and forwarded them to concerned State Governments/Union Territories. AAI has appointed a consultant to prepare Detailed Project Report (DPR) and other pre-tender activities for 10 water aerodrome sites across India.
