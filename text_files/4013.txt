                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3767






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.07.2019






Organic Cultivation Area




Kirit Premjibhai Solanki














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the total farm area, excluding forest area, currently under Organic Certification, State/UT-wise;(b)	  the  total  number  of  clusters formed  under  organic  farming  since  the inception of the scheme, State/UT-wise;(c)	 the details of the selection process and criteria used to choose the clusters for organic farming under the scheme; and(d)	  the  steps  taken  by  the Government  to  provide  the  latest technologies,  infrastructure  and  training regarding organic farming to farmers in the selected clusters?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER  OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):	 As per the report received from States and Agriculture Processed Food and Export Development Authority (APEDA),the total farm area currently  under Organic Certification is 27.70  lakh hectares in the country including 5.98 lakh hectares under Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) , 0.639 lakh hectares under  Mission Organic Value Chain Development for North Eastern Region (MOVCDNER) , 19.38 lakh hectares under National Program for Organic Production (NPOP)  and 1.70 lakh hectares under other initiatives. The State-wise details of the area covered under PKVY, MOVCDNER, NPOP and others is given at Annexure-I. (b):	  Farmers’ groups/ Clusters are encouraged under Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)   where as Farmers Producer Organizations are formed under Mission organic value Chain Development in North East Region (MOVCDNER) Scheme. The State/UTs-wise   detail of   total number of clusters formed under PKVY scheme   is given at Annexure ll. The State-wise   detail of total number of FPOs formed under MOVCDNER is given at Annexure lll. (c):	 Rainfed, tribal, hilly areas are given preference for organic farming. Similarly, areas where farmers are interested in organic farming are also considered by states for implementation of the  organic  farming   schemes. A group of 20 or more farmers having a total area of 20 hectare Contd…2/--2-are formed into a   Local Group and 25-50 such groups covering 500-1000 ha area constitutes one “Cluster”. There is no limit on the area that a farmer can include under the cluster however,  each  farmer in the group can avail the assistance up to a maximum of 2 hectares only .  A local resource person is identified with in each group for handholding the farmers. Small Farmers Agribusiness Consortium (SFAC) norms of FPO formation are followed under MOVCDNER.(d):	 Under PKVY,   assistance of RS 50,000 per hectare/ 3 years is allowed out of which Rs. 31,000 (61%) is provided to farmer   directly through DBT   for input (biofertilisers, biopesticides, vermicompost, botanical extracts etc) production/ procurement, marketing etc. Under MOVCDNER , assistance is provided to the farmers in a value chain mode starting from formation of Farmers Producer Organisations (FPOs), on/off farm input production, supply of seeds/ planting materials, post harvest infrastructure including collection, sorting, grading facilities,   establishment of integrated processing unit, refrigerated transportation, pre-cooling/ cold stores chamber, branding, labelling and packaging, etc . Awareness generation among farmers through trainings, field visits are integral part of organic farming schemes of PKVY and MoVCDNER.   National Centre for organic Farming (NCOF), the subordinate office of DAC&FW is also involved in extension activities and conducts   Farmers’ Training and Field Demonstration Programmes (FTFD) under “Sansad Aadarsh Gram Yojana (SAGY) and jaivik sammelan programmes. Need based trainings on Organic Farming are also organized by ICAR-IIFSR through All India Network Programme on Organic Farming in various states.
