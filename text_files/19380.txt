                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

648






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2021






Connectivity with NH in UP




Shyam Yadav Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the names of the districts proposed to be connected with the National Highways under the Bharatmala Pariyojana in Uttar Pradesh, Phase-1 and Phase-2; and (b)	the details of the works done so far in this regard; and (c)	the funds released and utilized for the purpose?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI) (a) 	About 51 districts (Kanpur, Fatehpur, Kaushambi, Baghpat, Shamli, Saharanpur, Meerut, Muzaffarnagar, Aligarh, Hathras, Etah, Mainpuri, Moradabad, Amroha, Hapur, Kannauj, Bijnor, Unnao, Raebareli, Hamirpur, Mahoba, Agra, Bareilly, Pilibhit, Kasganj, Budaun, Lakhimpur Kheri, Shahjahanpur, Hardoi, Lucknow, Barabanki, Bahraich, Prayagraj, Bhodohi, Mirzapur, Varanasi, Sultanpur, Pratapgarh, Jaunpur, Azamgarh, Ghazipur, Chandauli, Gorakhpur, Mau, Ayodhya, Basti, Sant kabir Nagar, Deoria, Kushinagar, Maharajganj, Pratapgarh) are  proposed to be connected with National Highways under Bharatmala Pariyojana Phase-I in the State of Uttar Pradesh. There is no approved Bharatmala Pariyojana Phase-II.(b) Projects of length 1624 km have been awarded till January, 2021 in the State of Uttar Pradesh. Out of which, 462 km length has been completed.(c) About Rs. 1,05,000 crores were released for construction as well as land acquisition. Out of which, about Rs. 93,000 crores were utilized.*****
