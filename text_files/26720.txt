                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2340






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.08.2021






Cattle Smuggling




Sukanta Majumdar













                                                    Raja Amareshwara Naik
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of cases of cattle smuggling along the Indo-Bangladesh border that came to light, cattle seized and smugglers arrested during each of the last two years;(b)	the remedial steps taken to check illegal trade of cattle operated in cattle markets/haats along the Indo-Bangladesh border;(c)	the length in kilometres of fencing of Indo-Bangladesh border which has been completed so far, State-wise;(d)	the length in kilometres of the border yet to be fenced along the reverine section of the borders along with the target time for completion;(e)	the number of illegal migrants who have crossed over to India during the last two years;(f)	whether there have been reports of involvement of Border Security Force personnel in illegal movement of people and animals along Indo-Bangladesh Border;(g)	if so, the details thereof and the action taken by the Government thereon; and(h)	the other steps being taken in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NISITH PRAMANIK)-2-LS.USQ. NO.2340 FOR 03.08.2021(a):	Details of cattle seized and smugglers arrested on Indo-Bangladesh border during last two years are as under:Year	2019	2020Total number of cattle seized	77,410	46809Apprehension of Smugglers 	703	460(b):	The Government of India has adopted a multi-pronged approach to check illegal trade of cattle along Indo-Bangladesh border which inter-alia includes round the clock surveillance and patrolling on the borders and establishment of observation post; increase in number of BSF personnel; construction of border fencing & floodlighting; use of water crafts/boats and floating Border Out Posts (BOP) for domination of riverine area; deployment of advance technological  equipments like Hand Held Thermal Imager (HHTI), Night vision Device (NVD), Twin Telescope, Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAV); upgradation of intelligence setup and enhanced coordination with the State Governments/concerned intelligence agencies.-3-LS.USQ. NO.2340 FOR 03.08.2021(c) and (d): About 76% Indo-Bangladesh border has been covered by fence.  Details of state-wise fencing along Indo Bangladesh border are as under:                                                                                            (Length in Km)State	Total length of International Border	International Border covered by Fence West Bengal	2216.70	1638Assam	263.00	210Meghalaya 	443.00	326Mizoram	318.00	155Tripura	856.00	812Total 	4096.73141	The balance length of International border will be covered by physical fencing and technological solutions in the form of Comprehensive Integrated Border Management System (CIBMS).  	The riverine border which is not feasible for fencing will be covered through technological solutions.   A pilot project in Dhubri, Assam along 61 Kms riverine border has been implemented.  The Government is regularly monitoring  the progress of work.  -4-LS.USQ. NO.2340 FOR 03.08.2021(e):	Border Security Force conducts regular patrolling, lay nakas, establish observation posts and carry out anti-tunneling exercise to stop illegal migrants. However, some illegal migrants are able to enter in a clandestine and surreptious manner, mainly due to difficult riverine terrain in parts of International border with Bangladesh which are not amenable to physical fencing. The details of foreigners who were apprehended along Indo-Bangladesh Border while attempting to infiltrate during last 2 years are as under:- Year	2019	2020Total 	1109	955(f) to  (h):	Whenever the involvement of a BSF personnel comes to light for illegal movement of people and cattle smuggling along Indo-Bangladesh border, BSF carries out investigation and appropriate disciplinary action is taken as per the rules. Since 2019, disciplinary action against 28 BSF personnel was initiated, out of which 22 personnel have been punished and disciplinary action is in process for 6 personnel. *****
