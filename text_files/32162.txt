                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3640






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.12.2021






Funds for Plantations




 Pratap Chandra Sarangi













                                                    Krishna Pal Singh Yadav
                                                








                                                    Vivek Narayan Shejwalkar
                                                








                                                    Rajbahadur Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the steps being taken by the Government for free plantation and conservation in declining forest areas in the country; (b)	whether there is any proposal to make it mandatory for every citizen to plant a certain number of plants each year; (c)	 if so, the details thereof along with the area of land in hectare where forests are likely to be developed in Odisha and Madhya Pradesh; (d)	whether the Government allocates budget to the State Governments under any scheme for plantation, if so, State-wise and year-wise amount of budget allocated in this regard; and (e)	whether the Government is taking any measures to monitor the plants so as to keep them alive after plantation and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to (d) The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change supports the States/Union Territories through various Centrally Sponsored Scheme namely National Mission for a Green India (GIM), National Afforestation Programme (NAP)and transfer funds under CAMPA for tree plantation and conservation of declining forests in the country. Also, afforestation is being carried out by the States/Union Territories under various schemes of Central Government and State Government. As per report compiled in the Ministry under twenty point programme, afforestation is being taken up annually over 2.0 million ha since 2019-20. The Ministry on various occasions like International Day of Forests, World Environment Day, Van Mahotsav, Wild life Week, International Day for Biological Diversity etc. promotes mass tree plantation and disseminates knowledge abouttree plantation and conservation of forests among public through various Conferences, Workshops, Brochures, Sign boards etc.Though there is no specific scheme in the Ministry to make it mandatory for every citizen to plant a certain number of plants each year, annual afforestation/tree plantation targets are fixed by the Ministry for all States/UTs including Odisha and Madhya Pradesh which are achieved through the concerted efforts involving all agencies. The State/UT wise details of afforestation/tree plantation targets fixed for the year 2021-22 are given in Annexure I.The State-wise, year-wise details of funds released under NAP and GIM during last three years (2018-19 to 2020-21) alongwith details of transfer of funds from the National Authority of CAMPA to States Authority are provided in Annexure II. (e)	Protection and monitoring the health of the sapling planted is primarily the responsibility of respective State/Union Territory Governments. Tree plantation/Afforestation effortsare also monitored by the scheme specific and State specific monitoring systems including third party evaluation and monitoring through independent agencies. *****
