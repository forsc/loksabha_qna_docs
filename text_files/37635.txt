                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    EXTERNAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5056






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.04.2022






Shortage of Staff in Passport Offices




Hibi Eden














                        Will the Minister of





EXTERNAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)    the total number of passport offices in the country and number of employees working therein indicating the number of approved posts and  vacancies under various categories, State-wise;(b)    whether the Government has taken any remedial measures to fill up these vacancies,  if so, details thereof and if not, reasons therefor; (c)    whether there is an increase in number of applications seeking PCC, passport, under the jurisdiction of Regional Passport Office (RPO) Kochi, if so, the details thereof during the last three years, year-wise;(d)    whether the Government has noticed that there is continued shortage of staff at various levels in RPO, Kochi, if so, the details thereof including the number of approved posts and number of vacancies under various categories; and (e)   whether the Government has records of awardees under the category ‘best passport office’, if so, the details of winners during the last ten years?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EXTERNAL AFFAIRS[SHRI V. MURALEEDHARAN](a)	There are 36 Passport Offices in India. The sanctioned strength of Central Passport Organisation is 2741 and the actual working strength of the Central Passport Organisation (CPO) Cadre as on 01.03.2022 is 1723. The Sanctioned and Working strength of Employees at different levels in Passport Offices (State/UT-wise) as on 01.03.2022 is at Annexure -A.(b)     The Government has taken the following measures to fill up the existing vacancies at the Passport Offices:Regular placement of indents for recruitment of Office Assistants (MTS), Junior Passport Assistants, Assistant Superintendents, Junior Translation Officers and Stenographers with Staff Selection Commission (SSC).  395 posts at various levels have been indented to SSC.   92 dossiers have been received recently from Staff Selection Commission.          Ministry has also hired 347 Data Entry Operators (DEOs) and 51 Multi Tasking Staffs (MTSs) from an Outsourced Agency as an interim measure against the vacant non-Gazetted posts for smooth functioning of the Passport Offices.       To fill up the existing vacancies at various grades in the CPO, regular meetings of the Department Promotion Committees (DPCs) have been held for the promotion of eligible officials to the next grade.       Appointing officers on deputation basis as per Recruitment Rules of Deputy Passport Officer and Passport Officer.(c)     There has not been any increase in the number of applications seeking PCC, Passports at the Regional Passport Office, Cochin.  The total number of applications received for PCC and passports at the Regional Passport Office, Cochin for the last three years is as under:<pre>YearTotal Applications Received (Passport, PCC, Surrender Certificate etc.)PCC IssuedPassports Issued20195,35,90947,7264,80,87320202,90,24426,5652,67,27420214,22,77046,3523,67,890</pre>(d)    There is shortage of manpower across the Regional Passport Offices due to the difference between the sanctioned strength (SS) and actual working strength (WS) in the Central Passport Organisation. At present the sanctioned and the working strength of Regional Passport Office, Cochin at different levels is as under:<pre>Group ‘A’ (Gazetted)Group ‘B’ (Gazetted)Group ‘B’ (non-Gazetted) and Group ‘C’SSWSSSWSSSWS10624198124</pre>      Recently, 92 dossiers of Junior Passport Assistants (Group ‘C’) were received from the Staff Selection Commission (SSC) for appointment in the Central Passport Organisation,   out of which ten appointment letters have been issued for the Regional Passport Office, Cochin.  RPO Cochin has also engaged 10 Data Entry Operators from the Outsourced Agency to ease the pressure of shortage of staff.(e)   The Government has started awarding the ‘Best Passport Office’ awards since 2015.
