                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1217






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2020






Hindi Language




Dean Kuriakose














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is contemplating / or is in the process of developing/making Hindi language mandatory in CBSE approved schools of Kerala, TamilNadu, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka and Telangana;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) the steps taken by the Government for popularizing the use of the language inthe Southern States;(d) whether it is a fact that the Government considers that only Hindi can work tounite the country and only one common language that is Hindi is vital for nation’sglobal identity; and(e) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) and (b): The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) has informed that theBoard has prescribed curriculum for classes IX to XII and scheme of studiesin the curriculum does not lay down a compulsory precondition of studyingHindi in these classes. It merely states that it is expected that all thestudents should have studied three languages up to class VIII.(c): CBSE has taken the following steps to promoting Hindi in non-Hindi speakingregions of the country:? Hindi is offered in all the schools affiliated to CBSE.? Hindi is offered at different levels in classes VI to XII, i.e. Hindi as a First andSecond language for classes VI to VIII, as Course-A and Course-B forclasses IX-X and as Elective and Core for classes XI-XII so as to facilitate thelearning of the language in non-Hindi speaking regions also.? In classes IX to XII either Hindi or English must be compulsorily taken by allstudents. Both can also be taken together. Hindi could also be offered as oneof the five main electives or as a sixth additional subject in classes IX to XII.? The schools are free to conduct teaching learning process in Hindi Medium.The Board provides facility of taking examination through Hindi medium forall of its examinations.(d) and (e): Government is committed to give due importance to strengthen all theIndian Languages including Hindi under the 8th Schedule of the Constitution of India.****
