                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4502






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






PLI Schemes




Arvind Ganpat Sawant













                                                     Bhavana Gawali (Patil)
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the total revenue generated by firms that are beneficiaries of the various Product Linked Incentive (PLI) Schemes of the Government in 2021;(b) 	the total amount of funds provided as incentive so far;(c) 	the details of the contribution of the various sectors to the generation of employment before and after the launch of the PLI Schemes;(d) 	whether the Government is planning to launch the PLI Scheme for other sectors in the near future to boost domestic manufacturing and employment; and(e) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ???????  ??? ?????? ???????? ??? ????? ?????? (???? ??? ??????)  THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI SOM PARKASH)(a) & (b):	Keeping in view India’s vision of becoming ‘Atmanirbhar’, PLI Schemes for 14 key sectors have been announced with an outlay of Rs. 1.97 lakh crore (over US$26 billion) to enhance India’s Manufacturing capabilities and Exports.The 14 key sectors are: (i) Mobile Manufacturing and Specified Electronic Components, (ii) Critical Key Starting materials/Drug Intermediaries &Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients, (iii) Manufacturing of Medical Devices (iv) Automobiles and Auto Components, (v) Pharmaceuticals Drugs, (vi) Specialty Steel, (vii) Telecom & Networking Products, (viii) Electronic/Technology Products, (ix) White Goods (ACs and LEDs), (x) Food Products, (xi) Textile Products: MMF segment and technical textiles, (xii) High efficiency solar PV modules,(xiii) Advanced Chemistry Cell (ACC) Battery, and (xiv) Drones and Drone Components.The schemes have been specifically designed to attract investments in sectors of core competency and cutting edge technology; ensure efficiency and bring economies of size and scale in the manufacturing sector and make Indian manufacturers globally competitive so that they can integrate with global value chains.PLI schemes are implemented by the concerned Ministries/ Departments. Approved beneficiaries under the PLI Schemes are eligible for incentives subject to meeting specific criteria under the scheme, as prescribed in the Scheme Guidelines by concerned Ministry/ Department. So far, 489 applications with expected investment of over Rs. 1.89 Lakh crore have been approved under 11 Schemes.(c):	The PLI Schemes in 14 sectors has the potential to create about 60 lakh new jobs over the next five years and more.(d) & (e):	The 14 sectors identified under PLI Schemes follow the broad framework of new and emerging technologies where India can leapfrog, overall economic gain accruing to the economy and export potential of the sectors. These sectors were recommended by NITI Aayog after detailed deliberations with concerned Ministries/ Departments followed by approval of the Union Cabinet. No proposal to add a new sector has been submitted for consideration of the Cabinet. ******
