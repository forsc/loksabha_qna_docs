                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2483






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.07.2019






Government School Teachers




Ritesh Pandey














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of teaching and non-teaching hours spent by Government school teachers, Statewise;(b) whether the Government plans to introduce legislation to restrict the number of non-teachinghours;(c) if so, the timeline for such a legislation; and(d) if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a): No such data is maintained at central level. Section 27 of the Right of Children to Free andCompulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009 states that no teacher shall be deployed for any noneducationalpurposes other than the decennial population census, disaster relief duties or dutiesrelating to elections to the local authority or the State Legislatures or Parliament, as the case maybe. The Ministry of Human Resource Development has also issued guidelines to all States and UTsthat emphasize that teachers should not be deployed for non-educational duties other than thosewhich are in accordance with the RTE Act. Further, the recruitment, service conditions anddeployment of teachers come under the purview of the respective State/ Union Territory (UT)Government.(b) to (d): As per the Schedule to RTE Act, 2009 the minimum number of working days/instructional hours in an academic year are as under:<pre>1. two hundred working days for first class to fifth class;2. two hundred and twenty working days for sixth class to eighth class;3. eight hundred instructional hours per academic year for first class to fifth class;4. one thousand instructional hours per academic year for sixth class to eighth class</pre>Further as per Schedule to RTE Act, 2009 the minimum number of working hours per weekfor the teacher are forty-five teaching including preparation hours.****
