                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3665






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2020






Facilities in Schools




Rajiv Pratap Rudy














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number and percentage of primary, middle, secondary and higher secondaryschools in the country, State-wise especially in Bihar, district-wise, that haveelectricity, toilets and internet and the details thereof;(b) the total number of schools under the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan in the country, withthe strength of students in each school, State-wise;(c) whether there is a shortage of classrooms in many of these schools; and(d) the details of the number of Government and private schools in the country, withan average class size of more than 40 students, State-wise?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a) As per Unified District Information System for Education (UDISE) 2017-18,details of State/UT-wise number and percentage of primary, upper primary, secondaryand higher secondary schools in the country, including Bihar, with electricity, toilets andinternet facilities (6 to 12 class) are at Annexure-I. District-wise information is availableon UDISE website www.udise.in.(b) to (d) The Department of School Education and Literacy has launched anIntegrated Scheme for School Education – Samagra Shiksha which subsumes threeerstwhile Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA), RashtriyaMadhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA) and Teacher Education (TE) from 1st April, 2018.Samagra Shiksha focuses inter alia on improvement in school infrastructure in allStates and Union Territories by providing support for various interventions likeupgradation of schools, strengthening of infrastructure of existing schools and provisionof composite school grant to every school for providing a conducive learningenvironment.State/UT-wise details of number of Government schools along with enrollment atElementary level in the country are at Annexure-II. State/UT-wise details showingaverage Student Classroom Ratio (SCR) of Government schools & percentage ofGovernment and Private schools with Student Classroom Ratio (SCR) more than 40are at Annexure-III.******
