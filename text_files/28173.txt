                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3480






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






Impact of Cyclones




Kunwar Danish Ali














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether number of cyclones and severe cyclones have hit the country during the last five years and the current year;(b) if so, the details of losses suffered and the number of persons killed and injured;(c) whether the Government has assessed the impact of the cyclones in the country, if so, the details thereof;(d) whether the Union Government has announced any relief package/provided any assistance to the affected States, if so, the details thereof, State-wise; and(e) the steps taken/being taken by the Government for coordination among all concerned departments to minimize the losses due to cyclones and other natural calamities in the future?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a):	Yes, Sir.(b) to (d):	The primary responsibility of disaster management rests with the State Government. The State Government concerned undertakes assessment  of  damages  and  provide  financial  relief  in  the  wake of 12 -2-L.S.US.Q.NO.3480 FOR 10.08.2021notified natural disasters including cyclone, from State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) already placed at their disposal. In case of disasters of ‘severe nature’, additional financial assistance is extended to the State from National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF) as per established procedure, which inter alia includes assessment of damages by an Inter Ministerial Central Team (IMCT). As per information provided by the States/Union Territories (UTs), a total of 720 person have lost their lives and 29.70 lakh houses/huts and 58.84 lakh hectare of crop area have been damaged during the period 2016-17 to 2021-22 (as on 03.08.2021), due to cyclones.	Further, details of the additional financial assistance provided from NDRF to the State Governments in respect of damages due to cyclones as per prescribed items and norms are at Annexure-I.  (e):	The National Crisis Management Committee (NCMC), headed by Cabinet Secretary, monitors and coordinates the situation with the affected States and Central Ministries/ Agencies concerned during major disasters. Indian Metrological Department (IMD) issues regular and accurate forecasts & warning bulletins to all the affected/ likely to be affected States/ UTs. -3-L.S.US.Q.NO.3480 FOR 10.08.2021	There are well established institutional mechanisms at the National, State & District levels in the country for appropriate preparedness and taking prompt response measures for effective management of natural disasters. Central Government has established a robust early warning system and has significantly improved the accuracy of weather forecasts. Mock exercises and community awareness programmes are being regularly conducted to create awareness among people. 	The measures taken by the Central and State Governments have significantly improved the disaster management practices, preparedness, prevention and response mechanisms resulting in significant reduction in casualties during natural calamities, including cyclones, in the country. Further, strengthening of the disaster management is a continuing and evolving process of governance.*******
