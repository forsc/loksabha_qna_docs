                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1153






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.06.2019






Diversion of Forest Land




Jayadev Galla














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of each of the proposals sent to the Union Government  by the State Government of Andhra Pradesh for diversion of forest land for infrastructure projects in the new capital of Amaravati;(b)	the status of each of the above proposalsand the reasons for pendency of the same; and(c)	whether there has been a long delay in clearance of the said proposals by the Forest Advisory Committee, if so, the details thereof and the corrective steps taken with regard to proposals from the States of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana?  






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE  IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO) (a) to (c) The details of eachproposal received from Government of Andhra Pradesh for infrastructure projects in the new capital of Amaravati and status thereof is annexed. The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change had received a total of 10 proposals from the State Government of Andhra Pradesh for seeking prior approval under Section-2 of Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 for infrastructure projects in the new capital of Amaravati.  Out of these 10 proposals one proposal has been accorded Stage-I/(in-principle) approval, two proposals have been rejected and for the remaining seven proposals, State Government has been directed to submit revised proposals minimising diversion of forest land and providing justification for such diversion. ***
