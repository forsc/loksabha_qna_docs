                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1881






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.07.2021






Setting up of Medical Oxygen Plants




Hibi Eden













                                                    Faizal P.P. Mohammed
                                                








                                                    Ranjanben Dhananjay Bhatt
                                                








                                                    Rajendra Dhedya  Gavit
                                                








                                                    Su Thirunavukkarasar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government proposes to set up medical oxygen plants in each district of the country and if so, the details thereof and the timelines proposed in this regard;(b) the current status of completion of the already sanctioned 162 Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA) oxygen generating plants with details about each unit including the funds disbursed as on date;(c) the extent to which it will bridge the shortfall of medical oxygen in the country;(d) whether the Government has launched any comprehensive mission to encourage entrepreneurship and generate employment in health sector keeping in view acute shortage of manpower there; and(e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR HEALTH & FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) to (e): Public health is a State subject. The Central Government supplements the efforts of the State Governments in this regard. Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA) is a technology that has been utilized by the Central Government to ensure that the public health facilities are made self-sufficient in medical oxygen. In the first phase, the Government sanctioned 162 PSA oxygen generation plants. Six plants out of these have been reallocated to DRDO for implementation. Of the remaining 156 plants, 153 plants have been installed and 151 plants have been commissioned. The details of these plants is at Annexure-I. A total of 1222 PSA oxygen generation plants with a capacity of 1771.76 MT (including the 162 plants sanctioned in the first phase) have been sanctioned by the Government to be set up in public health facilities across the country. Out of these 1222 plants, as on 20th July, 2021, 245 plants have been commissioned. The State/UT- wise list of these plants is placed at Annexure-II. Apart from this, 351 additional PSA plants are being installed by different Ministries of Central Government in different public health facilities. In order to augment human resource for Covid management so as to ensure adequate healthcare staff in hospitals, the Central Government after consulting the National Medical Commission (NMC) and Indian Nursing Council has issued an advisory to the States/UTs on 03rd May, 2021 vide which the following was conveyed:i. States/UTs were requested to utilize MBBS passed out doctors preparing for NEET (PG) to join Covid care duties.ii. The medical interns may be deployed in Covid Management duties under supervision of their faculty, as part of internship.iii. Services of final year MBBS students may be  utilized for providing tele-consultation and monitoring of mild Covid cases after due orientation under the supervision of faculty.iv. The service of final year PG students (both broad & super-specialty) as residents may continue to be utilized. The services of Senior Residents and Registrars may also continue to be utilized for Covid duties.v. B.Sc / GNM qualified nurses may be utilized in full time Covid nursing duties in ICU etc under supervision of senior doctors and nurses.vi. Final year GNM or B.Sc (Nursing) students awaiting final exam may be given Covid Nursing duties at Government / private facilities under the supervision of senior faculty.vii. The services of Allied Health Care professionals may be utilized for assistance in Covid management based on their training and certification.viii. The States / UTs were advised that the National Health Mission (NHM) norms for contractual human resource engagement by States / UTs may be considered for engaging additional manpower. In addition to above, the Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship has launched the ''Customized Crash Course Programme for COVID Frontline Workers'' under the central component of Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana 3.0 (PMKVY 3.0) with the aim to train about one lakh COVID warriors in six healthcare related job roles. Further, under PMKVY 2.0, approximately 1.16 lakh candidates have been trained and 0.55 lakh candidates placed in the Healthcare sector.
