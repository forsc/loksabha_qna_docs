                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2707






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.07.2019






Population of Livestock




Ashok Mahadeorao Nete














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total number of livestock in the country at present, State-wise;(b) whether the population of livestock is decreasing in the country;(c) if so, the details thereof during the last three years and the reasons therefor; (d) whether the Government is aware of the fact that more than the stipulated number of bovines and other stolen animals are being slaughtered in the slaughterhouses; and(e) if so, the action being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING (DR. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a) As per 19th Livestock Census 2012, the total number of livestock in the country is 512.06 million numbers. The State-wise livestock population is placed at Annexure-I(b) & (c) Livestock Census is conducted quinquennially. The total population of livestock has decreased from 529.70 million numbers in 2007 to 512.06 million numbers in 2012. (d) & (e) Under the distribution of legislative powers between the Union of India and States under Article 246(3) of the constitution, preservation of cattle is a matter on which the legislature of the State has exclusive powers to legislate. Therefore, it is up to the State to enact laws on slaughter of cows. Further, as per Article 48 of Indian Constitution the State shall endeavour to organize agriculture and animal husbandry on modern and scientific lines and shall in particular take steps for preserving, improving the breed, prohibiting the slaughter of cows, calves, other milch and draught cattle.*****
