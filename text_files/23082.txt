                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4166






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2021






Reducing Dependence on Import of Oil




Dushyant Singh













                                                    Mohammad Jawed
                                                








                                                    Uttam Kumar Nalamada Reddy
                                                








                                                    Rita Bahuguna Joshi
                                                








                                                    Preneet Kaur
                                                








                                                    Kumbakudi Sudhakaran
                                                








                                                    Rajendra Agrawal
                                                








                                                    A. Chellakumar
                                                








                                                    M. Selvaraj
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has set an aim of 10 per cent reduction of oil imports by 2022; (b)	if so, the details thereof including the year-on-year progress made since 2014 towards reducing India’s oil import dependence by 10 per cent by 2022 when compared to 2015-16; (c)	the year-wise data of the crude oil imported till the year 2020 including the year-wise data of the imports of crude oil as percentage of total domestic crude oil consumption till the year 2020; (d)	the data of the crude oil produced domestically and the current production capacity of pre- New Exploration Licensing Policy(NELP) blocks as a percentage of total domestic crude oil production, year wise; (e)	the details of cess that is currently charged on the domestic crude produced in pre NELP blocks, year wise; (f)	whether the Government looks to amend the rate of domestic cess and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; (g)	the details of actions taken by the Government to reduce India’s oil import dependence from 2014, including the resulting drop in oil import dependence, if any; and (h)	whether exploration and exploitation of petroleum resources within India’s borders is being undertaken by the Government either by itself or with some form of private partnership and if so, the details thereof in terms of geographical areas where exploration is taking place, the progress so far, and the private entities involved, if any? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN) (a)	& (b) Yes Sir. Government is making all efforts to reduce import dependence. India’s oil and oil equivalent gas import dependence in 2014-15 and 2015-16 were 68.9% and 72.2% respectively and during current April-January, 2020-21 is 77.1%(c)	The year wise details of crude oil imported and details of imports of crude oil as percentage of total crude oil processing are as below: 	2015-16	2016-17	2017-18	2018-19	2019-20	2020-21 (April - Jan) (P)Crude oil import  (In MMT)	202.9	213.9	220.4	226.5	227.0	162.8Crude oil processed (In MMT)	232.9	245.4	251.9	257.2	254.4	182.2Percentage of crude oil import out of total crude oil processed (in %)	87.1	87.2	87.5	88.1	89.2	89.4P: Provisional     (d)	Trends of the crude oil produced domestically and Pre-NELP blocks is as below:	2015-16	2016-17	2017-18	2018-19	2019-20Pre-NELP Blocks (in MMT)	9.0	8.6	8.3	8.4	7.3Country''s Production (in MMT)	36.9	36.0	35.7	34.2	32.2Pre-NELP Blocks % Contribution	24.4%	23.8%	23.3%	24.7%	22.6%(e)	& (f) Oil Industry Development Cess at the rate of 20% ad valorem is applicable on crude oils produced from pre-NELP blocks and at present, there is no such proposal to amend the rate of cess.(g)	Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas has taken several steps inter alia promotion of renewable and alternate fuels like ethanol, second generation ethanol, compressed bio gas and biodiesel, promotion of natural gas as clean fuel/feedstock in the country with a view to move towards a gas based economy, refinery process improvements, promoting energy efficiency and conservation, efforts for increasing production of oil and natural gas through various policies under Production Sharing Contract (PSC) regime, Discovered Small Field Policy, Hydrocarbon Exploration and Licensing Policy, Setting up of National Data Repository, etc. Government has also provided functional freedom to National Oil Companies and wider private sector participation by streamlining approval processes including electronic single window mechanism. Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas is also working in collaboration with various Central Government Ministries/stakeholders to make efforts to achieve reduction in import dependency on oil.(h)	 Exploration of Oil and gas in the country are taking place under:1.	Nomination Regime – by ONGC and OIL2.	PSC/RSC Regime – by Indian & Foreign Companies & JVs, including PSUsAt present, Exploration and Production activities are taking place ~2,75,000 sq.km area across different sedimentary basins across India in onshore and offshore areas under Nomination, PSC and RSC Regimes.******
