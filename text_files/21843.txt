                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

263






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Survey to Identify Heritage Sites




Rahul Kaswan














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has conducted any survey to identify the heritage sites in  the country and if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise;(b)	the details of the heritage sites for which development work has been started during the last three years and the current year;(c)	the details of development work carried out so far, heritage site-wise; and(d)	the details of the heritage sites in Rajasthan proposed to be taken up for holistic development? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE AND TOURISM(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)to(d)	A statement is laid on the table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (a) TO (d) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.263 FOR 15.03.2021(a)	Conducting survey/exploration to identify/ locate ancient sites / monuments is a regular   phenomenon of ASI. Details of potential monuments / sites identified for declaring them as Centrally Protected Monuments / Sites is at Annexure.(b)&(c)	Conservation   and preservation of Centrally Protected Monuments / Sites is taken up on regular basis as per need basis considering availability of funds and resources, which also covers provision for visitor facilities like toilets, drinking water, ramps for differently   abled, illumination, sound and light shows, pathways, signages, cafeterias,   etc. Providing such facilities wherever there is a scope and upgrading existing facilities is a continuous phenomenon.  (d)	There are 163 centrally protected monuments / sites within the state of Rajasthan. Their conservation, preservation and maintenance is planned during the next financial year 2021-22 as per need and priority considering availability of funds and resources, which also includes provision for new facilities and upgrading existing facilities.
