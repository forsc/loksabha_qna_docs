                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3571






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2022






National Rural Drinking Water Programme




Sadashiv Kisan Lokhande














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether any action is being taken under National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP) in the country especially in Shirdi Parliamentary Constituency and other backward areas of Maharashtra;(b) if so, the details of the latest position as on date especially in the said Constituency and other   backward areas; and(c) the details of central funds allotted and utilized along with the number of beneficiaries during the last three years and the current years, year and State-wise? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF JAL SHAKTI(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) to  (c) Since August, 2019,  Government of  India  in  partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) – Har Ghar Jal after subsuming erstwhile National Rural Drinking Water Supply (NRDWP) to make provision of tap water supply to every rural household of the country, including those in Ahmednagar District of Shirdi Parliamentary Constituency and other backward areas of Maharashtra, by 2024. At the time of announcement of Jal Jeevan Mission, out of 18.94 Crore rural households in the country, 3.23 Crore (17%) households were reported to have tap water connections. Since then tap water connection has been provided to additional 6.00 Crore (31.08%) rural households. Thus, as on 21.03.2022, out of 19.32 Crore rural households in the country, 9.24 Crore (47.38%) households are reported to have provision of tap water supply in their homes. States-wise and year-wise details of rural households with provision of tap water supply, are at Annex-ISimilarly, in Maharashtra, 48.44 lakh (33.16%) rural households were reported to have tap water connections. Since then, tap water connections has been provided to additional 53.37 lakh (36.54%) rural households. Thus, as of 21.03.2022, out of total 146.09 lakh rural households in Maharashtra, provision of tap water supply has been made to 101.81 lakh (69.69%) households. District-wise details of progress made in the districts under the Jal Jeevan Mission is in public domain on JJM Dashboard and can be accessed at:https://ejalshakti.gov.in/jjmreport/JJMIndia.aspxThe details of budgetary allocation, fund drawn and reported utilization by States/ UTs under erstwhile NRDWP during 2018-19 and under JJM during 2019-20, 2020-21 and 2021-22 (as on 21.03.2022) is at Annex-II.*****
