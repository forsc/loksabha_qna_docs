                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1233






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.02.2021






Agri-Market Infrastructure Fund Scheme




Vishnu Dayal Ram














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the Agri- Market Infrastructure Fund (AMIF) Scheme aimed at providing loans at lower rates to facilitate the transformation of Haats into Grameen Agricultural Markets (GrAM); (b) 	whether it is true that no State/ UT has submitted proposals under the said scheme; and (c) 	if so, the reasons therefor and the corrective measures taken/being taken by the Government thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????    (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (c):	The Government had approved an Agri-Market Infrastructure Fund (AMIF) with a corpus of Rs. 2000 crore for developing and upgrading agricultural marketing infrastructure in the Grameen Agricultural Markets (GrAMs) and Agriculture Produce Market Committee (APMC) Markets. Government had circulated the Scheme Guidelines to the States/Union Territories (UTs) for submission of proposal under the Scheme. To achieve above objective NABARD has setup AMIF fund of Rs. 2000 crore. State may avail loan from NABARD on recommendation of their proposal/DPRs by Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmer Welfare. Interest Rate to State will be Current market borrowing rate by NABARD plus 0.6% handling charges by NABARD minus fixed interest subvention of 3% by DAC&FW. It is demand driven scheme. States are being persuaded to submit the proposal/DPRs to avail the assistance under this scheme.  So far, State Government of Gujarat has submitted proposal for upgradation of agricultural marketing infrastructure in 133 APMCs.*******
