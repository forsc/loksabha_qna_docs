                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1704






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.09.2020






Restructuring the Railway Board




Sushil Kumar Singh














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has recently restructured the Railway Board;(b) 	if so, the details thereof;(c)	the benefits of such restructuring;(d)	the status of unification of all Group ‘A’ railway services into one service; and(e)	the deadline by which the whole unification of services is likely to take place?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a)	& (b)  Yes, Sir.  Railway Board has recently been re-organized on functional lines headed by Chairman, Railway Board & Chief Executive Officer (CEO) and comprising 4 other members namely:-i)   	Member (Infrastructure), Railway Board ii) 	Member {Traction & Rolling Stock (T&RS)}, Railway Board 	…2/..--2--iii) 	Member {Operations & Business Development (O&BD)}, Railway Board,  iv)      Member (Finance), Railway Board.The posts of Members of the Board are now open to all eligible Officers of Railways regardless of the Organised Service Cadres to which they belong.(c)	The objective of reorganization of Railway Board on functional lines is to put Railways firmly on the path of modernization by energizing the cadre of Railway Officers to work unitedly and single-mindedly with a vision to transform Indian Railways into a world class railway system.(d)	Alternate mechanism and Committee of Secretaries have been formed to consider and finalise the modalities of formation of Indian Railway Management Services (IRMS). (e)	Government had set a time frame of one year from 24.12.2019 to complete the process of unification of all Group ‘A’ services into one service.*****
