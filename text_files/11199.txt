                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

30






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2020






NGOs Working for MDMS




Ganesan Selvam













                                                    Heena Vijaykumar Gavit
                                                








                                                    Dhanush M Kumar
                                                








                                                    Gautham Sigamani Pon
                                                








                                                    Rebati Tripura
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the NGOs are providing meals to schools through centralised kitchens underMid-Day Meal (MDM) scheme and if so, the number thereof, State/ UT-wise;(b) the number of Rural and Urban schools covered by NGOs, State/UT-wise especiallyKanchipuram district of Tamil Nadu;(c) whether the samples collected from NGO’s centralised kitchen have been found to bein compliance with prescribed standards and nutritive values and if so, the detailsthereof and if not, the reasons therefor and the steps taken in this direction;(d) whether it is a fact that NGO’s engaged in preparing mid-day meals for children inrural areas have turned the scheme into a profit making exercise and deviated from thepurpose and if so, the details thereof;(e) whether the Government is considering to eliminate the role of NGOs in preparingmid-day meal in rural areas and if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the StateGovernments thereto; and(f) the other steps taken/being taken by the Government to ensure mid-day meal aremandatorily tested by accredited labs each month for maintaining quality and nutrition?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) & (b): The Mid-Day Meal Scheme (MDMS) is implemented in partnership withStates and UTs. The overall responsibility for providing cooked and nutritious middaymeal to the eligible children lies with the State Governments and UnionTerritory Administrations. State and UT Governments engage NGOs / VoluntaryOrganizations for preparing meals in Centralized kitchens for identified clusters ofschools. As per the information provided by the States and UTs, at present 17 Statesand UTs have engaged NGOs / Voluntary Organizations under the Scheme. In theState of Tamil Nadu no NGOs have been engaged for providing meals in schools.The State and UT-wise details of NGOs engaged and the numbers of rural and urbanschools covered by NGOs under the Scheme are given at Annexure-I.(c) to (f): The Government of India has issued guidelines on quality, safety and hygienein school level kitchens to all the States and UTs to address the quality issue. Theseguidelines inter-alia provide that the States/UTs must consider engaging CSIRInstitutes / NABL accredited laboratories and FSSAI accredited laboratories forundertaking testing of samples of MDM for food safety and contamination. Further,the MDM Rules, 2015 provide for mandatory testing of food samples byGovernment recognized laboratories to ensure that the meals meet nutritionalstandards and quality. As per the information provided by the States and UTs, 1483samples have been collected from schools covered by NGOs and all are found incompliance with the norms prescribed under the Scheme. The State and UT-wisedetails of these samples are given at Annexure-II.States and UTs have not reported any case of engagement of NGOs those haveturned the scheme into a profit making exercise in rural areas across the country. Asper revised guidelines for engagement of Civil Society Organizations (CSO) /NonGovt. Organizations (NGO) in Mid-Day-Meal Scheme. CSO/NGO may be engagedfor supply of hot cooked meal under MDMS for a cluster of schools in urban areasor in identified rural areas which have good road connectivity, for the purpose ofleveraging efficiency gains.********
