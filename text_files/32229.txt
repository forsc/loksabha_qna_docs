                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

321






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.12.2021






Cultivation of Spices




Anubhav Mohanty














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether under the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH), maximum cost of Rs.30,000/- per ha. is given for cultivation of spices like ginger and turmeric, out of which 40% is given as subsidy to the farmers for cultivation;(b)	 whether it is a fact that this cost is very low and needs to be enhanced atleast four folds with immediate effect to encourage and motivate farmers to increase the cultivation of such crops;(c) 	whether the Government will consider enhancing the cost of cultivation; and(d) 	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????                                          (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR) (a) to (d):      A statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (d) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.  321 DUE FOR REPLY ON 21ST DECEMBER, 2021.(a) to (d):	Yes Sir. Under Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH) scheme guidelines assistance is provided for area expansion of rhizomatic spices including ginger and turmeric at a rate of 40% cost norms Rs. 30,000 per hectare i.e. a maximum of Rs. 12000/Ha for cultivation of a maximum area of 4 ha per beneficiary in single installment. MIDH was originally approved for implementation upto 31.03.2020 which has been extended for period upto 31.03.2022. The scheme is being reviewed considering its all aspects for its implementation beyond 31.03.2022.******
