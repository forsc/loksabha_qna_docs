                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

623






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2021






Status of AMRUT Scheme




Diya Kumari














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the current status of implementation of Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) in the country including Rajasthan;(b)	the details/number of cities selected under this Centrally sponsored scheme in the State of Rajasthan along with the criteria of selection;(c) 	the funds allocated and spent under the scheme in Rajasthan during the last three years, year /city-wise; and(d)	whether the Government is planning to include Beawar city in its next phase of the scheme and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                        (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a).	Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) was launched on 25 June 2015 with focus on the development of basic urban infrastructure in the sectors of water supply, sewerage & septage management, storm water drainage, non-motorised urban transport and development of green spaces & parks in 500 selected cities across the country, including 29 cities in Rajasthan.  Under AMRUT, Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs has approved State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) of the States/Union Territories (UTs) amounting to ?77,640 crore for the entire Mission, which include committed Central Assistance of ?35,990 crore. So far, works have been completed for 3,273 projects costing ?15,342 crore, while 2,387 projects costing ? 63,571 crore are under implementation. Besides, 232 projects worth ?2,263 crore are at various stages of tendering. Some States/UTs have taken up projects in excess of their approved SAAP size. In such cases, the entire additional cost of projects shall be borne by the respective State/UT.Further, 97 lakh water tap connections and61 lakh sewer connections have been provided so far under AMRUT and in convergence with other schemes. For the State of Rajasthan, SAAPs amounting to ?3,223.94 crore, which include committed Central Assistance of ?1,541.95 crore, have been approved for the entire Mission period. So far, Government of Rajasthan has completed 65 projects worth ?713.63 crore, while 83 projects costing ?2,442.56 crore are at various stages of implementation. Further, one project costing ?15.41 crore is under tendering. Besides,5.04 lakh water tap connections and3.73 lakh sewer connections have been provided under AMRUT and in convergence with other schemes in the State of Rajasthan.(b).	List of 29 cities of Rajasthan selected under AMRUT has been attached as Annexure-I.  All Urban Local Bodies with a population of one lakh or more as per Census 2011, all other capital cities of States/UTs, all cities selected under Heritage City Development and Augmentation Yojana (HRIDAY) and identified cities on the stem of the main rivers, hill states, islands and tourist destinations were eligible for selection  under the Mission.(c).	Under AMRUT, central funds are allocated/released State-wise only and not city-wise. The details of Central Assistance released to the State of Rajasthan under various components of AMRUT during the last three financial years and the current financial year are given at Annexure II.(d).	Beawer city has already been covered under AMRUT scheme.                                   *****
