                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4133






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Area of Forest Land




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam













                                                    Ranjeeta Koli
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total area of forest land in Gujarat and Rajasthan at present;(b)	the area which is required to be set aside as forest land in various districts of Gujarat and Rajasthan;(c)	whether every district has requisite area of forestland at present;(d)	if so, the details thereof; and(e)	if not, the districts of Gujarat and Rajasthan which do not have the requisite forest area?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)Forest  Survey  of  India,  Dehradun  an organization  under  the  Ministry  carries out the                                                     assessment of forest cover of the country biennially and the findings are published in   India State of Forest Report (ISFR). The forest cover assessment is a wall-to-wall mapping exercise based on remote sensing supported by intensive ground verification and field data from National Forest Inventory.As per the latest report i.e. ISFR- 2019, the total forest cover of the Gujarat is 14,857.33 square kilometreand that of Rajasthan is 16629.51 square kilometre which comprises of 7.57% and 4.86% respectively of the total geographical area of the State. (b)to (e)The National Forest Policy, 1988 providesthat the national goal should be to have aminimum of one-third of the total land area of the country under forest or tree cover. In the hills and in mountainous regions, the aim should be to maintain two-third of the area under such cover in order to prevent erosion and land degradation and to ensure the stability of the fragile eco-system. This goal is a national level goal.
