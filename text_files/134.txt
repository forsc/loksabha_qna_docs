                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    EARTH SCIENCES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

69






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.06.2019






Clean Seas Campaign




Gowdar Mallikarjunappa Siddeshwara














                        Will the Minister of





EARTH SCIENCES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Ministry is proposing to join United  Nations Clean Seas Campaign; (b)	if so, the details of the observatory centres planned;(c)	the steps taken by Government to control marine litter like plastics and microplastics?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER FOR MINISTRY OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY AND MINISTRY OF EARTH SCIENCES(DR. HARSH VARDHAN)(a)	Yes Sir.(b)	As a part of this endeavour, it is proposed to monitor marine coastal pollution at two locations, one each on the east and west coasts of India.(c)	A number of steps have been initiated by the Government of India, including monitoring, banning of single use of plastic bags, generating awareness among the public, school and college students and tourists of better recycling systems adopted for the marine litter management.The data on source of marine litter for various beaches and coastlines being collected are provided to the concerned ministries for taking appropriate measures. Besides, periodical awareness and cleanliness campaigns are being conducted in selected locations, in coordination with various national and international organizations to promote awareness among the public on the importance and the need to preserve our coasts.
