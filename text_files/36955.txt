                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3955






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






Minimizing Adverse Effects of Development Projects




Gowdar Mallikarjunappa Siddeshwara













                                                    Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Wildlife Institute of India (WII) has given necessary suggestions for minimizing the adverse effects of linear development projects (Railway lines, Highways, etc.) passing through forest areas?(b)	if so, the details thereof?(c)	whether the Government is proposing to give them the status of legal obligation in view of the recommendations made by WII and for absolute implementation of these recommendations in linear development projects; and (d)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) and (b) A guidance manual titled “Eco-Friendly Measures to Mitigate the Impacts of Linear Infrastructure on Wildlife” was released by the Ministry in December 2016. The document, which was prepared by the Wildlife Institute of India, provides detailed guidance for planning, locating, designing, implementing and operating smart green roads, railways and power-lines.(c) and (d) The Standing Committee of National Board for Wild Life, in its 47th meeting held on 25th January 2018, recommended that user agencies should take into consideration the guidelines of the Wildlife Institute of India while designing linear infrastructure inside Protected Areas and Eco Sensitive Zones. The Ministry has advised the State/UT Forest Departments that linear infrastructure proposals should be accompanied by an animal passage plan prepared on the basis of the guidelines and in consultation with the State Chief Wildlife Warden.*****
