                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4364






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2019






Forest Rights Act, 2006




Karti P. Chidambaram














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is aware of status of implementation of the Forest Rights 	Act (FRA), 2006 and if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise; (b) 	the response of Central Government on the Supreme Court eviction order of more 	than a million Adivasis and other forest dwellers; (c) 	whether the Government is reviewing the repercussions of such judicial interventions 	and if so, the details thereof; (d)	 whether the Government proposes to amend Forest Rights Act (FRA), 2006 and 	if so, 	the details thereof; and (e) 	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) The Parliament has enacted ‘the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Right) Act, 2006’  (FRA in short) and notified for its operation on 31.12.2007 and the rules made thereunder for implementing various provisions of the Act was also notified on 01.01.2008.FRA is an Act to recognize and vest the forest rights and occupation in forest land in forest dwelling Scheduled Tribes and other traditional forest dwellers who have been residing in such forests for generations but whose rights could not be recorded; to provide for a framework for recording the forest rights so vested and the nature of evidence required for such recognition and vesting in respect of forest land.Ministry of Tribal Affairs is the nodal Ministry for implementation of this Act. As per information received from Ministry of Tribal Affairs, a total number of 19,64,048 titles (18,87,894 individual and 76,154 community claims) have been distributed over an area involving forest lands to the extent of 12938762.14 acres. State-wise status of implementation of Forest Rights Act (FRA), 2006 is given at Annexure.(b)&(c)	The Hon’ble Supreme Court in their recent judgment dated 13.02.2019 in Writ Petition No. 109/2008 in the matter of Wildlife First & Others versus Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change & Others, directed the State/UT Governments to evict the persons/parties whose claims under Forest Rights Act, 2006 have been rejected. Subsequently, Government of India filed an application on 26.02.2019 for modification of the above order. Considering the prayer in the application, the Hon’ble Supreme Court in its order dated 28.02.2019 stayed its earlier order dated 13.02.2019 regarding eviction. (d) &(e)No Sir. There is no such proposal. ***
