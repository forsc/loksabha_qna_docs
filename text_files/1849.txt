                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1687






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Housing for all vis-a-vis PMAY




Pushpendra Singh Chandel













                                                    Vinayak Bhaurao Raut
                                                








                                                    Shrirang Appa Barne
                                                








                                                    Pritam Gopinath Munde
                                                








                                                    Shrikant Eknath Shinde
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government had launched the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) in November, 2016 with the objective of providing pucca (permanent) houses to 2.95 crore rural households by2022; (b)	if so, whether the Government’s target of achieving “housing for all” by 2022 has become unlikely, with new surveys recommending an additional 3.54 crore prospective beneficiaries over the originally estimated 2.95 crore;(c)	if so, the facts in this regard;(d)	whether the Government has verified the number of identified beneficiaries by the State Governments;(e)	if so, the details thereof;(f)	whether the Government is considering to extend the target date for achieving housing for all;(g)	if so, the details thereof; and(h)	the details of people benefited in Bundelkhand from Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana during the last five years?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):	Yes sir. (b) to (g):	To achieve the objective of “Housing for All” by 2022, the erstwhile rural housing scheme Indira Awaas Yojana has been restructured into Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana - Gramin (PMAY-G) with effect from 1st April, 2016 and the target set under PMAY-G is based on the Socio Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011 data. To capture those households who are not included in the Permanent Wait List (PWL) of PMAY-G but fulfil the eligibility criteria, Ministry of Rural Development had developed a mobile application (Awaas+) to capture the details of such households alongwith the geo-tagged photograph of their present dwelling. The data captured through Awaas+ by the States / UTs is under scrutiny / verification.There is no proposal to extend the deadline for construction of 2.95 crore houses under PMAY-G.(h):	Year-wise number of houses constructed under IAY/ PMAY-G in 13 districts of Bundelkhand region in last 5 years is at Annexure I .The details of beneficiaries provided assistance under IAY/ PMAY-G for the past 5 years, i.e. 2014-15 to 2018-19 are available on A2 report of AwaasSoft at website https://.rhreporting.nic.in. The data is drillable till beneficiary level in Village/GP for all the States/UTs.*****
