                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1102






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.09.2020






National Medicinal Plants Board (NMPB)




Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar













                                                    Gautham Sigamani Pon
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH)  be pleased to state:(a) whether the National Medicinal Plants Board (NMPB) has achieved the objective for which it was setup and if so, the details thereof and if not, the corrective steps taken by the Government in this regard;(b) whether a significant requirement of medicinal plants are met from wild sources even as cultivation of medicinal plants has started gaining momentum across the country, if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor;(c) whether the Government is aware of the proposal to develop a corridor of medicinal plants along the banks of Ganga by the NMPB and if so, the details thereof; and(d) the steps taken/proposed to be taken by the Government for the conservation, cultivation and commercial exploitation of indigenous medicinal/aromatic plants along with the funds earmarked, allocated and utilized for the said purpose during each of the last three years, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE (DR. HARSH VARDHAN)(a):	Yes, the Government of India has setup National Medicinal Plants Board (NMPB) in year 2000 with the objective to develop an appropriate mechanism for coordination between various ministries/departments/organizations in India and implements support policies/programs for overall (conservation, cultivation, Research & development, trade and export) growth of medicinal plants sector both at the Central/ state and international level. Since its inception NMPB has been successfully implementing various schemes/programmes on above said activities for overall development of medicinal plant sector in the country.(b):	Yes, As per Demand & Supply study conducted by National Medicinal Plants Board, Ministry of AYUSH, an estimated 80% of the medicinal plants species diversity in annual commercial demand is sourced wholly or partially from the wild from both the forest and non-forest landscape. It includes a large number of species in high annual demand.(c):	 Yes,	National Medicinal Plants Board, Ministry of AYUSH has a proposal to develop a corridor of medicinal plants along the banks of River Ganga to cover more than 800 hectare under cultivation of medicinal plants. (d):	Ministry of AYUSH is implementing following schemes throughout the country for conservation, cultivation and commercial exploitation of indigenous medicinal plants:•	Central Sector Scheme on Conservation, Development and Sustainable Management of Medicinal Plants •	Centrally sponsored scheme of National AYUSH Mission The details of funds allocated and utilized under above schemes during the last three years are given in Annexure – I & IIIn addition to Ministry of AYUSH, other Ministries/Departments viz., Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change; Ministry of Science and Technology; Ministry of Agriculture; Ministry of Jal Shakti Government of India also supports Conservation, Cultivation of medicinal/aromatic plants.
