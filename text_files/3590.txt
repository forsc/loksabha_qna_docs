                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3372






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.07.2019






Zoos in Rajasthan




Sumedhanand Saraswati














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of functional zoos in Rajasthan along with the zoo-wise details of the number of animals in it;(b)	whether new animals have been brought in to such zoos during the last three years and if so, the details thereof;(c)	the steps being taken by the Government to increase the number of new animals every year and for the upgradation of these zoos;(d)	whether the Government has chalked out any plan for the upgradation of zoos of Rajasthan and if so, the details thereof; and (e)	whether the Government proposes to transfer animals from zoos of Rajasthan to other places or other zoos and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	As informed by the Central Zoo Authority, there are five Zoos in the State of Rajasthan that are recognized under Section 38-H of the Wild Life (Protection)Act, 1972. Number of animals reported by zoos located in the State of Rajasthan is given in the Annexure-I. (b)	Yes, Sir. Details on acquisition of animals by zoos located in the State of Rajasthan during last three years, approved by the Central Zoo Authority, under Section 38-I of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, is given in the Annexure-II. (c)	The Central Zoo Authority, as per the existing guidelines, approves Master Plan for long-term development of zoos and takes decision on proposals regularly received from the Zoo Operator for acquisition/exchange of animals according to approved Animal Collection Plan of the respective zoo. Further, the Central Zoo Authority regularly evaluates zoos and stipulates conditions for compliance to upgrade zoos as per standard norms prescribed in the Recognition of Zoo Rules, 2009.In case of recognized zoos located in the State of Rajasthan, the Central Zoo Authority Approved Layout Plan of the Machida Biological Park, Jodhpur Marudhara Biological Park, Bikaner, Nahargarh Biological Park, Jaipur and Sajjangarh Biological Park, Udaipur respectively.Respective Zoo Operator is responsible for establishment and operation of zoos including acquisition of captive animals as per approved Animal Collection Plan and upgradation of the zoo as per approved Master Plan. (d)	Yes, Sir, the Central Zoo Authority approves Master Plan for long-term development of zoos including that of Rajasthan. Master Plan of a Zoo is a comprehensive document dealing with planned development of infrastructure.(e)	  Proposal for transfer / exchange / acquisition of animals is made by the respective Zoo Operator. Details of proposals approved by the Central Zoo Authority, during last three years, under Section 38-I of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, for transfer or exchange or acquisition of animals from or to zoos located in the State of Rajasthan are at Annexure-II. ******
