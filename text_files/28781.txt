410

30.11.2021






Losses due to Rains and Floods




Su Thirunavukkarasar

                                                    Vijayakumar (alias) Vijay Vasanth
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether many States in the country have been affected by heavy rains and consequent flash floods, landslides, cloudbrusts, etc. in the recent months;(b) if so, whether any assessment has been made by the Government about the number of persons killed, displaced and missing along with loss of standing crops, cattle and the extent of damage to property and monetary loss, the details thereof, State/UT-wise including Telangana and Tamil Nadu and Kerala;(c) whether any Commission has been constituted to study the loss and environmental damage caused due to the incessant rain and floods and the details thereof;(d) the details of proposals received from the State Governments to compensate the said losses and assistance sought, sanctioned and released by the Union Government and through NDRF and SDRF along with the criteria for it during the last three years and the current year, year-wise and State/UT-wise including Telangana, Tamil Nadu and Kerala;(e) the details of NDRF teams deployed in the affected States to take up damaged works, help farmers, etc., along with the number of persons rescued, State/UT-wise; and(f) the measures taken to evolve a permanent mechanism to deal with such calamities and thereby ensure safety of people and property in affected States?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) to (d):  Various States in the country have been affected by natural calamities, including rains, flash flood, flood, landslides, cloudburst etc., during the monsoon season of 2021. State-wise details of the losses of human lives, damage to property and loss of crops due to hydro-meteorological calamities including floods, landslides, during the year 2021-22, as assessed/ reported by the State Governments/ Union Territories including the States of Tamil Nadu and Kerala, are at Annexure-I.  No such details of losses have been reported by the State of Telangana, so far.There are well established institutional mechanisms at the National and State level for the effective management of natural disasters. The primary responsibility  of disaster  management, including  undertaking  disbursal   of  relief   to  the   affected     people,  rests  with   the  State   Governments.   --2--LS.US.Q.NO. 410 FOR 30.11.2021For providing relief to the affected people, in case of 12 notified natural disasters including flood, State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF) is already placed at the disposal of the State Governments.  Additional financial assistance is provided from National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF), as per laid the down procedure in case of disaster of ‘severe nature’, which includes an on the spot assessment of damages by an Inter-Ministerial Central Team (IMCT). The financial assistance is towards relief and not for compensation of loss as suffered/ claimed.  The Guidelines on Constitution and Administration of National Disaster Response Fund (NDRF) is available on website at https:// ndmindia.mha.gov.in/ images/NDRFSDRF_06042017.pdf. No Commission has been constituted to study the loss and environmental damage caused due to rain and flood by this Ministry.State-wise details of allocation and releases from SDRF/ State Disaster Risk Management Fund (SDRMF) [after recommendations of 15th Finance Commission, which includes SDRF and State Disaster Mitigation Fund (SDMF)] and NDRF during the last three years and current year are given in Annexure-II. (e)    During the Monsoon season of 2021, National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) teams were deployed in the flood prone areas in the country and rescued/ evacuated stranded persons including farmers to safer places, as per details given in Annexure-III. --3--LS.US.Q.NO. 410 FOR 30.11.2021(f) 	There are institutional mechanisms at the National, State & district level in the country to develop appropriate preparedness and prompt response mechanism for effective management of natural disasters. Central Government has established a robust early warning system and has significantly enhanced the accuracy of weather forecasts.  Forecasting agencies are continuing their efforts for the improvement of warning and dissemination systems vigorously. Mock exercises and community awareness programmes are being regularly conducted to educate people/ farmers at the time of natural calamities.The measures taken by the Central and State Governments have significantly improved the disaster management practices, preparedness, prevention and response mechanism, resulting in significant reduction in casualties during the natural calamities in the country. Further, strengthening of the disaster management is a continuing and evolving process of governance.*****
