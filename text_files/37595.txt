                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5012






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.04.2022






Cargo Handling by Ports




Thomas Chazhikadan














                        Will the Minister of





PORTS, SHIPPING AND WATERWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of cargo handled by different ports in the country during each of the last three years, port-wise;(b) the details of financial assistance given to States including Kerala to develop ports under the Sagarmala project during the last five years, port-wise;	(c) the details of progress made in the construction of Vizhinjam and Trivandrum Ports and the expected date of the commissioning of the said ports; and(d) the details of the estimated annual cargo handling capacity of these ports?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	The details of cargo handled by Major Ports and Non-Major Ports during each of the last three years are given at Annexure-I and Annexure-II respectively.(b)	Sagarmala Scheme intends to achieve the broad objectives of enhancing the capacity of major and non-major ports and modernizing them to make them efficient, thereby enabling them to become drivers of port led economic development. Under Sagarmala Programme, Ministry has provided financial assistance to State Governments for port infrastructure projects, coastal berth projects, road & rail projects, fisheries harbours, skill development projects, coastal community development, international cruise terminal and unique and innovative projects such as Ro-Pax ferry services etc.The funds sanctioned for development of Port-led infrastructure in Coastal States including Kerala under Sagarmala Programme during the last five years are as under:- (Rs. in Crore)	<pre>	Financial Year	2017-18	2018-19	2019-20	2020-21	2021-22(as on date)Funds Sanctioned	479.07	360.09	376.89	246.86	378.60                                               </pre>(c)	Both Vizhinjam Port and Trivandrum Port are Non-Major Ports and they are under the administrative control of the Government of Kerala. As per information received from the State Authorities, the status of these ports are as under:-(i)	Vizhinjam Port: The progress made in the construction of Vizhinjam Port is given below:-<pre>Port Components	Progress3100-meter-long rubble mound breakwater	30%Dredging & Reclamation works (7 million m3)	33%Container Berth (800m x 60m)	50%Container Yard Development (53 Ha)	60%Port Equipment (ship-to-shore cranes, yard cranes, tugs, etc.)	50%                               </pre>(ii)	Trivandrum Port: Trivandrum Port, also known as Valiyathura Port, was one of the early ports in South Kerala. There is a 703 feet long concrete pier which still survives. Trivandrum pier was declared inactive in the early eighties and presently, the port is non-functional.(d)	During Phase – 1, the estimated annual cargo handling capacity of the Vizhinjam Port is 1 million TEUs.
