                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

290






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2021






Shortage of Warehouses




Ramesh Chander Kaushik














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether there is an acute shortage of warehouses and cold storages in all the States including Haryana; (b) 	if so, the details of existing warehouses and cold storages in the country, State-wise; (c) 	the details of the action plan of the Government to increase their capacity; (d) 	whether the Government has fixed any time limit in this regard; and (e) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E RMINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMERCE & INDUSTRY AND CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) to (e): 	A statement is laid on the Table of the House.**** STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (e) OF THE STARRED QUESTION NO. *290 DUE FOR ANSWER ON 16.03.2021 IN THE LOK SABHA.(a) to (e):  	Food Corporation of India (FCI) stores Wheat, Rice and Pulses which do not require cold storage. Requirement of storage capacity depends on the level of procurement & offtake under National Food Security Act (NFSA) & Other Welfare Scheme (OWS).  FCI continuously assesses and monitors the storage capacity and based on the storage gap assessment, storage capacities are created/hired. Total storage capacity [both covered and Cover & Plinth (CAP)] available with FCI (owned as well as hired) and State Agencies for storage of central pool stocks as on 31st March during the last five years & as on 31.01.2021 with corresponding stock position in central pool is given below:As on	Storage capacity owned by FCI	Storage capacity hired by FCI	Storage capacity with State agencies	Total Storage capacity  to store Central Pool Stock	Stock position31.03.16	154.07	203.82	456.95	814.84	366.9931.03.17	154.21	352.71	420.22	772.93	311.4031.03.18	154.44	208.06	480.53	843.03	381.0431.03.19	153.35	235.30	322.67	711.32	463.8631.03.20	153.79	258.24	343.91	755.94	569.3931.01.21	152.80	256.61	397.52	806.93	561.93 As such, there is sufficient capacity for storage of central pool foodgrains at the national level. State wise no. of storage available with FCI is at Annexure-I.Central Warehousing Corporation (CWC):CWC is operating 423 warehouses throughout the country with a total operational storage capacity of 131.23 lakh MT as on 1st February, 2021. CWC is not operating any cold storage facility in the country. The State wise details of CWC warehouses is at Annexure – II. ………..2/--2-	Central Railside Warehouse Company (CRWC):  The warehousing infrastructure based on the demand of the trade.  As on 11.03.2021 CRWC has 20 Railside Warehouse Complexes (RWCs) with a total storage capacity of 3.55 LMT. At these warehouses, the rail borne cargo is handled e.g. cement, fertilizer, sugar, salt, and FMCG goods. The State wise details of CRWC warehouses is at Annexure – III.As far as cold storage is concerned the National Horticulture Board (NHB), Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare is implementing only a scheme namely “Capital Investment Subsidy for Construction/ Expansion/ Modernization of Cold Storages and Storages for Horticulture Products”. The details of state-wise cold storage sanctioned including the State of Haryana since inception  1999-2000 to 2020-21 (as on 25.01.2021) is given at Annexure-IVMinistry of Food Processing Industries is implementing the Scheme for Integrated Cold Chain and Value Addition Infrastructure as one of the component of Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana with the objective of reducing post-harvest losses of horticulture & non-horticulture produce and providing remunerative price to farmers for their produce.The Ministry of Food Processing Industries has so far approved 324 Integrated Cold Chain Projects for financial assistance including 16 projects in the state of Haryana. Out of these 324 projects, 218 projects have been completed and commenced commercial operation with the combined capacity of 7.02 Lakh MT of Cold storage / CA /MA Storage/ frozen Storage and 2.38 Lakh MT of Cold storage / CA /MA Storage/ frozen Storage capacity is projected to be created out of the remaining 106 under implementation projects. State-wise number of cold chain projects is at Annexure-V. The details of 16 Integrated Cold Chain Projects approved by the Ministry of Food Processing Industries in the State of Haryana are at Annexure-VI.FCI is augmenting its storage capacity through following schemes :(i).        Construction of godowns under Private Entrepreneur Guarantee Scheme: Under this Scheme, covered storage capacity is created by private parties, CWC, SWCs and other State Agencies for guaranteed hiring by FCI. A capacity of 153.05 lakh MT has been sanctioned and capacity of 144.10 lakh MT has already been completed as on 31.01.2021 under this scheme. Thus, this scheme is almost in its final stage.………..3/--3-(ii).	Central Sector Scheme: Government is implementing a Central Sector Scheme for construction of godowns with focus on augmenting storage capacity in the States of North Eastern (NE) Region and a few other States. Under this Scheme, funds are released directly to FCI in the form of equity for land acquisition and construction of storage godowns and infrastructure like railway sidings, electrification, installation of weighbridge, etc. Funds are also released as grants-in-aid to the Governments of the North-Eastern States including Jammu & Kashmir for construction of intermediate storage godowns considering the storage gaps as well as difficult geographical & climatic conditions in these States. During 12th Five Year Plan (2012- 17), a total capacity of 1,84,175 MT has been created in NE States and other than NE States (1,37,680 MT by FCI and a capacity of 46,495 MT by State Governments). This scheme has been extended for 5 years from 01.04.2017 to 31.03.2022. A total capacity of 54,250 MT (45,870 MT by FCI and 8,380 MT by State Governments) has been created from 01.04.2017 to 31.01.2021. (iii).   CAP Hiring Scheme-2019: Cover & Plinth (CAP) storage facilities have been created under this scheme in major wheat procuring states viz. Punjab, Haryana, Madhya Pradesh & Uttar Pradesh mode by offering private investor guaranteed hiring by FCI for three years in 2019. Under this scheme a capacity of 16.13 LMT has been constructed.(iv) Construction of Silos Under PPP mode: In order to upgrade and modernize the storage facilities, Government of India has chalked out an Action Plan in 2016 for construction of steel silos on PPP (Public Private Partnership) mode in the country. Silos with capacity of 30.75 LMT at various locations throughout country have been awarded. Out of which a capacity of 8.25 LMT is complete and remaining is under various stages of development.  (v) Hiring of godown from CWCs/SWCs/State Agencies: FCI also hires godown from CWCs/SWCs on guaranteed reservation basis to meet the requirement of storage space.  As on 31.01.2021, a capacity of 29.46 LMT from CWCs and 88.75 LMT capacity from SWCs/State agencies have been hired.(vi) Private Warehousing Scheme (PWS): In addition, as and when, required godowns are hired through e-tendering under Private Warehousing Scheme (PWS) from private parties by offering guaranteed hiring of two year extendable by one year to address immediate / urgent requirement of storage space.  As on 31.01.2021, a capacity of 7.85 LMT has been hired through PWS.………..4/--4-In addition, State Governments have also been requested for augmentation of Storage Capacity based on the level of procurement and allocation under NFSA/OWS.Further, creation of Capacity is a continuous process and based on storage gap assessment, Storage Capacity is constructed /hired by FCI, from time to time.CWC has planned for creating 7.50 lakh MT additional storage capacity through its owned resources during the period from 2020-21 to 2024-25. Details placed at Annexure – VII.
