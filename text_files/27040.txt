                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    EXTERNAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2617






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.08.2021






Cultural Diplomacy




Riti Pathak














                        Will the Minister of





EXTERNAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is expanding cultural diplomacy around the World through Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR) Centres via various Indian cultural activities, cultural heritage  and in recent landscape through ancient yoga;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	the list of the countries where these centres are running; and(d)	the details of the employees working in these centres and the process of their recruitment?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EXTERNAL AFFAIRS(SMT. MEENAKSHI LEKHI)   (a)&(b)	The Government of India through the Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR), has been expanding cultural diplomacy around the world vide its activities in various fields including inter-alia, teaching of Yoga, Dance, Music and Hindi; sponsoring visits of performing art groups; organizing Indian festivals; hosting foreign cultural groups; organizing / supporting conferences /seminars /workshops in various fields,  promotion of Yoga & Ayurveda including celebration of International Day of Yoga; supporting Chairs of Indian Studies in various foreign universities; offering scholarships to foreign students to study in India; hosting eminent academicians/distinguished visitors in India;  gifting of busts/statues of Mahatma Gandhi and other national heroes; exchanging visual art exhibitions.(c)	ICCR runs 37 Indian Cultural Centres located at Georgetown (Guyana), Paramaribo (Suriname), Port Louis (Mauritius), Jakarta (Indonesia), Moscow (Russia), Berlin (Germany),  Cairo (Egypt), London (United Kingdom),  Durban (South Africa),  Tashkent (Uzbekistan), Nur-Sultan (Kazakhstan), Port of Spain (Trinidad & Tobago),  Colombo (Sri Lanka), Dushanbe (Tajikistan), Bali (Indonesia), Suva (Fiji), Kathmandu (Nepal), Kabul (Afghanistan), Beijing (China), Bangkok (Thailand), Tokyo (Japan), Kuala Lumpur (Malaysia), Dhaka (Bangladesh), Thimphu (Bhutan), Mexico City (Mexico), Budapest (Hungary), Prague (Czech Republic), Yangon (Myanmar), Dar-es-Salaam (Tanzania), Male (Maldives), Seoul (South  Korea), The Hague (Netherlands), Sao Paulo (Brazil), Tehran (Iran),  Sydney (Australia), Hanoi (Vietnam) and Tel Aviv (Israel).ICCR also extends support to the Indian Cultural Centre in Valladolid (Spain) as per the MoU signed in December 2018.(d)	A total of 110 teachers are deployed in Indian Mission/Post/ICCs for teaching Indian Dance, Music, Hindi and Yoga. Their selection is done through open advertisement. Support staff for administration, accounts & housekeeping are hired locally.***
