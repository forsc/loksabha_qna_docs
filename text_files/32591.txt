                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

121






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.02.2022






Optical Fibre




Sisir Kumar Adhikari













                                                    Rajan Baburao  Vichare
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has undertaken to connect all the villages of the country with optical fibre by 2023; (b) 	if so, the details thereof along with the amount of funds likely to be spend on it, State/UT-wise; (c) 	whether the Government plans to provide mobile connectivity in villages which are located in strategic and border areas; and (d) 	if so, the details thereof along with the timeline by which the said connectivity is likely to be provided in the aforesaid areas?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS (SHRI DEVUSINH CHAUHAN)(a) & (b) 	BharatNet project is implemented in a phased manner to provide broadband connectivity to all Gram Panchayats (GPs) of the country. The scope of BharatNet has now been extended upto all inhabited villages beyond GPs. On 30.06.2021, Union Cabinet accorded approval for a revised strategy for implementation of BharatNet through Public-Private Partnership (PPP) model in 16 States of the country covering about 3.61 lakh villages, and a Request for Proposal (RFP) was floated on 20.07.2021 for implementation of the same.The estimated maximum Viability Gap Funding (VGF) from Universal Service Obligation Fund (USOF), approved for the above PPP model, is Rs. 19,041 crore and the details of the same are given in Annexure-I.(c) & (d) 	Mobile services are provided in a phased manner by the Government and Telecom Services Provider (TSPs) in the uncovered villages of the country, including those located in strategic and border areas. The Government has the following schemes with USOF funding in this regard:(i)	Under 354 Uncovered Villages Scheme, border villages of Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh, Himachal Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Rajasthan, Gujarat, Uttarakhand and other priority areas are included. (ii)	Comprehensive Telecom Development Plan (CTDP) for mobile connectivity in the North Eastern Region. (iii)	Schemes for providing mobile services in Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected areas and Aspirational Districts.
