                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2022






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.02.2021






Decrease in Area of Dense Forest




Narayana Swamy Abbaiah














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the area of dense forest in the country has decreased and if so, the details thereof;(b)	 the area which was earmarked as dense forest during the last five years, Year-wise and State/UT-wise;(c)	whether any State-wise report on environment degradation and water deficiency of arid/acute drought prone districts is available and if so, the details thereof; (d)	whether the Government has launched any special projects which make afforestation in the arid/ drought prone regions, mandatory and if so, the details thereof; and (e)	the other measures being taken by the Government to increase the area of dense forest across the country?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) & (b) No Sir, as per latest India State of Forest Report (ISFR)-2019 prepared by Forest Survey of India, Dehradun, the total dense forest of the country is 4,07,750square kilometre (km2).  The ISFR-2019 shows an increase of 1274 km2 of dense forest cover over that of ISFR 2017. The State-UT wise details of dense forest as per ISFR-2019, ISFR-2017 and ISFR-2015 is given in Annexure. (c) There is no such report available in the ministry. (d) Ministry is implementing various afforestation schemes including National Afforestation Programme, Compensatory Afforestation Fund Management and Planning Authority (CAMPA) and Green India Mission under which afforestation projects are carried out on degraded forest/non-forest land which includes arid land as well as area which are prone to drought. (e) AR (Artificial Regeneration) and ANR (Assisted Natural Regeneration) are taken up for enhancing the growing stock of the forest. Further, the Working Plan prescriptions covering various silvicultural operations are aimed at improving the health and vigour of the natural forest. ********
