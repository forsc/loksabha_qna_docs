                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1342






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.11.2019






Excavations in Keeladi




M.K. Vishnu Prasad














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	Whether the archaeological excavations in Keeladi, Vaigai Plains, Tamil Nadu have confirmed rich cultural deposits dating back to 6th century BCE; (b)	if so, the details thereof; and(c)	The role of ASI in the excavations and the funds allocated for the purpose during the last two years?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE & TOURISM(PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) & (b)	The earliest period is represented by finer variety of Black & Red ware, associated red slipped ware, terracotta ring wells and brick structure. (c) 	The ASI issues approval for the excavation at Keeladi, being conducted by the Department of Archaeology, Tamil Nadu at present. The Government of Tamil Nadu is conducting the excavation within their available funds.
