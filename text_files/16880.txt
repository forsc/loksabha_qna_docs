                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

736






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Shortage of Drivers




Rajeshbhai Naranbhai Chudasama














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 whether it is a fact that the transport and logistics sector in the country has a shortage of 22 lakh drivers;(b)	 if so, the details thereof;(c)	 whether the Government is considering to provide any relaxation to the drivers of medium and heavy transport vehicles; and(d)	 if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) to (d) In order to tide over the shortage of drivers for transport and logistics sector in the country and considering that driving is more of a skill rather than possession of educational qualifications, the Government has notified GSR 681(E) dated 23rd September, 2019 to remove the condition of mandatory educational qualification required for driving transport vehicles under Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989. Further, the Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019 provides for the omission of provision under sub-section (4) of Section 9 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 which prescribed for such minimum educational qualification by the Central Government. *****
