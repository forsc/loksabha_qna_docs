                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    DEFENCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4818






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2021






Vacant Posts in Navy




Mala Rajya Laxmi Shah













                                                    Pushpendra Singh Chandel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





DEFENCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of vacant posts in Navy along with the details thereof; (b) the efforts made by the Government to increase the power and capacity of Navy during the last five years along with the details thereof; and (c) whether the Government has made efforts to strengthen maritime security in the Indian ocean region and has signed pact with various countries in this regard; and (d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE					(SHRI SHRIPAD NAIK)IN THE MINISTRY OF DEFENCE   (a) The details of vacant posts in Indian Navy as on 31 Jan 2021 are as under:-Category	Vacant PostsOfficers	1044Sailors	12317Civilians	18072(b)  Enhancing the combat capability/ modernisation of Indian Navy is an ongoing process and is undertaken in accordance with Long Term Integrated Perspective Plan (LTIPP).(c) & (d):  Yes, Sir. The Indian Navy maintains regular presence and surveillance in India’s maritime ‘Areas of Interest’. Indian Navy Operation in the Indian Ocean Region (IOR) are aimed at sustained surveillance for enhanced Maritime Domain Awareness (MDA), to show presence, address military challenges and tackle contingencies such as anti- Piracy, Humanitarian Aid and Disaster Relief (HADR) and Search and Rescue (SAR). To strengthen Maritime Security in the IOR, the Government has undertaken foreign cooperation initiatives with friendly foreign Navies through vast array of cooperative measures including capacity building, capability enhancement, constructive engagements and collaborative efforts etc. The details of pacts signed by the Indian Navy, to enhance maritime security in the IOR are as follows:-(i)	Hydrography - 7(ii)	White Shipping Agreement – 22(iii)	Reciprocal Logistics Agreement – 6Indian Coast Guard (ICG) has also signed bilateral Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)/ Memorandum of Cooperation (MoC) with Coast Guard of eight countries towards establishment of collaborative relation to combat transnational illegal activities at sea and develop regional cooperation.*****
