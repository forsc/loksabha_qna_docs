                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    DEFENCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4692






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2021






Training Aircraft




Ramcharan Bohra














                        Will the Minister of





DEFENCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of aircraft which are being used for the training of pilots of Indian Air Force and their types;(b) whether there is shortage of pilots in Indian Air Force;(c) if so, the details thereof; and(d) the steps being taken/proposed to be taken by the Government to meet the shortage?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE					(SHRI SHRIPAD NAIK)IN THE MINISTRY OF DEFENCE   (a)  Presently, 260 Aircraft are being utilized for training of pilots of Indian Air Force.  The trainer aircraft held by IAF includes Pilatus PC-7 Mk-II ac (Basic Trainer Aircraft), Kiran Mk-I/IA ac (Intermediate Jet Trainer) and Hawk Mk-132 ac (Advanced Jet Trainer).  For training of Helicopter Pilots 14 Mi-17 helicopters and for transport pilots 26 Dorniers and 16 AN-32 are being used.(b)  Yes, Sir. There is a shortage of pilots in IAF.(c) (i)  As on 01 March, 2021, sanctioned establishment and strength of pilots in IAF is as follows:-Sanctioned Establishment	Strength4239	3834      (ii)  There is a shortage of 405 pilots (as on 01 March, 2021) in the IAF against the authorized establishment. (d) (i)  The induction numbers have been increased and a total of 203 Joining Instructions were issued for the Flying branch (NDA/AFA) in 2020.      (ii)  Additional Selection Centre (5 AFSB) at Guwahati will commence testing from April, 2021.  The new centre will enable candidates from the North Eastern part of the country to appear for Selection.  The same will increase the total number of candidates tested and thereby address the shortage. *****
