                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    DEVELOPMENT OF NORTH EASTERN REGION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

569






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2020






Act East Policy




Tapir Gao














                        Will the Minister of





DEVELOPMENT OF NORTH EASTERN REGION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the progress of Act East Policy in connecting South East Asian countries along with the number of border trade centres that has been established other than Manipur and Mizoram; (b) the number of kilometres of road connectivity that has been constructed connecting South East Asian countries and the details of air connectivity that has been established; (c) the number of inland water connectivity that has been established under Act East Policy; (d) the total economic growth of border trades in the last three years in the North East; and (e) whether Manipur and Mizoram border trade centres have been established in world class with banking, currency exchange, IT etc.?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (Independent Charge) of the Ministry of Development of North Eastern Region[Dr. Jitendra Singh](a) to (c)    India’s ‘Act East’ policy focuses on the extended neighbourhood with the primary objective of promoting economic cooperation, cultural ties and develop strategic relationship with countries through continuous engagement at bilateral, regional and multilateral levels which would eventually provide enhanced connectivity to the states of North Eastern region. India''s projects in Myanmar includes Kaladalan Multi Modal Transit Transport Project and the construction of the India-Myanmar -Thailand Trilateral Highway, which includes the restoration of 69 bridges including an approach road and upgradation of Kalewa Yargyi road segment in Myanmar. Under connectivity with Myanmar, India had funded and constructed the 160 Km road segment (Tamu-Kyigone-Kalewa segment) of the Trilateral Highway from Moreh in India to Mae Sot in Thailand through Myanmar in 2001 and maintained it till 2009 before handing over to the Government of Myanmar. Projects in Bangladesh include Akhaura- Agartala rail link and Dredging of Jamuna and Kushiyara rivers in Bangladesh and signing of Memorandum of Understanding and Standard Operating Procedures with Bangladesh for the use of Chattogram and Mongla Ports of Bangladesh for movement of goods to and from India.  The Kaladan Multi Modal Transit Transport Project consists of a waterway component on Kaladan river from Sittwe to Paletwa in Myanmar and a road component from Paletwa to Zorinpui on the India-Myanmar border in Mizoram State for   interlinking India and Myanmar by providing alternate connectivity for India’s North Eastern states via Myanmar through road and inland water transport routes. It comprises of construction of Sittwe Port and IWT terminal at Paletwa; fairway development of Kaladan river (Sittwe - Paletwa stretch) and 109 Km roads component uptill Mizoram Border. Under the KMTTP, 158 Km Port and Waterways component and construction of 06 IWT vessels (300 tonnes each) is completed.   Other than Manipur and Mizoram, there are no operational border trade centres with Myanmar. Presently four Border Haats are operational at (i) Kalaichar  – Meghalaya; (ii) Balat  – Meghalaya; (iii) Kamalasagar – Tripura; (iv) Srinagar – Tripura. Besides, the Integrated Check Post (ICP) at Moreh in the state of Manipur, ICP at Agartala, Tripura is also operational.   As regards completing road connectivity to South East Asian countries, 86.20 Km out of 88.00 Km 2-Lane Road with paved shoulder from Lawngtalia -India /Myanmar Border has been completed  in Mizoram. Jairampur to Pangsupass section of NH-315 connecting India and Myanmar having length of 32.49 Km was completed under SARDP-NE in the year 2015 in Arunachal Pradesh. Besides, about 777 km (10 Nos) length of road are completed including maintenance and 1477 km (20 Nos) length of road is under progress by the Border Roads Organisation in Manipur and Mizoram. 28 Km length of Imphal-Moreh (Myanmar-Border) Section of NH 39 (2 laning) is complete.    At present NOK AIR is operating twice a week flight between Guwahati and Bangkok for air connectivity. (d)	 to(e)    India’s trade via North Eastern States during the last three years from 2017-18 to 2019-20 (April- November) is given at Annexure I.  Integrated Check Post (ICP), Moreh in the state of Manipur along India Myanmar   border has been developed to facilitate trade and passenger movement across the land border and is operational. The Land Customs Station at Zokhawthar, Mizoram has certain constraints relating to banking, internet connectivity etc.*****
