                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2759






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Difficulties Faced by MSMEs During Pandemic




Abhishek Banerjee














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether any survey has been undertaken to evaluate the condition of start-ups and MSMEs after Covid-19 pandemic and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether the Government provided any time extensions to start ups and MSMEs for contracts with PSUs and the Government and if so, the details thereof; and (c)  	the details of the current pendency of bill payments from PSUs and the Government to MSMEs and start-ups?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI BHANU PRATAP SINGH VERMA)(a):	An online study of 5,774 MSMEs spread across 32 States & UTs was conducted by National Small Industries Corporation (NSIC) in August 2020. NSIC’s study found that 91% of the MSME respondents were found to be functional. Khadi and Village Industries Commission (KVIC) has conducted a study of 10,519 respondent MSMEs in March, 2020. KVIC’s study found that 88% of beneficiaries of the Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP) scheme were negatively affected due to Covid-19 while the remaining 12% stated that they benefitted during Pandemic. In a study of 1,029 MSMEs spread across 20 States & 2 UTs conducted during September, 2021 - January, 2022 by Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI) 67% of respondent MSMEs were found to be temporarily closed for upto a period of 3 months.  (b):	No, Sir.(c):	The current pendency of bill payments from PSUs and the Government to MSEs as per the data available on SAMADHAAN Portal as on 14.03.2022 is Rs. 2,857.78 Cr.
