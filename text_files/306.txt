                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

289






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.06.2019






School Management Committees




Janardan Singh Sigriwal














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of assistance provided to States/UTs by the Government during the last three yearsto augment capacity for school monitoring and planning by School Management Committees(SMCs) members;(b) whether the Government has taken steps to address the low utilisation of funds under the‘SMC training and community mobilisation’ component by the States; and(c) if so, the details thereof ?
                                                





ANSWER




Minister of Human Resource Development(Shri Ramesh Pokhriyal ‘Nishank’)(a) to (c): Section 21(1) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act,2009 provides for a School Management Committee (SMC) in schools other than unaidedschools and envisages dynamic role of the community in effective monitoring of schools.Section 21(2) of the Act lays down the following functions of the SMCs namely: (i) Monitorthe working of the school (ii) prepare and recommend school development plan (iii) monitorthe utilization of the grants received from the appropriate Government or local authority orany other source (iv) perform such other functions as may be prescribed.Under erstwhile Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan(SSA) andRashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan(RMSA), there was provision for financial assistanceto States/UTs for training of the members of School Management Committees (SMCs)/School Management Development Committees(SMDCs). SSA and RMSA have now beensubsumed under the newly launched Centrally Sponsored Scheme of ‘Samagra Shiksha’from 2018-19. The integrated scheme assigns special importance to decentralised planningand implementation with active involvement of community members, teachers, parents, localbodies including Panchayati Raj Institutions, Municipal Bodies, and other stakeholders in themanagement of schools through SMCs.Under Samagra Shiksha, there is specific provision of financial assistance of up to Rs.1500per school under ‘Media and Community Mobilisation’ and upto Rs. 3000 per school forTraining of SMC/SDMC members for Government Schools. The outlay approved for thispurpose for the States/UTs during the last three years is given below:<pre>(Rs. In lakhs)Year Outlay Approved2016-17 19409.852017-18 18864.882018-19 29753.75</pre>Funds under the schemes are released in lump-sum and not component wise. Every yearduring the appraisal of the Annual Work Plan & Budget (AWP&B) of the States/UTs andthe Project Approval Board (PAB) meetings, the States/UTs are advised to fullyimplement the interventions approved, including the SMC training and CommunityMobilisation component. Utilisation under this component is also reviewed along withother components of the scheme through periodic meetings with EducationSecretaries/State Project Directors of States and UTs.*****
