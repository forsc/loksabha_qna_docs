                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4368






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.03.2022






Replacement of Ashoka Pillar on Uniform by Delhi Police




Kaushalendra Kumar














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the logo of the Ashoka Pillar is replaced with India Gate on the uniform of Delhi Police and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether Delhi Police has taken the said decision on their Foundation Day on 16 February;(c) if so, whether it is not a disrespect to the Ashoka Pillar; and(d) if so, the opinion of the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS (SHRI NITYANAND RAI) (a):  No, Sir.(b): State Police Forces, awarded with President’s colour, are authorized to wear its Insignia with the approval of the Director General of Police concerned.  As Delhi Police is also a recipient of President’s colour, the Commissioner of Police, Delhi has issued orders to wear Insignia by all ranks from 16th February 2022. The Insignia does not replace or modify the existing uniform or logo of Delhi Police but is only an addition. (c) and (d): Does not arise in view of reply to parts (a) and (b) above.*****
