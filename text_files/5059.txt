                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4619






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2019






Shortage of Staff in ESIC Hospitals in Maharashtra




Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)whether there is a shortage of doctors, including specialists/ superspecialists, medical and para-medical staff in the hospitals run by the Employees’ State Insurance Corporation (ESIC) in various States particulary in Maharashtra and if so, the details thereof, State/UT- wise;(b)the total number of posts sanctioned, in position and existing vacancies of the said categories in these hospitals, State/UT-wise and the reasons for shortfall and the steps taken to fill up the vacancies;(c)whether it has come to the notice of the Government that some doctors of ESI hospitals are reportedly engaged in Private Practice; (d)if so, the details thereof and the action taken by the Government in this regard; and (e)the steps taken/being taken by the Government to ensure availability of doctors and better delivery of healthcare services to ESI beneficiaries?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (IC) FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI SANTOSH KUMAR GANGWAR)(a) & (b):	Yes, Sir. There is a general shortage of Medical Officers, Specialists/Super Specialists and Nursing & Para-medical staff at Employees'' State Insurance Corporation (ESIC) Hospitals.  State/UT-wise details, including Maharashtra are at Annexure. The vacancy position is dynamic in nature and keeps changing due to new joining, retirement, attrition and other reasons, which are as under: Reason for the vacancies:-(i) ESIC, with an objective to expand the scheme across the country has been opening new hospitals regularly. Sanction of manpower for these hospitals adds to the vacancy position. Contd..2/-:: 2 ::(ii) Capacity of existing hospitals is also expanded which results in sanction of new posts. (ii) There is a general shortage of General Duty Medical Officer (GDMO), Specialist and Super Specialist in the country.(iii) Medical Officers leave job for better opportunity outside ESIC or to pursue higher studies.(c):		Yes, Sir. It has come to the notice that a few doctors of some ESI Hospitals are reportedly engaging themselves in private practice. (d):	Complaints of private practice against 11 Doctors of various ESIC Hospitals have been received by the vigilance as on date. Out of the above, in respect of 02 (two) cases, disciplinary proceedings have already been initiated against them. (e):		Steps taken by the ESI Corporation to meet the shortage:Regular recruitment in respect of GDMO, Specialist, Super Specialist, Teaching Faculty and Nursing & Paramedical Staff has already been initiated/finalized.Current Status:(i) ESIC has released advertisement on 21st December, 2018 for filling up about 2360 vacancies for Paramedical & Nursing cadre posts in 21 States.   (ii) Advertisement has been released on 5th October, 2018 for filling up about 771 posts of Insurance Medical Officer Grade-II. 250 Offer of Appointment have already been issued to qualifying candidates and candidates have already started joining in Delhi/Uttar Pradesh Region.  (iii) Advertisement has been released on 24th December, 2018 for filling up 257 posts of Specialist Gr. II (Junior Scale) and 72 posts of Specialist Gr. II (Senior Scale- Super Specialties) in 19 States. Result in respect of Gujarat/Madhya Pradesh Region has already been declared.  (iv) Field units have already been delegated the authority to engage GDMOs, Specialists and Teaching faculties on contractual basis to tide over the crisis of deficit in Medical Officers’ cadres. (v) Field Units have also been authorized to engage Nursing and paramedical staff through outsourcing as per instructions issued from time to time as per actual requirement.*                                                      ******
