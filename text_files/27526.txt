                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3114






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.08.2021






Funding Under POSHAN Abhiyaan




Gaddam Ranjith Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	the aims and objectives of Poshan Abhiyaan;(b)	whether it is true that so far only Rs.16,760 lakhs have been released for the State of Telangana from 2017-18 onwards and if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether it is also true that only 25 percent of the released funds have been utilized so far, if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor along with the steps being taken to utilize the remaining amount;(d)	the impact of COVID-19 on Poshan Abhiyaan in the country, State/UT-wise particularly in Telangana; and(e)	the details of allocation made for Poshan Abhiyaan in 2021-22 and how it compares with allocations made at Budget Estimates and Revised Estimates in the last three years, year-wise? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)  (a)     POSHAN Abhiyaan aims to reduce malnutrition in a phased manner, through a lifecycle approach, by adopting a synergised and result oriented approach. The objective of Abhiyaan is to achieve improvement in nutritional status of Children from 0-6 years, Adolescent Girls, Pregnant Women and Lactating Mothers by:i.	Preventing and reducing Stunting in children (0- 6 years).ii.	Preventing and reducing under-nutrition (underweight prevalence) in children (0-6 years).iii.	Reducing the prevalence of anemia among children (6-59 months).iv.	Reducing the prevalence of anemia among Women and Adolescent Girls in the age group of 15-49 years.v.	Reducing Low Birth Weight (LBW).  (b) and (c) 	Government is providing funds under Supplementary Nutrition Programme and ICDS (General) under Anganwadi Services Scheme in addition to the funds being granted under POSHAN Abhiyaan. The details of fund released and utilized by the state of Telangana from 2017-18 onwards under POSHAN Abhiyaan is at Annexure I. Further, the details of fund released and utilized by the state of Telangana from 2017-18 onwards in Supplementary Nutrition component of the Anganwadi Services Scheme is at Annexure II. Further, Mission Poshan 2.0, an integrated nutrition support programme which subsumes Supplementary Nutrition Programme and POSHAN Abhiyaan, has been announced in budget 2021-2022 for all States/UTs. It intends to strengthen nutritional content, delivery, outreach and outcomes with focus on developing practices that nurture health, wellness and immunity to disease and malnutrition.(d)        During the pandemic, as per the MHA guidelines, all Anganwadi Centres across the country were closed to limit the impact of COVID-19. However, to ensure continuous nutritional support to Anganwadi beneficiaries, Anganwadi Workers and Helpers distributed Supplementary Nutrition at the doorsteps of beneficiaries once in 15 days. (e)        For the year 2020-21, the Budget allocation for the Schemes comprising Anganwadi Services, POSHAN Abhiyaan and Scheme for Adolescent Girls was ?24,482 crore and the Revised Budget allocation was ?17902.31 crore. ln 2021-22, Schemes comprising Anganwadi Services, POSHAN Abhiyaan and Scheme for Adolescent Girls have been clubbed and named as ''Saksham Anganwadi and Poshan 2.0'' having budgetary provision of ?20,105 crore which is 12.3% more than the Revised Budget allocation of ?17902.31 crore.
