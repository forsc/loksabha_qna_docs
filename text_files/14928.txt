                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3554






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2020






Syllabus in Industrial Training Institutes




Umesh G. Jadhav














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the syllabus being followed by Industrial Training Institutes is considered outdated by the industries;(b)	 if so, the details thereof along with the reasons thereto;(c) 	whether it has created a negative impact on the employment opportunities of the students of ITIs, if so,the details thereof; and(d) 	the measures taken by the Government to make the syllabus contemporary as per the needs of theindustry?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	to (d)	No sir, the process of revision of ITI syllabus is a continuous process which is carried out as per the demand of industries, or on advent of latest technology and includes introduction of new trades, deletion of obsolete trades and revamping of existing trades. A“Recommendation Committee on Norms & Courses” under Directorate General of Training (DGT), Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship (MSDE) has been constituted which carries out this task based on suggestions of industry experts, State Directors and other stake holders which are members of this committee.  The curricula of all 137 trades implemented in ITIs under Craftsmen Training Scheme (CTS) have been recently revamped and are available at official website of DGT at https://dgt.gov.in/.
