                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1967






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.09.2020






Ease of Living Index




Shashi Tharoor














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                        (a) the list of parameters and indicators and their weightage to rank cities under the Ease of Living Index assessment launched to assess the improvements brought in quality of life by Government’s initiatives in 100 Smart Cities and 14 other Million Plus Cities across India; (b)  whether rankings also highlighted the data insufficiency of the indicators and if so, the details thereof; (c)  whether the Government has any plans to build a robust data culture by encouraging cities to share reliable disaggregated data for a more credible index and if so, the details thereof; and (d) whether a Citizen Perception Survey has being conducted from the 1st February, 2020 till 15th March, 2020 on behalf of the Government for the assessment exercise with respect to quality of life in the cities and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a): The list of parameters, indicators and their weightage to rank cities under the Ease of Living assessment framework-2019 for 111 cities, including Smart Cities, Capital Cities and Cities with million plus population Cities across India is at Annexure-I. The framework developed to assess cities under ‘Ease of Living-2019’ is comprehensive and covers critical pillars of Livability namely Quality of Life, Economic Ability, and Sustainability and uses 49 indicators across 14 categories.(b): The ranking of cities under Ease of Living Assessment Framework 2019 has not been finalized till now. (c) Yes, Sir. In order to create a robust data culture at the city level to collect, process, and share reliable data, the Ministry has launched the DataSmart Cities Strategy in Feb 2019.  Yes, Sir. A Citizen Perception Survey under the Ease of Living Assessment exercise was conducted from 16th January, 2020 to 20th March, 2020. Under this survey, more than 31 lakh citizens had given their feedbacks online and around 96 thousand responses were received from citizens through a face-to-face Survey.                                       *****
