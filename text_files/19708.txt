                                    QUESTION NO:

                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.02.2021






Venture Capital Fund Scheme for SCs




Shobha Karandlaje














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENTLOK SABHAUNSTARRED QUESTION NO.1194 TO BE ANSWERED ON 09.02.2021VENTURE CAPITAL FUND SCHEME FOR SCs 1194.    KUMARI SHOBHA KARANDLAJE: Will the Minister of SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT be pleased to state: (a) whether the Venture Capital Fund Scheme for Scheduled Castes was launched to promote entrepreneurship among SCs; (b) if so, the eligibility criteria for selecting the SC entrepreneurs under the scheme; (c) the details of the interest rate/ returns envisaged on investment and the amount of assistance provided under the Scheme; (d) the details and the number of the Companies owned by SC entrepreneurs sanctioned financial assistance under the scheme during the last five years, till March 31, 2020; (e) whether the budget allocated for scheme has come down drastically due to successive years of underutilization and if so, the details thereof; and (f) whether the Government has taken measures for creating awareness regarding this scheme through print and electronic media and if so, the details thereof ?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a):   Yes Sir, the Venture Capital Fund Scheme for Scheduled Castes was launched to promote entrepreneurship among Scheduled Castes (SCs) in FY 2014-15.(b):   Companies/Entrepreneurs having at least 51% stake holding by SC entrepreneurs with management control.(c):   The details of the interest rate / returns envisaged on investment and the amount of assistance provided under the Scheme are as under:(i) Interest @ 8% p.a. is charged on the assistance provided through Optionally Convertible Debentures (OCDs);(ii) Higher of 8% p.a. or valuation on investment;(iii) Financial assistance from Rs.10 Lakh to Rs.15 Crore is provided under the scheme;(iv) Companies owned by SCs enrolled / registered with Technology Business Incubators (TBIs) are eligible to get maximum Capital of Rs.30 lakhs over a period of 3 years i.e. Rs.10 lakhs every year.(d):  The details and the number of the Companies owned by SC entrepreneurs sanctioned financial assistance under the scheme during the last five years, till March 31, 2020 are as below and the detail is annexed as per Annexure.Financial Year	No. of Companies owned by SC entrepreneurs sanctioned financial assistance2014-15	22015-16	222016-17	202017-18	172018-19	262019-20	20(e):    No Sir, the Budget allocation is being made regularly to meet the projected need of funds.(f):   Yes Sir, Awareness about the scheme is being created through various mode of promotions viz. seminars, webinars, conferences including media and the details are as under:i.   Meets / conferences have been conducted across various states including Jammu and Kashmir UT, Punjab, Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Kerala, Rajasthan, Uttarakhand, Jharkhand, Bihar, Rajasthan, Chandigarh UT, Punjab, A.P., Telangana, Odisha, Tamil Nadu, Assam and West Bengal.ii.   Created awareness through participation in various conferences/exhibitions organized by leading Associations/Institutions/Industry Associations and Expo’s, Composite Camps, Credit Camps, Fairs and other events organized by MOSJ&E, Banks & various associations.iii.   Advertisements have been published in leading & local Newspapers across the country.iv. Use of social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn for spreading the awareness of the scheme.v. Exclusive website (i.e. www.vcfsc.in) has been developed to facilitate online applications.vi.  Promotion of scheme through State Scheduled Caste Development Finance Corporations, Industry Associations, Premier Educational Institutions across the country have also been done.vii .  Webinars are also being conducted frequently towards the awareness campaign.viii. Further, Investment Manager is also planning to organize weekly webinar in co-ordination with Dalit Indian Chamber of Commerce and Industry (DICCI) and various mentors appointed by the Ministry to spread awareness about the scheme.
