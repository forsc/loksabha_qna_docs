                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1998






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.02.2021






Shortage of Trained Nurses and Midwives




Pratima Mondal














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government is aware of the shortage of trained nurses and midwives in the country;(b) if so, the details thereof indicating the present nurse-patient ratio in the country; and(c) the number of diploma/degree schools and colleges with seats imparting nursing education in the country, State/UT-wise and the steps taken/proposed by the Government to open new nursing educational institutions in the country, location and State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b):  As per Indian Nursing Council (INC) records, there are around 22,72,208 Registered Nurses and Registered Midwives (RN&RM) and 9,91,425 Nurse Associates (9,34,583-Auxiliary Nurse Midwives and 56,842-Lady Health Visitors) in the Country.There are around 5085 Nursing Institutions in the country producing about 3.35 lakh nursing personnel annually.  The nurse-population ratio in the Country at present is 1.79 nurses per 1000 population.  However, the nurse-population ratio varies from State to State, District to District and Institution to Institution. (c) The State-wise details of the total number of diploma/degree schools and colleges with seats imparting nursing education in the country is annexed. In order to increase the number of nursing Institutes :i.	The requirement of land to construct building for School/College of Nursing and Hostel has been relaxed.ii.	The requirement of a 100 bedded parent hospital has been relaxed for hilly and tribal areas.iii.	The student teacher ratio for M.Sc.(N) programme has been relaxed from 1:5 to 1:10iv.	Student patient ratio for Nursing Institutions has been relaxed from 1:5 to 1:3v.	Maximum number of 100 seats for Nursing College will be given to those having parent hospitals with 300 beds without insisting on requirement of a Medical College.vi.	Distance from school to hospital has been relaxed from 15 km to 30 Km. However, for hilly and tribal areas the maximum distance is 50 Km.vii.	Relaxation for opening M.Sc. (N) programme. Super Specialty Hospital can start M.Sc. (N) without having under graduate programme.
