                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PLANNING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1639






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.12.2021






Beggar Free Country




Ramcharan Bohra














                        Will the Minister of





PLANNING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has any plan to make this country ‘beggar free’, in collaboration with the State Governments; (b)	if so, the details thereof along with the targets fixed in this regard; (c)	the action taken against the persons found guilty for child beggary at railway stations and other public places; and (d)	the steps taken by the Government to educate beggars and to provide employment opportunities to them?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF STATISTICS & PROGRAMME IMPLEMENTATION; MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF PLANNING AND MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF CORPORATE AFFAIRS (RAO INDERJIT SINGH)(a)	Yes sir. The Government (Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment) has formulated a scheme namely Support for Marginalized Individuals for Livelihood and Enterprise (SMILE) to implement through State Governments and other implementing agencies including NGOs etc. to make this ‘country beggar free’.(b)	The target fixed under the Component of Comprehensive Rehabilitation of persons engaged in the act of begging is about 20,000 (beggars) for next five years (2021-22 to 2025-26).  (c)	The Railways Act, 1989 (Section-144) prohibits hawking, begging etc. any violators  shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to one year, or with fine which may extend to two thousand rupees, or with both. Section 363-A of Indian Penal Code (IPC) provides that kidnapping or maiming a minor or a lawful guardian using a minor for purposes of begging is a criminal and punishable offense, accordingly violators are being punished. (d)	The Component of Comprehensive Rehabilitation of persons engaged in the act of begging under the above scheme provides for Survey/ Identification, Mobilization, Rescue/Shelter Home and comprehensive resettlement of beggars through educating, skilling and counselling to get in to decent jobs and lead normal life in the society. *****
