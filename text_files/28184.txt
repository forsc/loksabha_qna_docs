                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3561






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






National Disaster Response Force




Manickam Tagore B













                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details and the number of battalions of the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) at present; (b) whether the Government is undertaking adequate capacity building of NDRF to provide assistance to the States in the event of calamity, if so, the details thereof;(c) whether adequate training is provided to NDRF personnel in the event of a calamity, if so, the number of personnel trained and amount incurred therein;(d) the number of NDRF personnel deployed and number of persons rescued during the recent cyclonic storms which struck the country in the year 2020 and 2021;(e) whether the preparedness of the NDRF has been reviewed in view of unpredictable disaster in the country, if so, the details thereof; and(f) whether any compensation is paid to wards of deceased NDRF personnel who lost their life while being deployed during calamity, if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a):	At present, National Disaster Response Force (NDRF)                      has    16 Battalions (Bns). Out of these, 12  NDRF  Bns are  operational  and -2-L.S.US.Q. NO.3561 FOR 10.08.2021deployed as per vulnerability profile of the country to provide immediate response during disasters. The personnel of four new Bns are undergoing training before their deployment. The details of 12 operational Bns are at Annexure-I.(b) to (e):	Capacity building and operational preparedness of NDRF are monitored and reviewed at regular intervals. The details of various measures taken for capacity building, training and preparedness of NDRF are as under:(i)	Training is a continuous process and all NDRF personnel after induction, undergo 19 weeks “Basic Course of Disaster First Responder” at Bn level. In the year 2020-21 and 2021-22, a total of 3596 and 2605 NDRF personnel respectively have undergone induction training. (ii)	NDRF personnel are regularly trained for upgrading their skills for effectively responding to all types of disasters, natural as well as manmade including Chemical, Biological, Radiological and Nuclear (CBRN) emergencies.(iii)	NDRF is equipped with state of art equipment to deal with various manmade and natural disaster situations.-3-L.S.US.Q. NO.3561 FOR 10.08.2021(iv)	NDRF Academy is imparting training to NDRF personnel/ SDRF personnel in various disaster management courses.(v)	NDRF personnel are also nominated to participate in various international events i.e. training courses /seminars/workshops etc., which are aimed at updating their skills about best practices in Disaster Management, knowledge of sophisticated equipment & techniques being adopted globally. (vi)	Capacity building programmes of NDRF as well as of various stakeholders, including school safety programmes, are undertaken to enhance preparedness of the community.(vii)	NDRF regularly conducts mock drills with various stakeholders such as Indian Railways, State Disaster Management Authorities (SDMA), National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA), State Disaster Response Force (SDRF), Multi Accidental Hazardous Industries (MAH) and schools etc;(viii)	NDRF is relentlessly engaged in the “Community Capacity Building and Public awareness and preparedness programme” of SDRF, State Police, Home Guards, Civil Defence, Fire Services, National Cadet Corps (NCC), Non-Governmental Organisations (NGOs), Nehru Yuva Kendra Sangathan (NYKS), Students, Volunteers and other stakeholders. -4-L.S.US.Q. NO.3561 FOR 10.08.2021During the year 2020-21 and 2021-22, Rs. 1,27,47,436/- and Rs. 30,60,618/- (as on 05.08.2021) respectively have been spent by NDRF from the allotted budget for training of NDRF personnel. The details of NDRF deployment and number of persons rescued and evacuated during the recent cyclonic storms, which struck the country in the year 2020 and 2021 are as under:Year	Number of NDRF teams deployed	Persons rescued	Persons evacuated*2020	150	44	15,34,1092021	184	1030	24,77,190*NDRF teams assisted State Administrations in evacuation of affected persons to safer places (the evacuations figures are provided by the respective States).(f):	NDRF personnel are on deputation from various Central Armed Police Forces/Assam Rifles and all the financial benefits/compensation to the wards of deceased personnel, who lost their lives while being deployed during calamity, are given by his/ her parent Central Armed Police Forces/Assam Rifles. The details of compensation/facilities provided to the next of kin of deceased NDRF personnel are at Annexure-II.********
