                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1560






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.09.2020






Promotion of Indigenous Cattle Species




Ramdas Chandrabhanji Tadas














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has formulated any action plan to promote indigenous species of cattle for the cattle promotion sector; (b) if so, the present status of the above in the country including Maharashtra; and(c) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY & DAIRYING (DR. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a)	 In order to complement and supplement the efforts made by the States for conserving and promoting indigenous species of cattle Department of Animal Husbandry & Dairying has been implementing following schemes for development and conservation of indigenous bovine breeds:(i)	Centrally Sponsored Scheme: Rashtriya Gokul Mission for development and conservation of indigenous breeds in scientific and holistic manner. All indigenous bovine breeds are covered under the scheme.  (ii)	Central Sector Scheme: Breed Improvement Institutes comprises of seven Central Cattle Breeding Farms, four Central Registration Units and Central Frozen Semen Production and Training Institutes. These organization are  undertaking development and conservation of indigenous breeds namely (i) Tharparkar; (ii) Red Sindhi; (iii) Gir; (iv) Kankrej; (v) Ongole; (vi) Hariana and (vii) Rathi breed of cattle and (i) Surti; (ii) Murrah; (iii) Meshsana  and (iv) Jaffarabadi breeds of buffaloes. (b) &(c) Rashtriya Gokul Mission has been implemented throughout the country and till date an amount of Rs. 1764.4 crores has been released to the States and Union Territories since inception of the project in December 2014. Maharashtra is participating under Rashtriya Gokul Mission and an amount of Rs. 6896.78 lakh has been released for implementation of different components of Rashtriya Gokul Mission. *****
