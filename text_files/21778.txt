                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2824






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2021






Decrease in Textile Export




S. Jothimani














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) whether the export of textiles has declined in India since 2014; (b) if so, the average textile export during the last decade; (c) the details of the current textile export status in terms of revenue and volume; (d) whether the Government is planning to ameliorate the condition of the textile industry in India which is under stress due to competition from neighboring countries of Pakistan and Bangladesh; (e) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and (f) the funds that have been earmarked for revival of textile industry from the 20 lakh crore stimulus package announced by the Government?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF TEXTILES(SMT. SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a):	The export of textiles and apparel from 2014-15 to 2019-20 is at Annexure-A.(b):	The average textiles and apparel export stands at USD 35214mn for the period 2011-12 to 2019-20.(c):	Details of current textiles and apparel export (April 2020- December 2020) in terms of revenue and volume is at Annexure-B.(d) & (e):	To ameliorate the condition of textile industry in India which is under stress due to competition from neighboring countries, Cabinet has approved Production-Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme in the 10 key sectors for enhancing India’s manufacturing capabilities and enhancing exports- Atmanirbhar Bharat. Textiles products: MMF segment and technical textiles have been included among 10 key sectors with approved financial outlay of Rs 10,683 crore over a five year period for these products. This scheme will help Indian firms to grow into global champions. Union Budget 2021-22 has announcement regarding launch of Mega Integrated Textile Region and Apparel (MITRA) Parks scheme to enable the textile industry to become globally competitive, to attract investments and boost employment generation. 7 Textile Parks will be established over 3 years. This will create world class infrastructure with plug and play facilities. (f):	The Government of India had announced Aatmanirbhar Bharat Packages (ANB) 1.0, 2.0, and 3.0 on 13th May, 2020, 12th October, 2020 and 12th November 2020 respectively. There is no specific fund earmarked for any specific sector in the Aatmanirbhar Bharat Package. It is the composite package for all sectors mainly for helping them in improving their liquidity situation. This has been done in the light of COVID-19 for all MSMEs through the banking channel.
