                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1727






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.03.2020






Accessibility of Heritage Sites to Differently-abled Persons




 Chandra Sekhar Bellana














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	whether the country has 38 UNESCO World Heritage Sites from which 30 are cultural sites, 7 are natural sites and 1 is a mixed site which makes India a home of World Heritage sites and if so, the details thereof;b)	whether the Government is contemplating to make these heritage sites accessible to differently-abled persons which will further strengthen the country’s rank in upcoming editions of the Travel  Tourism Competitiveness Index (TTCI) and also establishing the country as a leading model of accessibility planning and implementation in the South Asian Region;c)	if so, the details thereof along with the steps taken by the Government in this regard;d)	whether the Government proposes to improve infrastructure in present museums and art galleries for differently-abled persons rather than building separate art galleries or sections at museums to meet the needs of such persons with disabilities; ande)	the efforts made to develop inclusive cultural spaces, technologies and learning programmes to encourage the participation of differently-abled persons?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE AND TOURISM(PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) Yes Sir, India has 38 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, out of which, 30 are cultural sites, 07 are natural and 01 is a mixed site. The list of world heritage sites is annexed at Annexure-I.(b) & (c)Yes Sir, arrangements have been made at most of the World Heritage Sites under Archaeological Survey of India to make these sites accessible to differently-abled persons by providing wheel chairs, ramps, Braile cultural notice boards, etc.(d) & (e) Visit of differently abled persons is facilitated at museums and art-galleries by way of providing wheel chairs, ramps, signages and brochures in Braille. In addition, special section ‘Anubhuti’ with tactile/brail enabled narrative has been installed at National Museum, Delhi.
