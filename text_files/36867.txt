                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TRIBAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3961






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






Forest Land Claims




Annasaheb Shankar Jolle













                                                    S. Muniswamy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of pending cases of forest land claims in the state of Karnataka along with the reasons for being the State with highest pending of such cases; and (b) the details of the reasons that Government is not inclined to disposing/allotting the forest lands to the deserving mainly the tribal population of the State?  






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	& (b):As per Forest Rights Act 2006 and rules made thereunder, the responsibility for implementation of the Act lies with State Governments/UT Administrations. As per the information received from State of Karnataka, the status of FRA applications received and disposed is as under.Category	No. of applications received	No. of title deeds distributed	Extent of land involved (in acres)	No of applications rejected	No. of applications pending for disposalSTs	47708	12674	17590.76	32869	2165OTFDs	240605	1976	2395.44	173967	64662Community	5938	1343	36340.40	3136	1459Total	294251	15993	56326.60	209972	68286Reasons for Pendency:   The state of Karnatka has provided information of district wise pendency of FRA applications, which may be seen in the Annexureenclosed.  It shows that highest pendency is in Shivmonga and Uttarakanda as shown below.•	46377 claims are pending in Shivmoga District. all application pending in Forest Right Committee at village level.•	Second highest pending in Uttarakannda District, 16316 applications are pending (8897 application pending in Forest Right Committee at  village level, 6855 applications in SDLC and 564 applications in DLC level)  Pendency of claims filed by OTFDs:  The state has submitted that out of 68,286 cases, there is pendency of 64,662 cases pertaining to OTFDs and has stated following reasons for pendency.i. Lack of documentary evidence in support of the claims. Mainly OTFD needs to prove antecedents of three generations or 75 years of ownership of forest land prior to 2005 or else they could not claim their rights over the forest land. Around 95% of pending case belongs to OTFD (64662 applications).ii. Claims filed for lands other than forest land or revenue land.iii. Multiple claims were filed for the same piece of land.iv. Multiple claims were filed from the same family.v. The term of office of DLC & SDLC nominated members from Taluk & District Panchayath members has been completed and the elections have not taken place. Pendency of claims filed by ST:  It is submitted by the State Government that there are only 2165 pending cases out of total 47,708 cases filed by STs, showing that 94.46% of the applications of Tribal’s are disposed, 5.54% of the applications are pending at different levels as shown below.
