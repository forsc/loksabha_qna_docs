                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

820






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






SCM in Madhya Pradesh




Krishna Pal Singh Yadav













                                                    Sanjay Seth
                                                








                                                    Arun Sao
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  the names of the cities selected in Madhya Pradesh out of the total numbers of one hundred major cities selected in the country under Smart City Mission (SCM); (b)   the details of stages adopted in the selection of these cities;(c)  the names of the cities in Madhya Pradesh where work has been started after budget allocation under the said mission;(d) whether necessary budget for Madhya Pradesh was allocated only after selection of the cities to develop as Smart City under the said mission; and (e) if so, the details of the required allocated funds and expenditure incurred thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) Seven Cities in Madhya Pradesh were selected to be developed as Smart Cities under the Smart Cities Mission. The cities of Bhopal, Indore and Jabalpur were selected in round-1 of Smart Cities Selection process in January, 2016; Gwalior and Ujjain were selected in round-2 in September, 2016; and Sagar & Satna were selected in round-3 in June, 2017. (b): The selection process of Smart Cities was conducted through a competition in two stages. In the first stage, States/Union Territories(UTs) shortlisted potential Smart Cities based on predefined scoring criteria. In the second stage, competing cities prepared their Smart City Proposals (SCPs) as per guidelines and submitted them as part of the National competition. A total of 100 cities were selected as Smart Cities in Four Rounds of National  Challenge from January, 2016 to June, 2018. (c): All seven Smart Cities of Madhya Pradesh have started implementing their projects.(d): As per Smart Cities Mission Statement and Guidelines, the Central Government proposes to give financial support to the extent of Rs. 48,000 crore over five years i.e. an average of Rs. 500 crore per city over the Mission period. An equal amount on a matching basis, is to be provided by the State/Urban Local Bodies.  An amount of Rs. 2 crore was provided to potential Smart Cities for preparing their Smart City Proposal prior to city challenge rounds.(e): The details of funds released by Central Government for Smart Cities in Madhya Pradesh and utilization thereof given below:                                                                                (Rs. in Crore)S.N.
