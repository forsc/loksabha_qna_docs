                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2581






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.07.2019






Rehabilitation of Homeless Persons




Sanjay Jaiswal














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has any data on the number of destitute and homeless persons living in inhuman conditions under flyovers, railway stations and parks etc. in the country, especially in metro cities including Delhi; (b) if so, the details thereof, State-wise; (c) whether the Government has any scheme/proposes to launch a scheme/ programme for the rehabilitation of these people so as to bring them into the mainstream of the society; and (d) if so, the details thereof along with the number and the percentage of such people rehabilitated during the last three years and the current year, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) & (b): As per Census-2011, the number of homeless persons in the country is 17,73,040 comprised in 4,49,787 households.As per this Census, a total number of 47,076 houseless persons, comprised in 23,175 households are in NCT of Delhi.A statement giving State/UT-wise houseless households and population as per Census 2011 is annexed.(c) & (d): As per information provided by Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, ‘Land’ and ‘Colonization’ are State subject, therefore, it is the primary responsibility of State Governments to implement appropriate scheme for the houseless. However, they are administering a Scheme of Shelter for Urban Homeless (SUH) as a component of Deendayal Antyodaya Yojna-National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM), through respective States/UTs. Objective of the scheme is to ensure availability and access of the urban homeless population to permanent shelters including the basic infrastructure facilities like water supply, sanitation, safety and security. Under SUH, the shelters will be a space for convergence and provisions of various entitlement of social security, food, education and health care systems. All homeless persons, in  shelters should be given priority under various schemes, and government programme. The Shelter Management Agencies and Committee would be responsible to ensure the availability of various entitlements and benefits to the homeless.The Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs does not maintain any data of the homeless persons rehabilitated.****
