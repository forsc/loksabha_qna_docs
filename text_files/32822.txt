                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

448






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2022






Light House Project




Pashupati Nath Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether Light House Project is an ambitious plan of the Government under which one thousand houses are to be constructed in each city and the target has been fixed to complete it within a year and if so, the details thereof;(b)	whether it is also a fact that long lasting houses are to be provided in view of local climate and ecology and if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether a number of cities and people in Jharkhand are benefitted under this scheme, if so, the details thereof, area-wise; and (d)	if not, the reasons therefor and the time by which they are likely to be benefitted under this plan?






ANSWER




                                                                              THE MINISTER OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                            (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)	& (b):Yes, Sir. Six Light House Projects (LHPs) consisting of about 1,000 houses each with physical and social infrastructure facilities are being constructed at six places across the country namely Indore (Madhya Pradesh), Rajkot (Gujarat), Chennai (Tamil Nadu), Ranchi (Jharkhand), Agartala (Tripura) and Lucknow (Uttar Pradesh). These LHPs are to be completed within a period of 12 months after receipt of all Statutory Approvals/ NOCs from the concerned departments of States/ Urban Local Bodies (ULBs).The details of LHPs are at Annexure. These LHPs are demonstrating construction of mass housing using innovative construction technologies suitable to the geo-climatic and hazard conditions of the region at an expedited pace as compared to conventional construction and are more economical, sustainable, of high quality and durability.(c)	& (d): For constructing the LHPs, a country-wide challenge among all States/UTs across the country was conducted by the Ministry, wherein six States with cities that scored the highest marks across the prescribed criteria were selected including Ranchi city in Jharkhand.The LHPin Ranchi comprising of 1,008 houses with related infrastructure is currently under construction. Selection of beneficiaries and allotment of houses is the responsibility of the State Government.                                          *****
