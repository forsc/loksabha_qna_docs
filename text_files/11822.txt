                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

705






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.02.2020






Implementation of AMRUT Scheme




Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is implementing the Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) scheme in Maharashtra; (b) if so, the details of activities carried out under the scheme so far in all the cities; and (c) the funds sanctioned for these projects and by when these projects would be completed?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                                  (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a): Yes, Sir. (b) : Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) scheme was launched on 25 June, 2015 for a period of five years i.e. up to 31 March, 2020 in selected 500 cities across the country. The thrust areas of the Mission are water supply, sewerage & septage management, storm water drainage, non-motorized urban transport and green spaces & parks.The Mission also has reform agenda and capacity building programme. The Mission is being implemented in 44 cities of the State of Maharashtra.  The State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) worth Rs.7,759.32 crore including Central Assistance (CA) of Rs.3,534.08 crore have been approved for the State of Maharashtra. Any additional expenditure on the projects is to be borne by the State.
