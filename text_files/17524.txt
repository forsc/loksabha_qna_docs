Set up of National Culture Fund

                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the objective of setting up of National Cultural Fund to facilitate public-private partnership (PPP) in the field of heritage conservation and promotion has been achieved;(b)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the steps taken by the Government in this regard;(c)	the details of projects undertaken and funds sanctioned and utilized since the inception of NCF;(d)	whether the Government has constituted a Council of NCF for its effective management and administration, and if so, the details thereof;(e)	whether many of NCF financed PPP conservation projects are reported to be sub-standard and if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor; and(f)	whether the Government has taken steps to review the functioning of NCF for its effective implementation, and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State (IC) for Culture & Tourism(Shri Prahlad Singh Patel)(a) to (c) Yes Sir. The Government of India has set up National Culture Fund (NCF) as a Trust under the Charitable Endowment Act, 1890 through a Gazette Notification published in the Gazette of India 28th November, 1996. The Fund enables institutions and individuals to support arts and culture directly as partners with its government and serves as a funding mechanism distinct from the existing sources and patterns of funding for the arts and culture in India. All contributions to NCF are given 100% tax exemption under Section 80 G (2) of the Income Tax Act of 1961. So far, NCF has completed 41 sponsored projects and 27 are ongoing. Details of the completed and ongoing projects are attached as Annexure-I.(d) Yes Sir, the Council of NCF has a maximum strength of 24 members, with a maximum of 19 eminent members representing the corporate sector, public sector, private foundations and NGOs.  The Hon’ble Minister for Culture is the Chairperson of the Council. The current composition of the Council was constituted vide notification dated 26.09.2018 for a tenure of 3 years.(e) No Sir.(f) NCF receives guidance from Governing Council and the Executive Committee for its effective functioning.Annexure-IAnnexure referred in part (a) to (c) of Lok Sabha unstarred Question No.1315 for Reply on 19.09.2020 regarding Set up of National Culture Fund PROJECTS COMPLETED  BY NCFS.No.	Name of Project	Name of the Donor MoU Signed on Approved cost Rs. in lakhs	ExpenditureRs. in lakhs	Public/Private 	Tangible/Intangible1	Humayun’s Tomb, New Delhi, 1999	Aga Khan Trust & M/s Oberoi Group of Hotels	April, 1999	30.46	30.25	Private sponsored public project 	Tangible2	Karnataka Chitrakala Parishad	NCF-Karnataka Chitrakala Parishad	November 1999	Not mentioned in MoU		Private sponsored private work	Tangible3	Jnana Pravaha Trust, Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh	Jnana Pravaha Trust	January 2000	1.00	1.00	Private sponsored private project 	Tangible4	Kishkinda Trust, Anegundi, Karnataka	Kishkinda Trust,	April 2000	1.85	1.24	Private sponsored private project 	Intangible5	Jantar Mantar, New Delhi	Apeejay Surrenddra Park Hotels Ltd.
