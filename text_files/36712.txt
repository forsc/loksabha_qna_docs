                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3532






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2022






Impact of Coal Shortage on MSMEs




Kirit Premjibhai Solanki














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has any data about the impact of coal shortage on MSMEs; (b)	if so, the details thereof; (c)	whether the Government has taken any steps or proposes to take any steps to address the coal shortage and minimise its impact on MSMEs; (d)	if so, the details thereof including progress made till date, and the timeline and targets for future; and (e)	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI BHANU PRATAP SINGH VERMA)(a)to(e): 	As per New Coal Distribution Policy (NCDP), 8 million tonnes of coal has been earmarked to be allocated to State Nominated Agencies (SNAs) for distribution to MSME’s and other consumers having annual requirement upto 10,000 metric tonnes. Based on the request received from the States, coal is allocated to their authorised SNA’s as per their requirement.  For FY 2021-22, Coal India Limited (CIL) has allocated 6.6743 million tonnes of coal to 13 States and 18 SNA’s.
