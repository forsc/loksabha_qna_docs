                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

248






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.07.2021






EWS Reservation




Margani Bharat














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENTLOK SABHAUNSTARRED QUESTION NO.248 TO BE ANSWERED ON 20.07.2021EWS RESERVATION 248. SHRI MARGANI BHARAT: Will the Minister of SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government is aware of the recent judgment of Supreme Court refusing to allow reservation above 50 percent, if so, the details thereof; (b) whether 10 percent Economically Weaker Section (EWS) quota given by the Government would also become null and void;(c) if so, whether the Government would consider amending necessary legislation and Constitutional provision; and(d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SUSHRI PRATIMA BHOUMIK)(a): Yes Sir. Supreme Court in its Order dated 5th May 2021 in Civil Appeal No.3123/2020- have confirmed the Orders of Supreme Court in Indra Sawhney case that imposed a overall limit of 50% on the reservations.(b): The reservations for the Economically Weaker Sections are not impacted by the above order. However, the reservation for the EWS category are under consideration of the Constitution Bench of Supreme Court separately.  (c): No Sir.(d): Question does not arise in view of   (c) above.*****
