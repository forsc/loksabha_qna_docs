                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

772






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2021






Commercial Pilot Training Centres




Prathap Simha













                                                    Devendrappa Y
                                                








                                                    Annasaheb Shankar Jolle
                                                








                                                    Umesh G. Jadhav
                                                








                                                    L.S. Tejasvi Surya
                                                








                                                    B. Y. Raghavendra
                                                








                                                    Sanganna Amarappa Karadi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of training centers in the country, especially in Karnataka, where commercial pilot training facility is available;(b) whether the Government proposes to introduce commercial pilot training facility in any university or qualified centers approved by DGCA as well and if so, the details of such centers;(c) whether there is any project for commercial pilot training centre under consideration by the Government; and(d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) At present, there are 34 Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) approved Flying Training Organizations (FTOs) in the country out of which two FTOs, Government Flying Training School, Jakkur, Bangalore and Orient Flight Aviation Academy, Mysore are in Karnataka. ;Airports Authority of India (AAI) has issued award letters on 31 May 2021 for establishing two FTOs each at Belagavi and Kalaburagi in Karnataka.(b) Indira Gandhi Rashtriya Uran Akademi (IGRUA) is the only FTO under the administrative control of the Central Government. Pilot training facilities are largely operated by private entities. DGCA approves the pilot training facilities as per the laid down regulations. At present, there is no proposal to start any new pilot training facility by the Central Government.  ;;(c) & (d)  Airports Authority of India (AAI) has issued award letters on 31 May 2021 and 29 October 2021 for nine FTOs to be established at five airports at Belagavi (Karnataka), Jalgaon (Maharashtra), Kalaburagi (Karnataka), Khajuraho (Madhya Pradesh) and Lilabari (Assam). Soft launch of two FTOs at Kalabuargi was done on 15 August 2021.******
