                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COAL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4814






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.03.2021






Coal Blocks Allocated to Private Players




Nalin Kumar Kateel













                                                    B. Y. Raghavendra
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COAL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a.	whether it is true that the private players are allocated coal blocks in the country;b.	if so, the details thereof;c.	the details of total number of coal blocks that are allocated to private players during the last three years; year-wise and coal block-wise;d.	whether the Government has achieved the target for revenue generation from allocation of coal blocks; ande.	if so, the response of the Government in this regard ?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF COAL, MINES & PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS(SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI) a.	Yes, Sir.b.	Total of 42 coal blocks have been allocated to private sector under Coal Mine (Special Provisions) Act, 2015 and Mine and Mineral (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 till date through a transparent & competitive bidding mechanism.c.	During the last 3 years, 20 coal blocks have been allocated to Private Sector companies under Coal Mine (Special Provisions) Act, 2015 and Mine and Mineral (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957. List of those mines may be found in the Annexure enclosed.d.	& e. Ministry of Coal allocates coal blocks with production target to the allocattees. Revenue generated from these mines is dependent on coal production and earned by the respective State Governments where mines are located. Central Government only gets tax and cess portion on such revenue such as Central Goods and Service Tax (CGST), GST compensation cess and National Mineral Exploration Trust (NMET).
