                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2920






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2021






Monkey Menace




Kaushalendra Kumar














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether a number of people particularly senior citizens and children are feeling unsafe in the streets due to rise in number of monkeys on the roads;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether monkey menace has made movement of people difficult in the streets in colonies like South Avenue in New Delhi; (d)	if so, the details thereof including steps taken to devise scientific ways to capture monkeys; and(e)	the other steps taken to address/combat the monkey menace and check their presence in the streets?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	to (e)	Reports from media and Government of National Capital Territory (NCT) of Delhi have been received in the Ministry regarding increasing number of monkeys on the roads. The Government of NCT of Delhi has also informed that such complaints have been referred to civic agencies as well as Non-Governmental Organizations for necessary action. The Civic Agencies capture and translocate monkeys from problematic areas and have released more than 23000 monkeys since 2001 in Asola Bhatti Wildlife Sanctuary. As per the information received from Government of NCT of Delhi, food is being provided to monkeys released in Asola Bhatti Wildlife Sanctuary.The Ministry has approved a project for undertaking immuno-contraceptive measures for population management of wild animals including monkeys to the Wildlife Institute of India, Dehradun and National Institute of Immunology, Delhi.The Ministry has also issued Advisory for handling human wildlife conflict situations, including for those relating to monkey. *****
