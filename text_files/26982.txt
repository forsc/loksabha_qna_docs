                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2672






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.08.2021






Communication Services in Tribal and LWE Affected Areas




Raja Amareshwara Naik













                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                








                                                    Rajveer (Raju Bhaiya) Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether tribal and Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected areas of the country are lagging behind when compared with other parts of the country in communication indicators like tele density, quality of services and network coverage, etc.; (b) 	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor; (c) 	the target achieved by the Government in terms of aforementioned communication indicators during each of the last three years, State/UT-wise including the region of Bolangir in Odisha; (d) 	whether the Government has taken any new initiatives for the expansion of telecom services in tribal and LWE affected areas including the region of Bolangir, Odisha; (e) 	if so, the details thereof and the other steps taken by the Government in this regard; and (f) 	the funds allocated/released/utilized by the Government in the said regions during the last three years?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS (SHRI DEVUSINH CHAUHAN)(a) to (c)	Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) has been monitoring the performance of Telecom Service Providers (TSPs) against the benchmarks, for various Quality of Service parameters, laid down by TRAI, by way of Quality of Service regulations, issued from time to time, through Quarterly Performance Monitoring Reports (PMRs). The performance is assessed for a License Service Area (LSA) as a whole. Separate information on performance on Quality of Services in District or in a particular area such as Tribal and Left Wing Extremism (LWE) affected areas is not available. 		Department of Telecommunications (DoT) does not maintain Tribal/LWE area-wise tele-density data. However, service area-wise rural and urban tele-density for last three years and current year as on dated 31.05.2021, is given at Annexure-I.However, DoT on the basis of a survey carried out in 2020, has estimated that around 5,72,551 out of 5,97,618 inhabited census villages of the country which also includes tribal and LWE affected areas, already have mobile network coverage, while 25,067 inhabited villages do not have the coverage. The Government through TSPs provide mobile network coverage in uncovered villages in the country in a phased manner. State-wise details of number of Base Transceiver Stations (BTS) installed by TSPs, to increase the mobile network coverage and capacity, in LWE affected districts are given at Annexure-II. To increase the mobile network coverage and capacity in Bolangir district of Odisha; 418, 312 & 113 number of BTSs have been installed by TSPs during Financial Year 2019, 2020 & 2021 respectively. Under BharatNet project, 259 number of Gram Panchayats(GP) have also been made service ready during Financial Years 2020 & 2021 in Bolangir, Odisha.(d) to (f)	The Government through various schemes/projects under Universal Service Obligation Fund (USOF) namely, (i) LWE (Phase-I &II) projects, (ii) Aspirational districts’ scheme, (iii) Commissioning of submarine Optical Fibre Cable connectivity from Chennai to Andaman &Nicobar Islands, (iv) scheme for connecting submarine Optical Fibre Cable from Kochi to Lakshadweep Islands, (v) BharatNet (Phase – I &II) projects, (vi) Comprehensive Telecom Development Plan (CTDP) for mobile connectivity in the North Eastern Region, (vii) Provision of 4G Mobile Coverage in uncovered villages and seamless 4G Mobile coverage of National Highway (NH - 223) in Andaman & Nicobar Islands, etc.; is providing mobile and internet services in rural areas (which also includes tribal and LWE affected areas) in the country. The details of Scheme/Programme-wise disbursement of funds from USOF during the last three financial years (2019 to 2021), is given at Annexure-III.The details of LWE Phase-I & II for the expansion of Telecom Services in LWE affected areas including the region of Bolangir, Odisha are as below:•	For provision of mobile services in LWE affected areas under Phase-I, the project has been completed by installing 2,343 towers. Under LWE Phase-I, mobile towers at Mahakhanda and Magurbeda locations in Bolangir, Odisha have been installed and are providing services.•	For the expansion of Telecom services in tribal and LWE affected areas, Government has approved the LWE Phase-II Scheme. Under this scheme, 2542 number of mobile towers at the locations identified by Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) are to be provisioned, including 483 mobile towers in the State of Odisha (Bolangir is not provisioned under this scheme).As on dated 09.07.2021, under BharatNet Phase-I & II, a total number of 1,57,919 GPs have been made service ready in the country. The scope of BharatNet has been recently extended upto all inhabited villages beyond GPs.Moreover, DoT has taken several policy initiatives to facilitate infrastructure growth, for delivery of quality services. These include permitting trading/sharing/liberalisation of spectrum, permitting passive & active infrastructure sharing, notification of Right of Way Rules - 2016, making government land/buildings available for installations of towers, etc. Nearly, 16.14 lakh additional BTSs for 2G/3G/4G-LTE services have been added by TSPs during the period from March-2014 (6.49 lakh BTSs) to 27thJuly 2021 (22.64 lakh BTSs) across the country.
