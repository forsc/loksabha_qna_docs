                                    TRIBAL AFFAIRS

                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3557






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2020






Smart Village




V. Srinivas Prasad














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether due to the remote and inaccessible location of Chamrajnagara district in Karnataka, the education of tribal students and the livelihood of tribals is being affected; (b) if so, the details thereof; (c) whether the villages in the said district lack connectivity as there is no motorable roads, electricity and also no proper water supply; (d) if so, the details thereof; and (e) whether the situation of tribals living in this place can be improved by installing solar power plants for providing electricity under the concept of “Smart Village” and through other measures?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) to (e): The scheme of Eklavya Model Residential Schools (EMRSs) is administered by Ministry of Tribal Affairs with an objective to provide quality middle and high-level education to Scheduled Tribes (ST) students in remote areas in order to enable them to avail of reservation in high and professional educational courses and get jobs in Government, Public and Private sectors. One Eklavya Model Residential School(EMRS) was sanctioned to the State of Karnataka at Village-Mangala, Block-Kollegal in Chamrajnagar district during 2010-11. The EMRS is reported to be functional. A total number of 275 students have enrolled in this school during the current year i.e. 2019-20. Besides this, status of EMRS in the state is at Annexure -I           Government has adopted a multi- pronged strategy for overall development of tribal people living across the country including Karnataka, which includes supports for Education, Livelihood, Electricity, Roads, Water Supply, Health, Sanitation, Drinking, Skill Development, Agriculture, Horticulture and Fisheries etc. Major part of infrastructure development and provision of basic amenities in tribal areas/regions in the country is carried out through various schemes/programmes of concerned central Ministries and the State Governments concerned, while the Ministry of Tribal Affairs provides additive to these initiatives by way of plugging critical gaps.              The interventions under most of the schemes/programmes of the Ministry are demand driven and funds are provided to State Governments/UTs based upon proposals received from the State Government.
