                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1293






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.11.2019






Skill  Development and Vocational Education




Rebati Tripura













                                                    Sandhya Ray
                                                








                                                    Subrata Pathak
                                                








                                                    Vijay Kumar Dubey
                                                








                                                    Dhanush M Kumar
                                                








                                                    Ganesan Selvam
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has signed any joint declarations of intent for cooperation in  the field of skill development and vocational education with Germany, if so, the details thereof along with its aims and objectives; (b) 	the details of the different sectors which will be given emphasis to enhance the quality of trainees; (c) 	whether the agreements will support India’s endeavour to establish an innovative system; (d) 	if so, the details thereof; (e)	the details of the benefits attained by the country from the said agreement; (f) 	whether the Government has set up centres to provide high class training for getting employment abroad and if so, the number of centres proposed to be opened along with the capacity thereof; and (g) 	the other steps taken by the Government to increase employment in innovative and sustainable sectors?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) to (e) The Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship (MSDE) has signed three Joint Declarations of Intent with Germany for cooperation in the field of Skill Development and Vocational Education and Training. Details are given in the Annexure. (f)	One of the priorities of the Government is to create a working population with skills at par with their counterparts in other parts of the world. To achieve this goal, India International Skill Centre (IISC) program was envisioned. MSDE under the ‘Skill India Mission’ and the Pravasi Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PKVY) Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), is setting up of the IISC Network through National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) to provide high quality skill training and certification for facilitating international mobility. In the pilot phase, 14 IISCs were launched and important lessons were learnt during its operationalization.  Out of the 583 candidates trained, 286 candidates were successfully certified, and 63 candidates were placed overseas. 	Besides, a Memorandum of Cooperation (MoC) between MSDE and the Ministry of Justice, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare of Japan (collectively referred to as the Ministry of Japan) was signed on 17th October, 2017 with the objective of transferring of technical skills from Japan to India appropriately and smoothly by advancing the Technical Intern Training Programme (TITP). The TITP programme is an On-the-Job-Training (OJT) programme providing employment opportunities for foreign nationals in Japan. Under the TITP programme, 27 Sending Organizations have been empanelled across the country and these Sending organizations have set up 24 Training centres across 11 Indian states for training and sending candidates to Japan as technical interns (TITP). More than 700 candidates are enrolled at these centres for undertaking Japanese language training. (g)   This Ministry has signed agreements with eight other countries for cooperation in skill sector including innovative and sustainable sectors to increase employment opportunities. In addition, under Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY), industry linked skill development training programmes are being conducted all over the country in several sectors including innovative and sustainable sectors. ANNEXUREAnnexure as referred to in reply to parts (a) to (e) of Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No. 1293 for answer on 25.11.2019 regarding “Skill Development and Vocational Education”.S.No.	Signatories of Joint Declarations of Intent	Aims & Objectives1.	The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE) and the Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF) and the Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development (BMZ) of the Federal Republic of Germany 	To conduct mutual consultations, advisory work and policy exchange at both ministerial and expert levels as well as technical assistance in the field of Skill Development and Vocational Education and Training (SD&VET), to establish an innovative system of SD&VET etc.2.	MSDE and BMBF 	The advancement of Dual VET and collaboration on Training of VET Cluster Managers and Indian Skill Development Officers.3.	MSDE, BMZ and Siemens Limited, India	To promote the Indo German Vocational Education and Training (IGVET) cluster approach and the Dual VET initiative at Government Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs), to address the qualification of vocational training personnel etc.
