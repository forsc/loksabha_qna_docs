                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total number of youths trained under skill development programmes in Jharkhand, West Bengal and Bihar during the last five years and the Gender-wise, Year-wise and the District-wise details thereof; and(b)	the total number of youths have been provided jobs in Jharkhand, West Bengal and Bihar after getting trained under these skill development programmes Gender-wise, Year-wise and District-wise details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE) is implementing its flagship demand-driven Centrally Sponsored Scheme, Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY), for imparting short duration skill development training through Short-Term Training (STT) and Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) to youth across all States/ Union Territories (UTs). Details of persons trained inJharkhand, West Bengal and Biharunder PMKVY year-wise, Gender-wise and District-wise during last five years is at Annexure-I.	Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) Scheme:To achieve the vision of Skilling India, the JSS Competent of scheme to support voluntary agencies for adult education and skill development has been transferred from Ministry of Education (erstwhile Ministry of Human Resource Development) to Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship on 2nd July, 2018.The Jan Sikshan Sansthan (JSS) initiative is a step in the direction of realizing the unique demographic potential of urban slums and rural India. The programs are based on the polyvalent or multi-dimensional approach. The main target of the JSS is to impart vocational skills to the non-literates, neo-literates and the person having rudimentary level of education of 8th standard i.eupto class 12th in the age group of 15-45 years with due relaxation in case of “Divyangjan” and other deserving cases. Priority is given to Women, SC, ST, OBC and Minorities. Details of persons trained in Jharkhand, West Bengal and Bihar under JSS year-wise, Gender-wise and District-wise during last five years is atAnnexure II).	National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS): This Scheme is for promoting apprenticeship training and increasing the engagement of apprentices by providing financial support to industrial establishments undertaking apprenticeship programme under the Apprentices Act, 1961. Training consists of Basic Training and On-the-Job Training / Practical Training at workplace in the industry. Details of persons trained in Jharkhand, West Bengal and Bihar under NAPS year-wise, Gender-wise and District-wise during last five years is at Annexure-III. Details of persons enrolled in Jharkhand, West Bengal and Bihar under ITIs year-wise, Gender-wise and District-wise during last five years is at Annexure-IV.
