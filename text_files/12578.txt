                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1513






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2020






MGNREGA in Karnataka




Narayana Swamy Abbaiah














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Centre owes Karnataka any funds under Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA), including labour and material cost;(b) 	if so, the details thereof, year-wise;(c) 	the steps taken and the status of paying the dues; and(d) 	the reasons for such delay in payment, if any?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (d): The Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA), 2005 is a demand driven wage employment programme. Fund release to the States/UTs is a continuous and dynamic process and Central Government is committed to making funds available keeping in view the demand for work. The Ministry releases funds to the States/UTs periodically in two tranches with each tranche consisting of one or more instalments based on agreed to Labour Budget (Persondays), opening balance, pace of utilization of funds, pending liabilities of the previous year, if any,  overall performance and submission of requisite documents. During the current financial year 2019-20 (as on 05.02.2020), the Ministry has released Rs. 4929.37 crore to Karnataka State under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS including pending wage liabilities.
