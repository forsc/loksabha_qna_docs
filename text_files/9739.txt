                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

284






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






NABARD Projects




Jual Oram














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the projects sanctioned by the NABARD in the country during each of the last three years, State and year-wise; (b) the fund provision made for those projects during the above period, State-wise including Odisha; and (c) the progress made on these projects so far?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF FINANCE(a) to (c): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House. Statement referred in reply to parts (a) to (c) of Lok Sabha Starred Question No. *284 to be answered on 09.12.2019 regarding NABARD Projects by SHRI JUAL ORAM(a) to (c): National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) provides loan assistance to State Governments/ State owned corporations for investments in creating agriculture and rural infrastructure. The major funds maintained and operationalized by NABARD out of which such loans are sanctioned are Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF), Long Term Irrigation fund (LTIF), Warehouse Infrastructure Fund (WIF) and Food Processing Fund (FPF). NABARD also provides loans to the State Governments to support various other initiatives of the Government in the rural sector like Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana-Gramin (PMAY-G), Swachh Bharat Mission- Gramin (SBM-G). Details of these interventions are as under:(i). Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF): NABARD has been providing loan assistance to State Governments for building critical rural infrastructure, agriculture development, improving rural connectivity and social sector development.Since inception in 1995-96 till 31st March 2019, cumulative loan of Rs. 3,44,142 crore has been sanctioned and Rs, 2,68,220 crore has been disbursed to various State Governments.The details of State-wise number of projects, sanction and disbursement during the last three years including in the State of Odisha, as reported by NABARD, are given in Annexure-I.(ii). Long Term Irrigation fund (LTIF): LTIF was instituted in NABARD in 2016-17 under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchyee Yojana-Accelerated Irrigation Benefits Programme (PMKSY-AIBP) which envisaged bringing 76 lakh hectare under irrigation through 99 identified projects.As on 31st March 2019, cumulative loans sanctioned and disbursed (including towards both Central and State share) were Rs. 75,769.9 crore and Rs. 34,248.73 crore, respectively.Details of state wise Sanction and Release in respect of LTIF, during the last three years including in the State of Odisha, as reported by NABARD is given in Annexure II.(iii). Part funding of Central Share under PMAY-G & SBM-G scheme of Govt. of India:With an objective of providing pucca house to each household under PMAY-G and to achieve universal sanitation in rural areas under SBM-G, NABARD provides loans to National Rural Infrastructure Development Agency (NRIDA) under Ministry of Rural Development and National Centre for Drinking Water, Sanitation and Quality (NCDWS&Q) under Ministry of Jal Shakti, respectively towards part funding of Central share for onward release to the State Government. The details of loan assistance provided (sanctions and release) under PMAY-G & SBM-G, during the last three years, as reported by NABARD, is given in Annexure III. NABARD has informed that State-wise details in respect of PMAY-G and SBM-G are not maintained by them.(iv). Warehouse Infrastructure Fund (WIF): WIF was instituted by the Government of India with a corpus of Rs. 5,000 crore in 2013-14 for augmenting scientific storage capacity for food-grains and perishable agricultural produce.Till 31st march 2019 NABARD has financed 13.09 million metric tonnes (MMT) of scientific storage facility, with an estimated capital investment of Rs. 9786 crore under WIF. The State wise details of number of projects sanctioned and loan disbursed under WIF during the last three years including in the State of Odisha, as reported by NABARD, are given in Annexure IV.                                                                   *****
