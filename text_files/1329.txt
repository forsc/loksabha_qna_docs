                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1222






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.06.2019






Trafficking and Killing of Snakes and Wild Animals




Sushil Kumar Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the cases of  trafficking and killing of snakes and other  wild animals have  come to the notice of the Government and if so, the  details thereof:(b)	whether  his Ministry is aware of a rescue of a rare two  headed snake from a house at Majra Paltanpurwa in Shawasti district (Bhinga Kotwali) of Uttar Pradesh by the  forest department  including  details  of the accused;(c)	if so, whether such  snakes cost crores in the international market and if so, the details thereof;(d)	whether provision of penalty and punishment for such trafficking is not  commensurate to the nature of crime;(e)	if so, whether the Government has collected State-wise and Union territory-wise details of such  cases of smuggling along with persons accused  and acquitted in this  regard during the last three years; and (f)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE  IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO) (a)		As per the information received from the State authorities, the number of cases of  trafficking and killing of snakes and other wild animals during  the last three years and current year are as under:Years 	Number of Trafficking and Killing Snake Cases	Number of Other Wild Animal cases2016	30	5352017	35	3072018	22	1852019	3	61(b)	One common Boa snake was rescued by Forest Department of Uttar Pradesh from the house of Noor Mohammad S/o Saboor resident of Mauja Paltanpurwa Kotwali-Bhinga and Sohrab S/o Noor Mohammad resident of Mauja Paltanpurwa Kotwali-Bhinga in a routine raid and search operation on 5.6.2019 in Shrawasti District. The rescued Boa snake was released in the natural habitat of Bhinga Reserve Forest. A Forest offence Range Case No.9/2019-20 was registered against the accused under the Wild Life (Protection) Act 1972. The case was compounded after realizing compensation of Rs.110000/- (Rs. one lakh Ten Thousand) from the accused.(c) The trading of wild animals is prohibited under the Wild Life (Protection) Act 1972 so the assessment of cost of Boa snake or other wild animals is not possible.(d), (e) and (f)The provisions of penalty and punishment provided in Wildlife (Protection) Act 1972 are stringent and commensurate to the nature of crime.As per the information received from the State Forest Department, the Year-wise & State-wise details of cases of Snakes smuggling along with person accused in the last three years & current years placed at Annexure. However data regarding persons acquitted is not collated in the Ministy. ***
