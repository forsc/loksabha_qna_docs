                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

774






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Availability of Beds in Hospitals




Asaduddin Owaisi













                                                    Amol Ramsing Kolhe
                                                








                                                    Sunil Dattatray Tatkare
                                                








                                                     Imtiaz Jaleel Syed
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                








                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether as per Human Development Report 2020 India ranks 155th among 167 countries on availability of beds in Hospitals to its population; if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government thereto;(b) whether the patient-bed ratio has improved in the country during the COVID-19 pandemic period as a result of improvement in healthcare infrastructure;(c) the number of Government hospitals along with number of beds available State/UT-wise particularly in Tamil Nadu and Maharashtra;(d) whether the Government has assessed the number of hospitals and beds required for patients in urban and rural areas, State/UT-wise, if so, the details thereof;(e) whether the Government has allocated funds to the States/UTs for setting up of hospitals and increasing beds during each of the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise; and(f) if so, the details thereof and other necessary steps taken in this regard? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):  As per the Human Development Report 2020 prepared by United Nations Development Programme, India ranks 131 in the Human Development Index. The said report can be accessed from  http://hdr.undp.org/en/2020-report. The country-specific ranking based on availability of beds in hospitals to population, as mentioned in the question, is based on newspaper articles. The methodology of the ranking adopted by the concerned newspapers is not known to this Ministry. However, there is no country-wise ranking mentioned in Human Development Report 2020 based on availability of beds in Hospitals to its population. (b):  India COVID-19 Emergency Response and Health System Preparedness Package was approved by Cabinet on 22nd April 2020 with an objective to prevent, detect and respond to the threat post by COVID-19. Under the Package, the States/UTs have been provided support for the improvement of infrastructure only in public health facilities. Accordingly, as reported by all the States/UTs on Health Management Information System maintained by this Ministry, the number of beds available in Government hospitals (i.e. PHC, CHC, SDH and DH) has increased from 631222 as on 1 April 2020 to 760100 as on 1 January 2021.(c):  The number of Government hospitals (i.e., PHC, CHC, SDH and DH) and number of beds available in these health facilities as reported by States/UTs on Health Management Information System maintained by this Ministry is placed at Annexure.(d) to (f):   Public Health and hospitals, being a State subject, the primary responsibility of providing accessible, affordable and quality healthcare including setting up of new hospitals, upgradation and strengthening of existing health facilities lies with the State Governments. Under the National Health Mission (NHM), however, financial and technical support is provided to States/UTs for strengthening their healthcare systems including for expansion of its public health institutions as well as for deployment of human resources to cater to public health services to all citizen based on the proposals received from States/UTs through Programme Implementation Plan (PIP). The details of State/UT wise allocation of funds through PIPs can be accessed from the website of National Health Mission (https://nhm.gov.in/). Apart from this, States are also being supported under NHM for expansion in capacities, viz. through provision of extra hospital beds, ICUs/HDUs, free diagnostics and free drugs/medicines wherever necessary. The PM Atmanirbhar Swasth Bharat Yojana has been announced for developing capacities of primary, secondary and tertiary care health systems, strengthen existing national institutions, and create new institutions, to cater to detection and cure of new and emerging diseases.
