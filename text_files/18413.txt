                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2085






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.09.2020






Relaxation of Regulation on Honey




Thomas Chazhikadan














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether it has come to the notice of the Government that the Apiculturists in Kerala are facing great distress due to the Gazette notification No.293 dated 03/08/2018 issued by FSSAI stating that the water content in honey shall be maximum 20 percent and anything above 20 percent is considered as adulteration;(b) whether Government has noticed that due to high humidity level in Kerala, the water content of the honey produced here is between 20 and 25 percent which has been allowed by BIS and KVIC;(c) whether the Government is considering to relax the prescribed conditions in the Gazette notification to help the Apiculturists of Kerala; and(d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) has received representation from Federation of Indigenous Apiculturist, Kerala in this regard.   (b) to (d):	The  moisture content in the Food Safety and Standards of honey has been notified based on the International standards prescribed by Codex & European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) and National standards by Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS), which is 20% by mass max. This issue was deliberated in the 15th Meeting of Scientific Panel on Sweets, Confectionery, Sugar, Sweetener and Honey wherein the Panel has requested the Federation of Indigenous Apiculturist, Thiruvanananthapuram to submit the relevant data and analysis report in support of their representation.
