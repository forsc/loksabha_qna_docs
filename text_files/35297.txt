                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2739






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Garbage Free Cities




 Hema Malini














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the goal of the Swachh Bharat Mission-Urban 2.0 (SBM-U 2.0) is to make cities garbage-free; (b) 	whether adequate funds have been allocated in the Budget 2022-23 for the SBM-U 2.0 and if so, the details thereof; and  (c) 	whether there is any proposal under the SBM-U 2.0 to establish round the clock cleanliness drive systems in cities having national-international tourism importance and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                                                THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE                     MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                             (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a) and (b) : Government of India, in partnership with States/ UTs, has rolled out Swachh Bharat Mission-Urban (SBM-U) 2.0 on October 1, 2021 with the vision of making cities “Garbage Free”. A Mission allocation of ?1,41,600 Crore have been stipulated for implementation of the SBM-U 2.0 for a period of five years from 2021 to 2026, which is nearly two and half times more than the allocation of ?62,009 Crore provisioned for SBM-U during the period 2014-2019. (c) : Under SBM-U 2.0, funding is proposed for construction of toilets, Solid Waste Management projects, including Material Recovery Facility (MRF), Waste-to-Compost (WtC) plants, Wate-to-Energy (WtE) plants, Construction & Demolition (C&D) plants, Legacy dumpsite remediation, Scientific Landfills (SLFs), etc and Used Grey and Black Water Management plants (in cities with less than 1 lakh population), which would contribute to cities’ cleanliness. Additionally, under SBM-U 2.0, special funding is being provided for construction of Public Toilets in “aspirational category” in all tourist destinations/ places with high footfall/ iconic cities/ religious destinations, etc places.                                            *****
