                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

172






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.06.2019






Supersitions in TV Programme




Ashok Kumar Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether many serials based on black magic, hypocrisy and superstitions are being broadcast on Doordarshan and private channels; (b)	if so, the details thereof along with the reasons therefor; and (c)	the steps taken or likely to be taken by the Government to stop the broadcast of such serials?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE; AND MINISTER OF INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING (SHRI PRAKASH JAVADEKAR)(a) to (c): The Cable Television Networks Rules, 1994 inter-alia provides that TV channels shall not carry any programme which encourages superstition or blind belief.  All TV channels including Doordarshan are required to conform to these Rules.  No serial based on Black Magic, hypocrisy and superstitions are being broadcast on Doordarshan.  The Government has from time to time issue advisory and warnings to private TV channels who are found to have violated the said Programme Code.                                               ***********************
