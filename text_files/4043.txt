                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

323






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.07.2019






Storage Facilities




S.R. Parthiban













                                                    Devusinh Jesingbhai Chauhan
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether there is a shortage of storage and cold storage facilities in the country; (b) 	if so, the details thereof indicating the total available capacity and shortage of such facilities along with the number of cold storage units in public, private and cooperative sectors, sector and State/UT-wise; (c) 	whether the Government provides financial assistance to States to set up these facilities and if so, the details of the amount allocated, sanctioned and disbursed to the States during the last two years and the current year, year and State-wise; (d) 	the schemes being implemented by the Government to provide incentives to entrepreneurs for setting up cold storages across the country; and (e) 	whether the Government has implemented any yojana to create storage facility in rural areas to prevent distress sale of their produce by the farmers and if so, the details thereof indicating the number of godowns set up along with the total capacity created during the last two years under the scheme, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????		 	  (SHRI NARENDRA  SINGH TOMAR) (a) to (e):	A statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (e) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 323  DUE FOR REPLY ON 16TH JULY, 2019.(a) & (b):	 As per a study on All India Cold-chain Infrastructure Capacity (AICIC-2015) conducted by NABCONS, it was reported that by 2014, there was cold storage capacity of 32 million tonnes in the country against an approximate requirement of 35 million tonnes.	As per the latest available information received from States, there are 8038 cold storages with capacity of 36.77 million tonnes in the country. The State – wise details are at Annexure – I.	Further, Base line Survey conducted during December, 2013 by M/s Hansa Research Group estimated that 92% of cold storage are owned and operated by private sector, 3% cooperative and remaining 5% are under Public Sector. 	Further, Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food & Public Distribution deals with the storage of foodgrains post procurement by Food Corporation of India (FCI) and State Agencies. Against total stocks of 741.41 LMT (as on 01.06.2019), the total storage capacity available with Food Corporation of India (FCI), Central Warehousing Corporation (CWC) and the State Agencies (both owned and hired capacity), is 862.45 LMT (as on 31.05.2019) comprising 739.76 LMT in covered godowns and 122.69 LMT in Cover and Plinth (CAP) storage. 	As such, there is sufficient capacity for storage of central pool foodgrains at the national level.(c) & (d):	 The Government is implementing the following schemes to provide incentives for setting up  of cold storages in the country:i)	Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH): Department of Agriculture Cooperation & Farmers Welfare is implementing MIDH under which financial assistance is provided for various activities on horticulture development   from production to post harvest management   including establishment of Contd…2/--2-cold storage infrastructure. The component is demand/entrepreneur driven through commercial ventures for which Government assistance in the form of credit linked back ended subsidy is available. The assistance is available at the rate of 35% (for general areas) and 50%  (for hilly and scheduled areas) of capital cost of the project for both public and private sector enterprises. During last two years 380 cold storages with capacity of 1.75 million tonnes were approved with Rs. 623.84 crore assistance under MIDH (upto March 2019). The year – wise and State – wise details of cold storages approved under MIDH during the last two years are at Annexure – II.  The allocations for MIDH Scheme as a whole which include allocation for cold storage activity for the current year are at Annexure – III. The usage for cold storage activities under the scheme is as per demand of this activity in the respective States. ii)	Pradhan Mantri Kisan SAMPADA Yojana (PMKSY):Ministry of Food Processing Industries is implementing a Central Sector Scheme of Integrated Cold Chain and Value Addition Infrastructure (now subsumed under Pradhan Mantri Kisan SAMPADA Yojana) to provide integrated cold chain and preservation infrastructure facilities. Under this scheme, Ministry provides financial assistance in the form of grant-in-aid at the rate of 35% for general area and 50% for North-East States, Himalayan States, ITDP areas and Islands for storage and transport infrastructure subject to a maximum grant-in-aid of Rs. 10.00 crore for setting up integrated cold chain projects with cold storage as one of the component. During last two years 160 cold chain projects with capacity of 3.52 lakh MT were approved with Rs. 1273.37 crore assistance under PMKSY.  The year – wise and State – wise details of cold chain projects approved under the scheme during the last two years is at Annexure – IV.  During current year (2019 - 20),  two cold chain projects with Rs. 12.24 crore as assistance under PMKSY have been approved in Andhra Pradesh and Karnataka.  Contd…3/--3-(e):	 The Government is promoting storage facilities in rural areas of the country through the scheme of “Agricultural Marketing Infrastructure (AMI)”, which is a sub-scheme under the Integrated Scheme for Agricultural Marketing (ISAM). AMI scheme is a back ended capital subsidy scheme in which rate of subsidy provided is 25% and 33.33% based on the category of eligible beneficiary and provided on capital cost of the project. Assistance under the sub-scheme is available to individuals, groups of farmers/growers, registered Farmer Produce Organizations (FPOs) etc. So far, a total of 38,964 storage infrastructure projects (Godowns), with storage capacity of 655.48 Lakh MT have been sanctioned across the country in rural areas. 	The scheme is demand driven and during last two years, a total of 1699 storage infrastructure projects (Godowns) have been assisted with storage capacity of 32.89 Lakh MT under storage component of AMI sub-scheme. The State-wise details are at Annexure V.
