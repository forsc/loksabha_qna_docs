                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3725






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.12.2019






BTS in Uttarakhand




Ajay Tamta














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the number of operational Base Transceiver Station (BTS) in the State of Uttarakhand are as per the requirement and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reaction of the Government thereto; (b) 	whether the Government will make efforts to increase the number of Base Transceiver Stations (BTS) for expanding mobile connectivity in Uttarakhand; (c) 	if so, the details thereof and the steps taken by the Government in this regard along with the names of the places where new BTS are likely to be installed; and (d) 	the district-wise number of BTS presently working in Uttarakhand?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS,  HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a)	The Government has taken several policy initiatives to facilitate infrastructure growth which include permitting trading/sharing/ liberalisation of spectrum, permitting passive & active infrastructure sharing, notification of Right of Way Rules 2016, making available government land/buildings for installations of towers etc. As a result, around 15.57 lakh additional Base Transceiver Stations (BTSs) for 2G/3G/4G-LTE services have been added by Telecom Service Providers(TSPs) during the period from April 2014 (6.49 lakh BTS) to 30th November, 2019 (22.06 lakh BTS) and 1.67 lakh mobile towers have been added during the period from Dec’ 2015 (4.15 lakh) to 30th November 2019 (5.82 lakh mobile towers) across the country. In the state of Uttarakhand, 25523 numbers of BTSs are operational as on 30.11.2019 & number of Mobile towers is 5878 as detailed at Annexure-I . Further, new BTSs are installed by Telecom Service Providers to improve coverage and/or capacity of their networks based on their techno-commercial requirements on continuous basis.(b) & (c)	The Government has planned to cover all uncovered villages in a phased manner, utilizing the Universal Service Obligation Fund (USOF). Provision of Mobile Services in Uncovered Areas of J&K and Ladakh region and part of Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand States and other priority areas are being covered under 354 Uncovered Villages Schemes Project. Department of Telecom has floated a tender dated 17.10.2019 on technology neutral and outcome based approach for the provisioning of Mobile Services in these 354 uncovered villages including 28 villages of Uttarakhand as detailed at Annexure-II.(d) 	The district-wise number of BTS presently working in Uttarakhand is given at Annexure-III.
