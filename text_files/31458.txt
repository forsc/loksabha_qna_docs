                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2622






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

14.12.2021






Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana




Jayadev Galla














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether any steps have been taken to raise awareness about the benefits of select Government schemes in rural area after the release of the evaluation of Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana Report;(b) 	the number of women received skill training as a part of MKSP programme;(c) 	whether the Government plans to implement training on sustainable agricultural practices to women; and(d) 	if so, the details thereof, State and district-wise particularly the State of Andhra Pradesh?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI) (a)	Yes Sir. The Government has undertaken steps to create awareness regarding this programme and its benefits among the farming community to increase the participation of women in agriculture. In this regard, Ministry is continuously working to create awareness about the programme through, Community Resource Persons, IEC (Information, Education and Communication) strategies and campaigns of National Mission as well as the State Rural Livelihoods Missions. The benefits of taking up sustainable practices in Agriculture, Non- Timber Forest Produce (NTFP), and livestock are discussed in meetings of the Self Help Groups (SHGs), Village Organization and Cluster Level Federation meetings. The learning''s from the practices adopted under Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana (MKSP) are now being scaled up by the Rural Livelihoods Missions of States/Union Territories (UTs) under their Annual Action Plans (AAPs). (b)	Under Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana- National Rural Livelihood Mission (DAY- NRLM) till now, about 38 lakh women farmers have been trained under Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana (MKSP). The learning of the interventions under MKSP has been expanded to various parts of the country under the Annual Action Plans of States/UTs. Accordingly, a total of around 1.44 crore Mahila Kisan (women farmers) have been covered under DAY-NRLM till date.(c)	Yes Sir. The Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana- National Rural Livelihood Mission (DAY-NRLM) is already facilitating trainings to the women farmers in the sustainable agricultural practices.(d)	The State-wise break up is given in Annexure-I and the district wise break up of Andhra Pradesh is given in Annexure II.*****
