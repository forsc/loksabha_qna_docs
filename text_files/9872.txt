                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3269






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






Accountability of Teachers




Aparajita Sarangi














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has any scheme in place, or plan to improve the attendance,performance and accountability of teachers in primary and secondary education;(b) if so, the details thereof, including the attendance rates, classroom performance and anyother metrics demonstrating the effect of the scheme and the timeline for its implementation;and(c) whether the Government has considered outlining education outcomes for primary andsecondary schools, including but not limited to measures such as centrally-determined testingand curricula and if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a) to (c): Education is in the Concurrent list of the Constitution. The recruitment and serviceconditions of the teachers come under the purview of the respective State/ Union Territory(UT) Government, who delineate the accountability mechanisms for teacher’s performance.Section 24 of The Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009provides that teachers attend school regularly and transact the syllabus in a timely manner,provide supplementary support to children who need such support, make an assessment ofchildren’s learning, and interact with parents.Further, The National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) hasdeveloped Performance Indicators for Elementary Education (PINDICS) for self evaluationof teacher performance in Government schools which has been shared with States and UTs.Teachers’ attendance is monitored by States and UTs through School ManagementCommittees/School Management Development Committees/ Block ResourceCentres/Clusters Resource Centres, by installing Bio-metric/ Electronic attendance systemand other means.The Central Rules to the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) wereamended on 20th February, 2017 to include reference on class-wise, subject-wise LearningOutcomes. NCERT conducts periodic National Achievement Surveys (NAS) to assesslearning achievement of children in Classes –III, V, VIII and X which are shared with theStates and UTs to help identify gaps and devise strategies and also provide feedback forteachers with a focus on areas of teaching learning that need improvement. Apart fromNAS, States and UTs conduct State Level Achievement Surveys (SLAS)/ Assessments atvarious levels to help them understand the level of achievement of children belonging todifferent regions/districts within the State/UT.****
