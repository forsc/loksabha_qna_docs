                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2784






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.08.2021






Status of JJM in Chhattisgarh




Vijay Baghel













                                                    Arun Sao
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the present status of Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) in the country including Chhattisgarh;(b)	the number of households which have been provided drinking water connections and the number of people benefited in Chhattisgarh including Bilaspur, Mungeli and Gaurella-Penta-Marwahi districts under this Mission, district-wise;(c)	the funds allocated to Chhattisgarh thereunder, district-wise; and(d)	the steps taken/being taken by the Government to achieve the target fixed under ‘Har Ghar Nal Se Jal’ scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) Government of India, in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) to make provision of potable tap water supply to every rural household in the country by 2024, with an estimated outlay of Rs. 3.60 lakh Crore. In August, 2019, at the time of announcement of Jal Jeevan Mission, 3.23 Crore (16.92%) households had the provision of potable water supply through tap connections. As on date, 7.87 Crore (41.15%) rural households have the provision of potable water supply through tap connections. So far, in 76 districts and 1,05,144 villages, provision of tap water supply to every household has been made in the country. In Chhattisgarh, out of 45.48 lakh rural households, 5.87 lakh (12.92%) have provision of potable tap water supply.(b) The district-wise details of number of households provided tap water connections in Chhattisgarh, as reported by the State, are annexed.(c)  District-wise fund allocation of a State, under JJM, is not maintained by this Department. (d) To achieve the plan under ‘Jal Jeevan Mission, the Government of India has taken many steps including holding joint review meetings with Chief Ministers/ Lt. Governors at the level of Union Minister; field visits; holding regular monthly and quarterly reviews with the States/UTs; displaying implementation details on the JJM dashboard to public ;provisioning for Project Monitoring Units (PMUs) at National, State and district levels to strengthen project planning, implementation and monitoring; conducting regular conferences, webinars, etc. Comprehensive planning exercise have been taken with States/ UTs to ensure provision of tap water supply to every home, school, anganwadi centre, ashramshala, health centre, community centres, etc. in a time-bound manner. *****
