Writing Skills




Jagdambika Pal 














                        Will the Minister of





SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of progress made with regard to Augmenting Writing Skills for Articulating Research (AWSAR) Initiative; (b) 	the total number of newspaper articles, magazines and blogs published under the same; and (c) 	the total number of scholars engaged therefor, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE; MINISTER OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY AND MINISTER OF EARTH SCIENCES(DR HARSH VARDHAN)????????? ?? ?????? ?????? ??????; ??????? ?? ???????????? ??????; ?? ?????? ??????? ????????. ???? ?????(a)	Augmenting Writing Skills for Articulating Research (AWSAR) is an initiative that aims to disseminate Indian research stories among the masses in a form that is easy to understand and interesting for a common man. Under this initiative, PhD Scholars and Post-Doctoral Fellows (PDFs) in Science &Technology (S&T) streams are encouraged to write at least one popular science article during the tenancy of their research, and to participate in a national competition. Best 100 entries in PhD category and 20 entries in PDF category are awarded prize of Rs. 10,000/- along with Certificate of Appreciation. Top three leading stories under PhD category are awarded with the cash prize of Rs.1,00,000/-, Rs.50,000/- and Rs.25,000/- respectively. One outstanding story under PDF category is awarded a prize of Rs.1,00,000/-.The programme was launched on 24 January, 2018. Since then a dedicated website on AWSAR (www.awsar-dst.in) has been launched that covers various aspects such as  timeline for submitting popular science story, eligibility, award categories, advertisements, sample research stories, details of workshops, etc. An encouraging response has been received from the scholars. Till March, 2019, AWSAR website visitor count was more than 2 lakhs. A total of 2,629 stories were received under ‘Call for Entries 2018’ in both PhD and PDF categories. These stories were evaluated and best 124 stories were selected and awarded on National Science Day, held on 28 February, 2019.(b)	Several articles, blogs, facebook posts, social media posts were published in leading newspapers, science magazines, brochures, flyers and social media platform, on AWSAR programme including those for its promotion.
