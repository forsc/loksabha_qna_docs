                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

906






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Shortage of Doctors in Telangana




Anumula Revanth Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government is aware of the fact that there is acute shortage of doctors in the state of Telangana especially in Primary and Community Health Centres which is adversely affecting the poor patients availing treatment at these centres generally and especially during the ongoing Coronavirus Pandemic;(b) if so, the details thereof and reasons for the shortage of doctors;(c) the number of Primary Health Centres that fulfil the parameters of infrastructure and manpower as laid down in the Indian Public Health Standards, district wise in Telangana;(d) the number and details of the Primary Health Centres that do not fulfil the parameters of infrastructure and manpower as laid down in the Indian Public Health Standards, district-wise in Telangana; and(e) the remedial measures which Government proposes to take in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b) : Public Health and Hospitals being a State Subject, all the administrative and personnel matters, including that of recruitment/appointment/engagement of doctors, lies with the respective State Governments. However, under National Health Mission, financial and technical support is provided to States/UTs to strengthen their healthcare systems including support for engagement of doctors based on the requirement posed by them in their Programme Implementation Plans (PIPs) subject to availability of resources.  As per Rural Health Statistics, 2018-19 the details of availability doctors and shortfall thereof at Primary Health Centres (PHCs) and Community Health Centres (CHCs), in the State of Telangana is given at Annexure-I.(c) : Out of 636 Primary Health Centers (PHCs) in the State of Telangana, 628 are having the infrastructure and man power as per Indian Public Health Standards.  District- wise  details of these PHCs is given at Annexure-II.(d) : A statement showing District-wise details of   Primary Health Centers (PHCs) in Telangana, which are not having the infrastructure and manpower as per the Indian Public Health Standards, is given at Annexure-III.(e) :  As stated above, Public health is State subject, the primary responsibility engagement of health human resource and creation of infrastructure in public health facilities including Primary Health Centres vests with the respective State/UT. However, under National Health Mission, financial and technical support is provided to States/UTs to strengthen their healthcare systems including support for  creation of healthcare infrastructure and engagement of manpower in public health centres,  based on the requirement posed by them in their Programme Implementation Plans (PIPs) subject to availability to resources.
