                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

144






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.11.2019






Cottage Industries




 Sunil Kumar Pintu













                                                    Rodmal Nagar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the present status of cottage industries in the country, particularly in Bihar; (b) 	the efforts being made by the Government for promotion of such industries; (c) 	whether the Government has formulated any scheme for revival and promotion of domestic cottage industries which are on the verge of extinction; (d)	if so, the details thereof, State-wise; and(e) 	if not, the plan under consideration of the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NITIN GADKARI)(a) to (e):	A statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO THE PARTS (a) TO (e) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION No.*144 FOR ANSWERON 28.11.2019(a)&(b):	‘Cottage Industry’ has not been defined under the Khadi and Village Industries Commission Act, 1956.  However, the broad contours of ‘Cottage Industry’ are covered under the ‘Village Industry’ vertical of the Khadi and Village Industries Commission (KVIC).The Village Industries under the purview of KVIC are broadly classified under six groups for the purpose of implementation of its programmes which are as follows:1.	Agro Based & Food Processing Industry (ABFPI) includes industries viz. Pulses & Cereals Processing Industry, Gur &Khandsari Industry, Palmgur Industry, Fruit & Vegetable Processing Industry, Village Oil Industry, Spices and Condiments, Collection of Minor Forest Produce, Bamboo, Cane & Reed, Medicinal Plant etc.2.	Mineral Based Industry (MBI) includes industries viz. Pottery for food industry, Lime, Plaster of Paris products, Stone cutting, polishing, crushing &carving, Brass metal and other metal crafts, etc.3.	Wellness & Cosmetics Industry includes Soap & Oil Industry, Aromatic Oils & fragrances, Cosmetic and beauty products, Hair Oils and Shampoos, Toiletries, etc.4.	Handmade Paper, Leather & Plastic Industry (HMLPI) includes industries viz. Handmade Paper and paper Products, Paper Conversion, Leather Industry, Plastic Industry, Natural Fibre other than Coir. 5.	Rural Engineering and New Technology Industry (RENTI) includes industries viz. Bio-Gas, Non-Conventional Energy, Bio-Manure, Vermi-compost, Carpentry & Black-smithy, Agri Implements & Tools, Electric & Electronic Products Industry, Dry Dairy & Manufacturing of household metal utensils & articles.6.	Service & Textile Industry (STI) includes Small Business, Maintenance and Servicing of electrical and electronic goods & Farm Aggregators (Pre & Post Farming).Most of the activities of these Village Industries sub-sectors are covered under the activities taken under Cottage Industries.Large part of the Village Industries are assisted under Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP), every year by providing Margin Money subsidy to set up new micro enterprises.(c)&(d):	Government has taken the following measures for revival and promotion of domestic cottage industries in the country:i)	PMEGP is a credit linked subsidy scheme, for setting up of new micro-enterprises and to generate employment opportunities in rural as well as urban areas of the country through KVIC, State Khadi & Village Industries Board (KVIB) and District Industries Centre (DICs).  General category beneficiaries can avail of margin money subsidy of 25% of the project cost in rural areas and 15% in urban areas. For beneficiaries belonging to special categories such as SC/ST/Women/PH/Minorities/Ex-Servicemen/NER, the margin money subsidy is 35% in rural areas and 25% in urban areas. The maximum cost of projects is Rs.25 lakh in the manufacturing sector and Rs.10 lakh in the service sector.State-wise details of Margin Money allocated and utilized, job opportunities created and number of units set up under PMEGP Scheme in the country, including the State of Bihar, during the last three years and current year is placed at Annexure-I.ii)	Coir VikasYojana is being implemented by Coir Board to cater to the multiple developmental needs of coir industry.  Interventions under the following components of the Scheme are taken up during the period from 2017-18 to 2019-20.1.	One of the components under this scheme is Market Development Assistance (MDA) which provides assistance to Coir Units through State Governments for the development of coir industry and improve the marketing of coir products. An assistance of 10% of average sales during the preceding 3 years is provided to the Coir Units.2.	Skill Upgradation and Mahila Coir Yojana (MCY):  Development of skilled manpower in coir industry in different facets of coir production and processing through appropriate training programmes, seminars, workshops, awareness programmes, exposure visits etc.  is the major objective under Skill Upgradation and Mahila Coir Yojana (MCY). Under skill upgradation and Mahila Coir Yojana honorarium shall be kept as Rs.15,000/-p.m. for trainer and Rs.3000/- p.m. for trainee subject to the norms of NSQF compliance.iii)	In addition to the above, Ministry of MSME is implementing the Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI)for making Traditional Industries more productive and competitive by organizing the Traditional Industries and artisans into clusters.Details of the number of SFURTI clusters sanctioned to the various States across the country, including the State of Bihar, during the last three years and current year (upto 22.11.2019) is placed at Annexure-II.(e):	Does not arise.
