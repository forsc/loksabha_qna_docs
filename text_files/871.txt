                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

796






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.06.2019






Effect of Heavy Rains on Railways




Manoj Kishorbhai Kotak














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                     (a) whether the Government is aware that heavy rains have severely affected rail services of the Central Railway in Mumbai and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has any plan to provide support to Mumbai’s lifeline local trains during the monsoon season; (c) if so, the details thereof; 	(d) whether locomotive engine has been modified so that it can move local trains as well as long-distance trains that get stuck on tracks due to water-logging; and (e) if so, the details in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY (SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a)	Rail services of Central Railway in Mumbai have not been affected during this year due to heavy rains, so far.  (b)	and (c) Running of Mumbai local trains, including during monsoon season, is being monitored rigorously. Steps being taken for smooth running of Mumbai local trains during monsoon season  include:-1.	Control room is being monitored round the clock by officers and running and operating staff is kept on alert and advised to attend office in short notice during flooding. 2.	Motor points are clamped in flood prone areas.3.	Precautionary measures are taken in close co-ordination with Metrological department, BEST and Municipal Authorities and information is given to public through announcement & media.4.	Water pumps have been installed at flood prone areas of railway tracks and points have been identified to be clamped in case of heavy rain. 5.	Patrolmen have been deployed at vulnerable locations by the engineering department.6.	Muck Specials are being run at frequent intervals to clear tracks of garbage etc.7.	Close co-ordination and liaisoning is done with various Municipal Bodies for arranging local transport and quick disbursal of passengers8.	Monsoon precaution booklets have been issued by engineering and safety department, precautionary measures are taken to avoid marooning of rakes by close monitoring.9.	Monsoon rakes are formed at various locations to meet unusual occurrences.10.	Crowd control at vulnerable locations viz. Foot Over Bridges (FOBs), Station entrance/exits by deputing ticket checking staff and co-ordination with Railway Protection Force (RPF)/Government Railway Police (GRP) staff.11.	Proper upkeep and maintenance of ticketing equipments like Unreserved Ticketing System (UTS), Automatic Ticket Vending Machines and Coin-operated Ticket Vending Machines (CoTVMs) to ensure proper condition of ticketing stock and to avoid down time of machines.12.	Availability of adequate stock of catering materials and drinking water at all catering stalls, is ensured.(d) and (e) No specific modification in diesel locomotives has been carried for this purpose.  Diesel locomotives can haul long distance as well as local trains within the existing operational conditions. *****
