                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.03.2020






Protection of Women from Crimes and Harassment




Jagannath Sarkar














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	the details of measures taken by the Government that has resulted in decline in cases of crime against women;(b)	the extent to which the funds under Nirbhaya Scheme have been utilized on various projects since its inception, State-wise; (c)	the details of cases reported/registered, arrests made, trials completed, convictions made along with pending cases under crime against women, State/ UT-wise including West Bengal; (d)	the reasons for not setting up of One Stop Centres in certain States, State/UT-wise including West Bengal; and (e)	whether the Government has proposed any new scheme under Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Scheme and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)(a) : The government accords utmost importance to safety of women and girls in the country, following are the steps taken by the government :(i)	A Women Safety Division has been set up in the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) to coordinate various policies and initiatives for women safety in the Ministry.(ii)	For effective legal deterrence against sexual assaults, Government has enacted the Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2018.  This Act prescribes even more stringent penal provisions including death penalty for rape of a girl below the age of 12 years.(iii)	The Criminal Law (Amendment) Act 2018 also inter-alia mandates completion of investigation and trials within 2 months each. MHA has launched on 19th February, 2019 an online analytic tool for police called “Investigation Tracking System for Sexual Offences” (ITSSO) to monitor and track time-bound investigation in sexual assault cases in accordance with Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2018.(iv)	MHA has launched the “National Database on Sexual Offenders” (NDSO) on 20th September, 2018 to facilitate investigation and tracking of sexual offenders across the country by Law Enforcement Agencies. It has data of over 5 lakh sexual offenders.(v)	Emergency Response Support System (ERSS), which provides a single emergency number (112) based computer aided dispatch of field resources to the location of distress has been operationalized in 28 States/ UTs in 2018-19.  (vi)	MHA has launched a cyber-crime portal on 20th September, 2018 for citizens to report obscene content. Further, Cyber Crime Forensic Labs have been set up in several States, and training of over 9,183 personnel, including 987 Public Prosecutors and Judicial Officers in identifying, detecting and resolving cyber-crimes against women and children has been imparted.(vii)	MHA is supporting Delhi Police in strengthening of Special Unit for Women & Children (SPUWAC) and Special Unit for North East Region (SPUNER) in Delhi.  Delhi Police has recruited 54 Social Workers and Counsellors in Police Stations and Sub-Division level offices to support and counsel women to report crimes on them without fear or shame. Other activities undertaken include Self Defence Training Camps for Women, ‘Good-Touch/ Bad-Touch’ Training programs in Schools, Gender Sensitization, Gender Communications and Soft Skills training programs for Officers and Women Safety Awareness programs in crime prone areas of Delhi. Delhi Police has also printed and circulated a Handbook on Investigation of Crimes against Women to its Officers under the project to facilitate timely investigations.(viii)	Using technology to aid smart policing and safety management, Safe City Projects have been sanctioned in phase I in 8 cities (Ahmedabad, Bengaluru, Chennai, Delhi, Hyderabad, Kolkata, Lucknow and Mumbai).(ix)	In order to aid timely and effective investigations, MHA has expedited the set-up of a state-of-the-art DNA Analysis lab at Central Forensic Science Laboratory (CFSL), Chandigarh.(x)	MHA has also sanctioned setting-up and upgrading of DNA Analysis units in State Forensic Science Laboratories in 13 States/UTs.(xi)	MHA has notified guidelines for collection of forensic evidence in sexual assault cases and the standard composition in a sexual assault forensic kit. To facilitate adequate capacity in manpower the MHA has initiated training and skill building programs for Investigation Officers, Prosecution Officers and Medical Officers. 8450 Officers have already been trained by BPR&D and NIC&FS in collection, handling and transportation of forensic evidence.  BPR&D has distributed 3,120 Sexual Assault Evidence Collection (SAEC) Kits to States/ UTs as orientation kit as part of training.(xii)	MHA is setting-up Anti-Human Trafficking Units (AHTU) in all districts of the country at cost of Rs.100 crore under Nirbhaya Fund. The AHTUs will provide counselling and support to the victims of trafficking.(xiii)	Ministry of Home Affairs is setting up and strengthening of Women Help Desks in Police Stations, including Police Stations in rural areas, at a total cost of Rs.100 crores.  This project would be implemented by the respective States and Union Territories. Women Help Desks would focus on making the Police Stations more women friendly and approachable, as they would be the first and single point of contact for any woman walking into a police station. Essentially, lady police officers would be deployed at these help desks. The officials of Women Help Desk would be trained to be sensitive towards women. These help desks would have enlisted panel of experts like lawyers, psychologists and NGOs to facilitate legal aid, counselling, shelter, rehabilitation and training etc.(b) :  The details of the funds utilized under Nirbhaya Fund (State/UT-wise) is as per Annexure-I.(c) : State/UT wise (including West Bengal) cases registered, arrest made, trials completed, conviction made and pending cases under crime against women in the year 2018 as per the details provided by National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) is at Annexure-II.(d) :  Setting up of One Stop Centres (OSCs) have been approved in all State/UTs by Government of India.  So far, 728 OSCs have been approved by Government of India in 724 districts and 680 OSCs have been operationalised by the States/UTs across the country. However, OSCs have not been set up in West Bengal. In this regard, no communication has been received from the State Government of West Bengal. (e) :  The Beti Bachao Beti Padhao Scheme was launched on 22nd January, 2015 as an awareness generation campaign to address the critical issue of declining Child Sex Ratio (CSR) in the age group of 0-6 years and aims to change mindsets to value girl child.  It is a triministerial effort of Ministry of Women and Child Development, Ministry of Health & Family Welfare and Ministry of Human Resource Development.  The BBBP Scheme is implemented in all 640 districts (as per census 2011) of the country since 8th March, 2018. The 405 districts are covered through Multi - sectoral intervention & Advocacy & Media component and 235 districts are covered through & Advocacy & Media component only.  The specific objectives of the scheme are to (i) prevent gender biased sex selective elimination, (ii) ensure survival and protection of the girl child, and (iii) ensure education and participation of the girl child through coordinated and convergent efforts. The BBBP Scheme is being implemented through the District Authorities.  The funds are directly transferred to the selected 405 district authorities for implementation of the scheme.
