                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2829






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






Tap Water Supply




Jyotirmay Singh Mahato














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of households in which drinking water is supplied by the Government through taps along with the percentage of people and the number of houses included under the said scheme, State-wise including West Bengal; (b) the total amount of funds allocated/released and spent under the said scheme during the last three years and the current year; (c) the State-wise amount of funds likely to be spent in this regard to achieve the target of providing drinking water through taps including West Bengal; and (d) the time by which drinking water is likely to be made available through taps in the households of all villages?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) State-wise details of number  and percentage of households being supplied with piped water supply through tap connection including West Bengal is at Annex-I. Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation does not maintain data regarding people having piped water supply with tap connection.(b) The details of total amount of fund allocated/ released and reported expenditure under erstwhile National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP) duringthe last three years and under Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) in the current yearis at Annex-II.(c) Under JJM, Government of India has planned an outlay of ?3.60 lakh Crore over the next five years i.e. upto 2024, out of which central share is ?2.08 lakh Crore and remaining amount as State share. States can spend additional funds from their own resources to achieve the target as drinking water is a state subject. In view of involvement of additional State fund, it is not possible to provide the exact amount likely to be spent by the States.(d) Government of India has launched Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM), which aims at providing potable water to every rural household through Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC) by 2024.***
