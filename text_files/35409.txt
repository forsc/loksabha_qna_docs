                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

243






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.03.2022






Quality Education under Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan




Unmesh Bhaiyyasaheb Patil













                                                    Heena Vijaykumar Gavit
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has taken steps to improve the standard of education and school infrastructure in rural India and if so, the details thereof;(b) the steps taken by the Government to improve education along with the funds disbursed for Government schools for infrastructure such as library, laboratory, sports facilities and teaching aids during the last three years; (c) whether the Government has taken any steps to establish high schools and inter colleges in the country particularly in Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra and West Bengal as per the norms of Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA) and if so, the details thereof; and (d) the number of new high schools and inter colleges required in the country particularly in Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra and West Bengal as per the norms of SSA?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) to (d): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House. ******STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (d) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 243 TO BE ANSWERED ON 21.03.2022 ASKED BY HON’BLE SHRI UNMESH BHAIYYASAHEB PATIL AND DR. HEENA GAVIT REGARDING QUALITY EDUCATION UNDER SAMAGRA SHIKSHA ABHIYAN(a) and (b): The Government is committed to provide quality education by enhancing the standard of education and school infrastructure in the country including rural India. The following initiatives have been taken by Government of India to improve the standard of school education at all levels:1. The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Samagra Shiksha has been aligned to the recommendations of NEP 2020. Samagra Shiksha is an Integrated Centrally Sponsored Scheme for school education extending from pre-school to class XII and aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education at all levels of school education. Samagra Shiksha focuses on improvement in quality of education by providing support for different interventions like in-service training of teachers and school heads, conduct of achievement surveys, composite school grant to every school for providing a conducive learning environment, grants for library, sports and physical activities, etc. Under the scheme, financial assistance is also provided to States and UTs for undertaking various activities to ensure universal access to education including opening/strengthening of new schools upto senior secondary level, construction of school buildings & additional classrooms, up-gradation and running of Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalayas, setting up of residential schools/hostels. 2. National Initiative for Proficiency in Reading with Understanding and Numeracy (NIPUN Bharat) has been launched under Samagra Shiksha for ensuring that every child in the country necessarily attains foundational literacy and numeracy (FLN) by the end of Grade 3, and e-content for the same has been released in DIKSHA platform.3. Integrated In-service teacher training programme - NISHTHA 1.0, 2.0, and 3.0 has been introduced under Samagra Shiksha for different stages of school education for Teachers, Head Teachers/Principals and other stakeholders in Educational Management.4 Learning Outcomes for the Secondary level have been notified.5. National Achievement Survey (NAS) 2021 has been conducted on 12th November 2021 to assess children''s learning competencies as an indicator of the health of the education system, to take appropriate steps for remedial actions at different levels. 6. A 70 indicator based matrix Performance Grading Index (PGI) has been developed to grade the States/UTs. District level Performance Grading Index (PGI) has been prepared for grading of Districts.7. The Structured Assessment for Analyzing Learning levels (SAFAL) framework has been developed by CBSE and launched for Competency-based assessment for grades 3, 5 and 8 focusing on core concepts, application-based questions and higher-order thinking skills.8. VIDYA PRAVESH—Guidelines for Three-month Play-based School Preparation Module, has been released to ensure that all children are exposed to a warm and welcoming environment when they enter Grade-I.9. Introduction of Indian Sign Language as a subject at secondary level by National Institute of open Schooling (NIOS).Further, the funds released for different infrastructure interventions under Samagra Shiksha including library rooms, laboratory, etc during last three years is as follows:<pre>Year	2018-19	2019-20	2020-21Funds released (Rs in lakh)	318142.94	352869.29	222753.61</pre>(c) and (d): Under Samagra Shiksha, States/UTs propose new schools in their respective Annual Work Plan and Budget (AWP&B) for consideration by Project Approval Board (PAB) which approves the new/upgradation of schools based on the availability of funds, viability of the proposal and physical & financial progress of earlier sanctioned works, as per the norms of the scheme in consultation with the State representatives.The state-wise details of secondary schools and senior secondary schools including Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra and West Bengal sanctioned under erstwhile scheme of Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA) till 2017-18 and Samagra Shiksha since the launch of scheme in 2018-19 are at Annexure.Also, as per GIS based mapping conducted in the year 2021-22 including government, government aided and private schools, 95.48% of villages were covered by Secondary schools within a distance of 5 km and 90.55% of villages were covered by senior secondary schools within a distance of 7 km. *****
