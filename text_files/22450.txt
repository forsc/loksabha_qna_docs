                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PLANNING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3582






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2021






Atal Innovation Mission




Diya Kumari













                                                    Kuruva Gorantla Madhav
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PLANNING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of schools selected in Rajasthan and Andhra Pradesh under Atal Innovation Mission; (b)	the number of schools that received grants for setting up Atal Tinkering Labs under the said Mission;(c)	the details of procedure of selection and the timeframe of the grant made available to the selected schools;(d)	whether the Government proposes to bring Rajsamand (Rajasthan) and Anantpur (Andhra Pradesh) district under the said scheme; and (e)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF PLANNING AND MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF STATISTICS AND PROGRAMME IMPLEMENTATION(RAO INDERJIT SINGH)(a)	Atal Innovation Mission (“AIM”) is Government of India’s endeavour to promote a culture of innovation and entrepreneurship. Its objective is to serve as a platform for promotion of world-class Innovation Hubs, Grand Challenges, Start-up businesses and other self-employment activities, particularly in technology driven areas. AIM has been launched to create an innovation and entrepreneurial ecosystem in India by establishing new incubation centres, scaling up established incubation centres and setting up Atal Tinkering Labs (ATL) in schools.Number of schools selected in Rajasthan 		– 	723Number of schools selected in Andhra Pradesh 	–	1040(b)	Till date, a total of 7138 schools have received the ATL Grant-In-Aid. The state-wise list is attached at ‘Annexure A’.(c)	Atal Innovation Mission is setting up Atal Tinkering Labs in schools selected through an application and selection process, followed by compliance and due diligence process. After the completion of necessary compliance and due diligence process, the ATL grant-in-aid is sanctioned to the school, subject to budgetary allocation.(d)&(e) The number ATLs in schools in the abovementioned districts are:District (State)	No. of schools selected	No. of schools fundedRajsamand (Rajasthan)	9	4Anantpur (Andhra Pradesh)	50	26*****Annexure ANumber of funded ATL schools in each State/UTsState/UT	No. of SchoolsAndaman And Nicobar Island	10Andhra Pradesh	570Arunachal Pradesh	30Assam	280Bihar	82Chandigarh	18Chhattisgarh	304Dadra And Nagar Haveli	3Delhi	192Goa	17Gujarat	501Haryana	291Himachal Pradesh	96Jammu And Kashmir	70Jharkhand	92Karnataka	577Kerala	326Madhya Pradesh	424Maharashtra	792Manipur	71Meghalaya	12Mizoram	22Nagaland	23Odisha	258Puducherry	17Punjab	168Rajasthan	329Sikkim	25Tamil Nadu	495Telangana	230Tripura	14Uttar Pradesh	608Uttarakhand	59West Bengal	132Grand Total	7138
