                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2500






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.08.2021






Rural and Urban Employment under MGNREGS in Kerala




Thomas Chazhikadan














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS) that aims to guarantee the ‘right to work’ is fulfilling its unique objectives for the rural poor especially the landless labourers and small and marginal farmers;(b) 	if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise;(c) 	the relevant details of rural and urban unemployment rates in Kerala, total number ofbeneficiaries, districts covered under MGNREGS, socioeconomic status scale over different districts, heterogeneous works and work age group and the male and female worker distribution;(d) 	whether the significant steps being taken by the Government to address the anomalies on the implementation side, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and(e) 	the noteworthy role of community-based organizations and women self-help groups under MGNREGS in Kerala?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a) & (b)  The mandate of Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (Mahatma Gandhi NREGA), 2005 is to provide at least 100 days of wage employment in a financial year to every rural household whose adult member volunteers to do unskilled manual work. State can provide additional mandays of work over and above of 100 days from its own resources. An additional 50 days of wage employment is provided over and above 100 days in the notified drought affected areas or natural calamity areas in the country on recommendation of the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare. States/UTs-wise number of households provided employment under Mahatma Gandhi NREGA in financial year 2020-21 and 2021-22 (as on 01.08.2021) is given at Annexure-I.(c)  As per information shared by State of Kerala, Mahatma Gandhi NREGA is implemented in all 14 districts of Kerala. A total of 39.69 lakh job cards are issued to the households under the Scheme in the State of Kerala, out of which 20.22 lakh job cards are active. In the current FY 2021-22 (as on 01.08.2021), 8.3 lakh households have been provided employment and 108.91 lakh person-days have been generated under the Scheme in the State of Kerala. District-wise details of age-wise registered persons and employed persons in current FY 2021-22 (as on 30.07.2021) under Mahatma Gandhi NREGS in the State of Kerala are given at Annexure II.District-wise number of registered workers, number of active job cards, number of active male workers and number of active female workers (as on 30.07.2021) in the State of Kerala under the Scheme are given at Annexure III.As per information shared by National Sample Survey (NSS), National Statistics Office (NSO), Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI), the Annual Report on Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) (July 2019 – June 2020), was published by the Ministry. As per PLFS report, in the State of Kerala, the estimate of Unemployment Rate (UR) (in per cent) according to usual status (primary status + secondary status) in rural area is 9.7% and in urban area is 10.4%.Also, from the PLFS conducted during July 2019-June 2020, Worker Population Ratio (WPR) in usual status (ps+ss) (in per cent) in different age group separately for male and female in Kerala are given below:Worker Population Ratio (WPR) (in per cent) according to usual status (ps+ss) for persons of different age group in Kerala during 2019-20.Gender	     15-29 years	15-59 years	15 years and above	all ages (0+)Male	       39.9	           72.9	             66.5	          52.2Female	       11.9	           30.5	             27.1	          22.4Person	       25.7	           50.0	             45.3	          36.5Source: Annual Report, PLFS, 2019-20(d) The responsibility of implementation of Mahatma Gandhi NREGA is vested with the Government of States /UTs (including State of Kerala). The Ministry has a comprehensive system of monitoring and review mechanism for Mahatma Gandhi NREGS. Some of the important elements of the above framework are listed below: (i)  The Ministry regularly reviews the performance of the implementation of Mahatma Gandhi NREGS in States/UTs through various fora viz., Mid-Term Review, Labour Budget meetings, Labour Budget Revision meetings, programme review meetings. Central Employment Guarantee Council and State Employment Guarantee Councils periodically monitor implementation of the programme.(ii) National Level Monitors, Common Review Missions and Officers of the Ministry visit States/UTs at regular intervals to review implementation of the programme. After the field visits, the findings/shortcomings and recommendations are shared with the States/UTs for appropriate action at their end. (iii) Auditing Standards for Social Audit have been issued and States/UTs have been advised to establish independent Social Audit Units, conduct Social Audit as per Audit of Scheme Rules, 2011 and training of Village resource persons for conducting Social Audit etc. Internal Audit Teams of the Department also conduct regular audit. (iv) All grievances/ complaints received in the Ministry are forwarded to the concerned States/UTs for taking appropriate action including investigation, as per law. (v) Steps have been taken to strengthen transparency and accountability which include geo-tagging of assets, Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT), National electronic Fund Management System (NeFMS), Aadhar Based Payment System (ABPS), Software for Estimate Calculation using rural rates for Employment (SECURE) and appointment of Ombudsperson in every district of States/UTs. (e)   As per the information shared by the State of Kerala, the State Government is utilizing the potential of community-based organisations and women self-help groups in the implementation of Mahatma Gandhi NREGS.  Kudumbashree is a Community Based Organisation (CBO) of poor families, represented by a woman from the family.  It is built as a three-tier structure viz.; Neighbour Hood Group (NHG) –Area Development Society (ADS) –Community Development Society (CDS) levels.The main role of women self help groups (Kudumbashree) in the field of Mahatma Gandhi NREGS activities are given below:1.
