                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4002






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.12.2019






Allocation of Funds for Research and Development




Meenakashi Lekhi














                        Will the Minister of





NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) The funds allocated for the research and development for renewable energy projects since 2016; and(b) The steps taken or proposed to be taken by the Government to promote renewable energy in Delhi?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (I/C) FOR NEW & RENEWABLE ENERGY, POWER and MoS for  SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP(SHRI R.K. SINGH)(a) Sir, the funds allocated for the research and development for renewable energy projects since 2016 are given as below: -Financial year 	Budget Estimate (BE)/Revised Estimate (RE) (in crores)2016-17	90.00/60.002017-18	144.00/81.002018-19	94.00/43.002019-20	60.00(b) The steps taken by the Government to promote renewable energy in the country including the state of Delhi inter alia, include the following: -•	Announcement of a target of installing 175 GW of renewable energy capacity by the year, 2022;•	Permitting Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) up to 100 percent under the automatic route. •	Waiver of Inter State Transmission System (ISTS) charges and losses for inter-state sale of solar and wind power for projects to be commissioned up to December, 2022.•	Notification of standard bidding guidelines to enable distribution licensee to procure solar and wind power at competitive rates in cost effective manner.•	Declaration of trajectory for Renewable Purchase Obligation (RPO) up to the year 2022.•	Implementation of Green Energy Corridor project to facilitate grid integration of large-scale renewable energy capacity addition.  •	Launching of New Schemes, such as, PM-KUSUM, Solar Rooftop Phase II, 12000 MW CPSU scheme Phase II, etc.*****
