                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

170






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2021






Retention Rate in Schools




Feroze Varun Gandhi














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the general retention rate of students in Government schools, at the primary,elementary, secondary, and higher secondary levels, State-wise;(b) whether the Government maintains information and data on the rate of drop-outsamong children belonging to weaker sections and disadvantaged groups admitted underSection 12(1) (c) of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act 2009;(c) if not, whether the Government is planning to do so;(d) if so, the retention rate of children with disabilities, at the primary, elementary,secondary, and higher secondary levels, State-wise; and(e) the retention rate of children belonging to Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes, atthe primary, elementary, secondary, and higher secondary levels, State-wise?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a): The Department of School Education and Literacy (DoSEL), Ministry of Education hasdeveloped the Unified District Information System for Education Plus (UDISE+) system torecord data on indicators of school education provided by all the States and UnionTerritories. As per Unified District Information System for Education Plus (UDISE+) 2019-20 the retention rate for 2019-20 of primary, elementary, secondary and higher secondarylevels, State–wise is at Annexure-I.(b) and (c): As per Unified District Information System for Education Plus (UDISE+) 2019-20, drop out rate for primary and upper primary levels for children belonging to differentsocial groups is at annexure-II.(d) and (e): The Retention rate of students is calculated gender-wise for the different levelsof school education. Improving retention rate of students in school is a continuous process.Government of India launched Samagra Shiksha - an Integrated Scheme for schooleducation, w.e.f. 2018-19, as an overarching programme for the school education sectorextending from pre-school to class XII and aims to ensure inclusive and equitable qualityeducation at all levels of school education.Further, in order to improve the education standard, the Government has taken severalsteps:-1. Central RTE Rules have been amended on 20th February, 2017 to includereference on class-wise, subject-wise Learning Outcomes at Elementarylevel.2. National Achievement Survey (NAS) is conducted to enable States/UTs toidentify gaps in learning outcomes.3. A 70 indicator based matrix Performance Grading Index (PGI) has beendeveloped to grade the States/UTs.4. NISHTHA (National Initiative for School Heads and Teachers HolisticAdvancement), an Integrated Teacher Training Programme has beenintroduced.******
