                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

306






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2020






Assistance for Agricultural Practices




Dushyant Singh














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the budget allocated for providing technical and financial assistances for polyhouse farming, bee-keeping and organic farming; (b)	 the number of farmers benefited and the details of the production of fruits, vegetables, root and tuber crops, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew, cocoa and bamboo with the assistance under MIDH Scheme, State-wise in the last five years; (c) 	whether there is a growing demand for organic fruits, spices, plants in foreign countries, if so, the details of exports, category-wise in the last five years; (d)	 whether the Government plans to increase assistance for horticulture considering the trend in global demand for Indian organic produce and if so, the plan thereof and the manner in which the said assistance would be increased; and (e)	 whether the Government has organized any international level workshop on horticulture, if so, the details thereof and the developments/benefits from the workshop and if not, the reasons therefor; and (f) 	the steps taken to increase exports?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER  OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):	 Government of India is providing technical and financial assistance for polyhouse farming, bee-keeping and organic farming through various programs. These programs include Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH), National Beekeeping & Honey Mission (NBHM), Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) & Mission Organic Value Chain Development for North Eastern Region (MOVCDNER). During 2019-20, an amount of Rs. 1551.55 crore (Revised Estimates) has been allocated for carrying out activities related to holistic development of horticulture (including polyhouse farming, bee-keeping and organic farming) under MIDH scheme.               During 2019-20, an amount of Rs. 25.00 crore has been allocated to National Beekeeping & Honey Mission (NBHM) for overall promotion and development of bee keeping, an amount of Rs. 563.43 crore has been allocated under PKVY & Rs. 160.00 crore under MOVCDNER for promotion of Organic farming.Contd…2/--2-(b):	 Under MIDH, the Central Sponsored Schemes like National Horticulture Mission (NHM) and Horticulture Mission for North East and Himalayan Region (HMNEH) are being implemented at State Level. Therefore, beneficiary level data is maintained at the State Level. However, details of the State-wise farmers benefitted during the last five years under the Central Sector Scheme like National Horticulture Board (NHB) and Coconut Development Board (CDB) is enclosed at Annexure I.           No data for production of horticultural crops exclusively with MIDH assistance is maintained by the Department. However, State wise details of total production of fruits, vegetables, mushrooms, spices, flowers, aromatic plants, coconut, cashew and cocoa during the last five years is at Annexure-II.(c):	 Yes. The category wise data of certified organic products during past five years is enclosed at Annexure-III.(d):	 At present there is no such proposal for increasing the assistance under MIDH scheme.(e):	 Under MIDH, no international level workshop on horticulture has been organized for the year 2019-20. However, the Coconut Development Board, Government of India and Government of Kerala jointly organized a two-day International Conference and Exposition on Coconut Development on November 2 and 3, 2019 at Kozhikode, Kerala, India. The objective of the conference was to promote industry led growth of coconut sector in the country.(f):	 In order to increase the exports, at present there is no quantitative restriction on export (except packaging restriction of mustard oil) of principal agriculture and horticulture commodities. Further, 3-5% reward is available under ‘Merchandise Exports from India Scheme (MEIS) scheme of Foreign Trade Policy (FTP 2015-20) of Department of Commerce on export of most of the agricultural produce to compensate on the cost incurred and thereby to promote their exports.
