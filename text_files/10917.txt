                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4284






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2019






Cases Registered under Prevention of Food Adulteration Act




Pashupati Nath Singh













                                                    Annpurna Devi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE  be pleased to state:(a) whether the provisions of the Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 are not being complied with strictly which has led to the rise in such crimes in violation of the said Act;(b) the reaction of the Government thereto;(c) the number of cases registered under the said Act during the last three years, State/UT-wise; and(d) the number and details of persons punished under the provisions of the said Act during the said period, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to (d):	 The Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 has been repealed with effect from 5th  August, 2011. Cases of food adulteration are now being regulated under Food Safety and Standards (FSS) Act, 2006 by Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI). Under the FSS Act, 2006, States/UTs are primarily responsible for enforcement of the FSS Act and Rules and Regulations made thereunder. Regular surveillance, sampling and inspection is carried out by the food safety officials of States/UTs and wherever any non-conformance is observed, appropriate  penal action is taken.  As per reports/data received from States/UTs, the relevant details of food samples analysed, found non-conforming and action taken against defaulting Food Business Operators for years 2016-17, 2017-18 and 2018-19 are at Annexure I, II and III respectively.
