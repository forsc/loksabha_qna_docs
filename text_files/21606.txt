                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2851






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2021






Spinal Muscular Atrophy




Vishnu Dutt Sharma














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether it is a fact that India has three lakh Pediatric patients suffering from Spinal Muscular Atrophy, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the proposal to waive off custom duty on costly medicinal injections for the treatment of children with Spinal Muscular Atrophy has been implemented;(c) if so, the details thereof and the extent to which it is likely to benefit the children suffering from the  above disease;(d) whether his Ministry has any scheme to provide assistance for such patients in view of higher treatment costs; and(e) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        (a): Data pertaining to the epidemiology of spinal muscular atrophy is not available as of now. However, the Indian Council of Medical Research has started a rare disease registry, (ICMR rare diseases registry, available at http;//bmi/icmr.org.in/irdr/index.php). Accordingly, the epidemiological data pertaining to spinal muscular atrophy may be available in future    (b): No.(c): Not applicable (d) & (e): Treatment modalities like assistive devices and physiotherapy and available at most tertiary care institutions in the country. Specific treatments like gene therapy and antisense oligonucleotides are quite expensive.However, no separate rehabilitation mechanisms are available for spinal muscular atrophy patients. The rehabilitation facilities available at tertiary care institutions are being utilized for these patients also.
