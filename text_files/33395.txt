                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

864






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2022






Beedi Workers




Thirumaa Valavan Thol














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)whether the Government has conducted any State-wise study about the economic status and the job related health hazards of the Beedi-rollers in India, if so, the details thereof;(b)the details of Labour protection measures taken by the Government so far for the Beedi workers, especially women and minors involved in this industry;(c)whether the welfare schemes available in the country for Beedi workers and the details of State-wise beneficiaries of these schemes; and(d)whether the Government has any plan to provide alternate job to rescue the Beedi workers from the job related health hazards?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a) to (c):	As per the data available, there are 49,82,294 registered Beedi workers in the country. Region-wise details are attached at Annexure. There exists a Labour Welfare Scheme (LWS) for the Beedi Workers. Components of LWS are as under:- (i) Health care facilities are provided through 10 (Ten) Hospitals and 285 dispensaries across the country and reimbursement of expenditure for specialized treatments (i.e. Cancer, Tuberculosis, Heart Diseases, Kidney Transplantation). Contd..2/-:: 2 ::(ii) Financial Assistance for education to the children of beedi workers from class-I to College/University ranging from Rs.250/- to Rs.15,000/- per student per annum, depending upon class/course.  (iii) Subsidy of Rs.1,50,000/- (per beneficiary) in three (03) instalments at 25:60:15 ratio i.e. Rs.37,500, Rs.90,000 and Rs.22,500 respectively for construction of pucca houses, under Revised Integrated Housing Scheme (RIHS) 2016. However, RIHS has now been converged with Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY).The benefits of Labour Welfare Schemes are provided to the beedi workers, which is demand driven. Hence, the benefits under the above mentioned schemes have been imparted to all the Beedi Workers beneficiaries as and when required by them. (d):		The Ministry of Labour and Employment in collaboration with National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) and Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship have been providing skill Development training to the Beedi Workers and their dependents to engage them in alternative jobs.*                                                   *******
