                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2917






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






Diseases Caused by Pollution of Chambal River




Anil Firojiya














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether Toxic water containing hazardous chemical is getting discharged from the industries set up in Nagda city of Ujjain and is causing various fatal diseases like cancer, paralysis in 22 villages located near Chambal river; (b) whether the Government has given any directions to these companies so that the people don’t fall victim to the diseases caused by toxic water containing chemical; (c) if so, the details in this regard; (d) whether the Government proposes to direct the industries located in Nagda city to formulate a concrete action plan in time bound manner to check flowing of toxic water into Chambal river; and (e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) No Sir. As informed by Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB), Delhi and Madhya Pradesh Pollution Control Board, Bhopal, such study has not been conducted.(b) to (e)  Madhya Pradesh Pollution Control Board has directed all the industry to maintain zero liquid discharge out of premises in Nagda except M/s. Grasim Industries (S.F.D.) which is discharging about 11 MLD treated effluent in Chambal river. M/s. Grasim Industries (S.F.D.) has submitted time bound action plan along with Bank Guarantee. As per proposed action plan the zero discharge condition will be achieved by the industries on or before January, 2021.*****
