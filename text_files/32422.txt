                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3854






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.12.2021






Prime Minister Address to the Nation




Ramesh Chander Kaushik














                        Will the Minister of





INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the various languages in which the Prime Minister’s speeches/messages to the Nation are broadcast; (b)	the steps taken by the Government to ensure that live telecast of the Prime Minister’s address to the Nation is also comprehensible to the non-Hindi speaking population; and (c)	whether the Government proposes to carry out direct translation of the telecast of Prime Minister’s messages in the languages mentioned in the Eighth Schedule of the Constitution and make them available to the regional channels?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF INFORMATION AND BROADCASTING; AND MINISTER OF YOUTH AFFAIRS & SPORTS(SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR)(a) to (c): Prasar Bharati live broadcasts the speeches of the Hon’ble Prime Minister in the language the speeches are delivered in, as-is.  Speeches of national importance are also recorded and re-broadcast with translated voice-overlay in local languages by various units of Prasar Bharati across India for radio and television from time to time. Apart from conventional mode of availability of the programmes, translated speech recordings in the various languages are also made available digitally for on-demand listening/viewing through platforms such as YouTube as well as through the Prasar Bharati’s App “NewsOnAir” available on iOS and Android and website NewsOnAir.com.
