                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2208






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2019






CICT




Balashowry Vallabbhaneni













                                                    Doddaalahalli Kempegowda Suresh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the Tamil language has separate Central Institute of Classical Tamil; (b)	if so, what are the reasons that other Classical languages of Telugu, Malayalam and Kannada does not have Central classical institute for each of their respective States; (c)	the efforts the Government is taking to set up separate classical institute for above three classical languages; (d)	the details of the allocations made, sanctioned, released and spent on Classical Telugu language since 2015-16; and (e)	the reasons for not spending even 25 per cent of money allocated to Classical Telugu since 2015-16? 






ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a)  :      Yes Sir.  Central Institute of Classical Tamil was established at Chennai which works for the development and promotion of Classical Tamil. (b) & (c) :   Centre of Excellence for Studies in Classical languages are functioning from their respective States. Centre of Excellence for Studies in Classical Kannada (CESCK) is functioning from Mysore since 2015. Similarly, the Centre of Excellence for Studies in Classical Telugu initially started functioning in CIIL, Mysore from 2018 and has now been shifted to Nellore, Andhra Pradesh from November, 2019. It has been decided to set up a Centre of Excellence for Studies in Classical Malayalam in Thunchath Ezhuthachan Malayalam University, Tirur, Kerala. (d) & (e) :      The details of funds provided for Classical Telugu through CIIL are given below:- <pre>                                             (Rupees in lakh)Year	Funds allocated/Sanctioned/ released	Funds utilised2015-16	100.00	192016-17	100.00	4.72017-18	100.00	02018-19	99.00	292019-20	107.00 	34.75</pre>The Centre of Excellence for Studies in Classical Telugu started functioning from CIIL, Mysore from March 2018. Thereafter, the post of Project Director was filled up in November, 2018. In the financial year 2018-19, there is an expenditure of Rs. 29.19 % of the total funds allocated and for 2019-20 expenditure so far has been 32.48 %.*****
