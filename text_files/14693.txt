                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3378






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.03.2020






Migratory Birds




Dulal Chandra Goswami














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is aware that more than 500 species of migratory birds come to India but their number has declined due to effects of climate change and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether the migratory birds are heading towards other colder countries in the absence of favourable weather in India and if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Government is formulating any plan for conservation of such birds and if so, the details thereof; (d) 	whether there has been a sharp decline in the number of migratory birds in Gogabeel lake of Bihar and other birds sanctuaries in Delhi NCR, Uttar Pradesh, Haryana and Rajasthan due to change in habitat of birds owing to developmental activities near bird sanctuaries; and (e)	if so, the details thereof and the corrective measures taken by the Government in this regard? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	Government is aware that about 460 species of migratory birds come to India, however, there is no report of decline of bird species.(b)	 There is no evidence that birds are heading towards colder countries in absence of favourable weather in India.(c)	 A Central Asian  Flyway National Action Plan (2018-2023)  was launched in 2018 with a goal of  conserving migratory species and the habitats (d) to (e) The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change has not undertaken any assessment in the fluctuation in number of migratory birds over different time periods.***
