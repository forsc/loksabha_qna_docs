                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1227






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2021






Special Campaign for Registration of Antiquities




Hibi Eden














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) has been asked to probe the validity of the antiques found at the residence of fake antiquities dealer Monson Mavunkal an accused in the antique fraud case and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the ASI has found that the copper plate manuscript on Sabarimala recovered from the residence is fake and  if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the ASI has conducted any special campaign for registration of antiquities in the possession of private entities in the country and if so, the details including the outcome of the campaign; and (d) whether a campaign is also being carried out to ensure that the antiquarian treasures are protected in the form of antiquity and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF TOURISM, CULTURE AND DEVELOPMENT OF NORTH EASTERN REGION(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) & (b) Yes Sir, the Archaeological Survey of India has been asked by the Crime Branch Central Unit-II, Ernakulam to examine the objects which were in the possession of Monson Mavunkal, the process for which has been initiated.(c) & (d) The Archaeological Survey of India had conducted a special campaign for registration of antiquities from 13th to 28th September 2019. Since then, a total of 4,509 antiquities have been registered till date. Registering Officers have been nominated at each Circle offices of ASI to register the antiquities. Registration of antiquities by ASI is a regular process.
