                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

981






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2021






AYUSH Centres/Hospitals




Manne Srinivas Reddy













                                                    Sanjay Jaiswal
                                                








                                                    S.R. Parthiban
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of AYUSH centres /hospitals that arefunctional at present in the country, State-wise;(b)	whether the Government proposes to set upmore AYUSH centres hospitals in the country;(c)	if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise especiallyin Tamil Nadu;(d)	the number of patients treated/recovered at theAYUSH hospitals, annually during the last five years, State-wise;(e)	the steps taken by the Government to promoteAYUSH at international level; and(f)	the funds granted/utilized during last five yearstill date, year-wise and State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AYUSH(SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a):The total number of Ayush hospitals and dispensaries in the country State/UT-wise as per the Annual Statistical Publication “AYUSH IN INDIA-2019” of the Ministry of Ayushis furnished at Annexure-I.(b) & (c):  Public Health being a State subject, setting up of Ayush Centre Hospitals is under the purview of respective State/UT Governments including Tamil Nadu. However, under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of the National Ayush Mission (NAM), there is provision of financial assistance for setting up of upto 10/30/50 bedded integrated Ayush Hospitals. The State/UT Governments may avail financial assistance for setting up Ayush Hospitals by submitting suitable proposal through State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) as per the provision of NAM guidelines. Further, under NAM, total 104 units of upto 50 bedded integrated Ayush Hospitals has been approved in different States / UTs including Tamil Nadu as per the proposals received from them in SAAPs. State /UT-wise status of approved Ayush Hospitals including Tamil Nadu is furnished as Annexure-II.(d):No such data is maintained as health is a state subject, however, the number of patients treated at the Government Ayush hospitals (including CHCs), annually during the last five years, State-wise as per data received from the States/UTs and compiled in the  Annual Statistical Publication “AYUSH IN INDIA” for the years 2019, 2018, 2017, 2016 and 2015of the Ministry of Ayush, is furnished at Annexure-III.(e): Ministry of Ayush has implemented Central Sector Scheme for Promotion of International Co-operation in Ayush with the mandate to promote and strengthen awareness about Ayush Systems of Medicine; to facilitate international promotion, development and recognition of Ayush Systems; to foster interaction of stakeholders and market development of Ayush at international level; to support international exchange of experts and information; to give boost to Ayush products in International market and to establish Ayush Academic Chairs in foreign countries.Ministry of Ayush deputes Ayush experts to foreign countries to participate in international meetings, conferences, training programmes, seminars and on special assignment of the Government of India for promotion and propagation of Ayush Systems including Ayurveda.  Financial assistance is also provided for presentation of Ayush related scientific research papers in international conferences, workshops, seminar etc.            Ministry of Ayush has set up 36 Ayush Information Cell in 33 countries to disseminate authentic information about Ayushsystems of Medicine.            Ministry of Ayush has entered into Country to Country MoUs for Cooperation in field of Traditional Medicine and Homoeopathy with 25 countries namely Nepal, Bangladesh, Hungary, Trinidad & Tobago, Malaysia, WHO Geneva, Mauritius, Mongolia, Turkmenistan, Myanmar, Germany (Joint declaration), Iran, Sao Tome & Príncipe, Equatorial Guinea, Cuba, Colombia, Japan (MoC), Bolivia, Gambia, Republic of Guinea, China, St. Vincent & The Grenadines, Suriname, Brazil and Zimbabwe. 23 MoUs for undertaking collaborative research in Ayush systems of medicine and 14 MoUs for setting up AyushAcademic Chairs have been signed with foreign institutes/ universities.             To boost export of Ayush products, incentive are provided to Ayush drug manufacturers for Market Authorization/ registration of Ayush products with USFDA/ EMEA/ UK-MHRA/ NHPD (Canada)/ TGA and other international regulatory agencies, etc. abroad for the purpose of export. So far more than 50 products (Unani and Ayurveda) have been registered in 08 countries namely Kenya, USA, Russia, Latvia, Canada, Oman, Tajikistan and Sri Lanka.Ministry of Ayush under its Fellowship Scheme extends financial support to the eligible foreign nationals for undertaking Undergraduate (UG), Postgraduate (PG) and Ph.D. courses in premier Institutes in India. 104 scholarships are offered every year to the eligible foreign nationals from 101 countries.A Panchakarma Centre has been established by the Ministry of Public Health, Cuba with the technical support of Ministry of Ayush in 2019.The Ministry of Ayush deputed two experts (Ayurveda and Siddha) to Ministry of Health, Malaysia underIndian Technical and Economic Cooperation(ITEC) Programme of MEA for providing their services in the Traditional and Complementary Medicine Unit at Government Hospitals, Malaysia. The Government of Malaysia had started dedicated wings for Shirodhara and external Basti therapy and Varman therapy in Malaysian Hospitals.Every year, Ministry of Ayush has been providing technical support to Indian Missions abroad for celebration of 21st June as International day of Yoga across the globe.(f): The funds granted/utilized under National Ayush Mission (NAM) during last five years till date, year-wise& state-wise is furnished at Annexure – IV.*****
