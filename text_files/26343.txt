                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2077






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.08.2021






Development of Public Enterprises in West Bengal




Sunil Kumar Mondal














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 the details of various schemes launched for the development of public enterprises in West Bengal and in various other States of the country during the last six years; (b)	 whether any new scheme is to be introduced in near future;(c)	 if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise and if not, the reasons therefor; and(d)	 the details of funds sanctioned for the development of the public enterprise in the budgetary allocation 2020 and percentage of funds released/ expended to West Bengal during Covid-19 pandemic and till date?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FINANCE(DR. BHAGWAT KISHANRAO KARAD)(a) to (d): Department of Public Enterprises is implementing the following schemes:i)	Research, Development and Consultancy (RDC) scheme which seeks to enhance capacity of officials of CPSEs through training, etc. andii)	Counselling, Retraining & Re-deployment (CRR) Scheme to enable Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS)/ Voluntary Separation Scheme (VSS) optees or their dependents to engage themselves in alternate vocations of self-employment/wage employment through re-skilling.No state-wise allocation is made under these schemes. During the year 2020-21 a sum of Rs 3.70 Crore was spent under the schemes. No new scheme are proposed to be introduced.*****
