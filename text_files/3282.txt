                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2994






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.07.2019






Construction of Four-lane Highways




A.K.P. Chinraj













                                                    T. R. Paarivendhar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether majority of the existing National Highways are two-lane and the same are 	being expanded to four-lane and if so, the details thereof;(b)	whether the Government has any proposal to construct four-lane roads in Perambalur, 	Thuraiyur, Musiri Kulithalai, Thokaimalai and Manapparai in Tamil Nadu; (c) 	if so, the details and the present status thereof; (d)	whether the Government has received any proposal to convert Thiruchengode to Sangagiri State Highway into National Highways and construct four-lane road on the 	same highway; and (e) 	if so, the details and the present status thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a)	At present, the total length of the National Highways in the country is about 1,32,500 km.  The lane- wise distribution is as under:Single Lane / Intermediate Lane	2-Lane	4-Lane	6/8 Lane36,310	65,123	28,412	2,655	The 4-laning works on NHs are taken up depending upon inter-se priority, traffic 	density 	and availability of funds.(b) & (c) The details of National Highway Projects envisaged for construction in Perambalur,     Thuraiyur and Musiri Kulithalai in Tamil Nadu are mentioned below:Sr. no.	Project stretch 	Approx. Length (km)	Present StatusI	4-laning of Thanjavur -Thiruvaiyaru - Perambalur - Krishnapuram - Athoor section of NH-136	126.00	The work for engagement of consultant for preparation of detailed project report initiated.II	4-laning of Musri - Namakkal section of NH-381B	43.70	The work for detailed project report initiated.III	4-laning of Trichy - Karur section of NH-83 (Old NH-67)	70.15	The work for detailed project report initiated.	Total	239.85	Manapparai is connected through Trichy-Dindigul section of NH-45 (new NH-83), which is already 4-laned. The Thokaimalai-Manapparai road is not a National Highway.(d) & (e)	The Ministry of Road Transport and Highways receives proposals for declaration of various State Roads/Highways as new National Highways (NHs) from various States/Union Territories (UTs) including the State Government of Tamil Nadu. The Ministry declares State Roads/ Highways as new NHs from time to time keeping in view the requirement of connectivity, inter-se priority and availability of funds. About 1,843 km of State Roads have been declared as National Highways during April 2014 to March 2019 in the state of Tamil Nadu. *****
