                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

262






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.03.2022






Establishment of Fisheries Centres




Saikia Dilip













                                                    Ramesh Chander Kaushik
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING ??????????, ??????? ?? ???????????  be pleased to state:(a)	 the total funds sanctioned and utilized under “Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries” during the current year, State-wise particularly in Haryana and North-Eastern States including Assam; and(b) 	the details of fisheries centres established under this scheme in Assam and Haryana including Mangaldoi and Sonipat Parliamentary Constituencies during the last two years and the current year? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a)	 and (b): A Statement is placed on the Table of the House.Statement referred to in reply to the Lok Sabha Starred Question No *262 put in by Shri Dilip Saikia and Shri Ramesh Chander Kaushik Members of Parliament for answer on 22nd March, 2022 regarding Establishment of Fisheries Centers.The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying implemented a Centrally Sponsored Scheme (CSS): Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of Fisheries for a period of five years from 2015-16 to 2019-20 and the CSS has ended on 31st March, 2020. Under the said CSS, central financial assistance of Rs.2577.49 crore was released to the State Governments/Union Territories including Haryana and North-Eastern States and other implementing agencies for development of fisheries and aquaculture. Further, the Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is now implementing a flagship scheme ‘Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY)’ with highest ever investment of Rs.20,050 crore with effect from the year 2020-21 in all the States/UTs including in Haryana, Assam and other North Eastern States. State-wise details of fund released under PMMSY during current financial year i.e., 2021-22 under PMMSY is at Annexure-I and States namely Chhattisgarh, Karnataka, Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh have reported the partial utilization of central funds against the release of funds during the current financial year. During the last two years 2019-20 to 2020-21and the current financial year, 2021-22, the Department of Fisheries has accorded approval to the fisheries development proposals of Government of Haryana at a total project cost of Rs. 159.99 crore and a sum of Rs.32.70 crore towards central share has been released. Details of central fund released and utilization reported by Haryana and North Eastern States during last two years and current year are furnished at Annexure-II. The approved activities include establishment of fish seed hatchery, creation of infrastructure for fish culture such as fish ponds, Bio-floc, Re-circulatory Aquaculture System, installation of cages in reservoir, establishment of fish marketing infrastructure and fish feed mills. Further, a sum of Rs.210.60 lakh as per the report received from Government of Haryana has been allotted to Sonipat District for construction of new ponds, development of waterlogged area, construction of saline and alkaline pond, Re-circulatory Aquaculture System and Bio-floc system. A regional centre of Central Institute of Fisheries and Education (ICAR-CIFE) has been established in Rohtak, Haryana which is adjacent district of Sonipat to facilitate the development of fisheries and aquaculture in the State.In case of Assam, the project proposals at a total outlay of Rs.204.60 crore involving central share of Rs.98.57 crores have been approved for fisheries development during the last two financial years from 2019-20 to 2020-21 and current financial year, 2021-22 and a sum of Rs.80.08 crore has been released. The approved activities include establishment of New Freshwater Finfish Hatcheries, Rearing ponds, grow out ponds, Stocking of Fingerlings in Wet Lands, ornamental fish, RAS, Biofloc culture system, Fish Feed mill, Fish marketing and Post harvest infrastructure projects Viz. Ice plants, Motor cycle/three-wheeler with Ice Box, fish kiosks, Matysa Seva Kendra. Further, Government of Assam has reported that a sum of  Rs.293.142 lakh has been sanctioned in Darang district including Mangaldoi for fish hatchery, new rearing pond, new pond construction, inputs for fish farming, inputs for integrated farming, fingerling stocking in Wetlands, large RAS, Cage culture, Pen culture, Motor cycle with ice box, cycle with ice box, Three wheeler with ice box, backyard ornamental unit, recreational fisheries, Medium biofloc, small biofloc, Mini feed mill, fish kiosk and livelihood support to fishers. A regional centre of National Fisheries Development Board (NFDB) in Guwahati, Assam has been established to facilitate development of fisheries and aquaculture in Assam and other North Eastern States. *****
