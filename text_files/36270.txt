                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    STEEL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3304






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2022






Activities under CSR




Doddaalahalli Kempegowda Suresh














                        Will the Minister of





STEEL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is aware that the Steel Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs) are taking up various activities under Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) ;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	the details of the total expenditure made under the CSR by Steel PSUs during the last three years;(d)	the details of the year-wise expenditure against respective PSUs; and(e)	whether the Government has any mechanism to ensure CSR fund should be spent as per the norms and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STEEL		   (SHRI RAM CHANDRA PRASAD SINGH)(a)to(e): The Government provides the broad framework for Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) through Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013 (‘Act’), Schedule VII of the Act and Companies (CSR Policy) Rules, 2014. Under the Act, CSR is a Board driven process and the Board of the company is empowered to plan, decide, execute and monitor CSR activities based on the recommendations of its CSR Committee. The CSR framework is disclosure based and CSR mandated companies are required to file details of CSR activities annually in the MCA21 registry.  For any violation of CSR provisions, action can be initiated by the Government against such non-compliant companies as per provisions of the Companies Act, 2013 after due examination of records and following due process of law. Department of Public Enterprises has also issued guidelines dated 10.12.2018 on CSR expenditure by Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs) to all administrative Ministries and CPSEs, inter-alia, stipulating that all the CSR activities are to be undertaken as per provisions of Section 135 and Schedule VII of Companies Act, 2013.The details of the total expenditure made under the CSR during last three years, year-wise, by Steel CPSEs are given at Annexure I.********
