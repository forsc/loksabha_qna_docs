                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2928






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.07.2019






FDI in Multi Brand Retail




Bidyut Baran Mahato













                                                    Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik
                                                








                                                    Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of norms for permitting multi brand retail trading in the country; (b) 	the number of foreign companies which have applied/registered to open multi brand stores in the country at present, State/ UT and country-wise; (c) 	whether the Government is considering any increase in the Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in multi brand retail sector in the country and if so, the details thereof; (d) 	whether the Government has made any study on the impact of FDI in retail market and on the employees attached thereto; and (e)	 if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor? 






ANSWER




???????? ??? ?????? ?????? (???? ????? ????) THE MINISTER OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI  PIYUSH GOYAL)(a):	Under the Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Policy, the details of norms for undertaking multi-brand retail trading in the country is enclosed as Annexure I.(b):    	India has received FDI in multi-brand retail from one foreign company of United Kingdom. State/UT-wise data of FDI inflow is not centrally maintained. (c ):	No such proposal is under consideration of the Government at present.(d)&(e): No Sir. The retail market sector depends on a number of factors, including FDI.  However, FDI is largely a matter of private business decisions. FDI  inflows depend on a host of factors such as availability of natural resource, market size, infrastructure, general investment climate as well as macro-economic stability and investment decision of foreign investors.******
