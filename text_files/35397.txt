                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2693






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Toilet Building in Rural Areas




Dipsinh Shankarsinh Rathod














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  whether the Government has come up with a scheme for toilet building in rural areas; and(b) if so, the details of the newly built toilets and the funds used thereon during the last five years?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF JAL SHAKTI(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) 	Government had launched Swachh Bharat Mission (Grameen) [SBM(G)] with effect from 2nd October, 2014 with the main aim to make the rural areas of the country Open Defecation Free (ODF) by 2nd October, 2019, by providing access to toilets to all the rural households.  Having achieved the outcomes of ODF, Phase-II of SBM(G) is now being implemented from 2020-21 to 2024-25 with the focus, inter alia, to sustain ODF status, to ensure that no one is left behind in having access to toilets and that any left out or new household is supported for construction of Individual Household Latrines (IHHLs).  (b)	As reported by the States/UTs on the online Integrated Management Information System (IMIS), 9.04 crore IHHLs have been constructed under SBM (G) during the last five years and funds amounting to Rs.87,596.31 crore have been spent for construction of IHHLs during these years. *****
