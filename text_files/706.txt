                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

567






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.06.2019






Fake Currency Cases




Sushil Kumar Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether cases of circulation of fake currency has been reported in the country during the last four years and the current year;(b) if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise;(c) the total number of persons against whom action has been taken and number of persons exonerated in each case during the said period;(d) whether the Government proposes for speedy trial of people involved in cases of circulation of fake currency and increase the current provision of punishment to the guilty persons; and(e) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a):-  Yes,  Sir.(b) & (c):-	As per data available with the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB), the State/UT wise details of Fake Indian Currency Notes seized, number of FIRs and number of accused during the last four years and the current year are given at Annexure A to E.-2-L.S.US.Q.NO.567 FOR 25.06.2019(d) & (e):-	Counterfeiting of currency notes or Bank Notes is an offence under Section 489 A to E of Indian Penal Code, 1860.  The damage to the monetary stability of India by way of production or smuggling or circulation of High Quality counterfeit Indian currency is a terrorist offence under Section 15 of the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967. The maximum punishment is life imprisonment in both the cases. High Quality Indian counterfeit currency cases investigated by National Investigation Agency are tried in Special NIA Courts, where hearings are held on a regular basis. ******
