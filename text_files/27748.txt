                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3226






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.08.2021






Works of PSUs under CSR




Annasaheb Shankar Jolle













                                                    Umesh G. Jadhav
                                                








                                                    Girish Bhalchandra Bapat
                                                








                                                    B. Y. Raghavendra
                                                








                                                    Pritam Gopinath Munde
                                                








                                                     Rahul Ramesh Shewale
                                                








                                                    Prathap Simha
                                                








                                                    Sanganna Amarappa Karadi
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the works undertaken by Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs) under Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) during the last three years and the current year in the country including Karnataka, PSU-wise; (b)	the amount of funds spent on different works during the said period and the number of persons benefitted therefrom, location and PSU-wise; (c)	whether the Government has received any Social Audit Reports conducted to track CSR funds by these PSUs; and if so, the details of the findings thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and(d)	whether the Government has conducted a study for assessing the impact of CSR projects undertaken by various PSUs and if so, the details thereof; (e)	whether there is a lack of initiatives and implementation gaps by CPSUs in the projects and if so, the reasons therefor; and(f)	whether the Government has implementation system for tracking the status of unspent CSR funds in a year and if so, the details thereof and the steps taken by the Government to ensure spending of CSR and ESR of PSUs? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(DR. BHAGWAT KISHANRAO KARAD)(a)	 & (b): As per information available with the Department of Public Enterprises with respect to 152 CPSEs during 2017-18, 150 CPSEs during 2018-19 and 148 CPSEs during 2019-20, an expenditure of Rs. 3442.42 crore, Rs 3873.32 crore and Rs. 5066.93 crore respectively was incurred for undertaking CSR activities/ projects in various states of the country including Karnataka. Year-wise and CPSE-wise details of funds spent on CSR activities during the last three years are given in Annexure-I, II and III respectively. CPSE/ location-wise information on number of persons benefitted is not maintained by Department of Public Enterprises (DPE).(c): No Sir, Department of Public Enterprises has not received any Social Audit reports to track CSR funds of CPSEs.-2-(d): No Sir, Department of Public Enterprises has not conducted any study for assessing the impact of CSR projects undertaken by various CPSEs.(e)& (f): CSR of corporates including CPSEs are governed as per the provisions of Section 135 of Companies Act, 2013, rules made there under and schedule VIII of the Act. As per the provisions of the Act, Board of the company is responsible to plan, approve, execute and monitor the CSR activities of company based on the recommendations of its CSR committee. The entire CSR architecture is disclosure based and CSR mandated companies are required to file details of CSR activities annually in MCA21 registry. Further, companies are required to disclose about its CSR spending in its Board report and shall specify the reasons if the company has not been able to spend the requisite amount within the financial year. The sub-sections (5) and (6) of Section 135 of the Act provides for the treatment of unspent CSR amount by the companies.*******
