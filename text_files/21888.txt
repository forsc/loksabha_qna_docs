                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

279






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Vocational Training Centres in Maharashtra




Prataprao  Jadhav














                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has implemented any scheme to set up vocational training centres to provide alternative employment to the unemployed persons in the country, particularly in the backward and rural regions of Maharashtra; (b) if so, the State-wise details thereof, particularly in the backward and rural regions of Maharashtra; (c) whether the Government proposes to open more such training centres in the country, particularly in the backward and rural regions of Maharashtra during the current year; and (d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) to (d):	A statement is laid on the Table of the House.  STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) to (d) of LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. †*279 TO BE ANSWERED ON 15.03.2021 RAISED BY SHRI PRATAPRAO JADHAV REGARDING ‘VOCATIONAL TRAINING CENTRES IN MAHARASHTRA’(a) to (d):	The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE) is implementing several schemes for imparting employable skills through short-duration and long-duration skill training. The details of vocational training centres in these schemes are given below:(1) MSDE is implementing the flagship scheme, Pradhan MantriKaushalVikasYojana(PMKVY), under which short-duration training is provided through three routes, namely Short-Term Training (STT) for freshskilling of school / college drop outs and unemployed youth; Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) to recognize the existingskills through orientation; and Special Projects to address skilling requirements of the vulnerable groups. Training under PMKVY is provided through empanelledTraining Providers / Centres. The State/ Union Territory (UT) - wise number of empanelledTraining Centres under PMKVY 2.0 (2016-2020), as of 19.01.2021, is given at Annexure-I.MSDE has also taken the initiative to create Pradhan MantriKaushalKendras(PMKKs) as visible and aspirational model training centres in every district of India. As of 19.01.2021, 738 PMKKs have been established. The State/ UT - wise number of PMKKs set up (as of 19.01.2021) are given at Annexure-II.(2) Under the scheme, Jan ShikshanSansthan(JSS), skill training is imparted to the socio-economically backward and educationally disadvantaged groups of urban / rural population such as neo-literates, semi-literates, Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, women and girls, slum dwellers, migrant workers, etc. The number of districts of sanctioned JSS, State/ UT – wise, is given at Annexure-III.(3) Under Craftsmen Training Scheme, long-duration skill training is imparted through Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) in 137 trades. The State/ UT – wise number of ITIs is given at Annexure-IV. In addition, the Government has approved the setting up of Indian Institute of Skills (IISs) in Public Private Partnership (PPP) mode in three locations in the country namely Mumbai, Kanpur and Ahmedabad. The first batch at IIS Mumbai has commenced training in December 2020.
