                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2386






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.07.2019






Collateral Free Loan to MSMEs




Sujay Radhakrishna Vikhepatil














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government proposes to introduce collateral-free loans upto Rs.50 lakhs for Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs); (b)	if so, the details thereof ;(c)	whether the Government provides subsidy on interest on the loan to MSMEs for modernisation of their machines ;(d)	if so, the details thereof; and(e)	whether the said subsidy is being provided to enterprises/establishments in all areas of the country or limited to the units related to area and products only and if so, the details thereof ?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(a) to (b) : As per the extant guidelines of Reserve Bank of India (RBI), Scheduled Commercial Banks (SCBs) have been mandated to extend collateral-free loans up to Rs.10 lakh to units in the Micro and Small Enterprise (MSE) sector.  In addition, Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) can avail guarantee under Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Micro and Small Enterprise (CGTMSE) scheme for loans up to Rs.200 lakh extended to them without furnishing any collateral security.(c)	to (e) : Government operates schemes like Credit Linked Capital Subsidy Scheme (CLCSS), Amended Technology Upgradation Fund Scheme (ATUFS), etc. across the country.  These provide capital subsidy on institutional finance availed for upgradation/modernisation of machinery by MSMEs for products/ sectors/segments as specified under the schemes.  On all working capital or term loan (fresh and incremental) to MSME borrowers for loans up to Rs.1 crore, Government has also implemented the ‘Interest Subvention Scheme for Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs), 2018’ which provides interest subvention calculated at 2% per annum.  This includes loans taken for technology upgradation.******
