                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

208






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.07.2019






Delay in Land Allotment to Premier Institutions




Geetha Viswanath Vanga














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether there are delays in land allotment which led to severe cost overrun in the construction of premier Institutions like Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), National Institutes of Technology (NITs), Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) for permanent campuses to house Centrally funded educational institutions, if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor;(b)	whether five IITs in Jammu, Dharwad, Tirupati, Bhilai and Palakkad are still in the process of constructing permanent campuses; and (c)	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (c): 	A statement is laid on the Table of the House.* * * * * * * * STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (A) TO (C) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 208 TO BE ANSWERED ON 08.07.2019 REGARDING DELAY IN LAND ALLOTMENT TO PREMIER INSTITUTIONS ASKED BY SHRIMATI VANGA GEETHA VISWANATH.(a):  Yes sir, there have been delays in land allotment by the State Governments concerned in respect of few Centrally funded Institutions, which is one of the reasons for cost overrun in the construction of these Institutes. The details are as per Annexure enclosed. (b) to (c):   Six new IITs have been established one each at Jammu (J&K), Bhilai (Chhattisgarh), Tirupati (Andhra Pradesh), Goa (Goa), Palakkad (Kerala) and Dharwad (Karnataka) in accordance with Budget Announcements 2014-15 and 2015-16. The Government approved construction of permanent campuses of these IITs at a total cost of Rs.7002.42 crores over a period of three years from 2017-18 to 2019-20.  The process for construction has started in all IITs except IIT Goa where the land is yet to be handed over.*****
