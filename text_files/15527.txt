                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4255






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






Infrastructure Development




Manoj Kishorbhai Kotak














                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has planned for infrastructure development in Kendriya Vidyalayas and Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas for Khelo India event and Fit India movement;  (b) if so, the details thereof;  (c) the number of sports events organized under Khelo India so far along with locations thereof and the number of participants; and  (d) the status of upcoming expansion programme, critical review and targets set under Khelo India and availability of the resources therein? 
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE)  FOR YOUTH AFFAIRS & SPORTS  { SHRI KIREN RIJIJU }  (a) and (b) The Kendriya Vidyalayas and Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas are under the aegis of the Ministry of Human Resource and Development and matters relating to planning and funding for their infrastructure development rests with that Ministry. No proposal for infrastructure development in Kendriya Vidyalayas and Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas is under consideration in this Ministry.  Fit India Movement is a people centric movement in which citizens, including school children, are encouraged to take part voluntarily and include any form of physical activities in their daily routine.  The Khelo India Scheme aims to infuse sports culture, achieve sporting excellence in the country and encourage sports all over the country.  (c) A total of four national level competitions have been organized under ‘Khelo India’ so far. The details are at Annexure.  (d) The Khelo India Scheme in its present form was started in October, 2017 for a period of three years. So far Rs. 1320.79 crore has been spent and Rs. 71.44 crore is available for the remaining period of the current financial year. It would be a continuing scheme and the Government is making adequate funds available for the scheme. An amount of Rs. 890.42 crore is being allotted for the financial year 2020-21 for the Scheme. ANNEXURE REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (c) OF LOK SABHA UNSTARRED QUESTION NO. 4255 TO BE ANSWERED ON 19.03.2020 REGARDING “INFRASTRUCTURE DEVELOPMENT” ASKED BY SHRI MANOJ KOTAK, HON’BLE MEMBER OF LOK SABHA  DETAILS OF NATIONAL COMPETITIONS ORGANIZED UNDER KHELO INDIA  <pre>S. No. Name of Competition Venue Period No. of Participants 1 Khelo India School Games 2018 Delhi  31.01.2018 to 08.02.2018 3,507 2 Khelo India Youth Games 2019 Pune  09.01.2019 to 20.01.2019 5,925 3 Khelo India Youth Games 2020 Guwahati  10.01.2020 to 22.01.2020 6,130 4 Khelo India University Games 2020 Bhubaneswar and Cuttack 22.02.2020 to 01.03.2020 3,182 </pre> ****
