                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1883






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.03.2020






Vegetables under Essential Commodities Act




K. Shanmuga Sundaram














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is having any proposal to bring onions, potatoes and other vegetables under Essential Commodities Act to prevent hoarding of these items by unscrupulous traders for artificially raising the prices; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; (c)	whether hoarding of these items will be made non-bailable offence under the provisions of the existing law; (d) 	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; (e)	whether the Government is having any proposal to set up Price Stabilization Fund; and (f)	if so, the objectives and details of the scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI DANVE RAOSAHEB DADARAO)(a)  & (b) :	‘Foodstuffs’ which means a substance used as food including onions, potatoes and other vegetables are scheduled as essential commodities under the Essential Commodities Act,1955.(c) & (d)  : 	No such proposal is under consideration with Government as of now. (e) & (f)   : 	The Price Stabilization Funds (PSF) was set up in 2014-15 to tackle price volatility in some agri-horticultural commodities viz. onion, potato and pulses. The objective of the PSF are to promote direct purchase from farmers/farmers’ association at farm gate/Mandi, to maintain a strategic buffer stock that would discourage hoarding and unscrupulous speculation and to protect consumers by supplying such commodities at reasonable prices through calibrated release of stock. 	  The Operational Guidelines of PSF may be seen at https://consumeraffairs.nic.in/sites/default/files/fileuploads/price_monitoring/PSF%20Operational%20Guidellines%20DOCA_0.pdf.*****
