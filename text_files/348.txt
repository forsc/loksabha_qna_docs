                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

410






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.06.2019






Educational Institutions




Balashowry Vallabbhaneni














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the present status of establishment of 11 educational institutions mandated to set up under 13thSchedule (Section 93) of Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014;(b) the details of funds required, sanctioned, released and spent since implementation of the said Act, yearand institution-wise;(c) the reasons behind releasing just Rs. 746 crores against the required amount of Rs. 12,446 crores; and(d) the time by which it is going to set up a Central University and a Tribal University in AndhraPradesh?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (d) As per the provisions of Andhra Pradesh Re-organisation Act,2014, total 08 CentralEducational Institutes are being established by this Ministry. The details are as below:S.No Name of Institute Status of Institution(admission of students/provision of infrastructureand buildings)1 IIT In pursuance of the Budget Announcement 2014-15, IIT Trupati has beenestablished as a part of Andhra Pradesh Reorganisatin Act, 2014. Theacademic session of IIT-Tirupati started in 2015-16 from its temporarycampus at Chandalwada Venkata Subaiah College of Engineering, ReniguntaRoad, Tirupati.Land measuring 589.55 acres at Merlapaka Village, Yerpedu (Mandal),Chittoor District (Near Tirupathi) has been approved for permanent campus.2The construction of Transit Campus is completed. The institute has a studentstrength of 631 and faculty strength of 55. As on date, an amount ofRs. 203.36 crore has been released to IIT-Tirupati.2 NIT NIT Andhra Pradesh has started functioning from its temporary campuslocated at Sri Vasavi Engineering College, Tadepalligudem since 2015. StateGovernment of Andhra Pradesh has provided land at Tadepalligudem, WestGodavari District for the construction of permanent campus. The entireconstruction work has been bifurcated into two segments ‘1A’ and ‘1B’ forsmooth operations. .Rs 460.50 Cr has been approved for construction of permanent campus.3 IIM The Institute started its operation from its temporary campus i.e. AndhraPradesh University Vishakhapatnam. PGP course has started. Cabinet on 5thSeptember, 2018 approved an amount of Rs. 594.32 crore for establishmentpermanent campus.The State Government has allocated 230.25 acres of land in VillageGambheeram, District Vishakhapatnam for permanent campus of IIMVishakhapatnam.4 IISER The Cabinet has approved the setting up of Indian Institute of ScienceEducation and Research (IISER), Tirupati with a capital cost of Rs. 1137.16crore. IISER Tirupati has started functioning on temporary campus since2016. Rs 525 Cr has been sanctioned under Higher Education FundingAgency.5 IIIT IIITDM Kurnool has been established as per the AP Reorganisation Act,2014. A site selection committee on 01.04.2015 ascertained the suitability ofland identified by Government of Andhra Pradesh at JagannathgattuDinnedevara Padu (v), Kurnool(M), Kurnool District, for construction ofpermanent campus. Since 2015 lllTDM Kurnool is functioning fromtemporary campus of IIITDM Kancheepuram. A sum of Rs. 297.0 Cr havebeen allocated as financial assistance out of which Rs. 256.0 Cr is forconstruction of the institute and Rs. 41 Cr for recurring expenditure. In July2018 the Institute has shifted to its permanent campus. The total studentstrength is of 328 students.6 CentralUniversity,Andhra PradeshThe Union Cabinet in its meeting held on 16th May, 2018 has accorded inprincipalapproval for establishment a Central University by the name ofCentral University of Andhra Pradesh in village Janthaluru AnantapurDistrict in the State of Andhra Pradesh with provision of funds of Rs.450crore for meeting the first phase expenditure. University of Hyderabad ismentor of the Central University of Andhra Pradesh. An amount of Rs.800.003lakhs has been released to University of Hyderabad (Mentor University) forCentral University of Andhra Pradesh during the financial year 2018-19.The Central University (Amendment) Bill 2018 was introduced in Lok Sabhaon 14.12.2018. However, the Bill could not be passed in the 16th Lok Sabhaand it lapsed as the 16th Lok Sabha dissolved.7 Tribal University,Andhra PradeshOperationalisation of the Central Tribal University in the State of AndhraPradesh from transit campus by a mentor University i.e. Andhra Universityhas been announced on 24th January 2019.An amount of Rs.50.00 lakhs has been released to Andhra University (MentorUniversity) for Central Tribal University of Andhra Pradesh, Vijayanagramduring the financial year 2018-19.The Central University (Amendment) Bill 2018 was introduced in Lok Sabhaon 14.12.2018. However, the Bill could not be passed in the 16th Lok Sabhaand it lapsed as the 16th Lok Sabha dissolved.8 Tribal University,TelanganaA site at Mulugu (V), Mulugu (M), Bhupalpally District, Telanganameasuring 512.47 acres has been finalized by Site Selection Committee forestablishment of the Tribal University in Telangana.The Govt of Telangana has been requested to furnish the status of land areaand removal of encumbrances and necessary clearance.
