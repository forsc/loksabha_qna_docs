                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

27






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.07.2021






Pension Schemes for BPL Persons




Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether any pension scheme is being implemented by the Government for widows, old aged and unemployed persons living Below Poverty Line (BPL);(b) 	if so, the details thereof;(c) 	the details of funds allocated, sanctioned and expended under this scheme during the last five years,State/UT-wise; and(d) 	whether the Government is considering to double the funds allocated under this scheme and the time by which it is likely to be implemented?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SHRI GIRIRAJ SINGH)(a) to (d):   A statement is laid on the Table of the House.  Statement referred in reply to Lok Sabha  Starred Question No. * 27 for answer on 20th July, 2021 raised by Shrimati Poonam Mahajan  (a) to (b) Under the National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP), monthly pension at the rate of Rs.300/- p.m  to widows in the age group of 40 to 79 years and Rs.200/- p.m to old age persons in the age group of 60 to 79 years belonging to  below poverty line households is being paid. The amount of pension is increased to Rs. 500/- per month in respect of these beneficiaries on reaching the age of 80 years and above.  There is no provision of pension to unemployed persons living below poverty line.    (c) The State/UT-wise details of funds allocated and expended under this scheme during the last five years is given at Annexure ‘A’. (d) No, sir. There is no such proposal for consideration to double the funds under NSAP.  *****
