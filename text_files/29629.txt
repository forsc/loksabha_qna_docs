                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1277






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2021






MDMS in Maharashtra




Supriya Sadanand Sule













                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the status of Mid-Day Meal Scheme (MDMS) in Maharashtra;(b) the total number of schools in which this scheme is being implemented in Maharashtra;(c) the quality of the food provided every day to each student and the amount spent thereonas on date;(d) the total number of cooks appointed in Maharashtra for the purpose of implementingthe said scheme; and(e) the details of the total areas covered by this scheme in Maharashtra?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EDUCATION(SMT. ANNPURNA DEVI)(a) to (e): Central Government has approved the Centrally Sponsored Scheme ‘PradhanMantri Poshan Shakti Nirman (PM POSHAN)’earlier known as ‘NationalProgramme for Mid-Day Meal in Schools’ for providing one hot cooked meal inGovernment and Government – aided Schools across the country includingMaharashtra. In Maharashtra, the Scheme covers 1,02,35,007 children enrolled in85979 schools. The State Government has engaged 1,62,991 cook-cum-helpers forimplementation of the Scheme.<pre>Components	Primary	Upper PrimaryCalories	450 Cal	700 CalProtein	12 gms.	20 gms.</pre>The above nutritional norms are met by providing the following food items to the children of primary and upper primary classes under the scheme all over the country including Maharashtra:<pre>S.No.	Items	Primary Stage	Upper Primary Stage		Food norms (in gms)	Food norms (in gms)1.	Foodgrains(Rice / Wheat)	100	1502.	Pulses	20	303.	Vegetables (Leafy & Others)	50	754.	Oil & Fat	5	7.55.	Salt & Condiments	As per need	As per need</pre>In addition to providing food grains under the scheme, cooking cost is also providedwhich is meant to meet the cost of pulses, vegetables, cooking oil, other condiments andfuel. At present the per child per day cooking cost is Rs. 4.97 for primary level and Rs.7.45 for upper primary level.*****
