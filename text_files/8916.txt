                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2456






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2019






Atrocities against Children and Women




Sougata Ray














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has taken note that large portion of the cases registered on atrocities against children and women has not resulted in conviction for the accused persons;(b) if so, the percentage of cases in which conviction has been achieved during the last three years, State-wise;(c) the details of number of cases registered and action taken regarding attacks on tribal women and girls in the country during the said period, State-wise;(d) whether the Government has also taken note of a recent judgement in a case at Walayar of Kerala in which the accused persons were freed due to the adjustment between prosecution and police;(e) if so, the details thereof;(f) whether the prosecutor appointed by the State Government has failed to present the evidence in the court; and(g) if so, the details thereof and the action taken thereon along with the steps taken to forward the case to the Central Bureau of Investigation?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a)& (b):	National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) compiles and publishes information on crimes in its publication “Crime in India”. The published reports are available till the year 2017. -2-L.S.US.Q.NO.2456 FOR 3.12.2019	State/UT-wise details of cases registered, case charge-sheeted, cases convicted, cases acquitted and discharged, conviction rate under Crime against Women and Crime against Children during 2015-2017 are given in Annexure-I and Annexure-II respectively. (c):	State/UT-wise details of cases registered, cases charge-sheeted and cases convicted under “Attacks on Scheduled Tribes (STs) Women” (Assault on women with intent to outrage her modesty, Rape and Attempt to Rape) during 2015-2017 are given in Annexure-III.(d) to (g):	‘Police’ and ‘Public Order’ are State subjects under the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution of India. The responsibilities to maintain law and order, protection of life and property of the citizens rests with the respective State Governments. The State Governments are competent to deal with such offences under the extant provisions of law.As per the information received from the Government of Kerala, the State Government has taken note of the Judgment in Walayar case in which all the accused were acquitted due to latches in the investigation and also due to inefficient conduct of prosecution. On evaluating the Judgment and evidence in the above case, Government of Kerala concluded that the prosecutor appointed by the State Government has failed to present the evidence to establish the case of prosecution before the Court. The service of the said prosecutor has already been terminated by Government of Kerala. Further, the Government of Kerala has filed an Appeal in the Hon’ble High Court. ******
