                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2260






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.03.2021






Identification of Rural Tourist Spots




Krupal Balaji Tumane














                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether there is ample scope for development of rural tourism in the country; (b) 	if so, the State-wise/Union Territory-wise number of districts and villages which have been identified and developed as rural tourist spots during the last three years and the current year; (c) 	whether the Government has received any proposals and suggestions from the States and Union Territories in this regard; and (d) 	if so, the details of the amount of funds allocated by the Government for the said purpose along with the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR TOURISM (INDEPENDENT CHARGE)(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) to (d):	Rural tourism has been identified as one of the Niche Tourism areas for development in the country by the Ministry of Tourism.      Ministry of Rural Development launched Shyama Prasad Mukherji Rurban Mission (SPMRM) on 21st February 2016 with an attempt to make the selected rural areas in form of the cluster, socially, economically and physically sustainable regions by providing economic, social and infrastructure amenities, thus leading to sustainable and balanced regional development in the country. 300 Rurban Clusters with thematic economic growth points are proposed to be developed across the country under this innovative Mission. To ensure an optimum level of development, twenty one components have been suggested as desirable for the Cluster development. Tourism Promotion is one of twenty one components.      70 percent of total investment is mobilised under the convergence with various Central as well as State schemes. The SPMRM provides an additional funding support of up to 30% of project cost (maximum upto Rs.15 crore for tribal cluster and Rs.30 crore for non-tribal cluster) as Critical Gap Fund (CGF) to enable development of Rurban Clusters. The funding under the mission is shared in ratio of 60:40 for the States and 90:10 for 8 North Eastern State, 3 Himalayan States. For Union Territories, 100 per cent funding is provided by the Government of India.      The States/UTs have proposed the activities related to tourism promotion under Integrated Cluster Action Plan. The list of the clusters wherein the activities related to tourism have been taken up, along with funds allocated to the activities related to tourism development- the list of Tourism Project under SPMRM is placed at Annexure.      Under the Swadesh Darshan Scheme, the Ministry is developing theme-based tourism circuits in the Country. Rural Circuit is one of the fifteen thematic circuits identified for development under the Scheme. Submission of project proposals by the State Governments/Union Territory (UT) Administration under the Swadesh Darshan Scheme are identified in consultation with the State Governments/UT Administrations and are sanctioned subject to availability of funds, submission of suitable detailed project reports, adherence to scheme guidelines and utilization of funds released earlier.
