                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3087






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.08.2021






Immunization of Children




Su Thirunavukkarasar














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a)	whether the Government is aware that about 40% of children under five years age in the country are not fully vaccinated as per the report of National Statistical Organisation (NSO);(b)	if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise and vaccine-wise;(c)	whether any steps are being taken by the Government to attain universal immunization;(d)	if so, the details thereof; and (e)	if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) & (b) National Statistical Organization (NSO) has conducted a recall based survey in 2017-18. The survey reports that full immunization of children in the age-group 0-5 years as 59.2%. The full immunization of 0- 5 years age is an average of 5 years data and also contains ineligible children of 0-1 year for full immunization. As per National Family Health Survey- 5(2019-20) data of 22 states /UTs, which has been recently published, indicates that 18 states/UTs have shown an increase in Full Immunization Coverage as compared to National Family Health Survey- 4 data(2015-16) (State/UT wise details at Annexure). More than two-third of children are fully immunized in most of the States and UTs. In almost three-fourths of districts, 70% or more children aged 12-23 months are fully immunized against childhood diseases. (c) to (e) Continuous efforts are being made by the Government to attain universal immunization by strengthening routine immunization with interventions like robust microplanning, regular reviews, effective inter personal communication, community engagement and social mobilization involving various stakeholders. Special vaccination drives like Mission Indradhanush, Intensified Mission Indradhanush, Gram Swaraj Abhiyaan (GSA), Extended GSA have been carried out to reach the left out and drop out children from vaccination. During such immunization intensification drives, interventions like head count survey, robust microplanning, social mobilization, community engagement etc. are emphasized. Under the various phases of Mission Indradhanush, 3.86 crore children and 96.8 lakh pregnant women have been vaccinated so far, across the country.Funds under National Health Mission Flexipool are allocated to carry out activities under Routine Immunization like microplanning, alternate vaccine delivery, review and monitoring, supportive supervision, cold chain maintenance, community mobilization etc. The details of funds released to states/UTs for Routine Immunization are as follows: Funds Released (in Rs. Crore)	17-18	18-19	19-20	20-21	233.16	277.58	279.90	282.75
