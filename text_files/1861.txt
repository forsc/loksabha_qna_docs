                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1672






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Presence of IS in Kerala




Suresh Kodikunnil














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is seized of reports indicating the heightened presence of Islamic State (IS) in Kerala and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has received intelligence inputs that boats carrying Islamic State terrorists had set of from Sri Lanka to the Lakshadweep islands and coastal areas of Kerala have been put on high alert;(c) if so, the steps taken towards enhancing coastal patrolling in Kerala;(d) whether the State Government of Kerala has requisitioned for additional coastal patrolling and interceptor boats;(e) if so, whether the Government has acceded to the request; and(f) if so, the details of funds allocated under the Central Government scheme in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a):  Some instances of individuals from different States including Kerala having joined IS have come to the notice of Central and State Security Agencies.  The National Investigation Agency (NIA) and the States Police have registered cases against IS members / sympathizers and have arrested 160 accused so far all over the country.  (b) to (c):	Intelligence inputs on terror threats are regularly shared with State   Governments/Agencies  concerned   with  advice  to  take  appropriate-2/…-2-LS.US.Q.No.1672 FOR 02.07.2019 measures and sensitize their field formations to prevent any untoward incident.  Advisories are issued regularly to all Coastal States / UTs to ensure proper patrolling and surveillance along their respective coastline.  (d) to (f):	 The Government of India has approved implementation of Coastal Security Scheme in phases, to strengthen security of coastal areas and augment the capabilities of police forces of coastal States / UTs for patrolling and surveillance in territorial waters.  Various components under the Coastal Security Scheme have been sanctioned after due consultations with all stakeholders concerned.  Under the Phase-II of the Scheme, 18 Coastal Police Stations, 44 boats, 4 jetties, 26 four-wheelers and 44 two-wheelers have been sanctioned for Kerala.   A sum of Rs. 12.68 crore has been released to the Government of Kerala under the scheme.  *****
