                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2714






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Upgradation of SH into NH




 Bhagirath Chaudhary














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the criteria fixed for upgradation of a State Highway (SH) into National Highway (NH);(b) the details of SHs which have been upraded to NHs during the last three years and the current year, State-wise;(c) whether the Government proposes to upgrade 99 km road from Nasirabad (dist. Ajmer) to Deoli (dist. Tonk) SH-26 as well as Ajmer-Tonk-Sawaimadhopur road into a four lane road with a pucca pavement for smooth travel of people; and(d) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) 		The existing criteria for declaration of State roads, including State Highways (SHs), as new National Highways (NHs) include roads running through length / breadth of the country, connecting adjacent countries, National Capitals with State Capitals / mutually the State Capitals, major ports, non-major ports, large industrial centers or tourist centers, roads meeting very important strategic requirement in hilly and isolated area, arterial roads which enable sizeable reduction in travel distance and achieve substantial economic growth thereby, roads which help opening up large tracts of backward area and hilly regions (other than strategically important ones), achieving a National Highways grid of 100 km, etc.(b)		State/UT-wise details of State Roads, including SHs, converted to NHs during the last 3 years and the current year are annexed.(c) and (d) The Ministry keeps on receiving proposals from various State Governments / Union Territories (UTs) etc., including from the State of Rajasthan, for declaration / upgradation of State roads, including SHs, as new NHs. The Ministry considers declaration of some State roads, including SHs, as NHs from time to time based on the requirement of connectivity, inter-se priority and availability of funds.
