                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4381






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






Quality of Food Grains




Nalin Kumar Kateel














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the production of foodgrains has reached record level in the last three years and if so, the details thereof, year-wise; (b) 	whether the huge quantity of foodgrains with Food Corporation of India (FCI) has shorter shelf life and these foodgrains gets shrivelled and lacks luster and if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Government has made any assessment of losses to the public exchequer from the huge wastage of foodgrains and if so, the details thereof; (d) 	whether the Government is taking any measures to safeguard the quality of foodgrains by setting up adequate infrastructure for its storage; and 






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E RMINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT AND CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a): 	The details on the production of Paddy & Wheat during the last three years and current year is tabulated as under:-(Fig. in Million Tonnes)KMS/RMS	Paddy	Wheat2018-19	116.48	103.602019-20	118.87	107.862020-21	124.37	109.592021-22*	127.93	111.32* As per second advance estimates, Directorate of Economics & Statistics, DA&FW........2/--2-(b): 	No Sir. Food Corporation of India (FCI) always stores foodgrains by adopting scientific method of preservation and even during longer storage, quality of foodgrains are maintained.(c): 	FCI handled large quantities of foodgrains for distribution under various welfare schemes of Government of India. Inspite of all precautionary measures only a meagre quantity of foodgrains gets damaged/become non-issuable mainly due to Natural Calamities like cyclone, flood, rain etc.(d) & (e): 	Government continuously assesses and monitors the storage capacity and based on the storage gap assessment, storage capacities are created/hired through following schemes:1.	Private Entrepreneurs Guarantee (PEG) Scheme.2.	Central Sector Scheme (CSS).3.	Construction of Silo''s under PPP mode.4.	Hiring of godown from CWCs/SWCs/State Agencies.****
