                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

101






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2020






National Employment Enhancement Mission




Andimuthu Raja














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the salient features of the National Employment Enhancement Mission;(b) the details of the funds sanctioned, allocated and utilized under this Missionduring the last three years and the current year across the country includingTamil Nadu;(c) the details of the number of students trained under this mission so far across thecountry including Tamil Nadu along with the number of students offered jobs;(d) whether the percentage of jobs offered is very low under this mission across thecountry;(e) if so, the reasons therefor and the action taken by the Government in this regard;(f) whether the scheme is not proving as per the expectations within the country; and(g) if so, the reasons therefor along with the steps taken/being taken by theGovernment to make it successful?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (g): The National Employability Enhancement Mission (NEEM) scheme was launched in2013 by All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) to offer on the job practical trainingby the industry to enhance employability of person between the age of 16-40 years pursuing PostGraduation/ graduation / Diploma in any technical or non-technical stream or who may havediscontinued studies after class 10th class. NEEM Scheme is not an employment guaranteescheme. The trainees are offered stipend by the industry concerned. No funds are released underthis scheme. NEEM facilitators which are approved by AICTE provide National SkillQualification Framework (NSQF) compliant industrial training. The training is provided for aperiod of 3 months to 36 months. Till date, 43 NEEM Facilitators and a total of around 4 lakhtrainees have been enrolled by different NEEM Facilitators including in Tamil Nadu. More than1, 60,000 trainees have been regularized / placed in their trainee company after the completionof their training under the Scheme.*****
