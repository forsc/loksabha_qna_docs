                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3177






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.12.2021






Access to potable Water




Thirumaa Valavan Thol














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has the data, State-wise, pertaining to the number of persons having access to potable water in the country during the past five years;(b) 	if so, the details thereof;(c) 	whether the Government has any target and plan to provide potable water to 100% of the population; and(d) 	if so, the details of the target and plan fixed in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL) (a) & (b)	As reported by States/ UTs, as on 13.12.2021, out of 17.01 lakh rural habitations in the country, 13.46 lakh (79.11%) having 79.44% population have provision of potable drinking water with supply level of more than 40 litre per capita per day (lpcd) and 3.18 lakh (18.72%) rural habitations having 18.80% population with supply level of less than 40 lpcd with sources at a reasonable distance and 0.37 lakh (2.17%) rural habitations having 1.76% population are reported to have water quality issues in drinking water sources. State/ UT-wise details are annexed.(c) & (d) 	Since August, 2019, to make provision of potable tap water supply to every rural household of the country by 2024, Government of India in partnership with States is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM). At the time of announcement of Jal Jeevan Mission, out of 18.93 Crore rural households, 3.23 Crore (17%) households were reported to have tap water connections. In last 27 months, 5.43 Crore (28.2%) rural households have been provided with tap water connections. Thus, as of date, out of 19.22 Crore rural households in the country, 8.66 Crore (45%) households are reported to have tap water supply in their homes.*****
