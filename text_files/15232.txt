                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3849






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2020






Cadre Strength of IAS/IPS in J&K




Hasnain Masoodi














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total number of Indian Administrative Service (IAS) and Indian Police Service (IPS) cadre strength for Jammu and Kashmir;(b) the ratio of Officers from State services to the total Indian Administrative Service (IAS) and Indian Police Service (IPS) cadre strength for Jammu and Kashmir;(c) the present position of the Ministry with respect to five years relaxation in age of the aspirants to All India Civil Services Examination; and(d) the functioning and funding/grants provided by the Ministry to the J&K Institute of Management, Public Administration & Rural Development (JKIMPA) and Indian Institute of Public Administration J&K Regional Branch?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a):	The sanctioned strength of Indian Administrative Service of Jammu & Kashmir cadre is 137 and the sanctioned strength of Indian Police Service (IPS) of Jammu & Kashmir Cadre is 147.(b):	As per the Government of Jammu & Kashmir, the current share of officers from State Service to the  Indian  Administrative  Services  (IAS) is -2-L.S.US.Q.NO.3849 FOR 17.03.202062:75. The current share of officers from State Police Service to the Indian Police Service (IPS) is 67:80(c):	There is no such relaxation at present in the Civil Services Examination (CSE) Rules, 2020 notified on 12.02.2020.  (d):	The Department of Personnel and Training (DoPT) provides funds to State/UT Administrative Training Institutes (ATIs) for undertaking various training programmes/courses in respect of the employees of the concerned States/UTs under Training For All (TFA) Scheme.   During 2019-20, a sum of Rs.72,87,500 has been provided by DoPT to JKIMPA under certain components namely, State Category Training Programme (SCTP), Trainers Development Programme (TDP) and Comprehensive online Modified Modules on Induction Training (COMMIT).Ministry does not provide any grant directly to J&K Regional Branch of IIPA.  Government of Jammu and Kashmir has provided a grant of Rs.15 lakhs in FY 2019-20 to J&K Regional Branch of IIPA.********
