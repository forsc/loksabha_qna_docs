                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3893






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.12.2021






Crop Damage due to Stray Animals




Kanakmal Katara













                                                    Keshari Devi Patel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the number of stray animals is increasing day by day which is causing damage to the crops of the farmers; (b)	if so, the district-wise details thereof in respect of Uttar Pradesh;(c)	whether there is any scheme to compensate the farmers for the loss of their crops caused by wild animals; (d)	if so, the details thereof; and(e)	whether the farmers are likely to be compensated for the loss of their crops through the insurance companies and if so, the details thereof ?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????              (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) & (b):	In the 19th Livestock Census 2012, the number of stray cattle in the country was 52.87 Lakhs. In the 20th Livestock Census 2019, the number of stray cattle declined to 50.21 lakhs. However as per the Livestock Census 2019, the number of stray cattle in Uttar Pradesh have increased as compared to Livestock Census of 2012. The district-wise details of number of Stray Cattle of Uttar Pradesh provided by Department of Animal Husbandry & Dairying (DAH&D) is given at Annexure. (c) to (e):	Under the Revamped Operational Guidelines of Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) effective from Kharif 2020, the States may consider providing add-on coverage for crop loss due to attack by notified wild animals wherever the risk is perceived to be substantial and identifiable. Ministry of Environment Forest & Climate Change (MoEF&CC)   also    provide    financial     assistance  to   the States/Union Territories under Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Development of Wildlife Habitats, “Project Tiger” and “Project Elephant” for management of wildlife and its habitats including ex-gratia relief for crop damage caused by wild animals as per the cost norms prescribed by the concerned state governments.The state governments also make provision to compensate farmers for the crop losses due to wild animals as per extant law of the respective states.
