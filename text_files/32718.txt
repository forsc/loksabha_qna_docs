                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

240






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2022






Privatisation of Airports




Debasree Chaudhuri













                                                    Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total number of airports controlled by private companies, the Union Government and the State Governments;(b) the details of the steps taken by the Government to ensure the welfare of workers in airports which have been privatised;(c) whether the Government proposes to impose a limit on the number of airports a single private entity can own and operate or any other steps to prevent the monopoly on airports by one corporate entity and if so, the details thereof;(d) the details of the bidding process and results for those airports which have been privatised, including total bidders and respective bids; and(e) the details of airports which the Government proposes to privatise in future?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a): Presently, there are 140 operational airports in the country, which includes 130 airports, 02 Water aerodromes and 08 Heliports. Details of these airports are given below:;;(i) 113 airports are under Central Government including airports of Airports Authority of India (AAI);(ii) 18 airports/Water Aerodromes/Heliports are under various State Governments;(iii) 05 airports are Private;(iv) 04 Non-AAI airports are Joint Venture Airports; (b): On implementation of Public Private Partnership (PPP) in AAI airports, employees of AAI have choice to either remain with AAI or opt to join PPP partner as their employee. Those who continue to be with AAI are governed by the terms and conditions of service of AAI and those who opt for private player are governed by the terms and conditions of service of the respective private company.;;(c):The Government of India is yet to decide the transaction modalities for next set of airports to be leased out under PPP mode.;;(d): During 2018-19, AAI had undertaken bidding for PPP at its six airports namely Ahmedabad, Jaipur, Lucknow, Guwahati, Mangaluru and Thiruvananthapuram.  The Per Passenger Fee (PPF) payable to AAI was the bidding parameter. A total of 32 bids from 10 entities were received for these six airports. The airport-wise details of PPF quoted by the winning bidder for these six airports are as under:;;;Ahmedabad- Rs. 177Jaipur- Rs. 174Lucknow- Rs. 171Guwahati- Rs. 160Mangaluru- Rs. 115Thiruvananthapuram- Rs. 168(e): As per National Monetization Pipeline (NMP), 25 AAI airports have been earmarked for leasing out over the years 2022 to 2025 namely Bhubaneshwar, Varanasi, Amritsar, Trichy, Indore, Raipur, Calicut, Coimbatore, Nagpur, Patna, Madurai, Surat, Ranchi, Jodhpur, Chennai, Vijayawada, Vadodara, Bhopal, Tirupati, Hubli, Imphal, Agartala, Udaipur, Dehradun and Rajahmundry.
