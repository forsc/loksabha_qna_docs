                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2505






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2019






Extreme Price Volatility in Vegetables




 Chandra Sekhar Bellana













                                                    Magunta Sreenivasulu Reddy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it is a fact that tomatoes, onions and potatoes, the three basic vegetables face extreme price volatility; (b) 	if so, the reasons therefor; and (c)	whether it is a fact that the Government is in a fix in fulfilling its dual objectives of ensuring remunerative prices for farmers and affordable prices for consumers?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI DANVE RAOSAHEB DADARAO)(a) & (b) : Price rise and fall of vegetables, including tomatoes, onions and potatoes, are influenced by their demand and supply which depend on factors like cost of inputs (fertilizers, rural wages), perishability and storability of the produce, transportation cost, loading/ unloading expenses, grading/sorting, wholesaler’s margin, losses during movement, cost and margin of retailers, supply chain constraints like lack of storage and cold chain facilities, artificial shortage created by hoarding and black marketing and adverse weather conditions etc.(c)	:  Government strives to ensure remunerative prices for farmers and affordable prices for consumers through its schemes/ programmes.*****
