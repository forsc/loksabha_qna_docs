                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1551






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2020






Temporary Storage Facility for Foodgrains




M V V Satyanarayana














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government aims to create temporary storage facility for 10 million tonnes of grains to create space for fresh wheat procurement from April, 2020; (b) 	if so, the details thereof; and (c) 	the current storage capacity of FCI as on date and the manner in which the Government plans to create the temporary storage facility and the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E R MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a) to (c): Government of India has granted in-principle approval for construction of 9 million tonnes of storage facilities under Covered and Plinth (CAP) hiring Scheme (2019) of Food Corporation of India (FCI) for accommodating the wheat stocks procured during Rabi Marketing Season (RMS) 2020-21.The State-wise details for creation of CAP capacities to the tune of 90 Lakh Metric Tonnes (LMT) is as given below: State	Capacity (in LMT)Punjab	35Haryana	20Madhya Pradesh	25Uttar Pradesh	10Total	90As on 31.12.2019, the total storage capacity available with FCI, Central Warehousing Corporation (CWC) and the State Agencies (both owned and hired capacity) including CAP storage for central pool stocks was 758.46 LMT. Out of this, the owned capacity available with FCI was 153.79 LMT.                                            ****
