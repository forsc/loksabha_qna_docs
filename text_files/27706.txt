                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3238






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.08.2021






Skill Development and Vocation Training in Rural Areas




Devji Mansingram Patel













                                                    Parbatbhai Savabhai Patel
                                                








                                                    Naranbhai Bhikhabhai Kachhadiya
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether there is a worrying picture of Skill Development and Vocational Training in rural areas, in the light of a report “Status of Education and Vocational Training in India”;(b)	whether according to the report in the year 2011- 12 only 2.4 per cent of the people aged 15 years and above in the country have technical degree or diploma or certificate and in rural areas their percentage is just 1.1; and(c)	if so, the steps taken by the Government to promote Vocational Training in rural areas; and(d)	the sectors in which formal Vocational Training has been given to men and women in rural areas during the last three years (2014-20) and the State-wise/ department-wise details thereof including Gujarat? 






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	The Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation through National Sample Survey Organisation (NSSO) has published the report ‘Status of Education and Vocational Training in India’ in 2011-12, on the basis of the NSS 68th round.(b)	As per report, among persons of age 15 years and above, 2.4% had technical degrees or diplomas or certificates. The proportion was 1.1% in rural areas and 5.5% in urban areas.(c)	The Government is implementing various schemes/programmes for imparting vocational training to the youth of the country both in rural and urban areas through short term and long term training. The Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship is implementing Pradhan Mantri Kaushal VikasYojana and Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) Scheme for short-term skill training, and Craftsmen Training Scheme (CTS) through the Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) for long-term vocational training in 142 trades. Further, District Skill Committees (DSCs), under the guidance of respective State Skill Development Missions (SSDMs), are playing a key role in addressing the skill gap and assessing demand at the district level including in rural areas. The DSCs have been entrusted with the development of District Skill Development Plans (DSDPs) to promote decentralized planning and implementation at the grassroots.  (d)	Under the PMKVY scheme, short term skill training is imparted in 36 sectors. The list of sector in which training is being imparted under PMKVY scheme is placed at Annexure I. The State wise details of candidates (total, including urban and rural both) trained under the scheme for last three (3) Financial Years is placed at Annexure II.	Under the Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS) scheme, the Non-Government Organisations (NGOs) engaged as the JSS go to the rural areas for outreach programmes for skill training. The list of sectors in which training is imparted under the JSS scheme is placed at Annexure III. The State wise details of male and female beneficiaries trained in the rural areas under the Scheme is placed at Annexure IV.	Under the CTS scheme, long-term vocational training is imparted in 142 trades through Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) across the country including rural areas.
