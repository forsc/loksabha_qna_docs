                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1102






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2020






Super Speciality Hospitals




Krupal Balaji Tumane














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the number of functional and under construction super speciality hospitals in the country, State/UT-wise, especially in the state of Maharashtra;(b) whether the Government proposes to establish more such hospitals in every State and UT;(c) if so, the details thereof;(d) the criteria fixed for the selection of location for each project and the progress made in regard of under construction projects, and the time by which they are likely to be completed;(e) the funds allocated and utilized for this purpose during each of the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise; and(f) the steps being taken by the Government for completion of the said projects in the country at the earliest?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	Under Pradhan Mantri Swasthya Suraksha Yojana (PMSSY),  Government has sanctioned setting up of Super Specialty Hospitals (SSH) or Trauma Centres in 73 existing Government Medical Colleges (GMCs)/ Institutes  in various parts of the country including following six GMCs in the State of Maharashtra :S.No.	Name of GMCs	Phase	Funds Allocated	Facilities being created under PMSSY	Status 1.		GMC, Aurangabad	III	Rs. 150 cr	220 bedded SSH including 52 ICU beds 	Civil Works Completed2.		GMC, Latur 	III	Rs. 150 cr	230  bedded SSH including 62 ICU beds 	Civil Works Completed3.		GMC, Akola 	III	Rs. 150 cr	231 bedded SSH including 56  ICU beds 	Civil work Progress  : 99%4.		Sri Vasantrao Naik GMC, Yavatmal	III	Rs. 150 cr	231 bedded SSH including 56 ICU beds	Civil work Progress : 92%5.		Grants Medical College and JJ Hospital, Mumbai	I	Rs. 120 cr	Up-gradation  mainly involved procurement of medical equipment. 	Functional 6.		GMC, Nagpur	II	Rs. 150 cr		FunctionalThe Government is also setting up an AIIMS at Nagpur under Phase-IV of PMSSY at an approved cost of Rs. 1577 crores. Undergraduate MBBS course with 50 seats started from the session 2018-19. OPD services have started from September, 2019. As per the mandate of the Cabinet the target date for completion of AIIMS Nagpur is September, 2020.However, new Super speciality hospitals are being constructed both in Public and Private Sector, and Health being a State subject no centralised data State / UT wise on number of functional / under construction Super Speciality hospitals is available. (b) & (c):	Health being State subject the primary responsibility to ensure the effective and sufficient health facilities including setting up of Super Speciality Hospitals lies with State Government. At present, there is no approved scheme for setting up of additional Super Speciality Hospitals.(d):	PMSSY aims at correcting the regional imbalances in the availability of the tertiary healthcare and medical education in different parts of the country. The Ministry has its own database under National Health Mission on penetration and reach of healthcare throughout the country. The Ministry has, while finalizing the Up-gradation projects given higher priority to the States generally considered “backward” with respect to the broad socio-economic indicators. All projects have also been selected on the basis of demands raised by States.(e):			The State/UT-wise funds allocated and Utilised for these projects during each of the last three years and the current year, is given in Annexure. (f):	To expedite completion of all ongoing PMSSY projects following steps have been taken by the Government : i.	The system of assigning the construction work to executing agencies on turnkey basis with empowerment to handle all contractual matters at their end has been introduced. ii.	The Ministry has also developed an online dashboard to facilitate a closer and more effective monitoring. iii.	The procedure for procurement of medical equipment has been simplified and made more efficient with greater delegation and empowerment of the Procurement Support Agency (PSA) entrusted with the work of procurement of medical equipments.iv.	Fortnightly progress review meetings with the Executing Agencies are being held at the level of Joint Secretary.v.	State level steering committees under the Chairmanship of Chief Secretary have been set up/ being set up in the States.vi.	Project Monitoring Group (PMG) meeting under the chairmanship of Chief Secretary of respective States are being held to review progress and resolve local issues. The Ministry ensures its representation in the PMG meetings and any issues/ hindrances with the State are flagged.
