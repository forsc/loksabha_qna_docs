                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3987






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2020






Trains from Jharkhand




Annpurna Devi













                                                    Pashupati Nath Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 the names of the States of which maximum number of railway passengers travel from Jharkhand;(b)	 whether the Government proposes to start new railway services from Jharkhand to the States having demand for such railway journey;(c)	 if so, the details thereof;(d)	 if not, the reasons therefor; and(e)	 the reaction of the Government thereto?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL) (a) to (e)  Indian Railways do not operate trains on state-wise basis as Railway network straddles across the state boundaries. Further, state-wise details of number of passengers is not maintained. During the last 02 years (i.e. during 2018-2019 and 2019-2020 upto February, 2020) Indian Railways have, on originating/terminating basis, introduced 26 trains serving the stations located in the State of Jharkhand. However, introduction of trains including those serving the stations located in the state of Jharkhand is an ongoing process subject to operational feasibility, commercial viability, resource availability, competing demands etc.*****
