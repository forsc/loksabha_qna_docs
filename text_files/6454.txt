                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

158






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.11.2019






Institute of Telugu Language




Kinjarapu Ram Mohan Naidu














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) the list of higher education institutions that provide Telugu Language educationin the country;(b) the student-teacher ratio of Telugu Language department in each suchinstitution;(c) the number of teachers of Telugu Language department working on contractualbasis in each such institution;(d) the steps taken by the Government for publishing of University level text-booksin Telugu Language;(e) whether the Government has taken any steps for providing Telugu Languageeducation outside the Telugu speaking region of the country; and(f) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): List of Universities offering programmes in the discipline of Telugu is given inAnnexure. Five Central Universities namely Aligarh Muslim University, BanarasHindu University, University of Delhi, University of Hyderabad and CentralUniversity of Andhra Pradesh are offering courses in Telugu language.(b): The details of student-teacher ratio in Central Universities offering courses inTelugu language is as under:Name of CUs Student-teacher ratioAligarh Muslim University 4:1Banaras Hindu University 20:1University of Hyderabad 24:1University of Delhi 32:1Central University ofAndhra PradeshFor 1st year 1:1For 2nd year 5:1University wise and Department wise details of teacher-students ratio for StateUniversities are not maintained by University Grants Commission (UGC).(c): The number of teachers of Telugu Language department working oncontractual basis in five Central Universities offering courses in Telugu Languageare as below:Name of CUs No of teachers oncontractual basisAligarh Muslim University NilBanaras Hindu University NilUniversity of Delhi NilUniversity of Hyderabad Guest Faculty-1Central University ofAndhra PradeshOn deputation-1Guest Faculty-2University wise details of contract teachers in State Universities are not availablewith UGC.(d) to (f): The UGC provides General Development Assistance/Block Grants to allthe eligible Universities which can be utilized for providing facilities to all teachingdepartments including Telugu Language Departments. An amount of Rs.4.25 crorewas released to Potti Sreeramulu Telugu University, Hyderabad under GeneralDevelopment Assistance Scheme during XII plan period. The UGC has also beenimplementing a scheme namely “Funding support to the State Universities for studyand research in indigenous and endangered languages in India”. Central Instituteof Indian Languages (CIIL) works for the promotion of all Indian languagesincluding Telugu which imparts training to non native school teachers, prospectiveteachers and general public via ten month diploma course to teachers across thecountry in all southern languages including Telugu. This centre conductedseminars, workshops and conferences on Telugu language. National TranslationMission (NTM) undertakes translation of higher education texts books includingTelugu language.***
