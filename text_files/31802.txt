                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3368






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.12.2021






AYUSH Hospitals and Dispensaries




Sudhakar Tukaram Shrangre













                                                    Ajay Kumar Mandal
                                                








                                                    Debasree Chaudhuri
                                                








                                                    Prasun Banerjee
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has taken any steps for standardization of Ayurvedic, ethnic and traditional medicinal herbs, if so, the details thereof and the time likely to be taken to implement the same; (b) whether COVID treatment and its vaccination is available in AYUSH Centres/Hospitals; (c) if so, the details thereof along with average number of vaccinations per day and if not, the reasons therefor; (d) the number of AYUSH hospitals along with the present staff strength, including doctors, in each of the traditional systems of medicine hospital/centres, State/UT, hospital and stream-wise; and (e) the corrective measures taken by Government to supplement the efforts of various State Governments to promote AYUSH in the country, State/UT-wise including West Bengal? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AYUSH (SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)a) Yes, Ministry of Ayush has setup Pharmacopoeia Commission for Indian Medicine & Homoeopathy (PCIM&H), Ghaziabad to lay down Pharmacopoeial Standards and Formulary specifications for ASU&H drugs, which serve as official compendia for ascertaining the identity, purity and strength of the drugs included therein. List of Pharmacopoeial and Formulary publications is attached as Annexure-I.	Further, PCIM&H is also serves as Central drug testing cum appellate laboratory for testing of ASU&H drugs; for ensuring compliance to the prescribed quality standards. The laying down/publication/revision of Pharmacopoeial Standards is a continuous process.(b) & (c). Yes, All India Institute of Ayurveda (AIIA) CHC was established in June, 18th 2020 for the treatment of mild to moderate cases of COVID-19, Holistic Ayurveda Protocol was made for treating Covid patients. Among the patients, who completed their treatment at AIIA, 99% patient who completed treatment become negative before discharge. 92% of the total admitted patients were treated through holistic Ayurveda treatment protocol 6% of patients were treated with both Ayurvedic & allopathic medicines and in only 2% patients, allopathic treatment was given. •	Both the Covid-19 Vaccination Cowaxin and Covishield are available.•	Daily Average of Cowaxin-89•	Daily Average of Covishield-122In addition, Ministry of AYUSH recommends self-care guidelines for preventive health measures and boosting immunity.•	General Measures•	Ayurvedic Immunity Promoting Measures•	Simple Ayurvedic Procedures•	During dry cough/sore throatI.	Guidelines for Registered Practitioners of respective system of AYUSH have been prepared by Director Generals of Research Councils and Directors of National Institutes with their team of experts and same is vetted by the Interdisciplinary AYUSH Research and Development Task Force of Ministry of AYUSH. These guidelines are made available in public domain for the benefit of registered AYUSH practitioners including Homoeopathy to help in the management of COVID 19 pandemic uniformly.II.	Government of India has released “National Clinical Management Protocol based on Ayurveda and Yoga for management of COVID-19” prepared by National Task force which in consensus from various expert committees.III.	Ministry of Ayush has also released Ayurveda, Unani and Siddha preventive measures for selfcare during COVID-19 and Guidelines for Ayush Practitioners for COVID-19 Patients in Home Isolation. IV.	Ministry of Ayush has also issued advisory for Ayush practitioners on ethical practices during covid 19 pandemic.V.	Ministry of AYUSH has released Home care guidelines for children and Advisory for AYUSH Practitioners about prophylactic care in Children during the COVID-19 Pandemic.VI.	Ministry of Ayush has released information for Ayurveda Practitioners for Prophylactic, Symptomatic Management of suspected and diagnosed cases of Mucormycosis.VII.	Ministry of Ayush has released "Ayush Recommendations for the public on holistic health and well-being Preventive measures and care during COVID-19 and LONG COVID-19".(d) & (e) The Ministry of AYUSH is implementing the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of the National AYUSH Mission (NAM) through States/UTs including West Bengal for promotion and development of AYUSH systems of Medicine. State/ UT Governments may avail the eligible financial assistance by submitting the suitable proposal as per NAM guidelines through their State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs). The Mission inter-alia makes following provisions for promotion of AYUSH systems.I.	Co-location of AYUSH facilities at Primary Health Centers (PHCs), Community Health Centers (CHCs) and Districts Hospitals (DHs).II.	Supply of essential drugs to AYUSH hospitals and dispensaries.III.	Up gradation of exclusive/standalone State Government AYUSH Hospitals and Dispensaries.IV.	Setting up of upto 10/30/50 bedded integrated AYUSH Hospital.V.	Infrastructural development of AYUSH under-Graduate and Post Graduate institutions.VI.	Establishment of new AYUSH colleges in the States where availability of AYUSH teaching institutions is inadequate in Government Sector.VII.	Establishment of AYUSH Health & Wellness Centres by upgrading AYUSH Dispensaries and existing Sub Health Centres.         Further, as Public Health is a State subject, the primary responsibility to fill up the posts of AYUSH doctors and maintaining their status comes under the purview of respective State/UT Government including West Bengal. However, under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of the National AYUSH Mission (NAM), the components of establishment of AYUSH Health & Wellness Centres by upgrading AYUSH Dispensaries and existing Sub Health Centres, setting up of upto 50 bedded integrated AYUSH hospitals and upgradation of exclusive standalone State Government AYUSH Hospitals inter-alia are having provision for contractual deployment of AYUSH doctors. The State/UT wise number of doctors approved for deployment under AYUSH Health & Wellness Centres and the number of AYUSH doctors approved for deployment in 50 bedded integrated AYUSH hospitals, upgradation of AYUSH hospitals under Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM) are furnished at Annexure-II (A) & I(B).        In addition, State/UT, hospital and stream-wise details of present staff strength, including doctors working in Research Councils viz. Central Council for Research in Ayurvedic Sciences (CCRAS), Central Council for Research in Unani Medicine (CCRUM), Central Council for Research in Homoeopathy (CCRH) & Central Council for Research in Siddha (CCRS) and All India Institute of Ayurveda (AIIA) under Ministry of Ayush are at Annexure-III.
