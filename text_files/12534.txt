                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1476






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2020






Internal Migration




P.P. Chaudhary













                                                    Kinjarapu Ram Mohan Naidu
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has maintained any updated data on internal migrants in the country and if so, the details thereof, State-wise;(b) whether the data includes other parameters like caste and income data;(c) the major reasons for internal migration in the country;(d) whether the Government has considered maintaining more regular data on internal migration given it’s prevalence and the increasing pressures to migrate due to climate change and loss of livelihood;(e) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor;(f) whether the Government proposes to set up a grievance redressal system for the inter-state migrants; and(g) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) & (b): As per last Census conducted in 2011, the State-wise number of all, Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe migrants due to change in place of last residence is given at Annex. Income data is not collected in Census. (c) to (e): The reasons for migration and number of migrants as per Census 2011 are given below at all India level: -2/…-2-L.S.US.Q.NO. 1476 FOR 11.02.2020S.No	Reasons for Migration	Number of Migrants1.	Marriage	21,11,86,4312.	Moved with household	6,59,59,9153.	Work/Employment	4,14,22,9174.	Moved after birth	3,38,55,8655.	Education	54,57,5566.	Business	35,90,4877.	Other reasons	9,43,14,450In every Census, data on migration are collected for the reasons which are enlisted in Census Population Enumeration (PE) Questionnaire.(f) & (g): Zonal Councils are statutory bodies set up under the States Re-organization Act, 1956 to provide a common meeting ground to the States and UTs in each of the five zones for resolution of inter-state and regional issues, fostering balanced socio-economic regional development and building harmonious Centre-State relations. *****
