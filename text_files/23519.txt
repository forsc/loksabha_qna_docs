New Schemes for Welfare of Senior Citizens




Rajiv Pratap Rudy














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENTLOK SABHAUNSTARRED QUESTION NO.4504 TO BE ANSWERED ON 23.03.2021NEW SCHEMES FOR WELFARE OF SENIOR CITIZENS 4504. SHRI RAJIV PRATAP RUDY: Will the Minister of SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government has announced any new schemes and programmes for the welfare of the elderly during the last five years; (b) if so, the details thereof and the budgetary allocation for the same; (c) whether the Government announces the “Vayoshreshtha Samman” awards every year, if so, the details thereof along with the details of winners during the said period; and (d) the number of beneficiaries of the Rashtriya Vayoshri Yojana so far, State and year-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) & (b): Yes, Sir. The Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment has announced new schemes/programmes for the welfare of senior citizens during the last 5 years. Details of these schemes/ programmes are at Annexure-I. (c):  The Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, in order to recognise the efforts made by eminent senior citizens and institutions involved in rendering distinguished services for the cause of elderly persons, especially indigent senior citizens, confers the “Vayoshreshtha Samman” - National Awards. The Awards are conferred every year as part of celebrations of International Day of Older Persons (IDOP) on 1st October. During 2020 Vayoshreshtha Samman could not be conferred due to COVID-19 pandemic. Details of Awardees during last 5 years are at   Annexure-II.(d):  State and year wise number of beneficiaries of Rashtriya Vayosrhi Yojana are at   Annexure-III.*** Annexure-IAnnexure referred to in reply to parts (a) to (b) in Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No. 4504  regarding “New Schemes for Welfare of Senior Citizens” , to be answered on 23.03.2021New Schemes announced by Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment during last 5 years(i)	Senior Citizens Welfare Fund (SCWF) -	SCWF was announced in the Budget Speech 2015 and notified in the Finance Act 2015. The Department of Economic Affairs is the Custodian of the Fund and Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment is the Nodal Ministry for administration of the Fund. An Inter-Ministerial Committee (IMC) has been constituted for administering the Fund and approving proposals of various Ministries/ Departments to be financed from SCWF. (ii)	Rashtriya Vayoshri Yojana (RVY) – Launched on 01.04.2017, the scheme aims to provide the Senior Citizens, suffering from any of the age related disability/infirmity, with assisted living devices which can restore near normalcy in their bodily functions, overcoming the disability/infirmity manifested such as low vision, hearing impairment, loss of teeth and loco-motor disabilities. The devices are provided to Senior Citizens belonging to BPL Category and Senior Citizens with the monthly income having not more than 15,000/- per Month.	Under RVY assisted living devices like walking sticks, wheelchairs, spectacles, hearing aid, artificial denture, etc. are provided free of cost to eligible senior citizens, in camp mode. Artificial Limbs Manufacturing Corporation (ALIMCO), a Public Sector Undertaking of this Ministry, is the implementing agency for RVY. Funding of RVY is from Senior Citizens Welfare Fund (SCWF). ALIMCO has been provided Rs. 124.01 crore till now for implementing the scheme. Till date a total of 1,68,004 senior citizens have been provided 4,43,171 assisted living devices in 181 camps. (iii)	State Action Plan for Senior Citizens (SAPSrC)The Scheme ofState Action Plan for Senior Citizens   (SAPSrC) was launched in November 2019 and subsumed under the National Action Plan for Senior Citizens. Objective of the Scheme is to provide recognition and opportunity to senior citizens to contribute and thereby feel useful to the society, to improve the quality of life of the Senior Citizens, by encouraging productive and active ageing by the State/ UT Governments through the Institutions of the State/ UT Governments/Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs)/Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRIs) / local bodies and the community at large. The States are expected to put in their own funds to augment the resources available for this purpose. The States/UTs are free to include their own strategies and programmes.	So far a total of Rs. 29.19 crore has been released to various States/ UTs and Rs. 2.50 crore has been released to National Institute of Social Defence (NSID), an autonomous institute under the Ministry.
