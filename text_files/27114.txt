                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2936






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.08.2021






Replacement of Old Aircrafts




Haji Fazlur Rehman














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether several old aircrafts of various airlines companies in the country are still in operation;(b) if so, the details of both Air India and private airlines companies in this regard airlines/ company-wise;(c) the time-limit fixed by the Government to phase out old aircrafts in a phased manner;(d) whether the Government proposes to bring new aircrafts in place of all old aircrafts; and(e) if so, the details of steps taken in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) to (c) The Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) has not prescribed guidelines specifying life for an aircraft to fly in India. There is no term as ''old aircraft'' specified by DGCA. Aircraft are considered airworthy provided the maintenance is as per approved schedule laid down by the manufacturer. The aircraft may be withdrawn from operation by an operator, in case it becomes ''beyond economic repair'' or is ''permanently withdrawn from use'' due to any reason such as spares not available.However, DGCA has laid down age of aircraft intended to be imported in India in Civil Aviation Requirement (CAR), Section F, Part XX - Age of Aircraft to be imported for Scheduled/ Non-Scheduled including Charter, General Aviation and other Operations. (d) & (e)  Does not arise in view of (a) to (c) above.
