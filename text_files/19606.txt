                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

849






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2021






Forest Area Coverage in Bihar




Mahabali Singh














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	the total forest area in Bihar currently; (b)	the total geographical area required to be under forest as per prescribed norms in each district of Bihar; and (c)	the names of all districts of Bihar which have prescribed forest area/do not have prescribed area under forest in accordance with the formally laid down norms/ guidelines?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)  Forest  Survey  of  India,  Dehradun  an organization  under  the  Ministry  carries out the                                                     assessment of forest cover of the country biennially and the findings are published in India State of Forest Report (ISFR). The forest cover assessment is a wall-to-wall mapping exercise based on remote sensing supported by intensive ground verification and field data from National Forest Inventory.As per the latest report i.e. ISFR- 2019, the total forest cover of the Bihar 7,306 square kilometre which is 7.76% of the geographical area of the State. (b)	The National Forest Policy, 1988 provide that the national goal should be to have a minimum of one-third of the total land area of the country under forest or tree cover. In the hills and in mountainous regions, the aim should be to maintain two-third of the area under such cover in order to prevent erosion and land degradation and to ensure the stability of the fragile eco-system. (c)	As per ISFR-2019, the district-wise forest cover of Bihar is given in Annexure.  The forest cover in the district of Jamui (647.95 square kilometre, 69.60% of the total geographical area of the district) and Kaimur (1056.39 square kilometre, 41.12% of the total geographical area of the district) in the State of Bihar is more than 33% of the geographical area of the district. ****
