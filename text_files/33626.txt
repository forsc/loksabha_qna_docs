                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PANCHAYATI RAJ






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1048






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.02.2022






Gram Panchayat Development Scheme




Arvind Ganpat Sawant














                        Will the Minister of





PANCHAYATI RAJ


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the steps taken/being taken to educate the selected representatives in Panchayati Raj Institutes for monitoring the Gram Panchayat Development Scheme; (b)	the total number of training institutes engaged in the training of selected representatives in Panchayati Raj Institutes State/district-wise particularly in the State of Maharashtra; (c)	the details of the number of training sessions conducted in training institutes in each State, the number of representatives selected and the number of women representatives selected who participated in such sessions;(d)	whether methods designed for effectiveness of the training sessions, monitoring of the sessions have been standardized for all States; and (e)	the total number of visits to Adarsh Gram Panchayats by selected representatives and the percentage of women representatives selected in each State during the last five years?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR PANCHAYATI RAJ(SHRI KAPIL MORESHWAR PATIL)(a)	The Ministry under the ongoing Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan (RGSA) has been providing financial support to States/UTs for various activities relating to Capacity Building and Training (CB&T) of Elected Representatives (ERs), functionaries and other stakeholders including training on different aspects of Gram Panchayat Development Plan (GPDP).(b)	The training programmes are conducted by States/UTs through their designated training Institutions, viz State Institute of Rural Development (SIRDs)/ State Panchayat Resource Centre (SPRC)/ Panchayati Raj Training Intuitions (PRTIs)/ Central Training Institute (CTI) etc at state level and through Extension Training Centre (ETCs)/ Panchayat Training Centre (PTCs) /District Panchayat Resource Centre (DPRCs) etc at District/ below State level. SIRDs perform as Apex training Institute at State level. As per the information available, State-wise details training Institute engaged in training of ERs on Panchayati Raj including Maharashtra is at Annexure-I.(c)	Details of training sessions conducted are not maintained centrally. The details of Elected Representatives (ERs) including Elected Women Representatives (EWRs), functionaries and other stakeholders imparted training including GPDP training; state-wise during 2018-19 to 2020-21 under RGSA are given in Annexure-II. (d)	The MoPR has shared with the states detailed National Capability Framework (NCBF) which lays down a comprehensive framework for building the capacity of PRIs and inter-alia provides guidelines for monitoring & evaluation, training programme norms, subjects, duration, target groups & pedagogical software for training. The Framework for implementation (FFI) of RGSA also lays down broad focus and priority areas for training under the scheme with flexibility to evolve training plan as per state specific context in conformity of NCBF and FFI of RGSA. States are also required to upload the training related activities under RGSA on the Training Management Portal, which inter-alia captures progress of trainings in the states. For effective monitoring of progress of approved activities including trainings at MoPR level, an online monitoring and reporting system (MIS) for RGSA has been developed and operationlised. (e)	The scheme of RGSA inter-alia provides support for exposure visits to ERs and functionaries of Panchayats to well performing Panchayats on various thematic areas, situated within and outside the state. Details of exposure visits provided to ERs and Panchayat Functionaries State/UT-wise under the scheme of RGSA during last three years as available with Ministry are given in Annexure-III.***
