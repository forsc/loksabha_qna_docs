                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3982






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2021






Synthetic Yarn




Prataprao  Jadhav














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of handlooms, power looms and other units engaged in manufacturing synthetic yarn, readymade hosiery apparel as on date location-wise; (b) the details of expenditure made by the Government on the development of textile industry during the last three years and till date; and (c) the steps taken/proposed to be taken by the Government to promote the export of textile manufactured by such units?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF TEXTILES(SMT. SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a):	 As per Nielsen baseline survey of Powerloom industry conducted during 2012-13, 24.86 lakh Powerlooms are installed in the country. The location-wise details is in ANNEXURE-I. Further, as per 4th All India Handloom Census (2019-20), there are 28,23,382 handlooms across the country on which handloom cloth/products are manufactured using varieties of yarn. A statement showing state-wise number of handlooms is at ANNEXURE-II. (b):    The details of funds allocation/expenditure under major schemes in the last three years and till date are at ANNEXURE-III.(c):    To boost exports, Government of India has announced a scheme namely "Scheme for Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported products'', (RoDTEP) to all export goods, including textiles and garments with effect from 1st January, 2021. Further, in order to boost exports in Man-Made Fibre (MMF) sector, Government of India has removed anti-dumping duty on PTA, a key raw material for the manufacture of MMF fibre and yarn. Government has also removed anti-dumping duty on acrylic fibre, raw material for yarn and knitwear industry. Assistance is also provided to exporters under Market Access initiative (MAl) Scheme.
