                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4421






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






Setting up of Food Processing Industries




Umesh G. Jadhav














                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether any steps are being taken to promote setting up of food processing industries at locations other than mega food park locations;(b)	if so, the details thereof; and(c)	the proposal for providing capital subsidy to food processing industries at locations other than mega food park locations?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI) (a) & (b): The Ministry of Food Processing industries (MoFPI)  is implementing a Central Sector umbrella scheme- Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana (PMKSY) with 8 component schemes for overall growth and development of food processing sector with an outlay of Rs. 6000 Crore for the period 2016-17 to 2019-20.  The period has since been extended to FY 2020-21.Under the component schemes of PMKSY, MoFPI provides mostly credit linked financial assistance (capital subsidy) in the form of grants-in-aid to entrepreneurs for setting up of food processing / preservation industries. There is no restriction for projects to be located inside Mega Food Parks in the component schemes of PMKSY, except  in the scheme “Creation /Expansion of Food Processing & Preservation Capacities (CEFPPC)”. However, the project proposals, having 100% stake from SC/ST promoter(s) and having eligible project cost of Rs. 1 crore or more, are also considered for approval under CEFPPC scheme irrespective of their locations in the country. (c):	 Financial assistance provided in the form of Grant-in-Aid for setting up of food processing industries varies under the different components of the scheme. The details are placed at Annexure. *****
