                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

432






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.09.2020






Construction/Renovation of Godowns/Warehouses in Rural Areas




 Rahul Ramesh Shewale














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	the Schemes/Programmes implemented by the Government for construction/renovation of godowns/ warehouses in rural areas of various States to enhance the storage capacity of agricultural produce in the country; (b) 	the number of godowns/warehouses constructed/ renovated under the said schemes/programmes during each of the last three years and the current year, State/ UT-wise and Scheme/Programme-wise; (c) 	the details of the funds allocated/ released/ utilised under the said schemes/programmes during the said period, State/UT-wise and Scheme/Programme-wise; (d) 	the cases of irregularities in implementation of the said schemes/programmes that has come to the notice of the Government during the said period, State/ UT-wise and Scheme/Programme-wise along with action taken/being taken thereon; and (e) 	the other steps taken/being taken by the Government to ensure adequate storage capacity of agriculture produce in the country?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????            ( SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)  (a):    Government is implementing Agricultural Marketing Infrastructure (AMI), a sub-scheme of Integrated Scheme for Agricultural Marketing (ISAM) under which assistance is provided for construction/ renovation of godowns/ warehouses in the rural areas of various States to enhance the storage capacity for agriculture produce.  AMI scheme is a demand driven, back ended credit linked subsidy scheme in which rate of subsidy provided is 25% and 33.33% based on the category of eligible beneficiary and provided on capital cost of the project.  Assistance under the sub-scheme is available to individuals, agri-preneurs, group of farmers/growers, registered Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs), cooperatives and state agencies etc.   (b):   The State/UT-wise number of godowns/warehouses assisted under AMI sub-scheme during each of the last three years and the current year is at Annexure-I. (c):   As AMI scheme is demand driven scheme, no State/UT-wise allocation of funds has been made under the scheme. However, the details of funds allocated and released under AMI scheme to the implementing agencies during the last three years and current year is as under: Financial Year	 Funds Allocation (Rs. In Crores)	Funds released to Implementing Agencies (Rs. In Crores)2017-18	486.89	378.322018-19	104.45	55.132019-20	79.04	56.132020-21 Upto 31st August, 2020	213.83	37.60(d):   No case of irregularities in implementation of the said schemes/programmes has come to the notice of Government during the said period. (e)   The Government is implementing Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH) under which assistance is provided for development of post harvest management and marketing infrastructure including cold storage facilities.          The Government is implementing Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana-Remunerative Approaches for Agriculture and Allied Sector Rejuvenation (RKVY-RAFTAAR) Scheme under which assistance is provided for development of marketing infrastructure including storage facilities.        Further, Warehouse Infrastructure Fund (WIF) of National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) envisages extension of low cost loans to Public and Private sectors for construction/modernization of warehouses, silos, cold storages, cold chain infrastructure. In addition to above, the Government has approved a new central sector scheme for Financial Facility under ‘Agriculture Infrastructure Fund’ to provide a medium-long term debt financing facility for investment in viable projects for post harvest management infrastructure including warehouses, silos etc.
