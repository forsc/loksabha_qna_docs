                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

559






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2021






Allocation of Funds under SFURTI




Hemant Tukaram Godse














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details about the allocation of funds under the Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI) for Maharashtra during the current financial year for setting up Common Facility Centres;(b)	the action taken by the Government to facilitate cluster based development to make the traditional industries more productive, profitable as envisaged under this scheme;(c)	whether the Government has also fixed target note through this scheme; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NITIN GADKARI)(a):  	Under the Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI), State-wise allocation of funds is not made. However, so far 20 proposals to set up SFURTI clusters have been approved in the State of Maharashtra with GoI assistance of about Rs.39.26 crore to benefit about 10200 artisans.  9 of these clusters are functional. (b):	Under the SFURTI scheme, traditional artisans and industries are organized into clusters to add value to their production and enhance their income by supporting them in:(i)   Up-gradation of infrastructure by developing Common Facility Centers (CFCs) with latest machineries and creation of Raw Material bank,(ii)   Training and skill development of artisans, exposure visits, product development,(iii)  Developing new market linkages, including e-commerce tie ups,(iv)  Partnerships with institutes of eminence for creation of new designs, etc.(c) & (d):	Between 2014-17 it was proposed to set up 71 SFURTI clusters and during 2018-20, it was proposed to set up 100 SFURTI clusters.    As against this, 335 clusters have been approved, of which 82 clusters are operational.  Rs.785 cr. is committed for these 335 clusters which will benefit about 1.99 lakh artisans.
