                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

743






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Financial Support to MSMEs




Rahul Gandhi














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of financial support announced by the Union Government to the MSME sector to mitigate the impact of COVID -19 pandemic and details of funds sanctioned and actually credited to the account of the beneficiaries;(b)	the details of the outstanding dues of the Union Government and Central Public Sector Undertakings (CPSUs) payable to MSMEs for supply of goods and services as on April 1, 2020; (c)	the details of the measures taken to address the adverse impact of delay in clearance of dues and to ensure the timely clearance in future; (d)	the details of amount paid by Union Government and CPSUs to MSMEs towards clearance of outstanding dues during the period from April-August, 2020; and (e)	whether any suggestions have been received from the MSME Sector seeking specific relief or support and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NITIN GADKARI)(a): 	Government has taken a number of initiatives under Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Abhiyan to support the MSME Sector in the country especially in Covid-19 pandemic. Some of them are: i.	Rs 20,000 crore Subordinate Debt for MSMEs. ii.	Rs. 3 lakh crore Collateral free Automatic Loans under Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) for businesses, including MSMEs. iii.	Rs. 50,000 crore equity infusion through MSME Fund of Funds.iv.	New revised criteria for classification of MSMEs. v.	New Registration of MSMEs through ''Udyam Registration'' for Ease of Doing Business.vi.	 No global tenders for procurement up to Rs. 200 crores, vii.	Scheme for easy access of credit to street vendors. Department of Financial Services which is Nodal Department for ECLGS has  reported that as on 09.09.2020, an amount of Rs. 163,103 crore has been sanctioned to 42,01,060  borrowers and an amount of   Rs. 117,885 crore has been disbursed to  25,01,216 borrowers under Collateral free Automatic Loans.  (b) to (d):  	As per the information available on the Samadhan Portal, the status of  dues of the Union Government and Central Public Sector Undertakings (CPSUs) payable to MSMEs for after Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Package  is under:  The status of MSME dues as on 12.09.2020 are as shown in the table below:<pre>Reported Month	Total Dues by close of month (in Rs. Crore)	Paid during the month (in Rs. Crore)	Pending at the end of month (in Rs. Crore)May 2020 (25 Ministries & 79 CPSEs Reported)	2349.53	1787.89	561.64June 2020 (25 Ministries & 86 CPSEs Reported)	2553.94	1905.11	648.83July 2020 (30 Ministries & 108 CPSEs Reported) 	4124.34	3155.16	969.19August  2020 (25 Ministries & 91 CPSEs Reportedon 12th  Sep., 2020 at 4.00 PM)	3527.33	2696.97	830.36</pre>However, Government has taken many steps to get the dues payable to the MSMEs cleared by the public sector units of the Central Governments. The Ministry has taken up the subject vigorously with the Central Ministries, CPSEs and State Governments and Corporate World. In addition to this Government of India has also taken various steps to resolve the problem of delayed payments to MSMEs which iner-alia include :-i.	Creation of the MSME Samadhaan Portal. ii.	Making it mandatory for all CPSEs to upload the information regarding their dues on the portal.iii.	Issuance of notification requiring all companies to submit half-yearly returns about their dues. iv.	Roping in NeSL Information Utility to do default broadcast of buyers from MSEs to their other creditors. v.	Issuance of direction by notification to companies with turnover above Rs.500 crore and to all CPSEs to get themselves  on boarded on this platformAs reported by the CPSUs and Ministry/Departments of the Government of India in the Samadhan Portal, an amount of Rs 9545 crore has been released in last four months i.e (May-August) to clear the pending dues of MSMEs.(e): 	Ministry of MSME receives suggestions/inputs from individuals as well as organizations on various issues pertaining to MSME ecosystem. Such suggestions/inputs help the Ministry in order to further refine its schemes and make them more effective for the sector.
