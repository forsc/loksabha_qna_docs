                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2006






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.03.2020






National Institute of Criminology and Forensic Science




Sanjay Bhatia














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to establish National Institute of Criminology and Forensic Science in the country including Karnal, Haryana and if so, the details thereof; and(b) the details of such institutes in the country and the number of youth trained at these institutes along with the number of persons who have got employment?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) and (b):	    No such proposal is under consideration.  However, Lok Nayak Jayaprakash Narayan National Institute of Criminology and Forensic Sciences, an Institute set-up by Ministry of Home Affairs, is functional in Rohini, Delhi. The Institute, inter-alia provides training to in-service personnel and also undertakes programme on Master of Arts (Criminology) and Master of Science (Forensic Sciences).A total of 2518 personnel have been trained in the year 2018-19. Further, a total of 47 candidates have passed out in the year 2019 in Master of Arts (Criminology) and Master of Science (Forensic Sciences). Of these, 41 participated in campus placement, and 20 candidates got employed. Further, from the academic session 2019-2020, the Institute has also commenced 3 Post Graduate Diploma Courses of one-year duration on Security Management, Victimology and Victim Assistance, and Investigation of Cyber Crime, Forensic Accounting and Law. ******
