                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

770






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2021






Leprosy and Mental Disease Hospitals




Ashok Mahadeorao Nete














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the number of hospitals providing treatment to children suffering from leprosy and mental diseases in the country and the total number of such hospitals opened during the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise;(b) the funds provided to such hospitals by the Government during the said period;(c) whether foreign aid is also provided to the said hospitals; and(d) if so, the details of foreign aid provided during the last two years?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a):	Medical services for Leprosy are integrated with other general health services, as there should not be any discrimination against patients including children, suffering from leprosy. Under the National Leprosy Eradication Programme (NLEP) under the aegis of National Health Mission (NHM), there are no exclusive government hospitals for treatment of children suffering from leprosy. Children as well as adults suffering from leprosy are treated in the same hospitals where people suffering from other diseases are given treatment. The treatment for uncomplicated leprosy patients is available even at the Primary Health Centre (PHC) level. The entire treatment for leprosy patients is provided free of cost across the board under NLEP. The complicated cases are treated in District Hospitals and Tertiary care hospitals free of cost.Medical services for Mental Health too are integrated with General Health care Services, The National Mental Health Program (NMHP) is also under the aegis of National Health Mission (NHM), There are no exclusive government hospitals for treatment of children suffering from Mental Health illnesses/diseases. Mental health services across the country are provided through the following facilities:•	25 Centres of Excellence (list enclosed as Annexure - A)•	48 Government Mental Hospitals (list enclosed as Annexure - B)•	District Mental Health Programme (692 approved,  list enclosed as Annexure - C)Mental Health Services are a part of comprehensive primary health care under the ambit of Ayushman Bharat Program. The entire treatment for mentally ill patients is provided free of cost across the board under DMHP (District Mental Health Program). The complicated cases are treated in District Hospitals and Tertiary care hospitals free of cost.No such hospitals opened during the last three years and the current year.(b) to (d):  In view of the above position that no hospitals providing treatment to children suffering from leprosy and mental diseases were opened during the last four years in the country, there is no question of providing funds and foreign aid to such hospitals.
