                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEAVY INDUSTRIES AND PUBLIC ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3359






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2021






Manufacturing of EVs




Keshari Devi Patel














                        Will the Minister of





HEAVY INDUSTRIES AND PUBLIC ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the scheme likely to be formulated by the Government with regard to manufacturing and operation of electric two wheeler and four wheeler vehicles;(b)	whether the Government has fixed any time-limit for the operation of electric run vehicles;(c)	if so, the details thereof;(d)	whether any route has been determined for the operation of electric run vehicles; and(e)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR HEAVY INDUSTRIES & PUBLIC ENTERPRISES(SHRI ARJUN RAM MEGHWAL)(a) to (e):	Sir, Department of Heavy Industry (DHI) formulated a scheme namely Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of (Hybrid &) Electric Vehicles in India (FAME India) Scheme in 2015 to promote adoption of electric/hybrid vehicles (xEVs) including electric two wheeler and four wheeler vehicles in India.  At present, Phase-II of FAME India Scheme is being implemented w.e.f. 01st April, 2019 for a period of 3 years with a total budgetary support of Rs. 10,000 crores.  This phase focusses on supporting electrification of public & shared transportation and aims to support, through subsidies, 7090 e-Buses, 5 lakh e-3 Wheelers, 55000 e-4 Wheeler Passenger Cars and 10 lakh e-2 Wheelers. In addition, creation of charging infrastructure is also supported to address range anxiety among users of electric vehicles.  The scheme is implemented on pan India basis.*****
