                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4306






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2019






Land Reserved for Forests




Diya Kumari














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total area of land reserved for forests in the country, State/UT-wise including Rajasthan, location-wise; (b) the total number of people illegally residing on the forest land, location-wise; (c) whether the Government has formulated any scheme for the rehabilitation/regularisation of these villages and if so, the details thereof; and (d) the details of the funds allocated and utilised for the said purpose during the last three years, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)The details of State/UTs wise Recorded Forest Area as published in the India State of Forest Report-2017 is given inAnnexure-I.(b)Details of forest area under encroachment, as per the information provided by the States/UTs, are given in Annexure-II. (c) to (d) There is no scheme for the rehabilitation/regularization of people illegally residing on the forest land. However, as per the provisions of the Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006, claims for “right to hold and live in the forest land under the individual or common occupation for habitation or for self-cultivation for livelihood by a member or members of a forest dwelling Scheduled Tribe or other traditional forest dwellers” is approved by the respective States/UTs.For voluntary relocation of villages inside Wildlife Sanctuaries/National Parks and Tiger Reserves, there are provisions under the Centrally Sponsored Schemes namely ‘Development of Wildlife Habitats’ and ‘Project Tiger’.Details of funds released during last three years under both the schemes are given in Annexure-III and IV.***
