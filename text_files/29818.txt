                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1474






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.12.2021






Increase in Stray Cattle




Arjunlal Meena














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware of major problems of increasing numbers of stray cattle in the State of Rajasthan;(b) if so, the details thereof from past three years for number increase State-wise including the State of Rajasthan district-wise;(c) whether the Government is taking any initiatives or is any budget allocated for active rehabilitations of stray cattle; and(d) if so, details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF  FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING (SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a) As per the information received from the State Government of Rajasthan, the State is aware of the problem of increasing the number of stray cattle in the state. (b)  The information on number of Stray cattle is being collected through Livestock Census which is conducted in every 5 years. In order to provide stray cattle adequate shelter and management, the Directorate of Gopalan, Government of Rajasthan are implementing the following programmes/ schemes:Rs. 40/- per day per head to large cattle and Rs. 20/- per day to small cattle are provided for 180 days period for their maintenance and feeding. Funds provided for this purpose to gaushals by the Rajasthan Government under Cow Promotion and Fund Rules,2016 are as under:Sl. No.	Financial Year	Amount (Rs. in Crore)01	2018-19	240.5702	2019-20	462.4503	2020-21	565.4804	2021-22( till 30.11.2021 )	488.76 The State of Rajasthan provided Rs.446.83 lakh and Rs.90.00 lakh during 2020-21 and 2021-22 respectively to eligible gaushalas for capacity building under Gaushala Development Scheme.In order to provide the shelter to the homeless cattle, Rs. 720.00 lakh were garnted at the rate of Rs. 45 lakh to 16 districts to open the Nandishala at the District level under the Scheme of NandishalaJansahabhagita by the State Government of Rajasthan.Rs. 20.00 lakh has been provided to Shree Gaushala, Padampur, SriGanganagar District for establishment of plant for disposal of Cow Dung under the scheme of Biogas Yojna.For the shelter of male stray cattle roaming on roads, as per the budget announcement by the State Government, the scheme has been approved for establishment of Nandishala at Panchayat level. Directions dated 27.08.2021 has been issued for the implementation of the scheme in the year 2021-22. Provision for Rs. 111.91 Cr. has been made for establishment of 70 Nandishalas in the current financial year.In addition to the above mentioned efforts by the State Government, the Animal Welfare Board of India (AWBI) also provides financial assistance to recognized Gaushalas for feed, fodder, & maintenance of Gaushalas under the scheme of Regular Grants as well as for establishment of Shelter, dispensary etc. under the scheme for provision of Shelter House. *****
