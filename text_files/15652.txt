                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4360






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.03.2020






UN Global Road Safety Week




 Chandra Sekhar Bellana














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government proposes to collaborate with and participate in the fifth UN Global Road Safety Week and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether India’s performance has been unsatisfactory since 2008 with regard to road crash deaths and if so, the details thereof along with the reasons therefor; (c) 	whether India has been successful in reducing road crash deaths under the Brasilia declaration on road safety; and (d) 	if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




  THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS      (SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a)	The Ministry of Road Transport & Highways did not participate in the fifth UN Global Road Safety Week which was held in May, 2019.   (b)       As per the available information, total number of road accidents, fatalities during period 2008 to 2018 are as under:- Year	Total Number of Road Accidents       (in numbers) 	Total Number of Persons Killed              (in numbers) 2008	484,704	119,8602009	486,384	125,6602010	499,628	134,5132011	497,686	142,4852012	490,383	138,2582013	486,476	137,5722014	489,400	139,6712015	501,423	146,1332016	480,652	150,7852017	464,910	147,9132018	467,044	151,417(b)	&    (d)     No Sir. Most of the countries in the World did not achieve the targets under Brasilia declaration on road safety. The 3rd Global Ministerial Conference was held recently in Stockholm, Sweden in February, 2020 to renew the commitment to safer roads.*****
