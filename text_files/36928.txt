                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3960






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






Signboards in Local Languages




Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao













                                                    Debasree Chaudhuri
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number and details of the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) monuments which have boards in    local languages;(b)	the new Indian languages which have been introduced on the boards of ASI sites;(c)	whether it is a fact that the Government is planning to increase the number of Indian languages on the signboards of ASI monuments;(d)	if so, the details thereof, State-wise; and (e)	whether there is any plan to make the information available digitally for tourists and visitors and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF CULTURE, TOURISM AND DEVELOPMENT OF NORTH EASTERN REGION (SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY) (a)  &    (b)	There are 3693 Centrally Protected Monuments/ Sites across the country. Signage in Hindi and English are provided at most of the monuments including World Heritage properties, ticketed monuments and other monuments which are visited by tourists. Local languages used on signages include Assamese, Bengali, Bodhi, Dimasa, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Marathi, Malayalam, Odia, Punjabi, Tamil, Telugu, Urdu, etc. on regional basis.   (c)     & (d)   Since resources have to be used intelligently, we don’t propose to use and increase the boards in other languages other than that of States.(e)    Yes, Sir. A mobile App has been prepared to provide information on all Centrally Protected Monuments, in multiple languages.
