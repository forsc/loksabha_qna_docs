                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3783






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2021






Shortage of Drinking Water




Umesh G. Jadhav














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there is a shortage of drinking water in the country and if so, the details thereof; State-wise including Karnataka; and(b) the measures being taken by the Government to overcome this shortage?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) 	As reported by States/ UTs, as on 15.03.2021, with sources at a reasonable distance, 79.29% rural habitations having 78.24% population have provision of minimum 40 litre per capita per day (lpcd) of potable drinking water and 17.88% rural habitations having 19.27% population have service level of less than 40 lpcd potable water, whereas 2.83% rural habitations having 2.49% of the rural population have water quality issues in ground water sources. State/ UT-wise details are annexed.(b) 	Government of India is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) in partnership with States to enable every rural household to have potable water in adequate quantity and of prescribed quality on regular and long-term basis through tap water connection by 2024. To meet the need of assured potable tap water supply to every household in water-scarce areas, quality affected-habitations, and in areas where dependable local water sources are not available, provisions have been made for planning and implementation of bulk water transfer and regional water supply schemes.Development of reliable drinking water sources and/ or augmentation of existing sources to provide long-term sustainability of water supply system in villages is an integral part of Jal Jeevan Mission. Provisions have been made for the preparation of Village Action Plan (VAP) by village community, which inter alia includes strengthening of drinking water sources in convergence with other schemes such as MGNREGS, Finance Commission grants to rural local bodies/ PRIs, MP & MLA’s Local Area Development Fund, District Mineral Development Fund, CSR fund, etc.****
