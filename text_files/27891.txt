                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3522






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






Data on Manual Scanvengers




Aparajita Sarangi














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENTLOK SABHAUNSTARRED QUESTION NO.3522TO BE ANSWERED ON 10.08.2021DATA ON MANUAL SCANVENGERS 3522. SHRIMATI APARAJITA SARANGI: Will the Minister of SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT be pleased to state: (a) the National data for the number of manual scavengers identified since 2010 in special reference to Odisha; (b) the data for the number of manual scavengers rehabilitated under the self-employment scheme since 2010, pan-India and in Odisha;(c) whether any survey to gauge the number of manual scavengers and rehabilitated persons is conducted periodically, if so, the details thereof of the periodicity and the body conducting it thereof; (d) whether a target is set by the Government to eradicate the practice of manual scavenging in the country and separately for Odisha, if so, details thereof; and (e) whether the abovementioned target set will be met by the Government in reference to the current progress, if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RAMDAS ATHAWALE)(a) & (b): The data of the number of people engaged in cleaning of the insanitary latrines prior to 2013, based on the State-wise survey, is given in the Annexure. In the state of Odisha, 230 people were engaged in manual scavenging before 2013. The details of rehabilitation of these people is given in the Annexure. (c) No periodic survey is undertaken to gauge the number of manual scavengers as it is a banned activity since 6.12.2013 under Sections 5 and 6 of the MS Act, 2013.   As per Section 12 of the MS Act, 2013, any person who claims to be a manual scavenger can apply for rehabilitation.  (d)and (e):  Manual Scavenging as defined under Section 2(1)(g) of the MS Act, 2013 is prohibited w.e.f. 6.12.2013 under Sections 5 and 6 of the Act.  The State Governments have informed that this practice is no longer prevalent in their States. There has been considerable progress after the Swachh Bharat Mission in which 10.72 Cr sanitary toilets in rural areas, and 62.5 lakh toilets in urban areas have been constructed, and the consequent demolition of the insanitary latrines.***** AnnexedAnnexed referred in reply to part (a) of the Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No. 3522 for answer on 10.08.2021 regarding Data on Manual Scanvengers.State-wise details of identified manual scavengers  and rehabilitation benefits provided under the Self Employment Scheme for Rehabilitation of Manual ScavengersS.No.	State/UT	Coverage		Manual Scavengers identified and provided One Time Cash Assistance	Skill Development Training	Capital subsidy for self employment projects and sanitation related projects for manual scavengers including sanitation workers1	A&N Islands	0	0	32	Andhra Pradesh	1793	180	113	Assam	3921	460	04	Bihar	131	18	05	Chhattisgarh	3	0	06	Gujarat	105	19	07	Jharkhand	192	34	08	Karnataka	2927	251	2949	Kerala	518	314	010	Madhya Pradesh	510	98	311	Maharashtra	6325	1332	012	Odisha	230	66	9213	Punjab	231	62	4114	Rajasthan	2673	824	015	Tamilnadu	398	29	7816	Uttar Pradesh	32473	10664	69417	Uttarakhand	4988	1475	7418	West Bengal	680	231	97Total	58098	16057	1387*****
