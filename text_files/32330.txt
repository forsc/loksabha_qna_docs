                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3762






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.12.2021






Subsidy for NHB




Sumedhanand Saraswati













                                                    Sudhakar Tukaram Shrangre
                                                








                                                    Ranjeeta Koli
                                                








                                                    Sunil Kumar Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total number of subsidy applications received and sanctioned by the National Horticulture Board (NHB)for integrated development of commercial horticulture in the country, State-wise  including  Rajasthan;(b) whether the Government has formulated any plan for promotion of post-harvest and cold chain infrastructure for horticulture products and if so, the details thereof; and(c) the fresh steps taken by the Government for simplification of subsidy scheme guidelines, documentation and processing of new applications ?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????  (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) & (b): 	The State-wise details of proposals received and sanctioned by National Horticulture Board (NHB) under the Scheme for “Development of Commercial Horticulture through Production and Post Harvest Management of Horticulture Crops”in the country including Rajasthan during the last three years (i.e. 2018-19 to 2020-21) is given at Annexure-I. 	Under the scheme, financial support in the form of back-ended subsidy is provided for setting up units relating to horticulture sector such as production related projects, Post Harvest Management (PHM) viz. Pack House, Ripening Chamber, Refer Van, Retail Outlets, Pre-cooling units, Primary processing etc.	In addition, NHB is also implementing a Scheme for “Capital Investment Subsidy for Construction /Expansion /Modernization of Cold Storages and Storages for Horticulture Products”. Under this scheme, credit linked back-ended subsidy @35% of the capital cost of the project in general areas and @50% in case of North East, Hilly States & Scheduled areas for Cold storage capacity above 5000 MT and up to 10000 MT is available for construction/ expansion/ modernization of cold storage and CA store.  In case of North EasternStates, the capacity above 1000 MT are also eligible.The State-wise details of proposals received and sanctioned under the above Scheme including Rajasthan during the last three years (i.e. 2018-19 to 2020-21) is given at Annexure-II.(c): 	The steps taken by the Government for simplification of subsidy scheme guidelines, documentation and processing of new applications are as under:1.	The existing 2 stage process of IPA Scheme has been changed to a single stage process for implementation of the scheme w.e.f. 01.04.2020.2.	Allowed sanction of Term Loan within 03 months prior to date of online application for IPA but without any disbursement of Term Loan before issue of IPA to avoid delay in project implementation.3. 	Extension in time limit for completion of projects from existing 18 months upto 24 months on case to case basis in justified cases.4.	Allowed start of preliminary project work i.e.  (i) Land leveling and digging of pits (ii) Compound wall-fencing with gate (iii) Tube well/bore well as a part of project (iv) Root stock plantation with drip in case of grape plantations, after submitting online IPA application using applicant''s margin money.5. 	In case of projects on leased land, minimum lease period of 10 years is now acceptable even if the lease deed was executed with one year prior to the date of online IPA application.	6.	It has been decided to make training of applicants/promotersas optional which was mandatory earlier.
