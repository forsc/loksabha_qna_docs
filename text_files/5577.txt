                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5046






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.07.2019






Optical Fibre between Chennai and Port Blair




Kuldeep Rai Sharma














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the status of laying of optical fibre cable from Chennai to Port Blair as sanctioned by the Government; and (b) 	the time by which the said project is likely to be completed?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF COMMUNICATIONS, LAW & JUSTICE AND ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI RAVI SHANKAR PRASAD) (a) & (b) 	The project for provision of submarine OFC connectivity between Mainland (Chennai) and 8 Islands of Andaman & Nicobar, being funded by USOF and executed by Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL). The work provision of submarine OFC connectivity was awarded by BSNL to M/s NECTI on 26.06.2018. Construction work for 9 Cable Landing Stations (CLS) is under progress by BSNL and expected to be completed progressively between August 2019 to December 2019. Marine and Land survey has been completed by M/s NECTI. The Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) / Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ) clearance has been received from Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change for four Islands in Andaman & Nicobar. The EIA/CRZ clearance for remaining four Islands is awaited from Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change. The project is targeted to be completed by June 2020.
