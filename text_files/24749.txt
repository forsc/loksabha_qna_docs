                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

581






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2021






Chemical Contamination of Drinking Water




Sarmistha Sethi














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the funds allocated under the National Rural Drinking Water programme during each of the last three years; State-wise; (b)	whether 40 per cent of villages of the country are affected by chemical contamination of water; (c)	if so, the details thereof, district and State-wise; and(d) the action taken by the Government in this regard so as to supply safe drinking water to each household?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI (SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) to (d) 	Since August, 2019, Government of India in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) – Har Ghar Jal after subsumingthe then National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP), to provide tap water connection to every rural household by 2024 inter alia which includes quality-affected habitations/ villages.Under JJM, habitations reported to have water quality issues in drinking water sources are given priority. As reported by States/ UTs, as on 20.07.2021, out of 17.01 lakh with sources at a reasonable distance, 13.46 lakh [79.12%] rural habitations have provision of minimum 40 litre per capita per day (lpcd) of potable drinking water and 3.07 lakh [18.07%] rural habitations have service level of less than 40 lpcd potable water and 0.48 lakh [2.81%] rural habitations are reported to have water quality issues in drinking water sources. Out of these 0.48 lakh rural habitations, in 3,732 habitations have been covered through Community Water Purification Plants (CWPPs) as interim measure to provide safe drinking water to every household at rate of 8-10 litre per capita per day to meet their drinking and cooking requirement. District &State/ UT-wise details of remaining 0.44 lakh habitations reported to have water quality issues are at Annex-I.The State/ UT-wise details of funds allocated, released and utilized for last three years i.e. under NRDWP in 2018-19 and under JJM in 2019-20 &2020-21are at Annex-II.*****
