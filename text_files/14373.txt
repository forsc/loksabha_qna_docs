                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2998






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2020






Setting up of Solar PV Power Plants




Ritesh Pandey














                        Will the Minister of





NEW AND RENEWABLE ENERGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has proposed to set up solar PV power plants by Government Producers and if so, the details thereof;(b) the details of financial assistance provided to the companies under the said programme by the Government, company-wise;(c) whether there are any Power Purchase Agreements also involved in manufacturing of solar PV power plants and if so, the details of such companies; and(d) the details of financial share between both the private and public companies to undertake the said project?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (I/C) FOR NEW & RENEWABLE ENERGY, POWER and MoS for  SKILL DEVELOPMENT AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP(SHRI R.K. SINGH)(a) & (b) The Government, through Ministry of New & Renewable Energy (MNRE), is implementing CPSU Scheme Phase-II Scheme (Government Producer Scheme) for setting up grid-connected Solar Photovoltaic (PV) Power Projects through Central Public Sector Undertakings (CPSUs)/ State PSUs/ Government Organisations, with Viability Gap Funding (VGF) support, for self-use or use by Government/ Government entities, either directly or through Distribution Companies (DISCOMS).The details of financial assistance (VGF) for which the successful bidders (Government Producers) are eligible under CPSU Scheme Phase-II, are given at Annexure-I.Under CPSU Scheme Phase-II, till date, MNRE has released Rs. 319 crore to SECI (including SECI’s charges at 1% of VGF). (c) & (d) The Solar Energy Corporation of India, a Central Public Sector Enterprise under the administrative control of Ministry of New & Renewable Energy (MNRE), has recently concluded bidding for a manufacturing linked Solar PV Power tender. The names of the two successful bidders, both private companies, under the said tender are (i) M/s Azure Power India Pvt. Limited; and (ii) M/s Adani Green Energy Four Limited.*****
