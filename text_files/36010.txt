                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COAL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3319






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2022






Functional Coal Mines




Sadashiv Kisan Lokhande














                        Will the Minister of





COAL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the present status of the functional coal mines in the country, State/UTs-wise;(b)	whether the Government proposes to modernize the coal mines and if so, the details thereof, State and mine-wise;(c)	the reasons for not modernising the coal mines of various States of the country including Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh and Andhra Pradesh; and(d)	the time by which all coal mines are likely to be modernised in the country?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS, COAL AND MINES (SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI)(a)	: The total number of functional coal mines in the country as on 31.03.2021 is442. The State/UT-wise details are given below:States/UT	Number of Mines	OpenCast (OC)	UnderGround (UG)	Mixed	TotalArunachal Pradesh	0	0	0	0Assam	2	1	0	3Chhattisgarh	24	28	1	53J & K	0	2	0	2Jharkhand	73	28	13	114Madhya Pradesh	22	37	2	61Maharashtra	38	16	0	54Odisha	23	8	0	31Telangana	19	29	0	48Uttar Pradesh	5	0	0	5West Bengal	20	43	8	71All India	226	192	24	442(b)	to (d):Coal companies are in constant endeavor to modernize the coal mines by adopting mechanisation of its mines, both Underground (UG) and Opencast (OC) mines in large scale.****
