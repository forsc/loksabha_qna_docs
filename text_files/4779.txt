                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4489






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.07.2019






Illegal Trees Cutting and Poaching of Wild Animals




Balubhau (Alias Suresh Narayan) Dhanorkar














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is aware of large scale illegal cutting of trees and poaching of wild animals in Tadoba Tiger reserve; (b)	if so, the details of such incidents reported in the last one year and the number of people arrested in such cases;(c)	whether the Government plans to make stringent rules to stop illegal cutting of trees and poaching; and (d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) & (b) As per information received from the State, no large scale illicit felling or poaching has been reported from the Tadoba-Andhari Tiger Reserve. Due to adequate protection measures, 31 people have been arrested for incidents of illegal felling amounting to 56 cubic meters besides 8 poaching cases including 2 tigers during the last one year.(c) & (d) Illegal felling and poaching are being adequately addressed by stringent provisions of the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972 as amended in 2006 and the Indian Forest Act, 1927. ***
