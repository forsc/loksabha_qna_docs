                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2480






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2019






Impact of Rural Development Schemes




Adhir Ranjan Chowdhury














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of villages and families covered under various rural development schemes along with the future projects/schemes, State/UT-wise;(b)	whether various schemes aimed at improving the lives of rural people have yielded the desired results, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor along with the Government’s reaction thereto;(c)	the details of implementation reports of various pro poor schemes in the country and the monitoring mechanism available with the Government to ensure that the objectives of the rural development schemes are achieved;(d)	whether the Government has any clear cut definition or criteria for rural development and if so, the details thereof; and(e)	whether the Government proposes to fix the criteria/definition in this regard within a definite time-frame and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)	:	All the villages in the country are covered under various rural development schemes. For development of rural areas through employment generation, strengthening of livelihood opportunities, promoting self-employment, skilling of rural youths, provision of social assistance, infrastructure development etc., the Ministry of Rural Development is implementing Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS), Deen Dayal Upadhyay – Gramin Kaushalya Yojana (DDU – GKY), Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana – Gramin (PMAY-G), Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY), Shyama Prasad Mukharjee RuRBAN Mission, National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP) and Sansad Adarsh Gram Yojana (SAGY) in the country including tribal areas. The State/UT wise details of number of households/villages/GPs benefited under different rural development schemes are placed at Annexure I(a) to I(g).(b)	& (c) : In order to ensure the programme benefits reach the rural poor of the country, the Ministry has evolved a comprehensive multi-level and multi-tool system of monitoring and evaluation of the implementation of rural development programmes, including Performance Review Committee Meetings, District Development Co-ordination and Monitoring Committee named as “DISHA” Committee meetings, National Level Monitors, Area Officers Schemes, Common Review Mission, Concurrent Evaluation and Impact Assessment Studies. While the comprehensive multi-level and multi-tool system of monitoring and evaluation over the year indicate successful implementation and desirable outcomes, it also brought out opportunity for mid-course correction for the Ministry.  Third-Party evaluation of Ministry of Rural Development’s Schemes is also carried out regularly and actions, as appropriate, are taken on the findings. Review of implementation of various Rural Development programmes is a continuous process. (d)	& (e): Poverty is multidimensional. Rural Development programmes and schemes are aimed at addressing multidimensional poverty through evidence based selection of areas and beneficiaries. Different Rural Development programmes are implemented based on their respective guidelines/framework for implementation. *****
