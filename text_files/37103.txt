                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ATOMIC ENERGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4438






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.03.2022






Integrated Perimeter Protection Projects




 Dr. T. Sumathy (a) Thamizhachi Thangapandian














                        Will the Minister of





ATOMIC ENERGY


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the Government has initiated the Integrated Perimeter Protection projects in various atomic and nuclear power stations of Nuclear Power Corporation of India (NPCIL) including KAPS, MAPS atKalpakkam and KKNP 1&2, 3&4 at Koodankulam in Tamil Nadu; and(b)	if so, the details thereof including the projects executed during the last seven years along with their value?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR PERSONNEL, PUBLIC GRIEVANCES & PENSIONS AND PRIME MINISTER’S OFFICE (DR.JITENDRA SINGH):    (a)	Yes, Sir. (b)	Integrated Perimeter Protection Systems have been implemented in all operating nuclear power stations from MAPS upto KKNPP 1&2 (MAPS 1&2, NAPS 1&2, KAPS 1&2, KGS 1&2, KGS 3&4, RAPS 3&4, RAPS 5&6, TAPS 3&4 and KKNPP 1&2). Further the systems are also upgraded from time to time as per the requirements specified by the Atomic Energy Regulatory Board (AERB). In respect of TAPS 1&2 and RAPS 1&2, the Integrated Perimeter Protection project has been initiated.The expenditure incurred on implementation/upgradation of Integrated Perimeter Protection Systems carried out during the last seven years including the current year is about Rs. 11.54 crore.*****
