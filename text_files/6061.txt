                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5614






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

26.07.2019






Shortage of Doctors in PHCs




Uday Pratap Singh














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government has paid attention towards fulfilling the huge shortage of doctors in Public Health Centres (PHCs) operated across the country, if so, the details thereof and corrective steps taken to improve the doctor-patient ratio particularly in the rural areas; (b) whether any scheme is under consideration of the Government in this regard, if so, the details thereof; (c) whether there is any existing scheme or proposal to upgrade the facilities at PHCs, if so, the details thereof; (d) whether any survey/assessment has been conducted regarding rural health centres during the last few years and the current year; and (e) if so, the details thereof and the reaction of the Government thereto?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b):  Public Health and Hospitals being a State Subject, all the administrative and personnel matters, including that of recruitment of doctors at Primary Health Centres (PHCs), lies with the State Governments. Also, Shortage of doctors in public health facilities varies from State to State depending upon their policies and context. However, as per Rural Health Statistics (RHS) – 2018, State/UT wise details of availability of doctors at PHCs and shortage thereof is given at Annexure-I.Under National Health Mission (NHM), financial and technical support is provided to States/UTs to strengthen their healthcare systems including support for recruitment of doctors on contractual basis, based on the requirements posed by them in their Programme Implementation Plans (PIPs) within their overall resource envelope.Under NHM, States are encouraged to adopt flexible norms for engaging doctors for public health facilities. Financial support is also provided to States for providing performance-based incentives, hard area allowance, providing accommodation and transport facilities in rural and remote areas, etc for engaging doctors in the public health facilities.Also, States are advised to put in place transparent policies of posting and transfer, and ensure rational deployment of healthcare professionals. State/UT Governments are impressed upon from time to time to fill up the vacant posts including of doctors.(c):	As per the budget announcement 2017-18, 1.5 lakh Sub Health Centres and Primary Health Centres are being transformed into Health and Wellness Centres across the country under Ayushman Bharat (AB-HWCs) for provision of comprehensive primary care that includes preventive healthcare and health promotion at the community level with continuum of care approach.The AB-HWCs will provide an expanded range of services to include care for non - communicable diseases, palliative and rehabilitative care, Oral, Eye and ENT care, mental health and first level care for emergencies and trauma as well as health promotion and wellness activities like Yoga apart from services already being provided for maternal and child health including immunization and communicable diseases.(d) & (e):	Rural Health Statistics (RHS) is an annual publication, based on Health care administrative data generated by the health care system. RHS provides reliable and updated information on rural health infrastructure and requirements of public health facilities in rural areas. As per the RHS-2018, the state-wise details of shortfall of health facilities including PHCs, infrastructural facilities available and status of shortage/ vacancy position of human resources are at Annexure-II to Annexure-VII.Further, Government has also a system of PHC Grading through which PHCs are monitored periodically.  Grading of PHC is done based on the information uploaded by States/UTs on the Health Management Information System (HMIS) portal. The details of the State/UT-wise number of PHCs graded on a scale of 0-5 and NE (Not Eligible) is placed at Annexure-VIII.
