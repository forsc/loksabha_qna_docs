                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2774






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






New Metro Rail Projects in Maharashtra




Rajan Baburao  Vichare














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Maharashtra Government has recently accorded cabinet approval to three metro routes from Gaimukh to Kashmira (Mira-Bhayandar) and Kalyan-Taloja (Navi Mumbai) including Wadala-CSMT (Mumbai) and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	the time by which and the place from where the said work will actually to get started; (c)	the amount of funds sanctioned for these projects;(d)	the time by which these projects are likely to be completed?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)	 to (d): Government of Maharashtra (GoM) has informed that three metro routes namely, Mumbai Metro Line-10 from Gaimukh to Shivaji Chowk (9.209 km), Mumbai Metro Line-11 from Wadala to Chattrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (12.774 km) and Mumbai Metro Line-12 from Kalyan to Taloja (20.756 km) have been approved vide GoM Resolution dated 6th September, 2019 at completion costs of Rs. 4,476 crore, Rs. 8,739 crore and Rs. 5,865 crore, respectively. Implementation of above mentioned metro routes is in initial planning stage and are likely to be completed by the years 2023, 2026 and 2024, respectively.     GoM has approved above metro routes under State Sector. Government of India has not sanctioned any funds towards these projects.                                                       *****
