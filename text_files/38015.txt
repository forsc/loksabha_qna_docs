                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5258






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.04.2022






Smart Cards to Labourers




Anubhav Mohanty














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)the total number of labourers in the unorganized sector in the Country; (b)the total number of smart cards that have been issued to the labourers in the unorganized sector under the Rashtriya Swasthya Beema Yojana; (c)whether in the absence of the smart cards the labourers are unable to use the facilities of the schemes that the Government has launched for their benefit; and (d)if so, the time by which the Government proposes to complete the process of issuing the smart cards to each one of the labourers from the unorganized sector and the details of the plan to complete the process with the deadline?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a):	 As per the Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) 2017-18 carried out by the National Sample Survey Organisation of the Ministry of Statistics &  Programme Implementation, estimated 38 crore people are working as unorganised workers in the country.  (b):	  Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri – Jan Arogya Yojana (AB PM-JAY) was launched on 23rd September, 2018 subsuming the Rashtriya Swasthya Beema Yojana to provide Rs. 5 lakh health coverage per family for secondary and tertiary hospitalization  free of  cost to the  eligible  labourers  in   the Contd..2/-:: 2 ::unorganised sector as per the criteria identified from the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) of 2011. This scheme is being implemented in all States/UTs except Delhi, Odisha and West Bengal.  As on 29.03.2022, a total of 17.90 crore individuals have been verified and issued with smart cards.(c):	 All eligible beneficiaries are entitled to free healthcare services under AB PM-JAY from the day of the implementation of the scheme in their State/UT. Beneficiaries can visit the empanelled hospitals to verify their eligibility and avail healthcare services. The empanelled hospitals subsequently raise pre-authorization requests for the treatment proposed to be given to the verified beneficiaries.(d):	  Issuance of AB PM-JAY smart card is a continuous process based on the demand for services and eligibility criteria identified from the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) of 2011. However, Government is making all-out efforts to scale-up the issuance of Ayushman cards across the country.********
