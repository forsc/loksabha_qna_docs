                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4383






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.03.2020






Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram




Shriniwas Dadasaheb Patil













                                                    Amol Ramsing Kolhe
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                








                                                    Manickam Tagore B
                                                








                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government is implementing Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram in the country, if so, the details thereof and achievement made since its inception; (b) whether the Government has made any assessment of the scheme through social audit; (c) if so, the details thereof and the outcome of the assessment; (d) the details of the number of children covered since the inception of the scheme, State/UT-wise along with the funds allocated and utilized during each of the last three years; (e) whether the Government has taken any steps for children identified with any health condition to be referred to an appropriate health facility for further management, if so, the details thereof; and (f) whether the Government proposes to set up high-end labs in Central Government controlled hospitals/ health centres for early detection of genetic disorders and other diseases through prenatal diagnosis and if so, the details thereof along with the funds allocated for the purpose?






ANSWER




                                                         ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to (e):    Government of India is implementing Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) under National Health Mission since 2013, for screening of all the children from 0-18 years of age for 4 D’s i.e. Disease, Defects at birth, Deficiencies, Development Delays including Disability.A total of 30 health conditions have been identified for further management at designated health facilities under the programmeContd…….-2-The screening services are provided through dedicated Mobile Health Teams placed in every block. These teams screen children in the age group 0 – 6 years twice a year at Anganwadi Centers while children enrolled in Government and Government aided schools from class 1st to class 12th are screened once a year. The programme is regularly monitored through quarterly review of reports, field visits and periodic meetings with state nodal officers. The technical support and financial support under National Health Mission is provided based on the gap analysis and proposals submitted by States in their annual Programme Implementation Plans.The details of children screened, fund allocated and utilized is placed at Annexures I   and II. (f):    There is no proposal for consideration at present to set up high-end labs in Central Government controlled hospitals/health centres for early detection of genetic disorders and other diseases through prenatal diagnosis.
