                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

495






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.06.2019






Control of Livestock Diseases




Rodmal Nagar













                                                    Ravindra Shyamnarayan alias Ravi Kishan Shukla
                                                








                                                    Raksha Nikhil Khadse
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	whether the Government proposes to make available any subsidy for setting up of Dairy industries for the purpose of increasing the milk production, keeping in view the shortage of milk and milk products in the country, and specifically to increase the participation of women in this sector; b)	if so, the details thereof;c)	whether the level of participation of women is likely to increase through this scheme; andd)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING (Dr. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a) & (b) The Department of Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing dairy development schemes namely National Programme for Dairy Development (NPDD), Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS) and Dairy Processing and Infrastructure Development Fund (DIDF) aiming at providing financial assistance for setting up of dairy industry across the country with the purpose of promoting growth of dairy sector including increasing milk production. These schemes provide for supporting farmers’ participation through enrolling them under Dairy Cooperative Societies (DCS) and increasing women farmers in cooperative dairy sector. The brief outline of above-mentioned schemes is Annexed. (c) & (d) Above dairy development schemes envisage to increase participation of women in Indian dairy sector. With implementation of Central and State Plan Schemes, around 4.9 million women members of DCS have been benefitted till March, 2018. Further, under DEDS as on 31.03.2019, 96085 women entrepreneurs have been benefitted.
