                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

506






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2021






Swachh Bharat Mission




Sunita Duggal














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)  The number of cities that have been certified as Open Defecation Free (ODF), ODF +, ODF ++, State-wise; (b) The number of household toilets and community/public toilets constructed or under construction under Swachh Bharat Mission during the last five years, State/year-wise; and(c) Whether the Government has launched any scheme for elimination of Single Use Plastic, air pollution and soil pollution and if so, the details thereof ? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                  (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a) : The State-wise break-up of Open Defecation Free (ODF), ODF+ and ODF++ certified cities is at Annexure-I.(b) : The state-wise break-up of total toilets constructed and under-construction in urban areas as on 31.06.2021 under Swachh Bharat Mission is at Annexure-II. Year-wise data, however, is not maintained.(c) : Government has launched National Clean Air Programme (NCAP) as a long-term, time-bound, national level strategy to tackle air pollution across the country in a comprehensive manner with targets to achieve 20% to 30% reduction in PM 10 and PM 2.5 level by 2024. The Plastic Waste Management Rules, 2016, prohibits manufacture, import, stocking, distribution, sale and use of carry bags and plastic sheets less than fifty microns in thickness in the country. The Solid Waste Management Rules 2016, notified by Ministry of Environment & Climate Change (MoEF&CC), inter-alia, addresses the issues relating to soil pollution in the country.Finance Minister in her budget speech has announced implementation of Swachh Bharat Mission 2.0, with a total financial allocation of ?1,41,678 Crore, over a period of five years from 2021-2026, addressing, inter alia, issues relating to single use plastic, air and soil pollution.                                            *****
