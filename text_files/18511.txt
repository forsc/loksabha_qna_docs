                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2261






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.09.2020






Poshan Abhiyaan




Jayant Sinha














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    the current levels of malnutrition, stunting, wasting, anemia among children between 6-59 months and anemia among women and adolescent girls in the age 15-49 years across the country, specially the targets set to overcome the same in Jharkhand, district-wise; and(b)      the nature of convergence across different schemes run by the Government with the Poshan Abhiyaan?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a) The status of malnutrition, stunting, wasting, anemia among children between 6-59 months and anemia among women and adolescent girls in the age 15-49 years in the country amongst children and adolescent girls is covered under the National Family Health Survey (NFHS) conducted periodically by the Ministry of Health & Family Welfare.  The details of status of national level and State of Jharkhand are as under:Indicators	NFHS-42015-16(National level)	NFHS-42015-16(Jharkhand)Wasting 	21%	29%Stunting	38.4%	45.3%Under-weight	35.8%	47.8%Anaemia in Children under 5 years	58.6%	68.9%Anaemia in women and adolescent girls in the age 15-49 years 	53.1%	65.2% District-wise status of Malnutrition and Anaemia of State of Jharkhand is at Annexure.  Target of POSHAN Abhiyaan  is  to reduce the level of stunting, under-weight and low birth weight among children @ 2% per annum and reduce the level  of anemia among children, Adolescent girls and women @ 3% per annum.  (b)      POSHAN Abhiyaan envisages convergence of various schemes and programmes for addressing malnutrition. The Anganwadi Services, Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana, Scheme for Adolescent Girls schemes of Ministry of Women & Child Development; Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY), National Health Mission (NHM) of Ministry of Health & Family Welfare; Swachh Bharat Mission of Ministry of Jal Shakti; Public Distribution System (PDS) of Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food & Public Distribution; Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS) of Ministry of Rural Development (MoRD); are some such schematic interventions.   Convergent approach of service delivery in partnership with other partner Ministries i.e.  Ministry of Panchayati Raj; Ministry of Tribal Affairs, NITI Aayog, Ministry of Education, Ministry of  Electronics and Information Technology, Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change, Ministry of New and Renewable Energy, Ministry of AYUSH, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare etc is also mandated under POSHAN Abhiyaan. Convergence at State, district and block levels is ensured under the Abhiyaan, through State level Convergence meeting on Quarterly basis with line departments of State chaired by Chief Secretary; District level Convergence meeting with line departments chaired by District Magistrate; and Block level Convergence meeting with line departments chaired by Sub-Divisional Magistrate. *****  Statement referred in reply to part (a) of Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No.2261 for 23.09.2020AnnexureDistrict-wise status of Malnutrition and Anaemia of Jharkhand (Source: NFHS 4)Districts	Children under 5 years who are underweight (weight-for age)	Children under 5 years who are stunted (height-for age)	Children under 5 years who are wasted (weight-for-height)	All women age 15-49 years who are anaemic	Children age 6 to 59 months who are anaemic (<11.0g/DL %)Bokaro	61.3	45.9	43.8	78.1	74.0Chatra	52.3	50.9	30.7	56.7	60.6Deoghar	46.3	45.7	23.4	57.4	64.6Dhanbad	45.0	41.0	31.2	70.6	69.9Dumka	54.3	44.6	42.1	65.6	74.9Garhwa	51.2	46.2	31.9	60.4	65.4Giridih	40.5	45.2	23.4	69.9	74.4Godda	47.1	55.1	25.6	71.8	80.7Gumla	48.6	46.5	32.0	70.4	73.8Hazaribagh	46.9	51.3	23.6	61.6	64.3Jamtara	49.9	43.6	29.9	65.7	73.7Khunti	54.9	40.8	43.8	64.8	64.8Kodarma	40.6	43.6	19.7	63.9	72.4Latehar	45.3	44.9	29.5	53.2	50.1Lohardaga	50.6	43.8	30.7	67.9	64.9Pakur	45.3	51.5	23.8	71.4	74.3Palamu	46.1	46.3	24.7	52.7	59.5Paschimi Singhbhum	67.5	62.9	37.9	75.4	83.8Purbi Singhbhum	62.6	50.2	48.0	74.8	66.5Ramgarh	52.3	44.9	29.7	70.2	71.3Ranchi	51.7	51.9	28.6	73.8	64.8Sahibganj	48.7	49.5	25.0	62.2	70.4Saraikela Kharsawan	56.0	47.1	24.2	83.4	81.9Simdega	49.6	40.0	37.7	78.9	80.0
