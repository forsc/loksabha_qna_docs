                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3229






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2022






Skill of Traditional Artisans




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether any steps are being taken to ensure traditional craftsmanship is passed on to the next generation or interested artisans; and (b) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR TEXTILES  (SMT. DARSHANA JARDOSH)(a) & (b):	Yes Sir, to ensure that traditional craftsmanship is passed on to the next generation or interested artisans, handicrafts training programmes in the National Skill Qualification Framework (NSQF) compliant courses under the component Skill Development in Handicrafts Sector Scheme of National Handicrafts Development Program (NHDP) of the office of the Development Commissioner (Handicrafts), Ministry of Textiles are being organised.Further, Ministry of Textiles is also implementing the Scheme for capacity building in textile sector also known as SAMARTH. Under this scheme, demand driven, placement oriented National Skill Qualification Framework (NSQF) compliant skilling programmes are imparted to create jobs in the textile related sector including traditional sector of handloom, handicrafts, sericulture & jute to enable provision of sustainable livelihood either by wage or self-employment.***
