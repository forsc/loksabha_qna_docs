                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3787






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2020






Releasing of Sugar Subsidy to Karnataka




Shobha Karandlaje














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of sugar subsidy released to the State of Karnataka during the last three financial years, year-wise; (b) 	the details of sugar subsidy yet to be released to the State of Karnataka; (c) 	whether the Government has reviewed the sugar subsidy scheme and if so, the details thereof; (d) 	whether it is a fact that the Government has reduced sugar subsidy being given to the States during the last two years; and (e) 	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                         MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a) & ((b):  		The State of Karnataka is not participating in the current sugar subsidy scheme effective from 01.06.2017. However, they had participated in earlier sugar subsidy scheme effective from June, 2013. Details of sugar subsidy released to Karnataka during last three financial years is as under:-(Rs. in Cr.)2017-18	2018-19	2019-204.79	Nil	Nil..........2/--2-(c), (d) & (e): 	The National Food Security Act (NFSA), 2013, is now being universally implemented in the entire country. There is no indentified category of Below Poverty Line (BPL) under the NFSA, 2013. However, the Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) beneficiaries are clearly identified. The Government has reviewed the sugar subsidy scheme in May, 2017 and has decided to continue it only for AAY families w.e.f. 01.06.2017. The Central Government is reimbursing a fixed subsidy of Rs. 18.50 per kg. per month per AAY family to the participating States/UTs. The States/UTs are allowed either to absorb the additional cost on account of handling, transportation and dealer’s commission or pass it on to the consumers by adding it to the Retail Issue Price (RIP) of Rs. 13.50 per kg under the Public Distribution System (PDS).****
