                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3055






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.08.2021






Forest Area




Jaswantsinh Sumanbhai Bhabhor













                                                    Devji Mansingram Patel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the forest cover area is declining due to the rising level of pollution in cities and urbanization;(b) if so, the details thereof and the steps taken by the Government to conserve forests on non-forest land, defined forest land and forests covered under the Assisted Natural Regeneration;(c) whether the special package has been announced under CAMPA fund to create the employment opportunities in the tribal areas of the country including Gujarat and Rajasthan, if so, the details thereof and the total fund released and utilised, State/UT-wise;(d) whether funds are being misused by the officials and forest department in absence of direct hold of gram sabha and local representatives on the CAMPA fund and if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise; and (e) whether the Government proposes to make the panchayats and gram sabha accountable for promoting afforestation and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY): (a) & (b)   Forest cover in country is increasing. There is an increase of 13,209 square kilometer of forest and tree cover at the national level in the ISFR-2019 as compared to the ISFR- 2015. Forest Survey of India (FSI), Dehradun, an organization under the Ministry carries out the assessment of forest cover of the country biennially since 1987 and the findings are published in India State of Forest Report (ISFR). The forest cover assessment is a wall-to-wall mapping exercise based on remote sensing supported by intensive ground verification and field data from National Forest Inventory.  As per ISFR 2019, the total forest and tree cover of the country is 8,07,276 square kilometers which is 24.56 % of the geographical area of the country. The State/UTs wise details of forest and tree cover as per ISFR-2019 is given in Annexure-I.  The State/UTs wise details of change in forest and tree cover as compared with ISFR-2019 and ISFR-2015 is given in Annexure-II. (c)  On 12.05.2020, Hon’ble Prime Minister announced a special economic package of Rs. 20,00,000 crore under Atma Nirbhar Bharat. This package also included Rs. 6,000 crore employment push using CAMPA funds to created job opportunities including for tribal communities in semi-urban and rural areas. Against this, the National Compensatory Afforestation Fund Management and Planning Authority (National Authority) has approved Annual Plan of Operation (APOs) of 31 States/UTs amounting to Rs. 7906.16 crores as on 31st March, 2021. As per information received from States/UTs 8,04,45,886 man-days have been generated till 31.03.2021 from 31 States/UTs. The State/UTs wise details are given in Annexure-III. (d) All complaints received in the National Authority are forwarded to the concerned States Government /UTs Administration for enquiry. In addition, the State Government UT Administrations also take action on their own on the complaints received by them and after enquiry, take necessary action including initiation of disciplinary proceedings against the guilty personnel (e) The forestry activities including afforestation programmes of the Government are undertaken in consultation as well as with active participation of local people/Panchayats/Gram Sabha/Village Forest Management Committee. **********
