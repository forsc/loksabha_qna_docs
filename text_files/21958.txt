                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    STEEL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3143






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Captive Mines




Kinjarapu Ram Mohan Naidu














                        Will the Minister of





STEEL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether all the steel PSUs have captive mines for iron ore and coal and if so, the details thereof;(b)	if not, the details of those steel PSUs which do not have captive mines along with the source of raw materials for them;(c)	the difference in cost of iron ore if a steel PSU does not have captive mines as compared to that having captive mines;(d)	whether the Government has any plans to allot captive mines to Rashtriya Ispat Nigam Limited and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STEEL			   (SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a)&(b): The details of the Steel PSUs which have captive mines for iron ore and coal and those which do not have captive mines are annexed. The demand of iron ore / coal of those CPSEs which do not have captive mines is met through domestic production / imports. (c)	The difference in cost of iron ore for Steel PSUs having captive mine and those not having captive mines may vary depending on various parameters viz location of the end use steel plant, location of the mines, logistic costs, levies imposed by the concerned State Governments, iron ore price etc.(d)	Rashtriya Ispat Nigam Limited (RINL) has requested various State Governments viz. Odisha, Chhattisgarh and Andhra Pradesh for recommending reservation of iron ore deposit under section 17A(2A) of MMDR Act, 2015 to the Ministry of Mines, Govt. of India. Ministry of Steel has also requested Government of Odisha for reservation of an iron ore block in favour of RINL.********
