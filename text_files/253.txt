                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

297






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.06.2019






Recovery of Bad Debts




Gitaben Vajesingbhai Rathva














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government proposes any special schemes to recover the bad debts through selling of equity of the closed companies;(b)	if so, the details thereof, company-wise;(c)	the details of restructuring process of the debt recovery scheme; and(d)	the manner in which the Government will comply with the Banking Regulation Act on such takeover process?






ANSWER




                                                        FINANCE MINISTER (a) to (d): Recovery and resolution under existing mechanisms include sale of pledged equity of defaulting borrowing companies by their lenders, and for conversion of defaulting borrowing company’s debt into equity (which may subsequently be sold) in name of the lenders, as per approved resolution plan, subject to guidelines issued by Reserve Bank of India. No special scheme for selling of equity of closed companies is under consideration.***
