                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3298






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.03.2020






Forest Area in Chhattisgarh




Arun Sao














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the total notified forest area in Chhattisgarh; (b) 	the details of the measures being taken for development, promotion and conservation of forest areas in the said State; (c) 	whether large scale encroachment and mining activities in forest areas of the State cause damage to environment, if so, the details thereof along with the reaction of the Government thereto; (d) 	whether the members of Forest Protection Committees have reported incidents of deforestation, if so, the details thereof; and (e) 	the action taken/likely to be taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)& (b) As per information received from the P.C.C.F. Chhattisgarh, the total notified forest area in Chhattisgarh is 59772.40 square kilometre.In the year 2019-20, under Hariyar Chhattisgarh Yojana, a total of 2.64 crores plants have been planted in the rainy season to increase the green cover of the State. To rehabilitate the open forests of the State, an area of 48, 812 ha has been treated under the Rehabilitation of Degraded Forests (RDF) scheme which included bamboo degraded forests. Likewise, to prevent erosion and to stabilize the banks of major river (Kharun, Arpa, Sankri and Indravati) of the State, an area of 546.68 ha has been treated with 6.01 lakh plants in the year 2019.Under the State CAMPA, an outlay to the tune of Rs. 500 Crore is sanctioned for the year 2019-20, through which activities related to forest development and conservation, wildlife protection, management and conservation, Infrastructure development, strengthening of forest roads inside forest villages, establishing hi-tech plantations and biodiversity parks, measures to control human-wildlife conflict in elephant inhabited areas are taken up as forest promotion measures in the State of Chhattisgarh. Similarly, for the year 2020-21, a target of 53,010ha of area is proposed to be treated in the open forests, under RDF schemes.(c) Minor incidents of encroachment of small forest area of the State aggregating to 354.735 ha by 705 encroachers in 272 cases have been reported during the last one year. As a measure of control, in 322 (old and new) cases, an area of 422.496 ha of forest land was evacuated from 1010 encroachers. Under the Forest (Conservation) Act 1980, an area of 14,147.311 ha of forest land in 78 cases of mining have been used for non-forestry purposes, as reported by the P.C.C.F. Chhattisgarh.(d) 	As reported by the P.C.C.F. Chhattisgarh, Forest Protection Committees have not reported any incidents deforestation.(e)		For Protection, one (1) Strike Force at the headquarter, six (6) at Circle, 34 at Division and 207 at Range level have been constituted for regular patrolling of forest. Regular beat inspection of 3,506 beats is being done by forest staff and a total of 14,112 concrete and permanent boundary pillars have also been constructed last year to check encroachments in the State of Chhattisgarh.*****
