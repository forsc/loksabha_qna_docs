                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

220

19.07.2021






Hike in Fuel Prices




 Dr. T. Sumathy (a) Thamizhachi Thangapandian













                                                    Nusrat Jahan Ruhi
                                                








                                                    Malook Nagar
                                                








                                                    Rajendra Dhedya  Gavit

                                                    S.R. Parthiban
                                                








                                                    Mahesh Sahoo
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of unprecedented price rise of petrol, diesel and LPG during the last three yearsand current year along with the reasons for hike in the prices despite second wave of Covid-19 pandemic and steps taken control/to uniform the prices of petroleum products across thecountry;(b) the details of effect of price rise on the consumers in terms of GDP and inflation rate, ifso, the steps taken to bring down the prices of these products along with policy to control theprices?(c) the details of the details of revenue and taxes generated by the Government through thesale of petrol, diesel and LPG in the country during the last three years, year-wise and monthwisealong with the usage of funds earned from petroleum products;(d) the details of impacts on common people due to rise in the prices of petrol, diesel andLPG cooking gas including the policies adopted by the Government to bring down it;(e) the details of increase/decrease in the price of crude oil in international market and thenumber of times the price of petroleum products came down/reduced in domestic marketduring the said period and pass on benefits to the consumers in the country, year-wise;(f) the details of percentage of crude imported to meet the demand along with the mechanismadopted by the Government to examine the prices of petrol and diesel fixed by variouspetroleum companies including private companies in conformity with the variation in theinternational crude oil prices; and(g) the details of new domestic cooking gas connections provided by the various marketingcompanies during the last one year, State-wise and month-wise?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a) to (e): The details of daily Retail Selling Prices (RSP) of petrol and diesel since01.01.2018 in four metro cities viz. Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata and Chennai are available at thewebsite of Petroleum Planning and Analysis Cell (PPAC) i.e. www.ppac.gov.in.Prices of petrol and diesel are market-determined with effect from 26.06.2010 and 19.10.2014respectively. Since then, the Public Sector Oil Marketing Companies (OMCs) have been takingappropriate decision on pricing of petrol and diesel on the basis of international product pricesand other market conditions. The OMCs have increased and decreased the prices of petrol anddiesel according to changes in international prices and rupee dollar exchange rate. Effective16th June, 2017, daily pricing of petrol and diesel has been implemented in the entire country.The prices of petrol in the States across the country varies due to freight rates, VAT/local leviesetc.The impact of increase in prices of petrol and diesel can be seen in their impact on inflationarytrend measured by Wholesale Price Index (WPI). The weightage of petrol, diesel and LPG inthe WPI index is 1.60% , 3.10% and 0.64% respectively.The details of total central excise duty including cesses on petrol and diesel and details centralexcise collected from petroleum products in the last three years are at Annexure-1.The average monthly price of Indian Basket of crude oil since April, 2018 is at Annexure-2.The details of the number of times the petrol/diesel prices have been changed in the last threeyears and current year at Delhi is at Annexure- 3.The State wise actual rates of VAT on petrol and diesel as on 1st April 2021are given asAnnexure-4.(f): The percentage details (%) of import dependency of crude based on consumption ofpetroleum products during the last three years and current year are as below:2018-19 2019-20 2020-21 (P) 2021-22(April-May) (P)83.8% 85.0% 84.4% 85.4%Based on data from 16 major oil & gas companies(g): Statewise data pertaining to new LPG domestic connections released by PSU OMCsduring the year 2020-21 is enclosed as Annexure - 5.x-x-x-x-x
