                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

811






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2022






Van Gram Vikas Yojana




Hemant Patil














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total number of forest villages located in the protected areas of the country under Van Gram Vikas Yojana in the country, especially in Maharashtra; (b)	whether the Government has received any proposal from the Government of Maharashtra for implementation of Van Gram Vikas Yojana in Hingoli, Nanded and Yavatmal district of the State; (c)	if so, the details thereof and works done in those villages under the said Yojana; (d)	 the details of funds released/used during the last three years and the current year; and (e)	the status of progress made and development done in these villages during the said period?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) The Ministry of Tribal Affairs had implemented a programme from 2005-06 to 2011-12 as a one-time measure for integrated development of forest villages with a view to raise the Human Development Index (HDI) of the inhabitants of the forest villages and for providing basic facilities and services in 2,847 forest villages/habitations that were covered under the scheme spread over twelve States in the country. The programme was implemented as a part of the special Area Programme ‘Special Central Assistance to Tribal Sub-Plan’. The programme included infrastructure works relating to basic services and facilities viz., approach roads, healthcare, primary education, minor irrigation, rain water harvesting, drinking water, sanitation, community halls etc. and activities related to income generation. The number of the forest villages in forest areas and villages inside the core/critical tiger habitat in the country, State-wise, including Maharashtra, is given at Annexure. (b) As informed by Maharashtra State Forest Department, no proposal for implementation of Van Gram Vikas Yojana has been submitted to Government of India by the State Government. (c)to(e) Question does not arise.*****
