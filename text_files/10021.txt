                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PANCHAYATI RAJ






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

313






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2019






Panchayati  Raj Institutions




Kanakmal Katara














                        Will the Minister of





PANCHAYATI RAJ


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has conducted any survey to assess the functioning of Panchayati Raj Institutions in the country;(b)if so, the details thereof; (c) whether the Government had prepared a proposal for providing online services and computerization of all Panchayats across the country; and(d)if so, the details thereof along with the details of the funds sanctioned in this regard during the last five years and the current year, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF PANCHAYATI RAJ(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)	to (d) A Statement is laid on the Table of the House. STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 313 ANSWERED ON 10.12.2019 REGARDING PANCHAYATI RAJ INSTITUTIONS(a)and (b) The Ministry of Panchayati Raj (MoPR) has  carried out  studies in the form of ‘Panchayat Devolution Index’ from year 2006 onwards, through independent institutions, to assess and rank each State on the parameter of devolution of Funds, Functions and Functionaries working in Panchayats. Twenty six States and six Union Territories (UTs) participated in the Panchayat Devolution Index study done by Tata Institute of Social Sciences, Mumbai for the year 2015-16. States/UTs ranking provided in the TISS study are at Annexure-I.(c)and (d)MoPR has developed a suite of core common software applications, collectively known as Panchayat Enterprise Suite (PES) Applications to aid Panchayats to undertake functions like planning, budgeting, implementation, accounting, monitoring and delivery of citizen services. ‘Panchayat’ being a State subject, providing infrastructure facilities including Computers and its peripherals to the Panchayats is primarily the responsibility of the State / UTs Governments. This Ministry, under its erstwhile scheme of Rajiv Gandhi Panchayat Sashaktikaran Abhiyan (RGPSA) implemented during year 2012-13 to  year 2015-16 had provided support to States/UTs for e-enablement of Panchayats including computers and peripheral for Gram Panchayats (GPs). Under the restructured scheme of Rashtriya Gram Swaraj Abhiyan (RGSA), being implemented with effect from year 2018-19, for developing governance capabilities of Panchayati Raj Institutions, MoPR has inter-alia provided financial support to States/ UTs for computers and peripherals on a limited scale. Fourteenth Finance Commission (FFC) grant to GPs has also 10% Operation and Maintenance provision which can be utilised by the Gram Panchayats also for computer and peripheral. The details of GPs with and without computers is at Annexure-II.BharatNet, a Project of Department of Telecommunication (DOT) to provide broadband connectivity to all the GPs in the country is under implementation by Bharat Broadband Network Limited (BBNL). As per information available as on date 06.12.2019, in 1,25,512 GPs are broadband service ready. The details of GPs State/UTs wise is at Annexure-III.	Common Service Centres (CSC) to provide online citizen services are set up by the CSC e-Governance Services India Limited an organisation under Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MEITY). As per information received from MEITY as on 31.10.2019, 2,68,398 Common Service Centres (CSC) are functional at GP level for delivering online services. The details of GPs States/UTs wise are at Annexure-IV. Further towards colocation of CSC in Gram Panchayats buildings, the MoPR has entered into a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with CSC e-Governance Services India Limited for providing online services in GPs. 	The State-wise details of funds sanctioned for e-enablement under the erstwhile schemes of RGPSA and Capacity Building Panchayat Sashktikaran Abhiyan (CBPSA) during year 2014-15 to year 2017-18 and fund sanctioned for computers during the year 2018-19 and year 2019-20 till date under RGSA is at Annexure-V. Finance Commission grants to States from year 2014-15 to year 2019-20 till date are in Annexure-VI.
