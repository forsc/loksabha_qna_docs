                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4265






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.03.2022






Sub-Mission on Agro-forestry




Dhal Singh Bisen














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)     whether the Ministry is operating a Sub-mission on Agro-forestry (SMAF) since 2016-17 for plantation of timber wood or firewood with an aim to increase the income of farmers, create employment and earning opportunities; (b)    if so, the details the States where this scheme is being implemented along with the criteria thereof, State-wise; and  (c)     whether this scheme is currently running in Madhya Pradesh and if so, the district-wise number and details of beneficiaries during the last five years?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????      (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):   Yes Sir. Sub-Mission on Agroforestry Scheme is being implemented by the Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare since 2016-17 to encourage tree plantation on farm land along with crops/ cropping systems to help the farmers get additional income and make their farming systems more climate resilient and adaptive. Multipurpose tree species like Sheesam, Teak, Ecalyptus, Neem, Ardu, Poplar, etc. are promoted. (b):  The scheme is presently implemented in 21 States viz. Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Jharkhand, Karnataka, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Odisha, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, U.P., Mizoram, Manipur, Meghalaya, Nagaland and 2 UTs viz. J&K and Ladakh with funding pattern of 60:40 between Centre and State Govt. for all States excepting NE & Hilly states, where it is 90:10 and 100% in case of UTs & National Level Agencies. The scheme is implemented in the States having liberalized transit-regulations for transport of timber. (c):    The scheme is currently implemented in Madhya Pradesh also. The District wise detail of beneficiaries in the State is attached at Annexure-I.
