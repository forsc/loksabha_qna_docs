                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1544






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.12.2021






Pradhan Mantri Matsaya Sampada Yojana in Maharashtra




Vinayak Bhaurao Raut














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the new projects taken up under the Pradhan Mantri Matsaya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY) in Maharashtra; (b)	whether any steps have been taken for seaweed development and special fisheries export ports; and (c)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor? 






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING		(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a) to (c):  The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing a flagship scheme Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY) with highest ever investment of Rs.20,050 crore  for a period of five years with effect from the financial year 2020-21 to 2024-25 in all States/Union Territories(UTs) including Maharashtra. Development of Seaweed is one of thrust areas envisaged under the PMMSY. Besides, the PMMSY also focuses on development/modernization of fishing harbours, fish landing centres and an investment of Rs.3490 crore has been envisaged under the PMMSY for this purpose.    Under the PMMSY, the Department of Fisheries, Government of India during last financial year (2020-21) and current year (2021-22) has accorded approval to the fisheries development proposals of Government of Maharashtra at a total cost of Rs.395.38 crore. The approved project activities also include development of seaweed culture through installation of 2000 Nos of seaweed rafts and 800 Nos monoline/tubenets with requisite inputs. The Department of Fisheries under the Blue Revolution Scheme implemented during 2015-16 to 2019-20, accorded approval to the proposals of Government of Maharashtra for development of two fishing harbours namely Karanja in Raigad District and Anandwadi in Shindhudurg District and modernization of existing fishing harbour at Sassoon Dock, Mumbai at a total cost of Rs.290.41 crore and central funds of Rs.103.80 crore has been released to the State Government for development. *****
