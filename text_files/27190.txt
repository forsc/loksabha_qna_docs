                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2909






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.08.2021






Tap Water Access




Sushil Kumar Singh














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether 10 million homes in encephalitis hot spots will be getting tap water access through Jal Jeevan Mission; (b) 	if so, the details thereof;(c) 	the peculiar features of the mission;(d) 	the details of the States that have performed better as compared to their counterparts in providing  tapped water to households; and (e) 	the details of the States where the overall percentage of tap water connection are still low?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER  OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)& (b) 	Government of India in partnership with States, is implementing Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) to make provision of potable tap water supply to every rural household in the country, by 2024, inter alia which includes all households in JE–AES affected districts, on priority. As reported by 5 affected States, as on 02.08.2021, details of rural households having tap water supply in 61 (then 60) Japanese Encephalitis–Acute Encephalitis Syndrome (JE–AES) affected priority districts of 5 States, namely Assam, Bihar, Tamil Nadu, Uttar Pradesh and West Bengal, is at Annex–I.(c)	Jal Jeevan Mission is a decentralized, demand-driven and community-managed programme. Water supply system are to be operated and maintained by the Gram Panchayat or its sub-committee i.e. Village Water & Sanitation Committee (VWSC) or Pani Samiti, having 10–15 members with 50% women and proportionate representation to weaker sections of the village. Village Action Plan of each village is to be prepared which includes strengthening of drinking water sources, in-village water supply system, grey water management, and operation & maintenance for long-term water supply.(d) & (e) 	State/ UT–wise details of rural households having provision of tap water supply and progress made in providing tap water connections to households since launch of JJM, is at Annex–II. The same is in public domain and available on the JJM dashboard at:https://ejalshakti.gov.in/jjmreport/JJMIndia.aspx*****
