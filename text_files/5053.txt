                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4788






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2019






Student-Teacher Ratio in Engineering Colleges




Lavu Sri Krishna Devarayalu














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of engineering students passing out annually, State-wise;(b) whether as per AICTE, student teacher ratio in engineering colleges should be 20:1;(c) whether the Government ensures that this ratio is strictly being followed by EngineeringColleges;(d) whether all engineering colleges are maintaining required faculty and if so, the details thereof;(e) whether there are any technical teacher training institutes in the country;(f) if so, the details thereof and whether there is any proposal to start a technical teacher traininginstitute in Andhra Pradesh and if so, the details thereof; and(g) if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): The state-wise details of total number of students (Under Graduate), who passed out ofEngineering Colleges approved by All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), in theAcademic Year 2017-18 is at annexure.(b) to (d) : As per AICTE norms, the Faculty Student ratio from the academic year 2018-19 is 1:20,which is a mandatory requirement. The institutes give details of the faculty under self-declarationevery year. However, to ensure compliance with the norms of Faculty Student ratio, AICTE conductssurprise visits to the institutes for Physical Verification of faculty and takes appropriate action againsterring institutes as per AICTE norms.(e) to (g): The Government has established four National Institutes of Technical Teachers’ Trainingand Research (NITTTRs) in the country at Chandigarh, Bhopal, Kolkata and Chennai. There is noproposal to establish another NITTTR in Andhra Pradesh, since NITTTR, Chennai is already cateringto the need of training teachers in the region. Besides, one of the extension centres of NITTTR,Chennai is located at Vijaywada in Andhra Pradesh. AICTE has decided to open AICTE Training andLearning (ATAL) Academies at Jaipur, Vadodara, Thiruvananthapuram and Guwahati. AndhraPradesh Govt. has also shown keen interest in setting up ATAL Academy in Andhra Pradesh.****
