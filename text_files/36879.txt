                                    TRIBAL AFFAIRS

                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4123






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






Vanbandhu Kalyan Yojana




Jyotsna Charandas Mahant














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	the schemes being run in the country for the overall development of the tribal people;b)	if so, the details thereof;c)	whether the Vanbandhu Kalyan Yojana launchedfor the purpose of delivery of services to the tribalpeople is in currency;d)	if so, the details of its functioning; ande)	the details of work undertaken for the promotionand preservation of tribal cultural heritage under theVanbandhu Kalyan Yojana in Chhattisgarh?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) & (b):Government has adopted integrated approach for welfare and development of tribal people across the country. Tribal Sub-Plan (TSP), now called as Scheduled Tribe Component (STC) is a dedicated source of fund for provision of support for education, health, agriculture, livelihood, housing, drinking water & sanitation, employment generation, skill development, women and child development, preservation of cultural heritage etc. 40 Central Ministries / Departments apart from Ministry of Tribal Affairs (MoTA) has been mandated to earmark Tribal Sub-Plan funds in the range of 4.3% to 17.5% of their total Scheme allocation every year for overall tribal development. Details of funds allocated by different Central Ministries / Departments under their schemes are reflected in the Union Budget as Statement 10B which is laid on the table of the House. State-wise details of total funds released for the welfare of tribals during the last three years under the STC of Central Ministries / Departments is at Annexure - 1.Details of funds allocated and spent by States / UTs under their Schemes for welfare and development of STs during 2018-19 to 2020-21 is at Annexure 2. (c) & (d): Ministry of Tribal Affairs had launched the scheme of ‘Vanbandhu Kalyan Yojana (VKY)’ on 28.10.2014 for holistic development of tribal people across the country. During the financial year 2014-15, an amount of Rs. 100.00 crore was allocated under VKY. Out of the total budgetary allocation, an amount of Rs. 10.00 Crores were provided to each of the ten States having Schedule V areas as gap filling for carrying out activities commensurating the objective of the Scheme.  During the financial year 2015-16, the gamut of the Scheme was extended to cover all the States having tribal population. Out of the total budgetary allocation to the tune of Rs. 200.00 Crore, funds were released to 21 States against their proposed activities. In 2016-17, token provision of Rs. 1.00 crore was released to State Government of Rajasthan. Since 2016-17, separate allocation of funds under VKY has been discontinued and it has been envisaged that the fund requirement under the VKY strategy will be fulfilled out of the TSP [now called as Schedule Tribe Component(STC) at central level] funds, which caters to sectoral development including support for education, health, power, roads, sanitation, water supply, livelihood etc.During 2016-17 to 2020-21, the Vanbandhu Kalyan Yojna was in operation comprising of following component schemes with financial support given as under:(Rs. in lakh)S.No.	Name of the Scheme	2016-17	2017-18	2018-19	2019-20	2020-211. This scheme adopts a habitat level development approach to improve the quality of life through intervention in housing, drinking water, education and health services, livelihood support, and cultural aspects.5	 	Pradhan Mantri Adi Adarsh Gram Yojna (PMAAGY)	The Scheme ‘Pradhan Mantri Adi Adarsh Gram Yojna (PMAAGY)’ has the coverage of all States and UTs having notified ST population. It envisions to transform 36,428 villages having at least 50 % tribal population and 500 STs into model villages (adarsh gram) during 2021-22 to 2025-26 by way of appropriate convergence of resources available with Central Government Ministries / Department and State Government meant for Scheduled Tribes. The scheme intends to support creation and improvement of infrastructure facilities in identified villages in priority sectors like health and nutrition, drinking water and sanitation, education, road and telecom / mobile connectivity. The scheme has the provision to provide a sum of Rs. 20.38 lakh per village to the State Governments / UTs as ‘gap-filling’ for approved activities including administrative expenses.6		Administrative Cost to States/ UTs for setting up PMU	The State PMU looks after all scheme and non-scheme initiatives under separate budgetary head within the Ministry and provide hand holding support to State PMUs. Under the above mentioned schemes, various projects related to Education, Health& Sanitation, Drinking water, Agriculture, Animal Husbandry, Connectivity, Skill Development, Scholarship, promotion and preservation of tribal cultural heritage etc. are considered for approval followed by release of funds as per scheme guidelines and other relevant financial norms / instructions in vogue.   (e):MoTA extends support to the State Governments through centrally sponsored Scheme of‘Support to Tribal Research Institute (TRI)’ including TRI, Chhattisgarh inter-alia for research &documentation activities and training &capacity building programmes,  Organization of tribal festivals/ yatras for promotion of unique cultural heritage and for promoting tourism and organization of "exchange visits by tribals", so that the tribal culture practices,languages and ritual are preserved and disseminated.  Under this scheme, MoTA has sanctioned a project for setting up of tribal freedom fighters museums in different parts of our country including Chhattisgarh to acknowledge the heroic and patriotic deeds of tribal people who struggled against the Britishers and refused to be bowed down so that the coming generations may know how tribals were involved in making sacrifices for the nation: (?.
