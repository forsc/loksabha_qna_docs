                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4300






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.03.2022






Expenditure on Pre/Post-Matric and NOS scholarships




D. Ravikumar














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENTLOK SABHAUNSTARRED QUESTION NO.4300TO BE ANSWERED ON 29.03.2022EXPENDITURE ON PRE/POST-MATRIC AND NOS SCHOLARSHIPS 4300. SHRI RAVIKUMAR D.: Will the Minister of SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT be pleased to state: (a) the total expenditure incurred on Pre-Matric, Post-Matric and National Overseas Scholarships for SC students during the last five years, year/State-wise; and (b) the reasons behind the new guidelines imposed by the Government on the overseas scholarship of SC students?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI A. NARAYANASWAMY)(a): The State-wise and year-wise details of total expenditure incurred on Post-Matric Scholarship and Pre-Matric Scholarship schemes for SC students in the last five years are attached as Annexure.Under the Central Sector Scheme of National Overseas Scholarship (NOS) scheme, there is no state-wise distribution of funds. Expenditure details of NOS during the last completed 5 years are attached as Annexure.(b):  This Department reviews the NOS scheme guidelines from time to time and has carried out many significant changes for simplifying the procedures & proper implementation of NOS scheme.  This year also Revisions were carried out after due consideration within the Ministry.  The number of seats have been increased from 100 to 125 from FY 2021-22. The Budget outlay has also increased to Rs 35.00 cr. and  in the recent years, unlike earlier, a larger percentage of awardees have traveled abroad under the scheme.*****Annexure1.
