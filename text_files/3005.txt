                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

255






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.07.2019






Telecommunication Facilities




Gowdar Mallikarjunappa Siddeshwara














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether telecommunication facilities are not available in a large number of villages in Karnataka; (b) 	if so, the details thereof along with the number of such villages in the State and the reasons therefor; and (c) 	the steps taken/being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF COMMUNICATIONS, LAW & JUSTICE AND ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI RAVI SHANKAR PRASAD)(a) to (c)	 A statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT TO BE LAID ON THE TABLE OF LOK SABHA IN RESPECT OF PARTS (a) to (c) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *255 FOR 10TH JULY, 2019  ON “TELECOMMUNICATION FACILITIES”.(a) to (c)  As per data collected from the Telecom Service Providers (TSPs) in 2018, it was estimated that out of 27,397 inhabited villages (as per the Census 2011) in the State of Karnataka, there were 869 inhabited villages which are not covered by mobile services. District-wise details are at Annexure. The reasons for not having mobile services in these villages include, inter-alia, the remote and tough terrain & areas diversely located with scattered population. Mobile coverage in such villages is being taken up by the Government and Telecom Service Providers in a phased manner. As per the information received from the Department of Telecommunication’s field unit, 273 incremental villages have been covered by mobile services in Karnataka during last one year and now there are 596 inhabited villages remains which are not covered by mobile services. Further, under the BharatNet project in Karnataka, 14010 Kms of Optical Fibre Cable have been laid in 6242 Gram Panchayats (including Block Head Quarters (BHQs)) out of which 6166 Gram Panchayats (including BHQs) are service ready for broadband connectivity.
