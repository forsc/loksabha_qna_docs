                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3180






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2020






Loss of Air India




Anil Firojiya













                                                    Lallu Singh
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether Air India has suffered a loss of Rs. 102 crores and if so, the reasons therefor;(b) the reasons for frequent delay in flights of Air India, now a days; and(c) whether any step has been taken to avoid frequent delay in flights and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a):  As per the audited accounts of Air India Ltd  for FY 2018-19, the company has suffered a Net Loss of Rs 8556.36 crore. However, the total expenses incurred by Air India Limited in FY 2018-19 on account of catering, hotel expenses, denied boarding compensation to passenger on delayed/cancelled flights were about Rs.102 crore.;;(b):  Some of the major reasons for the delays to Air  India flights include:;(i) Engineering related issues,(ii) Crew related issues,(iii)Ground Handling issues,(iv)Air Traffic Control delays, and(v) Air India being a network carrier operating on Hub and Spoke pattern, some of the flights are delayed to connect passengers with the international flights;;(c):  In order to alleviate delays, DGCA has issued Air  Transport Circular 05 of 2017 titled "Procedure to be followed to mitigate flight delays" in order to ensure enforcement of the flight Schedules.
