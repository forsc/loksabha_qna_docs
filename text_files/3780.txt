                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3479






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Courses in Central University




Balashowry Vallabbhaneni














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Central University in Anantapur district of Andhra Pradesh has started its academiccourses, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether only six courses at graduate and post graduate level are being offered;(c) if so, the time by which the University proposes to start courses in other courses, M.Phil andPh.D;(d) whether classes are being run in transit campus and if so, the details thereof; and(e) the details of funds allocated, sanctioned, released and spent so far in constructing its own campusand providing infrastructure and other facilities?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(Shri Ramesh Pokhriyal ''Nishank'')(a)to (e): University of Hyderabad, the mentor institute for Central University of Andhra Pradesh hasinformed that Central University of Andhra Pradesh has started its academic activities from 2018-19session. At present the University offers following courses:<pre>i) B.Sc. (Hons.) Economicsii) B.A. (Hons.) Political Scienceiii) B.Voc. Tourism and Travel Managementiv) B.Voc. Retail Management and ITv) M.A. Teluguvi) M.A. English Language and Literaturevii) M.A. Hindi</pre>The classes were started from a transit campus at Jawaharlal Nehru Technical University, Anantapur.Out of allocated amount of Rs.10.00 Crore, an amount of Rs. 8.00 Crore has been released in 2018-19out of which the University has spent Rs. 2.46 Crore towards the transit campus expenditure. For FY2019-20 a sum of Rs. 13 Crore has been allocated. Construction of permanent campus has not started.There is a provision of Rs. 450 Crore for meeting the first phase expenditure.*****
