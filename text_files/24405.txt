                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

341






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.07.2021






Beneficiaries under PDS




S.R. Parthiban














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the names of the commodities allocated to the States by the Union Government through Public Distribution System (PDS) during the last three years, the details thereof; (b) 	the number of households along with the number of beneficiaries who have received these commodities through PDS; and (c) 	the rates at which each household receives commodities through PDS along with the details of the commodities?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E RMINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT AND CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a) to (c):  The Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS) is governed as per provisions of the National Food Security Act, 2013 (NFSA) which provides for all India coverage of upto 75% of the rural population and upto 50% of the urban population which at Census 2011 come to 81.35 crore persons, for receiving subsidized foodgrains (Rice, Wheat and Coarsegrains). Coverage under the Act is under two categories- households covered under Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) and the priority households (PHH) to be identified by the State Governments/ Union Territories (UT) Administrations as per criteria evolved by them, within the coverage limit determined for the State/UT. Under the Act, while Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) households, are entitled to 35 kg of foodgrains per family per month, priority households are entitled to 5 kg of foodgrains per person per month at uniform subsidized prices of Rs. 3/2/1 per kg for rice/ wheat/ coarse grains respectively.
