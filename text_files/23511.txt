                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4460






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






Indian Sign Language




Vincent H Pala














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 whether the Indian Sign Language Research and Training Centre has signed an MoU with the Ministry of Education for release of NCERT material in sign language; (b) 	if so, the funds sanctioned for the same as well as the proposed timeline of completion; (b)	 the details of funds allocated for the development of Indian Sign Language for the year 2021-22; (d) 	the number of schools or institutions teaching Indian sign language and the funds allocated for the promotion  of such institutions during the last two years, State and year-wise; and (e) 	the details and the number of awareness drives conducted across the country to promote the use of Indian sign language? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI KRISHANPAL GURJAR)(a)  to (b) Yes, Sir. The Indian Sign Language Research and Training Centre (ISLRTC), New Delhi  signed an MoU with National Council of Educational Research & Training (NCERT) on 06.10.2020 to convert the textbooks of classes I-XII  and other teaching material into Indian Sign Language (ISL). No separate funds have been sanctioned for the same and the proposed timeline to complete the conversion of the textbooks and other teaching material into ISL is 2026.  (c) Government has set up the ISLRTC to develop Indian Sign Language. During 2020-21, Rs.3.88 crore has been released to ISLRTC as Grant-in-Aid. For 2021-22, Grant-in-Aid would be provided to the ISLRTC as per requirement from the budget provision for National Institutes under this Ministry.   (d) Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI), New Delhi has granted approval to 08 Institutions to conduct Diploma in Indian Sign Language Interpretation (DISLI) and Diploma in Teaching Indian Sign Language (DTISL) Courses as per State-wise list of these Institutions at Annexure. Out of these 08 Institutions, Grant-in-Aid has been provided to the following 03 Institutes by this Ministry:-Name of the Institutes 	Grant-in-Aid  released during the last two years (Rs. in crore)	2018-19	2019-20Ali Yavar Jung National Institute for Speech & Hearing Disabilities (AYJNISHD), Mumbai.	38.30	35.71Indian Sign Language Research & Training Centre (ISLRTC), New Delhi.	4.85	4.20Composite Regional Centre, Nagpur.	1.63	8.42  (e) ISLRTC promotes the use of Indian Sign Language (ISL) through various activities which include, conducting Diploma courses and Short-Term Training Programmes, organizing workshops and webinars, holding ISL competition for deaf students and celebrating International week of the Deaf.   *****
