                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

191






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.08.2021






Schemes to Develop India as Tourism Hub




Nihal Chand Chauhan














                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the various schemes being implemented by the Government with the objective of developing India as a tourism hub, State/UT-wise; (b) 	the budget earmarked for the said schemes along with the amount spent thereon during the last three years; (c) 	the steps being taken by the Government to ensure adequate security of foreign tourists in the country; and (d) 	the number of foreign tourists who visited India during the last three years and the number of cases of misbehaving with foreigners reported during the said period along with the action taken thereon?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF TOURISM 			        (SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (d): A statement is laid on the table of the House. ********** STATEMENT IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (d) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.†*191 ANSWERED ON 02.08.2021 REGARDING SCHEMES TO DEVELOP INDIA AS TOURISM HUB.(a):	The major schemes of the Ministry of Tourism applicable in all States/Union Territories for developing India as a tourism hub are:1)	Integrated Development of Theme Based Tourist Circuits – Swadesh Darshan2)	Pilgrimage Rejuvenation and Spiritual, Heritage Augmentation Drive (PRASHAD)3)	Assistance to Central Agencies for Tourism Infrastructure Development4)	Domestic Promotion and Publicity including Hospitality (DPPH)5)	Restructured Scheme of Overseas Promotion & Publicity including Marketing Development Assistance (MDA)6)	Financial Assistance to the IHMs/FCIs/IITTM/ICI/NCHMCT/ITIs/Polytechnic Institutes/Universities/Govt. Colleges/Govt. Vocational Schools/PSUs etc. 7)	Capacity Building for Service Providers 8)	Champion Services Sectors Scheme9)	Market Research Professional Services (MRPS)10)	Safe Tourist Destination for Women(b):	The total budget earmarked for the various schemes along with the amount spent thereon during the last three years is given at Annexure-I.  (c):	Safety and security of tourists is a State subject. However, Ministry of Tourism has taken several measures to ensure the safety and security of tourists which are:i)	24x7 Toll Free Multi-Lingual Tourist Info-Helpline in 12 Languages to provide support service in terms of information and guidance to tourists.ii)	The Ministry of Tourism has taken up the matter with all State Governments/Union Territory (UT) Administrations for setting up dedicated Tourism Police. With the efforts of Ministry of Tourism, the State Governments/UT Administrations of Andhra Pradesh, Delhi, Goa, Karnataka, Kerala, Maharashtra, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir, Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Punjab, Rajasthan, Sikkim and Uttar Pradesh have deployed tourist police in one form or the other.iii)	The Ministry of Tourism along with all stakeholders, including the Tourism Departments of all States and UTs, have adopted the ''Code of Conduct for Safe & Honourable Tourism'' which is a set of guidelines to encourage undertaking of tourism activities with respect for basic rights like dignity, safety and freedom from exploitation of both tourists and local residents in particular, women and children.iv)	Ministry of Tourism got a study conducted by Indian Institute of Tourism & Travel Management (IITTM), an autonomous institute under its control, to understand the requirement of tourist police and sensitizing the tourist police so created towards the needs of the tourists. Ministry of Tourism forwarded a copy of this Study report titled “Functioning of Tourist Police in States/UTs & Documentation of Best Practices” to all the State Governments/UT Administrations with the request to create a separate unit of police in the respective states/UT and also to sensitize them about the needs of the tourists.(d):	Details of Foreign Tourist Arrivals (FTAs) during the years 2018 to 2020 are given below:<pre>Year	FTAs in India (in Million)2018	10.562019	10.932020	2.74</pre>	The information received from National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) regarding the cases registered, cases charge-sheeted, cases convicted, persons arrested, persons charge-sheeted and persons convicted under crime against foreigners (including foreign tourists) during 2017-2019 is given at Annexure-II.********
