                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1722






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.03.2020






Ambedkar Navodaya Vidyalayas




Sarmistha Sethi













                                                    Suresh Kodikunnil
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there is any proposal to set up Ambedkar Navodaya Vidyalayas inthe lines of Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas to provide quality education tomeritorious poor and dalit students;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) the number of such schools are proposed to be set up in Odisha;(d) whether the Government is considering toestablish 150 Ambedkar NavodayaVidyalayas, on thelines of the Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya, for studentsbelonging to the Scheduled Caste (SC) community and if so, the detailsthereof;(e) whether the Government intends to establish Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayasin Kerala; and(f) if so, whether the Government received any proposal indicating thewillingness from the Kerala Government in this regard and if so, the detailsthereof?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (d): The Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment has informed that anew scheme, namely, ‘Ambedkar Navodaya Vidayalayas (ANV)’ for setting up ofresidential schools for Scheduled Castes (SCs) across the country is proposed to beintroduced. Under the scheme, 150 co-educational residential schools for SCs forclass VI – XII, on the lines of Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas, have been proposed tobe set up in districts having large concentration of SC population. The scheme aimsto nurture and promote the talent of the meritorious students belonging to ScheduledCastes by providing best opportunities and resources to boost overall improvementof their life and to compete with the other students for higher education leading tobetter choice of profession and better employment opportunities to them. Thescheme is, however, yet to be approved.(e) & (f) : The Navodaya Vidyalaya Scheme provides for opening of one JawaharNavodaya Vidyalaya (JNV) in each district of the country. All the 14 districts ofKerala have been covered under the scheme.*****
