                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

607






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.12.2021






Special Industrial Areas




Nishikant  Dubey














                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government proposes to set up special industrial areas extending special concessions to them in the country including Jharkhand;(b) 	if so, the details thereof including funds allocated for the purpose, number of industrial units established and estimated job opportunities created therefrom during the last two years and the current year, State/UT-wise; (c) 	the details of industries which are mainly responsible for environment pollution in the country; and(d) 	the corrective steps taken by the Government in this regard? 
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        ???????  ??? ?????? ???????? ??? ????? ?????? (???? ??? ??????)  THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI SOM PARKASH)(a):	Government of India (GoI) is developing various Industrial Corridor Projects as part of National Industrial Corridor programme which is aimed at development of greenfield industrial areas/region/nodes in India which can compete with the best manufacturing and investment destinations in the world. GoI has accorded approval for development of 11 corridors (32 projects) in four Phases as part of National Master Plan for providing multimodal connectivity to economic zones which are under various stages of conceptualization/development/implementation. The status of Projects is enclosed at Annexure I.  As part of the Amritsar Kolkata Industrial Corridor (AKIC), State Govt. of Jharkhand had identified an area of 2,500 acres in New Barhi for establishment of Integrated Manufacturing Cluster (IMC).Further, under the scheme namely, ‘Modified Industrial Infrastructure Upgradation Scheme (MIIUS)’ GoI is providing central assistance to State Implementing Agencies of the State Governments for development of industrial clusters in the country. It is stated that 21 projects have been sanctioned under MIIUS which includes one project namely "Tupudana Industrial Area" in the State of Jharkhand.  MIIUS has been discontinued on 31st March, 2017 and no new project is being taken up. (b):	Total funds released for infrastructure packages for industrial Corridor Project is given as under:<pre>(Rs. in Crore)Industrial Corridor Project	2019-20	2020-21	2021-22 (up to 25.11.2021)	TotalDelhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor (DMIC)	828.00	1,334.93	853.05	3,015.98Chennai-Bengaluru Industrial Corridor (CBIC)	0.00	1,037.46	68.88	1,106.34Extension of CBIC to Kochi via Coimbatore	0.00	0.00	2.50	2.50Total	828.00	2,372.39	924.43	4,124.82 </pre>A list of projects sanctioned under MIIUS along with details of funds allocation is enclosed at Annexure-II. However, State-wise and year-wise details of number of industrial units established and estimated job opportunities created therefrom is not centrally maintained.(c) & (d): 	Industries are categorized among the 17 highly polluting categories. Central Pollution Control Board(CPCB) carries out inspection-cum-monitoring of highly polluting industries based on computer generated SMS alerts, due to violation of effluent and emission standards, recorded in OCEMS (Online Continues Effluent/Emission Monitoring System) installed in industries. During the period, show-cause notices to 187 units and closure directions to 89 units were issued for non-compliances. Legal actions were initiated against 6 units and State-wise compliance status of 17 categories of highly polluting industries is given at Annexure-III. *******
