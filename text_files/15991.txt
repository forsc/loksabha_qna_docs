                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4413






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.03.2020






Healthcare Schemes for Fire Accident Victims




S. Muniswamy














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) whether the Government proposes to introduce new healthcare scheme for the victims of fire accidents; (b) if so, the details thereof; (c) the criteria laid down for getting the benefits by the victims; and (d) the funds allocated for the purpose, State/UTwise including Karnataka?






ANSWER




                                                         ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a)	to (c):     During 11th Five Year Plan a pilot programme was initiated in the year 2010 by Ministry of Health & Family Welfare in the name of “Pilot Programme for Prevention of Burn Injuries” (PPPBI).   The programme was initiated in 3(three) Medical Colleges and 6 (six) Districts Hospitals with 100% central assistance in 3 States.             The scheme continued during 12th Five Year Plan as “National Programme for Prevention & Management of Burn Injuries”(NPPMBI), wherein 47 Medical colleges  in 17 States were funded during this period. Presently the scheme is continuing as “National Programme for Prevention & Management of Trauma and Burn Injuries”(NPPMT&BI) and supporting the Burn Units approved during 12th Five Year Plan.             Under the scheme the Government of India has taken initiatives towards setting up of Burn Units  in the Medical Colleges with an overall objective to reduce incidence,  mortality, morbidity, disability due to Burn Injuries, improve awareness among the general masses and vulnerable groups especially the women, children, industrial and hazardous occupational workers, establish adequate infrastructural facility and network for behavior change communication, burn management and rehabilitation interventions and carry out research for assessing behavioural, social and other determinants of Burn Injuries in our country for effective need based program planning for Burn Injuries, monitoring and subsequent evaluation. These services are free and universal to all individuals.              Further, Under Ayushman Bharat Programme, 1.5 lakh  Sub Health Centres and Primary Health Centres across the country are to be transformed into Health and wellness Centres (AB-HWCs) by December, 2022,whose services are/ will be  free and universal to all individuals  residing  in the service area. The AB- HWCs are envisioned to provide an expanded range of services including primary management of emergency services. Emergency Services under AB- HWCs would include basic management of burns also.(d):   During 12th Five Year Plan under the scheme, “National Programme for Prevention & Management of Burn Injuries”(NPPMBI), total funds allocated to 47 burn units in 17 states is Rs. 309.213 crores out of which central share is Rs. 197.37 crores.  Detail of fund allocation is enclosed at annexure.………………….
