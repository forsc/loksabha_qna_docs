                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3093






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.08.2021






Setting up of Common Facility Centres




Rajeshbhai Naranbhai Chudasama














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has formulated any scheme for setting up of Common Facility Centres (CFCs) at block/municipal level for providing basic facilities for the development of handloom industry in the country; (b) if so, the details thereof; and (c) the requirement/eligibility criteria for sanctioning of such centres?






ANSWER




THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF TEXTILES  (SMT. DARSHANA JARDOSH)(a) to (c):	Under National Handloom Development Programme (NHDP) and Comprehensive Handloom Cluster Development Scheme(CHCDS), there is provision for setting up of Common Facility Centre (CFC) as  part of Block Level Cluster for housing the technical facilities for weavers viz. skill up-gradation/sample development/production, warping, dyeing, yarn depot, etc. CFC should have built-up area of about 3,000 sq. ft. with an estimated cost of not more than Rs.50.00 lakh excluding land cost. Design layout of the CFC, can be partially modified to meet local requirement. Implementing Agency may have their own land or take land on lease from Government/Government agency for atleast 15 years. Proposals are submitted by the Implementing Agency through the State government and viable proposals are taken up.***
