                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3414






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






Enrollment of Muslim Students




Harish Dwivedi














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the enrollment rate of Muslim students in the primary and upper primary level in thecountry, State-wise including Uttar Pradesh;(b) the details of the enrollment rate of Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and OtherBackward Classes, State-wise; and(c) the details of the steps taken/being taken by the Government to increase their enrollment?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF THE HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) & (b): The state-wise enrollment rate of Muslim, Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes andOther Backward Classes students in the Primary and Upper Primary level in the country is atAnnexure-A.(c): The RTE Act requires the appropriate government and every local authority to “ensure thatthe child belonging to weaker sections and the child belonging to disadvantaged groups are notdiscriminated against and prevented from pursuing and completing elementary education on anygrounds. Ministry of Human Resource Development is implementing flagship programme forEducation of all the children from Primary to class XII, namely Samagra Shiksha. Bridginggender and social category gaps at all levels of school education is one of the major objectives ofthe Samagra Shiksha. Consequently, the Samagra Shiksha attempts to reach out to girls andchildren belonging to SC, ST, Minority communities and transgender. The scheme has also givenattention to urban deprived children, children affected by periodic migration, and children livingin remote and scattered habitations.Major Interventions emphasized under the new scheme are:<pre>I. Opening of schools in the neighbourhood as defined by the State.II. Provision of free text-books to Girls up to Class VIIIIII. Uniforms to all girls, SC, ST children and Below Poverty Line (BPL) children up toClass VIIIIV. Provision of gender segregated toilets in all schoolsV. Teachers sensitization programmes to promote girls participation.VI. Provision for Self-Defence training for the girls from classes VI to XIIVII. Vocationalization of Secondary EducationVIII. Stipend to CWSN girls from class I to Class XIIIX. Upgradation of KGBVs for Girls from classes VI – VIII to classes VI – XIIX. Construction of residential quarters for teachers in remote/hilly areas/in areas withdifficult terrain.</pre>Also, under Samagra Shiksha special state specific projects for varied interventions underequity is emphasized for enhancing access, retention and quality for benefit of SC/ST/OBC/minority & other marginalized communities.Special Focus DistrictsThe Special Focus Districts (SFDs) have been identified on the basis of population i.e.,25% and above domination of SC and ST population and for having 20% and above of Muslimpopulation. Besides this focus is given to Left Wing Extremism (LWE) districts identified byMinistry of Home Affairs including 35 Worst Affected Districts and Minority concentrationdistricts identified by Ministry of Minority Affairs.***
