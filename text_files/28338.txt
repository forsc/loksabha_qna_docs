                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

161






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.11.2021






Ramsar Sites




Thalikkottai Rajuthevar Baalu














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	the details of Ramsar Sites, declared as such, under the United Nations Convention on Wetlands, State- wise;(b) 	whether it is a fact that the vast wetlands stretch of Pallikkaranai Marsh in Chennai which is a fully qualified wetland expanding over several hundred squarekilometres area, has not been declared a Ramsar Site;(c) 	if so, the reasons therefor and the action beingtaken by the Union Government to include Pallikkaranai Marsh wetlands under the scope of Ramsar Convention;and(d) 	pending its declaration as Ramsar Site, the plans for protecting the Pallikkaranai wetlands from degradation?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) So far, 46 Ramsar Sites have been designated in the Country. The State–wise details of these Ramsar Sites are annexed.(b) to (d) As per protocol, Ramsar Secretariat declares wetlands as Ramsar Sites after receipt of proposals from the Country’s designated Administrative Authority. The proposal for declaring Pallikaranai Marshland as “Ramsar Site” has been received by this Ministry from the State Government vide letter dated 20.11.2021. The same has been submitted for approval of competent authority.   Under the scheme of National Plan for Conservation of Aquatic Ecosystems (NPCA), an amount of Rs. 2.78 Crores has already been released as central share to the State Government of Tamil Nadu for the conservation and management of Pallikaranai Wetland. Further, as per the information received from the State Government, the following steps have been taken by them for protecting the Pallikaranai Wetland from degradation:•	A separate conservation authority namely, “Conservation Authority of Pallikaranai Marshland (CAPML)” has been constitutedunder the Chairpersonship of Principal Secretary, Department of Environment, Climate Change & Forests, Government of Tamil Nadu. •	Weekly meetings are also convened by the Member Secretary, Tamil Nadu State Wetland Authority for issuance of draft notification for 32 ‘Natural wetlands’ located in and around the Pallikaranai Marshland under the Wetlands (Conservation and Management) Rules, 2017.•	24X7 Monitoring of Pallikaranai Marshland through regular patrolling and field inspections.•	Advisory has been issued to the Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation to relocate the existing dump yards - Alandur dump yard and Perungudi Dump yard from Pallikaranai Marsh to any other suitable location.•	The Additional Chief Secretary, Municipal administration & Water Supply, Chennai has been advised for identification of the source of municipal solid wastes; sewage inlets points, etc., draining into Pallikaranai Marsh and to take suitable action for arresting the disposal of Municipal wastes, sewage, treated/ untreated effluents into the Pallikaranai Marsh.•	The Chairman, Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board has been advised to take samples from all the inlet channels and to report on health status of the Pallikaranai Marsh.•	The District Collector, Chennai District and the District Collector, Chengalpet Districtwere issued instructions for survey of encroachments, unauthorized settlements in Pallikaranai Marsh area. •	The Commissioner, Greater Chennai Corporation, Chennai; the Commissioner, Land Administration, Chepauk, Chennai have been instructed to identify and cancel the pattas that have been obtained illegally during the past.******
