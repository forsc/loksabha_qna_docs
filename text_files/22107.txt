                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3379






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2021






Tobacco Farming




Pankaj Chowdhary














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the area on which tobacco farming is taking place in the country, State-wise;(b)	whether the Government is contemplating/proposes to work out any scheme to promote cultivation of other crops in place of tobacco; and(c)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????             (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a):	Tobacco is grown in an area of 4.08 lakh hectares in the country. The state-wise area under tobacco during 2018-19 is as under:State	Area (‘000 Hectares)Andhra Pradesh	88.00Assam	0.14Bihar	10.81Gujarat	177.41Karnataka	83.70Maharashtra	0.46Meghalaya	0.80Mizoram	0.34Rajasthan	0.24Tamil Nadu	2.11Telangana	4.00Uttar Pradesh	26.00West Bengal	14.69All India	408.70(b)&(c): 	Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare (DAC&FW) is already implementing Crop Diversification Programme (CDP), an ongoing sub-scheme of Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) in tobacco growing states viz., Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Gujarat, Karnataka, Odisha, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh and West Bengal to encourage tobacco growing farmers to shift to alternative crops/cropping system w.e.f. 2015-16. An amount of Rs. 1000.00 lakh as Central Share has been allocated to implementing states during 2020-21 for implementation of the programme on 60:40 sharing basis between Government of India and State Government.*******
