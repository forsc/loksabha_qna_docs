INSPIRE Scheme




Rajashree Mallick














                        Will the Minister of





SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total number of students in various universities receiving scholarship under the INSPIRE schemes during the current academic year; University- wise; and(b)	whether there has been delay in releasing scholarships to the students and if so, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY & EARTH SCIENCES(DR. JITENDRA SINGH)??????? ?? ???????????? ??? ?????? ??????? ???????? ??  ????? ?????? (???????? ??????)  (??. ???????? ????)(a)	The Government is implementing “Innovation in Science Pursuit for Inspired Research (INSPIRE)” to attract, motivate, nurture and train talented and meritorious students to study science subjects and opt for careers in Research and Development (R&D) to build a pipeline of quality human resource, thereby widening the R&D manpower base of the country. The Scholarship for Higher Education (SHE) component of the INSPIRE Scheme aims to encourage meritorious students to study basic and natural sciences in B.Sc. and M.Sc. through attractive scholarship and mentorship opportunities. The scheme offers 12,000 (10,000 Direct Mode + 2000 Institutional Mode) scholarships every year to meritorious students in the age group of 17-22 years @ Rs 0.80 lakh per year. An important feature of the scheme is the mentorship support provided to students to carry out research during vacation periods. Students within top 1% cut-off marks at the Class XII Board (State or Central) examinations and pursuing science courses at undergraduate/ Integrated MSc or MS program are eligible for INSPIRE Scholarship under the Direct Mode. Under the Institute Mode, applicants receive their Scholarship for taking up undergraduate/integrated post-graduate degree courses in basic and natural sciences in various IITs, IISERs, NISERs etc. Selections are based on performance (top 1%) in Class XII examinations of State/Central School Examination Boards and/or selected in national level competitive examinations like IIT-JEE etc.  The continuation of Scholarship to the student is based on performance (minimum 60% or CGPA 7.0 on 10.0 scale) in examinations on annual basis as certified by the College Principal/ Registrar of the University. The number of students in various universities receiving scholarship under the INSPIRE Scheme during the current academic year, university-wise is annexed.(b)	No Madam. The release of scholarships to the INSPIRE beneficiaries are disbursed within 60 days from the date of uploading of requisite documents as per the Scheme norms through Public Financial Management System (PFMS) Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) Mode.
