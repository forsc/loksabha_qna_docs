                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1919






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2021






UNDAN Scheme and Transformation of Aviation Sector




Manoj Kishorbhai Kotak














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether UDAN Scheme has transformed the aviation sector making air travel accessible for common man;(b) if so, the number of small and medium cities which are connected by air service to big cities and the details of the routes where air service has started;(c) the number of people travelled so far under the UDAN Scheme; and(d) the number of additional airports which are operational now due to UDAN Scheme?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) to (d): Ministry of Civil Aviation has launched Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS)- UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik) on 21-10-2016 to enhance regional air connectivity from unserved and underserved airports in the country for making air travel affordable to the masses. Under RCS - UDAN, 948 valid routes have been awarded. Out of which, 395 Routes connecting 63 unserved and underserved airports including 6 Heliports & 2 water aerodromes have been operationalized. Approximately 8.1 million passengers have travelled on RCS - UDAN flights.
