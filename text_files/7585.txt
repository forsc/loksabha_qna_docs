                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1229






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.11.2019






Admission in Private Institutions




Malook Nagar














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether reservation in higher education is continuously opposed, whereas no objection israised for admission in medical and engineering courses in private institutions on low merit;(b) if so, whether the Government propose to regulate admission in private institutions totallybased on merit; and(c) the details of cut-off marks for admission in medical and engineering in Government as wellas in private institutions during the last year?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) No, Sir.(b) Question does not arise in view of the (a) above.(c) (i) Medical Courses: As per the regulations of Medical Council of India, to be eligible foradmission to undergraduate and postgraduate course, a candidate has to obtain minimummarks at 50th percentile in the NEET for Government and Private Medical Colleges/ Institutions.However in respect of candidates belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and otherBackward Classes, the minimum qualifying marks are 40th percentile and in respect ofcandidates with benchmark disabilities specified under the Rights of Persons with DisabilitiesAct, 2016, the minimum qualifying marks are 45th percentile for General Category and 40thpercentile for SC/ST/OBC.Based on the criteria of qualifying cut-off percentile provided for NEET-UG 2019 for admissionto MBBS and BDS Courses (General/ SC/ ST/ OBC and PwD of General/ SC/ST/OBC) are asfollows:<pre>(Cut-Off Percentile and Cut-Off Marks for the Year 2019)S.No Category Earlier Qualifying Criteria Revised Qualifying CriteriaCut-Off Percentile Cut-Off Marks Cut-Off Percentile Cut-Off Marks1.
