                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

50






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.02.2021






Rural Development Scheme in Chhattisgarh




Arun Sao














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the schemes being implemented by the Ministry during the last three years;(b) 	the funds allocated and released by the Government for various schemes during each of the last three years and the current year, State-wise and scheme-wise including Chhattisgarh;(c) 	whether any proposal has been forwarded to the Government by the State Government of Chhattisgarh during the said period; and(d) 	if so, the details thereof and the action taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)	The Ministry of Rural Development is implementing Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (Mahatma Gandhi NREGS), Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM), Deen Dayal Upadhayay – Gramin Kaushalya Yojana (DDU-GKY), Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana – Gramin (PMAY-G), Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY), National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP), Shyama Prasad  MukherjI Rurban Mission (SPMRM) and Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana(SAGY) to bring about overall improvement in the quality of life of the people in rural areas, strengthening of livelihood opportunities, promoting self-employment, skilling of rural youths, infrastructure development, provision of social assistance and other basic amenities. (b)	The funds allocated and released by the Government for various schemes during each of the last three years and the current year, State-wise including Chhattisgarh and scheme-wise is at Annexure.  Both DDU-GKY and RSETI Schemes are demand driven schemes and funds are allocated to States as per demands against approved Action Plans. Mahatma Gandhi NREGS is also a demand driven scheme and no separate allocation to the State is made. Under the Saansad Adarsh Gram Yojana framework, the development of Gram Panchayats is envisaged through convergence and implementation of existing Government Schemes and Programmes under the administrative control of respective Ministries without allocating additional funds. Under SPMRM, funds are being released to the States/ UTs based on fulfilment of certain conditions mentioned in the Mission guidelines.(c) & (d) The proposals received by the Ministry are examined as per the guidelines of the respective schemes.
