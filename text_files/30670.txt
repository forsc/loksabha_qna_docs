                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2162






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2021






Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakaram




Gautham Sigamani Pon













                                                    Janardan Singh Sigriwal
                                                








                                                    Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar
                                                








                                                    Dhanush M Kumar
                                                








                                                    C.N. Annadurai
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the present status of implementation of Janani Sishu Suraksha Karyaram(JSSK) under National Health Mission along with the details of funds allocated for the purpose and the number of beneficiaries, including rural women of SC/ST benefited during the last three years and current year, State-wise including  Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu; (b) whether the Government has achieved the target and objective for which the JSSK was launched and if so, the details thereof; (c) whether the Government has reviewed/assesses the implementation of JSSK in the country and if so, the details of findings thereof along with the shortcoming noticed and the corrective steps taken in this regard; (d) the steps taken to ensure that pregnant women , sick neonates and sick infants receive free care at public health institutions under JSSK ; and(e) whether the Government has launched awareness programme for successful implementation of JSSK, if so, the number of such programmes launched during the last three years and achievement made there under? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND                            FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a)	and (b):  Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakaram (JSSK) is implemented in all states/UTs of India uniformly in all public health institutions.The State/UT wise details of the number of beneficiaries including SC/ST and funds allocated under JSSK including Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu during last three years and current year is placed at Annexure 1 and 2 respectively.The Government has achieved the objective of increased access as well as service provision to over one crore pregnant women at Public Health Facilities every year for which the JSSK was launched.      (c) 	The Government assesses the implementation of JSSK by undertaking periodic Regional and State Level Review meetings and field visits in various States.		Since the launch of JSSK, nine Common Review Missions (CRMs) have been conducted.		The steps taken by Government of India for effective implementation and strengthening of JSSK are:i.	Communications through multiple channels with State Governments including letters, videoconferencing, etc.ii.	Popularizing the Scheme through Information Education & Communication (IEC) & Behaviour Change Communication (BCC) strategies including mass media.iii.	Field level workers like ANMs and ASHAs are promoting this programme at the ground level through interpersonal communicationiv.	A system of regular supportive supervisory visits to the states and districts to monitor the implementation of JSSK has also been put in place (d) To ensure that pregnant women, sick neonates and sick infants receive free care at public health institutions under JSSK, referral transport is provided to them by the States as per their local needs, using different models which include a network of emergency response vehicles using toll free numbers, government ambulances, available transport under public private partnership etc.In addition, the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare implements various initiatives to provide free care at public health facilities, which include:i.	Surakshit Matritva Aashwasan (SUMAN) provides assured, dignified, respectful and quality healthcare at no cost and zero tolerance for denial of services for every woman and newborn visiting public health facilities to end all preventable maternal and newborn deaths.ii.	Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA) provides pregnant women a fixed day, free of cost assured and quality antenatal check up by a Specialist/Medical Officer on the 9th day of every month.iii.	LaQshya aims to improve the quality of care in labour room and maternity operation theatres to ensure that pregnant women receive respectful and quality care during delivery and immediate post-partum. iv.	Monthly Village Health, Sanitation and Nutrition Day (VHSND) as an outreach activity at Anganwadi centers for provision of maternal and child care including nutrition in convergence with the ICDS.v.	Delivery Points- Over 25,000 ‘Delivery Points’ across the country have been strengthened in terms of infrastructure, equipment, and trained manpower for provision of comprehensive RMNCAH+N servicesvi.	MCP Card and Safe Motherhood Booklet are distributed to the pregnant women for educating them on diet, rest, danger signs of pregnancy, benefit schemes and institutional deliveries.vii.	Reproductive and child health (RCH)portal is a name-based web-enabled tracking system for pregnant women and new born so as to ensure seamless provision of regular and complete services to them including antenatal care, institutional delivery and post-natal care.(e) In order to generate awareness for various Maternal Health Programmes including Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram, budgetary approvals are accorded to proposals received from States/UTs under National Health Mission’s (NHM) Programme implementation Plans (PIP) of states, towards IEC/ BCC activities like-Media mix of Mass media/Mid media, Interpersonal Communication, IEC Campaigns, printing of various IEC materials etc. throughout the year.
