                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3007






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Funds For National Literacy Mission




Vijay Kumar














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of the fund sanctioned/spent under National Literacy Mission (NLM) during the last 3 years and in current year State/UT-wise; (b) the achievement made so far under the scheme; (c) whether the Government has any proposal to provide assistance to those States having lower adult literacy rate among females and to check high dropout rate in school education; (d) if so, the details thereof; and(e) the financial assistance provided by the Central Government for attaining 100 per cent literacy during the last 3 years and in the current year, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ''NISHANK'')(a) & (b) The scheme of Saakshar Bharat, the latest version of National Literacy Mission was implemented in the rural areas of 404 districts in 26 States and one UT, that had adult female literacy rate of 50 per cent and below as per Census 2001, including left wing extremism affected districts, irrespective of their literacy rates, with special focus on women and other disadvantaged groups, since October 2009. The scheme was extended upto 31.03.2018. A statement indicating details of State/UT-wise funds sanctioned/ released under the Saakshar Bharat scheme in 2017-18 and 2018-19 is at Annexure-I. During the implementation of Saakshar Bharat scheme, against the overall target of making 7 crore adult non-literates as literates, around 7.64 crore learners, having passed the biannual Basic Literacy Assessment Tests conducted by National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) between August, 2010 to March, 2018, were certified as literates. During 2019-20, there was no scheme of Adult Education under implementation.A new scheme of Adult Education “Padhna Likhna Abhiyaan” is under implementation during the current financial year 2020-21, with a target of making 57 lakh adult non-literates as literates. A statement indicating details of State/UT-wise funds sanctioned/ released under the Padhna Likhna Abhiyaan during the current financial year 2020-21 is at Annexure-II. In addition, the Department of School Education and Literacy has launched the Samagra Shiksha - an Integrated Scheme for School Education as a Centrally Sponsored Scheme with effect from the financial year 2018-19. This programme subsumes the three erstwhile Centrally Sponsored Schemes of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA), Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA) and Teacher Education (TE). Samagra Shiksha is an overarching programme for the school education sector extending from pre-school to class XII and aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education at all levels of school education.(c) & (d) The new Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Adult Education namely Padhna Likhna Abhiyaan (PLA) is under implementation during 2020-21 covering, among others, districts with female literacy rates less than 60 per cent as per latest Census.Under Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Samagra Shiksha, financial assistance is provided to the States and UTs for various activities to reduce number of dropouts, which include opening/ strengthening of new schools upto senior secondary level, construction of school buildings & additional classrooms, setting up, up-gradation and running of Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalayas, setting up of residential schools/ hostels, free uniforms, free text books, transport allowance and undertaking enrolment drives, residential as well as non residential trainings, Seasonal Hostels/ residential camps etc. Financial assistance is also provided for Inclusive Education of children with special needs. Also, Mid-Day meal is provided to students at the elementary level of education.   (e)	As mentioned in National Education Policy 2020, Government of India is committed to expedite the all-important aim of achieving 100% literacy as soon as possible by strong and innovative government initiatives for adult education - in particular, to facilitate community involvement and the smooth and beneficial integration of technology.A statement indicating details of State/UT-wise funds sanctioned/ released for achieving higher level of literacy rate under the Saakshar Bharat scheme during 2017-18 and 2018-19 and under the “Padhna Likhna Abhiyaan” scheme during the current financial year 2020-21 are at Annexure-I and Annexure-II respectively. *****
