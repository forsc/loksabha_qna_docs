                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

846






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






HRIDAY Scheme




Kirit Premjibhai Solanki














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the salient features of the HRIDAY scheme and the current status of its implementation in the country;(b)	the list of cities selected under phase one and the list of cities to be selected under the next phase, State/UT wise; (c)	the details of the selection criteria used to choose the cities under the scheme; and(d)	whether all selected cities have formulated a Heritage Management Plan and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a)&(b) Heritage City Development and Augmentation Yojana (HRIDAY), launched as a pilot Scheme on 21st January, 2015, supports the development of heritage linked core civic infrastructure projects in areas around heritage, religious, cultural and tourism assets of the cities. Under the Yojana, twelve cities namely, Ajmer (Rajasthan), Amritsar (Punjab), Amaravati (Andhra Pradesh), Badami (Karnataka), Dwarka (Gujarat), Gaya (Bihar), Kancheepuram & Velankanni (Tamil Nadu), Mathura & Varanasi (Uttar Pradesh), Puri (Odisha) and Warangal (Telangana) are covered. As the mission period of HRIDAY has ended on 31st March, 2019, no new city/project can be taken up now. The current status of implementation under HRIDAY scheme is attached at Annexure-A.(c) & (d) The cities have been identified on the basis of their rich heritage and cultural history. The Heritage Management Plan (HMP) has been prepared in discussion with the stakeholders at each city level. HMP includes detailed inventory of tangible and intangible heritage assets, delineated heritage zones around those assets and infrastructure facilities available in those zones.                                        *****
