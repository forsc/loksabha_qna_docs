                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2693






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.12.2019






Relocation of Head Post Office




Omprakash Bhupalsinh alias Pawan Rajenimbalkar














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether any scheme for relocation and prelocation of several head offices of Department of Posts is under consideration of the Government and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether Head Post Office situated at Osmanabad for the last 47 years jointly for Osmanabad and Latur districts has been relocated at Latur temporarily/irregularly; (c) 	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor; and (d) 	whether the Government proposes to take any action to prelocate the already established Head Post Office, which provides suitable and all facilities to the common people from Latur to Osmanabad and if so, the details thereof along with the time limit fixed therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF COMMUNICATIONS, LAW & JUSTICE AND ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI RAVI SHANKAR PRASAD)(a)	No, Sir. There is no such scheme for relocation and prelocation of Head Post Offices under consideration of the Department of Posts.(b)	No Sir. Latur Head Post Office and Osmanabad Head Post Office are functioning at their respective Head Quarters only. There is no relocation of any of the Head Post Offices.(c)	Does not arise in view of (b) above.(d)	No, Sir.************
