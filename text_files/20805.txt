                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1969






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.02.2021






AYUSH System of Medicines




Sumalatha Ambareesh













                                                    Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY,UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government has taken note of theincrease in the use of AYUSH system of medicines in thecountry, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has any proposal for further promotion of AYUSH systems among people within and outside the country, if so, the details thereof;(c) the corrective measures being taken up with adequate dissemination of information through electronic, press and social media; and(d) the budgetary provision made for the promotion of AYUSH system of medicines in the country, State/UT wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS AND ADDITIONAL CHARGE OF MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(SHRI KIREN RIJIJU)(a):   Yes, there has been an increasing trend in the number of people availing medicinal facilities under AYUSH for the last three years as per the table given below.Number of Patients who visited Government Health Care Facilities under AYUSH during 2015-16, 2016-17 and 2017-18Year	IPD	OPD2015-16	1156695	1594593792016-17	1685773	1727003132017-18	1957921	177375226Source: Annual Statistical Publication of Ministry of AYUSH namely “AYUSH In INDIA” for the year 2016, 2017 and 2018(b):   Ministry of AYUSH is implementing Centrally Sponsored Scheme namely National AYUSH Mission (NAM) through States/UTs for the promotion of AYUSH system among people within India. The Mission envisages better access to AYUSH services, strengthening of AYUSH educational institutions, enforcement of quality control of Ayurveda, Siddha and Unani & Homoeopathy (ASU &H) drugs and promotion of medicinal plants for sustainable availability of raw-materials for ASU & H drugs in the States/UTs. The National AYUSH Mission (NAM) inter-alia makes provision for the following activities: -i.	Co-location of AYUSH facilities at Primary Health Centers (PHCs), Community Health Centers (CHCs) and District Hospitals (DHs).ii.	Up gradation of exclusive State Government AYUSH Hospitals and Dispensaries.iii.	Setting up of up to 50 bedded integrated AYUSH Hospital.iv.	Upgradation of State Government Educational Institutions.v.	Setting up of new State Government AYUSH Educational Institutions in the      State where it is not available.vi.	Strengthening of State Government/ Public Sector Undertaking (PSU) Ayurveda, Siddha, Unani and Homoeopathy (ASU&H) Pharmacies and Drug Testing Laboratories (DTL).vii.	Cultivation and Promotion of Medicinal Plants. viii.	Upgradation of 12,500 Health facilities (10,000 AYUSH Dispensaries and 2500 Health sub-centers) into AYUSH Health and Wellness Centre under the Ayushman Bharat. With the mandate of promotion of AYUSH systems among people in foreign countries, Ministry of AYUSH undertakes various activities under Central Sector Scheme for promotion of International Cooperation, (IC Scheme). 33 AYUSH Information Cell have been set up in 30 countries to disseminate authentic information about AYUSH systems of Medicine. 	AYUSH experts are deputed to foreign countries to participate in international meetings, conferences, training programmes, seminars and on special assignment of the Government of India for promotion and propagation of AYUSH Systems.Ministry of AYUSH has signed 25 Country to Country MoUs for Cooperation in field of Traditional Medicine and Homoeopathy, 23 MoUs for undertaking collaborative research in AYUSH systems of medicine and 13 MoUs for setting up of AYUSH academic Chairs with various foreign institutes/ universities. Ministry of AYUSH under its Fellowship Scheme offers 104 scholarships every year to the eligible foreign nationals from 99 countries for undertaking UG, PG and Ph.D courses in various premier AYUSH Institutes in India.System of AYUSH (Ayurveda, Unani and Homeopathy along with Yoga) has been integrated with  the National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular Diseases & Stroke (NPCDCS).(c):	State / UT Government has submitted State Annual Action Plan (SAAP) under National AYUSH Mission (NAM) for various activities including Information Education Communication (IEC) and Behaviour Change Communication (BCC) as per their requirement. Accordingly, Ministry of AYUSH has approved BCC & IEC activity with adequate dissemination for States / UTs through electronic, press and social media. Through the dedicated Central Sector Scheme “Information Education Communication (IEC)” the Ministry has taken number of corrective measures with adequate dissemination of information through electronic, press, and social media in recent times. These are as under: i.	Upscaling of social media platform,ii.	Holding webinars on virtual platform, iii.	Launching Ayush Immunity campaign, iv.	Involving the state governments for spreading messages of this Ministry, v.	Creation of short videos with the support of NFDC, vi.	Community radio programmes for dissemination of information through community radios among rural population.  (d):	During 2020-21, Ministry of AYUSH has Released Rs. 31985.152 /- lakhs under National AYUSH Mission (NAM) for the promotion of AYUSH system of medicines. State/UT wise status of released grant is enclosed as Annexure-I. In addition, an amount of Rs. 17341.00 /- lakhs has been released during 2020-21 to the Central Research Councils, National Institutes and Homoeopathic Pharmacopoeia Laboratory, Ghaziabad under Ministry of AYUSH.
