                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

187






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.11.2019






Updation of Archaeological Survey of India Act, 1958




Shrikant Eknath Shinde













                                                    Sambhajirao Mane Dhairyasheel
                                                








                                                    Sujay Radhakrishna Vikhepatil
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government proposes to amend and update the Archaeological Survey of India Act, 1958 to bring it in line with current times as recommended by 15th Finance Commission;(b)	whether the Government plans to introduce private sector participation for up-keep of protected monuments under ASI;(c)	the details of unprotected heritage buildings, monuments, archaeological sites, and historic buildings in India and particularly in Maharashtra;(d)	the steps taken by the Government to enhance coordination and expansion of training in cultural research; and(e)	the details of the amount sanctioned & released under the National Culture  Fund (NCF) during the last five years, State-Wise and particularly in Maharashtra?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR CULTURE AND TOURISM(PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a)	No Sir.(b)	No  Sir. (c)	Information on built heritage and sites all over India are made available on website of  National Mission on Monuments & Antiquities (NMMA) and in public domain.So far 148 unprotected built heritage, monuments, sites have reported from Maharashtra.(d)	The Archaeological Survey of India imparts training under a Post Graduate Diploma in Archaeology programme through its Institute of Archaeology, which is extended to post graduates in Archaeology, History, Anthropology and Geology.  The courses content includes aspects related to heritage management, conservation and preservation of monuments. (e)	The details are at Annexure.
