                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3983






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






Schemes for Young Writers and Poets




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	whether the Government has taken any steps for the promotion of literature in Indian languages including Gujarati;b)	if so, the details thereof;c)	the schemes for young writers, poets in Indian languages; andd)	the number of youths benefited therefrom, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF CULTURE, TOURISM & DEVELOPMENT OF NORTH EASTERN REGION(G.KISHAN REDDY)(a) & (b) Yes Madam. The Government of India has taken a number of steps for the promotion of literature in Indian languages including Gujrati.Sahitya Akademi (an autonomous organization under M/o Culture) promotes literature and literary activities in 24 Indian languages. The Akademi publishes large volumes of material and confers a number of awards and grants. Every year, the Akademi holds at least 30 seminars, 200 literary gatherings, workshops and lectures, folklore, programmes for women writers, Gramalok in the rural areas. It has published over 7000 books in 24 languages.For the promotion of Gujarati Literature, there is an Advisory Board of eminent writers and scholars in Gujarati to orient the Akademi on publications, translations and literary programmes. As per the directions of the Advisory Board members, Sahitya Akademi organizes literary progammes in Gujarati. The Akademi brings out significant publications of Indian languages and translates them into Gujrati language and vice-versa.c) 	In 2011, the Government of India had constituted Sahitya Akademi Yuva Puraskar for 24 Indian languages. The Akademi also conducts Young Writers'' Meet. It organises programmes titled YuvaSahiti and Avishkar to encourage young writers writing in different Indian languages. It also provides travel grant to young writers (age 40 or below) under which a writer is offered to take a travel to a State other than his/her own to get acquainted with literary ambience of another language. Besides this, Akademi constituted a publication scheme titled Navodaya which undertakes publication of the first literary work of art of the writer.d) 	The Akademi invites writers-scholars on the basis of their contribution to their respective languages instead of states they belong to.
