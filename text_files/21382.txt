                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2734






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.03.2021






Allocation of Funds from USOF




Sanjay Jaiswal














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government proposes to allocate funds from the Universal Service Obligation Fund (USOF) to mobile companies to develop telecom infrastructure in remote areas in the country and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	the allocation made from the said fund to various companies during the last three years, company-wise; and (c) 	the number of villages equipped with telecom facilities through the use of this fund during the same period, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS, EDUCATION ANDELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a)	& (b) 	Subsidies are provided to mobile companies for mobile connectivity in inaccessible, rural and remote areas of the country including North Eastern Region, Left Wing Extremism Affected areas, Andaman and Nicobar Islands and Lakshadweep from Universal Services Obligation Fund (USOF).  Details of subsidy disbursed to various mobile companies from USOF under different projects during the last three years is enclosed as Annexure A. (c)		BharatNet project is being implemented in a phased manner to provide broadband connectivity to all Gram Panchayats (approximately 2.5 lakh) in the country.		During the last 3 years, a total of 1,20,478 Gram Panchayats (including BHQs) have been made Service Ready under BharatNet Project.  Further, mobile services have been provided in 1097 village during this period.
