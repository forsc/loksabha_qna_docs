                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

274






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2019






Trauma Centres




Dhal Singh Bisen














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state: (a) whether trauma centres have been set up in the State of Madhya Pradesh in collaboration with the Union Government; (b) if so, the locations/districts where the trauma centres have been opened/set up including the dates on which these centres were opened;(c) whether all the centres are functional at present and adequate number of doctors are working in these centres and if so, the details thereof; (d) if not, the reasons therefor and the time by which these centres are likely to become functional; and (e) whether the Union Government is likely to give permission for recruitment of doctors under the National Health Mission for these trauma centres so as to ensure smooth functioning of these centres?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE (DR. HARSH VARDHAN)(a) to (e):  A statement is laid on the Table of the HouseSTATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO LOK SABHASTARRED QUESTION NO. 274* FOR 6TH DECEMBER, 2019(a) & (b) Yes.  Under the scheme “Assistance for Capacity Building for Developing Trauma Care Facilities in Government Hospitals on National Highways”, establishing of 5(five) Trauma Care Facilities was approved during the 11th Five Year Plan in the State of Madhya Pradesh one each at Civil Hospital, Shivpuri; Gajra Raja Medical College (GRMC) Hospital, Gwalior; District Hospital, Narsimhapur; District Hospital, Sagar and District Hospital, Seoni. As per the information made available by the State Government, the said Trauma Care Facilities were opened during the year as indicated below:Trauma Care Facility	 Year Civil Hospital, Shivpuri	2008Gajra Raja Medical College (GRMC) Hospital, Gwalior	2010District Hospital, Narsimhapur	2017District Hospital, Sagar	2017District Hospital, Seoni	2017(c) & d) As reported by the State Government, the posts of doctors have been sanctioned as per the guidelines of the programme namely “Assistance for Capacity Building for Developing Trauma Care Facilities in Government Hospitals on National Highways”. However, recruitment is a continuous process and is carried out by State Government as and when vacancies occur.  As per information made available by the State Government, all five Trauma Care facilities are functional with the existing manpower/ staff. (e) Under National Health Mission (NHM), financial and technical support is provided to States/UTs for Trauma Centres to be established till District Hospital level if proposed in the Programme Implementation Plans (PIPs) of the State/ UTs. The support for Human Resources for trauma centres till District level is provided under NHM as per need which is decided jointly by the States and Centre during NPCC (National Programme Co-ordination Committee) meeting of the concerned State/ UT. Manpower is provided to trauma centre at District Hospitals are as per the norms Indian Public Health Standards (IPHS).
