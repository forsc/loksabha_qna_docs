                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

548






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2021






Programmes to Promote Rural Entrepreneurship




Rahul Gandhi














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has initiated programmes to promote rural entrepreneurship, especially among the rural poor adversely affected by the COVID-19 pandemic and if so, the details thereof; (b) 	whether the Government is implementing programmes for skilling rural youth and women to establish agricultural enterprises, particularly micro food enterprises and if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Government has initiated measures to enhance access to credit for micro enterprises in aspirational districts and credit deficit districts; and (d) 	if so, the details thereof particularly for Wayanad district?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NARAYAN RANE)(a): 	Ministry is implementing the following schemes across the country to promote rural entrepreneurship:i.	Credit-linked subsidy programme named Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP) since 2008-09 through Khadi and Village Industries Commission (KVIC) as nodal agency at the national level for generating employment in the country by setting up of micro-enterprises in the non-farm sector. Under the scheme, beneficiaries can avail loan up to Rs. 25 lakh in the manufacturing sector and Rs. 10 lakh in the service sector, with Margin Money subsidy of 15% to 35% for different categories. Around 80% of these micro units are set up in rural areas.Since its inception till 30th June 2021, around 7 lakh micro enterprises have been assisted providing employment to an estimated 57 lakh persons. During the financial year 2020-21, against the target of assisting 78,625 people to set up micro-units and to generate estimated employment for 6,29,000 persons, Banks have sanctioned and disbursed loans to 84,793 micro units, thereby providing employment to estimated 6,78,344 persons. This is the best performance under PMEGP since its inception in 2008-09 despite COVID 19 challenges.ii.	Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI) to organize traditional industries and artisans into clusters to make their products competitive and provide the artisans with sustainable employment. As on 19.07.2021, 433 clusters have been approved benefitting about 2.6 lakh artisans with GoI assistance of Rs.1102 crore.iii.	Following programmes under Gramodyog Vikas Yojana through KVIC:•	Honey Mission (Beekeeping Programme): To supplement the income of farmers, Adivasies and unemployed youth of the country, KVIC has launched Honey Mission during 2017–18, providing farmers with bee boxes, live bee hives, equipments and training.  A total number of 15,445 beneficiaries have been provided with 1,53,259 of Bee-Hives(Boxes) from 2017-18 to 2020-21.•	Kumbhar Sashaktikaran Programme (Mineral Based Industry): Under the programme, for up-liftment of the rural potter’s livelihood, skill up-gradation training and new home scale energy efficient equipment like electric pottery wheels, blunger, pug mill, kiln etc. are provided for producing quality products. A total of 21030 electric pottery wheels have been distributed under this programme since 2017-18 to 2020-21.(b):	Under A Scheme for Promotion of Innovation, Rural Industries and Entrepreneurship (ASPIRE), Ministry of MSME is setting up Livelihood Business Incubators (LBIs) for skilling and incubating unemployed youth and micro entrepreneurs in the Agro rural sector. Till 30.06.2021, 102 LBIs have been approved of which 9 LBIs are exclusively in micro food processing sector. 3479 beneficiaries have been trained in these 9 LBIs of which 1109 beneficiaries have set up their own micro enterprises in food processing. (c) & (d):	Under PMEGP, Ministry is providing access to credit up to Rs. 25 lakh in manufacturing sector and up to Rs. 10 lakh in service sector. Around 14% of the PMEGP units are set up in aspirational districts. Performance of PMEGP in aspirational districts during 2020-21 including Wayanad district is given at Annexure.Besides, Government is also implementing Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY)for providing loans upto 10 lakh to the non-corporate, non-farm small/micro enterprises across the country including aspirational districts.  These loans are given by Commercial Banks, RRBs, Small Finance Banks, Cooperative Banks, MFIs and NBFCs. The borrower can approach any of these lending institutions or can apply online.
