UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5107






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.04.2022






Transfer of Cases of NCLT




Chunni Lal Sahu














                        Will the Minister of





CORPORATE AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the total number of cases of the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT), Mumbai, which were under trial in Chhattisgarh before the transfer to NCLT, Cuttack, company-wise;(b) whether the complete documents of all the matters as in the part above have been transferred to NCLT, Cuttack and if so, the company-wise and date-wise details thereof;(c) whether the decision pertaining to transfer of documents is being delayed due to receipt of incomplete documents of the concerned company at NCLT, Cuttack from Mumbai, the case being as follows-” Case No. TP No. 2/CB/2021 (CP TA-No.11/MB/ 2016). Order Dated 8/11/2011 on 28/10/2021 in application no. TA-4/CB/2021 along with TP No. 2/CB/2021 were heard and reserved for orders”, if so, the details thereof; and(d) whether the culprits will be punished after investigating the above case and if so, the details thereof along with time by which it will be done?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF STATISTICS AND PROGRAMME IMPLEMENTATION; MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF PLANNING; AND MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF CORPORATE AFFAIRS(RAO INDERJIT SINGH)(a):	As per information furnished by National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT), a total number of 92 cases of Chhattisgarh were pending in NCLT, Mumbai, before their transfer to NCLT, Cuttack. (b):	All available documents of 80 matters of Chhattisgarh have been transferred from NCLT, Mumbai and also been received by NCLT, Cuttack. Available documents of remaining matters are under transfer, most of these matters are either disposed of, withdrawn or abated.  Company-wise details of the transferred cases and the cases under transfer are annexed.(c) and (d): The matter is under adjudication of the NCLT, a statutory Tribunal performing quasi-judicial functions, having jurisdiction over the subject.******ANNEXURE REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (b) OF THE UNSTARRED QUESTION NO. 5107 FOR ANSWER IN LOK SABHA ON 04.04.2022A.
