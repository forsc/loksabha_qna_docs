                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5398






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.07.2019






Sewage Treatment Plants




Amol Ramsing Kolhe













                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Heena Vijaykumar Gavit
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                








                                                    Dhanush M Kumar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)   the total number of Sewage Treatment Plants constructed under Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation during each of the last three years and the current year;(b)  whether all the Sewage Treatment Plants are functioning properly, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor along with the action taken/being taken by the Government in this regard;(c)   whether the management of sewage is becoming a serious issue in urban areas in the country and if so, the details thereof;(d)  whether the Government provides financial and technical assistance to the States for establishment of sewage treatment plants;(e)     if so, the details thereof during each of the last three years and the current year, State-wise;(f)    whether the Government has assessed the progress of the work carried out by the States in this regard; and(g)    if so, the details and the outcome thereof, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                       (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)& (b): Government of India has launched Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) on 25 June, 2015 to provide basic civic amenities in 500 selected Mission cities in the country. The thrust areas of the Mission are Water supply, Sewerage & Septage management, Storm Water drainage, non-motorized Urban Transport and development of Green spaces/parks in the Mission cities. Under the Sewerage & Septage management sector, projects related to decentralised, networked underground sewerage systems, including augmentation of existing and rehabilitation of old sewerage systems & Sewage Treatment Plants (STPs) can be taken up by the States/Union Territories (UTs). Further, projects related to cleaning, transportation & treatment of faecal sludge and mechanical and biological cleaning of sewers & septic tanks may also be taken up under AMRUT by the States/UTs.Out of the total State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) of ?77,640 crore approved by the Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs for the entire Mission period (25 June, 2015 to 31 March, 2020) under AMRUT, ?32,456 crore has been allocated to projects in the Sewerage & Septage management sector. So far, 761 projects worth ?32,816 crore, including projects related to STPs, have been taken up by the States/UTs in the sewerage & septage management sector, of which, contracts have been awarded for 638 projects worth ?26,784 crore including 112 completed projects worth ?1,200 crore. Further, 123 projects costing ?6,031 crore are at various stages of tendering. Some States/UTs have taken up projects in excess of their approved SAAPs. In such cases, the entire additional cost of the projects will be borne by the concerned State/UT.Under AMRUT, Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs approves only the SAAPs prepared and submitted by the States/UTs and releases committed Central Assistance as per Mission guidelines. Selection, appraisal, approval and implementation of projects, including their operation, maintenance and upkeep is the responsibility of the States/UTs/Urban Local Bodies (ULBs).(c) to (e):	Public health and sanitation is a State subject and it is the responsibility of the States/ULBs to ensure management of sewage in the cities. Government of India supplements the efforts of the States/ULBs through its various flagship schemes/missions. Government of India has approved SAAPs amounting to ?77,640 crore submitted by all the States/UTs for the entire Mission period under AMRUT, which include committed Central Assistance of ?35,990 crore. Central Assistance is not released project or sector-wise under the Mission. The State/UT-wise list of Central Assistance released during the last 3 years and the current year is placed as Annexure-I.	Under AMRUT, end-to-end support for project design, development, implementation and management is provided to ULBs/States by Project Development and Management Consultants. The Mission also provides for augmenting the capacity of the ULBs through its capacity building component. (f)& (g):	The Ministry is regularly reviewing the progress of the works carried out under the Mission through various meetings, video conferences apart from visits to the States/UTs by the senior officers of this Ministry. The State/UT-wise details of progress made in the Sewerage & Septage management sector is placed as Annexure-II.              *****
