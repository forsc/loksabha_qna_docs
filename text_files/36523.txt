                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3860






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2022






Health Infrastructure under AB-PMJAY




Abdul Majeed Ariff














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has reviewed the progress of implementation of AB-PMJAY in the country and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has taken note of lack of adequate health infrastructure in public sector hospitals in the country and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government proposes to take necessary steps to increase the health infrastructure in the country as part of AB-PMJAY and if so, the details thereof; and(d) the details of amount spent under AB-PMJAY separately, in public and private hospitals in the country during each of the last three years and the current year; year-wise and State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH ANDFAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) to (d):Performance of Ayushman Bharat –Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB-PMJAY) is assessed and monitored by Ministry.A dashboard with various Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) is designed to monitor the scheme. The performance of the scheme is objectively evaluated in terms of cards issued, number of hospital admissions, quality of treatment provided, timely settlement of claims etc. Additionally, National and regional review meetings are conducted with the concerned stakeholders including State Health Agencies. Necessary interventions based on the feedback received are put in place.The scheme is implemented through a network of empanelled public and private hospitals in the country. Only those hospitals (public and private), which meet the specified criteria including infrastructure, get empanelled under the scheme. Public hospitals are reimbursed at par with private hospitals and therefore, they can utilise the reimbursed amount to increase their health infrastructure including human resources.Year-wise and State/UT-wise details of claims amount reimbursed to public and private hospitals are at Annexure.
