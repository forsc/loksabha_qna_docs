                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS

                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

457






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2022






Schemes for Street Vendors




DNV Senthilkumar. S.













                                                    Amol Ramsing Kolhe
                                                








                                                    Sunil Dattatray Tatkare
                                                








                                                    Kuldeep Rai Sharma
                                                








                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar
                                                








                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                








                                                    Dhanush M Kumar
                                                








                                                    Ganesan Selvam
                                                








                                                    C.N. Annadurai
                                                








                                                    Gautham Sigamani Pon
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the Government has any data regarding the total number of street vendors in the country and if so, the details thereof, State-wise;(b)	whether the Government is able to launch concrete schemes targeting the welfare of street vendors and if so, the details thereof and if not the reasons therefor;(c)	the details of present schemes being implemented for the welfare of street vendors along with funds allocated and utilized during each of the last five years;(d)	the number of street vendors from Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu,  Andaman and Nicobar Islands benefitted under the above scheme during each of the last three years and the current year;(e)	whether the Government also proposes to provide financial assistance and extend welfare measures to street vendors in urban areas and if so, the details thereof;(f)	whether the Government also proposes to provide credit/ basic infrastructure facilities to them to strengthen their self- employment opportunities and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                                   THE MINISTER OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)	As reported by States/Union Territories, 49.48 lakh street vendors have been identified. State/ Union Territory-wise details are at Annexure-I. (b)&(c): Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA), through States/UTs, is implementing Support to Urban Street Vendors (SUSV), as a component of Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana- National Urban Livelihood Mission (DAY-NULM). It aims at addressing livelihood concerns of the urban street vendors. DAY-NULM is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme under which funds are released in a consolidated manner out of which inter-se allocation for various components are made by State Governments/Union Territories depending upon their requirement and capacity. State-UT wise details of central funds released and utilized during last five years are at Annexure-II.MoHUA, on June 01, 2020, launched PM Street Vendor''s AtmaNirbhar Nidhi (PM SVANidhi) Scheme to facilitate micro-credit to street vendors, vending in urban areas,to enable them to restart their businesses adversely affected during the Covid-19 pandemic. Under this, Street Vendors may avail a working capital loan of up to ?10,000 followed by loans of ?20,000 and ?50,000 in the second and third tranches respectively, on repayment of earlier tranche (s). The Loan amount is directly released to beneficiaries by the Lending Institutions.  However, funds allocated and utilized under PM SVANidhi scheme are as under:                   (Amount in ?Cr)Year	Funds Allocated	Funds Utilized2020-21	113.79	113.672021-22	200.00	156.29For socio-economic upliftment of Street Vendors, MoHUA launched, on 4th January, 2021, ‘SVANidhi se Samriddhi’ in 125 Urban Local Bodies (ULBs). It aims to link PM SVANidhi Scheme beneficiaries and their family members to select central Government welfare Schemes. (d):	A statement showing number of Street Vendors from Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu and Andaman & Nicobar Islands benefitted under DAY-NULM, PM SVANidhi and ‘SVANidhi se Samriddhi’ is at Annexure-III.(e)& (f): PM SVANidhi Scheme facilitates micro-credit to street vendors through Lending Institutions and under ‘SVANidhi se Samriddhi’ street vendors and their families are linked to select Central Government Welfare Schemes.
