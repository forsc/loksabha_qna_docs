                                    QUESTION NO:

                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2022






Industries in Tribal Dominated Districts




Guman Singh Damor














                        Will the Minister of





TRIBAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has any policy in place for setting up of the industries by the tribal youth of the country;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) the number of tribal youth which have opened such industries as per such policy in the country, State- wise;(d) whether the Government has any special plans to allow the youth of tribal dominated districts of Jhabua and Alirajpur to set up industries as they are deprived of such opportunities;(e) if so, the details thereof; and(f) if not, whether the same would be considered?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) to (e): Ministry of Tribal Affairs is implementing a scheme ‘Margin Money Support Scheme for ST Entrepreneurs’ through National Scheduled Tribes Finance and Development Corporation (NSTFDC) in order to finance projects under the Stand-Up India Scheme of Government of India, since December 2020. Under this scheme, the eligible ST Entrepreneurs are allowed to avail financial assistance of NSTFDC to the extent of 15% of the total project cost under Stand-Up India Scheme. No disbursement has been made till date under the scheme due to want of proposals from the Channelizing Agencies.The Ministry also provides grants to states  under Grant to States for 275(1) of the Constitution based on the proposals received from the States.   The Ministry has not received any request from the State specifically for Jhabua and Alirajpur to set up industries.  Cabinet has approved  new scheme of ‘Venture Capital Fund for Scheduled Tribes’ (VCF-ST), which is aimed at promoting Entrepreneurship among the STs. The VCF-ST scheme would be a social sector initiative to promote ST entrepreneurship and to support and incubate the start-up ideas by ST youth. An amount of Rs. 50 cr. has been sanctioned in the budget for the sheme.    NSTFDC) also extends concessional loans to the eligible ST persons for undertaking any income generation activities through its implementing agencies through the following schemes:  Term Loan Scheme: Under this scheme, term loan is provided for viable projects costing upto ?50.00 lakh per unit. Financial assistance is extended upto 90% of the cost of the project and the balance is met by way of subsidy/ promoter contribution/ margin money.Adivasi MahilaSashaktikaran Yojana (AMSY): Under the scheme, NSTFDC, provides loan upto 90% for projects costing upto ?2.00 lakh for economic development of Scheduled Tribes Women. Financial assistance under the scheme is extended at highly concessional rate of interest of 4% per annum.Micro Credit Scheme for Self Help Groups (MCF): Under the scheme, the Corporation provides loans upto ?50,000/- per member and maximum ?5 Lakh per Self Help Group (SHG). The details of Amount and beneficiaries in respect of these schemes is given in Annexure 1 for 2018-19, 2019-20 and 2020-21.Besides, assistance is provided to ST entrepreneurs under various schemes of Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises and Ministry of Food Processing Industries, as detailed at Annexure 2.
