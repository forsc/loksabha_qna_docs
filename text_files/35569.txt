                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2872






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.03.2022






Female Literacy Rate




Jayadev Galla














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware of lower female literacy rate in the country and has conducted a study to investigate the reasons for the same; (b) if so, the details of the study along with the reasons for the same; (c) if not, whether the Government plans to conduct such a research in the country along with the timeline for the same; (d) the schemes implemented to promote female education in the country; and(e) the details of funds provided thereof for the implementation of the aforementioned schemes, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EDUCATION(SMT. ANNPURNA DEVI) (a) to (e) The literacy data in the country is collected through decennial census operation conducted by the Registrar General of India. The last Census was held in 2011. As per the Census 2011, the female literacy rate in the country, in the age group of 7 and above, is 64.63 per cent. In order to improve the female literacy rate, a Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Adult Education namely “Saakshar Bharat” was implemented in the rural areas of 404 districts in 26 States and one UT, that had adult female literacy rate of 50 percent and below as per Census 2001, including left wing extremism affected districts, irrespective of their literacy rates, with special focus on women and other disadvantaged groups, since October 2009. The scheme was extended up to 31.03.2019. A statement indicating details of State/UT-wise funds sanctioned/ released as Central Share under the Saakshar Bharat scheme during the implementation period of the scheme, i.e. 2009-10 to 2018-19 is at Annexure-I. During the implementation of Saakshar Bharat scheme, against the overall target of making 7 crore adult (including 6 crore females) non-literates, as literates, around 7.64 crore learners (including 5.38 crore female), having passed the biannual Basic Literacy Assessment Tests conducted by National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) between August, 2010 to March, 2018, were certified as literates. A new Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Adult Education namely “Padhna Likhna Abhiyaan” is being implemented in rural and urban areas of 33 states/ UTs during the financial year 2020-21, with a target of making 48.16 lakh adult non-literates as literates. The scheme has further been extended upto 31.03.2022. A statement indicating details of State/UT-wise funds sanctioned/ released as Central share under the Padhna Likhna Abhiyaan during the financial year 2020-21 is at Annexure-II.In addition, the Department of School Education and Literacy has launched the Samagra Shiksha – an Integrated Scheme on School Education which envisages the ''school'' as a continuum from pre-school, primary, upper primary, secondary to Senior Secondary levels. The vision of the Scheme is to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education from pre-school to senior secondary stage. The Scheme is in accordance with Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) for Education and has now been aligned with the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 that is to ensure inclusive and equitable, quality and holistic school education. The scheme was initially approved from 2018-19 to 2020-21 and has now been revised and extended upto 2025-26. A statement indicating details of State/UT-wise central share released under Samagra Shiksha during the last 3 years and current year (from 2018-19 to 31.01.2022) is at Annexure-III. ****
