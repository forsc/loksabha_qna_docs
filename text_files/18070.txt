                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1615






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.09.2020






Landline and Mobile Phone Connections




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the number of landline and GSM Mobile phones of MTNL functioning in Delhi and Mumbai and the number of landline and GSM Mobile phones of BSNL functioning during each of the last five years and the current year, State/UT-wise; (b) 	the reasons for huge decline if any, in the number of landline and GSM Mobile phones of both the PSUs during the said period; (c) 	whether the Government has conducted any enquiry with regard to the working of higher officials of these PSUs in this regard; and (d) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS, EDUCATION AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a)	State/UT wise number of landline and GSM Mobile phones of Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) and Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Limited (MTNL) during each of the last five years and the current year, as reported by BSNL and MTNL,is at Annexure.(b)	BSNL and MTNL have informed that decline in landline numbers is due to general trend of shifting of subscribers to mobile services. As regards mobile connections, number of mobile subscribers of BSNL are increasing every year. However, the number of mobile subscribers is decreasing for MTNL. Stiff competition in telecom sector and absence of 4G service in the network of MTNL are the major reasons of decline in mobile connections of MTNL.(c)&(d) 	Department of Telecommunications (DoT) has conducted no enquiry with regard to the working of higher officials of these PSUs in this regard.
