                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4350






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.03.2022






Revamping of PMAY




Bhola Singh














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  	whether any steps are being taken to provide houses to people in rural areas;(b) 	if so, whether Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana (PMAY) is being revamped to speed up delivery of houses to the needy people of rural areas;(c) 	if so, whether the Government has identified hindrances coming in the way of implementation of PMAY; and(d) 	if so, the steps taken in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a) 	In order to achieve the target of “Housing for All” in rural areas, the Ministry of Rural Development is implementing Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana- Gramin (PMAY-G) with effect from 1st April, 2016 to provide assistance to eligible rural households with overall target to construct 2.95 crore pucca houses with basic amenities by March, 2024.  Out of the total target of 2.95 crore houses, a target of 2.60 crore (88.13% of total) houses has been allocated to States/UTs, 2.28 crore (77.28% of total) beneficiaries have been sanctioned houses by States/UTs, 1.76 crore houses have been completed as on 23.3.2022.  (b) to (d):     The Ministry is taking various steps to increase the pace of sanction & completion of houses. The government has identified hindrances coming in the way of implementation of PMAY-G and are taking various steps in this regard:i.	Timely allocation of targets to the States/UTs.ii.	Micro monitoring of house sanction and completion using PMAY-G Dashboard  and other IT tools & technologies. iii.	Regular follow-ups  with States / UTs on providing land to landless beneficiaries. iv.	Regular review by Minister/ Secretary/ Additional Secretary and Joint Secretary. v.	Separate review of States with high targets and sub-par performance. vi.	Timely release of funds to the States/UTs and follow up with the States for onward release to SNA.vii.	Adoption of House Design Typologies  by the States/UTs.viii.	Training to Rural Masons under Rural Mason Training (RMT) programme to make available pool of trained rural masons for faster construction of quality houses. ******
