                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

782






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.02.2022






Environmental Clearance to Mekedatu Dam Project




Prajwal Revanna














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether the environmental clearance to Mekedatu dam project in Karnataka is approved by the Ministry and if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasonstherefor;(b)	the details of forest area and villages likely to besubmerged/displacement of people likely to be affected;(c)	the time by which the environmental clearance islikely to be approved; and(d)	the action taken by the Government for quick environmental clearance to Mekedatu Project?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) to (d) The proposal for grant of Terms of Reference (ToR) to conduct Environment Impact Assessment (EIA) study for construction of Mekedatu Balancing Reservoir and Drinking Water Project in Ramanagara and Chamarajanagar Districts of Karnataka was submitted to the Ministry on 20.06.2019 in order to obtain prior Environmental Clearance(EC) under the provisions of the EIA Notification, 2006, as amended. The proposal was considered by the Expert Appraisal Committee (EAC), constituted by the Ministry to consider the proposal of River Valley and Hydro-electric project for grant of EC, in the 25th EAC meeting held on 19.07.2019. As per project feasibility report submitted by the project authority a total of 4996 ha area at FRL 440 m will be submerged due to this project, of which 2925.5 ha is part of Cauvery Wildlife Sanctuary and 1869.50 ha is part of Reserved Forest.The villages namely, Sangama, Kongedoddi, Madavala, Muthathi and Bommasandrafall in the areas under submergence. The EAC after detailed deliberations on the proposed project inter-alia opined that in view of inter-State issues between Tamil Nadu and Karnataka, an amicable solution needs to be arrived at between the two States. The MoEF&CC, after examination of the issues and in consultation with Ministry of Jal Shakti (MoJS), has decided to consider the proposal for grant of Terms of Reference(ToR) to Mekedatu Balancing Reservoir and Drinking Water Projectonly after acceptance of Draft Pre-feasibility Report (DPR) of the Mekedatu project by the Ministry of Jal Shakti (MoJS) and Cauvery Water Management Authority (CWMA). *****
