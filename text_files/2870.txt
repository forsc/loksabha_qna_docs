                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2604






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.07.2019






National Family Benefit Scheme




Anil Firojiya














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government proposes to extend the maximum age limit of beneficiaries from 59 years to 65 years under the National Family Benefit Scheme;(b)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; (c)	whether the Government is likely to reduce the age limit of widow beneficiaries from 40 years to 18 years under the Indira Gandhi Vidhwa Pension Yojana; and(d)	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (d):	No Sir. There are no such proposals under consideration of the Government either to extend the maximum age limit of beneficiaries under the National Family Benefit Scheme or to reduce the age limit of beneficiaries under the Indira Gandhi National Widow  Pension Scheme. The eligibility criteria is as per the National Social Assistance Programme(NSAP) Guidelines.*****
