                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2645






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.08.2021






Byndoor-Kannur Passenger Train




Kumbakudi Sudhakaran














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has received any petition seeking to reinstate the Byndoor-Kannur passenger train (06605/06606) and if so, the details thereof; (b) whether the Government is aware of the reasons for poor patronage for the suspended Byndoor-Kannur passenger train (06605/06606) service was due to the unscientific timing schedule, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; (c) whether the Government has received several requests seeking to change the unscientific timing schedule of the Byndoor-Kannur passenger train (06605/ 06606); (d) if so, the action taken by the Government on the matter; (e) whether the Government is willing to re-instate the Byndoor-Kannur passenger train (06605/06606) and extend the service further to Mangalore Central; and (f) if so, the timeline for implementation of the service and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMUNICATIONS AND     ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI ASHWINI VAISHNAW)(a) to (f) 56665/56666 Kasaragod-Mookambika Road Byndoor Passenger had been introduced w.e.f 09.02.2015 and was discontinued w.e.f 12.05.2017 owing to poor patronage. Representations, including that from the Hon’ble Members of Parliament for restoration/change in timings of 56665/56666 Kasaragod-Mookambika Road Byndoor Passenger have been received. The same have been examined but not found feasible for implementation, at present.*****
