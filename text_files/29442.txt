                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

110






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.12.2021






Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan




Manne Srinivas Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is reviewing and monitoring the performance of the Sarva ShikshaAbhiyan (SSA) regularly and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether instances of irregularities in the implementation of the scheme have been reported andif so, the details thereof and the action taken by the Government so far to address suchirregularities, State/ UT-wise; and(c) the other steps taken/being taken to implement the scheme effectively in future?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) to (c): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (A) TO (C) OF LOK SABHASTARRED QUESTION NO. 110 TO BE ANSWERED ON 06TH DECEMBER, 2021 ASKEDBY SHRI MANNE SRINIVAS REDDY, HON’BLE MEMBER OF PARLIAMENTREGARDING “SARVA SHIKSHA ABHIYAN”(a) to (c): The erstwhile Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA)programme was under implementation since 2000-2001 for universalization of elementaryeducation throughout the country. Now, SSA along-with other two centrally sponsored schemes ofRashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA) and Teacher Education (TE) have beensubsumed under a new Integrated Scheme for School Education - Samagra Shiksha, launched inthe country from 2018-2019. It is an overarching programme for the school education sectorextending from pre-school to class XII and aims to ensure inclusive and equitable qualityeducation at all levels of school education.The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Samagra Shiksha has an inbuilt concurrent evaluationand monitoring system. The Samagra Shiksha Manual on Financial Management and Procurementcontains detailed procedures on fund flow arrangements, accounting, financial reporting, internalcontrol and audit, external audit, procurement procedures, etc. Annual Reports of the StateImplementing Societies for implementation of the scheme at State / UT level are also laid on theTable of the Parliament every year. Appropriate corrective action is taken by the StateGovernments whenever instances of irregularities come to light in the execution of the scheme.To ensure effective implementation of Samagra Shiksha, there are robust monitoringsystems in place which includes an annual audit by an independent Chartered Accountantempanelled with the Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) of India, a regular CAG audit, asystem of concurrent financial reviews, as well as regular internal audit. An All India PerformanceAudit on Implementation of RTE Act. 2009 was conducted by the Comptroller & Auditor Generalof India (C&AG) for the period extending from 2010-11 to 2015-16 and accordingly Report No.23 of 2017 has been laid on the Table of the Parliament on 21.07.2017.The Ministry of Education convenes periodic meetings with State Education Ministers andSecretaries to review the programme. Educational data is collected through a Unified DistrictInformation System for Education (UDISE+) every year to assess the progress. NationalAchievement Surveys (NAS) are conducted at periodic intervals to assess the learning levels ofchildren. PRABANDH System is the online monitoring platform of Samagra Shiksha forenhancing transparency of the scheme, highlight the achievements, increase accountability ofdifferent stakeholders and implementing agencies, especially States and UTs, thereby improvingthe performance of implementation of the Samagra Shiksha. Performance Grading Index(PGI) isdesigned to catalyse transformational change in the field of school education, to propel States andUTs towards undertaking multipronged interventions to bring about the much-desired educationaloutcomes.*****
