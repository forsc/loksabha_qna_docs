                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

887






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.06.2019






Rapid Rail Transit System




Ajay (Teni) Misra














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether construction of first Rapid Rail Transit System (RRTS) in the country has been started and if so, the details thereof;(b) the name of cities of Uttar Pradesh likely to be benefited therefrom in the first phase of construction; and(c) the total cost involved in the said project and the target fixed by the Government for completion of the same? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                          (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a):	Yes, Sir. National Capital Region Transport Corporation (NCRTC) has informed that construction work of the priority section of 17 k.m. between Sahibabad and Duhai (including Duhai Depot) of the Delhi-Meerut Regional Rapid Transit System (RRTS) corridor has been started. (b):	NCRTC has informed that cities/towns namely Sahibabad, Ghaziabad, Guldhar, Duhai, Muradnagar, Modinagar and Meerut of Uttar Pradesh are likely to be direct beneficiaries of Delhi-Meerut RRTS corridor project.(c):	The total estimated cost of Delhi-Meerut RRTS corridor is      Rs.30,274 crores. The project is expected to be completed in six years.                                 *****
