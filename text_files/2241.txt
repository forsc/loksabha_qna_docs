                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2089






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.07.2019






Flight Disruption due to Fog




Doddaalahalli Kempegowda Suresh














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) has recently taken a serious note of the flight disruptions due to fog despite most airlines claiming to have CAT-III trained pilots;(b) if so, the details of the domestic and international flights cancelled or delayed due to fog during the past two months at Indira Gandhi International Airport; and(c) the losses suffered by the airlines due to fog, diversion of flights and hovering of aircraft due to congestion at airports?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) & (b) No, Sir. DGCA has not received any information in respect of disruption of flights due to fog in the recent past. ;There was no cancellation of flight at IGI airport, Delhi due to fog in the month of April 2019 & May 2019.;(c) DGCA is a safety regulator, its regulations do not require airlines to submit details of their financial losses.;Fog & Congestion may lead to delay as well as diversion of flights which lead to increase of operating costs of airlines.
