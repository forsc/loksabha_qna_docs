                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4108






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






Development of Religious Places of Rajasthan




Ranjeeta Koli













                                                    Sumedhanand Saraswati
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the State-wise/district-wise details of various schemes being implemented by the Government with the aim to develop India as a tourist hub;(b)	the budgetary allocation mode for the said schemes and the amount spent so far during the last five years;(c)	the steps being taken by the Government for the further development of historical and religious places viz. Khatu Shyam ji, Salasar Balaji, Harsh Bhairav Nath Temple, Jeen Mataji, Goverdhan Parikrama from the tourism point of view in Sikar and Bharatpur districts of Rajasthan along with the details thereof; and(d)	the number of foreign tourists who visited India during the last four years including the revenue earned by the Government through tourism during the said period?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF TOURISM 			                  (SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a):	The major schemes of the Ministry of Tourism applicable in all States/Union Territories for developing India as a tourism hub are:i)	Integrated Development of Theme Based Tourist Circuits–Swadesh Darshanii)	Pilgrimage Rejuvenation and Spiritual, Heritage Augmentation Drive (PRASHAD)iii)	Assistance to Central Agencies for Tourism Infrastructure Developmentiv)	Domestic Promotion and Publicity including Hospitality (DPPH)v)	Restructured Scheme of Overseas Promotion & Publicity including Market Development Assistance (MDA)vi)	Financial Assistance to the IHMs/FCIs/IITTM/ICI/NCHMCT/ ITIs/Polytechnic Institutes/Universities/Govt. Colleges/ Govt. Vocational Schools/PSUs etc. vii)	Capacity Building for Service Providers viii)	Champion Services Sector Schemeix)	Market Research Professional Services (MRPS)x)	Safe Tourist Destination for Women(b):	The details of the budgetary allocation for the various schemes of Ministry of Tourism during the last five years is given at Annexure-I. (c):	The Ministry of Tourism has provided financial assistance to State Governments/Union Territory Administrations under the ‘Swadesh Darshan’ and ‘National Mission on Pilgrimage Rejuvenation and Spiritual, Heritage Augmentation Drive (PRASHAD)’ Schemes for the development of tourism related infrastructure at various destinations/sites including historical and religious places. The list of projects sanctioned in the State of Rajasthan under the Swadesh Darshan and PRASHAD Schemes is given at Annexure-II. 	Under the Swadesh Darshan Scheme, the Ministry of Tourism has approved development works at Khatu Shyam Ji Temple, Sikar for Rs.29.50 Crore as part of its project sanctioned under Krishna Circuit theme and Rs.6.77 Crore for development works at Salasar Balaji as part of Spiritual Circuit theme project sanctioned in Rajasthan.(d):	Details of Foreign Tourist Arrivals (FTAs) in India during the period 2017 to 2021 is: <pre>Year	FTAs (in Millions)2017	10.042018	10.562019	10.932020	2.742021 (P)	1.41</pre>(P): ProvisionalSource: Bureau of Immigration, Government of India	Ministry of Tourism does not maintain data on revenue generated from tourism.
