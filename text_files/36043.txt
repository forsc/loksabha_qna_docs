                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3419






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2022






Development of Industrial Clusters




 Bholanath (B.P. Saroj)














                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government is developing industrial clusters in Uttar Pradesh, including incubation centre with joint ventures in various districts of Uttar Pradesh and if so, the details thereof and the progress made in this regard; and(b) 	the role of NASSCOM in setting up of warehouses in Uttar Pradesh and the progress made under Joint Venture with Uttar Pradesh Government?






ANSWER




                                                        ???????  ??? ?????? ???????? ??? ????? ?????? (???? ??? ??????)  THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI SOM PARKASH)(a): 	Yes Sir.1)	Government of Uttar Pradesh launched ‘One District One Product (ODOP)’ in January 2018 to strengthen the various traditional industrial cluster across the State. Under the ODOP program, 62 unique products are identified across 75 districts of Uttar Pradesh. Progress under ODOP scheme till date is placed at Annexure-I. 2)	Under the Industrial Infrastructure component of Micro & Small Enterprises - Cluster Development Programme (MSE – CDP) Scheme of Ministry of MSME, Govt. of India, two Flatted Factory (at Agra & Kanpur) and upgradation of two existing industrial infrastructure (Talkatora - Lucknow and Farrukhabad) have been accorded final approval and these four projects are under implementation.3)	The Government of Uttar Pradesh has undertaken development of following sector specific industrial parks In Uttar Pradesh to promote clusterisation:a.	Medical Device Park at Gautam Buddha (GB) Nagar – 350 acresb.	Toy Park at GB Nagar - 52 acresc.	Apparel Park at GB Nagar - 118 acresd.	Handicraft Park at GB Nagar – 40 acrese.	Logistics Hub (Tappal- Bajna)f.	Heritage City (Raya, Mathura)g.	Film City at GB Nagar - 1000 acresh.	Integrated Manufacturing Clusters (IMC): 1139 Acres at Prayagraj and 1060 acres at  Agrai.	Mega Food Park (Baheri)- 246 acres and Agro Park (Varanasi)- 259 acresj.	Plastic Park at Gorakhpurk.	Mega Leather Footwear and Accessories Cluster at Kanpur (235 acres)4)	The IT & Electronics Department, Government of Uttar Pradesh, in association with Software Technology Parks of India has developed Industrial Clusters in the form of IT/ITeS/Software Technology Parks in the State at Prayagraj, Lucknow, Kanpur and Meerut.5)	Till date 41 incubators are established in Uttar Pradesh across different districts with various institutions under the Uttar Pradesh Start Up policy. List of Incubators are placed at Annexure-II.(b):  	The Government of Uttar Pradesh under its Start-up Policy has recognized ‘NASSCOM 10000 Start-up Warehouse’ as a sector agnostic incubator for promoting start-ups in the state. The incubation centre has incubated more than 60 UP based start-ups which has been able to create  more than 200 job opportunities. The incubation centre has conducted multiple workshops, expert sessions, mentor sessions, investor demo days etc. The Government of Uttar Pradesh has provided grants of Rs. 5 Lakh each to four start-ups incubated in the centre (Edumpl, Talenttatto Private Limited, Ahods and Hindonics) in the form of seed capital under the Start-up policy. *****
