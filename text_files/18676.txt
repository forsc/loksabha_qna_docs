                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

19






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.02.2021






Welfare Schemes for Divyangs




Diya Kumari














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the data of registered divyangs in Rajasthan, district-wise;(b) the details of ongoing welfare schemes for divyangs, with special emphasis on those schemes related to the mobility of such persons;(c) whether the Government proposes to provide battery-operated scooters or two-wheelers to them as it is an efficient as well as environment friendly alternative for transport; and(d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(Dr. THAAWARCHAND GEHLOT)(a) to (d) A statement is laid on the table of the House.Statement referred to in reply to part (a) to (d) of Lok Sabha Starred Question No. 19 to be answered on 02.02.2021 regarding Welfare Schemes for Divyangs raised by MS. DIYA KUMARI.(a) As per Census 2011, there are 15,63,694 Persons with Disabilities (PwDs) in the State of Rajasthan with district-wise break-up at Annexure-I.(b) The Department is implementing a Scheme entitled ''Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids and Appliances (ADIP)'' under which    funds are released to various implementing agencies  to assist the eligible   persons with disabilities (Divyangjan) in procuring durable, sophisticated and scientifically manufactured, modern, standard aids and appliances  including mobility devices, that can promote their physical, social and psychological rehabilitation by reducing the effects of disabilities and enhancing their economic potential. A brief on the other major schemes of the Department is at Annexure-II. (c) and (d)  No, Sir. Under the Schemes of this Department, there is no provision to provide battery-operated scooters or two-wheelers to Divyangjan. However, there is a provision under the Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids and Appliances (ADIP) scheme, for distributing motorized tricycles and wheelchairs to severely disabled persons and to persons suffering from Quadriplegia, Muscular Dystrophy, Stroke, Cerebral Palsy, Hemipeligia and any other person with similar conditions, where either three/four limbs or one half of the body are severely impaired. The extent of subsidy provided under the scheme is Rs. 25,000/-.  This is provided once in ten years to persons aged 16 years and above.******
