                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4166






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2019






Global Bio-India Summit, 2019




Prataprao  Jadhav













                                                    Sanjay Sadashivrao Mandlik
                                                








                                                    Sudheer Gupta
                                                








                                                    Gajanan Chandrakant Kirtikar
                                                








                                                    Bidyut Baran Mahato
                                                








                                                    Raksha Nikhil Khadse
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    whether the Government has organized/proposes to organize Global Bio- India Summit for the first time in India to show case India’s potential in Bio-Technology Sector to the world and chart out actionable roadmap to catapult the industry on the global map, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether his Ministry has set a target of making India the top country in Science and Technology by 2030 and if so, the details and the steps taken by the Government in this regard;(c) the details of Memoranda of Understanding (MoUs) signed in the field of biotechnology by the Government with other countries, country-wise; and(d) the details of setting up of Biotechnology Parks and incubator across the country at present, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of Science & Technology, Earth Sciences and Health & Family Welfare(DR. HARSH VARDHAN)a.	The Department of Biotechnology (DBT) along with its Public Sector Undertaking, Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC) organized Global Bio-India 2019, the first ever event at global scale to showcase India’s potential in Biotechnology sector between 21st -23rd November 2019 at Aerocity, New Delhi. The event had a participation of 2500+ delegates, 190 exhibitors, 25+ countries, 230+ start-ups, 50+ incubators, 60+ research institutions, 800+ business meetings and representation from 9 Indian States. There were 40 sessions, 3 super sessions, 3 separate CEO panels, 2 workshops, policy dialogues, and boot camps in the agenda, across a range of topics. The event had dedicated parallel sessions on vaccine, biosimilars, medical devices & diagnostics, agriculture, industrial Biotechnology, bioservices, emerging therapeutics, Artificial Intelligence (AI), big data, drug discovery, regulatory compliance, Intellectual Property Rights (IPR), social innovation and entrepreneurship. The three day event witnessed Investors Reverse Pitching, International Ecosystem Connect, Translational Research, Global partnerships, conversations with policy leaders and CEOs, discussions with national and international product development experts and regulatory experts. b.	Ministry of Science and Technology is striving to make India a top country in Science and Technology. Autonomous Institutions and National Laboratories of departments under the Ministry of Science and Technology are undertaking focused basic and applied research in diverse fields of Science and Technology. Flagship and Mission programmes have been initiated to bring in Socio-economic transformation for “New India” through following strategy: a) Science - Strive for global impact, b) Technology - Catalyse innovation-driven industry, c) Human Resource - Nurture transdisciplinary leadership and d) Empowerment - Enable inclusive economic development. The Department of Biotechnology is implementing the recommendations of the National Biotechnology Development Strategy 2015-20 with an aim to make India a $100 bn economy by 2025 and a global bio-manufacturing hub. c.	Fourteen Memoranda of Understanding (MoUs) have been signed in the field of Biotechnology by DBT with other countries, namely; Australia (1), Belgium (1), Brazil (1), Canada (1), Costa Rica (1), Cuba (1), Denmark (1), Finland (1), Japan (1), Sweden (1), Switzerland (1), Tunisia (1) and United Kingdom (2).d.	DBT in collaboration with State Governments has established nine Biotechnology parks one each in Assam, Kerala, Jammu & Kashmir, Chhattisgarh, Uttar Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka and two in Tamil Nadu. Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC), a public sector undertaking of Department of Biotechnology has supported 41 incubators in various States and Union Territories, namely; Punjab (1), Delhi (4), Goa (1), Gujarat (4), Haryana (1), Karnataka (5), Maharashtra (3), Odisha (1), Tamil Nadu (7), Telangana (7), Uttar Pradesh (1), Uttrakhand (1), Andhra Pradesh (2), Assam (2) and Mizoram (1).***********
