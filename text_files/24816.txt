                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

519






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

22.07.2021






Audit of National Sports Federations




Shashi Tharoor














                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	the details of the funding share of the National Sports Federations in the Union Government’s overall sports budget since 2018 (year-wise and federation-wise);b)	whether the Government has undertaken an audit of all the National Sports Federations on an annual basis and has uncovered any case of financial irregularities and if so, the details thereof;c)	whether the Government has granted recognition to 54 national federations till September, 2020 and if so, the details thereof;d)	whether the usual recognition to National Sports Federations (NSFs) is not done annually; ande)	if so, the reasons for the Government to offer it only till September, 2020?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR YOUTH AFFAIRS & SPORTS(SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR)(a)	This Ministry supports the National Sports Federations (NSFs) primarily through ‘Assistance to NSFs Scheme’, the data of which is as below:-Scheme of Assistance to NSFs			(In ? crore)<pre>			Financial	BE	RE	Actual ExpenditureYear			 2017-18	302.18	302.18	277.68 2018-19	342.00	245.13	243.63 2019-20	245.00	300.85	300.85 2020-21	245.00	132.00	132.00 2021-22	280.00	-	34.93(till date) </pre> The Ministry provides some assistance to NSFs through the Scheme of Human Resource Development in Sports (HRDS) to support the training and development of their coaches and technical officials, based on proposals submitted by the NSFs from time to time. Details of such financial support is placed at Annexure I.The Ministry also provide some assistance through Khelo India National Programme for Development of Sports Scheme, based on the proposal submitted by them for organizing of sports events. Details of financial support is placed at Annexure-II.Federation wise details of the funding share of the National Sports Federations in the Union Government’s overall sports budget since 2018 is placed at Annexure-III.(b)Since 2010, in order to ensure transparency and accountability in functioning of NSFs, this Ministry introduced a system of annual recognition procedure, under which recognition is renewed on a year to year basis, on submission of detailed documentation including audited annual accounts of NSFs. Accounts of the NSFs receiving grant of more than ? 1crore in a year are subject to audit by the Comptroller & Auditor General of India (CAG).Audit of select NSFs is done by Comptroller and Auditor General and audit findings are sent to NSFs for necessary action at their end. Settlement of observations and audit paras are taken up by the respective NSFs with the Office of the CAG.(c)to(e) In the year 2020, the exercise of renewal of recognition of NSFs for 2020 was held up due to outbreak of COVID-19 pandemic and country-wide lockdown to contain the spread of COVID-19. Considering this unprecedented situation which had arisen due to COVID-19 outbreak and taking into account the interest of sports and sportspersons, this Ministry had issued letter dated 02.06.2020 renewing the annual recognition for 2020 provisionally till 30.09.2020 of the recognized NSFs. However, in compliance of the order dated 24.06.2020 passed by Hon’ble High Court of Delhi in Writ Petition (C) No. 195 of 2010, the Ministry on 25.06.2020 had withdrawn the said letter dated 02.06.2020. Subsequently the Ministry appealed against the Order of the Delhi High Court before the Hon’ble Supreme Court and the Supreme Court vide its order dated 17.9.2020 allowed the Ministry to grant annual recognition to NSFs by informing Delhi High Court. After Hon’ble Supreme Court’s Order, the Ministry renewed the recognition of NSFs on a case to case basis.****
