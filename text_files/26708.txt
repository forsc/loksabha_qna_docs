                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2410






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.08.2021






Schemes for Doubling the Income of Farmers by 2022




Sudhakar Tukaram Shrangre














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)  the details of new technology schemes of the Government working on doubling the income of farmers in the country by the year 2022, and the detailed account of the same thereby especially in the State of Maharashtra; (b) the details of any financial assistance to the farmers to buy cows, sheds for them, make vermicompost or to provide any financial assistance to the farmers in the form of animals/chicken; (c)  the details of the number of farmers who have been supported in the country, especially in the State of Maharashtra and the amount which has been given to each farmer or the applicant farmer during the last three years; and (d) whether it is true that there is a lot of difficulty in taking co-operativeness for animal husbandry, so farmers has been given a lot of work, if so, the reason for this?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING       					(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a)	The Government is implementing various animal husbandry and dairying related schemes to enhance production and productivity of bovines & small ruminants (sheep & goat), piggery & poultry through infusion of new technology and thereby doubling the income of farmers across the country including Maharashtra namely:i.	Rashtriya Gokul Mission(RGM)ii.	National Programme for Dairy Development (NPDD)iii.	Dairy Processing and Infrastructure Development Fund(DIDF)iv.	Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS)(discontinued from 2020-21)v.	Supporting Dairy Cooperatives and Farmer Producer Organizations engaged in dairy activities (SDC&FPO)vi.	National Livestock Mission(NLM)vii.	Animal Husbandry Infrastructure Development Fund(AHIDF)viii.	Livestock Health & Disease Control(LH&DC)ix.	National Animal Disease Control Programme (NADCP) Further, the Government is implementing Rashtriya Gokul Mission (RGM) for development and conservation of indigenous bovine breeds since December 2014 across the country including Maharashtra. The scheme is leading to multiplication of elite animals of indigenous breeds and increased availability of indigenous stock. The scheme supports for breed improvement through new technology interventions like In-Vitro Fertilisation, Sex Sorted Semen, Genomic Chip, Artificial insemination, establishment of ETT, etc.(b) & (c)  The details of financial assistances provided to farmers to buy cows, sheds for them, make vermicompost, etc. and the number of farmers supported in  the country and in the State of Maharashtra  under the following schemes are:  (i).  Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS) : Under DEDS, the details of back ended subsidy disbursed by NABARD throughout the country including Maharashtra during the last three years are as under:Year	All India	Maharashtra	Total Unit	Amount (Rs in crore)	Total Unit	Amount (Rs in crore)2018-19	38842	229.70	3826	18.052019-20	28714	183.33	2332	13.902020-21	23060	137.84	1222	5.97 (ii).
