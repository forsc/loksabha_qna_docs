                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

192






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.03.2020






IT Hubs in Small Cities




Ramcharan Bohra














                        Will the Minister of





ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY be pleased to state:(a)	whether the Government proposes to develop Information Technology (IT) Hubs in small cities and if so, the details thereof;(b)	  the steps taken by the Government in this regard; and(c)  whether the Government proposes to give an impetus to Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in this regard and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        (a) to (c) :    A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.							   STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *192 FOR 04.03.2020  REGARDING  IT HUBS IN SMALL CITIES*******(a)	 and (b):  Government of India has taken various initiatives for demographically distributing IT/ ITeS industry including in small cities. 60 Software Technology Parks of India (STPI) centres have been established till date of which 52 centres are in Tier II/ III cities. Further, Government has approved 23 new STPI centres in Tier II/ III locations.  List of existing and approved STPI Centres are at Annexure-I and II.In addition to the STPI centres, Government has notified 237 SEZs in IT/ ITeS Sector, majority of which are in Tier II/ III locations. Under the Electronics Manufacturing Clusters (EMC) scheme of Government of India, under implementation since 2017, 20 Greenfield EMCs and 3 Common Facility Centres (CFCs) have been established across the country aimed at promoting employment through electronic manufacturing primarily in Tier II/ III locations. Details of these EMCs are at Annexure-III. For providing job incentives and demographically distributing BPO operations, Government is implementing the BPO promotion schemes, namely India BPO Promotion Scheme (IBPS) and North East BPO Promotion Scheme (NEBPS). Under these Schemes, incentives are provided for setting up of BPO IT/ ITeS operation and thus, creating opportunities for establishing 53,300 seats. Since inception, 262 units have started BPO/ ITeS operations for a total of 49,009 seats in over 100 Tier II/ III cities across 28 States/ UTs of the country.Further, Government has notified National Policy on Software Products (NPSP) and National Policy on Electronics (NPE) in the year 2019 to move the IT-Sector up the value chain with special emphasis provided for Tier II/ III locations. In accordance with NPSP, Government has approved the Next Generation Incubation Scheme (NGIS) with a budgetary outlay of Rs. 95.03 crore for establishing software product incubation facilities in 11 Tier II/ III STPI centres.(c):    As per extant Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) policy, 100% FDI is allowed in IT sector under automatic route and in electronics manufacturing, FDI upto 100% is permitted under the automatic route, subject to applicable laws/ regulation; security and other conditionalities. Also, for easing FDI approvals, cell has been created in MeitY for expediting FDI proposal received.  Rs. 1,09,572 crore FDI has been invested in Computer Software and Hardware during FY 2016-2019 and Rs. 28,081 crore in current financial year till September, 2019.******* Annexure-IState-wise list of existing STPI centresS.
