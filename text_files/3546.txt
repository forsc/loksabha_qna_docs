                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3286






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.07.2019






Creation of Medical Seats




K. Subbarayan













                                                    Aparupa  Poddar
                                                








                                                    Pinaki Misra
                                                








                                                    M. Selvaraj
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether Government has created/ proposes to create 2750 medical seats in 25 new Government medical colleges thereby increasing the Medical seats by 4500 in the country for the academic year 2019-20, if so, the details thereof, the number of existing seats and those newly created additional seats, college, State/UT wise;(b) whether Government is aware that there is a shortage of faculty in the existing colleges and the increase of seats would add to this shortage thereby affecting the standard of education for the new medical colleges and if so, the details thereof and the estimated requirement of faculty for the existing as well as the newly added colleges and the manner in which the problem of demand supply ratio is likely to be solved;(c) the criteria adopted by the Medical Council of India (MCI) for the distribution of these seats amongst the existing Medical colleges and hospitals cum Medical Collegesand the number of district hospitals that have been included in the list for enhancement of Medical seats;(d) whether the Government has increased the retirement age of doctors in the country to 65 years of age and proposes to depute or shift surgeons over 60 years of age from hospitals to colleges i.e. technical staff to administrative staff thereto; and(e) if so, the details thereof and if not the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) Total 37 new Medical Colleges with 4500 MBBS seats comprising 25 Government Medical Colleges with 2750 and 12 Private Medical Colleges with 1750 MBBS seats were opened in the academic year 2019-20. Details of existing MBBS seats are given in Annexure-I and details of 37 new opened Medical Colleges are given in Annexure-II. (b) To address the issue of shortage of faculty, equivalence of DNB to MD/MS has been granted. Further, 21 hospitals under Armed Forces have been designated as teaching hospitals for purpose of teaching equivalence. The Consultant/Specialist working in a minimum 300 bedded non teaching District Hospital for more than 18 years/10 years and with more than 04/02 research publications in Indexed journals can be considered designated Professor/Associated Professor, respectively. The maximum age up to which a faculty can work in medical college is 70 years. This has been done with the objective of increasing the pool of teaching and designate Government doctors as teaching faculty. (c) The seats have been distributed on the basis of fulfilment of norms laid down by the Council, namely the Minimum Standard Requirement Regulations, 2000.  No seats have been enhanced by including district hospital to existing medical colleges.(d) & (e) The Central Government has increased the retirement age of Doctors serving in Central Government Institutions/Departments who belong to Teaching Sub-Cadre to 65 years. Further Teaching, Non-Teaching, Public Health Specialists and General Duty Medical Officer sub-cadre of the Central Health Service shall hold the administrative post till the date of attaining the age of 62 years and thereafter their service shall be placed in Non-Administrative positions.
