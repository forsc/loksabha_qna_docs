                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2797






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.03.2021






Damage to Crops by Elephants




S. Muniswamy














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is aware that elephants from the border of Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu forest areas frequently enter into agricultural lands and destroy crops in Malur, Bangarpet and Kolar Gold Fields constituencies in Kolar district of Karnataka; (b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether any representation has been received by the Government in this regard; and(d)	if so, the details thereof and the action taken by the Government thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a)	Yes Sir. Elephants from the boarder of Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu frequently enter into agricultural lands and destroy crops in Malur, Bangrpet and Kolar Gold Fields constituencies in Kolar district of Karnataka. (b)	The details of crop damage in damages caused by wild animals including elephants during the last three years in Kolar district of Karnataka are given below:(Rs in Lakhs)Details	2018-19	2019-20	2020-21(till 04.03.2021)	No of cases	Amount	No of cases	Amount	No of cases	AmountCrop Damage	357	41.541	1488	52.96	866	47.18(c) & (d)	Yes Sir.  A representation from Shri S.Muniswamy, Hon’ble Member of Parliament, was received by the Ministry on 28.12.2020 for measures to be adopted for mitigation of Human-Elephant Conflict.  Ministry referred the representation to the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests (Wildlife) and Chief Wildlife Warden, Government of Karnataka seeking comments and updated status on action taken in the matter.  A reply in this respect by the Hon’ble Minister, Environment, Forest and Climate Change to Hon’ble Member of Parliament has already been provided vide letter no. DO No. 8-1/2019-PE dated 3rd February, 2021. The copy of the letter is annexed.However, the responsibility of managing human elephant conflict lies with State Government.  Under ‘Project Elephant’ scheme, Ministry has provided financial assistance to State Government in which, inter alia, funding for managing human elephant conflict is one of component.  The details of fund released to State Government of Karnataka during the last three financial years are provided below:(Rs. in Lakhs) State/UTs	2018-19	2019-20	2020-21(as on 8/0/2021)Karnataka	353.91401	319.64799	330.40376***
