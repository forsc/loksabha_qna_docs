                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CHEMICALS AND FERTILIZERS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

431






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.07.2021






Waste to Organic Fertilizers




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam














                        Will the Minister of





CHEMICALS AND FERTILIZERS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is running any scheme to reduce the volume of waste by converting it into useful organic fertilizers;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	the status and progress under the city compost scheme State-UT wise, including Gujarat; (d)	whether the Government is planning to increase the number of villages adopted by the fertilizer companies to promote   the use of compost, and (e)	if so, the details thereof pertaining to Gujarat? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF HEALTH & FAMILY WELFARE AND CHEMICALS & FERTILIZERS (SHRI MANSUKH MANDAVIYA)(a) & (b):  Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA) has informed that under the Solid Waste Management (SWM) Component of Swachh Bharat Mission-Urban, (SBM-U),  processing of waste to compost is being practiced by Urban Local Bodies(ULBs)   to reduce volume of waste by converting to organic compost fertilizer. Further, under SBM-U, 35% of Central Financial Assistance (CFA) as Viability Gap Fund (VGF) is released for management of Municipal Solid Waste (MSW), including setting up of composting facilities. Information Education and Communication (IEC) Campaign is conducted regularly for educating ULBs and citizens about segregation and processing of wet waste and  scientific processing of Municipal Solid Waste through processing facilities for wet/ organic/ biodegradable and dry waste in all urban local bodies in the country.   Primary focus has been placed on door-to-door collection, source segregation of dry and wet components of waste and promoting conversion of wet waste into city compost (organic manure). At present, approximately 82% of City Wards in the urban areas are practicing source segregation of waste and approximately 68% of the waste is being processed.(c) :   The Government of India has approved a policy on promotion of city compost which has been duly notified by the Department of Fertilizers on 10.2.2016 wherein Market Development Assistance (MDA) of Rs. 1500/MT in the form of subsidy is provided for sale of city compost. The State/UT wise sale of city compost by marketing companies is at Annexure-I(d) & (e):   To promote the use of city compost, the Fertilizers marketing Companies   have   adopted 498 villages across the Country.  A list of 56 villages adopted by the fertilizer marketing companies in Gujarat is at Annexure -II  ********
