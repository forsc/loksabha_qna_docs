                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    EXTERNAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1559






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2021






Covid Assistance to Neighbouring Countries




Jagdambika Pal 














                        Will the Minister of





EXTERNAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has assisted neighbouring countries and other countries around the world during COVID-19 pandemic with health care and other materials, information and knowledge and if so, the details thereof;(b)	the details of monetary value of assistance provided to foreign countries related to COVID-19 pandemic;(c)	whether the country has received assistance from foreign countries in relation to COVID-19 pandemic; and(d) 	if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EXTERNAL AFFAIRS[SHRI V. MURALEEDHARAN](a) & (b)	India provided assistance in the form of medicines and medical equipment to over 150 countries, including grants-in-aid valued at around Rupees 80 crore to 82 countries. A list of these countries is attached at Annex I. In addition, India sent Rapid Response Medical teams to help some countries in their fight against the pandemic. Recently, India supplied Covid vaccines to Bangladesh, Myanmar, Nepal, Bhutan, Maldives, Mauritius, Seychelles, Sri Lanka, Bahrain and Oman.(c) & (d)	 India received assistance from a few countries including Japan, USA, France, Germany and Israel in the form of medical equipment and grants-in-aid.***
