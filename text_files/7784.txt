                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ATOMIC ENERGY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1496






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.11.2019






Functioning of Kudankulam Nuclear Power Plant




Kani K. Navas














                        Will the Minister of





ATOMIC ENERGY


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	whether  it is true that the second nuclear plant at Kundakulam in Tamil Nadu has stopped operation and the date of unit''s revival is unknown and if so, the details thereof;(b)	whether it has affected the quantum of power generation in the State of Tamil Nadu;(c)	if so, the details thereof and whether the Government has proposed action to provide a remedy in the current situation; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR PERSONNEL, PUBLIC GRIEVANCES & PENSIONS AND PRIME MINISTER’S OFFICE (DR.JITENDRA SINGH):    (a)	No, Sir.  The second unit of Kudankulam Nuclear Power Plant (KKNPP) -2 is operating at about 65% power.(b)to(d)KKNPP-2 is presently being operated at about 65% power as increased vibration in generator stator is observed at higher power levels. The generator stator is planned to be replaced during the next refueling outage planned from December 2019, following which the unit is expected to be operated at full power.	*****
