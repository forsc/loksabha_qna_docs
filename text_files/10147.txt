                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3616






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2019






Housing for All




Ranjeetsinha Hindurao Naik-Nimbalkar














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana-Gramin (PMAY-G) for providing ‘Housing for All’;(b)	the allocation of funds made for the above schemes along with the progress made under those schemes till date, State-wise; and(c)	the details of the share of Centre and State Governments therein?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)a):	To achieve the objective of “Housing for All” by 2022, in rural areas of the country, the Government has re-structured the erstwhile rural housing scheme Indira Awaas Yojana (IAY) into Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana- Gramin (PMAY-G) w.e.f. 1st April, 2016. The scheme aims to provide a pucca house with basic amenities to all houseless households and households living in kutcha and dilapidated house in rural areas. The target under PMAY-G is construction of 2.95 crore houses by year 2021-22 in two phases. The target for construction of houses during Phase I (2016-17 to 2018-19) and Phase II (2019-20 to 2021-22) are 1.00 crore and 1.95 crore, respectively. The minimum size of a PMAY-G house is 25 sq. mt. including a dedicated area for hygienic cooking. Unit assistance of Rs.1.20 lakh in plains and Rs.1.30 lakhs in hilly States/Union Territories (UTs), difficult areas and Integrated Action Plan (IAP) districts is provided to the beneficiaries. The beneficiary is also entitled to 90/95 person days of unskilled wages under National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (MGNREGS). Further, assistance of Rs. 12,000 is extended to eligible PMAY-G beneficiaries for construction of toilet through convergence with Swachh Bharat Mission – Gramin (SBM-G), MGNREGS or any other dedicated source of funding. b):	The State/UT-wise details of Physical and Financial progress made under PMAY-G is given in Annexure-I & II respectively.c):	The sharing pattern under PMAY-G between Central and State Government is in the ratio of 60:40 except in case of the UT of Jammu & Kashmir, North-Eastern States and Hilly States wherein the sharing pattern is 90:10. In case of UTs, the full cost of PMAY-G houses is borne by the Central Government.
