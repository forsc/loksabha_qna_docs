                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

607






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2020






Mobile Towers in Maharashtra




Vinayak Bhaurao Raut














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Union Government is considering to install more mobile towers of BSNL for better connectivity in Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg districts of Maharashtra; (b) 	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and (c) 	the time by which these mobile towers are likely to be installed?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR COMMUNICATIONS,  HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT AND ELECTRONICS & INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (SHRI SANJAY DHOTRE)(a) to (c) 	BSNL is continuously adding new equipments and upgrading its existing mobile network in both rural and urban areas in all of its Licensed Service Areas (LSAs) including Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg districts of Maharashtra state to improve its mobile coverage. BSNL has informed that in its Phase VIII.4 mobile expansion plan, out of total planned 76 Base Transceiver Stations (BTSs) in Sindhudurg district and 66 BTSs in Ratnagiri district, 48 BTSs and 49 BTSs respectively have been installed so far. The installation work of all remaining BTSs will be completed in financial year 2020-21. ************
