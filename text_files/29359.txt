                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1051






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.12.2021






Healthcare Facilities for HIV Affected Persons




Vishnu Dayal Ram














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government proposes to provide special healthcare facilities for persons affected by HIV during the COVID-19 pandemic;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether the Government took any preventive measures to ensure people with HIV do not get affected by COVID-19;(d)	if so, the details thereof; and(e)	the details of the data regarding HIV infected persons who were also infected with Coronavirus?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(Dr. BHARTI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a) to (d):  The Government has facilitated continued service provision for prevention, testing and treatment of HIV/AIDS and related co-morbidities, free of cost, to all target population during the COVID-19 pandemic through various interventions and facilities under the National AIDS Control Programme since March 2020. Initiatives like targeted care for each population group, multi-month dispensation of anti-retroviral drugs to people living with HIV, community-based screening for HIV and take-home dosing have been taken during the pandemic. National AIDS Control Organization (NACO) had instructed States not to deny any services to stranded migrants including for provision of anti-retroviral drugs. The Government is ensuring continuity of healthcare services to persons infected and affected by HIV during the COVID-19 pandemic through regularly review and appropriate reiteration of instructions and advisories. NACO has facilitated adherence to the COVID-19 appropriate preventive measures at all facilities under National AIDS Control Programme (NACP) through reviews and issuance of advisories and guidance.  This included facilitation of provision of masks, gloves, sanitizers for staffs and beneficiaries at all health facilities. States were also advised to prioritization of PLHIV for vaccination against COVID-19 as per extant guidelines. Community-based service delivery was facilitated to avoid travel by target beneficiaries and thus facilitating prevention of COVID-19 infections.(e): Such data is not maintained centrally.
