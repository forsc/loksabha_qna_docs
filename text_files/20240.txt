                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1728






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.02.2021






Maintenance of NH Projects in U.P




Maneka Sanjay Gandhi













                                                    Rekha Arun Verma
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the steps taken/being taken by the Government for development and maintenance of National Highways (NHs) in Uttar Pradesh (UP) during each of the last three years and the current year, NH-wise;(b) whether the works on NHs in UP are completed as per the schedule and if so, the details of the works completed, under progress and pending as on date, NH-wise;(c) whether the Government has set up any mechanism for monitoring the work on NHs and if so, the details thereof;(d) the details of NHs constructed or renovated in UP by National Highways Authority of India during the said period;  (e)  the other steps taken by the Government to improve the condition of NHs in UP;(f) the funds allocated and utilised on construction, maintenance and repair of NHs in UP during the said period, year-wise; and(g) whether any obstacles are being faced by the Government in the implementation of NH projects in the State and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)a) Government has identified and taken up development of various National Highways across the Uttar Pradesh by bridging critical infrastructure gaps through effective interventions like development of Economic Corridors, Inter Corridors and Feeder Routes, National Corridor Efficiency Improvement, Border and International connectivity roads and Green-field expressways. The maintenance of the National Highways is a continuous process and taken up as per schedule of maintenance and emergent needs. The details of the steps taken/being taken by the Government for development and maintenance of National Highways (NHs) in Uttar Pradesh (UP) during each of the last three years and the current year are at Annexure-A.b) The details of the works undertaken during each of last three years and current year with status are at Annexure-B. c) The projects are monitored by collecting real time data on a digital platform named “Project Monitoring & Information System (PMIS)”. Works are regularly monitored through frequent review meetings at various levels of Regional Officers, Director General Road Development & Special Secretary, Secretary Road Transport & Highways, Minister of State and Minster Road Transport & Highways. d) The details of NHs constructed or renovated in UP by National Highways Authority of India during the last three years and the current year are at Annexure-C.e) The government regularly advises its implementing agencies and field officers to frequently inspect the National Highways and take all necessary actions for improvements.   f) The details of the funds allocated and utilised on construction, maintenance and repair of NHs in UP during the last three years and current year are at Annexure-D. g) The obstacles faced in implementation of NH projects majorly pertain to preconstruction activities viz. land acquisition, forest clearance, utility shifting etc. However, these are resolved in close co-ordination with concerned authorities of central and state governments.
