                                    ANSWERED ON:

ICDS Entitlements




 Dr. T. Sumathy (a) Thamizhachi Thangapandian














                        Will the Minister of





WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the services other than supplementary nutrition programs within Integrated Child Development Scheme (ICDS) entitlements such as growth monitoring and home visits are being provided, if so, the details thereof along with ICDS implementation progress report, State/UT-wise;(b)	whether the Government has conducted any assessments of COVID-19 on undernutrition among children, if so, the details thereof;(c)	the additional allocations made under COVID-19 package for addressing the nutritional gaps for women and children; and(d)	whether there is any proposal for increasing the Supplementary nutrition programs norms under ICDS looking at the severe impact of COVID-19 on employment and food security, if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER MINISTER OF WOMEN AND CHILD DEVELOPMENT(SHRIMATI SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI) (a)     Under the Anganwadi Services Scheme there is a provision of providing growth monitoring and home visit services on a regular basis to the registered beneficiaries through Anganwadi Workers. In order to ensure growth monitoring of children and home visits, an incentive of Rs. 500 and Rs. 250 is provided per month to Anganwadi Workers (AWWs) and Anganwadi Helpers (AWHs). The State/UT wise details of growth monitoring of children is at Annexure I. State/UT wise details of ICDS implementation progress is at Annexure II (A to E). (b)    Malnutrition in the country is assessed periodically by specialised National Family Health Survey (NFHS) being conducted by Ministry of Health & Family Welfare with International Institute for Population Sciences (IIPS) being the nodal agency for conducting the surveys using a standardized methodology. NFHS – 4 and 5 were conducted during 2015-16 and 2019-20 respectively. However, the recently released report of NFHS-5 (2019-20) is available for 22 States/UTs. Further, the Comprehensive National Nutrition Survey (CNNS) was also conducted by Ministry of Health & Family Welfare during the year 2016-18. (c ) and (d) Several initiatives were taken by MoWCD to ensure delivery of services under Anganwadi Services Scheme to the target beneficiaries. The Ministry issued guidelines on Quality Assurance, Roles and Responsibilities of Duty Holders, procedure for procurement, integrating AYUSH concepts and Data Management and Monitoring through ''Poshan Tracker'' for transparency, efficiency and accountability in delivery of Supplementary Nutrition on 13th January, 2021. During the pandemic, as per the MHA guidelines, all Anganwadi Centres across the country were closed to limit the impact of COVID-19. However, to ensure continuous nutritional support to Anganwadi beneficiaries, Anganwadi Workers and Helpers distributed Supplementary Nutrition at the doorsteps of beneficiaries once in 15 days and have distributed medicines as per requirement.
