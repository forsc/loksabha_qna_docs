                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4517






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2021






Distribution of Foodgrains to Poor




Jyotsna Charandas Mahant














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the foodgrains allocated for the poor is being distributed on time in all the States; (b) 	whether adequate number of godowns have been constructed by the Government for storage of the said foodgrains allocated for the poor in the country including in Chhattisgarh; and (c) 	if so, the details thereof, State-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        A N S W E R MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a): 	Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS) is operated under the joint responsibilities of the Central and State/UT Governments. Inter-alia the operational responsibilities of distributing foodgrains to National Food Security Act (NFSA) beneficiaries lies with the respective State/UT governments. Whereas, this Department has issued the allocation order to all States/UTs as per the accepted coverage of beneficiaries under the Act for timely distribution to all NFSA beneficiaries through TPDS as per the distribution cycles followed by the States/UTs. (b)&(c): Requirement of storage capacity depends on the level of procurement, & offtake under National Food Security Act (NFSA) & Other Welfare Scheme (OWS).  FCI assesses and monitors the storage capacity and based on any storage gap assessment, storage capacities are created/hired.As on 28.02.2021, Storage capacity available with FCI and State agencies for storage of central pool foodgrain stock is 819.48 Lakh MT (Covered – 669.40 LMT and CAP – 150.08 LMT) against the stock holding of 577.78 Lakh MT.
