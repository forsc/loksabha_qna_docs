25.03.2021






Command Area Development Programme




Hanuman Beniwal














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the funds released and spent by the Government during the last three years under Command Area Development Programme (CADP); (b) the quantum of funds and the names of works for which funds have been spent out of the released funds during the last three years in concerned districts of Rajasthan under CADP; (c) whether it is a fact that schemes are not being implemented properly in command villages despite spending funds under the said programme; (d) if so, the details thereof; and (e) the current status of the said schemes related to Rajasthan?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a)  During the last 3 years (2017-18 to 2019-20) total Central Assistance (CA) of Rs.1690.35 crore has been released under Command Area Development and Water Management (CADWM) Programme to various States. The States have reported a total expenditure (including State share) of Rs.3444.12 crore. The Year-wise and State-wise details of CA release and funds spent under CADWM Programme is given at Annexure-I. (b) to (e)  Two projects from Rajasthan are presently included under the ongoing CADWM programme. While the ‘Narmada Canal project’ is included only for non-structural interventions, ‘Modernization of Gang Canal project’ involves both structural and non-structural interventions. The structural interventions mainly aim for last-mile connectivity through construction of lined field channels, whereas the non-structural interventions are for strengthening of participatory irrigation management.
