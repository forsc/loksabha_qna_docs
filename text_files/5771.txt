                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5266






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.07.2019






Utilisation of Funds




Khagen Murmu














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the funds provided by the Union Government to State Governments to augment water capacity are being utilised in all districts and if so, the details thereof; (b) whether pure drinking water is being supplied in all rural areas by utilising the said funds and if so, the details thereof; and (c) the percentage of funds spent by the concerned State Governments on water conservation out of the funds allocated by the Union Government?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) to (c) Water resources development being a State subject, the planning including allocation of funds, execution, and operation and maintenance of projects are undertaken by the State Governments.Ministry of Jal Shakti is implementing the Scheme for Repair, Renovation and Restoration (RRR) of water bodies which has multiple objectives like comprehensive improvement and restoration of water bodies thereby increasing tank storage capacity, groundwater recharge, increased availability of drinking water, improved agriculture/horticulture productivity, improved water use efficiency; by promotion of conjunctive use of surface and ground water. State-wise details of projects under RRR scheme are given at Annexure I.Under National Rural Drinking Water Programme, the Ministry monitors the coverage of drinking water in terms of Fully Covered (FC) Habitations (that is, getting more than 40 Litres per capita per day (LPCD), Partially Covered (PC) habitations (that is getting less than 40 LPCD) and Quality Affected (QA) habitations (that is containing chemical contamination). As per information furnished by the State on integrated management information system ( IMIS), 81.03% of rural habitations are fully covered, 51.61% are partially covered and 3.36% are quality affected.   Further, 76.25% of rural population is fully covered, 19.77% is partially covered and 3.98% is affected with contaminated water.***** Annexure- I Annexure referred to in reply to part (a) to (c) of Unstarred Question No. †5266 to be answered in Lok Sabha on 25.07.2019 regarding “Utilisation of Funds”Details of ongoing projects (since XII plan) under RRR of Water Bodies Scheme (up to June 2019)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        ( Rs.
