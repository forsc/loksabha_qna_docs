                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1322






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.07.2021






Pashu Sanjivani- An Animal Health Card




Prasun Banerjee














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has sanctioned funds under Rashtriya Gokul Mission for setting up of 21 gokul grams as integrated cattle development centres in the country; (b) if so, the present status of the project and the productivity therein; and (c) the details of present status in support of ‘Pashu Sanjivani- animal health card’ in the country thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWER THE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a) &(b)	Yes, Sir. Setting up of 21 Gokul Grams have been sanctioned under Rashtriya Gokul Mission and out of this funds have been released to the States for establishment of 16 Gokul Grams. Present status of the Gokul Grams along with productivity of animals therein is given at Annexure-I.(c)	  States and Union Territories have been supported under Pashu Sanjivni component of Rashtriya Gokul Mission for identification and issuing Nakul Swasthya Patra (animal health cards) to animals in milk.  Component Pashu Sanjivni has been discontinued since inception of National Animal Disease Control Programme (NADCP) in 2019. Under National Animal Disease Control Programme identification of animals is extended to all bovines as well as sheep, goat and pigs numbering total 53.5 crore. As on 22nd July 2021, 16.85 crore animals have been identified and their data on breeding and health has been uploaded on Information Network for Animal Productivity and Health.*****
