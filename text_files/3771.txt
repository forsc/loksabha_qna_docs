                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3468






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Vocational Education




Ranjanben Dhananjay Bhatt














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is considering to link the students passing out of school, collegesand Universities with vocational education;(b) if so, whether the Government has taken any action in this regard; and(c) if so, the details thereof and, if not, the reasons therefor?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (c): Yes, Sir. Government is working on multiple fronts to link the students passing out ofSchools, colleges and Universities with vocational education. The details of the same are asfollows:<pre>1. Samagra Shiksha is a School education scheme which integrates vocational education withgeneral academic education with the aim to prepare educated and employable human resources.National Skills Qualifications Framework (NSQF) compliant vocational courses are taught to thestudents from class 9th to 12th. At the secondary level i.e. class 9th and 10th, vocationalmodules are offered to the students as an additional subject. At Senior Secondary level, i.e. classXI and XII, vocational courses are offered as a compulsory (elective) subject.2. Expansion of skill education through Community Colleges.3. Implementation of Bachelor of Vocation Courses (BVoC) for students passing out of schools.4. Running of Deen Dayal Upadhyaya (DDU) Kausal Kendras, which provide basic andsupplementary vocational education to improve the learner’s employability, with the necessarywork skills (soft, communication, ICT skill etc) and also cater to school dropouts.5. Operation of a portal for facilitating Internships by AICTE.</pre>All the universities and colleges of the country may run these approved vocational courses.*****
