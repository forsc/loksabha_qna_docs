                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2743






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.07.2019






Competition in Manufacturing Field




Hibi Eden














                        Will the Minister of





COMMERCE AND INDUSTRY


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 whether the industries in the country are facing severe competition in      manufacturing field from China; (b)	  if so, the details thereof; (c)	whether the Government has any mechanism to safeguard domestic industrial sector; (d)	if so, the details thereof; (e)	whether the uncontrolled inflow of Chinese goods has negatively affected ‘Make in India’ programme; and (f)	 if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ???????? ??? ?????? ?????? (???? ????? ????) THE MINISTER OF COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI  PIYUSH GOYAL)(a)&(b):	India’s import from China was USD 61.2 billion in 2016-17, USD 76.3 billion in 2017-18. This has decreased to USD 70.3 billion in 2018-19. (c) to (f):	The following remedial steps have been taken in this regard:i.	In products where there has been injury to domestic industry, relief has been provided through anti-dumping duty on 97 products, countervailing measures on 3 products and safeguard duty for 1 product imposed against China. Details of trade remedial measures in force against import from China are placed in Annexure. ii.	Basic Custom Duty has been increased on several products to protect domestic industry. iii.	Quality control orders are being enforced to check sub-standard imports. iv.	The Government through its initiatives such as Make in India, Ease of Doing Business, Startup India and sectoral schemes/programmes promotes investment in domestic manufacturing and endeavours to improve the competitiveness of domestic industry. *******
