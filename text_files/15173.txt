                                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIAMINISTRY OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENTLOK SABHAUNSTARRED QUESTION NO. 3887TO BE ANSWERED ON 17.03.2020WELFARE SCHEMES FOR SCs AND OBCs 3887. SHRI POTHUGANTI RAMULU: Will the Minister of SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT be pleased to state: (a) the details of the schemes being implemented by the Government for the welfare of Scheduled Castes (SCs) and Other Backward Classes (OBCs) along with the details of beneficiaries during the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise; (b) the amount of funds allocated and utilized for each of these schemes during the said period with special reference to Telangana; (c) whether the Government is considering to implement more welfare schemes for these sections of the society; and (d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) and (b): The Government runs several schemes for the welfare of Scheduled Castes(SCs) and Other Backward Classes (OBCs). The details of these schemes are attached at Annexure-1. Central Assistance is released to States/UTs under the Centrally sponsored Schemes. The details of beneficiaries and amount of funds released during the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise is at Annexure-2.Central Assistance released to the States/UTs under these schemes is usually fully utilized by them. In case any amount remains unspent at the end of an year, the same is adjusted during the subsequent release of funds.Under the Central Sector Schemes, funds are released to beneficiaries directly from this department. State-wise data of beneficiaries and funds are, thus, not maintained for such schemes. The total number of beneficiaries covered and expenditure incurred during the last three years and current year under these schemes is attached at Annexure-3.(c) and (d): At present there is no such proposal****Annexure-1Statement referred to part (a) and (b) of the reply to the Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No. 3887 for 17.03.2020A.	CENTRALLY SPONSORED SCHEMES FOR WELFARE OF SCHEDULED CASTES1.	PRE –MATRIC SCHOLARSHIP FOR SC STUDENTS STUDYING IN CLASSES IX & X:The objectives of the scheme are to support parents of SC children for education of their wards studying in classes IX and X to reduce the incidence of drop – out. Parent/Guardian’s income should not exceed Rs. 2.5 lakh per annum.2.	POST MATRIC SCHOLARSHIP FOR SCHEDULED CASTE STUDENTS: The objective of the Scheme is to provide financial assistance to scheduled caste students studying at post matriculation or post–secondary stage to enable them to complete their education. Parent/Guardian’s income should not exceed Rs. 2.5 lakh per annum.3.	SPECIAL CENTRAL ASSISTANCE (SCA) TO SCHEDULED CASTES SUB- PLAN (SCSP):Special Central Assistance (SCA) to Scheduled Castes Sub plan (SCSP) Scheme started in 1980, under which 100 per cent grant is given to the States/UTs, as an additive to their Scheduled Castes Sub Plan (SCSP). The main objective is to give a thrust to family oriented schemes of economic development of SCs below the poverty line.4.	BABU JAGJIVAN RAM CHHATRAWAS YOJNA FOR GIRLS AND BOYS (BJRCY):The objective of the Scheme is to provide residential accommodation facilities to SC Boys and Girls studying in middle schools, higher secondary schools, colleges and universities.5.	PARADHAN MANTRI ADARSH GRAM YOJANA (PMAGY):The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of ‘Pradhan Mantri Adarsh Gram Yojana” (PMAGY) is being implemented for integrated development of village having morethan 50%  of Scheduled Castes (SC) Population through effective and targeted implementation of existing schemes of Central and State Governments in a convergent manner.6.	SCHEME FOR IMPLEMENTATION OF PROTECTION OF CIVIL RIGHTS AND PREVENTION OF ATROCITIES (PCR/POA) ACTS Under this Scheme, Central Assistance is released to the concerned State Governments/UTs for effective implementation of the two Acts viz Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955 and Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities ) Act,1989.7.	PRE-MATRIC SCHOLARSHIP TO THE CHILDREN OF THOSE ENGAGED IN OCCUPATION INVOLVING CLEANING AND PRONE TO HEALTH HAZARDThe objective of providing financial assistance to children of those whose parents/guardians belong to one of the following categories, to pursue Pre-matric education:-(a)	Persons who are Manual Scavengers as defined under section 2(I) (g) of Manual    Scavengers Act 2013;(b)	Tanners & Flayers;(c) 	Waste pickers and(d)	Persons engaged in hazardous cleaning as defined in section 2 (I) (d) of Manual Scavengers Act 2013.B.	CENTALLY SPONSORED SCHEMES FOR WELFARE OF OTHER BACKWARD CLASSES8.	PRE MATRIC SCHOLARSHIP TO THE OBCSThe aim of this Scheme is to motivate children of OBCs studying at Pre Matric stage. Scholarship are awarded to students belonging to OBCs whose parents/guardian’s income from all sources does not exceed Rs. 2,50 lakh per annum.9.	POST MATRIC SCHOLARSHIP FOR OBC STUDENTS:The Scheme is intended to promote higher education by providing financial support to OBC students studying at Post Matric/Post- Secondary levels leading to their earning Ph.D degree. The Scheme is a “Funds Limited” Scheme. At present, the parental income ceiling for eligibility under the scheme is Rs. 1.50 lakh per annum.10.	CONSTRUCTION OF HOSTELS FOR OBC BOYS AND GIRLS:The Scheme of Construction of Hostels for OBC Boys and Girls has been revised w.e.f 2017 -18. The Scheme aims at providing hostel facilities to Students belonging to socially and educationally backward classes, especially from rural areas to enable them to pursue secondary and higher education. C.	CENTRAL SECTOR SCHEMESFOR WELFARE OF SCHEDULED CASTES1.	TOP CLASS EDUCATION FOR SC STUDENTS.The Objective of the scheme is to promote quality education amongst SC Students, by providing financial support for pursuing studies beyond 12th class.2.	FREE COACHING SCHEME FOR SC AND OBC STUDENTSThe objective of the scheme is to provide coaching of good quality for economically         disadvantaged SC and OBC candidates to enable them to appear in competitive examinations and succeed in obtaining appropriate job in public/private sector.3.	NATIONAL FELLOWSHIP SCHEME FOR SC STUDENTSThe objective of the scheme is to provide fellowship in the form financial assistanceto students belonging to Scheduled Caste category to pursue higher studies leading to M. Phil, Ph.D in Science, Humanities, Social Science and Engineering and Technology, in Indian Universities/Institutions/Colleges recognized by University Grants Commission (UGC).4.	NATIONAL OVERSEAS SCHOLARSHIP FOR SC STUDENTSNational overseas Scholarship is meant to provide assistance to selected Scheduled Caste, Denotified, Nomadic, Semi–nomadic Tribes, Landless Agricultural Laborers and Traditional Artisan’s students for pursuing higher studies of Master’s level courses and Ph.D programmes abroad.5.	ASSISTANCE TO STATE SCHEDULED CASTES DEVELOPENT CORPORATIONS (SCDSs)The objective of the scheme is to provide financial assistance to States SC Development Corporations in form of margin money on low rate of interest and subsidy in order to reduce the repayment liability.6.	VENTURE CAPITAL FUND FOR SCHEDULED CASTES (VCF-SC) It is a social sector Initiative implemented nationally in order to promote entrepreneurship among the Scheduled Castes population in India.7.	CREDIT ENHANCEMENT GUARANTEE SCHEME FOR SCHEDULED CASTESThe objective of the Scheme is to promote entrepreneurship amongst the Scheduled Castes, by providing Credit Enhancement Guarantee to Banks and Financial Institutions who shall be providing financial assistance to different entrepreneurs. 8.	GRANTS-IN-AID FOR VOLUNTARY ORGARNIZATIONS (VOs)The objective of the scheme is to enhance the reach of development interventions of the Government and fill the gap in service deficient SC dominant areas in the education sector through the efforts of VOs and other organizations and to provide them environment for socio- economic upliftment.9.	NATIONAL SCHEUDULED CASTES FINANCE AND DEVELOPMENT CORPORATION (NSFDC)NSFDC is a fully owned GOI undertaking under this Ministry incorporated during 1989 as a ‘not-for-profit’ company under Section 25 of the Companies Act 1956 with the objectives of financing, facilitating and mobilizing funds for the socio-economic development of persons belonging to SCs having annual family income up to Rs 3.0 lakhs both in rural and urban areas.D.	CENTRAL SECTOR SCHEMESFOR WELFARE OF OTHER BACKWARD CLASSES10.	NATIONAL FELLOWSHIP FOR OBC STUDENTSThe scheme aims at providing financial assistance to the OBC students in obtaining   quality higher education leading to degrees such as M.Phil and Ph.D in universities, research institutions and scientific institutions. 11.	DR. AMBEDKAR SCHEME OF INTEREST SUBSIDY ON EUDCATIONAL  LOANS FOR OVERSEAS STUDIES FOR THE STUDNTS BELONGS TO OTHER BACKWARD CLASSES(OBCs) and ECONOMICALLY BACKWARD CLASSES(EBCs)The objective of the scheme is to award interest subsidy to meritorious OBC and EBC students so as to provide them better opportunities for higher education abroad and enhance their employability.12.	ASSISTANCE FOR SKILL DEVELOPMENT OF OBCS/DNTS/EBCSThe aim of the scheme is to involve the voluntary sector and National Backward Classes Finance and Development Corporation (NBCFDC) to improve educational   and socio- economic conditions of the target group i.e. OBCs/DNTs/EBCs, with a   view to upgrade their skill to enable them to start income generating activities on their own or get gainfully employed in some sector or the other.
