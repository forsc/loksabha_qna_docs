                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1185






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.07.2021






Technical Training to Agriculture Labourers




Sadashiv Kisan Lokhande














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the steps taken by the Government to impart technical training to the agriculture labourers in the country;(b)	if so, the details thereof, State-wise;(c)	whether the Government proposes to formulate any scheme to fix the wages of agriculture labourers based on their labour; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE ???? ?? ????? ?????? ??????(SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)	Government has taken steps towards skill development of agriculture labourers through the following interventions :-?	Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship through its flagship scheme Pradhan Mantri Kaushal VikasYojana (PMKVY) is imparting Skill Development training under various sectors including the Agriculture Sector. Under PMKVY, skill training is provided through Short Term Training (STT) and Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL). As on 15/07/2021, a total of 7.92 lakh candidates have been trained in Agriculture Sector under PMKVY. ?	The Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) has established a network of 725 Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) in the country mandated with Technology Assessment and Demonstration for its application and Capacity Development. KVKs organize Demonstrations, Training Programs and Skill Development programs for the benefit of farmers and farm women, rural youth and in-service extension personnel. The KVKs imparted skill training to the migrant labours on livelihood activities in 116 districts of Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Jharkhand, Rajasthan and Odisha. The KVKs organized 1922 skill training programs for 68136 migrant labours.(b)	The State-wise details of candidates trained in Agriculture Sector under PMKVY as on 10/07/2021 and migrant labour trained by Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs), ICAR may be seen at Annexure-I & Annexure-II respectively.(c)	& (d)  Under the provisions of the Minimum Wages Act, 1948, both the Central and the State Governments are appropriate Governments to fix, review and revise the minimum wages of the employees employed in the scheduled employments including agriculture under their respective jurisdictions. The Central Government and the State Governments are mandated under Section 3(1)(b) of the Act to revise the Minimum Rates of Wages payable to the employees engaged in the scheduled employments including agriculture in their respective jurisdictions at intervals not exceeding five years.
