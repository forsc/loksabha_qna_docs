                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3391






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.08.2021






Lifts and Escalators in Schools for Divyang Students




Sukhbir Singh Jaunapuria














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of Government schools/buildings where facility of lifts/escalators are provided for the divyang students in the country;(b) whether the Government proposes to issue guidelines to all concerned Government institutions/ schools for having adequate arrangement of lifts or escalators for such students; and(c) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) to (c):- The Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Samagra Shiksha launched from 2018-19, subsumes the erstwhile centrally sponsored schemes of Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA), Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA) and Teacher Education (TE). The scheme covers all Children with Special Needs (CwSN) with one or more disabilities as mentioned in the Schedule of disabilities of the Rights of the Persons with Disabilities (RPwD) Act, 2016. One of the objectives of the Scheme, is removal of architectural barriers in schools so that CwSN have access to classrooms, laboratories, libraries and toilets in the school. In Government and Government aided schools (classes I to XII) across the country, as per UDISE 2019-20, there are 1408235 ramps with handrails and 350120 disabled friendly toilets for children with disabilities available. The data pertaining to lifts is not captured in the present UDISE format.While executing infrastructure components, States and UTs are required to comply with the provisions contained in the “ Harmonized guidelines and Space Standards for Barrier Free Built Environment for Persons with Disability and Elderly Persons” February, 2016” issued by the then Ministry of Urban Development, Government of India.******
