                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

12






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2020






Folk Cultures of Gujarat




Mitesh Rameshbhai (Bakabhai) Patel














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of ancient folk cultures being preserved in Gujarat including the details of the measures adopted for the same along with the names of schemes under which these measures are taken/being taken by the Government; (b)	the names of places to which these folk cultures belong; and (c)	the amount of funds sanctioned/ allocated for these preserved folk cultures during the last three years?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (I/C) FOR CULTURE AND TOURISM   (SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) to (c)	A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.   STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (c) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 12 FOR 3RD FEBRUARY, 2020(a) & (b)	For preservation, promotion & conservation of ancient folk culture throughout the country including Gujarat, the Government of India has set up seven Zonal Cultural Centres (ZCCs) with headquarters at Patiala, Nagpur, Udaipur, Prayagraj, Kolkata, Dimapur and Thanjavur. Gujarat is the member State of West Zone Cultural Centre (WZCC), Udaipur. Apart from regular cultural programmes/ activities, these ZCCs also implement the following schemes for preservation of ancient folk culture: i.	Award to Young Talented Artistesii.	Guru Shishya Parampara Schemeiii.	Theatre Rejuvenation Scheme iv.	Research & Documentation Scheme v.	Shilpgram Schemevi.	Octave vii.	National Cultural Exchange Programme 	In so far as Gujarat is concerned, the following folk art forms have been identified for preservation under Guru Shishya Parampara Scheme:Art forms	PlacesBahurupi Kala	HalolWaman Madi Bhawai	MehsanaPutli Kala	AhmedabadLacquerware Work on wood	KutchBhawai Lok Natya	PatanSarnai Vadan	PorbandarTraditional folk song, “Maniharo”	PorbandarFurther, the following folk art forms have been documented in audio/video/print format:1.	Maniharo Raas, Porbander 2.	Dang  Darbar, Dang 3.	Bhawai, Monogram 4.	Turri Barrot, Gandhinagar 5.	Lok Dairo, Bhavnagar 6.	Vasantotsav, Gandhinagar 7.	Wall paintings of Charotar, Anand 8.	Gujarat Ke Lok Nritya  (c)	Ministry of Culture provides annual grant-in-aid to all the seven ZCCs for promotion & preservation of folk culture in their member States. No State/Art-wise funds are allocated. The funds allocated by the Ministry of Culture to West Zone Cultural Centre (WZCC), Udaipur during the last three years are as under:Year	Amount (in lakh)2016-17	Rs. 895.262017-18	Rs. 784.122018-19	Rs. 1130.00
