                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5288






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.07.2019






UDAN 3




Vinayak Bhaurao Raut














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether UDAN 3 has added certain routes proposed by the Ministry of Tourism in order to provide air connectivity to tourist spots within States and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Ministry of Tourism has agreed to finance such routes and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Union Government has consulted the State Governments to identify the routes under UDAN 3 and if so, the details thereof;(d) the details of the routes added in Maharashtra to provide air connectivity to tourist spots;(e) the number of airlines which have shown interest to fly under UDAN 3 particularly in Maharashtra; and(f) whether certain State Governments have agreed to provide additional monetary incentives to the airline operators and if so, the details thereof including the  names of States?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a):In the 3rd round of bidding under Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) -UDAN Tourism, 46 routes have been awarded to Selected Airline Operators (SAOs) on the request of Ministry of Tourism. Details of these routes are at Annexure-A.;;(b): Ministry of Tourism has agreed to pay 100% of Viability Gap Funding (VGF) for these routes awarded under UDAN Tourism.;;(c): Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS) - UDAN (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik) has been finalized after extensive consultation with all the stakeholders including State Governments. Ministry of Tourism had suggested routes under UDAN Tourism and Airports Authority of India - Implementing Agency offered such routes for bidding as per provisions of the scheme document.;;(d) & (e): Details of the RCS routes awarded in Maharashtra under RCS - UDAN 3.0 including route under UDAN Tourism awarded to SAOs are attached as Annexure B.;;(f): Details of the additional incentives offered by various States are as under:;i) Uttar Pradesh: Underwriting of certain Non-RCS seats.  ;ii)Haryana: VGF on balance 50% seats in addition to the VGF being provided by the Government of India on RCS seats, on the assumption of 9 seats on 18-20 seater aircrafts with 3 departures in a week.;iii) Karnataka: VGF of 20% (Over and above the state share) in the form of reservation of seats on Kalburgi-Bengaluru and Kalburgi-Mumbai routes and in 3 flights per week only.
