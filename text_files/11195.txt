                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

6






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.02.2020






Study in India




Kirit Premjibhai Solanki














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the salient features of “Study in India” programme announced in the budget;(b) the number of students from other countries who are studying in various IndianUniversities and colleges under this programme;(c) the details of the countries of origin of students from abroad studying in India; and(d) the State-wise/UT-wise number of foreign students studying in Indian institutions?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a): To facilitate Internationalization of Higher Education in India, a programme viz.‘Study in India’ was launched with the objectives to make India an education hub forforeign students; improve the soft power of India with focus on the neighbouring countriesand use it as a tool in diplomacy; to rapidly increase the inflow of inbound InternationalStudents in India through a systematic brand-building, marketing, social media and digitalmarketing campaigns; to increase India’s market share of global education exports;improvement in overall quality of higher education; to reduce the export-import imbalancein the number of international students; growth in India’s global market share ofInternational students; and increase in global ranking of India etc. The salient features ofthe scheme include:<pre>a. Identification of target 30+ countries for branding in initial phaseb. Selection of top 100 partner institutions on the basis of grading given byNational Assessment and Accreditation Council(NAAC)(3.26+) and rankingunder National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF)c. Creation of a dedicated web portal www.studyindia.gov.ind. Setting up of call centre supporte. Systematic brandingf. Undertaking of social media and offline advertisements campaignsg. Fee waivers upto 100% being offered by partner institutionsh. Adequate scholarships to meritorious students through IND-SAT Examinationi. Conduct of event management in the target countriesj. Conduct of Webinarsk. Setting up of Hostels of International Standardsl. Study in India Mobile Applicationm. Conduct of event management in the target countriesn. Conduct of National/ regional workshops to sensitize the partner institutes oninternationalization requirement</pre>(b) The number of students studying in various Indian Universities and colleges is 768 and3164 respectively for Admission Cycles 2019 and 2020.(c) As per All India Survey on Higher Education 2018-19, the country-wise details ofInternational students studying in India is at Annexure-I.(d) As per All India Survey on Higher Education 2018-19, the State-wise/UT-wisenumber of foreign students studying in Indian institutions is at Annexure-II.*****
