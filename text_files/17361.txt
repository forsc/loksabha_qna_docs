                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

795






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.09.2020






Ban on Chinese Aircrafts




Margani Bharat














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether as many as 20% of commercial aircrafts leased to Indian airlines is owned by Chinese entities;(b) whether Air India, Air India Express also have aircrafts on lease which are owned by Chinese entities, if so, the details thereof and the manner in which the Government plans to bar such Chinese companies in various sectors; and(c) whether the Government intends to terminate the above leased aircraft and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) to (c):  The leased aircraft in the current fleet of Indian airlines have been taken on lease from companies registered abroad. The leasing companies generally have complex ownership and global asset management and financing patterns. As per the records maintained by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation, the vast majority of the owners/ lessors of the aircraft leased to Indian airlines have their registered address in Ireland. A total of 07 narrow-bodied aircraft out of a fleet of 167 aircraft of the Air India group of companies are leased from Special Purpose Vehicles registered in Ireland which have been set up by Chinese companies. Presently, there is no proposal under consideration of the Ministry of Civil Aviation to ban any aircraft leasing entity.
