                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3674






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2019






Accessible India Campaign for Public Transport Sector




Narayana Swamy Abbaiah














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the progress of “accessible India” campaign especially in Public Transport Sector, State-wise;(b) whether State Transport Corporations are implementing it and if so, the details and the status thereof, State-wise; (c) the steps taken in this regard by Karnataka;(d) the details regarding employment of Other Backward Classes (OBCs) in Central PSUs, PSU-wise especially in Karnataka;(e) whether the status of employment of differently abled in private sector is available and if so, the details thereof;(f) whether any concrete measures are being taken by the Government; and(g) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI KRISHANPAL GURJAR)(a) 	The Government has launched Accessible India Campaign to create universal accessibility in built-up environment, transport system and Information and Communication Technology (ICT) ecosystem.  Government has released an amount of Rs.354.45 crores to make 1058 identified buildings of States/UTs accessible. State-wise detail is at Annexure-I. As regards central government buildings, CPWD has made 211 buildings accessible under Accessible India Campaign.State-wise detail of 336 identified websites made accessible is at Annexure-II. Out of 100 identified central government websites, 95 websites have been made accessible.Details of railways stations provided with the facilities for divyangjan are not maintained state-wise. Indian Railways is committed to make its stations accessible for divyangjan as part of Accessible India Campaign. Improvement/augmentation of amenities at Railway stations, including those for divyangjan is a continuous process. Provisions of facilities for divyangjan are prioritized depending upon the category of the station based on passenger handled and earnings of the station.  35 International Airports and 55 Domestic Airports have been provided accessible features. State-wise detail is at Annexure-III. Out of 1,45,287 buses, 35,720 (24.59%) have been provided with accessibility features. State-wise detail is at Annexure-I. (b) & (c): As per Section 57 of the Constitution, transport by road is a State subject.  State Road Transport Undertakings (SRTUs) work under administrative control of respective State Governments. State-wise details including Karnataka under Accessible India Campaign is at Annexure-IV.(d) to (g): There is no data available on employment of Other Backward Classes (OBCs) in Central PSUs and differently abled in private sector. Government is implementing the National Career Service (NCS) project which comprises a digital portal that provides a nation-wide online platform for the job seekers and employers for job-matching in a dynamic, efficient and responsive manner and has a repository of career content to job seekers.  Further, to encourage private sector to employ Persons with Disabilities (PwDs), under scheme of incentives to the employers in private sector for providing employment to Persons with Disabilities, payment of employers’ contribution to the EPF and ESI by the Government is up to 10 years, without any salary ceiling for the Persons with Disabilities employees. The government also bears one-third of the gratuity amount due and admissible to Persons with Disabilities employees.*****
