Converting Barren Land to Arable Land




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam













                                                    Ranjeeta Koli
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                                           LOK  SABHA                 UNSTARRED QUESTION No. 4494                 TO BE ANSWERED ON 23.03.2021              Converting Barren Land to Arable Land4494. SHRIMATI POONAMBEN MAADAM:       SHRIMATI RANJEETA KOLI:Will the Minister of RURAL DEVELOPMENT be pleased to state:(a) the area of barren land in hectares in the geographical areas of Rajasthan and Gujarat at present and its percentage and portion out of the total land in the said States; (b) the names of schemes implemented by the Union Government to convert barren land into agrarian land in Rajasthan and Gujarat during the last three years and the funds allocated /released/utilised for this purpose, year-wise; and (c) the area of barren land in hectares which has been converted into agrarian/arable land in Rajasthan and Gujarat along with the allocated/released funds under this scheme?






ANSWER




                                                                                                     ANSWER                      MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT                                        (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a)to(c):According to Directorate of Economics and Statistics, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, out of 342.24 lakh hectare geographical area of Rajasthan, 23.91 lakh hectare (6.98%) is barren and unculturable land. Similarly out of 196.02 lakh hectare geographical area of Gujarat, 25.52 lakh hectare (13.02%) is barren and unculturable land.As per the Seventh Schedule of Constitution of India, ‘land’ falls under the purview of State Government and, therefore, it is for the State Governments to take suitable steps for transforming barren land into arable land. However, Government of India supplements the efforts of State Governments through various programmes. The Department of Land Resources has been implementing Watershed Development Component of Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (WDC-PMKSY) erstwhile Integrated Watershed Management Programme (IWMP) for development of rainfed/ degraded areas.
