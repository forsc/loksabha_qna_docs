                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2996






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.08.2021






Measures to check Population Growth




A. Ganeshamurthi













                                                    Anumula Revanth Reddy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the total population of the country, State/UT-wise bifurcation thereof and percentage of growth of population, year-wise and State/UT-wise during last five years? (b) the details of steps taken by the Government to control population growth in the last five years along with the funds allocated, released and utilized for this purpose;(c) whether it is a fact that despite several steps taken in this regard, the Government has failed to effectively control population growth;(d) if so, the reaction of the Government thereto;(e) whether it is a fact that some States have achieved a growth in population of below replacement level;(f)  if so, whether the Government proposes to reward these States with any financial incentives; and(g) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND                            FAMILY WELFARE(DR. BHARATI PRAVIN PAWAR)(a)	: As per the Report of the Technical Group on Population Projections (TGPP) July 2020, chaired by Registrar General of India (RGI), the projected population of the country, State/UT-wise and percentage growth of population, State/UT-wise during last five years, are placed in Annexure I.(b) : Details of steps taken by the Government to control population growth in last five years are given below:1.	Mission Parivar Vikas has been introduced for substantially increasing access to contraceptives and family planning services in 146 high fertility districts in 7 high focus states. 2.	Expanded Contraceptive Choices: The current contraceptive basket has been expanded with inclusion of new contraceptives namely Injectable contraceptive (Antara programme) and Centchroman (Chhaya). 3.	Post-partum Intrauterine contraceptive device (PPIUCD) incentive scheme under which PPIUCD services are provided post delivery.4.	Redesigned Contraceptive Packaging: The packaging for Condoms, OCPs and ECPs has now been improved and redesigned so as to augment the demand for these commodities.5.	Family Planning Media Campaign: A holistic media campaign is in place to generate contraceptive demand.6.	The World Population Day & fortnight (July 11 – July 24) is observed each year to boost Family Planning efforts all over the country. 7.	Vasectomy fortnight is observed throughout the country in November each year to lay emphasis on male participation.8.	Scheme for Home Delivery of contraceptives by ASHAs at doorstep of beneficiaries has been taken up.9.	Family Planning Logistics Management Information System (FP-LMIS) is being implemented to ensure last mile availability of FP commodities.Details of funds allocated and utilized for Family Planning in last five years is placed in Annexure II.(c) & (d) : The Government has been implementing National Family Planning Programme which has been instrumental in controlling the population:  •	The TFR of the country has declined from 2.9 in 2005 to 2.2 in 2018 (SRS)•	28 out of 36 States/UTs have already achieved the replacement level fertility of 2.1 or less•	The Crude Birth Rate (CBR) has declined from 23.8 in 2005 to 20.0 in 2018 (SRS)•	The Decadal growth rate has declined from 21.54% in 1990-2000 to 17.64% during 2001-11 •	The Wanted Fertility Rate has declined from 1.9 in NFHS III to 1.8 in NFHS IV(e)  :Yes. (f) & (g): Family Planning is a target free programme, and the Government provides services based on voluntary and informed choice.
