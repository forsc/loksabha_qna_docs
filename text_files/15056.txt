                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3813






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2020






Mini Labs under KVKs




Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total number of mini labs established by the Government in Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) including in Maharashtra and the details thereof; and (b)	the number of farmers benefited under this scheme and the steps taken by the Government to develop more mini labs for soil testing?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????         (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a)	For testing the soil samples of farmers involved in frontline extension activities by Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) at district level, the Government has set up 1148 Mini Soil Testing Lab in 673 KVKs. The State-wise details of mini soil testing labs established in different Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) including in Maharashtra are at Annexure-I.(b)	As many as 33.47 lakh farmers have been benefitted through 9.54 lakh advisories provided by KVKs on Soil Health Management. In addition, 17.62 lakh Soil Health Cards have also been distributed to farmers after testing the soil samples by Mini Soil Testing Labs. The Government has also made provision for setting up of village level soil testing labs by village entrepreneurs under Soil Health Scheme by providing financial assistance up to 75% of the total cost of Rs. 5.0 lakh per project.  The cost is shared on 60:40 basis by Government of India and States (90:10 basis for North East and Hilly States).******
