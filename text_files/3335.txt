                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3034






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.07.2019






Infrastructure Development in Amaravati




Jayadev Galla














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether it has come to the notice of the Government that Capital Region Development Authority with the help of McKinsie and CRISIL which is looking after constructing new capital Amaravati for Andhra Pradesh has estimated that Rs.51,000 crores would be required for construction of infrastructure projects in the new capital;(b)	if so, the manner in which the Union Government is extending the helping hand to CRDA and State Government in developing infrastructure in the region; and (c)	the details of works done by the Union Government so far in compliance with the Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                   (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a): No, Sir.  However, Government of Andhra Pradesh has informed that the estimated cost of construction for holistic development of new capital city of Amaravati is Rs. 1.09 lakh crore and that for development of Phase - I, the estimated cost is Rs. 51,687 crore. (b) & (c): Under the Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014, the Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance has released an amount of Rs.1,500 crore as support for creation of new capital city. In addition, Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs has released an amount of Rs.1,000 croreas ‘One Time Special Financial Assistance’ for creation of essential urban infrastructure for the new capital region of the State of Andhra Pradesh.                            *****
