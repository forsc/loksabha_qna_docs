                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

767






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.11.2019






DGCA Advisory




Vellalath Kochukrishnan Nair. Sreekandan














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) has issued an advisory requiring the maintenance personnel not to work for more than 8-10 hours and in case of exigencies can be extended to a maximum to 12 hours, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the DGCA has made it clear that there should be a minimum rest period of 11 hours between two shifts and the personnel should be given a break after a gap of every four hours and they should not work for more than 48 hours in seven days or 60 hours including overtime; and(c) if so, whether the above rule was promulgated because of deaths of maintenance personnel at various places while in duty and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) & (b) Yes Sir, an Airworthiness Advisory Circular (AAC No 05 of 2019) dated 23 August, 2019 on Duty Time Limitation for Aircraft Maintenance Personnel has been issued by the DGCA which provides for the working periods and rest requirements for aircraft maintenance personnel.;(c) No, Sir.  The advisory on Duty Time Limitation for Aircraft Maintenance Personnel existed since February 2012. The 2012 advisory has been amended for effectiveness and to ensure that fatigued personal do not undertake work which may lead to lapses.
