                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3404






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.03.2022






Upgradation of IIHT




Suresh Pujari














                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has any plan in the coming financial year to upgrade different Indian Institute of Handloom Technology (IIHT) having 3 year diploma course to 4 year degree courses; (b) if so, whether the Government will consider upgradation of IIHT, Bargarh of Odisha to the next level from 3 year diploma course to 4 year degree course being the 5th such Institute in India; and (c) whether the Government will consider different specialisation courses in the Institute and simultaneously consider training of handloom artisans, and global marketing of Sambalpuri Sarees?






ANSWER




THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR TEXTILES  (SMT. DARSHANA JARDOSH)(a) & (b):	4 year degree course “B.Tech in Handloom & Textile Technology” has been introduced at IIHT, Salem and Varanasi from the academic years 2015-16 & 2021-22 respectively, in addition to the 3 year Diploma course in Handloom and Textile Technology. The Government of India has taken a policy decision to introduce said degree course in the IIHTs under its control including at Bargarh as per feasibility of introducing the degree course. (c):	At present, all the IIHTs are conducting 3 year diploma (DHTT) course. IIHTs at Salem and Varanasi also conduct 1 ½ year Post Diploma course in Textile Processing. Skill up-gradation of handloom weavers in technical areas viz. weaving, dyeing, designing etc. was earlier conducted under National Handloom Development Programme and Comprehensive Handloom Cluster Development Scheme and since 2020-21, under Scheme for Capacity Building in Textile Sector (SAMARTH). For marketing of Handloom at the international level, Handloom Export Promotion Council (HEPC) has been organizing International Fairs. ***
