                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TEXTILES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1918






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.07.2021






Skill Development in Textiles Sector




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam













                                                    Ranjeeta Koli
                                                








                                                    Omprakash Bhupalsinh alias Pawan Rajenimbalkar
                                                








                                                     Bhavana Gawali (Patil)
                                                








                                                    Sanjay Haribhau Jadhav
                                                








                                                    M.K. Vishnu Prasad
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





TEXTILES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of skill development programmes launched for capacity building of workers in textiles industry sector along with the textiles training centres operating in the country, State-wise; (b) the number of beneficiaries under the Integrated Skill Development Scheme (ISDS) and SAMARTH during the last three years and the current year, State-wise including Gujarat, Rajasthan and Maharashtra; (c) the amount of funds allocated and utilized for the said programme, State-wise; (d) whether the Government has ensured participation of organizations of various States in collaboration with the Government agencies for training at entry level and creation of employment opportunities in both the segments-traditional as well as organized sectors of Textile Value Chain; and (e) if so, the details in this regard particularly in relation to Maharashtra?






ANSWER




THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF TEXTILES  (SMT. DARSHANA JARDOSH)(a) & (b):	With a view to create a robust human resources for the textiles sector, particularly the need for trained and skilled workforce in all segment of the textiles sector, Ministry of Textiles has implemented various skill development schemes and programmes since the financial year 2010-11. Under the comprehensive Integrated Skill Development Scheme(ISDS), a total of 11.14 lakh persons have been trained during FY 2010-11 to 2017-18, in various diverse segments of textiles covering textiles and apparel, jute, spinning, weaving, technical textiles, sericulture, handloom and handicrafts of which 8.43 lakh persons have been employed. In further continuation, Ministry of Textiles has extended the skill development programme in the form of Samarth- Scheme for Capacity Building in Textile Sector for a period upto 2023-24 with the objective to provide demand driven, placement oriented National Skills Qualifications Framework (NSQF) compliant skilling programmes to incentivize and supplement the efforts of the industry in creating jobs in the organized textile and related sectors, covering the entire value chain of textiles, excluding Spinning and Weaving in organized sector. The COVID-19 pandemic has impacted the implementation of the scheme adversely. Training programme for about 3.3 lakh beneficiaries allocated to various implementing partners after due process of empanelling and physical verification of training centres is progressing at various stages. State-wise details of progress of training programme under Samarth scheme is given at Annexure-I. State-wise list of training centres under Samarth is given at Annexure-II.(c):	There is no state-wise allocation of funds under Samarth and the cost of training programme is released directly to implementing partners. The details of funds allocated and released under Samarth scheme during the last three financial years is as follows:(Rs. in crore)Financial Year	Budget Allocated	Expenditure Incurred2018-19	200.00	16.992019-20	100.50	72.062020-21	150.00	90.70(d) & (e):	Ministry, during 2018-19, have invited proposals from all the State Governments for partnering under the scheme. A total of 18 State Government agencies have entered into Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Ministry for participation in the training programme under Samarth. No State government agency from Maharashtra has signed MoU for undertaking training programme under Samarth.In addition, the Government has partnered with 92 textile industries, 10 industry associations and 4 sectoral organizations of Ministry of Textiles for training programme under Samarth.
