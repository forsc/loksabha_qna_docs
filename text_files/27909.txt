                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3524






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.08.2021






Inland Fisheries and Aquaculture




Ramesh Bidhuri














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the details of the steps taken for the development of Inland Fisheries and Aquaculture in the country; (b)	the details regarding allocation of funds in this regard, State-wise; (c) 	whether there is under utilization of funds; and (d) 	if so, the reasons therefor and the details of action taken in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERMINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a): The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing a flagship scheme namely Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY) - to bring about Blue Revolution through sustainable and responsible development of fisheries sector in India with highest ever investment of Rs. 20,050 crore for a period of 5 years from financial year 2020-21 to 2024-25 in all States/Union Territories (UTs). Development of inland fisheries and aquaculture is one of the thrust areas of the PMMSY through which financial support is provided for (i) establishment of brood banks, hatcheries, rearing and grow out ponds in fresh water, brackish water and saline areas, (ii) productive utilization of wetland and waterlogged areas, (iii)  cage culture in reservoirs and other open water-bodies, (iv) cold water fisheries development - trout raceways and hatcheries, (v) ornamental fisheries, (vi) technology infusion like Re-circulatory Aquaculture System (RAS), Pen culture, Biofloc culture, (vii) supply of boats and nets to promote inland capture fisheries, (viii) post harvest infrastructure like ice plants, cold storages, (ix) fish transport facilities like refrigerated and insulted vehicles, three wheelers and two wheelers,  (x) feed mills/plants, (xi) fish marketing  infrastructure and fish value added enterprises, (xii) disease diagnostic and quality testing labs, (xiii) organic aquaculture promotion and certification, (xiv) livelihood support for fishers during ban/lean period, (xv) insurance coverage for fishermen, (xvi) training to fishers, fish farmers and other stakeholders etc.  In addition, a dedicated fund namely, ‘Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund’ (FIDF) with a total fund size of Rs.7,522.48 crore has been set up for providing concessional finance for creation and strengthening of fisheries infrastructure facilities. FIDF inter-alia provides concessional financing for development of inland fisheries aquaculture. (b) to (d): The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying, during the financial year 2020-21, has accorded approvals to the proposals of 34 States/UTs and other organizations at a total cost of Rs. 2881.41 crore with central share of Rs. 1089.80 crore and released a sum of Rs.699.72 crore to the States/UTs and other organisation for development of fisheries under the PMMSY. The state-wise details of approved cost, central share/allocation and fund released during 2020-21 under PMMSY are furnished at Annexure-I. It has been observed that COVID-19 situation impacted the normal pace of implementation of PMMSY and utilization of sanctioned funds by State/UTs. After easing of the lockdown situation and restoring of logistics, the States/UTs are now geared up for implementation of the PMMSY and utilisation of the central funds released for the purpose. *****
