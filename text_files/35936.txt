                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2656






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Setting up of Cargo Terminals




Geetha Viswanath Vanga













                                                    Y. S. Avinash Reddy
                                                








                                                    Kotha Prabhakar  Reddy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to set up one hundred cargo Terminals for multimodal logistic facilities in the country including Andhra Pradesh and Telangana under PM Gati Shakti (NMP) during the next three years;(b) if so, the details thereof along with the steps being taken by the Government in this regard;(c) the funds sanctioned/spent for the purpose? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) and (b) Yes sir, Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH) as a part of the Bharatmala Pariyojana is undertaking development of Multi-Modal logistics Parks (MMLPs) at 35 strategic locations as approved by Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA). These 35 MMLPs are expected to cater for 50% of the road freight movement. These MMLPs are expected to enable seamless intermodal freight transfer and serve as major cargo consolidation and distribution hub. Moreover, Ministry of Railways has launched a new “Gati Shakti Multi-Modal Cargo Terminal (GCT)’ policy on 15.12.2021 and the target is to commission 100 Gati Shakti Cargo Terminals (GCTs) within the next three financial years, i.e. 2022-23, 2023-24 and 2024-25. The location of GCTs is being decided on the basis of demand from industry and potential of Cargo Traffic. So far, approximately 71 locations (annexed) including 04 in Andhra Pradesh and 05 in Telangana have been provisionally identified by Ministry of Railways for development of GCTs.Further, 02 MMLPs of MoRTH i.e. Anantapur & Visakhapatnam are proposed in Andhra Pradesh and 01 MMLP i.e. Hyderabad is proposed in Telangana. An MoU has been signed between Telangana State Industrial Infrastructure Corporation (TSIIC) and NHLML for MMLP, Hyderabad. MoU has also been signed between Andhra Pradesh Industrial Infrastructure Corporation (APIIC), RVNL and NHLML for MMLP Anantapur and Visakhapatnam.(c) Initial grant of Rs. 100 Crores to start with MMLPs of MoRTH has been provided by MoRTH to its SPV i.e. NHLML.
