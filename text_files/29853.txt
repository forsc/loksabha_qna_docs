                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1509






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

07.12.2021






Improving Quality Badminton Facilities




Rajiv Pratap Rudy














                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	whether Government has taken steps to improve access to quality badminton facilities in the country and if so, details thereof; b)	whether the Government has taken steps to increase availability of badminton coaches in the country, particularly at schools and in private coaching facilities for teenagers and if so, the details thereof;c)	the details and the number of Badminton players of the country ranked in the top 100 players of the world for men and women each;d)	whether there are any sponsorship schemes for lesser-privileged players with potential to get access to quality training of badminton and if so, the details thereof ?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER FOR YOUTH AFFAIRS & SPORTS (SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR)(a)	 Yes Sir, six National Centres of Excellence (NCOEs) have been approved under which three academies have been declared Target Olympic Podium Scheme-National Centres of Excellence (TOPS-NCOE) for all junior and emerging players. Further, 24 Badminton academies have been accredited under Khelo India Talent Search and development vertical, the details of which are given at Annexure-I(b)	 Sports is a ‘State’ Subject and development of Sports, including appointment of coaches and increasing their availability rests with the respective State Government and National Sports Federations (NSFs). As far as Sports Authority of India (SAI), an autonomous body under the aegis of Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, is concerned, there are 36 Badminton coaches working under it. These Coaches are deployed on priority basis in the schemes of SAI like National Centre of Coaching Excellence, Sports Training Centre, etc. Three Badminton Coaches are posted in private academies of Prakash Padukone Badminton Academy, Bengaluru and Gopichand Badminton Academy, Hyderabad. (c)	 The details and number of Badminton players of the country ranked in the top 100 players of the world for men and women each as per the latest ranking of Badminton World Federation are provided at Annexure-II.(d)	 Sports Promotion Schemes implemented through SAI Training Centres (STCs) and Extension Centres of STCs cover Badminton sport, the details of which are at Annexure-III. Further, under the Target Olympic Podium Scheme (TOPS) of the government, a total of 15 players in the Core Group and 27 players in Development Group are provided support, the details of which are given at Annexure-IV. Under the Khelo India Talent Search and development vertical, 142 athletes have been identified and are supported for training in Badminton sport. These schemes provide opportunity for players across the country, including lesser-privileged sportspersons with potential to get access to quality training of badminton.****
