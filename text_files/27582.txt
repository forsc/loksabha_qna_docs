                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

294






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.08.2021






Overall Development of Students




Sumedhanand Saraswati














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to provide premises/platforms for the overalldevelopment of Class I to Class XII students;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) the facilities likely to be provided to the students through the said premises/platforms;and(d) the other steps taken or proposed to be taken by the Government for the overalldevelopment of the students in the country?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) to (d) A statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (d) OF LOK SABHA STARREDQUESTION NO. 294 TO BE ANSWERED ON 9TH AUGUST 2021 ASKED BY HON’BLE MPSHRI SUMEDHANAND SARASWATI REGARDING OVERALL DEVELOPMENT OFSTUDENTS(a) to (d): National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 envisions holistic development of alllearners. NEP 2020 envisages key curriculum and pedagogy reform across all stages tomove the education system towards real understanding and towards learning how to learn -and away from the culture of rote learning. The aim of education is not only cognitivedevelopment, but also building character and creating holistic and well-rounded individualsequipped with the key 21st century skills. NEP 2020 covers the entire spectrum of schooleducation from pre-primary to senior secondary, and envisions universal access at all levelsof schooling from pre-primary to Class-XII and a new curricular and pedagogical structure(5+3+3+4). Accordingly, it has been decided to frame the new National CurriculumFramework (NCF) by incorporating and integrating local and indigenous flavour from acrossthe country through wider consultations, reflected under the State Curriculum Frameworks.Samagra Shiksha scheme is an integrated centrally sponsored scheme for schooleducation which has been launched in 2018 and further extended for next five years in 2021covers the entire gamut from pre-school to class XII. The scheme treats school education asa continuum and is in accordance with Sustainable Development Goal for Education (SDG-4) and NEP 2020 recommendations. The scheme provides support for ensuring that allchildren have access to quality education with an equitable and inclusive classroomenvironment which takes care of their diverse background, multilingual needs, differentacademic abilities and make them active participants in the learning process. The majorinterventions, across all levels of school education, under the Samagra Shiksha schemeare: (i) Universal Access including Infrastructure Development and Retention; (ii)Foundational Literacy and Numeracy, (iii) Gender and Equity; (iv) Inclusive Education; (v)Quality and Innovation; (vi) Financial support for Teacher Salary; (vii) Digital initiatives; (viii)RTE Entitlements including uniforms, textbooks etc.; (ix) Support for ECCE; (x) VocationalEducation; (xi) Sports and Physical Education; and (xii) Strengthening of Teacher Educationand Training.Further, the NIPUN Bharat Mission for foundational literacy and numeracy has beenlaunched under the aegis of Samagra Shiksha, which aims at both cognitive and holisticdevelopment of children of age 3-9 years.PM e-VIDYA, as a part of the Atma Nirbhar Bharat Programme, a comprehensiveinitiative for using Technology with Equity has been initiated which unifies all efforts relatedto digital/online/on-air education to enable multi-mode access to education. PM e-Vidya hasfollowing components:? DIKSHA – One nation, one digital education platform? One class, one TV channel – Swayam Prabha? E-content for Open School? Extensive use of Radio, Community radio and podcast? E-content for visually and hearing-impaired studentsThe above components have been developed in such a manner that thee-content available on one digital mode is also available on other digital modes, in acoherent manner.****
