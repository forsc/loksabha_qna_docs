                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2232






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.08.2021






Cause of Rising Unemployment




Arvind Ganpat Sawant














                        Will the Minister of





LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the causes of rising unemployment in India since Covid-19 Pandemic from March 2020; (b)	the number of recruitments done by the Government on a permanent basis during the last two years;     (c)	the company-wise number of job opportunities currently being provided by private companies in the country, along with the State and UT-wise details thereof including Maharashtra; (d)	whether there is any provision for private companies to provide jobs to the local people in the company and if so, the details thereof; (e)	the number of local people  who have got employment at present along with the company-wise and State-wise details thereof; and (f)	whether the Government has conducted any survey with respect to the number of people who have been dropped out of the workforce of the country between March 2020 and June 2021?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR LABOUR AND EMPLOYMENT(SHRI RAMESWAR TELI)(a): COVID-19 pandemic lockdowns have affected economies across the globe including India. Government has taken several initiatives to address the challenges and threats posed by the pandemic. The Government is providing fiscal stimulus of more than Rs. Twenty seven lakh crore as part of the Aatmanirbhar financial Package. Aatmanirbhar Bharat package comprises of various long term schemes/programmes/ policies for making the country self-reliant and to create employment opportunities.(b): The number of  recruitments carried out by three major recruiting agencies, i.e. Staff Selection Commission (SSC), Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) and Railway Recruitment Boards (RRBs), for group ‘A’, ‘B’ & ‘C’ in the Central government during last two years is given below:Group	Year	2019-20	2020-21Group ‘A’	No. of candidates recommended for appointment	5,230	3,609 Group ‘B’ & ‘C’	No. of candidates recommended for appointment	14,691	68,891(c) & (d): Jobs are provided by employers including private companies based on their requirement. Company-wise data is not maintained centrally.(e) & (f): As per the Annual Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS), conducted by National Statistical Office (NSO), Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation during 2019-20 (survey period July 2019 to June 2020),   the State and UT-wise details of estimated Worker Population Ratio (WPR) for persons of age 15 years and above on usual status basis including in Maharashtra is placed at Annexure. *****
