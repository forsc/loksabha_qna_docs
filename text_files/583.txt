                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

432






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.06.2019






Steps to Promote Animal Husbandry, Dairy and Fishery Sectors




Shivkumar Chanabasappa Udasi














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes to give more importance to Animal Husbandry, Dairy and Fishery sectors and if so, the details thereof; and (b) whether it has become necessary to pay more attention towards milch cattle due to irregular rain and shortage of water in various parts of the country and if so, the steps proposed to be taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(DR. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN) (a)  Yes, Sir. The Government is implementing various schemes to supplement the efforts of States/UTs for development of animal husbandry and fisheries sectors namely, I.	Rashtriya Gokul Mission II.	National Programme for Dairy Development III.	National Dairy Plan-IIV.	Dairy Entrepreneurship Development SchemeV.	Dairy Processing and Infrastructure Development FundVI.	Supporting Dairy Cooperatives and Farmer Producer Organizations engaged in dairy activitiesVII.	National Livestock MissionVIII.	Livestock Health & Disease ControlIX.	Blue Revolution: Integrated Development and Management of FisheriesX.	Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund In addition, recently, the Cabinet in its 1st meeting held on 31.05.2019 has approved an ambitious scheme “National Animal Disease Control Programme (NADCP) for control of Foot & Mouth Disease and Brucellosis” by vaccinating 100% cattle, buffalo, sheep, goat and pig population for FMD and 100% bovine female calves of 4-8 months of age for brucellosis. The programme has a total outlay of Rs.13,343.00 crore for five years (2019-20 to 2023-24).(b) Yes, Sir. The Government issues advisory to the States/UTs to mitigate drought or drought like situation due to deficit rainfall. The Government has also developed Disaster management plan in consultation with National Disaster Management Authority.******
