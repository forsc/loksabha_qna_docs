                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3843






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.12.2021






Incidences of Fraticide in CAPFs




Srinivas Kesineni














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government can provide data on incidences of fratricide in the Central Armed Police Forces (CAPFs), if so, the details on fratricide in CAPFs thereof during the last three years, State-wise;(b) whether the Government recognizes the increasing instances of suicides and fratricides in the CAPFs;(c) if so, the measures taken/being undertaken to promote the mental health and well-being of the jawans in the CAPFs and the details thereof;(d) whether stress management courses have been introduced in the CAPFs to tackle the problems of suicide and fratricide and if so, details thereof; and(e) whether the Government has been successful in administering the policy of “100 days with family” for the jawans in the CAPFs, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) to (d):	The state-wise details of fratricide incidents in CAPFs during last three years are given in Annexure-1.Improvement in working conditions of CAPFs and AR personnel is a constant endeavor. The measures undertaken to promote mental health and well-being of the Jawans in CAPFs and AR are given in Annexure-2. (e):	The Government has decided to take appropriate steps to facilitate 100 days per year stay of the CAPF personnel with their families. The modalities in this regard are being worked out.*****
