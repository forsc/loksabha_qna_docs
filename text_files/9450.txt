                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2940






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.12.2019






Sail Barrages on Rivers




Balubhau (Alias Suresh Narayan) Dhanorkar














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government proposes any steps to build sail barrages on the various rivers in Chandrapur district of Maharashtra for fulfilling the requirement of irrigation and drinking water; and (b) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) to(b)  The Water Resources Projects are planned, funded, executed and maintained by the State Governments themselves as per their own resources and priority. In order to supplement their efforts, Government of India provides technical and financial assistance to State Governments to encourage sustainable development and efficient management of water resources through various schemes and programmes.	Central Water Commission has informed that proposal to build such barrages on various rivers in Chandrapur District of Maharshtra has not been received from the State Government in the last five years. Further, the Government of Maharashtra has informed that five conventional barrages are either completed/under construction or contemplated in Chandrapur district.  Four of these 5 barrages have the provision to supply irrigation and drinking water.  Details of these four barrages are as under:Sr No	Name of Barrage	Water use in Mm3		Drinking	Irrigation	Status1	Dindora BarrageTa Warora	12.324	57.07(11933 ha)	Ongoing2	Dhanora BarrageTa Chandrapur	25(for Chandrapur city)	24.47(irrigation 5387 ha)	Planned3	Amdi BarrageTa Ballarpur	21.59(for Ballarpur and Rajura city)	21.59(irrigation 2890 ha)	Planned4	ArviDhanur BarrageTa. Gondapipri	3.90	31.73(irrigation 4710 ha)	Planned*****
