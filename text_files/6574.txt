                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

258






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

19.11.2019






Foodgrains Released to Maharashtra




Poonam (Mahajan) Vajendla Rao














                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the quantum of rice and wheat released and distributed to the State of Maharashtra through Public Distribution System under BPL/APL/AAY categories during each of the last three years; and (b) 	whether in view of recent floods in Maharashtra, extra quantum of rice released and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR MINISTRY OF CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI   DANVE  RAOSAHEB   DADARAO)(a):		Govt. of India makes allocation of foodgrains to the States/UTs under Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS) in terms of National Food Security Act, 2013.  The Act provides for coverage of upto 75% of the rural population and upto 50% of the urban population for receiving highly subsidized foodgrains under Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS), thus covering about two-thirds of the population or atleast 81.35 crore persons, as per Census 2011 population. Corresponding to the above coverage at All India level, State-wise percentage coverage has been determined by the then Planning Commission (now Niti Aayog). Under the Act eligible households, which comprise of Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) households and priority households, are entitled to receive foodgrains @ Rs 1/2/3 per kg for nutri-cereals/wheat/rice respectively. While priority households are entitled to receive 5 kg per person per month, AAY households, which constitute the poorest of the poor, continue to receive 35 Kg of foodgrains per household per month.  Responsibility for distribution of Foodgrains within the State/UT rests with the concerned State/UT Govt........2/--2-	Government of Maharashtra implemented National Food Security Act, 2013, with effect from February, 2014. The details of allocation and offtake of foodgrain in respect of Maharashtra during last three years is as under:(figures in MT)Commodity	Year	Allocation	OfftakeRice	2016-17	2021100	1850765	2017-18	2021100	1864673	2018-19	2021100	1839416	2019-20*	1178975	981687Wheat	2016-17	2584092	2387280	2017-18	2584092	2385980	2018-19	2584092	2208970	2019-20*	1507387	1268366 *-upto the month of October, 2019(b): 	In view of recent Floods in Maharashtra, this Department has made an additional allocation of 5387 tons of foodgrains (2693.50 tons of wheat and 2693.50 tons of rice) per month for a period of 3 months i.e. from August, 2019 to October, 2019 at MSP rate for wheat and MSP Derived rate for rice for flood relief during 2019-20.****
