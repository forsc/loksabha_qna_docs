                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2900






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

21.03.2022






Poaching Incidents




Bhartruhari Mahtab














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether number of poaching incidents in protected forest areas have increased during the last three years and if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise along with necessary measures taken by the Government in this regard;(b)	the number of tigers in the country, State/UT- wise including Odisha;(c)	the details of programmes being implemented by the Government for the conservation of tigers in the country; and;(d)	the number of cases registered in connection with hunting of tigers and smuggling of tiger skin during the last three years along with the action taken by the Government thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) The management of forest and wildlife, including control of poaching and smuggling of wild animals and their parts, is primarily the responsibility of State Governments/UT Administrations. As reported by the States, the details of tiger mortality during the last three years is at Annexure-I.The important steps taken by the Government for protection of wildlife include:(i)	The Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 provides for stringent punishment for violation of its provisions. The Act also provides for forfeiture of any equipment, vehicle or weapon that is used for committing wildlife offence(s). Rare and endangered species found in India, like Tiger, Snow Leopard, Great Indian Bustard, Gangetic Dolphin, Dugong, etc. have been listed in Schedule-I of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 thereby providing them highest degree of protection.(ii)	Financial assistance is provided to the State/Union Territory Governments under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of ‘Integrated Development of Wildlife Habitats’, for providing better protection to wildlife and improvement of habitat.(iii)	The local communities are involved in conservation measures through eco-development activities which help the forest departments in protection of wildlife. (iv)	The Wildlife Crime Control Bureau (WCCB) coordinates with State/UTs and other enforcement agencies to gather intelligence about poaching and unlawful trade in wild animals and animal articles. (v)	Alerts and advisories were issued by WCCB on poaching and illegal trade of wildlife to the concerned State and Central agencies for preventive action. (vi)	The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change has released an Advisory on management of Human Wildlife Conflict in February 2021 which also contains measures for improvement of wildlife habitats(b)	The number of tigers in the country, State/UT- wise including Odisha is at  Annexure-II.(c)	Financial assistance is provided under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of ‘Project Tiger’ to tiger range States for conservation of tigers. The activities undertaken under the scheme include raising awareness, habitat management, protection, eco-development, human resource and infrastructure development, and voluntary village relocation.(d)	Control of hunting and smuggling of wild animals and their parts is the responsibility of concerned State Governments/UT Administrations. As per records available with the Wildlife Crime Control Bureau based on the information received from the States/Union Territories Forest and Police Authorities, the number of Tiger cases during the last three years are as under:S.No	Year	Number of Tiger cases1	2019	252	2020	133	2021	26 ***
