                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1658






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

29.07.2021






Construction of New Airports




Rita Bahuguna Joshi














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of CIVIL AVIATIONbe pleased to state:-(a) whether initiative has been taken for construction of some new airports and revival of old ones recently;(b) if so, the State-wise details thereof;(c) whether some new airports have started operations recently and if so, the details thereof;(d) the estimate of the total expenditure for construction of new airports and revival of old ones; and(e) the State-wise number of the operational airports in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of State in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (GEN. (DR) V. K. SINGH (RETD))(a) to (c): Government of India (GoI) has accorded ''in-principle'' approval for setting up of 21 Greenfield Airports across the country namely Mopa in Goa, Navi Mumbai, Sindhudurg and Shirdi in Maharashtra, Bijapur, Hassan, Kalaburagi and Shimoga in Karnataka, Dabra (Gwalior) in Madhya Pradesh, Kushinagar and Jewar (Noida) in Uttar Pradesh, Dholera and Hirasar in Gujarat, Karaikal in Puducherry, Dagadarthi, Bhogapuram and Orvakal (Kunoor) in Andhra Pradesh, Durgapur in West Bengal, Pakyong in Sikkim, Kannur in Kerala and Hollongi (Itanagar) in Arunachal Pradesh. So far, six Greenfield airports namely,  Shirdi in Maharashtra, Durgapur in West Bengal, Pakyong in Sikkim, Kannur in Kerala, Orvakal (Kunoor) in Andhra Pradesh and Kalaburagi in Karnataka have been operationalized. Further, under Regional Connectivity Scheme (RCS)- Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik (UDAN), 59 airports  have been revived/operationalized since the launch of the scheme in October, 2016, state-wise details of which are at Annexure-I.;;(d): The responsibility of implementation of airport project including funding of the project rests with the concerned airport developer including Airports Authority of India and the respective State Government (in case the State Govt. is project proponent). Airports Authority of India has undertaken development of Hollongi (Arunachal Pradesh), Hirasar (Gujarat), Dholera (Gujarat) and Khushinagar (Uttar Pradesh) airports at original project costs of Rs. 646 Cr., Rs.1405 Cr., 1375 Cr (Phase I) and 448 Cr. respectively. ;;Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs has approved the proposal of budgetary support of Rs. 4500 crores for revival of existing unserved / underserved airports / airstrips/ heliports/ water aerodromes of the State Governments, Airports Authority of India, Public Sector Undertakings and Civil Enclaves. An airport which is included in the awarded routes of Regional Connectivity Scheme - UDAN and requires upgradation/development for commencement of RCS operations is developed under "Revival of unserved and underserved airports" scheme.;(e): The State-wise list of operational airports in the country is at Annexure-II.
