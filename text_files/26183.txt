                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2068






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.07.2021






Buffer Zone between Human Habitat and Reserved Forest Area




Abdul Khaleque














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government is aware that with growth and development, forest area is shrinking and human habitat is coming close to designated reserved forest areas and if so, the details thereof;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether the Government proposes to create such buffer zone for the preservation of forest and animals and restrict shrinking of reserved forest; and(d)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE (SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b)  Forest Survey of India (FSI), Dehradun, an organization under the Ministry carries out the assessment of forest cover of the country biennially since 1987 and the findings are published in India State of Forest Report (ISFR). The forest cover assessment is a wall-to-wall mapping exercise based on remote sensing supported by intensive ground verification and field data from National Forest Inventory.  As per ISFR 2019, the total forest and tree cover of the country is 8,07,276 square kilometers which is 24.56 % of the geographical area of the country. There is an increase of 13,209 square kilometer of forest and tree cover at the national level in the ISFR-2019 as compared to the ISFR- 2015. (c) & (d)  Eco Sensitive Zones (ESZ) are declared around National Parks and Sanctuaries through Notification under the provisions of Section 3 (2) (V) of the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 and Rules made there under. So, far, out of 670 National Parks and Sanctuaries,  notifications for Eco-Sensitive Zones around 573 National Parks and Sanctuaries have been issued by the Ministry. *****
