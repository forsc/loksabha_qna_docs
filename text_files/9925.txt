                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3431






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






Circulation of Currency




Sajda Ahmed














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a)	whether the Government has assessed the impact of digital push for the past one year and if so, the details thereof; b)	whether currency notes in circulation increased Post-demonetisation compared to Pre-demonetisation and if so, the details thereof;  c)	whether the Government proposed total elimination of cash from the market and if so, the details thereof along with the amount of fake currency recovered after demonetisation; andd)	whether the Government has taken note of the fact that non-availability of Rs.1000 currency causes problem to the citizens and if so, the measures taken in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        The MINISTER OF STATE (FINANCE)(SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR)(a):  	Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology has informed that no formal assessment to measure the impact of digital India programme has been conducted by them.  However, the statistics of e-transactions on e-Taal platform gives an indication of increase in digital penetration and usage of e-Government tools in India. More than 1454 crore e-transactions have been recorded for 3,702 services since January 2019.  (b): 	 The value of total notes in circulation as on March 31, 2016, March 31, 2017, March 31, 2018 and March 31, 2019 is as under:(Value in Rs. Billions)	31.03.2016	31.03.2017	31.03.2018	31.03.2019The value of total notes in circulation	16,415	13,102	  18,037	21,109(c):  No such proposal regarding total elimination of cash from the market is under consideration of the Government.  A Statement indicating details regarding amount of counterfeit currency notes recovered after demonetisation is at Annexure.(d)	The Indian economy has already adjusted itself to the new scenario where the earlier series of Rs.500 and Rs.1000 had ceased to be a legal tender and fresh series of Rs.500 were issued and a new denomination of Rs.2000 has been introduced.  *****
