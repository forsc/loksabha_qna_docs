                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2430






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2021






STARS and ASPIRE Initiatives




 Dr. T. Sumathy (a) Thamizhachi Thangapandian














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the status, timeline and framework for partnership with Asian Development Bank for rollout of the project Accelerating State Education Programme to Improve Results(ASPIRE) in 5new States;(b) the process to be followed to select these States?(c) the details of non-State actors with whom meetings have taken place for implementingelements of the Strengthening Teaching Learning and Results for States Programme(STARS)and ASPIRE projects, along with the components thereof ; and(d) whether there is a commitment to ensure all PPPs under the scheme likely to be solelypermitted with non-profit entities and if so, the details thereof ?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EDUCATION(SHRIMATI ANNPURNA DEVI)(a): The project of Accelerating State Education Programme to Improve Results(ASPIRE) has been approved by Expenditure Finance Committee (EFC). The timeline forASPIRE is from 2022-23 to 2026-27. The framework for the project is available athttps://www.adb.org/sites/default/files/project-documents/53386/53386-001-pam-en.pdf(b): The states of Assam, Gujarat, Jharkhand, Tamil Nadu, and Uttarakhand are coveredunder ASPIRE program. The five States were selected according to the Ministry ofEducation (MOE)’s Performance Grading Index 2017–2018, by considering a mix of Statesfrom different levels, population sizes, and geographic dispersion.(c) & (d): The STARS Program has two result areas: a national component that supportsefforts to improve overall monitoring and measurement activities in the Indian schooleducation system and a state component with five sub-components. The national componentbroadly aims at improving and tracking secondary school completion rates, fostering reformsin governance and monitoring improvement in states’ governance scores through the StateIncentive Grants (SIGs) and strengthening learning assessment systems at the national level.The state component encompasses strengthening of Early Childhood Education (ECE),improving learning assessment systems, improving teacher performance and classroompractice, strengthening the school-to-work/higher education transition, strengtheninggovernance and decentralized management etc. The program has offered flexibility to the sixstates to choose from among the five sub-components depending on their state-level contextand needs. Under the STARS Program it is up to the States to use any of the components toimprove service delivery.ASPIRE program is aligned with the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 to assist thestates to transform a set of government schools into achieving essential learning resources,scale up effective student-centred interventions to boost learning; and strengthen the coreelements of state education for overall system improvements. The program also aims tomitigate the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on students’ learning and mainstream digitallearning-teaching capabilities across the schools.*****
