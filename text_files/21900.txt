                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3130






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.03.2021






Setting Up Kendriya Vidyalayas in Tamil Nadu




V. Kalanidhi














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has approved for setting up of some more Kendriya Vidyalayas within Tamil Nadu during the last one year ; (b) 	if so, the details thereof areas-wise along with the number of Kendriya Vidyalayas approved for Chennai; (c) 	the details of the funds sanctioned, allocated and utilized for this purpose; (d) 	the details of the target set and the achievements made so far; (e) 	whether the Government has ever received any proposal from the State Government to open Kendriya Vidyalayas in North Chennai constituency of Tamil Nadu; and (f) 	if so, the reasons it has not been approved so far?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) 	50 new Kendriya Vidyalayas (KVs) were sanctioned in the country in March, 2019 including 4 KVs viz. ITBP, Idayapatti, ITBP, Shivgangai, Udumalpet & BSF, Kittampalayam in the State of Tamil Nadu. Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan has informed that 3 KVs viz. ITBP, Idayapatti, ITBP, Shivgangai & Udumalpet made functional in the year 2019-20 to 2020-21. (b)	No new KV has been approved for Chennai during the last one year. However, 10 KVs are already functional in Chennai. (c)	Grant-in-aid is released to Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan (KVS) under the three distinct heads viz, Salaries, General and Creation of Capital Assets. This allocation of funds is made on a consolidated basis and not State/Union Territory-wise. KVS has informed that during the year 2019-20, expenditure of Rs.243.69 crore has been incurred for KVs located in the State of Tamil Nadu.(d)	Opening of new KVs is a continuous process. Proposals for opening of new KVs are considered only if sponsored by Ministries or Departments of the Government of India / State Governments / Union Territories (UTs) Administrations, committing resources, including land, temporary accommodation etc. as per prescribed norms of Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan (KVS) for setting up a new KV. The proposals received from various sponsoring authorities for opening of new KVs complete in all respects have to compete with other proposals under the “Challenge Method”.(e) & (f) KVS has informed that no proposal in the prescribed proforma has been received for opening of new KV in North Chennai constituency of Tamil Nadu.*****
