                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5442






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.07.2019






National Highway Projects




Bhagwanth Khuba













                                                    Andimuthu Raja
                                                








                                                    Saptagiri Sankar Ulaka
                                                








                                                    Nalin Kumar Kateel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the on-going/pending NH projects in the country along with the funds allocated, sanctioned and utilised for the purpose during the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise;(b)	whether some of these projects are facing huge cost/ time overrun and if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor along with the intial estimated cost and targeted time for completion thereof;(c)	the steps taken/being taken by the Government to complete the pending projects within the stipulated time limit and without cost overrun;  (d)	the length of NHs constructed during the said period in the country, State/ UT-wise;(e)	the details of State Highways converted into NHs in the State of Karnataka during the said period;(f)	the target set by the Union Government for construction of NH in Karnataka during the current financial year along with the funds allocated for the purpose; and(g)	the details of mechanism established by the Government to monitor and check the construction quality and maintenance work of NHs ?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) 	The State/UT-wise details of the on-going NH projects in the country as on 31.03.2019 are at Annexure-I. The details of  funds allocated and utilised for construction/ development of NHs during the last three years and the current year are at Annexure-II. (b) & (c) There are 453 development projects under this Ministry which are delayed mainly due to land acquisition, utility shifting, non-availability of soil/ aggregate, poor performance of contractors, environment/ forest/ wildlife clearances, ROB & RUB issues with Railways, public agitation for additional facilities, arbitration/ contractual disputes with contractors etc. To fast track these projects, various steps such as streamlining of land acquisition & environment clearances, premium re-scheduling, close coordination with other Ministries, revamping of dispute resolution mechanism, frequent review meetings with project developers, State Governments and contractors at various levels etc. have been taken up.(d)	The State/UT-wise details of  length of NHs constructed in the country during the last three years are at Annexure-III.(e)	The details of State Roads including State Highways converted into NHs in the State of Karnataka during the last three years are as follows:Year	Length of State Roads converted into NHs2016-2017	2592017-2018	8892018-2019	132(f)	The target set by the Ministry for construction of NHs in Karnataka during the current financial year 2019-20 is 375 km. An amount of Rs. 625 Crore have been allocated for  development of NHs  in the  State of Karnataka during current financial year 2019-20 at present. (g)	There is a well-established mechanism for monitoring/ testing of quality of construction/ development of work of NHs by engaging a Consultancy firm of repute as Supervision Consultant/ Independent Consultant/ Authority Engineer. They supervise/ monitor the quality of work as per procedures laid down in various codes published by Indian Roads Congress (IRC), manuals & Ministry’s specifications for Road and Bridge works, etc. The routine inspections are also done by departmental Engineers on regular basis to assess quality of construction and maintenance of road.
