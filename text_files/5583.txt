                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COMMUNICATIONS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5098






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

24.07.2019






4G Services by BSNL




Rakesh Singh














                        Will the Minister of





COMMUNICATIONS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited has launched 4G network service in the country, if so, the details thereof and the names of places where the said service has been launched; (b) 	the steps taken by the Government to make the 4G services of BSNL competitive; and (c) 	whether BSNL has not launched 4G service in Jabalpur, if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF COMMUNICATIONS, LAW & JUSTICE AND ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY(SHRI RAVI SHANKAR PRASAD)(a) 	Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) has, in a limited manner, started 4G services in its Licensed Service Areas (LSA) by using existing spectrum in 2100 MHz band.  As on 31.05.2019, 5921 Base Transceiver Stations (BTSs) of 4G are working in BSNL network.  State/Circle wise details of 4G BTSs of BSNL is at Annexure.(b) 	BSNL has requested for administrative allotment of spectrum (5MHz in 2100 MHz band for all of its Licensed Service Areas (LSAs) except Rajasthan and 5MHz in 800 MHz band in Rajasthan) for providing 4G services with financial support from the Government.  Proposal received from BSNL is under consideration in the Department of Telecommunications.(c) 	BSNL has informed that presently 4G service has not been started in Jabalpur due to non-availability of separate spectrum for 4G service.
