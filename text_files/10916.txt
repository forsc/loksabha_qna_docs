                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4281






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2019






Menstrual Hygiene for Adolescent Girls




S. Jothimani














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether the scheme for Promotion of Menstrual Hygiene for Adolescent Girls residing primarily in rural areas of the country has been implemented on-ground, and if so, the details thereof, State/UT/ district-wise;(b) whether any study has been conducted/is likely to be considered in future to assess the impact of the aforementioned scheme on the menstrual health of women;(c) the issues faced by girls related to distribution or use of sanitary napkins at subsidized rates given by Accredited Social Health Activists (ASHAs) in schools and community;(d) whether any survey has been conducted to check if the women included under the scheme and given sanitary pads are using or utilizing them in the future, and if not, the reasons therefor; and(e) whether the Government is likely to consider publishing such a report in future and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):		 Government is supporting the Scheme for Promotion of Menstrual Hygiene for Adolescent Girls residing primarily in rural areas. Public Health being a State subject, the primary responsibility to strengthen programme including of menstrual hygiene, lies with respective State Governments. However under National Health Mission (NHM), support is provided to States/UTs based on proposals received from them. The State/UT-wise  number of adolescent girls covered is placed at Annexure (b) to (e):	  No study has been conducted to assess the impact of the Scheme, however, the Menstrual Hygiene Scheme is reviewed regularly and issues raised during the review meetings are sorted out and sent to the State/UT  governments for compliance.Department of Health Research is carrying out research and studies to look into the various methods of managing menstrual health and other sustainable alternatives to sanitary napkins for their safety, acceptability, affordability, efficacy and feasibility among women in public health program setting.
