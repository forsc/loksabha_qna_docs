                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3357






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.07.2019






Food Adulteration




Mansukhbhai Dhanjibhai Vasava













                                                    Vijay Baghel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND  FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether adulteration has been detected/reported in milk products, edibleoils, vegetables, sweets and other food products resulting in adverse impact on the health of the people of the country andif so, the details thereof;(b) the details of the quantum of adulteration in percentage in various food products that came to light in the countryduring each of the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise;(c) the details of the action taken against the culprits involved in such activities;(d) whether the Government is considering to set up centres to monitor the safety of food, fruits, etc. in various parts of the country, if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; and(e) the details of the remedial action being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a)	to (c): Cases of sale/supply of sub-standard and adulterated food products have been detected during the course of enforcement action taken by the States/UTs.  As per the enforcement reports/data received from States/UTs, a consolidated statement on food samples analysed, found not conforming and action taken against the defaulting Food Business Operators for the last three years is at Annexure-I.  Further, State/UT-wise data for last three years is at Annexure II, III and IV respectively.(d) to (e): As implementation and enforcement of Food Safety and Standards (FSS) Act, 2006 primarily lies with State/UT Governments, regular surveillance monitoring, inspection and random sampling of food products including fruits and vegetables are being carried out by the Food Safety Officers of States/UTs and action is taken as per the provisions of FSS Act, 2006 against the defaulting Food Business Operators. Every State/UT has appointed Commissioner of Food Safety as head of the State enforcement machinery along with district-wise Designated Officers and Food Safety Officers to monitor and ensure the availability of safe and wholesome food for human consumption.Further, in order to ensure the availability of good quality food products to the consumers and for keeping a check on the problems of food adulteration, presence of residual pesticides beyond Maximum Residue Limits etc., FSSAI has advised the State food authorities from time to time to keep a strict vigil by regularly drawing food samples from all sources viz. manufacturers, wholesalers and retailers and to take strict action against the offenders under the provisions of FSS Act, 2006.
