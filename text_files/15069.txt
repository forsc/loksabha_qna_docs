                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

324






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2020






Official Language




Arvind Kumar Sharma














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether Hindi has been given the status of official language in the Constitution but there is more use of English even in the work of the Union Government;(b) whether one of the main reasons for this is that the official language staff is most neglected and undermined, comparatively underpaid and having scant opportunities for promotion, leading to low morale and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government has taken/proposes to take any corrective measures in this regard; and(d) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) to (d):	    A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.--2--STATEMENT IN REPLY TO LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *324 (a) to (d) FOR ANSWER ON 17.03.2020 RAISED BY DR. ARVIND KUMAR SHARMA  REGARDING “OFFICIAL LANGUAGE”.(a):    Hindi has been enshrined as the Official Language on 14th September, 1949 by the constituent Assembly of India. Usage of the Official Language Hindi in the Central Government Offices etc. is on the rise. Department of Official Language is trying to increase the progressing use of Hindi in the Central Government work.(b) to (d):	No Sir, There is no deficiency in the morale of the Official language staff. The pay scale of Central Secretariat Official Language Service (CSOLS) is regulated and revised from time to time as per the recommendations of the Central Pay Commission. At present, all are being given pay scale as per 7th Pay Commission. Total sanctioned strength of CSOLS cadre is 1011. Department of Official Language is giving promotions to Officers of CSOLS Cadre regularly. It is mention worthy that Department of Official Language has promoted 279 Officers of CSOLS Cadre during 2019 whose description is given below:Sl.NO.	Details of promotion given	No. of Personnel promoted1	Joint Director to Director	032	Deputy Director to Joint Director	043	Assistant Director to Deputy Director	644	Sr. Translation Officer to Assistant Director	195	Sr. Translation Officer to Ad hoc Assistant Director	656	Jr. Translation Officer to Sr. Translation Officer	124*****
