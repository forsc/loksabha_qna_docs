                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

615






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

05.02.2020






Flexi Fare System




Talari Rangaiah














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there is any plan to increase the number of 3 tier coaches in long distance trains; (b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Railways is planning to re-consider its flexi fare scheme as, at times, these fares are more than the Air fare in the same sector; and(d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS AND COMMERCE & INDUSTRY(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) to (d) A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.*****STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (d) OF UNSTARRED QUESTION NO. 615 BY SHRI TALARI RANGAIAH TO BE ANSWERED IN LOK SABHA ON 05.02.2020 REGARDING FLEXI FARE SYSTEM (a) & (b) The demand pattern on Indian Railways is not uniform and it varies during peak and lean season across different sectors and routes. As such, with a view to provide more accommodation to travelling passengers, Indian Railways, subject to operational feasibility, traffic pattern and availability of resources, augments the load of existing services with all type of coaches including Sleeper Class Coaches and AC-III Tier coaches, which is an on-going process. Accordingly, of the 758 Coaches used for permanent  augmentation, during the period April-December, 2019, 224 Sleeper Class Coaches and 220 AC-III tier coaches have been utilized for augmentation of different train services over Indian Railways. (c) & (d) Based on the feedback from different quarters, a committee was constituted to review the flexi Fare scheme. Further, based on the committee’s recommendations and other representations, the matter has already been examined and  Flexi fare scheme in Rajdhani, Shatabdi and Duronto trains has been rationalized with effect from 15.11.2018 for journey commencing from 15.03.2019 as under:-(i) Discontinuation of Flexi fare scheme from 15 trains for full year and from 32 trains during pre defined lean period of 3 months (February, March & August).(ii) Maximum cap of flexi fare scheme has been reduced to 1.4 times in all flexi fare applicable classes.(iii) Graded discount has been introduced in flexi fare trains, where class-wise occupancy is less than 60%, 4 days prior to scheduled departure of the train as under:-Occupancy	DiscountUp to 70%	20% on last fare70 to 80%	10% on last fareAbove 80%	Nil                                                *****
