                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

5331






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.07.2019






Development of Coir Industry




Adoor Prakash














                        Will the Minister of





MICRO,SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the various schemes being implemented for development of the coir industry;(b)	the details of funds released under these schemes to various States during the last three years and the current year;(c)	the volume and value of coir products exported from the country during the last three years; (d)	the details of schemes implemented for the welfare of coir workers in the country and details of Central assistance provided for the implementation of such schemes during the last three years, State/UT-wise; and(e)	whether majority of coir produced in the country are from the State of Kerala and the Government would provide special assistance to the State for export of coir products and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF MICRO, SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES(SHRI NITIN GADKARI)(a):   Coir Board, a statutory body under the Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME), is implementing an umbrella Scheme “Coir Vikas Yojana” for development of coir industry in the country. The scheme has the following components:- 1.	Science and Technology2.	Coir Industry Technology Upgradation Scheme (CITUS)3.	Skill Upgradation and Mahila Coir Yojana4.	Export Market Promotion5.	Domestic Market Promotion6.	Trade and Industry Related Functional Support Services (TIRFSS)7.	Welfare Measures (Prime Minister Suraksha Bima Yojana)The following programmes are also being implemented in Coir industry through Coir Board:1.	Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI)2.	Prime Ministers’ Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP)(b):   	The details of funds released under Coir Vikas Yojana to Coir Board during the last three years and current year are at Annexure – I. (c):  	 The details of coir products exported from the country during the last three years are at Annexure – II.(d):	 Coir Board had been implementing a welfare scheme called “Coir Workers Group Personal Accident Insurance Scheme” for providing financial compensation to coir workers against death or disablement caused by accidents. The scheme was converged with the ‘Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana’(PMSBY) with effect from 1st June 2016.  Under PMSBY, a total number of 44634 coir workers have been enrolled so far and an amount of Rs. 2,74,824 was incurred by Coir Board as premium. The State-wise details of coir workers enrolled under the scheme are at Annexure – III.(e):	Majority of value added products of coir industry are produced by the State of Kerala.  Financial assistance is provided to State of Kerala under Coir Vikas Yojana (CVY) for overall development of Coir Industry.
