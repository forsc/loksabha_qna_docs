                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2187






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.09.2020






National Family Health Survey




Mahendrabhai Kalubhai Munjpara














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) whether it is true that between NFHS-3 and NFHS-4 the unmet need for family planning among currently married women in the age group of 15-49 years declined marginally from 13.9% to 12.9%, translating to about 30 million women;(b) if so, the details thereof; and(c) whether this unmet need/demand is due to shortage in supply on account of supply side gap, if so, the details thereof and the action taken to adress the shortcomings?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) & (b) Yes, the prevalence of the unmet need of family planning has declined from 13.9% to 12.9% between NFHS-3 (2005-06) and NFHS-4 (2015-16). Details of the findings can be accessed from the url https://nrhm-mis.nic.in/SitePages/NFHS.aspx. Further, the State/UT wise data on percentage of currently married women of age 15-49 with unmet need for family planning in NFHS-3 (2005-06) and NFHS-4 (2015-16) may be found in Annexure.(c) The unmet need for family planning is not due to any supply side gap.
