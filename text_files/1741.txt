                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

155






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.07.2019






Assistance to Women Farmers




Sunil Dattatray Tatkare













                                                    Supriya Sadanand Sule
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has started any schemes to provide facilities to the women farmers for the development of the agriculture sector in the country, if so, the details thereof along with the amount of funds allocated and utilized for the said purpose during the last three years and the current year;(b) 	the number of women farmers who have been trained in agriculture related fields under various Central schemes during the above period; (c) 	whether the Government is aware that the share of women in the production of main crops in the country is 75 per cent; (d) 	if so, the action taken by the Government in doubling the wages of the women working in the agriculture sector or providing assistance for doubling the income of the women working as self employed farmers; and (e) 	the other steps taken by the Government to release more funds for development of women farmers so that they could meet their specific needs and achieve socio-economic and technical empowerment?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????		 	  (SHRI NARENDRA  SINGH TOMAR) (a) to (e):	A statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (e) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 155 DUE FOR REPLY ON 2ND JULY, 2019.(a):	Yes, Sir. In line with the provisions of National Policy for Farmers (NPF) (2007), the Department of Rural Development, Ministry of Rural Development is implementing a programme exclusively for women farmers namely, Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana (MKSP), which is a sub-component of Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Rural Livelihood Mission (DAY-NRLM). The primary objective of MKSP is to empower women and to enhance their participation and create sustainable livelihood opportunities for them in agriculture. The State/UT wise fund allocation and release under MKSP during the last three years and the current year are given at Annexure-I.  The Department of Agriculture Cooperation and Farmers Welfare is also promoting mainstreaming of Gender Concerns in agriculture by ensuring flow of funds and benefits to the tune of 30% for women farmers under its beneficiary oriented Schemes and Programmes. (b): 		Over 1.39 crore women have been trained in agriculture related fields under various Central Schemes in last 3 years and the current year. The number of women farmers trained under different Schemes during the last 3 years and the current year are given at Annexure-II.(c):	There is no census data available highlighting share of women in the production of main crops in the country. However, as per Census, 2011 conducted by the Registrar General of India, Ministry of Home affairs, the extent of women participation in agriculture as cultivators (main and marginal) is to the tune of 3.60 crore (30.33 %) and as female agricultural labourer (main and marginal), it is to the tune of 6.15 crore (42.67%). Contd…2/- -2-(d):	Under the provisions of the Minimum Wages Act, 1948, both the State and Central Governments are the appropriate Governments to fix, review, revise and implement the minimum wages under their respective jurisdictions. The wages fixed by the appropriate Governments are equally payable to both male and female workers and the Act does not discriminate on the basis of gender. With a strategy to focus on farmers’ welfare, the Government have made various interventions on the recommendation of DFI Committee, the benefits of which flow to farmers including women farmers.(e):	 Government is providing additional support and assistance to women farmers, over and above the male farmers under various Schemes, the details of which are given at Annexure-III.
