                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CULTURE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1068






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.02.2021






Financial Assistance for the Rituals Accredited with Intangible Cultural Heritage




Abdul Majeed Ariff














                        Will the Minister of





CULTURE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government provides financial assistance for the rituals accredited with Intangible Cultural Heritage (ICH) status for preserving the heritage and if so, the details thereof;(b)	whether the Government has prepared detailed project report subsequent to accrediting the Chettikulangara Kumbha Bharani Kettukazcha in Alappuzha District in Kerala with Intangible Cultural Heritage(ICH) status;(c)	whether the Government has appointed any consultant for preparing the detailed project report; and(d)	if so, the details of financial assistance provided in this regard and the time by which the consultant is likely to be appointed?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE (IC) FOR CULTURE & TOURISM(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) Yes sir, the Government provides financial grants/assistance to the inscribed elements of the Representative List for Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, UNESCO, under the Scheme for “Safeguarding the Intangible Cultural Heritage and Diverse Cultural Traditions of India”.(b) Regarding Chettikulangara Kumbha Bharani Kettukazcha, dossier for this ICH was submitted to UNESCO in the year 2011 along with other nominations for inscription in the Representative List for Intangible Cultural Heritage for Humanity. At that time UNESCO was accepting multiple nominations from every country. In due course, the rules changed and UNESCO made it mandatory that every country can only submit one (1) nomination .As a  result,   dossiers submitted earlier , which includes dossier of  Chettikulangara Kumbha Bharani Kettukazcha, were put in a priority list that is visited every year by the Ministry of Culture for re-prioritisation.(c) and (d) No sir.
