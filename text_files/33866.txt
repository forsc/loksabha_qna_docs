                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1512






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2022






Regulation of Crude Oil Price




Kirit Premjibhai Solanki














                        Will the Minister of





PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	 whether it is true that the Government is working with international partners such as Japan, United States to reduce dominance of certain global group of oil-producing countries which are playing a role in inflating crude oil prices and if so, the details thereof;   (b)	 whether the Government has coordinated any strategy with international partners to counter the dominance of Global group; and    (c)	 if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS  (SHRI RAMESWAR TELI) (a)to(c): In a bid to stabilize rising oil prices, Government of India agreed in November 2021, to release 5 million barrels of crude oil from its Strategic Petroleum Reserves, in consultation and parallely with other major global energy consumers including United States and Japan.  Government has also been taking up the issue, bilaterally, with crude oil producing countries, with OPEC and with heads of other international fora to convey India''s serious concerns over crude oil price volatility, and India''s strong preference for responsible and reasonable pricing for consumer countries.*****
