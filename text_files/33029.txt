                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

599






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2022






Identification of Medicinal Plants and Rare Herbs




Hemant Patil














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has conducted any survey to identify medicinal plants and rare species of herbs in Maharashtra;(b)	if so, the details thereof; and(c)	the other steps taken/proposed to be taken by the Government thereon?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AYUSH(SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a): Yes Sir, Central Council for Research in Ayurvedic Sciences (CCRAS), an apex body under Ministry of AYUSH has conducted Medico Ethno Botanical Survey (MEBS) in Nagpur, Bhandar, Wardha, Amrawati, Chandrapur, Allapalli, Bhamragad, Gondia, West Melghat, Gadchiroli, Buldana, Yavatmal, Pusad, Akola, Vidarbha, Shahapur, Jawahar and Thane forest divisions of Maharashtra.(b) & (c): The outcomes of Medico Ethno Botanical Survey programme (MEBS) of CCRAS has been published in the form of Book entitled “Medicinal Plants of Nagpur and Wardha Forest Divisions, Maharashtra”. This book comprises the details of 300 plant species which are of medicinal importance along with Sanskrit / local names with brief description of the plants, flowering and fruiting season and medicinal uses. The folk medicinal uses of some plants are also included.Also, the National Medicinal Plants Board (NMPB), Ministry of AYUSH through its “Central Sector Scheme on Conservation, Development and Sustainable Management of Medicinal Plants” provides project based assistance for Survey, inventorization, in-situ conservation, Resource Augmentation of medicinal plants in forest areas and ex-situ conservation by the establishment of Herbal Gardens etc. throughout the country. ****
