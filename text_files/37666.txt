                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

31.03.2022






Benefits of Drone Technology




Y. S. Avinash Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the drones offer tremendous benefits for use in various sectors of economy viz. agriculture, defence, law enforcement and mapping;(b) if so, the steps taken by the Government to promote use of drone technology;(c) the details of public and private sector organisations which are using drones and the number of drone manufacturing plants which are coming up in the country; and(d) whether any such plants are being established in the State of Andhra Pradesh and if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        Minister of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Jyotiraditya M. Scindia)(a) to (d): A statement is laid on the Table of the House.Statement in reply to Lok Sabha Starred Question no 405 due for answer on 31 March 2022.; ; (a) to (d) Drones offer tremendous benefits to almost all sectors of the economy.  These include - agriculture, vaccine delivery, surveillance, search and rescue, transportation, mapping, defence and law enforcement to name a few.   ; The Government released the liberalised Drone Rules, 2021 on 25th August 2021. In order to make India a global hub for drones under the Atmanirbhar Bharat Abhiyan, Ministry of Civil Aviation had requested Union Ministries to encourage various entities under their Ministry to promote use of drones. An illustrative list of drone applications relevant to different Ministries is at Annexure I. ; ; Drone outreach programmes were conducted in various States and Union Territories to promote use of drones, encourage startups for innovation, research and development in the emerging sector. The Government has brought out a Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme for drones and drone components on 30th September 2021 with a total incentive of INR 120 crore spread over three financial years. ; As per Drone Rules, 2021, there is no requirement for drone manufacturers in the country, including those in Andhra Pradesh, to register on the Government''s DigitalSky platform. Any person can operate drones in a green zone without prior permission upto a height of 400 feet. Prior permission from the Central Government and the concerned Air Traffic Authority (ATC) is required to operate drones in red and yellow zones respectively. ; The list of public and private sector entities given conditional exemption or permission for red zone drone operations is at Annexure II. An illustrative list of drone manufacturers in India is at Annexure III.
