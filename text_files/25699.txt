                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    LAW AND JUSTICE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1581






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.07.2021






Sanctioned Strength of Judges




Haji Fazlur Rehman














                        Will the Minister of





LAW AND JUSTICE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of LAW AND JUSTICE be pleased to state:(a)	the sanctioned strength of judges in the Supreme Court and various High 	Courts in the country at present, court-wise; (b)	the total number of male and female judges in Supreme Court and various 	High Courts at present and the number out of them belonging to 	minority community and Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribes; and (c)	the details of the number of vacancies of judges in the Supreme Court and 	various High Courts?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF LAW AND JUSTICE(SHRI KIREN RIJIJU)(a)	to (c) : A Statement showing sanctioned strength of Judges, number of male & female Judges and vacancies in the Supreme Court and High Courts as on 20.07.2021 is at Annexure. :  2  :		Appointment of Judges of the Supreme Court and High Courts is made under Articles 124, 217 and 224 of the Constitution of India, which do not provide for reservation for any caste or class of persons. Hence no class/category wise data is maintained centrally. However, the Governement has been requesting the Chief Justices of High Courts that while sending proposals for appointment of Judges, due consideration be given to suitable candidates belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Other Backward Classes, Minorities and Women to ensure social diversity in appointment of Judges in High Courts. ***** AnnexureStatement referred to in reply to parts (a) to (c) of Unstarred Question No. 1581 to be answered in Lok Sabha on 28.07.2021 regarding ‘Sanctioned Strength of Judges’ by Shri Haji Fazlur Rehman  (Position as on 20.07.2021)Sl.
