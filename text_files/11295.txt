                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

344






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2020






Talks with Maoists




K. Subbarayan














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether it is a fact that the Government has not made any effort to have a talk with the Maoists since the last peace talks in 2004;(b) if so, the details and the reasons therefor;(c) whether it is also a fact that during the last three four years, there has been a rapid change in the attitude of the Maoists in different parts of the country and they are now open for a dialogue;(d) if so, whether the Government proposes to initiate a process of peace talks with them which may help to shun the violence; and(e) if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (e)    The Central Government is open to talks with the Left Wing Extremist groups if they renounce violence and agree to abide by the Constitution of India.       As no Left Wing Extremist group has shown willingness for this, no talks have been held with any group during the last few years.*****
