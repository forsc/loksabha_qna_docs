                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2262






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.09.2020






Man-Animal Conflict




Thomas Chazhikadan














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has taken note of the loss of human lives due to frequent attacks by wild animals encroaching into agriculture fields and human habitations near forest borders in various states;(b)	 if so, the total number of human lives lost during the last five years ending August 2020, State-wise;(c)	whether the Government is aware of the fact that the population of various wild species have multiplied during the last few years and that they are posing danger by encroaching into agriculture fields and nearby human habitations near forest borders due to insufficient food and water; and(d)	if so, the measures taken by the Government to control the man-animal conflict in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) & (b) Incidences of loss of human lives due to attacks by wild animals are reported in various parts of the country from time to time. The management of forests and wildlife is primarily the responsibility of the concerned State/UT Governments and Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change does not maintain the data of human lives lost due to wild animals. However, as per information received from Project Elephant and National Tiger Conservation Authority (NTCA), the number of human deaths caused by elephants and tigers, state-wise, during the last five years is given at Annexure I and II respectively.
