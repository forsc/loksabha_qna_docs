                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3281






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2021






National Livestock Mission in Kerala




Dean Kuriakose














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has data on the number of livestock registered under Rashtriya Gokul Mission up-to March 2021 in the State of Kerala, if so, the details thereof;(b) the number of beneficiaries of the sub-mission on skill development, technology transfer and extension under national livestock mission in the State of Kerala; and (c) the salient features of fisheries and aquaculture infrastructure development fund and the details of the various projects and amount of concessional finances provided for projects in the State of Kerala up-to March 2021?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING (DR. SANJEEV KUMAR BALYAN)(a) As per Information Network for Animal Productivity and Health (INAPH) database, a total of 18,09,964 animals have been registered in the State of Kerala as on date. (b)  The Government is implementing a sub-mission on skill development, technology transfer and extension under National Livestock Mission (NLM) across the country including Kerala. NLM is a demand driven scheme and no demands have been received from the State of Kerala under the sub-mission since its inception.(c) In order to address the infrastructure requirement for fisheries sector, the Government is implementing a scheme namely, Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund (FIDF) with a total outlay of Rs 7522.48 crore. FIDF provides concessional finance to the Eligible Entities (EEs), including State Governments/Union Territories and State entities for development of identified fisheries infrastructure facilities including small and marginal fisher folk. The activities like construction of ponds, establishment of hatcheries, installation of cages in reservoirs, fish feed mills, fish processing units, ice plants, cold storages are supported. Government of Kerala has submitted a proposal on development of Aruthunkal Fishing Harbour at a total project cost of Rs. 100 crore for concessional financing under FIDF.***
