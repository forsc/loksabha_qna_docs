                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CIVIL AVIATION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

76






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

06.02.2020






Setting up of New Greenfield Airports




Sujay Radhakrishna Vikhepatil













                                                    Sambhajirao Mane Dhairyasheel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CIVIL AVIATION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the details of per annum terminal/traffic capacity against the demand at all airports at present in the country, State/UT-wise;(b) whether the Government has made any assessment of the likely increase in number of air passengers by 2030 and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government has granted in-principle approval for setting up of new Greenfield Airports across the country to meet the demands and if so, the details thereof along with the present status of these airports, State/UT-wise; and(d) the expenditure involved along with the manner in which it will be met in respect of each of the airports being set up in the country?






ANSWER




                                                        The Minister of State (IC) in the Ministry of CIVIL AVIATION      (Shri Hardeep Singh Puri)(a) to (d): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.Statement in reply to part (a) to (d) of the Lok Sabha Starred Question No.76 for 06.02.2020 regarding ''''Setting up of New Greenfield Airports";;  (a): The terminal capacity available at all Indian airports is 374 million passengers per annum (MPPA).  During the year 2018-19, 344 million passengers have been handled at these airports.  The State / UT-wise capacity available and demand at all airports is given at Annexure-I. (b): Airports Authority of India (AAI) has made an assessment and has forecast that about 730 million passengers are likely to be handled at all the airports of the country during 2030. (c) & (d): Government of India, Ministry of Civil Aviation has granted ''in principle'' approval for setting up of 16 Greenfield Airports across the country namely Mopa in Goa (Rs. 3100 Cr in Phase I), Navi Mumbai (Rs. 16704 Cr), Sindhudurg (Rs. 450 Cr in Phase I) in Maharashtra, Bijapur (Rs. 150 Cr.), Hassan (Rs. 592 Cr) and Shimoga (Rs. 38.9 Cr) in Karnataka, Datia (Gwalior) in Madhya Pradesh (Rs. 200 Cr.), Kushinagar(Rs. 448 Cr.) and Noida (Rs. 3754 Cr in Phase I) in Uttar Pradesh, Dholera (Rs. 1685 Cr in Phase I) and Hirasar (Rs. 1405 Cr. in phase-I) in Gujarat, Karaikal (Rs. 50 Cr) in Puducherry, Dagadarthi (Rs 368.38 Cr), Bhogapuram (Rs. 2302.51 Cr in Phase I) and Oravakal (Kurnool) (Rs. 144.85 Cr.) in Andhra Pradesh and Hollongi, Itanagar (Rs. 955.37 Cr) in Arunachal Pradesh. The status of these airport projects is at Annexure-II. In addition, 5 Greenfield Airports at Shirdi (Maharashtra), Kalaburagi (Karnataka), Kannur (Kerala), Durgapur (West Bengal) and Pakyong (Sikkim) have been operationalized. Development and financing of the Greenfield airport project  is the responsibility of the respective project proponent.
