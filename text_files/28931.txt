                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

249






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

30.11.2021






Fish Production in Rajasthan and Uttar Pradesh




Kanakmal Katara














                        Will the Minister of





FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING ??????????, ??????? ?? ????? ?????? be pleased to state:(a)	the details of schemes being implemented to augment the fish production and to increase the income of fishermen in the States of Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan including Banswara-Dungarpur of Rajasthan; (b)	the details of the steps taken by the Government to provide training and financial assistance to fishermen under ‘Atmanirbhar Bharat Abhiyan; (c)	the number of camps organised in the States of Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan including Banswara-Dungarpur of Rajasthan and the number of people provided with training and the number amongst them joined trade of aquaculture/fish production, the details thereof, district-wise;(d)	whether the Government proposes to accord the status of agriculture to fisheries, if so, by when; (e)	the district-wise details of the funds issued for fisheries during the last five years in the States of Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan including Banswara - Dungarpur of Rajasthan; and(f)	the capacity of fish production in the States of Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan including Banswara Dungarpur of Rajasthan during the last five years, details thereof, district-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF FISHERIES, ANIMAL HUSBANDRY AND DAIRYING(SHRI PARSHOTTAM RUPALA)(a): The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying is implementing a flagship scheme namely “Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY) with highest ever investment of Rs. 20,050 crore for a period of 5 years with effect from the financial year 2020-21 to 2024-25 in all States/Union Territories including Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan as a part of Aatma Nirbhar Bharat Package. Besides, a dedicated fund namely, ‘Fisheries and Aquaculture Infrastructure Development Fund’ (FIDF) with a total fund size of Rs.7,522.48 crore has been set up for providing concessional finance for creation and strengthening of fisheries infrastructure facilities. These schemes implemented in all the States/UTs including Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan inter-alia aiming towards augmentation of fish production, productivity, enhance the income of fish farmers, generate employment opportunities in fisheries and aquaculture sector. (b): 	During the last financial year 2020-21 and in 2021-22, central fund to the tune of Rs.1223.96 crore has been released under the PMMSY to various States/UTs and other organizations including the State of Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan for development of fisheries and for providing financial assistance to eligible fishermen. Besides sustainable development of fisheries and aquaculture, PMMSY inter-alia lays special focus on training, skill development, skill up-gradation and capacity building through trainings, awareness building programmes and exposure visits to stakeholders especially fishers, fish farmers, fish workers, fish vendors, entrepreneurs, officials, fisheries Cooperatives and members of Fish Farmer Producer Organizations. An amount of Rs. 100 crore is earmarked in PMMSY for Training, Awareness, Exposure and Capacity Building and is being utilised to impart training through National Fisheries Development Board, ICAR institutes, Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs), other organisations and State Departments. A total of 3774 outreach events covering about 20.78 lakh beneficiaries have been completed so far.  (c):  The Government of Uttar Pradesh has reported that a total 55 number of training camps were organized in which 2360 persons were imparted training. It is further reported that most of the trainees are engaged in fish production activities. Similarly, the Government of Rajasthan has reported that 5 training-cum- awareness camps were organised to benefit 528 fishers including in the district of Banswara-Dungarpur. The Government of Rajasthan has further reported that a total of 135 trained beneficiaries have jointed trade of aquaculture and fish production.(d)  :  The Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying has no such proposal to accord the status of agriculture to fisheries. (e) and (f):  Year-wise district-wise funds released as reported by the Government of Uttar Pradesh and Government of Rajasthan are furnished at Annexure-I and Annexure-II respectively.
