                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2202






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2019






IIMs




Sumedhanand Saraswati














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	the details of the new Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) proposed by the Government; (b)	whether the new IIMs are facing shortage of faculty; (c)	if so, the details thereof;(d)	the details of the student-teacher ratio prescribed for IIMs; (e)	whether complete information is not provided for admission in new IIMs after releasing the marks of joint entrance exam; (f)	if so, the details thereof; and (g)	the steps taken/being taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (c):	There are seven new IIMs established at Amritsar, Bodh Gaya, Nagpur, Sirmaur, Sambalpur, Visakhapatnam & Jammu.  At present, there is no more proposal to set up any new IIM. There are no shortage of faculty in the seven new IIMs. (d):	In general, teacher – student ratio in IIMs is 1:10. However, the ratio keeps on varying, inter – alia, due to change in the number of students intake.(e) to (g):	No Sir. Complete information related to admission process in new IIMs is provided on their respective websites.*****
