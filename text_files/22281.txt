                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3305






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.03.2021






ITBP Battalion at Nooranadu in Kerala




Suresh Kodikunnil














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has given approval for the development of Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP) Battalion at Nooranadu in the State of Kerala;(b) if so, the details thereof and the status of each work being carried out at ITBP Nooranadu at present;(c) whether the Government is aware of incidents in which the completed works were dedicated to the nation in ITBP premises, Nooranadu without the presence of Public representatives; and(d) if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) to (b):	One Battalion (27th Battalion) of Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP) was established in Nooranadu in Kerala in 2013. Various works as per the authorisation for a Battalion have been sanctioned by the Ministry.  The details and status of works are given at Annexure.(c) to (d):	After completion of construction of Officers’ Mess and Subordinate Officer’s Mess in the Battalion, the same were opened to the Force personnel on 11.01.2020 and no public function was organised for this purpose.********
