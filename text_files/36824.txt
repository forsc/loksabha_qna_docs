                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3968






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.03.2022






NABARD Scheme in Kerala




Kumbakudi Sudhakaran














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has received theproposal from Kerala for improvement of rural roadsunder NABARD Scheme for the year 2021-22;(b) if so, the details thereof along with the numberof roads proposed for each district and if not, thereasons therefor;(c) whether the Government has a received proposalforimproving and providing BM&BC toEruvatty-Vimalasseri-Therthally Road in Kannur under NABARDScheme; and(d) if so, the details thereof and the timeline forexecution of this project and if not, the reasonstherefor? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(a) to (d) As reported by National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD), they have received 51 proposals from Govt. of Kerala for improving rural roads under Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF) Scheme during FY 2021-22.NABARD has also informed that BM&BC toEruvatty-Vimalasseri-Therthally Road project in Kannur District is included in the list of 51 projects forwarded by the State Government of Kerala.District-wise break up of proposals received by NABARD is given below:S.N.	District Name	Number of roads1	Alappuzha	42	Ernakulam	33	Idukki	34	Kannur	35	Kasargod	16	Kollam	57	Kottayam	28	Kozhikode	59	Malappuram	310	Palakkad	911	Pathanamthitta	312	Thiruvananthapuram	513	Thrissur	5TOTAL	51NABARD has further apprised that out of 51 projects, 12 projects has been sanctioned during 2021-22 after considering the RIDF allocation for the Kerala State Government.The Government of India had set up RIDF in NABARD during 1995-96, with the objective of providing loans to the States to enhance public investments in rural infrastructure projects prioritized by the State Governments, out of a list of 39 eligible activities approved by GoI.  The 39 eligible activities are broadly classified under three categories as (i) Agriculture and related sector, (ii) Social sector and (iii) Rural Connectivity. Allocation under RIDF is made by NABARD on the basis of identification and prioritisation of eligible projects and submission of Detailed Project Report (DPR) by the respective State Government, which is considered by NABARD subject to availability of normative allocation for the respective State Government and the project being found technically & financially viable.******
