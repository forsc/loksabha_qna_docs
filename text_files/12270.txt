                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

113






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2020






MICE Tourism




Shashi Tharoor














                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	the detailed information on India’s growth in Meetings, Incentives, Conferences and Exhibitions (MICE) Tourism; (b) 	whether India is ranked low at 24th position worldwide, with only 175 out of total 12,563 international meetings in a year, as per the Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce and Industry (FICCI); (c) 	if so, the reasons therefor; (d) 	the current accommodation space in square metres under MICE segment and the steps being taken to upgrade infrastructure and world-class convention centres in the country; and (e) 	the details of 12th edition of Conventions India Conclave, the premier international meet on MICE tourism, held at Kochi from August 29-31, 2019?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR TOURISM (INDEPENDENT CHARGE)(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) to (e): 	A statement is laid on the table of the House.********* STATEMENT IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (e) OF LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO.*113 ANSWERED ON 10.02.2020 REGARDING MICE TOURISM.(a) to (b): The Ministry of Tourism has recognized Meetings, Incentives, Conventions and Exhibitions (MICE) as a Niche Tourism product in order to overcome the aspect of ‘seasonality’ and to promote India as a 365 days’ destination and attract tourists with specific interests to India. As per International Congress and Convention Association (ICCA) reports, India’s global MICE industry rank (in terms of number of events held) has improved significantly since the year 2014. From 35th rank in the year 2014, India’s global rank has improved by 11 positions to 24th in the year 2017.  India hosted 175 such events in the year 2017 up by 50% from 116 held in the year 2014. As per ICCA reports, India has also improved its global MICE ranking in terms of number of participants per such event from 25th in 2014 to 22nd in the year 2017. Number of participants per event in India grew by 22% from 60,132 in the year 2014 to 73,213 in the year 2017. (c):	As it can been seen from the above details that India’s MICE global ranking on important parameters has steadily improved from the year 2014 to year 2017. Further, Ministry of Tourism has taken various initiatives for the growth and development of this area which includes financial assistance under Market Development Assistance to the industry stakeholders, establishment of India Convention Promotion Bureau, introduction of E-Conference visa for foreign visitors, regular promotional media campaign and assistance to MICE organisers under Champion Sector in Services Scheme.  (d):	Creation of infrastructure for MICE industry is primarily done by various stakeholders from Public sector & Private sector including Hotels, Convention centres & others. Further as per the study conducted by the Ministry of Tourism, it is estimated that MICE facilities are also offered by approximately 750 four & five star category hotels and 69 Convention Centres in the country. However, the details of the major convention and exhibition centres along with approximate area is annexed at “Annexure-I”. Important upcoming convention centres with government assistance of international standard facilities includes International Exhibition-cum-Convention Centre, Pragati Maidan in Delhi and India International Convention Centre in Dwarka, Delhi.  Ministry of Tourism provides Central Financial Assistance (CFA) under Swadesh Darshan scheme to the State Governments/UT Administrations for development of tourism infrastructure including the setting up of convention centres under the existing schemes. In the year 2016-2017, Ministry sanctioned CFA of Rs.98.73 crore for construction of Convention Centre at Bodhgaya, Bihar under Buddhist Tourist Circuit.  (e):	The details of 12th edition of Conventions India Conclave (CIC), International meet on MICE tourism, held at Kochi from August 29-31, 2019 is annexed at “Annexure -II”.*******
