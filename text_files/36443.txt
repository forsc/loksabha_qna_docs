                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3832






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

25.03.2022






National AYUSH Mission




Naranbhai Bhikhabhai Kachhadiya













                                                    Parbatbhai Savabhai Patel
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the period for which the National Ayush Mission(NAM) has been decided to continue as centrally sponsored scheme;(b) the total cost of the project and the amount to be spent by the Union Government and State Governments separately;(c) the conventional and non-conventional medical systems included in the mission; and(d) whether health programmes are also likely to be run in the schools through the said mission?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AYUSH (SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a) & (b) Union Cabinet has approved continuation of the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of the National AYUSH Mission (NAM) from 01.04.2021 to 31.03.2026 with financial implication of Rs. 4607.30 Crore (Rs. 3000.00 Crore as Central Share and Rs. 1607.30 Crore as State Share).(c) National AYUSH Mission (NAM) is being implemented through State/UT Governments for promotion and development of the AYUSH systems of the medicine. The Mission includes Ayurveda, Siddha, Sowa-Rigpa, Unani, Homoeopathy and Yoga & Naturopathy systems. (d) New AYUSH Public Health Programmes are included in the AYUSH Services component of the NAM for implementation through States/UTs which also includes AYURVIDYA: Healthy lifestyle through AYUSH for School Children. This programme is envisaged for implementation in 75000 schools for 3 years in tie up with AYUSH Health & Wellness Centers (HWCs) and AYUSH teaching hospitals. State/UT Governments may avail the eligible financial assistance by submitting the suitable proposals through their respective State Annual Action Plans (SAAPs) as per the NAM guidelines.
