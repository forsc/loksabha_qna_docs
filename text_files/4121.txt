                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3835






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.07.2019






Theft Cases in Marriage Ceremony




Janardan Singh Sigriwal














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is aware that theft cases in marriage ceremony has increased during the last two years in Delhi;(b) if so, the details thereof along with the number of such cases registered during the last two years;(c) the number of theft cases solved so far in Delhi during the said period; and(d) the steps taken by the Government in this regard so far?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) to (d):	As reported by Delhi Police, the number of theft cases in marriage ceremonies reported to them during the years 2017 and 2018 are 21 and 13 respectively, i.e. a decline of 38%.  Out of these 34 cases, 11 cases have been worked out and 21 persons have been arrested. 2.	Delhi Police has instituted a number of measures to both prevent and expeditiously detect crimes, of which major ones include, ensuring adequate police arrangements at the marriage venues on requests and during marriage seasons, sensitization and briefing of Police Station staff to be on the lookout for baggage lifters, thieves and snatchers, strategically placing Police Control Room (PCR) vans and Emergency Response Vehicles (ERV) at vulnerable places, ensuring  sufficient foot and motorcycle patrolling to cover the areas and taking appropriate legal action whenever any such crime comes to notice.******
