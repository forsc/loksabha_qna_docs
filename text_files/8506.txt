                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    STEEL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

188






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

02.12.2019






Utilisation of CSR Funds




Dhal Singh Bisen














                        Will the Minister of





STEEL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the guidelines for utilising Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) Funds;(b)	the details of the work for which the CSR amount is spent and the places where the amount is utilised;(c)	the Parliamentary constituencies coming under Nagpur Zone under which the mines of the Manganese Ore India Ltd. (MOIL) are located;(d)	the amount of CSR allocated by MOIL to the above areas from 2009 to 2019, year-wise; and(e)	the details of the CSR amount utilised for the said constituencies, work/year-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STEEL			   (SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a)to(e):  A Statement is laid on the Table of the Lok Sabha.******** STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (A) TO (E) OF THE LOK SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. *188 FOR ANSWER ON 02/12/2019 ASKED BY DR. DHAL SINGH BISEN, MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT REGARDING “UTILISATION OF CSR FUNDS”(a)&(b): The Government of India has enacted the Companies Act, 2013 in August 2013.  Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013 deals with the subject of Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) and lays down the qualifying criteria based on net worth, turnover, and net profit for companies which are required to undertake CSR.  The CSR activities are broadly listed under Schedule VII of the Companies Act, 2013, as amended from time to time. Subsequent to the said enactment, Ministry of Corporate Affairs has formulated CSR Rules under the provisions of the Act.  The Department of Public Enterprises has also issued Guidelines on Corporate Social Responsibility expenditure of Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs).  As per Section 135 of the Companies Act, 2013, Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs) under Ministry of Steel earmark at least 2% of the average net profits made during the three immediately preceding financial years for Corporate Social Responsibility(CSR) activities.  The thrust areas of the CSR activities are broadly listed in Schedule VII of the Companies Act, 2013 and inter-alia include promotion of education and health, women empowerment, sustainable income generation through Self Help Groups, assistance to divyangjans, access to water and sanitation facilities, village development, environment sustenance, sports coaching, promotion of traditional art and culture etc.  CSR activities/projects by CPSEs under Ministry of Steel are mainly carried out in and around the periphery of steel plants, steel townships and mines.    (c)	Two Parliamentary constituencies, namely Nagpur and Ramtek, come under the Nagpur District, in which MOIL mines are located.(d)	A statement showing budget allocated under CSR by MOIL, including above areas, from 2009 to 2019 is at Annexure-I.(e)	The details of the CSR amount utilized for the constituencies (Nagpur and Ramtek), work/year-wise, are at Annexure-II.********
