                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SHIPPING






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4257






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.07.2019






Capacity of Shipping Sector




 Hema Malini














                        Will the Minister of





SHIPPING


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the capacity of shipping sector has increased substantially and new avenues of employment has also been generated during the last five years;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	whether special strategic measures have been/are being taken by the Government to further increase the capacity to shipping and attract required investment; and(d)	if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                        (a) & (b)  Data on gross tonnage and number of vessels of Indian shipping fleet during the last five years is given below: <pre>Year (As on 31st March)	No. of Vessels	Gross Tonnage (GT)	% increase (GT)year on year2014-15	1210	105,06,388	0.12015-16	1273	108,58,288	3.32016-17	1316	115,61,608	6.52017-18	1384	125,81,592	8.82018-19	1407	127,88,813	1.6                                                </pre>                           Source: Directorate General of Shipping.(c) & (d) Various measures have been taken in recent years to make the shipping industry more attractive and competitive.  These are reduction in GST from 18% to 5% on bunker fuel used in Indian flag vessels; providing cargo support to  Indian shipping industry through Right of First Refusal (RoFR); allowing shipping enterprises based in India to acquire ships abroad and flag them in the country of their convenience; bringing in parity in the tax regime of Indian seafarers employed on Indian flag ships vis-à-vis those on foreign flag ships;  removal of licencing requirement for chartering of foreign registered ships by citizens of India; encouraging coastal movement of agriculture and other commodities, fertilizer,   EXIM Transshipment Containers and Empty containers etc.                                                       *****
