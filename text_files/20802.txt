                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1943






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.02.2021






Sanction for Ayurveda Doctors to Perform Medical Procedures




Raksha Nikhil Khadse














                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYURVEDA, YOGA AND NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY (AYUSH) be pleased to state:(a) whether the Government proposes to mandate the basic qualification of BAMS for Ayurveda doctors similar to MS surgery or MS ENT in Allopathic system in view of recent announcement by Central Council for Indian Medicine (CCIM);(b) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor;(c) whether the Government has mandated BAMS Doctor to mention the Degree so obtained by them on their display boards at clinics/hospitals so as to apprise the patient undergoing surgery that the medical practitioner is qualified for Ayurveda and not from the ConventionalModern Medicine Practitioner (Allopathy); and(d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS AND ADDITIONAL CHARGE OF MINISTER OF STATE (IC) OF THE MINISTRY OF AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(SHRI KIREN RIJIJU)(a) & (b):	No, Government has not allowed to any Ayurveda Doctor having just BAMS qualification to perform surgical procedure so notified vide Indian Medicine Central Council (Post Graduate Ayurveda Education) Regulations, 2016, dated 19th November, 2020. Only PG scholars of Shalya (General Surgery) and Shalakya (Diseases of Eye, Ear, Nose, Throat, Head, Oro-Dentistry) Departments are allowed to perform such surgical procedures as it is part of their curriculum. (c) & (d): As per Practitioners of Indian Medicine (Standards of Professional Conduct, Etiquette and Code of Ethics) Regulations, 1982,  it shall be compulsory for a practitioner of Indian medicine to affix the correct degree or diploma before or after his name.
