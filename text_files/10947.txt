                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4368






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

13.12.2019






Pruning of Trees along with Roads




Vijay Kumar














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has any scheme for timely pruning of trees along the roads to prevent road accidents; (b) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor; (c) whether Government proposes to plant trees at some distance from the roads to prevent road accidents; and (d) if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE(SHRI BABUL SUPRIYO)(a) to (b) Since forest and trees are managed by the States/Union Territories, responsibility of day-to-day maintenance including pruning of trees along the state highways and other roads lies with the respective State/UT Government. For the national highways, pruning of road-side trees is being carried out as per the IRC Guidelines.(c)to (d) Green Highways (Plantation & Maintenance) Policy, 2015 of Ministry of Road, Transport and Highways provides a policy framework for plantation along roads. It provides for plantation of adequate space keeping in mind safety hazard as well as natural growth of the plants.The policy provides for various plantation schemes viz. tree planting along highways and median plantation.  Small height trees are planted in first row with adequate spacing and 3m away from the toe of embankment.***
