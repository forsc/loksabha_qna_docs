                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    JAL SHAKTI






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2261






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.07.2019






Water Sharing with Pakistan and Bangladesh




Nama Nageswara Rao














                        Will the Minister of





JAL SHAKTI


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there is any proposal for sharing of water among India, Bangladesh and Pakistan; (b) if so, the details thereof; and (c) if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR JAL SHAKTI & SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(SHRI RATTAN LAL KATARIA)(a) to (c) India and Bangladesh are currently sharing the waters of river Ganga at Farakka, as per the Ganges Water Treaty of 1996. Both sides are currently engaged in analysing the river data pertaining to trans-boundary rivers namely, Teesta, Dharla, Dudhkumar, Feni, Manu, Muhuri, Khowai and Gumti.The use of waters of Indus river basin is governed by the Indus Water Treaty signed between India and Pakistan in 1960. There is no new proposal under consideration with regard to water sharing between India and Pakistan. *****
