                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

75






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2021






Forest Cover




Uttam Kumar Nalamada Reddy














                        Will the Minister of





ENVIRONMENT,  FORESTS AND CLIMATE CHANGE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the total area currently under forest cover in the country along with its percentage share out of the total geographical area;(b)	the details of forest area that is covered by monoculture plantations and plantations with only five or less species;(c)	 the details of natural forest land diverted for industrial use during the last five years along with the funds allocated to each State for the purpose of compensatory afforestation during the said period, State/UT-wise;(d)	the area of land covered under afforestation programmes along with the area having mono culture plantations and plantations with five or less species during the last five years, State/UT-wise; and(e)	the steps taken/proposed to be taken by the Government to make compensatory afforestation plantations more bio-diverse?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER FOR ENVIRONMENT, FOREST AND CLIMATE CHANGE  (SHRI BHUPENDER YADAV)(a) to (e) The statement is laid on the table of the House.***Statement refer to in reply to part (a) to (e) of Lok Sabha Starred Question No. 75 due for reply on 23.07.2021 regarding Forest Cover by Shri Uttam Kumar Reddy Nalamada.(a)	Forest Survey of India (FSI), Dehradun, an organization under the Ministry carries out the assessment of forest cover of the country biennially since 1987 and the findings are published in India State of Forest Report (ISFR). As per ISFR 2019, the total forest and tree cover of the country is 8,07,276 square kilometers which is 24.56 % of the geographical area of the country. (b)	The Ministry does not collect information on monoculture plantations or plantations with five or less species. The information regarding area covered under monoculture plantations and plantations with five or less species, as per details provided by respective State/UT Forest Departments is given in Annexure-I.(c)	During the last five years, a total of 275.12 hectare forest land has been diverted under the category of industry.   The State/UT wise funds disbursed by the Ad-hoc CAMPA during the year 2016-17 to 2019-20 is given in Annexure-II. After coming into force of the Compensatory Afforestation Fund (CAF) Act, 2016 with effect from 30.09.2018, an accumulated amount of ? 48,477.77 crore has been disbursed by the National Authority between 29.08.2019 to 31.01.2021 to 31 States/UTs who have established the State Compensatory Afforestation Fund (State Fund) under public accounts of such States and reconciled the amount head wise and component wise. The details are given in Annexure-III& IV. The above includes funds for the purpose of Compensatory Afforestation also.(d)	The area covered under various afforestation and tree planting programmes in the country, State/UT-wise, during last 5 years is given in Annexure-V. The Ministry does not collect information on monoculture plantations and plantations having five or less species on monthly/annual basis.(e)	As per provisions of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, Forest Conservation Rules and Guidelines, as far as practicable, a mixture of local indigenous species are planted and monoculture of the species is avoided.  The States are also taking plantation of local species in the areas identified for compensatory afforestation for ensuring the biodiversity in these areas. *****
