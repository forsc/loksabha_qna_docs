                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

598






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

04.02.2021






ARHCs in Maharashtra




Balubhau (Alias Suresh Narayan) Dhanorkar














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of Government buildings which are being used under the Affordable Rental Housing Complexes (ARHCs) scheme for migrants workers/ urban poor in the cities of Maharashtra;(b)	the number of new ARHCs whose construction have been started in Maharashtra;(c)	the number of migrant workers/urban poor residing in each of the complex in the city of Maharashtra; and(d)	the steps being taken to ensure that adequate social distancing is followed in such complexes to prevent the spread of COVID-19?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) OF THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                                (SHRI HARDEEP SINGH PURI)(a)	to (d): Scheme for Affordable Rental Housing Complexes (ARHCs), a sub-scheme under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana - Urban (PMAY-U), for providing accommodation at affordable rent to urban migrants/poor has been launched on 31.07.2020. States/Union Territories (UTs)/Urban Local Bodies (ULBs) will select the Concessionaire through Request for Proposal (RFP) for developing existing Government funded vacant housing complexes to be used as ARHCs for a period of 25 years under Repair/Retrofit, Develop, Operate and Transfer (RDOT) on  Public Private Partnership (PPP) Mode under Model-1. States/ UTs including State of Maharashtra are currently identifying existing vacant Government buildings which are readily available for converting into ARHCs after making them livable under Model-1.Under Model-2, Expression of Interest (EoI) has been issued to invite applications from Public/Private Entities to construct, operate and maintain ARHCs on their own available vacant land. The last date of submitting application is March 11, 2021 after which ARHCs will be constructed.                                               *****
