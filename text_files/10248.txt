                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    COAL






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3749






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.12.2019






Rely on Coal for Power Generation




Gaurav Gogoi














                        Will the Minister of





COAL


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether there is rise in the use of coal over the past five years and if so, the details thereof;(b) the extent to which India relies on coal for its power and total electricity generation; and(c) whether India’s continuous use of coal is likely to hamper the country’s fight against climate change and if so, the details thereof and the views of the Government  thereto ?






ANSWER




 MINISTER OF PARLIAMENTARY AFFAIRS, COAL AND MINES (SHRI PRALHAD JOSHI) (a) & (b): Central Electricity Authority (CEA) has informed that the total coal consumption (domestic & imported) by coal based thermal power plants, its generation and total generation in the country during last five years are as under:Year	Coal consumption  (in Million Tonnes) by Coal based power plants	Generation (in Billion Units)	Coal based generation out of total generation (in %)	Domestic Coal	Imported Coal	Total	Total (#)	Coal based	2014-15	439.2	91.2	530.4	1048.7	800.3	762015-16	465.3	80.6	545.9	1107.8	862.0	782016-17	508.2	66.1	574.3	1160.1	910.1	782017-18	551.1	56.9	608.0	1206.3	951.8	792018-19	567.0	61.9	628.9	1249.3	987.7	79[# Total generation from fuel sources (Thermal, Hydro and Nuclear stations of 25 MW and above only) and import from Bhutan] (c):  Government has set a target of installing 175 GW of renewable energy capacity by the year 2022 to comply the commitment of reduction of Carbon dioxide. ****
