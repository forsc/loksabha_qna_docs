                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3308






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

12.07.2019






Strike by Doctors




Poonamben Hematbhai Maadam













                                                    Su Thirunavukkarasar
                                                








                                                    Deepak Baij
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the details of the strikes held in Central Government hospitals and the number out of them caused by altercation between attendants and doctors during the last five years;(b) whether the Union Government has taken note of nation-wide agitation and strike by doctors in Government hospitals resulting in suffering and death of many patients due to non-availability of timely medical treatment recently, if so, the details thereof and the number of patients who died due to strike in Government hospitals in various parts of the country;(c) whether the Union Government has found particularly insensitive approach by the State Governments towards the agitating doctors, if so, the response of the Government thereto;(d) whether the Union Government has taken any action to halt the doctors strike including intervention to bring the situation under control in certain States and issue instruction to take action on doctor’s demands, if so, the details thereof; and(e) the other steps taken by the Union Government to ensure that no strike is held causing inconvenience/suffering to the patients in the future?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a) The details of strikes reported (in days) in Central Government hospitals during last five years is as under:-  	2015	2016	2017	2018	2019(till date)Safdarjung Hospital	NotAvailable	01	01	02	04LHMC & Associated Hospitals	01	01	Nil	Nil	01Dr. RML Hospital 	Nil	02	Nil	Nil	02Number of the strikes (in days) caused by altercation between attendants and doctors during last five years are as under:-	2015	2016	2017	2018	2019(till date)Safdarjung Hospital	NotAvailable	Nil	Nil	Nil	04LHMC & Associated Hospitals	Nil	01	Nil	Nil	01Dr. RML Hospital 	Nil	02	Nil	Nil	02(b)  As far as three Central Government Hospitals namely Safdarjung, Dr. RML and LHMC & Associated hospitals are concerned, emergency services of the hospital were not affected during strike. All patients attending the Emergency were seen and provided with treatment. No patient died due to non-availability of timely medical treatment due to strike recently.(c) to (e)   Since Health is a state subject, no such information is maintained centrally. However, Union Government from time to time requests State Government to take appropriate measures to ensure safety and protection of doctors on duty in the hospitals.       The   following   steps  have   been   taken   by   the   Government/hospitals   to   ensure that no strike is held causing inconvenience/suffering to the patients in future:i.	Security has been strengthened by increasing the number of security personnel, formation of Quick Response Teams (QRTs), installation of CCTV cameras etc.,  to protect doctors/staffs from any incidence of manhandling;ii.	Training to doctors in soft skills for handling patients in critical and emergency situations;iii.	Circulars  are  issued  from  time  to  time  by  the  hospital  authorities  sensitizing  the doctors/staffs  to  refrain  from  any  such  activity  which  would  disrupt  the  patient  care service in these hospitals;
