                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1163






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2020






Contractual Teachers in Government Schools and Kendriya Vidyalayas




Sunil Dattatray Tatkare













                                                    Subhash Ramrao Bhamre
                                                








                                                    DNV Senthilkumar. S.
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of ad-hoc/contractual teachers employed in Government schools and KendriyaVidyalayas during each of the last three years, State/UT-wise;(b) whether the Government has adopted any criteria for appointment of these adhoc/contractual teachers and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether these teachers who are not highly experienced are deployed to teach in higherclasses affecting career of students and if so, the corrective steps taken by the Government inthis regard;(d) whether the Government is aware that thousands of teachers working on ad-hoc/contractbasis in the country are reportedly not getting even minimum wages and if so, the detailsthereof and the reaction of the Government thereto;(e) whether the Government plans to extend basic facility and social security to these ad-hocteachers and if so, the details thereof; and(f) the measures taken/proposed to be taken by the Government to streamline the system andensure that at least minimum wages are paid to all contractual and ad-hoc teachers across thecountry?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)(a) to (f): The number of adhoc/contractual teachers in Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan (KVS)employed during the last three years is at Annexure. Part time teachers in KVS are engaged asper the eligibility criteria laid down by the KVS against existing/leave vacancy and on day today basis. The remuneration of part time teachers is regulated according to therecommendation of the Finance Committee duly approved by Board of Governors in its 94thmeeting held in December 2012.Further, education is in the concurrent list of the Constitution and majority of theschools come under the jurisdiction of the respective States and Union Territory (UT)Government which is the appropriate Government under the Right of Children to Free andCompulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009. The recruitment, service conditions and deploymentof teachers and other staff come under the purview of the concerned State/UT Government.Ministry of Human Resource Development has been requesting all the States and UTGovernments for filling-up the vacant posts of teachers and their rational deployment, forwhich the Ministry has been issuing advisories to all the States and UTs from time to time.Further, the Central Government, through the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of SamagraShiksha, provides assistance to the States and UTs for deployment of additional teachers tomaintain appropriate Pupil Teacher Ratio as per the prescribed norms for various levels ofschooling.
