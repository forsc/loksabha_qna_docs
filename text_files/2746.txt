                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2555






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.07.2019






Digital Modernization of Police Stations




Bhagwanth Khuba














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government is upgrading or carrying out Digital modernization of police stations in the country;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government has allocated special funds for developing the technology; and(d) if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI G. KISHAN REDDY)(a) & (b) : The responsibility of equipping the State Police Forces lies with the State Governments by virtue of ‘Police’ being a State subject.  However, Government of India supplements the efforts of State Governments by providing central assistance under the scheme of ‘Assistance to States for Modernisation of Police’ to strengthen police infrastructure. Under this scheme, the States have been provided central assistance for acquisition of latest weaponry, training gadgets, advanced communication and forensic equipment etc. including I.T. systems.--2--L.S.US.Q.NO. 2555 FOR 09.07.2019          Under the Crime and Criminal Tracking Network and Systems (CCTNS), the Central Government is interlinking Police Stations throughout the country for the purpose of digitization of crime and criminal records in Police Stations. The project envisages establishment of a national database by collecting information from Police Stations in an electronic eco-system. (c) & (d) : An amount of Rs.1680.72 crores has been released to States & UTs till 31st March, 2018 for implementation of the CCTNS project.****
