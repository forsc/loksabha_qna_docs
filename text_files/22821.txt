                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3820






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

18.03.2021






Waiting time at Toll Plazas




Shriniwas Dadasaheb Patil














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether any study has been undertaken regarding the average time spent by the vehicles in queue at each toll plaza on Asian Highway-47 (Old NH-4) stretch from Pune  to  Kagal;(b)  if so,  the  details  thereof;(c) if not, whether the Government proposes to undertake such a study and implement measures to minimize waiting time in long queues at toll plazas thereby minimizing the huge losses of time and fuel; and(d) the list of villages exempted from toll at Tasawade and Anewadi toll  plazas on AH-47 in Satara district of Maharashtra?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) & (b) A Control and Command Centre has been established at Headquarters of National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) where monitoring is being done by live Closed-Circuit Television (CCTV) cameras. Also, Global Positioning System (GPS) based software is being used to monitor wait time at fee plazas.(c) In order to promote digital fee collection, Government has declared all lanes of fee plazas on National Highways as FASTag Lane of fee plazas w.e.f. midnight of 15th/16th February 2021, which has resulted in reduced waiting time and minimized queues at fee plazas. (d) As per Fee Notification S.O. 647(E) dated 05.05.2005 published in Gazette of India for Taswade fee plaza, there is no provision of exemption in fee to nearby villages.In case of Anewadi fee plaza, as per the National Highways Fee (Determination of Rates and Collection) Rules, 2008, there is a provision of monthly pass for a person owning a non-commercial vehicle and residing within 20 kilometers from the fee plaza, provided a service road or alternative road is not available.*****
