                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1898






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

03.03.2020






Improving Organic Farming




Gaurav Gogoi














                        Will the Minister of





AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Government has any programmes or plans in place to improve organic farming in India;(b) 	if so, the details thereof; (c) 	whether the Government maintains data concerning the total number of farmers adopting organic farming practices in the country; and (d) 	if so, the details thereof, State/UT-wise?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF AGRICULTURE AND FARMERS WELFARE???? ??? ????? ?????? ??????     (SHRI NARENDRA SINGH TOMAR)(a) to (d):	Government of India has been promoting organic farming under two dedicated schemes namely Mission Organic Value Chain Development North Eastern Region (MOVCDNER) and Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) since 2015 through State Governments. Under these schemes, support is  provided for formation of farmers’ clusters/ Farmer Producer Organization; input procurement, value addition including post harvest infrastructure creation, packaging, branding, publicity, transportation, organic fairs etc. Organic Farming is  also  supported under other schemes viz Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) and Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH), Network Project on Organic Farming under ICAR. Third party certification of organic farming is promoted by Agriculture Processed Food and Export Development Authority (APEDA), Ministry of Commerce.The data of farmers practicing organic farming under Participatory Guarantee System (PGS) and 3rd party certification are registered on PGS- India Portal and TRACENET portal respectively. The state-wise detail of farmers registered on both portals is provided at Annexure-I.
