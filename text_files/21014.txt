                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2186






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.03.2021






Credit to MSMEs




Hemant Tukaram Godse














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has recently issued some guidelines to public sector banks to analyse credit issue with regard to Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has advised any monitoring mechanism in this regard and if so, the details thereof; and (c) the success of MSMEs in the country with special reference to Maharashtra? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF FINANCE(SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR)(a) to (b) Government had launched a ‘Support and Outreach programme for Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs)’ on 2nd November 2018. The programme inter-alia, included initiatives for MSMEs on access to credit, access to market, technology upgradation, ease of doing business, social security for MSME sector employees etc.To further consolidate the gains of the campaign, Government advised Public Sector Banks (PSBs) to designate a Chief General Manager / General Manager level officer to do in-depth analysis of issues in availability of credit faced by MSMEs under the supervision of Executive Director (ED) in charge of MSME financing of the bank. Government has taken various steps towards effective implementation in this regard which inter-alia include nomination of nodal officers, video conferences to monitor the progress, weekly reporting on specified format etc.(c) As per data compiled by Reserve Bank of India (RBI), outstanding credit of Scheduled Commercial Banks (SCBs) to Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) during the last three Financial Years (FYs) has grown steadily. Outstanding credit to MSMEs by SCBs all over India and in the State of Maharashtra is given below: Outstanding credit amount to MSMEs (in Rs crore)	As on March 31, 2018	As on March 31, 2019	As on March 31, 2020All over India	13,24,239.34	15,10,650.52	16,13,582.17In State of Maharashtra	2,35,191.58	2,62,887.01	3,03,670.86 ‘*****’
