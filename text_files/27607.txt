                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    Education






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3295






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.08.2021






Promotion of Indic Learning




Prathap Simha














                        Will the Minister of





Education


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the measures taken by the Government to promote Indic learning, higher educationin Indic subjects and study of Indian languages;(b) the steps taken or proposed to be taken by the Government to promote Sanskritlearning and Sanskrit languague;(c) the number of Sanskrit Universities receiving assistance under the Adarsh SanskritMahavidyalayas/ Adarsh Shodha Sansthans along with the quantum of suchassistance;(d) whether the Government is reviewing new applications for assistance under theAdarsh Sanskrit Mahavidyalayas/Adarsh Shodha Sansthans scheme;(e) if so, the details thereof and the number of such applications and status thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF EDUCATION(SHRI DHARMENDRA PRADHAN)(a) & (b) Indian Languages are taught in almost all of the Higher Education Institutions.UGC as a National Coordinator for Non-Technology Massive Open Online Courses(MOOCs) for SWAYAM Platform developed many MOOCs courses under Sanskrit, Hindi,and Jyotish Ganit. It has also developed e-content covering entire syllabus at PostGraduate level for the Sanskrit, Hindi, and Jyotish Ganit Under the e-PG Pathshala Projectof NME-ICT, Ministry of Education.Central Institute of Indian Languages (CIIL), Mysore works on promotion of allIndian Languages including Scheduled/non-Scheduled and Classical Languages. Hindi ispromoted by Kendriya Hindi Sansthan (KHS), Agra, Central Hindi Directorate (CHD), NewDelhi and Commission for Scientific and Technical Terminology (CSTT), New Delhi.2Further, there are three Central Universities namely Shri Lal Bahadur ShastriNational Sanskrit University, National Sanskrit University Tirupati and Central SanskritUniversity, Delhi for teaching and research in Sanskrit, to develop all-inclusive Sanskritpromotional activities and to provide for matters connected therewith or incidentalthereto. In addition to this there are 17 Central Universities which are having SanskritDepartments. Central Sanskrit University has twelve campuses spread across the country.Besides its 12 constituent Campuses, CSU manages 26 Adarsh SanskritMahavidyalaya/Adarsh Shodha Sansthan (ASM/ASS) recognized under Central Schemesand University has also granted affiliation to 58 Sanskrit institutions throughout thecountry for its courses of studies. One of the main objectives of the Central SanskritUniversity, Delhi is to promote and propagate Sanskrit learning, teaching and research.(c) Adarsh Sanskrit Mahavidyalayas/ Adarsh Shodha Sansthans are institutions which areprovided funds under the Scheme for Financial Assistance to Institutions recognized asAdarsh Sanskrit Mahavidyalaya/ Adarsh Shodha Sansthan which is implemented byCentral Sanskrit University (CSU), New Delhi. At present, there are 26 such institutions.These institutions are provided financial assistance to the extent of 95% of theadmissible expenditure for recurring item and to the extent of 75% for non-recurringitems subject to the maximum of Rs. 5 lakh per annum relating to development ofinfrastructure, as per applicable scheme. The Details of the Adarsh SanskritMahavidyalayas/Adarsh Shodha Sansthans and grant released during the Financial Year2020-21 are furnished in Annexure-I.(d) & (e) The new applications received under the Scheme for Financial Assistance toInstitutions recognized as Adarsh Sanskrit Mahavidyalaya/ Adarsh Shodha Sansthan areplaced in the meeting of the Grant-in-Aid Committee of the Central Sanskrit University(CSU), New Delhi. Based on the conditions of the Scheme, the applications areconsidered on their merit. So far, 28 new applications are received in CSU which areunder examination.*****
