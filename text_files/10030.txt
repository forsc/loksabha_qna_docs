                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

307






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.12.2019






Specially Abled  Persons




Maneka Sanjay Gandhi














                        Will the Minister of





SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the number of specially abled persons in the country, State/UT and disability-wise;(b)	the details of the schemes being implemented by the Government for empowerment of such persons and the results thereof during the last two years, State and UT-wise;(c)	the funds allocated and released under various schemes during the said period;(d)	whether the Government proposes to raise the percentage of reservation for the specially abled persons; and(e)	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND EMPOWERMENT(DR. THAARWARCHAND GEHLOT)(a)	to (e) A statement is laid on the Table of the House. Statement referred in reply to part (a) to (e) of Lok Sabha Starred Question No. 307 for answer on 10.12.2019 regarding “Specially Abled Persons” raised by Shrimati Maneka Sanjay Gandhi.(a)	As per Census 2011, there are 2,68,14,994 persons with disabilities (PwDs) in the country. Details of the number of specially abled persons in the country, State/UT and disability-wise is enclosed as Annexure I.(b)	Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities implements the following major schemes for the welfare of Persons with Disabilities (PwDs):1.	Deendayal Disabled Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS),2.	Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids/Appliances Scheme (ADIP),3.	Scheme for Implementation of Right of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 (SIPDA) with the following broad components:(i) Creation of barrier free environment for Persons with Disabilities,(ii) National Action Plan for Skill Development of Persons with Disabilities (PwDs),(iii) Accessible India Campaign,(iv) Establishment of District Disability Rehabilitation Centre (DDRC) in the Identified Districts, and(v) Unique Disability Identification (UDID) Project.4.	Scholarships for Students with Disabilities, and5.	The National Trust for the Welfare of Persons with Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Mental Retardation and Multiple Disabilities (National Trust).Details of the schemes and results thereof during last two years is enclosed as Annexure-II.(c)	Details of the funds allocated and released under various schemes during the last two year is enclosed as Annexure III.(d)	and (e) The Government through the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 has increased the percentage of reservation in Government Jobs from 3% to 4% and  from 3% to 5% in seats in Government/ Government-Aided higher educational institutions for persons with benchmark disabilities. *****
