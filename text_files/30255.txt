                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1997






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2021






Storm Water Drainage System under AMRUT




Gaurav Gogoi














                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban 	Transformation (AMRUT-II) has attained its aim of increasing 	the 	amenity value of 500 cities under it and if so, the details 	thereof;(b) 	whether the mission has succeeded in establishing its third 	mission component, namely storm water drainage, in all the 	500 	cities under it and if so, the details thereof;(c) 	whether the Government can provide the details of 	implementation 	of storm water drainage systems covered 	under AMRUT, especially 	in all capital cities/ towns of 	States/ UTs and if so, the details thereof;(d) 	whether the Government can provide the details of all capital 	cities/towns of States/UTs that have faced flooding since 2015 	where storm water drainage systems under AMRUT have been 	constructed and if so, the details thereof;(e) 	the percentage of the total construction completed so far; and(f) 	whether the mission would be completed at the end of its 	extension in 2022 and if so, the details thereof? 






ANSWER




                                                                 THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                      (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a):  	The Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation (AMRUT) was launched on 25 June 2015 in selected 500 cities and towns across all the States/ Union Territories, with focus on development of basic infrastructure in the sectors of water supply; sewerage and septage management; storm water drainage; green spaces and parks; and non-motorized urban transport. A set of reforms in urban management and capacity building of urban functionaries are also part of the Mission.	The achievements of the Mission so far are detailed as under:(i)	Against the target of providing 139 lakh water tap connections to achieve universal water supply in 500 AMRUT cities, 114 lakh tap connections have been provided under the Mission or in convergence with other Schemes.(ii)	Against the target of providing 145 lakh sewer connection to achieve substantial improvement in sewerage and septage management in 500 AMRUT cities, 87 lakh sewer connections (including households covered through Faecal Sludge and Septage Management) have been provided under the Mission or in convergence with other Schemes.(iii)	More than 1,800 MLD (million litre per day) of Sewerage Treatment Plant (STP) capacity have been created under AMRUT and another 4,200 MLD treatment capacity is under development. Waste water reuse/recycle capacity of 907 MLD have been created.(iv)	2,249 water-logging points have been eliminated so far, and 951 water-logging points are likely to be eliminated on completion of ongoing projects. (v)	Around 3,900 acres of green spaces and parks have been developed and another 1,500 acres of green space and parks are under development.(vi)	Around 47 kilometer cycling track, 2 multilevel parking and 200 kilometer of footpath /walkways have been constructed.(b) to (e):  Out of the ?77,640 crore total approved plan size of AMRUT projects of all the States/UTs, ?2,969 crore (4%) have been allocated to Storm Water Drainage sector. Against this allocation, 19 States/UTs have taken up 801 projects worth ?2,957 across 105 cities, which include some of the capital cities. Of these, 612 projects worth ?1,114 crore have been completed, 187 projects worth ?1,829 crore are under implementation and two projects worth ?14 crore are at tendering stage. 	The storm water drainage projects have been taken up by the States/ UTs in those cities which have been facing water logging problems. City-wise progress of implementation of storm water drainage projects is given at the ANNEXURE.	Overall, works worth ?2,158 crore, i.e. 73% of the works, have been completed physically.(f): 	AMRUT Mission was extended beyond 31stMarch 2021 and has been subsumed with AMRUT 2.0 after its launch on 1stOctober 2021. In accordance with the operational guidelines of AMRUT 2.0, ongoing AMRUT projects will be funded with central assistance up to 31stMarch 2023.                                 ******
