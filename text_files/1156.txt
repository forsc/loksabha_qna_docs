                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1018






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.06.2019






Holistic Development of Road Infrastructure




Raksha Nikhil Khadse














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has increased infrastructure spending and funding for road constructions in the country and if so, the details thereof;(b) whether a significant growth has been noticed in the number of highway construction projects as a result of the same and if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government has taken any steps to ensure safety of road users, workers as well as environment protection and if so, the details thereof; and(d) the schemes formulated and implemented for developing and growing for the tree plantation across the highways?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a)	The Ministry is primarily responsible for development and maintenance of National Highways (NHs). Details of fund allocated for development of NHs and expenditure incurred / fund released during last 5 years are as follows:- Amount in Rs. croreSr. No.	Year	Allocation	Expenditure / Fund Released 		Budgetary	IEBR * Target to raise	Total	Budgetary	IEBR* Actual raised	Total1	2014-15	24,707.96	7,611.11	32,319.07	24,417.62	3,344.00	27,761.622	2015-16	40,970.42	42,694.50	83,664.92	40,560.15	23,281.00	63,841.153	2016-17	41,431.17	59,279.00	1,00,710.17	40,654.02	33,118.00	73,772.024	2017-18	50,218.65	59,279.00	1,09,497.65	49,664.60	50,533.00	1,00,197.605	2018-19	68,110.72	62,000.00	1,30,110.72	66,803.86	61,217.00	1,28,020.86* Internal and Extra Budgetary ResourcesApart from this, the Ministry also allocates funds for development of State Roads under the Central Road Fund (CRF) Scheme and Economic Importance and Inter State Connectivity (EI&ISC) Schemes as per the provisions of the CRF Act, 2000 amended by the Finance Act from time to time. Year wise details of accrual/allocation of funds for States / UTs and expenditure/release of funds under CRF and EI and ISC schemes during last five years are as follows:- Amount in Rs. croreYear	CRF(State Roads)	EI&ISC	Accrual	Release	Fund Allocated	Expenditure incurred2014-15	2,635.60	2,094.78	150.00	93.112015-16	2,851.80	2,369.47	355.35	267.092016-17	7,175.00	5,069.82	805.00	746.352017-18	6,764.48	6,367.11	759.20	677.562018-19	6,998.93	6,784.50	500.00	474.82(b)	Length of NHs developed / constructed during last five years were as follows.Year	2014-15	2015-16	2016-17	2017-18	2018-19Length (km)	4,410	6,061	8,231	9,829	10,855(c)	NHs are designed and constructed as per Indian Roads Congress Codes and the Ministry’s relevant Guidelines / Policies. Road Safety measures and provisions are part of the design and construction of NHs. Road Safety audits are also carried out as part of NH development projects on Engineering Procurement Construction (EPC) / Build Operate Transfer (BOT) modes. Identification and rectification of road accident black spots, improvements on safety on NHs through Road Safety Annual Plan, taking up of Road Safety audits, installation of crash barriers at accident prone locations in Hilly terrain etc, are taken up to minimize accidents on NHs.	Safety of Road Construction Workers and Environmental Protection are also part of design and construction of NHs and these are implemented as per the relevant policies / guidelines and provisions of the Contract / Concession Agreements.(d)	The Ministry has promulgated the Green Highways (Plantation, Transplantation, Beautification & Maintenance) Policy, 2015 with regard to planting of trees in place of trees cut due to widening of NHs as per the Statutory conditions by different agencies. Planting of trees in lieu of the cut trees is generally done after substantial completion of Civil construction works to avoid damage to the planted saplings. Like tree cutting, planting is done by different agencies, including the Forest Department, depending upon the requirements of individual project and contractual conditions.*****
