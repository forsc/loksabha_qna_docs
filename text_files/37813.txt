                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4878






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

01.04.2022






Unani/Siddha Medicine Centres




Rajveer (Raju Bhaiya) Singh













                                                    Sukanta Majumdar
                                                








                                                    Jayanta Kumar Roy
                                                








                                                    Sangeeta Kumari Singh Deo
                                                








                                                    Bhola Singh
                                                








                                                    Vinod Kumar Sonkar
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















(a)	the number of Unani/Siddha Centres functioning in the country, State/UT-wise and location-wise;  (b)	the estimated number of patients getting treatment in these centres and the total amount of funds allocated/ spent during each of the last three years and the current year, State/UT-wise;(c)	whether the Government has received proposals from various States for opening of Unani/ Siddha medicine centres and if so, the details thereof;(d)	whether the Government has not been able to popularize Unani/Siddha system of medicine in the country compared to other traditional medicines; and(e)	if so, the details thereof and the reasons therefor along with the steps being taken by the Government to promote and expand the network of Unani/Siddha system of medicine?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF AYUSH(SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a)	The number of Unani, Siddha Hospitals and Dispensaries functioning in the country, State/UT-wiseas per Ministry of Ayush’s Annual Statistical Publication - “AYUSH IN INDIA-2020” is furnished at Annexure-I.(b)	& (c) Public Health being a State subject, opening of Unani, Siddha medicine centresand maintaining the record of patients getting treatment in these centres come under the purview of respective State/UT Government. However, under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM), financial assistance is being provided for setting up of up to 50 /30 /10 bedded integrated AYUSH Hospitals for providing treatment through different systems of AYUSH Medicines. The State/UT Governments may avail the financial assistance by submitting the suitable proposals through State Annual Action Plan (SAAP) as per the provision of NAM guidelines.Further, under NAM, Ministry of Ayush has approved 104 units of upto 50 Bedded Integrated AYUSH Hospitals in the country as per the proposal received from them. The State/UT wise list of approved upto 50 Bedded Integrated AYUSH Hospitals is furnished at Annexure-II.(d)	& (e): Ministry of Ayush is implementing Centrally Sponsored Scheme of National AYUSH Mission (NAM) in the country for promotion and development of AYUSH system including Unani and Siddha through States/UTs. NAM inter-alia makes provision for the following to promote and expand the network of the AYUSH system of medicine including Unani and Siddha. i.	AYUSH Health & Wellness Centres under Ayushman Bharat.ii.	Co-location of AYUSH facilities at Primary Health Centres (PHCs), Community Health Centres (CHCs) and District Hospitals (DHs). iii.	Upgradation of existing standalone Government AYUSH Hospitals.iv.	Upgradation of existing/setting up of new standalone Government/ Panchayat/Government aided AYUSH Dispensaries.v.	Setting up of up to 50 /30 /10 bedded integrated AYUSH Hospitals.vi.	Supply of essential drugs to Government AYUSH Hospitals, Government Dispensaries and Government/Government aided Teaching Institutional AYUSH Hospitals. vii.	AYUSH Public Health Programmes.viii.	Infrastructural development of State Government Undergraduate and Postgraduate Educational Institutions.ix.	Establishment of new AYUSH Colleges in the States where availability of AYUSH teaching institutions is inadequate in Government Sector.x.	Behaviour Change Communication (BCC)/Information Education and Communication (IEC).xi.	AYUSH Gram.
