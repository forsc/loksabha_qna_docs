                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    TOURISM






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3357






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

09.12.2019






Amount Spent in Backward Tourist Areas




Ashok Kumar Rawat














                        Will the Minister of





TOURISM


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether the places of tourist interest particularly in the backward areas of the country are being neglected and are not being looked after properly; (b) 	if so, the reaction of the Government thereto; (c) 	the expenditure incurred for tourism development during the last three years till date, State-wise; (d) 	the expenditure targetted to be incurred for the promotion of tourism during the current financial year, State-wise; and (e) 	the steps taken/proposed to be taken by the Union Government to allure domestic and foreign tourists?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE FOR TOURISM (INDEPENDENT CHARGE)(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a) to (e):	Ministry of Tourism launched Swadesh Darshan and PRASHAD Schemes on pan India basis including backward areas for development of theme based circuits and infrastructure at pilgrimage and Heritage sites respectively.State-wise list of projects sanctioned under the Swadesh Darshan & PRASHAD Scheme during the last three years is annexed. Ministry of Tourism promotes India as a holistic destination including its various tourism products in both international & domestic markets through its ongoing schemes. Ministry of Tourism undertakes promotion activities in domestic and international markets under the schemes Domestic Promotion & Publicity including Hospitality and Overseas Promotion & Publicity Including Market Development Assistance. The budget allocation during the current financial year under the above schemes at Budget Estimate (BE) stage is: (Rs. in Crore)<pre>Name of the Scheme	BE 2019-20Domestic Promotion & Publicity including Hospitality	129.50Overseas Promotion & Publicity Including Market Development Assistance	446.42</pre>	Several initiatives/programmes taken to attract domestic and foreign tourists are: i.	Ministry of Tourism has identified 17 Iconic Sites for in the country by seeking convergence with other Central Ministries/Departments/State Governments /Urban Local Bodies ii.	Launched the Adopt a Heritage Project for development and maintenance of tourist amenities at heritage sites/monuments and other tourist sites.iii.	Development and promotion of ‘Niche Tourism’ products to attract tourist with specific interest and to ensure repeat visits for the unique products in which India has a comparative advantage.iv.	Launched 24x7 toll free Multi-Lingual Tourist Helpline (1363).v.	Promoting India as a holistic tourism destination including its various tourism sites and products through Incredible India 2.0 Campaign.vi.	Providing facility of e-Visa for 5 sub-categories i.e. e-Tourist visa, e-Business visa, e-Medical visa, e-Medical Attendant visa and e-Conference visa for nationals of 169 countries.vii.	Revamp of Incredible India Website having information on tourism sites/destinations in different States/Union Territory Administrations.viii.	Launch of the Incredible India Tourist Facilitator Programme on an online digital platform to provide basic, advanced and refresher courses for tourist facilitators.ix.	E-Visa has been further liberalized and the visa fee has been substantially reduced.x.	New mountain peaks have been opened for Mountaineering/Trekking to give boost to adventure tourism in the country.xi.	Lowering of GST on hotels rooms with tariffs of ?1,001 to ?7,500/night to 12% and those above ?7,501 to 18% to increase India’s competitiveness as a tourism destination.********
