                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3573






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.07.2019






Survey on Cost of Medical Courses




Lavu Sri Krishna Devarayalu














                        Will the Minister of





HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has conducted a study/survey to assess if meritorious studentsfrom middle class socio-economic background can afford to pursue undergraduate andpostgraduate medical courses, if so, the details thereof;(b) whether the Government has any proposal to implement a policy to bring down the cost ofundergraduate and postgraduate medical courses in the country, if so, the details thereof; and(c) whether the Government is proposing to relax the requirements for establishing newmedical colleges without compromising on the quality of education, if so, the details thereof?
                                                





ANSWER




MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT(SHRI RAMESH POKHRIYAL ‘NISHANK’)<pre>(a) & (b) The information has been collected from Ministry of Health and Family Welfare. Ingovernment medical colleges, the Graduate and Post Graduate Medical seats are highlysubsidized.In the case of private unaided medical colleges, the fee structure is decided by the Committeeset up by the respective State Government under the Chairmanship of a retired High CourtJudge in pursuance of the directions of the Hon''ble Supreme Court of India. It is for theCommittee to decide whether the fee proposed by an Institute is justified and the fee fixed bythe Committee is binding on the Institute. As per Medical Council of India (MCI) regulations,private medical institutions shall pay stipend to post graduate students at par with Governmentcolleges in the State..2(c) For establishment of new medical colleges, regulations of Medical Council of India havebeen amended to provide the following relaxation:(i) Diplomate of National Board (DNB) qualification has been recognized for appointment asfaculty to take care of shortage of faculty.(ii) Enhancement of maximum intake capacity at MBBS level from 150 to 250.(iii) Relaxation in the norms of setting up of Medical College in terms of requirement for faculty,staff, bed strength and other infrastructure.(iv) Minimum requirement of land for establishment of medical college in metropolitan cities asnotified under Article 243P(c) of the Constitution of India has been dispensed with.(v) Consultant / Specialists working in District Hospital have been allowed to be designated asProfessor with experience of more than 18 years with 4 Research Publication and Associateprofessor with experience of more than 10 years with 2 Research Publication.(vi) Enhancement of age limit for appointment/ extension/ re-employment against posts ofteachers/dean/principal/ director in medical colleges upto 70 years.</pre>***
