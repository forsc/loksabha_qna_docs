                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2687






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

17.03.2022






Night Shelters




Rajeshbhai Naranbhai Chudasama













                                                    Mohanbhai Kalyanji Kundariya
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	whether the Government has introduced any scheme to provide night shelters to the homeless and other persons spending their nights on the city footpaths;(b)	if so, the details thereof;(c)	the amount of funds released under the scheme during the last five years, State-wise; (d)	the details of the cities where the said scheme is being run presently?






ANSWER




                                                                                THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE                        MINISTRY OF HOUSING AND URBAN AFFAIRS                            (SHRI KAUSHAL KISHORE)(a)&(b):	It is the primary responsibility of Governments of the States/ Union Territories to provide shelter for the urban homeless. However, in order to complement their efforts,Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs is administering a scheme ‘Shelter for Urban Homeless (SUH)’ under Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana– National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM), through respective States/UTs.It focuses on providing permanent shelters equipped with basic facilities to the urban homeless. 	As reported by States/UTs, so far, 2,414 shelters have been sanctioned, out of which, 1,678 shelters with a capacity of 96,386 persons are operational. (c):	Under DAY-NULM, funds are allocated to States/Uts in a consolidated manner, out of which appropriate amount is apportioned by States/Uts for Shelter for Urban Homeless (SUH) component, as per their requirements. During last five years i.e.FY 2016-17 to FY 2020-21, a total of ?2,792.07crore was released to States/Uts under DAY-NULM. The State/UT-wise information is at Annexure-I.(d):	The details are at Annexure-II.                               *****
