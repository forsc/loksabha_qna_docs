                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FINANCE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2142






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

08.03.2021






Financial Assistance / Loans to Tamil Nadu




Kani K. Navas














                        Will the Minister of





FINANCE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    a.	the details and number of people benefited by the financial assistance and loans in Tamil Nadu from the financial package released by the Union Government during Coronavirus pandemic and till date, district/ category-wise;b.	whether the Government had fixed any target for sanctioning loans by banks to various categories of people including cart-pullers and shopkeepers under the financial package for Coronavirus; pandemic and c.	if so, the details of circulars/orders issued in respect of Tamil Nadu in this regard? 






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (FINANCE)(SHRI ANURAG SINGH THAKUR)(a) to (c):   The Government had announced a Rs. 1.70 Lakh Crore ‘Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Yojana’ relief package on 26.03.2020 to combat the economic impact of COVID-19 on the poor and needy and to help them in the fight against Corona Virus.   The package comprised of schemes providing for insurance cover to health workers, additional allocation of foodgrain and pulses to PDS beneficiaries, release of ex-gratia of Rs. 500/- per month for three months to PMJDY women account holders, release of Rs.1000/- to around 3 crore aged widows and persons in Divyang category, free LPG cylinders to beneficiaries of Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana, release of 24% of the monthly wages into PF accounts of wage-earners earning below Rs 15,000 per month, in businesses with less than 100 workers, for three months to prevent disruption in employment and limit of collateral free lending from Rs. 10 to Rs. 20 lakh for women organized through 63 lakhs Self Help Groups who  support 6.85 crore households.  The Government of India had announced AatmaNirbhar Bharat Packages (ANB) 1.0, 2.0, and 3.0 on 13th  May, 2020 to 17th  May, 2020,  12th  October, 2020  and 12th November 2020 respectively.  Foodgrains & chana dal were also provided to migrants under Aatmanirbhar Bharat.  State Governments were directed to utilise funds under Welfare Fund for Building and Other Construction Workers to provide assistance and support to these workers to protect them against economic disruptions and also funds under District Mineral Fund (DMF) for supplementing and augmenting facilities of medical testing, screening and other requirements in connection with preventing the spread of COVID-19 pandemic as well as treating the patients affected with this pandemic.   A Statement indicating details of benefits provided to the State of Tamil Nadu is at Annexure-I.   Further, as per information provided by the National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company Ltd. (NCGTC), the implementing agency of the  Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme(ECLGS), as on 28.02.2021, a total amount of Rs.22,757.02 crore has been issued as guarantees to 5,54,884 borrowers in the State of Tamil Nadu under the scheme. The Government on 01.06.2020 also launched the  Pradhan Mantri Street Vendor’s AtmaNirbhar Nidhi (PM SVANidhi) for micro-credit facility to provide affordable loan of up to Rs.10,000/- to about 50 lakh Street Vendors working in Urban Areas who had their businesses operational on or before 24 March, 2020.   The scheme is valid until March 2022.  As on 05.03.2021, 38.78   lakh loan applications received, 22.31  lakh loan applications sanctioned and loans disbursed to 16.85 lakh applicants.  The State of Tamil Nadu has been given target of 2.62 lakh.  Against this, 2,52,151 loan applications have been received, 68,406 loan applications sanctioned and loans disbursed to 37,571 applicants.*****
