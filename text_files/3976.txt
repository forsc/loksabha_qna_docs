                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HOME AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3653






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

16.07.2019






e-Visa Facility at Airports




P. Ravindhranath














                        Will the Minister of





HOME AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has decided to introduce Electronic Travel Authorization facility (e-Visa) at nine airports across the country;(b) if so, the details thereof;(c) whether the Government is also planning to extend e-Visa facility in other airports; and(d) if so, the details thereof and the follow up action taken by the Government in this regard?






ANSWER




MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HOME AFFAIRS(SHRI NITYANAND RAI)(a) to (d) : The Government of India introduced e-Visa scheme on 27 Nov,2014.  It is presently available at 28 designated airports i.e. Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, Goa, Kolkata, Cochin, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad, Trivandrum, Calicut, Lucknow, Amritsar, Trichy, Jaipur, Mangalore, Pune, Nagpur, Coimbatore, Gaya, Bagdogra, Guwahati, Varanasi, Chandigarh, Vishakhapatnam, Madurai, Bhubaneswar and Port Blair, and 5 Seaports i.e. Mumbai, Cochin, Mangalore, Goa and Chennai.	  Extension of e-Visa facility to additional Immigration Check Posts (ICPs) is a continuous process which is done after appropriate consultations with all stakeholders and ensuring availability of necessary physical & electronic infrastructure and manpower.  *****
