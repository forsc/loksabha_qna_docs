                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RURAL DEVELOPMENT






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1340






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.07.2021






PMGSY Phase-III in Bihar




 Gopal Jee Thakur














                        Will the Minister of





RURAL DEVELOPMENT


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) 	whether many important rural roads have been constructed under Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana(PMGSY) phase-III;(b) 	whether the State of Bihar ranks at the bottom in the matter of road construction under PMGSY;(c) 	whether the Government has chalked out any road map to complete the work under PMGSY phase-III in Bihar on time so that the rural areas get the benefit of these roads quickly; and(d) 	if so, the details thereof and if not, the reasons therefor?






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF RURAL DEVELOPMENT (SADHVI NIRANJAN JYOTI)(a)       The Government has on 10th July, 2019 approved phase-III of PMGSY with an aim to consolidate 1,25,000 km Through Routes and Major Rural Links connecting habitations, inter-alia to Gramin Agricultural Markets (GrAMs), Higher Secondary School and Hospitals at an estimated cost of Rs 80,250 crore (Central Share- Rs. 53,800 crore, State Share- Rs. 26,450 crore). So far, 62,968.34 km of road length has been sanctioned under PMGSY-III to 16 States, against which 13,827.25 km has been completed. State-wise details of target, sanctioned and completed works under PMGSY-III as on 23.07.2021 is given at Annexure-I. (b) to (d)  Since inception of PMGSY, 7,67,784 km and 9,388 Long Span Bridges (LSBs) have been sanctioned to all the States/ UTs under all the interventions/ verticals of PMGSY, against which 6,70,340 km and 5,958 LSBs have been completed. State-wise details of the same as on 23.07.2021 is given at Annexure-II. The State of Bihar has been allocated a target of 6,162.50 km road length under PMGSY-III. The State has been sanctioned 1390.31 km of road length under PMGSY-III till 23.07.2021.As per the programme guidelines, the sanctioned works should be completed within a period of 12 working months from the date of issue of the work order. In this connection it has been further clarified in the programme guidelines that: where a package comprises more than one roadwork, the total time given for completion of the package shall not exceed 18 calendar months. Similarly, time period of 21-24 months would be allowed for completion of bridges exceeding 25-meter in length, depending on site conditions.  Accordingly Annual Action Plans are made for completion of roads. The same is constantly reviewed by the Ministry with the State Governments.PMGSY-III as a whole is targeted for completion by 31st March 2025 for all the States/ UTs.*****
