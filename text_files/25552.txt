                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1266






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.07.2021






PM Kisan Sampada Yojana




Sujay Radhakrishna Vikhepatil













                                                    Unmesh Bhaiyyasaheb Patil
                                                








                                                    Sambhajirao Mane Dhairyasheel
                                                








                                                    Heena Vijaykumar Gavit
                                                








                                                    Shrikant Eknath Shinde
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the number of Mega and Mini Food Parks, Cold ChainInfrastructure and Food Testing Laboratories established under the PM Kisan Sampada Yojana, State-wise including Maharashtra with reference to Ahmednagar, Nandurbar, Jalgaon, Kolhapur and Thane district;(b)	the details on the financial allocation and the investment made toimprove food processing infrastructure under the said Yojana, State-wise; (c) the details of the schemes under the Ministry for promoting and processing of citrus fruits and details of the funds released to Maharashtra under the same;(c)	whether the Government has made any assessment of the number offarmers benefited from this scheme, State-wise; and(d)	the details of the employment opportunities created especially in ruralareas through this scheme?  






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE FOR FOOD PROCESSING INDUSTRIES(SHRI PRAHLAD SINGH PATEL)(a):. The State-wise number of Mega Food Parks, Agro Processing clusters (Mini Food Parks), Integrated Cold Chains and Food Testing Laboratories approved/completed under respective component schemes of Pradhan Mantri Kisan SAMPADA Yojana (PMKSY) is placed at Annexure-I. Number of such projects approved in Ahmednagar, Nandurbar, Jalgaon, Kolhapur and Thane district of Maharashtra is placed at Annexure-II . (b): PMKSY is demand driven and state wise fund is not allocated under its component schemes. The Budget allocation and expenditure made under component schemes during last three Financial Years is placed at Annexure-III.(c) to (e) :  . Under component schemes of PMKSY, Ministry of Food Processing Industries (MoFPI) provides mostly credit linked grants-in-aid to entrepreneurs for setting up of food processing / preservation industries including Citrus fruit based Industries. The details of such projects located in Maharashtra along with estimation of farmers benefited is placed at Annexure-IV.                In the evaluation study of Integrated Cold Chain and Value Addition Infrastructure Scheme conducted by M/s NABARD Consultancy Limited (NABCONS) in Year 2020, it has been estimated that captive projects under the scheme have resulted in increase in farm-gate prices by 12.38 % and each project is estimated to benefit more than 9500 farmers and create about 600 direct/indirect employment.          Further, as part of Aatmanirbhar Bharat initiative, MoFPI is implementing centrally sponsored "PM Formalization of Micro Food Processing Enterprises (PMFME) Scheme" for providing financial, technical and business support for upgradation/ setting up of two lakh micro food processing units based on One District One Product (ODOP) approach in a period of five years from 2020-21 to 2024-25 with an outlay of Rs. 10,000 crores. Out of this, a total of 20,130 units have been allocated to Maharashtra for next five years.  Mandarin Orange in Amravati and Nagpur district and sweet orange in Jalna district of Maharashtra have been identified as ODOP and a total of Rs. 39.63 Cr. have been released to the State under the scheme so far. The scheme is expected to generate 9 lakh employment in the country.  ****
