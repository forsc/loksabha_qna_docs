                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

4539






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

20.03.2020






Patients of Silicosis




Manoj Rajoria














                        Will the Minister of





HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE be pleased to state:(a) the total number of patients of silicosis identified in the country, State/UT-wise;(b) whether the Government provides any sort of financial assistance to such patients and also in case of deaths occurring due to silicosis disease;(c) if so, the details thereof;(d) whether the silicosis disease happens predominantly to the labourers and workers engaged in mining professions and if so, the details thereof; and(e) the steps/measures taken/being taken by the Government towards creating awareness for its prevention and safeguards?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND FAMILY WELFARE(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a):	As per survey carried-out by the National Institute of Miners’ Health, Nagpur, during the years 2015 to 2019, 391 cases of silicosis have been detected. State/UT wise details are annexed.(b) & (c):	Compensation for Silicosis affected persons is given under Employee Compensation Act, 1923, which is administered by Compensation Commissioners of respective states.(d):	Any comparative data of silicosis between mining and other industries is not available. (e):	Following measures have been taken to protect the mine workers:-i.	Wet drilling.ii.	Periodic monitoring of dust levels at workplace.iii.	Personal Dust Exposure profile using Personal Dust Samplers.iv.	Use of Dust mask and Dust respirator as Personal Protective Equipments.v.	Initial Medical Examination before employment and periodical Medical Examinations of persons employed in mines at an interval of 5 years.vi.	Occupational Health Survey.vii.	Awareness Campaigns.
