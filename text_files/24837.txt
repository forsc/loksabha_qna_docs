                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

67






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

23.07.2021






National Medicinal Plant Board




Sudhakar Tukaram Shrangre













                                                    Naranbhai Bhikhabhai Kachhadiya
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





AYURVEDA,YOGA & NATUROPATHY,UNANI,SIDDHA AND HOMEOPATHY (AYUSH)


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    Will the Minister of AYUSH be pleased to state: (a) whether the National Medicinal Plant Board (NMPB) has formulated any new scheme to boost the cultivation and production of medicinal plants and herbs in the country; (b) if so, the details thereof; and (c) the fresh steps taken by the Government for development of quality planting material of medicinal plants and herbs in different agro-climatic zones?






ANSWER




                                                        ANSWERTHE MINISTER OF AYURVEDA, YOGA & NATUROPATHY, UNANI, SIDDHA AND HOMOEOPATHY(AYUSH) (SHRI SARBANANDA SONOWAL)(a)	to (c) A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO LOK SABHASTARRED QUESTION NO.67* FOR 23RD JULY, 2021(a): Yes, Sir, Ministry of AYUSH has prepared a draft scheme namely “Pradhan Mantri VRIKSH AYUSH Yojana” for the promotion of Medicinal Plant Cultivation, Post-harvest management and Marketing support by involving farmers and the AYUSH industry.(b): This proposal of ‘PRADHAN MANTRI VRIKSH-AYUSH YOJANA’ seeks approval of the Cabinet for implementation as a Central Sector Scheme. VRIKSH AYUSH aims to promote Cultivation of Medicinal Plant, Post-harvest management and Marketing support by involving farmers and the AYUSH industry.  It is expected to create assets in the form of plantation clusters of high value medicinal plants, which would ensure the supply of quality raw material to AYUSH industry while increasing the income of the farmers and local communities, including tribals. Under the proposed scheme, there is a provision for financial assistance to cultivate medicinal plants and establish Post-harvest Management Infrastructure. The proposed scheme shall be implemented as a Central Sector Scheme of Ministry namely “PRADHAN MANTRI VRIKSH-AYUSH YOJANA”. Proposal has been submitted for the approval of the Cabinet.(c): The National Medicinal Plants Board, Ministry of AYUSH is presently implementing Central Sector Scheme on Conservation, Development and Sustainable Management of Medicinal Plants under which there is a provision to provide project based financial support for establishing nursery and development of Quality Planting Material throughout the country. The State-wise number of projects supported for the development of Quality Planting Material from 2000 to 2021 is given at Annexure-I.In addition, the National Medicinal Plants Board, Ministry of AYUSH through its Regional Cum Facilitation Centres (RCFCs) is also providing financial and technical assistance to farmers/other stakeholders for development of Quality Planting Material of medicinal plants and herbs in different States. The detail of Quality Planting Material of Medicinal plants species developed, the number of training organised and farmers trained through Regional Cum Facilitation Centre Projects of NMPB since inception of Regional Cum Facilitation Centres i.e from year 2017-18 to 2020-21 is given at Annexure-II.NMPB has also signed MoU with National Botanical Research Institute-CSIR for  the development of Quality Planting Material of medicinal plants and herbs.
