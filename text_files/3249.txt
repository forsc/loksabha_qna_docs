                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

3024






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

11.07.2019






Vacant Posts in National Sports Universities




Deepak Baij














                        Will the Minister of





YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) the number of National Sports Universities functional in the country at present;(b) the number of posts of coaches lying vacant, State/University-wise;(c) the number of foreign coaches appointed in the country along with the details of their salary and allowances,State-wise;(d) the number of domestic coaches appointed in the country along with the details of their salary and allowances, Statewise;and(e) whether the Government proposes to promote domestic coaches over foreign coaches so as to provide opportunity to the talents of the country and if so, the reaction of the Government thereto?
                                                





ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE (INDEPENDENT CHARGE) FOR YOUTH AFFAIRS AND SPORTS(KIREN RIJIJU)(a): There is only one National Sports University (NSU) in the country which is being set up in Manipur (Imphal).(b): The post of coaches are not sanctioned State / University-wise. The stage of requirement of coaches in NSU has not yet come.(c): Central Government does not appoint coaches for States. The list of foreign coaches appointed on contract by Sports Authority of India (SAI) for National Sports Federations (NSFs) is at Annexure.(d): The Central Government does not appoint coaches for States. SAI has 882 regular and 126 contract coaches presently working for its various training facilities. Regular appointees are working in regular pay scale while present monthly remuneration of contract coaches ranges from Rs. 38,590/- to Rs. 61,150/-.(e): Development of coaches and improving their competencies are ongoing processes. Central Government is supporting NSFs under Human Resource Development Scheme (HRDS) for improving competency of Indian coaches. In the Annual Calendar for Training and Competitions (ACTC) meetings with various NSFs it has been impressed upon them to work out appropriate training competencies and upgradation programmes of Indian coaches. National Institute of Sports, Patiala and National Sports University, Manipur are running academic programmes for developing coaches. Government has taken a decision to set up National Centre for Sports Coaching.*****
