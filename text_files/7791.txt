                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    EXTERNAL AFFAIRS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1509






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

27.11.2019






Indians as Hostages




Jyotirmay Singh Mahato














                        Will the Minister of





EXTERNAL AFFAIRS


                        be pleased to state:-















(a) 	the number of incidents in which Indian citizens have been taken hostage on foreign lands during each of the last two years and which have come to the knowledge of the Government;(b) 	the respective number of Indians rescued and brought back to India who had been stranded in conflict zones including that in South Sudan where Indians had been caught in the war between the Government and the rebels a few years back; and (c) 	whether the Government has provided aid/ relief to the people affected due to such incidents and if so, the details thereof including the cost incurred thereto?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE IN THE MINISTRY OF EXTERNAL AFFAIRS[SHRI V. MURALEEDHARAN](a)  	As per available information, 65 Indians have been taken hostage in various countries during the last two years and the current year. Country-wise list is given at Annexure.(b) & (c)	During the last few years, Government has undertaken special operations to facilitate return of Indian nationals from conflict and disaster affected regions as per details given below. The safety and security of Indian nationals in foreign countries is a matter of foremost concern for the Government of India. The Government keeps a close watch on the situation in strife-torn countries and makes every effort to provide relief to the affected people and evacuate them.<pre>S. No.CountryNumber of IndiansAssistance details1Ukraine1,082 Indian medical students 1082 Indian medical students were evacuated by from Luhansk and Donetsk region of eastern Ukraine in 2014 and repatriated to India2Libya3,776 Indians3776 Indians were evacuated by providing them assistance during 2014 – 173Iraq7,195 IndiansIn 2014, 7,195 Indian nationals were provided assistance for travelling back to India including through air tickets to about 6,195 workers.4Iraq39 IndiansA group of 39 Indian workers were reportedly kidnapped by the ISIS terrorists during June 2014 from Mosul in Iraq.  Their mortal remains were later found buried in the areas recaptured by the Government of Iraq from the ISIS during late 2017.  The mortal remains were brought back in April 2018 and handed over to the families. 5Yemen6710 persons including 1947 foreigners 6710 persons were rescued from Yemen under Operation Rahat in March – April 20156South Sudan153 Indians and 2 citizens of Nepal153 Indians and 2 citizens of Nepal were rescued from South Sudan under Operation Sankat Mochan in July 2016</pre>Annexure to Lok Sabha Unstarred Question No. 1509LSFAQ 1509 reg Indians as Hostages during the period 2017 to 2019<pre>SNoName of CountryNumber of Indians Kidnapped/held hostages in foreign countries 1Afghanistan 92Cuba23Iraq14Kenya105Myanmar56Nigeria327Serbia18South Africa39South Sudan2Total65</pre>***
