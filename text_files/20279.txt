                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    RAILWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





STARRED   


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

124






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

10.02.2021






New Railway Lines




Kapil Moreshwar Patil














                        Will the Minister of





RAILWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the details of the new railway lines proposed in the last Budget;(b)	the status of the said railway lines; and(c)	the time by which the work on the said railway lines is likely to be completed? 






ANSWER




                                                        MINISTER OF RAILWAYS, COMMERCE & INDUSTRY ANDCONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD & PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI PIYUSH GOYAL)(a) to (c): A Statement is laid on the Table of the House.STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PARTS (a) TO (c) OF STARRED QUESTION NO.124 BY SHRI KAPIL MORESHWAR PATIL TO BE ANSWERED IN LOK SABHA ON 10.02.2021 REGARDING NEW RAILWAY LINES(a) & (b): During 2014-20, 58 numbers of New Line projects totalling 4,626 Km and costing `81,639 crore have been included in Budget subject to requisite approvals.Presently, 189 New Line projects totaling 21,343 km length and costing `4,04,853 crore are in different stages of planning/approval/execution.Considering the huge shelf of ongoing New Line projects, focus of Ministry of Railways is to complete the existing New Line projects. During 2014-20, 3395 km New Line projects have been commissioned. During 2020-21, one Umbrella Work of New Line costing `10,000 crore was included in Budget against which one project of Mahisasan (India) to Zero point (Bangladesh) has been sanctioned at a cost of `38.88 crore.(c):	Completion of Railway projects depend on many factors such as land acquisition, statutory clearances from various authorities, shifting of utilities, law and order situation in the project area and other factors beyond the control of Ministry of Railways.*****
