                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

2894






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

15.12.2021






Setting up of Hallmarking Centres




Kotagiri Sridhar













                                                    Goddeti Madhavi
                                                








                                                    Magunta Sreenivasulu Reddy
                                                












                        Will the Minister of





CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a)	the progress achieved in setting up of Hallmarking centres under Bureau of Indian Standards in 256 districts identified under mandatory hallmark testing; (b)	whether the Government is contemplating to expand mandatory hallmarking of gold jewellery to other districts and to set up such centres accross the country; and (c) 	if so, the details thereof?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF STATE CONSUMER AFFAIRS, FOOD AND PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION(SHRI ASHWINI KUMAR CHOUBEY)(a)       :	On 23 June 2021 Hallmarking was made mandatory in 256 districts. On the said date there were 941 Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) recognized assaying and hallmarking centres in the country and each of these 256 districts had at least one BIS recognized assaying and hallmarking centre. As on 25 November 2021, there are 976 BIS recognized assaying and hallmarking centres all over India and 10 more centres have been set up in the 256 districts where hallmarking has been made mandatory.(b) to (c) :	An Advisory Committee comprising officers from the relevant departments and representatives of stakeholders like jewellers, assaying &hallmarking centres, consumer organizations etc. has been constituted by the Government to examine various issues related to mandatory hallmarking across the country, including bringing in more districts under the fold of mandatory hallmarking.Govt. also provides subsidy for setting up assaying and hallmarking centres at deficient locations.*****
