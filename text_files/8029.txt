                                    GOVERNMENT OF INDIA
                                





                                    MINISTRY OF
                                    ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS






                                    LOK SABHA
                                





UNSTARRED 


                                    QUESTION NO:
                                

1623






                                    ANSWERED ON: 
                                

28.11.2019






Linking of NHs with other Roads




Raghu Ramakrishna Raju Kanumuru














                        Will the Minister of





ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS


                        be pleased to state:-















                                                    (a) whether the Government has taken up the work of inter-linking of district and State roads with National Highways (NHs) in the country and if so, the details thereof;(b) the number of road projects declared as NHs in the State of Andhra Pradesh;(c) whether the Government proposes to connect all State roads and district roads with NHs and if so, the details thereof; and(d) the funds sanctioned and released by the Government for the purpose so far?






ANSWER




                                                        THE MINISTER OF ROAD TRANSPORT AND HIGHWAYS(SHRI NITIN JAIRAM GADKARI)(a) to (d) The Government had approved the proposal for declaration of about 7,200 km length of State Roads as National Highways (NHs) in February, 2014 to facilitate connectivity to the District Headquarters with the NH and few stretches from the strategic consideration.About 11,870 km length of State Roads have been declared as NHs since then for providing connectivity to District Headquarters.		The Ministry considers declaration of some State Roads as new NHs from time to time based on requirement of connectivity, inter-se priority and availability of funds. Total Length of NHs in the country have been increased from about 91,287 km in April, 2014 to about 1,32,500 km at present. The length of NHs in the State of Andhra Pradesh (before bifurcation of the State of Telangana) was about 6,590 km in April, 2014. The length of NHs in the States of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana has been increased to about 6,913 km and 3,795 km respectively. The development and maintenance of NHs is a continuous process. The works are accordingly taken up on NHs from time to time as per inter-se-priority, traffic density, and availability of funds to keep the NHs in traffic worthy conditions. The State/ Union Territory (UT)/ Agency-wise funds allocated and expenditure incurred  for development of NHs during last three years and current year is annexed.
